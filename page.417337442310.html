5ZURICH (AP) -- FIFA President Sepp Blatter chaired an emergency meeting with continental soccer bodies Thursday while staying out of public view himself.
6Blatter called together leaders of FIFA's regions as world soccer is rocked by an American federal racketeering case.
7''There was a meeting today with the president with the representatives from the confederations to discuss the current situation,'' FIFA spokeswoman Delia Fischer said in a statement.
8The North American body, known as CONCACAF, said it was not represented at the meeting.
9CONCACAF President Jeffrey Webb, a FIFA vice president, was among seven men arrested in an early morning raid at a luxury hotel Wednesday by Swiss police at the request of U.S. authorities.
10Blatter is resisting calls from European soccer body UEFA to postpone Friday's FIFA presidential election by six months.
11UEFA has called the dual American and Swiss federal probes a ''disaster for FIFA,'' and is supporting Blatter's election opponent, FIFA vice president Prince Ali bin al-Hussein of Jordan.
12Blatter has continued to avoid appearing in public since Swiss federal agencies raided FIFA headquarters and the Baur au Lac hotel.
13He missed giving a scheduled speech to open a session of FIFA's medical conference in a Zurich hotel - his third skipped public appearance within 24 hours.
14Two FIFA vice presidents were among seven men arrested at the request of the U.S. Department of Justice.
15In a separate investigation, Swiss federal agencies seized evidence at FIFA headquarters for a probe of the 2018-2022 World Cup bidding contests.
16Blatter skipped two meetings of continental soccer organizations from Africa and South America on Wednesday.
17He typically would attend those ahead of Friday's annual congress of FIFA's 209 member federations.
18Late Wednesday, FIFA issued a statement on behalf of Blatter where the president insisted he was determined to root out misconduct.
19The FIFA president would also usually follow protocol and attend Thursday's UEFA meeting.
20He is scheduled to appear later in the day.
21Blatter is also expected to address member federations - the FIFA election voters - at the opening ceremony of the congress at 5 p.m. (1500 GMT).
