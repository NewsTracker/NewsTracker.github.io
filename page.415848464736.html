Court hears expert support for Bali Nine pair appeal

1An expert witness has argued for the death row challenge of Andrew Chan and Myuran Sukumaran to be heard in an Indonesian administrative court, testifying it is the correct forum.
2The court last month threw out a challenge against the rejection of clemency for the Bali Nine pair, determining the decrees by President Joko Widodo were not within its jurisdiction.
3The men sentenced for heroin smuggling in 2006 have no other legal avenues left and have resorted to the administrative court appeal to spare them from the firing squad.
4Otong Rosadi of Ekasakti University on Monday gave his opinion in support of the challenge.
5The law expert said in his opinion, all "products of the law" can be reviewed, even clemency, which he argued was not purely a matter of presidential prerogative.
6The constitutional rights of the president were still a product of the law, he said.
7"The legal product is the presidential decree and a presidential decree rejecting or granting clemency, it's a state administrative matter," he told the court in Jakarta.
8"The forum to challenge it is the state administrative court."
9Mr Otong maintained this view under questioning from lawyers for the state and judges, who asked if other constitutional rights of the president could also therefore be challenged in the administrative court.
10Lawyer for Chan and Sukumaran, Leonard Arpan, said he was confident Mr Otong's evidence was strong.
11"I think the expert has delivered all the statements that were required," he told reporters.
12Both sides are due to give their conclusions on Wednesday for a decision soon afterwards.
13If they are given the chance, lawyers for Chan and Sukumaran will argue President Joko Widodo didn't properly assess their case - including their rehabilitation - before refusing clemency because they are drug offenders.
14Several of the 10 prisoners in line for Indonesia's planned simultaneous mass execution are pursuing court action.
15Indonesia's Attorney-General HM Prasetyo has committed not to execute them before their legal avenues are exhausted, and has ordered those cases before the Supreme Court to be expedited.
16The 10th prisoner, Filipina Mary Jane Fiesta Veloso, will soon join the others on Nusakambangan island as her bid for a Supreme Court judicial review was rejected last week after a matter of days.
17The legal appeals of some of the death row prisoners Indonesia plans to execute alongside two Australians are being rushed through the courts.
18The move comes at the request of Indonesia's Attorney General H.M. Prasetyo, who wants to settle on a date for the firing squad.
19Myuran Sukumaran and Andrew Chan's execution on Nusakambangan island has stalled while Jakarta waits for them and other death row drug offenders to use up their legal avenues.
20Of the 10 awaiting the firing squad, four are pursuing cases in the administrative court and three are seeking Supreme Court judicial reviews - a move that has already failed for the Bali Nine pair.
21Mr Prasetyo's office on Thursday confirmed the judicial review application of a Filipino woman had been rejected after only days before the Supreme Court.
22It usually takes three months to consider such applications but Mary Jane Fiesta Veloso's was distributed to three judges on March 20 and rejected on Wednesday.
23Veloso was considered to have a good chance for a review and it was thought this would delay the executions for some months, as Indonesia insists on sending 10 prisoners to the firing squad simultaneously.
24Mr Prasetyo says he has negotiated with the Supreme Court to ensure the fastest turnaround.
25"It means there will be no more legal avenues left."
26The other two prisoners seeking judicial reviews are Frenchman Serge Atlaoui and Ghanian Martin Anderson.
27Chan and Sukumaran have an appeal in the administrative court, that if won, would see their lawyers argue the blanket rejection of clemency did not follow due process.
28President Joko Widodo is denying clemency to all death row drug offenders, regardless of rehabilitation or other factors, believing it will shock Indonesia out of its drug problem.
29An expert witness for Chan and Sukumaran's defence could not attend court on Wednesday and was scheduled for Monday.
30The attorney-general says the case is "unusual".
31"The last I heard, the two Bali Nine convicts couldn't bring their expert witness to prove their reasoning," he reportedly said.
32"Their challenge of clemency is unusual.
33"It's the president's discretion and so there shouldn't be anyone who can prevent it."
34Lawyers for Veloso thought she had a good chance for a judicial review because she did not have a qualified translator at her trial.
35The 30-year-old single mother was arrested at a central Java airport in 2010, with 2.6kg of heroin in her suitcase.
36Her lawyers and family say she's an unwitting victim of an international syndicate.
37Myuran Sukumaran and Andrew Chan's lawyers are set to make their case for an appeal of the presidential decree that denied them clemency.
