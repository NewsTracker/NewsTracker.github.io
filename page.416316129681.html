White House urges Greece to reach agreement on bailout terms with the EU

1The US administration has urged Greece to move quickly with negotiations and reach an agreement with the EU on terms for its bailout.
2A failure could lead to a crisis in the world economy.
3The Obama administration on Friday requested Athens to speed up negotiations on terms for its bailout agreement, with President Obama saying that Greece would also have to initiate change.
4"Greece needs to initiate reforms. They have to collect taxes. They have to reduce their bureaucracy. They have to have more flexible labor practices," German press agency dpa reported Obama as saying.
5He was speaking at a White House press conference with Italian Prime Minister Matteo Renzi.
6Separately, the US Treasury Secretary Jack Lew said in a statement that Greece needed to quickly negotiate with the International Monetary Fund (IMF) and the European Union (EU) and that a failure to do so would threaten the global economy.
7"We urge the [Greek] authorities to quickly and fully commit to technical negotiations with their international partners," Lew said at an IMF steering committee meeting on Friday in Washington.
8"Not reaching an agreement would create immediate hardship for Greece and uncertainties for Europe and the global economy more broadly," Lew added.
9Experts say the country could face a debt default this month if it does not submit a detailed list of reforms that satisfies its debtors enough to make them unlock 7.2 billion euros ($ 7.6 billion)- the last tranche of the 240-billion-euro bailout.
10Greece's Finance Minister Yanis Varoufakis recently refused an agreement on his country's debt deal as far as it was linked with unreachable economic targets.
11Varoufakis and Greek Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras' Syriza party came to power recently amid promises to undo harsh austerity measures the previous government had implemented.
12Responding to Syriza's concerns, Obama said on Friday that his government recognized the "need to show your people there's hope and that you can grow, and we will be supportive of some flexibilities in how you move forward so you can make investments."
13However, Greece needed to show some "flexibility in meeting fiscal targets," the president added.
14Greek Finance Minister Yanis Varoufakis has rejected both an exit from the eurozone and an agreement on its debt with unreachable economic targets.
15Meanwhile, the IMF refused a delay in Greek debt repayments.
16Greece is set to present a series of proposals to international creditors in a bid to try to unblock a tranche of loans and avoid a debt default.
17Germany has said it is opposed to giving Athens any more loans.
18Greece has agreed to repay its debt to the International Monetary Fund due this week, according to the Fund's director.
19Christine Lagarde held informal discussions with Greece's finance minister in Washington.
20Glimmers of hope in Brussels as Greece is said to formally ask its EU creditors to extend its financial lifeline.
21But Germany remains wary of a deal, saying new loans cannot be strings-free.
