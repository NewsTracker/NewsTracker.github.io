Egypt militants behind Cairo blast name new leader

1The group known as Ajnad Misr, or "Egypt's Soldiers," said on its Twitter account late Thursday that Izzeddin al-Masri has succeeded Hammam Mohamed Attia, who was killed Sunday, just hours before a bomb on a bridge in central Cairo killed a policeman and wounded at least two passers-by.
2The group also pledged to continue its jihad, or holy war.
3Ajnad Misr has carried out several attacks on security forces, especially after the 2013 military overthrow of Islamist President Mohammed Morsi.
4Militant groups say they are avenging a security crackdown on Islamists, while authorities blame Morsi's supporters for the violence.
5Most of the deadliest attacks have been claimed by a Sinai-based group previously known as Ansar Beit al-Maqdis.
6The organization pledged allegiance to the Islamic State group last year and now refers to itself as the Sinai Province of the organization's self-styled caliphate.
7The IS affiliate late Thursday claimed responsibility for the killing of two police officers in the restive northern Sinai Peninsula the day before.
8Security officials said the two officers were killed when their military vehicle hit a roadside bomb outside the provincial capital, el-Arish.
9The Sinai Province said in a statement posted late Thursday on its Twitter account that the attack was a "prompt retaliation for the random shelling by the renegade army of Muslims."
10It said the shelling on Wednesday killed 13 civilians.
11Military officials say a mortar struck a residential area south of Sheikh Zweid, a town in northern Sinai, on Wednesday, hitting a house and killing nine of its residents.
12In a separate incident, a missile landed on a house in another village, killing two civilians, according to the officials.
