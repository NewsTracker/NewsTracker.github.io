11CANNES, France - The 68th Cannes Film Festival was brought to a surprising close Sunday with Jacques Audiard's Sri Lankan refugee film taking the festival's coveted top honor, the Palme d'Or.
12The choice of "Dheepan," by a jury led by Joel and Ethan Coen, left some critics scratching their heads.
13While the dapper French filmmaker has drawn widespread acclaim for films such as "A Prophet" and "Rust and Bone," some critics were disappointed by the climax of Audiard's film.
14"Dheepan" is about three Sri Lankans who pretend to be a family to flee their war-torn country for a housing project in France.
15"This isn't a jury of film critics," Joel Coen said after the awards ceremony, alongside fellow jurors such as director Guillermo del Toro and actor Jake Gyllenhaal.
16"This is a jury of artists who are looking at the work."
17The win for "Dheepan" comes at a time when Europe is particularly attuned to the experience of immigrants, after the recent deaths of hundreds crossing the Mediterranean trying to get to Italy.
18The runner-up prize, the Grand Prix, went to "Son of Saul," a grim Holocaust drama by first-time Hungarian director Laszlo Nemes.
19Masterful Taiwanese filmmaker Hou Hsiao-Hsien, 68, won best director for his first feature in eight years: "The Assassin," a lush, painterly martial arts drama.
20The best actress prize was split and given to both Rooney Mara, half of the romantic pair of Todd Haynes' lesbian drama "Carol," and Emmanuelle Bercot, the French star of the roller-coaster marriage drama "My King."
21Best actor was awarded to Vincent Lindon, the veteran French actor of Stephane Brize's "The Measure of a Man."
22Greek filmmaker Yorgos Lanthimos took the jury prize for his "The Lobster," a deadpan dystopian comedy starring Colin Farrell and Rachel Weisz.
