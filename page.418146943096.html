Google denied appeal in Oracle software fight

1WASHINGTON - The U.S. Supreme Court on Monday sided with Oracle Corp. and declined to hear Google Inc.'s appeal in a major software copyright case that has been closely followed by the technology industry.
2The decision leaves intact a ruling which allows Oracle to charge licensing fees for the use of some of its Java programming language.
3Google had argued it should be free to use Java without paying a fee.
4The case involves how much copyright protection should extend to Java.
5Google, which used Java to design its Android smartphone operating system, said in court papers that an Oracle victory would obstruct "an enormous amount of innovation" because software developers would not be able to freely build on each others' work.
6But Oracle said effective copyright protection is the key to software innovation.
7A Google representative did not immediately comment on the court's decision, and an Oracle spokeswoman could not immediately be reached.
8Google's Android operating system is the world's best-selling smartphone platform.
9Oracle sued Google in 2010, and is seeking roughly $1 billion on its copyright claims.
10Shares of Google were down about 0.7 percent at $527.9 in early trading, while Oracle was down about 0.43 percent at $40.8.
11The Obama administration, which was asked to weigh in on the issue earlier this year, asked the Supreme Court not to take the case.
12The litigation now returns to a San Francisco federal court for more proceedings.
13The case is Google v.
14Oracle, U.S. Supreme Court, No.
1514-410.
