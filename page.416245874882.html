2 Years After Marathon Bombing, We Are ‘One Boston’

1But, no matter how you measure, Bostonians were forever changed on April 15, 2013, when two bombs killed three people, and injured more than 280 others.
2A few days later, MIT Police Officer Sean Collier was killed.
3At 2:49 p.m., the time the first bomb went off, the city will hold a moment of silence to mark the two-year anniversary of the attack.
4After the moment of silence, church bells will ring out across the city.
5We can mark the time that has passed, and count the money that's been raised.
6What's harder to measure is the way we feel.
7Today, Bostonians will something positive to tally: acts of kindness.
8April 15 marks the first "One Boston Day," which, according to its website, "will celebrate the resiliency, generosity, and strength of the people that make Boston the great city it is."
9The idea for One Boston Day came when many survivors expressed a desire to spread the good will and support they received after the 2013 marathon.
10Bostonians are encouraged to share what they'll be doing to give back by using the hashtag #OneBostonDay.
11One Boston Day comes exactly four months after One Fund Boston stopped accepting donations.
12The charity, which was formed by Gov. Deval Patrick and the late Mayor Thomas Menino the day after the bombings, raised nearly $80 million for victims and their families.
13Even with an emphasis on kindness, the anniversary is painful.
14The Boston Public Health Commission released a list of tips for coping with the bombing's second anniversary.
15The list, which was adapted from the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration, encourages people to "do what you want to do, rather than what you think you should do," and notes that "helping others might help you."
16But, the list also notes, "There is no need to talk about a distressing event unless you want to."
17And part of the way we remember is in silence.
18On Monday, about 32,500 people will run 26.2 miles in the 2015 Boston Marathon.
19They will run for charities.
20They will run to remember.
21They will run because two years, $80 million in donations, one trial, and 30 guilty charges later, we remain one Boston.
