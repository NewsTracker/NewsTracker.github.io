Baby born in field hospital after Nepal earthquake

1KATHMANDU, Nepal (AP) - After almost a week of heartbreaking news from earthquake-hit Nepal, there was a small but beautiful moment of joy.
2A baby girl was born Friday to a young Nepalese couple in one of the field hospitals set up by foreign nations to bring emergency aid to the country.
3Both the mother and the baby, who hasn't been named yet, are doing well.
4Nepalese woman Lata Chand,19, looks at her newly born baby girl at the Israeli field hospital in Kathmandu, Nepal, Friday, May 1, 2015.
5Lata Chand, 19, was heavily pregnant when the 7.8-magnitude earthquake struck last Saturday.
6She and her husband ran out of their house in panic.
7Their home was undamaged, but the hospital where she was to give birth was forced to close.
8On Friday they went instead to a field hospital set up by the Israeli military where the baby was born.
9The beaming midwife, Dganit Gery, said she hoped the birth would show all Nepalese women that there is hope for the future.
10Lata's husband Hariender Chand said they were terrified the quake would cause her to miscarry.
11"When the quake struck I was thinking 'will we survive?' because most of the pregnant women miscarried their babies. I was scared it would happen to us. Now we're safe, it's good."
12Nepalese woman Lata Chand,19, looks at her newly born baby girl as her husband Hariender Chand, 25, sits beside them at the Israeli field hospital in Kathmandu, Nepal, Friday, May 1, 2015.
