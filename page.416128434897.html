APPLE WATCH LIVE: Try-on visits, online orders begin

1From Beijing to Paris to San Francisco, the Apple Watch made its debut Friday.
2Customers were invited to try them on in stores and order them online.
3The watch is Apple's first new product category since the iPad came out five years ago.
4Analysts are waiting to see how well the watch will sell beyond devoted Apple fans.
5Apple has a better chance at succeeding than any other smartwatch maker so far, yet it will likely take time before sales reach the kind of numbers that Apple gets for iPhones and iPads.
6Watch prices start at $349, but can go as high as $17,000 for a luxury edition in gold.
7People can try the watch on in Apple stores, but for now all orders are being handled online.
8Shipments begin April 24.
9It's available in the U.S. and eight other markets around the world.
10In the U.S., the watch is available only in Apple stores.
11In some countries, select department stores and resellers also have it.
12In London's Covent Garden, a tourist hotspot, a buzz was growing in the Apple flagship store as dozens of fans came to check out the new gadget.
13Some crouched over glass counters to play with sample watches, while others who had pre-booked appointments got to try them on their wrists.
14"I've been waiting for this for a long time," said Carl Walsh, a 43-year-old company director.
15"It's beautifully developed, but I'll probably want to wait a bit and see what people say about the battery life."
16Regy Selsaas, 42, came to see if the watch would make a good gift for his wife.
17"It's more like a gadget than a phone," he said, wincing at the high price tag of the luxury version.
18"It's really beautiful but expensive. I'm not 100 percent convinced."
19Jay Carroll, 15, needed no persuading.
20He and his mother Sarah had placed an online order first thing Friday, but the two still wanted to try it out in store.
21"I'm looking forward to just having it there on my wrist, so I can be on my phone all the time," he said.
22The curious in Japan formed a long line in Isetan department store, where a special section was built just for the Apple watch.
23Only 20 customers were allowed in at a time, and only those with advance reservations or who showed up early enough to get one of 76 lottery tickets got to try the watch on.
24The rest could only look at a display of 19 watches under a glass showcase.
25They ranged in price from about 43,000 yen ($360) to 2,800,000 yen ($23,300) for the luxury edition in gold.
26In central Shanghai, potential Apple watch buyers stood in lines two to five people long over their lunch hour at an Apple store on Friday to try on the watch many said they already planned to buy.
27"It was beautifully made, like an expensive watch," said Li Hao, 27, a Web designer who owns a Mac, an iPad and an Apple TV.
28He has just traded up from an iPhone 4 to the new iPhone 6 Plus.
29China was among countries where the watch had its global debut Friday, reflecting the country's fast-growing status as one of Apple's most important markets.
30Li said he planned to buy the sport version of the watch at about 3,000 yuan ($500).
31"I cannot do sports with the mobile phone," he said.
32"I need a machine to record what I did and a screen to look at."
33Qi Tian, 26, who works in human resources for a real estate company, said he was "not a big fan" of Apple, though he owns four or five products.
34He said he planned to order a watch online that day.
