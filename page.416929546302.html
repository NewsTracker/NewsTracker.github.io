New Nepal earthquake: Magnitude 7.4 quake strikes Nepal

1A Map posted by the US Geological Survey indicating where the earthquake hit on May 12.
2A magnitude 7.4 (revised to 7.1) earthquake struck the Nepal-Tibet border on Tuesday, the US Geological Survey said.
3The quake, which struck near the base camp for Everest, 22k south-east of Zham, China, and 83km east of Kathmandu, Nepal, was measured at a shallow depth of about 18.5 km (revised from 10km).
4The European-Mediterranean Seismological Centre said an earthquake of magnitude 7.1 hit Nepal.
5Wire agencies are reporting the quake caused people to run out of buildings in the capital Kathmandu and shook structures as far as Delhi, India.
6The impoverished country has not yet recovered from a devastating 7.8-magnitude earthquake that struck on April 25, killing at least 8046 people and injuring more than 17,800.
7The Joint Australian Tsunami Warning Centre said it detected a magnitude 5.6 earthquake at 5:22pm AEST in the Banda Sea off Indonesia, that was felt near Darwin, Australia.
8The USGS reported that event as a 5.4 magnitude quake, at a depth of 154.7km, 427km east-northeast of Dili, East Timor.
9It issued a "no tsunami threat" alert to the Australian mainland, islands and territories.
10Rameshwor Dangal, an official at Nepal's Home Ministry, confirmed the strong tremor to Bloomberg.
11The South Asia region has a history of catastrophic earthquakes because the tectonic plate that carries the Indian subcontinent is pushing northward into the main Asian plate.
