A look at the knowns and unknowns in the Baltimore case

1BALTIMORE (AP) - A week and a half after 25-year-old Freddie Gray died, there are still many unanswered questions about the explosive case.
2WHY DID POLICE STOP HIM?
3Police say they went after him after he fled at the sight of an officer in a drug-infested neighborhood.
4Why he ran is anyone's guess.
5Police say they later found a switchblade in his pocket.
6During the arrest, officers on bicycles chased him down, pinned him to the sidewalk and then lifted him and took him, his legs dragging on the ground, to a police van.
7He pleaded for an inhaler during the arrest and asked repeatedly for medical help during the half-hour ride to the station.
8He died a week later.
9Police say Gray died of a "significant spinal injury."
10An attorney for the family says his spine was "80 percent severed in the neck area."
11An autopsy has been done, and while the findings have not been released, police say no bodily injury was evident during the post mortem and no limbs were broken.
12SO HOW AND WHEN DID THE INJURY HAPPEN?
13That's the big question.
14It's not clear whether he got hurt during the arrest, the ride, or both.
15Police say he was in leg irons and handcuffs in the van but was not belted in, a violation of policy.
16That has raised suspicions that Gray was deliberately taken on a "rough ride" to teach him a lesson and was injured when he got thrown around the back of the vehicle.
17Police say they have interviewed a second, unidentified man who was in the van, and he said the driver wasn't driving erratically.
18Police say this man didn't see anyone harm Gray but heard Gray "thrashing about."
19WHAT ABOUT THE OFFICERS INVOLVED?
20Police have suspended six in the meantime, and five of them have given statements to investigators.
21The sixth declined to do so.
22All of them joined the force between 1997 and 2012.
23Baltimore police are conducting an internal investigation and say they will share their findings by Friday with the Baltimore state's attorney for possible prosecution.
24It is not known when or if those findings will be made public.
25The U.S. Justice Department is also investigating, as is the Gray family attorney.
