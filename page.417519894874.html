In Trinidad, Former FIFA Executive Seen as 'Our Robin Hood'

1In one moment, Jack Warner is on TV telling his countrymen he fears for his life.
2An hour later, he's standing on a packed narrow street at a political rally, boasting that he fears nothing.
3Contradictions are a constant for Warner, who grew up with almost nothing in Trinidad and Tobago, and became a rich and powerful vice president of FIFA, world soccer's governing body.
4He rubbed elbows with world leaders on a regular basis before being forced out in an ethics scandal four years ago.
5Indicted last week by the U.S. on charges of racketeering, wire fraud and money-laundering, Warner scoffs at the accusations, insisting he's done nothing wrong.
6In Trinidad, they've heard it all before, though many residents say if Warner amassed riches without taking it from them, they're fine with the arrangement.
7"He is our Robin Hood," said Eraj Sagewan, a taxi driver in the capital of Port-of- Spain.
8That's how many in Trinidad see the 72-year-old Warner, now a member of Parliament.
9If he stole from the rich and gave to the poor, then they see no harm done.
10He is simultaneously a villain and a hero, known for getting what he wants, but also for personally arranging whatever help - doctors, food, anything - his constituents need.
11"If he didn't live so long, he would have died a hero," said Sunity Maharaj, a journalist who has long followed Warner.
12"He would have been the story of the little boy who grew up to be FIFA vice president."
13Warner doesn't hide his hubris and says the world's perception of him is nowhere near the reality.
14"Everybody knows Jack Warner is bad news. Jack Warner has Ebola," he says to an ovation - bolstered by piped-in cheers from an elaborate sound system.
15The rally starts late, with about 50 people.
16The neighborhood's barking dogs are louder than the crowd.
17Locals set up tables to sell crackers and snow cones.
18After an hour or so, Warner arrives, wearing the lime green colors of his Independent Liberal Party.
19The street is jammed, with the crowd having grown fivefold and police help with traffic and crowd control.
20The dogs can't be heard anymore.
21"I do this for you!"
22Warner proclaims.
23Said Ria Bisnath, an observer: "If this was America, you would call Jack Warner a rock star."
24His future is uncertain, something Warner acknowledges.
25The charges that revolve around allegations of bribery are the most serious he has faced, although he insists they are trumped up.
26"Nobody, no contractor, alive or dead, can say he gave me a kickback," Warner said.
27"Everything I have now, I had before politics. But there are some guys in politics now who had nothing before. How come nobody is concerned with that? ... But they're concerned about FIFA's money because they feel that that will make Jack Warner fall."
28U.S. prosecutors allege South Africa paid $10 million in 2008 to Warner and two other FIFA executive committee members as payback for supporting that nation's successful bid to host the 2010 World Cup over Morocco.
29South African officials say the $10 million was paid, though it does not characterize it as a bribe, calling it an "above-board payment" to help soccer development in the Caribbean region.
