1US Vice President Joe Biden "strongly condemned the violation of the ceasefire by separatist forces acting in concert with Russian forces, in and around the town of Debaltseve", the White House said in a statement Tuesday after he spoke to Ukrainian President Petro Poroshenko.
2"If Russia continues to violate the Minsk agreements, including the most recent agreement signed on February 12, the costs to Russia will rise," the White House statement added.
3Ukraine earlier appealed to the West to get "tough" on Russia after pro-Moscow separatists stormed the flashpoint town to attack thousands of government troops, violating the three-day-old ceasefire.
4But Russian President Vladimir Putin -- who denies backing Ukraine's insurgency -- said the conflict, which has killed more than 5,600 people, could not be solved by "military means" and urged Kiev's troops to surrender.
5"I hope that the Ukrainian authorities are not going to prevent the Ukrainian soldiers from laying down their weapons," Putin said in a press conference during a visit to Budapest.
6Poroshenko, in a phone conversation with German Chancellor Angela Merkel, said the assault on Debaltseve was a "cynical attack" on the truce brokered last week by Germany and France.
7He called for the European Union and international community to take a "tough reaction against the treacherous actions of the rebels and Russia".
8Kiev and pro-Russian rebels agreed a peace roadmap on February 12 after marathon negotiations in Minsk involving the leaders of France, Germany, Russia and Ukraine.
9But the insurgents have argued that Debaltseve, which sits on a railway line linking rebel-held Donetsk and Luhansk, is well inside their territory behind the frontline so should not be included in the ceasefire agreement, while Kiev says it controls the territory.
10The UN Security Council on Tuesday "called on all parties to immediately cease hostilities" and allow peace monitors to enter the town.
11Intense shelling was heard in the Debaltseve area Tuesday, AFP journalists reported from the neighbouring town of Soledar.
12Ukrainian officials admitted that pro-Russian rebels had taken parts of the town and surrounded some of the army units there, but said fierce fighting was continuing.
13"The hopes of the world for peace are being destroyed," said the deputy head of Ukraine's presidential administration, Valeriy Chaly.
14The rebels, who claimed to control 80 percent of Debaltseve, said they have killed "many" of the 8,000 soldiers they said were holed up in the town and taken dozens more prisoner.
15An estimated 5,000 civilians were also cowering in cellars, with little water or food.
16It was not known how many casualties the street-to-street combat by fighters armed with automatic weapons and grenade launchers had caused.
17Journalists and observers from the Organization for Security and Cooperation in Europe (OSCE) have been stopped by rebels from entering the town.
18The insurgents had waged an intense bombardment of the town last Saturday, the day before the ceasefire came into effect, and kept up rocket and mortar fire afterwards.
19That violence, and other breaches of the truce which both sides blamed on the other, prevented the warring forces from pulling back heavy weapons from the frontline in Ukraine's east as was meant to happen from Tuesday.
20Ukraine and the West accuse Russia of deploying troops and tanks to support the separatists.
21Moscow denies that and describes the Russian fighters seen in Ukraine as "volunteers".
22Canada on Tuesday announced new sanctions against Moscow and its sympathisers, including the state oil giant Rosneft.
23The new economic sanctions and travel bans target 37 Russian and Ukrainian individuals, as well as economic sanctions against 17 Russian and Ukrainian entities, including Rosneft.
24Russia has been hit by several rounds of Western sanctions over its perceived role in the conflict.
25US President Barack Obama has said arming Ukraine against the rebels was one option he could consider if things got worse, although he would prefer to pressure Russia through other means.
26The violence in Debaltseve has unsettled world powers and agencies after several failed attempts to broker a lasting ceasefire.
27EU foreign policy chief Federica Mogherini said that "we knew from the beginning that it (the truce) was going to be difficult, fragile," but the Russians and separatists know that "it's all the international community looking for the implementation of these agreements".
