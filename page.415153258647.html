Alzheimer's patient won't be charged in killing before exam

1LAKEWOOD, Colo.
2(AP) - Prosecutors will not file charges against an 87-year-old Alzheimer's patient accused of killing his roommate at an assisted living facility until they know if he is competent to stand trial.
3A Monday court hearing for Homer Castor was canceled by a judge, who gave prosecutors until June 1 to bring charges against him in the death of 76-year-old Gerald Propp.
4Police say Castor beat Propp to death on Feb. 21 in the room they shared at Atria Applewood in the Denver suburb of Lakewood.
5Police say Castor was difficult to understand and known to wander the facility at night.
6An earlier encounter between the roommates left Propp, a fellow dementia sufferer, with scratches on his neck.
7A judge ordered Castor to undergo a competency evaluation at a state mental hospital.
