US stocks edge lower in early trade as Greece talks proceed

1NEW YORK (AP) - U.S. stocks are edging lower in early trading as investors wait to see what happens at a summit meeting of European leaders to discuss Greece's strained finances.
2The Dow Jones industrial average was down 36 points, or 0.2 percent, to 17,649 as of 9:35 a.m. Eastern time.
3The Standard & Poor's 500 index lost two points, or 0.1 percent, to 2,066.
4The Nasdaq composite fell 17 points, or 0.3 percent, to 4,974.
5Materials stocks fell more than the rest of the market.
6Mining company Freeport-McMorRan dropped 5 percent, the most in the S&P 500.
7The price of oil stabilized a day after plunging 8 percent.
8U.S. benchmark crude edged down 21 cents to $52.31 a barrel.
9Bond prices rose.
10The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell to 2.22 percent.
