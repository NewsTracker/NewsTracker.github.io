Hillary Clinton officially announces 2016 presidential run

1It's official: Hillary Clinton is running for president.
2The former secretary of state's campaign head told donors and 2008 campaign alumni, ending months of behind-the-scenes planning as she geared up for a second run at the White House.
3"Hillary's running for president," Clinton campaign chairman John Podesta emailed Clinton backers and other Democrats.
4"She is hitting the road to Iowa to start talking directly to voters. There will be a formal kickoff next month."
5The official tweet was quickly followed up by a vote of confidence from one of Clinton's staunchest supporters: daughter Chelsea Clinton.
6"Very proud of you Mom!" the former first daughter wrote.
7Clinton's debut campaign video sought to portray her as a normal American.
8After 90 seconds of average voters talking about what they're "getting ready" for in their lives, Clinton makes her first appearance.
9"I'm getting ready to do something too-I'm running for president," she says.
10"Americans have fought their way back from tough economic times but the deck is still stacked in favor of those at the top. Everyday Americans need a champion and I want to be that champion so you can do more than just get by, you can get ahead and stay ahead."
11Clinton begins her campaign as the prohibitive front-runner for the Democratic nomination.
12Her handful of potential opponents are unknown to most voters and nearly all of the Democratic establishment has rallied around her this time-more than half of all Democratic senators and congressmen have already endorsed her, and many senior staffers from President Obama's past campaigns backing her as well.
13She plans to focus her campaign on helping increase economic mobility and equal rights for women, as she indicated in her roll out video.
14In New York, the excitement peaked early at Clinton's Brooklyn Heights headquarters.
15Congresswoman Caroline Maloney (D- NY) stopped by before the official announcement and found the office's headquarters closed.
16"(Hillary) is the most qualified candidate running. I think it'll be great for New York," said the Congresswoman, whose district covers the Upper East Side and parts of Queens and Brooklyn.
17"It's a very exciting day for us," Maloney added.
18"I'm so glad she has officially decided to run for president in 2016," New York Sen. Chuck Schumer said in his Midtown office on Sunday.
19"She has the experience and mindset to get results. She has the rare ability to take an understand the problems of hard-working Americans, and the rare experience to get things done."
20As soon as the word was out, champagne corks started popping as a group showed up at her campaign office to party.
21"I just kept refreshing (Twitter). It's official. She announced it. I'm so happy!" said Risa Levine, 52, a real estate lawyer ego volunteered for Hillary's 2008 campaign.
22"This is a very exciting day for us. We are ready. America needs Hillary," Levine said.
23"I've been waiting, waiting and waiting. We got the champagne."
24"This is a long time coming. I'm just ready for the journey ahead."
25Tom D'angora, 36, a theater producer from midtown, wore a Hillary hat show his alliance.
26"It feels like Christmas morning," D'angora said.
27"She's running...All the way to the White House this time," D'angora said.
28He said she proved her leadership skills as secretary of state.
29"I love Hillary. I don't care how many times they use the B-word. She gave America a facelift, with all the miles she traveled," he said.
30But not all New Yorkers-or all Democrats-were ready to embrace her.
31New York City Mayor Bill De Blasio, who ran Clinton's 2000 Senate race, said he wants to see "the substance" of her campaign before endorsing her during an interview on NBC's "Meet the Press."
32"I think progressives all over the country, I think everyday Americans are demanding that their candidates, the President and every other level, really say that we have a plan that we can believe in for addressing income inequality," he said.
33The path to the White House doesn't look smooth for the former secretary of State.
34She's faced a rough month over stories about her use of a private email server and donations from foreign leaders to the Clinton Foundation.
35Recent national polls still have her leading most of her Republican opponents, but those numbers have tightened in recent weeks following the spate of bad news.
36While Clinton is the heavy favorite for the nomination, she's looking to tamp down expectations and show she's not taking the primary for granted.
37Clinton is planning to head to Iowa and New Hampshire in the coming days for a series of events aimed at talking with local voters that she's aiming to have a more intimate feel than the big rallies she kicked off her last campaign with.
38Republicans are already taking aim at the former First Lady.
39Republican National Committee spokeswoman Ali Pardo stood outside Hillary's headquarters before the big announcement Sunday wearing a Stop Hillary shirt.
40"We've got people on the ground all across the country. We want her to answer to her record," she said.
41"She launched her camp aid under a could of secrecy She hasn't answered questions about emails. She hasn't answered questions about the Clinton Foundation."
