Poland Sends Doctors to Help Poles Hurt in Tunisia

1Foreign Minister Grzegorz Schetyna said Thursday morning that according to information he had at the moment, two Poles were killed in the attack, two were missing and nine were hospitalized with injuries that weren't life-threatening.
2The Foreign Ministry said later Thursday that one missing man was found in a hospital, with injuries but in good condition.
3Their names haven't been released.
4Some of those injured were expected to be flown to Poland on Friday, while some other Polish tourists were being flown back Thursday night to Warsaw.
5Around 300 Poles were vacationing in Tunisia at the time of the attack, according to tourist offices.
6National flags on government buildings were flown half-staff in a sign of mourning.
7On Friday, lawmakers will observe a moment of silence.
