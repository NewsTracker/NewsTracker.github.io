Egyptian lawyers protest colleague's shooting at courthouse

1CAIRO (AP) - Members of Egypt's lawyers union say it has suspended work in Cairo's Nasr City criminal court to protest a police officer shooting a lawyer there.
2A security official says that the accused officer, Mohamed el-Gaml, shot the lawyer in the chest after mistaking him for a fleeing suspect Saturday.
3The official says the shooting came after lawyer Mohamed Abdul-Hamid was seen arguing with prosecutors and police officers at the courthouse.
4The official, speaking Sunday on condition of anonymity because he was not authorized to speak to journalists, says the lawyer is hospitalized in intensive care.
5El-Gaml has been detained pending an investigation.
6Lawyer Khaled Abu Kirsha disputed the official's account and said his colleague should not have been shot.
7Lawyers in a Nile Delta court also have suspended their work.
