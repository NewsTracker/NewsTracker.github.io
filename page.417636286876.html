'Gigantic' blast hits fuel depot near Kiev, several feared dead

1Kiev (AFP) - Several Ukrainian firefighters were feared dead Tuesday outside Kiev when a ferocious blast tore through a fuel depot, sparking a "gigantic" fire that spread to nearby buildings.
2Videos posted on the Internet showed the force of the explosion and the heatwave it produced setting aflame several fire trucks that were rushed to the scene on Tuesday morning.
3"Everything is on fire," a voice in one of the videos posted on YouTube said.
4A fireman wearing a silver heat-resistant suit and mask was seen running away from the spot of the disaster for safety.
5"Everyone -- leave. Whoever is still here -- leave," one fireman said in a video.
6Interior Minister Arsen Avakov said the initial explosion hit a fuel depot near the Vasylkiv village some 30 kilometres (20 miles) southwest of Kiev.
7"Gigantic. Firemen have died," Avakov tweeted.
8His later Twitter updates said several firemen intially presumed to have died had been pulled out alive from the flames.
9A spokesman for Ukraine's Emergencies Ministry told AFP that it was aware of six people being injured.
10Kiev has been suffering from a dry heat spell for more than a week.
11But there was no initial information about what might have caused the blast.
12The fuel depot hit by the initial explosion is owned by a Ukrainian company called BRSM-Nafta that operates a chain of filling stations.
13The nearby filling station firm called KLO said it was also evacuating its staff from the scene.
14"The danger is real," KLO co-owner Vyacheslav Steshenko told the Interfax-Ukraine news agency.
