16The extremist group views ancient ruins as idolatrous and has looted or destroyed several heritage sites under its control in Iraq, raising fears about the fate of Palmyra - one of the best-known historical sites in the Middle East.
17The UNESCO world heritage site is famous for its 2,000-year-old towering Roman-era colonnades, other ruins and priceless artifacts.
18Before Syria's conflict began in 2011, thousands of tourists a year visited the remote desert outpost, a cherished landmark referred to by Syrians as the "Bride of the Desert."
19Syrian authorities say they moved hundreds of priceless artifacts to Damascus ahead of the IS takeover, but the fate of those ruins too large to move remains uncertain.
20Islamic State militants have already looted and vandalized a museum in the Iraqi city of Mosul and have massively damaged the ancient cities of Hatra and Ninevah - both UNESCO world heritage sites.
