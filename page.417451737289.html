FIFA scandal deepens as Blatter aide linked to payments

1Photo: FIFA secretary-general Jerome Valcke (L) has been linked to bank transfers central to the US bribery investigation.
2US prosecutors believe FIFA President Sepp Blatter's top lieutenant made $US10 million ($13.12 million) in bank transactions that are central to the bribery investigation of the world soccer body, a source familiar with the matter said on Tuesday (AEST).
3Jerome Valcke, FIFA's secretary general, is described in an indictment filed in federal court in Brooklyn, New York, as an unidentified "high-ranking FIFA official" who in 2008 transferred the sum to another FIFA official, Jack Warner.
4Valcke is not named as a defendant and has not been accused of any wrongdoing.
5He was not immediately available for comment.
6A spokeswoman for FIFA said the $10 million in bank transactions were authorized by the then-FIFA Finance Committee chairman.
7The Finance Committee chairman was Julio Grondona, who died last year.
8Valcke and Blatter are the top two officials within FIFA.
9Valcke's connection to the case was first reported by The New York Times.
10The Times said Valcke had written in an email to the newspaper that he neither had authorised the payment nor had the power to do so.
11As new questions arose in the FIFA scandal, more officials were arrested, suspended or banned on Tuesday, and countries were weighing a World Cup boycott amid controversy over the re-election of Blatter as FIFA president.
12As news broke of Valcke's alleged connection to the case, FIFA announced that Valcke would not attend the opening of the FIFA Women's World Cup Canada 2015 - due to begin on Sunday - as previously scheduled.
13"It is important that he attends to matters at FIFA's headquarters in Zurich," FIFA said in a statement.
14Warner, a former FIFA vice president, is among 14 FIFA officials and corporate executives charged by the U.S. Department of Justice last Thursday (AEST) with running a criminal enterprise that involved more than $US150 million ($196.7 million) in bribes.
15Warner left jail in Trinidad and Tobago on Friday after he was granted bail, according to local media.
16Why are there no investigations into Sepp Blatter?
17No other person has brought so much shame and disgrace on FIFA.
18"Why are there no investigations in Asia, or in Europe?"
19Warner asked German magazine Stern in an interview released on Monday.
20"Why are there no investigations into Sepp Blatter? No other person has brought so much shame and disgrace on FIFA."
21A court transcript released on Monday said Warner's son, Daryan Warner, secretly agreed in 2013 to cooperate with US authorities and to admit to participating in a World Cup ticket-reselling scheme.
22Like his brother Daryll, Daryan had agreed to assist US authorities as part of separate plea deals.
23Who are the FIFA officials charged?
24Nine current and former FIFA officials have been indicted for racketeering, conspiracy and corruption including two of the body's current vice-presidents who were among the seven arrested in Zurich.
25The transcript, ordered released by a federal judge in Brooklyn, New York, contained Daryan Warner's guilty plea.
26The $10 million payment is a key feature of the indictment accusing Jack Warner of taking a bribe in exchange for helping South Africa secure the right to host the 2010 World Cup.
27The indictment said an arrangement had been made with FIFA officials to have $US10 million that otherwise would have gone to South Africa to support the World Cup to the Caribbean Football Union, where Warner was president.
28The indictment said that the high-ranking FIFA official identified on Monday as Valcke caused $US10 million to be wired to accounts controlled by Warner, who subsequently diverted portions of the money for his personal use and to personal accounts, the indictment said.
29In Zurich, Enrique Sanz, the general secretary of CONCACAF (the Confederation of North, Central America and the Caribbean Association Football), was suspended and Congolese Football Association (FECAFOOT) officials Jean Guy Blaise Mayolas and Badji Mombo Wantete were provisionally banned by FIFA's ethics committee.
30In Paraguay, a judge on Monday ordered house arrest for the former president of South America's soccer federation, Nicolas Leoz, accused of involvement in the scandal.
31England called for a boycott but a senior UEFA official cast doubt on an outright move, while Sweden's soccer authorities have not ruled out the possibility of a boycott, Swedish FA chairman Karl-Erik Nilsson said.
32Following Blatter's re-election as FIFA president, the English Football Association's chairman Greg Dyke said his organisation would support any boycott led by UEFA.
