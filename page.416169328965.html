Rangers complete stellar regular season, defeat Capitals 4-2 (Yahoo Sports)

1WASHINGTON (AP) -- The New York Rangers completed one of their finest regular seasons in their long history Saturday.
2Now they say it's time to move on.
3Kevin Hayes and Dominic Moore each had a goal and an assist, Henrik Lundqvist stopped 22 shots and the Rangers capped their stellar regular season with a 4-2 win over the Washington Capitals.
4The Rangers (53-22-7), winners of the Presidents' Trophy as the league's top team in the regular season, set franchise records for wins and points (113) in a season.
5Next up for top-seeded New York is Sidney Crosby and Pittsburgh in the first round of the postseason.
6''Everything we've done, the Presidents' Trophy and everything, that's gone,'' Derick Brassard said.
7''When the buzzer sounded in the third period, everything's gone.
8Brassard and Jesper Fast (empty netter) also scored for the Rangers, and Martin St. Louis had two assists as New York won for the sixth time in seven games.
9''Obviously we had a good year,'' coach Alain Vigneault said.
10Lundqvist won five of his seven starts after returning from a neck injury and finished the regular season with 30 wins.
11''Coming back I kind of set a personal goal to win 30 games,'' he said.
12Alex Ovechkin scored his league-leading 53rd goal for Washington and Stanislav Galiev scored the first of his career.
13''We came out flat in the first and they capitalized,'' coach Barry Trotz said.
14The Capitals (45-26-11) return to the playoffs after a one-year absence.
15They finished second in the Metropolitan Division and will have home-ice advantage for their first-round series against the Islanders.
16Braden Holtby made 30 saves for Washington while extending his team record with his 25th consecutive start.
17He played in 73 games this season, tying Olaf Kolzig for the team record.
18New York opened the scoring at 12:36 of the first when Ryan McDonagh's shot hit Hayes, and Hayes backhanded the loose puck out of mid-air past Holtby for his 17th goal.
19Brassard made it 2-0 at 15:50 with a hard shot from the point on a power play.
20It was his career-high 19th goal of the season and the 100th of his career.

22Nicklas Backstrom had an assist, pulling into the league lead and hitting the 60-assist mark for the second straight season.
23Fast's empty-netter made it 4-1 before Galiev closed out the scoring.
