American in UAE Facebook case may have charges dismissed

Agony as star misses Aussie QF

1Ryan Pate, 30, appeared in an Abu Dhabi court Tuesday to present his defense on charges he defamed his Emirati employer, Global Aerospace Logistics.
2The Belleair Bluffs, Florida native posted about the company during a visit stateside following a dispute with his employer over sick leave.
3When he returned to Abu Dhabi, the oil-rich Emirati capital, he was told to report to a police station and was arrested for breaking local defamation laws.
4Pate's Facebook post has since been deleted.
5He says he can't remember exactly what he wrote, but he knows he called his bosses "backstabbers" and warned others not to work for them.
6In an interview after his hearing, Pate said his lawyer informed him that GAL has submitted paperwork seeking to dismiss the charges.
7He was cautiously optimistic that step will end his legal ordeal, though he was still waiting for final confirmation from the court.
8"I'm so grateful to the company for doing that. That was a huge thing for them to do," said Pate, adding that he regrets letting his emotions get the best of him.
9Pate says he spent about 10 days in jail before being released pending a verdict in the case.
10Authorities are holding his passport, a customary step for defendants in the Emirates, meaning he cannot leave the Gulf nation.
11Pate's congressman, Rep.
12David Jolly, has lobbied the U.S. State Department and Emirates officials for help.
13Jolly welcomed the news in a statement thanking those who helped Pate, including U.S. Ambassador Barbara Leaf, and saying he believed his constituent's comments were protected by the U.S. Constitution's first amendment because they were made on American soil.
14"I now look forward to Ryan being reunited with his family and friends back home in Florida," he said.
15GAL did not immediately return a call seeking comment.
1GIANT Pakistan fast bowler Mohammad Irfan was ruled out of the World Cup on Tuesday because of a stress fracture to his pelvis, an injury which captain Misbah-ul-Haq described as a "huge setback".
2The 32-year-old first felt the hip injury in the 129-run win over the United Arab Emirates two weeks ago when he bowled just three overs.
3The seven-foot one-inch fast bowler played in the crucial victory against South Africa, taking three wickets, before missing Sunday's game against Ireland which Pakistan won to secure their place in the last-eight.
4The injury is a major blow to the 1992 champions' chances of defeating four-time winners Australia in the quarter-final at the Adelaide Oval on Friday.
5"Irfan was one bowler who was different to all the others in the competition," Misbah told AFP.
6"It is a huge setback for us but we have to live with it."
7Irfan, at seven-feet one inch, the tallest man to play international cricket, took eight wickets in the five matches he played at the World Cup.
8His injury is the latest in a series of devastating setbacks suffered by Pakistan who have been stripped of an entire first-choice attack.
9"It's difficult because we lost two bowlers to suspension before the World Cup," said Misbah of ace spinner Saeed Ajmal and spinning all-rounder Mohammad Hafeez.
10"We also lost two good quality pacemen - Umar Gul and Junaid Khan - also to injury. Now just when we have started to perform well this injury hits us badly.
11"It's a worrying sign for a captain but we have to find a way to handle this."
12Junaid was announced in the 15-man squad for the World Cup but injured his leg at a training camp and was replaced by Rahat Ali.
13If Pakistan wins the quarter-final, it's understood that Junaid is set to be called up as a replacement for Irfan.
14Before Irfan was ruled out of the rest of the tournament, former captain Wasim Akram told AFP that the player needed to work on his fitness.
15"Irfan is different," said Wasim.
16"His body stretches more and needs careful handling.
17The Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB) earlier confirmed Irfan's injury.
18"Irfan underwent an MRI scan on Monday afternoon and the radiologist's report showed a stress injury of the pelvis but was inconclusive," said a PCB release.
19Brad Robinson, the Pakistan team's physiotherapist, ordered a more detailed scan, which was carried out Tuesday afternoon, and it reconfirmed a stress fracture in the pelvis.
20"This injury rules Irfan out of the World Cup," said Robinson.
