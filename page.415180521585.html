Kenya burns 15 tonnes of ivory in anti-poaching fight

Man sentenced to 17 years in West Chicago stabbing death

1NAIROBI - Kenya burnt 15 tonnes of ivory on Tuesday and promised to destroy all of its stockpile this year, the latest step in its campaign against elephant poaching.
2Poaching has surged in the last few years across sub-Saharan Africa, where gangs kill elephants and rhinos to feed Asian demand for ivory and horns for use in folk medicines.
3A 2014 UN and Interpol report estimated that about 20,000 to 25,000 elephants were killed in Africa every year, out of a total population of as many as 650,000.
4"As part of Kenya's continued policy to put ivory beyond economic use ... I will today burn 15 tonnes of ivory at this historic site in Nairobi National Park," President Uhuru Kenyatta said during a ceremony at the park.
5"In order to underline our determination to eradicate poaching, my government shall burn the rest of the stockpile within this year."
6The ceremony was to mark the African Union-designated Wangari Maathai Day, in honour of the late environmentalist and Nobel Peace Prize Laureate, and the World Wildlife Day.
7Kenya has imposed stiffer penalties -- longer jail terms and bigger fines -- for wildlife poaching or trafficking, saying poaching is harming tourism, a major earner of foreign exchange.
8In January 2014, a Kenyan court convicted a Chinese man of smuggling ivory and ordered him to pay a fine of 20 million shillings ($233,000) or serve seven years in jail, the first sentencing since Kenya introduced the new anti-poaching law.
9A Kenyan man is in police custody accused of being behind an international poaching syndicate involving elephant tusks and was linked to a three-tonne haul of ivory seized in Mombasa in June 2014.
10"Illegal trade in wildlife has become a sophisticated transnational form of crime, comparable to other pernicious examples, such as trafficking of drugs, humans, counterfeit items and oil," UNSecretary General Ban Ki-Moon said in a statement.
11"It is driven by rising demand, and is often facilitated by corruption and weak governance. There is strong evidence of the increased involvement of organized crime networks and non-State armed groups."
12In 2011, then-President Mwai Kibaki set fire to five tonnes of contraband ivory.
13His predecessor, Daniel Toroitich arap Moi, did the same in 1989.
1One of three men implicated in the gang-related stabbing death of a Wheaton man was sentenced to 17 years in prison Tuesday.
2Anthony Rios, 20, of West Chicago, agreed to plead guilty to armed violence and accept the sentence in exchange for prosecutors agreeing to drop other charges, including first-degree murder.
3He was one of three men implicated in the death of Thomas Tecuatl, 22, who was stabbed to death on the night of Nov. 16, 2013, at a party in West Chicago.
4Authorities said Rios and his friends, all reputed gang members, believed the victim was a rival gang member.
5An argument broke out and Tecuatl allegedly pulled out a knife.
6The three others began pelting him with bricks and bottles, and gave chase when Tecuatl tried to run.
7Police said the victim slipped on wet pavement about a block from the party, allowing the other three to catch him and stab him with his own blade.
8Rios punched and kicked the victim during the fight, according to prosecutors.
9The man who allegedly inflicted the fatal stab wound, Jonathan Jimenez, of Aurora, is awaiting trial on charges of first-degree murder.
10The other man implicated, Alejandro Martinez of West Chicago, is also awaiting trial.
11Rios must serve at least 85 percent of his sentence before he is eligible for parole.
12He was given credit for the 470 days he was spent in the DuPage County Jail awaiting trial.
