Facebook shareholders strike down 'one share, one vote' proposal

3Facebook shareholders shot down a proposal to give stockholders one vote per share, according to preliminary results from the company's annual meeting.
4The social-networking company's stockholders are currently broken up into two tiers, each with different voting power.
5Class B stockholders who invested before the IPO hold a greater voting power of 10 votes per share.
6Class A stockholders, who invested after the IPO, hold lesser voting power of just one vote per share.
7Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg, the largest shareholder, owns 54% of the voting power with approximately 422 million Class B shares, according to the proxy statement.
8The proposal to change the current voting practices was brought up by stockholder Northstar Asset Management.
9"What we're asking for is for Facebook to recapitalize the shares so that each share gets one vote," Northstar Asset Management founder and CEO Julie Goodridge told CNBC.
10The proposal to equalize this voting power was rejected, along with two other proposals that respectively called for a regular sustainability report, as well as a review of the sites policies of protecting the data and right of minors.
11Full disclosure of the proposals can be found here.
12Facebook's eight directors, including Netflix CEO Reed Hastings and Facebook Chief Operating Officer Sheryl Sandberg, were re-elected during the meeting at Facebook headquarters in Menlo Park, Calif.
