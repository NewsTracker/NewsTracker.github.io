Experts: New Mexico oil patch could lose 2,000 jobs

1State energy officials say the downturn in the oil and gas industry could cost New Mexico around 2,000 jobs.
2New Mexico Energy, Minerals and Natural Resources Secretary David Martin spoke Tuesday at an energy conference in Farmington.
3He says the number of drill rigs operating in the state has dropped from 85 to 60 over the past year.
4Martin says the state hasn't seen a decrease in production but activity is expected to level off and lead to fewer jobs and revenue in the coming months.
5Industry leaders say they're finding ways to make operations more efficient.
6The head of BP's onshore business says the company has no plans to sell its assets in the San Juan Basin, which includes thousands of operating and idle natural gas wells.
