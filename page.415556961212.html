Robert Durst is ?suicidal,' moved to jail for mentally ill

1Accused killer Robert Durst, the eccentric real estate heir and subject of an HBO documentary, was in a Louisiana jail Wednesday that treats the most severely mentally ill inmates, said an attorney for the Orleans Parish Sheriff's Office.
2"He's suicidal," said Blake Arcuri, an attorney who represented the Sheriff's Office in the case.
3Arcuri said that when Durst's attorneys opposed the transfer to the sheriff's facility at a state prison in St. Gabriel, La., Tuesday, he submitted Durst's medical records, which were sealed, to the court.
4He said all inmates undergo a medical exam when they're booked, but he could not comment on specifics in Durst's record because of privacy laws.
5"If he gets cleared, he'll come back" to the jail where he was held, Arcuri said, adding, "he is not getting treated differently than any other inmate. This is not a media circus."
6Arcuri said mental health issues that prompted Durst's transfer are unrelated to whether he is competent to stand trial.
7Arcuri said he had not been notified of any appeal by Durst's attorneys of the court ruling that allowed for his transfer late Tuesday.
8Staff at the Louisiana Supreme Court said they had not received an appeal.
9The move - which was approved after much legal wrangling - came on the same day that Durst's legal troubles played out in several jurisdictions across the country, including a police raid at his Houston residence and the announcement by Eureka, Calif., police that the 71-year-old may have information about the disappearance of a teenage girl there nearly two decades ago.
10"We are very interested in any information Mr. Durst may have in connection with the disappearance" of Karen Mitchell, said Eureka police Capt.
11Steve Watson, who did not identify Durst as either a suspect or person of interest in the 1997 case.
12Meanwhile, New York authorities remain interested in Durst as they continue to unravel the mystery of what happened to his first wife, Kathleen, who disappeared in 1982.
13FBI agents arrested Durst on Saturday in a New Orleans hotel at the behest of Los Angeles officials.
14Police in L.A. said they had uncovered new evidence linking Durst to the Benedict Canyon slaying in 2000 of writer and filmmaker Susan Berman.
15On Monday, Durst was charged with one count of murder.
16But when agents arrested Durst, a search of his room uncovered a revolver and a stash of marijuana, leading to charges in New Orleans that are complicating Durst's transfer to L.A. His extradition will be delayed for at least five days as authorities in New Orleans determine how to deal with the lesser allegations there.
17Suspicions about Durst exploded into a national sensation as they played out in a six-part HBO series, "The Jinx."
18Agents took Durst into custody the day before Sunday's finale, in which Durst uttered a possible confession after the filming stopped but his microphone continued recording.
19"Killed them all, of course," Durst muttered after stepping away from the set.
20The role of the documentary in assembling evidence against Durst could complicate a criminal trial, but LAPD Chief Charlie Beck said Tuesday he was confident that the prosecution would not depend on the broadcast.
21"It is a unique set of circumstances," he said.
22"But our case is independent of the documentary. Our case will stand on its own."
23Detectives had actively investigated Berman's death for nearly 15 years, Beck said.
24Its resolution had been delayed by the "legal maneuverings" of Durst and his attorneys as well as unrelated prosecutions in other states, he said.
25The chief declined to discuss details of the ongoing investigation, telling reporters that he did not want to jeopardize the case by making statements to the media.
26"We are glad that we have finally come to a point where we can request Mr. Durst be extradited to California, to Los Angeles, so he can face a jury of his peers in the homicide of Susan Berman," Beck said.
27The charges in New Orleans relate to an earlier case, in which Durst was found guilty of skipping bail.
28That felony conviction bars him from carrying a gun, according to court papers.
29He's also charged separately with having the gun in combination with a "controlled, dangerous substance" - legal parlance for the marijuana found in his room.
30The maximum penalty for these charges is a $10,000 fine and five to 10 years of hard labor, according to court papers.
31In L.A., Durst could face the death penalty for the murder charge with special circumstances.
32The indictment alleges that Durst lay in wait for Berman and that he acted to prevent her from speaking to police about the disappearance of Kathleen Durst.
33An attorney representing Durst, Dick DeGuerin, gave mixed signals about whether he would resist his client's extradition to Los Angeles.
34Durst appeared briefly in court Tuesday, shackled and wearing an orange prison jumpsuit and rubber sandals on bare feet.
35During the proceedings, Durst sometimes glared at the prosecutor.
36At other moments, he appeared to smile and talk to himself.
37Orleans Parish Assistant District Attorney Mark Burton asked Orleans Parish Magistrate Harry Cantrell Jr. to detain Durst for five days, "until a hearing to determine whether bail can be set, given his flight risk."
38Cantrell suggested a Friday hearing, but Burton said a longer delay would be needed to assemble relevant evidence.
39Cantrell also directed authorities to provide Durst with hydrocodone, a powerful opiate, as necessary for pain.
40The exact nature of the accused killer's medical condition became a subject of dispute over his housing.
41Durst was notified early Tuesday that he was being transferred to an Orleans Parish sheriff's facility at a state prison about an hour away - a facility that's designated for inmates with "acute medical conditions" in accordance with a federal consent decree.
42Durst's attorneys fought the transfer, calling him competent and suffering from only Asperger's syndrome, a mild form of autism that he was diagnosed with as a child.
43"He does not have an acute medical or mental condition," DeGuerin said, noting that his client's competency "is not in question."
44But the attorney for the Sheriff's Office, Arcuri, disagreed, arguing that Durst's acute medical and mental health issues required him to be transferred, and that being held at the remote facility would not violate his constitutional right to an attorney.
45Durst could consult with his attorneys by phone or in person at the jail next to the courthouse.
46"I can have him transported over to the jail so they can meet with him daily," Arcuri said.
47The magistrate sided with Durst's attorneys and ruled against a transfer to the state prison, but the Sheriff's Office appealed.
48Late Tuesday, a three-judge appellate panel reversed the earlier decision and allowed the transfer to proceed.
49Police executed search warrants at Durst's Houston home Tuesday afternoon, but it was unclear who ordered the searches or why.
50Louisiana State Police spokeswoman Trooper Melissa Matey said the warrants were not connected to the drug and weapons charges Durst may be facing there, and referred all questions to the LAPD.
51Cmdr. Andy Smith, the LAPD's chief spokesman, declined to comment on the searches or confirm if they were ordered by the department.
52And the 1997 disappearance of Karen Mitchell - who is formally classified as a missing person but whose vanishing is believed to involve foul play - emerged as possibly involving Durst.
53For several years, Durst had a home in Trinidad, about 20 miles from Eureka, and he was known to frequent other spots around Northern California.
54Watson, the Eureka police chief, said that he did not know the specifics of the Mitchell investigation but said that the department is working with the FBI and Humboldt County district attorney's office.
55In 1982, Kathleen Durst vanished after she expressed the desire for a divorce.
56She had been fighting with her husband the night she disappeared, according to a good friend, who said Kathleen Durst was worried about what her husband might do.
57Berman had acted as an "informal spokesman" for Durst, giving reporters his side of the story.
58Durst and Berman went to school together at UCLA.
59Berman died from a single gunshot wound to the head.
60Durst was residing in the L.A. area, but police said at the time that he was not a suspect.
61Less than a year later, Durst turned up in Galveston, Texas, in connection with the killing of an elderly neighbor, Morris Black.
62Black's dismembered body, in several plastic bags, was discovered in the waters offshore.
63A trail of clues led to Durst's arrest.
64The real estate heir quickly posted bail and, according to police, went on the lam.
65He was arrested soon after in a Pennsylvania grocery store, where police said he was caught shoplifting a chicken sandwich and a bandage, despite having $500 with him.
66Durst was returned to Texas, where he claimed he inadvertently shot Black while struggling to wrestle a gun from the man.
67The 2003 trial was a spectacle in which his attorneys argued that after the killing, a previously undiagnosed mental condition propelled Durst into a traumatized state similar to an out-of-body experience.
68The trial was capped by four days of testimony from Durst, in which he claimed he had no memory of cutting up Black's body.
