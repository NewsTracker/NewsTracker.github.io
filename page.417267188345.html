Orioles pitcher Brian Matusz suspended 8 games, appeals (Yahoo Sports)

1BALTIMORE (AP) -- Orioles left-hander Brian Matusz was suspended for eight games by Major League Baseball on Monday for having a foreign substance on his arm during a game in Miami.
2Matusz immediately appealed and was in uniform for Baltimore's game against the Houston Astros on Monday afternoon.
3He will continue to pitch until his appeal is heard.
4Matusz entered Saturday's game against the Marlins in the 12th inning.
5Miami manager Dan Jennings asked umpire Paul Emmel to inspect Matusz's right arm, and Emmel ejected the pitcher.
6''I went out there and told the pitcher I was going to touch his right forearm,'' Emmel said after the game.
7''That's where he was touching before he went to the ball.
8There was no telling when the appeal would be heard, although Orioles executive vice president of baseball operations Dan Duquette suspected a date would be set sometime this week.
9Milwaukee Brewers reliever Will Smith was suspended for eight games on Friday for having a foreign substance on his right arm during a game at Atlanta.
10Smith appealed the sentence, and has not yet had a hearing.
11''I'm more focused on getting through today, try to win a game, and see what each day brings,'' Orioles manager Buck Showalter said before Monday's game.
12''It's completely in their hands.
13I don't know what they're going to do.
14If Matusz is suspended, he cannot be replaced on the 25-man roster.
15Therefore the Orioles will have only 24 players available.
16Matusz is 1-2 with a 3.18 ERA in 14 games.
17He has limited left-handed batters to a .185 batting average.
18Showalter said the length of the suspension probably had much to do with the sentence Smith received.
19''They're basing it off precedent,'' the manager said.
20''Each case is different; that's why you have the appeal process.
21To make sure it's not just some blanket punishment.
22Speaking before Boston faced Minnesota on the road Monday, Red Sox manager John Farrell said pitchers caught using a foreign substance are probably trying to get a better grip on the ball, rather than make it move differently.
23''I don't think a grip gives you an ability to make the ball do different things,'' said Farrell, a major league pitcher for eight seasons.
24''You're looking for feel in the hand, overall command and a grip.
25I think even if you ask a hitter, they're not going to say a substance makes the ball do different things.
26Duquette agreed.
27''Pitchers, they're trying to grip the ball so they can make an effective pitch,'' Duquette said.
28In the wake of what happened to Smith and Matusz, Farrell expressed hope that MLB could do something that would enable pitchers to get a better grip - within the rules.
29''I would like to see an approved substance that pitchers can use,'' he said.
