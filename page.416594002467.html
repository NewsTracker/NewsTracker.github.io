Luxury hotel in Nepal turns away locals, only open for wealthy tourists

1A five-star hotel in the capital city of Nepal has been accused of only letting wealthy visitors into the grounds and refusing to help desperate homeless people by a furious Sherpa.
2Mountain guide Ang Tshering Lama claimed luxury hotel Hyatt Regency in Kathmandu were only letting "a few rich people and westerners" in to seek sanctuary in its 37 acres of open land.
3He took a series of pictures which he claimed showed people with rucksacks lounging on the grass while others were left outside to set up makeshift camps on roadsides outside the complex.
4Managers at the hotel have denied the allegation and say it is "not true".
5Thousands of Nepali people have been left homeless by the devastating 7.8 magnitude earthquake, which has claimed the lives of at least 4,000 people - although it is feared it could reach as high as 10,000.
6Many people have resorted to creating make-shift shelters amid the rubble, as the operation to find survivors continues.
7In a Facebook post that has been shared more than 4,000 times, Mr Lama urged people to start boycotting the 280-room hotel.
8"They had lots of empty spaces and they didn't let poor Nepali people enter in to take refuge in their open green lawns.
9"There were places which was cramped in and no place to sit, forget about laying down for a sleep. The only let few rich people and some westerners in... Hayatt is making money in Nepal and they don't take care of Nepali people.
10Sanjad Tapa, the director of sales at the Hyatt Regency, told the MailOnline that the Facebook post was not true.
11He said: "It is not true. The Facebook post, that is not the case.
12"In this sort of situation, even at private houses, people are going onto private land and we are a hotel and we have not stopped them.
13Following the earthquake Hyatt Regency was left without running water or electricity, and engineers are on site to make it structurally safe so international relief organisations can base their operations there.
14US-based company Hyatt runs 22 hotels across America in addition to hotels in Mumbai, Tokyo and Seoul.
15In a statement on their website called "Our Hearts Go Out To Nepal", vice president Brigitta Witt of the US-based company said they are also working with MercyCorps as part of broader relief efforts.
16She wrote: "The staff at the hotel has worked tirelessly since the quake struck to support guests, colleagues and neighbors.
17"The next step was doing what they could to help guests get home, so that staff could start to focus more on the relief effort.
18"The hotel is also readying support for colleagues and their families.
19She added: "We know Nepal has a long rebuilding process ahead, and we want to do what we can to support our colleagues, neighbours and the community in Kathmandu during this difficult time."
