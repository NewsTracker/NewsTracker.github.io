9More than 8,400 people work at the Nissan plant that built 648,000 vehicles last year, making it the highest-producing plant in North America.
10The plant, which opened in 1983, makes the Altima, Maxima, Leaf, Rogue, Pathfinder and Infiniti QX60.
11John Martin, the senior vice president for Nissan North America's manufacturing, purchasing and supply chain management, said the goal is to cut down on transportation costs and to have a more direct hand in quality control.
12The growth in the automobile sector means many suppliers are already reaching capacity at their current locations, and Nissan wants them to consider locating new production at the new supplier park instead of at existing facilities in the Midwest.
