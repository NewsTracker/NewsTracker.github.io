Jeb Bush rejects idea of raising federal minimum wage

1The six boats were kept in port on the advice of their skippers and race organizers to avoid the cyclone that caused massive destruction in the South Pacific nation of Vanuatu, claiming at least 11 lives.
2The fleet faces a daunting 6,776-nautical mile (12,549-kilometer, 7,798-mile) journey to their destination in Itaja, Brazil, battling treacherous conditions through the Southern Ocean, rounding Cape Horn and then sailing through the Atlantic for the first time since November.
3It's the toughest test for the crews taking part in the nine-month, 38,739-nautical mile (71,745 kilometers, 44,642 miles) race.
4Winds in the Southern Ocean are expected to regularly top 100 mph (160 kph) and waves around Cape Horn can reach 100 feet (30 meters) high.
5In the last edition of the race in 2011-12, five of the six boats sustained serious damage during the leg and two were forced to retire.
6Experienced sailors Stu Bannatyne of New Zealand and Damian Foxall from Ireland were recruited by Team Alvimedica and Dongfeng Race Team, respectively, just to take part in the leg.
7Bannatyne, a six-time race veteran, did not need much persuading.
8"This is the best sailing in the world," he said.
9Abu Dhabi Ocean Racing started the stage level on eight points with Dongfeng Race Team, but is the race leader courtesy of its superior record in the in-port series, which acts as the tie-breaker.
10It is trailed by Team Brunel (Netherlands) on 14 points, Team Alvimedica (Turkey/U.S.)
11and fourth-leg victors MAPFRE (Spain) on 16, and the all-women crew of Team SCA (Sweden) on 24.
12The boats are expected to arrive in Itaja around April 7.
13The fleet will cross the halfway point of the race during the fifth leg.
14In all, the boats will cover nine legs, visiting 11 ports.
15The race is scheduled to finish on June 27 in Gothenburg, Sweden, after starting in Alicante, Spain, in October last year.

2As governor, Bush opposed a 2004 ballot measure approved by voters in Florida that tied increases in the state minimum wage to inflation.
3Asked about the minimum wage at a pair of appearances on Tuesday, he said he doesn't want to abolish the existing federal minimum wage of $7.25 an hour, but also opposes raising it.
4Former Florida Gov. Jeb Bush, speaks at the South Carolina house republican caucus reception in Columbia, S.C., Tuesday, March 17, 2015.
5Bush said Tuesday that minimum wage increases should be left to businesses and state governments, opposing a hike in the federal pay floor as an impediment to individuals trying to escape from poverty.
6Democrats generally favor raising the minimum wage, while many Republicans oppose it.
7"We're moving to a world where it's sticky in the ends, where it's harder for people in poverty to move up, and where the rich are doing really well, and the middle is getting squeezed," Bush said at his first stop of the day in Greenville.
8"Any idea that perpetuates that is one that I would oppose, and I think this minimum wage idea is exactly one of those things," he said.
9Bush has yet to formally declare his intention to run for president in 2016, but winked at the idea during a Chamber of Commerce breakfast, telling the crowd "you'll be seeing a lot of me."
10He is spending two days this week in South Carolina, which hosts the South's first presidential primary next February, a few weeks after the Iowa caucuses and the New Hampshire primary.
11The South Carolina primary could draw more than 600,000 voters, far exceeding turnout in Iowa and New Hampshire.
12Bush said South Carolina would play a huge role as "the first big primary state."
13Bush's pitch Tuesday began with a biography, from his immediate family life to his experience in the private sector and public office.
14He credited his wife, Columba, with "bringing order to my life."
15Being "George's boy, Barbara's boy" means he "won the lottery."
16His wide-range of business ventures, he said, means, "I've signed the front-side of a paycheck. ... Washington has lost its way in this regard. I'm proud that I've been in business and know how it works."
17Eight years as Florida's chief executive gave him practical experience in a state that "looks like America," he said.
18"It's not completely red, not completely blue," Bush said, but "as purple as can be."
19But at an evening reception with state Republican lawmakers in Columbia, Bush emphasized that "good, solid, conservative governance" is his priority.
20"I happen to think that conservative governance ... would be helpful in Washington, D.C." he said, instead of "the food fight, the constant crisis, never solving problems, never trying to forge consensus."
21As he has in past trips to Iowa and New Hampshire, Bush defended his endorsement of Common Core education standards and an immigration overhaul, positions that put him at odds with many conservative voters.
22Bush said Congress should spell out that the federal government not influence curriculum or standards "directly or indirectly," a nod to the Obama administration considering whether a state has adopted the Common Core standards when it awards lucrative federal grants.
23"Look, I'm pretty convinced that my views on this are the right views for sustained economic growth," Bush said.
24"If there's a better idea, I'd like to hear it. But doing nothing? That's not a plan."
