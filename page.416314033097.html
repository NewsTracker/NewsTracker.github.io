Greek 'day of reckoning' shakes stock markets

1NEW YORK - Fear that Greece could default and abandon the euro is rattling global financial markets.
2News that negotiations between Greece and its international lenders are making little progress sent European stock markets down sharply on Friday, and the selling spread across the Atlantic.
3By the close of U.S. trading, stocks across industries were lower, with four of five stocks down.
4Investors shifted money into German government bonds, a perceived haven in troubled times.
5In the U.S., disappointing first-quarter financial results from several big companies fed the selling.
6After American Express reported revenue that fell short of expectations, investors drove down its stock more than 4 percent.
7"The day of reckoning" for Greece is fast approaching, said Uri Landesman, president of investment fund Platinum Partners.
8"People thought everyone would work it out, but if no one caves, there won't be a deal."
9For all the turmoil in the markets, major U.S. stock indexes closed the day with relatively modest losses.
10At one point, the Dow Jones industrial average was down 357, heading for its worst day in six months.
11The Dow regained some of those losses toward the close of trading, ending down 279.47 to 17,826.30, a drop of 1.5 percent.
12That was only the worst drop since March 25.
13The Dow has struggled since reaching a record high on March 2 and is now back where it started the year.
14The Standard & Poor's 500 index lost 23.81 points, or 1.1 percent, to 2,081.18.
15The Nasdaq composite fell 75.98 points, or 1.5 percent, to 4,931.81.
16Greece and its creditors are still struggling to find a deal that can keep the country from defaulting on its debt.
17The argument is over what reforms Greece should make in return for loans.
18Many think Greece will struggle to make payments to the International Monetary Fund due next month if it fails to reach a deal.
19The concerns have caused investors to demand higher rates for loaning money to Greece's government.
20The yield on the country's benchmark 10-year bond jumped to 12.72 percent Friday.
21That rate has more than doubled from 5.51 percent in September.
22In corporate news, Honeywell International fell $2.22, or 2 percent, to $101.70 after reporting disappointing first-quarter results.
23The industrial conglomerate posted earnings per share that beat estimates, but its revenue fell short.
24Advanced Micro Devices plunged 10 percent after reporting a larger loss than investors had expected after the market closed on Thursday.
25The chipmaker's stock fell 29 cents to $2.58.
26Investors have been bracing themselves for a disappointing earnings season.
27Companies in the S&P 500 are expected to report earnings per share fell 2.6 percent from a year earlier, according to S&P Capital IQ, a research firm.
28That would be the first drop since 2009.
29Jim Paulsen, chief investment strategist at Wells Capital Management, said stocks are now somewhat expensive compared with earnings and, along with a list of other worries, the news from Greece on Friday proved just too much to bear.
30"When you have more nervous investors, news becomes magnified," he said.
31Worrisome news out of China also weighed on investors.
32After markets closed in Asia, Chinese financial regulators issued warnings about that country's soaring stock market.
33Regulators said they will tighten rules on borrowing to buy stocks.
34They also plan to make it easier for investors to bet against the market there, The Wall Street Journal reported.
35Shanghai's stock market has more than doubled in the last year.
36"People are thinking maybe the party is over in China," said Doug Cote, chief market strategist for Voya Investment Management.
37"China recognizes that it could be creating a bubble, and now it wants to slow down. It's trying to rein back risk."
38Germany's DAX index dropped 2.6 percent.
39France's CAC 40 shed 1.6 percent and Britain's FTSE 100 fell 0.9 percent.
40Investors piled into German government debt, which is perceived as being among the safest investments denominated in euros.
41Yields on Germany's 10-year government note, which moves opposite to its price, fell to 0.07 percent, a record low, according to Tradeweb.
42The price of oil fell nearly 3 percent Friday on a slowdown in the reduction of working drilling rigs, but finished the week sharply higher.
43A closely watched industry count of drilling rigs showed a decline of 26 U.S. rigs for the week, compared with a decline of 42 last week.
44Benchmark U.S. crude fell 97 cents to close at $55.74 a barrel in New York.
45U.S. crude finished up 8 percent for the week, however.
46Brent crude fell 53 cents to close at $63.45 a barrel in London.
47- Wholesale gasoline fell 0.5 cent to close at $1.930 a gallon.
48- Heating oil fell 2.6 cents to close at $1.882 a gallon.
49- Natural gas rose 0.5 cent to close at $2.634 per 1,000 cubic feet.
50The dollar fell slightly to 118.86 yen while the euro rose to $1.0794.
51Bond prices fell after the U.S. government reported a slight increase in inflation last month.
52The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell slightly to 1.87 percent from 1.88 percent on Thursday.
53Precious and industrial metals futures didn't move much.
54Gold rose $5.10 to $1,203.10 an ounce, silver fell 6 cents to $16.23 an ounce and copper was unchanged at $2.77 a pound.
