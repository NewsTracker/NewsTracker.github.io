Shiite fighters leave Tikrit

1The paramilitary troops had helped Iraqi forces to retake the city from the "IS" fighters in a month-long battle.
2Iraq's Prime Minister Haidar al-Abadi, a moderate Shiite Islamist, decided to pull paramilitaries out of Tikrit after a meeting with local officials on Saturday, who complained of looting by Shiite armed factions.
3"Most of the (paramilitaries) were removed from the city," Ahmed al-Kraim, the head of the council of Tikrit and its province Salahuddin, said on Saturday.
4Hundreds of homes and shops have been robbed or burned since the home city of late Iraq dictator Saddam Hussein was taken by Iraqi forces on Wednesday.
5The city had been occupied by "Islamic State" ten months previously, and the terror group was only pushed out of the city after a month-long joint effort by Iraqi forces and paramilitary allies, supported by US airstrikes on the city.
6On Friday, after reports of paramilitaries ransacking and vandalizing the city, Prime Minister Abadi ordered security forces to arrest anyone breaking the law, and then scheduled his meeting with the governor and key officials.
7"It sent a clear message to everyone. Although it is very challenging, the prime minister is on the top of situation," said Abadi's spokesman Rafid Jaboori.
8"The situation now is calm," a police major in Tikrit said, speaking on condition of anonymity.
9Karim al-Noori, a spokesman for the Shiite paramilitary fighters, confirmed that 80 percent of the Shiite volunteers had left Tikrit.
10Head of Tikrit's council Al-Kraim called the Saturday talks with Abadi "very positive."
11"The looting and vandalism against residents' properties stopped today and things are getting better," al-Kraim said.
12Prime Minister Al-Abadi has called on Iraqi forces to oppose vandalism in the city of Tikrit, which was retaken from the 'Islamic State' this week.
13The city was "burnt in front of our eyes," local officials say.
14Iraq's prime minister says government troops have retaken central Tikrit from "Islamic State" militants.
15The month-long battle for the city is the government's largest military operation yet against the jihadist group.
16Pentagon sources say US-led aircraft bombed Tikrit to support Iraqi forces trying to recapture it from Islamic State fighters.
17The raids puts the US on the same side as Iraqi Shiite militias and their Iranian advisers.
