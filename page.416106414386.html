French network TV5Monde hit by pro-Islamic State hack

1Photo: TV5Monde's Facebook page was hacked by individuals claiming to belong to the Islamic State group.
2French television network TV5Monde was forced to broadcast only pre-recorded programs on Thursday after an "unprecedented" hack by self-proclaimed Islamic State militants, who also hijacked its websites and social networks.
3The Paris-based company, whose programs are broadcast in more than 200 countries worldwide, was the target of a cyber attack that is "unprecedented for us and unprecedented in the history of television", TV5Monde boss Yves Bigot said.
4"Since 5:00am (local time), we have only been able to put out a single program on all our channels.
5"For the moment, we are unable to produce our own programs.
6"When you work in television... and you find out that your 11 channels are down, of course that's one of the most dreadful things that can happen to you."
7The hackers took control of the station and its social media operations late Wednesday, blacking out the TV channels and posting documents on its Facebook page purporting to be the identity cards and CVs of relatives of French soldiers involved in anti-IS operations, along with threats against the troops.
8"Soldiers of France, stay away from the Islamic State! You have the chance to save your families, take advantage of it," read one message on TV5Monde's Facebook page.
9"The CyberCaliphate continues its cyberjihad against the enemies of Islamic State," the message added.
10The defence ministry in Paris said it was working to verify whether the documents were genuine.
11TV5Monde regained control of its social networks by 2:00am (local time) Thursday but television broadcasts were likely to take hours, if not days, to return to normal.
12The attack would have required weeks of preparation, Mr Bigot said.
13"We are putting out an emergency program so that we're not left with a black screen," TV5Monde's human resources director, Jean Corneil, said.
14French prime minister Manuel Valls said the hack was an "unacceptable attack on the freedom of information and expression", voicing "total solidarity with the editorial staff".
15Senior government members flocked to the station to show their support, with interior minister Bernard Cazeneuve saying: "We are up against determined terrorists... we are determined to fight them."
16Foreign minister Laurent Fabius said: "Everything is being done to find those who carried this out, punish them, re-establish the programs and prevent cyber terrorists threatening freedom of expression in the future."
17Culture minister Fleur Pellerin called a meeting of top French media chiefs to discuss the attack.
18The hackers had accused French president Francois Hollande of committing "an unforgivable mistake" by getting involved in "a war that serves no purpose".
19"That's why the French received the gifts of Charlie Hebdo and Hyper Cacher in January," it said on the broadcaster's Facebook page, referring to attacks by Islamist gunmen in Paris on the satirical magazine and a Jewish supermarket that left 17 people dead.
20France is part of a US-led military coalition carrying out air strikes against IS in Iraq and Syria, where the jihadist group has seized swathes of territory and declared an Islamic "caliphate".
21Close to 1,500 French nationals have left France to join the militants' ranks in Iraq and Syria, where they represent almost half the number of European fighters present, according to a report released Wednesday by the French Senate.
22Jihadists have become increasingly adept at using the internet to spread propaganda and attack media outlets.
23In February, the Twitter feed of Newsweek was briefly hacked and threats were made against US president Barack Obama's family.
24And in the immediate aftermath of the Charlie Hebdo attacks, hackers claiming to be Islamists hijacked hundreds of French websites, flooding them with jihadist propaganda.
25"This is certainly a step up," Gibert Ramsay, an expert on cyber-jihadism at St. Andrews University in Scotland, said.
26"For years now, low-level cyber attacks have been a routine part of Islamist mobilisation.
27"They have published manuals on how to hack websites. But this is an escalation."
