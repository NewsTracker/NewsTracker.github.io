Germanwings plane crash: Melbourne mother and son killed on board flight 4U9525

1Foreign Minister Julie Bishop addressed Parliament with news on the two Victorians who were killed on a Germanwings flight which crashed in the French Alps.
2The family of a Melbourne mother and son killed in the Germanwings plane crash has spoken of its shock and sadness at their loss.
3Carol Friday had travelled overseas to spend a few weeks on holiday with her son Greig, who was living in Germany, when they were killed in the air disaster.
4Carol and Greig Friday were killed in the Germanwings plane crash.
5Foreign Minister Julie Bishop read a statement from Friday family members to Parliament on Wednesday that said they were in "deep disbelief" and "crippled by sadness".
6Ms Friday was described as a loving mother-of-two to Greig and his sister Alex, and a devoted wife to her husband Dave.
7She had a sister and three brothers and celebrated her 68th birthday just two days ago.
8She was a registered nurse, midwife and maternal and child health nurse who worked for the City of Casey and lived in Ashwood in Melbourne's south-east.
9Greig Friday was an acoustic engineer who had studied at Monash University.
10"She was a talented artist and enjoyed spending time creating with her local art group," her family's statement said.
11"She loved to travel and has seen many of the world's greatest sights.
12"Carol loved life! She enjoyed every minute with her family and friends".
13Tributes at the Joseph-Koenig Gymnasium secondary school in Haltern am See, Germany on March 24, 2015.
14Mr Friday, an acoustic engineer, was due to turn 30 years old next month.
15He had studied at Monash University and worked at Jacobs Engineering for 18 months before leaving in February to work with his cousin in Germany who ran an organic vegetable business.
16The keen traveller had completed an English as a foreign language teaching course ahead of his European adventure.
17"Greig was a man who loved others and life with a full heart!" his family said.
18Debris scattered over the crash site near Seyne-les-Alpes in the French Alps on Tuesday.
19"They were both extraordinary people and exceptional people who were loved by many, who they loved in return," the family's statement said.
20"They will forever be with us in our hearts, memories and dreams".

25She said Mr Friday was going to teach English in France as a back-up plan, but really wanted to stay with his cousin.

43Mr Friday had been a volunteer with the Victorian Aid Council since 2007, becoming instrumental in the success of its Young & Gay Peer Education program in helping young people overcome homophobia and celebrate their sexual identity.
44VAC peer education coordinator Budi Sudarto said they would miss Mr Friday's "witty personality and his sense of humour".
45"Greig always put others first, and this is evident in his ongoing dedication to both Young & Gay and the Peer Education Program," he said.
46"We will remember Greig, and we want him to know that he left positive mark in the world and put smiles on others' faces as part of his legacy."
47VAC chief Simon Ruth said his organisation had only ever been possible with the support of people like Mr Friday.
48"Today we honour Greig contributions to VAC and the positive impact he has made on the lives of several young gay men in our community," Mr Ruth said.
49The Fridays were among 150 people killed in the Germanwings plane crash.
50They were were on board the Airbus operated by Lufthansa's budget airline Germanwings, which was travelling from Barcelona in Spain to Dusseldorf in Germany.
51It crashed in a snow-clad alpine area in the French Alps.
52Ms Bishop told Parliament that Australian officials were working with the French authorities to determine whether any other Australians citizens, dual nationals or permanent residents were on board the flight.
53Ms Bishop said Australian consular officials had established a mobile office nearby from where they would liaise with French authorities.
54She said it was premature to speculate on what had caused the terrible incident.
55"Relevant authorities will under take the relevant investigation," she said.
56"Lufthansa is working on the thought that it's an accident. When the black box is analysed this will hopefully provide more answers. Australia will of course assist in any way possible."
57The chief executive officer of the City of Casey, Mike Tyler, said the council was "incredibly saddened to learn about the tragic loss of our colleague and friend Carol Friday and her son".
58"Our thoughts are with the family at this time," he said.
59Victoria Premier Daniel Andrews said on Wednesday morning that Victorians had been "very sad to wake up to this tragic news".
60"This is a great tragedy; it's very, very sad news for all of us. Our thoughts, our prayers, our best wishes and our support, most importantly, will be with [the] family."
61Mr Andrews said the accident would be a "painful reminder" for those affected by recent air disasters, especially the shooting down of MH17 and the disappearance of MH370.
62"This incident will be a very painful reminder of other disasters in recent times - whether it's MH370 or MH17 - this will be a very raw moment for many Victorian families who have had to live through those tragedies just in the space of the last 12 months," he said.
63Germanwings said the passenger manifest included two babies.
64Among the other fatalities were 67 German nationals, including 16 high school students and two teachers from Haltern, western Germany, who were returning from an exchange in Spain.
65Prime Minister Tony Abbott also confirmed the news in Canberra.
