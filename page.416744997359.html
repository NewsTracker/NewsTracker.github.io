Baltimore police: Man arrested near scene of recent riots

1An officer speaks to members of the media Monday, May 4, 2015, in Baltimore.
2Lt. Col.
3Melvin Russell said police pursued a man who was spotted on surveillance cameras and appeared to be armed with a handgun.
4Police said the man was taken into custody after a brief chase, during which a gunshot was heard.
5Days after riots crippled Baltimore, tensions again escalated Monday after police said they arrested a man who appeared to be armed.
6The arrest drew a crowd of protesters who chanted, "Black community, control the police!"
7Russell said that police never fired their weapons and that no one was shot.
8Police recovered a handgun loaded with three rounds, one of which was spent.
9The suspect was not injured and did not want to go to a hospital, but was taken in an ambulance anyway, he said.
10Sen. Catherine Pugh, a Democat whose district includes the area where the situation occurred, said she went to a hospital to check on the man.
11She said she asked him if he was OK, and he told her he was.
12"There were no visible injuries," Pugh said, though she didn't know what happened to him.
13The man was visibly upset, but he was able to see his mother before he was released to police, who arrested him, Pugh said.
14The man was able to walk to a police van, where he was strapped in and taken to Central Booking, she said.
15Live television coverage and photos tweeted from the scene showed medics putting a man in an ambulance and a large police presence, with officers lining up to apparently block one street.
16The arrest happened in the same area where police first spotted Freddie Gray on April 12.
17He was arrested and fatally injured in police custody.
18Six officers were charged Friday in Gray's death.
19A few dozen police officers wearing helmets and carrying shields formed a line across North Avenue as crowds gathered across the street, occasionally shouting "Control the police!" at the officers.
20Minutes after police briefed reporters, the line of officers left the street and police began to allow traffic through the intersection.
21Meanwhile, about a dozen protesters briefly blocked traffic while chanting, "Kill the killer cop!" and "Black community, control the police!"
22Chimurenga Waller, an activist from St. Petersburg, Florida, with the Black is Black Coalition for Social Justice, Peace and Reparations, said that even if nobody was shot, the police overreacted to the situation.
23"I think black people are always going to be skeptical about stories by the police, just like we were skeptical about the story about Freddie Gray," he said.
24Pugh, who was nearby and arrived at the scene shortly after the incident, said she was trying to sort through conflicting narratives of what happened.
25She said witnesses insisted that police had shot someone, which police denied.
26She said police did a good job defusing tensions after the incident by quickly breaking down a barricade and scaling back the number of officers on the scene.
27But she said police should consider using different enforcement tactics while tensions are still high.
28"I think we need to keep police presence down at this time, especially in this area," said Pugh, a Democrat who represents west Baltimore.
29"I think we ought to just dial it back a little. Let's give the community a chance to heal."
30James Carter, manager of Metro PCS cellphone store, said the incident happened about 5 feet from his shop on North Avenue.
31He heard a single gunshot and waited to hear another, but didn't.
32Carter said he looked outside and saw no sign of a gun on the sidewalk.
33He went back into his shop, came back out and then saw a gun on the ground.
34He said he also saw a young African-American man lying on the ground.
35Carter said he saw no blood.
36He said the young man yelled as he was put into the ambulance.
37Carters said people are "extremely" jumpy.
38This is the biggest police presence at Pennsylvania and North avenues since Saturday, the last night of the curfew, he said.
