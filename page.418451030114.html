Ash and cinders prompt evacuation of village near Colima volcano in western Mexico

1MEXICO CITY - Ash and cinders spewing from the Colima Volcano in western Mexico have prompted authorities to evacuate 19 people from a nearby village.
2The volcano began erupting on Thursday and has become increasingly active, leading officials to relocate people from the community of Yerbabuena in the municipality of Comala.
3Civil Protection officials said Saturday the residents were taken to a temporary shelter.
4Residents of other communities were evacuating voluntarily.
5The department reported that 2 inches (5 centimeters) of ash had fallen on Yerbabuena.
6The Colima Volcano, which sits near the border of the western states of Colima and Jalisco, is also known as the Volcano of Fire.
