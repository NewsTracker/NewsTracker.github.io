Egypt and Saudi Arabia discuss manoeuvres as Yemen battles rage

1Saudi artillery fired shells at rebel positions from the Saudi border.
2Aden: The United Nations Security Council has imposed an arms embargo targeting the Houthi rebels and their allies who now control most of Yemen, as battles in the south of the country intensified.
3Egypt said it and Saudi Arabia had discussed holding a "major military manoeuvre" in Saudi Arabia with other Gulf states, following talks on the progress of the three-week-old Saudi-led campaign of air strikes against the rebels.
4The United Nations Security Council votes on an arms embargo targeting Yemen's rebels.
5The statement from the Egyptian presidency appeared to be a sign that members of the Arab coalition attacking the rebels may carry through on threats to eventually follow their air campaign with a ground intervention.
6Arab states have been bombing the Houthis and their allies, army units loyal to former president Ali Abdullah Saleh.
7The conflict, though rooted in local rivalries, has become a proxy battlefield for Saudi Arabia and Iran, the main regional powers.
8The UN resolution also demanded that the Houthis and their allies withdraw from areas they have seized, including the capital Sanaa.
9Saudi military spokesman Ahmed Asiri briefs journalists on the Saudi-led coalition's air strikes in the Saudi capital Riyadh.
10On the ground, southern militiamen claimed gains against the Houthis across southern Yemen, including districts of the port of Aden, the last stronghold of loyalists to Saudi-backed Yemeni President Abed Rabbu Mansour Hadi.
11Iran meanwhile prepared to submit a four-point peace plan for Yemen to the UN.
12Its proposal includes a call for an end to Saudi-led air strikes.
13The Security Council on Tuesday imposed a global asset freeze and travel ban on Ahmed Ali Saleh, the son of Ali Abdullah Saleh and former head of Yemen's Republican Guard, and on Houthi leader Abdulmalik al-Houthi.
14The elder Mr Saleh, who was forced to step down by "Arab Spring" protests in 2012, was blacklisted in November 2014.
15Police stand near their headquarters, destroyed by Saudi-led air strikes, in Yemen's north-western city of Saada.
16A statement from the Houthi leadership condemned the resolution, which it said supported "aggression".
17The Security Council voted 14-0 in favour, while Russia abstained.
18"The co-sponsors refused to include the requirements insisted upon by Russia addressed to all sides to the conflict to swiftly halt fire and to begin peace talks," Russian UN ambassador Vitaly Churkin told the Security Council after the vote.
19Iranian Foreign Minister Mohammad Javad Zarif, speaking in Madrid earlier on Tuesday, said Tehran's peace initiative involved a ceasefire, humanitarian assistance, a dialogue between Yemeni factions and a broad-based government.
20Huzam Mohammed al-Meghaly, 64, leads Yemeni expatriates in a protest outside Yemen's embassy in Cairo, demanding the right to return home.
21"This issue should be resolved by the Yemenis. Iran and Saudi Arabia need to talk but we cannot talk to determine the future of Yemen," he said.
22Al-Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula, which is based in Yemen, announced on Tuesday that its spiritual leader had been killed by a US air strike on Monday.
23Ibrahim al-Rubaish was a Saudi national released from the Guantanamo Bay prison camp in 2006.
24US special forces have withdrawn from Yemen since Mr Hadi's government fell, but US air strikes appear to be continuing.
25The Pentagon has armed drones based in the nearby African nation of Djibouti.
26In Washington, a senior US official refused to comment on the strike, but confirmed that the US has "capabilities postured in the area".
27"As we have in the past, we will take action to disrupt continuing, imminent threats to the United States and our citizens," he said.
