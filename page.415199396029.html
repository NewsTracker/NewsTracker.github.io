Putin says Nemtsov assassination a 'disgrace'

1Photo: Russian president Vladimir Putin says the killing of Boris Nemtsov has brought disgrace on Russia.
2Russian president Vladimir Putin has described the killing of opposition politician Boris Nemtsov as a tragedy that has brought disgrace on Russia, and warned against a rise in "extremist crime".
3"We must pay the greatest attention to crimes that prompt a major reaction, including those that are politically charged," he told interior ministry officials at a televised meeting on Wednesday.
4"We must rid Russia of disgrace and tragedies like the one we just saw... the audacious murder of Boris Nemtsov right in the centre of the capital."
5Mr Nemtsov, a longtime Putin critic and anti-corruption crusader, was gunned down near the Kremlin on Friday night in an attack that sent shockwaves through the Russian opposition and the world.
6Mr Putin has previously described Mr Nemtsov's murder as a "provocation" and vowed that everything would be done to bring to justice those who committed a "vile and cynical murder".
7Investigators have said they are examining possible motives for the murder, including Mr Nemtsov's opposition to Russian military actions in Ukraine and a link to his condemnation of the attack against the Charlie Hebdo satirical weekly in Paris in January.
8Mr Putin also warned against a rise in "extremist crime", without directly linking it to Mr Nemtsov's murder.
9"Extremists poison society with militant nationalism, intolerance and aggression," he said, adding that unrest in Ukraine was a consequence of such extremism.
10"It's important to react instantly to any signs of extremist action."
11The Kremlin has denied any involvement, saying that the killing was a "provocation" designed to discredit Mr Putin and strengthen his opponents.
12But Mr Nemtsov's friends say the Kremlin is to blame for fomenting an atmosphere of hatred towards its opponents.

14Look back at the career of Boris Nemtsov who served as a deputy prime minister under Boris Yeltsin before becoming one of the most prominent opponents of Russian president Vladimir Putin.
15Earlier on Wednesday, the director of Russia's federal security service said that an investigation had identified several suspects, without giving details.
16Russian news agencies reported that investigators were seeking a car in connection to the killing that may be connected to the finance ministry.
17The ministry said in a statement sent to Reuters that the car, a Ford, did not belong to the ministry itself but was owned by a state enterprise responsible for providing security to government institutions, including the finance ministry.
18The vehicle drove past the spot where Mr Nemtsov was shot after the killing and when police patrol cars had already arrived at the scene, the statement said.
