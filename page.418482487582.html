Nintendo president Satoru Iwata dies aged 55

1Satoru Iwata, the long-time chief executive and president of Nintendo, has died aged 55 of cancer, the company has said.
2The video games company announced his death in a short statement on Monday morning.
3"Nintendo deeply regrets to announce that President Satoru Iwata passed away on July 11, 2015, due to a bile duct growth," the company said.
4Mr Iwata, who was made the fourth Nintendo president in its 126-year history in 2002, oversaw the release of the Wii and the Nintendo DS, the best-selling home and handheld consoles respectively in Nintendo's history.
5More recently, Mr Iwata announced a partnership with game developer DeNA to put Nintendo games on mobile devices.
6The executive's health issues had been documented in the past.
7He took leave last year for surgery, forcing him to miss the company's annual meeting but told shareholders a year ago that the illness had been caught at an early stage.
8Nintendo highlighted that research and development head Genyo Takeda and creative guru Shigeru Miyamaoto remain at the company as representative directors, although it is unclear at this stage who will take charge of the company.
9Mr Iwata was the first Nintendo president who was not related to the Yamauchi family that founded the company.
