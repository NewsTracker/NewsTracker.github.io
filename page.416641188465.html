Baffert aiming at 1st 1-2 Kentucky Derby finish since 1948 (Yahoo Sports)

2LOUISVILLE, Ky.
3(AP) -- Bob Baffert was a big-shot quarter horse trainer when he took his cowboy hat and scuffed boots to the high-class world of thoroughbred racing.
4He gave himself three years to make a splash.
5After a slow start and some embarrassing losses, he found his footing.
6Now the Hall of Famer is saddling the top two favorites for the Kentucky Derby.
7Heady stuff for the kid who grew up watching the race on television in his border hometown of Nogales, Arizona.
8''Destiny,'' he jokingly proclaimed Thursday outside his cement-block barn at Churchill Downs.
9Baffert has early 5-2 favorite American Pharoah and 3-1 second choice Dortmund in what some believe is the strongest 1-2 punch since trainer Ben Jones won with Citation and finished second with Coaltown in 1948.
10''I've got (Jameis) Winston and (Marcus) Mariota in my barn,'' Baffert said, referencing the likely top two picks in the NFL draft.
11He experienced that scenario in 1998, when Real Quiet and Indian Charlie were 1-2 headed for home.
12Real Quiet won, but Victory Gallop broke up a Baffert exacta; Indian Charlie finished third.
13It was the second of Baffert's three Derby victories.
14He had two firsts and a second in the first three years he entered 19 years ago; his last victory was with War Emblem in 2002.
15''I thought, 'This is fun, this is easy,''' the white-haired, 62-year-old trainer said.
16Quite a difference from his humble beginnings, when he started with one horse.
17His brothers and a friend cheered him on at his first few races in Southern California.
18Baffert's horse was badly beaten and they declined his invitation to come back again.
19Baffert didn't apprentice under an established thoroughbred trainer, the usual preparation before going out on your own.
20Baffert is as eager as everyone else to see which is better: American Pharoah or Dortmund.
21''Until they get with all the other horses, you just don't know what you have,'' Baffert said.
22American Pharoah has glided over the Churchill Downs strip since arriving direct from an eight-length win in the Arkansas Derby.
23''We know that Pharoah is a brilliant horse,'' the trainer said.
24Dortmund has taken a better liking to Churchill's dirt surface than his home track at Santa Anita in California.
25He's unusually tall (standing 17 hands or nearly 5 feet, 8 inches) and weighs 1,280 pounds.
26''He's really, really quick,'' Baffert said.
27American Pharoah, by comparison, is just over 16 hands and weighs 1,170.
28Both colts go into the 1 1/4-mile Derby with big targets on their backs.
29''Anytime Bob Baffert has a couple of Kentucky Derby horses, you have to give them a lot of respect,'' said rival trainer Todd Pletcher, who will saddle three horses including 8-1 third choice Carpe Diem.
30Yet Baffert knows the sting of losing, too.
31He thought he had a couple of aces in 2001, with 9-5 favorite Point Given and third choice Congaree.
32But Point Given, who went on to win Horse of the Year, finished fifth and Congaree third.
33''The owners, they can dream in Technicolor,'' he said.
34''I dream in black and white.
35Baffert visited his first Derby winner, Silver Charm, this week and got teary thinking about his late parents and several of his former clients who provided him with talented horses before they died, too.
