Developments on Baltimore man fatally injured in custody

1A 25-year-old Baltimore man died a week after his spine was mysteriously injured while he was in police custody.
2A look at the developments Wednesday in the case of Freddie Gray.
3LYNCH MOB?
4A statement released by the Baltimore police union comparing protesters of Gray's death to a "lynch mob" drew criticism on Twitter by users who called it racially insensitive and inappropriate, given that the demonstrations have been peaceful.
5A little later, at a news conference, union President Gene Ryan backpedaled, saying, "Maybe I should reword that."
6"I don't want it to turn into a lynch mob," he said.
7"I'm afraid of the crowd becoming hostile," said a tense-sounding Ryan.
8"They have been very peaceful to this point. My main concern is for the public and the police officers that they remain peaceful and exercise their constitutional right to do what they're doing."
9Ryan said he feared violence because of protests in Ferguson, Missouri, and New York that followed police-involved deaths of unarmed suspects.
10At an evening protest later in the day, more than 100 demonstrators pushed on a police barricade at the Western District precinct, and about 30 police officers pushed back.
11Some protesters hurled obscenities and threw soda cans and bottles at the line of police behind the barricades, but none hit the officers and the demonstration remained largely peaceful.
12For the most part, police have ignored the insults and not reacted to provocations from the protesters.
13At least three people were detained.
14No one has been injured.
15While a group of protesters remained outside the police station, a second group led by a man with a bullhorn marched in the streets, stopping at various intersections for die-ins.
16The group would block an intersection briefly before moving on.
17They passed near the hospital where Gray died, and continued on to the Inner Harbor, where they briefly blocked a key downtown intersection before continuing on and stopping in front of City Hall.
18Bernadette Washington said she has been marching every day since Gray's death and she's frustrated that police don't seem to be embracing the message.
19"They think it's a joke. For real. They just stand there," Washington said, gesturing at officers deployed outside the district station.
20Washington said she grew up with Gray and called him a good person who didn't need to die.
21She didn't understand why the six officers involved in Gray's arrest are still drawing a paycheck while they're suspended.
22"All we want is justice," Washington said.
23Five of the six officers involved in Gray's arrest gave statements that day, a week before his death, police said Wednesday while declining to reveal the content of what they said.
24An attorney who works with the Baltimore Fraternal Order of Police had mentioned the statements earlier in the day as a way of showing that the officers involved have been cooperative.
25Police said the department's internal investigation into Gray's arrest will be turned over to the Baltimore State's Attorney's Office for review on May 1.
26The prosecutor's office can determine whether to file charges against the officers involved or present the case to a grand jury.
27The U.S. Justice Department has opened a civil rights investigation into Gray's death.
28Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake said she welcomed the Justice Department's involvement.
29She has called along for an outside review.
30"Whenever a police force conducts an internal investigation, there are always appropriate questions of transparency and impartiality," she said in a statement.
31"My goal has always been to get answers to the questions so many of us are still asking with regards to Mr. Gray's death."
32Gray's family has an attorney who is working on his own investigation.
33Maryland Gov. Larry Hogan said he has spoken with city and state officials about the situation and won't be ordering any further investigations.
34He said he believes the current investigations are being handled properly and that "the last thing we need is people stepping all over each other."
35"We don't want to make politics out of this tragic situation," Hogan told reporters after an Earth Day event in Annapolis.
36He said his heart goes out to Gray's family "and everyone involved in the entire incident."
