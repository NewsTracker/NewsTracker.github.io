Egypt court sentences 22 to death over 2013 attack on police

1The ruling was the latest action against alleged supporters of the ousted Mohammed Morsi, whose Muslim Brotherhood group has faced a violent government crackdown after his one-year rule ended and it was banned and declared a terrorist organization.
2Morsi himself faces a verdict on Tuesday over accusations he was responsible for the killing of protesters while president.
3The ruling will be the first in several ongoing trials against him.
4The Giza court said eight of the condemned were sentenced in absentia on charges that included murder, attempted murder, possession of unlicensed firearms, destruction of property and vandalism.
5The ruling, which can be appealed, also sentenced a minor involved in the case to 10 years.
6The attack took place in Kerdasa, a restive town west of Cairo considered a militant stronghold and where gunmen and rampaging crowds have killed policemen in other acts of violence.
7In the largest incident, 11 policemen were killed and their bodies mutilated in August 2013.
8That September, gunmen killed a police general during a raid.
9Egypt has been sharply criticized for issuing mass death sentences largely targeting Islamists.
10Over 22,000 people have been arrested since Morsi's ouster, including most of the Brotherhood's top leaders, as well as large numbers of others swept up by police during pro-Morsi protests.
11Also Monday, a roadside bomb hit an armored vehicle in the northern Sinai Peninsula, killing three soldiers, Egypt's official news agency said.
12MENA said the attack was carried out by Islamic extremists early in the day near the border town of Rafah.
13The team in the armored vehicle was searching for explosives.
14Militant attacks, mainly targeting Egyptian security forces, have spiked since Morsi's overthrow, which came after massive protests against his rule.
15Most of the largest attacks have been carried out in northern Sinai and were claimed by a local affiliate of the Islamic State group.
16At least 14 people, mostly soldiers, were killed in a wave of attacks in northern Sinai last week.
