Stocks rise as China rebounds, Greece works on reform plan

1Employee of the Tokyo Stock Exchange look at monitors during morning trading in Tokyo Thursday, July 9, 2015.
2Asian stocks markets were volatile on Thursday as China's main index seesawed after a prolonged sell-off while Hong Kong's benchmark rebounded strongly.
3Asian stocks rebounded on Thursday as Chinese government measures aimed at stabilizing free-falling share prices took hold.
4European benchmarks gained as Greece's government raced to finalize details of reforms as it seeks a third bailout from European nations.
5KEEPING SCORE: In early European trading, France's CAC-40 added 1.2 percent to 4,693.44 and Germany's DAX rose 0.9 percent to 10,842.77.
6Britain's FTSE 100 gained 0.6 percent to 6,528.71.
7Futures pointed to a rise on Wall Street following the previous day's sell-off.
8Dow futures were up 0.6 percent to 17,553.00.
9Broader S&P 500 futures jumped 0.8 percent to 2,054.70.
10CHINA TURMOIL: Frantic efforts by authorities to arrest the nearly monthlong slide in the country's stock markets appeared to be giving the market an artificial bounce, analysts said.
11A flurry of measures announced Wednesday included a government order to state companies and executives to buy shares.
12A directive from the China Securities Regulatory Commission requires investors owning more than 5 percent of a company's shares to not sell any of their holdings for the next six months.
13ANALYST VIEW: "Movements in the Chinese stock market don't necessarily correlate to China's economy," said analyst Jasper Lawler at CMC Markets in Singapore.
14"This applied on the way up when Chinese stocks rallied over 100 percent in the space of a year while economic growth was the slowest in six years. This should also be the case on the way down; just because stocks are crashing, it doesn't necessarily have a knock-on effect on China's economy."
15GREECE CRISIS: Greece's government is racing to finalize a plan of reforms for its third bailout, hoping the proposal will meet with approval from its European partners and stave off a potentially catastrophic exit from Europe's joint currency, the euro, within days.
16Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras was meeting Thursday with finance ministry officials after the government requested a new three-year aid program from Europe's bailout funds and promised to immediately enact reforms, including to taxes and pensions, in return.
17Details of Greece's reforms must be submitted by Friday morning, giving time for creditors to review them ahead of a summit of the European Union's 28 members set for Sunday.
18ASIA SCORECARD: The Shanghai Composite in mainland China surged 5.8 percent to 3,709.33 after initially opening sharply lower.
19Hong Kong's Hang Seng rebounded 3.7 percent to 24,392.79 a day after suffering its biggest drop since the financial crisis.
20Other markets turned from losses to gains.
21Japan's Nikkei 225 rose 0.6 percent to 19,855.50 and South Korea's Kospi added 0.6 percent to 2,027.33.
22Australia's S&P/ASX 200 ended little changed at 5,471.00.
23Markets in Singapore and Taiwan fell while Thailand and the Philippines rose.
24ENERGY: Benchmark U.S. crude rose 53 cents to $52.19 a barrel in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
25The contract fell 68 cents to close at $51.65 a barrel on Wednesday.
26Brent crude, an international benchmark, rose 75 cents to $57.80 in London.
27CURRENCIES: The dollar rose to 121.33 yen from 120.80 in late trading Wednesday.
28The euro slipped to $1.1040 from $1.1072.
