Group backing Hillary Clinton gets $1M from anonymous donors

1WASHINGTON (AP) - Hillary Rodham Clinton told a cheering crowd at her largest rally so far that "the endless flow of secret, unaccountable money" must be stopped.
2Two weeks later, the main super PAC backing her bid for the Democratic presidential nomination accepted a $1 million contribution that cannot be traced.
3The seven-figure donation - made June 29 to the pro-Clinton Priorities USA Action - came from another super political action committee.
4Its sole donors are nonprofits that are not legally required to reveal information about their contributors.
5Such contributions are sometimes called "dark money" by advocates for stricter campaign finance rules.
6One group has called on the Clinton super PAC to return the money.
7But a spokesman says it is "playing by the rules."
