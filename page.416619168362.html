Report: Defective Component Slowed Apple Watch Rollout

1Apple Inc (AAPL) limited availability of the Apple Watch after a key component supplied by a Chinese company was found to be defective, the Wall Street Journal reported on Wednesday, citing people familiar with the matter.
2The component, called the taptic engine, produces the sensation of being tapped on the wrist, the newspaper reported.
3Apple declined to comment on the story.
4Apple has not given any sales figures for its hotly anticipated Apple Watch since it began taking orders this month.
5Chief Executive Tim Cook said on Monday that demand continued to outstrip supply.
6Some taptic engines supplied by Shenzhen, China-based AAC Technologies Holdings Inc started to break down over time, the Journal said, citing the people.
7The company is one of two suppliers of these components to Apple, it said.
8As a result, Apple is now sourcing nearly all of its components from the other supplier, Japan's Nidec Corp. It may take time for the company to increase production, the people told the Journal.
9The Journal said an AAC spokeswoman declined to comment about the company's customers.
10Reuters was unable to reach the company for comment outside of regular business hours in China.
