3Three rival candidates entered the race in January: Prince Ali bin al-Hussein of Jordan, Luis Figo of Portugal and Michael van Praag of the Netherlands.
4Van Praag, however, dropped out of the race on Thursday May 21, 2015, and switched his support to FIFA vice president Prince Ali.
5Background: FIFA President (since 1998).
6Has worked for FIFA since 1975, including 17 years as secretary general.
7Nominated by: Not yet identified.
8Campaign Promises: No published manifesto.
9In a letter to 209 voting federations this month, Blatter said his priorities include more effort to tackle discrimination and match-fixing, promote women's soccer, protect players' health and "consolidate our relationships with our business partners."
10Favors 32-team World Cup.
11Key quote: "We have to go on (with) what we have done so far and improve."
12Background: FIFA vice president for Asia (2011).
13President, Jordan Football Association (1999).
14Founder, Asian Football Development Project.
15Nominated by: Jordan, Belarus, England, Georgia, Malta, United States.
16Campaign Promises: Restore continental rotation when choosing World Cup hosts.
17An open debate among all FIFA members about changing World Cup format beyond the 2022 tournament.
18Increase FIFA's revenues by improving the reputation and brand to attract sponsors.
19Fund scholarships for coaches to work in less developed soccer countries.
20End a "culture of intimidation."
21Key quote: "We need a change of culture and a departure from FIFA's authoritarian approach to strategy."
22Background: FIFA World Player of the Year in 2001.
23UEFA Champions League winner in 2002 with Real Madrid.
24FIFA Under-20 World Cup winner in 1991.
25Nominated by: Portugal, Denmark, Luxembourg, Macedonia, Montenegro, Poland.
26Campaign Promises: Expand World Cup to 40 teams or a 48-team tournament with two continents each hosting 24 teams in a group stage before a single country hosts the knockout rounds.
27Take $1 billion from FIFA's reserves to share among the 209 member federations.
28Guarantee a seat on FIFA executive committee to each country which has won the World Cup.
29Key quote: "I look at the reputation of FIFA right now and I don't like it. Football deserves better."
30Background: President, Dutch soccer federation (2008).
31UEFA executive committee member (2009).
32Nominated by: Netherlands, Belgium, Faeroe Islands, Romania, Scotland, Sweden.
33Withdrawal: Pulled out of election race on May 21 and switched his support to Prince Ali.
34Previous Campaign Promises: Expanded 40-team World Cup with "proportionally more countries outside Europe."
35Serve a single 4-year presidential term to "normalize" FIFA, cut costs and modernize the institutional culture.
36Publish the presidential salary.
37Retain the outgoing president as an adviser and head of a "Sepp Blatter Foundation" to help underprivileged children.
38Key quote: "The current state of disarray asks for a change in leadership."
