27"It is unclear whether Mrs. Clinton's practice ran afoul of federal laws and regulations governing the retention of official records," the Journal says.
28The laws and regulations regarding the handling of electronic communications among federal agencies has undergone several changes in recent years, including an amendment to the Federal Records Act that President Obama signed into law in November, 2014.
29The bill requires that e-mails dealing with official matters that are sent from a personal account must be forwarded to an official account within 20 days.
30A September 2013 bulletin from NARA suggests federal employees "should not generally use personal email accounts to conduct official agency business."
31The bulletin suggests exceptions could be "emergency situations" or on occasions where the employee is contacted through their personal e-mail account.
32Both the Federal Records Act amendment and NARA bulletin took effect after Clinton left the State Department in February, 2013.
