Station suspends DJ over posting on arrest record of Baltimore man fatally injured in custody

1PITTSBURGH - A Pittsburgh radio station has suspended a disc jockey over his online posting about the arrest record of a Baltimore man whose fatal injury in police custody led to rioting.
2Officials at FM station WAMO 100 said Wednesday the posting by Mike Jax was "too controversial" and partly inaccurate.
3They say listeners complained and some threatened to harm Jax, whose real name is Mike Jackson.
4Jax has been suspended for at least two weeks.
5He posted on Instagram a list of criminal charges he said Freddie Gray had faced in recent years and the comment, "He was pretty busy before he was unjustly killed at the hands of Baltimore PD. #prayforbaltimore."
6Jax apologized online.
7He says he wasn't trying to justify Gray's death, but just the opposite.
8WAMO bills itself as Pittsburgh's No.
91 choice for urban music.
