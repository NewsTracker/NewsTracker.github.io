Shia fighters leave Tikrit after looting, say Iraq officials

1Shia paramilitaries celebrate victory after returning from Tikrit, Iraq on April 4, 2015.
2Paramilitaries started leaving Tikrit on Saturday following a government deal after locals complained that some fighters had spent several days looting the Sunni city after helping retake it from the Islamic State in Iraq and the Levant (ISIL).
3"As of this afternoon, the [paramilitaries] started to leave the city towards the surrounding areas outside," said Ahmed al-Kraim, the head of the council of Tikrit and its province Salahuddin.
4"The looting and vandalism against residents' properties stopped today and things are getting better."
5The decision for the paramilitaries to exit came after a meeting between Prime Minister Haidar al-Abadi and officials from Salahuddin province.
6Tikrit was retaken from ISIL on Wednesday.
