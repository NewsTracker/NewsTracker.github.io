Ukraine fighting rages despite efforts to revive truce

Man who won heroism medal for thwarting a bank robbery now charged of with holding up a bank himself - and stealing $15,000

1Artillery was still raining down near Debaltseve, a strategic railway hub, on Thursday, and the Ukrainian military said its troops had come under fire elsewhere from rebels.
2Western nations have refused to give up on a peace deal negotiated last week even though rebels disavowed it to seize Debaltseve.
3Thousands of besieged Ukrainian troops pulled out of the town on Wednesday in one of the worst defeats for the Kiev government of a 10-month war that has killed more than 5,000 people.
4Pro-Russian separatists have broken the ceasefire more than 250 times since it went into effect earlier this week, a US official said on Thursday.
5"The United States condemns continuing attacks by Russia-backed separatists in and around Debaltseve, Mariupol and other locations in eastern Ukraine which violate the ceasefire and flout the Minsk agreements," Jen Psaki, State Department spokesperson, said.
6Psaki repeated US calls on Russia and the separatists "to stop their attacks immediately, withdraw heavy weapons, halt the flow of fighters and equipment from Russia into Ukraine".
7She also urged that OSCE monitors be allowed into the areas under rebel control to verify whether the ceasefire was holding.
8In Artemivsk, a government-held town north of Debaltseve where Ukrainian troops arrived after evacuating the besieged town, soldiers spoke of their flight under gunfire as they withdrew on Wednesday.
9At least 13 Ukrainian soldiers have been killed and dozens more went missing in the fighting in Debaltseve since the ceasefire earlier this week.
10Local military officials said rebels had launched mortar attacks on government-held positions further south, near the coastal city of Mariupol, and were building up forces there.
11"Right now there are mortar attacks on Shyrokine," a local military spokesperson said by phone, referring to a village about 30km east of Mariupol, along the coast of the Sea of Azov.
12"There is no attempt to seize our positions up to now. The rebels are bringing up reserves."
13Mariupol, a port of 500,000 people, is the biggest government-held city in the two rebellious provinces, and Kiev's biggest fear is that rebels will try to capture it.
14Western countries say Russia is behind the rebel advance, having deployed thousands of troops with advanced weaponry into eastern Ukraine to fight on the separatists' behalf.
15Russia denies it is behind the fighting.
16It sponsored a UN Security Council resolution calling for all sides to stop firing but never criticised the rebels for advancing on Debaltseve.
17President Vladimir Putin told Ukraine hours before the town fell that it should allow its troops there to surrender.
18The rebels have said Debaltseve is the only place where the ceasefire does not apply, and have suggested they now intend to implement the truce.
19They have announced that they are pulling back some heavy weapons, which is required under the peace deal.
20The deal was agreed at all night talks last week in the Belarus capital Minsk, with the leaders of France and Germany mediating between Putin and Ukraine's President Petro Poroshenko.
21The US was not around the negotiating table for the talks with Germany, France, Russia and Ukraine which hammered out the peace deal.
22Russia rejects claims it has been supporting rebels in Ukraine and says Kiev is responsible for breaking the ceasefire.
23Ukraine ceasefire has been repeatedly violated by shelling from both sides.
1Mark Rothwell, a 49-year-old Englishman, is accused of barging into a community bank in Portland, Oregon, with a 9mm handgun drawn.
2Police say he shouted at the tellers to hand over everything they had, then made off in a pickup truck with a bag stuffed with notes totaling $15,703.
3Officers tracked down his white Toyota Tacoma and found the cash stashed inside, along with the gun, according to an arrest affidavit.
4Sniffer dogs then led police Rothwell, who apparently confessed to the robbery on the spot.
5He reportedly told officers: 'It's me you want... I just robbed the bank'.
6It is a dramatic reversal for Rothwell, who was awarded a Civilian Medal for Heroism in 2010 for tackling a man to the ground as he tried to rob a Chase Bank.
7He knocked a gun - later revealed to be fake - out of the man's hand, then pinned him to the ground while officers arrived.
8However, Rothwell allegedly surpassed that would-be criminal by taking a real 9mm Baretta firearm into the Albina Community Bank and making off with the cash.
9Police are yet to suggest a motive for Rothwell, who was born in London but moved to the United States more than a decade ago and now runs a home renovation business.
10According to The Oregonian, bank employees as well as surveillance footage confirmed that Rothwell was the man involved in the robbery.
11Bank tellers were reportedly able to recognize him because he did not fully cover his face, and had a recognizably European accent.
12Giving an account of his earlier crime-fighting, in a 2010 interview Rothwell said he sprang into action after hearing the would-be robber threaten the teller, who reminded him of his daughter.
13'It's just one of those things - someone points a gun at a girl just a couple of years older than my own daughter.
14Rothwell, a father of two, told the Oregonian at the time that he had been a policeman himself, and had served in the military.
15At the time of the first robbery, he said police at first mistook him for the suspect.
16On Friday his public defender lawyer, Ruben Iniguez, declined to comment.
17Rothwell is due to be arraigned on March 12.
