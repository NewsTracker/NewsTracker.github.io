Lawyers: Millionaire Robert Durst illegally arrested

R&B singer The Weeknd, in sultry mood, performs during SXSW

1In the papers filed at the State Criminal District Court in New Orleans, William Gibbens and Dick DeGuerin asked a magistrate judge to schedule a preliminary hearing so that they can prove that there is no probable cause to keep Durst jailed.
2They say he should be released.
3The judge agreed to schedule that hearing during another court appearance by Durst on Monday in New Orleans.
4The attorneys also asked the judge to order that evidence be preserved from the hotel where Durst was arrested.
5They are seeking any and all records related to Durst and Everett Ward, the pseudonym Durst used to check into the hotel.
6The documents blasted the arrest, which the attorneys say was orchestrated "to coincide with the final episode of the HBO docudrama 'The Jinx.'" The six-part documentary concluded Sunday.
7Durst, a member of a wealthy New York real estate family, was charged with murder in California for the December 2000 shooting death of Susan Berman.
8Durst's arrest came shortly before the HBO finale about his links to three killings - that of his first wife, who disappeared in New York in 1982; Berman, the daughter a mobster and a close friend who acted as his spokeswoman after his wife disappeared; and a 71-year-old neighbor in Texas whose dismembered body was found floating in Galveston Bay in 2001.
9Durst has only ever been tried for the Texas killing, and he was acquitted of murder.
10Durst waived extradition in New Orleans but is being held there on charges that he was a felon in possession of a gun and with illegally carrying a weapon with a controlled substance.
11Court documents say investigators found nearly $43,000 in cash, a gun and a rubber mask that could cover his head and neck in his hotel room when he was arrested.
12Authorities have also said marijuana was found in the room.
13However, Durst's attorneys say his previous convictions are not serious enough to merit the gun charge.
