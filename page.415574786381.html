Poland sends doctors to help Poles hurt in Tunisia

1Poland sent a plane with doctors and diplomats to Tunisia on Thursday to help and bring back Poles injured in a gun attack in a museum in Tunis.
2Foreign Minister Grzegorz Schetyna said Thursday morning that according to information he had at the moment, two Poles were killed in the attack, two were missing and nine were hospitalized with injuries that weren't life-threatening.
3The Foreign Ministry said later Thursday that one missing man was found in a hospital, with injuries but in good condition.
4Their names haven't been released.
5Some of those injured were expected to be flown to Poland on Friday, while some other Polish tourists were being flown back Thursday night to Warsaw.
6Around 300 Poles were vacationing in Tunisia at the time of the attack, according to tourist offices.
7National flags on government buildings were flown half-staff in a sign of mourning.
8On Friday, lawmakers will observe a moment of silence.
9Poland's national white-and-red flag flying at half-staff on the Belweder Palace of Polish President Bronislaw Komorowski in Warsaw, Poland on Thursday, March 19, 2015, in a sign of mourning for Poles killed and injured in the armed attack on a museum in the Tunisian capital of Tunis on Wednesday.
10Officials said that two Poles were confirmed killed, and others were hospitalized, but stressed the figures could change as Tunisian officials were still identifying the victims.
