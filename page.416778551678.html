Review: Small life enhancements come with Apple Watch

1NEW YORK (AP) - Apple Watch isn't so much a lifestyle revolution as it is a collection of small enhancements that add up.
2Apple's smartwatch has a wider range of apps than rival watches from Samsung, Motorola and others.
3In my few weeks with one, I've been following my favorite baseball team more closely.
4I've walked to places without staring at a phone screen for directions.
5I've even stepped away from my desk more often - despite my annoyance at the watch for nagging me to move.
6You'll find ways to use it if you decide to spend $349 or more on one, though that isn't saying you absolutely need one.
7Think of it as a Swiss Army knife with a collection of tools that don't seem necessary - until you find yourself using them.
8Notifications are central to the watch experience, whether that's a new message, breaking news or a score change involving the New York Mets.
9In some cases, I can dictate a reply or select a pre-written response.
10The current temperature appears on my chosen watch face with Mickey Mouse.
11I glance at the watch throughout the day, but I need the phone for anything deep.
12Though I like not having to constantly pull out my phone, I have to resist feeling that everything's urgent.
13When the watch dinged during a massage, I held out for just a few seconds before the temptation to check took over.
14It was easier to resist with the phone in the backpack.
15It's a tricky balance getting the watch to enhance your life, rather than take it over.
16With Apple Pay on iPhones, I'm able to leave my wallet in my pocket to pay at several chains, including McDonald's and Whole Foods.
17With the watch, I'm able to leave my phone in the pocket, too.
18The watch can also generate barcodes for Starbucks' payment card, Fandango movie tickets and boarding passes for some airlines.
19I don't have to fish for paper or a phone while carrying luggage or popcorn.
20But if the merchant's scanner isn't hand-held or adjustable, you might have to twist your wrist to align the barcode with the scanner.
21Or your wrist might not fit under the scanner at all.
22While fitness trackers typically focus on counting steps, Apple Watch tracks calories burned and minutes of exercise.
23It also pushes you to stand up and move around throughout the day.
24Three colorful rings display your progress, turning fitness into a game.
25Apps from outside parties offer additional features, such as a record of the route you ran.
26I was worried that promised battery life is just 18 hours, given that I tax the watch and its heart-rate sensor with heavy exercise.
27But I haven't had problems as long as I charge it overnight.
28It's not designed for sleep tracking, as dedicated fitness devices such as Fitbit do.
29Offsetting the health benefits: I can use the Remote app to control video on Apple TV instead of getting up to grab the physical remote.
30I can also control the phone's camera for selfies.
31Parents with kids can set timers remotely for Nick Jr. and PBS Kids - and end arguments over screen time and bedtime.
32In many cases, I need to assemble features from multiple apps.
33For walking and driving directions, Apple's Maps taps my wrist when it's time to make a turn.
34But it doesn't do transit.
35Citymapper does, but lacks turn-by-turn navigation to get to the subway.
36It's back to Apple Maps for that.
37There no Facebook or any Google apps yet, though you get notifications that normally go to the phone.
38Facebook's Instagram is available and lets you like photos, but limits commenting to 24 emoticons.
39I'm continually learning about new apps, including Evernote for dictating reminders and other notes.
40It'll take time for app developers to write the capabilities and for me to discover them.
41It'll also take time to fully get used to all the gestures and controls, though I was much better at it after a few days.
42I have to remind myself that the Apple Watch is a first-generation product that's giving me a lot I didn't have before.
43With other smartwatches, I'm happy just to get basic functionality.
44Apple Watch is good at the basics, so I'm now demanding more.
