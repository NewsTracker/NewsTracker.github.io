Muhammadu Buhari takes over Nigeria in crisis

1ABUJA, Nigeria (AP) - Nigerians celebrated their newly reinforced democracy Friday, dancing, singing praises and releasing white doves as Muhammadu Buhari, the first candidate to beat a sitting president at the polls, became their president.
2U.S. Secretary of State John Kerry, accompanied by the commander of U.S. Africa Command, Gen. David M. Rodriguez, was the first foreign official to meet with Nigeria's new leader after the inauguration.
3A senior State Department official said Washington is ready to increase military aid and could quickly send more advisers.
4To roars of approval at the ceremony, President Buhari pledged to take personal charge of the fight against Boko Haram Islamic extremists and said he will root out human rights violations by the military - abuses that prevented full military cooperation from the U.S. and Britain.
5New Nigerian President, Muhammadu Buhari, salutes his supporters during his Inauguration in Abuja, Nigeria, Friday, May 29, 2015.
6Nigerians celebrated their newly reinforced democracy Friday, dancing and singing songs and praises at the inauguration of Muhammadu Buhari, the first candidate to beat a sitting president at the polls.
7Buhari, a 72-year-old former general who ruled briefly as a military dictator in the 1980s, calls himself a "born-again democrat" and has pledged to fight the endemic corruption that keeps a rich nation impoverished.
8"We see him as our only hope against this crippling corruption," said Efo Okorare, curator of an open-air exhibition of portraits of Nigerian leaders, pointing out the many in military uniforms.
9Buhari saluted Nigerians, whether or not they voted for him.
10"I belong to everybody and I belong to nobody," he said to applause.
11"I intend to serve as president to all Nigerians."
12Nigeria is Africa's most populous nation with its biggest economy and is the largest oil producer, but government coffers have been hit by massive corruption, a devalued naira currency, low oil prices and a $63 billion debt which grows as Nigeria borrows more to pay government salaries.
13Some nervous politicians feared Buhari's promise to retrieve ill-gotten gains signal a witch hunt.
14"These fears are groundless," Buhari said, though he said some of his predecessors had acted "like spoiled children, breaking everything in the house."
15He promised to "ensure that the gross corruption" is checked.
16Buhari, resplendent in cream traditional robes, took the oath of office at Eagle Square in Nigeria's capital.
17The retired major-general then inspected troops in the plaza, decked out in colors of Nigeria's green and white flag, and waved to supporters from the back of an open vehicle.
18A 21-gun salute boomed during the handover of power which is a turning point in Nigeria's democratic evolution.
19Outside, people danced and sang "Sai baba, sai Buhari," meaning "Only father, only Buhari."
20Some swept the road with reed brooms, signaling Buhari's promised clean-up.
21The newly elected government "is basking in a reservoir of (international) good will and high expectations," Buhari said, promising to take advantage of it.
22"Nigeria has a window of opportunity to fulfill our mission as our great nation."
23Kerry tweeted "Congratulations to @MBuhari & the Nigerian people. A privilege to be here to celebrate #Nigeria's historic & peaceful democratic transition."
24Many African leaders attended the inauguration along with France's Foreign Minister Laurent Fabius.
25Buhari thanked the leaders of neighboring Cameroon, Chad and Niger for contributing to a multinational offensive that this year has driven Boko Haram from towns where it had declared an Islamic caliphate.
26Suicide bombings, abductions and hit-and-run attacks in northeast Nigeria continue by what he called "a mindless, godless group."
27The insurgency has killed more than 13,000 people and driven more than 1.5 million from their homes.
28Buhari said he will not consider the war won without rescuing those held hostage by the extremists, including more than 200 schoolgirls whose mass abduction last year prompted international outrage.
29"This government will do all it can to rescue them alive," Buhari said.
30He blamed "official bungling, negligence, complacency or collusion" that allowed Boko Haram to become "a terrifying force."
31Political science professor Richard Joseph of Northwestern University said Buhari's victory is a sign of hope.
32"The world desperately needs a victory against cultist jihadism. Nigeria (under Buhari) can provide it," he wrote in a blog.
33Departing President Goodluck Jonathan last year halted U.S. training of a battalion to fight Boko Haram.
34No reason was given but his officials had expressed anger at U.S. refusals to sell Nigeria weapons including helicopter gunships.
35The United States was hindered by a law preventing certain weapon sales to countries whose militaries are accused of gross human rights violations.
36Nigeria's military is accused of killing detainees and civilians and burning their homes in revenge for Boko Haram attacks.
37Buhari addressed those concerns Friday, promising to overhaul rules of engagement to prevent abuses and to take "disciplinary steps" against human rights violators.
38Associated Press Diplomatic Correspondent Bradley Klapper contributed to this report from Abuja.
