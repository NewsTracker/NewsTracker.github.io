What next for the 7 men detained in the FIFA raids in Zurich

1ZURICH (AP) " One week after an American investigation of soccer corruption erupted at FIFA, seven men remain detained in Zurich prison cells.
2The seven officials " including two FIFA vice presidents, one member-elect of the FIFA executive committee and one FIFA staffer " were arrested in early morning hotel raids.
3All face extradition to the United States in a process which could last months.
4Only then could they be questioned about involvement in alleged racketeering, money-laundering and wire fraud in a $150 million bribe scheme spanning more than two decades.
5The seven in Zurich have until next Monday to appeal being detained by Swiss authorities.
6Their best chance of being released on bail lies with their lawyers either finding flaws in how American and Swiss agencies processed and handled the arrests, or a lenient judge.
7"Release on bail is possible, but it's very, very rare," said Folco Galli, spokesman for the Swiss Federal Office of Justice in Bern.
8The next deadline in the Swiss extradition process is July 3.
9The U.S. must submit its formal extradition request by then and may be in no hurry to do so.
10"We will publish a press release as soon as these requests have been submitted," Galli said Wednesday.
11The Swiss justice office will examine the merits of each request and likely issue an extradition order within a month or two.
12One condition is that the allegations each faces in the U.S. would also be punishable in Switzerland.
13The Swiss authorities will not have an opinion on the likely guilt or innocence of each detainee.
14That is for the American courts to decide, Galli said.
15An order granted can be appealed to the Swiss Criminal Court in Bellinzona, in the Italian-border canton (state) of Ticino.
16A further appeal route goes to the Swiss Federal Tribunal in Lausanne.
17That whole process could last at least six months, depending on how much resistance is put up by the seven.
18The outgoing FIFA president will not be detained in his home country on behalf of American prosecutors.
19As a Swiss national, Blatter cannot be extradited by Switzerland.
20FIFA tournaments are being played this month in New Zealand and Canada.
21Under normal circumstances, FIFA protocol would require Blatter to attend the Under-20 World Cup final in Auckland on June 20 and the Women's World Cup final on July 5 in Vancouver.
22FIFA said Wednesday that Blatter does not have any confirmed travel plans in the next two weeks.
23In a separate case, Swiss federal authorities are looking at possible criminal mismanagement and money-laundering in FIFA's decision to award the 2018 and 2022 World Cups to Russia and Qatar.
24Swiss police last week questioned some of the 10 non-Swiss residents who voted in the process and still held their FIFA seats.
25On Wednesday, the Swiss attorney general's office said it could not confirm which of the 10 were spoken to as "persons providing information."
26One voter who claims not to have been spoken to is FIFA senior vice president Issa Hayatou of Cameroon.
27Hayatou, the Confederation of African Football president, told media in his home country this week that no authorities approached him in Switzerland.
28"His announced resignation will have no influence on the ongoing criminal proceedings," the attorney general's office said.
