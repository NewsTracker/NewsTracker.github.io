The Latest: Spanish finance chief urges caution on Greece

1Greece's Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras attends a meeting with lawmakers of Syriza party at the Greek Parliament in Athens, Friday, July 10, 2015.
2Tsipras will seek backing for a harsh new austerity package from his party Friday to keep his country in the euro - less than a week after urging Greeks to reject milder cuts in a referendum.
3Spanish Economy Minister Luis De Guindos is advising caution, not optimism, about Greece's reform proposals, which he says he hasn't seen yet.
4The proposals are to be discussed by eurozone finance ministers on Saturday, ahead of an EU summit Sunday, following months of failed negotiations between Athens and creditors.
5De Guindos said "I would be cautious" about the chances of the proposals bringing a deal.
6Even so, he said the proposals moved Greece "back to a square before the referendum."
7A group of dissenters in Greece's governing Syriza party is urging the government to prepare to leave the euro and to reject any deal with creditors that involves more crippling austerity.
8The group known as the Left Platform submitted a letter to Syriza lawmakers who met in parliament early Friday.
9In it, the group said an exit from the euro "under present conditions is a difficult but feasible process that will allow the country to follow a different path."
10Syriza lawmakers met ahead of vote to authorize Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras to negotiate a deal with creditors in Brussels this weekend.
11His government submitted a list of reforms to creditors late Thursday in order to win support for a new financial rescue of the country.
12So far, most of the responses to the proposals have been positive.
13The president of Cyprus says the Greek government's reform proposals to its creditors are much improved from previous versions and that there's a "solid basis" for an agreement to prevent the country from going bankrupt.
14President Nicos Anastasiades says he's "more optimistic" now than he was in the past few days when there was much uncertainty whether Greece would meet a Thursday deadline to submit the proposals.
15Anastasiades said he fully supports the restructuring of Greece's debt to make it more sustainable which would help the country overcome its economic crisis.
16He said debt relief doesn't necessarily mean a write-off, but an agreement could see repayment deadlines extended and interest rates slashed.
17Anastasiades was speaking after talks aimed at reunifying ethnically divided Cyprus with breakaway Turkish Cypriot leader Mustafa Akinci.
18The International Monetary Fund's outgoing chief economist thinks Greece may need more money than the fund thought even last week.
19In a blog post, Olivier Blanchard said Greece will likely need more than the 60 billion euros or so that the Fund indicated in a report last week.
20He said a big portion of the additional help is needed to help Greece's banks which are exposed to shaky Greek government bonds and have been fighting an outflow of deposits.
21Blanchard also said Greece may need more generous debt relief than anticipated and that Greece's European creditors considered the analysis "as too pessimistic."
22Greece has sent creditors a list of reform proposals in order to get 53.5 billion euros ($59.5 billion) of financing for three years and is hopeful of getting some sort of debt relief in return.
23U.S. Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew has expressed cautious optimism that Greece's latest offer could resolve a debt standoff and allow the country to stay in the eurozone.
24He told a forum in New York on Friday that "It certainly looks like they're getting closer," adding, however, that "they've had trouble getting from close to closed."
25He says Greece needs to take difficult steps to reduce budget deficits and make its economy more efficient.
26He also says Greece's creditors need to restructure the country's debt, though that would not necessarily mean they would have to write off a part of what Greece owes them.
27He says "It's going to be a long slog through the next few days, but it should get worked out."
28Lew says the global financial system is better positioned to deal with Greece's exit from the euro than it was a few years.
29Still, he said: "It would be a better thing for the global economy if this thing gets resolved."
30French Prime Minister Manuel Valls laid out his hopes for an imminent Greek bailout deal after the country's government sent "solid, serious and complete" reform proposals to creditors.
31Valls' optimistic comments echo those made earlier by French President Francois Hollande.
32Valls warned of the economic as well as political risks of a Greek exit from the euro.
33"We should do everything so that Greece remains in the eurozone," Valls told reporters in remarks broadcast on French television.
34"No one can pretend to know the consequences, economic, politic, public order... of a Greek exit."
35Valls conceded that France and Germany had a 'different rapport' with regard to Greece but that difference didn't hurt the overall relationship between the two countries.
36A Greek opposition party leader predicted a "large majority" of lawmakers will approve the government's proposals to creditors in a crucial vote later.
37Potami party leader Stavros Theodorakis says there's no more room for discussions and that the Greek people want a deal ending "the anxiety and the stranglehold" that they feel.
38He said a talk with European Commission President Jean-Claude Juncker Friday left him with the sense that the international creditors feel the Greek proposals are "moving in the right direction."
39Theodorakis said some "details" remain that both the Greek government and the creditors have still to iron out.
40Greece met a Thursday deadline with a sweeping reform package that it hopes will convince creditors to turn lend it billions more, which would secure the country's place in the 19-country eurozone.
41Austrian Chancellor Werner Faymann says he sees a chance of an agreement with Greece after the country submitted reform proposals to creditors.
42Faymann said in a statement that "constructive forces must now be stronger that those who don't want a solution under any circumstances."
43He didn't specify who exactly he meant.
44Faymann says it must not be forgotten that the crisis in Greece is affecting its poorest people - "so it is our obligation to use this last opportunity constructively."
45European Commission spokesman Margaritis Schinas says four of the key players in the Greek debt crisis will hold a conference call later to discuss the reform proposals sent by Athens late Thursday.
46Schinas says the 1100 GMT (2 p.m. Athens time) call will involve eurozone's top official, Jeroen Dijsselbloem, European Commission President Jean-Claude Juncker, European Central Bank President Mario Draghi and International Monetary Fund chief Christine Lagarde.
47The call at comes as representatives from the Commission, IMF and ECB all assessing Greece's proposals.
48Schinas, speaking at a briefing in Brussels, would not give any early assessment of the Greek reform plans.
49He says the institutions will likely send their assessments to the eurogroup later Friday.
50Eurogroup finance ministers are scheduled to meet Saturday in Brussels to discuss the plans.
51The German government isn't giving an immediate verdict on the list of reform measures submitted by Greece, saying that it will wait for the assessment of the country's formal creditors.

53He said Berlin will wait for the European Commission, the European Central Bank and International Monetary Fund to deliver their verdict.
54Seibert says Merkel will be accompanied to a special European Union summit on Sunday by Finance Minister Wolfgang Schaeuble.

58Dijsselbloem says representatives from the European Commission, European Central Bank and International Monetary Fund "will do the math and see if it adds up, if it's right."
59Dijsselbloem and his peers in the 19-country eurozone, including Greek Finance Minister Euclid Tsakalotos meet Saturday to discuss the proposals.
60"Whichever way we go, we have to take a very far-reaching decision tomorrow, so let's do it carefully," he said.
