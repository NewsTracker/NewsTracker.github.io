Egypt police raid radio station, detain rights advocate

1Ahmed Samih, director of the Andalus Institute for Tolerance and Anti-Violence Studies, says police told him he is under investigation for broadcasting without a license on the web radio platform Horytna.
2"One of the main questions was around our political affiliation," he said in a phone call from the police station, adding that the radio station is not affiliated with a political party and is focused on human rights.
3An evolving crackdown on dissent has landed thousands of government critics behind bars and silenced many dissenting voices.
4Authorities contend they are fighting a violent wave of Islamic militancy that aims to destabilize the country.
5Also on Saturday, Human Rights Watch urged Egypt to halt the execution of six men condemned to death by a military tribunal for killing security forces, citing lawyers and family who contend some of them were in jail at the time of the attack.
6The New York-based rights group said in a statement that the men are accused of being members of the insurgent group Ansar Beit al-Maqdis.
7It said the men were part of a group of nine convicted of killing two officers in a 2014 shootout north of Cairo, and that they should be given a retrial before a civilian court.
