Court agrees Samsung illegally copied some iPhone features

1In this Sept 19, 2014 file photo, a customer shows off the Apple iPhone 6, right, and 6 Plus at a store in Tokyo.
2SAN FRANCISCO -- A federal appeals court has agreed that Samsung illegally copied some of the patented features in Apple's iPhone.
3But the court sided with Samsung on one point in a long-running dispute, which means the South Korean company may end up paying less than the $930 million in damages originally awarded in the case.
4The appellate court upheld most of the conclusions reached by a federal jury in California, after a closely watched trial in 2012.
5Apple had complained that Samsung smartphones copied the iPhone and infringed on Apple's technology patents.
6But the appeals court, based in Washington, D.C., said Monday that Apple can't claim damages for copying the overall appearance of the iPhone.
7That could reduce the damage award by about $382 million, according to Samsung's court filings.
