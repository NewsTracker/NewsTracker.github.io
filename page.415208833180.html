HBO will launch online streaming service in April for $15

1HBO will launch its standalone streaming service in April and it'll cost $15, according to the International Business Times.
2HBO is looking to launch HBO Now close to the premiere of season five of "Game of Thrones," but it wants to make sure that there are no issues upon launch.
3Last year, HBO Go users couldn't watch the premiere of George R.R. Martin's fantasy show because the servers were saturated.
4Apple will be behind the launch of HBO Now, according to the Times.
5By Apple securing its place as a launch partner, HBO Now could potentially be available on Apple TV through a standalone app.
6Apple TV started with a weak selection of channels, but over the last the two years it has been adding a lot.
7HBO Now would make the product more appealing.
8HBO Now's availability would be cross platform-similar to Hulu, Netflix and Amazon Prime.
9The service would be accessible from Roku, Amazon Fire Stick, Xbox One and PlayStation 4.
10There was no mention of the service being available of computers or mobile devices.
11It's still unclear as to whether HBO Now will offer everything HBO Go offers, which is all the live programming a cable user would originally get.
12Standalone subscription services have been increasing in popularity.
13NBC, CBS and Nickelodeon have all announced the release of a possible standalone online streaming services, ideal for cable cutters.
14Cable companies have also been launching their own online streaming services, without contract.
15Dish recently released Sling TV, which offers channels like ESPN, AMC, Food Network, TNT and CBS for $20 a month.
