Apple Watch begins its debut around the world

1Holding the Apple Watch Edition in San Francisco on March 9, 2015.
2From Beijing to Paris to San Francisco, the Apple Watch made its debut Friday.
3Customers were invited to try them on in stores and order them online.
4The watch is Apple's first new product category since the iPad came out five years ago.
5Analysts are waiting to see how well the watch will sell beyond devoted Apple fans.
6Apple has a better chance at succeeding than any other smartwatch maker so far, yet it will likely take time before sales reach the kind of numbers that Apple gets for iPhones and iPads.
7Watch prices start at $349, but can go as high as $17,000 for a luxury edition in gold.
8People can try the watch on in Apple stores, but for now all orders are being handled online.
9Shipments begin April 24.
10It's available in the U.S. and eight other markets around the world.
11In the U.S., the watch is available only in Apple stores.
12In some countries, select department stores and resellers also have it.
13The curious in Japan formed a long line in Isetan department store, where a special section was built just for the Apple watch.
14Only 20 customers were allowed in at a time, and only those with advance reservations or who showed up early enough to get one of 76 lottery tickets got to try the watch on.
15The rest could only look at a display of 19 watches under a glass showcase.
16They ranged in price from about 43,000 yen ($360) to 2,800,000 yen ($23,300) for the luxury edition in gold.
17In central Shanghai, potential Apple watch buyers stood in lines two to five people long over their lunch hour at an Apple store on Friday to try on the watch many said they already planned to buy.
18"It was beautifully made, like an expensive watch," said Li Hao, 27, a Web designer who owns a Mac, an iPad and an Apple TV.
19He has just traded up from an iPhone 4 to the new iPhone 6 Plus.
20China was among countries where the watch had its global debut Friday, reflecting the country's fast-growing status as one of Apple's most important markets.
21Li said he planned to buy the sport version of the watch at about 3,000 yuan ($500).
22"I cannot do sports with the mobile phone," he said.
23"I need a machine to record what I did and a screen to look at."
24Qi Tian, 26, who works in human resources for a real estate company, said he was "not a big fan" of Apple, though he owns four or five products.
25He said he planned to order a watch online that day.
26"I just came to see if the size fits," said Qi.
27Apple starts taking orders for the watch on its website and Apple Store app.
28Currently, this is the only way Apple is selling the watch.
29Even those visiting retail stores will have to order online -- either at home or at a Web terminal inside the store.
30The retail stores are meant for customers who aren't sure which watch case, band or size they want -- or aren't sure they even want one.
31Staff will be on hand to help customers try on the watches and answer questions before buying.
32Customers are encouraged to make an appointment online, though walk-ins will be accepted -- just expect a wait.
