Islamic State plot suspect to be transferred to NY

1A federal judge in Jacksonville ordered Abror Habibov's transfer during a detention hearing.
2He is scheduled to be in New York by March 11.
3Habibov was arrested in Jacksonville last week and charged with attempt and conspiracy to provide material support to a terrorist organization.
4The 30-year-old Habibov ran phone repair kiosks at malls in Florida, Georgia and Virginia, and federal prosecutors say he helped fund 19-year-old Akhror Saidakhmetov's efforts to join the Islamic State group in Syria.
5Saidakhmetov and another man, 24-year-old Abdurasul Hasanovich Juraboev, were arrested in Brooklyn.
6According to federal prosecutors, Saidakhmetov is a citizen of Kazakhstan, and worked for a time with Habibov at the kiosks.
7Habibov, a native of Uzbekistan whose visa had expired, is being without bail in Florida.
8One of the kiosks he operated was in the in suburban Jacksonville, but there were no state business records with Habibov listed as an owner.
9Both Saidakhmetov and Juraboev threatened attacks in the U.S. and planned to travel to Syria to join the Islamic State group.
10According to the federal complaint, Habibov was mainly a source of planning and money.
11On Tuesday, Habibov appeared in court dressed in jeans, Adidas sneakers and a black sweatshirt.
12His black hair was mussed and he had a beard.
13His attorney, Maurice Grant, a federal public defender, declined comment outside the courtroom.
14Inside, Habibov waived his right to hearings to confirm his identity and to a preliminary hearing in Jacksonville so that he could be transferred back to New York.
15Habibov answered "yes" when asked if he understood all he was waiving, and through an Uzbek interpreter asked the court to ensure he would be able to make phone calls in New York.
16The judge said he would be.
