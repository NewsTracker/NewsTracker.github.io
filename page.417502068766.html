Russia Says Ukraine Agrees To Maintain Gas Flow To Europe

1Ukraine has agreed to pump enough gas into its storage facilities to maintain pressure in the system so Russian gas can reach Europe in winter, Russian Energy Minister Alexander Novak said June 3.
2Around 40 percent of Russian gas exports to the European Union traverse Ukraine.
3Novak said that Ukraine had agreed to fill the storage facilities after talks in Brussels between Russia, Ukraine and the European Commission June 2.
4A source close to the talks told Reuters that Moscow and Kiev had agreed to the outlines of a protocol on the gas trade, and that ministers would meet later this month to try to resolve the key outstanding disagreements.
5Outstanding issues include how long the protocol would last, the formula applied to provide discounted gas prices for Ukraine, and the fees charged by Ukraine for the transit of gas over its territory.
6Novak told reporters in Vienna: "They agreed on some positions of the protocol, including on pumping no less than 19 bcm into the storages before the forthcoming winter."
7There was no immediate comment from Ukraine, which has said it needs from 14 to 19 billion cubic metres (bcm) in storage to keep Russian gas flowing to Europe.
8Novak told TASS the gas agreement also includes EU financing for Ukraine's stored gas purchases.
9"We agreed that the protocol will include the financial assistance of the European Commission for gas purchases by Ukraine in case such funds are needed," he said.
