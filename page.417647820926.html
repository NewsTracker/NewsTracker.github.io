Soriano agrees to minor league deal with Cubs (Yahoo Sports)

1FILE - In this Oct. 4, 2014, file photo, Washington Nationals relief pitcher Rafael Soriano (29) throws in the 16th inning of Game 2 of baseball's NL Division Series against the San Francisco Giants in Nationals Park in Washington.
2The Chicago Cubs and free-agent reliever Rafael Soriano have agreed to a minor league deal on Tuesday, June 9, 2015.
3CHICAGO (AP) -- Free-agent reliever Rafael Soriano has agreed to a minor league contract with the Chicago Cubs and would get s $4.1 million, one-year deal if added to the major league roster.
4A 35-year-old right-hander, Soriano has 207 saves and a 2.85 ERA in 585 major league appearances.
5His deal, which includes $4 million in performance bonuses, was announced on Tuesday.
6The contract could lead to a reunion for Soriano and Joe Maddon, who was the manager in Tampa Bay when the right-hander had 45 saves for the Rays in 2010.
7Soriano has three seasons with at least 40 saves.
8The Cubs began the day with a 3.68 bullpen ERA, good for 20th among the 30 major league teams.
9Closer Hector Rondon is 3-0 with 10 saves and a 2.96 ERA in 25 games.
10Soriano played for Washington last season and went 4-1 with 32 saves and a 3.19 ERA.
