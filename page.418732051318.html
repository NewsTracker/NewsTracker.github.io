Ferguson hires black police commander as interim chief

1FERGUSON, Missouri (AP) " Leaders in the city of Ferguson introduced a new interim police chief Wednesday, tasking the black police commander with building trust between residents of the predominantly black St. Louis suburb and its mostly white officers, nearly a year since the death of an unarmed African-American teen roiled racial tensions.
2Michael Brown's death at the hands of a white police who was never indicted sparked riots in Ferguson and touched off a national "Black Lives Matter" movement seeking changes in how police deal with minorities.
3Andre Anderson, a 50-year-old Army veteran with a quarter century in law enforcement, starts in Ferguson on Thursday.
4Touted for his knack for community outreach in policing, Anderson arrives in Ferguson as it is still working to get its law enforcement house in order after a scathing U.S. Department of Justice report in March pressed for reforms.
5That critique cited racial bias and profiling in policing as well as a profit-driven municipal court system that frequently targeted blacks, who make up about two-thirds of Ferguson's populace.
6Thomas Jackson, Ferguson's police chief for five years, resigned just days after that report.
