2EAST RUTHERFORD, N.J. (AP) -- Eli Manning didn't ask the New York Giants to make him the NFL's highest-paid quarterback.
3A perplexed and slightly miffed Manning on Wednesday shot down a report that he wants to be the league's top-paid quarterback, insisting that was not the goal in contract talks.
4''Never been said, never come out of my mouth,'' Manning said Wednesday.
5The two-time Super Bowl MVP is in the final year of a contract that will pay him a $17 million base salary.
6His agent, Tom Condon, has been negotiating a new deal, but the two sides are not close to an agreement, Giants co-owner John Mara said Monday.
7Manning insisted neither he nor Condon told the Giants to make him the highest-paid quarterback.
8He said he speaks with Condon occasionally, but he isn't looking for constant updates on the talks.
9''If something comes up, I ask him to call me and keep me updated or send me a text, but that's it,'' Manning said.
10Aaron Rodgers of the Packers currently is the NFL's highest-paid quarterback, earning $22 million annually.
11The 34-year-old Manning has spent all 12 of his NFL seasons with the Giants, so he knows how people in the New York-area react to headlines.
12For those who know him, it's totally out of character to make such a demand.
13Manning even received a telephone message from his father asking what was happening.
14''I don't know how all negotiating goes and what is being asked,'' Manning said.
15''I don't think I want to know.
16Manning said he does not compare himself to other quarterbacks by salary, and right now he is not thinking or concerned about his contract.
17''My focus is on practice and getting the best out of our practices and getting better,'' Manning said.
18''That's all I am focused on.
19Nothing has changed.
20Nothing is different.
21Reports are all wrong.
22I don't know where they are getting their information from.
