Apple Music, iTunes and App Store Suffer Outages

1Apple's Beats 1 station went down during the announcement of nominees for MTV's Video Music Awards.
2A host of Apple services were knocked out of service intermittently Tuesday morning, including the new Beats 1 radio station that was exclusively announcing the nominees for the MTV Video Music Awards.
3A support page on Apple's website said all store services were experiencing some kind of disruption beginning shortly before 10 a.m. ET, about the time the Beats 1 Twitter account said the VMA nominations soon would begin.
4Neither Apple nor MTV, a unit of Viacom , mentioned the stumble on their Twitter accounts, which proceeded to tweet the nominees as if nothing was wrong.
5The outage is affecting not only Apple's music service, but Apple TV, the iOS and Mac app stores, several iTunes services and more.
6Not all users were affected, Apple's support page said.
7It isn't clear how many people in total were unable to use the services, and whether the outage was related to people rushing to Apple services to hear the announcements.
8It stands to reason more people tuned in to Beats 1 on its launch day, and it's possible that Beats 1 was tripped up because it shares some internal infrastructure with other Apple services.
9We have reached out to Apple for comment on Tuesday's outage and will update this post when we hear back.
10Any time heavily used consumer technology services like Facebook , Apple and Google buckle, users race online to find out if the problem is widespread or "just me."
11The outages are common enough that Apple and Google, for example, have easily accessible websites tracking when services are interrupted.
12It's rarer, though, that the outage comes just as the company turns up the spotlight on itself, as with the choreographed MTV partnership.
13Later Tuesday, Apple is slated to report its earnings report for the fiscal third quarter.
