13Declaring Kenya at a "crossroads" between promise and peril, President Barack Obama on Sunday pressed the nation of his father's birth to root out corruption, treat women and minorities as equal citizens, and take responsibility for its future.
14Closing his historic visit with an address to the Kenyan people, Obama traced the arc of the country's evolution from colonialism to independence, as well as his own family's history here.
15Today, Obama said, young Kenyans are no longer constrained by the limited options of his grandfather, a cook for the country's former British rulers, or his father, who left to seek an education in America.
16"Because of Kenya's progress - because of your potential - you can build your future right here, right now," Obama told the crowd of 4,500 packed into a sports arena in the capital of Nairobi.
17But he bluntly warned that Kenya must make "tough choices" to bolster its fragile democracy and fast-growing economy.
18Obama's visit here, his first as president, captivated a country that views him as a local son.
19Thick crowds lined the roadways to watch the presidential motorcade speed through the city Sunday, some climbing on rooftops to get a better view.
20The audience inside the arena chanted his name as he finished his remarks.
21The president left Kenya Sunday afternoon, pausing longer than normal atop the stairs to Air Force One to wave to the crowd, a huge grin on his face.
22He arrived two hours later in Addis Ababa, the Ethiopian capital, where he met with diplomats at the U.S. Embassy in the evening.
23Obama has written emotionally about his first visit to Kenya as a young man nearly 30 years ago, and he recounted many of those same memories in his remarks Sunday.
24The battered Volkswagen his sister drove.
25Meeting his brothers for the first time.
26The airport employee who recognized his last name.
27"That was the first time that my name meant something," he said.
28The president barely knew his father, who died in 1982 after leaving the U.S. to return to Kenya.
29However, Obama has numerous family members in the country, including his half-sister Auma Obama, who introduced her brother Sunday.
30The bulk of Obama's address was a candid commentary on the East African nation's future.
31He spent considerable time warning about the risks of government corruption, calling it an "anchor" that could weigh down the country's promising future.
32"Too often here in Kenya corruption is tolerated because that's how it's always been done," he said.
33"Here in Kenya, it's time to change habits."
34Kenyan President Uhuru Kenyatta has taken steps to tackle corruption by suspending four Cabinet secretaries and 16 other senior officials amid an investigation into allegations of dishonesty.
35But the action has been met with skepticism by the public because in the past, suspensions of senior officials haven't resulted in anyone being convicted of a crime.
36Some officials even returned to their jobs before investigations were complete.
37Kenyatta has been under public pressure to address corruption following reviews of his two-year-old government that claimed his administration is more corrupt than previous administrations.
38The president drew on the recent debate in the U.S. over the Confederate battle flag, a Civil War-era relic that is seen by many as a racist symbol.
39The killing of nine people at a black church in South Carolina last month prompted a fresh debate over the flag, spurring some states to remove it from government grounds.
40"Just because something is a part of your past doesn't make it right," Obama said.
41Some of those in attendance for the president's speech said they were inspired by his appeal for progress in Kenya.
42Upenbo Abraham, a 23-year-old economics student from an area of western Kenya near Obama's relatives, said he was "encouraged, as a poor boy from a village next to his home."
43Ezekiel Oduor, an accountant, said Obama was "candid and clear" about Kenya's problems with corruption and his desire to help the country rise "to the next level."
44After his speech, Obama met with political opposition leaders, then with a group of African youth and civil leaders on ways to promote civil society efforts.
45He told the civil society group that "the country is going to be better off" if it can cultivate habits of public participation and freedom.
46Obama is expected to offer similar messages about good governance and human rights during his two days of meetings with leaders in Ethiopia.
47Human rights groups have criticized the president for visiting the Horn of Africa nation, which is accused of cracking down on dissent, sometimes violently.
48Obama planned meetings with Ethiopia's president and prime minister, and a separate session with regional leaders to discuss the situation in South Sudan, a young nation gripped by turmoil since civil war broke out in December 2013.
49Countering the al-Qaida-linked al-Shabab in neighboring Somalia also is on the agenda for Obama's meetings with Ethiopian leaders.
50That threat was brought into sharp relief Sunday when al-Shabab claimed responsibility for a suicide truck bombing at a luxury hotel in Mogadishu, Somalia's capital, that killed eight people and shattered a period of calm in the city.
51White House spokesman Ned Price condemned the attack, calling it "yet another reminder of the unconscionable atrocities that terrorist groups continue to perpetrate against the people of Somalia."
