MH17 crash site investigators finish recovering human remains in Ukraine

1Dutch and international investigators have finished recovering human remains and wreckage from the MH17 plane crash site in eastern Ukraine, the mission's head said today.
2All 298 passengers and crew on board the Malaysia Airlines jetliner, the majority of them Dutch, died when it was shot down over rebel-held eastern Ukraine last year.
3'We have done everything humanly possible,' in the recovery process, mission head Pieter-Jaap Aalbersberg said at a press conference in The Hague.
4Debris is driven away.
5A final flight with seven coffins filled with human remains is expected to arrive in the Netherlands on Saturday.
6'Many more' body parts were recovered over the past two weeks in the search near Petropavlivka, about 10 kilometres (six miles) west of Grabove where most of the debris fell, Aalbersberg said.
7'Many personal belongings like watches, passports... and other documents that is of great value to the victims' families,' were also recovered, he added.
8The Boeing 777 was on a routine flight between Amsterdam and Kuala Lumpur when it was shot down on July 17, 2014.
9Kiev and the West claim that the plane was shot down by pro-Russian separatists using a BUK surface-to-air missile supplied by Moscow.
10Russia has denied the charges, pointing the finger instead at Kiev.
11The Netherlands has been tasked with leading the investigation into the cause of the accident and identifying the victims.
12Dutch Safety Board (OVV) spokeswoman Sara Vernooij told AFP today the final report into the cause of the crash is expected to be ready around October.
13Meanwhile US Secretary of State John Kerry praised the Netherlands for leading the MH17 investigation.
14'Let me say how much we respect the leadership of the Netherlands on the issue of the Malaysia Airlines, MH17,' disaster, Kerry told reporters in Washington, where he met his Dutch counterpart Bert Koenders on Thursday.
15'They are leading the investigation and also leading the efforts for accountability, and that is a very significant step,' Kerry said at the meeting, held at the State Department.
16'This is a very crucial phase we are in, the repatriation phase has been finalised... And now it's important that we go in the direction of accountability,' Koenders said.
17The announcement comes more than two weeks after a haunting picture of a woman passenger killed in the disaster was found by MailOnline at the crash site.
18The find brought an emotional plea from her family for an end to Russian President Vladimir Putin's 'obstruction' in clearing the wreckage and bringing the guilty to justice.
19Investigators have not had unfettered access to the crash site amid the uneasy ceasefire between Ukrainian forces and pro-Russian rebels.
20The charred travel document spotted by MailOnline was identifiable as belonging to victim Mabel Anthonysamy Soosai, 45, a former university lecturer returning from Amsterdam to her native Malaysia with husband Paul Rajasingam Sivagnanam, 52, a Shell IT executive, and son Matthew, aged nine.
21A souvenir shot glass among the wreckage.
22They were known to friends as the 'ever-smiling family' but they were mercilessly killed along with the rest of the 298 passengers and crew on the flight to Kuala Lumpur.
23'It broke my heart to see the picture, her passport still in the debris of the plane,' her brother Clement, 47, told MailOnline in the Malaysian capital.
24The German government had been warned of a heightened risk of flying over eastern Ukraine just days before the MH17 disaster, it was claimed earlier this week.
25Despite being told of the risk of flying over Ukraine in diplomatic cables sent two days before the crash, Germany failed to pass on the warning, local media reported.
26German foreign ministry cables sent on July 15, cited an incident from the day before when a Ukrainian air force plane was shot down at around 20,000 feet over rebel-held territory.
27German intelligence had repeatedly warned of the risk to aviation security and had assessed the downing of the Ukrainian military plane on July 14 as a 'significant development because of its altitude', German public TV channels NDR and WDR and Sueddeutsche Zeitung reported.
28Earlier this month, the Dutch government - which lost 192 citizens in the disaster - said that, with nearly all of the victims identified, efforts had shifted to finding those responsible for shooting the plane down as it flew over Ukraine last July.

32'Now that we're very far with repatriation (of the victims' remains), investigation and prosecution are becoming more central,' he said in a statement.
33'The ministers believe that prosecution is not only important for the countries with victims on board.
34Forensics laboratories in the Netherlands have identified remains of 296 of the 298 MH17 victims and recovered large parts of the wreckage for investigation.
35Because two-thirds of the victims were Dutch citizens, the Netherlands has lead several post-crash investigations.
36The final report is due to be published in October.
37The dead from MH17 comprised 192 Dutch citizens (one also had US citizenship), 44 Malaysians, including the 15 crew members, 27 Australians, 12 Indonesians, ten Britons (one also had South African citizenship), four Germans, four Belgians, three Filipinos, one Canadian and one New Zealander.
