Secular blogger hacked to death in Bangladesh

1Machete-wielding assailants hacked to death a secular blogger in the Bangladesh capital Dhaka on Monday, the second attack in five weeks on a critic of religion in the South Asian nation.
2Authorities said Washiqur Rahman was killed on a busy street in the center of the city.
3Two suspects, believed to be students at a nearby religious school, were apprehended.
4A third alleged attacker escaped.
5"Police on duty near the spot caught two attackers red-handed with three machetes as they were fleeing the scene after the incident," police official Humayan Kabir told Reuters.
6Dhaka Metropolitan Police official Biplob Kumar Sarker said the suspects told police investigators that they targeted Rahman for his anti-Islamic writings.
7The killing comes just weeks after secular U.S. blogger Avijit Roy was hacked to death while returning with his wife from a book fair in Dhaka.
8His wife, Rafida Bonya Ahmed, suffered head injuries and lost a finger in the Feb. 26 attack.
9The incidents come during a period of political turmoil in the country, with the government and main opposition group locked in a months-long standoff that has created a sense of deepening insecurity across the country.
10In recent years, a string of secular-minded writers have been targeted in Bangladesh as the government has tried to crackdown on hardline groups seeking to create a state based on their interpretation of Sharia law.
11Blogger Ahmed Rajib Haider was killed in 2013 near his home in Dhaka after he led a protest demanding capital punishment for the leader of the Jamaat-e-Islami party.
12The party leader had been convicted of war crimes during Bangladesh's war for independence.
13In 2004, Humayun Azad, a secular writer and professor at Dhaka University, was also attacked by armed men while returning home from a Dhaka book fair.
14He later died in Germany while undergoing treatment for his injuries.
15Roy's wife, Ahmed, blamed her husband's murder in February on religious fanatics, and accused police on duty of not doing enough to stop the attack.
16Roy's father told reporters last week that "apparently no progress" had been made in resolving the case.
