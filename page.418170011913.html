US court agrees Apple violated antitrust law in e-book entry

1A federal appeals court in New York says Apple violated antitrust laws by colluding with publishers to raise electronic book prices in 2010.
2The 2nd U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals in Manhattan ruled 2-to-1 Tuesday that a lower court judge was right to find Apple violated the laws to upset Amazon.com's control of the market.
3The appeals court also agreed that Judge Denise Cote (koht) was right to order injunctive relief to ensure the Cupertino, California-based company didn't commit additional violations of antitrust laws.
4An Apple lawyer did not immediately return a message for comment.
5Cote ruled against Apple after a civil trial in summer 2013.
6She ordered the technology giant to modify contracts with publishers to prevent price fixing and appointed a monitor to review the company's antitrust policies.
