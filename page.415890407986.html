Report: Egypt, Nigeria Led World in Death Sentences in 2014

1The London-based human rights group expressed alarm at the 28 percent jump in death sentences: 2,466 people in 55 countries were condemned to death in 2014.
2At least 607 people were executed in 22 countries last year.
3Neither of those numbers is complete, as North Korea's closed-off stance means that no estimate there was available.
4Amnesty International also doesn't report numbers for China, where such information is considered a state secret.
5The Dui Hua Foundation, a U.S.-based prison research group, has estimated 2,400 executions happened in China for 2014.
6Amnesty International also said it was unable to confirm whether judicial executions took place last year in Syria, where civil war has raged for four years.
7The countries with the most recorded executions last year were Iran with at least 289, Saudi Arabia with at least 90, Iraq with at least 61 and the United States with at least 35, the rights group said.
8In Iran, hundreds more executions were "not officially acknowledged" and the total could be as high as 743, the organization said.
9Once again, the United States was the only country in the Americas to execute people in 2014, the report said.
10Texas and Missouri each carried out 10 executions.
11Other U.S. states that put people to death were Arizona, Florida, Georgia, Ohio and Oklahoma.
12The overall number of global executions last year dropped almost 22 percent from 2013.
13"The numbers speak for themselves: The death penalty is becoming a thing of the past, Amnesty International' secretary-general, Salil Shetty, said.
14But Shetty condemned the use of death sentences as a way to fight crime or "terrorism."
15Nigeria announced 659 death sentences, mostly for murder and armed robbery, but a military court in December sentenced to death 54 soldiers who had been accused of refusing to join operations against the extremist group Boko Haram.
16The soldiers testified that they had not been properly equipped to go after Boko Haram, which has since pledged allegiance to the Islamic State group.
17Nigeria's higher number of death sentences, up from 141 the year before, was also a result of more complete data offered by authorities.
18Egypt announced at least 509 death sentences last year, many of them in the mass trials that have been held since the ouster of Islamist President Mohammed Morsi in 2013.
19The practice has brought international criticism.
20In one case in December, 188 people were sentenced to death in the killing of 11 police officers.
21In the United States, at least 72 death sentences were announced last year.
22Amnesty International also expressed concern about countries that resumed the practice of executions, including Pakistan, which reinstated the death penalty in December after a Pakistani Taliban attack on a school killed 150 people, most of them children.
23"An alarming number of countries that used the death penalty in 2014 did so in response to real or perceived threats to state security and public safety posed by terrorism, crime or internal stability," the new report said.
24Other countries resuming executions in 2014 were Jordan, Singapore, Egypt, Belarus, the United Arab Emirates and Equatorial Guinea, the report said.
25Indonesia said it would resume executions for drug-related crimes, straining ties in particular with Australia and Brazil, which both have citizens on death row there.
26At of the end of last year, more than 19,000 people around the world were estimated to be living under death sentences.
