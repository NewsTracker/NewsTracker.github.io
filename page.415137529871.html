8The spacewalk on Sunday was the 187th for the space station and one of many planned for the coming year to prepare the station for a new era, when more commercial vehicles will carry astronauts to the station.
9Boeing and SpaceX are working on new craft that will carry astronauts to space, restoring the United States' access to low Earth orbit for the first time since the retirement of the space shuttle program in 2011.
10Boeing's crew vehicle, called CST-100, is planning its first flight with a pilot and astronaut in late 2017, and SpaceX hopes to follow soon after.
11Until then, the world's astronauts must pay $70 million per seat for transport aboard Russia's Soyuz capsules.
12The spacewalk went ahead despite an apparent problem with Colonel Virts's spacesuit that had allowed a small amount of water to build up inside his helmet after he finished his spacewalk on Wednesday.
13NASA experts determined that the crew had not been in any danger on Wednesday, and the men reported no problems during Sunday's spacewalk.
