Apple iOS 9 now available as part of public beta

1No need to wait: Apple's iOS 9 could be on your iPad and iPhone early if you're happy to take the risk.
2For the first time, Apple is making a major update to its iPhone and iPad software available to everyone before its official release.
3The iOS 9 public beta was launched on Friday, and anybody hankering for a glance at the future of mobile Apple products can sign up at the beta website and then grab the software through iTunes, although you should do so with caution.
4The whole purpose of a beta is to help Apple refine the release and fix any bugs, so while it won't cause your device to explode there could be niggles or serious issues that necessitate a factory reset.
5Apple suggests you only install this software on a secondary device, not on the iPhone you rely on every day.
6As always with betas, if you are planning to live dangerously and install the beta on a main device (or even a secondary one), make sure you back up before the install and periodically afterwards.
7This way you don't have to waste half a day setting up your device all over again if something goes wrong.
8A proper tethered backup with the "encrypt this backup" box checked is best.
9In the past Apple has run public betas for incremental updates (the last time being for iOS 8.3), but this is the first time those outside the developer community have been allowed to test a major overhaul.
10For those who only have one iOS device or are happy to wait, the full iOS 9 release is expected to launch alongside new, as-yet-unannounced iPhones later in the year (middle of September, if history is any indication).
11Apple is also running a public beta for the next version of its desktop software, OS X El Capitan, which was showcased alongside iOS 9 earlier this year.
