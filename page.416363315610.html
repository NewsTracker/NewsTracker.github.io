Baltimore investigates death of man in police custody

1BALTIMORE (AP) - Baltimore police were conducting a criminal investigation Sunday into the death of a man injured in their custody, and the mayor vowed to ensure the city held "the right people accountable."
2Freddie Gray, 25, of Baltimore, died Sunday at a hospital, a week after he was hurt following an arrest.
3A timeline released earlier in the week by police said Gray was taken by a van from the scene to a station, where an ambulance was called to treat him and take him to the University of Maryland R Adams Cowley Shock Trauma Center.
4Civilian video showed him being loaded into the van, but did not show the entire encounter.
5An attorney retained by Gray's family, Billy Murphy, described extensive injuries in a statement Sunday.
6"His spine was 80 percent severed at his neck. He lapsed into a coma, died, was resuscitated, stayed in a coma and on Monday, underwent extensive surgery at Shock Trauma to save his life," Murphy said.
7"He clung to life for seven days and died today at approximately 7 a.m."
8Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake and top police officials promised accountability and transparency Sunday at a news conference at City Hall.
9Deputy Commissioner Jerry Rodriguez said a criminal investigation was underway.
10"It's a two-part investigation. One is a criminal case, for Mr. Gray and also the officers. We always have that component in there to determine whether there is criminal culpability," Rodriguez said.
11Rodriguez declined to specify why four bicycle officers stopped Gray.
12"We had officers in a high-crime area known to have high narcotic incidents," he said "The officers believe that Mr. Gray was immediately involved or recently involved in criminal activity and decided to make contact."
13Rawlings-Blake said she had the same questions as reporters: "How was Mr. Gray injured? Were the proper protocols and procedures actually followed? What are the next steps to take from here?"
14She promised a thorough investigation and "real answers" for the community.
15"I will ensure we will hold the right people accountable," Rawlings-Blake said.
16Gray's family has declined, so far, to interact with police, said Police Commissioner Anthony Batts.
17He said the department would try again this week to share information with them.
18"Today, on this holy day, a mother has lost her son," Batts said.
19"Freddie Gray passed. My greatest hope and wish and desire is that any time we have an interaction as a police department or a contact, that everyone goes home safe. All lives matter. All lives matter in this city."
20The department, an independent review board and the Baltimore prosecutor's office will investigate the case, Batts said.
21The Justice League NYC, which organized a rally Saturday night at the station where Gray was taken, held another protest Sunday afternoon at the site.
22The first event was scheduled as the league's "March2Justice" reached Baltimore on its way to a rally Tuesday in Washington.
23"Enough is enough! Police brutality must end!" the group said in a statement.
24Gray was black; the race of the officers involved could not immediately be confirmed.
25His death came amid a national debate about the deaths of black men at the hands of police.
