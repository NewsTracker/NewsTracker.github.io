A history of the Islamic State group after Ethiopians killed

1CAIRO (AP) - The extremist Islamic State group, which controls a third of both Iraq and Syria in its self-declared caliphate, released a video Sunday purportedly showing militants killing two groups of Ethiopian Christians in Libya.
2The release of the 29-minute video comes a day after Afghanistan's president blamed the extremists for a suicide attack in his country that killed at least 35 people - and underscores the chaos gripping Libya after its 2011 civil war and the killing of dictator Moammar Gadhafi.
3FILE - In this Monday, June 16, 2014 file photo, demonstrators chant pro-Islamic State group slogans as they wave the group's flags in front of the provincial government headquarters in Mosul, 225 miles (360 kilometers) northwest of Baghdad, Iraq.
4April 18, 2010 - U.S. and Iraqi forces kill two top al-Qaida in Iraq leaders, allowing al-Baghdadi to become the leader of a terror group weakened by a concerted campaign aimed at ending a Sunni insurgency in the country.
5Oct. 31, 2010 - Al-Baghdadi's al-Qaida militants attack Our Lady of Salvation Catholic church in Baghdad during Sunday night mass, killing 58 people in the deadliest assault targeting Christians since the 2003 U.S.-led invasion there.
6The militants reportedly demand the release of Muslim women they claim were held by Egypt's Coptic Christian church.
7Oct. 4, 2011 - The U.S. puts a $10 million bounty on al-Baghdadi's head over a series of attacks he orchestrated.
8July 21, 2012 - In his first purported online message, al-Baghdadi promises to regain lost ground in Iraq and calls on militants to "chase and liquidate the judges, the investigators and the guards."
9Within days, his group begins a campaign of attacks, car bombings and other assaults killing hundreds.
10He also mentions Syria, in the grips of a civil war pitting largely Sunni rebels against embattled President Bashar Assad.
11By this time, al-Baghdadi already has begun to send fighters there.
12April 2013 - Al-Baghdadi announces his group has taken over the Nusra Front, the al-Qaida affiliate in Syria.
13Nusra denies the takeover, sparking anger and infighting that continues to this day.
14July 2013 - A military-style assault by al-Baghdadi's fighters on two Baghdad-area prisons free more than 500 inmates.
15January 2014 - Al-Baghdadi's forces sweep into Ramadi and Fallujah in Iraq's Anbar province, which Iraqi security forces had abandoned weeks earlier.
16That came after security forces killed demonstrators during a Sunni protest, effectively turning the unrest into an uprising.
17Early February 2014 - Al-Qaida breaks with al-Baghdadi's group, now known as the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant.
18Al-Baghdadi ignores al-Qaida as his group now has control of wide regions of Syria, including the city of Raqqa, which becomes the group's de facto capital.
19June 10 - Al-Baghdadi's fighters take over Iraq's second-largest city of Mosul, followed by Saddam Hussein's hometown of Tikrit and smaller communities in the Sunni heartland as government forces melt away.
20June 29 - The group declares the establishment of an Islamic state, or caliphate, in territories it controls in Iraq and Syria and demands allegiance from Muslims worldwide.
21It declares al-Baghdadi the leader of the new caliphate.
22The militants rename themselves the Islamic State group.
23July 5 - A man purporting to be al-Baghdadi makes his first public appearance, delivering a sermon at a mosque in Mosul.
24Aug. 8 - The U.S. begins targeting the Islamic State group with airstrikes, citing the humanitarian plight of Iraq's minorities, like the Yazidi.
25Aug. 19 - The Islamic State group releases a video showing a jihadi behead James Foley, a 40-year-old journalist from Rochester, New Hampshire, in response to the U.S.-led airstrikes.
26This marks the first of many videos showing militants behead Western captives.
27Sept.
282 - The Islamic State group releases a video showing a jihadi behead American-Israeli journalist Steven Sotloff.
2913 - The Islamic State group releases a video showing a jihadi behead British aid worker David Haines.
30Oct. 3 - The Islamic State group releases a video showing a jihadi behead British hostage Alan Henning.
31Nov. 8 - Iraqi officials say al-Baghdadi is wounded in an airstrike on an Iraqi town near the Syrian border.
32Days later, an online audio message purportedly from al-Baghdadi urges his followers to "explode the volcanoes of jihad everywhere."
33Nov. 16 - An Islamic State group video shows extremists behead a dozen Syrian soldiers and U.S. aid worker Peter Kassig.
34Jan. 10 - An online video shows Taliban fighters in Pakistan pledge loyalty to the Islamic State group and behead a man they identify as a soldier.
35Similar pledges previously arose from Egypt, Yemen and elsewhere in the Mideast.
36Afghan authorities later acknowledge a similar presence in their country.
37Jan. 24 - A message claims the Islamic State group beheads Japanese hostage Haruna Yukawa, a 42-year-old adventurer, after earlier demanding $200 million for him and captive Japanese journalist Kenji Goto.
38Japanese and Jordanian officials attempt to negotiate a prisoner swap to free him and captured Jordanian pilot 1st Lt. Mu'ath al-Kaseasbeh.
39Jan. 26 - Kurdish fighters take control of the Syrian border town of Kobani near Turkey after fighting the Islamic State group for months.
40U.S.-led airstrikes helped turn the tide for the Kurds.
41Jan. 31 - The Islamic State group releases a video saying it beheaded Goto.
42Feb. 3 - The Islamic State group releases a video of it burning al-Kaseasbeh to death in a cage, sparking outrage in Jordan, which launches new strikes targeting the militants.
43Feb. 6 - The Islamic State group claims a Jordanian airstrike kills American hostage Kayla Jean Mueller.
44U.S. officials later confirm her death, but say it wasn't caused by a Jordanian airstrike.
45Feb. 15 - Libyan militants who earlier pledged their loyalty to the Islamic State group behead a group of Coptic Christians from Egypt in an online video.
46Feb. 16 - Egypt launches airstrikes in Libya in retaliation for the beheadings.
47March 11 - After days of besieging Tikrit, Iraqi troops and allied Shiite militiamen enter the Islamic State-held city, backed by Iranian advisers and forgoing the air support of the U.S.-led coalition.
48March 20 - A group identifying itself as an affiliate of the Islamic State group in Yemen claims a series of suicide bombings killing 137 people and wounding 345, though U.S. officials express skepticism about the claim.
49March 25 - The U.S.-led coalition begins airstrikes on Tikrit after Iraqi efforts to take the city stall.
50Shiite militias pull out of Iraqi forces in protest, but later rejoin the offensive.
51April 1 - Iraq declares a "magnificent victory" over the Islamic State group in Tikrit, its biggest gain yet against the militants.
52April 18 - Afghan President Ashraf Ghani blames the Islamic State group for a suicide bombing in the country that kills at least 35 people and wounds 125.
53April 19 - Islamic State affiliates in Libya release a video showing them behead and shoot dead groups of Ethiopian Christians, slayings resembling the February beheadings of the Egyptian Coptic Christians.
