Baltimore deploys Guard troops as riots erupt

1Thousands of police and National Guard troopers were ordered to back up beleaguered officers in Baltimore on Tuesday after riots triggered by anger over the death of a black man in police custody.
2After an evening of violence, officials said calm appeared to be returning to the city as Monday gave way to Tuesday.
3At least 27 people were arrested and 15 police injured as stone and brick-throwing mobs, many of them high school students, clashed with law enforcement and attacked local businesses.
4The state of Maryland declared a state of emergency after furious rioters looted businesses, ransacking shops and stealing armloads of merchandise, and torched cars.
5The violence is the latest in a series of confrontations between US police and mainly young African American young men enraged by what they see as racism in the force.
6Last summer's fatal shooting of unarmed teenager Michael Brown by a white police officer in Ferguson, Missouri triggered coast-to-coast protests.
7The unrest in Baltimore started after the funeral of a black man who died April 19 of spinal injuries apparently suffered during his arrest this month.
8Despite appeals for calm from 25-year-old Freddie Gray's family, roving gangs of youths fought street battles with police that left 15 officers hurt, six of them seriously.
9Several news reporters were assaulted and had equipment stolen.
10Police said they were combing through video footage to identify other possible offenders after 27 people were arrested during the violence.
11But as continued to light up the night sky bright orange in the early hours of Tuesday, police said calm was returning.
12"I think for the vast majority the city is calming itself down slowly but surely, other than the car fires and the street fires," police chief Anthony Batts told a news conference shortly before midnight, urging parents to "take control of your kids."
13Baltimore Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake declared a curfew starting Tuesday night, while city schools said they would remain closed for the day.
14While most of the violence was in the west of the city, as night fell, a large building was also ablaze on Baltimore's east side.
15Maryland police superintendent Colonel William Pallozzi said he had ordered 500 police to the city and requested 5,000 more from the broader Mid-Atlantic region.
16And National Guard commander Adjutant General Linda Singh said she had 5,000 troopers ready and would deploy them in "massive force" to protect people and property.
17"Too many people have spent generations building up this city for it to be destroyed by thugs," said Rawlings-Blake, who has faced criticism that the city had been slow to act.
18President Barack Obama was briefed on the rapidly evolving situation by Rawlings-Blake and his newly sworn in Attorney General Loretta Lynch, the White House said.
19The University of Maryland's downtown campus, corporate offices and the city's famous Lexington Market shut down early.
20The Baltimore Orioles baseball team cancelled its evening game against the visiting Boston Red Sox as a precaution.
21Rioting erupted soon after Gray was buried -- possibly spurred by a cryptic social media message declaring an after-school "purge," street slang for random acts of lawlessness.
22Thousands converged on New Shiloh Baptist church in Baltimore's poverty-ridden Sandtown neighborhood earlier to pay respects to Gray.
23Gray's grieving family had explicitly asked for no protests.
24"Today of all days, the family was clear this was a day of sacred closure," said pastor Jamal Bryant of the city's Empowerment Temple church, who delivered the eulogy.
25Violence had also erupted on Saturday when 34 people were arrested, and six police officers injured, after an orderly rally for Gray outside Baltimore city hall.
26Police on Monday announced they had received a "credible threat" that criminal gangs in Baltimore had "entered into a partnership to 'take out' law enforcement officers."
27At the funeral, Gray's body was in a white casket next to a Los Angeles Dodgers baseball cap and a sign reading "Peace y'all".
28Crowds swayed to hymns, chanting "justice shall prevail, peace will prevail" in the church, where a photo of Gray was displayed among floral wreaths.
29Supporters, many dressed in white, filled the building's 2,200 seats and hundreds of others stood, with the words "Black lives matter and all lives matter" projected on the wall.
30Tensions have been on the rise in Baltimore since Gray's death, which his family's lawyers say was caused when his spine was mostly severed following his arrest.
31Six officers have been suspended with pay pending the outcome of a police investigation that is to be submitted to state prosecutors by Friday.
32The US Justice Department, which was already looking into Baltimore's use of force, has also opened a federal civil rights probe.
33Police confirmed Gray had requested medical help and an inhaler after he was detained and have acknowledged that he should have received medical attention sooner.
