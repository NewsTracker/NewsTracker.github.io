Text from Sepp Blatter's speech at FIFA congress

1You will agree with me, these are unprecedented and difficult times for FIFA.
2The events of yesterday have cast a long shadow over football and over this week's congress.
3Actions of individuals bring shame and humiliation on football and demand action and change from us all.
4We cannot allow the reputation of football and FIFA to be dragged through the mud any longer.
5It has to stop here and now.
6I know many people hold me ultimately responsible for the actions and reputation of the global football community, whether it's a decision for the hosting of a World Cup, or a corruption scandal.
7We, or I, cannot monitor everyone all of the time.
8If people want to do wrong they will also try to hide it, but it must fall to me to bear the responsibility for the reputation and well-being of our organization, and to find a way forward to fix things.
9I will not allow the actions of a few to destroy the hard work and integrity of the vast majority of those who work so hard for football.
10I must stress that those who are corrupt in football are in a tiny minority, like in society.
11But like in society they must be catched (sic) and held responsible for their actions.
12Football cannot be the exception to the rule.
13That is our responsibility at FIFA, and we will cooperate with all authorities to make sure anyone involved in wrongdoing, from top to bottom, is discovered and punished.
14There can be no place for corruption of any kind.
15The next few months will not be easy for FIFA.
16I'm sure more bad news will follow.
17But it is necessary to begin to restore trust in our organization.
18Let this be the turning point.
19More needs to be done to make sure everyone in football behaves responsibly and ethically, and everywhere, also outside of the field of play, where there is no referee, no boundaries and no time limit.
20Football, the fans, the players, the clubs, the world, deserve so much more.
21And we must respond tomorrow.
22Tomorrow, the congress, we have the opportunity to begin on what will be a long and difficult road to rebuilding trust.
23We have lost their trust, at least a part of it, and we must now earn it back.
24We must earn it back through the decisions we make, through the expectations we place on each other and through the way we behave individually.
25And that brings me to tonight's ceremony.
26You have seen that the opening ceremony is about football.
27The spirit of the game.
28The aspect of football that make it so loved by so many.
29Our cast of artists and dancers will bring the spirit to life tonight and I thank them all for their hard work in preparing for this show under these special circumstances.
30I'm sure you will agree they are a great and talented cast and crew.
31It is important that we do not lose sight of the spirit of football.
32I hope that this show will remind us what we are fighting for, and why we must continue to fight for positive change in football even when some among us let us down.
33So please enjoy our show, enjoy it.
34But I ask you also to reflect on why a vast majority, we are all in football and we like this game, not for greed, not for exploit (sic), not for power, but because of love for the game, for this game, to serve others and to achieve positive change through responsible leadership.
35Solidarity and unity is asked for the game, for the world, for peace.
