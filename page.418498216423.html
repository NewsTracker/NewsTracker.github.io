Tropical Storm Dolores strengthens off coast of Mexico

1Tropical Storm Dolores is gradually strengthening in the Pacific and is expected to become a hurricane.
2On Monday, Dolores had maximum sustained winds of 70 mph (110 kph).
3The storm is centered about 170 miles (275 kilometers) southwest of Manzanillo, Mexico and is moving west-northwest at 12 mph (19 kph).
4A tropical storm watch is in effect from Punta San Telmo to Cabo Corrientes.
5Forecasters with the National Hurricane Center in Miami predict additional strengthening, with Dolores becoming a hurricane later Monday.
6Meanwhile, Tropical Storm Enrique churns far from land, posing no threat.
7Enrique has maximum sustained winds of 40 mph (65 kph).
8It's centered about 1,190 miles (1,915 kilometers) west-southwest of the southern tip of Mexico's Baja California peninsula and is moving northwest near 10 mph (17 kph).
