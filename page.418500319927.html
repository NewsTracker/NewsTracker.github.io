14After grueling, often angry negotiations that tested the limits of European unity, Greece on Monday won a preliminary deal that averts financial catastrophe but also guarantees years more of hardship and sacrifice for its people.
15Jacob Kirkegaard, senior fellow at the Peterson Institute for International Economics, says the new deal for Greece puts more emphasis on much-needed structural reforms and less on short-term budget cutting.
16"It was the best deal the Greeks could get," he says.
17"They did not do too badly given the terrible, terrible, disastrous starting point the current government put them in."
18He says the negotiations also will have wide implications for the entire 19-nation eurozone.
19"(This) was not really about Greece. It was about the very clear public announcement by the Germans and others that they were negotiating with Grexit on the table," he says.
20"That is breaking a taboo that marks a significant, long-term change in the way the euro works ... The signal has now been sent that if you do not behave inside the euro, you may find yourself on the outside."
21The Greek government's junior coalition partner says it cannot back the agreement announced Monday between Greece and its European creditors - describing the proposed deal as a "coup staged by Germany and other countries."
22Defense Minister Panos Kammenos, leader of the right-wing Independent Greeks party, said he had no plans to leave the government but added he would not join a national unity government.
23"This deal introduced many new issues ... we cannot agree with it," Kammenos said after meeting with Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras.
24The announcement is a blow to Tsipras' six-month-old government, which is struggling to maintain its majority in parliament.
25The eurozone's top official thinks it will take around four weeks for a new bailout program for Greece to be worked out.
26Jeroen Dijsselbloem, who was re-elected Monday for a second two-year term as Eurogroup President, said finance ministers are assessing how to get Athens some immediate help - or bridge financing - to meet upcoming commitments.
27Speaking at the eurogroup's third meeting in as many days, Dijsselbloem said ministers had "yet to find the golden key to solve this issue."
28Dijsselbloem said he hoped the agreement between Greece and its European creditors will help put the country back on a sustainable economic path.
29U.S. Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew is welcoming the agreement between Greece and its European creditors as "an important step forward."
30In a statement Monday, Lew said: "The agreement provides a basis for restoring trust among the parties and creates the conditions for a path forward for Greece within the eurozone."
31Keeping Greece among the nations using the euro, Lew said, was in the best interests of Greece, Europe and the global economy.
32He noted Greece's "commitment to make deep and difficult fiscal and structural reforms" and a commitment by its creditors to "create a path for Greece to return to growth" and make Greece's debt burden more manageable.
33European anti-bailout parties have lashed out at their countries' governments over Monday's austerity agreement with Greece.
34In Ireland, Sinn Fein leader Gerry Adams has described the behavior by eurozone countries, including the Irish government, as "alarming."
35He says "they have closed down the Greek banking system and held the Greek government and people to ransom."
36Adams says "to its shame, the Irish government took the side of the strong against those in need of support."
37Spain's Podemos party leveled similar criticism.

39The new European bailout accord for Greece will face a debate and vote Wednesday in France's lower house of Parliament, where it is expected to win approval.
40French law doesn't require a vote, but Prime Minister Manuel Valls said Monday it's "important" to put the deal to lawmakers to ensure broad support for the idea of helping Greece.
41France's Socialist leadership has been the staunchest ally of Greece's leftist government this year.
42Mainstream parties on both the left and right in France have generally supported efforts to keep Greece in the shared euro currency for overall European unity.
43The International Monetary Fund says it "stands ready to work with the Greek authorities," after a deal was reached to prevent Greece from crashing out of the common euro currency.
44Under the deal reached Monday, Greece must take certain measures to reform its economy in order to start negotiations on a third multi-billion euro bailout package.
45One condition was for Athens to request continued IMF involvement in its finances.
46The IMF was part of Greece's two previous bailouts, but at the moment Greece cannot receive any further funds from it after missing a roughly 1.5 billion-euro debt repayment last month.
47Greece is the first developed nation to have missed an IMF repayment.
48Greek Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras went straight to his Athens office upon returning from the marathon eurozone summit in Brussels and into a meeting with Finance Minister Euclid Tsakalotos and other senior party officials.
49Tsipras must push several unpalatable measures through Parliament in the next two days, including sales tax increases and pension reforms, to guarantee that talks on a third multibillion-euro (dollar) bailout for Greece can start in earnest.
50He faces an uphill battle to persuade members of his own Syriza party, as well as his governing coalition partner, to back the deal after months of insisting he would not accept more austerity measures.
