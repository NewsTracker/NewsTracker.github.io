Baltimore Marchers Stage 'Die-In' Proesting Police-Custody Death

1Baltimore Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake says she is "profoundly disappointed" that the protests over the death of Freddie Gray turned violent.
2"Our city has a long history of peaceful demonstrations," Rawlings-Blake said Saturday night.
3"Ninety-five percent or more of the people who came out sought to protest in that spirit. Unfortunately a small group of agitators turned what was otherwise a peaceful demonstration into a violent protest."
4At least 12 people were arrested and two were injured in the mayhem.
5Fire officials estimate that roughly 1,200 people gathered at City Hall earlier in the day, and one person was transported to the hospital after falling down.
6Gray suffered a spinal injury while in police custody and later died at a hospital.
7Protesters have been angry, gathering for near-daily demonstrations since Gray died about a week ago.
8Freddie Gray's twin sister is appealing to protesters to "please, please stop the violence" after a small group of demonstrators smashed out storefronts and police cars.
9It was the first public comments from Gray's sister, Fredricka Gray, since he suffered an unexplained spinal injury in police custody and died.
10Fredericka Gray appeared with the mayor at a news conference and said her brother would not want to see the kind of violence that occurred in Baltimore on Saturday night.
11At least two people were injured and a dozen were arrested.
12Fans at the Orioles-Red Sox baseball game were told to stay in the stadium because of public safety worries.
13Police have cleared Freddie Gray protesters of an intersection near the Baltimore Orioles game, allowing traffic to once again start flowing.
14The demonstrators split off from the main rally Saturday, which drew thousands of people upset over the police-custody death of Gray.
15The number of what police called "agitators" are dwindling downtown, as a line of officers continue to push protesters away from the intersection they'd blocked traffic for hours.
16The head of a group that sponsored Saturday's rally and march said he was pleased with how everything went.
17Malik Shabazz, the president of Black Lawyers for Justice, said the crowd exceeded their expectations and included Gray's family, Baltimore residents and people from the national anti-police brutality movement.
18Asked about the second, more volatile portion of the demonstrations, he said protesters' anger is not a shock.
19"This is a problem that has not been solved. When there's no justice, they tend to want to take matters into their own hands," he said.
20The Baltimore police commissioner says he believes looters and other people causing trouble during a Freddie Gray protest are not from the city.
21Commissioner Anthony Batts tells The Associated Press that Batts said there have been 12 arrests, and crimes include two armed robberies, several assaults and looting of a convenience store.
22Batts says his officers are preparing to make a "mass arrest" after issuing several dispersal orders at one intersection still crowded with protesters.
23Batts said everyone attending the Orioles game at Camden Yards will be kept inside until the scene is cleared.
24Thousands of people came out to protest the police-custody death of Gray, who died after an unexplained spinal injury.
25Authorities are investigating his death.
26Six officers have been suspended with pay.
