Judge in Blazer case unsure how to pronounce FIFA

1FIFA may be famous throughout the world.
2In the Brooklyn court room of U.S. District Judge U.S. District Judge Raymond J. Dearie, not so much.
3During the November plea hearing of former FIFA executive committee member Chuck Blazer, Dearie told the defendant: "There are 10 changes, if I am not mistaken, 10 charges in total."
4Blazer, the former No.
52 of soccer's governing body in North and Central America and the Caribbean, responded: "That is correct," according to a transcript unsealed Wednesday.
6Dearie then was stumped by the acronym used by the Federation Internationale de Football Association, soccer's world governing body.
7"I don't know how you pronounce it," the judge said.
8"FIFA, your honor," said Evan M. Norris, and assistant U.S. attorney.
