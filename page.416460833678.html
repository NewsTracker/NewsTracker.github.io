Letters signal Bali Nine execution close

1Australian embassy officials will on Saturday be given notice of Indonesia's plans to execute Andrew Chan and Myuran Sukumaran, but not the date, the attorney-general's spokesman says.
2Tony Spontana told reporters the date hadn't been decided because there was no decision yet on an application for a judicial review for an Indonesian prisoner.
3"It depends on the Zainal Abidin decision," he said.
4Indonesia is required to give at least 72 hours' notice of the executions.
5Mr Spontana said the Saturday meeting of all foreign embassies concerned - Australia, Brazil, Nigeria, France and the Philippines - signalled the start of the execution process.
6A prisoner listed as being from Ghana, Martin Anderson, was actually Nigerian, Mr Spontana said.
7"It can be three days, it can be more," he said of the notice period.
8"We're only telling them that they are entering the stages of execution."
9Families and consular staff will get as much chance as possible to see the prisoners before execution, Mr Spontana said.
10"Certainly, until the time comes when they're only guarded by prison officers, doctors and psychiatrists," he said.
11The spokesman couldn't confirm which prisoners had been moved into isolation conditions, except for Mary Jane Veloso from the Philippines.
12Veloso was moved to Nusakambangan before dawn on Friday and was in isolation in one of the island's prisons, Mr Spontana said, because it had no special facility for women.
13For this reason, her transfer from a prison near Jogjakarta was delayed for as long as possible before the executions.
14Her two sons, aged six and 12, have arrived in Indonesia and had expected to visit her on Friday afternoon.
