Apple updates iPods for first time in 3 years

1On Wednesday, Apple (AAPL, Tech30) released its latest version of the iPod touch, giving it better cameras, the same A8 chip as the newest version of the iPhone, and a fresh coat of paint.
2The new iPod touch's rear camera is now 8 megapixels, up from 5 MP in the previous version.
3It can now take time-lapse video, pictures in slow motion, burst photos and panoramas.
4The iPod touch's 1.2 MP front-facing camera is exactly the same as the iPhone 6's.
5It allows people to adjust exposures, record in 720 HD video, and has a timer mode.
6Since it runs iOS 8, iPod touch customers can access Apple Music, the company's new streaming music service.
7Apple also gave all of its iPods new colors -- blue, pink, silver, gold and space gray.
8The iPod nano and iPod shuffle also have the new colors, but no hardware or software changes.
9Wednesday marks the first set of major upgrades to the iPod since October 2012.
10The iPod hasn't been a major contributor to Apple's bottom line in years, but the iPod label still holds a lot of brand value for the company.
11The new iPods are on sale online and in Apple Stores now.
12Prices for the iPod touch begin at $199 for a 16 GB model and swings up to $399 for a 128GB version -- the largest storage capacity ever for an iPod touch.
