CANNES WATCH: Salma Hayek eats Cannes' heart out

1CANNES, France (AP) - In a movie filled with strikingly lavish images, those of Salma Hayek eating the heart of a sea monster in Matteo Garrone's "The Tale of Tales" are hard to shake - especially for the actress.
2"Disgusting!"
3Hayek exclaimed Thursday morning after it showed for critics at the Cannes Film Festival.
4"The Tale of Tales" is based on the influential but little known 17th century Neapolitan fairy tales of Giambattista Basile.
5It's a Brothers Grimm-like world of kings and princesses, ogres and witches, in a series of grotesque fables with modern relevance.
6Critics mostly responded rapturously to its baroque weirdness at Cannes.
7Hayek plays a queen desperate to have a child.
8Advised by a warlock to eat the heart of a beast, her king husband (John C. Reilly) slays a monster, and Hayek subsequently chows down on the massive organ.
9"Our director here wanted ... the inside of the heart to be identical to the real heart," said Hayek.
10"Forbid I would take a bite and a doctor would realize an artery is missing."
11There were other challenges, said Hayek, including wearing Massimo Cantini Parrini's sumptuous costumes.
12"He never made me a dress that weighed less than 30 kilos (66 pounds)," said Hayek.
13Filming one scene repeatedly in which she runs through a labyrinth, Hayek said she got so tired that her heavy dress became stuck after Garrone told her to jump.
14Three men had to dislodge her.
15"Massimo was screaming, 'The dress! The dress! " said Hayek.
16"It was humiliating."
