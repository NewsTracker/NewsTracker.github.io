Aurora Theater shooter James Holmes' ex asked him to see therapist, trial hears

1Months before James Holmes opened fire in a Colorado movie theater, his ex-girlfriend said she urged him talk to his therapist after he mentioned having thoughts about killing people, but his thoughts 'seemed very philosophical' and not a concrete threat.
2Gargi Datta also testified Thursday that during their relationship, Holmes showed no interest in guns, including when they visited an outdoor store that sold weapons, and that she did not know about his meticulous plans for the July 20, 2012 attack or the arsenal he assembled.
3Datta and Holmes were graduate students at the University of Colorado when they began dating during their first semester in the fall of 2011.
4By February 2012, she did not want anything more than a casual relationship, and the two remained 'friends with benefits' until Holmes told her in early April he could not continue the relationship as friends after telling her that he loved her.
5After that, Datta said, she did not communicate with Holmes and only saw him in the classroom.
6Fears: In March 2012 Datta said Holmes sent her a message about wanting to 'do evil' and killing people to increase his self-worth, or human capital.
7District Attorney George Brauchler has said Holmes' breakup with Datta was a catalyst to the shooting at a suburban Denver movie theater.
8He said Datta was Holmes' first romantic relationship.
9Holmes has pleaded not guilty by reason of insanity in the attack that killed 12 people and injured 70.
10Prosecutors contend Holmes was sane, and they are seeking the death penalty.
11In March 2012, Datta said, Holmes sent her an electronic message about wanting to 'do evil' and killing people to increase his self-worth, or human capital, a belief previously discussed during the trial.
12She was concerned, so she showed a friend, and they both asked him to talk to his therapist about it.
13He assured them that he was.
14'Initially I was just thinking he was messing with me, that he was joking.
15It didn't make sense to me and it seemed a bit irrational,' said Datta, who had earlier suggested that Holmes see a therapist because of his social awkwardness.
16In court, Datta never looked directly at Holmes and repeatedly referred to him as 'the defendant' rather than his name.
17Holmes swayed in his chair as she spoke but had no other visible reaction.
18In contrast, defense attorney Tamara Brady showed a photo of Datta and Holmes, both smiling, on a hike they took before they severed ties and pointed out that Holmes had baked chicken and made shrimp for a candlelit Valentine's Day dinner for her.
19Brady also displayed four side-by-side images of how Holmes looked when he was arrested after the 2012 theater shooting - with wide-eyes and a shock of orange hair.
20Brady asked if she had ever seen Holmes look like that before.
21She said no.

23Datta concluded her testimony, but the judge told her she could be summoned back.
24She took the stand on a day when more victims testified about their experiences in the theater, including Farrah Soudani, who described being eviscerated by bullets that pierced her abdomen.
25She recalled bending down and picking up her intestines, trying to hold them inside her and then laying on the bloody theater floor while paramedics tried to carry her out of the theater.
