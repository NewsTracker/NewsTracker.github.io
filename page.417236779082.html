Greece warns it does not have the money to make IMF repayment

1Greek Finance Minister Yanis Varoufakis and Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras.
2Mr Varoufakis said it would be "catastrophic" if Greece left the euro.
3Greece cannot make debt repayments to the International Monetary Fund next month unless it achieves a deal with creditors, Interior Minister Nikos Voutsis said on Sunday, the most explicit remarks yet from Athens about the likelihood of default if talks fail.
4Meanwhile, Finance Minister Yanis Varoufakis said it would be "catastrophic" if Greece left the euro, predicting it would be "the beginning of the end of the common currency project".
5Shut out of bond markets and with bailout aid locked, cash-strapped Athens has been scraping state coffers to meet debt obligations and to pay wages and pensions.
6After four months of talks with its eurozone partners and the IMF, the country's leftist-led government is still scrambling for a deal that could release up to 7.2 billion euros ($10.1 billion) in remaining aid to avert bankruptcy.
7"The four instalments for the IMF in June are 1.6 billion. This money will not be given and is not there to be given," Mr Voutsis told Greek Mega TV's weekend show.
8Asked about his concern over a credit event if Athens misses a payment, he said: "We are not seeking this, we don't want it, it is not our strategy. Things have matured for a deal of logic.
9"We are discussing, based on our contained optimism, that there will be a strong agreement [with lenders] so that the country will be able to breathe. This is the bet."
10The Athens government has previously said it is in danger of running out of money soon without a deal, but has insisted it still plans to make all upcoming payments.
11The government is under pressure to agree to more cuts and reforms to secure the funding, but opposes measures which it says make the situation worse by preventing recovery from one of the deepest recessions in modern times.
12Mr Voutsis said the government was determined to fight against the lenders' strategy of "asphyxiation".
13"This policy of extreme austerity and unemployment in Greece must be hit," he said.
14"We will not escape from this fight."
15In an effort to placate the hard left faction of his Syriza party, Greek Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras said on Saturday the government was on a final stretch towards a deal but would not accept "humiliating terms".
16Finance Minister Yanis Varoufakis said Greece had made "enormous strides" at reaching a deal with its lenders to avert bankruptcy but it was now up to the institutions to do their bit.
17"We have met them three-quarters of the way, they need to meet us one quarter of the way," he told Britain's BBC on Sunday.
18Mr Varoufakis said it would be "catastrophic" if Greece left the euro and "the beginning of the end of the common currency project".
19He said in the last four months Athens managed to pay public-sector salaries, pensions and dues to the IMF by extracting 14 per cent of national output, doing "remarkably well" for an economy that doesn't have access to money markets.
20"At some point we will not be able to do it and at some point we are going obviously to have to make this choice that no minister of finance should ever have to make," Mr Varoufakis said.
