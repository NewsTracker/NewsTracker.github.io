Russell Westbrook Leads Thunder to Victory

Fighting rages in east Ukraine despite bid to revive truce

1They moved into a tie with Phoenix for eighth in the Western Conference.
2A version of this brief appears in print on February 20, 2015, on page B14 of the New York edition with the headline: Westbrook Leads Thunder to Victory.
1Western nations are refusing to give up on a peace deal brokered by France and Germany even though the rebels disavowed it to seize the important railway hub of Debaltseve.
2Shelling continued near that town on Thursday, and local officials in government-held territory said rebels had also fired mortar bombs at another town further south.
3Kiev fears they are massing for an assault near the major port of Mariupol.
4Thousands of weary and demoralised soldiers withdrew from Debaltseve on Wednesday in one of the worst defeats suffered by Kiev during 10 months of fighting in which more than 5000 people have been killed.
5European and US officials have expressed hope that the ceasefire will hold now that the rebels, fighting for territory Russian President Vladimir Putin has called "New Russia", have achieved their immediate goal of taking Debaltseve.
6But Reuters correspondents outside the rebel-held town of Vuhlehirsk said artillery shells were still falling on nearby Debaltseve, though with less intensity than earlier this week.
7Reporters in the main rebel stronghold of Donetsk said there was also shelling in the area.
8Kiev's biggest fear is of a rebel assault on Mariupol, a port of 500,000 people and by far the biggest government-held city in the two rebellious eastern provinces.
9"Right now there are mortar attacks on Shyrokine," a military spokesman said, referring to a village about 30 kilometres east of Mariupol, along the coast of the Sea of Azov.
10"There is no attempt to seize our positions up to now. The rebels are bringing up reserves," he said.
11Wednesday's withdrawal was a humiliating defeat for Ukrainian President Petro Poroshenko, who faces an economic crisis as well as the war.
12Images of captured Ukrainian soldiers were beamed across Russia.
13The Ukrainian Defence Ministry said 13 servicemen were killed and 157 wounded during the withdrawal and a further 82 were still missing.
14Ninety-three were taken prisoner.
15"There are no words to describe it. Along the entire way we were blanketed with shots, wherever there were trees they fired at us from machine guns and grenade launchers. They used everything," Vadim, a soldier from Ukraine's 30th brigade, told Reuters in Artemivsk, a government-held town north of Debaltseve where the soldiers assembled after they withdrew.
16Some blamed commanders for leaving them trapped in the besieged town after it became impossible to resupply it.
17"It felt like we'd been abandoned or betrayed," said a soldier from Ukraine's 55th brigade.
18The rebels have maintained that the ceasefire did not apply to Debaltseve, suggesting they may begin to observe it now that they have captured the town.
19They have announced that they are pulling back heavy guns as required under the truce.
20The leaders of Germany, France, Ukraine and Russia agreed in a telephone call to make a new attempt to enforce the ceasefire and ensure other terms of the peace deal are implemented.
21The White House said it was "deeply troubled" by reports of fighting and NATO's top military commander, US Air Force General Philip Breedlove, said he did not think the truce had ever even begun.
22"It is a ceasefire in name only," General Breedlove said during a visit to Kosovo.
23Western countries said Russia is behind the rebel advance, having deployed thousands of troops with advanced weaponry into eastern Ukraine to fight on the separatists' behalf, though Moscow denied this.
24British Defence Secretary Michael Fallon said Mr Putin posed a "real and present danger" to Estonia, Latvia and Lithuania and NATO was getting ready to repel any aggression.
25Vice-President of the European Commission Valdis Dombrovskis said "Russia is looking to redraw Europe's 21st century borders by force".
