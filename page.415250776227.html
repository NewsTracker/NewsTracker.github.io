News of robust hiring in US month sends bonds lower, dollar higher; Apple gains, will join Dow

1Apple rose 1 percent early Friday after it was announced that the company would be joining the Dow Jones industrial average, replacing AT&T.
2AT&T fell 1 percent.
3The Dow Jones industrial average was down 107 points, or 0.6 percent, to 18,024 as of 9:35 a.m. Eastern time.
4The Standard & Poor's 500 lost nine points, or 0.4 percent, to 2,091.
5The Nasdaq fell 10 points, or 0.2 percent, to 4,971.
6Bond prices fell, sending yields higher, after the Labor Department reported that U.S. employers extended a healthy streak of hiring in February.
7That could give the Federal Reserve room to raise rates.
