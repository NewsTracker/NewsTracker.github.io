Greek Finance Minister Angered Over Missing Copy of Bailout

1Greek Finance Minister Yanis Varoufakis attends a parliament session before the vote of an anti-poverty bill, the first piece of legislation from the left-wing government, in Athens, on Wednesday, March 18, 2015.
2Tsipras insisted Wednesday his government will honor an election promise to end budget austerity - a note of defiance ahead of an expected meeting with the leaders of Germany and France on the country's troubled bailout.
3Greece's finance minister says a copy of the country's original bailout agreement was missing from the ministry when he took over nearly two months ago.
4Speaking in parliament Thursday, Yanis Varoufakis said he felt "almost infinite anger" when told that a copy of the agreement was not available, adding that one was located three and a half days after he made the request.
5He blamed a tradition in Greece of ministers taking key documents with them when they leave office, and promised to change the rules to stop the practice.
6Varoufakis spoke before the Greek prime minister was to hold emergency talks in Brussels with lead lenders over the country's troubled bailout negotiations.
