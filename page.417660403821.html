4Scandal-hit FIFA will hold an extraordinary executive committee meeting in July to discuss dates for the election to replace president Sepp Blatter, who announced his resignation last week.
5Soccer's governing body said in a statement there are "various date options" for the extraordinary Congress and did not confirm a report by the BBC that the election would take place on December 16.
6Last week, FIFA's audit and compliance committee chairman and the man responsible for overseeing the election, Domenico Scala, said it could take place any time between December and March.
7"It requires an extraordinary executive committee that needs to confirm a date and agenda for the extraordinary elective congress," a FIFA spokesperson in a statement.
8"This extraordinary executive committee will convene in July, the precise date to be confirmed within this week. For this extraordinary elective congress (to elect Blatter's successor) there are currently various date options for discussion."
9Blatter tendered his resignation last Wednesday (AEST), less than a week after Swiss police staged a dawn raid on a luxury hotel in Zurich and arrested several officials on corruption charges filed by US prosecutors in New York and just a few days after being re-elected for a fifth term as FIFA president.
10However, Blatter is intent on staying in office until his successor is appointed and wants to lead the effort to clean up his federation.

13The BBC said that mid-December was Blatter's preferred option for electing a new president.
14Jordan's Prince Ali bin al-Hussein, who lost out to Blatter in the election, is tipped as a possible candidate while Chung Mong-joon, the billionaire scion of South Korea's Hyundai conglomerate, is also weighing up a bid to replace Blatter.
15Scala said last week that at least four months' notice was required for a presidential election to be held.
16"FIFA must also consider appropriate time to vet candidates and allow them to present their ideas for the organisation that set forth their vision," he said at the time.
17The executive committee must also decide the on the deadline by which candidates must formally declare their intention to stand.
18Candidates need to have written backing from five national associations to be eligible.
19The president is elected by FIFA's 209 member associations, which hold one vote each.
