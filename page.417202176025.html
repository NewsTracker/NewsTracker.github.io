Too many Rohingya to settle: Morrison

1Social services minister Scott Morrison says people who suggest countries in the region can resettle persecuted Rohingya misunderstand the scale of the problem.
2Thousands of boat people are stranded on boats in camps and at sea in South East Asia.
3The crisis comes amid reports that Foreign Affairs Minister Julie Bishop has been told by Indonesian officials that only around 30-40 per cent are Rohingya refugees and the rest are illegal workers from Bangladesh seeking jobs in Malaysia.
4Mr Morrison says he wasn't privy to the reported discussion and can't confirm the reports.
5Mr Morrison says he has visited Rohingya displacement camps and the issue is more complex than advocates made out.
6"There's a million Rohingya in Myanmar. The suggestion that somehow resettlement is the answer to that issue, I think completely misunderstands what is happening in that part of the world," he said in Sydney on Saturday.
