Ukraine wants global court to investigate crimes in Crimea and east

1Ukraine has already given the global court the authority to investigate crimes on its territory from November 21, 2013, to February 22, 2014, the period leading up to the fall of Ukraine's former president Viktor Yanukovich.
2The crises in Crimea and the east emerged later in 2014.
3"We are quite optimistic about more, definitely more, engagement of the ICC," Pavlo Klimkin told Reuters before meeting the court's president and prosecutor on Friday.
4An ICC referral would cover "everything under the (ICC) mandate, including crimes against humanity", he said.
5Klimkin named the attack on the strategic port of Mariupol in eastern Ukraine which killed 30 in January as an example of a crime against humanity.
6"The shelling killed, in seconds, more than 30 people and heavily wounded 100 people," Klimkin said.
7"If you deliberately shell and, I stress, deliberately shell cities, killing civilians, it's a completely different situation (from military operations) and we have to engage the ICC."
8The wider probe proposed by Klimkin would for the first time consider allegations of direct Russian involvement in the conflict in eastern Ukraine, which Moscow denies.
9ICC prosecutors began their preliminary inquiry last April after the government of Ukraine asked them to look into allegations that Yanukovich's troops had killed over 100 protesters in Kiev and other cities.
10The referral period runs up to just before Russia annexed Crimea, so the investigation so far excludes any crimes that might have been committed by Russian-backed troops.
11Alex Whiting, a former senior ICC prosecutor, said a new referral could force an ICC investigation of the wider Ukraine conflict.
12"The prosecutors have been kind of sitting on it because it's more complicated whether there have been war crimes," he said.
13"But here (Crimea and eastern Ukraine) there have been widespread allegations of war crimes and even crimes against humanity, so it will be much harder to ignore."
14Ukraine wants the International Criminal Court to investigate all alleged recent war crimes in Crimea and eastern Ukraine, Foreign minister Pavlo Klimkin said in an interview, broadening an existing probe.
