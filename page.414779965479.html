1Cairo: A video released by the Islamic State appeared to show the mass beheading of a group of Egyptian Christians by fighters in a recently formed Libyan arm of the militant group.
2Identical in style and details to earlier execution videos released by IS, this was the first video the group has released depicting a killing outside of its core territory in Syria and Iraq.
3It appeared to show much closer communication and collaboration between IS and its far-flung satellite groups than Western officials previously believed.
4Concern is already growing in Libya and the West that IS might capitalise on the chaos that has engulfed the country to establish and expand a base of operations there.
5At least three groups of Libyan fighters have already pledged loyalty to IS, one in each of the country's three regions - Barqa in the east, Fezzan in the south, and Tripolitania in the west.
6Relatives of the kidnapped Egyptians had protested in Cairo for the release.
7In Cairo, where the military-backed government has been working to defeat the Islamist factions in neighbouring Libya, supporters of the government cited the video released on Sunday as new evidence that those factions pose a growing threat to Egypt's own security.
8Confirming that those killed in the video were Egyptian Christians taken hostage in Libya weeks ago, Egyptian President Abdel-Fattah al-Sisi announced seven days of national mourning.
9In a televised address, he said Egypt would chose the "necessary means and timing to avenge the criminal killings."
10The White House offered support to Egypt's government and condolences to the victims' families, condemning the "despicable and cowardly" killings.
11IS promoted the video last week with a photograph from the scene that appeared in its English-language online magazine Dabiq.
12The main difference from other execution videos it has released is that the new one appears to have taken place on the southern shore of the Mediterranean, on a rocky beach said by militants in the video to be in western Libya, much closer to Europe than sites previously depicted.
13Fighters under the banner of the Tripolitania Province of the Islamic State announced last month they were holding about 20 Coptic Christians.
14A similar number of Egyptian Christians in Libya seeking work had disappeared in the mid-coastal city of Sirt.
15Officials of the Egyptian government and the Coptic Church confirmed that captives seen in a photograph with the announcement were the missing Egyptian Christians, and on Sunday confirmed they were killed in the video.
16In the video, titled "A message signed with blood to the nation of the cross", masked fighters identified as from the Tripolitania province of IS, dressed in black with machetes at their chests, parade along a rocky beach towards the camera with a row of bound captives in orange jumpsuits.
17The only sound is that of the crashing waves.
18The captives are made to kneel, and then one by one they are beheaded.
19The lead executioner, who wears a brown mask over his face, thrusts his dagger at the camera.
20"O people, recently you have seen us on the hills of al-Sham and Dabiq's plain, chopping off the heads that have been carrying the cross for a long time," he says in fluent English with an American accent.
21"And today, we are on the south of Rome, on the land of Islam, Libya, sending another message."
22The high-quality video, which bears the logo of al-Hayat, the official publishing arm of IS, is in stark contrast to the footage released in the past by affiliates of the group.
23The footage in those videos was shaky and grainy, suggesting an amateur production.
24By contrast, the five-minute clip released on Sunday is professional and cinematic, and is filmed in the same style as previous IS videos, including one that showed the mass beheading of captured Syrian soldiers last year.
