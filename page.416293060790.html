Cloudy execution drug was too cold, not tainted, Georgia officials say

1Georgia officials halted the March 2 execution of 46-year-old Kelly Renee Gissendaner, on death row for killing her husband in 1997, after officials observed the murky appearance of the pentobarbital solution set to be used in her death by lethal injection.
2A state investigation found the drug's appearance changed because it had been stored at a temperature below 59 degrees F (15 C), the Georgia Department of Corrections said in a statement.
3The department said testing of the samples confirmed that the drugs had not been adulterated.
4It added it planned to correct the mistake, but did not say whether Gissendaner's execution had been rescheduled.
5Gissendaner was scheduled to become the first woman executed by Georgia in 70 years.
6Georgia has also suspended the execution of another condemned inmate, Brian Keith Terrell, while an analysis of the drug supply was conducted.
7Terrell, who had been scheduled to die on March 10, was condemned for the 1992 murder of a 70-year-old man.
