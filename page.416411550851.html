Wall St mixed; euro rises against dollar

1NEW YORK (Reuters) - Wall Street equities were mixed and stocks on European and world markets moved higher on Tuesday, boosted by strong corporate earnings, while the euro reversed course and rose against the dollar.
2Investors held off making big U.S. equities bets as they waited for more earnings reports and news on Greece, said Scott Wren, senior global equity strategist at Wells Fargo Investment Institute in St. Louis.
3"The focus in the very short term is earnings," he said.
4"I didn't see any big beat or surprise in earnings today."
5Europe's pan-regional FTSEurofirst 300 index (.FTEU3) closed up 0.93 percent, with Publicis (PUBP.PA), Sky (SKYB.L) and ARM Holdings (ARM.L) all up more than 4 percent after reporting results.
6The MSCI gauge of stocks in major markets was up 0.3 percent at 437.13.
7The Dow Jones industrial average (.DJI) was down 51.37 points, or 0.28 percent, at 17,983.56.
8The Standard & Poor's 500 Index (.SPX) was up 0.04 points, or 0.00 percent, at 2,100.44.
9The Nasdaq Composite Index (.IXIC) was up 28.23 points, or 0.57 percent, at 5,022.84.
10The benchmark 10-year U.S. Treasury note fell 4/32, the yield at 1.9105 percent.
11The euro was up 0.26 percent at $1.076 (EUR=) after falling earlier in the day after following a European Central Bank proposal to increase the insurance it would demand in return for emergency funding to Greek banks.
12The dollar also reversed course and fell 0.19 percent against a basket (.DXY) of major currencies.
13It is roughly 23 percent higher than it was a year ago.
14"I'd say the dollar is overbought. In the near term you could see the dollar fall against the euro," said Wren, adding that U.S. monetary policy could be looser than investors expect and that European policy may be tighter than expected.
15Even after several rounds of negotiations, hopes are slim that Athens will be able to convince euro zone finance ministers to continue their financial support at meetings on Friday.
16This could mean it runs out of cash by the end of the month.
17An unprecedented debt default in the currency bloc could open the way for Greece to exit the euro, though ECB Vice President Vitor Constancio said on Monday that a country that defaults would not have to ditch the currency.
18Brent crude (LCOc1) dipped 0.9 percent to $62.88 per barrel but was still up 14 percent since the start of the month.
19U.S. crude (CLc1) contracts for May, due to expire later on Tuesday, fell 0.05 percent to $56.35, but were still up about 12 percent since April 8.
