Islamic State video purported to show killing of Ethiopian Christians

1Cairo: A new Islamic State video distributed on social media apparently shows the shootings and beheadings of two groups of Ethiopian Christians in Libya.
2The video purports to show the killings in two locations, one described as being in the country's south and the other a sunny Mediterranean beach.
3The same English-speaking fighter who presided over similar killings depicted in a video released in February declares, "We are back again."
4The 29-minute video, carrying the logo of the radical group's production arm, Al Furqan Media, echoed the February video, which showed the simultaneous beheadings of 21 men, 20 of them Egyptian Coptic Christians, on a Libyan beach.
5In response to the February incident, Egypt retaliated with airstrikes and tried without success to marshal regional support for military action against Islamist groups in Libya.
6Many of the executed Copts were from a few poor villages in southern Egypt.
7Impoverished and desperate labourers from nearby countries including Egypt, Ethiopia and Sudan continue to seek jobs in Libya despite the risks they run by being in the collapsing North African nation.
8Christians have been in particular peril.
9In the video released Sunday, the ringleader informs "the nation of the cross" that Christians falling under the Islamic State's control face death if they do not accept Islam, according to a transcript provided by the US-based SITE Intelligence Group, which monitors jihadi activity.
10The killings were carried out to "take revenge for Muslim blood," the chief executioner said.
11The exact number of victims could not be determined from the video.
12They were identified in a caption as adherents of "the hostile Ethiopian church."
13The video's date and locations could not be independently verified.
14Libya has fallen into chaos, with an heavily armed militias battling for political power and energy wealth.
15They are organised loosely into a Tripoli-based faction loyal to the Islamist-leaning former parliament, and an internationally recognised government based in the country's east.
