4Washington: Apple is diving deeper into the news business with a new application that could make the US tech giant a key industry player.
5Apple News, part of the upcoming iOS 9 operating system, aims to be the primary news source for users of the iPhone and iPad.
6In a surprising move, Apple has unveiled it will be hiring experienced journalists to manage its news feeds.
7Customers browse Apple Watches in New York.
8This marks a distinction from rivals such as Facebook, which is crafting formulas that aim to deliver articles users want based on their web habits, demographics and interests.
9"Apple is eager to have news created by human beings and not algorithms - it fits in with the brand statement Apple has been making," said Judd Slivka, a professor of mobile journalism at the University of Missouri.
10"The expectation is they will put together a smart team that works well broadly across news and specific content areas."
11Although Apple is likely to use some algorithms to filter stories, the hiring of experienced journalists is a positive step, said Dan Kennedy, a journalism professor at Northeastern University.
12"A lot of people don't want to be fed news that a robot has decided interests them," Professor Kennedy said.
13"Especially if you don't have any say how the robot makes that decision. The Facebook algorithm is highly mysterious, and people are starting to resent that."
14But Professor Kennedy was cautious about technology companies increasingly becoming gatekeepers for news.
15"I'm not crazy about the idea of shifting news to huge corporations like Apple and Facebook that have their own agendas," he said.
16Partner news organisations include Conde Nast magazines, ESPN, The New York Times, Hearst, Time Inc., CNN and Bloomberg, but Apple will be opening to other publishers and bloggers.
