New video surfaces showing Freddie Gray arrest in Baltimore

1A new video has surfaced revealing a key part of the arrest of Freddie Gray, the Baltimore black man whose death from injuries sustained in police custody sparked riots and led to a federal investigation.
2The video, shot by a bystander and posted on the Baltimore Sun's website on Wednesday, shows officers putting Gray in leg shackles and handcuffs before placing him in a police van head first and on his stomach.
3Gray died on April 19 from spinal injuries suffered during his arrest a week earlier.
4His death touched off protests and rioting in the largely black city and heightened national tensions over police treatment of minorities.
5Six officers have been charged in Gray's death, including one with a count of murder.
6The U.S. Justice Department is investigating the Baltimore Police Department for brutality and civil rights violations.
7Officers arrested Gray in west Baltimore for possessing a switchblade knife.
8They put him in a transport van without securing him with a seat belt, Baltimore City State's Attorney Marilyn Mosby said this month in announcing charges against the officers.
9The video lasts a few seconds and shows the van stopped a block away from the arrest site.
10Gray is halfway out of the wagon, his stomach flat on the floor and his legs hanging off the back.
11He does not move as four officers stand over him and place shackles around his ankles.
12Mosby said he also was put in handcuffs.
13She has said that following the stop, Gray sustained a neck injury from being handcuffed, shackled and unrestrained inside the police wagon.
14A city surveillance camera recorded part of the scene but did not show Gray at the back of the van, the Sun reported.
15Police said in a statement on Twitter that because of a technical glitch footage from the camera was never uploaded to the department's YouTube website with other surveillance video in the case.
16Police did not respond for a request for comment about the bystander's video.
17A Mosby spokeswoman had no immediate comment.
18Baltimore has been hit by an upsurge in violence since Gray's death.
19The city had recorded 96 homicides for 2015 by Tuesday, compared with 69 for the same period last year, and added one overnight, according to the police Twitter feed.
20Baltimore has posted 35 murders since Gray's death.
21That count includes Gray and a 2014 death that was ruled a homicide.
