Apple Fine-Tunes Timing on Apple Watch Arrivals

1An Apple Watch could be in your hand – or, rather, on your wrist – sooner than anticipated.
2Apple has consistently been telling buyers of its new smartwatch, which officially launches Friday, that they may need to wait to receive their device.
3But the company has ever-so-slightly changed its tune.
4We’re happy to be updating many customers today with the news that their Apple Watch will arrive sooner than expected.Our team is working to fill orders as quickly as possible based on the available supply and the order in which they were received.We know many customers are still facing long lead times and we appreciate their patience.
5We’re happy to be updating many customers today with the news that their Apple Watch will arrive sooner than expected.
6Our team is working to fill orders as quickly as possible based on the available supply and the order in which they were received.
7We know many customers are still facing long lead times and we appreciate their patience.
8Several readers of 9to5Mac say their order status has changed from “processing” to “preparing for shipment.” Some customers are also starting to see credit-card charges for their Apple Watch orders, which would only happen if the devices were ready to ship.
9Some may receive them as soon as Friday, according to MacRumors.
10A notice sent to some customers who preordered indicates an arrival that day.
11At its retail stores, the company requires you to make a reservation to order the watch, which then arrives at some point at the store or at your home.
12Apple has also been urging people to order the wearable over the Internet through the online Apple Store or the Apple Store app.
13The wait times listed online for most of the models now show a June time frame for delivery.
14But what if you haven’t preordered the Apple Watch and are now eager to get one?
15Apple has stated that the device won’t be available for immediate purchase at its Apple Stores.
16But an Apple Store isn’t your only option.
17The watch is also being sold at boutiques and fashion outlets.
18And you might just be able to walk out with an Apple Watch from one of these retailers.
19Such stores as Dover Street Market in Tokyo and London (but not the one in New York), Maxfield in Los Angeles, Colette in Paris, Corner in Berlin and 10 Corso Como in Milan all list the Apple Watch as “in-store 4.24.15” on their home pages, The New York Times reported Wednesday.
20But whether you can walk out of one of those stores with the smartwatch on your wrist depends on which model you choose.
21The Apple Watch, the company’s long-awaited entrant in the market for smart wearables, comes in three versions: the entry-level Sport version that starts at $349, the midlevel Apple Watch starting at $549 and the Apple Watch Edition with a starting price of $10,000.
22Adrian Joffe, chief executive of Dover Street Market, told the Times that his company would have around 350 Apple Watches in its Ginza store and 570 in its London store.
23But those of you willing to shell out the money for the gold Edition are out of luck – that version is currently on backorder at Dover Street Market.
