1Will Apple Inc. be able to win over cable customers and cord cutters?
2Stay tuned for a very special episode to debut this fall.
3Apple is in talks with networks, including ABC, CBS and Fox, to offer a bare-bones bundle of TV programming on iOS devices, according to the Wall Street Journal, citing sources close to the deal.
4The company will announce its new service in June and launch in September.
5While ABC, CBS and Fox are already in talks, discussions don't include NBCUniversal, for now.
6In addition to its broadcast network, NBCUniversal also oversees channels like USA and Bravo.
7Why would NBC hold out?
8According to the Journal's sources, a recent fallout between Apple and NBCUniversal may be the reason.
9Apple was working on a streaming platform last year with Comcast, NBCUniversal's parent company.
10Apple backed out of the partnership once it realized Comcast might be stalling while the cable company developed its own set-top box.
11It's hard to imagine Apple would launch without NBC, or that NBC would allow other networks to gain an edge while it pouts over the past.
12However, Dish Network recently launched Sling TV without Fox, NBC or Sony content.
13One of the primary reasons customers stick with cable is for the live action of NFL, NBA, MLB and college sports.
14The high programming fees for live events have made sports almost impossible to get online without a cable or satellite subscription.
15Apple is already talking to Disney about putting ESPN on its basic bundle.
16In addition, the networks also carry a significant number of sporting events.
