3The death toll is in the thousands following a 7.8 magnitude earthquake in Nepal.
4The quake also sparked an avalanche at Mount Everest.
5Rescue workers continue to search for missing and trapped people.
6The magnitude-7.8 quake, the country's worst in 80 years, packed hospitals in the capital of Katmandu and triggered avalanches in the Himalayas.
7At least 10 people were killed on Mount Everest.
8Tourism is a major source of revenue for the largely agricultural nation, which is one of the world's poorest.
9Visitors from around the world, particularly India and China, flock to its Buddhist temples, majestic mountain retreats and for expert climbers, the challenge of scaling Everest.

28But a rash of deaths in the nation's mountains threaten to check the industry's growth.
29In October, a series of snowstorms and avalanches contributed to the worst hiking disaster in the nation's story.
30About 40 people were killed on or near the Annapurna Circuit in northern Nepal, including international trekkers and their guides.
31An avalanche last April on Everest killed 16 Sherpa guides, the highest death toll ever for an avalanche on the world's tallest peak.
32The guides protested the government's lack of protection for them by refusing to work, canceling the climbing season.
33Hundreds of climbers make summit attempts on Everest each year.
34In 2013, tourist arrivals in Nepal fell slightly after four years of growth, according to the government.
35But visitors have continued to spend more each year, and foreign exchange earnings from tourism make up roughly 20% of the country's goods and services exports.
36Saturday's quake set off a scramble by would-be visitors to cancel plans.
37"We have received calls to cancel almost 90% of close to 1,000 bookings to Nepal," said Sharat Dhall, president of online travel agent Yatra, told India's Business Standard.
38Longer term, tourism to the country will depend on how widespread the damage is and the speed of recovery and rebuilding, said Henry Harteveldt, travel analyst and co-founder of Atmosphere Research.
39"Short term, there will undoubtedly be some kind of decline."
