26In this July 12 image made from video, Yahya, a 14-year-old from the Yazidi religious minority, talks about life in an Islamic State training camp, during an interview from a camp for internally displaced people outside Dohuk in the northern Kurdish region of Iraq.
27He explained how more than 120 children inside the camp had to line up and they were each given a doll and a sword, waiting for their next lesson by their Islamic State group instructors: Behead the doll.
28The children had all been shown videos of beheadings and told by their trainers with the Islamic State group that they would perform one someday.
29First, they had to practice technique.
30The more than 120 boys were each given a doll and a sword and told, cut off its head.
31A 14-year-old who was among the boys, all abducted from Iraq's Yazidi religious minority, said he couldn't cut it right.
32He chopped once, twice, three times.
33"Then they taught me how to hold the sword, and they told me how to hit. They told me it was the head of the infidels," the boy, renamed Yahya by his IS captors, told The Associated Press last week in northern Iraq, where he fled after escaping the IS training camp.
34When Islamic State extremists overran Yazidi towns in northern Iraq last year, they butchered older men and enslaved many of the women and girls.
35Dozens of young Yazidi boys like Yahya had a different fate: The IS sought to re-educate them.
36They forced them to convert to Islam from their ancient faith and tried to turn them into jihadi fighters.
37It is part of a concerted effort by the extremists to build a new generation of militants, according to AP interviews with residents who fled or still live under IS in Syria and Iraq.
38The group is recruiting teens and children using gifts, threats and brainwashing.
39Boys have been turned into killers and suicide bombers.
40An IS video issued last week showed a boy beheading a Syrian soldier under an adult militant's supervision.
41Last month, a video showed 25 children unflinchingly shooting 25 captured Syrian soldiers in the head.
42In schools and mosques, militants infuse children with extremist doctrine, often turning them against their own parents.
43Fighters in the street befriend children with toys.
44IS training camps churn out the Ashbal, Arabic for "lion cubs," child fighters for the "caliphate" that IS declared across its territory.
45The caliphate is an historic form of Islamic rule that the group claims to be reviving with its own radical interpretation, though the vast majority of Muslims reject its claims.
46"I am terribly worried about future generations," said Abu Hafs Naqshabandi, a Syrian sheikh who runs religion classes for refugees in the Turkish city of Sanliurfa to counter IS ideology.
47The indoctrination mainly targets Sunni Muslim children.
48In IS-held towns, militants show young people videos at street booths.
49They hold outdoor events for children, distributing soft drinks and candy - and propaganda.
50They tell adults, "We have given up on you, we care about the new generation," said an anti-IS activist who fled the Syrian city of Raqqa, the extremists' de facto capital.
51He spoke on condition of anonymity to preserve the safety of relatives under IS rule.
52With the Yazidis, whom IS considers heretics ripe for slaughter, the group sought to take another community's youth, erase their past and replace it with radicalism.
53Yahya, his little brother, their mother and hundreds of Yazidis were captured when IS seized the Iraqi town of Sulagh in August.
54They were taken to Raqqa, where the brothers and other Yazidi boys aged 8 to 15 were put in the Farouq training camp.
55They were given Muslim Arabic names to replace their Kurdish names.
56Yahya asked that AP not use his real name for his and his family's safety.
57He spent nearly five months there, training eight to 10 hours a day, including exercises, weapons drills and Quranic studies.
58They told him Yazidis are "dirty" and should be killed, he said.
59They showed him how to shoot someone from close range.
60The boys hit each other in some exercises.
61Yahya punched his 10-year-old brother, knocking out a tooth.
62The trainer "said if I didn't do it, he'd shoot me," Yahya said.
63"They | told us it would make us tougher. They beat us everywhere."
64In an IS video of Farouq camp, boys in camouflage do calisthenics and shout slogans.
65An IS fighter says the boys have studied jihad so "in the coming days God Almighty can put them in the front lines to battle the infidels."
66Videos from other camps show boys crawling under barbed wire and practicing shooting.
67One kid lies on the ground and fires a machine gun; he's so small the recoil bounces his whole body back a few inches.
68Boys undergoing endurance training stand unmoving as a trainer hits their heads with a pole.
69IS claims to have hundreds of such camps.
70The Syrian Observatory for Human Rights documented at least 1,100 Syrian children under 16 who joined IS this year.
71At least 52 were killed in fighting, including eight suicide bombers, it said.
72Yahya escaped in early March.
73Fighters left the camp to carry out an attack, and as remaining guards slept he and his brother slipped away, he said.
74He urged a friend to come too, but he refused, saying he was a Muslim now and liked Islam.
75Yahya's mother was in a house nearby with other abducted Yazidis - he had occasionally been allowed to visit her.
76So he and his brother went there.
77They travelled to the Syrian city of Minbaj and stayed with a Russian IS fighter, Yahya said.
78He contacted an uncle in Iraq, who negotiated to pay the Russian for the two boys and their mother.
79A deal struck, they met the uncle in Turkey then went to the Iraqi Kurdish city of Dohuk.
80Now in Dohuk, Yahya and his brother spend much of their time watching TV.
81They appear outgoing and social.
82But traces of their ordeal show.
83When his uncle handed Yahya a pistol, the boy deftly assembled and loaded it.
84And he will never forget the videos of beheadings IS trainers showed the boys.
85"I was scared when I saw that," he said.
86"I knew I wouldn't be able to behead someone like that. Even as an adult."
87Jannsen reported from Dohuk, Iraq.
