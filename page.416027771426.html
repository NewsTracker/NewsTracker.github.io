Kenya launches airstrikes against Islamic militants in Somalia

1Kenya launched airstrikes against Islamic militants in Somalia following an extremist attack on a Kenyan college that killed 148 people, a military spokesman said Monday.
2Warplanes attacked positions of the al-Shabab militant group Sunday afternoon and early Monday morning, said Col.
3David Obonyo of the Kenyan military.

9Al-Shabab, which is based in Somalia, claimed responsibility for the college attack Thursday in the Kenyan town of Garissa.
10Four gunmen died in the assault.
11The airstrikes occurred in the Gedo region of Somalia, Obonyo said.
12"This is part of continuing operations, not just in response to Garissa," he said.
13Kenyan President Uhuru Kenyatta had vowed harsh measures against al-Shabab following the slaughter at Garissa, which is near the border with Somalia.
14Kenya has troops in Somalia as part of an African Union force to attack al-Shabab and shore up the beleaguered Somali government.
15Kenya has carried out air strikes before.
16Al-Shabab said it attacked students at Garissa College University as a reprisal for Kenya sending troops into Somalia.
