Calbuco Volcano Erupts in Chile, and Nearby Town Evacuated

1The Calbuco volcano erupted Wednesday for the first time in more than 42 years, and Chilean authorities ordered the evacuation of the few thousand people living in a town a few miles away.
2The National Mining and Geology Service issued a high alert, barring access to the mountainous area around the volcano in southern Chile.
3The area is sparsely populated, with only a few small communities.
4The town of Ensenada was being evacuated as a precaution.
5Calbuco last erupted in 1972 and is considered one of the top three most potentially dangerous among Chile's 90 active volcanos.
