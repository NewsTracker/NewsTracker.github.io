Chad forces arrest Boko Haram leader, hold 74 others

1Chad's chief prosecutor says that security forces have arrested one of the leaders of the Nigerian extremist group Boko Haram.
2Prosecutor Alhassim Khamis said in a statement Monday that Bahna Fanaye, whose alias is Mahamat Moustapha, was apprehended Sunday, along with two others.
3He said Fanaye coordinated the trafficking of weapons between Nigeria, Cameroon and Chad for the radical Islamic network.
4Khamis said forces seized various weapons, communication materials, documents and more than 50 SIM cards from Fanaye's home.
5He said a man described as the financier of the group was also arrested in another operation.
6Khamis says that security forces have now detained 74 suspects.
7Chad has been a major military ally with Nigeria in the fight against Boko Haram, which has publicly threatened the Chadian president with retaliation.
