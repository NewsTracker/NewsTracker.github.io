Iraqi Troops Retake Some Ramadi Districts From Islamic State

1Smoke rises from the Haouz neighborhood during clashes with Islamic State group militants, in central Ramadi, 70 miles (115 kilometers) west of Baghdad, Iraq, Monday, April 20, 2015.
2Iraq security forces have recaptured areas lost earlier to the Islamic State group in and around the battleground city of Ramadi, security officials said Tuesday.
3According to police Maj.
4Omar al-Alawni, government forces regained control of the city's Pediatric and Maternity Hospital and the surrounding neighborhood late Monday night after fierce clashes with IS militants.
5The hospital is located about 500 meters (yards) from a complex of government offices.
6On Tuesday, Iraqi troops were engaged in intense clashes in an offensive to regain control of Soufiya, one of three villages that fell into the hands of the Islamic State group last week, said police Col.
7Mahdi Abbas.
8Both officials said the battles turned in favor of government forces after the arrival of reinforcements and weapons from Baghdad.
9At least 12 militants were killed in the clashes overnight, they said.
10Footage obtained by The Associated Press showed military black Humvees advancing in a residential area in Ramadi and Iraqi soldiers firing their rifles while taking shelter behind a wall.
11The security situation in Ramadi, the capital of Anbar province, sharply deteriorated after IS seized Soufiya and the two other villages, Sjariyah and Albu-Ghanim, forcing thousands of civilians to flee their homes.
12Elsewhere in Iraq, police said a bomb exploded Tuesday in a commercial street in the town of Madain, just south of Baghdad, killing three people and wounding four.
13Later in the day, a roadside bomb hit a police patrol in the capital's western suburbs, killing two policemen and also wounding four.
