Source: Mullin to replace Lavin at St. John's (Yahoo Sports)

1NEW YORK (AP) -- St. John's has decided its basketball future rests with the best of its past.
2Chris Mullin, St. John's all-time leading scorer and still the face of its basketball program three decades after his career ended, has agreed to coach the Red Storm, a person with knowledge of the discussions told The Associated Press on Monday.
3The person spoke on condition of anonymity because there was no formal announcement.
4Mullin, who led St. John's to the Final Four in 1985, has never coached at any level.
5He replaces Steve Lavin who agreed to leave last week, after five seasons during which the Red Storm reached the NCAA Tournament twice.
6Lavin had an 81-55 record at St. John's and the Red Storm went to the NCAA Tournament in his first and last seasons.
7His teams compiled a 2-9 postseason record in the Big East Tournament, NCAA and NIT.
8Lavin had one year left on his original six-year contract.
9Mullin, a New York native, was a five-time All-Star with Golden State, a member of the USA's gold-medal winning ''Dream Team'' in 1992 and was inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame in 2011.
10He was presented for induction by his coach at St. John's, Hall of Famer Lou Carnesecca.
11''This is a great day for St. John's, the whole university,'' Carnesecca said Monday.
12''He has always represented St. John's well and I'm sure he'll do a fine job as coach.
13It was during Mullin's years at St. John's that the program enjoyed its greatest days.
14Mullin and Georgetown's Patrick Ewing were the key parts of the early domination of the Big East Conference.
15In 1985 the Big East had three Final Four teams, St. John's, Georgetown and national champion Villanova.
16The Redmen and Hoyas were both ranked No.
171 during the season.
18Mullin and his sweet left-handed jumper became part of the lore of New York City basketball.
