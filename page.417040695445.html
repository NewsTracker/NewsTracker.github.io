Key events in Egypt since the 2011 uprising

1- Feb. 11, 2011: Autocrat Hosni Mubarak steps down after 18 days of nationwide protests against his nearly 30-year rule.
2The military takes over, dissolving parliament and suspending the constitution after the uprising leaves hundreds of protesters dead in clashes with security forces.
3- Nov. 28, 2011 - Feb. 15, 2012: The Muslim Brotherhood wins nearly half the seats in multi-stage elections for the first post-Mubarak parliament, while ultraconservative Salafi Islamists take another quarter.
4The remainder goes to liberal, independent and secular politicians.
5- June 18, 2012: The Muslim Brotherhood's candidate Mohammed Morsi defeats Ahmed Shafiq, Mubarak's last prime minister, with 51.7 percent of the vote in a runoff, taking office on June 30 as Egypt's first freely elected president.
6- Aug. 12, 2012: Morsi removes the defense minister and military chief, Field Marshal Hussein Tantawi, and replaces him with Lt. Gen. Abdel-Fattah el-Sissi.
7- Nov. 22, 2012: Morsi unilaterally decrees greater powers for himself, giving his decisions immunity from judicial review and barring the courts from dissolving a panel charged with drafting a new constitution.
8The move sparks days of protests.
9- Dec. 15 - Dec. 22, 2012: Egyptians approve a constitution drafted and hastily passed by Islamists amid protests and walkouts by other groups, with 63.8 percent voting in favor but a low turnout of 32.9 percent.
10- March 12, 2013: Egypt rejects an offer of a $750 million rescue loan from the International Monetary Fund.
11In the coming months, fuel and electricity shortages stoke discontent, while a campaign called Tamarod, or "Rebel," gathers signatures calling for Morsi's removal and early presidential elections.
12- June 30, 2013: On Morsi's anniversary in office, millions of Egyptians begin days of demonstrations demanding his resignation.
13The military gives him 48 hours to reach an agreement with his opponents, but he vows to remain in office.
14- July 3, 2013: El-Sissi announces Morsi's removal, installing Constitutional Court Chief Justice Adly Mansour as interim president.
15- July 8, 2013: Egyptian soldiers fire on Morsi supporters protesting outside a military facility in Cairo, killing over 50.
16Each side blames the other for the violence.
17Mansour sets a timeline for amending the constitution and electing a new president and parliament by mid-February.
18The Brotherhood boycotts the process.
19- Aug. 14, 2013: More than 600 people, mostly Morsi supporters, are killed when police clear two pro-Morsi sit-ins in Cairo.
20Islamists retaliate by torching government buildings, churches and police stations.
21Hundreds more die in subsequent violence.
22- Aug. 19, 2013: Suspected Islamic militants kill 25 policemen in the Sinai Peninsula.
23Militant attacks escalate in Sinai over the following months, with shootings, bombings and suicide attacks against security officials and troops.

2523, 2013: An Egyptian court orders the Brotherhood banned and its assets confiscated.
26- Oct. 9, 2013: The U.S. suspends delivery of tanks, helicopters and fighter jets to Egypt in a show of disapproval over the anti-Brotherhood crackdown.
27- Nov. 4, 2013: Morsi appears for the first time since his ouster at the opening of his trial on charges of inciting violence, the first of several court cases against him.
28Some charges against him carry the death penalty.
29- Dec. 25, 2013: The government designates the Muslim Brotherhood a terrorist organization.
30- Jan. 14, 2014: Egyptians vote in favor of amendments to the constitution adopted under Morsi.
31The referendum sees 98.1 percent of voters approve the measure.
32Turnout is less than 39 percent.
33- Jan. 25, 2014: Clashes between security forces and protesters on the third anniversary of the 2011 uprising kill least 49 people.
34- March 24, 2014: An Egyptian court sentences to death nearly 530 suspected Morsi supporters over a deadly attack on a police station, capping a swift, two-day mass trial in which defense attorneys were not allowed to present their case.
35The death sentences are reduced to 37 after review.
36- March 26, 2014: Ending months of anticipation, el-Sissi announces that he has resigned from the military and will run for president.
37- April 28, 2014: An Egyptian court sentences to death the Muslim Brotherhood's spiritual leader and 682 other people over violence and the killing of policemen.
38The verdict, which can be appealed, sparks an international outcry.
39- May 3, 2014: A three-week presidential campaign period begins.
40El-Sissi doesn't appear in public rallies, instead holding meetings and giving pre-recorded television interviews.
41His rival, leftist politician Hamdeen Sabahi, rallies around Egypt and has support from some youth groups.
42- May 26 - 28, 2014: Egyptians vote in a presidential election, with el-Sissi widely expected to win by a landslide.
43- June 3, 2014: Egypt's Election Commission officially announces that el-Sissi has won with 96.9 percent of the vote.
44Sabahi receives 775,000 votes, fewer than the 1.4 million invalid ballots cast.
45Turnout was 47.45 percent.
46- June 8, 2014: El-Sissi is sworn in for a four-year term.
47- Nov. 10, 2014: A Sinai-based jihadi organization called Ansar Beit al-Maqdis pledges allegiance to the Islamic State group, giving the organization a foothold in Egypt.
48-Nov. 30, 2014: A judge dismisses murder charges against Mubarak and acquits his security chief over the killing of protesters during the 2011 uprising.
49-March 3, 2015: An Egyptian court suspends the country's parliamentary elections, following an earlier Supreme Constitutional Court ruling deeming laws regulating the vote unconstitutional.
50The vote is the final phase in the transition process.
51-March 13-15: Egypt hosts an international investor conference that sees billions pledged to boost the battered economy.
52-April 21: In the first verdict issued against the country's first freely elected leader, an Egyptian court sentences Morsi to 20 years in prison on charges linked to the killing of protesters in 2012.
53-May 9: A Cairo court sentences Mubarak and his two sons to three years in prison on corruption charges during a retrial.
54-May 16: An Egyptian court sentences Morsi and more than 100 others to death over a mass prison break during the 2011 uprising.
55The case has been referred to the country's top religious scholar for a non-binding opinion.
