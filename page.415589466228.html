3CANBERRA, Australia (AP) - Australia's gaffe-prone prime minister publicly apologized Friday for comparing the opposition party leader to German World War II-era propaganda minister Joseph Goebbels - the second Nazi-related comment he has withdrawn in a month.
4Tony Abbott told Parliament on Thursday that Labor Party leader Bill Shorten was "the Dr. Goebbels of economic policy."
5Abbott immediately said he withdrew the comment, as opposition lawmakers yelled in protest.
6Abbott said in a radio interview on Friday that the remark "shouldn't have been said" and he apologized for it.
7"I accept that in the context of history and the way things developed, that was an over-the-top remark," Abbott told Australian Broadcasting Corp. broadcaster Jon Faine, who is Jewish.
8"All of us from time to time in the heat of debate - and you know how heated the Parliament can get - sometimes go too far. I accept that," Abbott said.
9But Abbott added that at least 11 lawmakers from the Labor Party had made similar references in the past, including Mark Dreyfus - one of only three Jewish lawmakers in Parliament.
10On Thursday, Speaker Bronwyn Bishop ordered Dreyfus out of the House of Representatives for rising from his seat to angrily berate the prime minister.
11Fellow Labor lawmaker Michael Danby, also Jewish, left the chamber with Dreyfus in solidarity.
12"There are no Nazis here and we shouldn't be making comparisons with the paradigm of the ultimate evil in politics to heighten political differences," Danby told The Associated Press later.
13"It's beneath him, and it goes to the question of his judgment. I think a lot of his backbench will be groaning and tearing their hair out," Danby added.
14Dreyfus, who in 2011 described an Abbott political campaign in a newspaper opinion piece as "Goebbellian," described Abbott's reference to the Nazi as inappropriate for a prime minister.
15Assistant Treasurer Josh Frydenberg, a Jewish member of Abbott's conservative Liberal Party, did not respond to a request for comment.
16Abbott last month apologized to Parliament for describing a 10 percent reduction in defense industry jobs under a former Labor government as a "holocaust of jobs."

18But he referred AP to the council's longstanding policy statement that it: "Deplores the inappropriate use of analogies to the Nazi genocide in Australian public debate."
