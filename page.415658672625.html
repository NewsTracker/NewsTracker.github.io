Cyclone Nathan to intensify off NT coast

1The entire population of a remote island in Cyclone Nathan's path has been evacuated as the storm makes its third landfall within two weeks.
2The long-lived weather system, which has already made landfall on Cape York and northeast Arnhem Land since it formed in the Coral Sea on March 10, is expected to intensify once again from a category 2 to 3 overnight before making landfall near Goulburn Island, 300km east of Darwin.
3A state of emergency has been declared over the island, and all 427 residents were flown to Darwin on Monday on 51 flights before gale force winds picked up.
4It's the second time in one month that the island has been emptied, since Cyclone Lam hit the Northern Territory on February 20, because there is no confidence that any buildings on the island could withstand a category 3 storm.
5Bureau of Meteorology Regional Director Todd Smith said Nathan was nearing a record-breaking life span.
6"Its longevity and the number of communities it has affected - because it's really rolled along that north coast - is pretty remarkable," he told AAP.
7The storm is expected to pass directly over Goulburn Island at about 4am, and then track inland to Jabiru in Kakadu.
8"As it moves over land we're expecting it to weaken very quickly, but gale force winds could extend inland as the system decays," Mr Smith said.
9A cyclone warning covers Cape Don to Milingimbi, including Croker Island, Goulburn Island, Gunbalanya, Jabiru and Maningrida, and residents are warned to proceed immediately to shelters if their homes are not safe.
10Goulburn Island residents arriving at Darwin Airport on Monday morning were exhausted after being awoken by the emergency evacuation siren at 4am.
11"We're worried now because it's going to be a lot closer," said resident Delilah Ngarlingarli.
