Fears for Syria-bound London schoolgirls suspected of trying to join Islamic State

1CBSN's Nancy Cordes and Elaine Quijano show us the amazing video.
1London: Three schoolgirls have fled the UK in a suspected bid to travel to Syria and join the Islamic State group.
2Shamima Begum, 15, Kadiza Sultana, 16, and an un-named 15-year-old, all from east London, flew to Istanbul, in Turkey, from Gatwick airport on Tuesday without leaving any messages behind for family or friends.
3Scotland Yard detectives urgently appealed for information about the missing girls, who all go to the Bethnal Green Academy school and were described as "straight-A students".
4Commander Richard Walton, head of the Metropolitan Police's counter terror command, said the runaways were good friends with another 15-year-old girl who fled to Syria in December.
5He said the force was becoming "increasingly concerned" about a growing trend of young girls showing an interest or intent in joining IS, an organisation now notorious for its barbaric treatment of hostages and oppression of women.
6Walton added the teenagers' families were "devastated" but there was a "good chance" the girls were still in Turkey.
