4The president of Vanuatu has described the devastating cyclone that blasted through the South Pacific island chain as "a monster" that killed at least 24 people and left 3,300 displaced, according to the United Nations.
5Cyclone Pam, possibly the worst cyclone in the Pacific's history, slammed into Vanuatu late Friday, with wind speeds of more than 165 mph, making it a Category 5 storm.
6At its peak, Pam's 165 mph winds made it one of only ten Category 5 storms on record in the waters east of Australia, according to the Weather Underground.
7It's also only the second Cat 5 in recorded history to make landfall on a populated island in that area.
8Thousands have been left homeless, and 90% of the buildings in the capital were destroyed or damaged, President Baldwin Lonsdale said.
9Lonsdale appealed for international aid and told the AP: "This is a very devastating cyclone in Vanuatu. I term it as a monster, a monster.
10"It's a setback for the government and for the people of Vanuatu. After all the development that has taken place, all this development has been wiped out."
