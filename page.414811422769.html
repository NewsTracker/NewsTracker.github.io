Asia stocks mostly rise, gains tempered by Greek standoff

1BEIJING (AP) - Asian stock markets were mostly higher Tuesday but gains were tempered after Greek debt talks broke down, raising the prospect Athens might leave the euro currency.
2KEEPING SCORE: Tokyo's Nikkei 225 fell 0.2 percent to 17,958.92 while the Shanghai Composite Index gained 0.8 percent to 3,249.24.
3Hong Kong's Hang Seng added 0.4 percent to 24,830.08 and Seoul's Kospi was up 0.2 percent at 1,961.69.
4Singapore, Sydney and New Zealand declined while Jakarta and the Philippines gained.
5On Monday, Germany's DAX fell 0.4 percent.
6France's CAC 40 and Britain's FTSE 100 both shed 0.2 percent.
7Wall Street was closed for a holiday.
8GREEK JITTERS: Greece's European creditors told it to ask for an extension to its existing bailout program before further talks on its financing can take place.
9After five years of punishing austerity, Greece is seeking more generous terms for paying off its huge debt.
10The ultimatum came after a meeting of the finance ministers of the 19 euro nations ended in seeming acrimony after just three hours.
11If no agreement is reached by the end of the month, financial markets think Greece might have little option but to default and stop using the euro currency.
12THE QUOTE: "Europe will remain an overarching risk story for investors," said IG strategist Evan Lucas in a commentary.
13"Russia and China are watching this situation very closely and both would be in Athens very quickly to discuss how they can assist the Greeks. In the more likely scenario of Greece remaining in the EU, the question remains of whether the ECB would provide additional support, looking to provide stimulus rather than forcing further austerity."
14ENERGY: U.S. benchmark crude was up 25 cents to $53.03 per barrel in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
15In the previous session, the contract gained $1.57 to close at $52.78.
16CURRENCIES: The dollar rose to 118.40 yen from 118.27 yen on Monday.
17The euro rose to $1.1339 from $1.1335.
