Greek banks to reopen Monday but cash restrictions remain

1Greek banks are reopening Monday after a forced 3-week closure but restrictions on cash withdrawals will remain.
2In a decree Saturday, the Greek government kept the daily cash withdrawal limit at 60 euros ($65) but added a weekly limit.
3For example, a depositor who doesn't withdraw cash on Monday can withdraw 120 euros ($130) on Tuesday, and so on, up to 420 euros ($455) a week.
4Bank customers will still not be able to cash checks, only deposit them into their accounts, and will not be able to get cash abroad with their credit or cash cards, only make purchases.
5There are also restrictions on opening new accounts or activating dormant ones.
6The decree also pushes back by a month, to August 26, the deadline for filing income tax returns.
7The degree came on the same day as Greece's coalition government swore in its new, reshuffled cabinet.
8Five prominent dissidents from the radical left Syriza party, the senior coalition party, were replaced.
9Four of them had voted against an agreement with Greece's creditors Thursday and the fifth had resigned before the vote.
10Greece closed its banks beginning June 29 to prevent a bank run after the European Central Bank did not increase emergency funding as Greece's second bailout expired.
11After the Greek Parliament passed an agreement Thursday to seek a third bailout and related austerity measures demanded by creditors, the ECB raised its emergency funding to the cash-strapped Greek banks.
12On Friday, German lawmakers voted 439-119 in favor of opening discussions on Greece's third bailout and the EU decided to release a short-term loan of 7.16 billion ($7.75 billion) to help Greece pay back a loan due Monday to the ECB.
13The Greek Parliament will vote on further austerity measures Wednesday.
