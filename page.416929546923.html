Magnitude 7.4 earthquake strikes in Nepal

1A second powerful earthquake in less than three weeks spread panic in Nepal on Tuesday, bringing down buildings weakened by the first disaster and killing at least 66 people, including 17 in neighbouring India and one in Chinese Tibet.
2Every public space in Kathmandu has become a makeshift city for the homeless after the country's second earthquake in less than three weeks.
3A 7.3 magnitude quake hit 76km east of Kathmandu around 12:35pm local time on Tuesday.
4The quake, which has so far killed around 65 people, came after a 7.8 magnitude earthquake on April 25 devastated the country and killed more than 8100 people.
5Tremors from the most recent quake were felt as far away as New Delhi.
6Seventeen of the dead were found in northern India.
7Adam Cathro from Plan International Australia was at his office desk in Kathmandu when he experienced a sudden jolt "as if someone had slammed the brakes on in a car".
8Speaking to AAP between aftershocks on Wednesday morning, Mr Cathro said he and colleagues ran to a courtyard and watched as their building was "rattled and swayed" by the quake.
9After accounting for the safety of his colleagues, his mind turned to the Nepalese people already traumatised by the April disaster.
10He said many people spent the nights on the streets and shelter is needed before the monsoon season arrives.
11"Every public space, every public park we saw was an impromptu tent city (on Tuesday night)" he said.
12"Monsoon season is coming in about two weeks and already we're seeing heavy rain."
13"Shelter was the most urgent need before and now it is even more important."
14Oxfam Australia CEO Helen Szoke has called the second quake "the worst thing" that could have happened in the midst of Nepal's recovery.
15She also said there have been reports of land slides, which could make getting essential services to rural areas difficult.
16Plan International is a child-focused NGO working in 51 developing nations.
17Mr Cathro said the organisation was deeply concerned for the mental health of children.
18"For the younger children its very difficult for them to understand what is going on," he said.
19"For older children, their parents are stressed and anxious and they're stressed and anxious."

23A 7.3 magnitude earthquake killed at least 37 people and spread panic in Nepal, bringing down buildings already weakened by a devastating tremor less than three weeks ago and unleashing landslides in Himalayan valleys near Mount Everest.
24Most of the reported fatalities were in villages to the east of Kathmandu, only just beginning to pick up the pieces after the April 25 quake that left more than 8,000 people dead.
25The new earthquake was centred 76 km (47 miles) east of the capital in a hilly area close to the border with Tibet, according to coordinates provided by the U.S. Geological Survey.
26Aid workers reported serious damage to some villages seen from the air and witnesses reported seeing rocks and mud crashing down remote hillsides lined with roads and small hamlets.
27Politicians dashed for the exit of Nepal's parliament building and office towers swayed as far away as central New Delhi.
28The tremors could be felt in Bangladesh and were followed by a series of powerful aftershocks.
29Terrified Kathmandu residents crammed into public spaces, too nervous to venture inside.
30"I am very scared and I am with my two sons.
31The school building is cracked and bits of it, I can see they have collapsed," said Rhita Doma Sherpa, a nurse with the Mountain Medicine Center in Namche Bazaar, a departure point for trekkers headed to Everest.
32"It was lunchtime. All the kids were outside. Thank god."
33Three students were injured in another town in the Everest region, locals said.
34Residents in the Indian town of Siliguri, near the border with Nepal, said chunks of concrete fell off one or two buildings.
35In Nepal the death toll reached 37, with 1,066 injured, police spokesman Kamal Singh Bam said.
36Five people were killed in Indian states bordering Nepal, according to officials, and Chinese media reported one person died in Tibet after rocks fell on a car.
37Medecins Sans Frontieres emergency coordinator Dan Sermand said the village of Charikot near the epicenter had suffered the worst damage of the three villages the organisation had surveyed by helicopter.
38An Indian Air Force team brought 11 injured people to Kathmandu from the Charikot area.
39Nepal had barely begun to recover from the devastation caused by last month's 7.8-magnitude earthquake, the country's worst in more than 80 years, which killed at least 8,046 people and injured more than 17,800.
40Hundreds of thousands of buildings, including ancient Hindu and Buddhist temples, were destroyed and many more damaged.
41Mountaineers seeking to scale the world's tallest peak have called off this year's Everest season after 18 people died when last month's quake triggered avalanches on the mountain.
42Dambar Parajuli, president of Expedition Operators' Association of Nepal, said there were no climbers or Nepali sherpa guides at Base Camp.
43"All of them have already left," Parajuli said.
44In Lukla, the departure point for treks to Everest, buildings cracked and small landslides were triggered when the ground shook.
45Susana Perez from Madrid was on a 10-day trek with her husband to Island Peak in the Everest region and was about to reach Lukla.
46"We saw the mountain in front of us fall down - earth and rocks. There were some houses underneath but it was not clear if they were hit," Perez said.
47In Kathmandu, people panicked and rushed outdoors when the tremors began at around 12.30, Reuters witnesses said.
48The quake was followed by at least half a dozen aftershocks, including one as big as 6.3 magnitude.
49Parents clutched children tightly, and hundreds of people frantically tried to call relatives on mobile phones.
50Shopkeepers closed their stores and the streets were jammed with people rushing to check on their families.
51Some foreign rescue teams have returned home from Nepal, but may need to be pressed into service again.
52At a welcoming ceremony for an Israeli military rescue delegation on Tuesday, Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu said: "I know that you are already prepared for the next mission, anywhere it might be required.
53" At least four people were killed in Chautara town in Sindhupalchowk district, north of the Nepali capital Kathmandu, a spokesman for the International Organization for Migration said.
54The new temblor also triggered at least three big landslides in the district, which suffered the heaviest death toll in last month's quake.
55A district official there said 12 people had been injured.
56Two Adelaide mothers have travelled to Nepal to set up tents and distribute medical supplies to an orphanage that was heavily damaged in last month's devastating earthquake.
57Lynne Rawson and Jo Bourchier arrived in Kathmandu earlier this week, where they were reunited with Ishwor Ghimire, a Nepali teenager they both hosted as part of a program run by Adelaide's Pulteney Grammar School.
58Ghimire had helped bring more than 50 children to safety during the earthquake, which caused huge cracks in its walls making it too dangerous to live in.
59The children are now forced to sleep outside.
60Ghimire said it has been difficult to keep them warm and healthy living under nothing more than plastic covering.
61"The earthquake's destroyed the lives of people and if people start thinking negative, they will destroy themselves," he said.
62"So this is the time that we really need to work together hand-in-hand and move forward."
63Rawson, who is a mother of four, said she wanted to offer love and support to the children.
64"In a good world, everyone is equal but our world isn't always like that," she said.
65"I think these children are as special as any other child. They are as special as a child in Australia.
66"I think we need to look after every child and they should be treated equally. So, I would like to see that these kids get as much help as anybody else."
67She and her friend Jo Bourchier have vowed to stay and help at the orphanage for at least a month.
68The orphanage is run by Rajan Bishwokarma, known locally as Mother Rajan.
69Ms Bourchier said the orphanage is yet to received any help from the government or other organisations.
70"We're just hoping that we can lighten Mother Rajan's load," she said.
71"She has been under so much strain and I think she felt very alone and because they haven't had any help."
72The earthquake flattened or damaged more than 600,000 houses, with eight million of Nepal's 28 million people were affected by the quake, according to the United Nations.
73At least two million people are expected to be in need of tents, water, food and medicine over the next three months.
74Many countries have pledged money that will be necessary to rebuild homes, hospitals and historic buildings.
75The death toll has reached at least 7,759, and more than 16,400 people were injured.
76Bad weather has cut links with a remote village in Nepal where dozens of villagers and trekkers are believed to be buried under an avalanche set off by last month's devastating earthquake, officials said on Tuesday.
77The death toll from the April 25 earthquake in the Himalayan nation has reached 7,557, the government said.
78About 100 bodies were recovered on Saturday and Sunday at Langtang village, 60 km north of Kathmandu, which is on a trekking route popular with Westerners.
79The entire village, which includes 55 guesthouses for trekkers, was wiped out by the avalanche and rescuers are digging in the snow for signs of about 120 others believed buried.
80Gautam Rimal, assistant chief district officer in the area where Langtang is located, said authorities had not made contact with Langtang for more 24 hours because of bad weather.
81"We hope to send a rescue helicopter today (Tuesday) to the area," he said.
82"We'll know the situation then."
83The dead include at least seven foreigners, but only two had been identified, he said.
84The government has begun asking foreign teams to wrap up search and rescue operations as hopes of finding people alive in the rubble receded.
85"They can leave. If they are also specialists in clearing the rubble, they can stay," Rameshwor Dangal, an official at Nepal's home ministry, told Reuters on Monday.
86A European Union source said only about 60 citizens from the 28-nation bloc were still unaccounted for.
87Last week a senior EU official had estimated around 1,000 EU citizens were missing after the quake.
88The number is "going down by the hour" as rescue teams reach remoter areas, the EU source said.
89A US State Department spokesman said helicopters chartered by the embassy in Kathmandu had rescued 17 US citizens in total from remote areas hit by the quake.
90The United States has provided $14.2 million in humanitarian aid since the quake.
91The government has said it has not closed Mount Everest, the world's highest peak, to climbers, although the route to the summit has been damaged by the earthquake.
92At least 18 people were killed on Everest when the earthquake struck.
93Climbers pay $11,000 each to climb Everest, and 357 were registered for this climbing season.
94Last year, the government extended permits when teams abandoned their expeditions after an avalanche killed 16 Sherpa mountain guides.
95The United Nations has said 8 million of Nepal's 28 million people were affected by the quake, with at least 2 million needing tents, water, food and medicines over the next three months.
96The United Nations Children's Fund said more than half a million children were being vaccinated to prevent measles outbreaks.
97Around 1.7 million children remain in urgent need of humanitarian aid in the worst-hit areas, it added.
98The Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade is deploying a team to search for an Australian missing in Nepal, more than a week after a deadly earthquake rocked the country.
99In a statement issued to SBS, a DFAT spokeswoman said the department was concerned for the welfare of Tyronne White, who is currently unaccounted for in Nepal.
100"Officials in Canberra and Nepal have been providing consular assistance to Mr White's family in Australia," she said.
101"We are deploying a team of Australian officials to travel to Langtang village, Mr White's last known location, today which will supplement the two Australian officials already in the region.
102"We are liaising closely with Nepalese authorities and with officials from other countries, including the US, UK, Canada and Spain, to broaden and coordinate our ongoing efforts to confirm the welfare of Mr White."
103Brett White told SBS he's still waiting to hear from his brother Tyronne, who is believed to be trekking when the quake hit.
104He said in early April, his brother Tyronne told him via a Facebook message that he was setting out on a three-week trek between Kathmandu and the Chinese border.
105"I've tried to call everyone and anyone," he said.
106"I've called them [DFAT] frequently and they say they're working with the Americans and also the British and couple of other countries about handing his information over and they'll try to actually look for him."
107Brett White said he is considering flying over to Nepal but doesn't know if he will be much help.
