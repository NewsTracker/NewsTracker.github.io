Boko Haram suicide bomber kills himself, 4 others at Nigerian bus station

1POTISKUM, Nigeria -- A suicide bomber blew himself up and killed four hawkers Saturday at a bustling bus station in the northeast Nigerian town of Damaturu, witnesses said of the latest in a string of attacks this week by Nigeria's home-grown Boko Haram extremists.
2Five bodies were brought to the hospital mortuary and 15 wounded people are being treated, according to a nurse who spoke on condition of anonymity because he is not allowed to speak to reporters.
3Most victims are women and children from among hawkers who sell smoked fish and packets of water at the exit gate to Damaturu Central Motor Park.
4The attack comes two weeks after Nigeria's military said it destroyed about 20 insurgent camps and freed some 700 kidnapped women and children.
5Officials have said the main fighting force of the insurgents is trapped inside the vast Sambisa Forest.
6But on Wednesday night, hundreds of militants tried to attack the biggest military base in northeastern Nigeria, but were repelled by fierce resistance by troops at Giwa Barracks in Maiduguri, the capital of Borno state and the most populous city in the northeast.
7On Friday, Boko Haram seized back the border town of Marte and hoisted its black and white flag, deputy Gov. Zannah Umar Mustapha told reporters.
8Maiduguri is some 200 kilometres northwest of the nearest reaches of the Sambisa Forest, and Marte is even further north.
9A week ago, a gunman and suicide bomber attacked a business school in Potiskum, near Damaturu, killing a student and leaving many injured.
10A multinational offensive of troops from Nigeria and its neighbours had driven the extremists from all northeastern towns in a 14-week-long offensive.
11But the Nigerians appear to have bogged down in the Sambisa Forest, where Boko Haram has laid land mines and booby traps.
