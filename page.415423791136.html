Cyclone Pam hits Vanuatu with 270 km/h winds

1Vanuatu (in red) is a sprawling country of 83 islands and 260,000 people.
2The UN Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs said in a release there were unconfirmed reports of deaths in Vanuatu's northeastern islands after Cyclone Pam moved off its expected track.
3The Category 5 cyclone had average wind speeds of 250 km/h to 270 km/h, with gusts up to 340 km/h, according to the office.
4It said the periphery of the eye of the storm had passed over islands that are home to several thousand people and was expected to hit or come close to the island of Efate, home to the capital, Port Vila.
5Located about a quarter of the way from Australia to Hawaii, Vanuatu has a population of 267,000 spread over 65 islands.
6About 47,000 people live in the capital.
7Vanuatu had earlier issued a red alert to its residents, urging them to take shelter from Pam.
8Authorities said they feared the cyclone would destroy homes as well as cause landslides and severe coastal flooding.
9The cyclone has already destroyed some homes and caused damage to other Pacific islands including Kiribati and the Solomon Islands.
10David Gibson, acting director of the Vanuatu meteorology and geo-hazards department, said the winds could cause severe damage to the nation's buildings.
11Alice Clements, a spokeswoman for relief agency UNICEF who is in Port Vila, said earlier Friday the capital was like a ghost town as people took shelter.
12She said the pelting rain was blown horizontally by the wind.
13She said many people in Vanuatu live in flimsy shelters, especially those on the more remote outer islands.
14She said those people are extremely vulnerable.
15She said she expects a huge cleanup and urgent medical needs after the storm passes.
16Clements also said large seas had caused significant flooding in remote and low-lying Kiribati.
17The Solomon Islands Broadcasting Corporation reported several homes and a school classroom were destroyed and crops were flooded.
18Authorities in New Zealand are preparing for the storm, which is forecast to pass north of the country on Sunday and Monday.
