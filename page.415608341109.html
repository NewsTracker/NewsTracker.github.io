Exclusive - Private equity firms prepare bids for Informatica: sources

R&B singer The Weeknd, in sultry mood, performs during SXSW

1Silver Lake Partners LP has teamed up with Hellman & Friedman LLC ahead of a deadline for final bids for Informatica that is set for early April, the people said on Friday.
2Advent International Corp, Permira Advisors LLP, Thoma Bravo LLC and Canada Pension Plan Investment Board's private equity arm are also vying for Informatica, the people said.
3Some of these four firms could also team up to try to take Informatica private, the people added.
4Informatica has a market capitalization of $4.8 billion.
5Shares in Informatica rose 83 cents or 1.9 percent on the news Friday, hitting a multiyear high of $45.45.
6The sources asked not to be identified because the deliberations are confidential.
7Spokespeople for Informatica and the private equity firms declined to comment.
8The biggest leveraged buyout so far this year has been the agreed acquisition of gym operator Life Time Fitness Inc by buyout firms Leonard Green & Partners LP and TPG Capital LP for more than $4 billion, including debt.
9Redwood City, California-based Informatica helps companies connect software and enterprise applications and store data.
10It competes in the integration software market with Tibco, a company that went private in December in a $4.3 billion sale to buyout firm Vista Equity Partners.
11Activist hedge fund Elliott Management Corp disclosed an 8 percent stake in Informatica on Jan. 26 and said in a regulatory filing it was speaking to the company's management and board about ways to maximize shareholder value.
12A person familiar with the matter said at the time that Elliott may call for Informatica to be sold.
13An auction for Informatica run by investment banking boutique Qatalyst Partners LP was put on hold in early January after interest received from private equity firms failed to lead to a deal, Reuters previously reported.
14A number of well-known technology companies have been targeted by activist investors in recent years as the sector undergoes rapid change and older technology companies sit on large amounts of cash.
15JPMorgan Chase & Co is now advising Informatica, in addition to its previous financial adviser, Qatalyst Partners LP.
16JPMorgan declined to comment while Qataylst could not be reached for comment.
