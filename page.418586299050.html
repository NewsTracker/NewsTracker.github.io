News24.com | Migrants stranded in Athens without water, food

1A man rests at the Omonia square in central Athens.
2Athens - Shading himself from the scorching sun in a busy Athens square was a Syrian refugee named Haisam, one of 800 to 1 000 who ended up in the Greek capital on Thursday, stranded in a recession-hit city not equipped to receive him.
3"We arrived this morning, around 1 000 of us, mostly Syrians. We have no food, water or accommodation. We are desperate," he said, standing on a corner of the central Athens Omonia square with his brother Uiseam and other migrants.
4Global attention has focused on Greece's brush with financial collapse over the past few weeks.
5But meanwhile, one of the other great crises facing Europe has continued out of the spotlight, with a steady stream of migrants arriving in Athens.
6The Greek capital was used to arrivals of about 120 migrants daily.
7On Thursday, the number jumped, and it is expected to keep rising, as a growing number of arrivals reach the eastern islands of Lesbos, Chios and Kos from Turkey, trying to make their way to other European Union countries.
8They arrive in Athens by paying for their fare on passenger ferries, shipping company officials say.
9But the capital has no public infrastructure to help them, city officials said.
10"We can arrange no accommodation or food for them. All they can do is try to get access to the regular help that is provided daily for the homeless people of Athens. And that's not unlimited," a spokesperson from the mayor's office said.
11More than 77 000 people have arrived in Greece by sea so far this year.
12More than 60% of them Syrians, with others fleeing Afghanistan, Iraq, Eritrea and Somalia, the United Nations refugee agency said last week.
13Greece, who has often been criticised by human rights groups for its treatment of migrants and asylum seekers, urgently needs help to cope with 1 000 refugees arriving each day, the UNHCR said.
14It called on the EU to step in before the humanitarian situation deteriorates further.
15"After arriving at [Athens'] Piraeus port this morning, we all came to the centre of Athens, at Omonia square," said Mohammad Talap, a 20-year-old who fled the city of Daraa in Syria.
16Talap said he wants to travel away from recession-hit Greece to EU economic powerhouse Germany.
17"But I have no money to do that. I am hoping my family in Syria will manage to send me some money, but so far I couldn't even contact them as the government is bombing my city."
18If banks re-open in Greece as expected next week, getting additional funds wired from abroad may be easier, at least for those with friends who can help.
