Europol to battle Islamic State social media propaganda

1FILE - In this Jan. 16, 2015 file photo, the head of the European Union's police agency Europol, Rob Wainwright, answers questions during an interview in The Hague, Netherlands.
2Rob Wainwright told The Associated Press on Monday, June 22, 2015 a new unit at Europol will next month start fighting Islamic State propaganda and recruiters in cyberspace in a crackdown on the terror network's social media activities.
3THE HAGUE, Netherlands (AP) - A new unit at the European Union's Europol police agency will start fighting Islamic State group propaganda and recruiters in cyberspace next month in a crackdown on the terror network's sophisticated use of social media.
4Europol Director Rob Wainwright told The Associated Press on Monday the new unit will start July 3 with some 15 experts working closely with social media companies to take down accounts run by IS.
5Wainwright said IS is thought to have tens of thousands of social media accounts churning out propaganda and reaching out to potential recruits.
6He said it is unrealistic to think they all can be taken down.
7But Wainwright believes that combining police investigations from forces across Europe with intelligence gathered by Europol will allow the new unit to target the most influential accounts.
8"That allows us then to get smarter with where we target our resources and try to identify who are the principle drivers and motivators in this ecosystem that ISIS have established," he said.
9EU justice ministers called for the creation of the unit in March in response to the growing number of people recruited and groomed by IS online.
10Europol estimates that 4,000 to 5,000 European citizens have traveled to Syria and Iraq to join the militant group.
11The team will include Arabic speakers and experts with years of experience monitoring Islamic extremist websites.
12He stressed that the team will work closely with social media companies, though he declined to identify them by name.
13"This is not Europol imposing police powers on social media companies, this is us helping them to enforce their own terms of service," he said.
14"The final decision in each case rests entirely with them."
