Arrests in Baltimore following spurts of violence during Freddie Gray march

1At least 2,000 demonstrators attended the march to City Hall, the largest turnout since 25-year-old Freddie Gray died a week ago in a police van.
2A group of around 100 protesters splintered from the main march and threw objects at police officers and their cruisers, authorities said.
3The windows of several businesses were smashed, Police Commissioner Anthony Batts said.
4Police arrested 12 people who ignored orders to disperse, Batts said.
5At least one officer was hurt in the violence that ensued.
6Baltimore Mayor Rawlings-Blake, who has called for answers in Gray's death, said agitators at the Saturday demonstration disrupted the otherwise peaceful political action.
7"I am profoundly disappointed to see the violence in our city this evening," she said.
8Fredericka Gray, Freddie's twin sister, joined Baltimore Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake at the news conference where she urged people to keep calm.
9"Freddie's father and mother do not want violence, violence does not get justice," she said.
10Gray died on April 19 after suffering a spinal injury while in police custody.
11With his death, Gray joined a long list of black men who have died under questionable circumstances during police encounters in recent months.
12The highly publicized incidents have triggered an outcry over the use of force by law enforcement against African-Americans.
13Last year, weeks of protests followed the fatal shooting of unarmed black teen Michael Brown by a white officer in Ferguson, Missouri, and the chokehold death of Eric Garner in New York City.
14Six Baltimore police officers have been suspended in the Gray case, and an internal police investigation is under way.
15Much of the violence occurred near the Camden Yards ballpark, where the Baltimore Orioles played the Boston Red Sox as scheduled.
16Towards the end of the game, fans were told to stay in place because of safety concerns.
17Protesters are calling for the prosecution of the six officers involved in Gray's arrest and a reform of policing tactics.
18The US Justice Department is investigating whether Baltimore police violated a man's civil rights when he died in custody.
19An autopsy has found that Freddie Gray sustained a fatal spinal cord injury.
20The death of an African American man at the hands of the police has raised tensions in the US city of Baltimore.
21Local officials are demanding answers.
