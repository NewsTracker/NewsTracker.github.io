German bund yields climb, lift euro; stocks ease

1NEW YORK, May 12 (Reuters) - German bond yields climbed on Tuesday on optimism that inflation may have bottomed in the euro zone, lifting demand for the euro, while volatility in global bond markets weighed on stock indexes.
2U.S. 10-year Treasury yields hit six-month highs before steadying as some buyers came back to the market, helping the government sell $24 billion in new three-year notes.
3U.S. stocks ended down slightly as the move in bond yields unsettled investors already concerned about an eventual Federal Reserve interest rate hike.
4"In the short term, the market is a hostage to interest rates," said Jim Awad, managing director at Plimsoll Mark Capital.
5"To the extent you have an increase in interest rates that the Fed doesn't control, you're getting an unwanted tightening in the financial markets."
6Oil prices jumped as dollar weakness trumped concerns about oversupply.
7German Bund yields have surged in recent weeks, boosted by optimism that inflation may have bottomed in the euro region, according to some analysts.
8The move has been exacerbated by investors unwilling to enter the market until the sell-off shows signs of stabilizing.
9Benchmark 10-year Treasuries were last up 5/32 in price to yield 2.25 percent, after earlier hitting 2.37 percent.
10The stronger tone helped the Treasury auction three-year notes to strong demand at a 1.00 percent yield.
11Wednesday's $24 billion 10-year note sale and Thursday's $16 billion 30-year bond sale may be more challenging.
12German 10-year Bund yields hit a session high of 0.74 percent on Tuesday.
13Less than a month ago, German 10-year yields hit a record low of 0.05 percent, driven down by a 1-trillion-euro European Central Bank bond-purchase scheme intended to boost inflation.
14MSCI's all-country world index of stock performance in 46 countries was down 0.3 percent.
15The Dow Jones industrial average fell 36.94 points, or 0.2 percent, to 18,068.23, the S&P 500 lost 6.21 points, or 0.29 percent, to 2,099.12 and the Nasdaq Composite dropped 17.38 points, or 0.35 percent, to 4,976.19.
16Elevated U.S. yields mean higher corporate borrowing costs, which could hit shares across the world.
17In corporate takeover news, AOL shares jumped after Verizon Communications said it would buy the company in a $4.4 billion deal.
18The U.S. dollar slumped against the euro for the first time in four trading sessions following the spike in German Bund yields.
19The euro was last up 0.54 percent against the dollar at $1.12155.
20Investor concerns that debt-burdened Greece could run out of cash also weighed on stocks.
21Greek officials told Reuters they had emptied an International Monetary Fund holding account to repay 750 million euros to the global lender on Monday, avoiding default but underscoring the dire state of the country's finances.
22In the energy market, Brent crude rose $1.95 to settle at $66.86 a barrel.
23U.S. crude jumped $1.50 to settle at $60.75.
24Gold rose as the dollar declined.
25Spot gold touched a session high of $1,196.60 an ounce and was up 0.8 percent at $1,193.35.
