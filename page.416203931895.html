800,000 children forced from homes in Boko Haram violence

1LAGOS, Nigeria (AP) - The children's drawings show men with guns, a coffin, a car exploding.
2One picture has stick-like figures of eight siblings missed by their teenage sister.
3The disturbing images come from some of an estimated 800,000 children forced from the homes by Boko Haram extremists, according to a UNICEF report published Monday.
4It says the number of refugee children has doubled in the past year, making them about half of all the 1.5 million Nigerians made homeless in the Islamic uprising.
5"Children have become deliberate targets, often subjected to extreme violence - from sexual abuse and forced marriage to kidnappings and brutal killings," the report says.
6"Children have also become weapons, made to fight alongside armed groups and at times used as human bombs."
7The number of children absent from primary school in Nigeria has increased from 8 million in 2007 to 10.5 million- the highest figure in the world, it says.
8Boko Haram has targeted schools, destroying or severely damaging more than 300 and killing 314 students and 196 teachers, UNICEF says.
9The nickname of Nigeria's home-grown Islamic extremist group, Boko Haram, means "Western education is forbidden" or sinful.
