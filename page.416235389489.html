Artists to NYPD: Return Edward Snowden bust we put in park

1NEW YORK (AP) - The mysterious artists who planted a bust of Edward Snowden on a Revolutionary War monument in a New York City park want the sculpture back.
2The artists' lawyer, Ronald Kuby, sent police a letter Tuesday demanding the return of the fiberglass likeness of the exiled National Security Agency whistleblower.
3Parks officials ordered it removed from the war memorial in Fort Greene Park April 6, hours after it appeared overnight.
4Police have said they're holding it while investigating.
5Police and parks officials haven't immediately responded to inquiries Tuesday about the matter.
6Kuby wouldn't identify his clients, citing the police investigation.
7They say in a statement that returning the statue would honor New York's history as a place for free thinking.
