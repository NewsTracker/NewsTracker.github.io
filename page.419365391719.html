'Empire,' One Direction win at 17th Teen Choice Awards

1Jussie Smollett, from left, Gabourey Sidibe and Bryshere Y. Gray of the "Empire" cast perform at the Teen Choice Awards at the Galen Center on Sunday, Aug. 16, 2015, in Los Angeles.
2Hosts Ludacris, from left, Josh Peck and Gina Rodriguez speak onstage at the Teen Choice Awards at the Galen Center on Sunday, Aug. 16, 2015, in Los Angeles.
3The 17th Teen Choice Awards threw its full support behind the hit series "Empire," honored late "Furious 7" star Paul Walker and paid homage to a respected elder: Britney Spears.
4The two-hour teen celebration, broadcast live Sunday night, kicked off with a victory lap for the weekend box-office hit "Straight Outta Compton."
5Ice Cube and the young stars of the N.W.A biopic, which earned an estimated $56.1 million over the weekend, welcomed the young crowd to Los Angeles' Galen Center.
6Christopher "Ludacris" Bridges, the rapper and "Furious 7" co-star, was one of three hosts, alongside "Jane the Virgin" star Gina Rodriguez and actor Josh Peck.
7Bridges' film, one of year's biggest big-screen hits, was one of the night's top winners, winning best action movie and best actor in an action-adventure for the late Paul Walker.
8"Paul Walker is here in spirit with us," said Vin Diesel, a star in the street racing franchise.
9He applauded a "special, special" teen in the crowd: Walker's daughter Meadow.
10The Teen Choice Awards spread awards across movies, music, TV, fashion, sports and digital media, celebrating the favorites - from Channing Tatum to Wiz Khalifa to Stephen Curry (all winners Sunday) - of one of media's most powerful demographics.
11As a pop star, Spears was a regular at the Teen Choice Awards, collecting its trademark trophies - surfboards - many times over the years, including its version of a lifetime achievement award in 2009.
12On Sunday, Spears, her blonde hair partially dyed blue and purple, was honored for being a style icon.
13She dedicated the award to her sons, Sean Preston and Jayden James, and niece Lexie, who were in the audience.
14The Teen Choice Awards' new reigning powerhouse, One Direction, landed eight awards, bringing their "lifetime" total to 23.
15The British boy band, currently on tour, accepted the awards in a taped video.
16The hip-hop drama "Empire" won breakout TV show and provided one of the show's most memorable moments.
17While Jussie Smollett and Yazz performed "You're So Beautiful," a song from the series, the show's Gabourey Sidibe made a surprise cameo, exuberantly dancing among the back-up dancers.
18Ellen DeGeneres won for best comedian.
19Adding a Teen Choice Award to her People's Choice Award, DeGeneres requested awards from other age groups: the elderly and babies.
20Several awards went to "The Hunger Games: Mockingjay, Part 1," including best actor in a sci-fi or fantasy for Josh Hutcherson.
21He reflected on the end of the franchise, with this fall's "Mockingjay, Part 2," like a coming graduation.
22"'Hunger Games' has been a huge part of my life for almost five years now. It's coming to an end and it's really sad," said Hutcherson.
23"So if we want to cry together, we can do that because it breaks my heart."
