Names of suspended Baltimore police officers released

1Edward Brown speaks at a protest outside City Hall about Freddie Gray in Baltimore, Monday, April 20, 2015.
2Baltimore's top police officials, mayor and prosecutor sought to calm a "community on edge" Monday while investigating how Gray suffered a fatal spine injury while under arrest.
3Six officers have been suspended, but investigators say they still don't know how it happened.
4BALTIMORE (AP) - The six officers suspended in the investigation of a black man who died after his arrest had experience on the force ranging from nearly two decades to three years, officials said.
5The officers were identified by city officials Tuesday.
6They have been suspended with pay while authorities investigate the death of Freddie Gray, who was handcuffed, placed in a transport van with his legs later shackled, and driven around in the van for about 30 minutes before being rushed to the hospital in critical condition, officials said.
7Gray died of a "significant spinal injury" on Sunday, a week after his arrest, Deputy Police Commissioner Jerry Rodriguez said Monday.
8Police have not said exactly how Gray was injured.
9Gray, 25, was arrested on April 12 after police "made eye contact" with him and another man and the two started running, authorities said.
10The lawyer for Gray's family said he believes the police had no reason to stop the man in the first place.
11What led to that injury - and why Gray was initially pursued by police - is still unknown.
12According to court documents, Officer Garrett Miller accused Gray of carrying a switchblade, which was discovered in Gray's pocket after he was stopped.
13- Lt. Brian Rice, 41, with the department since 1997.
14- Sgt. Alicia White, 30, with the department since 2010.
15- Officer Caesar Goodson, 45, who has been there since 1999.
16- Officers William Porter and Edward Nero, who along with Miller, all joined in 2012.
17Their specific roles in the arrest were not released.
18Police Commissioner Anthony Batts said the reason for Gray's stop is "a question we have to dig into."
19Billy Murphy, the lawyer hired by Gray's family, said he believes the officers had no probable cause to stop Gray.
20"They've made concessions on lack of probable cause," Murphy said.
21"Running while black is not probable cause. Felony running doesn't exist, and you can't arrest someone for looking you in the eye. You have to believe he committed a crime and have an objective basis for that belief. They had none of that."
22Gray's death has prompted daily protests and a vigil was planned Tuesday evening at the spot where he was arrested.
23The death comes six months after Baltimore officials released a plan to reduce police brutality and misconduct.
24The plan followed a request by city officials to the U.S. Justice Department to review police policies and procedures.
25The Justice Department's review should include a broad investigation of whether officers have committed civil rights violations, said the Rev.
26Cortly C.D. Witherspoon, president of the Baltimore chapter of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference.
27He said he plans to send a letter with that request by Wednesday.
28Officials on Monday vowed transparency and pledged to hold those found responsible accountable.
29Batts said the investigation will be completed by May 1 and the results will be sent to the state attorney's office to determine whether criminal charges will be filed.
30Batts also said he is ordering that police review and rewrite "effective immediately" its policies on moving prisoners and providing them with medical attention.

32"I'm angry that we are here again, that we have had to tell another mother that their child is dead. I'm frustrated that not only that we're here, but we don't have all of the answers. I want to know why the officers pursued Mr. Gray. I want to know if the proper procedures were followed."
