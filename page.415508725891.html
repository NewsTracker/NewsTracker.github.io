1Friday, Oct. 17, 2014.
2WASHINGTON (AP) - An American health care worker who contracted Ebola while volunteering in a Sierra Leone treatment unit has been downgraded to critical condition at the National Institutes of Health, doctors said Monday.
3The agency said in a statement that the patient's status was changed from serious condition.
4He is being treated at the National Institutes of Health's hospital near Washington.
5"We are intensively treating the patient," said Dr. Anthony Fauci, director of the National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases at NIH.
6"He's in our special clinical studies unit and, hopefully, that will be able to turn this around and the patient will recover, but it's too early to say."
7The patient was flown in isolation from Sierra Leone on a chartered plane last week and arrived early Friday morning.
8His name and age have not been released.
9The man is a clinician working with Partners in Health, a Boston-based nonprofit organization.
10The group has been treating patients in Liberia and Sierra Leone since November.
11The man in Dallas had contracted the virus in his native Liberia.
12He transmitted the disease to two nurses, resulting in widespread concern in the U.S., with questions raised about emergency department screening of patients, monitoring of ill travelers from Africa and even disputes over the disposal of potentially infectious waste from hospitals.
13The World Health Organization has estimated the virus has killed more than 10,000 people, mostly in the West African nations of Guinea, Liberia and Sierra Leone.
14The current outbreak is the largest ever for the disease.
15While deaths have slowed dramatically in recent months, the virus appears stubbornly entrenched in parts of Guinea and Sierra Leone.
16Besides the man at NIH, there are 12 other Partners in Health workers being brought to the United States for monitoring.
17A Centers for Disease Control and Prevention spokesman said that includes four going to Atlanta to be near Emory University Hospital; one arrived Friday.
18On Saturday, four health care workers arrived at the Nebraska Medical Center in Omaha; another was expected Monday.
19Three arrived in the Washington area on Sunday to be near the NIH campus in Bethesda.
20One of those being monitored in Nebraska has developed symptoms of Ebola and was being moved to an isolation unit, hospital officials said in a news release Monday.
21Nebraska Medical Center spokesman Taylor Wilson said the individual developed symptoms Sunday evening and was hospitalized as a precaution.
22Wilson declined to describe the symptoms, but said they had resolved Monday.
23None of those being monitored has tested positive for Ebola.
24The National Institutes of Health said it has no other pending admissions of additional patients with the Ebola virus or who have been exposed to Ebola.
25CDC workers in Sierra Leone are involved in investigating the illness of the first patient, including looking for other people the person was in contact with.
26It's possible other people will be transported to the United States for monitoring, said the spokesman, Tom Skinner.
27AP Medical Writer Mike Stobbe in New York and Jennifer Garske in Washington contributed to this story.
