Thirty Killed In East Ukraine Mine Explosion

More than 30 killed in mine blast in rebel east Ukraine: local authority

1"The total deaths amount to 33 people," Interfax news agency said, citing the region administration's press service.
2Earlier, local authorities said another 16 people were injured in the blast at the mine in Zasyadko, Donetsk.
3Families of those caught up in the disaster have been gathering at the entrance to the mine.
4One of the survivors, who was taken to a Donetsk hospital, said: "I was blown away by an explosion. I came round. There was dust everywhere and people were moaning."
5Mine officials say the blast was likely to have been caused by gas and was not linked to recent fighting in the area.
6Donetsk has seen fierce clashes between government troops and separatist rebels, who control the region.
7Ukraine's prime minister Arseny Yatseniuk has accused the separatists of blocking rescue workers dispatched by central authorities.
8"... The Russian terrorists did not let them reach the scene of the accident," he said, using a term commonly used by Kiev to describe the rebels.
9The Zasyadko mine has a long history of fatal accidents, including a blast in 2007 which left 106 people dead.
10Many of the miners killed are buried in a cemetery next to the pit.
11The mines in eastern Ukraine are among some of the most dangerous in the world due to the high levels of methane produced which increase the risk of explosions.
12Some 300 men die in mine accidents in the region every year.

25The Zasyadko coal mine produced 1.4 million tonnes of coal in 2013.
