1The doodle encapsulates the work that Anna became famous for, using cyanotypes, also known today as 'blueprinting' to spell out the name of the search engine with leaf prints.
2Originally born Anna Children, Atkins was born on 16 March, 1799 in Tonbridge, Kent - her mother died shortly after her birth, leaving her father to raise her.
3Anna's father John George Children was a respected scientist, secretary of the Royal Society and was associated with the British Museum, it is from him that she received her scientific education.
4Much of Atkins' early work involved helping her father, her most noted contribution was producing 250 detailed engravings to illustrate his book.
5Anna used the cyanotype process to illustrate the first photographic book with images of all of the types of algae found in the British isles - the process consists of mixing the substance to be recorded, with ammonium citrate and potassium ferricyanide before exposing it to sunlight and washing it with plain water, the end result turns the paper around the object a deep blue colour, creating a detailed print of it.
6Atkins' first book: Photographs of British Algae, published in 1843, used the technique and featured handwritten caption along with the individually produced cyanotypes.
7Anna married John Pelly Atkins in 1825, before starting a collection of dried plant specimens and being made a member of the Botanical Society of London in 1839.
