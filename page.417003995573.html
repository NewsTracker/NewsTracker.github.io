Northern Mexico farmworkers reach agreement for better wages, working conditions

1MEXICO CITY - Mexican farmworkers fighting low wages and poor working conditions in the border state of Baja California have reached an agreement with the government and their employer.
2The Mexican interior ministry says daily wages will go from 100 to 200 pesos ($13 a day).
3Workers will be added to the social security system and will be allowed to unionize.
4The measures were among 13 agreements announced late Thursday by the interior department.
5The farmworkers have been striking for months against what they called exploitation and bad health and housing conditions.
6Fidel Sanchez, leader of the laborers of the San Quintin Valley, told a Baja California radio station that the workers are satisfied.
7The pay increase is set to begin May. 24.
