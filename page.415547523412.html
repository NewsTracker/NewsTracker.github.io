Israel voters chose 'occupation' not talks: Palestinians

Bangladesh court indicts 8 in killing of atheist blogger

1"Israel chose the path of racism, occupation and settlement building, and did not choose the path of negotiations and partnership between us," senior Palestine Liberation Organisation official Yasser Abed Rabbo told AFP.
2In a snap election to Israel's 120-seat parliament on Tuesday, the Likud beat centre-left rivals the Zionist Union by 30 seats to 24.
3"We are facing an Israeli society that is sick with racism, and a policy of occupation and settlement building... and ahead of us is a long and difficult road of struggle against Israel," Abed Rabbo said.
4"We must complete our steps to stop security coordination (with Israel) and go to the Hague tribunal to move against settlements and Israel's crimes in its war on Gaza."
5Relations between Israel and the Palestinians have deteriorated sharply since US-brokered talks between president Mahmud Abbas's Palestinian Authority and Netanyahu's government collapsed in April last year.
6The breakdown was followed by violence in Jerusalem, a bloody war between Israel and Islamist movement Hamas in the Gaza Strip, and Palestinian moves against Israel at the United Nations and the International Criminal Court in The Hague.
7The Palestinians say they will seek action against Israel at the ICC as early as next month.
8In the final stages of the campaign, Netanyahu ruled out the establishment of a Palestinian state if reelected, effectively reneging on his 2009 endorsement of a two-state solution.
9He also pledged to build thousands of homes for Jewish settlers in annexed Arab east Jerusalem to prevent future concessions to the Palestinians.
1The Metropolitan Sessions court issued the indictments after accepting the police investigation into the killing of blogger Ahmed Rajib Haider in front of his house in Dhaka's Mirpur area.
2The indictments mean a trial will begin for Mufti Jasimuddin Rahmani, head of the Ansarullah Bangla Team, and six students at North South University who earlier confessed involvement in the killing.
3A seventh student said to have planned the attack is in hiding.
4All of the students were suspended by the university after their arrests.
5Despite their earlier confessions, Rahmani and the six students pleaded not guilty on Wednesday.
6The judge set April 21 for the start of the trial, and the seventh student will be tried in absentia.
7Islamic extremism has made few inroads in Bangladesh, a Muslim-majority nation of 160 million people, but there have been a series of similar attacks in recent years blamed on militants.
8Avijit Roy, a Bangladeshi-American atheist who wrote against Islamic radicalism, was hacked to death about two weeks ago on the campus of Dhaka University.
9Roy's wife was also injured.
10A suspect has been arrested and investigators say Islamist radical groups were responsible for the attack.
11In their investigation report, police said Rahmani incited the seven students to murder Haider in sermons in which he said all atheist bloggers should be killed to protect Islam.
12Haider had criticized the Prophet Muhammad and Islam in his blog.
13He was also a leading campaigner for banning the Islamist Jamaat-e-Islami party, which opposed Bangladesh's independence from Pakistan in 1971.
14Police said the student who allegedly planned the attack is a member of Jamaat-e-Islami's student wing, Islami Chhatra Shibir.
15The party has denied involvement in the killing.
