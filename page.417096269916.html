U.S. women cruise to a 5-1 win over Mexico in World Cup tune-up match

2Sydney Leroux realized what she'd been missing.
3Leroux made her first start of the year and scored twice in a 5-1 victory for the United States over Mexico on Sunday night.
4The forward started off 2015 with a right foot injury and had been working her way back into peak form.
5"Coming out there and scoring those goals, it made me miss scoring a lot," she said.
6"You don't realize it until it happens."
7Abby Wambach also scored twice in the second-to-last send-off match for the United States in advance of the Women's World Cup next month in Canada.
8Wambach, who came in as a substitute to start the second half, scored on a penalty kick in the 58th minute and added a header in the 72nd minute to bring her career total to 182 goals.
9Lori Chalupny added a goal for the United States early in the second half.
10Ariana Calderon scored on a header for Mexico late in the first half, snapping Hope Solo's shutout streak at five matches.
11It was the first goal the U.S. conceded to Mexico since November, 2010, when Las Tri surprised the Americans with a 2-1 victory in World Cup qualifying.
12The Americans were playing without forward Alex Morgan, who has a bone bruise in her left knee.
13Morgan also may miss the final send-off match against South Korea at Red Bull Stadium in New Jersey on May 30.
14"She's doing well," coach Jill Ellis said.
15"We still have not had her back in full training, but she's feeling better and we're going to keep it precautionary and be conservative with her through the end of this camp and potentially through the next camp as well."
16Mexico goalkeeper Cecilia Santiago was the star early on, fending off two tremendous attempts by Megan Rapinoe in the 14th minute.
17Santiago played during the 2011 World Cup at age 16, becoming the youngest women's player to appear in goal in a tournament match.
18Leroux scored from a seemingly impossible angle in the 28th minute, her first goal in 14 matches, to put the United States up 1-0.
19It was her 34th career goal.
20"She's gotten herself fit," Ellis said about Leroux.
21"I said coming into this camp, I just sense the focus in her. She's been training really well, deserved the start and she did great."
22Nayeli Rangel got a shot off when Solo was occupied in the 36th minute, but it went slightly wide of the far post.
23Las Tri evened it just before the half on Calderon's header.
24Chalupny entered the game to start the second half and scored on her first touch, with a feed from Wambach in the 46th minute, to put the Americans up 2-1.
25After Wambach took advantage of the penalty kick when Arianna Romero was called for a handball, Leroux added her second on a fast break in the 61st minute.
26Wambach's header closed out scoring.
27"Obviously I'm happy to get on the field and get the minutes any way I can, but more than that I'm happy to contribute and score goals," Wambach said.
28Earlier this week, both teams played closed-door matches against Canada, which also was training in Southern California.
29Canada beat Mexico 1-0 on Thursday.
30A day earlier, Canada and the United States played to a 1-1 draw.
31Because the matches were closed, they do not count as international friendlies.
32It is the policy of U.S. Soccer not to comment on such matches, but Ellis said she was pleased with her team's performance.
33"We got everybody in the game, so it was good to see our depth," Ellis said, ticking off what she saw.
34"Played it on turf. I thought at times our speed of play was very, very good. We created a lot of opportunities. It was just really good for us to play such a good opponent this close to the World Cup."
35The United States was officially coming off a 3-0 victory over Ireland last Sunday in the first of a three-match send-off series before the World Cup.
36Wambach scored twice and defender Julie Johnston added a goal.
37Mexico coach Leo Cuellar told The Associated Press that he will likely wait to announce his World Cup roster until closer to the May 25 deadline, because of injuries that have impacted his squad.
38Mexico forward Tanya Samarzich watched the match from the sidelines on crutches with her right foot in a boot.
39Samarzich, who played for USC, was apparently injured in one of Mexico's closed-door matches.
40"Obviously when the U.S. has so many opportunities, something is not right," Cuellar said after Sunday's match.
41"I think we needed to step on the field stronger mentally in order to execute what we've been practicing. It's very difficult to defend all the time against the U.S. if you don't keep possession of the ball."
