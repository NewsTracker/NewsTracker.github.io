Egypt's el-Sissi replaces interior minister in reshuffle

1FILE - In this Monday, Jan. 26, 2015 file photo, Egypt's Interior Minister Mohammed Ibrahim speaks during a press conference in Cairo, Egypt.
2Egyptian President Abdel-Fattah el-Sissi reshuffled his Cabinet on Thursday, March 5, 2015, replacing the powerful minister in charge of the nation's police along with five other ministers, state television reported.
3CAIRO (AP) - Egyptian President Abdel-Fattah el-Sissi on Thursday replaced the country's powerful interior minister as part of a Cabinet reshuffle, in what was taken as a sign of growing frustration at the security forces' inability to staunch an increasingly virulent insurgency.
4Interior Minister Mohammed Ibrahim played a key role in el-Sissi's 2013 ouster of President Mohammed Morsi and the subsequent bloody crackdown on his Islamist supporters that left hundreds dead and thousands in detention.
5But despite his bloody track record, Ibrahim's forces had struggled to combat a burgeoning insurgency in the strategic Sinai peninsula and failed to halt a series of low-grade bomb attacks in Cairo and other cities.
6Ibrahim was replaced by another police general, Magdy Abdel-Ghaffar, a career officer in the feared State Security Agency who briefly led the agency in 2011 and 2012.
7The choice of a veteran officer indicated no letup in the government's heavy-handed treatment of its opponents.
8El-Sissi later named Ibrahim an adviser to the prime minister, a largely ceremonial position, in what appeared to be a symbolic show of gratitude for his support of the 2013 military takeover.
9The move resembled one taken in December, when el-Sissi pensioned off intelligence chief Mohammed Farid el-Tohamy while awarding him one of the state's highest awards.
10El-Tohamy, a longtime patron of el-Sissi, was also instrumental in the crackdown on the Brotherhood.
11Ibrahim's removal followed an uptick in bomb attacks blamed on Islamic militants targeting the heart of the heavily protected capital.
12The latest such attack was on Monday, when a bomb at a police checkpoint killed two people outside the nation's highest appeals court in downtown Cairo.
13Another bomb killed two people in the southern city of Aswan on Sunday, and a similar wave of explosions rocked the Cairo district of Giza last week, killing one and injuring about 10.
14On Wednesday, a massive fire destroyed most of the city's showcase convention center in an eastern suburb.
15No foul play was suspected, but the fire was widely seen as the latest example of government negligence.
16The Cabinet reshuffle also saw the replacement of the ministers of culture, tourism, education, telecommunications and agriculture.
17El-Sissi introduced two new portfolios, for vocational training and population.
18It was the first Cabinet reshuffle since el-Sissi took office in June, nearly a year after he ousted Morsi amid widespread protests against Egypt's first freely elected leader.
19Morsi had named Ibrahim interior minister in January 2013, but six months later Ibrahim and his largely militarized police force sided with the millions of protesters who took to the streets demanding Morsi step down.
20Ibrahim later led the crackdown on Morsi's now-outlawed Muslim Brotherhood as well as the liberal and secular youth activists behind the 2011 uprising that toppled longtime autocrat Hosni Mubarak.
21Thousands of people, mostly Islamists, have been detained, and hundreds were killed when security forces violently dispersed pro-Morsi protest camps in Cairo in August 2013.
22The following month, Ibrahim escaped unharmed from a suicide car bomb that targeted him.
23The police since then have largely succeeded in stifling protests, but they have also been dogged by allegations of brutality and negligence.
24Last month, police used tear gas against fans trying to enter a Cairo stadium ahead of a popular soccer match.
25At least 20 were killed in the resulting stampede.
26In January, police allegedly shot dead an unarmed female protester in a peaceful demonstration in downtown Cairo.
27El-Sissi had publicly ordered Ibrahim to bring Shaimaa al-Sabbagh's killers to justice.
28The president mentioned the 36-year-old mother again in an address to the nation last week, saying no official will escape justice if found responsible.
