Jinx doco star arrested for mob murder

1FBI agents arrested Durst at a New Orleans hotel Saturday on a warrant from Los Angeles for the murder of a mobster's daughter 15 years ago, authorities said.
2Durst was ordered held without bond pending another hearing Monday.
3His lawyer, Chip Lewis, said Durst will agree to be taken to Los Angeles to face the first-degree murder charge.
4Durst participated in the HBO documentary The Jinx: The Life and Deaths of Robert Durst, which is screening in New Zealand on Sky's SoHo channel, giving an extensive interview to filmmaker Andrew Jarecki.
5But Lewis said nothing his client revealed changes his innocence.
6Durst's estranged and fearful relatives thanked authorities for tracking him down.
7"We are relieved and also grateful to everyone who assisted in the arrest of Robert Durst. We hope he will finally be held accountable for all he has done," said his brother, Douglas Durst, in a statement.
8Durst, 71, has always maintained his innocence in the 2000 murder of Susan Berman, whose father was an associate of Las Vegas mobsters Bugsy Siegel and Meyer Lansky.
9Berman, a writer who become Durst's spokeswoman, was killed at her home with a bullet to the back of her head as New York investigators prepared to question her in the unsolved 1982 disappearance of Durst's wife, Kathleen.
10The climax of last week's episode revealed a hand-written address on a letter, recovered by the slain woman's relative, that Durst had sent to Berman.
11The handwriting seems virtually identical to an anonymous letter alerting Beverly Hills police to a "cadaver" in Berman's home.
12Even the word "Beverly" is misspelled as "Beverley" on both documents.
13Durst observes in the documentary that only Berman's killer could have sent the letter to police.
14The Los Angeles Police said the arrest resulted from "investigative leads and additional evidence that has come to light in the last year."
15Lewis said he has no doubt the arrest was orchestrated in coordination with HBO's broadcast of the final episode in the United States at the weekend.
16After Berman's death, Durst moved to Texas, where he lived as a mute woman in a boarding house until his arrest in 2001 after dismembered parts of the body of his elderly neighbor, Morris Black, were found floating in Galveston Bay.
17Durst then became a fugitive until he was caught shoplifting in Pennsylvania, even though he had $500 cash in his pocket and $37,000 in his rental car " along with two guns and marijuana.
18Lewis told the jury that Durst shot Black in self-defence and suffered from Asperger's syndrome.
19Despite admitting that he used a paring knife, two saws and an axe to dismember Black's body before dumping the remains, Durst was acquitted of murder.
20Durst "has been incredibly lucky that so many people who've investigated him have dropped the ball, but I think that luck may be running out," said Former Galveston County District Court Judge Susan Criss, who presided over the Texas murder trial.
21The Durst family is worth at least US$4 billion, according to the Forbes list of richest Americans.
22The oldest son of the late real estate mogul Seymour Durst, whose Durst Corporation manages 1 World Trade Center, Robert Durst became estranged from his family when his brother Douglas was chosen instead of him to run the family business.
23Robert Durst had known tragedy from an early age " when he was 7, his father called him onto the roof of a building to try to persuade his suicidal mother not to jump. He left before seeing her fall.
24In 1982, Robert Durst reported that his wife, Kathie, had suddenly disappeared from their cottage in New York state.
25No one was ever charged.
26More recently, Douglas Durst has said he fears his brother would kill him.
27Robert Durst has denied that.
28But in 2012 and 2013, his family members had restraining orders taken out against him.
29In 2013, Durst tried unsuccessfully to claim $82,000 from his missing wife's estate, even though his personal net worth has been estimated at about $100 million.
30"The story is so operatic," Jarecki said in an interview beore his documentary aired.
31Jarecki told a fictionalized version of Durst's story in All Good Things, a 2010 film starring Ryan Gosling.
32Then he got a call from Durst himself, who wanted to see it and eventually agreed to talk on camera.
