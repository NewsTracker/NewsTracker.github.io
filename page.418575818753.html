8That's up 13% from $12.67 billion a year ago.
9Google will hold an earnings call with analysts on Thursday afternoon.
10This is the first earnings call in which Ruth Porat, who joined as chief financial officer from Morgan Stanley in May, will speak with investors.
11"We believe she has incentive to make sure that she meets or exceeds Street expectations in her first quarter out to start off on the right foot with investors," said Piper Jaffray analyst Gene Munster.
12Wall Street is hoping for more details from Porat on the performance of Google businesses such as YouTube and mobile search advertising.
13It's also hoping Google might share some of its $60 billion cash stockpile through a buyback or a dividend.
14Munster says the latter is unlikely.
15"Some investors are hoping that Porat's arrival may also mean a more open approach to capital return. Overall, we believe that CEO Larry Page (much like Steve Jobs at Apple) has been the bottleneck to capital return instead of the CFO seat," Munster wrote in a research report.

18In the spotlight: revenue growth as Google's lucrative search advertising business slows.

20Wall Street is worried that search ads are eroding on mobile devices.
