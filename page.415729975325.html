Hundreds abducted by Boko Haram are being used as human shields, Nigeria says

1Hundreds abducted by Boko Haram are being used as human shields, Nigeria says Add to...
2Hundreds of civilians, including many children, have been abducted and are being used as human shields by Boko Haram extremists, a top Nigerian official confirmed Wednesday.
3Several hundred people were taken captive by the Islamic militants as they retreated earlier this month from Damasak in northeastern Nigeria, Mike Omeri, the Nigerian spokesman for the fight against Boko Haram, told Associated Press on Wednesday.
4He said he could not specify how many were taken captive but local reports say as many as 500 people were seized.
5When troops from Chad and Niger advanced toward Damasak, Boko Haram began taking captives, said Omeri.
6"Boko Haram rushed to primary schools they took children and adults that they are using as shields to protect themselves from the menacing advance of troops," said Omeri.
7"They are being used as shields by Boko Haram."
8Damasak, near the border with Niger, was recaptured from Boko Haram on March 16.
9The mass abduction happened as the extremists were fleeing the advancing troops and information about the seizures of civilians has only been confirmed now.
10The soldiers who recaptured Damasak found the town largely deserted.
11Damasak had been held for months by Boko Haram, who used the trading town as an administrative centre.
12The troops from Chad and Niger who now hold Damasak have discovered evidence of a mass grave, Chad's ambassador to the UN, Mahamat Zene Cherif, confirmed Wednesday.
13International assistance is needed for the thousands of Nigerian refugees who have fled the violence, said the head of the UN refugee agency.
14Some 74,000 Nigerians have fled to neighbouring Cameroon, according to the agency.
15Over 100,000 more have flooded into Chad and Niger.
16Troops from the three countries are now helping Nigeria to combat the militants and win back Nigerian towns.
17The refugee agency will funnel more resources to Cameroon, said UN High Commissioner for Refugees Antonio Guterres on Wednesday while visiting Maroua, the capital of Cameroon's Far North region.
18He stressed that additional assistance is needed.
19"Cameroon is today not only a very important protection space for refugees, but it is in the first line of defence of the international community," he said.
20The UN agency says the Nigerian crisis is one of the most underfunded in the world.
21In February, the agency asked for $71-million to assist displaced people in Nigeria and neighbouring countries; already that figure appears to be too low, it said this week.
22Thus far, it has received only $6.8-million in donations, he said.
