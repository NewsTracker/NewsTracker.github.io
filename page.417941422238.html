A short chronology of the Islamic State group

1FILE - In this undated image posted by the Raqqa Media Center, in Islamic State group-held territory, on Monday, June 30, 2014, which has been verified and is consistent with other AP reporting, fighters from the al-Qaida linked Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant (ISIL) ride tanks during a parade in Raqqa, Syria.
2April 18, 2010 - U.S. and Iraqi forces kill two top leaders of al-Qaida's branch in Iraq.
3Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi becomes the terror group's new leader.
4March 2011 - Syrian conflict erupts following an uprising against President Bashar Assad that turns into an insurgency and eventually civil war, attracting scores of al-Qaida militants from Iraq.
5April 2013 - Al-Baghdadi announces his group has taken over the Nusra Front, the al-Qaida affiliate in Syria.
6The combined group is variously called the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant, or ISIL, and the Islamic State of Iraq and Syria, or ISIS; eventually it is known as the Islamic State group.
7Nusra denies the takeover, sparking infighting that continues to this day.
8March 5, 2013 - Rebel factions including the Nusra Front take over Raqqa, a provincial capital in north-central Syria, the largest city to be wrested from Assad's government by rebels.
9May 2013 - IS fighters burst on stage and show their strength by shooting three peoples in a main square in Raqqa.
10The group begins seizing towns and villages across northern Syria.
11January 2014 - Al-Baghdadi's forces sweep into Fallujah and parts of Ramadi in Iraq's Anbar province, which Iraqi security forces had abandoned weeks earlier.
12In Syria, they seize full control of Raqqa after driving out rival groups.
13Infighting with rivals erupts across north and eastern Syria.
14June 10, 2014 - Al-Baghdadi's fighters take over Iraq's second-largest city of Mosul and, the next day, overrun more of Nineveh province, including the village of Eski Mosul.
15They soon sweep south, capturing Saddam Hussein's hometown of Tikrit and much of the Sunni heartland nearly to the outskirts of Baghdad as government forces melt away.
16When they threaten Shiite holy sites, Iraq's top Shiite cleric issues a call to arms against the group, bringing a flood of volunteers to militias.
17June 29, 2014 - The IS group declares the establishment of a "caliphate," or Islamic state, in territories it controls in Iraq and Syria, and al-Baghdadi is declared the caliph.
18The militants rename themselves the Islamic State group.
19To celebrate, the militants "unite" the territories by knocking down sand berms in the desert that mark the border between the two countries.
20They soon after seize several actual border crossings.
21Aug. 8, 2014 - The U.S. begins targeting the IS group with airstrikes, citing the humanitarian plight of Iraq's minorities, like the Yazidis.
22Aug. 19, 2014 - IS releases a video showing the beheading of American journalist James Foley in response to the U.S.-led airstrikes.
23This marks the first of many videos showing militants beheading foreign captives.
24Jan. 23, 2015 - Kurdish peshmerga fighters regain control of Eski Mosul and several neighboring towns, driving out IS fighters.
25Jan. 26, 2015 - Kurdish fighters take control of the Syrian border town of Kobani near Turkey after fighting IS militants for months.
26U.S.-led airstrikes helped turn the tide for the Kurds.
27Feb. 3, 2015 - IS releases a video of Jordanian pilot Muath al-Kaseasbeh being burned to death inside a cage.
28April 1, 2015 - Backed by U.S.-led coalition airstrikes, Iraqi forces retake the city of Tikrit, their biggest gain yet against the IS group.
29May 17, 2015 - Ramadi, the capital of Iraq's Anbar province, falls to IS as Iraqi forces abandon their weapons and armored vehicles to flee.
30May 20, 2015 - IS captures the historic desert city of Palmyra in central Syria after Assad's forces collapse and withdraw.
31June 16, 2015 - Kurds deal the Islamic State a major setback by capturing the Syrian town of Tal Abyad on the Turkish border, which had provided a strategic supply route to IS.
32FILE - In this Monday, June 16, 2014 file photo, demonstrators chant pro-Islamic State group slogans as they wave the group's flags in front of the provincial government headquarters in Mosul, 225 miles (360 kilometers) northwest of Baghdad, Iraq.
33In this photo released on Jan. 31, 2014 by a militant website, which has been verified and is consistent with other AP reporting, members of the Islamic State group, left, distribute niqabs, enveloping black robes and veils that leave only the eyes visible, to Iraqi women in Mosul, northern Iraq.
34In areas controlled by the Islamic State group in Iraq and Syria, women must not only be covered, but usually are required to wear all black, with flat-soled shoes; for men, Western clothes or hair styles - even hair gel - can draw suspicion.
35FILE - This undated file image made from a video released by Islamic State militants Tuesday, Aug. 19, 2014, purports to show the killing of journalist James Foley by the militant group.
36FILE - This Friday, May 27, 2011 file photo shows journalist James Foley, of Rochester, N.H., in Boston.
37On Aug. 19, 2014, the Islamic state released a video showing the beheading of the American journalist in response to U.S.-led airstrikes.
38It marked the first of many videos showing militants beheading foreign captives.
39FILE - In this Friday, Jan. 30, 2015 file photo, workers raise a banner with a photo of Jordanian pilot Lt. Muath al-Kaseasbeh, held captive by the Islamic State group, outside a tent for supporters in Amman, Jordan.
40Lt. al-Kaseasbeh, who crashed over Syria in December, was killed by the militants and a video of his death was released on Tuesday, Feb. 3, 2015.
