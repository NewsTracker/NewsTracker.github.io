Blatter 'under investigation in US'

1Fifa president Sepp Blatter is being investigated by US officials as part of their inquiry into corruption at the world football body, US media say.
2The news came hours after Mr Blatter announced that he was stepping down from his role.
3US prosecutors launched a criminal inquiry last week, with seven Fifa officials arrested in Switzerland, part of a group of 14 people indicted.
4Two days after the arrests, Mr Blatter was re-elected president of Fifa.
5However, he said on Tuesday that it appeared the mandate he had been given "does not seem to be supported by everyone in the world".
6Mr Blatter said: "Fifa needs profound restructuring."
7The BBC's Nick Bryant in New York says that several US officials told the New York Times that they are hoping to gain the co-operation of some of the Fifa figures now under indictment on charges of racketeering and money laundering to try to build a case against Mr Blatter.
8In announcing the US prosecution last week into what she called the "rampant, systemic and deep-rooted" corruption in world football, US Attorney General Loretta Lynch did not address Mr Blatter's position directly.
9But she did say that this was the beginning of the investigation, not the end.
10She also called for a new start at Fifa.
11Our correspondent says it is primarily the US investigation that has plunged the world football governing body into its most serious crisis.
12Earlier the FBI, Internal Revenue Service and the US Attorney for the Eastern District of New York, who is involved in the US prosecutions, all said they would not comment on the Blatter resignation.
13In its prosecution, the US justice department said 14 individuals were under investigation worldwide for allegedly accepting bribes and kickbacks estimated at more than $150m ( 97m) over a 24-year period.
14Two vice-presidents were among the seven Fifa officials arrested in Zurich.
15They all await US extradition proceedings.
16The arrests overshadowed the vote for a new president, which Mr Blatter won, defeating his sole challenger, Prince Ali bin Al Hussein of Jordan.
17Prince Ali withdrew despite forcing a second round, having lost the first by 133 votes to 73.
18Announcing his exit on Tuesday, 79-year-old Mr Blatter called an extraordinary Fifa congress "as soon as possible" to elect a new president.
19The Swiss, who has been Fifa president since 1998, said: "The next ordinary Fifa congress will take place on 13 May, 2016 in Mexico City.
20"This would create unnecessary delay and I will urge the executive committee to organise an extraordinary congress for the election of my successor at the earliest opportunity.
21"This will need to be done in line with Fifa's statutes and we must allow enough time for the best candidates to present themselves and to campaign."
22The extraordinary congress is expected to take place between December 2015 and March 2016.
23Further allegations of corruption emerged on Tuesday with claims that Fifa general secretary Jerome Valcke was linked to an alleged $10m ( 6m) payment of bribes over South Africa's bid to host the 2010 World Cup.
24He denies any wrongdoing.
25A separate criminal investigation by Swiss authorities into how the 2018 and 2022 World Cups were allocated is also under way.
26Sepp Blatter is to resign as president of Fifa amid a corruption scandal and is reportedly under investigation in the US.
