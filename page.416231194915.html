Pew Research Center: Teens still use Facebook

1Think again.
2Facebook remains the most used social media site among American teens ages 13 to 17, according to a new study from the Pew Research Center.
3And, surprisingly, boys visit the site more often than girls.
4Aided hugely by smartphones and other mobile devices, 71 percent of teens surveyed said they use Facebook, with the same percentage saying they use more than one social network of seven options they were asked about.
5Half the teens said they also use Instagram and four in 10 said they used Snapchat, according to the study released April 9.
6Among 22 percent of teens who use just one site, 66 percent use Facebook, 13 percent use Google Plus, 13 percent are Instagram users and 3 percent use Snapchat.
7The Pew center didn't compare the latest survey to its previous studies on the subject because it switched its national representative sample from telephone interviews to an online format, potentially skewing results when comparisons are made.
8The latest study points to Facebook as a dominant force in teens' online lives even as Instagram and Snapchat have gained momentum.
9Facebook was the site they used most frequently, at 41 percent, followed by Instagram at 20 percent and Snapchat at 11 percent.
10Boys are more likely than girls to report they visit Facebook most often - at 45 percent versus 36 percent of girls.
11Pew's data also revealed a distinct pattern in social media use by socio-economic status.
12Teens from households with earnings of less than $50,000 are more likely to use Facebook the most - 49 percent compared with 37 percent of teens from households with earnings of $50,000 or more.
13As far as how much time is spent on social media, 24 percent of teens overall go online "almost constantly," the study said, with 56 percent saying they go online several times a day and 12 percent reporting once-a-day use.
14Among other sites the 1,060 teens were asked about are Twitter, Vine and Tumblr.
15Thirty-three percent said they use Twitter and Google Plus, while 24 percent said they use Vine and 14 percent said Tumblr.
16Six percent said they use Twitter most often.
