Egypt, Ethiopia and Sudan sign 'historic' deal for Nile dam construction

1The leaders of Egypt, Ethiopia and Sudan all gathered in Khartoum on Monday to sign the agreement of principles on Ethiopia's Grand Renaissance dam project.
2Egypt, heavily reliant for millennia on the Nile for agriculture and drinking water, feared that the Grand Renaissance Dam would decrease its water supply.
3However Abdel Fattah al-Sisi, Egypt's president, said on Monday that "this is a framework agreement and it will be completed".
4"We have chosen cooperation, and to trust one another for the sake of development."
5He said the final accord will "achieve benefits and development for Ethiopia without harming Egypt and Sudan's interests".
6Sudan's President Omar al-Bashir hailed the deal as "historic".
7Ethiopia began diverting the Blue Nile in May 2013 to build the 6,000 MW dam, which will be Africa's largest when completed in 2017.
8Egypt believes its "historic rights" to the Nile are guaranteed by treaties from 1929 and 1959 which grant it 87 percent of the river's flow and the power to veto upstream projects.
9But Nile Basin countries, including Ethiopia, signed another deal in 2010 allowing them to work on river projects without Cairo's agreement.
10In protest, Egypt withdrew from the Nile Basin Initiative (NBI), a forum to discuss management and development of the region's resources, but later resumed participation.
11Neither Sudan nor Egypt has signed the 2010 Nile Basin deal, however.
12Sudan, like Egypt, relies on Nile resources but has said it does not expect to be affected by Ethiopia's Grand Renaissance project.
