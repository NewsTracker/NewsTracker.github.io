Salt Lake City will soon offer Google's high-speed Internet

1SALT LAKE CITY (AP) - Salt Lake City will soon join a handful of U.S. cities in offering high-speed internet service through Google Fiber.
2Google Fiber spokesman Devin Baer said the company plans to start construction for the network after several months of planning with city officials.
3He said they don't yet have a definitive pricing structure, but expect to offer service similar to what currently exists in another Utah city, Provo.
4Google offers three levels of service to customers who pay the $30 activation fee in Provo, one of the first cities selected for the project.
5Residents there can chose a free 5 megabit connection, a high-speed 1 gigabit connection for $70 per months or the high speed connection plus TV service for $120 per month.
6Google Fiber will not provide the number of households connected to its service, but said Baer said things in Provo have been "really great."
7Earlier this year, the company announced that it will soon introduce its service for small businesses in Provo, which is about 45 miles south of Salt Lake City.
8Baer said he hopes to bring the small business option to Salt Lake City as well.
9Google chose Salt Lake City because it has a "thriving tech community" and is a natural extension from Provo, he said.
