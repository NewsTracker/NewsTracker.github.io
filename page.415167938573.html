2The 49-year-old appeared in court early on Monday and was taken to a hospital the same morning.

4He's being held without bail.

6Knight faces life in prison if convicted of killing Terry Carter, 55, during an row at a Compton burger stand in late January.
7Kenner has claimed Knight was ambushed, but that hasn't been said in court yet.
8Knight claims he is blind in one eye and has about 15% vision in the other.
9This is the third time that he has been taken to hospital from court since he was charged with murder in early February.
10Last year he says he was shot six times and has a blood clot in his lungs along with other medical complications.
11He also told the judge that he lost weight as a result of his injuries.
12Brandlin transferred Knight's case to another judge but he left for medical care before his case could be called in Judge Ronald Coen's courtroom.
13Kenner told the judge that Knight has been jailed for too long and there should be a hearing to set bail as soon as possible.
14Knight's next hearing will be on 9 March, although a bail hearing may not happen until 20 March.
1Mourners queued from the early hours of Tuesday morning to file past the coffin of the opposition leader Boris Nemtsov, lying in state in central Moscow.
2The former deputy prime minister and long-time critic of Russian President Vladimir Putin was shot dead last Friday close to the Kremlin.
3Gennady Gudkov, a prominent Kremlin opponent, said in a speech given next to the coffin: "The shots were fired not only at Nemtsov but at all of us, at democracy in Russia."
4One mourner told Sky News' Moscow Correspondent Katie Stallard he was a "great politician" whose death was a "tragedy for Russia".
5Mr Nemtsov was later buried at the Troekurovskoye cemetery on the outskirts of Moscow.
6No suspects have been arrested following his murder, considered the most shocking political assassination of Mr Putin's rule.
7Leading Russian opposition figures have suggested the murder was politically motivated, although authorities deny any involvement, saying it could have been a provocation aimed at tarnishing Mr Putin's image.
8The chief witness to the killing, Mr Nemtsov's Ukrainian girlfriend Anna Duritskaya, who has now left Russia after claiming she was barred from leaving by investigators, has said she did not catch sight of the killers.
9The Kremlin has pledged to hold a full investigation into what Russian foreign minister Sergey Lavrov has described as a "heinous crime".
10Although one mourner told Sky's Katie Stallard she did not feel confident the killers would be brought to justice.
11Several leading international figures are among the crowds of mourners, including former British prime minister Sir John Major and US ambassador John Tefft.
12"If anyone believes that his (Nemtsov's) voice will be silenced by his murder, then I believe they have made a very serious error," the former prime minister told Sky News.
13He called for a "thorough, comprehensive investigation" into the killing.
14Meanwhile, a number of Polish and Latvian officials say they were barred from entering Russia to attend the funeral.
15Poland's foreign ministry said Polish senate speaker Bogdan Borusewicz was refused entry in retaliation for European Union sanctions over Ukraine.
16Latvian MEP Sandra Kalniete said she was not given a reasonable explanation for the ban, but told news agency AFP she thought her "clear and explicit" opposition to Russia's actions in Ukraine may have played a role.
17According to colleagues, Mr Nemtsov had been working on a report which apparently included concrete evidence that Russia was directly involved in the separatist movement which erupted in Ukraine last year.
18He had also spoken of his fear of assassination following the murders of a string of other prominent opposition figures since Mr Putin came to power 15 years ago.
19On Sunday tens of thousands of protesters marched through the streets of central Moscow to honour his legacy.
20Opposition leaders said the protest was also aimed at stopping a "campaign of hate" directed at those who question Mr Putin's rule.
21Many of the demonstrators carried portraits Mr Nemtsov, as well as placards declaring "I am not afraid" and "He died for Russia's future".
