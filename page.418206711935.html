3Cleveland Cavaliers owner Dan Gilbert promised LeBron James he would do whatever it took financially to surround him with a team capable of ending the city's 51-year championship drought.
4On the first day of free agency, Gilbert spent nearly a quarter of a billion dollars to back that up.
5All without addressing James' contract yet.
6The Cavaliers agreed to terms with Kevin Love on a maximum contract of five years and more than $110 million and gave swingman Iman Shumpert a four-year, $40 million deal, two people with knowledge of the negotiations told The Associated Press on Wednesday.
7The people requested anonymity because the deals had not been announced.
8It was also reported by several outlets, first by ESPN, that forward Tristan Thompson will sign a five-year, $80 million deal, bringing Gilbert's financial outlay to a whopping $230 million before the NBA's fiscal year was even 24 hours old.
9The contracts cannot be signed until July 9, when the salary cap is established and it becomes clearer just how steep a tax bill Gilbert will be facing.
10But that was all part of the plan when James decided to return home last summer.
11He made it clear what his expectations were, and Gilbert didn't hesitate to oblige.
12After James carried the injury-plagued Cavaliers through the playoffs to a six-game loss to Golden State in the finals, he chose not to exercise the option on his contract for next season and become a free agent.
13He never intended to leave, but the message was clear: make it happen.
14Gilbert and GM David Griffin certainly did on Day 1.
15The day started with Love announcing in The Players Tribune that he was coming back to the Cavaliers after a few weeks of speculation that he could head somewhere else after one year with the Cavs.
16Love's season ended with a dislocated left shoulder in the first round of the playoffs and he had some testy moments with James in their first season together, but he made it clear on Wednesday that Cleveland is where he wanted to be.
17''We're all on the same page and we're all in,'' Love wrote.
18The Lakers, Celtics and Suns were among the teams believed to be interested in Love, who was viewed by many across the league as gettable after he struggled at times to find his place with James and Kyrie Irving.
19But he was spotted at a pool meeting with James and other teammates earlier this week, a meeting he alluded to in the piece he published on Wednesday.
20''Yeah, of course I've heard the free agency rumors,'' Love wrote.
21After being the face of the Timberwolves franchise for six seasons, Love had a difficult time adapting to his new role as the third wheel in Cleveland.
22His averages of 17.5 points, 10.4 rebounds and 13.5 shots per game were all the lowest since he came off the bench as a rookie in Minnesota, and every tweet and quote between him and James was heavily scrutinized and dissected to try to decipher how the two All-Stars were getting along.
23Love said all along that he just wanted to win, and the Cavs turned a worrisome 19-20 start into a blazing 34-9 finish to surge to the No.
242 seed in the Eastern Conference.
25Just as Love was figuring out how best to play with James and Irving and his team entered the postseason for the first time in his seven-year career, it all came to an end.
26Celtics forward Kelly Olynyk pulled Love's shoulder out of its socket during their tense first-round series, ending Love's season.
27Irving was injured in Game 1 of the NBA Finals, and James carried the Cavaliers the rest of the way.
28Watching from behind the bench made Love even more determined to return healthy and give James the help he needs.
29''After Game 1 of the NBA Finals, that's when it really struck me,'' Love wrote.
30''Sitting on the sidelines, I never wanted to play in a game more than that one.
31I had dreamed of playing in the NBA Finals and I just wanted to help my guys win.
32While Love was the scorer from outside and the flashy outlet passer, Thompson provided the toughness and hustle in the paint.
33He averaged 9.6 points and 10.8 rebounds in the playoffs and James said in the postseason that the rugged Thompson, who is represented by the same agency as James, should be a Cavalier for life.
34Shumpert was a key addition midseason, coming over with J.R. Smith in a trade with the New York Knicks to give the Cavaliers some perimeter defense.
35The fourth year of his deal is a player option.
36''You don't buy jewelry in this league, you earn it,'' Shumpert posted on Instagram while shouting out his agency, Relativity Sports.
37So, too, will King James.
38Now that Gilbert has spoken with his wallet and kept the team together, the path is cleared for James to sign another contract.
