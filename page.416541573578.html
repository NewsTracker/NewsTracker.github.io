Amy Winehouse's family criticizes documentary about singer due to premiere at Cannes

1LONDON - The family of Amy Winehouse has criticized a documentary about the late singer that is due to have its premiere at next month's Cannes Film Festival.
2A statement issued Sunday by family spokesman Chris Goodman said director Asif Kapadia's "Amy" is "misleading and contains some basic untruths."
3The statement said the film suggested family members did too little to help the singer, who died in July 2011 at age 27 of accidental alcohol poisoning.
4The soul diva, whose 2006 album "Back to Black" won five Grammy Awards, had battled drug and alcohol abuse for years.
5The family statement said the movie did not reflect the "huge effort from all concerned to help Amy at all stages."
6"Fundamentally, the Winehouse family believes that the film does a disservice to individuals and families suffering from the complicated affliction of addiction," it said.
7The filmmakers said in a statement that they began work with "total objectivity" and the support of the Winehouse family.
8They said the documentary was the result of interviews with about 100 people who knew the singer, including "friends, family, former partners and members of the music industry that worked with her."
9Kapadia won wide acclaim with "Senna," a documentary about the late motor racing champion Ayrton Senna.
10"Amy" is due to screen out of competition at the May 13-24 Cannes festival.
