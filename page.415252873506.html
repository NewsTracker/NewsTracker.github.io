This Congressman Said Wolves Could Solve Homelessness

Apple to replace AT&T on Dow Jones

1Don Young of Alaska this week suggested the furry predators might be a way to solve homelessness.
2Young, a Republican, was carrying on during a House Natural Resources Committee hearing on Thursday and objecting to members of Congress asking for the gray wolf to be a protected species, according to an account in The Washington Post.
3"And we get 79 congressmen sending you a letter, they haven't got a damn wolf in their whole district," he said, aiming his comments at the witness, Interior Secretary Sally Jewell.
4"I'd like to introduce them in your district," Young continued.
5"I'd introduce them to your district and you wouldn't have a homeless problem anymore."
6So what was the congressman actually trying to say?
7The "analogy was purposely hyperbolic to stress the point that these predators pose serious threats to wildlife management and their listing has damaging impacts to local communities," a spokesman for the congressman told the Post.

2Despite being one of the most successful US firms, Apple was excluded from the Dow because its high stock price would have distorted the price-weighted index.
3But a change in the structure of Visa shares, which is in the same sector, has made room for the iPhone maker.
4From 18 March, Visa shares will be split four-for-one, which reduces the weighting of the information technology sector in the overall index.
5"As the largest corporation in the world and a leader in technology, Apple is the clear choice for the Dow Jones Industrial Average," said David Blitzer, chairman of the Index Committee at S&P Dow Jones Indices.
6Apple's own seven-for-one stock split last June also helped pave the way for it to join the Dow Jones.
7The Dow Jones Industrial Average is calculated by taking the sum of the share prices of its 30 stocks and dividing the total by a number known as the Dow Divisor.
8The divisor is continually adjusted to accommodate structural changes to companies and to maintain continuity.
9As the Dow includes only 30 companies, critics argue it does not accurately reflect the broader market.
10The S&P index by contrast includes 500 stocks.
11"This brings the Dow into reality and the 21st Century," said Richard Sichel, chief investment officer at Philadelphia Trust Co, referring to Apple's entry.
12"It will make the Dow a more interesting index to watch, but also more volatile since it is replacing a nice, steady old name with an interesting and exciting tech and retail company."
13With a market value of around $736bn, Apple is the biggest US company measured by market capitalisation.
14Since the launch of the iPhone in June 2007 its shares have risen 700%.
15AT&T is worth $176.5bn and its exit from the Dow Industrials leaves Verizon as the only telecoms company on the index.
16AT&T had an almost unbroken run on the Dow stock from 1916 with only a brief absence in 2004 to 2005.
