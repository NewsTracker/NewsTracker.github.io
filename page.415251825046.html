5I am really excited to be the Artist-in-Residence for the next three months; what a great blog and forum for sharing thoughts and ideas on being a parent.
6A little bit about me: I grew up on a horse farm surrounded by woods and fields.
7In this natural setting, I played with my three siblings, burying rodent skulls and handcrafted treasure maps, building miniature creek dams and snow forts.
8The slower pace of childhood and time spent in nature are often themes in my work.
9Now, as a mother of two, I encourage my children to explore the outdoors and use their imaginations.
10Since becoming a mother, my art has switched focus from large installation and abstract work to portraits and painting the little fleeting moments of childhood and these early years of motherhood.
11My goal this year is to finish and publish my own children's books as well as illustrating books for others.
12I recently finished illustrating "None for Me Thanks" with my friend and neighbor, Margaret Gilmour.
13It's our first book.
14We're creating a series, about two young neighbors, Otto and Iris, who form an unlikely bond through their imaginative play in nature.
15For now, I'm enjoying this work, commission projects like portraits and landscapes, and art I create and sell through my shop on Etsy.
16My big, long term dream is to curate a children's museum composed of artist installations: a place of magic, and of wonder.
17The Motherlode blog welcomes our new artist-in-residence, illustrator Abigail Gray Swartz.
