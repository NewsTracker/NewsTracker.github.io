Burundi takes to Facebook to declare coup 'fanciful'

1A protester stands in front of a burned barricade during a protest against Burundian President Pierre Nkurunziza's decision to run for a third term in Bujumbura, Burundi on Wednesday.
2Johannesburg: The president's office of the Republic of Burundi took to Twitter to deny there had been a military coup on Wednesday and again, later, to announce it had survived it.
3After a top general announced on radio that President Pierre Nkurunziza had been overthrown, the presidency tweeted that there was no coup and the situation was under control.
4It then tweeted again, tagging several journalists, stating the attempted coup had failed.
5The presidency then issued a statement on Facebook, declaring the coup to be "fanciful" and announcing those responsible would be arrested and face justice.
6It said steps had been taken to maintain national security and called for calm.
7A female protester holds an ax during a protest against Burundian President Pierre Nkurunziza's decision to run for a third term in Bujumbura, Burundi.
8It was not clear as night fell who would end up in control of the nation.
9Burundi, situated in one of East Africa's most volatile regions, is experiencing its worst crisis since the end of civil war in 2006, triggered by Mr Nkurunziza's determination to seek a third five-year term in office.
10The country has seen weeks of protests over his bid, which opponents say violates the constitution and the Arusha Agreement, which ended the war.
11Mr Nkurunziza's supporters contend there's no violation because he was elected by parliament in his first term of office, not by the public.
12Burundi's President Pierre Nkurunziza.
13With Mr Nkurunziza in Tanzania, where African leaders were holding a crisis meeting to discuss the situation in his country, Major General Godefroid Niyombareh announced that the president had been overthrown and a "salvation committee" had been set up to rule.
14The general had been sacked by the president as intelligence chief in February.
15"Active forces of the nation have decided to take charge of the country," General Niyombareh said in a radio address.
16He said the aim of the move was to "restore national unity and resume the electoral process in a peaceful and fair environment".
17In a statement to reporters at an air base, he accused the president of "arrogance and defiance of the international community".
18Later, he announced that the airport and borders had been closed.
19At least 20 people have been killed in recent demonstrations, as police have at times opened fire on protesters using live ammunition.
20Demonstrators take part in a protest in Bujumbura, Burundi, on Wednesday.
21Earlier on Wednesday, police fired tear gas to disperse crowds, but after the coup was announced police presence melted away, as protesters celebrated in the streets, news agencies reported.
22There were reports of gunfire in the capital as troops surrounded the state-owned radio station.
23Mr Nkurunziza has been under international pressure to postpone presidential elections scheduled for June 26, amid fears the unrest over his third term bid could see the country slide back into civil war.
24The European Union, African Union and United States have called for the ballot to be postponed.
25The top US diplomat for Africa, Linda Thomas-Greenfield, flew to Tanzania to attend Wednesday's emergency meeting in the capital, Dar es Salaam.
26A State Department statement said she would express US concern about the situation in Burundi and support the Arusha Agreement.
27She would press for political dialogue among all parties to ensure peaceful, credible and inclusive elections, the statement said.
28US.officials have suggested Mr Nkurunziza's third term bid violates the Arusha Agreement.
29Last week in a television interview African Union Chairwoman Nkosazana Dlamini-Zuma lquestioned the legality of Mr Nkurunziza's bid for a new term.
30Mr Nkurunziza, 51, promised he wouldn't seek a fourth term, but pressed ahead with his third term bid.
