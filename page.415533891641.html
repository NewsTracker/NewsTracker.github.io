1ABUJA (Reuters) - The Nigerian army said on Tuesday it had repelled Boko Haram from all but three local government districts in the northeast, claiming victory for its offensive against the Islamist insurgents less than two weeks before a presidential election.
2At the start of this year, Boko Haram controlled around 20 local government areas, a territory the size of Belgium, in its bloody six-year-old campaign to carve out an Islamic state in religiously mixed Nigeria.
3But a concerted push by Nigeria's military and neighbors Chad, Cameroon and Niger has regained considerable ground.
4At the weekend, Nigerian government forces recaptured the city of Bama, the second biggest in northeasterly Borno state.
5"We have three local governments remaining, Abadam, Kala-Balge and Gwoza, and we are optimistic that with time we will liberate those," Lieutenant-General Tobiah Minimah, the army chief of staff, told reporters.
6President Goodluck Jonathan, who is running for re-election, had been criticized for not doing enough to tackle the insurgency.
7His challenger, Muhammadu Buhari, has been campaigning on his reputation for being tough on security matters when he was military ruler of Nigeria in the 1980s.
8It is not clear how much impact the military advances will boost Jonathan's election chances in the tightly fought race.
9The militants were progressively chased out of Adamawa and Yobe states since the start of the year, and cornered into an ever shrinking area of Borno, the heartland of their insurgency.
10The elections were postponed in February until March 28, with the military citing Boko Haram as warranting the delay.
11The electoral commission plans to set up polling booths in camps for the more than a million Nigerians displaced by the fighting, but there are fears that potentially millions of people may be unable to vote in areas affected by the violence.
12Asked whether the vote could hold in the recaptured areas, Minimah said that would be up to the electoral commission.
13"The areas have been liberated, but structures of governance ... will need to be reinstated so citizens can go back to their areas and (vote). How soon? I don't know."
14Boko Haram's worsening atrocities against civilians hit international headlines in April last year, when the militants kidnapped more than 200 schoolgirls from the village of Chibok, triggering a search for them that has been fruitless thus far.
15Minimah said there was no news on the whereabouts of the Chibok girls for now, but that the military was optimistic they would be able to get more information on it soon.
