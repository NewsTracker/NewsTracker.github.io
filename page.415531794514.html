Robert Durst mixed up in story as Fred Durst of Limp Bizkit

Syria monitor says army kills six in gas attack, military denies it

1The Associated Press accidentally mixed up troubled millionaire real estate heir Robert Durst, 71, and 44-year-old Limp Bizkit frontman Fred Durst.
2The misidentification came rollin' in around 6:30 p.m. local California time Monday in the second paragraph of a story about drug charges filed against Robert Durst in New Orleans, according to the media blog Romenesko.
3The first line referenced Robert Durst by name, but the second referred to him as "the former Limp Bizkit frontman."
4The AP later limped out a correction and told the blog in a statement that the mix-up was in "a brief that appeared solely on the California broadcast wire last night."
5Robert Durst is awaiting extradition to Los Angeles to face a murder charge stemming from the 2000 killing of a close female friend.
6His arrest Saturday in the Big Easy came a day before the finale of a shocking, six-part HBO documentary called "The Jinx" in which the oddball suspect appeared to confess he "killed them all, of course."
7"He did not kill Susan Berman, and he doesn't know who did," Durst's high-powered lawyer Dick DeGuerin told the Daily News on Monday.
8Limp Bizkit, an alt-metal band that rose to fame in the mid-1990s with hits like "Nookie" and "Break Stuff," is currently on tour in California.
1A Syrian military source described the report of an attack in the village of Sarmin in Idlib province as propaganda.
2"We confirm that we would not use this type of weapon, and we don't need to use it," the source said.
3President Bashar al-Assad's government has previously denied accusations that it has used chemical weapons against rebel-held areas in the four-year-old war.
4Officials were not available for comment.
5An army statement said dozens of militants were killed in other areas of Idlib province overnight in clashes and attacks.
6The Britain-based Syrian Observatory for Human Rights, which tracks the conflict through a network of sources, said the six dead included a man, his wife and their three children.
7It cited medical sources as saying they died as a result of gas from barrel bombs dropped late on Monday and that the chemical used was likely chlorine.
8Dozens more were wounded in the attack, the Observatory said.
9Reuters could not independently verify the report.
10The Idlib branch of the Syrian Civil Defence rescue organization, which operates in insurgent-held areas, posted seven videos on YouTube, some at nighttime and some in a medical center.
11One video showed three children and a woman, all apparently unconscious, in a medical center.
12A voice off camera said the name of the village, Sarmin, and Monday's date.
13"One of the infants, only a few months old," a male voice says, shaking, as he films a baby on a gurney with liquid around its mouth.
14Two more infants with limp bodies are brought in, one by a man wearing a gas mask and another carrying a young girl.
15"She's still alive doctor," a man checking the girl says.
16"Doctor, doctor, she is still breathing."
17The Syrian Civil Defence includes more than 2,000 humanitarian volunteers, known as the "White Helmets" for the hard hats they wear, who work as first responders in a country where the medical infrastructure has broken down.
18Another video posted by the group and shot at night showed a very young girl, naked except for underwear and pink shoes, being doused in liquid by people wearing white helmets, her horrified expression illuminated by their headlamps shining on her face.
19The Syrian Civil Defence said on Twitter there had been more than 100 casualties, including its own volunteers.
20Kurds in neighboring Iraq have said that Islamic State insurgents used chlorine gas against military police and peshmerga forces in December and January.
21An officer said the gas was used three times, and that a number of military police were treated in hospital.
22The United States is aware of the reports and is seeking additional information, White House spokesman Josh Earnest told reporters on Tuesday.
23"If these allegations are confirmed, this would tragically be only the latest example of the Assad regime's atrocities against the Syrian people," Earnest said.
24A U.N. inquiry found in late 2013 that sarin gas had likely been used in several rebel-held districts of the Syrian capital Damascus in August 2013, where hundreds of people were killed.
25The inquiry was only looking at whether chemical weapons were used, not who used them.
26The government and the opposition have each accused the other of using chemical weapons during the civil war which has killed 200,000.
27Both sides deny it.
28Assad agreed with the United States and Russia to dispose of his chemical weapons after hundreds of people were killed by sarin gas on the outskirts of the capital.
29The Organization for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons also said it had "compelling evidence" that chlorine gas was used in rebel-held villages in northern Syria last year.
30It did not say who carried out the attack.
31Officials in Washington regularly accuse Assad of gassing his own people and say he has lost all legitimacy.
32But U.S. Secretary of State John Kerry said on Sunday that Assad should be included in negotiations to reach a political transition, although a spokeswoman later explained that Kerry was not specifically referring to Assad.
33The Syrian Foreign Ministry said on Tuesday that France and Britain were "still engaging in policies that embroil the European Union in the ongoing aggression against Syria."
34The statement, released after a cabinet meeting, appears to be an attempt to drive a wedge between Assad's biggest enemies in Europe, Britain and France, and other European Union states.
35The Syrian government has said it is willing to reengage with countries who stop supporting insurgents.
36Opposition activists have reported many chlorine attacks over the past year, which they said occurred when explosive canisters were dropped from army helicopters.
37Khaled Khoja, president of the opposition National Coalition, blamed Assad on Tuesday for the most recent reported attack: "The psychopathic dictator continues to kill by chemicals and challenges the international laws."
38Hamish de Bretton-Gordon, an expert on biological and chemical weapons, said there had been several chlorine bomb attacks in recent days in the area.
39"On the face of it, the video of the attack looks genuine. While chlorine itself it not that deadly, it is being very effectively used as a weapon of terror, first in Syria and now in Iraq. It killed three children, who are too little to run away after an attack."
