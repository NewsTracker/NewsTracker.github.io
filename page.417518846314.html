Apple Watch to hit retail stores on June 26

1Customer Hajime Shimada uses his newly purchased Apple Watch in front of Dover Street Market Ginza in Tokyo April 24, 2015.
2Apple Inc said it would start selling some models of its watch at its retail stores this month, and also roll out the gadget in seven more countries.
3The watch has been on display in Apple stores around the world since April 10, when it became available for preorder online and at shops including trendy fashion boutiques in Paris, London and Tokyo.
4Apple had directed people to order online, preventing long queues around its stores that have become a norm with the company's rollout of new products.
5"We believe making the Apple Watch available for customers to purchase at an Apple retail store will drive increased sales volumes," Cantor Fitzgerald analysts wrote in a note.
6The company said all orders, except one model, placed through May would be shipped to customers within two weeks.
7"The response to Apple Watch has surpassed our expectations in every way," said Jeff Williams, senior vice president of operations.
8"We're also making great progress with the backlog of Apple Watch orders."
9The company has not given any sales figures for the watch since it began taking orders, but has often said demand was outstripping supply.
10"We believe the company has made significant progress in reducing its backlog as production ramps," S&P Capital IQ analyst Angelo Zino said in an email.
11Shipments of at least 30 million watches were plausible next year, Zino said.
12Apple Watch will be launched in Italy, Mexico, Spain, South Korea, Singapore, Switzerland and Taiwan on June 26, the company said.
13The watch is currently available in the United States, Australia, Canada, China, France, Germany, Hong Kong, Japan and the UK.
14Apart from online stores, customers in these countries can also buy the devices at Apple's retail stores and some authorized resellers.
