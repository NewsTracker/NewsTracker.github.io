Google profit tops analyst estimates as company curbs costs

1Google profit tops analyst estimates as company curbs costs Add to...
2Google Inc.'s second-quarter earnings topped analysts' estimates amid slower spending, reassuring investors who had been concerned about the Internet search giant's ability to keep costs in check.
3Profit before certain items was $6.99 (U.S.) a share, the company said in a statement Thursday.
4Sales, minus revenue passed on to partners, rose 13 per cent to $14.35-billion.
5Analysts on average had projected $6.73 a share in profit on $14.3-billion in sales, according to data compiled by Bloomberg.
6Chief executive officer Larry Page has been seeking to manage costs even as the company has stepped up investments in projects outside its main Web search-advertising business.
7Operating expenses rose 13 per cent from a year earlier, a smaller jump than the 21-per-cent increase in the first quarter.
8The company in May brought in new chief financial officer Ruth Porat from Morgan Stanley, and investors expect her to seek ways to rein in expenses.
9The shares of Mountain View, Calif.-based Google rose as much as 8 per cent in extended trading after the report.
10The stock gained 3.1 per cent to $601.78 at the close in New York, leaving it up 13 per cent this year.
11The number of clicks on ads in the second quarter rose 18 per cent, compared with a 13-per-cent increase in the first quarter, while the average cost per click fell 11 per cent after declining 7 per cent in the first quarter.
12Second-quarter profit was $3.93-billion, compared with $3.35-billion a year earlier, Google said.
13Porat brings extensive experience with Wall Street after holding the CFO position at Morgan Stanley.
14She replaced Patrick Pichette, who had emphasized during recent quarterly earnings calls that the company is committed to managing costs.
15Porat inherits an increasingly complex company that has been slow to break out more financial details about key services, such as video-sharing unit YouTube and mobile advertising.
16The company has expanded its reach after investments in fledgling businesses - such as driverless cars and Google Glass computerized eyewear - and sometimes hefty acquisitions - such as Nest Labs, which makes Internet-connected gear for the home.
17Google also has devoted money to improving its core advertising services, including new tools to enable purchases directly from ads and features that aim to make the buying process simpler for marketers.
