Canada votes for air strikes on Islamic State in Syria

1OTTAWA: Canadian lawmakers voted on Monday to extend a campaign of airstrikes against the Islamic State group and for the first time strike at them in Syria as well as in Iraq.
2Prime Minister Stephen Harper's Conservatives, wielding a majority in the house of commons, pushed through a motion on enlarging the mission in a vote 142 to 129, despite opposition parties' vigorous objections.
3"Our government believes that we must act to protect Canadians against the threat of terrorism at home and abroad," he said.
4Canada first joined the US-led airstrikes on the IS group in November.
5Its expanded air campaign was authorized until March 30, 2016.
6Harper has defended the need for sorties into Syria, saying the IS group "must cease to have any safe haven in Syria."
7He pointed to its movement of heavy equipment across the Iraqi border into Syria.
8The prime minister also noted that IS fighters have threatened Canada in propaganda videos.
9"As a result of ISIL's specific threats against Canada and Canadians, our Government has worked closely for the past six months as part of a broad international Coalition, including our closest allies, to help degrade and disrupt ISIL's ability to inflict harm.
10At least six Canadians have died over the last two years fighting alongside extremists in Syria and Iraq.
11In the aftermath of two jihadist-inspired attacks in Ottawa and rural Quebec last October, a majority of Canadians have told pollsters they support the military mission against the IS group.
12Opposition parties, however, warned that airstrikes against the IS group in Syria may implicitly aid Syrian President Bashar al-Assad, and embroil Canada in a regional conflict that could drag on for decades.
13New Democratic Party Leader Thomas Mulcair decried the move "expanding a misguided war in Iraq to a dangerous new phase in Syria."
14"This is simply not Canada's war to fight," he said.
15Canadian airstrikes in Syria are not authorized by the United Nations, nor is it a NATO mission.
16Canada has also deployed about 70 special forces soldiers to train Kurds in northern Iraq.
17They came under mortar and machine gun fire while training Iraqi troops near the frontlines in mid-January, and a Canadian soldier was killed by friendly fire on March 6.
18Harper said that militarily, the "mission will remain focused on targeting ISIL from the air and advising and assisting Iraqi Forces to fight ISIL more effectively on the ground."
