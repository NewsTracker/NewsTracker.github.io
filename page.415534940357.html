1Syria claims to shoot down U.S. aircraft Syrian state media said the country's air defenses shot down a U.S. reconnaissance aircraft Tuesday in a northwestern province along the Mediterranean coast.
2The Pentagon said Tuesday it is looking into a report by Syrian state media that Syrian defense forces shot down a U.S. aircraft over a northwestern province along the Mediterranean coast.
3The Defense Department said in a statement that U.S. military controllers lost contact with an unarmed and unmanned Predator drone operating over northwest Syria at approximately 1:40 p.m. ET Tuesday.
4The department said it did not have information to corroborate press reports that the aircraft was shot down and was looking into the incident to determine what happened.
5The SANA state news agency said the aircraft was downed north of the coastal city of Latakia but provided no other details, the Associated Press reported.
6State television broadcast footage of what it said was the wreckage, including a wheel and electronic parts.
7Soldiers in camouflage could be seen loading some of the debris into the back of a truck, according to the AP.
8If confirmed that the drone was shot down, it would mark the first time an American aircraft has been downed over Syria since the U.S.launched airstrikes against the Islamic State militant group in Syria last September.
9U.S. aircraft in Syria have avoided targeting the military forces of Syrian President Bashar Assad's regime, which is in the fifth year of a civil war against the Islamic State and other rebel groups.
