Shocks terrify survivors of Nepal quake that killed 2,500

1Shell-shocked and sleeping in the streets, tens of thousands of Nepalis braced against terrifying aftershocks Sunday while digging for survivors of the massive earthquake that ripped across this Himalayan nation a day earlier, killing more than 2,500 people.
2Acrid, white smoke rose above Nepal's most revered Hindu temple, where dozens of bodies were being cremated at any given time.
3Aid groups received the first word from remote mountain villages - reports that suggested many communities perched on mountainsides were devastated or struggling to cope.
4Landslides hindered rescue teams that tried to use mountain trails to reach those in need, said Prakash Subedi, chief district official in the Gorkha region, where the quake was centered.
5"Villages like this are routinely affected by landslides, and it's not uncommon for entire villages of 200, 300, up to 1,000 people to be completely buried by rock falls," said Matt Darvas, a member of the aid group World Vision.
6"It will likely be helicopter access only."
7Saturday's magnitude 7.8 earthquake spread horror from Kathmandu to small villages and to the slopes of Mount Everest, triggering an avalanche that buried part of the base camp packed with foreign climbers preparing to make their summit attempts.
8At least 18 people died there and 61 were injured.
9With people fearing more quakes, tens of thousands spent the day crowding in the streets and the night sleeping in parks or on a golf course.
10Others camped in open squares lined by cracked buildings and piles of rubble.
11Helicopter blades thudded periodically overhead.
12Crows screeched as the ground shook with the worst of the aftershocks - magnitude 6.7.
13Panicked residents raced outdoors.
14"We don't feel safe at all. There have been so many aftershocks. It doesn't stop," said Rajendra Dhungana, 34, who spent the day with his niece's family for her cremation at the Pashuputi Nath Temple in Katmandu.
15"I've watched hundreds of bodies burn. I never thought I'd see so many ... Nepal should learn a lesson from this. They should realize proper buildings should be built. There should be open spaces people can run to."
16By late Sunday, the aftershocks appeared to be weakening.
17A magnitude 5.3 quake shook an area east of Kathmandu.
18Nepal authorities said Sunday that at least 2,430 people died in that country alone, not including the 18 dead in the avalanche.
19Another 61 people died from the quake in India and a few in other neighboring countries.
20At least 1,152 people died in Kathmandu, and the number of injured nationwide was upward of 5,900.
21With search-and-rescue efforts far from over, it was unclear how much the death toll would rise.
22Three policemen died during a rescue effort in Kathmandu, police spokesman Komal Singh Bam said.
23The capital city is largely a collection of small, poorly constructed brick apartment buildings.
24But outside of the oldest neighborhoods, many in Kathmandu were surprised by how few modern structures collapsed in the quake.
25While aid workers cautioned that many buildings could have sustained serious structural damage, it was also clear that the death toll would have been far higher had more buildings caved in.
26Aid workers also warned that the situation could be far worse near the epicenter.
27The U.S. Geological Survey said the quake was centered near Lamjung, about 80 kilometers (50 miles) northwest of Kathmandu.
28In the aftermath, United Nations officials were concerned about thunderstorms that could threaten the many people staying outdoors and about a shortage of vaccines that could invite disease.
29As planeloads of supplies, doctors and relief workers arrived from neighboring countries, journalists reported on social media that aftershocks forced some aircraft to circle the Kathmandu airport while waiting to land.
30The earthquake was the worst to hit the South Asian nation in more than 80 years.
31It destroyed swaths of the oldest neighborhoods of Kathmandu and was strong enough to be felt all across parts of India, Bangladesh, China's region of Tibet and Pakistan.
32Nepal's worst recorded earthquake in 1934 measured 8.0 and all but destroyed the cities of Kathmandu, Bhaktapur and Patan.
33Rescuers aided by international teams spent Sunday digging through rubble of buildings - concrete slabs, bricks, iron beams, wood - to look for survivors.
34Because the air was filled with chalky concrete dust, many people wore breathing masks or held shawls over their faces.
35Hundreds of people in Kathmandu's western Kalanki neighborhood nervously watched the slow progress of a single backhoe digging into the rubble of the collapsed Lumbini Guest House, once a three-story budget hotel.
36Police officer RP Dhamala, who was coordinating the rescue efforts, said they had already pulled out 12 people alive and six dead.
37He said rescuers were still searching for about 20 people believed to be trapped but had heard no cries, taps or noises for a while.
38Most areas were without power and water.
39The United Nations said hospitals in the Kathmandu Valley were overcrowded and running out of emergency supplies and space to store corpses.
40Most shops in Kathmandu were closed after the government declared a weeklong period of recovery.
41Only fruit vendors and pharmacies seemed to be doing business.
42"More people are coming now," fruit seller Shyam Jaiswal said.
43"They cannot cook so they need to buy something they can eat raw."
44The quake will probably put a huge strain on the resources of this impoverished country best known for Everest, the highest mountain in the world.
45The economy of Nepal, a nation of 27.8 million people, relies heavily on tourism, principally trekking and Himalayan mountain climbing.
46The first nations to respond were Nepal's neighbors - India, China and Pakistan, all of which have been jockeying for influence over the landlocked nation.
47Nepal remains closest to India, with which it shares deep political, cultural and religious ties.
48India suffered its own losses from the quake, with at least 61 people killed there and dozens injured.
49Sunday's aftershock was also widely felt in the country, and local news reports said metro trains in New Delhi and Kolkata were briefly shut down when the shaking started.
50Other countries sending support Sunday included the United States, Canada, the United Arab Emirates, Britain, Germany, France, Poland, Italy, Israel and Singapore.
51An American military plane left Delaware's Dover Air Force Base for Nepal, carrying 70 people, including a disaster-assistance response team and an urban search-and-rescue team, and 45 tons of cargo, the Pentagon said.
