Robert Durst's re-emergence puts spotlight on Fox News' Jeanine Pirro, a former prosecutor

1She has spent 15 years on Durst's trail, making him wary of her and giving her a unique perch from which to comment on Durst's latest arrest for the Fox News Channel, where she's the host of "Justice with Judge Jeanine."
2The re-emergence of Durst in HBO's "The Jinx" and his recent arrest in a California murder case has once again brightened the spotlight on the tough-talking Pirro after a career that made plenty of headlines.
3That has included a painful U.S. Senate run against Hillary Rodham Clinton, a spot on People magazine's "most beautiful people" list and winning re-election as district attorney while her husband served a prison term for tax fraud.
4Last weekend, she was portrayed in a "Saturday Night Live" skit as telling Durst, "I'm always right behind you, Robert, and I'm gonna catch you with my own two gorgeously manicured hands."
5She doesn't pretend to be objective.
6In an interview with The Associated Press, she said, "Every ounce of my prosecutorial being says this guy is an evil person."
7She insists he was responsible for the death of his first wife, though he's never been charged; should not have been acquitted in the killing and dismemberment of a Texas neighbor; and was behind the killing of his friend in California.
8In 2000, Pirro, then the district attorney in Westchester County, New York, reopened the cold case of the 1982 disappearance of Durst's first wife, Kathie.
9Durst was never charged, but Pirro refused to rule him out as a suspect.
10The case has since been declared a homicide.
11Pirro told The AP she was driven to solve the killing partly because of a connection she felt with Kathie Durst.
12"She was in med school not long after I was in law school," Pirro said.
13"There really weren't a lot of women in professional schools in those days. We were both trying to make it in a man's world."
14As part of the reopened investigation, Pirro said, she planned to question Susan Berman, who was Durst's confidante and spokeswoman at the time of Kathie Durst's disappearance.
15On Christmas Eve 2000, Berman was shot to death in her home.
16"The first person I thought of was Robert Durst," Pirro said.
17Durst was charged last month with Berman's murder.
18In 2003, when Durst went on trial for another killing - his admitted shooting and dismemberment of a neighbor, Morris Black - Pirro made several appearances in the courtroom in Galveston, Texas.
19Durst claimed self-defense, arguing he cut up the body in a panic and fled because he knew Pirro was looking into his wife's death and no one would believe him.
20That was also why he sometimes posed as a woman, his lawyer said.
21The judge included Pirro in a gag order, and Durst's lawyer said in closing arguments, "If Ms. Pirro kept her mouth shut, none of this would have happened."
22Durst's acquittal astonished Pirro.
23"I believe that jury in Galveston got that just plain wrong," she said.
24Pirro, a popular Republican in an increasingly Democratic county, won three terms as district attorney.
25But her political career was probably stunted by her husband, attorney Albert Pirro, who acknowledged he fathered a child with another woman and was convicted in 2000 of tax evasion and conspiracy after an embarrassing trial that laid out the couple's lavish lifestyle.
26She won a third term as district attorney while he was imprisoned.
