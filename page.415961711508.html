Calipari, Kaminsky take top honors from The Associated Press (Yahoo Sports)

1They call him the embodiment of everything that's wrong in the one-and-done world of college basketball.
2He's a crook who landed two programs on probation, a snake-oil salesman who only won a national title because he was able to sell Kentucky to some top prospects.
3Perhaps that perception has finally started to change.
4After molding a roster full of McDonald's All-Americans into a 38-0 juggernaut that's two wins shy of another championship, Calipari was voted AP coach of the year on Friday.
5He received 40 first-place votes from the 65-member national media panel.
6Tony Bennett of Virginia was runner-up with nine votes and Notre Dame's Mike Brey got five.
7Meanwhile, Wisconsin forward Frank Kaminsky was honored as the AP player of the year.
8''I know this, I'm the same guy I've always been,'' Calipari said, when asked whether opinions of him - particularly those in the media - have softened over the years.
9''Well, not really.
10A lot of things change as you get older.
11My heart's the same,'' Calipari added.
12''Hopefully I've matured and grown up a little bit.
13Speaking of growing up, Kaminsky has certainly done that the past couple of years.
14After averaging 10 minutes a game as a sophomore, then having a breakout junior campaign, Kaminsky took another step forward in guiding the Badgers back to the Final Four.
15The senior was rewarded with 58 first-place votes.
16Duke freshman Jahlil Okafor received five, and Willie Cauley-Stein of Kentucky and Jerian Grant of Notre Dame each received one.
17''I thought about it a little bit,'' Kaminsky said of winning the award.
18''Obviously, wasn't one of my main priorities.
19Getting back to the Final Four was it.
20But being here and being honored by the AP is awesome.
21It's hardly surprising that Kaminsky was such an overwhelming pick.
22Besides being a dominant inside-outside threat, the fuzzy 7-footer also has wicked sense of humor.
23He's become one of the ringleaders on the fun-loving Badgers, his effervescent personality endearing himself to coaches, players and fans alike.
24''I saw him out in the hallway,'' said Calipari, whose team plays Wisconsin in the Final Four on Saturday night.
25Probably about as tired as many folks are of seeing Calipari this time of year.
26He began making a habit of the NCAA Tournament with UMass in the late 1980s and early '90s, leading the Minutemen to the Final Four.
27After a failed stint in the NBA, Calipari returned to the college game - and winning - with Memphis in 2000.
28But like his first Final Four appearance, another trip with the Tigers in 2008 was later vacated by the NCAA.
29Calipari's reputation was sullied, even though both transgressions were not entirely his fault: UMass star Marcus Camby was ruled ineligible for contact with an agent, and allegations of academic fraud unrelated to the university brought down Memphis.
30''Fifty years from now when we're all gone,'' Calipari said, ''people will look back without emotion and say, 'What has he done?
31What did he do for people?
32What did he do for the universities?' Not just me, but all coaches.
33At least this season, by getting what could have been a mess of egos to play as a team.
34Players accustomed to being the stars accepted fewer minutes and bit roles.
35They decided that in basketball-mad Kentucky, they would eschew any individual spotlight so the Wildcats could chase something greater: The first perfect season since Indiana's in 1976.
36In that respect, this may have been Calipari's toughest coaching job yet.
