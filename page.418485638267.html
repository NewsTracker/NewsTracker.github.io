Mexico finishes in a disappointing 0-0 tie with Guatemala

1Mexico forward Carlos Vela (11) gets tackled by Guatemala defenseman Jorge Aparicio during the first half of a CONCACAF Gold Cup soccer match Sunday, July 12, 2015, in Glendale, Ariz.
2Mexico midfielder Andres Guardado kicks the ball in front of Guatemala defender Deniss Lopez (15) during the first half of a CONCACAF Gold Cup soccer match Sunday, July 12, 2015, in Glendale, Ariz.
3Mexico dominated play but couldn't penetrate the Guatemala defense and the teams finished in a 0-0 tie in a CONCACAF Gold Cup match on Sunday night.
4Mexico, one of the Gold Cup favorites, was frustrated by a series of near misses in front of a sellout crowd of 62,910, almost all the fans decked out in the red, white or green colors of Mexico.
5The crowd booed heartily when the game came to an end, while the tiny contingent of Guatemala fans cheered as if it was a victory.
6Mexico is 1-0-1 in Group C competition, Guatemala 0-1-1.
7Mexico plays Trinidad and Tobago (2-0) on Wednesday night in Charlotte, North Carolina.
8Guatemala faces Cuba (0-2).
9Nearly all of the game was played in Mexico's offensive end of the field.
10Giovani Dos Santos' shot that bounced off the crossbar exemplified the team's woes.
11The fans had shouted "Gio! Gio!" before coach Miguel Herrera finally sent Dos Santos into the game in the 82nd minute.
12Five minutes later, Dos Santos slammed a shot from in front of the net just outside the penalty area but it ricocheted off the crossbar.
13It was one of a handful of near misses for Mexico, which finished with four shots on goal.
14Guatemala had no shots on goal and really only one opportunity that came close.
15It came early, when Mexico turned the ball over at midfield.
16Guatemala's Minor Lopez got the ball in front of the net but his kick only grazed it.
17Still, it bounced just outside of the goal.
18The rest of the game, Guatemala settled for playing defense.
19Mexico couldn't score even with a man advantage when Jose Contreras drew a red card, for his second yellow, in the 77th minute.
20Guatemala slowed the game even more in the four minutes of extra time.
21When fans thought Carlos Vela had been fouled late in the game for what would have been a penalty kick, and none was called, many tossed their hats from the upper deck of University of Phoenix Stadium in protest.
22Mexico had two other near misses.
23In the 36th minute, Oribe Peralta sent a cross to team captain Andres Guardado in front of the net, but Guatemala keeper Paulo Motta charged toward the ball and deflected Guardado's left-footed attempt.
24Much later, in the 76th minute, Hector Herrera sent a header to Peralta, who pushed the ball with his left foot just wide of the net.
25In the first half, Mexico had three shots on goal and Guatemala none.
26Mexico managed one shot on goal in the second half.
27Guardado drove the ball into the net in the 40th minute but it was waved off because a foul had been called against Mexico well before the kick.
28Mexico is without Real Madrid standout Javier Hernandez.
29The Mexican known to fans as "Chicharito" fractured his collarbone in a Gold Cup warmup friendly against Honduras.
30The Mexican national team, known as "El Tri," opened Gold Cup competition with a 6-0 rout of Cuba in Chicago on Thursday.
31Guatemala lost to Trinidad and Tobago 3-1.
32In the first match of Sunday's doubleheader, Trinidad and Tobago defeated Cuba 2-0.
33The Cuban team has had two players defect to the United States - striker Keilen Garcia and midfielder Arael Arguellez.
