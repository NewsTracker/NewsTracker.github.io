Apple to replace some iPhone 6 Plus cameras over blurry photos

1A woman holds up the iPhone 6 Plus at the Apple Store at Tokyo's Omotesando shopping district September 19, 2014.
2Apple Inc said it would replace a limited number iPhone 6 Plus phone cameras due to faulty back cameras that take blurry photos.
3The affected phones were mostly sold in a 4-month period between September 2014 and January 2015, Apple said on its website.
4The company, whose shares were set to open at their lowest this year on Monday, said a small component in the affected 6 Plus's iSight back camera may fail.
5Apple said it would replace the phone's camera free of charge if it takes blurry photos and falls into a particular serial number range.
6Eligible serial numbers can be checked on Apple's website.
