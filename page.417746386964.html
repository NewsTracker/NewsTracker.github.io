City paid $30K to settle Ebola fears lawsuit over girl, 7

1MILFORD, Conn.
2(AP) - The insurance carrier for a Connecticut city paid $30,000 to settle a lawsuit filed by the family of a third-grader who was barred from school for several days because of Ebola fears.
3School officials in Milford initially told 7-year-old Ikeoluwa Opayemi to stay out of school for three weeks last October after she attended a family wedding in Nigeria.
4The girl showed no symptoms of Ebola and Nigeria had no known cases of the virus.
5She was allowed to return to school after her father filed a federal lawsuit.
6The New Haven Register sought the agreement under the Freedom of Information Act.
7The newspaper (http://bit.ly/1L5vBpT ) reports the lawsuit was settled on Oct. 30 and the city's insurance carrier sent a check for $30,000 to the attorney for the family.
