Nepal quake survivors recount horror

1Survivors of the deadly Nepal earthquake have started recounting the chaotic moment it struck.
2Camille Thomas was in Langtang village, just north of the capital Kathmandu, when the 7.8-magnitude earthquake struck on Saturday, claiming the lives of more than 2500 people.
3"It was pretty scary, pretty horrible, nothing you can really explain," Ms Thomas told 3AW radio on Monday.
4Ms Thomas said when the owner of her guest house started urging her and her friend to find cover, she thought it was a joke.
5"My friend and I sort laughed about it. We thought it was something small, you know. We ran and hid under some stuff and it all started coming down.
6"Snow and rocks and houses, everything. An avalanche."
7Ms Thomas said the surrounding area was blanketed with snow after the quake, which destroyed villages around Lantang.
8"From where we were, there was nothing you could see. All the villages were gone," she said.
9Len Forder said his 20-year-old daughter Ballantyne, who was volunteering at an orphanage in Nepal, managed to contact her family on Sunday.
10"We really haven't spoken with Billie but there has been contact, we do know she's alive," Mr Forder told 6PR radio station in Perth.
11"We're not out of the woods by any means, we've still got to get her to safety.
12"She wouldn't want to be leaving them without care."
13Aid groups say infrastructure has been so badly hit that families could face a very long wait for news, particularly from anyone travelling in regional and remote parts of the country.
14CARE Australia staffer Prajana W Pradhan said the earthquake seemed to go on forever.
15"This quake was so big," she said.
16"We were at a football match when everything started to shake and we had to rush to an open space for safety.
17"We stayed there waiting for the shaking to stop for what seemed like forever."
18Nepal's historic Dharahara tower, located in Kathmandu, collapsed on residents.
19"Responders are trying to dig people out now," MS Pradhan said.
20"We've been feeling tremors for four hours. I just felt another big jolt."
21Meanwhile, officials are scrambling to confirm reports an Australian died at Mount Everest's base camp during the earthquake.
22Foreign Minister Julie Bishop says more than 830 Australians who were in Nepal when the quake struck on Saturday are safe.
23But authorities are still trying to contact many more from the 1250 families and friends who have reported missing Australians in Nepal.
24Prime Minister Tony Abbott is urging Australians in Nepal to continue to try to use whatever phone and internet services are still functioning to advise they are OK.
25A DFAT crisis response team has been deployed to Nepal to help track down those who remain unaccounted for.
