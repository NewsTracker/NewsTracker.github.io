Baseball's Orioles postpone game in riot-scarred Baltimore

1(Reuters) - The baseball game between the Baltimore Orioles and Chicago White Sox scheduled for Tuesday night in riot-hit Baltimore has been postponed and Wednesday's game will be closed to the public, team officials said.
2The Orioles said Wednesday's scheduled 7:05 p.m. game with the White Sox would be played at 2:05 p.m. before an empty Camden Yards stadium.
3Major League Baseball officials said they were not aware of any previous game being played without fan admittance.
4"After conversations with the Orioles and local officials, we believe that these decisions are in the best interests of fan safety and the deployment of city resources," MLB Commissioner Rob Manfred said in a statement.
5"Our thoughts are with all those who have been affected by violence in Baltimore, and everyone in our game hopes for peace and the safety of a great American city."
6Riots in Baltimore forced the cancellation of Monday night's game.
7Hours later, Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake announced a week-long curfew in the city beginning on Tuesday.
8The violence in Baltimore on Monday followed the funeral of Freddie Gray, a 25-year-old black man who died while in police custody.
9Nineteen buildings and 144 vehicles were set on fire, and 202 people were arrested, the mayor's office said.
10In a statement, the Orioles said the decision to postpone the scheduled 7:05 p.m. game on Tuesday was made after "consultation with Major League Baseball and state and local officials."
11The Orioles said games Friday through Sunday against the Tampa Bay Rays, scheduled to be played at Camden Yards, will be moved to Tropicana Field in St. Petersburg, Florida.
12This week's postponed Orioles-White Sox games will be made up as part of a doubleheader at Camden Yards on May 28.
