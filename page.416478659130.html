Dutch forensic expert fired for exposing photos of MH17 victims

1A senior Dutch forensic expert involved into the investigation of the MH17 plane crash has been dismissed, with further sanctions against him considered after he showed images of plane crash victims to his students.
2Professor George Maat, who was involved in MH17 victim identification "has been suspended from his work and we'll see if other measures need to be taken," Thomas Aling, spokesman for the Dutch national forensics investigation team LTFO, told AFP on Thursday.
3He added that the expert was apparently fired for showing "photos that cannot be shown at a public meeting."
4The photos exposure became "shameful and very shocking for victims' relatives, this adds to the grief," said the deputy head of the MH17 Air Disaster Association, Evert van Zijtveld.
5Maas would possibly not have been incriminated in disclosing the photos if the lecture that took place in early April for an audience of about 150 people had been students-only, AFP reports.
6But an announcement on Facebook page clearly said the lecture was open to the public.
7"It appears that other people were there, I hadn't realized," Maat said he said in a statement released by the police.
8"I'm very sorry to have hurt or distressed victims' loved ones," he said, claiming he believed the lecture was restricted solely to medical students.
9"The collaboration with George Maat has been terminated," Justice Minister Ard van der Steur later informed lawmakers.
10Maat's showing of disturbing photos to medical students was first exposed by journalists of the private television channel RTL Nieuws.
11During the lecture organized by a medical students' association, the anthropology professor used the photos of victims and body parts as dramatic example of how the identification process is organized.
12Van der Steur denounced the lecture at Leiden University, in the southern Netherlands city of Maastricht, as "completely inappropriate and in bad taste."
13Ailing noted that during the lecture Maat had made comments on issues he does not specialize in, specifically on what caused the MH17 to crash, which according to the spokesman is "incorrect."
14The Malaysian Boeing 777 airliner was downed over eastern Ukraine on July 17, 2014, killing all 298 people on board.
15Due to the fact that most of the passengers aboard MH17 were Dutch, the Netherlands was charged with leading the investigation.
