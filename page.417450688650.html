Islamic schools face government scrutiny

1Islamic schools across Australia will be scrutinised in a federal government crackdown on the way they are run.
2The federal education department will audit all six schools run by the Australian Federation of Islamic Councils to investigate concerns relating to their financial management and governance, Education Minister Christopher Pyne said on Tuesday.
3Mr Pyne is also worried by reports about the curriculum taught at the schools, segregation of male and female students, and movement of senior staff.
