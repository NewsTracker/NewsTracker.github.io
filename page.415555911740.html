1The West Africa Ebola outbreak that has killed more than 10,000 people is declining but the disease has remained stubbornly entrenched in parts of Guinea and Sierra Leone.
2Liberia, the third country severely affected, currently has no Ebola cases.
3In the drive to zero cases in Sierra Leone, the government will again ask residents to stay in their homes for three days, Alfred Palo Conteh, head of the country's National Ebola Response Center, said Wednesday.
4The shutdown is expected to take place March 27 through 29, according to Patrick Fatoma, another official with the Ebola response center.
5The government has done this before, and some experts said it was unexpectedly effective in providing information about to control the disease.
6Sierra Leone is "hoping it will be the last push to end Ebola outbreak. We want it to be community-owned and for people to do the proper things," said Fatoma.
7There are currently about 30 cases in treatment centers across the country, Fatoma said, a far cry from the height of the epidemic when hundreds of people were falling sick each week in Sierra Leone.
8But experts say eradicating the last cases in a country can be the most difficult work.
