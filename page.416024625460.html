Bali nine: Myuran Sukumaran and Andrew Chan lose appeal

1It is unlikely the lives of Bali nine organisers Myuran Sukumaran and Andrew Chan will be spared after the pair lost lost their last ditch legal appeal.
2Jakarta: Bali nine organisers Myuran Sukumaran and Andrew Chan have lost their last-ditch legal appeal to save them from the firing squad.
3However their lawyers have vowed to keep on fighting and will this week take legal action in the constitutional court.
4The State Administrative Court of Jakarta threw out the appeal on Monday afternoon on the grounds it did not have jurisdiction to rule on presidential decrees.
5The prospect for Chan and Sukumaran's lives being spared is now extremely bleak.
6Andrew Chan and Myuran Sukumaran have lsot their last-ditch appeal.
7Their lawyer, Leonard Arpan Aritonang, said he was disappointed by the result but it would not stop the legal team's efforts to win clemency.
8They will ask the constitutional court to clarify the Indonesian president's obligations in relation to clemency.
9The action will be in collaboration with the National Commission on Human Rights in Indonesia (Komnas HAM) and other non-government organisations.
10Asked if the constitutional court action would postpone the execution, Mr Aritonang said : "We hope the government respects the ongoing legal process."
11However human rights lawyer Professor Todung Mulya Lubis earlier told Fairfax Media that any decision made by the constitutional court would not be retrospective and therefore would not affect the outcome for Chan and Sukumaran.
12The men will remain in semi-isolation on Besi Prison on Nusakambangan until the outcomes of the legal cases of other death row inmates are known.
13No date has yet been set for the execution.
14The Indonesian government has vowed to kill the 10 drug felons simultaneously, because, it says, staggering the executions would affect the psychological state of the inmates.
15Chan and Sukumaran's lawyers had hoped to challenge in the Administrative Court Indonesian President Joko Widodo's refusal to grant 64 drug felons mercy on the grounds Indonesia is facing a drug emergency.
16They said he should have examined Chan and Sukumaran's clemency pleas on an individual basis and taken into account their rehabilitation.
17However Chief Justice Ujang Abdullah upheld an earlier decision on February 24 that the court did not have jurisdiction over presidential clemency.
18Professor Mulya told Fairfax before the decision was brought down that the legal team would have to think "outside the box" if the appeal failed: "We will have to find our way and ground to launch another legal action."
19The Judicial Commission is also investigating claims by the men's previous lawyer that the judges who sentenced the men to death offered lighter sentences in exchange for bribes.
20However that investigation is unlikely to affect the executions.
21The Attorney-General's Office is now waiting for the Supreme Court's decision on case review requests filed by Serge Areski Atlaoui of France and Martin Anderson of Ghana.
22Lawyers for Indoensian man Zainal Abidin will lodge a request for a judicial review into his case next week.
23The Supreme Court says it will expedite the cases of those on death row.
24Filipina maid Mary Jane Fiesta Veloso had her judicial review request rejected in days, when the process normally takes up to three months.
