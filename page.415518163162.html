2Slain U.S. Marshal remembered for strength, courage JACKSON, Miss.
3- A deputy U.S. Marshal who was killed in the line of duty earlier this month was remembered by everyone at his funeral Tuesday, including U.S. Attorney General Eric Holder, as the definition of a true hero.
4Josie Wells was described as a man of deep selflessness, faith, integrity, and love for everyone he came across.
5Wells, who was killed in Louisiana as he and other members of a task force attempted to apprehend a man accused in a double homicide March 10, touched countless lives in his 27 short years.
6Holder and U.S. Marshals Service Director Stacia Hylton both spoke to the standing-room only crowd at the Wade Baptist Church in south Mississippi.
7Attorney General-designate Loretta Lynch also was present in a show of support from Washington for the fallen four-year-veteran of the U.S. Marshals Service.
8Just before Holder read a letter from President Obama to Wells' wife, Channing, he called Wells "a man - a patriot - who gave his life in the service of his country. And I am honored to stand among so many friends, colleagues and family members who are proud to call this true American hero their own."
9Hylton, who swore Josie Wells into the U.S. Marshals Service, addressed his widow, telling her that he had her picture all over his desk at work.
10"His love for you is stronger than just now. It's forever," she said.
11"And it's evident to all of us why he fell in love with you, like all of us have this week."
12In speaking of Wells' life, Holder also addressed the rest of the men and women of the Marshals Service.
13"He knew well the risk involved in his work, but he did not hesitate to put his own life on the line in order to protect the country he loved," Holder said.
14"And the importance of his efforts, and those of his colleagues - in maintaining order; in keeping the peace; in apprehending dangerous criminals; and in safeguarding our system of justice - would be difficult to overstate."
15Deputy U.S. Marshal Rebecca Woods, who was in Wells' recruit class, read Proverbs 28:1, "The wicked flee though no one pursues, but the righteous are as bold as a lion."
16"Josie Wells was a lion on March 10. I know that he will always be with us," she said, as her voice became emotional.
17"May God bless us and grant us the strength to conquer the evils of the world. When we put on our star, may it always be in remembrance of you."
18Holder also spoke of Wells' "deep and uncommon valor," his unshakable love of his wife, and the unborn son he prayed for all his life.
19"He was a loving father to a child not yet born - a son who will be named Josie Wells Jr., in honor of a hero who gave his last full measure of devotion in the service of his country," Holder said.
20"Josie Jr. will enter a world without his namesake. And it will be up to every one of us to share with him the story of his father's extraordinary life."
21Holder said Wells' son will know the importance of the justice, integrity and service that his father was committed to, and the "indomitable courage with which he carried it out."
22When Channing Wells eulogized her husband, she told stories of their life together and of what it was like to know him better than anyone else.
23She said their son is due on what would have been her husband's birthday, and she said that out of all his dreams, the greatest was to be a father.
24She also acknowledged that her husband's courage took them down the road that led to last week's events.
25"But if it wasn't for Josie, I wouldn't have been as strong as I am," she said, adding that she is grateful to his parents for making him the man he was.
26"He's everything I prayed for before I even knew what I wanted."
27She was very proud of him all throughout his life, she said, but never more than in his last breath.
28She told those gathered that his last words to the men in the ambulance with him were, "Pray with me.
29"If that doesn't make me proud, I don't know what does," she said emotionally.
30Marshals said everyone from Wells' first duty station in St. Louis, as well as the other members of the task force he was serving with in Louisiana when he was killed and his co-workers from the Jackson, Miss., field office were all in attendance in addition to family and friends.
31"As we take him to his rest today I would ask that you remember this true American hero not for how he lost his life but for how he lived it - with faith, with compassion and with the joy that only those who truly give, truly give, can ever really experience," Holder said.
32"This was a good and decent man - an example to us all. May he rest in peace."
