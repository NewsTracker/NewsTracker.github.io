Melbourne man to fight against Islamic State

Egypt strikes Islamic State group in Libya after video of mass killing

1Khamis Gewargis Khamis is based on the frontline in the town of Baqofa, about 30 kilometres north of IS-controlled Mosul, the ABC reported on Monday night.
2Mr Khamis, who is married with two children, joined an independent Assyrian militia known as Dwekh Nawsha, meaning "self sacrificers", intending to fight until his ancestral land is free from IS.
3He said he was concerned that he may be breaking Australian laws prohibiting Australians from fighting in foreign wars, but said he was "prepared to stand in front of the law".
4"[IS fighters] don't discriminate when it comes to killing, torture and so on," Mr Khamis told the ABC.
5"So we would like the entire international community to be stronger, to get together and urgently help [the Assyrians] from humanitarian side, and also to do what they can to eradicate [IS], to eliminate them, to drive them out of these towns, villages and these territories so the people can return back to their homes and their lands. This is our message. Help us. Please."
6Despite Assyrian community leaders in Australia insisting that people such as Mr Khamis are fighting, rather than joining, IS or planning terror attacks, a spokesman for Attorney-General George Brandis told the ABC that anybody fighting for Dwekh Nawsha may be breaking Australian law.
1An armed forces spokesman announced the strikes on state radio, marking the first time Cairo has publicly acknowledged taking military action in neighboring Libya, where extremist groups seen as a threat to both countries have exploited the chaos following the 2011 uprising.
2The statement said the warplanes targeted weapons caches and training camps before returning safely.
3It said the "intense strikes" were "to avenge the bloodshed and to seek retribution from the killers."
4"Let those far and near know that Egyptians have a shield to protect and safeguard the security of the country and a sword that amputates terrorism and extremism," it said.
5Libya's air force meanwhile announced it had launched strikes in the eastern city of Darna, an extremist stronghold that was taken over by an Islamic State affiliate last year.
6The announcement, on the Facebook page of the air force chief of staff, did not provide further details.
7The video purporting to show the mass beheading of the Coptic Christian hostages was released late Sunday by militants in Libya affiliated with the Islamic State group.
8The killings raise the possibility that the extremist group - which controls about a third of Syria and Iraq in a self-declared caliphate - has established a direct affiliate less than 500 miles (800 kilometers) from the southern tip of Italy.
9One of the militants in the video makes direct reference to that possibility, saying the group now plans to "conquer Rome."
10The militants had been holding 21 Egyptian Coptic Christian laborers rounded up from the city of Sirte in December and January.
11It was not clear from the video whether all 21 hostages were killed.
12It was one of the first beheading videos from an Islamic State group affiliate to come from outside the group's core territory in Syria and Iraq, and displayed the sophisticated techniques used in previous videos.
13Libya in recent months has seen the worst unrest since the 2011 uprising that toppled and killed longtime dictator Moammar Gadhafi, which will complicate any efforts to combat the country's many Islamic extremist groups.
14The internationally recognized government has been confined to the country's far east since Islamist-allied militias seized the capital Tripoli last year, and Islamist politicians have reconstituted a previous government and parliament.
15Egypt has strongly backed the internationally recognized government, and U.S. officials have said both Egypt and the United Arab Emirates took part in a series of mysterious airstrikes targeting Islamist-allied forces last year.
16The Egyptian government declared a seven-day mourning period after the release of the video and President Abdel Fattah el-Sissi addressed the nation late Sunday night.
17"These cowardly actions will not undermine our determination" said el-Sissi, who also banned all travel to Libya by Egyptian citizens.
18"Egypt and the whole world are in a fierce battle with extremist groups carrying extremist ideology and sharing the same goals."
19The U.N. Security Council meanwhile "strongly condemned the heinous and cowardly apparent murder in Libya of 21 Egyptian Coptic Christians by an affiliate of the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant," it said in a statement.
