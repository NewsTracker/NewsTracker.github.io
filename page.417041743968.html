U.S. hits Islamic State in Syria as jihadists press attacks

1WASHINGTON/BEIRUT, May 16 (Reuters) - U.S special forces killed a man identified as a senior Islamic State leader during a raid into Syria, U.S. officials said, as the jihadists battled for the Iraqi city of Ramadi and ancient Palmyra in Syria.
2The White House said President Barack Obama ordered the raid that killed the Islamic State figure identified as Abu Sayyaf, who helped direct the group's oil, gas and financial operations.
3U.S. officials said his wife was captured in the raid.
4It is the first declared special forces operation by U.S. forces in Syria since their failed attempt to rescue a number of American hostages held by Islamic State last summer.
5The United States is leading a coalition in a military campaign to roll back the jihadist group whose self-declared caliphate in Syria and Iraq has reshaped the region.
6In Ramadi, U.S.-led air strikes forced Islamic State to withdraw from a government building over which the group's black flag had flown on Friday, the major and a tribal leader said.
7In Syria, the group fought Syrian government forces for control of the ancient city of Palmyra, an attack that has raised fears its UNESCO World Heritage site could meet the same fate as monuments destroyed by Islamic State in Iraq.
8While Washington is working closely with Iraq in the fight against Islamic State, it has shunned the idea of cooperating with Syrian President Bashar al-Assad, who it says has lost legitimacy to rule and must leave power.
9The United States said it did not warn Assad in advance or coordinate with his officials over the special forces raid.
10Conducted by U.S. personnel based out of Iraq, the raid targeted area called al-Amr in eastern Syria, an Islamic State stronghold that bridges territory the group controls in Syria and Iraq.
11"During the course of the operation, Abu Sayyaf was killed when he engaged U.S. forces," White House National Security Council spokeswoman Bernadette Meehan said.
12The operation was conducted "with the full consent of Iraqi authorities".
13Assad said in February he had been informed about U.S.-led air strikes against Islamic State in Syria via third parties including Iraq, with which his government has close ties.
14A U.S. official said about a dozen fighters were killed in the overnight raid, which appeared to mark a departure from the reliance on air strikes to hit Islamic State in Syria.
15Syrian state TV initially credited the Syrian army with the raid, saying it had killed 40 Islamic State militants and the group's "oil minister", whom it identified by a different name.
16The Syrian Observatory for Human Rights, a UK-based organisation that tracks the war using sources on the ground, said at least 19 Islamic State members were killed in an air strike in the area which it said was carried out by U.S. forces.
17Twelve of those killed were non-Syrians, it said.
18"WHAT IS THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY DOING?"
19Islamic State, the most powerful insurgent force in Syria, has in recent months launched frequent attacks on government- and rebel-held areas beyond its strongholds in Deir al-Zor and Raqqa provinces.
20Its attack on Palmyra got underway this week.
21The group has seized positions within 1-2 km (about 1 mile) of the city, also known as Tadmur and home to extensive ruins of one of the most important cultural centres of the ancient world.
22The Observatory said on Saturday Islamic State fighters had made further gains in areas around the city.
23A military source said fighting was ongoing but at "a distance far from the city", which he said had been secured by army reinforcements.
24"We are in the fourth or fifth day (of the attack). What is the international community doing? Is it waiting to weep and despair as it did in northern Iraq?"
25Syrian government antiquities chief Maamoun Abdulkarim told Reuters.
26The Observatory reported Islamic State had seized a gas field east of Palmyra - a report denied by the military source.
27The source said Islamic State was keeping up its attack on the city but the fighting was at a lower intensity.
28The Observatory says Islamic State has carried out two mass executions to the north of Palmyra.
29On Friday, it killed 23 people including five women and nine minors, it reported.
30In Ramadi, 100 km (60 miles) west of Baghdad, Islamic State had left buildings booby trapped or on fire as they were forced to retreat by U.S.-led air strikes, the major and the tribal leader said.
31The insurgents attacked Ramadi overnight Thursday to Friday, using six suicide car bombs to reach the city centre where the Anbar provincial government compound is located.
32An army source said an operations command centre was surrounded and being struck by mortars.
33Security sources said Iraqi security forces defending the city were outnumbered.
34A speech was delivered in the name of Islamic State in the city's main mosque, vowing to punish anyone who was deemed to harm Muslims, witnesses said.
35A hospital source said seven members of the armed forces and 10 civilians had been killed since Friday.
36Islamic State issued an audio recording on Thursday it said was by its leader, Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi.
37It was his first message since a number of media reports said he was wounded this year in Iraq.
38Some said he was no longer running the group and others suggested he was dead.
