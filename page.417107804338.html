Premarket: Euro, bond yields tumble as ECB hints at faster buying

2The euro tumbled on Tuesday and the region's stocks and bonds jumped after the European Central Bank suggested it may speed up its 1 trillion euro bond-buying campaign slightly to account for lower market liquidity in high summer.
3World stocks were already testing all-time highs after another jump in Chinese stocks and a record close on Wall Street, and European markets shot up after top policy maker Benoit Coeure talked of adjusting the ECB's buying program.
4He said the speed of the recent spike in bond yields, which has effectively wiped out the benefits of QE, was worrisome and said the ECB could "moderately" increase its buying in May and June to ensure it doesn't fall behind on its target over the summer.
5The euro was back at $1.12 for the first time in a week and the FTSEurofirst 300 jumped 1.6 per cent as gains of 1.9 and 2 per cent on Germany's DAX and in Paris outpaced a 0.4 per cent rise on London's FTSE.
6Bond yields, which move inversely to prices, also tumbled, with those on 10-year German Bunds down 7 basis points and Italian and Spanish equivalents down 12 basis points.
7"There is a sense the comments from the ECB indicate a growing push back against the sell-off in bond markets that's been in place for the past month or so, and a push back against both euro strength and market volatility," said Manik Narain, a UBS strategist.
8The moves came ahead of German sentiment data from the ZEW institute at 0900 GMT that will be closely watched after some mixed signals from Europe's largest economy in recent weeks.
9Fears of a Greek bankruptcy also rumbled in the background even as the country's top politicians vowed to conclude a cash-for-reform deal with its lenders.
10"I think we are very close (to a deal) ... let's say in a week," Greek Finance Minister Yanis Varoufakis said in a TV interview on Monday night.
11"Another currency is not on our radar, not in our thoughts," he added.
12China's surging stock market and a jump in the New Zealand dollar after a rise in inflation dominated attention overnight in Asia.
13The CSI300 index surged 3.4 per cent and the Shanghai Composite Index rose 3.0 per cent, as investors welcomed Beijing's 2015 guidelines for economic reform.
14They prioritized a further opening of the country's capital market and a restructuring of state enterprises.
15"You need a vibrant stock market to push forward economic reforms, whether it's about asset securitisation or industry consolidation," said Tian Weidong, analyst at Kaiyuan Securities in Xian.
16"With such a policy backdrop, investors are emboldened to stay in the market."
17Japan's Nikkei also ended up 0.7 per cent at a three-week high as the dollar nudged down the yen.
18Expectations of more easing from the Bank of Japan kept the Japanese currency in check.
19The BOJ meets on Friday and is seen expanding its massive stimulus program in October, according to most economists polled by Reuters.
20Among commodities, U.S. crude futures edged up after slipping on Monday on the stronger dollar and oversupply concerns triggered by a jump in Saudi Arabian exports U.S. crude rose about 0.1 per cent in Asian trade to $59.48 a barrel, while Brent edged down 0.2 per cent to $66.15.
21Safe-haven gold, meanwhile, fell for the first time in six sessions, dropping about 0.3 per cent to $1,218 an ounce.
22It had hit a three-month high a day early after disappointing U.S. economic data dented expectations of Federal Reserve rate hikes this year.
