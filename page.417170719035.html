Former Boko Haram captives still held _ by Nigerian military

2All 275 women, girls and children rescued from Boko Haram and taken to the safety of a northeast Nigerian refugee camp have been taken into military custody amid suspicions that some are aiding the Islamic extremists, a camp official and a Nigerian military intelligence officer said Wednesday May 20, 2015.
3YOLA, Nigeria (AP) - The accusations against a woman who was rescued from Boko Haram abductors came from fellow former captives during a group counseling session.

6As scores of young Nigerian women and children are rescued from the clutches of Boko Haram, they face suspicions that they may still be in contact with their former captors.
7These fears apparently have led to an entire group of 275 women and girls rescued from the extremists last month being forced to remain in custody - held this time by the Nigerian military.
8The Defense Ministry spokesman, Maj.
9Gen. Chris Olukolade, claimed Thursday that they were moved to get "proper medical attention and psycho-social therapy" under a program organized by the national security adviser.
10Olukolade was quoted by PR Nigeria, an agency that puts out Nigerian government news.
11One of the trauma counselors who had been working with the women said the group had nearly completed their therapy and camp officials had been preparing to reunite some with their families, before the military took them.
12After creating havoc in northeastern Nigeria for years, Boko Haram militants have suffered a series of defeats this year from an offensive by better-equipped Nigerian troops bolstered by forces from neighboring Chad, Niger and Cameroon.
13As the militants retreat, the many captives they seized are being rescued, but that doesn't mean their ordeal is over.
14Many remain traumatized by their harsh captivity - including rape, sexual slavery and beatings - as well as suspicions that some cooperated with their captors and may still support them.
15In counseling sessions at the Malkohi Camp outside the northeastern city of Yola, witnesses described some women accusing another one of having ties to their former Boko Haram captors.
16Relations between soldiers guarding the camp and some of the rescued women had been tense after a fracas witnessed May 17 by a reporter in which a soldier falsely claimed that one of the women tried to grab his gun and told him she was a Boko Haram member.
17On Tuesday, soldiers took the group of more than 200 children - almost all younger than 5 - and 67 girls and women from the camp, the National Emergency Management Agency has confirmed.
18Agency spokesman Sani Datti said he had no other information because it was an "entirely military affair."
19The group was put on a military plane and flown to an unknown destination, possibly the capital of Abuja, a camp official and a military intelligence officer told The Associated Press on condition of anonymity because they were not authorized to talk to the media about the issue.
20Their move from the camp only became known after an AP story about the heroism of one teenager, Binta Ibrahim, prompted two readers to send $500 to her.
21An AP reporter who went to the camp to find out how to get the money to her discovered the group was no longer there.
22It is not known how many girls, women and children have been kidnapped by Boko Haram over the years.
23Many have escaped and Nigerian troops reported freeing some 700 last month since the multinational offensive chased the militants from towns in the northeast.
24Some of the girls and women were used by the militants as suicide bombers, sending them into crowded markets and bus stations.
25The president of the International Committee of the Red Cross, Peter Maurer, expressed alarm Thursday at the scale of the humanitarian needs and "the horrific mental and physical scars" that the violence by Boko Haram has left on the people of northeast Nigeria.
