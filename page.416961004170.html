El Paso Electric seeks to raise rates in southern New Mexico

1A Texas-based utility that serves electric customers throughout southern New Mexico wants to raise rates to cover the costs of a new power plant and transmission lines.
2El Paso Electric announced Tuesday it has filed its request with the New Mexico Public Regulation Commission.
3If approved, residential customers could see their monthly bills increase by an average of 9 percent.
4The rate hike would generate about $8.6 million for the utility.
5El Paso Electric CEO Tom Shockley says that will allow the utility to recover investments made in the system over the past several years and ensure reliable service at a reasonable cost.
6Shockley says the utility is seeing growth in the region.
7The last time El Paso Electric sought such a rate hike in New Mexico was 2009.
