Australian Twitter user encouraged Islamic State fighters to attack prophet Mohammad cartoon contest in Texas: report

1A Twitter account held by an Australian from Melbourne that was one of several used on social media to call for an attack on an anti-Islam event in Texas has been suspended.
2The tweets were made before two men were killed by police as they opened fire outside the event in Dallas.
3The man, whose account was held under the name "Australi Witness" posted a map of the community centre where the shooting took place and shared calls for Islamic State fighters and supporters to attack "with your weapons, bombs or with knifes", The Australian has reported.
4Investigators box up an assault weapon outside the Curtis Culwell Centre in Garland, Texas, after two men were killed in an attack on a provocative contest for Prophet Mohammed cartoons.
5As the event, in which attendees were to present cartoons of the prophet Mohammad in competition for a $US10,000 ($12,745) prize, took place in an event centre in the Suburb of Garland, outside Dallas, on Sunday two men armed with assault rifles drove up to the building and opened fire, wounding a security guard, Bruce Joiner.
6An officer with the Garland Police returned fire with a handgun, killing both men.
7The bodies of gunmen Elton Simpson and Nadir Soofi are removed from behind a car during an investigation by the FBI and local police in Garland, Texas.
8"He did a very good job, and probably saved lives," a Garland Police spokesman said at a news conference on Monday.
9Authorities have now identified one of the men as Elton Simpson, 30, of Phoenix, Arizona.
10The second man has been reported to be his flat-mate, Nadir Soofi.
11Mr Simpson faced charges in 2010 of travelling to Somalia to engage in "violent jihad" but was only convicted of lying to an agent and was sentenced to three years' probation, the New York Times reported.
12An aerial view shows the car that was used the previous night by two gunmen, who were killed by police.
13The FBI and Garland Police have been contacted for comment.
