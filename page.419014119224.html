Boko Haram fight: Nigeria's army says 178 captives rescued

1Women and children rescued by Nigerian soldiers from Boko Haram extremists in the north-east of Nigeria arrive at the military office in Maiduguri.
2Abuja: Nigeria's army said it had rescued 178 people held by Islamist militant group Boko Haram in Nigeria's Borno state, the heartland of the insurgency.
3Spokesman Colonel Tukur Gusau said 101 of those freed were children, 67 were women and the rest were men.
4He added that a Boko Haram commander had also been captured and several militant camps were cleared around the town of Bama, about 70 kilometres south-east of the state capital Maiduguri.
5Boko Haram has been waging a six-year insurgency in the north-east of Africa's biggest economy in an attempt to establish an Islamist state adhering to strict sharia law.
6Nigeria's air force also said that it helped ground troops repel an attack by Boko Haram around the village of Bitta on the southern edge of the Sambisa forest reserve, a stronghold of the militant group.
7Bitta is also west of Gwoza, a town near the Cameroonian border that was believed to be the militants' headquarters until a major offensive was launched this year by combined Nigeria, Nigerien and Chadian forces.
8Boko Haram was pushed out of most of the vast swathes of territory it controlled at the start of the year but they have dispersed and returned to their guerilla tactics of hitting soft targets with bombs, and raiding towns.
9President Muhammadu Buhari has vowed to crush the group and a multinational taskforce of 8700 troops from Nigeria, Cameroon, Niger, Chad and Benin is being set up in the Chadian capital N'Djamena with that aim.
10The force was supposed to start operations on July 31 but has been dogged by a lack of funding and political will.
11Mr Buhari visited Cameroon this past week in an effort to smooth over differences over cross-border pursuit, and then to Benin.
