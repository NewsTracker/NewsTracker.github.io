CIA failing to recruit and promote minorities, study finds

1FILE - In this Dec. 11, 2014 file photo, CIA Director John Brennan pauses during a news conference at CIA headquarters in Langley, Va.
2The CIA is failing to hire and promote sufficient numbers of minorities despite years of vowing to do so, Brennan said Tuesday, as he announced a new effort to tackle the problem of a largely white workforce whose lack of diversity is undercutting the spy agency's intelligence mission.
3The CIA is failing to hire and promote enough minorities despite years of vowing to do so, Director John Brennan said Tuesday, announcing a new effort to tackle the problem of a largely white workforce whose lack of diversity he says is undercutting the spy agency's intelligence mission.
4Brennan handed out an internal study that paints a stark picture of a CIA that does not reflect America.
5The CIA's senior executive ranks are only 10.8 percent racial and ethnic minorities, the studied concluded, in a nation where non-whites comprise about 30 percent of the population.
6The agency has seen the percentage of minorities hired slip since 2008, the study found, despite previous diversity pushes by past directors.
7"We didn't achieve the goals and objectives that we had as an institution," Brennan said.
8The director pledged to change course, promising to hold senior managers accountable for hiring and promoting minority candidates.
9That has not happened to date, said the study, which was led by Vernon Jordan, a prominent African-American figure in Washington who serves on CIA advisory boards.
10The team interviewed more than 200 current and former CIA employees, Jordan said in a letter, and used results of a workforce survey that got a 40 percent response rate.
11"The agency does not hold its officers accountable for creating and maintaining a diverse and inclusive workplace," the study said.
12Brennan acknowledged that some employees will greet his move with cynicism, having heard such rhetoric before.
13"We're not kidding," he said.
14"This is real, this time."
15But Brennan, who is fluent in Arabic and led the CIA station in Saudi Arabia, spoke of a pressing, mission-driven need for more diversity.
16"Individuals who look like me, they're not going to be able to operate clandestinely in as many parts of the world," said Brennan, who is white.
17Racial and ethnic minorities - defined by the CIA as anyone other than whites of European descent - comprise 23.9 percent of the entire workforce, but they are below 20 percent of the elite job categories of operations officer and intelligence analyst, the study found.
18Operators who gather intelligence as well as analysts who interpret it are the driving force of the organization.
19While the CIA has done a good job integrating white women into its upper ranks, minority women have not fared nearly as well, the study found.
20Strikingly, the agency lost ground in black recruitment and promotion since 2004 after making progress from 1984 to 2004.
21The study attributed the drop in part to a weakening of informal mentoring networks of black officers.
22And its recruitment of minorities in general since 2008 has not kept pace with the current workforce percentage, the study found.
23A CIA official, who spoke under agency ground rules that he not be named, said that while large numbers of minorities are expressing interest in the CIA, the rate at which they make it through the lengthy and arduous hiring process, including a year-long wait without pay for a detailed background check, has plummeted.
24That may be in part due to the economy, Brennan said, acknowledging that CIA starting salaries can't typically compete with corporate job offers.
25But he also acknowledged that the byzantine security clearance process - bemoaned for years as an impediment to hiring non-traditional candidates or those with foreign relatives - must be streamlined.
26In March of 2011, then-CIA Director Leon Panetta gave a speech at Atlanta's historically black Morehouse College touting the agency's "comprehensive initiative to strengthen its workforce diversity," which he said began in 2009.
27That effort failed, the study made clear.
28Brennan promised not to repeat that failure.
