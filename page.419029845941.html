CDC head says Sierra Leone in strong place with Ebola

1Sierra Leone is in a stronger place than it was six months ago to fight Ebola, but the new challenge is to get to zero cases, the director of the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention said Monday during a visit, as new cases emerged in the city and in an area that had not seen the deadly virus for months.
2Two new cases of Ebola surfaced in Sierra Leone's Northern Tonkolili District after a man died last week.
3The district had not had a single case of Ebola in five months.
4The victim contracted the disease in the capital, traveled to his home village, and was treated for a fever at the local hospital but authorities didn't call the Ebola emergency number.
5He didn't have a burial that followed special procedures required for Ebola victims.
6The man's mother and brother contracted Ebola, the National Ebola Response Centre's OB Sisay said Sunday.
7Authorities moved quickly to contain the spread, but more cases are feared, he said.
8Authorities quarantined 500 people.
9"We now know where the virus is and we are tracking its movement, by surrounding, containing and eradicating its last remaining chain of transmission," Sisay said.
10Dr. Thomas Frieden, CDC director, said it was important that the case was identified and not missed, and he was impressed with the speed of the response.
11"The challenge now is to get to zero cases and that is not going to be easy," he said.
12"Authorities must not let down their guard. The country should keep its guard up."
13He said a new cluster of cases in an area of Freetown means Sierra Leone must still put in place measures that have worked in the past.
14This Ebola outbreak has killed more than 11,200 people worldwide with nearly all deaths in Guinea, Sierra Leone and Liberia.
