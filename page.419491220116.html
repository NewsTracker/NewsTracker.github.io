Donald Trump tells Alabama crowd: 'I'd like to have the election tomorrow'

1Republican presidential candidate Donald Trump at a rally at the University of South Alabama's stadium.
2Donald Trump arrived in Mobile, Ala., for his biggest rally yet in typically bombastic style: in a private jet that had just flown several laps around the football stadium where thousands of his supporters were waiting.
3Wearing a red baseball cap emblazoned with his campaign slogan -- "Make America Great Again" -- Trump entered the stadium Friday night as Lynyrd Skynyrd's "Sweet Home Alabama" boomed.
4In an energetic, if meandering speech, the businessman, reality television star and Republican presidential front-runner criticized America's leaders as "stupid," complained about children born to immigrants in the country illegally, and bragged about his personal wealth.
5He vowed that if elected president, he would put his business interests aside and devote his acumen to improving the lives of the American people.
6"I won't care about that company," Trump said.
7"My whole energy, my whole being is going to be to make this country rich. In order to make this country great, I have to make it rich again."
8Dismissed at first as a long-shot candidate, Trump has soared in the polls since declaring his candidacy in June.
9His off-the-cuff style and outspoken critique of the Washington political establishment clearly resonated with many members of the crowd, who at one point interrupted him with chants of "Trump" and "U.S.A."
10Trump, not known for his attention span in speeches, interrupted himself many times, at one point to highlight a sign being waved in the audience and at another to point out a plane flying overhead.
11A recent telephone survey found a majority of likely Republican voters think Trump will be their party's nominee for president of the United States in 2016.
12A Rasmussen survey found 57% of likely Republican voters thought Trump was likely to be the party's nominee, while 25% thought it was very likely.
13Trump said he he was ready to capitalize on that momentum.
14"I'd like to have the election tomorrow," he said to laughter.
15"I don't want to wait."
16He talked at length about illegal immigration, the issue that many believe has gotten him here.
17Trump began his speech by reciting the names of several victims of crimes committed by immigrants in the country illegally, and repeated his call to end birthright citizenship for children born in the U.S. to immigrants in the country without legal status.
18That policy, enshrined in the 14th Amendment, encourages immigrants to enter the country unlawfully to have children, Trump said.
19He pointed out that many other countries have different policies.
20"We're the only place just about that's stupid enough to do it," he said.
21Trump said 300,000 children each year are born to women in the country illegally.
22Those are children, he told the audience, "that you have to take care of, we have to take care of."
23Several thousand people attended the rally at the University of South Alabama's football stadium.
24Although not a typical primary campaign stop, Alabama is one of eight Southern states that will hold its presidential primary March 1, 2016.
25The crowd is believed to be the largest yet to turn out for a Republican presidential candidate.
26Still, the more than 40,000-seat stadium was far from full.
27With considerable fanfare this week, Trump's campaign moved the rally from a smaller venue after it said it received more than 40,000 RSVPs.
28In recent days, Trump has bragged about the tens of thousands of supporters he said would come.
29Still, Trump did not seem disappointed by the turnout.
