Apple Pay: Everything you need to know

1Apple Pay is said to be launching in the UK on July 14.
2Retailers are preparing for Apple Pay's roll-out across the UK, meaning customers will be able to pay for goods and services with their Apple Watches and iPhones for the first time.
3What is Apple Pay?
4Apple Pay uses the near-field communication (NFC) chips embedded in your iPhone 6, iPhone 6 Plus and Apple Watch to pay for goods and services by holding the device near a card reader, as you would a contactless debit card.
5You can also use Apple Pay to make single touch purchases within apps.
6Apple Pay does away with the need to enter a PIN to verify your identity when making a purchase by using its Touch ID sensor, inbuilt into the iPhone 6 and 6 Plus home button.
7Approaching a card reader automatically wakes up Apple Pay - without having to open an app - and pressing a finger to the sensor verifies the payment.
8Your phone will vibrate and beep to let you know its been received.
9When paying with an Apple Watch, double tapping the side button triggers your stored cards.
10Holding the Watch up to the contactless reader makes the payment, and, like on iPhone, it will vibrate and beep to confirm it's gone through.
11You may have used the Passbook app in the past to store your electronic boarding pass when flying, but it's been given a makeover as a one-stop digital wallet.
12Passbook will be rebranded as Wallet in the next software update iOS 9 due in the autumn, but until then it will remain as Passbook.
13Once Apple Pay has been released, you'll be able to store your debit and credit cards digitally within Passbook.
14You can add the card associated with your iTunes account (the one you may already use to download albums, apps etc) by tapping the Plus sign in the upper right and entering the card security code within the app.
15The initial card you enter will automatically be your default, but you can select a new default or switch between cards for payments as you please.
16How can I add new cards?
17To add a card on Apple Watch, you have to open the Apple Watch iPhone app > Passbook & Apple Pay > Add Credit or Debit Card.
18For iPad, open Settings > Passbook & Apple Pay > Add Credit or Debit Card.
19You can also remove cards by selecting the one you want to get rid of, and tapping Remove Card.
20So I can't use my iPhone 5s?
21You won't be able to use older iPhones with Apple Pay, as they don't have an inbuilt NFC chip.
22You can theoretically use an iPad mini 3 or iPad Air 2 to make payments, but due to their size they're probably more suited to making in-app payments than within shops.
23The device must be running iOS 8.1 or later.
24However, the Apple Watch works with the iPhone 5 upwards, but it must be running iOS 8.2 or above.
25Can I use it to pay for anything else?
26Londoners can also use Apple Pay to travel around the capital after Apple struck a deal with Transport for London (TfL), so you can use your watch or phone to travel around the Tube, bus and rail networks.
27Is there a limit?
28At launch, the majority of payments using Apple Pay will be capped at 20, the same as transactions made using a contactless debit card.
29However, some retailers will set their own limit and may do away with it altogether in time, according to executive director of mobile at Visa, Jeremy Nicholds.
30From September, the UK Cards Association says the limit will increase to 30.
31What banks and cards are eligible?
32American Express, First Direct, HSBC, Ulster Bank, Nationwide, NatWest, Santander and RBS will all be available from launch.
33Bank of Scotland, TSB, Halifax, Lloyds Bank, M&S Bank and MBNA customers will have to wait until later in the autumn.
34Barclays is the only major bank not to support Apple Pay.
35The lender has tweeted to say it is still holding "constructive" with Apple about the payments system.
36The technology will be supported in 250,000 shops at launch all around the UK.
37They include the following well-known outlets, some of which will display the Apple Pay logo.
38Generally, the majority of shops which currently support contactless payment should be able to process Apple Pay transactions.
39Apple is keen to point out how your card numbers are not stored on the device you're using for Apple Pay, nor on their servers.
40Whilst your most recent purchases are listed in Passbook, an on-going record of your transactions is not maintained.
41When a card is added, a unique Device Account Number is created and encrypted.
42This number is stored in a chip within your device called the secure Element.
43When you go to make a transaction, the Device Account Number is matched with a dynamic security code unique to that specific payment, which is then processed.
44In the event of your iPhone, iPad or Apple Watch being lost or stolen, you can suspend Apple Pay remotely or wipe it fully using Find My iPhone.
45Can I return goods I've bought using Apple Pay?
46Yes. Just like how you'd return something you'd paid for on debit or credit card, take the item you'd like to return back to the shop and the retailer will be able to transfer the money back into your account by selecting the card you used to make the payment and holding it near the reader.
47The member of staff can then process your return.
