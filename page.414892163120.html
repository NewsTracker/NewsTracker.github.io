3CAIRO, Egypt - New images released online Wednesday show large numbers of Islamic State militants patrolling streets in the Libyan city of Sirte.
4Kalashnikov-wielding fighters in beige fatigues, their faces covered in black balaclavas, ride in a convoy of pick-up trucks waving the group's black flags.
5The images are clearly aimed as a show of force - an announcement that the Islamic State is now a power in Libya.
6The release comes one day after a similar attention-grabbing move: a mass beheading of 20 Egyptians near Sirte.
7But just how powerful is the Islamic State's Libyan franchise?
8Islamic State operatives are believed to have arrived in Libya as early as last summer - around the same time the group's fighters overran a large part of northern Iraq in a sweeping offensive, a security official told The Wall Street Journal.
9Adopting a different approach than it had in Syria and Iraq, where it fought against other Islamist groups, it chose to reach out to Libyan militants, including members of al-Qaeda, widely seen as its rival.
10Since the beginning of the year, the Islamic State has launched increasingly high-profile attacks, beginning with an assault on Tripoli's Corinthian Hotel that left nine dead, including an American contractor.
11In early February gunmen professing loyalty to the group attacked a French-Libyan oil field, killing nine guards.
12The militants took control of the eastern city of Derna late last year and took power of significant parts of Sirte in early 2015.
13The 20 Egyptians killed by the Islamic State earlier this week were abducted in December and January from Sirte, the hometown of longtime strongman Muammar Gaddafi, who was ousted in 2011.
14Yet despite the high-profile attacks, the group's reach in Libya is unclear.
15Residents report the Islamic State is administering Derna much as it has cities in Iraq and Syria: levying taxes and setting up police and court systems.
16Reports from Sirte suggest it is rapidly following suit.
17"We have seen pictures of (militant morality police) telling people who sell perfumes not to sell it anymore, taking mannequins out of shop windows and telling hairdressers to shut down," says Mohamed Eljarh, non-resident fellow with the Rafik Hariri Center based in the Libyan city of Tobruk.
18Eljarh adds there have been reports of members of the Interior Ministry repenting to the Islamic State.
19According to Libyan Minister Omar al-Sinki, there are nearly 400 Tunisian and Yemeni militants in Sirte, and the militants had established its headquarters in the city's main convention center, the Associated Press reported.
20The homegrown extremist group Ansar al-Sharia, which preceded the Islamic State in both Derna and Sirte, has seen many of its members join up with the militants.
21As in other areas under its control, the Islamic State has named an "Emir of Tripoli" - a Tunisian known as Abu Talha, to run the group's operations in western Libya.
22Abu al-Baraa el-Azdi, a Yemeni based in Derna, reportedly sent by Islamic State leadership in Mosul, is said to lead in the east.
23Meanwhile, Libya's elected government, which has fled to the eastern city of Tobruk, has no control over the country's resources or population centers.
24Since last summer when the Islamist-led General National Council refused to accept the results of national elections, Libya has had two rival governments.
25The GNC is based in Tripoli and is backed by the militant group Libya Dawn, made up of Misratan and Islamist fighters.
26Opposing them in the east, a rogue secular general named Khalifa Haftar commands a force known as "Operation Dignity," which has also been fighting Ansar al-Sharia and other Islamists.
27There are signs that the Islamic State may struggle to win widespread support in areas where it is trying to gain influence.
28Many in Misrata - a stronghold of Libya Dawn support - are wary of the militants' takeover in neighboring Sirte, and have called for an end to the conflict and the need for a political solution.
29"Within Misrata there are voices that are increasingly saying we should end the war against each other," says Eljarh.
30"Misrata is under threat from IS."
31Meanwhile, the ongoing chaos provides fertile ground for the Islamic State to expand its Libyan outposts.
