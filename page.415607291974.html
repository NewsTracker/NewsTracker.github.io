Blood clots again sideline NASCAR driver Brian Vickers (Yahoo Sports)

1(AP) -- Brian Vickers has been sidelined again by blood clots that prevent him from safely driving a race car.
2Vickers' third bout with blood clots was announced Friday by Michael Waltrip Racing, which replaced him with Brett Moffitt for Sunday's NASCAR race at Auto Club Speedway.
3''We support Brian,'' said Michael Waltrip, the team founder and co-owner.
4''We love his heart.
5The 31-year-old driver must go back on blood-thinning medication, which makes it too dangerous to race.
6If Vickers got injured in the car, any bleeding would be difficult or impossible to stop.
7''He's in the care of his doctors and expects to be able to operate normally today,'' Waltrip said.
8''That's really all the information I have on what his current state is, other than he feels really good and he's really sad.
9''If he wasn't a race car driver, you wouldn't know there was an issue.
10Waltrip and Vickers were in New Orleans together on Wednesday for a sponsor event before traveling Thursday to the Los Angeles area.
11MWR general manager Ty Norris learned of Vickers' condition early Friday morning.
12''He's getting the right treatment,'' Norris said.
13Norris declined to say where Vickers' blood clots were located.
14Waltrip's team has only preliminary information about Vickers' condition, but the driver is in the care of doctors in Los Angeles.
15Moffitt will return to the No.
1655 Toyota this weekend after driving it to an impressive eighth-place finish at Atlanta.
17Although Vickers has been sidelined by medical issues for the fourth time in his NASCAR tenure, Waltrip wouldn't speculate on whether Vickers' racing career is over.
18''He would not say that, and therefore I can't say that,'' Waltrip said.
19Vickers missed the first two races of this year following offseason heart surgery to repair a patch on a hole in his heart that his body was rejecting.
20The heart issue was first discovered in 2010 when Vickers was sidelined with his first bout with blood clots.
21The blood clots caused Vickers to miss the final five races of the 2013 season because he was placed on blood thinners to treat a clot in his right calf.
22He also missed 25 races during the 2010 season when clots were discovered in his legs and lungs.
23After his December heart surgery kept him out of the car for two weeks, Vickers returned to racing two weeks ago.
24He was 15th at Las Vegas in his return, but was involved in a crash on the first lap at Phoenix last week and finished 41st.
25''Thankfully, because I recognized the signs and symptoms, the doctors caught this early and I'm going to be OK,'' Vickers said in a statement.
26''I had finished my treatment for the clot I had in my leg back in 2013 and I haven't needed to be on a blood thinner for a clot in my leg or lung since.
27Now I won't be able to race because I'll need to be back on a blood thinner.
28Vickers also noted that his latest setback is occurring during ''Blood Clot Awareness Month.'' He was scheduled to help promote the issue at Fontana with Janssen Pharmaceuticals, one of his partners.
29Moffitt had been scheduled to drive this weekend for Front Row Motorsports, a ride he landed after his impressive stint for Vickers at Atlanta.
30Front Row needed a driver to step in for David Ragan, who is replacing the injured Kyle Busch at Joe Gibbs Racing.
31Moffitt was 37th at Las Vegas and 32nd at Phoenix for Front Row.
32The MWR development driver was using the Front Row opportunity to get seat time.
