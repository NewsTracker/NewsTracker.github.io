News24.com | Mexico prosecutor: Video has suspects in journalist killing

1A photograph of murdered photojournalist Ruben Espinosa sits among flowers in front of his casket inside a funeral home before his wake begins in Mexico City.
2Mexico City - Prosecutors released a surveillance video on Tuesday showing suspects calmly leaving an apartment building where five people were found tortured and shot to death, including a photojournalist who had taken refuge in the capital after feeling threatened in the Mexican state he covered.
3The video, stamped just after 15:00 on Friday, shows one man with a roller suitcase walking away and another getting into a red Ford Mustang and driving away.
4A third suspect is seen crossing the street five minutes later.
5All three walk normally, and the driver of Mustang takes his time pulling out.
6Mexico City Prosecutor Rodolfo Rios Garza said the three men are the prime suspects in the killing of journalist Ruben Espinosa, cultural promoter Nadia Vera and three other women, including a domestic employee.
7All were found tied up and with gunshot wounds to the head.
8Espinosa showed signs of torture, and some of the women appeared to have been sexually abused, officials have said.
9In June, Espinosa had fled the Gulf coast state of Veracruz, where 11 journalists have been killed since 2010, after he was followed and harassed by strangers waiting outside his house.
10Once in Mexico City, he still didn't feel safe, telling friends that strangers approached him on two different occasions asking if he was the photographer who had left Veracruz.
11Espinosa knew Vera from Veracruz, where she was an outspoken activist and organizer critical of the state government of Gov. Javier Duarte.
12In an earlier video interview, Vera said Duarte and his Cabinet were responsible for "anything that could happen to us as people involved in and organisers" of the protest movement.
13When asked if Duarte would be called to give a deposition, Rio said Mexico City officials were in contact with him and his attorney general.
14But officials appeared to be focusing on robbery and a female victim thought to be Colombian as its target, even as they repeated a commitment made on Monday that they are not discounting any motive or line of investigation.
15Rios said the crime was committed sometime before 15:00 on Friday, though he did not say when the suspects arrived at the apartment.
16The killers would have had only a half hour to 50 minutes to torture and shoot five people, ransack the apartment and pack a suitcase with stolen goods, according to witnesses who spoke to The Associated Press.
17Espinosa was last heard from in a text message to a friend at 14:13, saying he was leaving the apartment to return home.
18A neighbour in the next building said he saw one of the victims, the woman believed to be Colombian, on the street at 14:30 talking normally with one of the men who later entered the building and left.
19The prosecutor's office said it had nothing to add when asked about the tight time frame.
20The Mustang was found abandoned in the southern part of the capital on Monday.
21Rios said investigators were trying to reach the registered owner, but he believed the car was being used by the apparently Colombian victim, though she has not officially been identified as a citizen of that country.
22Officials have not released any of the victims' names apart from Espinosa and Vera.
23Their bodies were found in Vera's apartment around 09:00 on Friday by a roommate who had left that morning to go to work.
24Rios said Espinosa and another friend had arrived at the apartment around 02:00 on Friday with Vera.
25He said they stayed up all night and went to sleep around 6 a.m. The housekeeper came about 09:00.
26During that time, Espinosa's friend, who has not been identified, left the apartment as did Vera's roommate.
27The suspects are seen leaving the building at 15:02.
28"It is during this time that the killings occurred," Rios said.
