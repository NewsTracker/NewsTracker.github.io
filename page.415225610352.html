1The pachyderms will no longer be packing 'em in to watch "The Greatest Show on Earth."
2The Ringling Bros.
3and Barnum & Bailey Circus vowed Thursday to get rid of all elephants in its traveling act - after years of battling lawmakers and animal-rights groups.
4"There's been somewhat of a mood shift among our customers. A lot of people aren't comfortable with us touring with our elephants," said Alana Feld, of Feld Entertainment, a parent company of the circus.
5Ringmasters will remove all 13 of the big, friendly beasts from the act by 2018 and take them to a conservation center near Orlando, Fla., the firm said.
6"Of course, we're thrilled. But our primary concern is that three years is much too long," said Delcianna Winders, deputy general counsel for the animal-rights group PETA.
7The company needs time to build new structures for the retiring pachyderms, which cost roughly $65,000 each to care for annually, a circus rep said.
8Circus-elephant bans in cities such as Oakland and Los Angeles played a role in the company's decision remove the creatures from the show, the rep said.

1Officials said Interior Minister Mohamed Ibrahim was replaced by another police general, Magdy Abdel-Ghafar.
2Ibrahim was among the top Egyptian officials that Human Rights Watch urged to be probed for "crimes against humanity" over deadly 2013 clashes between security forces and protesters in the country.
3An audio recording obtained by Al Jazeera last month suggested that Ibrahim gave the green light for using automatic weapons against protesters.
4Ibrahim's removal comes after an uptick in bomb attacks blamed on armed groups targeting the heart of the heavily protected Egyptian capital, Cairo.
5Yehia Ghanem, a well-known Egyptian journalist and resident fellow at New York's City University, told Al Jazeera that "the main objective of such a reshuffle is to calm down segments of society angry over eroding security and flagrant violations of human rights".
6He also said the fact the reshuffle came just a few days before the country's major economic investment conference showed that Sisi believed political change was needed urgently.
7"The situation in Egypt is getting more critical, because if it was not that critical no sane person would make that reshuffle just days before that very important conference on rehabilitating the Egyptian economy," he said.
8It is the first cabinet reshuffle since Sisi, a solider-turned-politician, took office in June.
9A year ago he led the military ouster of the Mohamed Morsi, the nation's first freely-elected leader.
10Leaked audio appears to show interior minister advising in 2014 on how to shoot protesters without creating "martyrs".
