Colorado rampage trial juror says other panelist discussed prosecutor tweet

1CENTENNIAL, Colo.
2The capital murder trial of Colorado movie theater gunman James Holmes came to a halt on Tuesday when a juror told the judge another panelist had talked about a prosecutor's Twitter message relating to the trial.
3Last week District Attorney George Brauchler sent a tweet that referred to a videotaped interview that a psychiatrist conducted with Holmes, who killed 12 moviegoers and wounded dozens more when he opened fire in a suburban Denver theater in 2012.
4The judge has repeatedly told jurors not to read media reports or material on social media about the trial and not to discuss the case among themselves or with others.
5Holmes, a 27-year-old California native, has pleaded not guilty by reason of insanity to multiple counts of first-degree murder, attempted murder and explosives charges stemming from the rampage.
6His lawyers say he suffers from schizophrenia.
7Holmes' defense attorneys brought the message to the judge's attention on Friday and Brauchler has apologized for the tweet.
8On Tuesday, Arapahoe County District Judge Carlos Samour said in court he had received a note from a juror about the tweet.
9He then called in the panelist who sent the note.
10The reporting juror, a woman, said another person on the jury had talked on Monday about seeing a report about a message on Twitter last week that related to the trial and came from one of the attorneys in the case.
11She said a third juror also heard the conversation.
12Judge Samour excused the juror who reported the issue from the courtroom and told attorneys for both sides he was concerned about the episode and the fact that the other juror who was present at the conversation had not reported it.
13"That concerns me as well," he said.
14Samour called a recess to give defense attorneys time to consider their next step.
