Iraq exhumes remains of 164 from Tikrit graves

1"Search teams have discovered the remains of 164 (victims) so far in four mass graves during work over the past week," ministry spokesman Kamel Amin told AFP.
2He said documents and mobile phones that have been found indicate the dead are victims of the infamous Speicher massacre, named for the military base near which up to 1,700 mostly Shiite recruits were abducted by the Islamic State (IS) jihadist group last year.
3But DNA testing is required to confirm their identities, Amin said.
4The remains were discovered inside former president Saddam Hussein's palace compound in Tikrit, which officials say holds 10 mass graves, while three more are located outside it.
5The killing of the recruits -- which IS documented in photos and videos posted online -- stoked widespread anger and helped rally support for the battle against the jihadist group.
6The mass grave sites were discovered after Iraqi forces retook the northern city of Tikrit earlier this month in their biggest victory so far against IS.
