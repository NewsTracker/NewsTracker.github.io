Islamic State uses ancient Roman ruins in Palmyra to stage a mass execution

1The ancient Roman city of Palmyra.
2Beirut: Islamic State has released a video purportedly showing the execution of dozens of Syrian government soldiers in the ancient ruins of Palmyra.
3The footage shows 25 men kneeling on the stage of the site's historic Roman theatre, their hands tied behind their backs.
4With ritualistic precision, the same numbers of executioners then step out in unison, each taking up position behind one of the condemned.
5A large black IS flag is draped over the columns behind them.
6Wearing matching long brown robes and brown bandannas the executioners appear to be boys or young teenagers - few look old enough to grow a beard.
7Footage filmed from cameras trained on the soldiers' faces show that the men have been beaten.
8Then the child jihadists transform the world-class tourist attraction into a killing ground.
9Simultaneously raising their pistols, they spray bullets into the heads and backs of the men, leaving them lifeless and bloody on the ground.
10The video, circulated on Saturday on pro-IS social media accounts, is believed to have been filmed in the days after IS took control of Palmyra in May, when local activists reported that hundreds of captured soldiers were murdered by the jihadists.
11At the time, residents of the city were rounded up and forced to watch the killings, the Syrian Observatory for Human Rights monitoring group said.
12The footage, filmed using professional techniques, appears designed to strike fear into Syrian regime supporters and counter accusations by IS' detractors that, unlike other jihadist groups in Syria such as al-Qaeda's Jabhat al-Nusra, IS has not focused on fighting President Bashar al-Assad.
13Syrian rebel groups have long accused IS of colluding with Damascus, citing examples where the jihadists have sold oil to the regime.
14Palmyra's ancient ruins are listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and there has been concern IS might seek to destroy its heritage, as it has done elsewhere in Syria and Iraq.
15The jihadists appear to have realised these fears.
16Extremists posted pictures last week of men smashing antiquities in Palmyra, including a 2000-year-old lion statue originally unearthed from the temple of the pre-Islamic Arabian goddess al-Lat.
