Twister kills 10 in Mexico; flash flooding hits Texas

1Twister kills 10 in Mexico; flash flooding hits Texas Add to...
2A tornado raged through a Mexico border city early Monday, destroying homes, flinging cars like matchsticks and ripping an infant from its mother's arms.
3At least 10 people were killed, authorities said.
4In Texas, 12 people were reported missing after flash flooding from a line of storms that stretched from the Gulf of Mexico to the Great Lakes.
5The baby was missing after the twister sent its infant carrier flying.
6Rescue workers began digging through the rubble of damaged homes for more victims of the tornado that hit Ciudad Acuna, a city of 125,000 across from Del Rio, Texas, shortly after daybreak.
7"It hit an area of about seven blocks," said Victor Zamora, interior secretary of the northern state of Coahuila, describing the neighbourhood as "devastated."
8Officials confirmed 10 dead, but that number could climb, Zamora said.
9Photos from the scene showed cars with their hoods ripped off, resting upended against single-story houses.
10One car's frame was bent around the gate of a house.
11A bus was seen flipped and crumpled on a roadway.
12The twister struck around the time buses were preparing to take children to school, Zamora said.
13The system dumped record rainfall on parts of the Plains and Midwest, spawning tornadoes and causing major flooding that forced at least 2,000 Texans from their homes.
14A vacation house in Texas was swept away by a rain-swollen river.
15In the U.S., the system was blamed for three deaths Saturday and Sunday, including two in Oklahoma and one in Texas, where a man's body was recovered from a flooded area along the Blanco River, which rose 26 feet in an hour and created huge piles of debris.
16The line of storms was expected to linger over a large swath of the region Monday.
17Among the worst-affected communities were Wimberley and San Marcos, which are in Central Texas along the Blanco River in the increasingly popular corridor between Austin and San Antonio.
18"It looks pretty bad out there," Hays County emergency management co-ordinator Kharley Smith said of Wimberley, where an estimated 350 to 400 homes were destroyed and where three people remained missing late Sunday.
19"We do have whole streets with maybe one or two houses left on them and the rest are just slabs."
20Kristi Wyatt, a spokeswoman for San Marcos, said about 1,000 homes were damaged throughout Hays County, which includes Wimberley and which will be toured Monday by Gov. Greg Abbott.
21Five San Marcos police cars were washed away and the fire house was flooded.
22Rivers swelled so quickly that whole communities awoke Sunday surrounded by water.
23The Blanco crested above 40 feet - more than triple its flood stage of 13 feet - swamping Interstate 35 and forcing parts of the busy north-south highway to close.
24Rescuers used pontoon boats and a helicopter to pull people out.
25After a surge of mud and water flooded their cottage in Wimberley, John and Valerie Nelson fled through waist-deep waters in darkness early Sunday with transformers sparking and trees crashing around them.
26The single-story house, which had been Valerie Nelson's grandmother's, had been carefully rebuilt on stilts so that it would be able to withstand even the worst flooding.
27"I'm absolutely dumbfounded," said Valerie Nelson, who has owned the property for about 50 years.
28"I didn't think the water would ever get that high."
29Hundreds of trees along the Blanco were uprooted or snapped, and they collected in piles of debris that soared 20 feet high.
30"We've got trees in the rafters," said Cherri Maley, the property manager of a house whose entire rear portion had collapsed with the flooding, carrying away furniture.
31About 1,000 residents were evacuated from roughly 400 homes near an earthen dam at Lake Lewis, about 50 miles north of Houston.
32Montgomery County emergency management officials fear the dam could fail due to the flooding.
33Agency spokeswoman Miranda Hahs said it was not clear when residents would be allowed to return home.
34A tornado briefly touched down Sunday in Houston, damaging rooftops, toppling trees, blowing out windows and sending at least two people to a hospital.
35Fire officials said 10 apartments were heavily damaged and 40 others sustained lesser damage.
36Dallas faced severe flooding from the Trinity River, which was expected to crest near 40 feet Monday and lap at the foundations of an industrial park.
37The Red and Wichita rivers also rose far above flood stage.
38This May is already the wettest on record for several Plains cities, with days still to go and more rain on the way.
39Oklahoma City set a new monthly rainfall total this weekend - 18.2 inches through Saturday, surpassing the previous record of 14.5 in 2013.
40So far this year, Oklahoma City has gotten 27.37 inches of rain.
41It got only 4.29 inches all of last year.
42The recent rainfall may officially end the drought that has gripped the region for years, according to Forrest Mitchell, a meteorologist at the weather service's office in Norman, Oklahoma.
43He said many lakes and reservoirs are full.
