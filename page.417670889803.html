Swiss attorney general seizes evidence from FIFA offices

1BERN, Switzerland (AP) " The Swiss attorney-general's office says it has seized more evidence from FIFA headquarters for its investigation into the 2018 and 2022 World Cup bidding contests.
2The Swiss federal agency says FIFA handed over seized "IT data" to its officials.
3The evidence gathering follows two weeks after FIFA's offices were raided for the investigation.
4Switzerland's Attorney General Michael Lauber opened criminal proceedings "against persons unknown" two weeks ago in a case probing possible criminal mismanagement and money laundering in the World Cup bid contests won by Russia and Qatar.
5FIFA filed the criminal complaint last November.
6The Swiss agency says FIFA president Sepp Blatter and Secretary General Jerome Valcke are not suspects in the case.
