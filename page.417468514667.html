British actor joins Kurds fighting Islamic State group

1BEIRUT (AP) - A British actor who has had minor roles in Hollywood films has joined Kurdish fighters battling the Islamic State group in Syria and appeared in an online video Tuesday.
2Michael Enright, who played a deckhand in "Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest," appeared in a video released by the Kurdish People's Protection Units, known as the YPG.
3The video showed him in a trench with other fighters firing an assault rifle.
4"ISIS are dangerous to every human being alive," Enright says in the video, posted on the YPG's Facebook page on Tuesday, referring to the IS group.
5He called for weapons and medical aid for the Kurdish fighters, describing them as "my havals," the Kurdish word for comrades.
6The YPG has emerged as a key fighting force against IS in Syria.
7With the help of U.S.-led coalition airstrikes, they have succeeded in liberating dozens of towns and villages in northeastern Syria from IS.
8Dozens of other Westerners now fight with the Kurds, both in Syria and Iraq.
9In an interview with Dubai-based Al-Aan TV from the Kurdish city of Hassakeh, Enright said he became aware of IS when the extremist group "cut off an American journalist's head."
10But the "straw that broke the camel's back" came in February, when the group released a video showing a captured Jordanian pilot being burned alive.
11Enright said he is willing to die for the cause.
12"I didn't come here to play games, I wrote to all my friends and family because I might not see them again," he told Al-Aan.
13Enright also played minor roles in "Knight and Day" and "Old Dogs," according to the IMDb online entertainment database.
