Egypt's Sisi promises tougher legal system as prosecutor buried

1CAIRO Egypt's president told mourners on Tuesday at the funeral of the country's top prosecutor, killed by a car bomb, that he would unveil tough new security policies in the coming days, vowing that death sentences would be carried out.
2Monday's killing of public prosecutor Hisham Barakat, the most senior Egyptian official to die in such an attack in years, cast doubt on Egypt's ability to contain an Islamist insurgency that is picking increasingly high-profile targets.
3In his address at the funeral, President Abdel Fattah al-Sisi said: "The hand of justice is tied by laws ... We will not wait for that. We will not sit for five or 10 years putting on trial the people who kill us."
4The funeral fell on the second anniversary of the start of mass protests that preceded the overthrow of Islamist President Mohamed Mursi in July 2013 by the army, then under Sisi's leadership.
5Since then, Egyptian courts have handed down preliminary death sentences to hundreds of alleged members of the now banned Muslim Brotherhood.
6Mursi also faces the death penalty.
7Sisi did not give details of his plans for the legal changes but said they would be unveiled "within days".
8"A death sentence will be issued, a death sentence will be implemented. A life sentence will be issued, a life sentence will be implemented," he said.
9Western governments have criticized the mass death sentences but are unlikely to take strong measures against Egypt, seen as a vital partner for security in a region beset by turmoil.
10The government has also expanded the jurisdiction of military courts to try civilians accused of terrorism, part of a crackdown that first targeted Islamists but has expanded to include liberal activists.
11Militant attacks that have killed hundreds of soldiers and police -- focused mainly in the North Sinai region but also extending to Egypt's main cities -- have increased since Mursi's toppling.
12In June, a suicide bomber blew himself up near the ancient Karnak Temple in the city of Luxor, a major attraction in Egypt where tourism is economically vital.
13On Tuesday, at least three people were killed and others wounded when explosives went off in a car near a police station in Cairo's western suburb of 6th of October City, security sources said.
14A policeman was killed in the southern Cairo district of Helwan in a gunfight with militants, a security source told the state news agency MENA.
15Judicial sources told Reuters that new policies alluded to by Sisi may restrict the number of appeals available to people convicted of certain crimes to one from two, and judges may be granted final say on which witnesses can testify.
16Some of Egypt's judges have been accused of blatant bias in trials against Islamists, but the judiciary says it is independent of the government and military.
