Timeline of events in 2013 Boston Marathon bombing

1A protestor from Veterans for Peace waits outside the Moakley Federal Court in Boston, Friday, May 15, 2015, after the U.S. Attorney's office announced that there was a verdict in the penalty phase of the trial of Boston Marathon bomber Dzhokhar Tsarnaev.
2The federal jury must decide whether the 21-year-old Tsarnaev should be sentenced to death or life in prison for his role in the deadly attack in 2013.
3FILE - In this Monday, April 15, 2013 file photo, the elite men start the 117th running of the Boston Marathon in Hopkinton, Mass.
4A timeline of events related to the Boston Marathon bombing, which killed three people and injured 260 others on April 15, 2013.
5A federal jury on Friday sentenced Dzhokhar Tsarnaev to death.
6March 2011: Russian FSB intelligence security service gives FBI information that Tamerlan Tsarnaev, of Cambridge, Massachusetts, is a follower of radical Islam.
7June 2011: FBI closes investigation after finding nothing to link Tamerlan Tsarnaev to terrorism.
8Sept.
912, 2011: Bodies of three men are found in Waltham, Massachusetts, with their throats slit and marijuana sprinkled over them.
10Late 2011: U.S. officials add the Tsarnaevs' mother to a federal terrorism database after Russia contacts CIA with concerns they were religious militants about to travel to Russia.
11She later says she has no links to terrorism.
12January 2012: Tamerlan arrives in Russia, where he spends time in two predominantly Muslim provinces, Dagestan and Chechnya.
13July 2012: Officials in Dagestan say Tamerlan applies for a new passport but never picks it up.
14Russian officials say they have him under surveillance but lose track of him after the death of a Canadian man who had joined an Islamic insurgency in the region.
15November 2012: Islamic Society of Boston Cultural Center in Cambridge says Tamerlan has an outburst that interrupts a sermon about it being acceptable for Muslims to celebrate American holidays.
16January 2013: Islamic Society says Tamerlan has a second outburst after a sermon that includes praise for the Rev.
17April 15, 2013: Bombs go off at the finish line of the Boston Marathon.
18April 16, 2013: Federal agents say the bombs were made from pressure cookers packed with explosives, nails and other shrapnel, but they still don't know who detonated them or why.
19April 17, 2013: President Barack Obama signs emergency declaration for Massachusetts and orders federal aid to supplement local response.
20April 18, 2013: Investigators release photos and video of two suspects and ask for public's help identifying them.
21Later that night, Massachusetts Institute of Technology police officer Sean Collier is shot to death in his cruiser by Tamerlan and Dzhokhar Tsarnaev.
22They steal an SUV at gunpoint from a Cambridge gas station.
23The driver is held for about a half-hour, then released unharmed.
24April 19, 2013: Tsarnaevs have an early morning gunbattle with authorities who have tracked them to Watertown.
25Tamerlan, who is run over by his younger brother, dies.
26Dzhokhar escapes, and at around 6 a.m., authorities tell residents of Boston and surrounding communities to stay indoors.
27All mass transit is shut down.
28That order is lifted around 6:30 p.m., just before authorities trace Dzhokhar to a Watertown backyard, where he is found hiding in a boat and taken into custody.
29April 22, 2013: Dzhokhar Tsarnaev, injured in the shootout, is charged in his hospital room with using and conspiring to use a weapon of mass destruction.
30April 30, 2013: Two friends of Dzhokhar's are charged with attempting to destroy evidence by disposing of a backpack and laptop computer taken from his room after they found he was a suspect in the bombing.
31Another is charged with lying to investigators.
32May 9, 2013: Tamerlan Tsarnaev is secretly buried in Virginia after a weeklong search for a cemetery willing to take the body.
33May 22, 2013: An FBI agent in Orlando, Florida, fatally shoots Ibragim Todashev, a friend of Tamerlan's, after he lunges at law enforcement officials questioning him about the Waltham killings.
34Officials say that before he died, he had agreed to give a statement about his involvement.
35July 10, 2013: Dzhokhar Tsarnaev pleads not guilty to 30 federal charges.
36July 23, 2013: Marc Fucarile is the last survivor of the bombings to leave the hospital.
37Jan. 30, 2014: Prosecutors announce they will seek the death penalty against Dzhokhar.
38April 15, 2014: Ceremonies and events mark the anniversary of the attacks.
39April 21, 2014: The 2014 Boston Marathon features a field of 36,000 runners, 9,000 more than 2013 and the second-biggest field in history.
40May 30, 2014: Khairullozhon Matanov, 23, of Quincy, is arrested on charges of obstructing the investigation by deleting information from his computer and lying to investigators.
41July 21, 2014: Azamat Tazhayakov, a college friend of Dzhokhar's, is convicted of obstruction of justice and conspiracy for agreeing with another friend to get rid of a backpack and disabled fireworks they took from his dorm room three days after the attack.
42July 22, 2014: Stephen Silva, believed to have provided the gun used by the Tsarnaevs to kill Collier, is arrested on drug and weapons charges.
43Aug. 22, 2014: Dias Kadyrbayev, 20, pleads guilty to impeding the investigation by removing incriminating evidence from Dzhokhar's dorm room.
4424, 2014: Judge grants delay and pushes start of trial to Jan. 5, 2015.
45Oct. 28, 2014: Robel Phillipos, 21, of Cambridge, is convicted of lying to federal agents about being in Dzhokhar's room.
46Nov. 25, 2014: Federal judge rejects a request from lawyers for Tsarnaev to order prosecutors to turn over evidence about his older brother's possible participation in the Waltham slayings.
47Dec. 18, 2014: Tsarnaev appears in court for first time since his July 2013 arraignment.
48Jan. 5, 2015: Jury selection begins in Tsarnaev's trial.
49March 4, 2015: Tsarnaev's lead defense attorney, Judy Clarke, declares in opening statements: "It was him."
50April 6, 2015: Prosecutors and defense present closing statements.
51April 7, 2015: Jury begins deliberating verdicts.
52April 8, 2015: Jury convicts Tsarnaev; will weigh possible death sentence in forthcoming penalty phase of trial.
53April 15, 2015: Second anniversary of Boston marathon bombings.
54April 20, 2015: 119th running of the Boston Marathon.
55April 21, 2015: Penalty phase of Tsarnaev's trial begins.
56May 13, 2015: Prosecutors and defense make closing statements on sentence; jury begins deliberating.
57May 15, 2015: Exactly 25 months after the attack, jury sentences Tsarnaev to death.
