Islamic State Accepts Allegiance Of Boko Haram

1Islamic State has accepted a pledge of allegiance from the Nigerian terror group Boko Haram and called on Islamists to fight in Africa.
2Abu Mohammad al Adnani, a spokesman for IS, announced in an audio message the group's leader, Abu Bakr al Baghdadi, had accepted the loyalty of the Nigerian militants.
3"Our caliph, God save him, has accepted the pledge of loyalty of our brothers of Boko Haram so we congratulate Muslims and our jihadi brothers in West Africa," he said.
4It comes after an audio statement attributed to Boko Haram leader Abubakar Shekau was released on Saturday through the group's Twitter account.
5The statement urged "Muslims everywhere to pledge allegiance to the Caliph".
6The apparent alliance highlights increased co-ordination between the two terror movements across North Africa and the Middle East.
7IS has declared a caliphate and captured large swathes of territory in Iraq and Syria.
8In his audio message, al Adnani called on Muslims who could not join IS in Iraq and Syria to enter combat in Africa instead.
9He added that Boko Haram's pledge had opened a "new door for you to migrate to the land of Islam and fight".
10The group, which rejects all but its own limited interpretation of early Sunni Muslim theology as heresy, also issued a threat to Jews and Christians.
11"If you want to save your blood and money and live in safety from our swords... you have two choices: either convert or pay jezyah," he said, referring to tax for non-Muslims under Islamic rule.
