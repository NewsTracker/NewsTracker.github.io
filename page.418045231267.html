Taylor Swift will ‘happily’ stream ‘1989’ on Apple Music

1Turns out there won't be a blank space in the Taylor Swift category on Apple Music.
2The 25-year-old songstress took to Twitter on Thursday to reveal her album will indeed be featured on the new streaming service.
3"After the events of this week, I've decided to put 1989 on Apple Music ... and happily so," Swift wrote to her close to 60 million followers.
4"In case you're wondering if this is some exclusive deal like you've seen Apple do with other artists, it's not," she continued.
5"This is simply the first time it's felt right in my gut to stream my album. Thank you, Apple, for your change of heart."
6Swift's tweet follows her slamming the tech giant over the weekend on her Tumblr page for their plans not to pay artists, writers or producers during the free trial period of the upcoming streaming service, which debuts June 30.
7"I find it to be shocking, disappointing and completely unlike this historically progressive and generous company," she wrote, vowing to keep her successful album "1989" off of it.
8"#AppleMusic will pay artists for streaming, even during customers' free trial period," Eddy Cue, Apple's senior vice president of Internet Software and Services, wrote later Sunday following Swift's declaration.
