Ukraine evacuates hundreds from site of deadly Kiev fuel blasts

1A Ukrainian firefighter retreats from the fire at the fuel depot outside Kiev.
2Kiev: Hundreds of residents were evacuated from the site of a series of fuel depot blasts near the Ukrainian capital that set off a ferocious fire and left several people missing and at least one confirmed dead.
3The defence ministry said it was also taking emergency measures to prevent the flames engulfing a nearby military airbase which has MiG-29 fighter jets and munitions on site.
4Security Council Secretary Oleksandr Turchynov and Defence Minister Stepan Poltorak rushed to the scene to oversee the rescue operations.
5Ukrainian firemen tackle the blaze.
6"We are facing a serious threat... because 50 metres from the edge of the fire, we have a military installation stocked with an arsenal," Mr Turchynov said in televised comments.
7The interior ministry said at least one person had died in the explosions in a leafy town called Vasylkiv about 30 kilometres south-west of Ukraine's capital Kiev.
8Fourteen people - including seven rescue workers - were being treated for burns and other injuries.
9Residents living around the plant were evacuated.
10The lightning speed with which the flames spread to neighbouring petrol stations and fuel tanks resulted in initial confusion among emergency workers and contradictory toll reports.
11Interior Minister Arsen Avakov said officials were still trying to establish contact with three of the 30 firemen who were intially sent to the spot of the first blast.
12But more explosions soon followed and dozens of additional firetrucks and ambulances were dispatched to the scene, where thick black smoke billowed into the sky.
13An AFP reporter saw three underground fuel storage tanks on fire within a few hundred metres of the initial accident.
14Videos posted on the Internet showed the force of the first explosion and the heatwave it produced setting ablaze several firetrucks and ambulances that had been rushed to the scene.
15A fireman wearing a silver heat-resistant suit and mask was seen running away from the disaster for his own safety.
16"Everyone - leave. Whoever is still here - leave," one fireman said in a video.
17Ukraine's emergencies ministry began evacuating residents living within two kilometres of the fuel depots.
18"If necessary, we will evacuate people living within 10 kilometres," the ministry statement added.
19Kiev has been suffering from a dry heat spell that has seen temperatures soar towards 30 degrees for more than a week.
20Security Council chief Mr Turchynov said the accident most likely resulted from human error and "safety rule violations".
21"But the second possibility is sabotage," he added in televised comments.
22The damaged fuel depots are owned by a Ukrainian company called BRSM-Nafta that operates a chain of petrol stations.
23BRSM-NAfta spokesman Oleksandr Melnychuk said several explosive devices had been discovered near his company's storage tankers since last August.
24"We view this as an attempt to put pressure on the company owner," Mr Melnychuk said in a telephone interview.
25"They wanted to nationalise [the company] - to take it away from us."
