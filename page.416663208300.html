5 months after infection, man spreads Ebola via sex

1NEW YORK (AP) - Health officials now think Ebola survivors can spread the disease through unprotected sex nearly twice as long as previously believed.
2Scientists thought the Ebola virus could remain in semen for about three months.
3But a recent case in West Africa suggests infection through sex can happen more than five months later.
4Based on the case, officials are now telling male Ebola survivors to avoid unprotected sex indefinitely.
5They had previously advised using condoms for at least three months.
6A report Friday detailed the case of a Liberian woman whose infection likely came from a man who had Ebola symptoms last September.
7She fell ill in March, a week after sex with him.
8She died.
9Another woman he had sex with around the same time tested negative.
