Boko Haram beheading video suggests group is 'incorporating itself into Islamic State' with 'Isis-like' production techniques

Iraqi forces pursue battle to retake Tikrit from ISIL militants

1The film, released via social media on Monday, shows militants standing behind two men who are bound and on their knees.
2Its graphics and advanced editing techniques echo beheading videos and well-produced propaganda released by Isis through its various media arms.
3The video, entitled 'Harvest of Spies', sees one of the bound men asked to say his name and where he is from.
4He introduces himself as Dawoud Muhammad, a farmer from the Nigerian town of Baga, and says he was one of three men bribed by police to spy on the Islamist militant group.
5The second man says he is from Michika in Adamawa.
6A militant stands behind him brandishing a knife in a scene comparable to the actions of Jihadi John, the British Isis militant, who was seen standing behind hostages from the UK, the US and Japan and wielding a knife.
726-year-old 'Jihadi John' is believed to have left Britain in 2012-13 Nigeria's army claimed territories in Baga were recaptured from Boko Haram in recent months.
8In January, Boko Haram claimed responsibility for the massacre of up to 2,000 civilians in Baga.
9A flag can be clearly seen in the corner of the video, another production aspect well established in Isis videos.
10Speech is translated into English, French and Arabic in subtitles accompanying the footage.
11The film moves to another scene showing their decapitated bodies.
12It was not possible to confirm the film's authenticity.
13In February, Boko Haram released a trailer followed by a feature-length piece of propaganda.
14Isis released a trailer six months before followed by a full length 'Flames of War video threatening US troops in Iraq.
15The US-based Terrorism Research & Analysis Consortium (TRAC) monitor said Boko Haram's image is being "shaped" by Isis's media wing.
16"This latest release shows Boko Haram is not a mere copycat of Isis; rather, it is incorporating itself into the Islamic State," Veryan Khan, editorial director of the TRAC said.
17Peter Pham, director of the Africa Centre at the Washington-based Atlantic Council think tank who testified at a US Congressional hearing on Boko Haram, said that there were "tantalizing clues that should not be lightly dismissed" of a merging of Isis and Boko Haram, according to Al Arabiya.
18Abubakar Shekau, leader of Boko Haram, in a video He cited the salutations of Boko Haram leader Abubakar Shekau's towards Isis's self-declared 'caliphate' and the black Islamist flag, similar to that used by Isis, as an example of this.
19Boko Haram is also pursuing territories in a "caliphate-orientated strategy" like that of Isis, he added.
20Jacob Zenn, an analyst at the Washington-based Jamestown Foundation think tank, said "there are real links between the groups, beyond only shared ideology".
21Nigeria's President Goodluck Jonathan has said Boko Haram is allied to both al-Qaeda and Isis, though that has not been confirmed by the group itself.

5It is thought an assault to retake that city could begin as early as next month.
6An Iranian military commander is said to be partially coordinating the current offensive, involving some 30,000 men, hailed as the biggest such assault since ISIL seized huge swathes of Iraq last summer.
7The presence of Iranian military commander Qassem Soleimani on the frontline highlights neighbouring Iran's influence over the Shi'ite fighters who have been key to containing the militants in Iraq.
8In contrast, the US-led air coalition which has been attacking ISIL across Iraq and Syria has not yet played a role in Tikrit, the Pentagon said on Monday, perhaps in part because of the high-level Iranian presence.
