Clinton already uniting prospective GOP presidential field

1A van nicknamed "Scooby" and driven by the Secret Service was crossing a 1,000-mile swath of the country Monday from Clinton's home in the New York City suburbs, setting the stage for a community college appearance the next morning and more campaign events in a state where she ran third in the 2008 caucuses.
2Clinton announced her bid for the Democratic nomination in a video Sunday, then hit the road.
3Already the GOP is treating her as the Democratic nominee.
4Announcing his own 2016 campaign to top donors Monday, Florida GOP Sen. Marco Rubio branded the former first lady, senator and secretary of state as "a leader from yesterday who wants to take us back to yesterday."
5Kentucky Sen. Rand Paul, another declared candidate, put Clinton at the center of his first TV ad, titled "Liberty, not Hillary."
6Prospective candidates piled on, too.
7Wisconsin Gov. Scott Walker criticized Clinton's "Washington-knows-best mentality" in tweets.
8Former Florida Gov. Jeb Bush derided the "Obama-Clinton foreign policy" in a video message.
9While there are serious policy differences among the dozen or so major Republicans considering a run for president, they appear to have all concluded there's little downside in starting early on Clinton.
10Even former Massachusetts Gov. Mitt Romney, the party's 2012 nominee, couldn't resist, saying on "Fox News Sunday" that "she's a creature of Washington" and "just not trustworthy."
11The road trip was Clinton's idea, aides said.
12Longtime adviser Huma Abedin, in a conference call with Clinton alumni, said Clinton's van stopped at a gas station in Pennsylvania, where she met a family from Michigan.
13She was expected to reach Iowa later in the day.
14Rubio's team was initially concerned that Clinton's announcement and activities would overshadow his splashy rally Monday evening that's expected to kick off his 2016 campaign.
15But they concluded the almost overlapping events would draw a contrast between Rubio, the 43-year-old senator, and Clinton, who is almost 25 years his senior.
16It's a calculus that didn't occur, or didn't matter, to Clinton.
17Her video, shared hundreds of thousands of times in the first hour after being posted online, said nothing about anyone else in the 2016 race.
18An old Clinton rival from Arkansas who is also considering a run for president, former Gov. Mike Huckabee, predicted her silence won't last.
19"Anyone who thinks that she's going to get into this halfheartedly, well, they've never ever encountered the will, the spirit, the heart and the determination of the Clinton political machinery," he said on ABC's "This Week."
20Bush tapped supporters with a fundraising appeal to help him stop Clinton's "liberal agenda."
21Paul started selling "Hillary's Hard Drive" on his website, a not-so-subtle reference to Clinton's use of a personal email account and server while serving as secretary of state.
22The Republican National Committee and conservative groups are in the anti-Clinton fray, too.
23At least 10 political organizations list defeating Clinton as their primary mission in their filings with the Federal Election Commission, and Democrats say they're preparing to face much as $500 million worth of attack ads during the 2016 election.
24On Monday, the Republican National Committee said its "Stop Hillary" ad was shown repeatedly on all the major networks.
25Its campaign will continue this week with plans to distribute #StopHillary beer Koozies at the Washington Nationals baseball game.
26"Ninety-eight percent of our focus has been on Hillary for the last year," said Sean Spicer, chief strategist and communications director of the committee.
27Associated Press writer Philip Elliott contributed to this report from Washington.

29Clinton has spent nearly two years tiptoeing around the decision to run for president, one that much of the political world assumed was a done deal.
30As 2014 opened, Clinton was discussing her White House ambitions with an ever-widening circle.
31She convened economic discussions that centered on income inequality and the widening achievement gap.
