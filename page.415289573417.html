Two mummified bodies found on Mexico's highest peak

1Two mummified bodies found on Mexico's highest peak appear to be embracing each other and their discovery could put an end to the mystery of climbers who vanished after an avalanche in 1959, officials said.
2A mission to recover the bodies from the Pico de Orizaba was suspended until next Monday in order to build a special container to carry the remains intact, one of the rescue workers said Friday.
3The expedition in the central state of Puebla was launched on Thursday after climbers found a frozen head sticking out of the glacier near the summit earlier in the week.
4As two members of the expedition began to dig around the body, they discovered a second body, said Francisco Rodriguez, one of the mountaineers.
5"Their position surprised us because they are apparently in an embrace. The arm of one of them is on the body of the other," Rodriguez told Radio Formula, adding that reddish pieces of clothing were on the bodies.
6In pictures and video distributed by a local town council, the upper torso of one of the mummified bodies can be seen along with pieces of fabric jutting out of the snow in the very steep upper reaches of the mountain.
7Foggy conditions only allowed two of the 12 rescuers to reach the site of the bodies, 300 meters (1,017 feet) from the peak of the 5,610-meter (18,405-foot) mountain.
8The discovery raised hopes that the two bodies could finally end a 55-year-old mystery.
9Veteran climber Luis Espinoza Ruiz, who is in his 70s, told AFP that he believes the bodies belong to two of three friends who were buried under an avalanche during a climb on November 2, 1959.
10The day of their climb, they set out at 3:00 am.
11They split into two teams to pass a crevice on the north side of the volcano, but at around midday, "we heard a big noise and two weeks' worth of snow collapsed," Espinoza recalled.
12"I have hope. I'm almost certain that it's them," Espinoza said, noting that one of his friends had a red sweater.
13Rescuers say it is possible that they could find a third body, as they only dug half a meter to the right and left of the first body.
14The recovery effort will likely resume on Monday, depending on weather conditions, said Juan Navarro, mayor of the town of Chalchicomula de Sesma at the foot of the mountain, who has been receiving reports about the mission.
15Navarro said he had received phone calls from Spain and Germany of people suspecting the bodies could be relatives of theirs.
16But Rodriguez said they appear to be Mexican.
17The Puebla state attorney general's office will seek to identify the bodies through DNA tests.
18The mountain, also known as the Citlaltepetl volcano, is popular among climbers, though some have got lost or suffered fatal accidents in the past.
19The peak is part of the Mexico's volcanic belt, and Pico de Orizaba is a dormant volcano.
