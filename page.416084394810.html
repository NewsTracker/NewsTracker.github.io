UEFA gives Russia win in match abandoned due to fan violence

1The March 28 Euro qualifying match between Russia and Montenegro had to be suspended due to crowd violence.
2UEFA has given Russia a 3-0 win over Montenegro by forfeit after crowd violence forced a European Championship qualifying match to be stopped and later abandoned.
3UEFA says its disciplinary panel ordered Montenegro to play its next home qualifier in an empty stadium.
4The Montenegro federation was fined 50,000 euros ($54,000 US).
5The Russian federation was fined 25,000 euros ($27,000) for improper conduct by supporters.
6UEFA did not activate sanctions deferred after disorder by Russian fans at Euro 2012.
7The game on March 27 in Podgorica was called off at 0-0 after 67 minutes when Russian player Dmitry Kombarov was hit by an object thrown from the stands.
8Earlier, play was suspended for 30 minutes after Russia goalkeeper Igor Akinfeev was struck in the head by a flare.
