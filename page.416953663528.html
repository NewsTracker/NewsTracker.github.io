Facebook Begins Hosting News Articles From Nine Publishers

1Facebook will host articles from nine media organizations on its mobile app starting Wednesday as part of an experiment that offers attractive terms for publishers, but also risks deepening their reliance on the social network for traffic.
2The New York Times , National Geographic, BuzzFeed, NBC, The Atlantic, The Guardian, BBC News, Spiegel and Bild are Facebook's initial partners in the program, dubbed Instant Articles.
3Talks between Facebook and publishers began last year with some, including The Wall Street Journal, adopting a more cautious approach.
4Facebook said it will allow publishers to sell ads in their articles and keep all of the revenue, an arrangement earlier reported by The Wall Street Journal.
5Publishers can sell excess ad inventory through Facebook's advertising network and keep 70% of the revenue.
6Instant Articles will let publishers "deliver fast, interactive articles while maintaining control of their content and business models," said Facebook Chief Product Officer Chris Cox, in a blog post.
7Facebook has about double the user engagement of other social networks, Barclays analysts said in a note Tuesday.
8Late last year, Facebook eclipsed Google as the leading referrer of traffic to news sites.
9Some sites say Facebook accounts for as much as 70% of their traffic.
10Many publishers hope Facebook can help them better tap into the fast-growing mobile advertising market.
11A recent Pew Research Center study found that 39 out of 50 news sites now have more traffic coming from mobile users than from desktop users.
12Under the initiative Facebook will be able to load and display news articles ten times faster than the current norm.
13Web articles take an average of eight seconds to load on smart phones, making news one of the slowest aspects of the social network.
