1The Welsh government is now consulting on bringing in new measures to tackle the crime.
2Every 15 minutes rubbish is fly-tipped or illegally dumped somewhere in Wales.
3FTAW programme manager Gary Evans said: "It equates to 100 incidents every day. That's far too many.
4"The Welsh taxpayer has to foot the bill to clear-up. That money could be far better spent on other services like education and health."
5Across the UK, it is estimated that waste crime costs 568m a year in clean-up costs and lost tax revenue.
6The cost to Welsh councils is approaching 2m a year.
7Figures obtained by BBC Radio Wales' Eye on Wales programme suggest the annual cost to the Welsh fire services of attending waste fires is around 5m.
8To further the fight, the Welsh government is consulting with the UK government on plans to follow Scotland and introduce fixed penalty notices.
9Currently cases have to be taken to court, which is expensive and time consuming for councils.
10Natural Resources Minister Carl Sargeant said: "We're very keen to pursue this illegal activity.
11"We're saying, 'if you fly-tip in Wales, we are coming looking for you' and we will actively seek to prosecute you.
12/p> Eye on Wales is on BBC Radio Wales at 12:30 GMT on Sunday 22 March.
