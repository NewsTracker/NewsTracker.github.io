Kerry in Russia to discuss conflicts with Putin

1US Secretary of State John Kerry has arrived in Russia to meet President Vladimir Putin with an eye on easing badly strained relations over conflicts in Ukraine and Syria.
2Kerry flew to the Black Sea resort of Sochi on Tuesday for the highest-level US visit to Russia in two years, to discuss issues including the Iran nuclear talks, Yemen and Libya.
3With relations between the two countries at their lowest level since the Cold War, the trip appeared designed as much to maintain contact as anything else.
4"It's important for us to keep these lines of communication open. It's important to try to talk to the senior decision-maker," said a senior US state department official who briefed reporters travelling with Kerry.
5"We have a lot of business that we could do together if there is interest," said the official, who spoke on condition of anonymity.
6Putin's spokesman, Dmitry Peskov, welcomed the meeting as a positive step.
7"Through dialogue, it is possible to find ways for a normalisation - closer coordination in dealing with international problems," Peskov told reporters.
8But he added: "Russia was never the initiator of this cooling of relations."
9Kerry is also expected to meet Russian Foreign Minister Sergei Lavrov while in Sochi.
10Relations between Washington and Moscow have sunk since Russia annexed the Crimea peninsula in March of last year and backed pro-Russian rebels in eastern Ukraine.
11Moscow accuses Washington of orchestrating last year's overthrow of a Ukrainian president who was backed by Russia.
12The US has accused Russia of failing to withdraw heavy equipment such as air defence systems, tanks and artillery from eastern Ukraine in violation of a peace plan agreed in February and known as Minsk 2.
13Russia denies Western and Ukrainian accusations that it is arming the pro-Russian separatists battling the government and supporting them with its own military forces.
14More than 6,100 people have been killed since April 2014 in the Ukraine crisis.
15Arrival of 300 US Army soldiers to train Ukraine's national guard sparks warnings by Russia of escalation in conflict.
