1Bali nine pair Andrew Chan and Myuran Sukumaran have spent three lonely days at Nusakambangan, with their families so far blocked from visiting them on the penal island where Indonesia's government plans to execute them.
2Refusing to show any latitude to the emotionally battered families, Indonesian officials are saying they can only see the pair between 9am and 1pm on Mondays and Wednesdays.
3Australian consular staff are negotiating for some leniency but the head of the Central Java justice office, Mirza Zukarnain, said there could be no compromise.
4An anti-drugs protester in Cilacap prays in front of an effigy of a drug smuggler.
5"No exception. We can't make allowances for certain families only. It won't be fair for the others," he said.
6The standoff comes as Indonesian President Joko Widodo insisted there were "no problems" with its relationship with Australia, despite outrage at the extraordinary military and police operation to transfer the reformed drug smugglers to Nusakambagan.
7"We are still good friends, good neighbours but the death penalty is our legal sovereignty," Mr Joko told reporters in East Java.
8Julian McMahon and Australian consul-general Majell Hind board the ferry at Wijaya Pura port in Cilacap headed for Nusakambangan.
9Foreign Affairs Minister Julie Bishop described the "disproportionate" transfer of Chan and Sukumaran and the release of photos showing them with a smiling Bali police chief as "undignified and degrading" on Friday.
10"It seems that our citizens have been singled out for treatment designed to maximise publicity that was certainly at odds with the treatment of other citizens of other countries in the same position," she said.
11Dozens of heavily armed police and military personnel accompanied Chan and Sukumaran during the transfer, while hundreds more secured roads and airports.
12Sukhoi fighters jets shadowed the plane that carried them from Bali to Central Java.
13Other drug felons due to be executed were transferred in a far more low key fashion, often in just a single prison van.
14A spokesman for the Indonesian attorney general, Tony Spontana, defended the militarised operation, saying it was required "to ensure a smooth transfer".
15Foreign Affairs deputy secretary Greg Moriarty, who was Australia's ambassador in Jakarta until last year, spoke with ambassador Nadjib Riphat Kesoema on Thursday night to register Australia's displeasure.
16Ms Bishop intends to meet with the ambassador next week.
17Chan and Sukumaran are being held in isolation cells on Besi prison, or Iron prison, one of seven jails on Nusakambagan.
18They are incarcerated in separate cells but can call out to each other.
19They have no contact with other inmates at the prison other than Nigerian Raheem Agbaje Salami, who is also due to be executed by firing squad.
20On Friday morning, the men's lawyer, Julian McMahon, and Australia's consul-general to Bali, Majell Hind, pushed through a frenzied media mob and anti-drugs protesters to board the ferry to Nusakambangan.
21Demonstrators carried a grisly post wrapped in sheets with a face scrawled with coloured pen, to resemble a corpse wrapped in a traditional Muslim shroud for burial.
22The demonstrators urged that the Australians be shot, carried signs such as "We love Indonesia. Save Indonesia generation" and "Go to hell".
23They marched to the ferry dock singing the Indonesian national anthem.
24The scenes came as Indonesia flagged a delay in the execution of Chan, Sukumaran and eight others slated to be killed alongside them.
25The length of the delay remains uncertain, though.
26The pause should give the Bali nine duo's lawyers time to pursue a legal appeal lodged on Monday with the state administrative court in Jakarta.
27The action is the second attempt to challenge the rejection of Bali nine drug smugglers clemency pleas by Mr Joko.
28Lawyers for the pair say Mr Joko rebuffed Chan and Sukumaran's petition for mercy without properly considering their specific circumstances.
29With the guidance of prison staff, the pair transformed Kerobokan penitentiary in Bali into a model of rehabilitation.
30On December 9 last year, Mr Joko said: "The clemency requests are not on my table yet. But I guarantee that there will be no clemency for convicts who committed narcotics-related crimes."
31Mr Spontana, said "we have to respect the ongoing legal process", a reversal of the position of the attorney-general HM Prasetyo elucidated as recently as Tuesday.
32He has maintained repeatedly that a rejection of a clemency application nullified any future legal redress.
33All of the ten drug offenders due to be executed have seen their pleas for clemency rejected by Mr Joko.
34Even so, five of the condemned 10 have legal appeals in the works.
35One of the death row drug felons, Mary Jane Fiesta Veloso, had her case before the courts on Tuesday before it was adjourned.
36It was not immediately unclear if Mr Spontana was talking about all the legal appeals or just the Veloso matter.
37Confusion, contradiction and delay have been a regular feature of Indonesia's handling of the executions in recent weeks.
38"There could be other reasons for the delay [but] I hope, in my heart, that it's a change of mind," Ms Bishop said.
39Indonesia's Foreign Minister, Retno Marsudi, stressed on Friday that Indonesia was determined to "maintain good relationship with Australia, with all countries".
40She said a United Nations account of a human rights dialogue quoting Indonesian diplomats saying that the country could introduce a moratorium on the death penalty was a misrepresentation.
41"We protested because we never stated it and the uploaded version from the Office of the UN Human Rights High Commissioner has been revised in accordance to what was stated by the Indonesian delegation," she said.
42At the time of writing, the UN had not changed the document.
43Ms Retno also insisted that the Indonesian ambassador in Canberra had not been called in to DFAT for a rebuke.
44Bali police spokesman Hery Wiyanto has revealed that an investigation will be launched into the media leak of a photo of a smiling Denpasar police chief with his arm on Chan's shoulder.
45Mr Hery said Senior Commissioner Djoko Hari Utomo did not violate any rules and would not be investigated.
46"It wasn't a selfie moment, it wasn't a pose," Mr Hery said.
47"He was talking to and encouraging the boys."
48However, he said intelligence officers and the internal affairs division of the police force were investigating who took the photo and how it reached the media.
49Disciplinary action would be taken if the investigation discovered the photo had been sold to the media.
