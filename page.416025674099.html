Kenya Attacks Al Shabaab Camps In Somalia In Response To Garissa University Massacre

1NAIROBI, April 6 (Reuters) - The Kenyan air force bombed two al Shabaab camps in Somalia on Sunday, a Kenya Defense Forces (KDF) source said, in the first major military response to last week's attack by the militant group on a Kenyan university.
2Gunmen from the al Qaeda-aligned group killed 148 people on Thursday when they stormed the Garissa University College campus, some 200 km (120 miles) from the Somali border.
3Jets pounded the camps in Gondodowe and Ismail, both in the Gedo region bordering Kenya, the military source said on Monday.
4Cloud cover made it difficult to establish how much damage the bombings caused or estimate the death toll.
5"We targeted the two areas because according to information we have, those (al Shabaab) fellows are coming from there to attack Kenya," he said.
6Kenya has struggled to stop the flow of al Shabaab militants and weapons across its porous 700 km border with Somalia.
7Al Shabaab militants have killed more than 400 people in Kenya since April 2013.
8An African Union peacekeeping force that includes Kenyan troops, and which is fighting the group in Somalia, carried out arrests and seized ammunitions in an al Shabaab camp in Gondodowe last August.
