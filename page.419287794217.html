U.S. believes Islamic State likely used mustard agent in Iraq attack: WSJ

1Kurdish security forces gather at the site of a bomb attack in Erbil, the capital of Iraq's Kurdistan region, April 17, 2015.
2WASHINGTON The United States believes Islamic State militants likely used mustard agent in an attack on Kurdish forces in Iraq earlier this week, the first indication the militant group has obtained a banned chemical weapon, the Wall Street Journal reported on Thursday.
3"We have credible information that the agent used in the attack was mustard," a senior U.S. official told the Journal.
4Islamic State could have obtained the mustard agent in Syria, whose government admitted to having large quantities of the blistering agent in 2013, when it agreed to give up its chemical weapons arsenal, the newspaper reported.
5"That makes the most sense," the Journal quoted a senior U.S. official as saying about the possibility that Islamic State obtained the mustard agent in Syria.
6Islamic State could also have obtained the mustard agent in Iraq, the Journal reported.
7The newspaper did not specify where or when the attack occurred, or whether it caused casualties.
8The White House's National Security Council said it was aware of the reports and was seeking more information.
9"We continue to take these and all allegations of chemical weapons use very seriously," spokesman Ali Baskey said in a statement.
10U.S. intelligence agencies have said in the past they believed Islamic State has used chlorine gas in attacks in Iraq, the Journal reported.
11Chlorine is not a banned chemical agent.
