Pakistan deploys troops to prevent more rioting after church attacks

1LAHORE, Pakistan - A Pakistani police officer says paramilitary troops have been deployed to prevent more rioting by minority Christians after the deadly Taliban church attacks in the eastern city of Lahore.
2Lahore police chief, Amin Wains, says the soldiers have fanned out on the streets Tuesday as funerals of the last of the victims were underway.
3He says those attending the burial services will go through strict security checks.
4The twin Taliban suicide bombings struck two churches in a Christian neighbourhood of Lahore on Sunday, killing 17 people.
5The attack set off two days of rioting in which angry Christians burned to death two people they suspected of being involved in the bombings and two Christian protester died after being hit by a car that tried to drive through their road block.
