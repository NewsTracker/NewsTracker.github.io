Masked gunmen storm university in Kenya

1NAIROBI, Kenya -- Gunmen attacked Garissa University College early Thursday, shooting indiscriminately in campus hostels, killing at least two people and wounding four others, police said.
2Police and military surrounded the buildings and were trying to secure the area in eastern Kenya, police officer Musa Yego said.
3Explosions could also be heard, he said.
4Kenya's National Police Service said in a written statement that armed attackers forced their way onto the campus by shooting at guards, triggering a "fierce shootout" with police guarding student hostels.
5However, the attackers managed to get into the hostels.
6The statement, which did not mention a death toll, said police and members of other security agencies were "engaged in an elaborate process of flushing out the gunmen from the hostels."
7A policewoman at the scene told the Reuters news agency the gunmen were likely to have taken hostages because many students remain trapped.
8No group has claimed responsibility for the raid yet, Reuters reports.
9Kenya's northern and eastern regions, which border Somalia, have been most affected by attacks blamed on al Qaeda-linked al-Shabaab Islamic extremists from Somalia.
10They have vowed retribution against Kenya for sending its troops to Somalia.
11Kenya took that step in 2011 to fight al-Shabaab following cross-border attacks.
12In February, al-Shabaab released an online video in which it appeared to call for attacks on Western malls.
13The video specifically named the Mall of America in Minnesota, the West Edmonton Mall in Alberta, Canada, and London's Oxford Street.
14In 2013, al-Shabaab claimed responsibility for an attack on an upscale shopping mall in Kenya.
15More than 60 people were killed in the four-day siege when attackers stormed the Westgate Mall in the capital, Nairobi.
16Last month, al-Shabaab claimed responsibility for attacks in the county of Mandera on the Somali border in which twelve people died.
17Four of them died in an attack on the convoy of Mandera County Governor Ali Roba.
18Al-Shabaab carried out large-scale attacks in Mandera last year.
19The militants hijacked a bus and singled out 28 non-Muslims forcing them to lie on the ground before shooting them dead.
20Ten days later, 36 non-Muslim quarry workers were killed by the extremists.
21Police statistics show that 312 people have been killed in al-Shabaab attacks in Kenya from 2012 to 2014.
22Thirty-eight people were killed and 149 wounded in Garissa in the same period, according to police statistics.
