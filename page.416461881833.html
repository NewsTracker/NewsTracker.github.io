Michael Brown's family sues Ferguson over shooting death

1The family of Michael Brown filed a wrongful death lawsuit against the city of Ferguson on Thursday, seeking unspecified damages and police reforms after the black teen's killing by a white policeman prompted a national debate on law enforcement and race.
2The civil lawsuit filed in St. Louis County, Missouri, names the city of Ferguson, former Police Chief Thomas Jackson and former police officer Darren Wilson as defendants.
3The lawsuit seeks $75,000 in compensation, as well as unspecified punitive damages, and calls for a court order prohibiting the use of police techniques "that demean, disregard, or underserve its African-American population".
4The shooting last August sparked protests and a national movement questioning police use of deadly force, especially against minorities in cities around the country.
5The lawsuit alleges that Wilson destroyed evidence after he shot Brown on the street of the St. Louis suburb last August, saying he washed blood off his hands and cleared and bagged the gun used in the shooting.
6"We expect to put on evidence that you never heard about before, that you have never seen," Anthony Gray, one of the lawyers for Brown's family, said at a news conference on Thursday to discuss the lawsuit.
7Gray said evidence had not been presented properly in the previous investigations.
8A grand jury in Missouri decided last year not to bring criminal charges against Wilson.
9The U.S. Department of Justice later cleared the police officer of any civil rights violations.
10While Brown was unarmed, some witnesses said he had charged Wilson in the incident.
11"We have not had a chance to read through (the lawsuit), but because it is pending litigation, the city won't be able to make any comment," Jeff Small, a spokesman for Ferguson, said in a telephone interview.
12The city previously said it would establish a citizen review board, recruit diverse police officers and put into effect use of body and dashboard camers by police officers and patrol cars.
13Lawyers for Wilson were not immediately available to comment.
14The lawsuit has been expected since last month, when the family expressed disappointment in the Justice Department investigation and said it would sue.
15The department's report did find racial bias and a pattern of illegal actions against African-Americans by the Ferguson Police Department.
16Ferguson Police Chief Jackson resigned last month, within days of the report's release.
17The civil lawsuit filed on Thursday listed incidents of alleged unlawful stops and arrests of African-Americans in Ferguson.
18It asked for the court to assign a monitor to the city's police force for five years or until "the city of Ferguson has fully and effectually trained all of its police officers on the constitutional requirements of the use of deadly force."
