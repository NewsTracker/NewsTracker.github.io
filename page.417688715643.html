3 Austrian swimmers hit by bus at European Games

1Three teenage synchronized swimmers from Austria were injured Thursday when they were hit by a shuttle bus in the athletes village on the eve of the inaugural European Games, Austrian Olympic officials said.
2Two of the three girls - all 15 years old - were being flown home to Austria after being treated in the intensive care unit at a Baku hospital.
3The third was released with minor injuries.
4The Austrian Olympic Committee said Vanessa Sahinovic had multiple fractures and other severe injuries, while Luna Pajer received arm injuries.
5Verena Breit "got off lightly with slight injuries," the committee said.
6Uliya Gurbanova, a doctor at the Baku city hospital which treated the three, told The Associated Press by telephone that Sahinovic and Pajer were taken to the airport by Azerbaijani doctors and had already left on a flight to Austria.
7The organizing committee for the games, BEGOC, said that its medical services "responded to an incident this morning at the transport hub within the athletes' village in Baku which led to three Austrian athletes being immediately transported to hospital."
8"BEGOC places the safety and welfare of all athletes as its number one priority."
9The opening ceremony of the games will be held Friday, with the first competitions starting Saturday.
