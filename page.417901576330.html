Tekmira says Ebola drug unlikely to work in study

1NEW YORK (AP) - Drugmaker Tekmira said Friday that an experimental treatment for a strain of Ebola will probably fail in a clinical trial.
2The Canadian company said the mid-stage study reached a statistical futility boundary, meaning it's unlikely the trial will show the drug worked.
3Tekmira said it stopped enrolling new patients as a result.
4It is analyzing data from the trial and will make the results available as soon as possible.
5The company's shares dropped $1.74, or 11.6 percent, to $13.28 on Friday.
6Tekmira Pharmaceuticals Corp. calls the drug TKM-Ebola-Guinea and it is designed to target the strain of the virus that is responsible for the active outbreak in West Africa.
7More than 11,000 people have died since that outbreak began in December 2013.
8The company has been studying the drug in Ebola patients in Sierra Leone.
9Tekmira is also running an early-stage trial of a drug called TKM-Ebola, which targets a slightly different strain of the virus.
10That study is happening in the U.S. and it involves only healthy patients.
11Tekmira expects results from that study in the second half of this year.
12The company does not have any approved drugs and is also studying a potential treatment for hepatitis B.
