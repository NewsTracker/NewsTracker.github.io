2MADRID - Investigators said on Tuesday they believe remains found under a Madrid convent include those of Miguel de Cervantes, the author of "Don Quixote" and considered the father of the modern novel.
3Nearly 400 years after his death, the quest to find Cervantes had led Spanish scientists deep into the sub-soil of a 17th century convent.
4Some of bones dug up in recent months almost certainly belong to Cervantes, they said.
5"Everything coincides to lead us to believe that Cervantes is there," forensics expert Francisco Etxeberria told a news conference.
6Investigators may never be able to guarantee with absolute certainty that it was his body, Etxeberria added, even though DNA tests are to be carried out.
7Cervantes - whose masterpiece about an errant, daydreaming knight and his faithful servant Sancho Panza has delighted readers around the world - had requested to be buried in the convent.
8The Trinitarian religious order had helped to pay a ransom to release him from slavery after he was captured by Moorish pirates.
9He died in 1616 - the same week as William Shakespeare.
10But the exact location of his tomb was lost in a subsequent rebuilding of the convent.
11Historians hope to establish a burial site to attract literary pilgrims.
12Madrid mayor Ana Botella said on Tuesday that authorities were looking into the possibility of opening up the site to visitors.
13Investigators began their search almost a year ago in the cloistered convent, still home to a dozen elderly nuns.
14They recently found bone fragments and as well as a dilapidated piece of a wooden coffin with the letters "M" and "C" on it.
15None of the bones showed signs of the injuries Cervantes was known to have sustained during his life, including lesions to his left arm when he fought in the Battle of Lepanto in 1571, the experts said.
16The Cervantes search takes place after a similar quest in Britain, where investigators found the remains of medieval monarch King Richard III in 2012 under municipal car park.
17He will be reburied in a ceremony next week.
