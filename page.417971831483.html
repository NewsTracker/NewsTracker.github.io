THE LATEST: Greek demonstrators march in Athens to show support for staying in the euro

1Another meeting, and again a lack of clear Greek proposals, German Finance Wolgang Schaeuble groused Monday.
2In four months of negotiations, international creditors have complained time and again that Athens was either too late or too vague in proposing reforms it would make in exchange for loans.
3Schaeuble said Monday ahead of a finance ministers' meeting on the issue: "We are still in the same situation as last Thursday," when the 19 ministers of the eurozone last assessed Greece's proposals.
4Greek Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras offered new proposals over the weekend.
5Several European Union officials, however, complained that the proposals were never discussed at a technical level, where negotiators examine them to make sure the figures make sense.
6"We cannot prepare a statement for the summit" of government leaders due later Monday.
7The leaders would have to make political decisions based on the advice of the finance ministers.
8As uncertainty hangs over Greece's financial future, Polish Prime Minister Ewa Kopacz is advising Poles traveling to Greece to take a "larger amount of cash" with them.
9Greeks have been withdrawing money in increasing amounts in recent days amid concerns over the country's financial plight.
10In a worst case scenario, the Greek government could have to put limits on the amount of money Greeks can withdraw in order to prevent a bank run.
11"If you are going to Greece on vacation, please keep checking the situation there, because it will be very dynamic," said Kopacz.
12"Please, do not count only on your ATM cards and on ATM machines, but take a larger amount of cash with you," she added.
13Greece is holding emergency talks with its creditors in Brussels on Monday.
14It needs more loans to not default on a debt repayment due June 30.
15Finland's Finance Minister Alexander Stubb was pessimistic about the chances of a breakthrough on Monday.
16"I think this day's going to be a non-entity I'm afraid," he told reporters as he arrived for talks with his eurozone counterparts, ahead of a leaders' summit in the evening.
17"I don't foresee a breakthrough today," he said.
18His Irish counterpart, Michael Noonan, also said he has "very low expectations for a deal today."
19Noonan said there was confusion with proposals that Greek officials submitted overnight.
20"There hasn't been any preparation" he said.
21"It's still not clear what the proposals are" from Greece he said.
22Noonan predicted that the eurozone finance ministers would have to meet again Thursday, ahead of a summit of the 28-nation EU.
23French President Francois Hollande says "progress has been made in the negotiations" between Greece and its creditors, which include eurozone states like France.
24Hollande is urging Greece to find an agreement at a Monday summit in Brussels between Greece and its creditors.
25"We must do everything so that an agreement is found tonight," Hollande said at an event in Paris before heading to Brussels.
26If Monday's talks are inconclusive, Hollande insists an agreement would need to be found "within the next days."
27"France and Germany are aware that Greece must remain in the eurozone," he said.
28The European Central Bank has increased the amount of emergency credit it allows Greek banks to draw on to remain afloat, and remains on call in coming days to revise the amount should that be necessary, a banking official says.
29The official, who spoke Monday only on condition of anonymity as the ECB had not made the decision public, declined to provide the amount.
30The credit helps support Greek banks, from which worried savers have been pulling money out ahead of a critical European summit on Monday on how to keep the country solvent.
31Figures have not been officially released, but reports suggest Greeks withdrew about 4 billion euros last week.
32A top European Union official says that debt talks between Greece and its international creditors have made some progress but that a deal to avoid potential bankruptcy remains elusive.
33European Commission President Jean-Claude Juncker said Monday that the sides have made "progress over the last few days but we are not yet there."
34His comments came as Greek Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras arrived for talks ahead of an important summit of leaders from the 19-nation eurozone.
35The meeting is aimed at staving off bankruptcy in Greece and avoiding a potentially disastrous exit for the country from the euro single currency.
36Amid the high-stakes negotiations in Brussels over Greek debt, many Greeks have been taking their cash out of banks in large amounts.
37Some two billion euros (nearly $2.3 billion) was withdrawn over just three days last week.
38Yannis Nikolopoulos said in Athens says Monday "everyone's going (to the banks) to take money."
39He said people are taking "some money to have at home for 10, 15 days - say 1,000, 500 euros, because if the banks shut it'll be a problem to go shopping."
40He said a deal between Greece and its creditors "is mandatory at all costs, otherwise we're doomed."
41Vassiliki Papanagiotou, a lawyer in Athens, is urging "prudence on both sides," warning that "otherwise Greece will collapse."
42European stock markets are surging on hopes that Greece and its creditors will reach a deal to get the country more loans before it goes bankrupt at the end of the month.
43The Athens stock exchange led the charge, jumping 7.1 percent.
44The Stoxx 50 of top European shares was up 2.7 percent, with Germany's DAX gaining 2.8 percent.
45Leaders of the 19-nation eurozone are gathering in Brussels for a last-ditch summit aimed at clinching a deal between Greece and international lenders as a major debt payment deadline looms.
46Greek Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras presented the leaders of Germany, France and the European Union over the weekend with a new proposal for a deal.
47No details of it were made public.
