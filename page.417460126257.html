New electrical substations go online in southern New Mexico

1Xcel Energy says two new substations and a pair of transmission lines are now in service in southeastern New Mexico.
2The company announced Monday it has hit a milestone in its project to boost the high-voltage transmission and distribution system in the region by bringing the new infrastructure online.
3Project manager Shannon Close says the substations and lines came in on time and on budget.
4They're capable of adding 25,000 horsepower to the region's industrial base.
5Horsepower is a measurement of the power needed to run oil pump jacks and other equipment.
6One horsepower is equivalent to 746 watts of power.
7In all, Xcel has plans to invest $3 billion in new and improved power lines, substations and generating equipment across its Texas-New Mexico service area between now and 2020.
