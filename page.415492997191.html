Is Gold About To Plunge To $1,000 Or Even Lower?

Saddam Hussein's tomb destroyed as battle for Tikrit rages

1Since warning about a possible correction in gold and silver in late-January, gold has fallen by $125 and silver by $1.64.
2Though I am a longer-term fan of precious metals, I have been concerned in recent years about the risks posed by the ending of the commodities supercycle as well as the rising U.S. dollar, which trades inversely with precious metals and other commodities.
3I intend to invest in precious metals for the longer run, but I have been waiting for the current bearish cycle to run its course before I commit.
4Like any investor, my goal is to get the most for my money, so I have been analyzing precious metals carefully to determine when they have finally bottomed.
5I believe that technical or chart analysis is very helpful for investment timing decisions like this.
6In the shorter-term, gold has been falling in a channel pattern due, in large part, to the soaring U.S. dollar and the bearish influence of falling commodities prices.
7The yellow metal is currently sitting just above its $1,150 support level that formed in November and December 2014.
8If gold eventually falls decisively below both $1,150 and $1,130 (the intraday low in November), it would represent a major technical breakdown that could foreshadow a move to $1,000 per ounce (the next obvious psychological level) or even lower.
9On the other hand, if $1,150 proves to be a solid support, gold may attempt to rebound to the recent highs - particularly $1,300.
10Looking at the longer-term chart, gold has been trading in a range between $1,200 and $1,400.
11The next major directional move will be signaled by a convincing break above or below one of these levels.
12If gold stays under $1,200 and a solid bearish follow-through occurs, $1,000 is the next price target to watch.
13It is worth noting that commercial futures hedgers (often considered the "smart money") have scaled back their bearish position, which may indicate that they expect a bounce in the near future.
14Like gold, silver has been in a downtrend channel since late-January and is now sitting just above its key psychological level at $15 per ounce, which also acted as a support in November and December.
15If silver breaks below $15, it could continue to drop and approach the low-teens or even $10 per ounce.
16If $15 proves to be a solid support, however, silver may attempt to rebound toward its recent highs around $18.
