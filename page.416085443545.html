Fired Ferguson clerk fired over emails says she's not racist

1The St. Louis suburb fired Mary Ann Twitty after the Justice Department called the emails into question in its report that found widespread racial bias in Ferguson's policing efforts and the municipal court system.
2The emails frequently belittled black residents and President Barack Obama.
3The scrutiny followed uproar over August's shooting death of black 18-year-old Michael Brown by a white police officer.
4Twitty told The Associated Press on Wednesday the emails "were jokes," and that "of course I'm not racist."
5Ferguson Mayor James Knowles III declined to discuss Twitty's firing, saying it's a personnel matter.
