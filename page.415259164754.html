Snowden: Asylum In Switzerland A 'Great Political Option'

1Edward Snowden wants Switzerland to grant him asylum.
2The NSA leaker made the remark as he spoke to an audience in Geneva via a video link from Moscow, where he has been living in exile to avoid U.S. prosecution on espionage charges.
3"I would love to return to Switzerland, some of my favorite memories are from Geneva. It's a wonderful place," Snowden told the International Film Festival and Forum on Human Rights, where the Oscar-winning documentary about his case, Citizenfour, was screened.
4"I do think Switzerland would be a sort of great political option because it has a history of neutrality," he said.
5Snowden, who fled first to Hong Kong in the summer of 2013 after the publication of secrets he leaked on U.S. electronic surveillance, later landed in Russia, where he has lived since.
6In April, he appeared on Russian television for a question-and-answer with President Vladimir Putin.
7And, earlier this month, he participated via video link at an event sponsored by the American Civil Liberties Union in Hawaii.
8Speaking at the Geneva event, the former National Security Agency contractor said he'd asked 21 countries, "the majority in central and Western Europe," for asylum.
9"Unfortunately no country said yes," he said.
10He accused the Obama administration of "political interference."
11Switzerland and the U.S. enjoy a mutual extradition treaty, so it's not clear whether Snowden could remain there.
12Reuters also notes that "[under] current Swiss laws, an applicant has to be on Swiss territory to lodge an asylum request."
13However, Snowden also says he is working with his lawyers to secure a guarantee of a "fair trial" in the United States.
14"Unfortunately, the Department of Justice is unwilling to agree," he said.
15"The only thing they have said at this point is that they would not execute me, which is not the same as a fair trial."
16In August of last year, Snowden was granted a three-year Russian residency permit.
