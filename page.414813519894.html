8In a Canadian newspaper exclusive, Platzman touches on the band's meteoric rise, the dreaded 'second album syndrome' and the Grammy controversy surrounding rockers Queens of the Stone Age - which led to their frontman, Josh Homme, declaring "f--- the Imagine Dragons!"
9The album seems like a natural progression from the first record.
10Was this the goal?
11That was the goal.
12We weren't trying to fall into any of the pitfalls that come with a second album.
13Night Visions was really successful and so I feel like we could have gone into it with the mindset of, 'Let's re-create everything we just did.
14Let's do it again because everyone seemed to like it.' But that's not coming from an honest place.
15Really, we just wanted to make music we could stand behind.
16Did you feel any pressure going into the studio from either yourselves or from outside sources?
17We pretty much live in a bubble.
18Our friends and family were the ones telling us 'Things are changing, things are crazy.' For us, we were just continuing to write music and playing shows the audience was slowly getting bigger and bigger and the back wall was getting further and further back (laughs).
19We didn't have a chance to feel any of that pressure.
20We were pretty focused.
21Going from playing tiny clubs to arenas in a few short years, winning a Grammy, a platinum album in 12 countries does the rapid and successful rise of the band blow you away at all?
22It's definitely not something that we expected or felt entitled to at all.
23Does is surprise me?
24Sure. But we are also doing this because we love playing shows.
25All the awards and record-breaking stuff is incredible but it's not why we're doing this.
26We're still very satisfied to get up on stage and to bring our music around the world.
27That's what it's really all about.
28When you guys played the Air Canada Centre in Toronto last year, lead singer Dan Reynolds said, "We have Vegas luck on our side."
29Do you think this is the case, or do you think you make your own luck with hard work and determination?
30There are plenty of amazing bands out there that had all the hard work and determination in the world, but didn't get the opportunities they deserved.
31Luck absolutely plays a part in it.
32Detractors inevitably come with success.
33How do you guys deal with negative press or the backlash that any band gets after gaining a huge amount of popularity?
34We're starting to get a little bit of negative press.
35We were just talking about this last night.
36Our bass player Ben McKee said, 'When we started this band, we were dreaming about being successful enough to get negative press' (laughs).
37It's pretty amazing that we're at a point that we're getting it.
38We take it with a grain of salt.
39Queens of the Stone Age frontman Josh Homme was pretty upset that you beat his band for the Best Rock Performance Grammy last year.
40What did you think of him dropping an F-bomb in the direction of your band?
41We've actually hung out with Josh since then and we're totally cool.
42It's rock 'n' roll.
43I don't think he was that upset with us we've buried the hatchet since then, and we even did a photo for his charity.
