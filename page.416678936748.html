Nigerian troops free 234 Boko Haram hostages

1Nigerian troops have freed another 234 women and children from Boko Haram's stronghold in the Sambisa forest, according to the military.
2The defence headquarters said in a statement that the hostages were rescued on Thursday through the Kawuri and Konduga end of Sambisa forest.
3About 500 women and children have already been rescued by the military in the past few days.
4"They have been evacuated to join others at the place of ongoing screening," the military said.
5It said the "assault on the forest is continuing from various fronts and efforts are concentrated on rescuing hostages of civilians and destroying all terrorists camps and facilities in the forest".
6The military had pledged to free more hostages from the Islamists after hundreds were rescued earlier this week.
7It was still not clear if any of the 219 girls snatched in April 2014 from their school in the northeastern town of Chibok were among the freed hostages.
8The military said they were still screening the freed hostages with a view to establishing their identities.
9The military announced on Thursday that about 160 hostages had been rescued from Sambisa in additional to 200 girls and 93 women freed on Tuesday.
10The numbers underlined the scale of the tactic of mass abduction used by the militants, who according to Amnesty International have seized about 2,000 women and girls since the start of last year.
11Female former hostages have described being subjected to forced labour and sexual and psychological abuse as well as sometimes having to fight on the frontline alongside the rebels.
12The military had released a series of photographs purporting to show some of the rescued women and children in an undisclosed location, huddled on the ground watched over by soldiers.
13The mass kidnapping in Chibok prompted global outrage and forced President Goodluck Jonathan to accept international help in the search operation for the missing girls.
14Mr Jonathan had come under severe criticism for not doing enough to free the Chibok girls or to end the six-year-old Boko Haram insurgency that has claimed about 13,000 lives and forced at least 1.5 million people to flee their homes.
15Many analysts believe the protracted Boko Haram uprising was partly responsible for Mr Jonathan's defeat in the 28 March presidential election to former military ruler Mohammadu Buhari.
