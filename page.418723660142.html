European court rejects Financial Fair Play challenge (Yahoo Sports)

1NYON, Switzerland (AP) -- UEFA says it has won at the European Union's supreme court after a legal challenge to ''Financial Fair Play'' rules that curb club spending was thrown out.
2The case went through a local Brussels court after the European Commission decided it had no merit.
3The case was brought by a player's agent in Belgium and fan groups linked to Manchester City and Paris Saint-Germain.
4UEFA fined both clubs 20 million euros ($21.8 million) in 2014 for breaking FFP rules.
5They argued that FFP protects elite clubs, and were represented by lawyer Jean-Louis Dupont.
6He helped win the landmark Bosman case on the contractual freedom of players 20 years ago.
