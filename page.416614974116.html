New Mexico AG: Uber, Lyft drivers need drug testing

1New Mexico Attorney General Hector Balderas says drivers of ride-booking services Lyft and Uber in the state should face drug testing requirements like taxi drivers.
2Balderas sent state regulators a letter Wednesday and asked them to strengthen public safety standards around ride-booking services.
3Last week, the state's Public Regulation Commission voted 4-1 on new regulations clearing way for the companies to function under guidelines separate from those that govern traditional taxi services.
4But drug testing requirements were dropped.
5The San Francisco-based Lyft and Uber use smartphone programs to connect people seeking rides with people who have cars.
6Both had been operating in New Mexico despite complaints for taxi companies.
7Spokeswomen for Lyft and Uber did not immediately return emails from The Associated Press.
