17 Indian pilgrims killed in Nepal bus accident

1KATHMANDU, Nepal (AP) - A bus plunged off a mountain highway near Nepal's capital on Wednesday, killing at least 17 Indian pilgrims and injuring 27 others, officials said.
2The bus veered off the Prithvi highway about 16 kilometers (10 miles) west of Kathmandu, police officer Tejendra said.
3He said the bus fell about 300 meters (1,000 feet) before it slammed into trees at the bottom of a hill.
4The Indian Embassy in Kathmandu confirmed the death toll and said five of the 27 injured people were in critical condition and getting treatment at various hospitals in Kathmandu.
5One person was reported to be missing.
6The bus was carrying Hindu pilgrims from the Indian state of Gujrat on a visit to the Pashupatinath temple in Kathmandu.
7They were returning home Wednesday morning when the bus plunged off the highway.
8The bodies were not immediately identified.
9One of the injured passengers, who identified himself as Jagdish, said the pilgrims were traveling in two buses.
10He said the driver of one of the busses was trying to avoid a motorcycle coming from the opposite direction and lost control of the vehicle, which flipped over before rolling off the highway.
11The two-lane highway, the most popular route out of Kathmandu, is narrow, has sharp turns and is busy during the morning hours.
