Official: Ebola survivor may have infected new Liberia case

1MONROVIA, Liberia (AP)?
2A woman who tested positive for Ebola in Liberia last week is dating a survivor of the disease, a health official said Tuesday, offering a possible explanation for how she became the country's first confirmed case in weeks.
3Ebola is typically transmitted through contact with the blood, vomit or feces of people who are sick.
4Survivors of Ebola are not generally considered contagious, but the virus lingers in semen, and so scientists urge survivors not to have sex for three months after recovering.
5Even though sexual transmission of Ebola has not yet been documented, it may be possible.
6Samples have been taken from the boyfriend for testing, said Dr. Francis Kateh, acting head of Liberia's Ebola Case Management Team.
7The woman tested positive for Ebola on Friday, and her case has caused concern because she didn't seem to be linked to any of the people on an Ebola contacts list and said she did not travel to an infected country.
8The patient is now being treated at the Monrovia Medical Unit, a U.S.-built field hospital staffed by health workers from the U.S. Public Health Service, said Kate Migliaccio, a spokeswoman for the service.
9The case deflated hopes that Liberia's outbreak might soon be declared over.
10Liberia has seen more than 4,300 of the more than 10,000 deaths in the West African Ebola outbreak.
11But since it discharged its last case on March 5, it was counting down the 42 days that a country must wait before being declared Ebola-free.
