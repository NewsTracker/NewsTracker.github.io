Facebook opens Messenger app up to ESPN, J.J. Abrams [Photos]

1The upgraded Facebook Messenger app will add a wider range of video, voice and location-sharing capabilities, the company announced at its F8 developers' conference Wednesday.
2"We think this service has the potential to let people express themselves in new ways," CEO Mark Zuckerberg said.
3Already, Facebook serves about 3 billion videos per day, he noted.
4Mobile users click here for Fast Company's live coverage of the event.
5The new Messenger app will support animated GIFs and will let users overlay special effects -- including a feature from J.J. Abrams' Bad Robot production company that will let people modify videos sent in messages to their friends, and a tool from JibJab Media.
6Among other partnerships, Facebook's Messenger will show users the best sports moments of the day from ESPN, said David Marcus, Facebook VP of messaging products.
7In addition, the new Facebook Messenger app will let users communicate directly with businesses, such as letting consumers track the status of their packages with shipping companies.
8"We're turning Messenger from a mobile-messaging app to a full-fledged conversational platform," Marcus said in an interview prior to F8.
9The Messenger app will support media content across group conversations; so, that would enable, for example, a group karaoke experience, according to Marcus.
10Over all, Zuckerberg said in his opening remarks, Facebook's focus is on making the service more stable and secure.
11He cited steps the company has made to make the social service a safer destination for users and advertisers.
