Ukraine urges EU to keep up sanctions pressure on Russia

Violence erupts between army and rebels in DR Congo

1EU President Donald Tusk said the decision reached at a two-day summit showed the common resolve of the 28 EU nations in the face of Russian involvement in the conflict.
2It also underscored the support of the EU for Ukraine, which had urged the member nations to keep up pressure on Russia until it meets all its obligations under a deal brokered in the Belarus capital of Minsk last month.
3"We have to maintain our sanctions until the Minsk agreement is fully implemented," Tusk said.
4Russia has denied any military involvement in the fighting in eastern Ukraine and calls EU and U.S. sanctions unjustified.
5Ukrainian Prime Minister Arseniy Yatsenyuk met Thursday with Tusk ahead of the summit and accused Russian President Vladimir Putin of trying to divide Europe over Ukraine.
6He told Tusk that a European show of unity would be "the best answer."
7Dutch Prime Minister Mark Rutte said the decision showed that "the EU summit is united."
8"The Minsk agreement must be executed by all parties fully and within deadline," he told reporters.
9"The option of more sanctions, if the situation worsens, must clearly remain open."
10The European leaders did not impose any new sanctions, in part because a ceasefire called for in the Minsk agreement is largely holding.
11More than 6,000 people have been killed in the year-long conflict between the Ukrainian army and Russia-backed separatists.
12The EU has visa bans and asset freezes in place targeting 150 individuals, including high-ranking Russians, and 37 entities such as banks, companies and rebel groups.
13It also has economic sanctions in place that have hit Russian financial and energy interests " as does the United States.
14The sanctions expire later this year, but the leaders can extend them when they next meet in Brussels in June.
15Yatsenyuk also expressed optimism about European backing for his government's call to the U.N. for international peacekeepers to be sent to eastern Ukraine.
16It was not immediately clear whether the EU approved such a force Thursday.
17"Everyone wants to get peace in Europe. One of the tools to reach this peace is to deploy peacekeepers," Yatsenyuk said.
18Also Thursday, an influential European Parliament committee voted in favor of a plan to provide Ukraine with 1.8 billion euros ($1.92 billion) in medium-term loans to help lift the country out of recession.
1Government forces have been fighting rebels of the Alliance of Patriots for a Free and Sovereign Congo (APCLS) in the Masisi territory west of Goma, the capital of North Kivu province.
2"The rebels attacked last night," an army officer told AFP from the front line.
3The number of casualties remained unclear but government officials told AFP one child had been killed.
4Emmanuel Balume, a resident of Nyabiondo village in North Kivu, described the situation as "very serious".
5He told AFP that "bombs" had been dropped overnight, and that people were seeking refuge at the UN base in Goma.
6The chief of Nyabiondo, Abraham Wetewabo, also said residents were packing their belongings and hastily leaving the area.
7But local government official Sukisa Nayambaje insisted people had begun to return to their homes as the army was regaining control of the situation.
8Fighting in the area first broke out over the weekend.
9The APCL, led by former army general Janvier Buingo Karairi, is one of the many armed groups battling for control of the mineral-rich region.
10Tens of thousands of people died and more than 100,000 fled their homes during clashes between the APCLS and army forces in February and March 2013, according to the UN.
