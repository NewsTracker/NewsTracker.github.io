Hugo Selenski who killed Michael Kerkowski and Tammy Fassett escapes death penalty

Justice Dept. examining Ferguson racial discrimination

1Hugo Selenski was convicted last week on two counts of first-degree murder in the 2002 strangulation deaths of Michael Kerkowski and Tammy Fassett.
2Police found their bodies, along with three other sets of human remains, in Selenski's yard in 2003.
3Gruesome discovery: Police say the bodies of Tammy Fassett and Michael Kerkowksi were found buried in Selenski's back yard in Kingston Township in 2003.
4First Assistant District Attorney Sam Sanguedolce said Wednesday in his closing argument that Selenski tortured Kerkowski before killing him and has a long history of violent crime.
5Those are two aggravating circumstances the jury can use to sentence Selenski to death.
6Pennsylania's governor recently declared a moratorum on the death penalty, but the law remains on the books.
7Prosecutors said Selenski, 41, and a co-conspirator brutally beat Kerkowski to compel him to reveal the location of tens of thousands of dollars he kept in his house then used flex ties to strangle him and Fassett.
8Authorities found their decomposing bodies about a year later, along with at least three other sets of human remains on Selenski's property.
9Selenski, who is already serving decades in prison for an unrelated robbery, escaped from prison months after his 2003 arrest using a rope fashioned from bed sheets, and he spent three days on the run before turning himself in.
10The jury reached its verdict last week after deliberating more than 11 hours over two days.
11It convicted Selenski of eight of 10 counts, including first-degree murder and robbery.
12Prosecutors and defense lawyers, under a gag order, were unable to comment on the verdict.
13One of Selenski's sisters cried quietly and left the courtroom after hearing the verdict.
14The victims' relatives remained stoic, hugging prosecutors after the jury exited.
15'Thirteen years,' murmured Kerkowski's mother, Geraldine Kerkowski.
16Later, Selenski's brother Ronald Selenski Jr. rushed toward an elevator holding the victims' relatives and prosecutors, and pointed a finger at them.
17Sheriff's deputies walked him away from the elevator and put him in handcuffs.
18It wasn't immediately clear whether he would be charged.
19Hugo Selenski has been a familiar face in northeastern Pennsylvania since his 2003 arrest on charges he killed a pair of drug dealers whose charred remains also were found on the property north of Wilkes-Barre.
20In 2006, a jury acquitted him of one homicide and deadlocked on another but convicted him of abusing the men's corpses.
21After the verdict, authorities immediately charged him with killing Kerkowski and Fassett.
22Kerkowski, from Hunlock Creek, had pleaded guilty to running a prescription drug ring that netted at least $800,000 and was about to be sentenced when he and Fassett were reported missing in May 2002.
23They were both 37 years old.
24Selenski saw an opportunity to scam Kerkowski, getting the pharmacist to give him tens of thousands of dollars for legal work he never performed, prosecutors alleged.
25But Selenski quickly burned through all the money and needed another $10,000 to cover a check that his girlfriend, Christina Strom, had written to purchase a house, giving him a motive to rob and kill Kerkowski before the pharmacist headed to prison on the drug charges, prosecutors said.
26The defense contended Selenski was framed by another man, Paul Weakley, who led police to Selenski's yard in June 2003.
27Weakley later pleaded guilty in federal court, testified against Selenski to avoid the death penalty and could ask for a reduction of his life prison sentence because of his cooperation.
28Weakley, who met Selenski in prison in the 1990s, told jurors how he plotted with Selenski to kill Kerkowski and then helped him carry out the crimes and bury the bodies.
29He described how he and Selenski bound the victims and covered their eyes with duct tape.
30Weakley said Kerkowski, who was beaten with a rolling pin, told them where to find his hidden bags of cash.
31He said Fassett was killed simply because she was with Kerkowski when they showed up at the pharmacist's house.
32After the killings, Selenski stole tens of thousands more dollars that Kerkowski had given to his father for safekeeping, pointing a gun at the father and threatening him, other witnesses said.
