Susan Rice: Bad Iran nuclear deal is worse than no deal

Donations drying up for Islamic State group but Syria war refugees could be prime for recruiting: U.S. spy chief

1Susan Rice is speaking to the annual meeting of the pro-Israel lobby AIPAC.
2Her speech comes a day before Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu plans to argue against U.S. nuclear talks with Iran during an address to Congress.
3Modifying a quote used by President Ronald Reagan, Rice says the U.S. approach to Iran is "distrust, but verify."
4She says any U.S. deal must verifiably cut off any pathway for Iran to develop a nuclear weapon.
5Rice peppered her remarks to AIPAC with frequent phrases in Hebrew.
6Ahead of Rice's speech, a prominent rabbi ran a full-page ad in The New York Times accusing her of being a bystander to genocide.
1"I think there is change afoot in the Mideast," said James Clapper, director of national intelligence, referring to perceptions of the Islamic State group in the region.
2"It's not going to occur overnight. But I think these brutalities, publicized brutalities by ISIL (Islamic State), beheadings, immolation and the like, have really had a galvanizing effect even in the Mideast," Clapper said at an event in New York organized by the Council on Foreign Relations.
3As a result, donations to the extremists in Islamic countries were dropping off, according to Clapper.
4But he added that private donations accounted for "less than 1 percent" of the group's income, with most of its revenue coming from bank robberies, extortion and oil smuggling in areas under Islamic State control.
5The United Arab Emirates, Qatar, Saudi Arabia and other Sunni-led states have long been accused of allowing donations to flow to Islamist extremists in Syria, but those countries have denied the allegations.
6Vice President Joe Biden had to issue an apology to UAE, Turkey and Saudi Arabia last year after he suggested that their governments sent money and weapons to Sunni militants fighting the Syrian regime.
7Clapper also said the Islamic State group was adept at propaganda and that a "counternarrative" was needed to defeat the jihadis.
8The conflict would be decided ultimately by winning "hearts and minds" and "a counternarrative starting with education," he said.
9The U.S. spy chief expressed concern about Islamic State militants recruiting fighters from among the huge numbers of refugees who have fled the conflict in Syria.
10Millions of refugees have put a "huge strain" on neighboring Lebanon, Jordan and Turkey, and often are living in grim conditions, he said.
11The pool of refugees creates an "environment for recruiting" volunteers for the Islamic State, he said.
12"That's a great concern that I have."
13His comments came after the United Nations last week warned Syria's refugee crisis was approaching a "dangerous turning point."
14Close to 12 million people have been displaced by the four-year war in Syria, including 3.8 million who have fled to neighboring states.
