Backstrom seals Capitals' 2-1 overtime win over Isles (Yahoo Sports)

1UNIONDALE, N.Y. (AP) -- Nicklas Backstrom scored 11:09 into overtime to lift the Washington Capitals to a 2-1 victory on Tuesday night, and even their first-round playoff series against the New York Islanders.
2Alex Ovechkin also scored and assisted on the decisive goal, which came off a faceoff to the right of the Islanders net.
3Backstrom dug the puck out in the right corner and worked his way up the boards.
4He snapped a shot near the blue line that went through a crowd and sneaked inside the far post.
5Casey Cizikas scored for the Islanders as the series shifts to Washington for Game 5 on Thursday.
6It was the second consecutive game decided in overtime, after John Tavares needed 15 seconds to seal the Islanders' 2-1 win on Sunday.
7The Capitals had two of the best scoring chances to open overtime, and both set up by Marcus Johansson.
8Five minutes in, he circled the Islanders net and sent a pass through the middle to Evgeny Kuznetsov, who was stopped by Jaroslav Halak.
9Three minutes later, with the Isles caught on a line change, Johansson got the puck at the New York blue line and passed to Jason Chimera breaking in alone.
10Halak kicked out his right pad to make the save.
11Halak finished with 28 saves.
12Ovechkin opened the scoring at 13:06 of the first off a faceoff to the left of the Islanders net.
13Backstrom won the draw and got the puck back to John Carlson at the right point.
14Carlson blasted a shot which Ovechkin deflected in while cutting across the middle.
15It was Ovechkin's second goal of the series and only second in 13 road playoff games dating to Game 2 of Washington's second-round series against the New York Rangers on April 30, 2012.
16Cizikas tied it with 13 seconds left in the opening period.
17Cal Clutterbuck sparked the play by driving through the middle and getting off a bouncing shot.
18Braden Holtby stopped the shot, but couldn't control the rebound which Cizikas flipped in while driving to the left post.
19The third period featured both teams trading chances.
20Cizikas nearly scored four minutes in when he deflected Calvin de Haan's point shot, only to have it stopped by Holtby.
21Holtby had 36 saves.
22Halak was sharp during a Capitals power play that began 6:40 into the period.
23First he stopped Carlson's hard shot from the blue line, and then foiled Ovechkin attempting to convert the rebound.
24Later, Halak was late getting across to his left, but reached back just in time to stop Marcus Johansson, who was set up in front by Ovechkin.
25The Islanders then nearly scored a short-handed goal, when Clutterbuck had his shot go off the crossbar during a two-on-one break.
26It was a tightly played game throughout.
27The Capitals had stretches of controlling the puck in the Islanders zone, and New York countered with its speedy transition game.
28Ovechkin had a golden opportunity to score his second goal late in the second period, when Backstrom set him up for a one-timer above the left circle.
29Ovechkin didn't get all of it, and the shot fluttered to Halak, leaving the Capitals star looking to the rafters in frustration.
30Seconds later, the Islanders pounced on the transition.
31Brock Nelson sped up the right boards and flipped a centering pass to Josh Bailey, who got off a good shot.
32Holtby made the stop by getting his right pad down just in time.
33Islanders veteran defenseman Lubomir Visnovsky did not return after being bowled over by hard-charging Capitals forward Tom Wilson in New York's right corner.
34Visnovsky was slow to get up and held a towel to his nose while skating off to the locker room.
35Wilson was penalized for charging.
36Capitals defenseman Brooks Orpik had a scary moment when he was cut across the right side of his face by Carlson's skate blade with a minute left in the first period.
37Orpik immediately dropped his gloves and skated toward the gate leading to the Capitals' locker room.
38He returned for the start of the second period.
39Orpik was falling forward over Carlson during a scramble in front, when the toe of Carlson's skate caught Orpik just to the right of his mouth.
40The ''Let's Go Islanders!'' chants started 40 minutes before game time, with a large group of jersey-wearing fans gathered near the team's on-ice gate.
41The Islanders are guaranteed playing at least one more game at Nassau Veterans Memorial Coliseum.
42The franchise is leaving its 43-year-old home and moving to Brooklyn next season.
43After distributing orange-colored towels that read, ''Let's Make History.
44One More Time,'' on Sunday, the Islanders distributed blue-colored ones on Tuesday.
45NOTES: Capitals coach Barry Trotz made two changes to his lineup.
46Rookie LW Andre Burakovsky made his playoff debut and C Michael Latta returned after sitting out the past two games.
47Curtis Glencross sat and Eric Fehr is to miss at least two games after sustaining an upper-body injury in Game 3.
