Footage of Russian-backed rebels ransacking MH17 luggage is sickening, Julie Bishop says

1Foreign Minister Julie Bishop is sickened by footage apparently showing Russian-backed rebels ransacking luggage of passengers in the aftermath of the shooting down of Malaysia Airlines flight MH17.
2Bishop said she couldn't verify the authenticity of the video published by News Corp Australia.
3"It is sickening to watch and 12 months on from the downing of MH17 it is deeply concerning that this footage has emerged now," Bishop told the Nine Network.
4Bishop said it had been a tough year for grieving families and it would be an emotional day ahead.
5"Their grief is inconsolable and the burden of grieving and then seeing this footage will be almost too much to bear," she said.
6The disaster killed 298 people, including 38 Australians.
7Bishop has flagged a second investigation report into the tragedy will be released in October.
8The next step was establishing an international criminal tribunal, but Bishop acknowledged it would be tough to get it approved by the United Nations Security Council.
9An estimated 200 of their family members will attend a commemoration at parliament house in Canberra on Friday, which the prime minister, Tony Abbott, says he hoped will bring some comfort to the families left behind.
10"I know that nothing anyone can do can bring back their loved ones, nothing anyone can say can make it easier for them," Abbott told ABC TV on Friday.
11"This was not just a tragedy. It was an atrocity."
12Abbott suspects Russian president Vladimir Putin is horrified by the event and does not believe the Russian leader knew anything in advance.
13"The point that I made to (Putin) when we spoke about this was that, as a parent and as a human being, he owed it to the families of the victims to do what he could to try to get to the bottom of this," he said.
14It would be a credit to Russia if there was full co-operation with the ongoing criminal investigation into the plane's downing, Abbott said.
15Toowoomba man Paul Guard, who lost his parents Roger and Jill, is in Canberra for the ceremony along with nine other family members.
16"I think it will be a difficult day, but hopefully a useful part of the healing process," he told ABC TV.
17Abbott will join friends and family of the 38 Australian citizens and residents who died to unveil a plaque at the memorial service.
