8The Fynes have described the discovery of the note as "emotionally devastating."
9The report recommends that for "greater clarity" military police adopt a specific policy on suicide notes.
10Aside from criticizing the length of time the NIS took to hand over the suicide note, the report is also critical of NIS senior officers for not explaining to Langridge's family why it was withheld in the first place or whether foul play was ruled out before the soldier's funeral.
11During the 2012 hearings, NIS officers said they had withheld the note in the interests of Langridge's family in case he had been the victim of foul play and the suicide note forged.

Langridge's suicide note," said commission chair Glen Stannard.
13-Better supervision of military police investigators, especially when complex facts and allegations are involved.
14-Meaningful and substantive briefings for families during sudden death investigations.
15-Increased opportunities for military police investigators to improve their skills through secondment to civilian police forces.
16-Experienced federal, provincial or municipal police investigators should be brought in to lead sudden death investigations.
17-Property seized as part of an investigation should be immediately returned to families when no longer needed as evidence.
18-Suicide notes should be withheld only if there are compelling reasons for suspecting foul play.
