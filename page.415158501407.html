Russia, Ukraine reach gas deal to avoid cuts this month

1Russia and Ukraine's energy ministers are holding emergency talks in Brussels as Russian gas giant Gazprom threatens to cut off deliveries to Ukraine for lack of payment.
2European Union energy spokesperson Anna-Kaisa Itkonen said Monday that the EU's executive Commission will mediate between the sides in an attempt to make sure gas keeps flowing despite the dispute.
3Russian Energy Minister Alexander Novak, center, speaks to the media as he arrives at EU headquarters in Brussels on Monday, March 2, 2015.
4Russian Energy Minister Alexander Novak speaks with the media as he arrives at EU headquarters in Brussels on Monday, March 1, 2015.
5BRUSSELS (AP) - Russia and Ukraine reached an agreement late Monday to ensure natural gas supplies to war-torn Ukraine for the month of March, averting possible cuts that could have hit supplies to the European Union.
6The two countries have fought several gas price wars in past years and Moscow had been threatening to turn off the taps to Ukraine on Tuesday unless it received outstanding pre-payments, as agreed in a deal they clinched last October, the so-called winter package.
7"I am satisfied that we managed to safeguard the full application of the winter package for the supply needs in Ukraine," said EU energy chief Maros Sefcovic after mediating emergency talks between Russian Energy Minister Alexander Novak and his Ukrainian counterpart Volodymyr Demchyshyn.
8Demchyshyn said that both parties had agreed "to obey the rules" until the end of March.
9Under the deal sealed in Brussels, Ukraine's Naftogaz will pre-pay and order sufficient quantities of gas to ensure all domestic consumption for March and guarantee undisrupted supplies to the EU.
10Gazprom commits to supply a set quantity of natural gas each day at agreed on delivery points.
11The European Union imports around 40 percent of its gas from Russia, half through Ukraine.
12Complicating the dispute has been deliveries to Ukraine's rebel-held east, where fighting between Kiev's forces and Russia-backed rebels has killed more than 6,000 people.
13Kiev cut gas supplies to rebel-held areas last week, prompting Russia to pump gas there directly.
14Russia said those deliveries should be counted in gas exports to Ukraine.
15Under the agreement, Kiev and Moscow acknowledged that the supply issue in eastern Ukraine "is highly complex in legal, technical and political terms", and they agreed that more discussions would be required, a statement said.
16The EU's executive Commission has asked the two to propose possible agenda points for further talks before the end of March aimed at agreeing a new gas arrangement for coming months.
