U.N. seeks $174 million to support refugees fleeing Boko Haram

1Boko Haram fighters have killed thousands of people in their six-year bid to create an Islamist emirate in northeastern Nigeria.
2Though a military campaign by Nigeria and its neighbors has driven Boko Haram out of many positions it previously held, the U.N. still expects refugee numbers to rise from 192,000 to 240,000 by the end of this year.
3The refugees, some of whom walked or even swam long distances to escape the violence, are spread out across the countries bordering Lake Chad in a mostly arid zone that encompasses some of the poorest regions of the world.
4Most of them are sheltering with local residents, rather than in refugee camps, partly out of fear of becoming targets for cross-border reprisal attacks.
5"Boko Haram has been so vicious in targeting civilians that there is a psychosis among the refugees," said Robert Piper, the U.N.'s Regional Humanitarian Coordinator for the Sahel region.
6"But this means they are spread out and logistically it is much more difficult to reach them," he said.
7The 23 UN agencies and NGOs involved in the support plan each drew up their cost estimates, which came to a combined $174.4 million.
8In addition to the refugees, there are more than 1.1 million people displaced within northeast Nigeria by Boko Haram, the U.N. says.
