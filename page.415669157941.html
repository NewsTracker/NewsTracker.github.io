Nine British medical students cross into Syria to help Islamic State

1Up to nine British medical students, five men and four women, who had been studying in Sudan, are understood to have flown to Istanbul 10 days ago and slipped into Syria overland.
2British Foreign Office officials are providing consular support to several of the families who have congregated close to the border, desperately trying to urge their children home.
3Although the students do not appear to have volunteered to fight, the families nevertheless fear they have been "brainwashed" into helping the militants.
4The parents have been in regular contact with their children by mobile phone and are pledging to stay there until they can successfully bring them home.
5The alarm was raised after one of the women, named in Turkey as 19-year-old Lena Maumoon Abdulqadir, sent a social networking message to her sister in Britain on March 12, telling her: "Don't worry, we've reached Turkey and are on our way to volunteer helping wounded Syrian people."
6Her father, Maumoon, informed the British police before setting off himself for Turkey, and alerting the police there.
7He also made contact with Mehmet Ali Ediboglu, a Turkish opposition politician, who has helped co-ordinate the efforts of the families, who are understood to be from the British Sudanese community.
8Mr Ediboglu said the nine had travelled with two other Sudanese students.
9It is understood that British Foreign Office officials are supporting seven families.
10Meanwhile, the University of Medical Sciences and Technology in Khartoum confirmed that five of its students with British passports had gone missing.
11A spokeswoman for the British Foreign Office said: "We are providing consular assistance to families of British nationals who are believed to be missing after travelling to Turkey We are working closely with the Turkish police to establish their whereabouts."
12A British government spokesman said the students would not necessarily face charges if they returned to Britain, provided they could prove they had not been fighting.
