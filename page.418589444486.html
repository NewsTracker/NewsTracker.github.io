Ukraine, Australia mark sombre anniversary of Malaysian jet's downing

1A relative of an Australian victim of Malaysia Airlines jet MH17 reacts before placing a floral tribute at a memorial that was unveiled outside Parliament House in Canberra, Australia.
2Residents of the Ukrainian village where a Malaysian airliner was shot down with 298 people aboard a year ago began a procession to the crash site on Friday, while the Australian prime minister remembered the "savagery" of the disaster by unveiling a plaque in Canberra that's set in soil from the place where the plane went down.
3The two ceremonies come amid an escalating war of words over who was responsible for destroying the plane.
4Ukrainian and Western authorities say the plane was downed by a missile fired either by rebels or Russian troops who allegedly back them.
5The rebels and Moscow say it was hit by a Ukrainian warplane or a Ukrainian-fired missile.
6In Hrabove, villagers carrying flowers gathered in the church in the centre of the village at the start of a procession to the site in nearby fields.
7The commemoration, organized by local leaders and the Russia-backed separatist rebels who control the area, will include the dedication of a small stone with a plaque in the field.
8The passengers included 38 Australian citizens and permanent residents who were among the 298 people on Malaysia Airlines Flight 17, which was heading from Amsterdam to Kuala Lumpur on July 17, 2014, when it was shot down.
9In Canberra, Australian Prime Minister Tony Abbott unveiled a plaque inscribed with the names of Australian victims, which is set in soil that a police officer brought back from Ukraine.
10A wreath is placed on a cross with an inscription that reads "Save and protect" next to the site of the downed Malaysia Airlines flight MH17, near the village of Hrabove in Donetsk region, eastern Ukraine.
11"He knew that the place where MH17 came to rest was sacred and that a piece of it should come back to Australia," Abbott said.
12"It was a humane and decent thing for him to know and do. It was a contrast to the savagery that brought down the plane."
13Abbott and his wife then lay a wreath at the base of the plaque.
14Dozens of family members of the victims followed, many in tears as they lay flowers alongside the wreath in honour of their loved ones.
15Some kissed the bouquets before they placed them down, while others kissed their fingers and pressed them against the plaque.
16Australia, Belgium, Malaysia, the Netherlands and Ukraine have asked the United Nations Security Council to establish an international criminal tribunal to prosecute those responsible for shooting down the plane.
17Russia, however, is opposing a proposed resolution to establish a tribunal for the crash, and has veto power in the Security Council.
