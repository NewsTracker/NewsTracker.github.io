5NEW YORK (AP) - Nearly three-quarters of Facebook's advertising revenue now comes from mobile ads, as many more users access the social network on smartphones and other hand-held gadgets.
6On that front, the company is doing fine - better, even, than it has in previous quarters.
7But the rate of its overall revenue growth slowed in the first three months of this year, causing its high-flying stock to tumble after the results came out.
8Wednesday marked the first time since early 2013 that Facebook failed to top Wall Street's expectations with its results, and shares of the Menlo Park, California, company fell more than 2 percent after-hours trading.
9Facebook has grown mobile ad revenue steadily since 2012, when it started showing ads for the devices' smaller screens.
10In the previous quarter, mobile represented 69 percent of total advertising revenue.
11The social network had 1.44 billion monthly active users as of March, up 13 percent from a year earlier.
12The number of users who accessed Facebook on mobile devices at least once a month grew 24 percent to 1.25 billion.
13Facebook's share of the $42.6 billion worldwide mobile advertising market was 17.4 percent last year, according to research firm eMarketer, up from 16.4 percent a year earlier.
14In comparison, Google's share was 38.2 percent, down from 46 percent a year earlier.
15The company reported adjusted earnings of 42 cents per share in the January-March quarter, above the 41 cents per share that analysts polled by FactSet were expecting.
16Revenue increased 46 percent to $3.54 billion, from $2.5 billion a year earlier.
17Analysts had expected $3.56 billion.
18Net income declined as Facebook's expenses grew 83 percent from a year earlier.
19The company earned $512 million, or 18 cents per share, down 20 percent from $642 million, or 25 cents per share.
20Facebook, which bought the popular photo-sharing app Instagram and the messaging service WhatsApp, has been coming out with its own stand-alone mobile apps to capture more of the time people spend on phones.
21Besides its Messenger app, though, Facebook's home-grown efforts have had limited success.
22On Wednesday, it introduced Hello, a voice-calling app designed for Android phones.
23In essence, it's meant to replace the voice dialer on your phone.
24But based on Facebook's inability to persuade users to install its Home app, which lets Facebook take over the home screen on Android, users may not be too keen on replacing existing smartphone tools with Facebook-branded versions.
25Facebook finance chief David Wehner said mobile "continues to be a key driver" of Facebook's growth.
26He said Facebook will continue to experiment with apps for mobile.
27For now, the money-making focus is on Facebook itself and, slowly, on popular, established apps such as Instagram.
28Shares in Facebook Inc. fell $1.76 to $82.87 in extended trading after the earnings report.
29At the close of regular-session trading Wednesday, the stock stood at $84.63, an 8.5 percent increase since the start of the year.
