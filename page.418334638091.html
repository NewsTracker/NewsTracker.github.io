Rookies Bryant, Pederson make All-Star team; A-Rod left out (Yahoo Sports)

1NEW YORK (AP) -- Less than three months after making his major league debut, Chicago Cubs third baseman Kris Bryant is an All-Star, one of two rookies selected along with Los Angeles Dodgers outfielder Joc Pederson.
2But there was no room on the roster for Alex Rodriguez, enjoying a renaissance in his return to the New York Yankees following a season-long drug suspension.
3Dodgers catcher Yasmani Grandal also was a first-time selection Monday for the July 14 game in Cincinnati.
4Grandal became the third All-Star this year who served a drug-related suspension for conduct connected to the Biogenesis of America clinic.
5Seattle slugger Nelson Cruz and St. Louis shortstop Jhonny Peralta were elected by fans to start.
6Albert Pujols of the Angels will start at first base for the AL in place of Detroit's Miguel Cabrera, who injured a calf muscle Friday.
7Pittsburgh's Andrew McCutchen will start in the NL outfield instead of Miami's Giancarlo Stanton, who broke his hand on June 26.
8The 23-year-old Bryant made his big league debut April 17 and began Monday with a .279 average, 12 homers and 49 RBIs.
9''Obviously, I've been at some All-Star Games, but I don't think anything can compare to making the major league All-Star team,'' he said.
10''It is kind of weird right now.
11He's open to participating in the Home Run Derby.
12''It's all happened so quick.
13I've just been having so much fun with this, my baseball career,'' Bryant said.
14Pederson, who also expects to be in the derby, had 20 homers but was hitting just .234.
15He was especially pleased fellow players elected him.
16''They respect the way I play the game,'' he said.
17A record six Royals were picked for the game, with players electing pitcher Wade Davis, and AL manager Ned Yost of Kansas City selecting reliever Kelvin Herrera.
18They join Royals outfielders Lorenzo Cain and Alex Gordon, catcher Salvador Perez and shortstop Alcides Escobar, who were elected on Sunday as starters.
19''The one thing that Kelvin did that a lot of these guys didn't do was pitch in Game 7 of the World Series last year,'' Yost said.
20Royals third baseman Mike Moustakas is among the five players on the AL fan ballot for the 34th and final roster spot.
21Rodriguez is not.
22''We spent a lot of time debating his name,'' said Yost, who preferred the versatility of an extra infielder or outfielder.
23''We felt we were covered in the DH.
24The league that wins the All-Star Game gets home-field advantage in the World Series.
25Rodriguez is hitting .284 during a season in which he passed Willie Mays for fourth on the career home run list and topped 3,000 hits.
26But Texas' Prince Fielder, who was elected by players, joined Cruz as the only DHs on the AL roster.
27''As I've said all season, my number one goal is helping the Yankees win a championship.
28I'm excited that we're in a good position to get there,'' Rodriguez said in a statement.
29Yankees reliever Dellin Betances was elected by players, and first baseman Mark Teixeira was picked to fill Cabrera's roster spot.
30But this will be the first All-Star Game with no Yankees starters since 1999.
31St. Louis tops the NL with five players.
32Peralta was elected to start along with outfielder Matt Holliday, who isn't sure when he'll be activated from the disabled list.
33Catcher Yadier Molina and reliever Trevor Rosenthal were elected by players, and NL manager Bruce Bochy of San Francisco picked pitcher Michael Wacha.
34Cincinnati closer Aroldis Chapman was elected by players and is the second member of the hometown Reds on the NL roster.
35Third baseman Todd Frazier was elected to start.
36Pittsburgh pitcher A.J. Burnett, in his 17th and what he says will be his final season, became an All-Star for the first time.
37At 38, he's the oldest player headed to the game.
38''It's cool,'' Burnett said.
39Washington outfielder Bryce Harper, a three-time All-Star at age 22, is the youngest.
40Harper said Monday he won't participate in the Home Run Derby because his father isn't available to pitch to him following shoulder surgery.
41There are 16 All-Stars born outside the 50 states, including six Venezuelans, five Dominicans, three Cubans, one Canadian and one Puerto Rican.
42AL starting pitchers include Seattle's Felix Hernandez, Detroit's David Price, Chicago's Chris Sale, Houston's Dallas Keuchel, Oakland's Sonny Gray and Tampa Bay's Chris Archer.
43Among the NL starting pitchers are Washington's Max Scherzer, San Francisco's Madison Bumgarner, the Dodgers' Zack Greinke, the Mets' Jacob deGrom, Pittsburgh's Gerrit Cole and Atlanta's Shelby Miller.
44''New team.
45New league.
46It's just getting a look at everybody,'' said Scherzer, who left Detroit to sign a $210 million deal with the Nationals.
47''Everybody's getting their first look at me and the things I've done.
48I'm sure they'll make adjustments the rest of the year.
49Joining Moustakas on the ballot for the AL's final spot are Boston shortstop Xander Bogaerts, Detroit outfielder Yoenis Cespedes, Minnesota second baseman Brian Dozier and Yankees outfielder Brett Gardner.
50The NL ballot includes pitchers Johnny Cueto of Cincinnati, Jeurys Familia of the Mets, Clayton Kershaw of the Dodgers and Carlos Martinez of the Cardinals along with Colorado shortstop Troy Tulowitzki.
51Voting runs until Friday afternoon.
