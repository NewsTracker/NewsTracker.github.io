Fans push to preserve Jim Morrison's New Mexico home

1Fans of Jim Morrison, the late singer for The Doors, are in a push to preserve his childhood home in New Mexico.
2A group of fans want his northeast Albuquerque home designated a historical site and placed in a list of tourist attractions in the city, KOAT-TV reports (http://goo.gl/pzWW0t).
3Historian and fan Anthony Gomez says because Morrison spent important years in New Mexico's biggest city, his childhood home should be protected as a remembrance.
4"Living in Albuquerque for a while, I think he should have a spot here," Gomez said.
5Thelma Lue, who owns the house, said fans of the rock legend sometimes stop by the house.
6"It doesn't bother me," she said.
7"They've all been very thoughtful and nice."
8The 1960s singer of hits such as "Light My Fire" and "Riders on the Storm" lived in Albuquerque from age 12 to 14 while his father, George S. Morrison, worked at Kirtland Air Force Base.
9The elder Morrison commanded U.S. ships in the Gulf of Tonkin during the clash that helped escalate the Vietnam War.
10The young Morrison attended Monroe and Wilson middle schools.
11In the biography "No One Here Gets Out Alive," Jim Morrison speaks of witnessing a car accident in the desert involving an American Indian family where some might have died.
12The accident, which likely happened in New Mexico, later influenced his songs, poetry and interviews.
13Morrison's family later moved to Alexandria, Virginia.
14The Melbourne, Florida-born Morrison died in July 1971 at the age of 27 in a Paris bathtub.
