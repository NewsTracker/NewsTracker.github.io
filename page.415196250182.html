Ukraine coalmine blast in Donetsk kills dozens

1Donetsk, Ukraine: At least 32 miners have died in a blast at a coalmine in a rebel-held area of east Ukraine, the president of Ukraine's parliament says.
2"A terrible tragedy took place this morning at Zasyadko [mine]. There are victims, currently numbering 32," Volodymyr Groysman told parliament in Kiev on Wednesday, calling on MPs to observe a minute of silence.
3The blast took place in the city of Donetsk, epicentre of a nearly year-long conflict between government forces and pro-Russian rebels.
4Emergency vehicles are parked outside Zasyadko coalmine in Donetsk on Wednesday.
5A spokeswoman for the ministry of emergency situations of the self-proclaimed Donetsk People's Republic told AFP earlier one miner was killed and 14 injured in the gas explosion.
6"At the moment there are still 70 people underground, but the situation is constantly changing as we bring people to the surface," spokeswoman Julia Bedilo said.
7A union spokesman said 207 miners were underground at the time of the blast, with 53 in the affected area.
8Miners arrive to help with the rescue effort in the Zasyadko coal mine.
9"The fate of 47 miners is unknown, they could be dead," union spokesman Mikhail Volynets told AFP.
10Five miners with serious burns had been evacuated, he added.
11Zasyadko mine has been the scene of several deadly accidents in recent years.
12Family members wait outside the coal mine.
