FIFA bans bid evaluation chief over 'confidentiality' breach

1Photo: FIFA's ethics committee adjudicatory chamber has banned Harold Mayne-Nicholls.
2Football's world governing body FIFA has banned the former head of their bid evaluation committee for the 2018 and 2022 World Cups for seven years for "confidentiality" breaches.
3Harold Mayne-Nicholls, a former Chilean Football Association (FA) president, wrote the report that gave Qatar the worst ranking of the candidates for the 2022 World Cup finals which the wealthy country went on to win.
4FIFA's ethics committee adjudicatory chamber banned Mr Mayne-Nicholls, 54, "from taking part in any kind of football-related activity at national and international level for a period of seven years", a statement said.
5The world governing body did not give details of the charges against Mr Mayne-Nicholls, but said it was for a breach of a confidentiality rule in FIFA's code of ethics.
6Mr Mayne-Nicholls revealed in November he was under investigation over emails he sent to the Aspire Academy in Qatar, seeking internships for his son and a nephew.
7He also sought tennis coaching opportunities for a brother-in-law.
8He was at the time considering running against Sepp Blatter for the FIFA presidency.
9The Chilean said the internships were "unpaid", British media reported.
10However, Aspire Academy turned down Mr Mayne-Nicholls' requests.
11The reports quoted an email sent to Mr Mayne-Nicholls by FIFA ethics committee deputy chairman Cornel Borbely, which said the approaches were made "shortly after the inspection tour and prior to the issuance of the evaluation report on Qatar".
12"Furthermore, said behaviour seems to be a cause enough to doubt the integrity of the inspection process and your evaluations," the email read.
13Mr Mayne-Nicholls denied at the time he had done anything wrong.
14He did not immediately comment on the FIFA sanction.
15FIFA said Mr Mayne-Nicholls was present at a hearing where the ban was decided and that more detailed information would be given after the final decision becomes effective.
16The FIFA evaluation committee gave Qatar its worst ranking, mainly because of the summer heat in the Gulf state.
17FIFA has since held an inquiry into the bid process for the 2018 World Cup in Russia and the 2022 tournament, and has changed the date of the Qatar tournament to November-December because of concerns about the heat.
