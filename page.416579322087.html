Ukraine says rebels firing rocket launchers again

1KIEV, Ukraine (AP) - Ukrainian military officials said Tuesday that separatist rebels in the east have resumed the use of rocket launchers that should have been withdrawn under a February peace deal.
2The army said in a statement that rebels fired Grad rockets Monday evening at the government-held town of Avdiivka, which lies on the fringes of the main rebel stronghold of Donetsk.
3There has been a recent uptick in clashes along the front separating government and rebel forces.
4Speaking at an investor conference in Kiev, President Petro Poroshenko warned that the resumption of full-blown war is a perennial threat.
5"War could start at any moment, but we are ready to do everything possible to dispel any room for doubts or retreats," he said.
6A cease-fire tortuously negotiated by France, Germany and Russia in Minsk, the capital of Belarus, requires the warring sides to pull back their most powerful arms by distances over 50 kilometers (30 miles).
7Responsibility for checking whether the deal is being implemented lies with the Organization for Security and Cooperation in Europe.
8But the OSCE's special monitoring mission said late Monday that its monitors have been prevented by rebels from visiting a location where heavy arms have allegedly been deployed.
9For the third time in four days, the rebels have prevented the mission "from freely accessing the eastern part of Shyrokyne," the OSCE said in statement.
10Shyrokyne lies directly on the front line and is a short distance east of the key industrial port city of Mariupol, which is in government hands.
11Fighting there has never entirely subsided despite the Minsk agreement, but clashes appear to have intensified in recent days.
12The OSCE has said the clashes it saw Sunday in Shyrokyne were the worst it had seen since fighting began in the area in mid-February.
13The mission said it observed dozens of tank shots and the deployment of plenty of other weapons proscribed under the peace deal.
14U.S. State Department spokesman Jeff Rathke said in Washington that Russia has deployed more air defense systems into eastern Ukraine and positioned several near the front lines.
15Kiev would like to see international peacekeepers on the ground, but that proposal has been greeted with hostility by Russia and coolness by Western countries.
