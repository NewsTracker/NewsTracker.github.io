Punishment for Kenyan gang rapists lifted from cutting grass to 15 years' jail

1Nairobi: A Kenyan court has convicted three men for the brutal gang rape of a schoolgirl, boosting their original punishment of being made to cut grass to 15 years in jail.
2"Today's sentencing is sure to have a ripple effect across the nation, and hopefully the region at large," said lawyer Kimberly Brown, from the campaign group Equality Now, which supported the schoolgirl.
3Worldwide outrage over the punishment prompted more than 1.8 million people to sign an online petition demanding justice.
4The then 16-year-old victim, known by the pseudonym Liz, was reportedly attacked, beaten and then raped by six men as she returned from her grandfather's funeral in western Kenya in June, 2013.
5The gang dumped her, bleeding and unconscious, in a deep sewage ditch.
6She suffered a broken back, caused either by the beating or by being hurled down into the pit, as well as serious internal injuries from the rape.
7The case made global headlines after it emerged that three of the alleged rapists whom Liz identified were ordered by police to cut grass around the police station as punishment.
8Other suspects are on the run, with the public prosecutor ordering they be "apprehended and brought to justice without further delay".
9Sentencing had been set for last Friday but was delayed after one of the men skipped bail and failed to turn up, but police arrested him over the weekend.
10Rape is a serious problem in Kenya but is seldom taken seriously by the police, rights groups say.
