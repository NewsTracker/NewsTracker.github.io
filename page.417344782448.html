US stocks fall after drop in China, Greece deadline looms

1U.S. stocks were modestly lower in afternoon trading Thursday, following a steep sell-off in the Chinese stock market and the approaching deadline for cash-starved Greece to make a debt payment.
2KEEPING SCORE: The Dow Jones industrial average lost 66 points, or 0.4 percent, to 18,097 as of 2:20 p.m. Eastern.
3The Standard & Poor's 500 index lost six points, or 0.3 percent, to 2,117 and the Nasdaq composite lost 15 points, or 0.3 percent, to 5,091.
4MORE M&A: Chipmakers Avago Technologies and Broadcom agreed to merge in a $37 billion deal.
5Avago was down 91 cents, or 1 percent, to $140.58.
6Broadcom fell $1.44, or 3 percent, to $55.71.
7Both stocks had jumped sharply Wednesday - Broadcom by 22 percent and Avago by 8 percent - on rumors those companies were in merger talks.
8CHINA DIVE: The Shanghai Composite sank 6.5 percent in another volatile move.
9Stock market commentator Hexun attributed the fall to several factors including brokerages tightening margin lending, selling by speculators and a Chinese sovereign wealth fund selling shares in two state banks.
10Despite a deepening economic slowdown, the index has gained 40 percent in the past three months alone.
11Chinese leaders have tried to tap brakes on the stock market boom, fearing it could run out of control and disrupt economic reform plans.
12GREECE FACTOR: Traders continue to get mixed messages about the progress of talks between Greece and its creditors.
13Greece said Wednesday it expected to reach a deal to get more bailout loans in time for it to make a key debt repayment on June 5.
14But its creditors, however, were quick to temper expectations.
15Greece says it aims to clinch a deal with its creditors by Sunday, which would allow it to receive the desperately needed final installment of its international bailout plan and avoid a default.
16Greek stocks fell 1.7 percent.
17Germany's DAX lost 0.8 percent, France's CAC-40 lost 0.9 percent and the U.K.'s FTSE 100 index rose 0.1 percent.
18Greece has given investors a headache for years now, and many are skeptical that this round of talks will resolve any of the country's debt issues.
19"They're likely to kick the can down the road as they have been," said Scott Wren, a market strategist with Wells Fargo.
20"At this point, I think we're likely looking at Greece leaving the eurozone down the road."
21MEANDERING: There's been a lot of "wait and see" for investors this month.
22Along with Greece's debt problems, Wall Street is looking for insight into when the Federal Reserve might start raising interest rates.
23The Fed is expected to increase rates as early as September, but the bank's policymakers say any increase will depend on how the U.S. economy performs.
24MARKUPS: Abercrombie & Fitch rose $2.31, or 12 percent, to $21.96 after the company said it saw sales improve last quarter.
25The teen apparel retailer has been under pressure in recent years to revamp its image.
26ENERGY: Benchmark U.S. crude fell 27 cents to $57.24 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
27Oil and gas stocks fell along with the price of oil.
28Chesapeake Energy, Transocean and Pioneer Natural Resources each lost 3 percent or more.
29BONDS, CURRENCIES: U.S. government bond prices rose slightly.
30The yield on the 10-year Treasury note slipped to 2.13 percent from 2.14 percent.
31The euro rose to $1.0945 from $1.0899.
32The dollar was little changed against the Japanese currency at 123.75 yen.
