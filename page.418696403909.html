Greece says new bailout talks to begin after lawmakers pass bank resolution laws Wednesday

1ATHENS, Greece - Greece's government spokeswoman says talks with the country's creditors for a new multibillion-euro rescue package will begin right after parliament approves laws on propping up and winding up banks.
2Olga Gerovasili said in a statement Tuesday that the vote on the bank laws will take place Wednesday.
3She said negotiations on the new bailout will conclude on Aug. 20.
4Gerovasili said Greece already met other conditions outlined by creditors for bailout talks to begin when lawmakers voted through package of measures including wide-ranging sales tax hikes.
5She said a vote on pension reform has been pushed back with the creditors' consent and insisted that there won't be any vote on changing how farmers are taxed.
