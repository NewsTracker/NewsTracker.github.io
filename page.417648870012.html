Judge in Colorado theater shooting trial dismisses 3 jurors

1FILE - This Jan. 15, 2015, file photo shows a view of the jury box, right, inside Courtroom 201, where jury selection in the trial of Aurora movie theater shootings defendant James Holmes was set to begin at the Arapahoe County District Court in Centennial, Colo.
2Three jurors in the Colorado theater shooting trial were dismissed Tuesday, June 9, 2015, amid concern they had been exposed to media coverage of the case and were discussing it among themselves.
3CENTENNIAL, Colo.
4(AP) - The judge in the Colorado theater shooting trial dismissed three jurors Tuesday after learning that one was exposed to news coverage of the case and discussed it with the others.
5Judge Carlos A. Samour Jr. found the three women violated his orders to avoid outside information on James Holmes' death penalty trial and to not talk about the case with anyone.
6The first woman told Samour her husband called her and told her, on speakerphone, that the district attorney had sent a tweet during testimony, which had been in the news.
7The other two jurors sometimes socialized with the first juror on breaks, and they were dismissed because they likely overheard her.
8Samour and attorneys from both sides had concerns about whether the three jurors were being completely forthcoming about what they had heard and discussed.
9The judge refused a request to dismiss a fourth juror who informed him about the situation, saying she had been honest with the court.
10And he also kept a fifth juror who said she heard the juror who got the phone call mention the word "mistrial" but didn't know what it was about.
11A total of 21 jurors and alternates remain, with the trial more than halfway done.
12Last week, the judge scolded District Attorney George Brauchler for tweeting from the courtroom about a video that was shown to jurors.
13Brauchler told the judge it was an accident and apologized.
14The tweet and defense requests for mistrials were discussed while jurors were away from the courtroom.
15Jurors could know about them only from outside sources, such as social media or news reports.
16Holmes has pleaded not guilty by reason of insanity to opening fire on a crowded theater in 2012.
17He killed 12 people and injured 70.
18The judge was concerned that none of the other jurors came forward to report the incident.
19Under questioning from Samour, the first juror said she got the call from her husband during lunch last week.
20She said the phone was on speaker at the time, and he asked her about Brauchler's tweet while another juror was sitting next to her.
21The juror said she didn't ask what the tweet said and noted she and her husband argued because she knew she wasn't supposed to discuss the case.
22When asked why she didn't report the incident, she responded: "I just really don't pay attention to my husband most of the time. So it wasn't really important, at that time."
23The juror wiped away tears when Samour told her she was dismissed.
24During jury selection, the mother of three said she did not follow the news and said staying away from coverage about the trial would not be a problem.
25A total of 24 jurors and alternates were seated in the case - a process that took nearly three months after 9,000 summonses were sent.
26None of them will know which 12 will decide the case until deliberations are about to begin.
27At that point, the remaining alternates will be dismissed.
28The judge chose the large number of panelists so there would be plenty of alternates to replace anyone who has to be dismissed for health or other reasons.
29Jurors are allowed to go home every night, but they can't discuss the case with anyone or see or read anything about it.
30They are, however, allowed to use their phones on breaks.
31While being questioned Tuesday, some admitted they sometimes see headlines about the trial while online.
32Brauchler's tweet came shortly after the defense began cross-examining a state-appointed psychiatrist, Dr. William Reid, who conducted 22 hours of interviews with Holmes and concluded Holmes was sane at the time of the shooting.
33Reid's testimony was interspersed video of his with interviews with Holmes.
34The district attorney tweeted from courtroom: "I agree on the video. I hope the jury does too."
35Brauchler said he meant to send a text, not a tweet, and apologized to the judge.
