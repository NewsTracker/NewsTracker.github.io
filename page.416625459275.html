Fin24.com | Samsung regains smartphone lead over Apple

1The new Galaxy S6, right, and S6 Edge are displayed during a Samsung Galaxy Unpacked 2015 event in Barcelona.
2Washington - Samsung retook the lead in the global smartphone market in the first quarter, as gains in emerging market sales helped it overtake Apple, a research group said on Wednesday.
3IDC said Samsung sold 82.4 million smartphones in the first three months of the year, for a 24.5% market share.
4Even though the number sold was down 7% from a year ago, it outpaced Apple, whose iPhone sales slipped slightly from the final quarter of 2014.
5Apple held an 18.2% market share in early 2015 after selling 61.2 million iPhones.
6The figures were impacted by seasonal and other factors.
7Samsung notably launched its flagship Galaxy S6 and S6 Edge phones at the end of the quarter in April, while Apple iPhone sales dipped compared with the stronger holiday quarter.
8In the fourth quarter, Apple and Samsung were virtually tied with around 20% of the market each, according to several surveys.
9"The challenge made by Apple for the top spot in the fourth quarter returned to a clear lead for Samsung in the first quarter, despite the soaring global demand for the iPhone 6 and 6 Plus," said IDC analyst Anthony Scarsella.
10"Samsung's shipments, given that the S6 was not launched into the market for the full quarter, were driven by large volumes into emerging markets and steady demand for its midrange and lower-priced smartphones."
11Chinese manufacturer Lenovo was third after completing its acquisition of Motorola from Google.
12Its 5.6% market share placed it ahead of fellow Chinese group Huawei, with 5%, and South Korea's LG at 4.6%.
13IDC said the overall global smartphone market grew 16.7% from a year earlier, with total shipments of 337 million units.
