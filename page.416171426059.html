Video: Islamic State destroys ancient ruins of Nimrud

1Video: Islamic State destroys ancient ruins of Nimrud BAGHDAD (AP) - An online video purports to show Islamic State militants bombing ruins at the ancient Iraqi city of Nimrud.
2The video, posted late Saturday, showed militants using sledgehammers, jackhammers, a bulldozer and ultimately explosives to level the site, located near the militant-held city of Mosul.
3Some of the figures in the video appeared to have rebar, ribbed bars of steels designed to reinforce concrete that are a technique of modern building.
4An Iraqi Antiquities Ministry official, speaking Sunday on condition of anonymity, said all the items at Nimrud were authentic.
5In March, both Iraqi and United Nations officials warned the site had been looted and damaged.
6The video conformed to other Associated Press reporting about the militants' attack.
7One militant on the video said: "God has honored us to remove all of these idols and statutes."
8The Islamic State group, which holds a third of Iraq and neighboring Syria in its self-declared caliphate, has been destroying ancient relics they say promote idolatry that violates their fundamentalist interpretation of Islamic law.
9Authorities also believe they've sold others on the black market to fund their atrocities.
