'Suge' Knight taken to hospital after court hearing

1LOS ANGELES (AP) - Former rap mogul Marion "Suge" Knight told a judge that he is suffering from blindness and other health complications moments before he was taken to a hospital Monday morning.
2Knight told Los Angeles Superior Court Judge James Brandlin that he had fired attorneys handling his murder case and was receiving inadequate medical treatment while in custody.
3The Death Row Records co-founder said he was blind in one eye and had only about 15 percent vision in his other eye during a brief court appearance on Monday.
4Knight said he was having difficulty comprehending the proceedings and told the judge he had been shot six times last year and had a blood clot in his lungs and other complications.
5He also said he had lost 35 pounds as a result of his injuries.
6Brandlin transferred Knight's case to another judge, and he was taken for medical care before his case could be called in Judge Ronald Coen's courtroom.
7Coen said before calling the case that Knight had been taken to a hospital, but he did not elaborate.
8Knight has pleaded not guilty to murder, attempted murder and hit-and-run charges after he struck two men with his truck, killing one, on Jan. 29.
9He remains held without bail.
10Attorney David Kenner, who has represented Knight since the case was filed, told Coen that his client has been jailed too long and a hearing to set bail should be convened as soon as possible.
11Coen ordered another hearing for Knight on March 9, although a bail hearing may not occur until March 20.
12Deputy District Attorney Cynthia Barnes on Monday repeated a previous statement that she plans to introduce evidence of several crimes Knight was never charged with during the bail hearing.
13Knight said he had fired Kenner and his law partner, telling Brandlin, "I fired these lawyers."
14Monday's hearing marks the third time Knight has been taken from a courthouse for medical care since he was charged with murder in early February.
15Knight repeatedly tried to speak during the hearing in Brandlin's courtroom, but the judge admonished him not to interrupt.
16Knight sat down and muttered an expletive while Brandlin quickly concluded the proceedings.
17Knight wore orange jail attire and glasses during the hearing, and he spoke while flanked by deputies.
18Paramedics were summoned to look at Knight before the hearing, and deputies placed a trash can in front of him after he began his tirade about his health and lawyers.
19Knight, 49, was shot six times at a nightclub in August.
20Within days of his release from the hospital, he was accused by a celebrity photographer of stealing her camera.
21Prosecutors charged Knight and comedian Katt Williams with robbery over the incident, and both men have pleaded not guilty.
22Knight faces up to life in prison if convicted of killing Terry Carter, 55, during a confrontation at a Compton burger stand in late January.
23Kenner has said Knight was ambushed, but that assertion hasn't been made in court yet.
