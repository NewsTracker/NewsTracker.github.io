Five ways victory for Israel's Netanyahu matters

1Five ways victory for Israel's Netanyahu matters Five ways Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's victory matters.
2Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's ruling Likud Party scored a resounding victory in the country's election, final results showed Wednesday.
3It was a stunning turnaround after a tight race.
4Iran nuclear talks.
5The Israeli leader has been a strident critic of U.S. talks with Iran on curbing that country's nuclear program.
6Back in power, he may work with fellow critics in the Republican-controlled Congress to undermine any agreement the U.S. reaches with Iran on the grounds that Iran can't be trusted to halt a program that could produce nuclear weapons.
7Palestinian state.
8Peace talks have stalled under Netanyahu, who declared on the eve of the election that he would oppose the creation of a Palestinian state during a new term.
9If he keeps his word, that hard-line stance will be a roadblock to U.S. efforts to revive peace talks aimed at creating an independent state for Palestinians.
10Israeli settlements.
11Over U.S. objections, Netanyahu has allowed construction within existing settlements on land claimed by Palestinians as part of a future state.
12Back in office, Netanyahu would have no incentive to curb settlement activity opposed by President Obama but favored by Israeli nationalists who returned him to office.
13U.S. relations.
14Because of their many policy differences, Obama and Netanyahu have as bad a personal relationship as any U.S. and Israeli leader ever.
15Now that Netanyahu is in line for a fourth term as prime minister, it could be a miserable final two years for Obama when Mideast issues arise.
16Israel's economy.
17Netanyahu's party won despite unhappiness among Israelis with the high cost of living under his free-market economic policies.
18But after a pre-election scare that he might lose because of bread-and-butter issues, Netanyahu may push for higher wages or a better social safety net to make necessities more affordable.
