Iraq calls for more intel from allies against Islamic State

1PARIS (AP) - Iraq's prime minister has called for more intelligence and more action from international allies against Islamic State extremists.
2Haider al-Abadi says the flow of foreign fighters across the border into Iraq has not slowed, and the radical Sunni group now is majority foreign.
3A year ago, he said, it was 60 percent Iraqi.
4Tuesday's meeting in Paris of Iraq and international allies, including the United States and France but not Russia, Iran or Syria, comes after IS conquered both the Iraqi city of Ramadi and the historic Syrian city of Palmyra.
5More than 4,100 airstrikes by the U.S.-led coalition have failed to stem gains by Islamic State radicals.
6France and the U.S. have pushed al-Abadi's government to open to Sunnis.
