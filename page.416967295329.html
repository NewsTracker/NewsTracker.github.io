Cannes kicks off with French drama, Coen brothers, ballet

1CANNES, France (AP) - The Cannes Film Festival unrolled its red carpet with a socially minded French drama as the usually glitzy festival began Wednesday on a more serious note after January's terror attacks in Paris.
2"Standing Tall," a film about a juvenile delinquent co-starring Catherine Deneuve, premiered as the festival got underway beneath hazy French Riviera skies.
3Joel and Ethan Coen, co-presidents of the Cannes jury this year, also presented their fellow jury members - including Jake Gyllenhaal, Guillermo del Toro and Sienna Miller - who will decide who gets Cannes' prestigious Palme d'Or top prize.
4Julianne Moore, the best actress winner at last year's festival for her performance in David Cronenberg's "Maps to the Stars," declared the festival open.
5The ceremony featured a ballet performance choreographed by France's Benjamin Millepied that was inspired by Alfred Hitchcock's "Vertigo."
6Deneuve said the selection of "Standing Tall," directed by French actress-filmmaker Emmanuelle Bercot, for the opening night at Cannes could be seen as "a way for the festival to respond to a difficult year in Europe and particularly in France."
7France has been grappling with questions of security and identity since the deadly attacks on the satirical magazine Charlie Hebdo and a kosher supermarket by three young men who were born and raised in France but motivated by radical Islam.
8Although Bercot's film has been in the works for five years, the director said there was a link between the Paris attacks and the movie, which depicts decent professionals - including police, court and prison staff - working to help a delinquent youth (Rod Paradot) get his life back on track.
9The three Paris attackers who were killed by police in January "were not protected, they were not educated," said Bercot.
10"So the film does provide an answer, in a sense. It shows what work can be done."
11"Standing Tall" received a lukewarm response from critics, but even that was an improvement over last year's glamorous but immediately panned "Grace of Monaco," a Grace Kelly drama starring Nicole Kidman.
12Bercot received a standing ovation at the opening ceremony.
13She is only the second woman director to open the Cannes Film Festival, which has sometimes been chided for a dearth of female filmmakers - a topic gaining increasing scrutiny in the film industry.
14"For the selection we pick films, artists - not men and women, young and old," Thierry Fremaux, festival director, said in an interview Tuesday.
15"But I don't want to say that it's not a problem. It is a problem. We need more women, more female directors, in world cinema."
16In the United States, the American Civil Liberties Union on Tuesday asked U.S. state and federal agencies to investigate the hiring practices of major Hollywood studios, networks and talent agencies, claiming widespread gender discrimination in the hiring of directors.
17Some of the biggest directing names at this year's Cannes are on the jury.
18Joel Coen, whose films have frequently premiered at Cannes, said the timing was perfect for him and his brother, who are waiting until February 2016 to release their next film, "Hail, Caesar!"
19But the dual presidents could mean unusual jury politics.
20"We've all been split into either the Ethan group or Joel group," joked Gyllenhaal.
21Making a particularly striking pair on the red carpet Wednesday was Millepied and his wife, Natalie Portman, who will later in the festival make her directorial debut with "A Tale of Love and Darkness," an Israeli drama.
22Thursday will bring George Miller's sequel "Mad Max: Fury Road" along with stars Tom Hardy and Charlize Theron.
23Woody Allen will premiere his latest, "Irrational Man," starring Emma Stone and Joaquin Phoenix.
24And the Pixar animation film "Inside Out" will take a bow at Cannes' Palais des Festivals.
