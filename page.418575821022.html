Australia remembers MH17 victims

1Prime Minister Tony Abbott hopes a memorial ceremony for victims of the Malaysia Airlines MH17 plane wreck brings some comfort to the families left behind, one year on from the crash.
2"I know that nothing anyone can do can bring back their loved ones, nothing anyone can say can make it easier for them," Mr Abbott told ABC TV on Friday.
3Mr Abbott suspects Russian President Vladimir Putin is horrified by the event and does not believe the Russian leader knew anything in advance.
4"The point that I made to (Putin) when we spoke about this was that, as a parent and as a human being, he owed it to the families of the victims to do what he could to try to get to the bottom of this," he said.
5It would be a credit to Russia if there was full co-operation with the ongoing criminal investigation into the plane's downing, Mr Abbott said.
6Toowoomba man Paul Guard, who lost his parents Roger and Jill, is in Canberra for the ceremony along with nine other family members.
7"I think it will be a difficult day, but hopefully a useful part of the healing process," he told ABC TV.
8Mr Abbott will join friends and family of the 38 Australian citizens and residents who died to unveil a plaque at the memorial service at Parliament House on Friday.
9Former Australian Defence Force chief Angus Houston was charged with travelling to Ukraine to find Australian bodies and bring them home.
10"The risks were considerable," he told ABC TV.
11"There was a war going on around the crash site. There was artillery exchanges."
12He said the Canberra ceremony was a day to show solidarity for the families affected.
13"These families have, I think, suffered greatly because of the way their loved ones were lost. Essentially they were murdered," he said.
