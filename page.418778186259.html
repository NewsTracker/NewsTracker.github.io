14Greece is formally inviting the International Monetary Fund to join its negotiations with European creditors over a vital third bailout - talks that were delayed Friday by logistics but are expected to conclude before Greece faces another big repayment Aug. 20, Greek officials said.
15Negotiators are now expected to arrive in Athens over the weekend with talks probably starting Monday, the officials said.
16Athens is looking to secure yet another bailout - the third since its finances imploded in 2009 - worth 85 billion euros ($93 billion) over three years.
17Without the money, the country faces imminent bankruptcy and a probable exit from the shared euro currency.
18Greek government spokeswoman Olga Gerovasili said the final bailout agreement will be brought to Greece's parliament for approval on Aug. 18.
19"Clearly, the negotiations will be constant until then," she said Friday.
20Gerovasili initially said the IMF invitation had been sent, but other government financial officials subsequently said it would be sent later Friday.
21Greece has a debt repayment of around 3.2 billion euros ($3.5 billion) to the European Central Bank on Aug. 20.
22Greece's five-year financial crisis took a dramatic turn for the worse this summer, after talks between its radical left-led government elected in January and the country's creditors nearly collapsed amid sharp disagreements over the reforms required in return for the rescue loans.
23The new bailout was only possible after Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras made a sharp U-turn from years of vehemently opposing further cutbacks.
24Since its first bailout in 2010, Greece's economy has shrunk by a quarter, while unemployment has rocketed to record peacetime highs and incomes have shrunk an average of 40 percent.
25Much of the pain has been attributed to the income cuts and tax hikes demanded by creditors.
26It's unclear to what extent IMF participation - if confirmed - would complicate the latest bailout talks.
27The fund has been critical of many of the demands insisted upon by Greece's European creditors but it also says Greece needs deep, meaningful debt relief - something that Germany has ruled out.
28Gerovasili said the IMF position on Greece's crippling debt load - the highest in the 19-country eurozone - "is clearly something we agree with."
29But she added the fund "is tougher in negotiations, with harsher terms, and is not very agreeable to us as a negotiator."
30Gerovasili dismissed reports that security concerns delayed the talks Friday, blaming "technical issues" instead.
31"Greece is a safe country," Gerovasili said.
32"Both sides are trying to expedite the start of talks."
33The final hurdle Greece had to clear before talks could restart came early Thursday when Greek lawmakers approved creditor-demanded judicial and banking reforms.
34A week earlier, parliament approved new laws introducing steep sales tax increases.
35In spite of a revolt from his own Syriza party, Tsipras managed to get both reforms passed with the help of pro-European opposition parties.
36Top Greek banking and finance ministry officials met with Greek business leaders Friday to discuss ways of easing financial transactions, which are being restricted by capital controls that were introduced last month.
37Banks reopened Monday after being closed for more than three weeks, albeit for limited transactions.
38Daily withdrawals at ATMs are still limited to 60 euros ($65) per account holder, and the Athens Stock Exchange has been closed indefinitely.
39"With time, the situation is returning to normal. We aim to have things return to the way they were before," Deputy Finance Minister Dimitris Mardas said after the meeting.
40Small Business Association Chairman Giorgos Kavvathas said the daily limit for businesses to transfer money abroad has been raised to 100,000 euros ($155,080) but Greek authorities are vetting those money transfers.
41Kavvathas said check transactions are still posing a problem for businesses.
42Mardas said authorities are looking at ways of dealing with that and a decision will be announced soon.
