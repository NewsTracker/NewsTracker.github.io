Wall St. Is Higher Ahead of Apple Earnings

1United States markets were moderately higher on Monday as investors looked ahead to a busy week for earnings news and a meeting by the Federal Reserve on interest rates.
2The slight gains follow big ones last week.
3The Standard & Poor's 500-stock index rose to yet another record, and the Nasdaq finally climbed back from its losses in dot-com crash to hit its first high in 15 years.
4KEEPING SCORE Near midday, the Dow Jones industrial average rose 0.29 percent and the S.&P.
5500 was up 0.17 percent.
6The Nasdaq composite index gained 0.15 percent.
7EYE ON EARNINGS More than 150 companies in the S.&P.
8500 are expected to report quarterly results this week, including Ford, Visa, Pfizer and Exxon Mobil.
9Investors are anxious because falling oil prices and a strengthening dollar have hammered first-quarter results at some companies.
10Per-share earnings for the S.&P.
11500 are expected to fall 1 percent from a year earlier, according to S&P Capital IQ, a provider of financial data.
12That would be the first drop since 2009.
13EYE ON APPLE Apple stock rose 2 percent as investors waited for quarterly results due out after the markets close.
14The world's No.
151 company by market value is being closely watched for signs of how its new Apple Watches are doing.
16Apple has soared 63 percent in the past 12 months, and is the biggest component in the S.&P.
17500 and Nasdaq indexes.
18DEAL DROP Mylan dropped 3 percent after the generic drugmaker rejected a $40 billion buyout offer from Teva Pharmaceuticals, a cash-and-stock deal that Mylan says undervalues it.
19SEMICONDUCTOR SLUMP Applied Materials fell 7 percent after calling off its $9.4 billion acquisition of Tokyo Electron.
20The two big semiconductor industry suppliers said that they were told by the United States Justice Department that there were antitrust concerns.
21U.S. ECONOMY Investors were preparing for Wednesday's meeting of the Fed board amid concern that data this week will show that the American economy weakened in the first quarter.
22The Fed has no plans to update forecasts.
23Still, the meeting "will be closely watched to see if markets had been too eager to push out Fed hike expectations, especially now that oil prices have pushed above $55 a barrel," DBS said in a report.
24EUROPEAN MARKETS In Germany, the DAX rose 1.2 percent and in France, the CAC 40 was up 1.7 percent.
25The FTSE 100 was up 0.6 percent in Britain.
26JAPAN DOWNGRADE The Fitch ratings agency said Monday that it had cut its credit grade for Japan to A from A+ citing a lack of government reforms to the budget, weak growth, and high debt.
27The Japanese government has increased spending to stimulate growth but analysts say the plan has not been followed up sufficiently well with reforms to control high public debt.
28The rating announcement came after Tokyo's market closed.
29ASIA'S DAY The Nikkei 225 declined 0.2 percent in Japan, while in Hong Kong, the Hang Seng gained 1.3 percent.
30The Shanghai Composite Index added 3 percent in China.
31The Kospi declined 0.1 percent in South Korea.
32In Australia, the S.&P./ASX 200 added 0.8 percent, while the Sensex in India was off 0.6 percent.
33GREECE Investors took in their stride a lack of progress in Greece's bailout talks with European creditors.
34Greece's Yanis Varoufakis was rebuked Friday for failing to come up with a list of reforms the creditors want before they release new loans.
35The eurozone's top official, Jeroen Dijsselbloem, said he hoped "some extra urgency" will be injected into the process.
36Greece is forecast to have enough money for its government to pay its bills for another few weeks.
37ENERGY Benchmark United States crude fell 12 cents to $57.05 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
38The contract shed 49 cents to close at $57.15 in the previous session.
39CURRENCIES AND BONDS The dollar rose to 119.32 yen from Friday's 118.95.
40The euro declined to $1.0833 from the previous session's $1.0870.
41Bond prices fell, pushing up the yield on the 10-year Treasury to 1.93 percent.
