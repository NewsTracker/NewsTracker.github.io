Huge explosion hits Sinai police station; 1 killed

1The explosion comes hours after a roadside bomb killed six soldiers traveling south of el-Arish in an armored vehicle.
2The region has been hit by an Islamic insurgency by a group that recently pledged allegiance to the Islamic State.
3EL-ARISH, Egypt (AP) - A suspected car bomb exploded at the entrance of a main police station in the capital of Egypt's northern Sinai province, killing at least one police officer and injuring several civilians, Egyptian officials said.
4The officials say the explosion badly damaged the 3-story building.
5Civil defense forces were looking through the rubbles for casualties, the officials said.
6At least 15 civilians who were in the vicinity of the blast have been hospitalized, said the officials, who spoke on condition of anonymity because they were not authorized to brief reporters.
7Residents say the explosion outside one of four main police stations in el-Arish shook the surrounding neighborhood.
8One woman said her house walls cracked.
9Cars outside the station were on fire.
10At approximately the same time, a military checkpoint in the town of Rafah, south of el-Arish, came under attack from militants, who fled the scene.
11There was no immediate word on casualties.
12Sunday is eastern orthodox Easter in Egypt, and police have been on high alert against attacks.
13Sunday's police station bombing comes hours after a roadside bomb killed six soldiers, including an officer, traveling south of el-Arish in an armored vehicle.
14The attacks took place as the country's defense minister carried out a limited military reshuffle, replacing the commander of the army division responsible for securing northern Sinai.
15Maj. Gen. Mohammed el-Shahat, who only commanded Egypt's second field army for about a year, was promoted to head of military intelligence; el-Shahat's deputy, Maj.
16Gen. Nasser el-Assi, will replace him.
17El-Assi inherits the simmering Sinai-based Islamic insurgency that continues to target army soldiers and police officers despite an intensive military campaign.
18The attacks surged following the 2013 military ouster of Islamist President Mohammed Morsi.
19The largest Sinai-based militant group, Ansar Beit al-Maqdis, pledged allegiance to the Islamic State group last year and now refers to itself as the group's Sinai Province.
20The group claimed responsibility on Twitter for Sunday's roadside bombing, but no one had yet claimed responsibility for the police station attack.
21Earlier this month, the IS affiliate in Sinai killed at least 16 soldiers and three civilians and kidnapped a conscript.
22On Friday they posted a video showing the kidnapped soldier pleading with Egyptians not to join the army before being shot to death.
23The reshuffle in military leadership also included the commander of the Egyptian navy at a time when the navy could begin playing an increasingly prominent role, because of the Yemeni crisis.
24Egyptian warships are already deployed off the coast of Yemen to secure the strategically vital Bab el-Mandab strait - the gateway to the Suez Canal.
25Egypt is currently a main member of the Saudi-led military coalition launching airstrikes against Shiite rebels who have conquered the Yemeni capital, Sanaa, and forced out the Western-backed president.
26Egyptian military leaders have repeatedly stated their willingness to commit ground troops, if necessary, to the operation.
