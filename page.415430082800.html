Small Pacific Nation of Vanuatu hit by powerful cyclone PAM

1The U.N. Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs said in a release there were unconfirmed reports of deaths in Vanuatu's northeastern islands after Cyclone Pam moved off its expected track.
2The Category 5 cyclone had average wind speeds of 250 kph to 270 kph, with gusts up to 340 kph, according to the office.
3It said the periphery of the eye of the storm had passed over islands that are home to several thousand people and was expected to hit or come close to the island of Efate, home to the capital, Port Vila.
4Located about a quarter of the way from Australia to Hawaii, Vanuatu has a population of 267,000 spread over 65 islands.
5About 47,000 people live in the capital.
6Vanuatu had earlier issued a red alert to its residents, urging them to take shelter from Pam.
7Authorities said they feared the cyclone would destroy homes as well as cause landslides and severe coastal flooding.
8The cyclone has already destroyed some homes and caused damage to other Pacific islands including Kiribati and the Solomon Islands.
9David Gibson, acting director of the Vanuatu meteorology and geo-hazards department, said the winds could cause severe damage to the nation's buildings.
10Alice Clements, a spokeswoman for relief agency UNICEF who is in Port Vila, said earlier on Friday the capital was like a ghost town as people took shelter.
11She said the pelting rain was blown horizontally by the wind.
12She said many people in Vanuatu live in flimsy shelters, especially those on the more remote outer islands.
13She said those people are extremely vulnerable.
14She said she expects a huge cleanup and urgent medical needs after the storm passes.
15Clements also said large seas had caused significant flooding in remote and low-lying Kiribati, local media reported several homes and a school classroom were destroyed and crops were flooded.
