Australian nurse says he was forced to work for Islamic State

1In this undated photo released on June 22, 2015, by a militant website, which has been verified and is consistent with other AP reporting, two of Islamic State militants stand on the wreckage of one of two mausoleums after blowing it up, in the historic central town of Palmyra, Syria.
2SYDNEY, Australia -- An Australian nurse who says he was forced by Islamic State militants to work as a medic in Syria will appear in a Sydney court on Saturday on terrorism-related charges, police said.
3Adam Brookman, 39, was arrested on arrival at Sydney International Airport on Friday night on a Victoria state warrant relating to his alleged involvement in the conflict in Syria, Australian Federal Police said in a statement.
4He will appear in the Parramatta Local Court on Saturday, where members of the Melbourne city Joint Counter Terrorism Team will seek his extradition to Victoria.
5Police did not detail his charges.
6Brookman surrendered to Turkish officials in Turkey on Tuesday.
7He voluntarily flew back to Australia with a police escort.
8Brookman, a Muslim convert and father of five children who live in Melbourne, told Fairfax Media in May that he went to Syria last year to do humanitarian work for civilians caught in the war.
9He said he was innocent of any crime.
10Brookman said he was forced to join Islamic State militants after being injured in an airstrike and taken to a hospital controlled by the group.
11"After I recovered, they wouldn't let me leave," he told Fairfax.
12He won the militants' trust by working as a medic and was able to escape to Turkey in December.
13Brookman told Fairfax that he opposed the violent and extreme actions of the militants, including the beheading of their captives.
14"Of course there will be an investigation. That is fine. Hopefully things don't look that bad," Brookman told Fairfax.
15It is not clear whether Brookman was still in Syria on Dec. 4, when Australia made a presence in the Islamic State stronghold of al-Raqqa province in Syria a crime punishable by 10 years in prison.
16If charged, the onus would be on Brookman to prove he had a legitimate reason to be in the terrorist hotspot.
