21 dead, dozens missing as landslide buries Nepal villages

1KATHMANDU, Nepal (AP) - A landslide triggered by heavy rainfall buried at least 21 people and left dozens missing as they were sleeping at night in six villages in Nepal's mountainous northeast, officials said Thursday.
2Rescuers dug out the bodies and searched the rubble for survivors in Taplejung district, about 500 kilometers (310 miles) east of the capital, Kathmandu, said police official Shanti Raj.
3At least 24 people are missing but the number could be higher, he said.
4The weather improved, allowing a rescue helicopter to reach the area and evacuate eight injured people.
5The nearest town is at least five hours' away on foot when the weather is good, but getting to the site was taking much longer for rescuers on Thursday because of rains and fog, said Home Ministry official Laxmi Dhakal in Kathmandu.
6There are no government offices or police stations in the area.
7Landslides are common in mountainous Nepal during the rainy monsoon season, which began in June and ends in September.
8The Himalayan nation is still recovering from earthquakes in April and May that killed more than 8,700 people and caused massive damage, with many of the roads cut off by landslides.
9Meanwhile, the government announced Thursday it would hire international experts to study trekking routes in the mountains of Nepal to see if they are safe for hikers to return.
10Nineteen people were killed and scores injured in an avalanche at Mount Everest base camp triggered by the April 25 quake.
11Also, the trails around the Langtang valley in northern Nepal were completely damaged and an entire village buried by a landslide and avalanche set off by the earthquake.
12Tens of thousands of foreigners come to Nepal every year to trek on the foothills of the Himalayan peaks.
13The next trekking season starts in September.
