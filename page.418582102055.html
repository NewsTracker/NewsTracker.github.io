Timeline of events leading to guilty verdicts in Colorado theater shooting case

1Dec. 13, 1987 - Holmes is born in San Diego County, California, to Robert and Arlene Holmes.
22006 - Graduates from Westview High School in San Diego.
32010 - Graduates from the University of California, Riverside, with a B.S. in neuroscience.
42011 - Holmes enrolls in the Ph.D. neuroscience program at the University of Colorado's Anschutz Medical Campus in the Denver suburb of Aurora.
5October 2011 - Begins dating Gargi Datta, another first-year student in the program.
6February 2012 - Holmes and Gargi Datta break off their relationship.
7March 21, 2012 - Begins seeing psychiatrist Lynne Fenton of the University of Colorado and tells her he is having homicidal thoughts.
8Fenton later tells a campus police officer about Holmes' homicidal statements and that he sent her a threatening email.
9May 10, 2012 - Orders tear gas canisters online.
10Over the next few weeks, he also buys two handguns, a shotgun, an assault rifle, 6,295 rounds of ammunition and body armor, in person or online.
11June 10, 2012 - Files paperwork to withdraw from the neuroscience program but doesn't say why.
12June 11, 2012 - Meets with psychiatrist Lynne Fenton for the last time.
13July 20, 2012 - Holmes slips through a back door of the Century 16 theater in Aurora, where about 420 people are watching a midnight showing of a Batman movie, "The Dark Knight Rises," and opens fire.
14Ten people die at the scene, two die at hospitals, and 70 are wounded.
15Officers find Holmes leaning against his car behind the theater and arrest him without a struggle.
16Later, police find booby-trapped bombs and a Batman mask at Holmes' apartment.
17The bombs are defused.
18July 23, 2012 - Holmes makes his first court appearance, his hair dyed orange-red, face unshaven, eyes appearing dazed.
19Oct. 11, 2012 - Prosecutors finalize the charges against Holmes: 24 counts of murder and 140 counts of attempted murder, two counts for each person killed and two for each person injured.
20He's also charged with possession of explosives.
21Nov. 15, 2012 - Holmes is taken to a hospital psychiatric ward and held for several days, sometimes in restraints.
22Jan. 10, 2013 - The judge rules the evidence is sufficient to put Holmes on trial.
23March 27, 2013 - Holmes' attorneys say he's willing to plead guilty in exchange for avoiding the death penalty.
24Prosecutors later reject the plea offer, saying it isn't genuine and that Holmes is sane.
25April 1, 2013 - Prosecutors say they'll seek the death penalty.
26May 13, 2013 - Defense lawyers tell the judge Holmes wants to plead not guilty by reason of insanity.
27June 4, 2013 - The judge accepts Holmes' insanity plea and orders Holmes to undergo a mental evaluation.
28July 10, 2013 - Holmes' lawyers acknowledge he was the shooter but say he was "in the throes of a psychotic episode."
29Jan. 20, 2015 - Jury selection in Holmes' trial begins.
30April 14, 2015 - Twelve jurors and 12 alternates are chosen to hear the case.
31April 27, 2015 - Prosecutors and defense lawyers make their opening statements.
32June 19, 2015 - Prosecution rests after two months of heart-wrenching testimony from theater shooting survivors and accounts from psychiatrists who interviewed Holmes.
33June 25, 2015 - Holmes' defense team opens its case, starting with jail video showing Holmes falling backward off his bunk, followed by a psychiatrist who determined Holmes was legally insane at the time of the shootings.
34July 15, 2015 - Jurors begin deliberating.
35July 16, 2015 - Jurors find Holmes guilty on 165 counts of murder, attempted murder and weapons charges.
