Russia is sending more tanks and troops into Ukraine to take the next battlefront, Kiev says

1Only about 1000 of the town's pre-war population of 27,000 remained, most of them elderly people too infirm or too stubborn to leave.
2Out for the first time after a week of shelling and street fighting, they moved about in groups, with blackened faces and gritty clothes, busily scavenging for food in the ruins.
3"The last week was just horrible," said Vasily Yegelsky, 77, a retiree who was limping along in the morning sunshine, headed to the outskirts of town.
4Out there, where Ukrainian soldiers were dug in for months before retreating in haste on Wednesday in the face of an assault by Russian-backed separatists, Mr Yegelsky and others wandered through abandoned trenches, looking for something to eat.
5Bloody shreds of mattress hung from tree limbs, and stray dogs skulked nearby.
6But also strewn about were canned foods and much-prized bags of macaroni.
7As they picked through the detritus of war, they talked darkly of bodies lying uncollected in the streets through the fighting, of the sick and wounded stranded in basements.
8As yet there is no reliable estimate of the civilian casualties; European monitors with the Organisation for Security and Co-operation in Europe were not due to arrive until Saturday local time.
9But judging by the scenes of destruction, it is likely to add significantly to a wartime toll that the United Nations estimates at more than 5600 soldiers and civilians since combat began in April.
10Debaltseve was under Ukrainian control when Chancellor Angela Merkel of Germany and President Francois Hollande of France brokered a ceasefire between Ukraine and the rebels in Minsk, Belarus, on February 12.
11On Friday, at a news conference in Paris, the beleaguered leaders insisted that the terms of the ceasefire be honoured.
12"The ceasefire has been violated several times," Mr Hollande said.
13"It must now be fully respected along the entire front line."
14But according to Ukraine, it is serving purely as diplomatic cover while the rebels, and Russia, pursue their war aims.
15"In recent days, despite the Minsk agreement, military equipment and ammunition have been sighted crossing from Russia into Ukraine," said the Ukrainian military's spokesman, Andriy Lysenko.
16He said more than 20 Russian tanks, 10 missile systems and busloads of troops had crossed the frontier.
17Russia did not immediately respond to the accusation.
18Moscow has always denied accusations in the past that its forces are fighting in Ukraine.
19Kiev's biggest worry is that rebels will continue their advance to threaten Mariupol, a highly strategic port of 500,000 people that is the biggest city still under government control in the two rebellious eastern provinces.
20Novoazovsk, where Kiev said Russia was reinforcing, lies 40 kilometres to the east along the coast near the Russian border.
21The United States, which is considering tightening sanctions against Russia and arming Kiev, also said it had sighted Russian reinforcements.
22The US State Department said Russian support for the rebels was undermining international diplomacy and would bring "costs" on Moscow.
