Turkey says training, arming of Syrian rebels delayed

1ANKARA, Turkey (AP) - Turkey's foreign minister says the start of a joint Turkish-U.S. program to train and arm Syrian rebels fighting the Islamic State group has been delayed.
2Mevlut Cavusoglu said in an interview on Turkish state television Monday that the program has been pushed back due to technical reasons and not because of any disagreement with the United States.
3He did not say when the program would start.
4Turkey and the U.S. reached a deal on training and arming the rebels in February after several months of negotiations.
5The U.S. Defense Department has said it is aiming to train 5,000 Syrians a year for three years in Turkey, Saudi Arabia and Qatar.
