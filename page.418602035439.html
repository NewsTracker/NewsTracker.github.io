Mexico City cabbies slam regulations on ride-sharing apps

1Representatives of Mexico City's regular cab drivers are criticizing new regulations published this week on ride-sharing apps such as Uber.
2Pedro Soria of the medallion drivers' group in the capital said at a news conference Friday that the new rules "are totally in favor" of the apps and will create "a playing field that's anything but level."
3Under the rules, 1.5 percent of each fare from ride-sharing cabs will go to a "Taxi, Mobility and Pedestrian Fund."
4Much of that money may go to regular cabs, to improve their service.
5The number of ride-sharing cars won't be limited, but drivers won't be allowed to accept cash payments and must keep their vehicles at home.
