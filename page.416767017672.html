Microsoft is reportedly evaluating possible bid for Salesforce.com Inc

1Microsoft Corp. is evaluating a bid for Salesforce.com Inc., after the cloud software provider was approached by another would-be buyer, people with knowledge of the matter said.
2Salesforce, which has a market value of $47 billion, is working with two investment banks to determine a response to approaches, two of the people said.
3The San Francisco-based company's options could include rebuffing any buyer, or working out a sale, people with knowledge of the matter told Bloomberg last week.
4Microsoft isn't in talks with Salesforce, and no deal is imminent, the people said.
5Still, Redmond, Washington-based Microsoft has long expected it might compete for Salesforce if it was for sale, one of the people said.
6Another company was in talks with Salesforce as recently as April, spurring Microsoft's actions, two people said.
7For any buyer, Salesforce offers a leading position in customer relationship management, or CRM, software, as well as cloud computing - the delivery of business software and services via the Internet.
8Microsoft and Salesforce reached an agreement last year to make the companies' software work better together.
9Microsoft, which sells its own customer-management software, lags behind Salesforce.
10Last week Microsoft set a goal of reaching a $20 billion revenue run rate for its commercial cloud business by the fiscal year ending June 30, 2018.
11Tony Imperati, a spokesman for Microsoft, and Chi Hea Cho, a spokeswoman for Salesforce, declined to comment.
12Oracle Corp. Chief Executive Officer Safra Catz said last week an acquisition of Salesforce would create disruption in the software market.
13She declined to comment on whether Oracle was interested in buying Salesforce.
14Salesforce was involved in strategic-alliance discussions with SAP SE last year, people with knowledge of the matter said last week.
15SAP said it's not considering a bid for Salesforce.
