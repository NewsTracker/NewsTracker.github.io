Yankees extend reign as most valuable, Blue Jays 22nd -- Forbes

1The 27-time World Series champion Yankees were valued at $3.2 billion, a 28% jump from last year and comfortably above the second-ranked Los Angeles Dodgers, whose value rose 20% to $2.4 billion.
2The average MLB team is now worth $1.2 billion, a 48% spike from last year, which Forbes attributed to richer national broadcasting deals and higher overall profitability.
3The Yankees, who last year missed the playoffs for only the third time since 1994 yet saw their revenue rise 10% to an MLB-high $508 million, have topped the list since Forbes began tracking MLB team valuations in 1998.
4The Boston Red Sox ($2.1 billion), World Series champion San Francisco Giants ($2 billion) and Chicago Cubs ($1.8 billion) rounded out the top five.
5The Toronto Blue Jays, who have the longest playoff drought at 21 seasons, were valued at $870 million in 22nd spot.
6The two Florida franchises, Miami Marlins ($650 million) and Tampa Bay Rays ($625 million), were at the bottom of the list in 29th and 30th respectively.
