Ghani Praises British Troops' Sacrifice For Afghanistan

American who contracted Ebola arrives at Maryland hospital

1Ghani said in Kabul on March 13 that the 453 British troops who were killed in Afghanistan made the "ultimate sacrifice to enable us to live in freedom, in hope for peace, prosperity, and dignity."
2Ghani's comments came ahead of ceremonies to be held in Kabul and London to commemorate the end of British involvement in the war in Afghanistan.
3Ghani also praised Britain's long-term commitment to a British-run officer training academy outside of Kabul.
4His comments contrast with his predecessor, Hamid Karzai, who toward the end of his term in office complained that the presence of British forces in Helmand Province had worsened the situation there.
5Briish forces were part of a U.S.-led coalition that invaded Afghanistan in 2001.
6The last British combat forces left in October ahead of the end of the NATO-led military campaign in December.
1(AP) - An American health care worker who contracted Ebola while volunteering in a Sierra Leone treatment unit is in serious condition after arriving at the National Institutes of Health's hospital near Washington, officials said Friday.
2Meanwhile, the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention said it was checking on people in Sierra Leone, including other Americans, who had contact with the health care worker and may have been exposed to the virus.
3One of those people was traveling Friday on a chartered airplane to the Atlanta area to be near Emory University Hospital, where several patients have been treated for the disease, according to a CDC statement.
4That person has shown no symptoms, is not diagnosed with Ebola and is expected to "voluntarily self-isolate" for a 21-day incubation period.
5The NIH patient was flown in isolation from Sierra Leone on a chartered plane and admitted at 4:44 a.m., NIH officials said in a statement.
6The patient's name, age and gender were not released.
7The person is a clinician working with Partners in Health, a Boston-based nonprofit organization, according to a statement on the group's website.
8Partners in Health declined to release more information, citing a need for privacy for the patient and his or her relatives.
9The group has been treating patients in Liberia and Sierra Leone since November.
10The patient is the 11th person with Ebola to be treated in the U.S. and the second admitted to the NIH Clinical Center.
11Like Emory, NIH has one of the few specialized isolation units nationwide set up to treat Ebola patients.
12Previously, an American nurse was treated there after she contracted Ebola while caring for a patient in a Dallas hospital.
13The nurse, Nina Pham, survived and is Ebola-free.
14None of the volunteers in Sierra Leone who had contact with the NIH patient have tested positive for Ebola.
15The CDC and the U.S. State Department have developed contingency plans for flying home other Americans in that group, who would also be expected to isolate themselves for 21 days.
16The World Health Organization estimated Thursday that the virus has killed more than 10,000 people, mostly in the West African nations of Guinea, Liberia and Sierra Leone.
17The current outbreak is the largest ever for the disease.
18While deaths have slowed dramatically in recent months, the virus appears stubbornly entrenched in parts of Guinea and Sierra Leone.
