Former Baltimore cop plans blackface performance to raise money for indicted officers

1ANNAPOLIS, Md.
2- A former Baltimore police officer said Wednesday that he plans to perform an Al Jolson routine in blackface to raise money for the six Baltimore officers who have been indicted in the death of Freddie Gray.
3Bobby Berger, whose performances as Jolson created tension with the department in the 1980s, said Wednesday that 610 tickets have been sold in eight days at $45 each for the Nov. 1 fundraiser in Glen Burnie.
4But the venue where Berger intended to hold the event, Michael's Eighth Avenue in Glen Burnie, posted a notice on its website that the fundraiser will not be hosted there.
5"No contract was signed with Mr. Berger," the notice said.
6"Michael's does not condone blackface performances of any kind."
7Berger said in an interview he doesn't believe there is anything racist about his routine.
8"It's coincidence," Berger said about the fact that the entertainer he impersonates wore blackface.
9"There's no racial overtones to this show. There's nothing racial to the show."
10Michael Davey, an attorney for the Baltimore City Fraternal Order of Police, said officers do not support the fundraiser.
11Gray was black.
12He died of injuries received in police custody.
13Davey said no money would be accepted from the fundraiser.
14"They've been put in a pretty bad position without their knowledge," Davey said of the police officers.
15Tessa Hill-Aston, president of the Baltimore branch of the NAACP, says the idea is "very distasteful."
16"This is showing no sensitivity to the family of Freddie Gray," Hill-Aston said.
17Gray's death prompted a wave of arson, looting and open confrontations with riot police in Baltimore in April.
18Berger, who is 67, has performed the blackface signing act for decades.
19He was fired from the police force in the 1980s for performing the act in his spare time.
20He was reinstated to his job following an appeal, but later retired.
21His performance at a retirement dinner for a white Baltimore County officer in 1996 prompted a black officers group to protest outside the dinner.
22Berger said in an interview Wednesday that he only intended to help the officers who were indicted in the Gray case.
23"I want to get these people some money," Berger said.
24"I know they need it, and that's the long and short of it."
