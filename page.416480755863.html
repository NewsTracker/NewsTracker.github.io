Stocks mostly up after Nasdaq record, despite no Greece deal

1Market data is displayed on the screens at the Nasdaq MarketSite in New York, Thursday, April 23, 2015.
2The Nasdaq composite has closed at a record high for the first time since the dot-com bubble of 2000.
3BEIJING (AP) - Stocks turned higher in European trading on Friday, despite a lack of progress on Greece's bailout, as investors cheered the Nasdaq's record close.
4Chinese and Japanese shares fell on weaker global manufacturing data.
5KEEPING SCORE: France's CAC-40 rose 0.5 percent to 5,204.63 and Germany's DAX gained 0.6 percent to 11,793.67.
6Britain's FTSE 100 added 0.3 percent to 7,078.10.
7On Wall Street, futures for the S&P 500 and Dow were both 0.1 percent higher.
8GREECE: A meeting between Greece and its eurozone creditors on Friday ended without a deal, but only the warning from a top official that "significant" more progress is needed in the talks.
9Hopes were low going into the gathering and pressure is growing on Greece, whose finance minister faced "critical" remarks from his eurozone colleagues, according to Jeroen Dijsselbloem, the head of the eurozone finance meetings.
10The lack of a deal keeps uncertainty over Greece, which is forecast to have enough money only for another few weeks.
11NASDAQ RECORD: Investor sentiment was buoyed after the Nasdaq rose on Thursday to 5,056.06, above the record of 5,048.62 it set on March 10, 2000, at the height of the dot-com bubble.
12The Nasdaq today is dominated by Apple Inc., which makes up 9.7 percent of the index, instead of shooting stars such as Pets.com, Geocities or WebVan that flared and died in the dot-com bust.
13MANUFACTURING WEAKNESS: A survey by HSBC Corp. found China's manufacturing activity weakened this month to its lowest level in a year in a new sign of economic weakness.
14A survey of Eurozone manufacturers by Markit Economics found activity slowed from March's four-year high.
15A similar survey in Japan showed a third straight monthly deceleration, suggesting industries are not fully in recovery from a recession brought on by a sales tax hike in April 2014.
16ASIA'S DAY: Tokyo's Nikkei 225 fell 0.8 percent to 20,020.04 and the Shanghai Composite Index lost 0.5 percent to 4,393.69.
17Seoul's Kospi shed 0.6 percent to 2,159.80.
18Sydney's S&P/ASX 200 rose 1.5 percent to 5,933.30 and Hong Kong's Hang Seng gained 0.8 percent to 28,060.98.
19Singapore, Jakarta and New Zealand also advanced.
20ENERGY: Benchmark U.S. crude was down 10 cents to $57.64 per barrel in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
21The contract rose $1.48 on Thursday to close at $57.74.
22CURRENCIES: The dollar declined to 119.55 yen from Thursday's 119.62 yen.
23The euro rose to $1.0825 from $1.0817.
