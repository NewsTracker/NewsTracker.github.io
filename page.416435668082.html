UEFA, FIFA warn Greece over government's planned new laws

1NYON, Switzerland (AP) " UEFA and FIFA say they have sent a final warning to Greece that it faces suspension from international football for government interference.
2The Greek government wants to pass laws punishing violence at sports events, including imposing fines and threatening to cancel matches.
3Football's governing bodies have strict rules protecting their member federations' independence to run their own affairs.
4UEFA general secretary Gianni Infantino says a joint letter with FIFA was sent to the Greek government on Wednesday.
5Infantino hopes the threat "will make them reconsider their actions, so we can still work together to resolve the issues affecting Greek football."
6Teams and officials from suspended countries cannot take part in international matches and meetings.
7In 2006, FIFA briefly suspended Greece when European champion for government interference.
