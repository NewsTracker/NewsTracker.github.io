Greece: Extreme right leader released from jail

1Leader of Greece's extreme right Golden Dawn party Nikos Michaloliakos waves his hand to supporters as he leaves from a prison near Athens on Friday, March 20, 2015.
2Panel of judges on Friday ordered the release of lawmaker Michaloliakos after he served the 18-month maximum in pre-trial detention allowed under Greek law.
3He is charged with participation in a criminal organization as his party came third in Jan. 25 general elections with 6.3 percent of the vote.
4ATHENS, Greece (AP) - The leader of Greece's Nazi-inspired Golden Dawn party walked free from a prison in Athens Friday after he served the 18-month maximum in pre-trial detention allowed under Greek law.
5The 57-year-old Nikos Michaloliakos is charged with participation in a criminal organization after a judicial inquiry investigated links between the party and violent attacks against immigrants and left-wing activists.
6The trial starts on April 20.
7Despite the probe, Golden Dawn came third in the Jan. 25 general elections with 6.3 percent of the vote, amid popular anger against politicians following a six-year recession.
8Before going on trial, Michaloliakos will be banned from leaving the country and remain under house arrest but will be allowed to attend parliament sessions.
9A judicial panel also ordered the release of Golden Dawn parliament member Yiannis Lagos, who was arrested on similar charges and walked free Friday.
10Golden Dawn is planning an Athens rally against illegal immigration on Saturday.
