Australia unveils memorial for victims of Malaysian plane shot down over Ukraine 1 year ago

1SYDNEY - Australia is holding a memorial service to mark the anniversary of the Malaysian jetliner that was shot down over eastern Ukraine.
2There were 38 Australian citizens and permanent residents among the 298 people on Malaysia Airlines Flight 17.
3Everyone on board was killed.
4Australian Prime Minister Tony Abbott on Friday unveiled a permanent memorial to the victims in the nation's capital, Canberra.
5The memorial features a plaque inscribed with the names of the Australians on board.
6Family members of several of the victims attended the ceremony.
7Many cried as they placed flowers at the base of the plaque.
8The plane was heading from Amsterdam to Kuala Lumpur when it was shot down over war-ravaged eastern Ukraine.
9Ukraine has blamed Russian-backed separatist rebels, while Moscow blames Ukraine.
