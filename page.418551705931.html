AP source: Chiefs, Houston reach 6-year, $101 million deal (Yahoo Sports)

1KANSAS CITY, Mo.
2(AP) -- The Chiefs and linebacker Justin Houston agreed on a six-year, $101 million contract just hours before the Wednesday afternoon deadline to reach a long-term deal, a person familiar with the situation told the Associated Press.
3The person, speaking on condition of anonymity because the contract had not been announced, said Houston will receive $52.5 million in guarantees.
4That makes the All-Pro's contract not only the richest in Chiefs history but the richest for a linebacker in NFL history.
5The only defensive player to ever sign for more is defensive tackle Ndamukong Suh, who signed a six-year, $114 million pact with Miami earlier this year.
6The sides have been trying to work out a contract to keep Houston in Kansas City since last offseason, but they had made little progress.
7The Chiefs put the franchise tag on him, which would have been a one-year deal worth about $13.1 million.
8But negotiations picked up with the approaching deadline.
9Houston's representatives met with the Chiefs on Tuesday, and enough progress was made that they eventually came to terms.
10Houston was coming off the final year of his rookie contract, a breakout season in which he broke Derrick Thomas's franchise record with 22 sacks.
11He had four alone in a dynamic season-ending performance against San Diego, leaving him a half-sack short of the NFL's single-season record set by Michael Strahan of the New York Giants in 2001.
12Those gaudy totals sent Houston to his third consecutive Pro Bowl and earned him first-team All-Pro honors for the first time in his four-year career.
13Sent his asking price for a new contract soaring, too.
14Houston was considered one of the top linebackers in the draft coming out of Georgia, but a failed test for marijuana at the scouting combine caused his stock to slide.
15The Chiefs wound up taking him in the third round, in retrospect a massive bargain - or downright steal.
16He has only made roughly $3 million over the course of his rookie contract.
17For that small price, Kansas City got 200 tackles, 48 1/2 sacks and five fumble recoveries.
18Houston has also grown into one of the leaders in the locker room, helping keep things together when a rash of injuries struck last season.
19Despite losing linebacker Derrick Johnson and tackle Mike DeVito in the opener, the Chiefs still had one of the AFC's top defenses.
20Houston skipped the Chiefs' entire offseason program, including their mandatory minicamp, rather than signing his franchise tender.
21But all along, coach Andy Reid expressed optimism that the sides would reach an agreement before the start of the season.
22Reid also wasn't concerned about the missed workouts.
23''I think he's working out, absolutely,'' Reid said at the end of offseason work.
24Fellow linebacker Tamba Hali, who went through a similar negotiation with the Chiefs in 2011, said he had been in touch with Houston throughout the offseason.
