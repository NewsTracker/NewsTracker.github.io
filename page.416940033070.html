Bangladeshi blogger Ananta Bijoy Das, who promoted secularism, hacked to death by suspected Islamic extremists

1Bangladesh forensics police investigate the site where blogger Ananta Bijoy Das was hacked to death by a masked gang wielding machetes, in Sylhet.
2Dhaka, Bangladesh: A blogger who wrote for a website that promoted secularism was killed on Tuesday by a group of men wielding sharp weapons, a police official said.
3It was the third fatal attack on a Bangladeshi blogger since February.
4Four men chased the blogger, Ananta Bijoy Dash, through streets near his home in the northeastern city of Sylhet and attacked him, said Mohammad Rahamat Ullah, a police official in Sylhet.
5No arrests have been made, Ullah said.
6The assailants walked away after the attack, leaving Dash's body near a pond, Ullah said.
7Relatives of deceased Bangladeshi blogger Ananta Bijoy Das react after seeing his body in Sylhet.
8Imran Sarker, the head of an organisation of secular bloggers in Bangladesh, said Dash had written for Free Mind, a website that the Bangladeshi-American blogger Avijit Roy had moderated before being killed in February by machete-wielding assailants while leaving a book fair in Dhaka, the capital.
9Five weeks after Roy's death, another blogger, Oyasiqur Rhaman, was killed by three men with machetes in Dhaka.
10The deaths recalled the 2013 killing in Dhaka of the blogger Ahmed Rajib Haider.
11Haider, Roy and Rhaman were all part of a movement known as Shahbag, which called for the death penalty for Islamist political leaders who were implicated in atrocities committed during Bangladesh's 1971 war for independence from Pakistan.
12Young Islamic activists reacted with fury to the Shahbag movement, dividing young people on the question of whether Bangladesh should be a secular or Islamic state.
13Bangladeshi students and social activists participate in a protest against the killing of blogger and author Ananta Bijoy Das, in Dhaka, Bangladesh.
14Sarker said Dash was also an activist with the Shahbag movement, organising street protests in Sylhet.
15According to Free Mind, Dash had also edited a magazine called Logic several years ago that published essays on secular humanism.
16The leader of the Indian branch of al-Qaeda claimed responsibility for the deaths of Roy and Haider in a video posted on jihadi forums on May 2.
17The video was translated by the SITE Intelligence Group, which monitors extremist activity online.
18Police have arrested one person over Roy's murder: Shafiur Rahman Farabi, who had called for Roy to be killed in a Facebook post.
19Seven university students and the leader of a hard-line Islamist group were charged with Haider's killing in March.
20Bangladeshi social activists hold a banner displaying a portrait of blogger and author Ananta Bijoy Das during a protest against his killing, in Dhaka, Bangladesh.
21Sarker called the state's response to the string of attacks inadequate, noting that few arrests had been made in the recent cases.
22"This is frustrating, that one by one we are killed and the government is doing nothing," he said.
23The recent killings have drawn international condemnation.
24Jen Psaki, a spokeswoman for the US State Department, called Roy's killing "a cowardly assault on the universal principles enshrined in Bangladesh's Constitution."
