Greek 'day of reckoning' shakes stock markets

1NEW YORK (AP) - U.S. stocks are broadly lower in afternoon trading Friday following steep declines in Europe.
2Investors are worried that Greece may default on its debt and exit the eurozone.
3Shares of several big U.S. companies, including American Express, dropped after disappointing results.
4KEEPING SCORE: The Dow Jones industrial average slumped 323 points, or 1.8 percent, to 17,779 as of 3:01 p.m. Eastern time.
5The Standard & Poor's 500 index lost 29 points, or 1.4 percent, to 2,075.
6The Nasdaq fell 87 points, or 1.8 percent, to 4,920.
7All 10 industry groups in the Standard and Poor's 500 fell.
8GREEK WORRY: Greece and its creditors are still struggling to find a deal that can keep the country from defaulting on its debt.
9The argument is over what reforms Greece should make in return for loans.
10Many think Greece will struggle to make payments to the International Monetary Fund due next month if it fails to reach a deal.
11The concerns have caused investors to demand higher rates for loaning money to Greece's government.
12The yield on the country's benchmark 10-year bond jumped to 12.72 percent Friday.
13That rate has more than doubled from 5.51 percent in September and is the highest it's been since December 2012.
14THE QUOTE: "The day of reckoning" for Greece is fast approaching, said Uri Landesman, president of investment fund Platinum Partners.
15"People thought everyone would work it out, but if no one caves, there won't be a deal," he said.
16EUROPE SLUMPS: Germany's DAX index dropped 2.6 percent.
17France's CAC 40 shed 1.6 percent and Britain's FTSE 100 fell 0.9 percent.
18Investors piled into German government debt, which is perceived as being among the safest investments denominated in euros.
19The yield on the 10-year German government bond fell to 0.08 percent from 0.09 percent.
20Yields fall when bond prices rise.
21CHINA SHORTS: After markets closed in Asia, Chinese financial regulators issued warnings about that country's soaring stock market.
22Regulators said they will tighten rules on borrowing to buy stocks.
23They also plan to make it easier for investors to bet against the market there, The Wall Street Journal reported.
24Shanghai's stock market has more than doubled in the last year.
25"People are thinking maybe the party is over in China," said Doug Cote, chief market strategist for Voya Investment Management.
26He added, "China recognizes that it could be creating a bubble, and now it wants to slow down. It's trying to rein back risk."
27AMEX TROUBLE: American Express fell $3.67, or 5 percent, to $77.23 after its quarterly results took a blow from the sharp rise in the U.S. dollar.
28Amex's profits increased 6 percent, but revenue fell.
29The stronger dollar made the company's overseas earnings worth less after being converted back into U.S. currency.
30ANOTHER REVENUE MISS: Honeywell International fell $2.12, or 2 percent, to $101.80 after reporting disappointing first-quarter results.
31The industrial conglomerate posted earnings per share that beat estimates, but its revenue fell short.
32CHIP DIP: Advanced Micro Devices plunged 14 percent after reporting a larger loss than investors had expected.
33After the market close on Thursday, the chipmakers said that losses, adjusted for restructuring costs and stock option expense, came to 9 cents per share versus an expected loss of 6 cents per share.
34The stock fell 42 cents to $2.46.
35FUN AND GAMES: Mattel's stock jumped $1.47, or 6 percent, to $26.98.
36After the market closed Thursday, the toymaker reported a smaller quarterly loss than analysts had expected.
37Mattel's revenue also trumped estimates, helped by strong sales of Hot Wheels.
38BLOOMBERG DOWN: Bloomberg LP's trading terminals, which are used by most of the world's biggest financial firms, went down for a few hours on Friday due to apparent technical problems, prompting the British government to postpone a planned 3 billion-pound ($4.4 billion) debt issue.
39ASIA'S DAY: The Shanghai Composite index rose 2.2 percent after data showed that China's economy grew at the slowest pace since 2009 during the first quarter.
40The data stoked expectations that the country would introduce further stimulus measures to achieve its annual growth target.
41The market closed before news about the tighter borrowing rules came out.
42South Korea's Kospi rose 0.2 percent.
43Hong Kong's Hang Seng shed 0.3 percent.
44Japan's Nikkei 225 fell 1.2 percent, while Australia's S&P ASX 200 was down 1.2 percent.
45ENERGY: U.S. benchmark crude oil fell 97 cents to close at $55.74 a barrel in New York.
46The contract rose 40 cents to settle at $64.01 on Thursday, its highest price this year.
47Brent crude fell 53 cents to close at $63.45 a barrel in London.
48CURRENCIES, BONDS: The dollar fell slightly to 118.86 yen while the euro rose to $1.0794.
49Bond prices fell after the U.S. government reported a slight increase in inflation last month.
50The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell slightly to 1.87 percent from 1.88 percent on Thursday.
51METALS: Precious and industrial metals futures didn't move much.
52Gold rose $5.10 to $1,203.10 an ounce, silver fell 6 cents to $16.23 an ounce and copper was unchanged at $2.77 a pound.
