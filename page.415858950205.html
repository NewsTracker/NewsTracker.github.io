Wild rhino rampages in Nepal city, fatally gores woman

1KATHMANDU, Nepal -- A wild rhino charged into a city in southern Nepal on Monday, killing a woman, injuring several others and chasing panicked people through the main market and a hospital, an official said.
2Attempts to force the rhino out of Hetauda city by honking car horns and beating drums were unsuccessful, with the animal running from one area to another, government administrator Ram Prasad Thapaliya said.
3Thapaliya said the rhino fatally gored a 61-year-old woman on the outskirts of Hetauda, a city of about 135,000 people that borders a forested area.
4An unknown number of people were injured, he said.
5It chased people around the main downtown market and the city bus station, he said.
6"The whole town is terrorized by this animal. It even entered the local hospital, forcing terrified people to jump over the fences," Thapaliya said.
7Hetauda is about 50 miles south of Kathmandu, Nepal's capital.
