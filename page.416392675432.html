Book: Elon Musk almost sold Tesla to Google

1Elon Musk almost sold Tesla Motors to Google two years ago, but then the electric-car maker's fortunes turned around and deal was called off, according to a new book on the maverick entrepreneurial billionaire.
2Today, Tesla is rolling along, selling thousands of its Model S sedans a year and on track to put its new luxury electric crossover on the market later this year.
3But back in March, 2013, Tesla was precarious.
4Bloomberg News says the new book Elon Musk: Tesla, SpaceX, and the Quest for a Fantastic Future by one of its reporters, Ashlee Vance, asserts that Tesla had gone into crisis mode to save itself from bankruptcy.
5At that time, Musk reached out to Google CEO Larry Page, a friend, about selling.
6Vance cites two unnamed sources in writing that Musk proposed that Google buy Tesla for $6 billion, plus come up with $5 billion more to finance plant expansion.
7He also wanted assurances that Google wouldn't break up or sell Tesla before it produced what it now calls Model 3, its mass-market electric car, still at least a couple of years away.
8Perhaps the best part of the deal, however, was that Musk came with it.
9He would have been able to run Tesla for eight years.
10Page took the deal, but as lawyers worked through the details in the following weeks, Tesla's fortunes improved.
11Cars were delivered, customers became happier and the electric-car maker posted its first quarterly profit.
12Crisis averted.
13The deal fell through.
14And Tesla today is on a much firmer track.
