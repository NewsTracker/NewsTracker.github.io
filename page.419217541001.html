Police shooting and protests put Ferguson back on edge

1Riot police contain roughly 200 protesters after violent anniversary of police shooting of black teenager Michael Brown.
2Protesters regrouped in Ferguson, Missouri, after a state of emergency was declared, aimed at preventing a repeat of violence the night before on the anniversary of the police shooting of an unarmed black man.
3Police in riot gear contained roughly 200 protesters who had gathered in the streets of Ferguson on Monday evening.
4The demonstrators, some waving flags, beating drums, and shouting anti-police slogans, marched along a street that was a flashpoint of last year's riots which erupted after white police officer Darren Wilson shot dead 18-year-old black teen Michael Brown.
5Nine people were arrested late on Monday after a group of protesters briefly blocked the roadway, though demonstrations late into the evening were largely peaceful.
6Authorities had declared a state of emergency on Monday for the St Louis suburb and surrounding areas after police officers shot and critically wounded a man in an exchange of gunfire Sunday night, marring what had been a day of peaceful demonstrations.

9Among the demonstrators on Monday evening was 51-year-old Ferguson resident Roberta Lynch, who said relations between the police and community had improved little over the past year.
10"They are doing the same old stuff, taking our rights," Lynch said.
11"They need to give us our space."
12Monday's demonstrations capped a day of civil disobedience called by activists to protest the shooting of Brown and other unarmed black men across the United States by police.
13Clergy and civil rights groups led a series of protests, at one point staging a demonstration at a courthouse in St Louis where 60 people were arrested, including Princeton University professor and activist Cornel West, according to a protest organiser.
14Hours later, police arrested dozens of protesters who blocked rush-hour traffic on Interstate 70 a few kilometres from Ferguson, according to a Reuters news agency witness.
15The death of Brown and a grand jury's decision to spare the white officer from criminal charges led to a wave of demonstrations that boiled over into rioting and arson at times and spawned sympathy rallies across the country.
16Brown's death also prompted greater scrutiny of racial bias within the US criminal justice system, giving rise to the "Black Lives Matter" movement that gained momentum from other high-profile killings of unarmed minorities by white police in cities such as New York, Baltimore, Los Angeles, Cincinnati, and most recently, Arlington, Texas.
17Tensions grew as darkness fell on Monday, with several confrontations between police wearing riot gear and demonstrators.
18Small unmanned aerial drones circled above the crowd.
19St Louis County Police Chief Jon Belmar, standing across the street from the demonstrators, told reporters that police officers were going to give protesters leeway to march, but said the authorities also had to maintain public safety.
20"We are going to let them vent and we are going to manage it the best we possibly can," Belmar said.
21"Last night was pretty out of control at times. Unfortunately, all the good work that's happening on both sides of the street has been marred by violence."
22The violence, according to Belmar, erupted Sunday when two groups of agitators apparently began shooting at each other, disputing what had been peaceful demonstrations.
23At one point a gunman darted across a parking lot and was confronted by four officers who pulled up in an unmarked vehicle.
