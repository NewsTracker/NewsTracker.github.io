Tunisia's Bardo museum reopens for officials, a week after militant attack

1The Bardo museum in Tunis has reopened for officials, almost a week after 20 foreign tourists were killed in a militant attack.
2But it will remain shut to the public until Saturday.
3Tourists were allowed to gather around the main gates, with police standing guard.
4"We decided not to cancel our trip and today we've even decided to change our itinerary to come and take pictures - and to show our solidarity with Tunisians," said Rose Marie, a Canadian tourist.
5At least two gunmen opened fire on visitors as they got off buses at the museum last Wednesday.
6ISIL claimed responsibility for the attack.
7Security forces later killed two men, who are said to have been recruited at mosques in Tunisia and trained at a jihadist camp in Libya.
8It was Tunisia worst attack in more than a decade, testing the country's young democracy.
9Meanwhile, those who work in Tunisia's tourism industry have joined a march, to also show their solidarity and to remember the victims of the rampage.
