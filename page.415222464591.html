Ukraine bans Russian media outlets from official events

PCSO raced to scene of fatal crash - only to discover the schoolboy victim was her nephew

1Tass quoted an unidentified Ukrainian foreign ministry official as saying, "from this week we no longer accredit Russian media."
2On Feb. 12, Ukraine's parliament voted to ban accreditation for Russian media outlets in the country.
3Last week, Ukraine's Security Service said it had drawn up a list of about a hundred Russian news organizations that would be banned, but that list has not been released publicly.
4Ukraine has repeatedly accused Russia's state-controlled media of stirring up animosity toward Ukraine and of falsely portraying the conflict in the country's east, where Russia-backed separatists have been fighting Ukrainian troops since April.
1PCSO Lisa Crocker, 39, was on duty when she was called to the collision involving her young nephew Samuel Crocker in Crediton, Devon.
2The 'generous and kind' schoolboy was knocked down as he walked into the town centre to visit a friend on Saturday afternoon.
3He was taken to Royal Devon and Exeter Hospital in Exeter but later died from his injuries.
4His family said a 'bright light had been extinguished'.
5Cameron Trivett, 24, of Crediton, has been charged with causing death by careless driving and failing to stop after a collision.
6He will appear at Exeter Crown Court later this month.
7It has now emerged that Mrs Crocker, who is part of Devon and Cornwall Police's neighbourhood team in Crediton, arrived at the scene to discover the victim was her brother's son.
