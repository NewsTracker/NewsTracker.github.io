This Is Why Apple Won't Say How Many Watches It Sold

1When Apple's Apple's quarterly numbers come out, it will report revenues, profits, earnings per share, gross margins, guidance and - perhaps most important of them all - how many iPhones it sold.
2Now, here's what it won't report, at least not in a straightforward manner: exactly how many Apple Watches it sold.
3Instead, analysts will have to dig for that information in the "other products" category, where Beats headphones, Apple TV, and accessories also live.
4The math shouldn't be that hard: last quarter Apple posted $1.7 billion in revenue in that category.
5Analysts are all over the place on the watch, so we don't have a solid unit number to use.
6Also, we don't have a solid average price for the watch, so it's hard to figure a consensus revenue estimate for the watch.
7The analysts at Stifel estimates Apple sold 3.3 million watches.
8If we use a $500 average selling price, then that would be an extra $1.7 billion in revenue for the "other" category.
9In short, whatever numbers are out there won't be official or even exact - or, crucially, subject to questioning by analysts and reporters during the earnings call.
10Apple might tell you it doesn't want to tip its hand to competitors, now that smart watches are officially a mainstream thing.
11Apple watchers will say the company doesn't want the market to know just how badly its first new product created after Steve Jobs' passing has failed.
12Research firm Slice Intelligence laid the groundwork for this particular storyline last week in a report that suggested orders for Apple Watch dropped 90 percent since the device's debut.
