What were YOU looking for this time last year? Google now lets you download your entire search history

1From medical problems to travel plans, your Google searches can reveal a intimate snapshot of your life.
2While it can be easy to think of these searches as ephemeral, if you were logged into a Google account, all these queries have been saved.
3Now Google is offering users the option of downloading their entire search archive, as well as deleting part, or all, of the record.
4Are you curious about what you were searching for on Google this time last year?
5Visit history.google.com and log in with your Google account.
6Click onto a calendar view to take a look at what you searched for on any given date.
7The settings button on the top right corner can download the database.
8To delete your entire search history, click settings, remove items and pick a time frame, such 'from the beginning of time'.
9The feature exports your searches to Google Drive in a ZIP archive, with files divided by year and quarter.
10'You can download all of your saved search history to see a list of the terms you've searched for,' a Google support page says.
11To download your history visit history.google.com and log in with your Google account.
12Then click onto a calendar view to take a look at what you searched for on any given date.
13For those want to keep a record of their Google searches, clicking the settings button on the top right corner can download the database.
14Items in search can be deleted by checking the box next to them and clicking the 'remove items' option.
15Google stresses that only account holders can see their data.
16The company has also warned user not download the search archive on a public computer to maintain privacy.
17According to the unofficial Google Operating System Blog, the California-based began testing its download feature last year.
