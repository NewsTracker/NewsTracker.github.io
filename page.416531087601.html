Boko Haram Attacks Niger Army Base, Defense Ministry Says

1Suspected Boko Haram fighters in motorized canoes attacked a Niger army base on an island in Lake Chad, the body of water touching all four countries that have been targeted by Nigeria's Islamic insurgency, Niger's defense ministry said Saturday.
2The army may have suffered "heavy losses" in the attack on Karamga Island, said a Niger military source in the region who spoke on condition of anonymity because he is not authorized to speak to the press.
3Casualty figures were not provided in a defense ministry statement broadcast Saturday night on state television.
4A similar attack on Karamga Island in February killed seven Niger soldiers, and 14 Boko Haram fighters were also killed, military officials said at the time.
5In the latest attack, the assailants approached at dawn on Saturday in about 10 motorized canoes, the defense ministry statement said.
6"Operations are ongoing with the aid of allies" to put an end to the insurgent movement, the statement said.
7Niger joined a regional offensive earlier this year that is credited with retaking large swaths of territory in Nigeria from Boko Haram.
8The original plans called for an 8,750-member regional force with troops also coming from Nigeria, Chad, Cameroon and Benin.
9In addition to the Lake Chad violence, Niger towns bordering Nigeria were targeted in the early weeks of the offensive.
