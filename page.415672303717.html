Jurors to consider life or death for Alabama grandmother convicted in girl's running death

1- Just as when she was run to the point of collapse as punishment for a lie, 9-year-old Savannah Hardin's final moments alive in a hospital bed were drowned out by her grandmother's shouts, Savannah's mother testified Monday.
2Heather Walker testified, at times sobbing, that her former mother-in-law showed no remorse over the girl's death and was haranguing the child even as a physician turned off a breathing machine to see if Savannah could breathe on her own.
3"During this time Joyce was just screaming at Savannah saying, 'Savannah, you need to breathe,'" Walker said.
4Left with a swollen brain and little neural activity from seizures, the girl couldn't draw a breath.
5She died after her father decided against putting her back on life support.
6Now, the same jurors who convicted Garrard of capital murder for making the girl run as punishment will decide whether to recommend life or death for the 49-year-old Garrard.
7Walker took the stand in support of prosecutors who want the death penalty because the girl's death was so cruel.
8Walker said Garrard "shouldn't be spared" even if she didn't wake up that morning planning to kill Savannah.
9"I think there was a point where she knew what she was doing," Walker said during cross-examination.
10Seated just a few feet away at the defense table, Garrard showed no emotion.
11Meanwhile, much of Walker's testimony was hard to understand because she was sobbing uncontrollably.
12The trial and other court documents portray Savannah as a sweet kid who loved horses, cheerleading and the competition of running at school, yet also a child whose life was marred by tension almost from the day she was born prematurely.
13Her parents split when she was 3.
14With Savannah staying with her mother in Florida, welfare officials there investigated whether the child was being mistreated or living in dangerous conditions at least found times but took no action, records show.
15Walker said she and Savannah's father later became locked in a "custody battle," part of which was staged in the same courtroom where Garrard's trial was held, and before the same circuit judge, William Ogletree.
16He awarded custody of Savannah to the father, and Walker said she quickly lost contact with the girl.
17Walker said she didn't see Savannah for two years before her death because her ex-husband's family kept changing phone numbers and never seemed to be home when she'd try to stop by.
18Walker said she sent packages to Savannah, but they never seemed to make it through.
19With her son overseas as a State Department contractor, Garrard and her husband Johnny stepped in to help Hardin's next wife care for Savannah and her younger half brother.
20That's why Garrard was there in the first place that day Savannah ran until she had a seizure and collapsed.
21Walker said she's seen no signs of regret from her one-time mother-in-law since.
22"I personally feel like I see no remorse and she took away my baby's life," Walker said of Garrard.
23At the hospital, even as Garrard yelled it was obvious Savannah wouldn't survive within moments of going off life support, Walker said.
