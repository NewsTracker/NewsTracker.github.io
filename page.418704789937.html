Apple Reports 38% Rise in Profit as iPhone Sales Surge

1Apple's profit soared 38 percent and revenue rose 33 percent in its fiscal third quarter, as sales of iPhones continued to surge.
2On Tuesday, the company, based in Cupertino, Calif., reported net income of $10.7 billion, compared with $7.7 billion in the same quarter a year earlier.
3Revenue climbed to $49.6 billion, slightly above the expectations of Wall Street analysts, who had predicted revenue of $49.3 billion, according to a survey of analysts by Thomson Reuters.
4"We had an amazing quarter, with iPhone revenue up 59 percent over last year, strong sales of Mac, all-time record revenue from services, driven by the App Store, and a great start for Apple Watch," Timothy D. Cook, Apple's chief executive, said in a statement, adding that excitement for Apple Music, its new streaming music service, was "incredible."
5As is often the case for Apple, strong iPhone sales lifted results.
6Apple sold 47.5 million iPhones during the quarter, up 35 percent from a year ago.
7The jump in iPhone sales is noteworthy because the spring season tends to be when device sales slow down for Apple and other tech companies.
8Sales usually heat up between September and December, when consumers stock up on new devices for the back-to-school and holiday shopping seasons, and cool down afterward.
9Apple also reported $2.6 billion in revenue for a category it calls "Other Products."
10The category - which includes products like the iPod - is being closely scrutinized this quarter because it also includes the company's new device, the Apple Watch.
11Apple did not break out specific sales data for the smartwatch, which became available in April.
12Before the report, analysts had estimated that if revenue in the category exceeded $5 billion, it would be a sign that Apple had sold at least five million watches, much higher than the number of iPhones and iPads that were sold in their first quarters on the market.
13That sales performance, analysts say, is a litmus test for whether Apple can continue successful product introductions since the death in 2011 of Steve Jobs, who drove the company to new heights with products like the iPhone and iPad.
14"There's a belief that, look, at some point iPhone units will slow," said Toni Sacconaghi, a financial analyst for Sanford C. Bernstein.
15"What's going to make up for that? And the hope is, could it be the watch?"
16In an interview, Luca Maestri, Apple's chief financial officer, said the watch's sales in its first nine weeks exceeded those of the iPhone and iPad in their first nine weeks of availability.
17Apple noted that international sales accounted for 64 percent of the quarter's overall revenue.
18Sales in the greater China region, one of Apple's prime growth areas, more than doubled in the quarter to $13.2 billion.
19The company also reported healthy growth for its Macintosh computer business.
20Apple sold 4.8 million Macs, up 9 percent from a year ago.
21Yet sales of the iPad, which have experienced a decline in the last year, continued to shrink.
22The company posted sales of 10.9 million iPads, down 18 percent from the 13.3 million sold in the same quarter a year earlier.
