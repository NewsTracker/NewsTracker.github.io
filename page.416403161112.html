Premarket: Upbeat earnings lift world stocks, Greek anxiety hits euro

2World stocks climbed back towards all-time highs on Tuesday as upbeat European earnings reports and expectations of a sixth straight rise in German business confidence helped offset worries about a possible Greek default.
3European trading started strongly with the pan-regional FTSEurofirst 300 index up over 0.6 per cent in early deals, after a 1.4 per cent jump by the Nikkei in Tokyo and a 2 to 2.5 per cent rises in China's main markets had lifted Asia.
4Publicis, Sky, ARM Holdings were all up more than 4 per cent after reporting results, with the overall picture of a weaker euro and improving economic conditions - driven by the European Central Bank's bond-buying stimulus plan - drawing investment flows into equity markets.
5Germany's closely-watched ZEW business confidence indicator, due at 0900 GMT, was expected to confirm the brighter picture in Europe's biggest economy, with its sentiment gauge seen rising to 55.3 from 54.8 and current conditions to 56.0 from 55.1.
6"In macro terms at least, the worst of the crisis looks like it is over for the euro zone and certainly my own 'misery indices' are looking less bad," said Neil Williams, chief economist at fund manager Hermes in London.
7On the ZEW, he added: "The euro will have helped confidence... but Greece should still remain on the radar with it looking like it is going to have to restructure its debt."
8Even after several rounds of negotiations, hopes are slim that Athens will be able to convince euro zone finance ministers to continue their financial support at the latest in a series of meetings on Friday.
9The country could run out of cash by the end of the month.
10The euro had dropped to $1.0670 by 0800 GMT, well off Friday's peak of $1.0849, while Greece's 2-year bond yields were closing in on 30 per cent and benchmark 10-year yields rose to 13.58 per cent.
11An unprecedented debt default in the currency bloc could open the way for Greece to exit the euro, though ECB Vice President Vitor Constancio said on Monday that a country that defaults would not have to leave the euro.
12The dollar pushed up for a third straight day, trading at 98.385 against a currency basket.
13Moving in the opposite direction, the Australian dollar tumbled to $0.7685 from $0.7723 after the minutes of the Reserve Bank of Australia's (RBA) April meeting showed the bank putting rate cuts on the table.
14Oil and gold prices fell back as the dollar strengthened.
15Brent dipped to $62.90 per barrel, while U.S. crude eased to $55.94, not far from last week's four-month high of $57.42.
