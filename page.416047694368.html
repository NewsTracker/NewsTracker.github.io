Opposition-called general strike shuts down Nepal, as protesters clash with police

1KATHMANDU, Nepal - A nationwide general strike enforced by opposition political parties has crippled life in Nepal, as protesters clashed with police and set vehicles on fire in the capital.
2The alliance of 30 opposition parties is demanding the Himalayan nation's new constitution draft be passed by a national consensus.
3The ruling coalition has been attempting to get the draft approved through a vote in parliament after negotiations failed with the opposition.
4Protesters and police clashed in Kathmandu, the capital, on Tuesday, the first day of the three-day strike.
5Riot police fired tear gas shells, while the protesters retaliated by throwing stones.
6The demonstrators also set a taxi and a motorcycle on fire.
7Schools were closed and markets shuttered across Nepal.
8Highways were deserted, with protesters allowing only emergency vehicles on the roads.
