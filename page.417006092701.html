CarGo hits 3-run HR in 9th, Rockies top Dodgers to stop skid (Yahoo Sports)

1LOS ANGELES (AP) -- The Colorado Rockies ended their 11-game losing streak in dramatic fashion, thanks to a guy they've always relied on to come through in clutch situations.
2Carlos Gonzalez hit a three-run homer with two outs in the ninth inning, and Colorado sloshed its way to a 5-4 victory over the Los Angeles Dodgers on Thursday night following a rare rain delay at Chavez Ravine.
3''I really can't put it into words how hard and difficult it's been for the club and for myself,'' Gonzalez said.
4''It's one of those stretches that you really want to get over with, and it finally happened tonight.
5It took longer than what we expected, with all the rain, but we kept grinding.
6That shows a lot of respect.
7The Rockies were rained out three times during their most recent homestand, including a game with the Dodgers last Saturday that was rescheduled for June 2 at Coors Field.
8''That cloud has been following us everywhere we go,'' Gonzalez said with a laugh.
9''We've had to battle against other teams and weather conditions and rain delays, so it's hard to keep your body in the game.
10To stay in the clubhouse for an hour and a half, I'm sure it affects the other team, too.
11Wilin Rosario also went deep for the last-place Rockies in the opener of a four-game series between the top and bottom teams in the NL West.
12Play was halted for 85 minutes during the sixth inning at a ballpark that hasn't had a weather postponement in 15 years.
13Dodgers rookie Yimi Garcia (2-1), who has been filling in as closer with Kenley Jansen on the disabled list, gave up a leadoff single to Nick Hundley in the ninth and a one-out single by Drew Stubbs.
14Charlie Blackmon was called out on strikes before Gonzalez drove a 2-1 pitch halfway up the right-field pavilion for his third homer of the season.
15''I was watching Garcia warming up and he was throwing a lot of fastballs, so I was ready to swing at a good fastball,'' Gonzalez said.
16It was Garcia's third blown save in four chances.
17''We kind of felt like it was Yimi's inning,'' Dodgers manager Don Mattingly said.
18''We were kind of out of righties, for the most part.
19We're kind of at that spot where we felt like Yimi was our guy.
20He's had two outings that didn't go his way, but he's a young guy.
21Rafael Betancourt (1-1) pitched a perfect eighth and John Axford worked a 1-2-3 ninth for his fourth save.
22Brett Anderson tossed 5 1-3 solid innings for Los Angeles, allowing two runs with eight strikeouts on a chilly night at Dodger Stadium.
23The game-time temperature was 59 degrees and the teams played through showers, thunder and lightning.
24Adrian Gonzalez gave Los Angeles the lead in the first with a two-run double that put him within two RBIs of 1,000.
25Anderson had a run-scoring double in the second for his second career RBI.
26The Dodgers were ahead 4-1 when play was stopped in the sixth.
27The game resumed at 10:23 p.m., and Rosario hit a routine grounder that four-time Gold Glove shortstop Jimmy Rollins booted on the freshly manicured infield for an error that allowed Carlos Gonzalez to score.
28Chad Bettis threw 106 pitches over five innings for the Rockies after getting recalled from Triple-A Albuquerque.
29For the second straight night, Rockies bench coach Tom Runnells ran the team following manager Walt Weiss' emergency appendectomy Wednesday while the club was playing in Anaheim against the Angels.
30Weiss was released from the hospital Thursday and returned to the team hotel in Los Angeles with head athletic trainer Keith Dugger.
31Rockies: Rookie reliever Brooks Brown was placed on the 15-day disabled list, retroactive to May 4, with inflammation in his shoulder.
32Dodgers: RHP Pedro Baez was placed on the 15-day DL because of a strained pectoral muscle and was replaced on the roster by LHP Daniel Coulombe, who was recalled from Triple-A Oklahoma City.
33The last weather postponement at Chavez Ravine was on April 17, 2000, when the Houston Astros were in town.
34There have been 17 rainouts since the ballpark opened in 1962.
35Rockies: RHP Eddie Butler (2-3) will make his seventh start of the season and third against the Dodgers.
36He allowed two runs against them in each of the first two outings.
37Dodgers: LHP Clayton Kershaw (1-2) makes his fifth attempt at his 100th regular-season win against the team he no-hit last June.
