Army Corps of Engineers in Baltimore has new commander

1The Baltimore District of the Army Corps of Engineers is welcoming a new commander.
2Col.
3Edward Chamberlayne took over Friday from Col J. Richard Jordan in a ceremony at Fort McHenry.
4Jordan served in the position for three years and is retiring.
5Chamberlayne most recently served as a U.S. Army War College fellow at the Institute of World Politics.
6He is a native of Alexandria, Virginia.
7As commander and district engineer, he will oversee engineering, construction and real estate activities for the Army, Air Force and other Defense Department agencies in the District of Columbia, Delaware, Maryland, Pennsylvania, West Virginia, southern New York and northern Virginia.
