1Washington: Hillary Clinton exclusively used a personal email account to conduct government business as secretary of state, State Department officials have said, and may have violated federal requirements that officials' correspondence be retained as part of the agency's record.
2Mrs Clinton did not have a government email address during her four-year tenure at the State Department.
3Her aides took no actions to have her personal emails preserved on department servers at the time, as is required by the Federal Records Act.
4A spokesman for Mrs Clinton, Nick Merrill, defended her use of a personal email account and said she has been complying with the "letter and spirit of the rules".
5But those who have accused her of concealing key details of the attack on the US consulate in Benghazi which killed four Americans, including the US ambassador to Libya, Chris Stevens, are likely to seize upon this controversy involving the woman widely assumed to be the Democratic Party's frontrunner for the 2016 presidential nomination.
6It was only two months ago, in response to a new State Department effort to comply with federal record-keeping practices, that Mrs Clinton's advisers reviewed tens of thousands of pages of her personal emails and decided which ones to turn over to the State Department.
7All told, 55,000 pages of emails were given to the department.
8Mrs Clinton stepped down from the post of top US diplomat in early 2013.
9Her expansive use of the private account was alarming to current and former National Archives and Records Administration officials and government watchdogs, who called it a serious breach.
10"It is very difficult to conceive of a scenario - short of nuclear winter - where an agency would be justified in allowing its cabinet-level head officer to solely use a private email communications channel for the conduct of government business," said Jason Baron, a lawyer at Drinker Biddle & Reath who is a former director of litigation at the National Archives and Records Administration.
11When Mrs Clinton first took the secretary of state's job in early 2009, Washington was still digesting a major controversy over Bush administration officials using personal email addresses to conduct government business.
12In June 2007 a congressional oversight committee had asked the Bush administration for emails related to the sacking of US attorneys, allegedly for political reasons, but it turned out the administration had conducted millions of emails' worth of business on private addresses and that the archives had been deleted.
13Oversight committee chairman Henry Waxman accused the Bush administration of "using non-governmental accounts specifically to avoid creating a record of the communications".
14Mrs Clinton is not the first government official - or first secretary of state - to use a personal email account on which to conduct official business.
15But her exclusive use of her private email, for all of her work, appears unusual, Mr Baron said.
16Even the Bush administration officials involved in the attorneys scandal used their official addresses as well.
17"I can recall no instance in my time at the National Archives when a high-ranking official at an executive branch agency solely used a personal email account for the transaction of government business," said Mr Baron, who worked at the agency from 2000 to 2013.
18Mr Merrill declined to detail why Mrs Clinton had chosen to conduct State Department business from her personal account.
19Mrs Clinton's successor, Secretary of State John Kerry, has used a government email account since taking over the role, and his correspondence is being preserved contemporaneously as part of State Department records, according to his aides.
