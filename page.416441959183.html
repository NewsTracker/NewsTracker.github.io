Calbuco volcano erupts in Chile, and nearby town evacuated

1SANTIAGO, Chile (AP) - The Calbuco volcano erupted Wednesday for the first time in more than 42 years, billowing a huge ash cloud over a sparsely populated, mountainous area in southern Chile.
2Authorities ordered the evacuation of the 1,500 inhabitants of the nearby town of Ensenada, along with residents of two smaller communities.
3The National Mining and Geology Service issued a high alert, barring access to the area around the volcano, which lies near the cities of Puerto Varas and Puerto Montt, a little more than 1,000 kilometers (620 miles) south of Chile's capital, Santiago.
4"For us it was a surprise," said Alejandro Verges, regional emergency director of the Los Lagos region where the eruption took place.
5He said Calbuco wasn't under any special form of observation.
6Authorities said a large amount of ash was observed but no hot rocks or lava so far.
7Calbuco last erupted in 1972 and is considered one of the top three most potentially dangerous among Chile's 90 active volcanos.
