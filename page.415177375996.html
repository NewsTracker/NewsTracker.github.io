Greek prosecutors seek ultra-rightist's release from jail

1ATHENS, Greece (AP) " Greek prosecutors are recommending the head of the Nazi-inspired Golden Dawn party " the country's third-largest " be freed from prison pending trial for alleged participation in a criminal group.
2Prosecutors proposed to a council of judges Tuesday that Nikos Michaloliakos, one of the party's 17 lawmakers, should pay 125,000 euros ($140,000) bail and be placed under house arrest until the trial ends.
3Michaloliakos was arrested in September 2013, in a crackdown following the murder of rap singer Pavlos Fyssas, allegedly by a Golden Dawn volunteer.
4All 18 Golden Dawn lawmakers in Greece's previous parliament face criminal charges that carry a 20-year prison sentence.
5They deny wrongdoing.
6No trial date has been set.
7Michaloliakos was due for release by March 29, when the 18-month maximum pretrial detention period expires.
