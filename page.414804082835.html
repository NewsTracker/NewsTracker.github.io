Egypt seeks international intervention against IS in Libya

1CAIRO, Feb. 16 (UPI) -- Egyptian President Abdel Fattah al Sisi called for international intervention against Islamic State sites in Libya after the terror group beheaded 21 Egyptian Christians there.
2The first wave of airstrikes targeted IS training camps and weapons storage sites.
3The second wave followed suit.
4"Avenging Egyptian blood and punishing criminals and murderers is our right and duty," the Egyptian military said in statement.
5Meanwhile, al Sisi called for the international community to intervene against IS, calling what happened in Libya a threat to world peace and security.
6He spoke by phone to French President Francois Hollande and Italian Prime Minister Matteo Renzi about their respective countries' involvement.
7He also sent Foreign Minister Sameh Shukri to speak with U.N. officials in New York.
8"Leaving the situation as it is in Libya without a firm intervention to curtail these terrorist organizations would be a threat to international peace and security," the Egyptian Foreign Ministry said.
9Egyptian Ambassador to Britain, Nasser Kamel, told the BBC IS is a particular threat to Italy, and thus, Europe as a whole.
10"[There are] boat people who go for immigration purposes and try to cross the Mediterranean," he said.
11"In the next few weeks if we do not act together, there will be boats full of terrorists also."
