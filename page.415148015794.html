Ukraine urges peacekeeping mission

1President Petro Poroshenko's office said the appeal for a contingent of peacekeepers will be addressed to the United Nations and the European Union.
2His office gave no specific details on the mission's composition or any timetable for it but Russia is strongly against the idea.
3Fighting has waned substantially in eastern Ukraine in recent days as a ceasefire deal forged last month increasingly takes effect, but both sides have complained of sporadic violations.
4Military spokesman Colonel Andriy Lysenko said that one serviceman was killed today and another four were wounded over the previous day.
5He did not specify the circumstances of those casualties.
6The UN human rights office has raised its toll of the fighting, saying more than 6,000 people have died since the conflict began in April.
7Under the terms of the ceasefire accord, the warring sides must pull back their heavy weaponry by distances of between 50 kilometres (30 miles) and 140 kilometres (90 miles) from the front line.
8That drawback started last week, although progress has been uncertain.
9Separatist military spokesman Eduard Basurin said that rebels have pulled back all their weaponry as stipulated in the peace agreement.
10That claim has yet to be confirmed by the Organisation for Security and Cooperation in Europe, which is overseeing the withdrawal process.
11Col Lysenko accused separatists of making a show of drawing back weapons only to return them to their original frontline positions at night.
12He said Ukrainian troops would continue their own withdrawals only if the situation did not worsen.
13"But it is too early to talk about this as every day we see violations of the ceasefire, including with the use of heavy weapons that the militants ought to have withdrawn," he said.
14US secretary of state John Kerry met with his Russian counterpart in Geneva in what appeared to be less than amicable talks amid the continuing tensions over Ukraine.
15Neither man smiled or spoke substantively as they shook hands at the start of the talks, which took place less than a week after Mr Kerry told Congress that Russian officials have lied "to my face" about Moscow's role in Ukraine.
16That comment drew a rebuke from the Russian foreign ministry.
17US officials have pointed out that Mr Kerry did not specifically accuse Russian foreign minister Sergey Lavrov of lying to him.
18While Russia denies its troops are fighting in Ukraine, the UN has cited "credible reports (that) indicate a continuing flow of heavy weaponry and foreign fighters" from Russia.
19Ukraine has been steadily intensifying its war-readiness since the conflict in the east broke and has embarked on numerous waves of partial military mobilisations.
20On Monday, Mr Poroshenko presented legislation to parliament to boost the size of the country's armed forces to 250,000.
21Interfax-Ukraine news agency cited prime minister Arseniy Yatsenyuk as saying last week that boosting the size of the army to that amount would require enlisting an additional 68,000 troops.
