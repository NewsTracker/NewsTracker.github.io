Kansas freshman Kelly Oubre Jr headed for NBA draft (Yahoo Sports)

1Kansas plays New Mexico State on Friday.
2LAWRENCE, Kan.
3(AP) -- Kansas swingman Kelly Oubre Jr. announced Wednesday he is leaving for the NBA draft, skipping his final three seasons of eligibility after an up-and-down freshman year.
4Oubre revealed his intentions in a statement released by the school.
5''It's always been a dream to play in the NBA,'' he said.
6The 6-foot-7 Oubre started 27 games this past season, helping the Jayhawks win their 11th straight Big 12 title.
7He averaged 9.3 points and five rebounds, and showcased his potential when he poured in 25 points in a victory over TCU in the Big 12 Tournament.
8His decision to leave for the NBA is based more on potential than productivity.
9He scored three points on 1-for-3 shooting in a loss to Iowa State in the Big 12 title game, then had just nine points in in a loss to Wichita State in the round of 32 in the NCAA Tournament.
10But with impressive athleticism, stifling defense and a deft outside touch, Oubre left a strong impression with NBA scouts who came through Lawrence last season.
11''This should come as really no surprise to anybody that has followed us,'' Kansas coach Bill Self said.
12''Kelly came in being well thought of by NBA personnel and certainly, nothing has changed in that regard.
13Oubre, who is expected to be among the first 20 players chosen in the June draft, said he spoke with several former Kansas players before making his decision.
14Among them was Joel Embiid, who was chosen third overall last year - two spots behind Jayhawks teammate Andrew Wiggins.
15''JoJo said he knows what I am going through and he told me his process and some of the things he went through,'' Oubre said.
16Oubre is the first player to announce he is leaving Kansas, but he may not be the last.
17Fellow freshman Cliff Alexander's future remains in question after he was forced to miss the final few weeks of the season due to an investigation into improper benefits.
18Leading scorer Perry Ellis could also opt to skip his senior season, though few mock drafts have him rated highly.
19Kansas has already signed five-star power forward Carlton Bragg, and Self has met with 7-foot center Mike Thorn Jr., who is transferring from Charlotte.
20The Jayhawks are also deeply invested in Jaylen Brown and Malik Newman, two of the top uncommitted recruits in the country.
