Egypt Court Sentences 11 to Death Over 2012 Soccer Riot

1A criminal court in Egypt's coastal city of Port Said on Tuesday sentenced 11 people to death over a 2012 soccer riot that left more than 70 dead and several hundreds injured.
2The verdict, read by presiding judge Mohammed el-Said, came at the end of the retrial of 73 defendants in a case that sparked deadly riots in 2013 in Port Said, prompting then-President Mohammed Morsi to declare a state of emergency in the city.
3Also Tuesday, the court sentenced 40 defendants to up to 15 years in prison and acquitted the rest.
4The verdicts can be appealed.
5The earlier trial ended in March 2013 when 21 defendants were sentenced to death, while others received jail terms that ranged from one to 25 years in prison.
6Twenty-eight were acquitted.
7The rulings were appealed and a retrial was ordered by Egypt's Court of Cassation in February last year.
8The February 2012 riot began at the end of a league match in Port Said between Cairo's Al-Ahly, Egypt's most successful club, and home side Al-Masry.
9The riot, Egypt's worst and among the world's deadliest, led to the temporary suspension of Egypt's top flight soccer league.
10The league later resumed, but with matches played in empty stadiums.
11The first Egyptian Premier League game in which fans were allowed back into the stadiums was played in February this year, but that occasion was also marred by the death of 22 fans in a stampede outside the grounds.
12The stampede followed the use of tear gas by police to stop what authorities at the time said was an attempt by fans to storm the military-owned stadium in a suburb east of Cairo.
13In the Port Said incident on Feb. 2, 2012, Al-Masry fans attacked Al-Ahly supporters with knives, clubs and rocks after the match.
14Witnesses and survivors described victims falling from the bleachers as they tried to escape.
15Hundreds of others fled into an exit passage, only to be crushed against a locked gate with their rivals attacking from behind.
16At one point, the stadium lights went out, plunging it into darkness.
17The match's TV sportscaster explained that authorities shut them off to "calm the situation."
18Among those sentenced on Tuesday were Port Said's police chief and another senior police officer, both receiving five-year jail sentences.
19Al-Masry's executive director Mohsen Shettah and a stadium official in charge of the lighting were also sentenced to five years in prison.
20Most of the victims of the 2012 riot belonged to Al-Ahly's "Ultras," an association of hard-core fans who have long been at sharp odds with the nation's highly militarized police, taunting them with offensive slogans during matches and fighting them in street battles.
21Hard-core fans of other clubs also identify themselves by variations on the Ultras name.
22Ultras members have been credited with playing a major role in the 18-day popular uprising that toppled longtime autocrat Hosni Mubarak in 2011, as well as subsequent street clashes with police.
23They were among the chief defenders of Tahrir Square, the uprising's epicenter in central Cairo, when Mubarak loyalists charged protesters on Feb. 2, 2011, one of the deadliest days of the uprising.
24Tuesday's verdict followed a court ruling last month that banned the Ultras belonging to Al-Ahly and other sports clubs over charges that the groups are involved in terrorism.
25Judge el-Said on Tuesday said the various Ultras associations have been led astray by individuals with "ulterior" motives, filling their heads with ideas like sacrificing their lives for their clubs as a duty.
