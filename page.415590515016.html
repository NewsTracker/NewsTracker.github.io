Obama pledges security cooperation after Tunisia attack

Ohio court awards wrongly imprisoned man over $1 million

1Obama spoke by phone on Thursday with Tunisian President Beji Caid Essebsi.
2The White House says Obama offered his condolences, sympathy and support.
3Obama also commended Tunisians for their response and called Tunisia's democracy a "powerful example" for the region.
4The White House says Obama offered to keep providing assistance to Tunisia as the investigation proceeds.
5The Islamic State group has claimed responsibility for the attack at the National Bardo Museum.
6The U.S. and coalition partners are fighting IS extremists in Iraq and Syria, and the White House has been concerned about the extremist group's spread to other parts of the region.
1Ricky Jackson, 58, was one of three men sent to death row in 1975 after being convicted of aggravated murder in the slaying of a businessman outside a corner store in Cleveland.
2Cuyahoga County prosecutors at the time relied on the testimony of a 13-year-old boy to convict Jackson and two brothers who were Jackson's best friends.
3The boy, Eddie Vernon, said he saw the slaying, but he was in a school bus with other children about a block away when the businessman was shot.
4The now-grown Vernon signed an affidavit that the Ohio Innocence Project filed last March, saying police coerced him into testifying against Jackson, Wiley Bridgeman and Ronnie Bridgeman, now known as Kwame Ajamu.
5Based on the recantation and corroborating testimony that Vernon lied in 1975, a judge dismissed the charges against the three men in November.
6Jackson's payment comes after a judge ruled last month that all three men were entitled to compensation.
7The Court of Claims ordered the state to pay Jackson $1,008,055 for the nearly 39 years he spent in state prison.
8When Jackson was released in November he was believed to have served the longest sentence for someone wrongfully convicted.
9Neither Jackson nor his attorney could be reached for comment on Thursday.
10Wiley Bridgeman also was released from prison in November.
11He originally was paroled in 2002 but was sent back to prison several months later after an argument with a parole officer.
12Ajamu was paroled in 2003.
13Bridgeman and Ajamu haven't received their compensation yet.
