Somali extremists al-Shabab warn of more attacks in Kenya

1"Kenyan cities will run red with blood," said al-Shabab according to the SITE intelligence monitoring group.
2The Islamic militants said the attack on Garissa college was in retaliation for killings carried out by Kenyan troops fighting the rebels in Somalia.
3A woman cries after she viewed the body of a relative killed in Thursday's attack on a university, at Chiromo funeral home, Nairobi, Kenya, Saturday, April 4, 2015.
4Al-Shabab gunmen rampaged through a university in northeastern Kenya at dawn Thursday, killing scores of people in the group's deadliest attack in the East African country.
5Four militants were slain by security forces to end the siege just after dusk.
6"No amount of precaution or safety measures will be able to guarantee your safety, thwart another attack or prevent another bloodbath," said the al-Shabab statement.
7Five people have been arrested on suspicion of involvement in the Garissa attack, a Kenyan official said.
8Kenyan security agencies arrested three people trying to cross into Somalia, said Interior Ministry spokesman Mwenda Njoka in a Twitter post.
9He said the three are associates of Mohamed Mohamud, also known as Dulyadin Gamadhere, a former teacher at a Kenyan Madrassa Islamic school who authorities say coordinated the Garissa attack.
10Kenyan authorities have put a $220,000 bounty for information leading to Gamadhere's arrest.
11Two other suspects were arrested at Garissa college.
12A survivor of the killings at Garissa University College was found on Saturday, two days after the attack by Islamic extremists killed 148 people.
13Cynthia Cheroitich, 19, told The Associated Press from her hospital gurney that she hid in a wardrobe and covered herself with clothes, refusing to emerge even when some of her classmates came out of hiding at the demands of the gunmen from the al-Shabab group.
14She was rescued shortly before 10 a.m., according to Kenyan officials.
15Cheroitich said she didn't believe that rescuers urging her to come out of her hiding place were there to help, suspecting at first that they were militants.
16"How do I know that you are the Kenyan police?" she said she asked them.
17Only when Kenyan security forces had one of her teachers appeal to her did she come out, she said.
18"I was just praying to my God," Cheroitich, a Christian, said of her ordeal.
19Cheroitich appeared tired and thirsty, sipping on yoghurt and a soft drink, but otherwise seemed in good health.
20She said she drank a body lotion because she was so thirsty and hungry while in hiding.
21Authorities displayed the bodies of the alleged attackers before about 2,000 people in a large open area in central Garissa.
22The bodies lay on the bed of a pickup truck that drove slowly past the crowd, which broke into a run in pursuit.
23Soldiers monitored the crowd.
24There was shouting and clouds of dust rose as the vehicle left the area.
25Spectator Yusuf Mohamed applauded the display, saying authorities wanted to "win the hearts of the people" and clear any doubts that the attackers had been killed.
26Kenyan authorities said Thursday that the attackers had been strapped with explosives which went off like bombs when they were shot.
27But the four bodies shown Saturday were intact.
28The bodies of many of those killed in Garissa have been transported to the capital, Nairobi, where grieving Kenyans gathered to view the remains of family members.
