PM Netanyahu's party trails in polls before Israel election

Pope Francis Says His Tenure May Last Less Than 5 Years

1Poll results in the Yediot Ahronot newspaper on Friday gave the Zionist Union, comprised of labor and a center-left party, 26 seats over 22 for Netanyahu's hawkish Likud party.
2It polled 1,032 people with a margin of error of 2.5 percent.
3Other polls had similar results.
4The March 17 election is widely seen as a vote of confidence in Netanyahu, whose campaign mainly focuses on security.
5But after years of rapidly increasing living costs, many Israelis are turning to dovish and centrist parties that focus on social and economic issues.
6Netanyahu showed concern over the declining numbers in local media this week and called on his supporters to turn out.
7In a sign of internal discontent in Likud, Dan Meridor, a former leading Likud politician, told Yediot he will not vote for Netanyahu.
1In an interview with the Mexican broadcaster Televisa published Friday, the Argentine Pope predicted a "brief" tenure for himself.
2"I have the feeling that my pontificate will be brief: four or five years; I do not know, even two or three," Pope Francis, 78, said.
3"Two have already passed. It is a somewhat vague sensation."
4"Maybe it's like the psychology of the gambler who convinces himself he will lose so he won't be disappointed and if he wins, is happy. I do not know. But I feel that the Lord has placed me here for a short time, and nothing more But it is a feeling. I always leave the possibility open," he said.
5The papal post is traditionally held until death, though Francis's predecessor Pope Benedict XVI resigned in 2013 after roughly seven years in office, becoming the first pope to step down in nearly 600 years.
6The current Pope has suggested in the past that he would lead a short papacy and he has not ruled out retiring.
7In the interview released Friday, Francis said he does not dislike being Pope, but said one thing in particular does bug him.
8"The one thing that I would like is to go out, without anyone recognizing me, and go to a pizzeria to eat pizza," Francis said.
