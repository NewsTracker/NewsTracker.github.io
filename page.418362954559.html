China leads dive in Asia stocks as Greece rattles investors

1Chinese stocks led a slump in Asian markets on Wednesday as the sell-off in Shanghai intensified and Greece's future in the euro remained highly uncertain.
2KEEPING SCORE: China's Shanghai Composite Index tumbled 5.5 percent to 3,521.20 and Hong Kong's Hang Seng dived 5.1 percent to 23,693.95.
3Japan's Nikkei 225 lost 3.1 percent to 19,737.64 as the yen strengthened on safe haven buying.
4South Korea's Kospi was down 1.1 percent at 2,016.21.
5Other regional markets were also down, including Taiwan, Singapore, Australia and the Philippines.
6CHINA SELL-OFF: A slew of official measures to provide support to Chinese stock markets have failed to halt a recent sell-off.
7The Shanghai benchmark is down more than 30 percent since peaking June 12 after a sizzling yearlong rally.
8Millions of novice investors piled into the market when it was at its most frothy.
9Some made big profits but the slump has left many with shares worth less than they paid and hoping for a rebound so they can sell.
10ANALYST TAKE: "Greece and China dominate once again, but throw in massive falls in bulk and base metals and you are left with a worrying mix for market participants," Chris Weston, chief market strategist at IG in Melbourne, Australia said in a commentary.
11"Can the Federal Reserve divert attention away from these issues in today's FOMC minutes? I am skeptical, even though it is actually the most important U.S. economic release," he said.
12"Still, for traders there are only two games in town and these remain China's equity markets and the Greek negotiations."
13GREECE CRISIS: Frustrated European leaders fearing for the future of their common currency gave the Greek prime minister a last-minute chance Tuesday to finally come up with a viable proposal on how to save his country from financial ruin.
14Overcoming their surprise when Alexis Tsipras failed to present them with a detailed reform blueprint, the leaders reluctantly agreed to a final summit on Sunday.
15Indebted Greece needs a financial lifeline from its European creditors who have been pressing for more austerity in exchange for bailout funds.
16Without that it's expected to default, paving the way for its exit from the euro currency.
17WALL STREET: U.S. shares rose moderately on domestic factors.
18The Standard & Poor's 500 rose 12.58 points, or 0.6 percent, to 2,081.34.
19The Dow Jones industrial average climbed 93.33 points, or 0.5 percent, to 17,776.91.
20The Nasdaq composite inched up 5.52 points, or 0.1 percent, to 4,997.46.
21ENERGY: Benchmark U.S. crude was down 31 cents to $52.01 a barrel in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
22The contract fell 20 cents to close at $52.33 in New York on Tuesday.
23Brent crude, the international benchmark, slipped 79 cents to $56.06 a barrel in London.
24CURRENCIES: The euro was little changed at $1.1007 from $1.1008 on Tuesday.
25The dollar fell to 122.86 yen from 122.52 yen.
