Swiss FIFA case has 28 new 'money laundering' acts to study

1Swiss federal prosecutors have new evidence of 28 acts of possible money laundering linked to FIFA's 2018 and 2022 World Cup bid contests.
2The office of Switzerland's attorney general says it now has 81 "suspicious activity reports" filed by banks in the country.
3Attorney-General Michael Lauber said last month his team had 53 suspicious incidents to study in a "huge and complex case."
4Swiss prosecutors are working with a separate United States probe of racketeering and bribery implicating senior FIFA officials.
5Though Lauber's case focuses on the 2018-2022 contests won by Russia and Qatar, respectively, he said it could include other FIFA business.
6His office says Sunday that all new reports "are related to the (attorney general's) investigation into the allocation of the football World Cups 2018 and 2022."
