AC/DC to put music on Spotify, Apple Music

1The world of streaming is about to get Thunderstruck.
2Spotify, Rdio and Apple Music will all have AC/DC's music as early as Tuesday, according to The New York Times.
3The decision to join the services is a big deal for the band, who only joined iTunes in 2012.
4"I know the Beatles have changed but we're going to carry on like that," Angus Young told The Guardian in May 2011, after the Beatles released their tracks to iTunes.
5"For us it's the best way. We are a band who started off with albums and that's how we've always been."
6AC/DC isn't the first rock band to bring their sound to streaming, with Led Zeppelin, Metallica and Pink Floyd all having made their back catalogues available.
7While they are joining the services, some artists have later opted out of having their music available.
8Taylor Swift pulled her albums from Spotify earlier this year, arguing that she deserved to get paid for her music.
9"I think there should be an inherent value placed on art," she explained.
10"I didn't see that happening, perception-wise, when I put my music on Spotify.
11"Everybody's complaining about how music sales are shrinking, but nobody's changing the way they're doing things," she adds.
12"They keep running towards streaming, which is, for the most part, what has been shrinking the numbers of paid album sales."
