UPDATE 2-Apple expected to focus on watch, music at developer conference

1SAN FRANCISCO, June 8 (Reuters) - Apple is expected to unveil a new music service and better tools to build apps for its smartwatch at its annual gathering of developers on Monday.
2Developers waited in long lines outside of San Francisco's Moscone Center hours ahead of the keynote speech.
3Inside the event, Apple advertised its operating systems for its iPhone, Mac and Watch but gave no other indication of the announcements to come.
4But at a recent tech event, a top Apple executive said the tech giant's legion of independent developers will get a preview of a new software kit for the two-month-old Apple Watch at the conference.
5Apps built with the new toolkit will likely start arriving in the fall, said Jeff Williams, Apple's senior vice president of operations.
6Like other Apple products, the watch's commercial success will likely hinge on a compelling collection of apps.
7But early apps for the timepiece have been tethered to the iPhone, placing some limits on what developers could do.
8The expanded software kit should lead to better and faster watch apps, said Bob O'Donnell, an analyst at TECHnalysis Research.
9Many analysts expect that Apple will use Monday's event to debut a new music streaming service, and in an interview in Cannes, France, the CEO of Sony Music, Doug Morris, confirmed the launch although he did not provide many details, according to a report in VentureBeat.
10The company behind the iPod and iTunes has long been a leader in digital music, but it has lost ground in recent years as subscription services such as Spotify have caught on with consumers.
11Analyst Van Baker of Gartner said he expects Apple to release a new service to win a bigger share of the streaming business.
12"This is catch-up for Apple," he said.
13An Apple spokesman did not immediately respond to a request for comment.
