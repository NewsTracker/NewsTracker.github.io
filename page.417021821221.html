Mexico judge returns girl to custody of US mom after 8 years

1Alondra Diaz Garcia arrives for a court hearing in Los Reyes, Mexico, Tuesday, May 12, 2015.
2The teenage girl at the center of an international custody case won't be sent to the United States until Mexican authorities confirm her identity through DNA testing.
3The search for the 13-year-old attracted wide attention last month after a Mexican court erroneously sent another girl named Alondra to Texas in a case of mistaken identity.
4Dorotea Garcia, who lives in Houston, has been searching for her daughter since 2007 when the girl allegedly was taken into Mexico by her father.
5MEXICO CITY (AP) - A Mexican judge returned a long-missing teenager to her U.S. mother Friday, ending the woman's eight-year search and a cross-border custody case in which another girl was mistakenly sent to Texas against her will.
6Alondra Diaz, 13, and Houston resident Dorotea Garcia emerged from the courthouse together under heavy police guard following the afternoon ruling in Los Reyes, a town in the southwestern state of Michoacan.
7The previous day, DNA tests confirmed her identity.
8Clad in jeans, a turquoise T-shirt and matching backpack, Alondra climbed into the backseat of a police van with her beaming mother and the two embraced.
9They did not comment publicly, but Judge Cinthia Elodia Mercado called the case closed.
10"The recovery of a minor by an applicant mother has happened," Mercado said.
11"This is over."
12Alondra Diaz was taken to Mexico in 2007 by her father, Reynaldo Diaz, without her mother's consent, and her whereabouts had not been known until recently.
13Garcia has indicated that she would drop legal complaints against the father if she got custody.
14The case gained international attention last month after Mercado erroneously ruled that 14-year-old Alondra Luna was the missing girl and ordered her turned over to Garcia.
15Video recordings circulated widely of Alondra Luna screaming and desperately resisting as police dragged her away.
16DNA testing performed after the girl was taken to the United States showed she was not Garcia's daughter, and she returned to her real family in Guanajuato.
17With a media spotlight now on the case, Reynaldo Diaz delivered the real Alondra Diaz to family members who then presented her to authorities, saying she was prepared to go live with her mother.
18Garcia had an emotional reunion with her daughter this week in a courtroom in Los Reyes.
19In the case of the first girl, the judge denied requests by her and her family for DNA tests, saying it was not within her authority.
20This time Mercado waited for DNA confirmation.
21The difference in treatment prompted Alondra Luna and her parents to travel to Los Reyes to stake out the courthouse Friday and demand an apology.
22"We have been here since 9 a.m. and the judge does not want to see us, nor will she open the door, and she says that if we remain here she will call police to remove us," said Susana Nunez, the girl's mother.
23"We want to make it clear that my girl's rights were trampled."
24Nunez said the family intended to file formal complaints next week but wanted to meet face-to-face with Mercado first to express their displeasure.
25"I see this as a kidnapping that was ordered by the judge," the girl's father, Gustavo Luna, said.
