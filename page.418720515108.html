U.S. stock futures fall as Apple results disappoint

1U.S. stock index futures fell on Wednesday as corporate earnings disappointed, especially from Apple (AAPL.O), the world's largest company.
2* Apple shares slumped 6.2 percent to $122.61 in premarket trading, a day after the iPhone maker's revenue forecast for the fourth quarter fell below expectations.
3* Disappointing earnings from other technology giants such as Microsoft (MSFT.O) and Yahoo (YHOO.O) also added to losses.
4* Microsoft fell 3.9 percent to $45.45 after reporting its biggest quarterly loss, as the company wrote down its Nokia phone business and demand fell for its Windows operating system.
5* Yahoo (YHOO.O) was down 2.2 percent at $38.85 after it forecast lower-than-expected revenue for the current quarter as it struggles to revive its core online advertising business.
6* U.S. stocks had closed lower on Tuesday as results from IBM (IBM.N) and United Technologies (UTX.N) dampened early optimism over earnings season.
7* While markets are near record highs, June-quarter earnings of S&P 500 companies are expected to dip 1.9 percent, according to Thomson Reuters data, well below the 5.9 percent gain forecast on Jan. 1.
8* Dow components Coca Cola (KO.N) and Boeing are scheduled to report before the bell and American Express (AXP.N) results are expected after the close.
9* Data expected on Wednesday includes existing home sales data.
10Home sales, which reached a 5-1/2-year high in May, are expected to have risen 1.2 percent at an annual rate of 5.40 million units in June.
11The data is expected at 10 a.m. ET (1400 GMT).
12* GoPro (GPRO.O) rose 2.9 percent to $63.90 after the action camera maker reported a better-than-expected quarterly profit and forecast current-quarter revenue above market estimates, helped by strong sales in Asia.
13* S&P 500 e-minis ESc1 were down 8.75 points, or 0.41 percent, with 92,578 contracts traded.
14* Nasdaq 100 e-minis NQc1 were down 54 points, or 1.16 percent, on volume of 18,672 contracts.
15* Dow e-minis 1YMc1 were down 71 points, or 0.4 percent, with 9,709 contracts changing hands.
