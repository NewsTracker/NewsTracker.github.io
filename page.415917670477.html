Updated: Apple Watch release date, price and features

1Apple Watch launches this month, Friday, April 24 - you can set your friend's inferior Android Wear smartwatch to that release date - and we know everything about what's been hiding up Tim Cook's sleeve.
2It's an iPhone-compatible smartwatch officially dubbed Apple Watch, shedding the fabled iWatch moniker, as the company is focusing on its first chic wearable over geeky tech.
3To that lavish point, when pre-orders begin on April 10, the Apple Watch price ranges from $349 ( 299, AU$499) for the entry-level Sport model to $17,000 ( 13,500, AU$24,000) for the top-tier 18-karat gold editions with VIP treatment.
4You can read emails, summon Siri, receive calls and track fitness goals on your watch.
5Facebook alerts, WeChat messages and Twitter trends can also be beamed your wrist without pulling out your smartphone.
6But is this really the first smartwatch that matters, like Cook says?
7Does its 18-hour battery life hold up?
8Is it water resistant?
9We finally have those answers and the first apps days before the Apple Watch launch.
10The wait is almost over for the Apple Watch release date, which is officially set for April 24.
11Pre-orders begin April 10, the same day you can try it on at the Apple Store during 15-minute appointments.
12It'll be available in nine countries: US, UK, Australia, Canada, China, France, Germany, Hong Kong and Japan, with more countries due to get the Apple Watch at later date.
13Tim Cook promised.
14By the end of this month, the Apple Watch vs Android Wear comparison can really get underway.
15We finally have an answer to the question: "How much will the Apple Watch cost?"
16The answer: More than we were hoping to pay in some cases.
17Only the aluminum Sport edition "starts at $349 ( 299)" and that's for the smaller 38mm version.
18The 42mm size is slightly more at $399 ( 339), revealed the Apple CEO at its latest event.
19The glossier stainless steel "Watch" collection starts at $549 ( 479) and goes up to to $1099 ( 949), depending on the Apple Watch size and band configuration.
20The gold Watch Edition will be in limited supply at Apple Stores and cost a whopping $10,000 ( 8,000) to $17,000 ( 13,500, AU$24,000), confirming prior rumors that said it'd be incredibly expensive.
21More designs may come when Apple introduces an Apple Watch 2, which many critics hope will include a radically different round watch face and significantly cheaper price.
22Why Apple Watch costs so much?
23It's not just the notorious Apple tax pushing the price tag well beyond $349, it's the components involved.
24For example, the all-metal Link Bracelet has 100 components and takes an insane nine hours to cut.
25Apple also recruited high-profile people throughout the watch and biometrics industries who have wound up on its Apple Watch team.
26That talent comes at a price.
27Then there's a hidden cost.
28While a subsidized iPhone 6 and iPhone 6 Plus are cheaper upfront in the US, stores make up the difference with contract kickbacks.
29Not so with a smartwatch.
30Stores need a cut too.
31For the sake of comparison, Android Wear's initial watches were the Samsung Gear Live at $200 ( 170, AU$250) and LG G Watch at $230 ( 160, AU$250).
32The more stylish Moto 360 is priced at $249 ( 199, AU$329) and LG Watch Urbane is set to be even more expensive.
33Apple is clearly aiming for luxury given the sapphire glass-protected display, an imposing digital crown, two sizes and even the 18k gold edition.
34What does Apple Watch actually do?
35Apple's first wearable gadget beams messages, Facebook updates and simplified apps to our wrists, eliminating the all-too-common need to take out our devices to constantly check notifications.
36There are more than a dozen ways to interact with the watch, from receiving glanceable notifications, to feeling "taptic" feedback, to calling upon Siri, according to the developers' WatchKit software.
37Apple Watch can also work without an iPhone for select apps, including Apple Pay, Passbook flight check-ins, listening to music during a run and tracking fitness metrics.
38Oh, and for telling the time accurately too.
39The 8GB of storage with some restrictions limit the amount of photos and music that can be stored directly on the smartwatch, but it's still double the space of 4GB Android Wear watches, and most content streams from the iPhone.
40It's going to become especially convenient to pocket the 4.7-inch iPhone 6 and even bigger 5.5-inch iPhone 6 Plus in your jeans, or to always stow the thin, but still 9.7-inch iPad Air 2 in a bag.
41Is that phone call from a telemarketer not worth your time or an emergency from a loved one?
42Apple Watch makes mundane notifications easy to dismiss while keeping you in the loop with important alerts.
