Cyclone leaves trail of destruction

Friday's recap: Hornets 101, Bulls 91

1Cyclone Pam tore through the tiny South Pacific archipelago with winds of 270 kilometers (168 miles) per hour early on Saturday, leaving a trail of destruction and unconfirmed reports of dozens of deaths.
2Power remained out across Vanuatu throughout Saturday and people on many of the outer islands had no access to running water or outside communications, according to Chloe Morrison, a World Vision emergency communications officer in the capital Port Vila.
3Ms Morrison said communications have been so problematic that her aid group has not yet been able to account for many of its own 76 staff on the islands and authorities have been unable to assess the extent of the damage.
4"I can say that for anybody who wasn't in a secure shelter last night, it would have been a very, very tough time for them," she added.
5Vanuatu has a population of 267,000 spread over 65 islands.
6About 47,000 people live in the capital.
7Ms Morrison said authorities did a good job of moving thousands of people in Port Vila into 23 evacuation centres.
8With the winds and rain easing on Saturday, many people stepped out only to find that their homes were missing a roof or had disappeared, and were forced to return to the shelters.
9Teetering trees and downed power lines in Port Vila have made many areas hazardous, she said, adding that she had heard reports of entire villages being destroyed in more remote areas.
10"It's still really quite dangerous outside. Most people are still hunkering down," she said.
11Unicef estimated that 54,000 children were among those affected by the cyclone.
12UN Secretary General Ban Ki-moon said the impact and scope of the disaster caused by the cyclone was not yet clear, but he feared the damage and destruction could be widespread.
13"We hope the loss of life will be minimal," he said during the World Conference on Disaster Risk and Reduction in Japan.
14The UN said it was preparing to deploy emergency rapid response units.
15The president of Vanuatu, Baldwin Lonsdale, who was attending the conference, told participants: "I do not really know what impact the cyclone has had on Vanuatu.
16"I am speaking to you today with a heart that is so heavy. I stand to appeal on behalf of the government and the people to give a helping hand in this disaster."
17Ms Morrison said the first priority was to ensure people had adequate food, drinking water and shelter.
18Beyond that, she said, there would need to be a long and concerted rebuilding effort in the months ahead.
19She said the winds peaked between about midnight on Friday and 1am on Saturday.
20A westward change of course put populated areas directly in the path of Cyclone Pam.
21The UN Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs said there were unconfirmed reports of 44 deaths in Vanuatu's northeastern islands after Pam moved off its expected track.
22New Zealand has pledged one million New Zealand dollars ( 497,000) to help with relief efforts.
23Australia was preparing to send a crisis response team to Vanuatu if needed, Australian Foreign Minister Julie Bishop said.
24"There are destructive winds, rain, flooding, landslides, sea surges and very rough seas and the storm is exceedingly destructive there," she said.
25"We are still assessing the situation, but we stand ready to assist."
26The small island nation, located about a quarter of the way from Australia to Hawaii, has repeatedly warned it is already suffering devastating effects from climate change with the island's coastal areas being washed away, forcing resettlement to higher ground and smaller yields on traditional crops.
27Scientists say it is impossible to attribute single weather events like Cyclone Pam to climate change.
28The cyclone has already caused damage to other Pacific islands, including Kiribati and the Solomon Islands.
29Authorities in New Zealand are preparing for Cyclone Pam, which is forecast to pass north of the country on Sunday and Monday.
