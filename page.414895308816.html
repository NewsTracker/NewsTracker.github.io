Virginia ex-first lady to be sentenced for corruption

Clinton charity defends donations from foreign governments amid possible Hillary Clinton bid

1(Reuters) - Former Virginia first lady Maureen McDonnell was sentenced on Friday to 12 months and one day in prison for her federal corruption conviction for taking bribes from a businessman.
2McDonnell, 60, was also sentenced by U.S. District Judge James Spencer to two years of probation following release from prison.
3McDonnell, who lawyers for her estranged husband, former Republican Governor Robert McDonnell, blamed for triggering the corruption scandal, tearfully apologized during the three-hour sentencing hearing in a crowded courtroom.
4"I was the one who let the serpent into the mansion, and the venom from the serpent poisoned my marriage, my family and the Commonwealth I love," said McDonnell, who never testified during the five-week trial that brought down her and her husband.
5McDonnell and her husband were convicted in September for accepting $177,000 in sweetheart loans and lavish gifts from Star Scientific Inc Chief Executive Jonnie Williams in exchange for promoting his company's main product, the anti-inflammatory Anatabloc.
6The trial laid bare rifts in the couple's marriage and tarnished Virginia's reputation for clean government.
7Witnesses described the former first lady, the mother of five, as unstable and driven by greed.
8Spencer said during the hearing that the defense strategy of blaming the former first lady was "let's throw mama off the train."
9Although the McDonnells are living apart, the former governor gave his wife a highly visible kiss on the cheek when she entered the courtroom.
10The gifts from Williams included a $6,500 Rolex watch, wedding and engagement presents and golf outings and equipment.
11He provided a $50,000 loan and a $15,000 "gift" to cover wedding expenses for McDonnell's daughter.
12He also gave a $70,000 loan to a corporation that the governor and his sister used to manage beach properties.
13Maureen McDonnell was convicted of eight counts of corruption.
14Robert McDonnell, who left office in January 2014, was convicted on 11 counts and sentenced to two years in prison last month.
15Federal prosecutors had sought an 18-month sentence for the former first lady.
16Defense lawyers had argued for a community service sentence, saying she had been humiliated enough.
17The couple remain free pending appeals.
18McDonnell is the first Virginia governor to be convicted of a felony, and state lawmakers have advanced ethics measures prompted by the case.
1The foundation run by the Clintons and their daughter, Chelsea Clinton, has come under scrutiny for its practices of raising money from foreign governments as Hillary Clinton considers a presidential campaign in 2016.
2The Wall Street Journal reported the foundation received money in 2014 from the United Arab Emirates, Saudi Arabia and Oman.
3The Washington Post found the foundation had raised nearly $2 billion since the former president launched it in 2001.
4About one-third of the foundation's donations of $1 million or more came from foreign governments or entities based outside the United States, the Post analysis found.
5The Clinton Foundation said in a statement Thursday it receives support from "individuals, organizations and governments from all over the world" and that its programs improve the lives of millions of people.
6"Should Secretary Clinton decide to run for office, we will continue to ensure the foundation's policies and practices regarding support from international partners are appropriate, just as we did when she served as secretary of state," the foundation said.
7The foundation stopped raising money from foreign governments in 2009 when Hillary Clinton became President Barack Obama's secretary of state amid concerns the foundation's dealings with foreign entities might present a conflict of interest.
8The foundation resumed fundraising among foreign governments in 2013, after she left the administration.
9The foundation also agreed to disclose its donors online and noted in the statement that it has a "record of transparency that goes above what is required of U.S. charities."
10The foundation has said its Clinton Global Initiative has improved the lives of 430 million people in 180 countries since 2005.
11While Hillary Clinton remains the leading Democratic presidential contender should she launch a campaign, the foundation has been aggressively raising money in recent months with a goal of building a $250 million endowment to provide long-term stability.
