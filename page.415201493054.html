1Highlights include a summer 2016 run of Matilda the Musical, a Canadian premiere production of the Tony and Olivier Award winning show based on Roald Dahl's beloved novel; A Gentleman's Guide To Love & Murder, winner of the 2014 Tony Award for Best Musical; a production of David Hare's The Judas Kiss, starring Rupert Everett, a spring engagement of Ayad Akhtar's Pulitzer Prize-winning drama, Disgraced; and the return of the beloved Phantom of the Opera.
2As announced Wednesday, the shows in the Mainstage Subscription Series include Motown: The Musical (September-November 2015), the Broadway hit that follows the rise of superstar career builder Barry Gordy - the man behind the music of Diana Ross, Smokey Robinson and Michael Jackson.
3In time for Christmas comes Rodgers & Hammerstein's Cinderella, the Tony Award winning Broadway musical that offers glass slippers and sumptuous costumes, but with a contemporary take on the classic tale.
4For January and February of 2016, a production of the 1930s psychological thriller Gaslight (which became a 1944 film with Ingrid Bergman) comes to the Royal Alexandra, followed by The Judas Kiss (March-April 2016), with Rupert Everett playing Oscar Wilde in a tragic tale of love and betrayal.
5The contemporary Broadway musical If/Then - a tale of life in New York City today - plays at the Princess of Wales Theatre in April and May of next year.
6The play looks at one woman's two possible paths in life.
7A Gentleman's Guide To Love & Murder (May-June 2016) tells the story of one distant heir's solution to getting the money: murder!
8The story of Monty Navarro involves dusting off the eight relatives who stand between him and the money, even while keeping a girlfriend, a fiance and the police looking the other way.
9The highly anticipated Matilda The Musical starts in July 2016; the show, which won a record-breaking seven Olivier Awards, plays here with an all-Canadian cast.
10The 'Off-Mirvish' series of plays at the Panasonic includes the dark comedy Seminar, starring Tom McCamus (November 2015); Big Mouth, the remarkable show about rhetoric created, directed and performed by Valentijn Dhaenens (January-February 2016); and Disgraced, Ayad Akhtar's drama about big city aspiration and cultural assimilation (March-April 2016).
11Additionally, The Phantom Of The Opera returns to the Princess of Wales Theatre for a two-month run in December 2015 and Traces By 7 Fingers, the dance-and-acrobatics spectacle, plays at the Panasonic Theatre for two weeks, also in December.
12Other bonus shows include Oh Canada, What A Feeling!, a multi-media theatrical celebration of this country's music that includes the work of Gordon Lightfoot, The Guess Who, Anne Murray, Loverboy, Paul Anka and Neil Young, among many others (August 2015) and The Australian Bee Gees Show, a retrospective concert and theatre mix that explores the Bee Gees' musical legacy.
