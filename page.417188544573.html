Week in Tech: Week in Tech: Laser eyes, Apple's home invasion, and a new era for Spotify

1You know how the air feels when a thunderstorm is coming?
2This week's a bit like that, but we're expecting things with Lightning connectors instead of lightning bolts.
3Apple's about to unveil plans to control your home, car, wrist and the odd computer too, Spotify's getting into TV, Netflix is getting a new look and LG's covered things in cows.
4Apple's coming for your house.
5It's been using an app called Home to control HomeKit-based devices, and that app could be part of the imminent iOS 9.
6We're just weeks away from Apple's developer conference, WWDC, and we're expecting to see new versions of OS X and iOS - versions that might use the San Francisco font from the Apple Watch for their default system font.
7We're expecting to see Apple TV (the box, not the much rumoured, quite unlikely and thoroughly canned TV set) as a HomeKit hub, an earlier-than-usual iPhone 6S launch and probably nothing about the Apple Car we know Tim Cook's working on in his garage.
8You'll never guess who thinks the Apple Watch is "spectacular".
9No, not Jony Ive: Eric Migicovsky, CEO of Pebble.
10He's quite happy to see the big guns of tech move into the smartwatch space, because Pebble's offering is different: "We're focused on keeping it simple, affordable and useful."
11Meanwhile Apple addressed some of the initial flaws in Watch OS 1.0 by releasing - yes!
12- Watch OS 1.0.1, but rumoured features such as Find My Watch and enhanced Apple TV controls won't appear this side of WWDC.
13The Netflix interface is getting a big makeover, with the Web version getting it first and the many other Netflix-powered apps and devices getting it in June.
14It's faster, looks better, makes it easier to find information and generally makes it simpler to find the best Netflix TV shows.
15Good news for anybody who's always fancied blasting lasers from their eyes: the FOVE eye-tracking VR headset is coming to market, hopefully.
16Rumours of Microsoft interest didn't turn into anything concrete, so it's off to Kickstarter for a crowdfunding campaign.
17Eye-tracking isn't just a gimmick.
18It solves one of the biggest problems with VR, which is that you can't always focus on what you want to look at.
19By tracking your eye movements FOVE can tell where your pupils are pointing and adjust the display accordingly.
20If you fancy joining the Kickstarter it's $349 ( 225/AU$440).
21Big changes are afoot in the world of streaming: Spotify is getting a brand new version that includes not just music, but podcasts and even video content.
22It's also getting a running tool that will choose music according to how fast you're going, and which will turn up in Nike's Nike+ app later this year.
23Deezer's getting into podcasts too.
24Thanks to last year's acquisition of Stitcher, Deezer now offers more than 20,000 different podcasts and radio shows on the web and in its apps.
25It hopes to be a one-stop shop for everything aural, with freemium and premium services to take on Spotify, Tidal and Apple's Beats Music relaunch.
26Mobile versions of Windows suffer from the app gap, with rivals Android and iOS packing thousands more apps into their respective app stores.
27Is the gap closing?
28David Nield says yes, but also no.
29While the big hitters are largely there, "to buy a Windows Phone is to shut yourself out from most of the interesting, newer apps appearing on the scene as well as pretty much everything Google makes."
30Does the LG G4 offer anything new other than a leather case?
31We pitted the LG G4 against the LG G3 to find out.
32The leather isn't as nice as it looks in photos - it is "too thinly stretched over the back of the phone" - and the new processor is quad-core rather than octa-core like some other firms' flagships, but "it is indeed a more accomplished smartphone" and "has fixed some of the biggest issues with LG's last handset".
33That said, the G3 will be around for a while yet, and it'll cost a lot less.
34Our in-depth LG G4 review can help you decide whether you'd be better off with the outgoing model, or if the G4 will make you grin.
35Did Apple just leak the iPhone 6C?
