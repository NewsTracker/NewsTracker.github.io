Google Earnings: What to Watch

1Google (GOOGL) will report earnings after the bell on Thursday and investors will be watching some key metrics that are indicative of the company's performance.
2Analysts are predicting $17.5 billion in revenue for the quarter ending in March, according to Thomson Reuters data.
3Earnings per share is expected to come in at $6.60.
4This earnings are "primarily going to be about operating expenses and capital expenditure," said Sameet Sinha, analyst at B.
5Riley & Co. Google has been "showing steady revenue growth, but their expenditures continue to be very high."
6Analysts are expecting $6.3 billion in operating expenses and $2.5 billion in capital expenditures.
7Sinha is also watching Google's Shopping Express business, a delivery service that was expanded last year, in an effort to compete with Amazon (AMZN).
8There was an initial free trial period, but Sinha is wondering if Google will be able to convert those users to paid users.
9Increased viewership for mobile video should help YouTube's business, expects Sinha.
10"All of a sudden you find online video is seeing a hockey stick."
11More smartphones and faster broadband connections has made video "easily accessible by most people."
12James Cakmak, analyst at Monness Crespi Hardt, is focused on paid click growth, which has been decelerating.
13Cakmak is predicting an increase of 14% in paid click growth.
14Yahoo (YHOO) became the default search engine on the popular Mozilla Firefox browser late last year, a move that hurt Google's market share.
15Users are also spending more time on apps, resulting in decreased search engine usage.
16Google is bringing in a new CFO this upcoming quarter, Ruth Porat.
17Porat previously served as the chief financial officer for Morgan Stanley (MS).
18Cakmak said that Porat is expected to help the company with cost-cutting measures.
19"The market is hopeful the company will become more prudent on costs and investments."
20Google shares are up 4% year-to-date and are currently trading around $547.
21The company has a market cap of $374 billion.
