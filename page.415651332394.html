German tourist killed in Egypt shark attack

1The attack happened at a resort on Egypt's Red Sea, and police have revealed it is the first fatal shark attack in the region since 2010.
2The 52-year-old man's body has been moved to a local hospital after the attack yesterday.
3Officials are conducting an investigation into the attack, which saw the man's leg bitten off at the knee.
4State news agency Al-Ahram report that the tourist was swimming with his wife when the attack happened.
5An official from the German Embassy in Cairo says they received reports that a German citizen died in the area but are not aware of the cause of death.
6It was the first fatal shark attack in Egypt since a string of maulings in 2010 that left a German woman dead and three Russian tourists wounded in another Red Sea resort.
7In 2010, the German pensioner was mauled to death as she snorkelled in relatively shallow waters around the resort, which is famed for its scuba diving sites.
8She died just a day after Egyptian authorities lifted a 48-hour ban prompted by four other savage shark attacks.
9One victim lost a hand and another lost a leg.
10The Red Sea is home to several species of shark, but the type believed to have been behind the 2010 attacks was the oceanic whitetip common to the region.
11The attacks in 2010 forced the government to close off a stretch of beach in the Sharm el-Sheikh resort for a week.
12In 2009, a shark killed a French woman off another resort close to where the German tourist was attacked on Sunday.
