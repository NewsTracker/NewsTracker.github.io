Baltimore awakes to state of emergency, week-long curfew

1Baltimore's mayor and police commissioner urged calm Tuesday evening as the city prepared for a second night of unrest following riots Monday that left parts of the city in rubble and disarray.
2"For the most part, the city has been calm today," said Commissioner Anthony Batts at a joint press conference with the mayor late Tuesday.
3He said police made several arrests after some "opportunists" tried to loot some businesses in the eastern part of the city, but in general, residents pulled together to bring "calm and peace" back to the city, which remains under a state of emergency.
4Batts said the presence of law enforcement in the city has been boosted by the arrival of state troops, the Maryland National Guard as well as officers from New Jersey, Pennsylvania, the D.C. area and several Maryland counties.
5The entire city is under curfew from 10 p.m. to 5 a.m. ET, but groups of demonstrators continued to march in the streets through the evening, with musicians, marching bands and cheerleaders lending a festive air to the gatherings.
6"We have a long night ahead of us," said Maryland Gov. Larry Hogan.
7Hogan said authorities have "a lot of manpower on the streets" to ensure that officers "don't get overwhelmed as happened last night."
8He said about 2,000 members of the National Guard and 1,000 law enforcement officers would be in place overnight.
9Hogan said that so far, people have welcomed the presence of National Guard and state troops on Baltimore streets.
10"People are concerned. They want us to restore law and order," he said.
11"Despite the tension, a lot of people are thanking us for being here."
12Baltimore spent Tuesday cleaning up after riots that kicked off soon after the funeral of Freddie Gray, a 25-year-old black man who died in Baltimore police custody.
13Hundreds of volunteers swept the streets of broken glass and other debris and boarded up the windows of looted and burned-out businesses and buildings.
14Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake thanked all those who "have spent all day yesterday, all day today trying to figure how we can come together as a city, how we can heal."
15She said she was forced to keep public schools closed Tuesday because many teachers had called to say they would not come to work.
16They will reopen Wednesday.
17Many businesses and government offices were closed Tuesday.
18The Baltimore Orioles cancelled their Tuesday game at Camden Yards and - in what may be a first in baseball's 145-year history - said their Wednesday night game against the White Sox would be closed to the public.
19A demonstrator has his faced washed off after police reacted with pepper spray when items were thrown at officers outside a looted and burned CVS pharmacy in Baltimore on Tuesday.
20By contrast, the Baltimore Symphony Orchestra announced it would give a free noontime concert Wednesday.
21Authorities remained vigilant against the possibility of another outbreak of looting and arson overnight.
22"We're not going to leave the city unprotected," Hogan said earlier in the day during a visit to a West Baltimore intersection where cars were burned and windows smashed the night before.
23Commissioner Batts responded to criticism that police had been unprepared for Tuesday's escalation of violence and looting by saying that several hundred officers had been deployed to the area around Mondawmin Mall in West Baltimore where posts on social media had urged demonstrators to gather even before the riots began but had deliberately held back when clashes broke out because those participating were teenagers.
24"Do you want people using force against 14-,15-, 16-year-old kids?" he said.
25"They're old enough to know better. They're old enough to be accountable. But they're still kids."
26State police and law officers from other jurisdictions joined Baltimore police in patrolling the streets Tuesday.
27National Guardsmen in riot helmets with face shields surrounded City Hall, standing behind bicycle-rack barriers.
28It was the first time the National Guard was called out to quell unrest in Baltimore since 1968, when some of the same neighbourhoods burned after the assassination of the Rev.
29Maryland National Guardsmen patrol near downtown businesses in Baltimore, Tuesday, a day after looting and arson erupted.
30As firefighters doused smoldering fires around the city, many lamented the damage done by the rioters to their own neighbourhoods.
31Hundreds of volunteers helped shopkeepers clean up as helmeted officers blocked a stretch of North Avenue in the neighbourhood where Gray was arrested earlier in this month in a case that has become the latest flashpoint in the national debate over the police use of deadly force against black men.
32Baltimore Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake tours the city Tuesday in the aftermath of Monday's rioting.
33Hardware stores donated trash bags and brooms, and city workers brought in trucks to haul away mounds of trash and broken glass.
34With schools closed, Blanca Tapahuasco brought her three sons, ages 2 to 8, from another part of the city to help sweep the brick-and-pavement courtyard outside a looted CVS pharmacy.
35"We're helping the neighbourhood build back up," she said.
36"This is an encouragement to them to know the rest of the city is not just looking on and wondering what to do."
37CVS store manager Haywood McMorris said the destruction didn't make sense: "We work here, man. This is where we stand, and this is where people actually make a living."
38The rioting started in West Baltimore on Monday afternoon - less than 2 km from where Gray was arrested - and by midnight had spread to East Baltimore and neighbourhoods close to downtown and near the baseball stadium.
39Marchers from the 300 Men March arrive at Pennsylvania Avenue and and W.
40North Avenue in Baltimore.
41The rioters set police cars and buildings on fire, looted a mall and liquor stores and hurled rocks, bottles and cinderblocks at police in riot gear.
42Police responded occasionally with pepper spray or cleared the streets by moving in tight formation, shoulder to shoulder.
43At least 15 officers were hurt.
44There were 144 vehicle fires, 15 structure fires and about 250 arrests over Monday and Tuesday.
45"They just outnumbered us and outflanked us," Batts said earlier Tuesday.
46"We needed to have more resources out there."
47The rioting was the worst such violence in the U.S. since the turbulent protests that broke out over the death of Michael Brown, the unarmed black 18-year-old who was shot by a white police officer in Ferguson, Mo., last summer.
48"I understand anger, but what we're seeing isn't anger," Rawlings-Blake lamented.
49"It's disruption of a community. The same community they say they care about, they're destroying. You can't have it both ways."
50State and local authorities found themselves responding to questions about whether their initial response had been adequate.
51Rawlings-Blake waited hours to ask the governor to declare a state of emergency, and the governor hinted she should have come to him earlier.
52People clean up a CVS drug store Tuesday that was looted and set on fire during clashes with police on Monday in Baltimore.
53"We were all in the command centre in the second floor of the state House in constant communication, and we were trying to get in touch with the mayor for quite some time," Hogan said at a Monday evening news conference.
54"She finally made that call, and we immediately took action."
55Rawlings-Blake said officials initially thought they had gotten the unrest under control.
56Maryland National Guard spokesman Lt. Charles Kohler said that about 2,000 members would be deployed through the day and that the force could build to 5,000.
57"We are going to be out in massive force, and that just means basically that we are going to be patrolling the streets and out to ensure that we are protecting property," said Maj.-Gen. Linda Singh, adjutant general of the Maryland National Guard.
58Also, State police said they were putting out a call for up to 500 additional law enforcement officers from Maryland and as many as 5,000 from around the mid-Atlantic region.
59Gray was arrested April 12 after running away at the sight of police, authorities said.
60He was held down, handcuffed and loaded into a police van.
61Leg cuffs were put on him when he became irate inside.
62He died of a spinal cord injury a week later.
63Authorities said they are still investigating how and when he suffered the injury - during the arrest or while he was in the van, where authorities say he was riding without being belted in, a violation of department policy.
64A report into the death is expected Friday, CNN reported.
65Six officers have been suspended with pay while the investigation continues.
66While they are angry about what happened to Gray, his family said riots are not the answer.
67"I think the violence is wrong," Gray's twin sister, Fredericka Gray, said late Monday.
68"I don't like it at all."
