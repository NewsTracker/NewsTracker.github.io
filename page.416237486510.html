Fighting in eastern Ukraine rages on overnight despite talks

1SHYROKYNE, Ukraine (AP) - Fighting raged overnight and in the early hours on Tuesday on the outskirts of the rebel stronghold of Donetsk in eastern Ukraine despite an agreement reached by the Ukrainian and Russian foreign ministers a day earlier.
2The yearlong military conflict between Russian-backed rebels and government forces has claimed more than 6,000 lives and left large parts of Ukraine's once industrial heartland in ruins.
3Fighting in the east had largely subsided following a cease-fire deal signed in February but has rekindled in recent days.
4Heavy shelling was heard in Donetsk late Monday evening and in the early hours on Tuesday.
5Ukrainian military spokesman Andriy Lysenko told a televised briefing that six troops were killed and 12 wounded in a 24-hour period, while rebels reported one fighter dead and five wounded in the overnight clashes.
6The death toll is the highest since the February cease-fire was signed.
7Lysenko also reported civilian casualties, including two teenagers who were wounded in shelling in the Horlivka area, north of Donetsk.
8Russia and Ukraine agreed in Berlin on Monday to call for the pullback of smaller-caliber weapons from the front lines of the conflict that has claimed more than 6,000 lives.
9On the ground, however, even the previous agreement that called for a cease-fire and a withdrawal of large-caliber weaponry appeared to be shaky.
10The U.S. State Department supports the steps taken in Berlin, spokeswoman Marie Harf said Tuesday, but she also voiced concern about the ongoing fighting.
11"The Russian-backed separatists continue to take aggressive action in Ukraine," she said.
12"And the Russian-backed separatists continue to stall Minsk implementation."
13A Russian journalist was injured Tuesday morning when he stepped on a land mine in Shyrokyne during a visit to the village on the Azov Sea that has been the epicenter of recent fighting.
14Andrei Lunev, a videojournalist who works for the Zvezda television station, received first aid at the scene and was then whisked away to the nearest hospital.
15At one point during the visit the group had to lie on the ground because of an exchange of fire nearby.
16Shyrokyne has changed hands repeatedly throughout the conflict.
17An Associated Press reporter saw a column of at least 10 infantry combat vehicles moving Tuesday morning from the rebel-occupied area by the Azov Sea to Donetsk.
