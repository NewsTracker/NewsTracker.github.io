Workers clear 80 percent of debris from Germanwings crash

1Lufthansa representative Carsten Hernig said Wednesday that cleanup teams had cleared 35 tons of waste as of Tuesday.
2Hernig is overseeing the operation to recover pieces of the shattered plane, operated by Lufthansa's low-cost carrier Germanwings.
3He said workers will ensure that the debris, including 4 tons of kerosene and 90 liters of lubricants, are cleared safely and without leaving environmental damage to the Alpine mountainside.
4Hernig spoke to reporters alongside French officials in the town of Digne-les-Bains.
