21The excitement continued to build on Sunday, as fellow survivors who volunteer at the centre arrived to do Ms Sankoh's hair and help her pick a new outfit for her release.
22Early on Monday morning, staff arrived in full protective gear to collect her old clothes and escort her through a chlorinated shower, which they have dubbed "the happy shower".
23When she emerged, she was dressed all in white, in a traditional gown and headscarf, Mr Boyes said.
24She was then invited to add her hand print to a survivors' wall and left the centre at the head of a procession of singing, clapping and drumming caregivers.
25"Although my child died of Ebola, I am very happy that I have survived," Ms Sankoh told the waiting crowd, according to the AP.
26She appealed to the president to provide more help for survivors, noting that she might need care again.
27The disease has left some patients with persistent health problems, including lost vision, joint and muscle pain, chronic fatigue, depression and psychological trauma, said Dr Vanessa Wolfman, the International Medical Corps' medical director in Sierra Leone.
28At the same time, it has ravaged local healthcare facilities, some of which became sources of infection for employees and patients.
29As the country struggles to rebuild confidence in its battered health system, it is also grappling with the economic effect of a disease that has kept farmers from their fields and left many communities under quarantine for months.
