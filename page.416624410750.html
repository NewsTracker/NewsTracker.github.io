AP Source: Thunder, Donovan in talks about coaching vacancy (Yahoo Sports)

1GAINESVILLE, Fla.
2(AP) -- Florida coach Billy Donovan is entertaining a move to the NBA again.
3This time with the Oklahoma City Thunder, a person familiar with the search said.
4Eight years after leaving Florida to coach the Orlando Magic and then changing his mind the following day, he appears poised to make the jump and not look back this time.
5Donovan spent Wednesday in talks with the Thunder about their coaching vacancy, the person told The Associated Press on condition of anonymity because details of the search have not been released.
6The person said Donovan and Thunder general manager Sam Presti have spoken repeatedly about the job, which came open when Scott Brooks was fired last week, but that there is ''still big-picture stuff'' to work out before contract negotiations could begin.
7Donovan did not respond to text messages or a phone call from the AP seeking comment Wednesday.
8Yahoo Sports reported Donovan is ''enthusiastic'' about the possibility of coaching the Thunder and is seeking an annual salary around $6 million.
9Donovan signed a one-year contract extension with Florida in December that pays him an average salary of $4 million through 2020.
10The buyout on the contract is $500,000.
11The coach has strong ties to the Oklahoma City franchise.
12Former Florida assistant Mark Daigneault was hired as Oklahoma City's D-League coach last year, while former Florida video coordinator Oliver Winterbone is a scout with the Thunder.
13Presti and Donovan have a long relationship, and Presti reportedly spent time visiting Donovan in February.
14Donovan, who will turn 50 next month, has spent the last 19 years in Gainesville.
15He led the Gators to two national championship, four Final Fours, seven Elite Eights and 14 NCAA Tournament berths.
16He has always maintained an interest in the NBA, even saying last year that basketball's elite level intrigues him because of the ability to coach and teach daily.
17Last season was a down year by Donovan's standards.
18Florida finished 16-17 and missed the postseason for the first time since 1997.
19Three players have already left the program in what many believed was a house cleaning.
20Donovan also hired former Alabama coach Anthony Grant as an assistant, reuniting Donovan, Grant and John Pelphrey - the trio that made hoops relevant in Gainesville.
21It's unclear if Grant and Pelphrey would follow Donovan to the NBA.
22This much is certain: with Kevin Durant and Russell Westbrook in the fold, Oklahoma City is one of the NBA's premier coaching jobs.
23Brooks was 338-207 (.620) in seven seasons with the Thunder and was the 2009-10 NBA Coach of the Year.
24Starting in 2010-11, he led the team to the Western Conference finals three out of four years, and the franchise reached the NBA Finals in 2012.
25This season, a broken right foot limited Durant to 27 games and right knee surgery kept defensive stalwart Serge Ibaka out for the final month of the season.
26Westbrook won the scoring title and posted 11 triple-doubles, but the Thunder were knocked out of the playoff race on the final night of the regular season.
27Presti praised Brooks' effort and said his decision was not based on this season's performance.
28Presti said he knows he's taking a chance by replacing Brooks a year before Durant can become a free agent, but he felt the franchise could regress if a change wasn't made.
29Durant and Ibaka are expected to be ready next season, and the Thunder should again be among the favorites to contend for the title.
30The next day or so could reveal whether Donovan will be coaching them.
31Donovan has strong ties to Gainesville, which is what kept him from keeping the Magic job in 2007.
32Donovan's two oldest children are out of high school - his son had been a walk-on at Florida - but his youngest son is in his junior year of high school and youngest daughter is in eighth grade.
33Donovan also was instrumental in helping build St. Francis Catholic High School in Gainesville.
34Donovan talked with the Minnesota Timberwolves and Cleveland Cavaliers about openings last season, but decided to stay at Florida.
35AP Sports Writer Cliff Brunt in Oklahoma City contributed to this report.
