Colombia stuns France 2-0 in Women's World Cup (Yahoo Sports)

1France's Elise Bussaglia, right, and Colombia's Daniela Montoya battle for the ball during the first half of a FIFA Women's World Cup soccer game in Moncton, New Brunswick, Canada, on Saturday, June 13, 2015.
2Lady Andrade and Catalina Usme scored goals as Colombia stunned third-ranked France 2-0 Saturday in one of the biggest upsets in Women's World Cup history.
3The underdog Colombians opened the scoring in the 19th minute.
4Andrade broke free from a pair of defenders for a tap-in goal.
5Usme clinched her country's first World Cup win with a goal on the counter attack in the 93rd minute.
6''We've proved that we're not just here to make up the numbers.
7We knew that we'd have to play intelligently and fight for every ball.
8My players are real battlers,'' Colombia coach Fabian Taborda said of his 28th-ranked team.
9The French lost despite outshooting Colombia 21-3 in Moncton, New Brunswick.
10Much of that was thanks to the play of Colombian goalkeeper Sandra Sepulveda, who made a number of sprawling saves that increasingly frustrated the French attack.
11''There were numerous aspects that weren't quite right (Saturday).
12We weren't accurate enough with our passes at key moments.
13Despite that, we still created chances, but their goalkeeper had a great match,'' France coach Philippe Bergeroo said.
14France was unlucky as well.
15Colombian defender Daniela Montoya appeared to touch the ball with her hand inside the penalty area in the 67th minute, but a foul wasn't called.
16France wraps up group play against Mexico on Wednesday, while the Colombians face England.
