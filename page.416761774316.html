Lava explodes from Hawaii?s Kilauea volcano after crater collapses

1Smoke and lava explode from Kilauea volcano on 's Big Island.
2HONOLULU - Molten lava, rocks and gas went flying through the air on Hawaii's Kilauea volcano after an explosion was caused by the partial collapse of a crater wall.
3The collapse triggered a small explosion, spreading lava and debris around the rim of Kilauea's Halemaumau Crater, the U.S. Geological Survey's Hawaiian Volcano Observatory says.
4Janet Babb, a geologist with the USGS, compared the blast on Sunday to taking a hammer to the top of a bottle of champagne.
5"You look at the bottle and you see the liquid, but you don't see the gas," she said.
6"There's a lot of gas in the lava. And so, when that rock fall hits the lava lake, it's like the moment you knock the top of the champagne bottle off and that gas is released and it hurls molten lava and rock fragments."
7Rocks overhanging the lava lake are altered by gases coming from the lava, Babb said.
8The rocks eventually give way and collapse into the lava, causing an explosion.
9Video of the event shows a wall of rocks sliding into a lava lake that last week rose to a record-high level.
10The slide caused an explosion that sent fist-size chunks of rock onto the closed Halemaumau visitor overlook, according to the Geological Survey.
11The area has been closed since 2008, when the lava lake formed, and no one was injured.
12There could be fallout of ash and dust from this type of event, but it's very unlikely that anyone could be injured, Babb said.
13Wind direction dictates the amount of debris that lands in visitor areas, and it is relatively common, she said.
14The last time molten lava was visible in the crater was in 1982, when a fissure erupted.
15The last time there was a lake similar to this one was in 1974.
16The vent within Halemaumau Crater has been rising and falling since it first opened, but it reached a record high last week.
17Even at its previous highest level in October 2012, the lake was too low for people to see.
18During the day, people could view the gas rising from the lake, and at night people could see the orange glow from the lava.
19From the early 1800s up until 1924, there was a continuous lake of lava at Kilauea summit within Halemaumau.
20At that time, the crater was about half the diameter of what it is now.
21In 1924, there was a huge eruption inside the volcano that doubled the size of the crater.
22Since 1924, lava lakes have been present at different times.
23In 1967 and 1968, the entire crater was filled with molten lava.
24You can still see a "bathtub ring" on the walls of the crater where the lava had risen to at that time.
25A magnitude 3.6 earthquake was felt in the area early Monday morning, according to the Geological Survey.
