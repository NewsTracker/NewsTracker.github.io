A fresh Start: Microsoft's Windows 10 wins plaudits

1A Microsoft logo is seen on an office building in New York City, July 28, 2015.
2The global launch of the Microsoft Windows 10 operating system will take place on July 29.
3(Reuters) - Microsoft Corp's (MSFT.O) launch of its first new operating system in almost three years, designed to work across laptops, desktop and smartphones, won mostly positive reviews for its user-friendly and feature-packed interface.
4The launch was seen critical for the company, particularly after the poor response to Windows 8, an ambitious redesign that confused many users by ditching the start button menu and introducing a new layout.
5The new OS, launched on Wednesday, will be available as a free upgrade for users of Windows 7, Windows 8.1 and Windows Phone 8.1.
6Wall Street Journal's Geoffrey Fowler said Windows 10 fixes most of the issues with Windows 8 and improves on Windows 7, Microsoft's most popular OS in recent years.
7For one, the start button is back.
8"Three months of testing Windows 10 determined that this familiar yet fresh overhaul far outweighs any problems," Fowler wrote in his review.
9By offering free upgrades, Microsoft aims to put the system on as many devices as possible.
10The company would then make up for any lost revenue by selling services such as Office over the Internet, or cloud.
11FBR Capital Markets analyst Daniel Ives said the cloud strategy could generate a new revenue stream.
12"It's a step in the right direction. They went back to their core DNA around software and a cloud-centric model and ultimately Windows as a service, a subscription model," Ives told Reuters.
13Microsoft, which unveiled Windows 10 in September, skipped Windows 9 altogether, to put some distance between the new system and Windows 8.
14At least one prominent reviewer was skeptical, saying the new OS was unlikely to boost PC sales or salvage Microsoft's Windows phone business.
15Re/code's Walt Mossberg said some features were promising but the new OS was "surprisingly buggy".
16"I doubt it will convert many Mac owners, spur a shopping spree in new PCs, bring in droves of new developers, or save the Windows Phone," Mossberg wrote.
17Almost all critics lauded the return of the Start button.
18"The newly revamped Start menu in Windows 10 is a useful combination of Windows past and present and a metaphor for the entire direction of Windows 10,".
19Windows 10 also comes equipped with 'Cortana', a digital assistant similar to Apple Inc's (AAPL.O) Siri and Google Inc's (GOOG.O) Now.
