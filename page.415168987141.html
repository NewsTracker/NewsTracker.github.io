1But the fact that an English prince met yesterday with "red princeling" Xi Jinping, Chairman of the Chinese Communist Party and the President of the People's Republic of China, is still, if you think about it, strikingly odd.
2What could the symbolic heir to the British empire and China's avowedly anti-imperialist new leader have to talk about?
3Not history.
4Since coming to power in 2013, Xi has spoken at length about the great "rejuvenation" of the nation.
5The message is that after suffering centuries of humiliation at the hands of foreign powers, the country, under the leadership of the Chinese Communist Party, is once again on the rise.
6So while Prince William toured Beijing's Forbidden City, his handlers probably did not mention the fact that the British burned the city's other great palace, Yuanmingyuan, on Oct. 18, 1860.
7Or that Anglo-French forces looted its treasures.
8Nor can they talk about Hong Kong.
9The fate of the former British colony returned to Chinese sovereignty in 1997 might be old news by now, if not for the months-long protests that shuttered parts of the Chinese Special Administrative Region last fall.
10Many, including the protesters themselves, say the movement was a grassroots push for genuine universal suffrage.
11Beijing blamed "hostile foreign forces."
12And they certainly can't talk about family.
13Prince William and Xi Jinping are both royalty in their own right - the former, a Windsor, the latter, a scion of China's red royalty.
14(Xi's father Xi Zhongxun was a contemporary of Mao and a hero of the Long March.)
15But don't expect either to bring that up.
16Xi rarely trades on his pedigree, at least publicly, preferring to cast himself as just another cadre.
17And William, if he's wise, will know that royalty is a touchy subject in China, not least because his grandfather, the racist-gaffe-prone Prince Philip once warned an exchange student about "slitty-eyed" Chinese, and his father Prince Charles once called China's leaders "appalling old waxworks."
18It's safe to guess that absolutely none of this came up during the first two days of the tightly choreographed three-day tour.
19After landing in Shanghai, the prince opened a festival and met with business leaders including Alibaba's Jack Ma.
20On Tuesday, local time, he watched Chinese students play soccer (football) to mark the addition of the sport to the Chinese curriculum.
