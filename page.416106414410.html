Wisconsin man accused of trying to join Islamic State group

1(AP) - A Wisconsin man traveled to the Middle East in a failed attempt to join the Islamic State group, federal authorities said Thursday.
2Joshua Van Haften, 34, of Madison, was arrested Wednesday night at O'Hare International Airport in Chicago after returning on a flight from Turkey.
3He is charged in a criminal complaint with attempting to provide material support to a foreign terrorist organization.
4Van Haften was scheduled to appear Thursday morning in federal court in Madison.
5The criminal complaint, which had been under seal since Oct. 28, alleges that Van Haften talked to people about his plan before leaving the United States in August for Istanbul.
6The complaint cites Facebook posts from Van Haften that said he was unable to cross the border from Turkey into Syria.
7In one post, Van Haften complained that the people who were supposed to help him just wanted his money, that he had almost no cash left and that he was dropped off on a road in the country and never picked up.
8Wisconsin court records showed several previous convictions for Van Haften, including felony battery in 1998, second degree felony sexual assault in 1999 and disorderly conduct, a misdemeanor, in 2007, online court records show.
9All of the offenses occurred in Rock County.
10Van Haften was sentenced to three years' probation on the battery conviction in 1998, but his probation was revoked in 1999 and he was sentenced to a year in jail.
11He was sentenced to eight years' probation on the sexual assault charge in 1999, but that was revoked in 2000.
12He was then sentenced to seven years in prison.
