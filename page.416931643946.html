Global stocks ruffled by bond gyrations, Greek cash crunch

1SEOUL, South Korea (AP) - Asian stock markets were tepid Tuesday after the latest talks between Greece and its European creditors failed to reach agreement on bailout terms as the Greek government runs low on cash.
2Chinese stocks were a bright spot, boosted by an interest rate cut on the weekend.
3KEEPING SCORE: Japan's Nikkei 225 was flat at 19,624.84 and South Korea's Kospi was also little changed at 2,096.77.
4Hong Kong's Hang Seng was down 0.5 percent to 27,574.84.
5But Australia's S&P/ ASX 200 rose 0.9 percent to 5,674.70 and China's Shanghai Composite index advanced 1.1 percent to 4,382.01.
6GREEK TALKS: Eurozone official Jeroen Dijsselbloem said progress was made at Monday's meeting among finance ministers from Greece and other European countries but more time and effort was needed to reach a deal.
7Greece is facing an acute cash crunch that many in financial markets think could see the country default on its debts, impose restrictions on capital flows and possibly leave the euro currency bloc.
8THE QUOTE: "A failure to resolve the Greek funding crisis is adding to the pressure on local shares," Michael McCarthy, chief market strategist at CMC Markets, said in a commentary.
9"While the rest of the world is now largely economically insulated from Greece, risks remain."
10CHINA CUT: Chinese stocks continued to get a boost from Sunday's interest rate cut, which was the third cut in half a year aimed at shoring up sputtering economic growth.
11The central bank's latest move came after trade data released on Friday showed imports and exports declined in April suggesting domestic and foreign demand are slowing.
12At the same time, inflation remains low, giving policymakers leeway to ease monetary policy as they strive to keep growth from falling below a 7 percent target.
13WALL STREET: The U.S. stock market closed lower on Monday.
14The Dow fell 0.5 percent to 18,105.17.
15The Standard & Poor's 500 lost 0.5 percent to 2,105.33.
16The Nasdaq composite slipped 0.2 percent to 4,993.57.
17ENERGY: Benchmark U.S. crude slipped 10 cents to $59.15 a barrel in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
18The contract fell 14 cents to close at $59.25 a barrel on Monday.
19Brent crude, a benchmark for international oils, fell 16 cents to $65.46 in London.
20CURRENCIES: The dollar fell to 120.07 yen from 120.16 yen in the previous trading session.
21The euro strengthened to $1.1199 from $1.1147.
