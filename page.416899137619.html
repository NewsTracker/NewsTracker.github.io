2 babies die, 29 sickened from bad vaccines in south Mexico

1MEXICO CITY (AP) - Mexico's public health system has suspended infant vaccines and mounted an investigation after two babies died and 29 were sickened in an impoverished community in southern Mexico.
2Six of the 29 babies are in grave condition after receiving vaccinations for tuberculosis, rotovirus and Hepatitis B, which are generally administered between 0 and 6 months, according to a national schedule.
3The cause of the adverse reactions is not known, the Mexican Institute for Social Security said Sunday.
4The institute said it stopped vaccines nationwide on Saturday as a precaution.
5The Rev.
6Marcelo Perez, a Roman Catholic priest, told The Associated Press that families of the babies said they became sick within hours.
7The adverse reactions started Friday and the babies were being treated in a hospital in Simojovel, Chiapas, where 93 percent of the people live in poverty, 69 percent in extreme poverty, according to government statistics.
8The hospital "doesn't have adequate personnel or equipment," Perez said.
9"The real problem is the terrible conditions we have ... so that when a baby comes in with convulsions, he leaves dead."
10The federal and state government, in a statement Sunday, promised the best medical care for the babies and to stay in contact with the parents to answer all their questions.
11Perez said he was helping the families collect all the information that could help officials discover the cause of the adverse reactions.
