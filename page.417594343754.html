Britain to send 125 more troops to Iraq in fight against IS

1LONDON (AP) - Prime Minister David Cameron says Britain will send 125 more troops to Iraq to train local forces fighting the Islamic State group.
2The deployment brings to 275 the number of U.K. personnel helping the Baghdad government and Kurdish authorities battle the militants.
3Cameron made the announcement Sunday at a meeting of G-7 leaders in Germany, saying IS terrorism poses one of the world's biggest challenges.
4The government says most of the new troops will train local forces in dealing with roadside bombs.
5They will be based around the country.
6Britain says the troops will remain on their bases and the U.K. will not send combat troops to Iraq, where 179 British personnel died between 2003 and 2009.
7British warplanes are part of the U.S.-led coalition attacking IS targets in Iraq.
