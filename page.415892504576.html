Some parents of missing Mexico youths ask help of gang boss

1Federal prosecutors have alleged that local police in Guerrero state turned the students over to a different drug gang, Guerreros Unidos, which killed the young men and burned their bodies.
2Relatives of the missing have rejected the government's account of what happened in the city of Iguala.
3Several families hung two banners in Iguala on Tuesday asking for help from Santiago Mazari Hernandez, the alleged leader of the Reds gang, a rival of Guerreros Unidos.
4In February, the Reds put up banners saying the gang had nothing to do with the students' disappearance Sept.
526 but adding that Mazari was open to meeting with relatives to talk about the case.

7He said the banners were not part of the group activities, but were put up some families out of desperation.
