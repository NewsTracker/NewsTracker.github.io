Black box from Germanwings crash in French Alps found: minister

1A rescue helicopter from the French Securite Civile flies over the French Alps during a rescue operation after the crash of an Airbus A320, near Seyne-les-Alpes, March 24, 2015.
2An Airbus plane operated by Lufthansa's Germanwings budget airline, en route from Barcelona to Duesseldorf, crashed in a remote snowy area of the French Alps on Tuesday and all 150 on board were feared dead.
3SEYNE-LES-ALPES, France (Reuters) - French Interior Minister Bernard Cazeneuve said on Monday that one of the black boxes from the Germanwings plane that crashed in the Alps has been found.
4"A black box that we found a few hours after the crash will immediately be examined to help the investigation move forward quickly," he told reporters.
5He said the crash site would be secured this evening to ensure emergency services could more easily enter the area.
