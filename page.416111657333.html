U.S. man charged with trying to support Islamic State

1Joshua Ray Van Haften, 34, of Madison, Wisconsin, intended to travel to Iraq or Syria to join the group, also known by the acronyms ISIL and ISIS, prosecutors said in a statement.
2Van Haften was arrested at Chicago's O'Hare International Airport on Wednesday after arriving on a flight from Turkey.
3He had been in the custody of Turkish immigration authorities since October, according to U.S. Attorney John Vaudreuil.
4A U.S. magistrate judge ordered Van Haften detained after a brief hearing in U.S. District Court in Madison on Thursday.
5"Our charge was that he was planning to commit violent acts with ISIL and aid them as a terrorist organization," Vaudreuil told reporters after the hearing.
6If convicted, Van Haften faces up to 15 years in prison.
7Vaudreuil said prosecutors do not have evidence that Van Haften made physical contact with Islamic State militants.
8Reached by email, Van Haften's attorney, Joseph Bugni, with Federal Defender Services of Wisconsin, said he had no comment.
9Prosecutors allege that Van Haften left the United States on Aug. 26, 2014, bound for Turkey, which borders Syria and Iraq.
10While there, he exchanged a series of Facebook messages with friends and interacted through the social network with other alleged Islamic State supporters, according to prosecutors.
11In one series of messages with a former roommate in the United States in October, he was asked when he would return home.
12He replied: "Turkey is looking like it is divided at the moment about attacking ISIS, which if they do, I'm gonna attack them," according to prosecutors.
13He added: "I was about to cross to Syria ... but I didn't make it. Hopefully soon though. ... I'm tired of this life man."
14A grand jury is set to consider the case later in April.
15Van Haften is scheduled to appear in court next on April 24.
