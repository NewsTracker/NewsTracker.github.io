Aust to recall ambassador to Indonesia

1The Abbott government will recall Australia's ambassador to Indonesia Paul Grigson following the executions of Bali Nine drug smugglers Andrew Chan and Myuran Sukumaran on Wednesday.
2Prime Minister Tony Abbott described the executions as "cruel and unnecessary".
3The Bali Nine ring leaders were among eight foreign nationals shot dead by firing squad on the island of Nusakambangan.
4"We deplore what's been done and this cannot be simply business as usual," he told reporters in Canberra on Wednesday.
5Mr Abbott said Australia's reaction could not be "simply business as usual".
6"For that reason ... our ambassador will be withdrawn for consultations."
7Mr Abbott said Mr Grigson would be withdrawn once arrangements for the Chan and Sukumaran families had been sorted.
8The prime minister acknowledged it was "unusual, indeed unprecedented" for Australia to withdraw an ambassador, but the government thought it was necessary.
9"Whatever people think of the death penalty, whatever people think of drug crime, the fact is that these two families have suffered an appalling tragedy," he said.
10"As a parent, as a family member myself, I feel for these families at what is a very, very difficult time."
