Microsoft presents Surface 3 - a cheaper version of Surface Pro 3 tablet

1This product image provided by Microsoft shows the company's new Surface 3 tablet.
2Microsoft is making the cheaper version of its Surface Pro 3 tablet computer in an effort to reach more people.
3Microsoft is making a cheaper version of its Surface Pro 3 tablet computer in an effort to reach students and budget-conscious families.
4Graphic artists, engineers and finance professionals needing to run complex software might still want the company's higher-end Pro 3, said Dennis Meinhardt, director of program management for Surface.
5But the Surface 3 should be good for everyday tasks, he said, and brings the Pro 3's premium feel to a device that will be affordable to more people.
6Priced in Canada at $639 (vs $849 to $1,999 for the Pro 3), the new tablet has a slightly smaller screen - 10.8 inches rather than 12 - a slower processor, and less flexible kickstand - just three angles rather than unlimited positions.
7A version with more storage and memory will cost $599.
8A keyboard cover, one of the Surface's distinctive features, adds another $129.
9Microsoft bills the Surface line as a laptop replacement when used with the cover.
10And Microsoft will sell a stylus separately for $49.
11(It's included with the Pro 3.)
12Panos Panay, Microsoft's vice president for surface computing, introduced the Surface Pro 3 tablet in May 2014.
13The new Surface model is thinner and lighter, partly because it gets rid of the fan - similar to Apple's new MacBook laptop.
14At 214 pixels per inch, the screen resolution is comparable to the Pro 3's.
15Battery life is promised at 10 hours for video playback.
16The Surface 3 runs the regular version of Windows 8.1, just like the Pro 3.
17In the past, Microsoft has used a lightweight version called RT in its cheaper tablets.
18The Surface 3 also has a USB port, a feature that distinguishes the Surface line from most rival tablets.
19Microsoft Corp. expects to ship the new device around May 5, with advance orders starting immediately.
20Versions with LTE cellular access will sell for $100 US more in the U.S. at a later date through T-Mobile and Verizon, with other carriers expected.
21Data plans cost extra.
