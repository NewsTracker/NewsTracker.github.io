Microsoft Office finally available for Android

1Wishing you could write documents, make spreadsheets and create presentations on your smartphone?
2Well, you're in luck, because Microsoft Office apps for Android are now available, the company said in a blog post on Wednesday.
3After releasing preview versions of the apps five weeks earlier, Word, Excel and Powerpoint are now available to the general public.
4The free cloud-connected apps are designed for screens 10.1 inches and smaller and allow users to view, edit and create documents.
5For full access, users need a Microsoft Office 365 subscription.
