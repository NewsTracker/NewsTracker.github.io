10According to an agreed statement of facts, Gervais was approached by a reporter at the National War Memorial for an interview because he was wearing a Canadian Armed Forces dress uniform.

14Members of the Canadian Forces quickly noticed problems with his attire and the medals he was wearing, prompting a flurry of scrutiny and eventually the criminal charges against Gervais, who lives in Cantley, Que., just north of Ottawa.
15The police investigation revealed that Gervais had never been employed by the military, although he had been a cadet for several years in the 1990s.
16Police also discovered that Gervais was still in uniform after the Remembrance Day ceremony when he and his wife went to the Canadian War Museum to hear author Rod McLeod promote his book called Vigil.
17McLeod and a reservist chatted with Gervais for about 20 minutes about the fake soldier's struggles in the Royal Canadian Regiment due to "language barriers," court heard.
18He also shared stories about being a paratrooper and how he earned the medal of bravery.
19"All of these stories are untrue," the agreed statement of facts said.
