California woman sues Facebook for discrimination

Vacasa, Simple among 23 Oregon Technology Awards finalists

1Chia Hong filed the lawsuit this week in a San Francisco Bay Area superior court, the Los Angeles Times reported Thursday (http://lat.ms/1B5itdx).
2She is being represented by Lawless & Lawless, one of two firms representing the interim chief executive of Reddit, Ellen Pao, in her gender discrimination lawsuit against a Silicon Valley venture capital firm.
3Hong worked at Facebook as a product manager and then as a technology specialist in finance for about three years before she was terminated in October 2013.
4The Taiwanese woman says she was replaced by a less-qualified, less-experienced man.
5Facebook disputes the claims.
6The lawsuit alleges company officials asked Hong "why she did not stay home and take care of her children," and regularly ignored or belittled her professional opinions at meetings where she was one of few women.
7The suit says they also ordered Hong to organize parties and serve drinks to male colleagues, which wasn't part of her job description.
8In addition, Hong claims she was told "that she was not integrated into the team because she looks different and talks different than other team members," according to the lawsuit.
9Facebook maintains it did not mistreat Hong.
10"We work extremely hard on issues related to diversity, gender and equality, and we believe we've made progress," the company said in a statement to the newspaper.
11"In this case, we have substantive disagreements on the facts, and we believe the record shows the employee was treated fairly."
1The Technology Association of Oregon has narrowed in on the best of the best when it comes to the state's tech sector with this year's slate of Oregon Technology Award finalists.
2Twenty-three companies ranging from pre-revenue startups to publicly traded firms are vying in seven categories.
3The winners will be announced at a ceremony April 30.
4More than 500 of the region's tech leaders are expected to attend.
5Earlier this year, the group announced the winners of two of its top awards.
6CEO of the Year is going to Sam Blackman, co-founder and CEO of Elemental Technologies.
7The Lifetime Achievement Award is going to Bill Campbell, principal at Equilibrium Group.
