New Mexico cities top others in US for export growth

1Three New Mexico cities have been ranked among the top five metropolitan areas in the country with the most export growth, New Mexico Gov. Susan Martinez said.
2Martinez said a report from the U.S. Department of Commerce shows Las Cruces, Farmington, and Santa Fe broke records for export growth.
3Las Cruces and Farmington came in at No.
41 and No.
52, respectively.
6Santa Fe was ranked fifth.
7"I've always said that if we provide an environment that is friendly to job creators, we can level the playing field with neighboring states and become a leader in exports," Martinez said in a news release Friday.
8Martinez said the rankings confirm the state's strides to diversify its economy and grow in the private sector.
9But the state had more work to do to be seen as a leader in international trade, she added.
10According to the department report, the Las Cruces area dominated other cities with an export growth of more than 210 percent in the last year.
11That amounted to a gain of $913 million with an overall total of $1.3 billion in growth.
12Farmington saw export growth of 140 percent in the last year.
13The city's export business generated $83 million, an increase of $49 million.
14Santa Fe's export growth rose by 102 percent last year, totaling $38 million.
15A majority of the exports were to markets where the U.S. has trade agreements.
16Key exports from the state include appliances, electronic parts, plastic and rubber products and transportation equipment.
17The state's reach touched North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA) countries and places in Europe and Asia.
18New Mexico Economic Development Department Cabinet Secretary Jon Barela credited the gains to tax policy changes, trade missions and flourishing relationships with leaders in other countries.
