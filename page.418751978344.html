Been there, done that - it's all on Google Maps app

1Google is introducing a new feature to its Maps application that allows users to see where they have been on any given day, month or year.
2The search company already tracks the location of Android users, as long as they choose to share their location history with Google.
3This allows the company to provide more relevant and contextualised information within applications and ads.
4For example, Google Now provides notifications when there are traffic incidents along your commute, or reminds you where you parked your car.
5The new feature, called Timeline, puts this information on to a map, letting users "visualise your real-world routines, easily see the trips you've taken and get a glimpse of the places where you spend your time", according to Google.
6People who use Google Photos - Google's new app that assigns tags to objects in photos and automatically arranges them into albums - can also see all the photos they took in a specific place or on a specific day on their Timeline.
7"Have you ever wanted a way to easily remember all the places you've been - whether it's a museum you visited during your last vacation or that fun bar you stumbled upon a few months ago?" said Google product manager Gerard Sanz in a blog post.
8"Well, starting today, Google Maps can help ... Your Timeline is private and visible only to you; and you control the locations you choose to keep."
9The new feature is available on desktop and Android.
