Greece 'will pay' April loan tranche on time

1Greece will pay a loan tranche due on 9 April to the International Monetary Fund on time, its deputy finance minister said.
2Greece is fast running out of cash and its euro zone and International Monetary Fund lenders have frozen bailout aid until the government reaches agreement on a package of reforms.
3That prompted the interior minister to suggest this week that Athens would prioritise wages and pensions over the roughly 450 million payment to the IMF, though the government denied that was its stance.
4Euro zone officials then said Greece told them it will run out of money on 9 April, which the finance ministry denied saying.
5"We strive to be able to pay our obligations on time, Dimitris Mardas told Greece's Skai TV. "
6Adding to the confusion, German magazine Der Spiegel quoted a finance ministry general secretary, Nikos Theocharakis, assaying Greece would probably not pay next week's IMF tranche, prompting another denial from the Greek finance ministry.
7Mr Theocharakis said Greece would be "close to the end" on 9 Apriland called the technical teams from its creditors "completely useless," according to an extract of the article due to be published on Saturday.
8"Mr Theocharakis never characterised the technical teams of the institutions with the phrase attributed to him," the ministry said in a statement.
9"On the contrary ... he referred to them as saying they include 'top-notch people with impressive skills. "
10Athens has not received bailout funds since August last year and has resorted to last-ditch measures such as borrowing from state entities via repo transactions to tide it through the cash crunch.
11The government is hoping approval of its latest reforms package will unlock remaining aid of 7.2 billion under its EU/IMF bailout and lead to the return of about 1.9 billion in profits made by the European Central Bank on Greek bonds.
