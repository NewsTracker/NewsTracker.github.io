Thousands expected to attend Freddie Gray's funeral in Baltimore - Dozens arrested as Freddie Gray protests turn violent in Baltimore - VIDEO: Cops: Gray wasn't buckled into seatbelt

1BALTIMORE - Thousands were expected Monday at a funeral for a man who died after sustaining serious spinal injuries while in the custody of Baltimore police.
2Funeral services were planned for 11 a.m. Monday for Freddie Gray, the 25-year-old black man who died April 19 after an encounter days earlier with police left him with grave spinal injuries.
3Pastor Jamal Bryant, who was to deliver Gray's eulogy, said he expected Baltimore's New Shiloh Baptist Church to be filled for the service.
4A cemetery burial was to follow.
5In Washington, the White House said the head of President Barack Obama's initiative for young men of color would attend.
6Broderick Johnson, chairman of the My Brother's Keeper Task Force and a Baltimore native, is to be joined by two other administration officials, a White House statement said.
7Mourners who didn't even know Gray filed in a steady stream for hours into a funeral home for his wake Sunday afternoon.
8Some supporters stood outside the Vaughn Green East funeral home with signs that read, "We remember Freddie" and "Our Hearts Are With The Gray Family."
9Inside, mourners passed by Gray's silk-draped, white coffin where he lay dressed in a white shirt, black pants, white sneakers and an all-white Los Angeles Dodgers baseball cap.
10Above the lid to the coffin was a floral arrangement and inside the lid was a pillow with a screen-printed picture of Gray flanked by doves and the quote, "Peace, Y'all" at the bottom edge.
11Melissa McDonald, 36, who said she was Gray's cousin, wore a shirt with "Freddie Forever" printed on the back.
12She described her cousin as a nonviolent person.
13"He didn't deserve to die the way he did," she said.
14Gray's wake followed demonstrations Saturday that turned violent.
15Roughly 1,200 protesters rallied outside City Hall on Saturday afternoon, officials said.
16A smaller group splintered off and looted a convenience store and smashed storefront windows.
17A protester tossed a flaming metal garbage can toward a line of police officers in riot gear as they tried to push back the crowd.
18Earlier, a group of protesters smashed the windows of at least three police cars.
19Some 34 people were arrested, according to Baltimore Police Department, and six police officers sustained minor injuries.
20During a news conference Sunday, Baltimore Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake called for protesters to be peaceful.
21"At the end of the day, we are one Baltimore. We need to support peaceful demonstration and continue to enforce in our communities that rioting, violence, and looting will not be tolerated in our city," the mayor said.
22"Together we can be one Baltimore and seek answers as we seek justice and as we seek peace."
23Gray's death has prompted near-daily demonstrations.
24Gray was arrested one week before he died when officers chased him through a West Baltimore neighborhood and dragged him into a police van.
25Police said Gray was arrested after he made eye contact with officers and ran away.
26Officers held him down, handcuffed him and loaded him into the van.
27While inside, he became irate and leg cuffs were put on him, police have said.
28Gray asked for medical help several times, beginning before he was placed in the van.
29After a 30-minute ride that included three stops, paramedics were called.
30Authorities have not explained how or when Gray's spine was injured.
31Police acknowledged Friday that Gray should have received medical attention on the spot where he was arrested -- before he was put inside a police transport van handcuffed and without a seat belt, a violation of the police department's policy.
