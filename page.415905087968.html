News24.com | Nepal doctors stay away from work

1Dr Govinda KC, who is on a hunger strike, lies on a hospital bed at Teaching Hospital in Kathmandu, Nepal.
2Kathmandu - Thousands of doctors in Nepal stayed away from work at clinics and hospitals on Wednesday to support a colleague who has been on a hunger strike for 10 days demanding reforms in medical education and services.
3More than 5 000 doctors took part in the action and only emergency services were open in hospitals across the Himalayan nation, said Dr Nirmal Rimal of the Nepal Medical Association.
4The strike left most people in Nepal without access to doctors.
5The physicians are supporting Dr Govinda KC, who is demanding that the government make medical education affordable to more students and medical services available to all citizens.
6Only three of Nepal's 20 medical colleges are run by the government.
7The private colleges charge huge fees and are unaffordable for the majority of the population.
8KC is also alleging widespread corruption among officials in granting permits to private medical colleges and is demanding that the officials be dismissed and punished.
9The government formed a committee to negotiate with the doctors, but there was no agreement reached to end the strike.
10KC had gone on a 15-day hunger strike last year for similar demands.
11He ended the hunger strike and doctors withdrew their protests after the government assured them there would be changes in the country's medical education system.
12KC now says the government has not done enough.
13Hundreds of supporters lined up outside Kathmandu's Tribhuwan University Teaching Hospital, where KC is staging his hunger strike, to visit him on Wednesday.
14Also, a group of doctors briefly scuffled with police officers while protesting outside the Nepal Medical Council office in Kathmandu, Nepal's capital.
