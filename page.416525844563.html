Grizzlies hold off Blazers rally to win 115-109, go up 3-0 (Yahoo Sports)

1PORTLAND, Ore.
2(AP) -- Stung by the loss of point guard Mike Conley, the Grizzlies gritted out their latest victory over the Trail Blazers.
3Memphis took a 3-0 lead in its first-round playoff series against Portland with a 115-109 victory on Saturday night.
4But it came with a cost: Conley left the game in the third quarter with a facial injury and was hospitalized for further evaluation.
5The Grizzlies were already without backup point guard Beno Udrih, who sprained his right ankle in Game 2 and sat out Saturday's game.
6Game 4 is set for Monday in Portland.
7''Without Mike, without Beno, I know how competitive we are,'' said Marc Gasol.
8Gasol finished with 25 points and seven rebounds, while Courtney Lee had 20 points and Zach Randolph added 16 against the team that drafted him.
9Conley had 14 points before he appeared to be elbowed in the face by Portland CJ McCollum and fell to the floor.
10He was whisked to the locker room holding a towel to his face.
11With Udrih also out, the Grizzlies turned to Nick Calathes, who finished with 13 points.
12Calathes averaged 4.2 points and just over 14 minutes in 52 games during the regular season.
13The Grizzlies led by as many as 14 points, but Portland closed within 90-82 with 7:28 left after a trio of free throws from Nicolas Batum.
14Gasol responded with two jumpers to end the threat until Arron Afflalo's 3-pointer pulled Portland to 94-87.
15Batum's 3-pointer got Portland as close as 94-91 with 2:23 left.
16But Tony Allen's basket ended Portland's 9-0 run and Randolph added free throws.
17Allen's fast-break dunk with 1:14 left all but sealed it for the Grizzlies.
18Batum had another 3-pointer to make it 100-94 but Portland couldn't catch up.
19''We had the momentum, we hit some shots, the crowd was behind us, and I thought it was going to be one of those games where we find a way to pull it out,'' Blazers coach Terry Stotts said.
20Batum finished with 27 points for the Blazers, who have dropped seven straight games, including four to wrap up the regular season.
21McCollum added 26.
22Memphis made the most of getting home-court advantage for just the second time in the team's playoff history, taking the first two games including Wednesday's 97-82 victory in Game 2.
23The Grizzlies have won seven straight against Portland, sweeping the four-game regular season series.
24Udrih, who had 20 points in the opener, hurt the ankle in the first half of Game 2, but returned in the third quarter and finished with 10 points.
25He warmed up Saturday night but ultimately didn't play.
26Kosta Koufos made a hook shot that gave Memphis its biggest lead of the first half, 40-28.
27Memphis went on to lead 62-49 at halftime, scoring 30 points in the paint and shooting 57.5 percent.
28LaMarcus Aldridge struggled from the field in the first half, going just 1 of 10.
29He finished with 21 points.
30Randolph's layup midway through the third put the Grizzlies up 70-56.
31''I've got to give credit to Memphis, they definitely forced us into some tough shots,'' Aldridge said.
32''I was watching film from the first half: Every time I took a shot they had a guy right there, and sometimes three guys.
33Afflalo returned to the Blazers after missing the first two games of the series with a right shoulder strain.
34After coming to the Blazers via a trade with Denver at the deadline, Afflalo was a key contributor when starter Wesley Matthews ruptured his Achilles in March and was lost for the season.
35Grizzlies: The 62 first-half points are a playoff record for the team.
36... Memphis has never had a 3-0 lead in a playoff series.
37... The Grizzlies set a team playoff record by making 39 free throws; their 43 attempts were off of their playoff high of 47.
38Trail Blazers: The only time the Blazers have rallied from a 2-0 deficit in a playoff series was in the 1977 NBA Finals.
39That remains Portland's lone championship.
40... Blazers backup C Chris Kaman, who missed Wednesday night's game against the Grizzlies because of a left ankle sprain, was listed as questionable for the game but played.

42The wounded Blazer was walking on his own without the help of the scooter he's used in the past several days.
