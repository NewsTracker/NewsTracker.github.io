Fox News apologizes for erroneous Baltimore shooting report

1Baltimore police said Monday that a Fox News report of a black man being shot by police in the city was not true, and the cable news network quickly issued an apology.
2"On behalf of (reporter) Mike Tobin and the rest of our crew there, and the rest of us at Fox News, I'm very sorry for the error and glad we were able to correct it quickly," network anchor Shepard Smith said on the air.
3The network had quoted its reporter as saying the man was shot as he was running from authorities.
4Baltimore police later tweeted that the report of a man being shot was not true and that a suspect had been arrested at the scene with a handgun.
5Police said the man's firearm went off accidentally while officers were arresting him.
6Live television coverage and photos tweeted from the scene showed medics putting a man in an ambulance and a large police presence, with officers lining up to apparently block one street.
7The incident took place in the Penn North neighbourhood, where police first spotted Freddie Gray on April 12.
8Gray, a 25-year-old black man, died from injuries suffered while in police custody.
9The city has been on edge after looting and arson last Monday that followed Gray's funeral.
10A night curfew that had been imposed was lifted by Baltimore's mayor on Sunday.
11The mayor said the Maryland National Guard would begin withdrawing from the streets over the next week.
12The city's chief prosecutor has brought criminal charges against the six police officers involved in Gray's arrest.
