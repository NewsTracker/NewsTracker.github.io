Egypt, Saudis consider military exercises amid Yemen air strikes

1CAIRO - Egypt and Saudi Arabia are considering whether to hold joint military exercises in the kingdom as air strikes by a Saudi-led coalition pounded Shiite rebels in Yemen on Wednesday.
2The air strikes targeting Yemeni rebels known as Houthis began March 26 in an effort to halt their advances across the Arab world's poorest country.
3Hundreds have been killed, and more than 121,000 have been displaced amid the turmoil.
4The Houthis have captured Yemen's capital, Sanaa, and forced Western- and Persian Gulf-backed President Abed Rabbo Mansour Hadi to flee the country.
5A statement late Tuesday from Egyptian President Abdel-Fattah el-Sissi's office after he met Saudi Defense Minister Mohammed bin Salman said they had agreed to form a commission to investigate whether to hold "a major strategic exercise" in the kingdom, involving Egyptian, Saudi and other gulf forces.
6The statement gave no further details about the exercise.
7Egypt, a member of the anti-Houthi coalition, has said in the past that it would send ground forces into Yemen if the military deemed it necessary.
8The coalition said that operations are still in the air phase but that all options remain on the table.
