Ex-CIA chief will be sentenced for leaking military secrets

3CHARLOTTE, N.C. (AP) - Former CIA Director David Petraeus, whose career was destroyed by an extramarital affair with his biographer, was expected to be sentenced Thursday in federal court in Charlotte for giving her classified material while she was working on the book.
4Petraeus will appear at the sentencing, which comes two months after he agreed to plead guilty to a misdemeanor count of unauthorized removal and retention of classified material.
5The plea agreement carries a possible sentence of up to a year in prison.
6In court papers, prosecutors recommended two years of probation and a $40,000 fine.
7But the judge is not bound by that and could still impose a prison sentence.
8The agreement was filed in Charlotte, where Paula Broadwell, the general's biographer and former lover, lives with her husband and children.
9The affair ruined the reputation of the retired four-star Army general who led U.S. forces in Iraq and Afghanistan.
10As part of his deal, Petraeus agreed not to contest the set of facts laid out by the government.
11Prosecutors said that while Broadwell was writing her book in 2011, Petraeus gave her eight binders of classified material he had improperly kept from his time as the top military commander in Afghanistan.
12Days later, he took the binders back to his house.
13Among the secret information contained in the "black books" were the names of covert operatives, the coalition war strategy and notes about Petraeus' discussions with President Barack Obama and the National Security Council, prosecutors said.
14Those binders were later seized by the FBI in an April 2013 search of Petraeus' Arlington, Virginia, home, where he had kept them in the unlocked drawer of a desk in a ground-floor study.
15Prosecutors said that after resigning from the CIA in November 2012, Petraeus had signed a form falsely attesting he had no classified material.
16He also lied to FBI agents by denying he supplied the information to Broadwell, according to court documents.
17Petraeus admitted having an affair with Broadwell when he resigned as CIA director.
18Both have publicly apologized and said their romantic relationship began only after he had retired from the military.
19Broadwell's admiring biography of him, "All In: The Education of David Petraeus," came out in 2012, before the affair was exposed.
20Petraeus held the CIA post less than a year, not long enough to leave a significant mark on the spy agency.
21The core of his identity has been a military man.
22Petraeus' command coincided with the "surge" of American forces in Iraq and a plan to pay Sunni militias to fight al-Qaida in Iraq.
23With American help, the Sunni tribes were able to push out insurgents and enable U.S. troops to withdraw in 2011.
24Those same Sunni areas are now controlled by the Islamic State group, which evolved from the remnants of al-Qaida after Iraqi's Shiite-led government proved weak.
