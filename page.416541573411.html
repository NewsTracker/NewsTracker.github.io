Photographer says Baltimore police beat him during protest

1BALTIMORE (AP) - A photo editor for a Baltimore newspaper says he was beaten by police at a protest over the death of Freddie Gray.
2J.M. Giordano, who works at the City Paper, says Baltimore police "swarmed over" him and hit him repeatedly.
3A video posted to the newspaper's website Sunday shows at least two police officers in riot gear hitting and kicking Giordano as the person filming screams, "He's a photographer! He's press!"
4The 41-year-old Giordano says his head hit the ground during the beating, which he says only stopped when someone pulled him out of the fray.
5Sait Serkan Gurbuz, a photographer with Reuters, says police detained him as he shot pictures of the scuffle.
6Reuters says Gurbuz was cited with failure to obey orders.
7A Baltimore police spokesman hasn't immediately commented.
