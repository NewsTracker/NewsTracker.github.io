Kid who won ESPN bracket contest gives Xbox to Make-A-Wish

1ESPN was stingy, but the kid stiffed by the "Worldwide Sports Leader" certainly isn't.
2Sam Holtz, 12, who was not allowed to claim ESPN's top prize despite completing a near-perfect bracket in the massive annual contest, is donating an Xbox to the Make-A-Wish Foundation.
3"I feel really good about doing it," he told the Daily News.
4"I'm really hoping that another kid just like me is going to be happy when they get it and happy when they start playing."
5The Illinois sixth grader finished in a tie for first for accurately predicting the NCAA men's basketball tournament.
6But he didn't walk away with the grand $20,000 prize and trip to the Maui Invitational basketball tournament because he wasn't 18, the maximum age to qualify.
7Best Buy chipped in to ease the pain, giving Sam a $1,000 gift card.
8Instead of holding a grudge, the Hawthorn Woods boy used the money to buy one console for himself and another to give to the nonprofit which helps children with life-threatening diseases.
9Sam was inspired by his older cousin, a cancer patient who was able to go to Disneyland because of Make-A-Wish - as well as his grandparents, who were volunteers with the foundation for several years.
10"I decided to repay Make-A-Wish," Sam said.
11"When I first got the Xbox, I got really excited, and I decided why not make another kid feel just as excited."
