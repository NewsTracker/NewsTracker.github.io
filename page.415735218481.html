Minute by minute, the demise of Germanwings flight that crashed in the Alps with 150 aboard

1The 24-year-old A320 single-aisle jet arrives in Barcelona from Duesseldorf.
2Germanwings Flight 9525 takes off from Barcelona, slightly delayed because of airport congestion, for the return trip to Duesseldorf, about 1,300 kilometers (800 miles) away.
3Aboard were 144 passengers, two pilots and four cabin staff.
4Among them: babies, a group of German high-school students, an American contractor and her daughter, three generations of the same family.
5The pilots make their last contact with air traffic control in a routine message seeking and obtaining permission to continue course.
6At the time, the aircraft had reached its cruising altitude of 38,000 feet, and was over the Mediterranean Sea approaching the region around Marseille.
7The plane begins its unexplained descent above the French coast, just as its flight path crosses over land.
8Air traffic controllers try to contact the pilots, but receive no response.
9The last radar position of the plane is registered, at 6,175 feet - only slightly higher than the peaks of the southern French Alps below.
10The French Air Force sends a Mirage fighter jet to the plane's last known location.
11An air force official says the Mirage arrived too late to help, and never crossed paths with the A320.
