Last of Islamic State’s ‘Britani Brigade’ believed dead

1The last of a group of young men who travelled from Portsmouth to fight with Islamic State has died, it has been claimed.
2Assad Uzzaman, 25, was one of five men who styled themselves the Britani Brigade Bangladeshi Bad Boys and travelled to Syria to fight as jihadis.
3The group were spotted on camera walking through Gatwick airport in October 2013 as they headed for the war-torn country.
4Of the group, four are now dead and a fifth, Mashudur Choudhury, is serving a prison term in the UK.
5A sixth man, Ifthekar Jaman, was one of the first Britons to join Isis and encouraged others to fight with him in Syria.
6He was killed in December 2013.
7Uzzaman, Jaman's cousin, is thought to be the 50th Briton killed while fighting in Syria and Iraq.
8His death was reported by Shiraz Maher, a senior research fellow at the international centre for the study of radicalisation (ICSR) at King's College in London, which monitors social media accounts of alleged jihadis.
9He said: "The last of the Portsmouth cluster of fighters with Islamic State, Assad Uzzaman (Abu Abdullah) has died."
10Maher said the exact nationality of one of those on the ICSR's database was unclear, so the figure of 50 Britons was uncertain.
11Uzzaman travelled to Syria almost two years ago with four others from Portsmouth - Choudhury, 31, Muhammad Mehdi Hassan, 19, Mamunur Mohammed Roshid, 24, and Muhammad Hamidur Rahman, 25.
12Jaman told the BBC's Newsnight just weeks before his death that he was engaged in jihad, or holy war, to help set up a state based on Islamic religious law.
13He told the BBC he posed no threat to the UK and had no plans to return.
14Rahman is thought to have died in July last year while Roshid was killed in October.
15Hassan also died in October, fighting in Kobani, Syria.
16Choudhury, a father of two, was jailed for four years last December for travelling to Syria to attend a terrorist training camp.
17During his trial the jury heard he used Skype to send messages to Jaman and that, in a series of text messages Choudhury exchanged with his wife, she told him: "Go die in battlefield. Go die, I really mean it just go. I'll be relieved. At last. At last."
