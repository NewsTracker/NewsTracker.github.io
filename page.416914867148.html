Liberia's government holds celebration to mark Ebola's end

1Liberians are gathering in the streets of the capital to celebrate the end of the Ebola epidemic in this West African country.
2MONROVIA, Liberia: Liberians are gathering in the streets of the capital to celebrate the end of the Ebola epidemic in this West African country.
3Monday's festivities come after the World Health Organization declared over the weekend that Liberia was finally Ebola-free.
4More than 4,700 people died during the Ebola crisis in Liberia, the country hardest hit by the outbreak.
5Ebola continues to claim victims in Sierra Leone and Guinea, where health officials have had difficulty tracing new cases.
6The president of Togo along with guests from the African Union, Ghana and Nigeria are attending the Liberian event.
7Liberia's chief justice Francis Korkpor called the Ebola crisis "an unprovoked attack on our nation that posed a serious threat to the survival of our existence."
