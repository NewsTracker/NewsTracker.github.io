ISIS claims stronghold in West Africa following Boko Haram's allegiance

1The development comes as both movements, which are among the most ruthless in the world, are under increasing military pressure.
2Islamic State seized much of northern and western Iraq last summer giving it control of about a third of both Iraq and Syria.
3But it is now struggling against Iraqi forces seeking to recapture Saddam Hussein's hometown of Tikrit, while coming under fire from US-led coalition air strikes in other parts of the country and in Syria.
4Boko Haram, meanwhile, has been weakened by a multinational force that has dislodged it from a score of northeastern Nigerian towns.
5But its new Twitter account, increasingly slick and more frequent video messages and a new media arm all were considered signs that the group is now being helped by IS propagandists.
6Then on Sunday, Boko Haram leader Abubakar Shekau posted an audio recording online that pledged allegiance to ISIS.
7Today, the Islamic State group's media arm Al-Furqan, in an audio recording by spokesman Abu Mohammed al-Adnani, said that Boko Haram's pledge of allegiance has been accepted, claiming the caliphate has now expanded to West Africa.
8Al-Adnani had urged foreign fighters from around the world to migrate and join Boko Haram.
9"We announce our allegiance to the Caliph of the Muslims ... and will hear and obey in times of difficulty and prosperity, in hardship and ease, and to endure being discriminated against, and not to dispute about rule with those in power, except in case of evident infidelity regarding that which there is a proof from Allah," said the message.
10The Boko Haram pledge of allegiance to ISIS comes as the militants reportedly were massing in the northeastern Nigerian town of Gwoza, considered their headquarters, for a showdown with the Chadian-led multinational force.
11Boko Haram killed an estimated 10,000 people last year, and it is blamed for last April's abduction of more than 275 schoolgirls.
12Thousands of Nigerians have fled to neighbouring Chad.
13The group is waging a nearly 6-year insurgency to impose Muslim Shariah law in Nigeria.
14It began launching attacks across the border into Cameroon last year, and this year its fighters struck in Niger and Chad in retaliation to their agreement to form a multinational force to fight the militants.
15Boko Haram followed the lead of ISIS in August by declaring an Islamic caliphate in northeast Nigeria that grew to cover an area the size of Belgium.
16The Islamic State had declared a caliphate in vast swaths of territory that it controls in Iraq and Syria.
17The Nigerian group has also followed ISIS in publishing videos of beheadings.
18The latest one, published March 2, borrowed certain elements from ISIS productions, such as the sound of a beating heart and heavy breathing immediately before the execution, according to SITE Intelligence Group.
