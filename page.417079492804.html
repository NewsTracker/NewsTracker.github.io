1MIAMI (AP) -- The Miami Marlins will have a new manager for the start of a four-game series Monday night against Arizona.
2They're not yet saying who it will be.
3Mike Redmond was fired Sunday after the Marlins were nearly no-hit in a 6-0 loss to Atlanta that completed a three-game sweep.
4The defeat dropped Miami to 16-22.
5''We haven't played to our capabilities,'' president of baseball operations Michael Hill said.
6A news conference was scheduled for Monday morning to announce Redmond's replacement.
7Bench coach Rob Leary also was fired.
8Redmond had managed the Marlins since 2013, and in his final game, he had a hard time finding anyone who could get a hit.
9Shelby Miller was one out from a no-hitter when Justin Bour singled.
10The Marlins had the same 16-22 record in May 2003 when owner Jeffrey Loria fired Jeff Torborg as manager.
11Replacement Jack McKeon led a turnaround that resulted in an improbable run to the World Series title.
12The Marlins haven't been to the postseason since.
13Following a busy offseason highlighted by the signing of slugger Giancarlo Stanton to a record $325 million contract, the Marlins had high hopes of ending their playoff drought.
14But their bullpen has been woeful, and the batting order has struggled aside from major league RBIs leader Stanton and batting leader Dee Gordon.
15The Marlins have lost nine of their past 13 games and are fourth in the NL East.
16''We're looking for that consistency,'' Hill said.
17''We're swinging the bat well at times, but not when we need to do it.
18We're pitching well at times, but not when we need to do it.
19Loria has a reputation for impatience with managers.
20Along with the early season firing of Torborg, he dismissed Fredi Gonzalez in June 2010, and fired Joe Girardi and Ozzie Guillen after one season each.
21Redmond went 62-100 in his first year as a major league manager with the Marlins in 2013.
22They improved to 77-85 last year, ending a streak of three consecutive last-place finishes in the NL East.
23Their 15-win improvement tied for the best in the National League by a 100-loss team since 1986.
24On the final day of last season, Redmond's contract was extended two years through 2017.
25Guillen is in the final year of his contract, which means Loria will be paying three managers this year.
