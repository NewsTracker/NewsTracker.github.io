Microsoft phones infringe patents: Judge

1NEW YORK - Microsoft Corp lost a round in a potentially costly patent battle when a U.S. International Trade Commission judge on Monday found that the software giant used InterDigital Inc's technology in its mobile phones without permission.
2The judge, Theodore Essex, said that Microsoft infringed two wireless cellular patents owned by InterDigital, a patent licenser, and said it would not be against the public interest to ban the Microsoft devices from being imported into the United States.
3The judge's decision must be reviewed by the full commission before any import ban is enacted.
4The ITC has the authority to stop the import of products that it determines infringe a U.S. patent.
5Companies frequently sue at the ITC to win an import ban and in district court to win damages.
6Wilmington, Delaware-based InterDigital first accused Nokia Corp of infringing its patents in 2007.
7Microsoft acquired Nokia's handset division last year.
8The InterDigital patents relate to moderating a mobile phone's power to reduce signal interference.
9The ITC originally cleared Nokia of infringement, but in 2012 the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit, the nation's top patent court, overturned that decision and sent it back to the ITC.
10Microsoft called Monday's decision one step in the process.
11"We have a successful track record challenging patent assertion entities that misuse industry standards," the company said in a statement.
12InterDigital was not immediately able to comment on the decision.
13The case at the ITC is No.
14337-613.
