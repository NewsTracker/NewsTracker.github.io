Premarket: Euro at three-month high, bond yields key

1Premarket: Euro at three-month high, bond yields key Add to...
2The euro hit a three-month high against the dollar on Thursday, drawing further support from a sustained surge in euro zone government bond yields that again kept global stock markets on the defensive.
3Investors digested figures from the previous day that showed relatively strong euro zone economic growth in the first quarter, contrasting with disappointingly weak U.S. retail sales in April.
4The euro rose above $1.14, bringing its gains against the U.S. currency in the last month to nearly 9 per cent as the difference between benchmark U.S. and euro zone 10-year yields shrinks from the euro-lifetime high touched in March.
5European stock markets were a sea of red on Thursday, with investors worried about volatility and the tightening of financial conditions resulting from the plunge in bond prices and spike in yields.
6Asian stocks ex-Japan were broadly flat while Japan's Nikkei 225 index fell 1 per cent, weighed down by the yen's strength against the sagging dollar.
7"The euro is continuing to rebound, supported by the ongoing adjustment higher in euro zone yields. (This)... is in part supported by strengthening growth in the euro zone and higher inflation expectations," said Lee Hardman, currency strategist at BTMU in London.
8The euro was up more than 0.5 per cent on the day at $1.1415 , as the U.S.-German 10-year yield spread narrowed to a three-month low of 152 basis points.
9In mid-March, the yield advantage in favour of the dollar was over 190 basis points.
10This pulled the dollar index, a measure of its against a basket of six currencies, down to a four-month low of 93.175.
11With higher U.S. interest rates seeming ever more distant, investors bailed out of long dollar positions, taking the index's losses to around 7 per cent from a 12-year peak of 100.390 set in March.
12Wednesday's weak U.S. retail sales report prompted investors to push back the likely lift-off date for a rate hike by the Federal Reserve, giving gold a steer to five-week highs above $1,218 an ounce.
13But this failed to reverse the bond selling, as German and U.S. bond yields still surged to their highest in over five months.
14The startling rise in yields has made equities look more expensive in comparison to debt and kept global share markets subdued.
15Australian, Singaporean and Thai stocks declined, while Chinese and South Korean shares posted modest gains.
16In early European trade, the FTSEuroFirst 300 index of leading European shares was down 0.6 per cent, Germany's DAX down 0.5 per cent and Britain's FTSE 100 was 0.7 per cent.
17"The bond market moves are making investors quite anxious," said Oanda senior market analyst Craig Erlam.
18In bonds, the yield on German 10-year paper rose as high as 0.778 per cent, closing in on the 2015 high of 0.799 per cent touched last week, before easing back towards 0.72 per cent.
19The 10-year Treasury yield eased back from Wednesday's five-month closing high of 2.28 per cent, but analysts say the momentum in yields remains upwards.
20While Fed officials keep insisting a rate hike could come from June onward, markets are not convinced the U.S. central bank will be able to move at all this year.
21Bond moves are likely to be the main market driver on Thursday, but investors will also keep close tabs on talks between Greece and its creditors.
22Finance Minister Yanis Varoufakis said on Thursday Greece's debt was not viable and repayments to the European Central Bank should be pushed back.
23ECB president Mario Draghi will deliver a speech to the International MOnetary Fund in Washington later on Thursday.
24In commodity markets, oil gave back a little of recent hefty gains after weak U.S. data raised prospects of lower global demand.
