Islamic State seizes vast Damascus refugee camp

1In a separate advance, rebel groups that have received backing from Assad's Arab and Western foes captured a border crossing with Jordan, the first time Damascus has lost it since the start of the conflict, sources on both sides said.
2The gains by groups at opposite ends of the insurgent spectrum show the pressures still facing Assad, who has sought to shore up his control over western Syria including the border zone with Jordan and Israel - the last notable foothold of the mainstream rebels who have largely been eclipsed by jihadists.
3They come just days after an alliance of Islamist groups captured the northwestern city of Idlib from the government.
4The Nusra Front, al Qaeda's wing in Syria and one of the groups involved, announced on Wednesday the city would be run according to sharia law.
5The ultra-hardline Islamic State already controls swathes of eastern Syria and Iraq and is being targeted by a U.S.-led campaign of airstrikes.
6Until Wednesday's assault on the Yarmouk refugee camp, the group did not have a major presence in the area around the capital, where insurgents have mainly been loyal to other groups.
7The Yarmouk refugee camp, which was home to half a million Palestinians before the conflict began in 2011, has been held by anti-Assad insurgents and besieged by government troops since the early days of the war.
8It is a few kilometres from the heart of Damascus, which is still firmly in government control.
9Mirroring the way Islamic State has grown elsewhere in Syria, its fighters seized control of areas of the camp from other insurgents, helped by rebels from the rival al Qaeda-linked Nusra Front who switched sides, a political activist in the area said.
10"They pushed from the Hajar Aswad area and Nusra fighters have joined them, they have pledged loyalty to Daesh (Islamic State)," the activist said, speaking via Skype.
11Anwar Abdel Hadi, the Palestine Liberation Organisation representative in Damascus, said: "(Islamic State) went into Yarmouk today. There are clashes between the militants at the moment."
12The United Nations said it was extremely concerned about the safety and protection of Syrian and Palestinian civilians there.
13It estimates the population of Yarmouk at about 18,000, with most of its residents having long since fled.
14Government officials could not be reached for comment.
15Hassan Hassan, an analyst and author of a book about Islamic State, said the group had long wanted a foothold close to the capital but that it was unlikely to be able to storm the centre.
16"The regime has established strong checkpoints and infrastructure to prevent forces from actually getting inside Damascus. But they are edging closer."
17An alliance of mainstream rebel groups known as The Southern Front said it had captured the Nasib crossing at the border with Jordan.
18A staunch U.S. ally, Jordan had previously used its influence over the rebels to stop them from taking the crossing, fearing it could risk a military response from Damascus.
19A security source on the government's side confirmed the crossing had fallen, saying the army had withdrawn.
20"It has been liberated," said Bashar al Zoubi, leader of the Yarmouk Army, one of the rebel groups in southern Syria that was involved in the offensive.
21"The crossing is with us," he added, in a message sent to Reuters via Internet.
22The southern rebel groups have launched a counter offensive against a recent bid by Damascus and allied militia to reclaim the south, an area of vital strategic importance for reasons including its proximity to Israel.
23The rebels say states that want to see Assad gone from power responded to the offensive by sending them more weapons.
24The Nusra Front, which has a presence in the south, joined the attack on the crossing after the rebels had already launched it, according to the Syrian Observatory for Human Rights.
25The rebels said they had expelled Nusra from the area.
26Nusra Front shares Islamic State's jihadist ideology but does not recognise its rival's declaration last year of its leader Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi as "caliph", or ruler of all Muslims.
27On Thursday Nusra Front declared that Idlib, which was seized from government forces on Saturday by a coalition of militant groups, would now be ruled according to sharia law.
28"We salute the people of Idlib and their stand with their sons, the Mujahideen, ... and God willing they will enjoy the justice of sharia, which will preserve their religion and their blood," Nusra Front leader Abu Mohamad al-Golani said in an audio recording posted online.
29Idlib, around 30 km (20 miles) from the Turkish border is only the second provincial capital to slip from government hands after Raqqa in the east, which Islamic State has turned into its de facto capital.
30The groups that seized Idlib have rejected a call by the internationally recognised Turkey-based opposition to headquarter an interim government there.
31The Observatory said insurgents were destroying cigarettes and tobacco, which many Islamists view as banned in Islam.
32The news agency of the Vatican's missionary department reported on its website that a Greek Orthodox priest had been kidnapped by "jihadist militias" in the city.
33Golani said Idlib residents would be treated well and public property would be protected.
34He urged employees of essential facilities including bakeries to go back to work and said an Islamic court should be set up to settle disputes.
35Nusra Front's influence has been expanding in northwestern Syria.
36It has crushed at least two Western-backed rebel groups in recent months and seized weapons including U.S.-made anti-tank weapons from them.
