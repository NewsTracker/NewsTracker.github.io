State of emergency declared in Tunisia

1Tunisia's president declared a state of emergency overnight, more than a week after a gunman at a popular resort attacked foreign tourists on the beach, killing 38 people.
2Aftermath of Tunisia shooting.
3President Beji Caid Essebsi decided to declare the state of emergency on overnight, saying the country was 'not safe' from extremists.
4The state news agency said the president would address the nation later today.
5Thirty of the 38 dead in the Tunisian resort of Sousse were British tourists.
6The gunman was killed by police and the Islamic State group claimed responsibility for the massacre, a blow to Tunisia's budding democracy and tourism industry.
7Tunisia's government has promised new laws to increase police powers and provide for harsher penalties for terrorism convictions.
8Immediately after the attack, the prime minister pledged to post armed guards at tourist sites and close mosques outside government control.
9The country has previously been under a state of emergency from January 2011, at the outbreak of the Arab Spring, until March 2014.
10It initially included a curfew and a ban on meetings of more than three people.
11Although those measures were relaxed, police and the military retained powers to intervene in unrest or for security reasons.
