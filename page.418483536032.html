3Apple music has competition.
4MANY have scoffed at Apple's decision to offer a music streaming service that charges $11.99 when its closest competitor, Spotify, offers an all-important, advert-supported free option.
5Apple Music may have secured a PR victory by landing Taylor Swift (her music remains absent from Spotify), but what you're really getting for that money becomes clear even before you listen to a single song.
6The app first asks what genres and artists you like.
7By tapping in your preferences, you essentially create a virtual music nerd, ready to recommend you things based on its encyclopaedic knowledge and library of 30 million songs.
8You're instantly given dozens of playlists to dip into.
9If you tell the app you like Billie Holiday, you'll get curated playlists featuring cuts from Ella Fitzgerald, Dinah Washington and Nina Simone.
10If you say you like Rihanna and dance music, you're given a selection of the Barbadian pop star's finest remixes.

14Also sweetening the deal is Beats 1, a radio service programmed by a variety of experts, such as ex-BBC DJ Zane Lowe and hip-hop don Ebro.
15Beats 1 is another justification for the monthly fee even if it's a more haphazard way of finding new sounds.
16Creating and sharing playlists of your own is an integral part of the service, while song-based radio stations are an even less labour-intensive way of keeping the music you like coming.
17Apple has been derided for arriving late to the streaming game, but by watching what has worked and failed for the competition, being tardy might actually work in its favour.
18There's nothing to lose by taking advantage of the service's three-month free trial (something Tidal doesn't offer).
19But be warned that Apple won't tell you when time's up, so it's best to turn off the auto-renewal option, as there's a good chance you may forget.
