NFL finds Patriots employees probably deflated balls (Yahoo Sports)

1New England Patriots quarterback Tom Brady speaks at a news conference in Foxborough, Mass., Thursday, Jan. 22, 2015 as he addresses the issue of the NFL investigation of deflated footballs.
2NEW YORK (AP) -- An NFL investigation released Wednesday said that New England Patriots employees likely deflated footballs used in the AFC Championship and that quarterback Tom Brady was probably ''at least generally aware'' of the rules violations.
3The NFL began investigating after the Patriots defeated the Colts 45-7 on January 18.
4The Colts complained that several footballs were under inflated and the NFL confirmed that 11 of the 12 footballs were under the limit.
5The investigation started as the Patriots were preparing for the Super Bowl - which they won two weeks later.
6Footballs with less pressure can be easier to grip and catch.
7Some quarterbacks prefer footballs that have less air.
8The NFL requires balls to be inflated between 12.5 and 13.5 pounds per square inch, and each team is responsible for the balls it uses on offense.
9Brady said he prefers footballs inflated to 12.5 pounds per square inch.
10On many occasions, Brady said he never asked for balls to be deflated outside of the rules.
11The NFL report said ''it was more than probable'' that Jim McNally, the officials locker room attendant, and John Jastremski, an equipment assistant for the Patriots, were involved in ''a deliberate effort to release air'' from the footballs after they were examined by the referee.
12The report includes text messages between McNally and Jastremski that imply Brady was requesting footballs deflated below 12.5 pounds per square inch.
