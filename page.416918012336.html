Mexico opens bidding for oil, gas drilling in 26 areas

1MEXICO CITY (AP) - Mexico has announced the opening of bidding for oil and gas drilling rights in 26 areas, the latest in a sweeping oil reform that allows participation of foreign companies.
2The National Hydrocarbons Commission approved the auction for the land-based areas in five states in southern and northern Mexico, which together could yield 35,000 barrels of oil and 225 million cubic feet of gas per day.
3This is the third of five sets of areas Mexico plans to auction in a first round.
4The previous two auctions were for areas in shallow water.
5This first round of auctions has put in motion the historic reform of the energy sector passed last year.
6It signaled the end of a period that began with the nationalization of the oil industry in 1938.
