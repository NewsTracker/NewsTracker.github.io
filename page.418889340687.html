Russian veto expected as Security Council votes on MH17

1Russia on Wednesday is expected to veto a U.N. Security Council resolution to set up an international criminal court to prosecute those responsible for shooting down a Malaysia Airlines plane over Ukraine a year ago.
2The foreign ministers of the Netherlands, Australia and Ukraine will address what is likely to be an emotional meeting over the downing that killed all 298 people on board Flight MH17.
3The countries are among the five nations investigating the incident.
4Ukraine and the West suspect the plane was hit by a surface-to-air missile fired by Russian soldiers or Russia-backed separatist rebels.
5Russia denies that.
6Russia has said it opposes the resolution, and it offered its own draft that demanded justice for those responsible for the crash without calling for a tribunal.
