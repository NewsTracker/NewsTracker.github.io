Centenarian survives Nepal quake, worries he's testing fate

1BIDUR, Nepal (AP) - It was an unlikely escape.
2By his own account, he should have died.
3But when the humble hut of stacked grey stones collapsed onto Funchu Tamang, who believes himself to be 101 years old, his daughter-in-law quickly pulled him from the wreckage.
4He was hit in the chest by a rock during the quake.
5A couple of ribs were broken, giving him trouble breathing.
6The next day, just when his son arrived from Nepal's northwest, Tamang was weak and delirious.
7"I thought my father was dead," 58-year-old Bal Bahadur Tamang said Monday.
8But the old man had only fallen unconscious.
9They revived him, but a few days later it became clear he needed more medical help.
10His wheezing was getting worse.
11He was tired and often dizzy.
12They called the Nuwakot district hospital for help, which sent a police helicopter to land near his village and bring him here on Saturday.
13On Monday, doctors monitored Tamang's vital signs at the makeshift clinic set up in the parking lot of the district's medical office in the partially ruined town of Bidur.
14Funchu Tamang's ordeal is yet another of the extraordinary stories of resilience and survival coming out of Nepal after it was rocked by a 7.8-magnitude quake on April 25.
15It killed thousands and left hundreds of thousands more homeless.
16When he arrived, doctors treated Tamang for a partially collapsed lung.
17They bandaged cuts he received in the chaos after the quake.
18Two days later, he was alert and sitting upright, chatting.
19His blood pressure was a cool 101/62.
20His injuries were mostly healed, to the point where doctors said he could go home Tuesday.
21Funchu Tamang is so old, he has lived through two such devastating quakes.
22But he barely remembers the 1934 temblor that hit with a magnitude of 8 and leveled cities.
23The more recent scare, however, is fresh in his mind.
24"This one was really bad. I was afraid. I felt as if I would die," he said from his parking lot hospital bed.
