Blatter wins re-election despite FIFA corruption scandal (Yahoo Sports)

1ZURICH (AP) -- In the end, nothing could stop Sepp Blatter.
2Not a far-reaching corruption scandal.
3Not a tarnished international image.
4Not a young prince who gave him a stronger-than-expected challenge.
5Despite the biggest crisis in FIFA's 111-year history, Blatter emerged victorious once again Friday, winning re-election as president of world soccer's governing body for a fifth term and proving he is the sport's ultimate survivor.
6''I am now the president of everybody,'' the 79-year-old Blatter crowed after defeating Prince Ali bin al-Hussein of Jordan to secure another four years in office as one of the most powerful men in sports.
7Blatter was declared the victor after Prince Ali withdrew following the first round of secret balloting among FIFA's 209 member federations.
8Blatter won the first ballot 133-73, seven votes short of the two-thirds majority required for victory.
9Before the start of the second round, where a simple majority would be enough for victory, the 39-year-old prince conceded defeat.
10By preventing Blatter from securing an outright first-round triumph, Prince Ali gave Blatter a symbolic bloody nose and showed that his previous iron grip on the organization has weakened.
11''I want to thank all of you who were brave enough to support me,'' Prince Ali told the delegates.
12With FIFA in turmoil amid a pair of U.S. and Swiss corruption investigations, Blatter had remained defiant and refused to step down - as demanded by European soccer's governing body, UEFA.
13The result of the one-vote-per-country election proved that Blatter retains the loyalty of the many smaller countries in Africa and Asia, a bloc that is enough to counter his critics in Europe and elsewhere.
14''I like you.
15I like my job,'' Blatter said to the assembly after receiving a mix of cheers and jeers as he stepped to the stage for his victory speech.
16Then he exhorted the delegates: ''Together we go!
17Let's go FIFA!
18The election took place two days after seven soccer officials were arrested in dawn raids at a luxury Zurich hotel.
19The U.S. Justice Department indicted 14 people on charges of bribery, racketeering, money-laundering and other charges.
20In a separate investigation, Swiss authorities are looking into FIFA's awarding of the 2018 World Cup to Russia and the 2022 tournament to Qatar.
21And Britain's Serious Fraud Office said Friday it is assessing ''material in its possession'' relating to allegations of FIFA corruption.
22Blatter himself was not implicated in the U.S. indictments, but prosecutors have said the investigations are far from over.
23FIFA's big-money sponsors have also called for change within FIFA.
24Visa warned it could pull out of its contract, which is worth at least $25 million a year through 2022.
25Blatter, who has been in office for 17 years, portrayed himself as the man who can guide FIFA through the tumult and restore trust in an organization that has been left battered and reeling from years of corruption accusations.
26''I have been made responsible for this storm,'' he said in his final speech to the voters before the election.
27''That's fine, that's fine.
28I take that responsibility.
29I take it.
30The election went ahead after U.S. and Swiss federal investigations struck at the heart of Blatter's circle.
31Two FIFA vice presidents and a recently elected executive committee member were still in custody Friday as the votes were counted.
32''I thank you that you accepted me for the next four years,'' Blatter told the assembly.
33He cited God and Allah in his speech, saying they would help guide FIFA out of its crisis.
34''I promise you, in the end of my term I will give this FIFA to my successor in a very, very strong position, a robust FIFA and a good FIFA,'' he said.
35Blatter won despite direct calls for his resignation from UEFA president Michel Platini, who sat still during the congress and did not clap during the victory remarks.
36''I am proud that UEFA has defended and supported a movement for change at FIFA, change which in my opinion is crucial if this organization is to regain its credibility,'' Platini said.
37UEFA is scheduled to hold meetings next week in Berlin ahead of the Champions League final.
38Platini said before the vote that UEFA could pull out of FIFA and withdraw from the World Cup if Blatter was re-elected.
39In what appeared to be a warning to UEFA, Blatter pledged Friday to change the representation of his influential executive committee, where Europe currently has eight of 25 voting members.
40Blatter also said he would retain a 32-team World Cup and resist expanding what is FIFA's cash cow.
41England Football Association chairman Greg Dyke, who voted for Prince Ali, said Europe's opposition to Blatter would not wane.
42Sunil Gulati, president of the U.S. soccer federation and a member of FIFA's executive committee, also voted against Blatter.
43''While we are disappointed in the result of the election, we will continue to push for meaningful change within FIFA,'' he said.
44Blatter did have one big ally in Europe - Russia, the site of the next World Cup.
