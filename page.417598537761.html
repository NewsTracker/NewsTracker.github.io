F1 leader Lewis Hamilton wins Canadian Grand Prix

1Mercedes driver Lewis Hamilton, of Great Britain, steers his car at the hairpin followed by team mate Nico Rosberg, of Germany, during the Canadian Grand Prix auto race in Montreal on Sunday, June 7, 2015.
2Hamilton won the race while Rosberg placed second.
3MONTREAL (AP) - Lewis Hamilton quickly put his Monaco pit stop blunder - and everyone else in the Formula One field - behind him.
4The reigning world champion won the Canadian Grand Prix on Sunday, leading from the pole to the checkered flag with no threat from anyone except his Mercedes teammate, Nico Rosberg.
5Hamilton's problems in the previous race were also internal: A late strategic decision by his team cost him a chance at a fourth victory of the year.
6He made up for it the very next chance he got.
7"Did I need this?" he asked the cheering crowd after returning to the top of the podium for the first time since winning three of the first four races of the season.
8A four-time winner on the Circuit Gilles Villeneuve and the current leader in the Formula One standings, Hamilton finished the 70 laps on Montreal's Isle Notre-Dame in 1 hour, 31 minutes, 53.145 seconds - 2.285 seconds faster than Rosberg in the other Silver Arrow Mercedes.
9"Great to get back on the top step," said Hamilton, who expanded his lead over Rosberg in the championship standings to 151-134.
10"It was a great race. I don't know how good it was to watch."
11Hamilton's only stumble was when he locked his brakes a few laps from the end, and other than that the main excitement was a groundhog wandering onto the track and then quickly scurrying back to safety.
12Rosberg briefly took the lead when his teammate pitted during lap 29, but Hamilton took it right back when the German stopped to change his tires a lap later.
13"I was pushing like mad to try to put the pressure on, but he didn't make any mistakes," said Rosberg, who had won the previous two races.
14"It was a good race. Unfortunately, I couldn't make it happen. But second place - still OK."
15Valtteri Bottas was third, giving Williams its first appearance on the podium all season, thanks to a spinout by Kimi Raikkonen in the 27th lap.
16Despite posting the fastest lap of the day - with an average speed of 126.7 mph (203.9 kph) - the Finn finished fourth, right ahead of Ferrari teammate Sebastien Vettel.
17The 2013 Canadian GP champion and No.
183 in the points race, Vettel started near the back of the pack after failing to make it out of the first qualifying session.
19He was pushed to 18th on the starting grid because of a five-position penalty for passing another car with the red flag out in practice.
20But the four-time F1 champion steadily moved up and solidified his hold on third in the standings, with 108 points.
21Hamilton won three of the first five races this season and finished second two other times heading into the Monaco Grand Prix on May 24.
22He was leading there when a late crash brought out the safety car, and Mercedes called him in to change tires.
23Rosberg and Vettel passed him, and it was too late for Hamilton to retake the lead; he finished third, gritting his teeth all the way to the podium.
24Mercedes technical director Paddy Lowe apologized, but Hamilton insisted he was looking forward, not back.
25On Sunday, he said he was not discouraged by the strategic error because he knew he still had the fastest car.
26Asked if he was relieved to put overcome the Monaco mishap, he said, "I didn't feel I needed to be relieved."
27The day was not as good for McLaren, with both cars failing to finish.
28Jenson Button had a disastrous weekend, unable to get onto the track in qualifying and retiring 16 laps early on Sunday.
29Fernando Alonso was the first one out of the race, complaining that he lost power in the 44th lap.
30After the race, Alonso tweeted to Button a picture of the order of finish upside down.
