FIFA election chief reminds Blatter of need to step down

1Sepp Blatter was warned from within FIFA on Sunday not to attempt to backtrack on his pledge to quit as president.
2Domenico Scala, who is overseeing the presidential election, said that a leadership change is an essential component of the far-reaching reforms required to overhaul FIFA amid deepening criminal investigations into soccer corruption.
3Scala's blunt comments came after Swiss newspaper Schweiz am Sonntag, citing unnamed sources, reported that Blatter is considering trying to retain the presidency after receiving support from Africa and Asia.
4FIFA did not comment on the report.
5Blatter announced on June 2 - only four days after being re-elected for a fifth term - that he would stand down from the post after a successor was chosen sometime between December and February.
6Scala, head of FIFA's audit and compliance committee, is urging Blatter not to consider back-tracking on that pledge.
7The 79-year-old Blatter has already reneged on a 2011 promise to leave office in 2015.
8"For me, the reforms are the central topic," Scala said in a statement.
9"That is why I think it is clearly indispensable to follow through with the initiated process of leadership change as it has been announced."
10The FIFA executive committee will meet in Zurich on July 20 to set the election date.
11Lawmakers from the 28-nation European Parliament last week voted on a resolution calling for Blatter to speed up his announced resignation and let FIFA appoint an interim leader.
12Scala set out the election process at FIFA headquarters on June 2 directly after Blatter's sudden resignation statement and said that significant work was required to "regain the trust of the public," including imposing term limits for the president.
13Blatter has been portraying himself as leading that reform process, via his Twitter account and in FIFA's internal media outlets.
14And Blatter used his column in the in-house weekly magazine to praise FIFA's handling of the worst crisis in the organization's 111 years - despite his leading adviser, communications director Walter De Gregorio, abruptly leaving on Thursday.
15"The fact that our organization continues to function smoothly and provides football with a firm foundation at this time of crisis makes me all the prouder," Blatter wrote on Friday.
16Asked on Sunday if Blatter was categorically ruling out standing in the new election, FIFA said: "We refer you to the remarks from FIFA President Blatter from 2 June. FIFA has no further comment."
