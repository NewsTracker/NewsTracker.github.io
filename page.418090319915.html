What does Google have in store for the future?

1A Google logo is seen at the garage where the company was founded on Google's 15th anniversary in Menlo Park, Calif., Sept.
226, 2013.
3It seems like there is no rest for the tech giant that Google has become.
4What was once intended to be simply a backbone search for larger (at the time) companies, such as Yahoo, has grown in epic proportions.
5Did you know that Google has been known to rent goats to "mow" its grass at the Googleplex complex in Mountain View, Calif.?
6I wonder if that was part of the inspiration for the recently released Goat Simulator?
7It turns out that it's not all goats and green living in Mountain View, however.
8Google has been experimenting with all kinds of things.
9Most recently, it has given users of their popular Gmail system the ability to "undo" sending an e-mail.
10Although this feature has been available for a long time in its labs, it is now available to everyone.
11After hitting send, you have around 30 seconds to "undo" the send to think about it.
12Many of us get upset and send messages we wish we hadn't.
13Or sometimes, you might have told a coworker you loved them thinking it was a close friend (those that have too many people named "Chris" in their life might know what I'm talking about here).
14Whatever your reason, whatever your mistake, this new feature could be as useful as saving lives as blood transfusions.
15What if your next user interface was virtual?
16If Google has anything to do with it, it just might be like that.
17Smaller than a fingernail, Google's Soli uses radar radio waves to detect movement and images - as many as 10,000 per second.
18Whatever device it's connected to can receive inputs from movements you make with your hands and fingers (and possibly other parts of your body in the future).
19An example that I have seen shows someone turning a dial "virtually," causing a change on the screen.
20Turn your fingers forward and the dial spins forward.
21Go backwards, and the correct result shows on the screen.
22Except, there is no dial.
23You just make the movements like there is one.
24Google lost out on buying the live-streaming site Twitch last year.
25Everyone reported Google was the main suitor and suddenly Amazon swooped in and paid a staggering $970 million for the service which is mainly used by gamers who stream their gameplays for others to watch.
26Twitch has gone on to become a huge success.
27Rather than just cry about what went wrong and how they lost out on the deal, Google has been hard at work creating its own "Twitch killer."
28Part of YouTube Gaming will launch later this summer and adds features that Twitch doesn't have, such as the ability to pause and rewind live streams.
29Twitch is likely to follow suit, which proves competition is a good thing.
30Of course, Google isn't just all about streaming and websites.
31Its investments in things, such as windmills and clean energy should not go unnoticed.
32The company clearly has its sights set on a larger global domination picture.
33Google is even trying to better understand bees with its beekeeping adventures.
34It would seem that Google-branded honey tastes pretty darn good.
35Of course, now Google is working on the Google Brain (which I thought was just what its employees called the search engine around the office).
36It seems like not everything they make turns to gold but that's okay.
37Microsoft has suffered from the same problem over the years.
38The thing is, Google has enough money and technical prowess behind it to keep pushing the mediocre stuff until it's really good or just throw in the towel when it realizes it has made a mistake.
39This would cause most companies to go out of business or have major issues, but when you are Google it doesn't matter.
40The most amazing thing about Google, to me, is that the company isn't even 20 years old.
41That's right, it's not even of legal drinking age in its home country.
42Oh, how times have changed.
