4NEW YORK-Back in January Sony Network Entertainment announced that it would pull the plug on its Music Unlimited service on March 29, and partner instead with Spotify on a new service called PlayStation Music.
5Suffice it to say, Music Unlimited was not exactly a smashing success.
6On Monday, PlayStation Music goes live in 41 countries, with Spotify front and center.
7It will be available on the PS3 or PS4 consoles as well as on Sony's Xperia smartphones and tablets.
8Gamers can access any of Spotify's 30 million tracks on demand to supply a soundtrack as they fend off evil monsters or try and hit a fastball.
9Or they can stick to in-game audio.
10It all seemed neatly integrated during a quick demo I was treated to at Spotify's New York City offices.
11Spotify gets a prominent spot on the PlayStation content launcher.
12You can skip tracks and change the volume through your phone or tablet (via Spotify Connect), without interrupting a game.
13You can also use the PlayStation controller.
14The system can display relevant album cover art and track information or keep things in the background when you don't want to be distracted playing a game.
15But it's not all about gameplay.
16With the console in your living room and tied to your stereo system, Sony and Spotify are counting on you using PlayStation whenever you're in the mood to listen to music, and not just when you're busy playing.
17All of Spotify's curated playlists (Throwback Thursday, The Indie Mix, etc.)
18and genres will be available in PlayStation Music as well as content from your own library.
