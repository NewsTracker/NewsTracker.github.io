6LAGOS, Nigeria (AP) - Nigeria's military has rescued 234 more girls and women from a Boko Haram forest stronghold in the country's northeast, an announcement on social media said Saturday.
7It brings the number of females declared rescued this week to more than 677.
8"FLASH: Another set of 234 women and children were rescued through the Kawuri and Konduga end of the #Sambisa Forest on Thursday," said a message on the official Twitter account of the Nigerian Defence Headquarters posted early Saturday.
9It comes as the army deployed ground troops following weeks of punishing air raids on the Sambisa Forest.
10President Goodluck Jonathan, whose term ends this month, said Thursday that the forest is the last holdout of the Islamic militants and he pledged to "hand over a Nigeria completely free of terrorist strongholds."
11There have been reports that some women fought the troops, with Boko Haram using them as an armed human shield for its main fighting force.
12It is not known how many girls, women, boys and men Boko Haram has kidnapped over its nearly 6-year-old rebellion.
13The military says it is screening the girls and women to find out what villages they came from.
14Some women the soldiers tried to rescue even shot at their rescuers, a military spokesman has said, indicating that some might now identify with Boko Haram after months of captivity and forced marriages.
15It also remains unclear if some of the women had willingly joined Boko Haram, or are family members of fighters.
16The Nigerian military Friday released photos of some of the girls and women they said were taken between Tuesday and Thursday in the Sambisa Forest.
17The photos show 20 or so women, children and babies looking generally healthy physically.
18But at least one child looks emaciated and some of the children have the orange-colored hair signaling severe malnutrition.
19There has been no announcement yet on whether any of those rescued are the students who were kidnapped from the Chibok school a year ago, a mass kidnapping that outraged much of the world.
20Some photos were taken in an open courtyard with a high wall and leafy trees beyond.
21A military man in a flight suit, an assault rifle held by his side, stands among them.
22A young military medic with blue rubber gloves and a surgical mask dangling from his ears appears to be checking several of the children.
23Muhammad Gavi, a spokesman for a self-defense group that fights Boko Haram, said some of the hundreds of women and girls who were freed are pregnant, citing information he got from some group members who have seen the females.
