6Late last year, Pakistan's prime minister lifted the death-penalty moratorium specifically for terrorism-related cases after a December Taliban attack on a school in Peshawar killed 150 people, most of them children.
7Last week, in a controversial step, the government completely lifted the death-penalty ban for all cases.
8Human rights groups estimate Pakistan has roughly 8,000 prisoners on death row.
9One of the most closely watched execution cases is that of Shafqat Hussain, who family members say was 14 when he was sentenced to death by a court in Karachi for the murder of a seven-year-old boy.
10Hussain's family proclaims his innocence and Justice Project Pakistan, the legal group handling his case, says Hussain was tortured into making a false confession.
11Hussain is scheduled to be hanged on March 19.

2Netanyahu has also pushed for strong international action against arch-enemy Iran's nuclear facilities and has focused his campaign on security issues.
3- Zionist Union: The main opposition party and challenger to Netanyahu's lengthy rule.
4Headed by Isaac Herzog, it is a blend between his Labor Party - which governed the country from its founding in 1948 until 1977, and twice since - and former Foreign Minister Tzipi Livni's centrist Hatnua party.
5The new alliance has pledged a renewed push for peace with the Palestinians and for mending relations with Israel's most important ally, the United States, which have deteriorated under Netanyahu.
6The party has also attacked Netanyahu on economics, blaming him for high housing prices and the economic struggles of the middle class.
7- Yesh Atid: Founded by former TV personality Yair Lapid, the centrist party represents secular, middle class interests and focuses mostly on economic issues - saying less money should be spent on settlements and stipends for the ultra-Orthodox.
8The party was the surprise of the previous vote in 2013 and then joined Likud as its senior coalition partner.
9The charismatic Lapid saw his popularity drop as finance minister but has recovered since Netanyahu fired him in December and called the new election.
10The party has attacked Netanyahu as out of touch with average Israelis, but hasn't ruled out cooperating with him again.
11- Kulanu: The new party headed by former Likud government minister Moshe Kahlon is shaping up to be the kingmaker of this election.
12Kahlon is running on an economic platform that deals almost exclusively with bread-and-butter issues, while all but ignoring Israel's diplomatic challenges.
13He is demanding to become finance minister in the next government and could tip the scales in favor of either Netanyahu or Herzog.
14Kahlon, the son of Libyan immigrants, is popular with working class Israelis thanks to his Middle Eastern background, his modest upbringing and for reforming the local mobile-phone market.
15- The Joint List: A recently established alliance of four small, largely Arab-backed parties representing Israel's 20-percent Arab minority that could emerge as the third-largest bloc.
16Despite deep differences between socialists, Palestinian nationalists and traditional Islamists, the list's leader, 40-year-old lawyer Ayman Odeh, says the unprecedented union could dramatically increase Arab turnout at the polls and clout in Israel's parliament.
17- Yisrael Beitenu: The far-right secular party headed by Foreign Minister Avigdor Lieberman takes a hard line against Israel's Arab minority and says it will push for the death penalty for terrorists.
18The party has shrunk in the polls since breaking up an alliance with Likud and following a series of corruption scandals that forced out several key players.
