7Authorities said screening the documentary could have caused public disorder, but critics accused the government of being more concerned with the country's reputation than the safety of its women.
8The December 2012 gang rape of a young physiotherapy student highlighted the frightening level of violence against women in the world's second most populous country and triggered mass protests.
9It led to a major reform of India's rape laws, speeding up trials and increasing penalties, although many campaigners say little has changed for women on the ground.
1The full extent of the damage is unknown, with limited communications in place after Super Cyclone Pam, a maximum category five storm, slammed into the island nation late on Friday with gusts up to 320km/h.
2The UN had unconfirmed reports of 44 people killed in one province, and said late on Saturday there was no clear number of deaths or injuries but the impact of the cyclone had been "catastrophic".
3The eye of the storm passing over Vanuatu early on Saturday.
4Vanuatu President Baldwin Lonsdale used a speech at a UN conference in Sendai, Japan, to appeal for international assistance on Saturday.
5"I stand to appeal on behalf of the government and people of Vanuatu to the global community to give a lending hand in responding to these very current calamities that have struck us."
6Aid agencies were preparing to send teams to Vanuatu, whose main island is home to more than 65,000 people, with a UN disaster assessment and coordination team expected to arrive late on Sunday.
7"While it is too early to say for certain, early reports are indicating that this weather disaster could potentially be one of the worst in Pacific history," UNICEF New Zealand's Executive Director Vivien Maidaborn said in a statement.
8"The sheer force of the storm combined with communities just not set up to withstand it, could have devastating results for thousands across the region."
9Aurelia Balpe, head of the Pacific regional office of the International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies, said there were unconfirmed reports of casualties in the capital Port Vila.
10But they had greater fears for outlying southern islands, home to more than 33,000 people.
11"We are very worried just because there are less permanent structures in that part of the country," Balpe said.
12Residents of Port Vila spent the night hunkering down as the storm raged.
13"The scene here this morning is complete devastation - houses are destroyed, trees are down, roads are blocked and people are wandering the streets looking for help," said Save the Children's Tom Skirrow.
14The storm crossed the main Vanuatu island and a group of islands further south after affecting the Solomon Islands and Tuvalu, where there were no reports of fatalities.
15Aid agencies have launched appeals and are hoping to start flying in emergency supplies of food, shelter and medicine from Sunday, when the airport in Port Vila is expected to reopen.
16Australia's Foreign Minister Julie Bishop said medical and search and rescue staff were on standby while New Zealand announced $NZ1 million ($A959,500) in initial funds to assist Vanuatu, Fiji, Tuvalu and the Solomon Islands.
17The European Union also pledged 1 million euros ($A1.38 million) for emergency relief efforts while the UK promised 2 million pounds ($A3.87 million).
18United Nations chief Ban Ki-moon said while the impact was not yet clear, "we fear the destruction and damage would be widespread".
19Fiji Weather Service meteorologist Neville Koop said the cyclone was weakening as it slowly moved away from Vanuatu, and would pass between Fiji and New Caledonia before brushing the North Island of New Zealand on Monday.
