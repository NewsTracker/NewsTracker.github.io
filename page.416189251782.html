Clinton making first campaign trip to Iowa on Tuesday

1Clinton will tour a community college in Monticello on Tuesday and visit a produce distribution company in Norwalk on Wednesday, according to a schedule provided by her campaign Sunday.
2She will also hold meetings with Democratic elected officials and activists in the leadoff caucus state.
3Clinton announced Sunday that she'll seek the presidency for a second time.
4Her team has made clear that she wants to avoid big campaign events and instead focus on more personal interactions with voters in early voting states like Iowa.
5Clinton's 2008 campaign faltered in Iowa, where she placed third in the caucuses.
6After that loss, she did not return to the state for seven years.
7Last fall, Clinton came to Iowa for former Sen. Tom Harkin's final "Steak Fry" fundraiser and to campaign for Democratic Senate candidate Bruce Braley.
8So far this year, Iowa Democrats have not seen Clinton - though several other hopefuls, including former Maryland Gov. Martin O'Malley and former Virginia Sen. Jim Webb have made visits to the state.
9Many Iowa activists say she would be wise not to take the nomination for granted.
10Even those who like Clinton say they want a competition, not a coronation.
11Delpha Middleswart, 60, saw O'Malley speak last week at a fundraiser for a state representative.
12"We all know Hillary. We need to hear from some other folks," said Middleswart, of Indianola.
13"I think she's got good things. The Democrats need to have choices."
14Clinton's announcement video featured several Iowans, including Julie Stauch, of West Des Moines, who talks about growing tomatoes.
15She said she knew she was filming footage for Clinton, but didn't know she might be in the announcement.
16She said she wanted to participate because she's happy to do anything possible to help Clinton win.
17Stauch, who supported Clinton in 2008, said: "I think that she's planning to work hard and she'll do very well here. I think she'll do what it takes."
