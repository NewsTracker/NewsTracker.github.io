26BALTIMORE (AP) - A 25-year-old Baltimore man died a week after his spine was mysteriously injured while he was in police custody.
27A look at the developments Wednesday in the case of Freddie Gray.
28LYNCH MOB?
29A statement released by the Baltimore police union comparing protesters of Gray's death to a "lynch mob" drew criticism on Twitter by users who called it racially insensitive and inappropriate, given that the demonstrations have been peaceful.
30A little later, at a news conference, union President Gene Ryan backpedaled, saying, "Maybe I should reword that."
31"I don't want it to turn into a lynch mob," he said.
32"I'm afraid of the crowd becoming hostile," said a tense-sounding Ryan.
33"They have been very peaceful to this point. My main concern is for the public and the police officers that they remain peaceful and exercise their constitutional right to do what they're doing."
34Ryan said he feared violence because of protests in Ferguson, Missouri, and New York that followed police-involved deaths of unarmed suspects.
35One of two groups of protesters marched in the streets for 20 blocks Wednesday night, blocking intersections and disrupting traffic for short periods of time before moving on.
36They passed near the hospital where Gray died, and continued on to the Inner Harbor before stopping in front of City Hall.
37Once there, some paused in front of a fountain and raised their hands, saying, "Hands up, don't shoot."
38They then approached barricades and officers protecting a courtyard directly in front of City Hall.
39A man with a bullhorn chanted, "No justice, no peace."
40The group then marched to an onramp of Interstate 83, a highway that cuts through the city.
41The ramp was blocked by several police cruisers and a line of officers, their arms linked.
42Pastor Wesley West of Faith Empowered Ministries linked arms with Gray's cousin, Carron Morgan, and they faced the crowd.
43After West led chants for justice and peace, and called for a moment of silence, protesters headed away from the interstate.
44Morgan, 18, turned and quickly shook one of the officer's hands.
45Asked why, he said: "At the end of the day, I don't think all cops are bad, just that some are corrupt."
46Baltimore police took to Twitter to provide updates of where the marchers were, using the hashtag, #WeHearYou.
47At a second protest at the Western District police precinct, more than 100 demonstrators pushed on a barricade, and about 30 police officers pushed back.
48Some protesters hurled obscenities and threw soda cans and bottles at the line of police behind the barricades, but none hit the officers and the demonstration remained largely peaceful.
49For the most part, police ignored the insults did not react to provocations from the protesters.
50At least three people were detained.
51No one was injured.
52Mark Hill said anger in the community reached a boiling point after Gray's death, but that the frustration is longstanding.
53"It's getting charged out here because people are really getting tired," Hill said.
54"There's a fear in the community of what police might do to you.
55He said he has little confidence that police will be forthcoming about what happened to Gray.
56Bernadette Washington said she has been marching every day since Gray's death.
57Washington grew up with Gray and called him a good person who didn't need to die.
58She didn't understand why the six officers involved in Gray's arrest are still drawing a paycheck while they're suspended.
59"All we want is justice," Washington said.
60Five of the six officers involved in Gray's arrest gave statements that day, a week before his death, police said Wednesday while declining to reveal the content of what they said.
61An attorney who works with the Baltimore Fraternal Order of Police had mentioned the statements earlier in the day as a way of showing that the officers involved have been cooperative.
62Police said the department's internal investigation into Gray's arrest will be turned over to the Baltimore State's Attorney's Office for review on May 1.
63The prosecutor's office can determine whether to file charges against the officers involved or present the case to a grand jury.
64The U.S. Justice Department has opened a civil rights investigation into Gray's death.
65Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake said she welcomed the Justice Department's involvement.
66She has called along for an outside review.
67"Whenever a police force conducts an internal investigation, there are always appropriate questions of transparency and impartiality," she said in a statement.
68"My goal has always been to get answers to the questions so many of us are still asking with regards to Mr. Gray's death."
69Gray's family has an attorney who is working on his own investigation.
70Maryland Gov. Larry Hogan said he has spoken with city and state officials about the situation and won't be ordering any further investigations.
71"We don't want to make politics out of this tragic situation," Hogan told reporters after an Earth Day event in Annapolis.
72He said his heart goes out to Gray's family "and everyone involved in the entire incident."
