Blatter could remain as FIFA head, close adviser tells Sky

1LONDON (Reuters) - Sepp Blatter is actively considering reversing his promise to stand down as FIFA president, one of his advisers has told Sky News.
2Klaus Stoehlker, who advised Blatter during the recent election campaign, said Blatter could remain head of world football's governing body if a "convincing candidate" to replace him did not emerge.
3Blatter stepped down as FIFA president almost two weeks ago, as the Swiss-based body was rocked by corruption allegations.
4Stoehlker, who Sky News says stressed he is not an official spokesman, was in a meeting when contacted by Reuters and unable to comment immediately.
5Switzerland's Schweiz am Sonntag newspaper reported on Sunday that Blatter, 79, may seek to stay on.
6FIFA refused to be drawn when contacted by Reuters, saying only that "Klaus Stoehlker's mandate from the FIFA President ended on 31 May 2015" and emailing a link to Blatter's two-week-old resignation speech.
