Three die in new migrant tragedy off Greece

1The International Organization for Migration has received a distress call from a sinking boat in the Mediterranean carrying more than 300 people, with at least 20 people reported dead.
2IOM's Rome office received a call for help from one of three boats floating near each other in international waters, spokesman Joel Millman said.
3"The caller said that there are over 300 people on his boat and it is already sinking (and) he has already reported fatalities," he wrote in an email.
4The organisation has contacted the coast guard with the coordinates for the vessels, but said "they do not have the assets to conduct these rescues right now".
5"The weekend incident has tied up a lot of resources," he said, referring to yesterday's migrant tragedy when about 700 people were feared killed after a boat capsized off Libya.
6"The coast guard will probably try to redirect commercial ships to the area," he added.
7Meanwhile, at least three people have drowned after a wooden sailboat carrying dozens of immigrants ran aground off the coast of the Greek island of Rhodes, the Greek coast guard said.
8More than 90 people were rescued in the incident and 30 of those were taken to hospital.
9The boat was totally wrecked, the coast guard said.
10"We have recovered three bodies so far - that of a man, a woman and a child," a Greek coast guard official said.
11Greek TV footage showed parts of the boat in the latest disaster floating in the water off Rhodes and people holding onto it or swimming to the shore.
12There has been a surge in migrants trying to reach Greece's Aegean islands from the Turkish coast in the last ten days.
13Rescuers are continuing to search a wide area of the Mediterranean Sea, after a fishing boat crowded with hundreds of migrants capsized near the Italian island of Lampedusa yesterday.
14The Italian authorities say one survivor told them as many as 950 people were on board, including hundreds who had been locked in the holds by people smugglers.
15A meeting of EU foreign ministers is taking place in Luxembourg, to discuss the number of migrants who have lost their lives attempting to cross the Mediterranean from Africa.
16Ministers held a minute's silence before the start of today's meeting.
17EU leaders have come under pressure to tackle the increasing number of migrants being drowned, as they attempt to enter southern European countries.
18EU foreign policy chief Federica Mogherini said it is the EU's moral duty to tackle the migrant crisis, and the bloc could call an emergency summit of leaders later this week.
19Arriving at the meeting in Luxembourg, she said: "We must build a common sense of European responsibility ... knowing that there is no easy solution, no magic solution."
20In a statement earlier, the commission said: "The reality is stark and our actions must therefore be bold. These are human lives at stake, and the European Union as a whole has a moral and humanitarian obligation to act."
21"What we need is immediate actions to prevent further loss of life as well as a comprehensive approach to managing migration better in all its aspects."
22Minister for Foreign Affairs Charlie Flanagan has said that Ireland needs to give priority to the most vulnerable, but he refused to be drawn on how many refugees Ireland should accept.
23Speaking on RTE's News At One, Mr Flanagan said that saving lives is a priority, but that there is no magic solution.
24He said that Ireland will play its part, but that it is important that the EU plays a greater part.
25Mr Flanagan said that the EU must engage in the burden-sharing that is necessary in order to alleviate the crisis.
26Minister for Justice Frances Fitzgerald is also attending the meeting, where she said: "An urgent response is required to stem the flow of migrants to Europe and to tackle the criminal activity that drives this horrendous trade in human misery."
27Ms Mogherini said she would present a set of proposals to Libya, one of the main trafficking routes, at today's meeting.
28"We need to save human lives all together, as all together we need to protect our borders and to fight the trafficking of human beings," she said, urging that the task be shared and not "left only to the southern countries".
29Meanwhile, German Chancellor Angela Merkel said Germany and its European partners need to move swiftly to agree new measures to avert more deadly boating disasters in the Mediterranean.
30A spokesman for the Chancellor denied that there was a difference of opinion in Berlin about how to proceed.
31It came after officials sent different signals yesterday about whether to revive the so-called 'Mare Nostrum' rescue mission that Italy ended late last year.
32Illegal immigration, mostly from Africa, has rapidly expanded with ruthless people smugglers based in chaotic Libya taking overcrowded, unseaworthy boats north, mostly to Italy and Greece.
33Southern EU countries have complained that they are bearing too much of the brunt of the rescue work and processing of migrants.
