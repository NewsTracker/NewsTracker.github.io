Volcano in southern Chile erupts, thousands evacuated

Earthquake measuring 6.4 magnitude strikes off Indonesia: USGS

1The Villarrica volcano erupted around 3 a.m. local time, according to the National Emergency Office, which issued a red alert and ordered evacuations.
2Local media showed images of the volcano bursting at the top, glowing in the dark amid heavy smoke and rivers of lava.
3Authorities worried that mudslides caused by melting snow could endanger nearby communities.
4The 9,000 foot (2,847-meter) volcano in Chile's central valley, 400 miles (670 kilometers) south of Santiago, sits above the small city of Pucon, which has a population of about 22,000 people.
5"It was the most amazing thing I've ever seen," 29-year-old Australian tourist Travis Armstrong said in a telephone interview from Pucon.
6"I've never seen a volcano erupt and it was spewing lava and ash hundreds of meters into the air. Lightning was striking down at the volcano from the ash cloud that formed from the eruption."
7Chilean authorities had issued an orange alert on Monday because of increased activity at the volcano.
8About 3,500 people have been evacuated so far, including tourists, said Interior and Security Minister Rodrigo Penailillo.
9Penailillo warned that the eruption was causing numerous rivers in the area to rise as snow along the sides of the volcano began melting.
10Villarrica is covered by a glacier cap covering some 40 square kilometers (15 square miles) and snow from about 1,500 meters (about 5,000 feet) on up.
11Authorities were keeping an eye on four nearby communities that could be endangered by mudslides as the snow melts.
12Officials were also monitoring nearly 200 people who were cut off from main roads when two bridges were destroyed by rising waters from nearby rivers.
13President Michelle Bachelet arrived in Pucon amid cheers and boos later Tuesday to check on safety preparations, and declared an agricultural emergency to help local farmers.
14"You never know when an eruption will take place but what we do know is that the activity is lower, that's visible," Bachelet said after flying over the affected areas and meeting local authorities.
15Witnesses said Pucon looked like a deserted town at dawn.
16But as the volcanic activity decreased, some local residents had decided to return to their homes, more cars were seen in the streets, and some people had even decided to sunbathe at a nearby lake.
17Jose Manuel Reyes, the 37-year-old manager of La Bicicleta hostal in downtown Pucon, said visitors from France, Canada, Australian, South Africa and Brazil watched the early morning eruption from the building's terrace.
18"We're still a bit nervous because we don't know what's happening," said Reyes.
19"There was nervousness, but we haven't seen any panic."
20Tourists flock to the area around Villarrica for outdoor activities like kayaking, horseback riding, fishing and hiking around the volcano, which last had a major eruption in 1984.
21Dozens of tourists were among those evacuated.
22The Villarrica has a crater of about 200 meters (yards) in diameter and a lake of lava about 150 meters (yards) deep.
23It has periodic eruptions every 10 or 15 years.
24Chile has more than 2,000 volcanoes in the Andes cordillera and about 90 of them remain active.
25Villarrica is considered among the country's most dangerous.
1The quake was 37 km (23 miles) deep and centred 179 km (111 miles) offshore.
2It was felt by people on land but there was no panic, Indonesia's disaster agency said.
3There was no word on a tsunami.
4Indonesia, badly hit by the Indian Ocean tsunami in 2004, straddles the so-called Pacific Ring of Fire, a highly seismically active zone where different plates on the earth's crust meet and create a large number of earthquakes and volcanoes.
