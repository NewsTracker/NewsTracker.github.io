Key Witness Didn't See Nemtsov's Killer

1A man visits the site of Boris Nemtsov's death in Moscow on Monday.
2MOSCOW-A woman who was walking with opposition activist Boris Nemtsov when he was gunned down near the Kremlin last weekend said Monday that she didn't see the killer.
3Mr. Nemtsov, a former deputy prime minister who later became a fierce critic of the Kremlin, was crossing a bridge with Anna Duritskaya, a 23-year-old Ukrainian model, when investigators say he was shot four times in the back from a passing car.
4In their last update on Sunday, investigators said they were questioning witnesses and were checking recordings from security cameras.
5They said the killing was planned and that the perpetrators knew the route that Mr. Nemtsov would take.
6Investigators have said they are probing several theories as to the motive, but state television has focused on suggestions that it could have been part of a plan by the opposition and the West to destabilize the government of President Vladimir Putin.
7The Kremlin has called the killing a provocation, a word that in Russian implies it was done to discredit authorities.
8Mr. Nemtsov's funeral is scheduled for Tuesday.
9A Kremlin spokesman said a government representative would attend.
10Speaking to the online news-channel Dozhd and Ekho Moskvy radio, Ms. Duritskaya said she had dined on Friday evening with Mr. Nemtsov, an acquaintance of three years.
11They were then crossing the Bolshoi Moskvoretsky Bridge in the direction of his apartment when he was killed shortly before midnight.
12She told the media outlets that Mr. Nemtsov's assailant had approached them from behind and that a light-colored vehicle drove away from the scene of the shooting, but that she didn't see his assailant nor remember the make or license plate of the car.
13Anna Duritskaya poses for a photo for a modeling portfolio in Kiev, Ukraine, on Aug. 28, 2012.
14She is a key witness to the killing of Boris Nemtsov.
15Ms. Duritskaya said she ran up to the driver of a passing snowplow and asked for the telephone number for emergency services.
16Police arrived 10 minutes after she dialed, she said.
17Ms. Duritskaya said she was staying at a friend's apartment in Moscow but was under round-the-clock guard and that investigators weren't allowing her to leave Russia.
18She said she had given all the evidence she could, and wanted to return home to Ukraine to her mother.
19Speaking in Geneva, Russian Foreign Minister Sergei Lavrov pledged an exhaustive investigation and criticized what he called "politicized, unsubstantiated and provocative interpretations."
20A spokesman for German Chancellor Angela Merkel said she urged Mr. Putin to ensure the killing is solved and the culprits apprehended.
21"We have been witnessing for some time, and we are witnessing with concern, a climate where the Russian civil society gets intimidated. This climate, this atmosphere, is of course reinforced by this murder. We can only call on Russia to meet its obligations such as those laid out in the European Convention on Human Rights," said the spokesman, Steffen Seibert, in Berlin.
22-Andrea Thomas in Berlin contributed to this article.
