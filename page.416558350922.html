7He said Andrew and Feby, as she is known, celebrated in Besi prison with family and friends.

9Michael said the family would like to celebrate with them again on Tuesday.
10"Hopefully the president will still show some compassion so these young people can continue with their lives."
11Late on Monday it was revealed Indonesia's Constitutional Court agreed to consider a last-ditch legal challenge to the country's clemency laws on May 12.
12Febyanti visits Andrew Chan in March.
13The couple were married on Monday.
14However Attorney General spokesman Tony Spontana said the Indonesian government would not wait for the Constitutional Court's decision.
15"It is not directly related to their case," he said.
16Lawyers for Chan and Myuran Sukumaran and Indonesian human rights groups asked the court to clarify the president's obligations when considering clemency pleas.
17Any ruling made by the Constitutional Court would not be retrospective and the Attorney-General has previously stressed the case would not prevent the executions from proceeding.
18A man prepares coffins ahead of the executions of Bali nine pair Andrew Chan and Myuran Sukumaran.
19Chan asked Feby to marry him in Bali's Kerobokan jail, where they met through a mutual friend.
20Feby, who is an Indonesian pastor, regularly went to the jail as part of her outreach work.

24"He's been in isolation so I thought he wasn't serious."
25He eventually granted the request.
26The wedding was officiated by family friend and Salvation Army minister David Soper.
27Chan has also asked Mr Soper to be his religious adviser in his final hours and witness his execution.
28The families have been told they will have to say their final farewells at about 2pm on Tuesday.
29Nine prisoners, including Chan and fellow Bali nine organizer Myuran Sukumaran, are expected to be executed shortly after midnight.
30Sukumaran's brother, Chinthu, said he had spent the last five years watching young children play with their parents.
31"And I ask the president not to make orphans out of children, Widows...."
32He said.
33"There are family members crying inside the prison as we count down the hours."
34Father Charles Burrows has been here before.
35Seven years ago the Cilacap priest witnessed the execution of two Nigerian heroin smugglers on Nusakambangan.
36It took the men up to eight minutes to die and while they moaned he sang "Amazing Grace".
37This time Father Charles will attend the execution of Brazilian Rodrigo Gularte, who has been diagnosed with schizophrenia.
38"I'm dead against the death penalty," he said after visiting Gularte.
39"The fifth commandment is thou shalt not kill. It's disgusting they are going to do that. No human being has the right to take another's life."
40Father Charles said the reaction to the mass executions would be bad for Indonesia and its people.
41He said polling, which typically finds about 70 per cent of the population supports capital punishment, was done in the cities not the villages.
42"I work in villages and everywhere I go no villages have agreed with the death penalty," he said.
43Father Charles said all those on death row and their families were feeling sad and angry.
44Asked how he felt about witnessing yet another execution, Father Charles said: "I'm happy to be accompanying Rodrigo because I've been with him for seven or eight years. I'm happy to be with him until the end."
