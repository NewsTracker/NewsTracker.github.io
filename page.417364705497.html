Boko Haram clash 'kills 4 Chad soldiers, 33 Islamists'

1A clash between Chad's army and Boko Haram on a Lake Chad island has killed four soldiers and 33 Islamists, a military spokesman said Friday.
2"Boko Haram elements on Wednesday attacked our forces in Choua," military spokesman Colonel Azem Bermandoa Agouna told AFP, referring to an island on the lake in Chadian territory.
3"The Chadian army violently repelled the attack. We killed 33 Boko Haram members and recovered several light arms and anti-tank grenades. We regret the loss of four soldiers and also recorded four wounded," Agouna said.
4He added that Chadian military planes had been targeting Boko Haram positions on the lake since Thursday and had destroyed food, fuel and munitions belonging to the insurgents.
5Since January, Chad has been part of a regional military operation aiming to oust the Islamist group from swathes of territory which it had seized in northeast Nigeria.
6Heavy clashes have taken place in recent months on and around Lake Chad, which is the meeting point for the borders of Nigeria, Chad, Cameroon and Niger.
7In addition to the fighting in Nigeria, Boko Haram has launched repeated attacks in Niger and Chad.
8Chadian soldiers have also fought the militants in Cameroon's northern reaches, which has suffered from ongoing Boko Haram attacks in the last two years.
9Sources in Chad's military say about 5,000 of its soldiers are taking part in the fight against Boko Haram.
10In April Chad announced that 71 of its troops had been killed in operations against the Islamists.
