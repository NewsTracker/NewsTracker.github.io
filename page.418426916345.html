Ex-FIFA official Webb agrees to extradition on U.S. charges: source

1FIFA President Joseph ''Sepp'' Blatter (R) gestures next to CONCACAF President Jeffrey Webb during a news conference at the CONCACAF congress in Panama City in this file April 19, 2013 photo.
2NEW YORK/ZURICH Jeffrey Webb, one of seven current and former officials of soccer's world governing body FIFA arrested in Switzerland in May, has agreed to be extradited to the United States to face corruption charges, a source familiar with the matter said on Friday.
3The Swiss Federal Office of Justice (FOJ) had said earlier on Friday that one of those detained had agreed not to fight extradition but it did not identify the official.
4The source, speaking on condition of anonymity, confirmed to Reuters that Webb, a former FIFA vice president and president of the CONCACAF regional soccer federation, was the official concerned.
5Bloomberg News first reported that Webb, a citizen of the Cayman Islands, was the person.
6A U.S. lawyer for Webb declined to comment.
7Without identifying Webb, the Swiss FOJ said that a transfer of the person would take place within 10 days.
8A spokeswoman for U.S. prosecutors in Brooklyn, New York, declined to comment.
9Webb was one of the powerful figures arrested in Zurich on May 27 two days before FIFA's annual congress, stunning world soccer.
10An indictment unsealed by U.S. prosecutors in Brooklyn charged soccer officials and marketing executives with exploiting the sport for their own gain through bribes of $150 million over 24 years.
11The scandal put pressure on FIFA President Sepp Blatter, who said on June 2 he planned to resign.
12Webb is charged with racketeering conspiracy, wire fraud and money laundering.
13He has been provisionally banned from his posts at FIFA and CONCACAF.
14The U.S. indictment describes Webb as using his influential positions to solicit bribes from sports marketing companies in exchange for the commercial rights to soccer matches.
15One $500,000 bribe payment from a sports marketing firm called Traffic went to build a swimming pool at Webb's house in Loganville, Georgia, the indictment charged.
16Last week, Cayman Islands officials announced separate charges against Webb in an unrelated healthcare fraud case.
17U.S. prosecutors say their investigation - which is running parallel to a separate Swiss inquiry - exposes complex money laundering schemes, millions of dollars in untaxed incomes and tens of millions in offshore accounts held by FIFA officials.
18The United States last week filed extradition requests for the seven officials.
