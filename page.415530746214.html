Islamic State group reports Tunisian leader killed in Libya

1The Islamic State group posted a eulogy on a militant website for Ahmed al-Rouissi after he was killed in clashes around the Libyan city of Sirte - a stronghold for the Islamic State affiliate.
2Tunisian Interior Ministry Spokesman Mohammed Ali Aroui told the state news agency that al-Rouissi's death had been confirmed through "scientific means" and in coordination with Libyan authorities.
3There has been fighting around Sirte between IS militants and the Misrata-based 166th Battalion, which is loyal to the Libyan government in Tripoli.
4Libya's internationally recognized parliament has been forced from the capital and meets in the eastern city of Tobruk.
5It is currently in talks with its Tripoli-based rival about forming a national unity government.
6Al-Rouissi, 48, was considered the mastermind of a string of attacks carried out in Tunisia by the radical Ansar al-Shariah movement, including the 2013 assassinations of left-wing politicians Chokri Belaid and Mohammed Brahmi.
7Their deaths plunged Tunisia into a political crisis that eventually led to the resignation of the elected Islamist government.
8Ansar al-Shariah founder Seifallah Ben Hassine, has been implicated in the September 2012 assault on the U.S. embassy in Tunis which resulted in severe property damage and the death of four protesters when security forces intervened.
9Ben Hassine and Al-Rouissi fled to Libya where they began fighting under the banner of the Islamic State group, which already controls about a third of Syria and Iraq in a self-declared caliphate.
10There are an estimated 3,000 Tunisians that have left the country to fight with extremist groups.
11Citing Tunisian Interior Ministry officials briefing the parliament on Tuesday, the state news agency said some 60 Tunisians had been killed so far in Syria.
12"The greatest danger comes from Libya which is not just a transit country but one where terrorist groups have taken root and built training camps in many cities," said the report.
