Lawyer: Louisiana gun case against millionaire Durst dropped

1FILE -In this Tuesday, April 14, 2015 file photo, Robert Durst leaves Federal Court in an Orleans Parish Sheriff's vehicle after his arraignment, in New Orleans.
2An attorney for jailed real estate heir Robert Durst says Thursday, April 23, 2015, state weapon charges have been dropped against the millionaire, clearing the way for federal proceedings in New Orleans on similar charges.
3Durst, who faces a murder charge in an unrelated California case, remains jailed without bond in Louisiana.
4NEW ORLEANS (AP) - An attorney for real estate heir Robert Durst says state weapon charges against him have been dropped, clearing the way for federal proceedings in New Orleans on similar charges.
5Lawyer Dick DeGuerin said in an email Thursday that state and federal prosecutors had been discussing the possibility of the state case being dropped.
6He says he was pleased with the decision and that there shouldn't be two prosecutions in state and federal court for the same crime.
7Assistant New Orleans District Attorney Christopher Bowman confirmed that the case has been dismissed.
8He says District Attorney Leon Cannizzaro reserved the right to bring the case up again once the federal case is resolved.
9Durst was arrested at a New Orleans hotel in March.
10Prosecutors say he had a gun and marijuana in his room.
