Kenya demands UN move massive Dadaab refugee camp across border to Somalia

1This is the worlds largest refugee camp with a population of 355,000 people, mostly from Somalia.
2Kenya has given the United Nations three months to remove a camp housing more than half a million Somali refugees, as part of a get-tough response to the killing of 148 people by Somali gunmen at a Kenyan university.
3Nairobi has in the past accused Al Shabab of hiding out in Dadaab camp which it now wants the U.N. refugee agency UNHCR to move across the border to inside Somalia.
4"We have asked the UNHCR to relocate the refugees in three months, failure to which we shall relocate them ourselves," Deputy President William Ruto said in a statement.
5"The way America changed after 9/11 is the way Kenya will change after Garissa," he said, referring to the university that was attacked on April 2.
6UNHCR officials were not immediately available for comment.
7Dadaab hosts more than 600,000 thousand Somali refugees, according to Ruto, in a remote, dry corner in northeast Kenya.
8Ruto said Kenya had started building a 440-mile wall along the entire length of the border with Somalia to keep out members of the Somali armed group Al-Shabab.
9"We must secure this country at whatever cost, even if we lose business with Somalia, so be it," he said.
10On Tuesday, Kenya closed 13 informal money remittance firms, hawalas, to cut off funding to suspected radicals.
11Ruto said any business that collaborated with Al-Shabab would be shut down.
12Al-Shabab has killed more than 400 people on Kenyan soil in the last two years, including 67 during a siege at Nairobi's Westgate mall in 2013, damaging tourism and inward investment.
13On Monday, the Kenyan air force launched air strikes against Al-Shabab targets in Somalia, a country where it has been militarily engaged against the armed group for several years.
