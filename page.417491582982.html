Apple Inc recalls Beats Pill XL speakers due to fire hazard from overheating batteries

1It appears Apple Inc. inherited a fire hazard from Dr. Dre and music producer Jimmy Iovine.
2The Cupertino, Calif.-based company said it is recalling Beats Pill XL portable wireless speakers due to a fire hazard from overheating batteries.
3The product was introduced by Beats by Dre in November 2013, prior to Apple's US$3.2-billion acquisition of the Beats - which also makes headphones - in May 2014.
4Customers can return their Beats Pill XL speaker to receive an Apple Store credit or electronic payment of $395, Apple said in a statement.
5The company also advised customers to stop using the product.
6The iPhone maker will recall about 222,000 speakers in the United States and about 11,000 in Canada, the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) said on Wednesday.
7Apple has received eight reports of overheating speakers, the CPSC said in a statement.
8One person's finger was burned and another reported damage to a desk.
9They were sold at Apple's stores and websites since January 2014.
10It was also sold at other retailers.
11The company said the recall does not affect any other Beats or Apple products.
