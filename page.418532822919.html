9About 420 people were watching a midnight showing of "The Dark Knight Rises" on July 20, 2012, in the Denver suburb of Aurora when Holmes opened fire, killing 12 people.
10Fifty-eight others were wounded by gunfire, and 12 were injured in the scramble to escape.
11Holmes surrendered to police outside the theater.
12Holmes is charged with 24 counts of murder and 140 counts of attempted murder - two counts for each person killed and two for each person injured.
13He's also charged with possession of explosives.
14The trial began with opening statements April 27.
15Over the next 11 weeks, jurors heard from more than 250 witnesses, viewed more than 24 hours of video and saw more than 1,500 photos, some of them disturbing images of the victims.
16They also examined scores of pieces of evidence, including Holmes' guns and ammunition.
17Holmes didn't testify.
18SANE OR INSANE?
19Holmes pleaded not guilty by reason of insanity, which under Colorado law means he acknowledges committing the acts but believes he wasn't responsible because he couldn't tell right from wrong.
20Two court-appointed psychiatrists testified he was legally sane; two defense psychiatrists told jurors he was legally insane.
21Under Colorado law, the jurors will determine whether Holmes was sane or insane.
22If they find he is guilty, they will decide on the sentence - death or life in prison without parole.
23If Holmes is convicted of murder, he could be sentenced to execution - which prosecutors want - or to life in prison without the possibility of parole.
24If he's found not guilty by reason of insanity, he would be committed indefinitely to the state mental hospital.
25That means if he were some day declared to be sane, he could be released, although experts say that's unlikely.
26The 12-member jury and seven alternates were chosen after closing arguments Tuesday.
27Deliberations begin Wednesday.
28The judge originally seated 12 jurors, plus 12 alternates to replace any jurors who had to be dismissed for health or other reasons.
29Five had been dismissed, either for seeing news reports about the case or, in the case of one juror, having a family member injured in an unrelated crime during the trial.
30Counseling will be available to the jurors after they have reached a verdict and the trial is over.
31It took years for the judge, the prosecution and the defense to plow through the mountain of evidence and hash out numerous legal questions.
32Further complicating matters were the number of victims, the prosecution's decision to seek the death penalty and Holmes' decision to plead not guilty by reason of insanity.
33It took nearly three more months to choose the jury after 9,000 summonses were sent.
34Holmes offered to plead guilty if prosecutors would agree to a life sentence without parole.
35Prosecutors rejected the offer.
