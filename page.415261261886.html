Apple is joining the Dow Jones index

1Apple will become one of the 30 corporations that comprise the Dow Jones industrial average, according to S&P Dow Jones Indices, the company that manages the index.
2The tech titan - the biggest U.S. company with a market capitalization of nearly $728 billion - will replace AT&T, the telecommunications giant that first joined the Dow in 1916, when there were only 16 companies listed.
3"As the largest corporation in the world and a leader in technology, Apple is the clear choice for the Dow," said David Blitzer, chairman of the index committee at S&P Dow Jones Indices.
4The move, announced Friday and effective March 18, comes as little surprise to Wall Street, which had widely expected Apple to become part of the index after its 7-for-1 stock split last June reduced its share price enough to make it a good fit.
5Apple's then-$700 stock price would have overwhelmed the Dow, which is weighted according to a company's share price rather than its market value.
6Apple's stock price of around $127 as of Friday will only give it the sixth-biggest weighting, with a 4.3% share.
7Goldman Sachs currently is the most expensive stock in the Dow, at about $187.
8But Apple's inclusion in the 116-year-old index gives the Dow - which for most finance pros takes a back seat to the Standard & Poor's 500 as a true market benchmark - a needed shot in the arm, analysts said.
9"It's high time this happened," Uri Landesman, president of investment management firm Platinum Partners LLC, told the Daily News.
10"No one can argue that AT&T was more important than Apple, so it seems an important move by a stodgy (index) to look like it's finally catching up to 2015," Landesman said.
11"The Dow is still the index the public looks at, but its collection of older, blue-chip stocks made it become somewhat dated and synonymous with not being sexy anymore," Michael Mullaney, chief investment officer Fiduciary Trust, told The News.
12"This definitely adds pizazz. They realized they had to do something to make the index more relevant."
