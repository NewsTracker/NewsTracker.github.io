5Earlier this month, Sir Simon signalled a desire to return but said London lacked a world-class concert hall, dismissing the 1,940-capacity Barbican Hall as merely "serviceable".
6"You have no idea how wonderful an orchestra like the London Symphony Orchestra can sound in a great concert hall," he said.
7His campaign for a new venue has the support of George Osborne, who announced a feasibility study into the idea.
8Announcing his new appointment at a press conference at the Barbican, Sir Simon said coming to the LSO was not contingent on the hall being built.
9But he will continue to lobby for the project.
10"It's going to be incredibly important, not only for the orchestra and the city but the country as a whole - to be able to bring a new vision to music, one that can include as many people as possible."
11The LSO's principal conductor, Valery Gerviev, stands down at the end of this year and the orchestra will be without a leader until Sir Simon takes up his post.
12Sir Simon is the first to hold the title of LSO music director since Claudio Abbado in the 1980s.
13The role is greater than that of principal conductor, with education and outreach at its heart.
14"Frankly, what goes on in the primary school is going to be just as important as what goes on in the concert hall. Until everybody sees that as a continuum, we're going to have a problem," he said.
15A key aim is to bring classical music to a new audience.

17"We have to make sure this information gets through to people. It may not be for absolutely every person but every person should have the possibility of experiencing it and seeing what it can bring to their lives."
18Liverpudlian-born Sir Simon, 60, became Britain's youngest music director at 25, when he took charge of the City of Birmingham Symphony Orchestra.
19He joined the Berlin Philharmonic in 2002, and during his time there fought for its musicians to be paid a better wage.
