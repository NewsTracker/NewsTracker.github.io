Israel's Benjamin Netanyahu Faces New Scandal Over Alleged Lavish Expenses

1The uproar jolted an election campaign in which Netanyahu's opponents have tried to portray him as enjoying a lavish lifestyle and being out of touch with the struggles of average Israelis.
2Netanyahu's allies angrily dismissed the allegations as part of a campaign to deflect attention from more serious issues over Israel's security challenges.
3In its report, the state comptroller, an official watchdog agency, cited large sums of public money spent on food, furniture and gardening at the couple's official residence and their private home in the exclusive coastal city of Caesarea.
4It also said the couple pocketed proceeds from recycling bottles that had been purchased for entertaining guests.
5The Netanyahus are no strangers to such scrutiny.
6The prime minister has long been saddled with an image as a cigar-smoking, cognac-drinking socialite, while his wife has come under fire for her own expensive tastes and alleged abusive behavior toward staff.
7The Netanyahus accuse the Israeli media of a longstanding witch hunt against them.
8In a statement, Netanyahu said he respects the findings of the comptroller and pledged to implement recommendations to curb wasteful spending.
9At the same time he lashed out at the "ongoing media campaign" aimed at toppling him from power.

11"There is absolutely no indication of any assault on the public's integrity and certainly no indication of any criminal transgressions," he said.
12A former member of their housekeeping staff has filed a lawsuit claiming he was mistreated and verbally abused by Sara Netanyahu.

14In another, he claimed that she chastised him because some flowers in a vase were a day old.
