MLB: Orioles win in return to Baltimore

1Orioles 5, Blue Jays 2: Manny Machado, Chris Davis and Adam Jones hit homers, and the Orioles won in their return to Baltimore.
2Because of rioting and violence in the city, the Orioles had not played in front of their home fans since April 26.
3Yankees 11, Rays 5: CC Sabathia won for the first time in more than a year, and Alex Rodriguez hit one of five New York homers.
4Sabathia (1-5), who missed most of last season with a knee injury, earned his first win since April 24, 2014.
5Rodriguez's eighth homer of the season was the 662nd of his career.
6Pirates 4, Phillies 3: Gerrit Cole pitched seven effective innings, and Starling Marte hit a three-run homer to spark Pittsburgh.
7Brewers 10, White Sox 7: Elian Herrera and Khris Davis homered in the eighth inning to boost Milwaukee.
8Herrera's two-run shot off Zach Duke broke a 7-all tie.
9Chicago starter Jeff Samardzija was tagged for eight hits and seven runs -- five earned -- in six innings.
10Cubs 4, Mets 3: Kris Bryant and Anthony Rizzo homered in the first inning to power Chicago.
11The Cubs opened the bleachers in left and center for the first time this season at Wrigley Field, and Bryant provided a stirring welcome with a two-run shot to left for the prospect's first homer at home.
12Rangers 8, Royals 2: Adrian Beltre hit his 399th career home run, Thomas Field hit his first, and Texas beat a weary Kansas City team.
13The Royals didn't arrive in Texas until 5:45 a.m. Monday after a rain-delayed win in Detroit.
14Braves 2, Reds 1: Atlanta pinch-hitter Phil Gosselin singled off Aroldis Chapman to open the ninth, stole a base and scored on the Cincinnati closer's wild pitch.
15Nationals 11, Diamondbacks 1: Denard Span and Ryan Zimmerman homered in the first inning, and Washington cruised to its fifth straight win.
