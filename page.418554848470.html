Liberia confirms new Ebola case as outbreak spreads

1A man walks by a health information mural in Monrovia, Liberia.
2Monrovia: A Liberian woman has died of Ebola in a hospital shortly after being admitted, becoming the sixth confirmed case and second death since the virus resurfaced last month, a senior medical official said.
3The victim, Kebbeh Kollie, lived near the capital Monrovia.
4She was the sister of Cassius Kollie, one of five Ebola cases discovered in neighbouring Margibi County, where the disease re-emerged after a seven-week lull.
5Her detection raised fears that the infection may be spreading in the Montserrado County area of Liberia, which includes the capital.
6A quarantined home is seen in Nedowein, Liberia on Wednesday.
7More than 11,200 people have died from Ebola since an epidemic broke out in December 2013 in neighbouring Guinea.
8Liberia was declared Ebola-free on May 9 but reported a new case nearly two months later.
9The outbreak is also still active in Sierra Leone and Guinea.
10"There is one new case," Liberia's chief medical officer, Dr. Francis Kateh, said.
11"This time, the response area is Montserrado county. The person died in Monrovia."
12A health worker sprays disinfectant on an ambulance in Nedowein, Liberia on Wednesday.
13A health report sent to officials in the anti-Ebola response unit said the woman died a few hours after admission, indicating that surveillance of known contacts from the earlier cases had not been rigorous enough.
14Sixteenpeople were under quarantine in the Kollie family's unfinished house and the woman's brother was being treated at a specialised unit in the capital..
15On Monday, a study published in the Journal of Clinical Investigation in the US, said a single dose of a new Ebola vaccine that can be inhaled has been found to neutralise the deadly virus in monkeys.
16It was the first attempt to use an aerosol to vaccinate monkeys against a hemorrhagic viral fever, researchers from University of Texas and National Institutes of Health in Bethesda, Maryland, said.
17But the success of the vaccine in monkeys is no guarantee that it will work in people.
18This year, an Ebola drug that had been effective in monkeys failed to help humans.
19The first detected case in the current virus resurgence in Liberia, 17-year-old Abraham Memaigar, died before his infection was confirmed.
20Neighbours voiced sympathy for the Kollie family in their quarantine but also concern over the spread of the disease.
21"Their daughter has died and now someone there could be sick," said Junior Flomo.
22"I am afraid for myself and my family. Family came to visit them and now someone has died of Ebola. That could happen to us, too."
23Health officials say the virus probably remained latent in Liberia during the lull and could have been reactivated by a survivor, via sexual transmission.
