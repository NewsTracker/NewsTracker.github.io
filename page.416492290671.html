Review: Getting your Apple Watch? Here's how to use it

1NEW YORK (AP) - Apple Watches have arrived to the first group of people who had ordered one two weeks ago.
2Those who haven't bought one yet will likely face a wait.
3Apple's retail stores won't have them, with the company taking orders over the Internet only - for delivery as late as July for some models.
4Although a handful of other stores are carrying them, quantities are limited - and you have to make it to the store.
5Maxfield in Los Angeles is carrying the watch, along with boutique and department stores in Paris, London, Tokyo and Berlin and authorized resellers in China and Japan.
6Whenever you get yours, get ready for a new way of interacting with your digital life.
7A better way?
8I'll need more time with the watch to say.
9Update your iPhone's software before starting.
10You need at least iOS 8.2.
11You also need the password for your Apple ID account.
12Select your language on the watch and open the Apple Watch app on the iPhone.
13Hit "Start Pairing" on both devices.
14Here's where it gets fun: Use your iPhone camera to photograph the watch animation, which has an embedded code so the phone knows to connect to that specific watch.
15Follow the prompts on the phone app.
16At the screen for installing apps, choose "Install All" to get watch versions of apps already on your phone.
17It took about 12 minutes for all my apps, contacts and calendar entries to sync.
18Use that time to watch video tutorials on your phone.
19You can adjust watch settings by going to "My Watch" in the phone app.
20Don't spend too much time at first.
21See how the watch fits in with your lifestyle.
22Initially, notifications on the watch will match those on the phone.
23If that gets annoying, customize the settings so that some notifications go to the phone only.
24For email, you can designate specific contacts as VIPs and have only their messages reach your watch.
25The watch borrows many gestures and controls from the phone, but that isn't always practical with a smaller screen.
26Getting used to the changes will take time.
27- Lightly press the dial, known as the Digital Crown, to toggle between the watch face and the menu of apps.
28It's also typically how you exit apps.
29Double press to get your most recently used app.
30A hard press gets you Siri, Apple's voice assistant.
31Turn the dial to zoom, scroll or flip through menus.
32My instinct was to turn the dial like a knob, but it's easier to slide your finger over it, like rolling the wheel on a toy car.
33- The other button gives you quick access to friends - favorites from your phone's contact list and what you add under "Friends" in the iPhone's watch app.
34You can call or message - or send doodles and vibrations if your friend has an Apple Watch.
35Double click this button to use Apple Pay at retail stores.
36- Swipe down to get notifications.
37Swipe up to get Glances - highlights such as weather, calendar and stock quotes.
38Swipe left and right to go through the various Glances.
39The one on the far left is the settings, such as muting.
40- Interact with icons and buttons by lightly tapping on the screen.
41A hard press, known as a Force Touch, will activate other functions.
42It changes from app to app, and the best way to learn is to try it and see what it does.
43Force Touch the watch face to choose other designs and options.
44- For a screenshot, press both side buttons simultaneously- lightly, or it won't work.
45Images appear in your phone's Photos app.
46One of the Glances is for the Activity app.
47Go through the prompts so the watch can nag you when you've been sitting too long.
48The watch uses the GPS sensor on your phone to measure distance during workouts.
49Without the phone, it counts steps, which tend to be inaccurate.
50However, if you walk or run with your phone a few times, the watch will calibrate those steps for better accuracy offline.
51You can listen to music or view photos stored on the device.
52Go to the phone's Apple Watch app to choose playlists and albums to sync.
53Go to "Passbook & Apple Pay" on the phone app to enable mobile payments at retail stores.
54You can then pay without the phone with you.
55To add apps, visit the phone's Apple Watch app.
56Visit an Apple store.
57No appointment is needed.
58Besides the video tutorials, Apple is also offering free video chats online.
