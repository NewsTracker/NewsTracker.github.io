Accused theater gunman James Holmes says he won't testify

1Centennial, Colorado (CNN)As his mass murder trial nears its end, accused movie theater shooter James Holmes told a judge Thursday that he won't testify.
2Arapahoe County Judge Carlos Samour advised Holmes of his rights and asked him a series of questions about testifying.
3He was told he would be cross-examined if he chose to testify and that the jury could ask questions, with the judge's approval.
4"I choose not to testify," he said.
5The jury was out of the room at the time.
6The jurors in the case have heard from Holmes through 22 hours of videotaped interviews with a court-mandated independent psychiatrist.
7Those interviews were recorded two years after Holmes killed 12 people at an Aurora, Colorado, movie theater.
8Holmes, a former doctoral student in neuroscience, is standing trial on charges of capital murder and other offenses.
9He is accused of killing 12 people and injuring 70 more on July 20, 2012.
10He faces 165 counts.
11Holmes has pleaded not guilty by reason of insanity.
12Prosecutors have said they will seek the death penalty.
13Holmes admits to the shootings but has said he was suffering "a psychotic episode" at the time.
