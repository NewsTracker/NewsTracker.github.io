Recap: Toronto vs. Baltimore

1Toronto, ON (SportsNetwork.com) - Drew Hutchison carried a perfect game into the sixth inning, and the Toronto Blue Jays held off a comeback effort in the ninth to notch a 7-6 victory over the Baltimore Orioles that completed a series sweep.
2Hutchison (2-0) held the Orioles to two runs and four hits while fanning seven without a walk over eight outstanding innings.
3He exited with a 7-2 lead that was cut down to a run after Baltimore scored four times in the ninth.
4Manny Machado's second homer of the night, a three-run shot off Miguel Castro, brought the Orioles within 7-6 before the Toronto rookie retired the next three hitters to finish off the Blue Jays' first three-game sweep of Baltimore since June of 2013.
5The Blue Jays have amassed 47 runs in winning five of their six meetings with the Orioles this season.
6Josh Donaldson went 2-for-4 with a two-run homer for Toronto, which battered Chris Tillman en route to building a 7-0 lead after five innings.
7Machado broke up Hutchison's perfect game bid with a solo homer in the sixth, while Adam Jones finished 2-for-4 with two runs scored in the Orioles' fourth consecutive loss.
8Tillman (2-2) turned in a second straight dud against Toronto, which rocked the righty for seven runs in 2 2/3 innings in Baltimore on April 12.
9He was plagued by three walks while permitting five hits -- four of which went for extra bases -- in Thursday's outing.
10Tillman's command began to desert him in the bottom of the third inning, which began with a walk to Danny Valencia and Pillar's infield single.
11Following a sacrifice bunt and Devon Travis' fielder's choice grounder in which Valencia beat Chris Davis' throw home, Donaldson crushed a flat changeup off the facade of the upper deck in left for a 3-0 lead.
12More control problems came Tillman's way in the fourth, as he walked the first two hitters prior to yielding Valencia's RBI double that plated Justin Smoak.
13Pillar then smashed a ball off the third-base bag for a two-run double that increased Toronto's advantage to 6-0.
14"Other than a few pitches, I feel like I beat us tonight," Tillman said.
15"I walked way too many, I fell behind way too much."
16Donaldson's leadoff double in the fifth led to another run charged to Tillman when Russell Martin greeted reliever Brad Brach with a two-out double.
17Hutchison, meanwhile, kept on cruising.
18The right-hander needed just 28 pitches his first time through the Baltimore lineup and threw only 56 while still perfect after five.
19"I felt like I was doing a good job being aggressive in the zone," Hutchison said.
20"I got a good tempo going, got my fastball going and just keeping ahead of guys, that was the difference."
21Machado drove his initial offering in the sixth over the wall in left, however, to end the bid.
22The Orioles put up another run in the seventh when Jones doubled, took third on a Delmon Young single and came home on Travis Snider's fielder's choice force out, then put quite a scare into the home crowd in the ninth.
23After Jones, Young and Snider all singled off Liam Hendricks to produce a run, Machado worked the count full against Castro before drilling a fastball into the left-field seats to trim the deficit to 7-6.
