Hillary Clinton promises to release more emails

Chelsea still leading the pack

1A House committee investigating the Benghazi, Libya, attacks issued subpoenas Wednesday for the emails of Hillary Rodham Clinton, who used a private account exclusively for official business when she was secretary of state - and also used a computer email server now traced back to her family's New York home.
2The subpoenas from the Republican-led Select Committee on Benghazi demanded additional material from Clinton and others related to Libya, spokesman Jamal D. Ware said.
3The panel also instructed technology companies it did not identify to preserve any relevant documents in their possession.
4The development on Capitol Hill came the same day The Associated Press reported the existence of a personal email server traced back to the Chappaqua, N.Y., home of Clinton.
5The unusual practice of a Cabinet-level official running her own email server would have given Clinton - who is expected to run for president in the 2016 campaign - significant control over limiting access to her message archives.
6The practice also would complicate the State Department's legal responsibilities in finding and turning over official emails in response to any investigations, lawsuits or public records requests.
7The department would be in the position of accepting Clinton's assurances she was surrendering everything required that was in her control.
8Late Wednesday, a message was sent on Clinton's Twitter account that remarked on the growing controversy.
9"I want the public to see my email. I asked State to release them," according to the tweet from (at)Hillary Clinton.
10"They said they will review them for release as soon as possible."
11Congress said it learned last summer about Clinton's use of a private email account to conduct official State Department business during its investigation of the Benghazi attacks on a U.S. mission in which four Americans died.
12"It doesn't matter if the server was in Foggy Bottom, Chappaqua or Bora Bora," House Speaker John Boehner said.
13"The Benghazi Select Committee needs to see all of these emails, because the American people deserve all of the facts."
14The chairman of the Benghazi committee, Rep.
15Trey Gowdy, R-S.C., told reporters: "I want the documents. Sooner rather than later."
16"Everything I've seen so far has led me to believe that this is an effort to go after Hillary Clinton, period," said Rep.
17Elijah Cummings of Maryland, the top Democrat on the committee.
18The questions about Clinton's email practices left the Obama administration in an awkward position.
19The White House said it was her responsibility to make sure any emails about official business weren't deleted from her private server.
20Meanwhile, the AP said it was considering legal action under the U.S. Freedom of Information Act against the State Department for failing to turn over some emails covering Clinton's tenure as the nation's top diplomat after waiting more than one year.
21The department has never suggested that it doesn't possess all Clinton's emails.
22It was not immediately clear exactly where Clinton's computer server was run, but a business record for the Internet connection it used was registered under the home address for her residence as early as August 2010.
23The customer was listed as Eric Hoteham.
24An aide to then-first lady Clinton was identified in a 2002 congressional report as Eric Hothem, whose name is spelled differently than in the Internet records.
25Hothem, a financial adviser in Washington, was not available to take an AP reporter's phone call at his office Wednesday.
26He was a special assistant to Clinton as far back as 1997 and considered one of the family's information technology experts.
27In most cases, individuals who operate their own email servers are technical experts or users so concerned about issues of privacy and surveillance they take matters into their own hands.
28Clinton - who emailed so frequently using her BlackBerry as secretary of state that it became an Internet meme - is particularly sensitive about disclosures of personal files based on her experiences in confronting congressional investigations and civil lawsuits during her husband's election and presidency and her own roles as first lady, senator, presidential candidate and Cabinet official.
29State Department spokeswoman Marie Harf said Clinton as Cabinet secretary never used a government email account on the agency's separate network for sharing classified information, which Clinton would have been prohibited from forwarding to her private email account.
30"She had other ways of communicating through classified email through her assistants or her staff, with people, when she needed to use a classified setting," Harf said.
31Government employees generally use servers run by federal agencies where they work.
32Clinton has not described her reasons for using a private email account - hdr22(at)clintonemail.com, which appears to include a nod to her middle name, Diane.
33A spokesman for her did not respond to requests seeking comment from the AP on Tuesday or Wednesday.
34Operating her own server would have afforded Clinton additional legal opportunities to block government or private subpoenas in criminal, administrative or civil cases because her lawyers could object in court before being forced to turn over any emails.
35And since the Secret Service was guarding Clinton's home, an email server there would have been well protected from theft or a physical hacking.
36But homemade email servers are generally not as reliable, secure from hackers or protected from fires or floods as those in commercial data centres.
37The Hoteham registration is also associated with a separate email server, presidentclinton.com, and a nonfunctioning website, wjcoffice.com, all linked to the same residential internet account as Mrs. Clinton's email server.
38The former president's full name is William Jefferson Clinton.
39Hothem, the former Clinton aide, surfaced in at least two Clinton administration controversies.
40A congressional report in 2002 investigating pardons said a Citibank account linked to Hothem wired $15,000 to President Clinton's brother, Roger, in March 2001, while investigators were trying to compel Roger Clinton to testify about his role in several pardon cases.
41The president's lawyer told investigators the money came from a personal account of the Clintons and was intended for Clinton's brother to hire a lawyer.
42In early 2001, Hothem was also named by a former White House chief usher as saying the Clintons were permitted to take furniture when they left the White House that later was determined to belong to the government.
43Mrs. Clinton's email options included using an official State Department account or even a secret agency email address, which the AP revealed in 2013 as a common practice across the U.S. government and by previous administrations.
44Many senior U.S. officials use alternate addresses that aren't disclosed to the public for official business so they are not inundated with unwanted messages.
45But the State Department's email system might not have been attractive to Clinton because it is frequently targeted by hackers.
46The AP revealed in 2006 and 2014 that the agency had suffered significant electronic break-ins.
47In the most recent incident, the department took the unprecedented step of shutting down its entire unclassified email system.
48While Clinton was secretary of state in 2010, a U.S. soldier, Chelsea Manning, stole 250,000 diplomatic cables and turned them over to WikiLeaks, which published them online.
