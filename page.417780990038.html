Morrison breaks through at CWS in TCU's 10-3 win over LSU

1TCU's Derek Odell, left, celebrates his double as LSU first baseman Chris Chinea, right, looks on in the sixth inning of an NCAA College World Series baseball game in Omaha, Neb., Sunday, June 14, 2015.
2Only one pitcher in TCU history has won more games than Preston Morrison, and none has worked more innings.
3His accomplishments are even more remarkable when you consider he joined the Horned Frogs as a walk-on.
4Before Sunday, though, he had never won a game in eight career NCAA Tournament starts.
5The senior finally added that to his resume, pitching seven dominant innings in the Frogs' 10-3 victory over LSU in the College World Series.
6"He's the best pitcher in the history of the school, bar none, and he's pitched great in the postseason throughout his entire career," TCU coach Jim Schlossnagle said.
7"He hasn't had a lot to show for it because when you're in the postseason, you're facing another great pitcher."
8That looked to be the case again in the matchup with LSU left-hander Jared Poche', who had come to Omaha having allowed one run in 16 1/3 innings in the national tournament.
9But Poche' had a stunning middle-inning meltdown after retiring nine in a row to start the game.
10TCU capitalized and won easily in the first game of its second straight CWS appearance.
11Morrison (12-2), whose 37 career wins are the most of any active Division I pitcher and one behind TCU record-holder Frank Johnstone, limited LSU to five hits and a run in seven innings.
12He walked none, struck out five and retired the last 12 batters he faced.
13"The most important thing for me was getting another step closer to our ultimate goal, the national championship," Morrison said.
14The Horned Frogs (50-13) will play a Bracket 2 winners' game against Cal State Fullerton or Vanderbilt on Tuesday night.
15The Tigers (53-11) meet one of those teams in an elimination game that afternoon.
16LSU, in the CWS for the 17th time, showed up as the No.
172 national seed and 5-0 in the NCAA Tournament.
18The Tigers cruised along nicely for three innings, with Poche' (9-2) striking out the side in the first and retiring the next six batters.
19But in the fourth, Cody Jones hit a chopper back to Poche' leading off and wound up on third when Poche' overthrew first baseman Chris Chinea.
20Then, Jeremie Fagnan sent a comebacker to Poche', who looked back Jones at third before pulling Chinea off the bag with another high throw, putting runners at the corners.
21Connor Wanhanen and Dane Steinhagen followed with RBI singles.
22"He comes out in the first inning with all the pressure in the world on his shoulders, and he strikes out the side and gets nine up, nine down and should have had 11 up, 11 down," LSU coach Paul Mainieri said.
23"The next two batters hit feeble groundballs right back to him and he didn't execute the play. It spiraled from there."
24Poche' couldn't explain his struggle to make the routine throw to first.
25"I'm not sure why I lobbed it like that," he said.
26"If I could do it again, I'd set my feet and hit him in his chest."
27In the fifth, TCU loaded the bases against Poche' on two walks and a single.
28Conner Hale booted a grounder at third, allowing one run to score, and Poche' plunked Fagnan, bringing home another.
29Connor Wanhanen's single drove in two more to put the Frogs up 6-1 run and end Poche's day.
30TCU extended its lead to 10-1 in the seventh, batting around against three relievers and scoring four runs, all coming with two outs.
31Morrison threw strikes on 63 of his 89 pitches, but Schlossnagle took him out to start the eighth.
32Brian Howard came on and gave up Jared Foster's drive into the left-field bullpen.
33It was the third home run in three CWS games, matching last year's 16-game total.
34Schlossnagle said he wants Morrison well-rested because he anticipates needing the Chicago Cubs' eighth-round draft pick again.
35"You're looking at a TCU hall of famer and a guy who can pitch in the big leagues, and I have no doubt about that," Schlossnagle said.
