The Internet flooded with tributes to Nintendo chief

1A shopper walks in front of Nintendo's Super Mario characters at an electronics store in Tokyo, Monday, July 13, 2015.
2Satoru Iwata, who led Japanese video game company Nintendo Co. through years of growth with its Pokemon and Super Mario franchises, died on the weekend of a bile duct tumor, the company said Monday.
3He was 55.
4Shoppers move on an escalator under Nintendo's Super Mario characters at an electronics store in Tokyo, Monday, July 13, 2015.
5TOKYO (AP) - Nintendo Co. President Satoru Iwata's death from an illness at 55 is being mourned by game fans around the world.
6They're sharing Iwata Mii avatars, creating pictures with farewell messages on the Nintendo drawing game Splatoon and sending Tweets with "ThankYouIwata" hashtags.
7A petition is circulating, amassing thousands of signatures, urging Nintendo to create an "amiibo" figurine in the likeness of Iwata.
8The small dolls packed with sensors, which already come in Nintendo characters such as Super Mario and Kirby, are used to play games.
9"Even if the amiibo itself lacks in-game functionality, it serves as a small reminder of Iwata's legacy and accomplishments," the petition says.
10Nintendo had no comment.
11An illustration showing Nintendo characters weeping around Iwata lying down with his eyes closed on a bed of flowers is going viral on Twitter.
12People said they found the drawing moving and a reminder that there would have been no Pokemon without him.
13"Thank you, Mr. Iwata," the words say above the picture.
14One of the quotes people were remembering from Iwata was: "On my business card, I am a corporate president. In my mind, I am a game developer. But in my heart, I am a gamer."
15A YouTube clip of his keynote speech, delivered in English, at the Game Developers Conference in San Francisco 10 years ago, was attracting droves of views.
16He showed his business card, pointed to his head and then brushed his chest as he opened his speech with that comment.
17Various Iwata images are circulating in online homage, such as the one of him staring at a bunch of bananas, which had set off parody offshoots when it first popped up in 2012, becoming an Iwata meme.
18A video tribute by IGN includes that image as well as many others, including most recently Iwata's appearance as a puppet at the E3 conference earlier this year.
