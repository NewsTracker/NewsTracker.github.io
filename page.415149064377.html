6Three young men charged in New York with plotting to join the Islamic State group and carry out attacks on American soil.
7A masked, knife-wielding militant from London who is the face of terror in videos showing Western hostages beheaded.
8They are among tens of thousands of Muslims eager to pledge allegiance to the Islamic State group.
9An estimated 20,000 have streamed into the territory in Iraq and Syria where the group has proclaimed what it calls a "caliphate" ruled by its often brutal version of Islamic law.
10But how rooted in Islam is the ideology embraced by this group that has inspired so many to fight and die?
11President Barack Obama has insisted the militants behind a brutal campaign of beheadings, kidnappings and enslavement are "not Islamic" and only use a veneer of Islam for their own ends.
12Obama's critics argue the extremists are intrinsically linked to Islam.
13Others insist their ideology has little connection to religion.
14The group itself has assumed the mantle of Islam's earliest years, purporting to recreate the conquests and rule of the Prophet Muhammad and his successors.
15But in reality its ideology is a virulent vision all its own, one that its adherents have created by plucking selections from centuries of traditions.
16The vast majority of Muslim clerics say the group cherry picks what it wants from Islam's holy book, the Quran, and from accounts of Muhammad's actions and sayings, known as the Hadith.
17It then misinterprets many of these, while ignoring everything in the texts that contradicts those hand-picked selections, these experts say.
18The group's claim to adhere to the prophecy and example of Muhammad helps explain its appeal among young Muslim radicals eager to join its ranks.
19Much like Nazi Germany evoked a Teutonic past to inspire its followers, Islamic State propaganda almost romantically depicts its holy warriors as re-establishing the caliphate, contending that ideal of Islamic rule can come only through blood and warfare.
20It maintains its worst brutalities - beheading captives, taking women and girls as sex slaves and burning to death a captured Jordanian pilot - only prove its purity in following what it contends is the prophet's example, a claim that appalls the majority of the world's 1.6 billion Muslims.
21Writings by the group's clerics and ideologues and its English-language online magazine, Dabiq, are full of citations from Quranic verses, the Hadith and centuries of interpreters, mostly hard-liners.
22But these are often taken far out of context, said Joas Wagemakers, an assistant professor of Islamic Studies at Radboud University Nijmegen in the Netherlands, who specializes in Islamic militant thought.
23Muslim scholars throughout history have used texts in a "decontextualized way" to suit their purposes, Wagemakers said.
24But the Islamic State goes "further than any other scholars have done. They represent the extreme," he said.
25It would be a mistake to conclude the Islamic State group's extremism is the "true Islam" that emerges from the Quran and Hadith, he added.
26Despite its claim to the contrary, the Islamic State group is largely political, borne out of the conflicts in Syria and Iraq, said Khaled Abou El Fadl, an Islamic law scholar at the University of California, Los Angeles.
27Ahmed al-Dawoody, an assistant professor at the Institute for Islamic World Studies at Zayed University in Dubai, agreed.
28The phenomenon of reading religious sources out of context "has existed throughout the ages," he said.
29"We should not grant any legitimacy to those who violate Islam, then hijack it and speak on its behalf."
30"This is not Islamic terror, this is terror committed by Muslims," he said.
