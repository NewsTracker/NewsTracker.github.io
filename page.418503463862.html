Man planning Boston Marathon-style bombing attack arrested

1Boston Marathon bombers Dzhokhar (left) and Tamerlan Tsarnaev just before their pressure-cooker bombs went off on April 15, 2013.
2Boston: A heavily armed Massachusetts man who was building bombs in his apartment and planned to attack a crowded university campus cafeteria on behalf of Islamic State has been arrested, US authorities say.
3Alexander Ciccolo, 23, was arrested on July 4 for the unlawful receipt of multiple guns, the US Justice Department said in a news release.
4His indictment was unsealed on Monday.
5The university he intended to target was not disclosed.
6FBI agents put Mr Ciccolo under surveillance after being alerted to some of his posts on social media.
7They watched him buy at least one pressure cooker at a Wal-Mart store in Massachusetts, according to an affidavit by FBI special agent Paul Ambrogio.
8Pressure-cooker bombs were used in the attacks on the Boston Marathon in 2013, killing three and injuring 264 people.
9Mr Ciccolo is accused of being a supporter of Islamic State.
10Mr Ciccolo told an informant he wanted to build bombs similar to the ones used in the attacks on the marathon, according to the affidavit.
11After his arrest, authorities said they searched his apartment and found partially built bombs.
12"These incendiary devices contained what appeared to be shredded Styrofoam soaking in motor oil," Mr Ambrogio said in his affidavit.
13Mr Ciccolo allegedly said the Styrofoam would cause the fire from the exploded devices to stick to people's skin and make it harder to put the fire out, according to the affidavit.
14Mr Ciccolo was arrested after taking delivery of two Glock handguns, a Colt AR-15 rifle and a SigArms high-powered rifle, according to authorities.
15After his arrest while at the Franklin County House of Correction, Mr Ciccolo is accused of stabbing a nurse in the head with a pen.
16The assault left a hole in the nurse's skin and caused the pen to break in half, according to Mr Ambrogio's affidavit.
17It did not immediately know whether Mr Ciccolo had an attorney.
