Greece makes IMF payment, another looms

1FILE - In this Thursday, Sept.

3Wednesday May 6 marks five years since Greece voted in its first bailout deal in the face of violent popular protests that left three dead.
4The day was followed by years of turmoil in which the country tried to overhaul its economy in the midst of a downturn matched in depth and length only by the Great Depression.
5ATHENS, Greece (AP) - Greece is making a 200 million euro ($222 million) repayment to the International Monetary Fund, though another, larger one looms next week that it will struggle to manage.
6A Greek finance ministry official said Wednesday: "The payment is proceeding normally."
7The official spoke only on condition of anonymity in line with government regulations.
8Greece is struggling to agree on a deal with its creditors to get more rescue loans and is gradually running out of cash.
9The sides hope to make progress by May 11, when they will hold a eurozone meeting.
10On May 12, Greece is due to repay 750 million euros to the IMF, money it will have to scrape together from local reserves, such as those of local governments and hospitals.
