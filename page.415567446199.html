Two more U.S. healthcare workers repatriated for Ebola monitoring

1(Reuters) - The last two members of a group of U.S. healthcare workers whose colleague is being treated for the Ebola virus returned on Wednesday from Sierra Leone to the United States, where they are being monitored for possible exposure to the deadly virus.
2Their repatriation brought the total number of Americans brought back from the West African nation to 18 since last Friday, the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention said Wednesday night.
3"They were part of the same group that have been coming back slowly, said CDC spokeswoman Jennifer McQuiston.
4Neither of the two healthcare workers brought back Wednesday has tested positive for Ebola, but they are being monitored because they might have been exposed to a colleague who has contracted it.
5The infected healthworker is being treated at a biocontainment unit run by the National Institutes of Health in Maryland, McQuiston said.
6The CDC has not yet said how the initial patient was exposed to the disease.
7The others are being observed at several different locations in the United States, including Atlanta, Maryland and Nebraska.
8Most of the healthworkers were employed by the aid group Partners in Health, though CDC has not divulged their names.
9McQuiston did not say where the two who arrived on Wednesday entered the country, or where they were being sent for monitoring.
