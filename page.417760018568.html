Egypt to inaugurate Suez Canal expansion

1Construction work on Egypt's New Suez Canal project.
2Ismailiya, Egypt: Egypt will inaugurate a "new Suez canal" shipping route in August aimed at speeding up traffic along the existing waterway and boosting revenues, officials say.
3Dubbed the Suez Canal Axis, the new 72-kilometre project will run part of the way alongside the existing canal that connects the Red Sea to the Mediterranean.
4"The digging and dredging operations will be completed on July 15," Suez Canal Authority chief Mohab Mameesh told reporters in the canal city of Ismailiya.
5A ceremony will be held on August 6 to inaugurate the project, he said.
6Egyptian President Abdel Fattah al-Sisi launched the project in August and set an ambitious target of digging the new canal in one year.
7"Once the president inaugurates it, vessels will start moving through the new waterway," Mr Mameesh said.
8He said 85 per cent of the project, which is being executed by the army, has been completed so far.
9Authorities raised $US9 billion ($11.6 billion) to build the new canal by selling shares in the project to domestic investors, with private Egyptian companies tasked with its construction.
10The new canal is expected to more than double Suez revenues from $US5.3 billion expected at the end of 2015 to $US13.2 billion in 2023, according to official estimates.
