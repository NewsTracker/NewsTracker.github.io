ISIS loses battle for Tikrit as government forces raise flag in city

1Iraq claims it has liberated the city of Tikrit - its biggest victory in the fight against Islamic State terrorists to date.
2Security and allied forces, backed by U.S.-led coalition aircraft retook the hometown of former president Saddam Hussein and the Iraqi tricolour was raised.
3The operation began on March 2 and had looked bogged down before Iraqi forces made rapid advances over the past 48 hours.
4Prime Minister Haider al-Abadi 'announces the liberation of Tikrit and congratulates Iraqi security forces and popular volunteers on the historic milestone', his official Twitter account said.
5He was referring to paramilitary groups which played a major role in the fighting to retake Tikrit, a Sunni Arab city which ISIS had controlled since it captured swathes of Iraq in June last year.
6Iraqiya state television showed footage of houses previously used by the terror group in liberated areas, but it was not clear whether any pockets of resistance remained.
7The provincial government headquarters was retaken on Monday, and on Tuesday the Iraqi tricolour replaced the black ISIS flag on the building.
8There no immediate information on how many fighters were killed, wounded or captured in the fighting.
9The government has not provided any casualty figures since the operation started a month ago.
10Iraqi army and police forces, as well as volunteers and Iran-backed Shiite militias, completely surrounded Tikrit within two weeks of launching the operation.
11There was a lull in fighting when government and allied forces apparently balked at the number of snipers, booby traps and trenches which ISIS was using to defend the city centre.
12Iran was Baghdad's top foreign partner in the early stages of the operation, but Iraqi air force strikes were proving insufficient to break the back of IS resistance.
13The government eventually requested strikes from the U.S.-led coalition which has been assisting Iraqi forces elsewhere in the country since August last year.
14American jets began bombing ISIS targets in Tikrit on March 25.
15France also took part in the campaign.
16The move sparked a freeze in the participation of the Popular Mobilisation units, an umbrella organisation for volunteers and militias which accounted for the bulk of the manpower involved in the Tikrit battle.
17The Pentagon had expressed unease at the role played by Iran and its proxies in the battle and said it conditioned its intervention on regular forces taking the lead.
18On Friday, it hailed the withdrawal from the fight of 'those Shiite militias who are linked to, infiltrated by, (or) otherwise under the influence of Iran'.
19But after giving themselves political cover by declaring that they do not want to work with each other, both sides took part in the Tikrit operation this week.
20Tikrit, which once had an estimated population of around 200,000, had been largely emptied of civilians by the time the government operation was launched at the start of March.
21The fate of those who were believed to have remained in the city was unclear on Tuesday.
22Thousands of people displaced last year or more recently from Salaheddin province, of which Tikrit is the capital, have started returning to their homes in outlying liberated areas.
23But the level of destruction Tikrit is believed to have suffered and the threat posed by unexploded bombs mean residents of the city itself could take longer to return.
24Tikrit holds both strategic and symbolic importance.
25It was the hometown of executed dictator Saddam Hussein, remnants of whose Baath party collaborated with ISIS last summer.
26Since June, Iraqi forces had tried and failed several times to retake the city, seen as a key stepping stone to recapturing Mosul, the jihadists' largest hub in Iraq.
