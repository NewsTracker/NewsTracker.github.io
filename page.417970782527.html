Officials: North Carolina Teen Planned to Help ISIS

1CHARLOTTE, N.C. (AP) - A North Carolina man is in the custody of federal authorities, who say he planned assassinations in the United States in support of the Islamic State terrorist group.
2Federal authorities said in a news release Monday that 19-year-old Justin Nojan Sullivan of Morganton faces several charges, including one count of attempting to provide material support to the Islamic State group.
3Assistant Attorney General for National Security John Carlin says Sullivan "was planning assassinations and violent attacks in the United States."
4Sullivan also is charged with charges related to receiving a silencer.
5Sullivan was arrested Friday at his home without incident and is expected to make his initial appearance Monday in federal court in Charlotte.
6It wasn't immediately known whether he had an attorney who could be contacted for comment on the case.
