U.S. asked for custody of Joaquin (El Chapo) Guzman: Mexico

1Mexico's government received an extradition request from the United States for Joaquin (El Chapo) Guzman a couple of weeks before the drug lord's escape from prison last Saturday, a government spokesman said on Friday.
2The request was made on June 25 via a diplomatic note, the spokesman said in response to a question from Reuters.
3The confirmation followed comments made by Interior Minister Miguel Angel Osorio Chong to reporters late on Thursday that suggested a request had, in fact, been made.
4Mexico had previously said it expected a request but had indicated in January that Guzman would not be handed over because he would serve time in his home country.
5Guzman is wanted by U.S. authorities for a variety of criminal charges including cocaine smuggling and money laundering.
6He broke out of Mexico's top maximum security prison through a tunnel that surfaced right into his cell.
