Mexico Reaches Gold Cup Final After Heated Win Over Panama

1ATLANTA - Andres Guardado scored two goals on penalty kicks, including the tiebreaker in extra time, and Mexico beat a short-handed and angered Panama squad, 2-1, in a Gold Cup semifinal on Wednesday night.
2Panama's Roman Torres scored on a header early in the second half, and the 1-0 lead held until Guardado's penalty kick in stoppage time.
3The penalty kick, which followed a 10-minute delay as tempers flared, set up the extra time.
4Mexico will play Jamaica, which beat the United States earlier, in the final on Sunday in Philadelphia.
5Panama played a man down after forward Luis Tejada drew a red card and was ejected for his contact with Mexico's Francisco Rodriguez about 25 minutes into the match.
6Tensions erupted again after the game as Panama's players charged the officials, who had to be escorted off the field by security.
7Guardado, a midfielder, also scored the decisive goal on a penalty kick in Mexico's 1-0 quarterfinal win over Costa Rica - also on a call that received plenty of criticism.
8In the 88th minute, a penalty was called on Torres for touching the ball in the penalty area after he stumbled and fell onto it.
9Panama players protested and fans began to throw debris onto the field for the second time in the match.
10Chaos broke out on the sideline as players from both teams argued and appeared to be on the verge of an all-out melee before slowly being separated.
11Following the delay, Guardado's left-footed penalty kick found the right corner of the net for the tying goal.
12Panama was denied its second straight final.
13It lost to the U.S. in the 2013 final after beating Mexico in the semifinals.
14A header by Torres early in the second half broke the scoreless tie.
15Torres was in position to intercept a corner kick from Erick Davis in front of the goal, and his header to the ground bounced past goal keeper Guillermo Ochoa.
16Torres jumped in glee, his fists clinched, as Panama took its fifth straight 1-0 lead in the tournament.
17In each of its first four games, Panama took 1-0 leads only to allow the equalizer.
18In the 25th minute, Tejada jumped to attempt a header when he hit Rodriguez with his arm and hand, knocking Rodriguez to the ground.
19Tejada protested and had to be restrained by his teammates when the red card was shown.
20There was tension from both teams as Mexico players pushed Panama's players away from Rodriguez, who remained on the ground.
21Tejada was very slow to leave the field, stopping and circling back as fans hurled cups and plastic bottles in his direction.
22Tejada's teammate Adolfo Machado attempted to serve as an escort off the field but finally gave up when Tejada continued to linger.
23Finally, Tejada departed into the tunnel, leaving Panama with the difficult task of taking on Mexico with 10 players.
24Mexico's Miguel Layun cleared the debris from the field, picking up the cups and bottles, so play could resume.
