FIFA's lowest-ranked team advances in World Cup qualifying

1Bhutanese players, in white defend a goal attempt by Sri Lankan players during their 2018 World Cup qualifier in Colombo, Sri Lanka,Thursday, March 12, 2015.
2THIMPHU, Bhutan (AP) - The lowest-ranked team in world soccer won again Tuesday and advanced to the second round of World Cup qualifying.
3Bhutan, a Himalayan kingdom ranked 209th by FIFA, beat Sri Lanka 2-1 at home in the second leg and advanced 3-1 on aggregate.
4Chencho Gyeltshen scored both goals for Bhutan while Zohar Mohamed Zarwan scored for Sri Lanka.
5Bhutan had lost all 18 matches it played from 2009 to 2014, including a 20-0 rout against Kuwait in an Asian Cup qualifying match.
6In the next round, Bhutan will be placed into one of eight groups of five teams and could end up facing Asian powers like Japan, Iran or South Korea.
7India, East Timor, Cambodia and Taiwan also advanced to the second round of Asian qualifying.
8The match between Pakistan and Yemen in Lahore was postponed for safety and security reasons, and FIFA said it will announce a new date and venue later.
9Yemen won the first leg 3-1.
