David Cameron: Britain to stand with France and Tunisia against terrorism

1Britain will stand alongside Tunisia and France and fight the “evil of terrorism”, David Cameron has said as he warned that “we all face this threat”.
2Philip Hammond, the Foreign Secretary, chaired a meeting on Friday of the government’s emergency Cobra committee to work on co-operating with authorities in France, Tunisia and elsewhere.
3Speaking at a Brussels summit, Mr Cameron also urged Europe to do more to “co-ordinate and build our capacity on counter-terrorism”.
4It comes amid growing concern in Britain over Europe’s failure to share passenger data to try to help stop terrorists.
5He said: “Our hearts all go out to the victims of these appalling terrorist acts.
6I spoke to President Hollande and offered my sympathy and our solidarity with the French people at this time.
7“This is a threat that faces all of us.
8These events have taken place today in Tunisia and France but they could happen anywhere.
9“We have got to do all we can to help.
10That means co-operating and building our capacity on counter-terrorism, dealing with the threat at source whether that is Isil in Syria and Iraq or other extremist groups and we have to deal with this poisonous, radical narrative that is turning so many young minds.
