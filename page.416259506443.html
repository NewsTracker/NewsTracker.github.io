Obama says Ebola-fighting goal is to prevent any new cases

1President Barack Obama is citing major gains in fighting the Ebola outbreak in West Africa and says the goal now is to prevent any new cases.
2Obama was meeting Wednesday with the presidents of Liberia, Guinea and Sierra Leone to get a handle on the current situation.
3Ebola has killed more than 10,000 people in the yearlong-plus outbreak.
4But Obama says there were fewer than 40 cases in the region last week.
5The United States was part of an international effort to confront Ebola.
6At its height, there were fears of a much wider outbreak.
7Obama says the three African countries at the center of the outbreak need to have their health systems rebuilt and their economies restored.
8President Barack Obama and Vice President Joe Biden, back right, meet with, from left, Sierra Leone President Ernest Bai Koroma, Liberian President Ellen Johnson Sirleaf, and Guinean President Alpha Conde, in the Cabinet Room of the White House in Washington, Wednesday, April 15, 2015, to discuss progress made in the international Ebola response.
