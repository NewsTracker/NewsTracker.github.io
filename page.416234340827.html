Big score: College bowl game payouts surpass $500 million (Yahoo Sports)

1According to an NCAA report to be released Tuesday, the 39 postseason FBS games distributed $505.9 million to the participating conferences and schools.
2The schools spent $100.2 million to take part in bowl games.
3Total payouts from 35 postseason games from the 2013-14 season were $309.9 million while schools spent $97.8 million to participate.
4For the 2012-13 season, payouts were $300.8 million and expenses were $90.3 million.
5The increase in payouts is due mostly to the increased value of media deals with the seven games - six bowls and a national championship game - that are part of the College Football Playoff.
6The College Football Playoff replaced the BCS last season.
7The playoff matched the four top teams in the nation, as selected by a selection committee, in two semifinals with the winners playing for the national title a week later.
8The BCS paired the top two teams in country chosen by polls and computer rating in a national championship game.
9ESPN pays the FBS conferences and schools that run the College Football Playoff about $470 million a year for the media rights to the three playoff games and four other bowls.
10The final BCS TV deal was worth about $180 million per year to the conferences.
