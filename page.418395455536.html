8Greece's government was racing Thursday to finalize a plan of reforms for its third bailout, hoping this time to get its European partners' approval and to stave off a looming, potentially catastrophic exit from Europe's joint currency, the euro.
9Details of Greece's reforms are to be submitted Thursday to give creditors time to review them ahead of a summit of the European Union's 28 members on Sunday.
10Hopes for a deal rose after Donald Tusk, who chairs the EU summits, said Greece's plan would have to be accompanied by creditors' suggestions on how to make the country's debt manageable in the longer term.
11"The realistic proposal from Greece will have to be matched by an equally realistic proposal on debt sustainability from the creditors. Only then will we have a win-win situation," Tusk said.
12Easing the terms of Greece's existing bailout loans has been a key dividing issue in the bailout talks for months - with Greece and the International Monetary Fund pressing in favor and key European states like Germany resisting the idea.
13Tusk's comments boosted confidence that the sides will be able to find a compromise.
14The Stoxx 50 index of top European shares jumped 2.6 percent.
15Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras met with finance ministry officials and was holding a cabinet meeting Thursday afternoon to finalize his country's plan, a day after his government requested a new three-year aid program from Europe's bailout fund and promised to immediately enact reforms, including to taxes and pensions, in return.
16The last-minute negotiations come as Greece's financial system teeters on the brink of collapse.
17It has since June 29 put limited cash withdrawals to 60 euros ($67) per day to stanch a bank run.
18The banks and the stock market have been shut for just as long.
19Pensioners without bank cards have been particularly hard hit as they have struggled to access their accounts.
20Some branches have been opened so the elderly and unemployed without bank cards can withdraw a maximum weekly sum of 120 euros each.
21Hundreds lined up outside banks Thursday morning, many facing hours-long waits in the heat.
22Meanwhile, many ATMs had a shortage of 20 euro notes, effectively reducing the daily withdrawal limit to 50 euros.
23If Tsipras does not get a deal, Greece faces an almost inevitable collapse of the banking system, which would be the first step for the country to fall out of the euro.
24"I believe he will have to get an agreement. We will pay dearly for it, but at least we'll get an agreement," said mechanic Pantelis Niarchos, walking down the street in central Athens.
25After months of fruitless negotiations with Tsipras' government, elected in January on promises to repeal bailout austerity, the skeptical eurozone creditor states have said they want to see a detailed, cost-accounted plan of the reforms.
26Greece's financial institutions have been kept afloat so far by emergency liquidity assistance from the European Central Bank.
27But the ECB has not increased the amount in days, leaving the lenders in a stranglehold despite capital controls.
28German ECB governing council member Jens Weidmann argued Thursday that Greek banks should not get more emergency credit from the central bank unless a bailout deal is struck.
29He said in a speech it was up to eurozone governments and Greek leaders themselves to rescue Greece.
30The central bank "has no mandate to safeguard the solvency of banks and governments," he said.
31The ECB capped emergency credit to Greek banks amid doubt whether the country will win further rescue loans from other countries.
32The banks closed and limited ATM withdrawals because they had no other way to replace deposits.
33Weidmann said he welcomed the fact that central bank credit "is no longer being used to finance capital flight caused by the Greek government."
