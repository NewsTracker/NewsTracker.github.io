Greece 'agrees deal with eurozone'

1Greece and eurozone nations have agreed a deal to extend financial aid after bailout talks in Brussels.
2Eurozone finance ministers reached an agreement to extend Greece's financial rescue programme by four months.
3Dutch finance minister Jeroen Dijsselbloem, head of the Eurogroup, said that Athens had pledged to honour all its debts.
4"This is a very positive outcome," he told a news conference on Friday night.
5In return for the extension, Greece has agreed to present an initial list of reform measures by Monday, he added.
6Yanis Varoufakis, the Greek finance minister, said he would work night and day between now and Monday to devise the new list.
7Eurozone officials will then review the reforms and see if they go far enough to appease creditors.
8If finance chiefs are left unsatisfied, there is still a chance the deal will be scrapped.
9Mr Varoufakis added that Greece had not used threats or bluff during the talks: "The four-month period will be a time to rebuild new relations with Europe and the IMF."
10However, he said the deal would be "dead" if the list of Greek reforms was not agreed, reported the AFP news agency.
11Friday's extension was sealed just days before Greece's bailout programme ends.
12The BBC's chief business correspondent Linda Yueh says the Greeks have won some flexibility, but their reforms must be made within set fiscal parameters.
13Greece and Germany have stepped back from the brink.
14And for now Greece remains in the eurozone.
15But there will be months of fraught negotiations before it will be clear whether the economy and finances of this recession-battered nation have been put back on a stable footing.
16In fact, what was agreed on Friday night guarantees there will be no fresh crisis - no fears of Greece quitting the eurozone - for a full two days.
17Because by Monday night, the Syriza government has to submit a preliminary list of proposed economic reforms - which will form the basis of negotiations till the end of April on a new financial settlement for the country.
18A Greek government official said Athens now had time to negotiate a "new deal".
19"Greece has turned a page," the official said.
20"We have avoided recessionary measures."
21Greece had been seeking a six-month extension of the bailout but the Eurogroup opted for four months.
22The agreement removes the immediate risk of Greece running out of money next month.
23Christine Lagarde, head of the International Monetary Fund, said: "We are pleased that work can actually begin."
24She was involved in Friday afternoon talks between Mr Varoufakis and Wolfgang Schaeuble, the German finance minister, that helped pave the way for the deal.
25The agreement helped send Wall Street to new record highs, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average ending up 154 points, or 0.9%, at 18,140.44 points, while the broader S&P 500 added 0.6% to 2,110.3 points.
26The euro gained against the US dollar on Friday following the announcement, adding 0.3% to $1.1403.
