Warriors eliminate Rockets, end 40-year NBA Finals drought (Yahoo Sports)

1OAKLAND, Calif.
2(AP) -- After a generation of wishing and waiting, the Golden State Warriors have finally arrived on basketball's biggest stage again.
3Stephen Curry had 26 points and eight rebounds, Harrison Barnes added 24 points and the Warriors advanced to the NBA Finals for the first time in 40 years with a 104-90 victory over the Houston Rockets on Wednesday night.
4''Why not us?'' Curry said to a roaring, golden-yellow shirt wearing crowd after the Warriors received the Western Conference trophy from Alvin Attles, the coach of their last championship team in 1975.
5''The Bay Area's been waiting for 40 years,'' Curry said later.
6The Warriors shook off a slow start and sweated out a shaky finish in Game 5 to close out the Rockets and set up a matchup with LeBron James and the Cleveland Cavaliers beginning June 4.
7Yellow streams and confetti fell from the rafters when the final buzzer sounded.
8The Warriors shared hugs and handshakes, and the crowd chanted ''M-V-P!'' for Curry.
9He relished the moment on the court with his 2-year-old daughter, Riley, who joined him in his postgame news conference - laughing, playfully interrupting him and walking around the room again.
10''I think she's taking advantage of the moment for sure,'' Curry quipped.
11Dwight Howard led Houston with 18 points and 16 rebounds.
12But MVP runner-up James Harden had a forgettable finale, with a playoff-record 13 turnovers and 14 points on 2-of-11 shooting.
13''Tried to do a little bit too much and turned the ball over and gave them easy baskets in transition,'' Harden said.
14''This isn't where we wanted to end at.
15It's a really good season for us.
16It was a tough way for the Rockets' run to end.
17They overcame a knee injury that sidelined Howard half the season to finish second in the West, played without starters Patrick Beverley and Donatas Motiejunas in the playoffs and rallied from a 3-1 deficit to beat the Los Angeles Clippers in the second round.
18The Warriors were one obstacle Houston couldn't clear.
19''The guys fought hard,'' Rockets coach Kevin McHale said.
20Curry said he had no lingering effects from his frightening fall in Game 4 that left him with a bruised head and right side.
21The MVP wore a protective yellow sleeve on his right arm, which he shed in the third quarter after shooting 4 for 12 and the Warriors clinging to a 52-46 halftime lead.
22Things got tougher on Curry and the Warriors when backcourt mate Klay Thompson faked a shot that drew Trevor Ariza in the air early in the fourth quarter.
23Thompson absorbed Ariza's knee to the side of his head, sending him to the floor.
24Thompson, who finished with 20 points, lay on the ground for a minute before walking to the locker room.
25He came back to the bench after receiving stitches on his right ear.
26The Warriors initially said Thompson could've returned after passing a concussion test.
27The team said he began not feeling well after the game and started to show concussion-like symptoms.
28He will continue to be evaluated.
29The Warriors did just fine without him, starting the fourth on a 13-4 run and holding off Houston's last-ditch efforts on free throws.
30Barnes highlighted the decisive spurt with a dunk that gave Golden State an 87-72 lead with 7:10 remaining.
31He flexed his muscles to the sellout crowd of 19,596, which spent the final quarter on its feet in anticipation of a long-awaited celebration.
32Now it's LeBron vs. Curry.
33King James vs. the Baby-Faced Assassin.
34The four-time NBA MVP vs. the newly crowned MVP.
35The hype has already started for two of the most popular and entertaining players in the world to take center stage for the championship, and it has a week to build even more before starting at rowdy Oracle Arena.
36''I can't wait for June 4,'' smiling Warriors co-owner Joe Lacob said in the locker room.
37The conference title is the biggest accomplishment yet in what has been a rapid rise for a Warriors team that is beloved in the basketball-united Bay Area despite decades of futility.
38Lacob and co-owner Peter Guber, sitting courtside next to rapper Kanye West, have turned the franchise into a contender since they bought the team in 2010.
39General manager Bob Myers, the NBA Executive of the Year, has constructed a talented roster around Curry that has exceeded all expectations.
