Germanwings plane crash: Lufthansa to require two crew members in cockpit at all times

1Berlin: Lufthansa said it will introduce new rules requiring two crew members to be in the cockpit at all times after one of the pilots at its Germanwings unit crashed a plane in the French Alps.
2Prosecutors believe Andreas Lubitz, 27, locked himself alone in the cockpit of the Airbus A320 on Tuesday and deliberately steered it into a mountain, killing all 150 people on board.
3German co-pilot Andreas Lubitz,27, is believed to have used the cockpit door lock to prevent the captain from returning to the cockpit so he could crash Germanwings Flight 4U9525.
4Lufthansa had said on Thursday that it did not see any reason to hastily change its procedures, but many other airlines swiftly changed their own rules.
5"The passenger airlines of the Lufthansa Group will put this new rule into place as soon as possible in agreement with the relevant authorities," Lufthansa said in a statement on Friday.
6The Lufthansa Group also includes Germanwings, Austrian Airlines, Swiss Air and Eurowings.
7The rule has been agreed by all German airlines, aviation association BDL said in a statement.
8Air Berlin had announced plans on Thursday to introduce the new procedure.
9Scandinavian airline SAS also said on Friday that it had changed procedures to ensure two people are in the cockpit at all times.
10Lufthansa also said it was creating a new role of group safety pilot, who will check and develop flight safety procedures and report directly to Chief Executive Carsten Spohr.
11Currently, each airline has its own safety pilot.
12The group safety pilot role will be held by Werner Maas, currently the safety pilot for the Lufthansa brand.
