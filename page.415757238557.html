Marlins' Cosart says MLB reviewing his Twitter account (Yahoo Sports)

1JUPITER, Fla.
2(AP) -- Miami Marlins pitcher Jarred Cosart said Major League Baseball is reviewing his deleted Twitter account as part of an investigation into gambling-related tweets posted there and he is cooperating with the investigation.
3''Obviously, I was caught off guard by the whole situation,'' Cosart said Thursday.
4The 24-year-old Cosart deleted his Twitter account Tuesday after screen shots of comments he purportedly made appeared on the website of Miami New Times.
5MLB rules prohibit players and other employees from betting on baseball games.
6A player who bets on a game not involving his team faces a one-year suspension and a player who bets on or against his own team faces a lifetime ban.
7The commissioner, at his discretion, can discipline a player for placing other types of bets with an illegal bookmaker.
8Marlins President of Baseball Operations Michael Hill said the club first learned about the tweets from media.
9''We're fully aware of the investigation,'' Hill said.
10FILE - This is a 2015, file photo showing Jarred Cosart of the Miami Marlins baseball team.
11Cosart deleted his Twitter account following the initial Miami New Times report.
12He said MLB is reviewing other accounts claiming to be his.
13Cosart had been scheduled to pitch Friday against Houston, but the right-hander was scratched because of a blister on the middle finger of his pitching hand.
14Hill said skipping Cosart's turn is ''completely unrelated'' to the MLB investigation.
15''We're going to push back his start and get his blister under control,'' Hill said.
16Cosart was 4-4 with a 2.39 ERA in 10 starts last year for the Marlins, who acquired him from Houston on July 31.
17He is 0-2 with an 8.49 ERA in four spring training starts over 11 2-3 innings.
