10His predecessor resigned following an attack in Mandera last year that killed 36 people.
11Al-Shabab, an Islamic extremist group, has vowed to inflict violent attacks on Kenya because it has contributed troops to the African Union force supporting the western-backed government in Somalia.
12In November, al-Shabab fighters hijacked a bus near Mandera, singled out non-Muslims and non-Somalis, and shot dead 28 passengers.
13Twenty-two of those killed were teachers.
14Ten days later al-Shabab massacred 36 quarry workers who were non-Muslims.
15Hundreds of teachers in parts of northern Kenya that borders Somalia have refused to return to work, fearing attacks by the Islamic extremists despite government reassurance of increased security.
