Hillary Clinton will address Latino leaders in Las Vegas

1Hillary Rodham Clinton will discuss Latino issues at a national conference this month in Las Vegas.
2Event organizers say the presidential candidate will speak at the 32nd annual National Association of Latino Elected and Appointed Officials conference at the Aria Resort and Casino on June 18.
3Preliminary plans are for the former secretary of state to address issues important to the Latino community for about 30 minutes starting at noon.
4The appearance is only open to conference attendees and the media.
5Clinton also spoke in 2008 at the annual event, which organizers say draws 1,200 Latino policymakers and is the largest Latino political convention in the country.
6It marks the second Nevada stop for the Democrat this year.
7Clinton held small-scale sessions on a May 5 visit.
