Taylor Swift says no to Apple Music

1When Apple Music debuts later this year, don't expect to turn up "Shake It Off."
2As it turns out, Taylor Swift's "1989" album won't be available on the streaming service, reps for Swift's label Big Machine Records and Apple confirm to BuzzFeed News.
3Swift's previous albums, though, will be available on Apple Music though.
4The news doesn't come as much of a surprise as Swift and her team made a similar call with Spotify when "1989" dropped last fall.
5A rep for her record label said there "are currently no plans to release '1989' to any streaming service in the near future."
6Apple Music will cost $10 a month for unlimited music streaming, when it comes to computers and iOS devices June 30.
