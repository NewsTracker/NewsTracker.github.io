1Ferguson: Two police officers who were shot in Ferguson were victims of "an ambush", the St Louis County police chief said on Thursday, hours after gunfire rang out in front of the police station, throwing into panic what had been a largely peaceful protest.
2Demonstrators and police officers alike hit the ground when the shots echoed through the crisp air shortly after midnight, striking the two officers.
3Many people ran for cover, and police officers clad in riot gear dragged their wounded comrades to safety.
4Other officers crouched behind cars and walls, drawing their handguns or rifles as they rapidly surveyed their surroundings.
5Protestors demonstrate outside the Ferguson Police Department on Wednesday.
6The injured officers were released from the hospital hours later, on Thursday morning.
7"We're lucky by God's grace we didn't lose two officers last night", Chief Jon Belmar of the St Louis County Police Department said at a midmorning news conference.
8It was clear that the police were the targets, he said, adding, "This is really an ambush, is what it is".
9Echoing Mr Belmar's comments, US Attorney-General Eric Holder called the shootings "heinous and cowardly attacks".
10Police mobilise in the parking lot of the Ferguson Police Station after two police officers were shot on Thursday.
11"This was not someone trying to bring healing to Ferguson," Mr Holder said.
12"This was a damn punk, a punk, who was trying to sow discord."
13President Barack Obama weighed in on Twitter, writing: "Violence against police is unacceptable. Our prayers are with the officers in MO. Path to justice is one all of us must travel together."
14Later in the morning, in an action that officials said was part of the investigation into the shooting, police SWAT units surrounded a house a few blocks from the shooting scene, and officers climbed onto the roof and broke through a vent there to gain access.
15Police take cover after two officers were shot while standing guard in front of the Ferguson Police Station.
16"People have been taken in for questioning," said a police spokesman, Sergeant Brian Schellman.
17"No arrests at this point."
18Mr Belmar said people had a right to protest peacefully, but also said "there is an unfortunate association with that gathering" and the shooting.
19"I feel confident that for whatever reason they were observers, whatever you want to call it, with the group of individuals that were down there protesting," he said.
20"This is no reflection, again, on any of those guys; they can't help it."
21Witnesses among the demonstrators denied any link to the shootings, saying that they believed the shots originated from the top of a hill about 220 metres directly across from the station.
22Mr Belmar did not specify a location but estimated the distance at 125 metres.
23Police shine a light on a helmet as they investigate the scene where two police officers were shot outside the Ferguson Police Department.
24"There's just no way anybody I know did that," said Bob Hudgins, a protester who is running for city council.
25"This was completely out of the blue, and they were not attached or embedded" with the demonstration.
26The shooting threatened to ratchet up tensions and undo efforts to change the way Ferguson police do business and to heal the anger and frustration that have boiled over since August 9, when a white police officer, Darren Wilson, fatally shot an unarmed 18-year-old black man, Michael Brown.
27The Brown family, in a statement from its lawyer, denounced "the actions of stand-alone agitators" who tried to derail the protests.
28"We reject any kind of violence directed toward members of law enforcement," the statement said.
29"It cannot and will not be tolerated."
30The city of Ferguson said in a statement that it was "diligently working to make systematic changes necessary to instil confidence," but added, "we cannot continue to move forward under threats of violence and destruction".
31In his comments, Mr Holder said that Ferguson officials had made some progress in addressing the city's problems but that significant work remained.
32He announced an program intended to help reduce racial bias and improve relations between police and the residents, part of an initiative started in the aftermath of the Brown shooting.
33The house that police raided is the home of Iresha Turner, 22, and her six-year-old son, according to Ms Turner's grandmother, Iris Turner, 59.
34She said her granddaughter and a male friend had been taken in for questioning by the police, but she insisted that Iresha Turner would not have had anything to do with the shooting.
35Iresha Turner and her son were at the protests the night before, the elder Ms Turner said, but they fled when shots were fired.
36She said her granddaughter dropped a friend off, then returned home and found the police already there.
37The shooting came just hours after the Ferguson police chief, Thomas Jackson, announced that he would resign, a move greeted with praise from protesters.
38Ever since the shooting of Brown, Mr Jackson had been at the centre of criticism that he ran a racially biased and abusive department.
39Last week, the US Justice Department released an investigation report that accused the city of racially biased and unconstitutional practices in law enforcement.
40Since then, in addition to Mr Jackson, the city manager, the municipal judge, and two police commanders have said they were leaving their jobs, and the court's chief clerk was fired - changes that city officials had hoped would persuade residents that they were prepared to start anew.
41The officers who were shot on Thursday were standing side by side, part of a cordon from multiple police departments, keeping protesters away from the police station.
42There had been as many as 69 officers in the evening, dwindling to about 49 at the time of the shooting, Mr Belmar said.
43Mr Belmar said the shooting realised his worst fears over the months of unrest since Brown's killing.
44"When you look at the tenor of at least some of the people involved in the protest or civil unrest, it can be troubling," he said.
45"I think it's a miracle that we haven't had any instances similar to this over the summer and fall."
46He drew a parallel to the fatal shooting in December of two New York City police officers, Rafael Ramos and Wenjian Liu, by a man who said he was enraged by killings by the police, including that of Brown.
47"We were very close to having happen what happened to NYPD," Mr Belmar said.
48He said officers had seen muzzle flashes, and later found shell casings that may have been from the shooting.
49Based on the sound of the shots and the officers' wounds, he said, the weapon was a handgun, not a rifle.
50The Webster Groves officer who was shot is 32 years old and a seven-year veteran of the force, while the St Louis County officer is 41 and has 14 years' experience, the county police said.
51Both were treated at Barnes-Jewish Hospital in St. Louis.
52The younger officer was shot in the cheekbone, just below his right eye, and the bullet lodged behind his right ear, Belmar said.
53A bullet struck the other officer in the right shoulder, and exited his back on the right side.
54No officers returned fire.
55Emotions quickly ran high after the shooting.
56One Ferguson officer, standing among the protesters as things calmed down, said: "This is what they wanted to happen."
57When a protester told him that no one wanted this to happen, the officer was adamant, saying that was his opinion and that the protester could differ if he wanted.
58Protesters on the scene were quick to point out that the gunfire had not come from among them, rather from a distance behind them.
59Kim Tihen, a Ferguson City Council member and former Ferguson police officer, released a statement saying: "While I have always been supportive of the peaceful protesters who have simply wanted their message heard, I am beyond outraged by the behaviour and lawlessness of the protesters who want nothing more than to destroy our city."
60Earlier in the night, the protests took on a familiar pattern that had not been seen here since after a grand jury announced in November that it would not indict Wilson for the fatal shooting of Brown.
61It was as much a celebration of Mr Jackson's resignation as it was a call for more action.
62"Not just Jackson, we want Knowles, Ferguson has got to go!" they yelled in reference to James Knowles III, the mayor of Ferguson.
63Protesters engaged in a cat-and-mouse game of sorts with the police.
64Dozens of them flooded into the street in front of the Police Department, blocking cars as they tried to pass.
65Things occasionally became tense between the protesters and motorists, some of whom refused to back away when demonstrators lined up in front of their vehicles.
66On several occasions, drivers inched forward, tapping the knees of protesters who dared them to hit them.
67Each time, one side would eventually give in: either the driver would back away, or the protesters would let them through.
68But on other occasions, police officers wearing helmets and clutching transparent shields and batons would race into the street to clear away the demonstrators.
69The police made a handful of arrests.
70Some of the tensest moments came when the protesters fought among themselves.
71Some people urged others to block cars, while others urged their fellow demonstrators to get out of the street and direct their anger at the officers in front of the police station.
72There were tussles between protesters who did not recognise one another, with the fault line appearing to be those who had been demonstrating for many months versus those who were new to the movement.
73Some took offence to the supposedly new protesters trying to tell them what to do.
74At one point, fist fights broke out in the street as the police stood by watching.
75The protesters stood on the east side of South Florissant Road, while the police held their line on the west side, in front of the station.
76Then, without any warning or seeming escalation, the shots rang out.
77"I heard the bullets go past my head," said Bradley Rayford, a freelance photographer, who said he was standing near the officers when the shots were fired.
78After the first one, Mr Rayford said, he looked up at a hill across from the police station and saw muzzle fire from that direction.
79He said he heard about four shots, after which he moved to the ground.
80After the shots were fired on Thursday, the remaining protesters scattered, and the St Louis County Police Department's homicide unit was sent to investigate.
81Dozens of officers from around the region also arrived to secure the area.
82A group of officers in tactical gear carefully walked up the hill across from the police station where Mr Rayford and other witnesses said the shots had come from.
83By 2am, a handful of people remained in the lot across the department, which was encircled in yellow police tape.
84Investigators tried to interview people in the group.
85But in a sign of the chasm that remained between law enforcement and this protest movement, many of them were reluctant to talk.
