Misery, tuberculosis in prisons of war-torn east Ukraine

4The disease spreads prodigiously in jails and develops into hard-to-treat forms unless properly addressed.
5"TB is so common within the penitentiary system that many inmates don't see it as a deadly disease - they see catching TB as a normal part of life in prison," Doctors Without Borders said in a recent report.

7The gray, squat, three-story building stands inside a perimeter lined with barbed wire and observation towers.
8Zhdanivka has since last year been under the control of the armed Russian-backed separatists who established the would-be breakaway state of the Donetsk People's Republic.
9Supplies from the Ukrainian government have ground to a halt - and the ragtag rebel authorities have been at a loss to fill shortfalls.
10Prison authorities allowed Associated Press journalists to visit the hospital at the Zhdanivka prison last week, albeit under close supervision.
11The deputy prison director, dressed in a military-style uniform and seated under a black-blue-and-red Donetsk People's Republic flag, coldly explained the rules: No walking without escort.
12No contact with prisoners.
13No filming of prison walls or towers.
14After being handed sanitary masks and passes, visitors entered detention wings while being followed closely by a prison minder.
15The minder frequently restricted AP movements: "You cannot film there".
16"Don't go there, repair work going on".
17"That employee is off-limits."
18Most prisoners were friendly and happy to talk, but were prevented from doing so by wardens.
19"My folks probably think I'm dead by now," says one prisoner, before the conversation is cut short by a minder.
20The only prisoners unwilling to speak were in Block 3 - a group of around 70 who rejected medical treatment in protest who said they are afraid doctors are testing unregistered drugs on them.
21Responsibility for treating inmates in the underfunded prison shifted as early as 2011 to organizations like Doctors Without Borders, known by its French acronym MSF.
22But the group's role - and its burden - has grown sharply since war broke out last year.
23MSF says 170 patients under treatment at five pre-detention centers and jails in areas surrounding the conflict zone have developed drug-resistant TB.
24Janette Olson, field coordinator of the MSF multidrug-resistant TB program, said prisoners are refusing to take drugs with unpleasant side effects, making long-term treatment complicated.
25MSF says that while the official death rate from tuberculosis in Ukraine is 15 per 100,000 people, the number is 10 times higher among prisoners.
26A church built by prisoners sits behind the walls of the jail.
27Sick inmates go there daily to pray.
28Few distractions ease the monotony of life.
29Prisoners float like ghosts through the unlit corridors of the hospital and prison wards.
