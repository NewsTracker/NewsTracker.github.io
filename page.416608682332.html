18The United Nations is appealing for $415 million to provide for vital needs in Nepal over the next three months.
19It intends to support government efforts in provide shelter, water and sanitation, emergency health, food, and protection.
20The U.N.'s humanitarian coordinator for Nepal, Jamie McGoldrick, said the response to date was encouraging, but those efforts need to be maintained, especially in remote areas.
21He noted that the coming monsoon season would like add a logistical challenge to relief efforts, adding to the urgency.
22Helicopters have rescued some 210 foreign trekkers and local villagers stranded in the Lantang area north of Nepal's capital.
23The international trekkers were stranded in Langtang, a popular trekking route bordering Tibet, since the earthquake on Saturday that killed more than 5,000 people.
24Government administrator Gautam Rimal said the trekkers and villagers were flown to the nearby town of Dhunche.
25But with landslides cutting off the roads from there to Kathmandu, the trekkers will have to trudge for at least four hours before they can board a bus to the Nepalese capital.
26The number of people missing after a mudslide struck a village in central Nepal appears to be lower than first thought.
27The government had said Tuesday that 250 people were missing after a mudslide and avalanche hit the village of Ghodatabela near the quake epicenter.
28Government administrator Gautam Rimal said on Wednesday, however, "We believe now there are only a few people who were swept by the mudslide."
29Authorities withdrew the figure of 250 after getting more information from local residents, but Rimal didn't have an amended figure.
30Nepalese rescue helicopters are taking advantage of breaks in the rain to bring out the injured from remote mountain villages where aid is only beginning to trickle in, four days after the massive earthquake.
31A rescue mission on Wednesday landed in the village of Darkha, about 100 kilometers (60 miles) northwest of the capital, Kathmandu, and unloaded boxes of aid supplies.
32Nepalese soldiers disembarked and carried back on a stretcher the 69-year-old Ek Bahadur Thapa and others in need of treatment.
33He suffered leg injuries and has had to wait for medical care.
34The government says more than 10,000 people were injured.
35The first 44 Spaniards who were stranded in Nepal have returned home on an air force plane with Foreign Minister Jose Manuel Garcia Margallo accompanying them.
36The group is the first of some 127 to be evacuated by the government.
37About 500 Spaniards were affected by the earthquake and 103 have yet to be located.
38More than 20 were able to return by their own means.
39A second Spanish plane was due to bring back another group of Spaniards as well as other European and Latin American citizens later Wednesday.
40The quake occurred while Margallo was on an official visit to New Delhi.
41In another sign of life inching back to normal, banks in Kathmandu opened for few hours Wednesday and stuffed their ATMs with cash.
42At the Standard Chartered Bank in the city, people are lining up.
43"I needed money in cash so I can take my family out of Kathmandu. I want to be out of here for at least a few days," said Suraj Shrestha.
44He wasn't sure if ATMs outside the city were dispensing cash and wanted to carry "as much as possible."
45About 200 people have blocked traffic in Kathmandu to protest the slow pace of aid delivery.
46The protesters faced off with police and there were minor scuffles but no arrests were made.
47One protester says they haven't received any relief.
48"We are hungry, we haven't had anything to drink. We haven't been able to sleep. I have a 7-year-old child who is sleeping in the open. It's getting cold and people are getting pneumonia," he said.
49He accused the government of not doing enough.
50The death toll has climbed to 5,093 and more than 8 million people have been affected.
51Police in Nepal say the death toll from Saturday's earthquake has topped 5,000.
52The Kathmandu police say 4,989 have died and another 10,260 people have been injured in Nepal.
53The quake that was centered just outside Kathmandu also triggered an avalanche that killed at least 18 people at the Everest base camp.
54Another 61 were killed in neighboring India and Bangladesh, and China's official Xinhua News Agency reported 25 dead in Tibet.
55Police have arrested dozens of people on suspicion of looting abandoned homes as well as causing panic by spreading rumors of another big quake.
56Police official Bigyan Raj Sharma says 27 have been detained for stealing from homes whose owners moved out following Saturday's quake and powerful aftershocks.
57The aftershocks have waned but people are still anxious, many preferring to stay out in the open.
58Sharma says another four people were arrested for spreading false rumors of an impending quake through social media and text messages.
59The first aid shipments have reached Dhading district, close to the epicenter of the devastating earthquake in Nepal.
60U.N. food agency emergency officer Geoff Pinnock says the distribution in remote quake-hit villages will start Wednesday, but cautions it would take time.
61In Gorkha, the neighboring district to the west, five cargo trucks filled with rice, cooking oil and sugar stood on a grassy field in Majuwa village waiting for a helicopter from Kathmandu to take the supplies to the hardest-hit areas of that district.
62The World Food Program also expects the delivery of high-energy biscuits, which will be send out to areas without enough water for cooking.
63A man pulled from the rubble of a collapsed building in Kathmandu more than three days after the deadly Nepal earthquake says he drank his own urine to survive.
