Illegal border tunnel found leading from Mexico

1SAN DIEGO (AP) - U.S. Border Patrol agents have found a suspected drug tunnel with lighting and a rail-car system that connected to a home across the border in Tijuana, Mexico.
2Immigration and Customs Enforcement spokeswoman Lauren Mack said agents patrolling west of the San Ysidro port of entry Tuesday morning found a sinkhole close to the U.S. border fence that opened up into the tunnel.
3Mack says the tunnel was about 220 yards (201 meters) in length, had a cart inside and the walls shored up.
4But it was still under construction.
5Macks says no drugs were found, and no arrests have been made.
6Drug cartels have increasingly turned to tunnels to smuggle their loads into the United States.
7More than 75 tunnels have been discovered along the border since 2008.
