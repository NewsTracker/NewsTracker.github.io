Sebastian Vettel wins thrilling F1 race in Hungary

1Lewis Hamilton started the Hungarian Grand Prix with a 17-point lead over Mercedes teammate Nico Rosberg, but he was lucky to leave the Hungaroring as the top driver in the standings after a comedy of errors left him struggling to finish in the top 10 - but a shocking finish saw Hamilton finish in front of Rosberg, but miles behind race winner Sebastian Vettel.
2The two Ferrari drivers darted into the lead after getting a magnificent start off the line, while pole sitter Hamilton slipped back to fourth.
3On the very first lap, Hamilton lost control and cut across a gravel trap, and blamed his teammate Rosberg for cutting across his line.
4Following the mistake, Hamilton battled back up to fourth place, and a safety car period brought out by Nico Hulkenberg bunched up the field.
5During a chaotic restart, Hamilton made contact with Red Bull's Daniel Ricciardo in the first corner, causing damage to Hamilton's front wing.
6Hamilton was forced to pit to change his wing, and a few laps later was assessed a drive-through penalty for causing a collision, dropping him out of the points entirely.
7Rosberg, meanwhile seemed content to finish in second place behind Vettel, and fended off a charging Ricciardo for a number of laps.
8Ricciardo finally went to overtake Rosberg with less than 10 laps remaining, but locked up his tires and went wide in the first turn.
9Rosberg drove underneath Ricciardo to retake the position, but as he cut across the track Rosberg's rear tire made contact with Ricciardo's front wing, causing the tire to be cut.
10Rosberg limped back to the pits and changed his tire, but the mishap ruined his race and he would go on to finish in eighth.
11Hamilton, on the quicker option tires, drove through the back of the pack to finish in sixth.
12With the misfortune of the Mercedes cars and Kimi Raikkonen's mechanical troubles, Vettel cruised to victory, while 21-year-old Daniil Kvyat finished in second for his first career podium, ahead of teammate Ricciardo.
13Formula 1 is often criticized for not being exciting, but the Hungarian Grand Prix was two hours of madness.
