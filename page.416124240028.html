APPLE WATCH LIVE: Try-on visits, online orders begin

1Customers were invited to try them on in stores and order them online.
2The watch is Apple's first new product category since the iPad came out five years ago.
3Analysts are waiting to see how well the watch will sell beyond devoted Apple fans.
4Apple has a better chance at succeeding than any other smartwatch maker so far, yet it will likely take time before sales reach the kind of numbers that Apple gets for iPhones and iPads.
5Watch prices start at $349, but can go as high as $17,000 for a luxury edition in gold.
6People can try the watch on in Apple stores, but for now all orders are being handled online.
7Shipments begin April 24.
8It's available in the U.S. and eight other markets around the world.
9In the U.S., the watch is available only in Apple stores.
10In some countries, select department stores and resellers also have it.
11Apple starts taking orders for the watch on its website and Apple Store app.
12Currently, this is the only way Apple is selling the watch.
13Even those visiting retail stores will have to order online - either at home or at a Web terminal inside the store.
14The retail stores are meant for customers who aren't sure which watch case, band or size they want - or aren't sure they even want one.
15Staff will be on hand to help customers try on the watches and answer questions before buying.
16Customers are encouraged to make an appointment online, though walk-ins will be accepted - just expect a wait.
17In central Shanghai, potential Apple watch buyers stood in lines two to five people long over their lunch hour at an Apple store on Friday to try on the watch many said they already planned to buy.
18"It was beautifully made, like an expensive watch," said Li Hao, 27, a Web designer who owns a Mac, an iPad and an Apple TV.
19He has just traded up from an iPhone 4 to the new iPhone 6 Plus.
20China was among countries where the watch had its global debut Friday, reflecting the country's fast-growing status as one of Apple's most important markets.
21Li said he planned to buy the sport version of the watch at about 3,000 yuan ($500).
22Qi Tian, 26, who works in human resources for a real estate company, said he was "not a big fan" of Apple, though he owns four or five products.
23He said he planned to order a watch online that day.
24"I just came to see if the size fits," said Qi.
