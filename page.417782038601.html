Tropical Storm Carlos Batters Mexico's Southwest Coast

1Officials ordered schools closed Monday and readied shelters along Mexico's southwestern coast as Tropical Storm Carlos battered beaches with high waves and strong wind as it swirled just offshore.
2Carlos was downgraded from a hurricane Sunday, but while it regained some strength later in the day, forecasters said it probably would not intensify much more while gradually edging toward land over the next 48 hours.
3The U.S. National Hurricane Center cautioned, however, that just a slight strengthening would return Carlos to a hurricane.
4The storm was centered 95 miles (150 kilometers) west of Acapulco late Sunday with top sustained winds of 70 mph (110 kph), the hurricane center said.
5The storm was moving northwest about 6 mph (9 kph).
6Beaches in Acapulco were much less busy on Sunday than usual for a weekend as six-foot waves washed away more than a dozen small palm-frond huts and strong winds knocked down some trees.
7Workers cleared away hammocks and lounge chairs from beachside hotels and cafes.
8Jonathan Capote, spokesman for Guerrero state's Civil Protection agency, said no serious damage had been reported and only one injury was known, someone who fell from a fence.
9"Aside from a few fallen billboards and trees, we haven't had any damage," he said.
10Out in the sea, two surfers braved the dangerous conditions to ride the big crashing waves.
11Sergio Pina, a business risk manager from Mexico City, stood among a group of spectators watching the wild weather.
12"It's impressive. It's very strong," he said.
13"There are launches turned over, fallen cables."
14Officials said schools would be closed Monday and urged residents to stay inside their homes.
15State authorities said 507 shelters, including 98 in Acapulco, had been prepared along Guerrero's coast.
16Coastal areas stretching north from Acapulco also were taking precautions and beaches there were less busy than usual.
17Carlos, on Saturday, briefly became the third hurricane of the 2015 eastern Pacific season.
18Forecasters said it still threatened to bring heavy rains that could cause life-threatening flash floods and mudslides.
19Rain accumulations of 6 inches to 10 inches were possible in southwestern Mexico, with a chance for up to 15 inches in some areas, according to the hurricane center.
20A hurricane warning extended from Tecpan de Galeana in Guerrero to Punta San Telmo in Michoacan, and a hurricane watch from west of Punta San Telmo to Manzanillo in Colima state.
