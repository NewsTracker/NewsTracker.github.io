Pakistan makes 339-6 against UAE in World Cup Pool B match

Iraq jihadists slow Tikrit advance with bombs and snipers

1Shehzad, who was dropped when he was 11 and 1992 champion Pakistan was struggling at 27-1 in the ninth over, was eventually run out as he approached his seventh one-day international century.
2Sohail was out for his second-highest score in ODIs.
3Both fell during a batting powerplay in which Pakistan stumbled from 148-1 to 177-3, losing some of its momentum before captain Misbah-ul-Haq made 65 to lead his team past 300 for the first time at the tournament.
4Sohaib Maqsood scored 45 from 31 balls, sharing a 75-run partnership with Misbah for the fourth wicket.
5Pakistan has struggled for batting form throughout the tournament, making 224 in losing to India by 76 runs, 160 in losing to the West Indies by 150 runs and 235-7 in beating Zimbabwe by 20 runs.
6Its failures with the bat were more glaringly evident in a tournament in which teams have surpassed 400 twice and 300 12 times in 24 matches.
7Pakistan had the chance to repair that record Thursday when UAE captain Imran Tauqir won the toss and surprisingly chose to bowl on a pitch at McLean Park on which teams batting first have averaged 304 in the last 10 ODIs.
8But Pakistan again struggled to give its innings early impetus.
9It was only 10 in the fourth over when opener Nasir Jamshed, who has scores of 0 and 1 in his previous innings at the tournament, fell to a mis-timed pull shot which was caught by Khurram Khan from the bowling of Manjula Guruge.
10Pakistan struggled to 68-1 after 15 overs but Shehzad and Sohail put on 80 in the next 15 overs to give their team a chance, with wickets in hand, of a score in excess of 300.
11The loss of the second-wicket pair in relatively quick succession set back the Paksitan innings and allowed UAE to pull back the scoring rate with its slower bowlers.
12Sohail first holed out to Shaiman Anwar off the bowling of Mohammad Naveed and Shehzad took a miscalculated run and was dismissed at 176-3.
13Misbah was cautious at first, then hit out, reaching his half century from 39 balls.
14He and Umar Akmal were out to consecutive balls in the 49th over, bowled by Guruge (4-56).
15Shahid Afridi added gloss to the Pakistan total, hitting an unbeaten 21 from seven balls.
1The government has mobilised a 30,000-strong force for the push to retake Tikrit made up of Shiite militiamen and Sunni tribesmen as well as troops and police.
2Outnumbered and outgunned, the jihadists who have held Tikrit since June 2014 have been resorting to guerrilla tactics to disrupt the government's advance.
3"They are using urban warfare and snipers, so we are advancing in a cautious and delicate way, and we need more time," a lieutenant general on the ground told AFP.
4Iraqi forces are moving on Tikrit from three directions, with units targeting the towns of Al-Alam and Ad-Dawr to the north and south, while another large contingent drives from the east.
5"We are close to Ad-Dawr, but Daesh is still in the centre," the senior officer said, using an Arabic acronym for IS.
6Units were also moving from a variety of other directions, with military sources saying the plan was to encircle and besiege IS fighters in Tikrit.
7The jihadist group announced in a radio bulletin Tuesday that a US national from its ranks had carried out a suicide attack against Iraqi forces near Samarra, the other main city in Salaheddin province.
8The operation, the government's largest since it started attempting to regain the ground it lost to jihadists last summer, was announced Sunday by Prime Minister Haider al-Abadi.
9Both Iraqi and Iranian media said Qassem Soleimani -- the commander of the Al-Quds Force covert operations unit of Tehran's elite Revolutionary Guards -- was in Salaheddin province to help coordinate operations.
10The recapture of Tikrit is of both strategic and symbolic importance.
11Located about 160 kilometres (100 miles) north of Baghdad, it is the hometown of former president Saddam Hussein, the remnants of whose Baath party have collaborated with IS.
12Commanders have said Tikrit is a stepping stone for an even more ambitious operation aimed at retaking Mosul, the large northern city which has been the main Iraq hub of IS.
13The battle for Tikrit, which the government has already tried and failed to retake several times, is seen as a test of how effectively such diverse fighting units work together.
14"The battle for Tikrit and other towns in Salaheddin province will provide a mini-preview of what awaits further to the north in Mosul," said the Soufan Group intelligence consultancy.
15Tikrit and its surroundings are Sunni and some tribes have been accused of involvement in the massacre of hundreds of new, mostly Shiite, recruits at the nearby base called Speicher in June.
16Some leaders, including the powerful head of Iraq's Popular Mobilisation units Hadi al-Ameri, have explicitly said the Tikrit operation would be an opportunity to exact revenge.
17The UN warned that operations "must be conducted with the utmost care to avoid civilian casualties, and with full respect for fundamental human rights principles and humanitarian law."
18General Martin Dempsey, chairman of the US Joint Chiefs of Staff, said Iran's military assistance in the Tikrit offensive could be positive as long as it did not fuel sectarian divisions.
19"This is the most overt conduct of Iranian support," Dempsey told senators, which came "in the form of artillery" and other aid.
20Dempsey said Shiite militia -- which are armed by Tehran -- account for about two-thirds of the force while Iraqi government army troops make up the remainder.
21A senior US defence official had suggested last month that the Mosul offensive should begin in April or May, a move that irked Iraqi commanders who argued the timing was theirs to decide.
22On Monday, US President Barack Obama's envoy for the fight against IS stressed there was no timeline.
23"The point about Mosul, or the point about any aspect of the counter-offensive, is less about the timing than about the preparation," John Allen said.
24Australia announced Tuesday it will send another 300 troops to Iraq in a joint mission with New Zealand to help prepare Iraqi forces for the fightback.
25And two cargo planes of Turkish military aid landed in Baghdad Tuesday, a sign of Ankara's apparently increasing willingness to help tackling IS.
26Allen said that preparation was not just military but should also include measures to assist the affected population.
27Tents have been set up in Samarra to shelter civilians fleeing Tikrit and joining what the International Organization for Migration says are 2.5 million people already displaced in Iraq.
