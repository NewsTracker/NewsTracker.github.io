Orioles play home games in Florida after riots in Baltimore

1Baltimore Orioles players stand for the singing of the national anthem before a baseball game against the Tampa Bay Rays in St. Petersburg, Fla., Friday, May 1, 2015.
2Baltimore Orioles manager Buck Showalter looked out of place.
3Wearing his home uniform, Showalter took his normal spot in the visiting dugout at Tropicana Field before Friday night's game against Tampa Bay.
4But the attire is appropriate this weekend with Baltimore "hosting" the relocated three-game series in Florida.
5"We all understand why," Showalter said of shifting the series.
6"Hopefully it's a positive thing. I hope it's a positive thing when we get home."
7The games were moved from Camden Yards to Tropicana Field this week after violence broke out in Baltimore following the funeral of Freddie Gray, who died in police custody after suffering a spinal cord injury under mysterious circumstances.
8After the riots, the Orioles hosted the Chicago White Sox in a game played behind closed doors on Wednesday, no fans were allowed in.
9Now, with this series being relocated, Orioles will bat last on the road.
10Showalter said after the first pitch, everyone's focus will be on the game.
11"White pants, grey pants, it's still baseball between the line," the manager said.
12Start times remain the same as were scheduled in Baltimore.
13Based on rule 2.00 in the MLB rule book, the home team is the team on whose grounds the game is played, so the statistics from the games will be recorded as home games for the Rays and road for the Orioles.
14Tampa Bay will finish the season with 84 home games and the Orioles with 84 on the road, but the attendance figures will reflect home attendance for the Orioles.
15"A little unique," Rays manager Kevin Cash said.
16"A little more unique when we're managing the game. The guys, they're fine. They understand the situation."
17Baltimore infielder Steve Pearce, from nearby Lakeland, Florida., not only is playing the unusual home game, but is making his pro debut at second base.
18"It's a lot of stuff," Pearce said.
19"It's all part of the game."
20All tickets are being sold as general admission in the lower level, which holds around 18,000.
21The Rays have averaged just 17,545 in home attendance this season.
22The Rays will take batting practice after gates are open.
23They are normally off the field before fans enter.
24Because of the flip, Baltimore's pregame workout will be done before fans are let in.
25Showalter thanked the Tampa Bay organization for its cooperation not only for baseball, but "our city."
26This is the fifth time since 2007 in which the home team batted first, according to STATS.
27- July 23, 2013: At San Francisco, the Giants batted first against Cincinnati in Game 2 of a doubleheader, which was a makeup of a game rained out on July 4 in Cincinnati.
28- June 24-26, 2011: A three-game series was relocated from Miami to Seattle due to a conflict with a U2 concert.
29- June 25-27, 2010: A three-game series was relocated from Toronto to Philadelphia following security concerns with the G20 Summit the same weekend.
30- Sep 26, 2007: In Seattle, the Mariners batted first against Cleveland in Game 1 of a doubleheader.
31The game was a makeup of a rained out game in April in Cleveland.
32Mary Kidwell, left, of Sarasota, Fla., and Melinda Piquette, of Tampa, Fla., hold signs outside Tropicana Field before a baseball game between the Baltimore Orioles and Tampa Bay Rays in St. Petersburg, Fla., Friday, May 1, 2015.
