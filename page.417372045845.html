Warriors guard Klay Thompson diagnosed with concussion (Yahoo Sports)

2OAKLAND, Calif.
3(AP) -- The initial hit caused Klay Thompson plenty of pain.
4The final diagnosis delivered another blow to him and the Golden State Warriors as they prepare for the NBA Finals.
5Two days after he got kneed in the head by Houston's Trevor Ariza, the Warriors said Friday that Thompson has a concussion and will not return to practice until he is symptom free.
6The All-Star guard went through neurological tests that confirmed the concussion.
7He was injured in the fourth quarter of Golden State's 104-90 win over the Rockets in the Western Conference finals clincher Wednesday night.
8The NBA Finals start Thursday against Cleveland.
9And while the Warriors are optimistic Thompson will be cleared before then, he must pass through the league's concussion protocol first.
10''This break has turned out to be good for us,'' Warriors coach Steve Kerr said after Friday's practice.
11''And maybe good for Cleveland, too, because they've got some injuries.
12Cavaliers guard Kyrie Irving has been slowed by knee tendinitis and a sore foot, and four-time NBA MVP LeBron James has been banged up as well.
13But neither of those injuries seems as serious as the one the Warriors are facing now.
14Thompson was at the team's facility Friday but did not participate in the workout.
15The Warriors will practice again Saturday and take Sunday off.
16Kerr said he's not concerned about Thompson staying sharp.
17''Klay picks up his rhythm very quickly,'' Kerr said.
18It's unclear how long Thompson could be away from the court - and that's the concern for the Warriors.
19The NBA's concussion policy requires players to pass a series of steps without experiencing symptoms before they can return.
20That starts with riding a stationary bike and progresses to jogging, agility work and non-contact team drills, according to the league.
21Players must start over if they experience any symptoms.
22The protocol also requires the team physician to discuss the process with Dr. Jeffrey Kutcher, the director of the NBA's concussion program, before a player can return.
23Kerr insisted he will heed the advice of the medical staff as he always has with injuries.
24''I just do what the doctors say we should do,'' Kerr said.
25''Obviously, we want to be as careful as possible and make sure our players are safe and sound and healthy.
26Team doctors initially cleared Thompson to return to the game.
27He ended up not playing, and the team said he started developing symptoms after the game, fueling questions about the league's concussion protocol.
28Thompson's father, former NBA player Mychal Thompson, had to drive his son home after the game because he was feeling ill.
29He said Thursday that Klay vomited a couple of times but had been feeling ''better by the hour'' and was confident that doctors would clear him before the finals begin.
30Thompson also needed stitches on his right ear after the hit caused blood to spill down the side of his head.
31It was the second consecutive game the Warriors were under scrutiny for letting an All-Star player return after getting banged up.
32Point guard and MVP Stephen Curry got hurt in the second quarter of Game 4 when he jumped as Ariza was about to go up for a shot.
33Ariza saw him and stopped abruptly, causing Curry to tumble over him.
34Curry's head and right arm hit the court, where he remained for several minutes.
35Curry returned in the third quarter after passing the concussion protocol.
36He was diagnosed with a bruised head and a bruised right arm - but has not shown any signs of a concussion, the team said.
37Curry called it a strange coincidence and said he's confident in the system in place.
38''The amount of times that that's happened, I don't think that's too prevalent in basketball,'' Curry said.
39''You kind of just go based on how you feel, the test that they run, and you have confidence that if they say you're OK to go out and play.
40But in the cases that I had, if you feel not yourself, you let them know and they pull you out and kind of reassess.
41''That's why the protocol is what it is.
42You go through tests, if you pass, you're able to go back in there.
43You make a judgment call.
