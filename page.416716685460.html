Baltimore mayor lifts curfew

1BALTIMORE - The mayor of Baltimore on Sunday lifted a night curfew imposed on the city last week to stem a spate of looting and arson that followed the funeral of a young black man who died from injuries suffered while in the police custody.
2Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake said she believed sufficient calm had returned to allow her to end the 10 p.m.-5 a.m. curfew, which took effect last Tuesday after protests over the death of 25-year-old Freddie Gray turned violent on Monday.
3"My goal has always been to not have the curfew in place a single day longer than was necessary," the mayor said.
4"I believe we have reached that point today."
5The mayor said the Maryland National Guard would begin withdrawing from the streets over the next week.
6The surprise announcement on Friday by the city's chief prosecutor that she was bringing criminal charges against the six police officers involved in Gray's arrest has helped to defuse outrage over his death.
7The protests in the mostly black city of 625,000 have been a reprise of nationwide anger that erupted last year after the deaths of unarmed black men in confrontations with police in Missouri, New York and elsewhere.
8"What we saw over the past few days is not just the resiliency of our city, but also our communities coming together," the mayor told a news conference.
9"We want to heal our city."
10Republican Governor Larry Hogan welcomed the decision by the Democratic mayor, who had faced criticism for what some saw as a slow initial response to Monday's violence.
11"It's going to take a while for us to get totally back to normal, but I think lifting the curfew is a good idea," he said at a separate news conference on Sunday.
12He said an additional 1,000 police officers and 3,000 National Guard troops had been brought in to help keep the peace but were now going home.
13Hogan was among those who expressed concern over the financial impact of the curfew, which forced bars and restaurants to close early.
14Baltimore's downtown Inner Harbor district, a tourist destination, resembled a ghost town on Friday and Saturday nights, normally its busiest times.
15"Baltimore curfew is seriously hurting businesses glad to see it lifted," wrote someone on Twitter using the name God First.
16But many tweets accused authorities of having applied the curfew selectively and mostly in black neighborhoods.
17People rallying in Baltimore on Saturday praised the decision by prosecutor Marilyn Mosby to charge one of the officers involved in Gray's arrest with murder and five others with lesser crimes.
18Mosby said the state medical examiner ruled Gray's death a homicide.
19She said he was unlawfully arrested and the officers repeatedly ignored his pleas for medical help while he was handcuffed, shackled and lying face down in the back of a police van.
20Her swift decision to bring charges contrasted with decisions last year by grand juries to clear officers in the Missouri and New York deaths.
21The mood of Saturday's rally was almost celebratory, in sharp contrast to the outrage expressed earlier in the week, especially on Monday, when more than a dozen law enforcement officers were hurt and more than 200 people were arrested.
22Still, Baltimore police arrested at least a dozen people on Saturday for violating the curfew.
