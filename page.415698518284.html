With business activity at near 4-year high, survey finds eurozone economy gaining momentum

1LONDON - The 19-country eurozone economy appears to be gaining momentum as a closely-watched survey found business activity at a near four-year high in March.
2In its monthly survey, financial information company Markit says its purchasing managers' index for the region rose to 54.1 points in March from 53.3 in February.
3That puts the index at its highest level since May 2011.
4Anything above 50 indicates expansion.
5Markit says the upturn was largely fueled by new orders and that the improvement was broad-based across sectors.
6The survey found Germany, the currency bloc's biggest economy, doing particularly well and France improving.
7Chris Williamson, Markit's chief economist, says Tuesday that the European Central Bank's recently launched monetary stimulus has started "at a time when the eurozone's economic upturn is already starting to gain traction."
