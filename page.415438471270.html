Children rescued from Boko Haram so traumatised they have forgotten their names

1Nairobi, Kenya: After years in a Boko Haram camp, the children had forgotten their native language.
2They couldn't even remember their names.
3They just stared past Christopher Fomunyoh when he tried to engage them.
4It was a rare glimpse at the human toll left by the extremists who have been fighting to create an Islamic state in Nigeria and surrounding areas.
5Mr Fomunyoh, regional director at the Washington-based National Democratic Institute, visited the children recently at an orphanage in the city of Maroua in northern Cameroon.
6They had been rescued by the country's military in November from a squalid Boko Haram encampment near the border with Nigeria.
7This is what he saw: 100 children, aged 5 to 17, without shoes in an orphanage built for 20.
8Hard benches for sleeping.
9A shortage of rice.
10Boys who appeared to be speaking broken Arabic, rather than one of the many languages native to Cameroon.
11Boko Haram is now under siege by Nigerian and other African troops.
12If the insurgents are pushed back, hundreds more children like these could soon be found.
13But what condition will they be in?
14Mr Fomunyoh and others working with the newly rescued children were left to unravel the mystery of what happened to them.
15Were they fighters or merely students?
16How long had they been captive?
17How had they forgotten their names?
18Where were their parents?
19"Right now, there's not full comprehension of the damage of this crisis," said Mr Fomunyoh, who is trying to provide assistance to the children.
20"Even if kinetic operations were to end soon and Boko Haram was taken off the battlefield, it would take years to really address consequences in humanitarian terms."
21In April 2014, Boko Haram grabbed the world's attention when fighters abducted 200 schoolgirls in northern Nigeria.
22But it wasn't the first time the group conducted a mass kidnapping.
23The rate and scale of the group's abductions have increased steadily since 2013, according to a report from the US-based Watchlist on Children and Armed Conflict.
24Many of the victims have been raped, and only a few have escaped, activists say.
25The group "forcibly converts the Christian women and girls they capture to Islam and often coerces them and other female abductees into marriage", Watchlist's 2014 report said.
26According to Human Rights Watch, Boko Haram has allegedly recruited boys as young as 12 to fight for the group.
27Still, Mr Fomunyoh wasn't prepared for what he saw.
28And he worried about what would come next for the children.
