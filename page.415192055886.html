9Professor Chapman says tapering subsidies creates a disincentive to hike fees too high.
10He and colleague David Phillips worked with education department staff to develop the proposal.
11If adopted, the plan could allay concerns about skyrocketing fees under the government's plan to deregulate the university sector.
12Labor has derided it as a "great big new tax".
13"This is a plan that would actually see fees skyrocket," said opposition higher education spokesman Kim Carr.
14The Greens labelled it a fine for universities that overcharged.
15But the government insists the measure isn't a fine and rejects suggestions it's a tax.
16"In no way is the government considering taxes on universities," Assistant Education Minister Simon Birmingham told the Senate.
17Senator Carr's "fantasy" was a gross misrepresentation of Prof Chapman's proposal, he said.
18Even if the Chapman proposal was adopted, every university student would still attract some level of government subsidy and would be able to take out HECS loans to cover fees.
