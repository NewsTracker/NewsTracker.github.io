Dad confident pilot of missing helicopter in Nepal, who is from Wichita, will be found (VIDEO)

1Search and rescue teams were still looking Wednesday evening for a missing U.S. Marine Corps helicopter in Nepal that was reportedly flown by Capt.
2Chris Norgren, a Wichita native.
3The helicopter was declared missing Tuesday evening.
4U.S. defense officials said six U.S. Marines, including Norgren, and two Nepalese soldiers were in the helicopter, which was delivering disaster aid to earthquake victims.
5Ron Norgren, Chris' dad, said he is confident his son is still alive.
6Ron Norgren sat in a reclining chair Wednesday afternoon and talked about his son.
7A picture of Chris in his uniform hangs in the hallway next to a picture of his sister Jennifer.
8Ron also pointed out a picture of Chris in uniform holding a gun, taken when he was in Afghanistan.
9Two American flag "Welcome Home" magnets are on the fridge door.
10Ron said he last heard from Chris on Mother's Day, when Chris sent flowers home to his mom, Theresa, while he was in Nepal.
11"I told him I was very proud of him, that he's over in Nepal helping people out and that he still remembered Mother's Day," Ron said.
12"He said he was glad to hear I was doing so well. He texted me back and thanked me for the compliment, and that was about it."
13Ron said Marine Corps representatives came to his house Tuesday night in northwest Wichita to tell the family Chris was missing.
14They told him to call his wife to come home.
15Theresa works for Families Together, an agency that helps families of children with disabilities and special needs.
16Ron said he couldn't wait on his wife for the news because he was thinking the worst.
17He said Marine Corps officials visited him again on Wednesday but didn't have any updates - just that Chris was still missing.
18But one silver lining for Ron is that rescuers haven't reported any smoke, fire or emergency sirens in the search area.
19"I know my son," Ron said.
20"He's a very determined individual, and I really truly believe he'll come out of it OK."
21The Marine helicopter was last seen after another helicopter in the area "picked up some (radio) chatter about a fuel problem," Army Col.
22Steve Warren, a Pentagon spokesman, told the Marine Corps Times.
23"Right now we are hopeful that there was no crash. There has been no (emergency) beacon, no other signs - no flames, no smoke, no hole in the ground - to indicate that there was a crash," Warren said.
24Chris Norgren was born in Wichita and turned 31 in February.
25He graduated from Bishop Carroll Catholic High School in 2002.
26He played football all four years in high school and was a photographer for the student newspaper.
27"I would put my life in his hands in a minute," said Alan Schuckman, Chris' high school football coach, who still coaches at the school.
28Schuckman said he visited Ron and Theresa on Wednesday.
29"When I talked to the family and they told me Chris was the pilot of the plane, there was a sense of calmness that came across my body," Schuckman said.
30"If he was in charge, it will be all right. I just have that much confidence in that guy."
31Schuckman said he last talked with Chris before he left for Japan in the fall.
32Kollen Long, adviser of the school newspaper and journalism teacher at Bishop Carroll, said he's obsessed over the news coverage about Chris.
33"I guess he's in his 30s now, but to me, he's still a high school kid who sat at the front table of the newspaper class," Long said.
34"I know he's a big tough Marine now, but I just envision him as a young high school kid."
35Long said Chris was one of the best photojournalists he's taught and worked to recruit him for the student newspaper.
36Long said Chris would always have a stack of books next to him in the dark room while he waited for his film to develop, because he didn't want to waste any time.
37Leticia Nielsen, president of the high school, said she hopes to hold a prayer vigil for Chris but doesn't have a time set because of graduation this weekend.
38Chris received a bachelor's degree in aerospace engineering and mathematics from the University of Missouri-Rolla, where he played football.
39He also coached football at Bishop Carroll Catholic High School and worked for Cessna Aircraft Co. He has worked in a state park in Alaska, where he ended up facing a bear, his father said.
40"I'm serious; the kid can do just about anything he wants to," Ron said.
41Chris got laid off from Cessna.
42Ron said Chris had a private pilot's license and always wanted to fly, so he signed up for the Marine Corps.
43Ron said the Marine Corps guaranteed Chris that he could be a pilot, even though most must enlist before being placed in that position.
44Chris trained for the Marines in Virginia and then led a group of about 40 reservists in Afghanistan for roughly eight months, because flight schools were full at the time.
45After returning to the states, he went to flight school in Florida, first flying fixed-wing aircraft and then switching to helicopters.
46Ron said he then transferred to California for the rest of his training to become a captain.
47When in the states, Chris spends most of his time in San Diego, where he rented a house with other Marines.
48Ron said he comes to Wichita occasionally to visit family.
49His schedule calls for him to return to the states later this month.
50Chris' 26-year-old sister, Jennifer, who is developmentally delayed, survived leukemia.
51When Ron talked about his two kids, he started to cry.
