Tunisian museum hit by terrorist attack delays reopening

1TUNIS, Tunisia - The curator of the Tunisian museum targeted by a terrorist attack announced Tuesday it is postponing its reopening, for logistical and security reasons.
2The National Bardo Museum in Tunis was scheduled to reopen Tuesday for the first time since gunmen opened fire on tourists March 18.
3The attack killed 21 people, and two gunmen were killed by police.
4Curator Moncef Ben Moussa told The Associated Press that there will be a "symbolic" ceremony Tuesday for select guests, but the museum will not open to the public until Sunday.
5He blamed the delay on "organizational" problems.
6Museum spokeswoman Hanane Srarfi said the problems were primarily logistical, but added, "There is obviously also the security aspect being taken into account."
7She said there have been no threats against the museum, but "we should be vigilant."
8Authorities have arrested several people in the attack, the worst in Tunisia in 13 years, and are still hunting for the suspected mastermind.
9Five leading security officials were fired after the attack, and a police officer charged with surveillance of the museum was jailed.
10Officials have said none of the museum's major archaeological treasures suffered damage.
11Tuesday's ceremony will include a tribute to the victims of the attack and a concert by the Tunis symphony orchestra.
12Activists also plan a "march against terrorism" to the museum.
