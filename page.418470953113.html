The Latest: Mexico drug lord 'El Chapo' Guzman escapes

1A manhunt remains underway for Joaquin Guzman, head of the powerful Sinaloa Cartel.
2The organization has an international reach and is believed to control most of the major crossing points for drugs at the U.S. border with Mexico.
3Associated Press journalists near the Altiplano report that the roads are being heavily patrolled by Federal Police, who had also set up checkpoints.
4Flights were also suspended at Toluca airport near the penitentiary in the State of Mexico.
5Mexico's top security official says top drug lord Joaquin "El Chapo" Guzman has escaped through a 1.5-kilometer (1 mile) tunnel that opened into the shower area of his cell.
6Security Commissioner Monte Alejandro Rubido tells a news conference Sunday that Guzman used an elaborate escape hatch built allegedly without the detection of authorities.
7With his escape Guzman has done what Mexican authorities promised would not happen after his re-capture last year - to slip out of a maximum security prison for the second time.
8Rubido says 18 employees from various part of the Altiplano prison 90 kilometers (56 miles) west of Mexico City have been taken in for questioning.
