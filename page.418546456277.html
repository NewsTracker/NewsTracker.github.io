Israeli premier criticizes Iran deal; his rival to lobby US

1Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu stepped up his criticism Wednesday of the U.S.-led nuclear deal with Iran, warning that Israel has a "great and mighty" strength to defend itself.
2Netanyahu's war of words came as his political rival, Isaac Herzog, announced he would go to the U.S. to lobby for a compensation package to insure Israel's military advantage in the region.
3Herzog's trip reflects the broad opposition to the deal in Israel, where most politicians fear the deal will fail to stop Iran from developing nuclear weapons while strengthening the Islamic Republic's support for Israel's staunchest enemies.
4Addressing parliament, Netanyahu reiterated that Israel was not bound by the deal and said Israel would continue to oppose it.
5"All the more so, we will reserve our right to defend ourselves against all of our enemies," he said.
6"We have strength, and it is great and mighty."
7Despite Netanyahu's tough rhetoric, Israel's options appear limited, given the wide international support for Tuesday's agreement.
8Israel's first course of action is expected to be a lobbying effort in the U.S. Congress ahead of a review of the deal.
9But even there, its influence will be limited since President Barack Obama does not need congressional approval.
10The Republican-led Congress will instead likely try to derail the deal by passing new sanctions or preventing Obama from lifting existing sanctions - the key incentive for Iran to comply with the deal.
11Obama has said he would veto any resolutions undermining the deal.
12In the first sign of Israeli diplomatic action, Herzog soon will travel to Washington.
13"We must enter dialogue with our big ally, the U.S.," Herzog said in parliament.
14In a videotaped statement posted on his Facebook page, Herzog said he had spoken with officials of pro-Israel lobbying group AIPAC and other U.S. groups, and would be headed to the U.S. "to ask that Israel gets a security umbrella and a series of steps to allow Israel to maintain its advantage in the region due to the new reality coming out of the deal."
15Herzog has criticized Netanyahu over the deal, saying the Israeli leader unnecessarily antagonized the U.S. and other allies during negotiations.
16In March, Netanyahu voiced his opposition to the emerging deal in a speech to the U.S. Congress that enraged the White House.
17But in a meeting with Netanyahu late Tuesday, Herzog promised "to do everything for the security of Israel" in light of the Iranian deal, according to a statement from Netanyahu's office.
18"Our national security is not a matter of dispute," Netanyahu's office quoted Herzog as saying.
19Israeli politicians say the deal won't block Iran from building nuclear weapons.
20They also say that by easing sanctions, Iran will gain access to tens of billions of dollars, some of which could be funneled to militant anti-Israel groups like Hamas and Hezbollah.
21Israeli leaders say a nuclear Iran is an existential threat to Israel because Iranian leaders have called for Israel's destruction.
22"Not everyone has learned the lessons of history. Today, the world powers have fallen in a trap of smiles set by the tyrannical Iranian regime," Netanyahu said.
23Netanyahu said he reviewed the deal at night, and that it includes "absurd things."
24He objected to a clause that requires international inspectors who suspect violations of the deal to give Iran 24 days before inspectors can actually enter the site.
25"It's like giving a crime organization that deals in drugs a 24-day notice before checking its drug lab," Netanyahu said.
26He also objected to a clause that allows new investors in Iran to continue investments if additional sanctions are imposed on Iran in the future.
27And he openly wondered whether one of the names of people listed as those who would be freed of sanctions, Gen. Qassem Soleimani, was the man who heads Iran's elite Quds Force of the Revolutionary Guard.
28"If this is a different Soleimani, it would be good to know, because if not the absurdity crosses all lines," Netanyahu said.
