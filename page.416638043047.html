Harbaugh, Lewis, Ravens visit schools in riot-torn Baltimore

1Former Baltimore Ravens NFL football player Ray Lewis hugs 17-year-old Azariah Bratton-Bey Jr., a senior running back on Frederick Douglass High's football team, during a visit to the school Thursday, April 30, 2015, in Baltimore.
2Lewis, Ravens coach John Harbaugh, and other players visited schools in downtown Baltimore in the aftermath of riots that ravaged the city.
3Ravens coach John Harbaugh, his players and former star linebacker Ray Lewis visited Baltimore schools to help quell tension in the city following days of rioting.
4Harbaugh says several Ravens players unloaded supplies for a food bank Thursday and spoke to students at Frederick Douglass High School and Matthew A. Henson Elementary School.
5The coach says, "We've got a bunch of guys on our team that care deeply for the city and believe in Baltimore."
6The contingent included around 55 players, several Ravens coaches and Lewis, who played 17 seasons with Baltimore.
7Several Douglass students were involved in confrontations with police on Monday, when violence escalated following the funeral of Freddie Gray, who died of spinal cord and other injuries while in police custody.
