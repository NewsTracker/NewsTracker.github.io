Samsung overtakes Apple as biggest smartphone maker, but profits slump

1Samsung's mobile devices division accounts for nearly 60 per cent of its total profit.
2Samsung Electronics overtook Apple to recapture the title of world's top smartphone maker by volume in the first quarter of 2015, research firm Strategy Analytics said on Wednesday.
3It said Samsung shipped 83.2 million smartphones worldwide and captured 24 per cent market share in the quarter, down from 31 per cent a year earlier, but better than Apple's 18 per cent.
4"Samsung continued to face challenges in Asia and elsewhere, but its global performance has stabilised sufficiently well this quarter to overtake Apple and recapture first position as the world's largest smartphone vendor by volume," Strategy Analytics Executive Director Neil Mawston said.
5However, having reclaimed the lead Samsung on Wednesday posted a near 40 per cent fall in first-quarter net profit, missing analyst estimates despite a surge in memory chip demand.
6Amid increasing competition from arch-rival Apple and smaller Chinese manufacturers, the world's top handset maker reported a net profit of 4.6 trillion won ($5.36 billion) for the January-March period, down 38.9 per cent from a year earlier.
7Operating profit fell 29.6 per cent to 6 trillion won, largely in line with the firm's earlier forecast.
8But in a sign that the South Korean electronics giant might be turning a corner after successive quarters of significant declines, the operating profit was up 13.1 per cent from the fourth quarter of last year.
9Samsung Electronics may have put a floor under its mobile margins, but sceptics say profits will undergo a new test with the latest flagship Galaxy smartphones, among the most costly the South Korean company has ever made.
10Samsung's mobile devices division, which accounts for nearly 60 per cent of its total profit, boosted its operating margin to 10.6 per cent in January-to-March.
11That's the highest in three quarters.
12Analysts say Samsung's roll-out of new mid-range products with revamped designs in key markets such as India likely boosted sales.
13Mobile earnings slumped 42 per cent last year due to intense competition in both the top and low-end segments.
14Samsung was forced to dump unsold inventory at steep discounts, pushing quarterly margins into the single digits for the first time since 2010.
15Investors hope the new flagship Galaxy S6 smartphones that went on sale earlier this month will help the company's profits rebound from 2014.
16Samsung is expecting record shipments.
17In April-to-June, mobile margins may rise to 13.6 per cent with the shipment of 22 million Galaxy S6 phones, IBK Securities analyst Lee Seung-woo estimated.
18But some analysts say they need to see sales data before determining whether margins will extend their rebound.
19Difficulty in producing enough of the new S6 phones to keep up with demand could be a short-term constraint.
20Samsung would also need to contend with Apple, which sold 61.2 million iPhones in the quarter ended March 28.
21"The second-quarter performance should be a bit better than the first, but I would need to see concrete sell-through data for the business before determining whether there will be a sustained earnings recovery," said Hana Daetoo Securities analyst Nam Dae-jong.
22Samsung's recent share price weakness suggested investors were cautious, he said.
