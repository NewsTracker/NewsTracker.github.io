Jihadists destroy proposed world heritage site in Mali

1BAMAKO, Mali (AP) - Authorities say Islamic militants have destroyed an historic mausoleum in central Mali that had been nominated as a UNESCO World Heritage site.
2Armed men are believed to have used dynamite to blow up the mausoleum of Cheick Amadou Barry, a religious leader who died in the 19th century.
3One of his descendants, Bologo Amadou Barry, confirmed to The Associated Press that the site had been partially ruined in Hamdallahi village on Sunday night.
4The development in central Mali comes two years after radical jihadists were ousted from power in the north.
5During their rule in 2012, the extremists also destroyed heritage sites in Timbuktu.
6The jihadists left behind a note Sunday warning they would attack those who did not follow the teachings of Islam's prophet.
