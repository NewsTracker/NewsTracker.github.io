Orioles' game in Baltimore postponed due to safety concerns (Yahoo Sports)

1BALTIMORE (AP) -- The Baltimore Orioles' home game against the Chicago White Sox was postponed Monday night because of safety concerns after riots broke out near Camden Yards following the funeral of Freddie Gray.
2Baseball Commissioner Rob Manfred, at the stadium for a previously planned visit, said Tuesday night's game between the Orioles and White Sox could be moved - possibly to Nationals Park in Washington - if safety continued to be an issue.
3Rioters looted stores and threw rocks and bricks at Baltimore police about three miles from the stadium in the wake of the mysterious death of Gray.
4He died April 19 after suffering a severe spinal injury in police custody.
5The Orioles announced the postponement about 40 minutes before gametime.
6About 1,000 fans were inside the stadium at the time, and many gates already had been closed.
7The team said the game was called off after consultation with police.
8The White Sox were in Baltimore for the start of a three-game series.
9''We feel like we made the decision that would provide us the greatest possible security in terms of protecting the fans, the players, the umpires, everybody involved in the game,'' Manfred said.
10Prior to the game, Orioles players gathered around a television in the clubhouse and watched the violence unfold.
11Police blocked off several streets near Camden Yards.
12''There's so many things that go on that you get challenged with, obviously this is a different level,'' Orioles manager Buck Showalter said.
13''So you try to take each moment as it comes and I know there were a lot of calls to some guys' families about making sure they knew what was going on from a safety standpoint.
14But guys are watching it.
15They are all aware of what's going on,'' he said.
16White Sox manager Robin Ventura said his team was aware of the escalating situation.
17''I mean we're definitely keeping tabs on it.
18If there's anything to do, to go ahead and respond to it.
19But you know, it's concerning to anybody, I think, any time it becomes a violent protest, everybody is on high alert and you're trying to do what's best for the game and the players,'' he said.
20There was no immediate word on a makeup date for Monday's game.
21Major League Baseball has postponed and shifted games in the past because of unrest.
22In 1992, the Dodgers had four games postponed in Los Angeles following the Rodney King verdict.
23In 1967, the Orioles and Tigers had a game postponed because of riots in Detroit.
24The next two games were shifted to Baltimore.
