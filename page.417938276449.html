Netanyahu gives cool reception to French peace initiative

1Israel's prime minister said Sunday he will "fiercely reject" any international proposals meant to bring about a solution to the conflict with the Palestinians.
2Benjamin Netanyahu made the remarks ahead of a visit by French Foreign Minister Laurent Fabius.
3France has said it will propose a resolution in the United Nations Security Council with a framework for negotiations toward resolving the Israeli-Palestinian conflict.
4"The way to reach an agreement is only through negotiations and we will fiercely reject attempts to impose international dictates," he told his weekly Cabinet meeting.
5Netanyahu said that attacks against Israelis will continue with any international proposal that does not take Israel's security concerns into account.
6Early Sunday, a Palestinian assailant stabbed and critically wounded an Israeli policeman in Jerusalem's Old City.
7The policeman shot the attacker, critically wounding him, before collapsing and being rushed to a hospital.
8On Friday, a Palestinian shot an Israeli hiker to death in the West Bank.
9Fabius is coming to the region to sound out leaders about a planned Security Council resolution that aims to restart peace talks after a more than year-long lull.
10Palestinian officials and French diplomats have said the proposal would call for basing the borders between Israel and a future Palestinian state on the lines that existed before Israel captured the West Bank, east Jerusalem and Gaza Strip in the 1967 Mideast war.
11It also would set a two-year deadline for an agreement.
12Israel rejects a return to its pre-1967 lines, saying they are indefensible.
13It also opposes deadlines.
14Two decades of talks brokered mainly by the United States have failed to produce a two-state solution.
15The latest peace push, led by U.S. Secretary of State John Kerry, fell short in April 2014 after nine months of tense negotiations and the gaps between Israeli and Palestinian positions remain vast.
