Florida State rusher suspended, charged with punching woman (Yahoo Sports)

1TALLAHASSEE, Fla.
2(AP) -- Florida State indefinitely suspended leading running back Dalvin Cook after he was charged Friday with punching a woman outside a downtown bar - the second time in two weeks that an FSU player has been charged with punching a woman.
3The incident happened June 23 after the popular bar closed at 2 a.m. A witness told police a group of football players began arguing with the alleged victim after one suggested taking her home and having sex and she refused, saying she had a boyfriend.
4The woman, Madison Geohegan, told police that during the argument Florida State wide receiver Travis Rudolph came too close to her and she pushed him.
5At that point, Cook stepped in, asked her why she pushed Rudolph and began swinging at her, she told police.
6Cook was restrained by friends, but while held back punched Geohegan in the face, she told police.
7Police said she had swelling and a bleeding lip.
8She identified Cook through a photographic lineup.
9State Attorney Willie Meggs interviewed Geohegan and Cook before issuing the arrest warrant on a misdemeanor battery charge.
10Police are giving Cook the option of turning himself in.
11He denied striking the woman.
12Cook, 19, led Florida State last year with 1,008 rushing yards as a freshman.
13He was also second on the team with eight touchdowns.
14Cook rushed for 177 yards and a touchdown and caught five passes for 43 yards in Florida State's 37-35 victory over Georgia Tech to win the ACC championship.
15He also rushed for 103 yards in Florida State's 59-20 loss to Oregon in the Rose Bowl.
16Cook is 5-feet-11-inches tall and 200 pounds.
17Geohegan is 5-feet-4-inches tall and weighs 140 pounds, according to a police report.
18It's the second time in two weeks that an FSU player has been charged with punching a woman.
19Freshman quarterback De'Andre Johnson was caught on a surveillance video punching a woman at another bar near campus on June 24.
20He was suspended after being charged the next day and kicked off the team earlier this week when the video was made public.
21Johnson, however, had yet to play a game for Florida State, while Cook has been expected to be a key to the team's offense this year.
22''We spend a good deal of time educating our student-athletes about appropriate behavior and their responsibilities as representatives of Florida State.
23The majority of our players are exemplary, but clearly we must place an even stronger emphasis on this,'' coach Jimbo Fisher said in a statement released by the school.
24''I remain committed to educating our young men and holding them accountable for their actions,'' Fisher said.
25''We will do better.
26University President John Thrasher said he has asked Fisher and the school's athletics director to develop a plan to help the school's athlete's understand the consequences of their actions.
27''This will include Coach Fisher meeting immediately with his team to reiterate, in no uncertain terms, our expectations of them.
28I also plan to meet with the team, and we will be asking professionals who deal with these matters, including State Attorney Willie Meggs, to speak with them,'' Thrasher said.
29A phone number for Cook was not immediately available, and it was unclear if he has a lawyer.
30Geohegan didn't immediately return a call seeking comment.
