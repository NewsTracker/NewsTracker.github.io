Most Asia stocks rise after Greece approves austerity bill

1FILE - In this July 6, 2015 file photo, an American flag is draped on the exterior of the New York Stock Exchange.
2Global stock markets mostly rose on Wednesday, July 15, 2015, after China posted better-than-expected economic growth in the second quarter.
3Investors are awaiting the Greek parliamentary vote on a bailout deal and the Fed chair's speech on the U.S. economic outlook.
4Asian stocks edged higher Thursday after Greek lawmakers approved an austerity bill needed for another bailout and the dollar rose after the Fed chief indicated that U.S. interest rates could start rising later this year.
5KEEPING SCORE: Japan's Nikkei 225 rose 0.5 percent to 20,561.84 and South Korea's Kospi edged up 0.3 percent to 2,078.54.
6Hong Kong's Hang Seng slipped 0.2 percent to 24,993.20.
7The Shanghai Composite Index in mainland China rose 0.1 percent to 3,810.83 in a sign it was stabilizing after big drops the past two days had raised fears of another rout.
8Australia's S&P/ASX 200 gained 0.5 percent to 5,661.40.
9GREEK RELIEF: In a late-night vote, lawmakers overwhelmingly approved a strict austerity bill that creditors demanded in order to begin negotiations on a new bailout package worth about 85 billion euros ($93 billion) for the debt-stricken Mediterranean country.
10Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras insisted it the deal was the best he could come up with to avoid Greece defaulting and leaving the euro currency.
11US ECONOMY: In comments to lawmakers, Federal Reserve Chair Janet Yellen gave an optimistic assessment for the world's biggest economy.
12She said the economy is reviving after a harsh winter and said the U.S. central bank could start raising interest rates before the end of the year if improvements stay on track.
13However she also said rates will remain at very low levels "for quite some time after the first increase."
14Years of easy credit has boosted global stock markets but the looming possibility of a rate hike is tempting investors to pull out of shares.
15ANALYST QUOTE: "The fact the grey clouds that are China and Greece have cleared slightly only brings a sharper focus on the biggest macro cloud of 2015: Fed lift off," IG market strategist Evan Lucas wrote in a commentary.
16CURRENCIES: The euro fell to its lowest level since the beginning of June, slipping to $1.0929 from $1.0954 in late trading Wednesday.
17The dollar slipped slightly to 123.88 yen from 123.92 yen.
18WALL STREET: Major U.S. benchmarks were little changed.
19The Dow Jones industrial average slipped less than 0.1 percent to 18,050.17 and the Standard & Poor's 500 edged down less than 0.1 percent to 2,107.40.
20The Nasdaq composite fell 0.1 percent to 5,098.94.
21ENERGY: Oil rebounded from a sharp drop triggered by a report on U.S. supplies that showed a smaller-than-expected decline last week.
22Benchmark U.S. crude rose 37 cents to $51.77 a barrel in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
23The contract fell $1.63, or 3 percent, to close at $51.41 a barrel on Wednesday.
24Brent crude, a benchmark for international oils, rose 43 cents to $57.55 a barrel in London.
