Iraq Launches Large-Scale Military Operation To Oust Islamic State From Anbar Province

1BAGHDAD (AP) - A military spokesman says a large-scale military operation to dislodge Islamic State militants from Iraq's western Anbar province has begun.
2The spokesman for the Joint Operations Command, Brig.
3Gen. Yahya Rasool, announced in a televised statement that the operations started at dawn Monday.
4Rasool didn't clarify whether the U.S.-led international coalition is taking part, mentioning only government forces and allied Shiite and Sunni paramilitary troops.
5This is not the first time the Iraqi government has announced an operation to retake Anbar - where several key towns, including the provincial capital Ramadi, remain under Islamic State control.
6In May, authorities announced an operation to retake Ramadi, but there has not been any major progress on the ground since then.
7Rasool didn't provide any further details on the ongoing operations.
