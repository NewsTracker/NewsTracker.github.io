US-led coalition launches 15 air strikes in Syria and Iraq against Isis

1The US and its allies conducted 15 air strikes against Islamic State militants in Iraq and Syria during the past 24 hours, a US military statement said on Saturday.
2It said Isis targets near various Iraqi cities, including Mosul and Baiji, were hit in nine air attacks during the 24 hours to Saturday morning.
3In Syria, five air strikes were carried out near the northern border town of Kobani and one near Hasakah, the statement said.
4Strikes have been ongoing in Iraq since 8 August and Syria since 23 September.
5More than 2,000 have been carried out, hindering but not defeating Isis fighters who last year took control of large areas of both countries and have since executed a number of foreign hostages.
6Shia paramilitaries started leaving Tikrit on Saturday following a government deal after locals complained that some fighters had spent several days looting the Sunni city after helping retake it from Isis.
7"As of this afternoon, the (paramilitaries) started to leave the city towards the surrounding areas outside," said Ahmed al-Kraim, the head of the council of Tikrit and its province, Salahuddin.
8"The looting and vandalism against residents' properties stopped today and things are getting better."
9The decision for the paramilitaries to exit came after a meeting between Prime Minister Haidar al-Abadi, a Shia Islamist, and officials from Salahuddin province.
10Tikrit was retaken from Isis militants on Wednesday.
11In the US this week, two women in New York and one in Philadelphia were arrested on terrorism charges which authorities said involved attempts to join or be inspired by Isis.
12A United Nations report said that under Isis, Syria and Iraq had become "finishing schools" for foreign extremists.
