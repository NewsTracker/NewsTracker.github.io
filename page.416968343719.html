Brotherhood leader dies in detention in Egypt -sources

1CAIRO, May 13 (Reuters) - An imprisoned leader of Egypt's banned Muslim Brotherhood died on Wednesday, security sources said, with supporters claiming his death was caused by authorities' failure to provide proper medical attention.
2Farid Ismail, 57, was a parliamentarian under president Mohamed Mursi, the Brotherhood leader overthrown in mid-2013 by then-army chief Abdel Fattah al-Sisi following mass protests.
3Sisi, now president, pledged to eradicate the Brotherhood and launched a security crackdown that has landed thousands of Mursi supporters in prison.
4The security sources said Ismail had died of liver failure in a Cairo hospital, where he had been moved days earlier from a jail in the Nile Delta town of Zagazig.
5Judicial sources said Ismail had been sentenced to seven years in prison last year on charges related to violence following the clearing by security forces of two Cairo protest camps in August 2013 in which hundreds of demonstrators were killed.
6The website of the Brotherhood's banned political party said the authorities had deprived Ismail of medicine and care.
7An earlier article said he had suffered a stroke this month while in solitary detention.
8"We asked the court multiple times in the past month to move him to an outside hospital because his health was deteriorating," his lawyer Abdel Moneim Abdel Maqsood told Reuters.
9"The court agreed but the prison never implemented (the order)."
10Reuters could not immediately reach a judicial official for comment on the allegations.
11Other Brotherhood leaders have been handed death sentences or long prison terms in the past year.
12Mursi himself received a 20-year sentence last month.
