Yahoo pulls the plug on China office

1The Beijing research centre is Yahoo's only remaining physical presence in the country after it sold its Chinese operations to Alibaba in 2005.
2A spokesperson said "around 350 jobs" would be eliminated.
3"We are constantly making changes to align resources, and to foster better collaboration and innovation across our business," Yahoo said in a statement.
4The news that Yahoo was closing its last remaining office in mainland China travelled fast.
5Outside the Yahoo research centre, an employment agency staged a guerrilla-style PR campaign offering the sacked staff new jobs.
6Many Yahoo employees appeared unimpressed as they walked by men dressed up in silver, alien outfits.
7One employee said the news was "very sad."
8He added, "I had good relations with my colleagues and now I need to say goodbye."
9The move by the internet giant, however, doesn't come as a huge surprise.
10Yahoo has been rapidly retreating from the Chinese market.
11In 2013, it stopped offering services to email users in China.
12It's had an often fraught relationship with the Chinese government.
13Controversially the firm handed over information to the authorities that led to the jailing of two Chinese dissidents.
14But according to reports, this closure was nothing to do with government pressure or censorship.
15Instead it's been driven by the internet giant's desire to cut its global operating costs.
16Yahoo has been struggling to maintain profit growth in the face of growing competition from rival online search portals like Google.
17The Sunnyvale, California-based firm has also been trying to adapt to consumers increasingly using mobile devices, which attract less advertising revenue.
18As a result, Yahoo Chief Executive Marissa Mayer has made a slew of acquisitions in a bid to diversify business and add new revenue streams.
19Earlier this year, Yahoo also announced plans to spin off its stake in Alibaba, China's largest e-commerce company, which is valued at more than $30bn.
20The tech giant also owns a lucrative stake in Yahoo Japan.
21Yahoo had a global workforce of about 12,500 workers at the end of 2014.
22Yahoo is the latest US technology firm to exit operations in China.
23Google partially withdrew in 2010 after clashing with Chinese authorities over censorship.
24Earlier this year gamemaker Zynga closed its office and shed more than 70 jobs after its products failed to gain traction against local competition.
25Shaun Rein, managing director of consultancy China Market Research said it was very difficult for consumer-facing foreign internet players to find success in China.
26"Unlike rest of the world, they're facing well-funded, aggressive home-grown players," he said.
27"In China you have really strong entrepreneurship in the online space and they're able to react quicker to consumer trends."
28Mr Rein added that it makes sense for Yahoo to "cull through headcount and business lines that aren't making a profit".
29"When you're as weak as Yahoo globally, it doesn't make sense to have a research and development arm in China since its too expensive."
