Spieth arrives at St. Andrews, resuming Grand Slam quest (Yahoo Sports)

1ST. ANDREWS, Scotland (AP) -- There was no escaping Jordan Spieth even before he arrived Monday at St. Andrews to resume his pursuit of the Grand Slam.
2Behind the practice range, one British television network talked to any player willing to stop.
3After a few gratuitous questions about the weather (cool and cloudy) and the Old Course (magnificent shape), the reporter got to the point.
4What about Spieth's chances?
5Spieth is only the fourth player since 1960, the year Arnold Palmer proposed the concept of the modern Grand Slam in golf, to win the first two majors of the year only to be stopped at the British Open.
6Palmer and Jack Nicklaus (1972) finished one shot behind.
7Tiger Woods was in good position in 2002 until he was blown away by the wind and rain at Muirfield and shot 81 in the third round.
8Spieth only added to the hype by winning the John Deere Classic in a playoff Sunday for his fourth win of the year.
9He flew on a charter flight from Illinois with other players at the John Deere Classic, arriving in Scotland about 10:30 a.m. and checking into his house before coming out to the Old Course to a quiet reception.
10In need of a shave - he's 21, but yes, he shaves - Spieth and his manager walked through a group of about 100 fans who camped out near the entrance to the player lounge to get his autograph.
11Spieth signed everything shoved his way, turned and tossing the last item to a delighted young British boy.
12The jet lag apparently wasn't an issue Monday.
13Spieth planned to hit balls and play nine holes if he felt up to it.
14He wound up going 18 holes, and the fans who hung around into the evening were in for a rare treat - Spieth playing, with Woods right behind him.
15Woods used to tee off before breakfast, but waited until afternoon when most of the drizzling weather cleared out.
16They chatted briefly on the 16th green, and Spieth had some fun on the Road Hole at No.
1717. He hit three shots out of the famous bunker, all of them onto the green.
18''I didn't come here to play boring golf,'' Spieth said.
19He tried it four times, and didn't reach the green with any of them.
20What makes Spieth different from other players in pursuit of the Grand Slam - or even Rory McIlroy, who won the last two majors a year ago, is that he does not overpower golf courses.
21He just scores.
22And he wins.
23And that's what golf is all about, anyway.
24''He beats you with better golf,'' Geoff Ogilvy said.
25''He doesn't beat you because he hits it further.
26Tiger's intimidation was that he always did something amazing.
27Jordan - don't get me wrong, his body of work is amazing - but he doesn't beat you with a crazy par, or a crazy chip-in from the back of the 14th at Muirfield (Village).
28Ogilvy was asked how he would feel going into the final round two shots behind Spieth compared with going against Woods or McIlroy.
29''It would be different.
30It shouldn't be,'' he said.
31''There's more of an intimidation factor to Rory because he's going to hit it 30 (yards) past you all day, and it appears like he's better than you.
32Jordan doesn't appear like he's better than you.
33That now is in reach.
34With his victory at the John Deere, Spieth is within range of his goal of reaching No.
351 in the world.
36He would have to win The Open this week to get past McIlroy, who is out with an ankle injury with no clear indication when he will be able to return.
37''What he's doing is phenomenal,'' Nick Faldo said.
38''And he's confident.
39... He's seeing the right shot, he's visualizing the shot.
40... And he keeps churning out good shots.
41When you're doing that, you just jump back on the saddle and ride again.
42Only in this case, Spieth had to first jump on a plane.
43Not long after he won the U.S. Open, questions arose whether Spieth would change his schedule and skip the John Deere Classic so he could get to Scotland earlier.
44He never gave it a second thought.
45He wanted competition at a tournament where he picked up his first win.
46He wasn't worried about jet lag.
47He wasn't interested in changing his routine for the sake of an extra practice round or two on a course that has been around as long as golf has been played.
48The R&A put Spieth in the same group with Dustin Johnson and Hideki Matsuyama for the opening two rounds.
49Johnson had a 12-foot eagle putt on the final hole at Chambers Bay to win the U.S. Open.
50He three-putted for par, and Spieth was halfway home to the Grand Slam.
51More than the Old Course, the real challenge is the players Spieth has to beat.
52One of those figures to be Johnson, who was asked what he thought about the young Texan's chances in this unlikely bid to win all four majors in one year.
