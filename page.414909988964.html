The Islamic State threatens to come to Rome; Italians respond with travel advice

1Citizens in Rome do not seem too concerned.
2In a recently released video that showed the killing of 21 Christians in Libya, all but one of them Egyptian, the Islamic State issued an ominous warning: "Today we are south of Rome," one masked militant said.
3"We will conquer Rome with Allah's permission."
4To make matters worse, Rita Katz, director of SITE Intelligence Group, pointed out that supporters of the Islamic State have begun using a hashtag to warn of their plan to reach "Rome."
5Exactly what "Rome" means to the Islamic State is unclear -- some experts say it may actually be a reference to the United States or Turkey, or even the West in general.
6But Italy is worried.
