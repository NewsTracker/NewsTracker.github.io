Grizzlies acquire Matt Barnes from Charlotte Hornets (Yahoo Sports)

1The Memphis Grizzlies acquired forward Matt Barnes from the Charlotte Hornets for guard Luke Ridnour, who later was sent to Oklahoma City.
2The Grizzlies announced the trade Thursday, and the Thunder said shortly after they had acquired Ridnour and a conditional 2016 second-round draft pick from Charlotte for guard Jeremy Lamb.
3Both deals were first reported by Yahoo Sports.
4The 35-year-old Barnes averaged 10.1 points, 4 rebounds and a career-high 29.9 minutes for the Los Angeles Clippers last season.
5He shot 136 of 376 from 3-point range.
6The Hornets acquired the 6-foot-7 forward along with center Spencer Hawes last week for guard Lance Stephenson.
7Barnes averaged 8.1 points, 4.5 rebounds and 1.7 assists in 779 games over 12 seasons with the Clippers, Sacramento, New York, Philadelphia, Golden State, Phoenix, Orlando and the Los Angeles Lakers.
8Ridnour averaged 4 points and 2 assists for the Orlando Magic last season.
9The Grizzlies had acquired him Wednesday for the rights to forward Janis Timma, a second-round draft pick in 2013.
10The 23-year-old Lamb played 47 games for the Thunder last season and averaged 6.3 points and 2.3 rebounds in 13.5 minutes.
11''Jeremy Lamb is a quality young wing player who we believe has the talent to help our team,'' Hornets general manager Rich Cho said in a release Thursday.
12Lamb joins a revamped Charlotte backcourt that includes Nicolas Batum, who was acquired in a trade with Portland on Wednesday.
13Batum is expected to start at the two-guard for the Hornets, Cho said Wednesday night.
14The Hornets made three trades already this offseason hoping to improve a team that finished last in the league in 3-point shooting.
15Charlotte traded away Stephenson and Gerald Henderson.
16The Hornets acquired 7-foot-1, 245-pound power forward/center Hawes and Barnes as part of the Stephenson deal last week.
17AP Sports Writer Steve Reed in Charlotte, North Carolina, contributed to this report.
