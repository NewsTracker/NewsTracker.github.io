Latest on Boston bombing trial: Victims arrive at court

1Tsarnaev is charged with conspiring with his brother to place two bombs near the marathon finish line in April 2013, killing three and injuring 260 people.
2A spectator at the 2013 Boston Marathon has testified that she watched her close friend die on the pavement next to her after the first bomb exploded.
3Karen Rand McWatters spoke Wednesday at the federal death penalty trial of Boston bomber Dzhokhar Tsarnaev (joh-HAHR' tsahr-NEYE'-ehv).
4She described the last moments of her friend Krystle Campbell's life.
5She says Campbell "very slowly said that her legs hurt," and the two held hands.
6McWatters says, "Shortly after that, her hand went limp in mine, and she never spoke again."
7McWatters choked back tears as she recalled screaming for help.
8McWatters herself lost her left leg when the first of two bombs went off.
9The defense didn't cross-examine the four victims who testified Wednesday.
10Testimony has ended for the day.
11Trial is set to resume at 9 a.m. Thursday.
12A woman who lost her left leg after the first bomb went off at the 2013 Boston Marathon says she believed that was going to be the day she died.
13Rebekah Gregory testified that she went to the marathon with her 5-year-old son, her boyfriend and his family to see his mother run.
14She says she remembers being hoisted into the air and thrown back by the strength of the bomb.
15Gregory says when she looked down at her leg, "my bones were literally laying next to me on the sidewalk."
16Gregory says she had 17 surgeries on her leg before deciding to have it amputated.
17That surgery was performed in November 2014.
18A Boston College law professor tells The Associated Press that the defense's opening statement sets the tone for their approach to the marathon bombing trial: They want to spare their client from the death penalty.
19Robert Bloom is a former prosecutor who isn't involved in the bombing case.
20He tells the AP on Wednesday that defense attorney Judy Clarke is trying to keep jurors sympathetic to Tsarnaev and maintain a good relationship with them.
21Bloom says the defense will likely use every opportunity to show Tsarnaev was influenced by his older brother, Tamerlan.
22He says prosecutors will methodically lay out the horror of the attacks in court and will want to make the crime "so unsympathetic that the jury will want the death penalty."
23The manager of a store near where the first bomb exploded on the day of the 2013 Boston Marathon has choked back tears while recalling the smell of burned hair and the sight of blood.
24Sean O'Hara is the manager of Marathon Sports.
25His voice trembled as he described the moments after an explosion shattered a window and people came streaming inside, many of them wounded.
26Prosecutors showed a video taken from the store's surveillance cameras showing O'Hara and other employees ripping handfuls of clothing off the racks, then racing outside to help victims.
27In one part of the video, O'Hara is seen wrapping a tourniquet around a woman's leg as blood runs down.
28Jurors in the Boston Marathon bombing trial have been shown a video of the first explosion.
29The video shows a huge blast of smoke, then police officers running toward the blast.
30Runners looked over their shoulders and spectators turned their heads toward the sound of the bombing.
31The jury watched the video intently, many looking somber.
32Twenty-nine-year-old Krystle Campbell was killed by the first bomb.
33She was among the three killed and 260 injured after twin bombs exploded near the marathon finish line.
34The first witness to testify is the executive director of the organization that puts on the marathon.
35Thomas Grilk is the head of the Boston Athletic Association.
36Grilk on Wednesday described the history of the marathon, beginning with the first race in 1897, when just 15 people ran.
37In recent years, tens of thousands of people have run the marathon.
