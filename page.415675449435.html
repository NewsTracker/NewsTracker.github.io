Tunisia PM fires police commanders after attack on Bardo Museum

1Tunis: Tunisian Prime Minister Habib Essid fired six police commanders on Monday, including the head of tourist security, after a militant attack on the national museum last week in which 20 tourists were killed.
2The premier's office spokesman, Mofdi Mssedi said the six also included an intelligence brigade chief, the Tunis district police chief, the traffic police commander, a Bardo security chief and a commander for the capital's Sidi Bachir district.
3Tunisian Prime Minister Habib Essid visited the Bardo Museum and "took note of several security failures there".
4"Prime Minister Habib Essid visited the Bardo Museum yesterday...and took note of several security failures there," Mssedi said.
5Militant gunmen killed 20 foreign tourists, including Japanese, Polish, Italian and Spanish visitors, last Wednesday as they got off buses at the Bardo Museum, inside the parliament compound that is normally heavily guarded.
6It was the worst attack in more than a decade in Tunisia, testing the North African country's young democracy four years after the revolt that overthrew autocrat Zine El-Abidine Ben Ali.
7The mother of a victim of the Bardo Museum attack in Tunis.
8Two gunmen were shot dead at the scene and authorities have so far arrested more than 20 people, 10 of whom officials believe were directly involved in the attack.
9Some had recently returned from fighting for Islamist militant groups in Syria, Iraq and Libya.
10Islamic State militants fighting in Iraq and Syria claimed that their supporters carried out the attack although a local al-Qaeda affiliated group known as Okba Ibn Nafaa has also published details and comments on the assault.
11Tunisia has been largely spared the violent aftermath of the Arab Spring uprisings of 2011, with secular and Islamist parties overcoming their divisions to compromise, approve a new constitution and hold free elections.
12Hardline Islamist groups also emerged after the revolt against Ben Ali swept away his one-party rule.
13Since then security forces have been caught up in a growing battle with extremists, some of whom are returning from training and fighting overseas.
14Authorities say the two gunmen killed in the Bardo attacked had trained in jihadi camps in Libya.
15More than 3000 Tunisians left to fight in Syria and Iraq, and hundreds have returned.
