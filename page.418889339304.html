Chiefs' Berry cleared to return after lymphoma treatments (Yahoo Sports)

1ST. JOSEPH, Mo.
2(AP) -- Chiefs safety Eric Berry is returning to the practice field, less than eight months after a stunning cancer diagnosis threatened to derail his Pro Bowl career.
3Berry was cleared by the Kansas City medical staff and his own doctors after a battery of tests Tuesday.
4He will join quarterbacks, rookies and select injured players Wednesday morning for the first practice of training camp on the campus of Missouri Western State University.
5Berry was diagnosed with Hodgkin's disease last December, shortly after complaining of pain in his chest following a game against Oakland.
6He was treated at Emory University in Atlanta almost immediately, and went through the final round of treatment in June.
7''He's kept himself in good shape, believe it or not,'' Chiefs coach Andy Reid said earlier Tuesday, before Berry was cleared to return.
8Hodgkin's disease, also called Hodgkin's lymphoma, is a cancer of the immune system that makes up about 8 percent of all diagnosed lymphomas.
9According to the National Cancer Institute, more than 9,000 people will be diagnosed with it in the United States this year.
10The survival rate in 1960 was about 40 percent, but advancements in medicine combined with earlier detections have increased the survival rate to more than 80 percent.
11According to Dr. Christopher Flowers, who directs the lymphoma program at Emory's Winship Cancer Institute, Berry was scheduled to undergo ''standard chemotherapy approaches.'' Beyond that, Berry and the team have been reluctant to reveal details of his treatment.
12Berry is scheduled to speak to reporters after Wednesday's practice.
13While questions remain about Berry's fitness and whether he will be ready for the season opener in September, it would not be unprecedented for an athlete to return at a high level.
14Hall of Fame hockey player Mario Lemieux was diagnosed with the same disease in 1993, went through treatment and ultimately finished his career with the Pittsburgh Penguins.
15Cubs first baseman Anthony Rizzo was diagnosed with Hodgkin's lymphoma in 2008 and was successfully treated with chemotherapy, and was later voted to his first All-Star game.
16While football became an afterthought as Berry battled cancer, his absence from the field was still noteworthy for a team that just missed reaching the playoffs last season.
17The Chiefs' defense was among the league's best against the pass, but it struggled mightily to defend the run.
18The 26-year-old Berry happens to be one of the best run-stopping safeties in the game, a hard hitter who fearlessly crashes the line of scrimmage.
19The fifth overall pick in the 2010 draft, Berry has played his entire career in Kansas City, becoming one of the most popular players on the team.
20He missed all of the 2011 season with a torn knee ligament, but returned to start all 16 games the following year.
21He has started 53 games in all, piling up 323 tackles with 5 1/2 sacks and eight interceptions.
22His return, regardless of how much he'll be able to participate, will certainly boost some spirits in Kansas City.
23Earlier in the day, Reid announced that Pro Bowl defensive tackle Dontari Poe had surgery on his back last week and would miss all of training camp.
24Poe was diagnosed with a herniated disc.
25He had surgery on July 15.
26''He stands a reasonable chance (of returning) in the early part of the season.
27Whether that's the beginning or somewhere early in the season for his return, you just have to see and see how things recover, how he recovers there,'' Reid said.
