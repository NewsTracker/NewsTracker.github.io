Software developers try on Apple watch, see more apps coming

1SAN FRANCISCO, April 24 (Reuters) - Software developers who tried on an Apple Watch for the first time on Friday predicted a rush of new apps over the next few months, particularly in areas including health and messaging.
2Developers, who had been limited to using software simulators of the watch, discovered new possibilities.
3Ross Cohen, chief operating officer of BeenVerified.com, which makes a caller ID app for the watch, said he only realized on Friday that the watch goes on and off automatically as you raise or lower your wrist.
4The simulator did not have a microphone, leaving some developers to guess how well it could hear.
5Having the watch on hand will speed development, said Danielle Keita-Taguchi, marketing analyst at Y Media Labs, which has designed apps for companies like American Express, EMC and eBay.
6"A lot of Fortune 500 companies really want to leverage this new technology quickly," she said.
7"Health, transportation and social media will be the three main industries that will utilize the Apple Watch."
8Tracking firm App Annie counted 3,061 total apps supporting Apple Watch on Friday.
9Some 10 percent were games.
10Productivity apps were 8 percent and lifestyle and health/fitness each accounted for 7 percent.
11Notable absences included social network Facebook, message service Snapchat and any apps from Google Inc. A Google spokesperson said "only time will tell" if it would make any apps for Apple's device.
12Snapchat could not be reached for comment.
13Earlier this week Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg told analysts who asked about the watch, "We're going to watch closely and build what our community wants us to."
14So far developers see the watch's main benefits as saving time or the labor of frequently taking out a phone.
15"I've got my phone with me already, why do I also need the watch?" asked Matt McIlwain, managing director of Madrona Venture Group, which backs a host of software companies.
16"What is the extension benefit to having it on my wrist versus in my pocket?"
17McIlwain's firm has invested in Redfin, the online real estate service, which has one answer.
18Its Apple Watch app that let users find nearby homes for sale and dictate notes on homes visited.
