Great Barrier Reef region braces for Cyclone Nathan

1Tropical Cyclone Nathan first threatened the coast last week.
2The category three storm has since backtracked on its route and headed towards the area of Cooktown - some 260 kilometers (160 miles) from popular tourist destination Cairns.
3The Bureau of Meteorology said Nathan was expected to make landfall as a category four cyclone on Friday morning, with wind gusts of up to 230 kilometers per hour.
4It was not due to coincide with the high tide however.
5"Not all residents will receive category four- or category three-strength winds, but if you are in the warning zone it's important to continue or commence your preparations now if you have not already started," said Rob Webb, the bureau's Queensland director.
6Emergency officials said on Thursday that hundreds of residents on the coast, as well as workers at a silica mine and the Lizard Island resort on the Great Barrier Reef, had been evacuated.
7Cyclone Nathan comes just a month after twin storms crashed into northern Australia.
8Cyclone Lam hit a sparsely populated area of the Northern Territory.
9Cyclone Marcia, however, slammed into the southern part of the same coast expected to be hit by Nathan on Friday.
10The category five cyclone caused billions of dollars in damage and left thousands homeless or without electricity.
11Last Friday, the nearby archipelago of Vanuatu was also left devastated by super cyclone Pam.
12Villagers, particularly on the outer islands, have been left in desperate need of food and water, with many also lacking access to any means of communication.
13Australia's worst cyclone in history was recorded on December 25, 1974 when hurricane Tracy hit Darwin, killing 65 people and flattening over 90 percent of the city's homes.
14Relief workers have been working to deliver aid to the cyclone-stricken islands of Vanuatu.
15Villagers, particularly on the outer islands, have been in desperate need of food and water since Saturday.
16Officials in Vanuatu are struggling to assess the scale of devastation after Cyclone Pam slammed into the remote archipelago over the weekend.
17Aid agencies warn it could take days to distribute urgently needed supplies.
18Twin storms have crashed into north Australia, with eastern Queensland state set to experience the worst.
19No damages were reported yet, but officials have warned of a calamity.
20Australia's iconic reef is under threat from pollution and climate change.
21Jon Day, recently resigned as the reef's Director of Heritage Conservation.
22He told DW that plans to dump spoil will put the reef at risk.
23Aid workers have been confronted with scenes of destruction as they reach remote areas of storm-ravaged Vanuatu.
