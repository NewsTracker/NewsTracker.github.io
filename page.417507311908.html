Greek PM says agreement is 'in sight'

1Greek Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras has said agreement on his country's debt crisis is in sight after late-night talks in Brussels.
2He said Greece would make a payment due to the IMF tomorrow, but the government still rejects benefit cuts and tax rises its creditors want before fresh loans are released to avert a bankruptcy.
3Commission President Jean-Claude Juncker hosted Mr Tsipras for a late dinner at the EU executive's headquarters along with the chair of eurozone finance ministers Jeroen Dijsselbloem.
4Creditors have signalled a will to compromise, notably by lowering how much surplus they want a new budget to generate to service Athens debts.
5But as Mr Tsipras's party battles to retain support from voters who elected in January to end years of austerity, its spokesman in parliament renewed tough rhetoric at home.
6Nikos Filis told a Greek broadcaster the government would not sign any "ultimatum" and would rather hold a new election if forced into a corner.
7Mr Tsipras is balancing a hardline faction in his party that is keen to spend Greece back towards economic growth with impatient lenders in the rest of the eurozone and the wish of a large majority of Greeks not to be forced to give up the EU currency.
8Hours after Mr Filis warned that Greece might not repay the 300m in International Monetary Fund loans that fall due tomorrow if a deal was not at hand, Mr Tsipras gave assurances in Brussels that Athens would continue to honour its commitments.
9"Don't worry about it," he told reporters.
10"We have already paid 7.5bn, so we will continue."
11Of the negotiations, he reprised the positive tone he has taken since he secured a four-month extension of a bailout package in February: "I believe an agreement is in sight," he said.
12"But we need to conclude the discussions with a realistic point of view," he added, making clear his reservations.
13Referring to what negotiators say have been reductions in the targets creditors are setting for Greece's primary budget surplus - what it has left from other expenses to fund its debts - Mr Tsipras said: "We are very close to an agreement on the primary surplus. That means all sides agree to go further without tough austerity measures of the past."
14But he also again ruled out scrapping a particular benefit for low-income pensioners or a value-added tax change that he said would raise the tax on electricity by ten percentage points.
15This week has seen the creditors sketch out a possible compromise and the government draft its own proposals.
16"At the end of the day," Mr Tsipras said, "I think the proposals on the table will be the proposals of the Greek government."
17Mr Juncker has been active in trying to broker a deal and has ruled out Greece exiting the euro, an outcome many EU officials fear would undermine the long-term stability of the project.
18Mr Juncker's office issued only this short statement: "It was a good, constructive meeting. Progress was made in understanding each other's positions on the basis of various proposals. It was agreed that they will meet again. Intense work will continue."
