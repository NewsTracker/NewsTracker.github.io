US Senate veterans committee plans hearing in Aurora to scrutinize over-budget VA hospital

1DENVER - The U.S. Senate Veterans Affairs Committee will hold a hearing in Colorado next week to scrutinize the bungled VA hospital project and visit the construction site.
2The hearing will be held April 24 in suburban Aurora, where the hospital is being built, the committee announced Thursday.
3The House Veterans Affairs Committee grilled the Department of Veterans Affairs at its own hearing in Washington on Wednesday.
4The hospital is expected to cost $1.73 billion, about $1 billion more than budgeted.
5The VA has blamed problems in the planning process, extravagant design elements and other factors.
6An internal investigation is underway.
7The department wants to complete the facility using money that was allocated to improve veterans' access to medical care nationwide, but some members of Congress are refusing to go along.
8Sen. Jerry Moran, R-Kansas, a member of the Senate veterans committee, said Thursday he opposes that idea.
9Sen. Cory Gardner and Rep.
10Mike Coffman, both Colorado Republicans, are sponsoring legislation to divert the VA's multimillion-dollar bonus budget to the Denver hospital until it's finished.
11Gardner and Sen. Michael Bennet, D-Colorado, requested the Senate Veterans Affairs Committee hearing.
12Neither is on the veterans committee, but both of them plan to attend.
13Bennet and Gardner said the hearing will help make the case for funding the hospital.
14Both have argued that the VA must change the way it handles major construction projects.
