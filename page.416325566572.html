CAS hears Serbia, Albania appeal after Euro 2016 drone match

1The Court of Arbitration for Sport has heard appeals by the Serbian and Albanian football federations challenging UEFA sanctions after their European Championship qualifier was abandoned.
2A chaotic match in Belgrade last October was stopped before halftime then called off when a drone carrying an Albanian nationalist banner flew into the stadium.
3Players clashed on the pitch and the Albania team refused to play after being attacked by Serbian fans.
4UEFA ordered Albania to forfeit the match, and awarded Serbia a 3-0 default win.
5However, Serbia was deducted all three points for the win and ordered to host two Euro 2016 qualifiers in an empty stadium.
6UEFA also fined both federations 100,000 euros ($108,000).
7CAS said in a statement that the two federations' appeals were heard Thursday and Friday.
8The court panel aims to give a verdict before Serbia plays at Denmark on June 13 in the next round of qualifying match.
9The Balkan rivals' return match is scheduled in Albania on Oct. 8.
10Tensions between the countries were fueled by Kosovo, a province dominated by ethnic Albanians, declaring independence from Serbia in 2008.
11Serbia already served one match of its empty-stadium sanction.
12It lost to Denmark 3-1 in November and next hosts Armenia on Sept.
13At the midway point in the five-team group, Portugal and Denmark are in the two automatic qualifying places for Euro 2016 in France.
14Albania is in third place, level on points with Denmark, which guarantees at least a spot in the playoffs.
15The Albanians have seven points, six ahead of fourth-place Serbia.
