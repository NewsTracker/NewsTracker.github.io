Iran to appoint first female ambassador since Islamic Revolution: reports

1DUBAI - Iran will appoint its first female ambassador abroad since the Islamic Revolution in 1979, the semi-official Fars and Mehr news agencies said late on Monday, citing unspecified reports.
2The post would go to Foreign Ministry spokeswoman Marzieh Afkham, one of Iran's most high-profile female public figures, although it was not clear where she would be sent, the agencies said.
3This would make Afkham the second female ambassador in the history of Iran and the first since the 1979 Islamic Revolution.
4The only other female ambassador was Mehrangiz Dolatshahi, who served in Denmark in the 1970s.
5Human rights groups frequently criticize Iran's record on women's rights, including a high rate of domestic violence and girls forced into marriages at ages as young as 10.
6President Hasan Rowhani promised to improve women's rights as part of his election campaign in 2013, but progress on domestic reform has been slow.
7His predecessor Mahmoud Ahmadinejad appointed the Islamic Republic's first female minister in 2009.
