Donald Trump pinata all the rage in Mexico

1US Republican presidential candidate, real estate mogul and TV personality Donald Trump.
2Mexico City: Few presidents of Mexico have managed to unite the country in the way Donald Trump did after he launched his US presidential bid with a string of broadsides against America's southern neighbour.
3"Imbecile", "racist", "absurd" and "ignorant" were some of the less vulgar words used by prominent Mexicans about Mr Trump after the flamboyant real estate magnate described migrants from Mexico to the United States as drug smugglers and rapists.
4Mr Trump's provocative comments, including a pledge to build a "great wall" on the US southern border paid for by Mexico, were the latest in a series of swipes against a country where he has become more famous for controversies than boardroom success.
5A pinata depicting US Republican presidential candidate Donald Trump hangs outside a workshop in Reynosa, Mexico.
6Mexicans rich and poor, cabinet ministers and staunch critics of the government alike trained their fire on Mr Trump.
7On Thursday, the outrage prompted US Spanish-language television network Univision to drop the Miss USA pageant that the Republican hopeful co-owns.
8"If you look up imbecile in the dictionary, you'll see it's a synonym of Trump," Joaquin Lopez-Doriga, news anchor of key Univision shareholder Televisa and one of Mexico's most influential journalists, told his viewers on June 17, a day after Trump announced his White House bid in New York.
9On Thursday, Mr Trump said he has great respect for Mexico and loves Mexicans.
10He defended his more divisive remarks on the grounds that he was worried about border security, jobs in the United States and trade arrangements.
11One newspaper cartoon mocked Mr Trump by twisting his famous hair into the shape of a Nazi swastika, while Fher Olvera, singer of popular Mexican rock band Mana, likened him to the leader of the Third Reich during a concert last week.
12"I haven't heard a speech so violent and so filled with hate since Hitler," Olvera told a crowd in Los Angeles.
13Mr Trump could not immediately be reached for comment.
14Mr Trump said last week on Twitter: "I like Mexico and love the spirit of the Mexican people."
15That was not enough to placate former Mexican president Felipe Calderon, who fired back with one word: "Hypocrite."
16Not everyone has attacked Mr Trump.
17Ximena Navarrete, Mexico's 2010 winner of the Trump-backed Miss Universe pageant, stood up for the 69-year-old, saying he had been "really kind" to her.
18The son of a Scottish immigrant mother, Trump had raised Mexican hackles in February with a series of tweets following filmmaker Alejandro Gonzalez Inarritu's best director triumph at the Oscars, the second Mexican to win in a row.
19"Oscars were a great night for Mexico & why not - they are ripping off the US more than almost any other nation," Mr Trump tweeted.
20He then complained about Mexico's patchy justice system, before adding "Don't do business with Mexico!"
21Business has proved a test for Mr Trump in the country.
22In 2009, Mr Trump was sued by dozens of prospective investors over a failed luxury resort that he had lent his name to, on the Baja California peninsula, according to media reports.
23The suit was settled four years later, the reports said.
24In 2007, Mexican media reported that Mr Trump planned to invest in a resort on the Caribbean island of Cozumel, but it never happened, a spokeswoman for the state of Quintana Roo said.
25Mr Trump's most recent media foray has provided at least one Mexican with his own business opportunity: pinatas cast in the American's image.
26Sporting a flange of blonde hair and a big mouth, the Trump pinatas - containers traditionally filled with sweets that are battered open at parties - were proving a hit, said their maker, Dalton Ramirez.
27"This pinata especially is the one everyone wants to break," he said in the northern border city of Reynosa.
