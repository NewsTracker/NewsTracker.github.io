2Is there anything Apple Apple can't do?
3First it revolutionized the personal computing business.
4Then, with the launch of the iPod in 2001, it forced the music industry to change its tune.
5Against initial market reservations, the company succeeded at making Star Trek-like tablets hip when it released the iPad in 2010, and in the last quarter, a record 75 million units of its now-ubiquitous iPhone were sold around the globe.
6The smartphone's operating system, iOS, currently controls a jaw-dropping 89-percent share of all systems worldwide, pushing the second-place OS, Google Google's Android, down to 11 percent from 30 percent just a year ago.
7As you might already know, the company that Steve Jobs built is history's largest by net capitalization.
8In its last quarterly report, Apple posted a record $75 billion in revenue and is now sitting pretty on a mind-boggling $180 billion in cash.
9On Friday, it was announced that Apple will be joining the Dow Jones Industrial Average, replacing AT&T AT&T.
10Many analysts believe the company will reach a jaw-dropping $1 trillion in market cap.
11So what's Apple's next trick?
12How about moving the world's gold market?
13This April, Apple will be venturing into the latest wearable gadget market, the smartwatch, joining competitors such as Samsung, Garmin Garmin and Sony.
14All of the models in Apple's stable of watches look sleek and beautifully designed-just what you'd expect from Apple-and will no doubt be capable of performing all sorts of high-tech functions such as receiving text messages, monitoring the wearer's vitals and, of course, telling time.
15But the real story here is that the company's high-end luxury model, referred to simply as the Apple Watch Edition, will come encased in 18-karat gold.
16What should make this news even more exciting to gold investors is that the company expects to produce 1 million units of this particular model per month in the second quarter of 2015 alone, according to the Wall Street Journal.
