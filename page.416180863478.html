Attacks on troops in Egypt's Sinai kill at least 13

1The explosion comes hours after a roadside bomb killed six soldiers traveling south of el-Arish in an armored vehicle.
2The region has been hit by an Islamic insurgency by a group that recently pledged allegiance to the Islamic State.
3EL-ARISH, Egypt (AP) - At least 13 people, mostly Egyptian policemen, were killed Sunday in separate operations when militants attacked a police station in the provincial capital of Egypt's northern Sinai, and detonated a roadside bomb against a passing armored vehicle, officials said.
4Northern Sinai has witnessed a series of complex and successful attacks targeting Egyptian security forces, many of which have been claimed by a local affiliate of the Islamic State group.
5Twitter accounts affiliated with the group claimed responsibility for the Sunday attacks.
6In the largest of the day's attacks, a suspected car bomber detonated his vehicle at the entrance of a large police station in el-Arish, the capital of North Sinai province, killing at least six, including five policemen, and injuring several civilians, the Interior Ministry said.
7By sundown, Health Ministry Spokesman Hossam Abdel-Ghaffar in Cairo said at least seven bodies were pulled from the rubble, while 40 people were injured, including some in critical condition.
8The blast left a deep crater in the residential area, and damaged several homes nearby.
9Officials said rescue crews were still searching through the rubble for survivors and the death toll was expected to rise.
10The explosion was the second attack Sunday on troops in Sinai.
11Earlier in the day, six soldiers, including an officer, were killed when a roadside bomb struck their armored vehicle traveling south of el-Arish.
12In a third smaller attack, militants clashed with soldiers at a mobile checkpoint in Rafah, south of el-Arish, wounding one police officer and two soldiers.
13Sunday is Eastern Orthodox Easter in Egypt, and police have been on high alert against attacks.
14The attacks took place as Egypt's defense minister carried out a limited military reshuffle, replacing the commander of the army division responsible for securing northern Sinai.
15Maj.
16Gen. Mohammed el-Shahat, who only commanded Egypt's second field army for about a year, was promoted to head of military intelligence; el-Shahat's deputy, Maj.
17Gen. Nasser el-Assi, will replace him.
18In a statement posted on its official Facebook page, the Interior Ministry -which oversees the police - said a suicide bomber in a small truck drove through a checkpoint outside the police station, causing guards to open fire before the vehicle exploded.
19The ministry said five policemen and a civilian were killed in the explosion.
20An official said the dead include two ranking police officers and two conscripts.
21The officials all spoke on condition of anonymity because they were not authorized to brief reporters.
22Residents say the explosion outside one of four main police stations in el-Arish shook the surrounding district.
23One woman said her house walls cracked.
24Cars outside the station were on fire.
25Abu Mohammed, a resident who lives near the police station, said body parts littered the area after the huge explosion, which also split an armored car into two.
26He said the suicide bomber kept on driving past sand mounds and a cement blast wall securing the station - all despite coming under heavy fire from the guards.
27"Those at the other end of town heard the explosion. It was humongous. It terrified us," Abu Mohammed said, using an alias for fear of reprisals from the militants.
28"I am not afraid of them. I only fear for those around me. We will not leave our town until we liberate it from those terrorists."
29The new army commander in the area, el-Assi, inherits the simmering Sinai-based Islamic insurgency that continues to target army soldiers and police officers despite an intensive military campaign.
30The attacks surged following the 2013 military ouster of Islamist President Mohammed Morsi.
31The largest Sinai-based militant group, Ansar Beit al-Maqdis, pledged allegiance to the Islamic State group last year and now refers to itself as the group's Sinai Province.
32Earlier this month, the IS affiliate in Sinai killed at least 16 soldiers and three civilians and kidnapped a conscript.
33On Friday they posted a video showing the kidnapped soldier pleading with Egyptians not to join the army before being shot to death.
34The reshuffle in military leadership also included the commander of the Egyptian navy at a time when the navy could begin playing an increasingly prominent role, because of the Yemeni crisis.
35Egyptian warships are already deployed off the coast of Yemen to secure the strategically vital Bab el-Mandab strait - the gateway to the Suez Canal.
36Egypt is currently a main member of the Saudi-led military coalition launching airstrikes against Shiite rebels who have conquered the Yemeni capital, Sanaa, and forced out the Western-backed president.
37Egyptian military leaders have repeatedly stated their willingness to commit ground troops, if necessary, to the operation.
