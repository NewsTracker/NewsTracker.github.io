Nigerian, Cameroon troops oust Boko Haram from town, village

1"FLASH: Nigerian troops have this afternoon routed terrorists from Bama ... Mopping up operation is ongoing," the Nigerian Defense Headquarters announced on Twitter referring to a town held by the insurgents for more than six months.
2An earlier tweet Monday said they had "completed clearing terrorists out of Goniri" in neighboring Yobe state.
3By last week, the regional offensive that began at the end of January had liberated 36 towns, Mike Omeri, the Nigerian government spokesman on the insurgency, said Wednesday.
4Cameroon Col.
5Jacob Kodji said forces from his country attacked Boko Haram militants from Borno state's Ndaba village on Thursday and Friday, killing several of the extremists and destroying some of their vehicles and ammunition.
6The Cameroonians acted after Ndaba residents reported that Boko Haram militants were massing in the hills around the village, 20 kilometers (12 miles) from the border with Cameroon, Kodji said.
7The militants were believed to have been fleeing from Nigerian air raids on Bama, he said.
8Boko Haram seized Bama in September and in December published a video showing gunmen mowing down civilians lying face down in what looks like a school dorm there.
9A leader says they are being killed because they are "infidels" or non-believers.
10There are so many corpses in the video that the gunmen have difficulty stepping to reach bodies still twitching with life.
11Boko Haram this month joined the Islamic State group in the Middle East, which last week urged fighters to go to its "West Africa Province."
12This has increased fears about the internationalization of a conflict that for nearly six years has been largely limited to northeast Nigeria.
13Some 10,000 people were killed in Nigeria' Islamic uprising last year, according to the Washington-based Council on Foreign Relations.
14Boko Haram was little known outside the region until its abduction last April of more than 200 schoolgirls who remain missing.
15Associated Press writer Edwin Kindzeka Moki contributed to this report from Yaounde, Cameroon.
