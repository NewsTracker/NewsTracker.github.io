Obama pledges help for Baltimore riots after Gray funeral

1WASHINGTON (AP) - President Barack Obama is pledging the federal government's help to respond to riots that have broken out in Baltimore following the funeral of Freddie Gray.
2Obama spoke on Monday with Baltimore Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake.
3The White House says the mayor updated Obama on the situation and that Obama told her his administration would provide assistance as needed.
4Obama's senior adviser also spoke Monday with Maryland Gov. Larry Hogan.
5The White House says Obama also discussed the rioting with Attorney General Loretta Lynch, who was sworn in hours earlier.
6Obama and Lynch met in the Oval Office while violent scenes of rioting in Baltimore played out on television.
7Gray died April 19 after suffering a mysterious spinal injury while in police custody.
8The Justice Department and local authorities are investigating.
