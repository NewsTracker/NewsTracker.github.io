FIFA issues warning to Wambach after officiating remarks

1FILE - In hits June 16, 2015, file photo, United States' Abby Wambach, left,, celebrates her goal against Nigeria during the first half at the FIFA Women's World Cup soccer tournament in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada.
2Despite the lack of firepower, the U.S. has advanced to the quarterfinals.
3The second-ranked Americans play China on Friday in Ottawa, in the first World Cup meeting between the two teams since the U.S. defeated the Chinese on penalty kicks for the title in 1999.
4FIFA has issued a warning to U.S. forward Abby Wambach for comments she made about the officiating in a match against Colombia at the Women's World Cup.
5During the match Monday in the knockout round, U.S. midfielders Megan Rapinoe and Lauren Holiday were issued yellow cards.
6Because both had yellows from previous matches at the tournament, both will have to sit out Friday night's quarterfinal match against China.
7Wambach was asked whether the yellow cards were deserved.
8"I don't know if they were yellows," she said.
9"It seemed like she (the referee) was purposefully giving those yellows to maybe players that she knew were sitting on yellows. I don't know if that was just a psychological thing, who knows. Who knows?"
10The comments concerning French referee Stephanie Frappart might have merited discipline by FIFA, soccer's international governing body.
11Canadian striker Christine Sinclair made comments about the officiating following her team's loss to the United States at the London Olympics, and she was later suspended for four matches for unsportsmanlike behavior.
12Wambach apologized the next day, calling it wrong to suggest what the official was thinking.
13"I'm not in the referee's head, and that's something I definitely take ownership of and apologize for," she said.
14FIFA said in a statement Thursday night that the apology factored into the decision.
15"The chairman of the FIFA Disciplinary Committee took into consideration all the circumstances of the case, including the fact that Abby Wambach took full ownership and apologized for her comments in a timely manner," the statement said.
16FIFA said it informed Wambach of the decision earlier in the day.
17The warning cited article 57 of FIFA's disciplinary code, which concerns sportsmanlike conduct.
18U.S. defender Meghan Klingenberg said she had no issue with what Wambach initially said.
19"Quite frankly, that's Abby just having our teammates' backs," she said.
20"She wants them to play. I see no problem with that. And we're moving forward."
