Denny Hamlin pulls out of Bristol race with neck injury (Yahoo Sports)

1(AP) -- Denny Hamlin pulled out of Sunday's race at Bristol Motor Speedway because of a neck strain that worsened during a rain delay of nearly four hours.
2Hamlin said he felt something strain in his neck 12 laps into the race.
3The race was stopped for rain on Lap 22, and Hamlin went to his motorhome to see if he could get his neck to feel better.
4Instead, it stiffened and he was hardly able to turn his head when NASCAR summoned drivers back to their cars.
5''I can't move my head or neck like I need to, and I'm not doing this team any justice if I go out there like this,'' Hamlin told The Associated Press from the garage.
6''I have no idea what happened.
7I think it's just a pull, something where you jerk your head around and you feel a pain.
8Since Hamlin won last month at Martinsville Speedway, he's already earned a spot in the Chase for the Sprint Cup championship and didn't see the point in getting back into his No.
911 if he wasn't going to be competitive.
10Joe Gibbs Racing turned to 18-year-old Erik Jones, one of its development drivers, to replace Hamlin.
11''We've got a win, so go let this kid get some laps,'' Jones said.
12Jones has no Sprint Cup experience, but is coming off his first career Xfinity Series victory last week at Texas.
13When the race resumed after a rain delay of 3 hours, 58 minutes, Jones got a few dozen laps before a competition caution gave him a chance to catch his breath.
14He radioed that the steering wheel was too close, but the team did not have an opportunity to make any adjustments.
15Prior to the rain delay, Brad Keselowski wrecked teammate Joey Logano and both Team Penske cars suffered significant damage.
16Persistent rain delayed the start more than an hour, and NASCAR rushed to get the event underway during a brief window.
17Although it was damp and cold when the race began, there weren't any issues until the 20th lap, when Keselowski spun trying to get around a lapped car.
18He spun directly into Logano, and both cars were heavily damaged.
19Logano, who led all 300 laps Saturday to win the Xfinity Series race, thought Keselowski's issue was a loose car and not rain.
20''He just got loose.
21His car was loose,'' Logano said.
22Keselowski was dejected as he looked at both damaged cars.
23''I hate racing in the rain, but I understand the position that NASCAR is in.
24They want to get the race going and this is one of those days where it's going to just keep raining off and on and we're trying to get as many laps in at a time as we can to give the fans the best race possible,'' Keselowski said.
25The incident happened just as it began to rain again, and NASCAR stopped the race to wait out the shower.
26That delay turned the event into a night race under Bristol's lights, and it went on without Hamlin and Kurt Busch crew chief Tony Gibson, who was battling a kidney stone.
27''Watching race in my motorcoach is agonizing!'' Gibson tweeted.
28The weather ruined what Bristol officials had hoped would be a successful weekend for their spring race.
29Once one of the toughest tickets to get in all of sports, Bristol boasted a streak of 55 consecutive sellouts.
30But attendance has been on a five-year decline for Bristol's spring race in part because of an ever-changing race date that moved the event earlier and earlier into March.
31Last year's sparsely-attended race was a water-logged disaster as two rain delays totaled more than five hours.
32One lap before the finish, the caution lights were inadvertently triggered, and before NASCAR could figure out what had happened or how to finish the race under green, the sky opened again and the event was called with Carl Edwards getting the win.
33Bristol Motor Speedway seats 160,000 around its bullring, and the August night race is still one of the top draws in NASCAR.
34Convinced the issues for the spring race were weather-related, Speedway Motorsports Inc. owner Bruton Smith had been pushing for the track to be moved back to an April race date.
35A scheduling adjustment this season got Bristol back into April - a date that Bristol general manager Jerry Caldwell called ''a game changer'' - and ticket sales had been brisk until this week when the forecast called for a 100 percent chance of rain on Sunday.
