Google engineer killed in Everest avalanche

1This photo provided by Azim Afif shows the scene after an avalanche triggered by a massive earthquake swept across Everest Base Camp, Nepal on Saturday, April 25, 2015.
2Afif and his team of four others from the Universiti Teknologi Malaysia (UTM) all survived the avalanche.
3An American was killed in the avalanche on Mount Everest that resulted from the massive Nepal earthquake on Saturday, a tour company said.
4Dan Fredinburg, a Google executive based in California, had been scaling Everest for the past three weeks with an expedition team through Jagged Globe, a U.K.-based tour company.
5"It is with the greatest sorrow that we report the death of one of our Everest team members, Daniel Fredinburg. Two other team members have non-life threatening injuries, as a result of the avalanche that struck base camp during the earthquake and its aftershocks," Jagged Globe posted on its website.
6No other team members or sherpas were injured, the company added.
7Fredinburg is the first American confirmed dead from Saturday's 7.8-magnitude quake and the Everest avalanche it triggered.
8It was not known whether other Americans are among the reported 1,100 deaths, which includes at least 10 deaths on Everest.
9Fredinburg made headlines for dating actress Sophia Bush in 2013 and 2014, and was the head of privacy for innovation lab Google[x].
10He described himself as an "Inventor, Adventurer, and Energetic Engineer" on his Google+ page.
11In an eerie coincidence, Fredinburg had been on Everest during another avalanche - and survived.
12Bush tweeted last April after an avalanche killed 16 sherpas, "For all concerned, @danfredinburg & team are safe at Camp 1 on #Everest after the avalanche. Please keep the Sherpa's in your prayers."
13Fredinburg's sister wrote on his Instagram account that he died of a head injury.
14"We appreciate all of the love that has been sent our way thus far and know his soul and his spirit will live on in so many of us. All our love and thanks to those who shared this life with our favorite hilarious strong willed man. He was and is everything to us. Thank you," she wrote.
15Fredinburg had been documenting his Everest journey online.
16His last post, on Friday, was a photo of him taking a cappuccino break on the mountain.
17Tributes poured in online to Fredinburg, a University of California-Irvine graduate who lived in San Francisco.
18"Dan was weird and driven and selfless and rambunctious and thoughtful and always going for it. Devastating," tweeted investor Chris Sacca.
19"One of the great souls and lights to have entered our lives. We love you. Rest in Peace Dan Fredinburg," tweeted Elliott Bisnow, founder of Summit, which brings together innovators and engineers.
