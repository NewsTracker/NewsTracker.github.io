How Tesla almost sold to Google in 2013

1Tesla CEO Elon Musk almost struck a deal to sell his electric luxury car company to Google via Larry Page when it was struggling in early 2013.
2Tesla wasn't able to turn many initial pre-orders into sales, according to a new book due out May 19 by Ashlee Vance titled "Elon Musk: Tesla, SpaceX, and the Quest for a Fantastic Future," excerpted by Bloomberg.
3In order to solve the problem, Musk pulled staff from every department into a meeting to manage the crisis.
4"If we don't deliver these cars, we are f---ed," Musk told the employees, a person at the meeting told Vance.
5"So I don't care what job you were doing. Your new job is delivering cars."
6The staffers were ordered to start picking up the phones and closing deals.
7So many customers had deferred orders by the first week of March 2013 that Musk quietly shut down Tesla's factory.
8Musk reached out to Page to strike a deal, two people familiar with the talks told Vance.
9Musk proposed that Google buy Tesla outright for $6 billion and provide another $5 billion in capital for factory expansions.
10Musk had other demands as well.
11He asked for a guarantee that Google wouldn't break up or shut down his company before it produced a third-generation electric car for the mainstream auto market.
12Musk also wanted to run the new Google-owned Tesla for eight years, or until it began producing such a car.
