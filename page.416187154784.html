Clinton to announce presidential bid

1Hillary Clinton has launched her bid to become the first woman to win the White House, and announced a campaign tour to showcase her support for "everyday Americans".
2"I'm running for president," Clinton, a former secretary of state and US first lady, said in a video on her campaign website that went live on Sunday.
3"Everyday Americans need a champion, and I want to be that champion, so you can do more than just get by. You can get ahead, and stay ahead," she said in the two-minute video.
4"Americans have fought their way back from tough economic times. But the deck is still stacked in favour of those at the top," she said.
5Clinton, 67, becomes the third major figure to announce her US presidential aspirations.
6Senator Marco Rubio is widely expected to announce his campaign on Monday, and ex-Florida governor Jeb Bush is actively considering a run.
7She also said in the video that she will soon be "hitting the road to earn your vote".
8Presumptive campaign chairman John Podesta told donors and other backers in Sunday emails that Clinton's first campaign stops will be in Iowa - the state that votes first in the primary process early next year to decide the parties' nominees - and that "a formal kick-off event" will be held next month.
