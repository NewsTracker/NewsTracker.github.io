The Latest on Nepal: In Ravaged Hamlets, Lives Were Spared

1The official death toll from Saturday's earthquake in Nepal has increased.
2Nepal police late Wednesday reported 5,045 deaths had occurred in Nepal.
3It also said more than 10,100 people had been injured and 7,400 of them remained hospitalized.
4In addition, 19 people were killed on Mount Everest, 61 people were killed in India and China reported 25 deaths in Tibet.
5The Nepal Mountaineering Association has increased to 19 the number of fatalities from the avalanche on Mount Everest.
6It said Wednesday that five of the dead were foreign climbers and 14 were Nepalese Sherpa guides.
7The avalanche triggered by Saturday's 7.8-magnitude quake also injured dozens of people when it swept through base camp, knocking over tents and people.
8The association said 10 Sherpas have been identified.
9The Americans were Daniel Fredinburg and Marisa Eve Girawong, and the other foreigners were Ge Zhenfang from China, Renu Fotedar from Australia and Hiroshi Yamagata from Japan.
10All climbers have now left Everest, ending the climbing season early for the second year.
11Last year, climbs were cancelled after 16 Sherpa guides were killed in an avalanche while hauling gear between camps.
12Hundreds of people attempt to scale the world's tallest mountain each year, and Nepal earns significant income from the permit fees.
13The tiny hamlets clinging to the mountainsides of Gorkha District were ravaged by the earthquake.
14But lives were spared.
15But the death toll in villages like Gumda was far lower than feared.
16The quake on Saturday struck at midday, and many people were working outdoors in the rural region in spring.
17Of Gumda's 1,300 residents, five were killed in the quake and 20 more were injured.
18The official overall toll as of Wednesday evening exceeded 5,100 in Nepal, India and Tibet.
19The United Nations is appealing for $415 million to provide for vital needs in Nepal over the next three months.
20It intends to support government efforts in provide shelter, water and sanitation, emergency health, food, and protection.
21The U.N.'s humanitarian coordinator for Nepal, Jamie McGoldrick, said the response to date was encouraging, but those efforts need to be maintained, especially in remote areas.
22He noted that the coming monsoon season would like add a logistical challenge to relief efforts, adding to the urgency.
23Helicopters have rescued some 210 foreign trekkers and local villagers stranded in the Lantang area north of Nepal's capital.
24The international trekkers were stranded in Langtang, a popular trekking route bordering Tibet, since the earthquake on Saturday that killed more than 5,000 people.
25Government administrator Gautam Rimal said the trekkers and villagers were flown to the nearby town of Dhunche.
26But with landslides cutting off the roads from there to Kathmandu, the trekkers will have to trudge for at least four hours before they can board a bus to the Nepalese capital.
