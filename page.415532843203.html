3SAN FRANCISCO - Facebook users in the U.S. will soon be able to send cash to friends over the giant social network's Messenger app.
4Users will be able to tap a $ icon placed above the keyboard, enter an amount, tap "pay" in the top right corner and enter their debit card number.
5Facebook is not charging a transaction fee.
6The widely anticipated feature was announced Tuesday and will roll out in coming months on Android, iOS and desktop.
7Facebook is the latest Internet company buddying up to the crowded market to instantly send money known as peer-to-peer payments.
8It will compete with services such as Square and Venmo (owned by PayPal) as well as messaging service Snapchat which rolled out a similar pay by text feature called Snapcash in November.
9Facebook watchers have been awaiting the new feature.
10Last summer Facebook hired PayPal President David Marcus to run all of its messaging products.
11Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg also mentioned payments in Messenger during an earnings call.
12Such payment services are already popular overseas.
13WeChat, for example, already allows its users to send money by instant message.
14Messenger has more than 500 million monthly users.
15Last year Facebook spent $22 billion to buy another popular messaging app WhatsApp.
16WhatsApp now has more than 700 million users around the globe.
