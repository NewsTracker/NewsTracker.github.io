Thousands expected at Native American art show in New Mexico

1This undated imaged provided by the Santa Fe Indian Market shows a silver necklace by Navajo jeweler Abraham Begay, who is among the 1,000 artists showing and selling their work at the 94th annual Santa Fe Indian Market in Santa Fe, N.M. The market begins Saturday, Aug. 22, 2015.
2Tens of thousands of people are expected to descend upon downtown Santa Fe, New Mexico, for what organizers say has become the world's largest juried show of indigenous artwork.
3The 94th annual Santa Fe Indian Market begins at sunrise Saturday and runs through Sunday.
4Dallin Maybee, the chief operating officer of the Southwestern Association for Indian Arts, said the market is not just about promoting and selling art but rather "unfolding the history and legacy of Native traditions and cultures."
5And part of that is the evolution of Native art, he said.
6This marks the first time that contemporary art forms that don't fit into the more traditional categories will get their own separate show dubbed "Indian Market Edge."
7About 1,000 artists from across the United States and Canada will be showing and selling their work.
8The market has used a jury process for decades to select the artists, helping to make it one of the most prestigious venues.
9Most of the artists are full-time professionals who have won awards for their work.
10Since the artists span generations and represent many different tribes, there's everything from traditional weavings and pottery to jewelry, carvings, paintings and beadwork.
11Some die-hard collectors have been known to camp out at their favorite artist's booth to get first shot at the artwork when the market opens Saturday morning.
12Hundreds of booths will line streets in the downtown area, and the "Edge" show will be at the Santa Fe Community Convention Center.
13Organizers estimate the market attracts about 150,000 people to Santa Fe, including collectors who are looking to buy direct from the artists.
14Many artists reported record sales last year, fundraising for the Southwestern Association for Indian Arts was also up and the overall economic impact for the city and the state has been pegged at $80 million.
15One of the highlights is the Native Haute Couture Fashion Show on Saturday afternoon.
16Some of Indian Country's hottest designers will be featured and it's usually one of the most photographed events of the market.
17There will also be panel discussions on art and activism and then there's the food and music.
18The market offers no better place to get a full cross-section of Native American culture, Maybee said, because so many tribes are represented.
19The market is located on the Santa Fe Plaza and the surrounding area.
