Baltimore police give findings into Freddie Gray death to prosecutor

1Baltimore police turned over their findings into the death of Freddie Gray to the state prosecutor on Thursday, a day earlier than their self-imposed deadline.
2The results of the investigation were sent to State's Attorney Marilyn Mosby on Thursday morning, Police Commissioner Anthony Batts announced.
3"We have exhausted every lead at this point. But this does not mean the investigation is over," he said.
4The 25-year-old African American died from a severed spine on April 19, a week after he was arrested by Baltimore police and transported by van.
5Police are investigating how Gray, who was cuffed with his hands behind his back and his legs in irons, was injured.
6The police findings into the death, which has led to days of protests and a fevered Monday night of rioting and looting, were scheduled to be completed by Friday, but were completed early.
7The state's attorney has the final say on whether to charge any of the six officers with a state crime.
8The officers have been suspended with pay.
9"By turning these documents, our findings, over to the state's attorney's office as quickly as we can, we are being accountable to them so that we can be accountable to the public," spokesman Capt.
10Eric Kowalczyk said Wednesday.
11Police have already acknowledged that department policy was breached when Gray was placed in the van but not buckled into a seat belt and when officers failed to get him medical care in a timely fashion.
12The federal Justice department is also investigating whether there were any violations of federal civil rights law.
13Police have said that the van stopped three times while carrying Gray to a police precinct.
14At one stop, Gray was taken from the van and placed in irons.
15At the last stop, another inmate was also placed in the van but was separated from Gray by a metal barrier.
16According to the Washington Post, the prisoner sharing the police van told investigators that he could hear Gray "banging against the walls" of the vehicle and believed that he "was intentionally trying to injure himself."
17The Post quoted from what it said was a Baltimore police document that was included in an application for a search warrant, which is sealed by the court.
18The Post said it was given the document under the condition that the prisoner not be named because the person who provided it feared for the inmate's safety.
19The document offers the first official look at what happened in the van.
20Baltimore police have said they do not know whether Gray was injured during the arrest or during his ride in the van.
21Gray was found unconscious in the wagon when it arrived at a police station on April 12, then taken to a hospital, where he died in a coma a week later.
22Jason Downs, one of the attorneys for the Gray family, told the Post that the family had not been told of the prisoner's comments to investigators.
23"We disagree with any implication that Freddie Gray severed his own spinal cord," Downs said.
24"We question the accuracy of the police reports we've seen thus far, including the police report that says Mr. Gray was arrested without force or incident."
258:29 a.m.: This story was updated with police announcement about their investigation into the death of Freddie Gray.
