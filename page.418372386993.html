Judge voids $532M award in iTunes infringement case

1A federal judge has thrown out a $532.9 million award against Apple and ordered a new trial on damages, in a case where a jury found that the iPhone maker's iTunes software infringed three patents owned by a Texas company.
2In a decision on Tuesday, U.S. District Judge Rodney Gilstrap in Tyler, Texas, said jurors who on Feb. 24 awarded the damages to Smartflash because of Apple's willful infringement might have been confused by his instructions on how to properly calculate royalties.
3Apple had argued that the damages were too high because jurors might have improperly considered the entire market value of the products, rather than distinguishing between patented and unpatented features.
4Gilstrap said his jury instructions were legally correct but not applicable to the facts of the case.
5This "may have created a skewed damages horizon for the jury," he said.
6He set a new trial only on the issue of damages for Sept.
714 in Tyler, where Smartflash is based.
8Apple, its law firm and Smartflash's law firm did not immediately respond to requests for comment.
9Smartflash accused Apple in a May 2013 lawsuit of infringing patents for accessing and storing songs, videos and games, enabling the Cupertino, California-based company to sell more of its iPhone, iPad and iPod Touch products.
10The lawsuit said Patrick Racz, a co-inventor of Smartflash's patents, had discussed some of his ideas in 2000 with officials of a European company, including Augustin Farrugia, who later became a senior director at Apple.
11Smartflash originally sought damages of $852 million.
12The trial took place in the federal court for the Eastern District of Texas, a favorite for lawyers representing patent holders because of its reputation for high damages awards.
