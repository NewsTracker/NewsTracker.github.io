WWDC 2015: Apple finally puts women on stage

1Apple relaunches its music operations including a new global radio station called Beats 1 but will it compete with already popular services like Rdio and Spotify?
2The big news at Apple's Worldwide Developers Conference always revolves around new apps and updated operating systems.
3This year's event marked a completely different turning point for Apple: women took the stage during a keynote presentation.
4Jennifer Bailey, vice-president of Apple Pay, appeared before the crowd of software developers and journalists to discuss progress in mobile payments.
5A second woman, Susan Prescott, gave the pitch for Apple's forthcoming News app.
6It marks the first time female executives have made an on-stage appearance during an Apple keynote presentation since 2010, when Zynga's Jen Herman gave a demonstration of the game Farmville.
7Like its competitors, Apple wants to be seen as working against the image of Silicon Valley as a male-dominated industry.
8Over the weekend, Apple CEO Tim Cook acknowledged that the industry needs to do a better job on gender issues.
9Jennifer Bailey, vice-president of Apple Pay, takes the stage at WWDC 2015.
10The company's much-hyped and highly-scrutinised public events have long been noticeable for the absence of prominent women.
11Christy Turlington, a legendary fashion model, was the only woman to appear during the recent Apple Watch presentation.
12If you're keeping track of these presentations as a way to measure progress towards gender equality in Silicon Valley, you could easily make the case that Turlington's cameo should actually count against Apple.
13Few people are complaining about a lack of beautiful women being used to draw attention to gadgets.
14Compare Apple's performance to this year's Google's I/O developers conference, which took place at the end of May. Arguably the biggest news of the day -Google Now on Tap - was presented by Aparna Chennapragada, the company's director of product management.
15Chennapragada was one of three women who spoke during the keynote presentation.
16Ellie Powers, product manager for Google Play, has presented for three years running.
17The last time a Google didn't have a woman on stage during its annual I/O keynote was 2011.
18Microsoft also seemed to make a point of having more women on stage at the keynote for its developer conference, Build, held in late April.
19Three women took the stage for the keynote, walking through technical subjects like SQL databases and Saas applications.
20At the 2014 conference, the only female voice on stage came from Cortana, who sounds like a lady but is really a piece of artificial intelligence technology.
21Apple CEO Tim Cook shows of the Apple Watch and model Christy Turlington Burns in March.
22Developers conferences have never been a paragon of gender diversity.
23A regular tradition at Apple's Worldwide Developers Conference are tweeted photos showing a line for the men's room that extends down the hall next to a completely empty women's bathroom.
24When female developers had to wait to use the facilities at I/O this year, some people actually held it up as a triumph.
25Tech gender diversity visualized in the que for the restroom at WWDC.
26Of course, counting keynote speakers and measuring bathroom lines isn't a perfect way to measure the current levels of gender inequality in the tech industry.
27Google's workforce isn't actually any more diverse than Apple's.
28About 30 per cent of each company's employees are women, according to recent diversity reports.
29(Here are reports for Apple, Google, and Microsoft.)
30Apple actually employs more women in tech positions than Google (20 per cent versus 18 per cent), and women make up a higher percentage of leadership positions (28 per cent to 22 per cent.)
31There are three women on Google's board compared to Apple's two.
32Microsoft lags behind both companies, with only 17 per cent of its tech jobs and 18 per cent of its leadership roles held by women.
33The fact that Silicon Valley's biggest companies are starting to give their female employees a chance to be a part of telling their stories seems like progress.
34But the real achievement will be when it's not news that women are participating in these kind of events.
