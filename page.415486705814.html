Robert Durst, from documentary 'The Jinx', arrested on murder charge

1New York: Robert Durst, the scion of a New York real estate family, has been arraigned in New Orleans after being arrested on first-degree murder charges in a killing 15 years ago in Los Angeles, law enforcement officials said.
2For years, questions have swirled around Mr Durst about the unsolved killing of a close friend in Los Angeles in 2000, about his first wife's disappearance in 1982, and about the shooting and dismemberment of a Texas neighbour in 2001.
3Mr Durst is the subject of an HBO documentary series examining the unsolved killings of the friend, Susan Berman, and the ex-wife, Kathleen Durst.
4The sixth and final episode of the documentary, The Jinx: The Life and Deaths of Robert Durst was scheduled to air in the United States on Sunday night.
5The series is due to air in Australia on Showcase on May 7.
6An undated handout photo of Robert Durst after his arrest.
7Mr Durst's lawyer, Chip Lewis, said that the arrest of his client on Saturday, in the lobby of the JW Marriott Hotel in New Orleans, was on murder charges in the death of Berman.
8The Los Angeles district attorney recently reopened an investigation into Berman's death, and is tying it to the case of Mr Durst's missing wife in New York.
9Mr Durst went on trial in 2001 for the murder of the Texas neighbour, Morris Black.
10A jury found him not guilty, though Mr Durst admitted having dismembered Black's body.
11The case of Berman's death was reopened after the producers of the documentary contacted the Los Angeles district attorney more than a year ago to discuss new evidence they had turned up in the course of working on their series.
12A flier from the investigation of the 1982 disappearance of Kathleen Durst.
13In episode five of the series, Mr Berman's stepson, Sareb Kaufman, was shown giving the producers a 1999 note from Mr Durst to Berman, in which the lettering was similar to an anonymous note sent to the Beverly Hills Police Department alerting them to a "cadaver" at Berman's home.
14Berman was found dead in December 2000 at her home in Benedict Canyon.
15Investigators said that she had been shot execution-style and that there were no signs of a forced entry.
16Mr Durst, in an interview last week after episode five aired in the US, said he felt increasingly bitter toward the producers - Marc Smerling and Andrew Jarecki - and Mr Kaufman.
17Mr Durst said that he did not have the "faintest idea" who killed Berman, nor did he know what happened to his first wife, who has been declared legally dead.
18Speaking in a gravelly monotone, he did say that he felt "complicit" in the disintegration of their marriage and the violent episodes that accompanied it.
19On Sunday morning, in an appearance at the Orleans Parish Criminal District Court, Mr Durst was arraigned on a charge of first-degree murder.
20He appeared in a nearly empty courtroom wearing an orange jumpsuit.
21He was accompanied by two lawyers; four FBI agents were also present.
22Judge Juana Lombard ordered him held without bond and an extradition hearing was scheduled for Monday morning.
23Mr Lewis said earlier on Sunday that Mr Durst would waive extradition.
24"We're waiving extradition so that we can get back to Los Angeles so that we can get back and fight the charges," Mr Lewis said.
25The details of the warrant were not released on Sunday.
26A spokeswoman for the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department said the warrant was issued by the Los Angeles Police Department.
27Officials from that police agency did not return calls or emails seeking comment.
28Joseph Becerra, the New York State Police investigator who reopened Kathleen Durst's case in 2000, said his office and the Westchester County district attorney's office were monitoring the developments in the Berman case.
29"We'll proceed from there," he said, adding, "ours is a separate investigation."
30Mr Durst, 71, was arrested on Saturday by FBI agents and state police officers as he walked into the lobby of the JW Marriott Hotel.
31He was booked into the Orleans Parish jail just before 11pm, according to an online record of his arrest.
32Craig Betbeze, a spokesman for the FBI in New Orleans, confirmed the arrest, but would not comment further.
33John Lewin, the lead prosecutor in Mr Durst's case, declined to comment on Sunday.
34Mr Lewin, who was not involved in the earlier investigation, has made a reputation for getting convictions in cold cases, some of them decades old and whose victims' bodies were never found.
35Mr Durst's brother Douglas, the chairman of the Durst Organization, said on Sunday that the family was "relieved and also grateful to everyone who assisted in the arrest of Robert Durst. We hope he will finally be held accountable for all he has done."
