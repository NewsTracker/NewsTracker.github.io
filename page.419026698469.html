N.M. governor: "Coward" who set off 2 church blasts will be caught

1LAS CRUCES, N.M. -- New Mexico Gov. Susana Martinez is promising that the "coward" who set off explosions outside two Las Cruces churches will be caught.
2The small blasts just 20 minutes and a few miles apart shocked congregants Sunday morning at the churches in southern New Mexico.
3There were no injuries or deaths from the blasts outside Calvary Baptist and Holy Cross Roman Catholic in Las Cruces, Las Cruces police spokesman Danny Trujillo said.
4Each building sustained minor damage.
5Authorities are working to determine who planted the explosives and have not said if more than one person is being sought.
6They are also trying to determine what materials were used and whether the blasts were related.
7"It doesn't appear to be coincidental because of the timing, but you never know," Trujillo told The Associated Press.
8Several agencies including the FBI, the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives and New Mexico State Police are assisting with the investigation.
9"These devices were intended to do harm, I believe, and could have done harm to people," New Mexico State Police Chief Pete Kassetas said, according to CBS affiliate KRQE.
10Martinez told reporters late Sunday that Las Cruces residents should continue to attend houses of worship.
11She said that if the intention was to bring fear to those who worship, it was a failed effort.
12The first explosive device went off at about 8:20 a.m. in a mailbox on a wall near the administrative entrance to Calvary Baptist.
13According to police, several worshippers were inside the church at the time, but services hadn't started.
14According to churchgoers, the blast shook the building and there was debris around the damaged mailbox.
15Las Cruces police Sgt. Robert Gutierrez described seeing "just a lot of paper shreds."
16The next blast came from a trash can outside Holy Cross Catholic at about 8:40 a.m. as Monsignor John Anderson was helping pass Communion.
17"I was right in the middle of saying the words 'take and eat, this is my body,' and there was a pow! I mean, I knew it had to be more than a gunshot," Anderson told the Las Cruces Sun-News newspaper.
18"I didn't know if it was a shotgun blast, I didn't know what. But it was very loud, and I just kept on saying the words."
19Ann Marie Sullivan, a college student attending Mass, said it "sounded like something had fallen off and shattered the glass in the back."
20The trash can was near a glass entryway, which was damaged.
21Police arrived and evacuated the church and blocked off surrounding streets.
22Bomb-sniffing dogs were brought in to search the building.
23Services were canceled for the rest of the day at both churches, which are less than 4 miles apart.
24Trujillo said he didn't know of any recent threats involving either church.
25Other churches in the city have contacted police about what, if anything, they should do.
26Trujillo said police are advising them to look out for anything suspicious, but don't inspect anything themselves.
27"Don't go poking around in trash cans or mailboxes," Trujillo said, according to the Sun-News.
28"If you have any suspicion of anything that's out of the ordinary, please call to police and leave it up to the officers to look it over."
