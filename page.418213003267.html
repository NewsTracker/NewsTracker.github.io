Switzerland: US seeks extradition of 7 FIFA officials

1ZURICH (AP) - The United States has submitted a formal request for Switzerland to extradite seven FIFA officials arrested as part of a corruption probe in Zurich in May, Swiss officials said Thursday.
2Switzerland's Federal Office of Justice said the requests were received July 1, within the 40-day deadline required.
3They are based on a U.S. federal probe into alleged bribery and racketeering worth more than $150 million involving high-ranking officials at soccer's world governing body.
4All seven men, who face around 20 years in prison, have already objected to extradition.
5They will be heard by Zurich police and granted a 14-day period to respond to federal officials about the extradition request, after which the Federal Office of Justice will rule on whether to extradite them.
6That ruling can be appealed to Switzerland's top criminal court and supreme court.
7The seven men, arrested May 27 in early-morning raids at a luxury Zurich hotel, include FIFA vice president Jeffrey Webb of the Cayman Islands and former FIFA vice president Eugenio Figueredo of Uruguay.
8Costa Rican soccer federation president Eduardo Li was arrested two days before he was due to formally join FIFA's executive committee.
9Former Brazilian federation chief Jose Maria Marin led the 2014 World Cup local organizing committee and is a member of the FIFA panel organizing the Rio de Janeiro Olympics tournaments.
10The others are Venezuela FA chief Rafael Esquivel; FIFA staffer Julio Rocha, a development officer from Nicaragua; and Costas Takkas, a Briton who works for CONCACAF President Webb.
11The seven are among 14 indicted by the U.S. Justice Department.
12Four more men have entered guilty pleas in the wide-ranging corruption case.
