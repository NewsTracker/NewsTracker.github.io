Barack Obama mandates a 40 percent cut in government emitted greenhouse gases

1Los Angeles prosecutors are asking a judge to set bail in a murder case against former rap music mogul Marion "Suge" Knight at $25 million, citing his violent history.
2The motion filed by Deputy District Attorney Cynthia Barnes cites 31 incidents in which Knight is accused of threatening others or using violence.
3The motion also claims Knight has been involved in money laundering and extortion schemes targeting up-and-coming rappers in recent years.
4The 49-year-old has pleaded not guilty to murder and attempted murder after striking two men with his truck during a fight in a Compton, California, parking lot in late January.
5The Death Row Records co-founder is scheduled to appear in a Los Angeles courtroom on Friday morning for a hearing regarding his bail.
6A phone message for Knight's attorney, Matthew Fletcher, was not immediately returned.
1Obama's executive order also directs the government to ramp up use of renewable energy sources to 30 percent of the federal government's consumption.
2The White House said U.S. taxpayers could save up to $18 billion in electricity costs by reducing greenhouse gases 40 percent over the next decade, compared to 2008 levels.
3'These are ambitious goals, but we know they're achievable goals,' Obama said at the Energy Department on Thursday, where he toured a rooftop solar panel installation after signing the executive order at the White House.
4Major companies that sell to the federal government like GE, HP, Northrup Grumman and Honeywell will also announce voluntary commitments to cut their own emissions of the heat-trapping gases blamed for global warming, the White House said.
5IBM, for example, said it will cut its energy consumption 35 percent by 2020 and buy at least 20 percent of its power from renewably sources by that year.
6The operator of an estimated 360,000 buildings and 650,000 vehicles, the federal government is the country's single largest energy consumer.
7The Department of Defense alone is responsible for roughly half of that consumption, a senior administration official said.
8All told, the government pollution cuts along with industry contributions will have the effect of keeping 26 million metric tons of greenhouse gases out of the air by 2025, or the equivalent of what about 5.5 million cars would pump out through their tailpipes in an average year, the White House said.
9Brian Deese, a senior adviser to Obama, told reporters that the president's mandate is a 'triple win' for the environment, the economy, and the taxpayer.
10Yet it was unclear exactly how either the government or private companies planned to meet its targets.
11While at the Energy Department, Obama also discussed the new emissions targets at a roundtable with federal suppliers that do more than $1 billion per year in business with the government.
12The U.S. government is responsible for only a small portion of the world's greenhouse gas emissions, but the Obama administration is hoping that taking aggressive steps at home will increase the political pressure on other countries to do the same.
13'Certainly our hope is that we are laying forth template that other countries could also learn from and look at as well,' Deese noted.
14Climate change was reportedly a topic of discussion at the president's Oval Office meeting on Thursday afternoon with Prince Charles, who is next in line for the throne in Britain, and the Duchess of Cornwall Camilla.
15A major global climate treaty, in the works for years, is supposed to be finalized in December at a summit in Paris, but most countries have yet to announce what their national contributions to the pact will be.
16Earlier this month the European Union unveiled its contribution, vowing to cut greenhouse gas emissions at least 40 percent by 2030, compared to 1990.
17The U.S. has yet to announce its contribution to the treaty.
18But in a bid to build momentum last year, Obama set a U.S. goal to cut emissions up to 28 percent by 2025 - compared to 2005 levels - in a joint announcement with China that boosted hopes that an aggressive climate treaty may come to fruition.
19The president hasn't fully explained how he'll meet that goal, but his aides have suggested that unprecedented pollution limits he's imposing on power plants will get the U.S. much of the way there.
20Obama's executive order directs the federal government to cut energy use in federal buildings 2.5 percent every year between 2015 and 2025 and reduce the amount of water used in federal buildings 2 percent every year through 2025.
21It also advises federal agencies to decrease greenhouse gas emissions from government-owned vehicles by 30 percent per mile by 2025, compared to the levels in 2014, and put more hybrid and zero-emission vehicles in their fleets.
22Additionally, it seeks to ensure federal agencies are getting 25 percent of their energy - both heat and electricity - from clean sources by 2025.
