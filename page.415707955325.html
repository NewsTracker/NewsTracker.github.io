Boko Haram kidnapped hundreds in northern Nigeria town -residents

1There was no immediate official confirmation of the figure, but the Islamist group has previously carried out mass kidnappings.
2Boko Haram's abduction last April of nearly 300 schoolgirls in the region stirred international outrage and drew global attention to the group's six-year insurgency.
3"They took 506 young women and children (in Damasak). They killed about 50 of them before leaving," a trader called Souleymane Ali told Reuters in the town.
4"We don't know if they killed others after leaving, but they took the rest with them."
5Troops from Niger and Chad last week found the bodies of at least 70 people in an apparent execution site under a bridge leading out of Damasak, where the streets remain strewn with debris and burnt-out cars after the fighting.
6Ali said his wife and three of his daughters were among those seized.
7"Two of them were supposed to get married this year. (Boko Haram) said 'They are slaves so we're taking them because they belong to us'," he said.
8Mohamed Ousmane, another trader, said the militants took his two wives and three of their children.
9A 40-year-old resident who gave her name as Fana said fighters had rounded up captives in the main mosque before taking them out of town.
10She said she saved her two children by hiding them in her house.
11Boko Haram wants to carve out a caliphate in northern Nigeria.
12A sharp increase in violence forced a delay in planned elections last month in Africa's most populous country.
13Nigerian, Chadian and Niger forces have driven militants out of a string of towns in simultaneous offensives over the past month.
14Nigeria says all but three of the 20 local government areas occupied at the beginning of the year have been freed.
15Nigeria's rearranged election is now due to take place on Saturday.
16Niger troops distributed food on Tuesday to a handful of residents who remained in Damasak.
17A few others returned to check their houses but left for the bush again.
18Ali said he was just hoping life would return to normal.
