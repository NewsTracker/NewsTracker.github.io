7NEW YORK (AP) - Optional leather backs and manual camera controls are two ways LG is seeking to distinguish its new G4 phone from Apple's iPhones and Samsung's Galaxy smartphones.
8LG's mobile chief, Juno Cho, said a wireless trade show in Barcelona, Spain, last month confirmed LG's belief that smartphones have become clones of one another.
9"I was almost shocked," Cho told The Associated Press.
10"Almost all the phones on display and introduced looked very (much the) same - the same metal casing and emphasis on thinness, overall form factors that are very similar."
11The message at Tuesday's announcement of the new G4: We're not like the others.
12Apple and Samsung dominate the smartphone market, with LG Electronics Inc. and other companies vying for third place with market shares of less than 5 percent each.
13LG will make phones with traditional backs, too - using metal or ceramic.
14Prices weren't announced, though LG said leather models will cost more in some markets.
15The G4 is available immediately in LG's home country of South Korea.
16It will debut around the world in the coming weeks and is expected to reach the U.S. in early June.
17Apple has long made design a priority, while Samsung began to embrace that this spring with phones that sport a metal frame and a glass back, rather than the plastic used in previous models.
18In many ways, Samsung's new Galaxy S6 came to resemble Apple's older iPhones.
19(The iPhone now has metal backs.)
20At Tuesday's announcement, LG made a dig at Samsung, without naming it, by pointing out that glass backs can be marred with fingerprints.
21The G4 will have a choice of leather finishes.
22Some of Samsung's older phones had imitation leather backs.
23The G4 uses real leather and has stitching down the middle - for looks, more than anything.
24If leather isn't for you, metal and ceramic backs are options.
25Motorola's Moto X phone offers leather, wood and other non-traditional materials as made-to-order options.
26With LG, they are part of standard models.
27LG is adding features to improve low-light images.
28These are improvements over the average smartphone, though Apple and Samsung have also been incorporating these features - namely, the inclusion of a lens that can open wider to let in more light and reduce blur.
29The G4 also has a plethora of manual controls, rivaling those of full-body cameras known as single-lens reflex, or SLR.
30LG said no other major smartphone released "this year" has that capability.
31LG was careful about the phrasing, as older phones from Nokia - now part of Microsoft - have that level of control.
32The feature was popular among some experienced photographers, though most people are fine with automatic settings.
33The G4 differs in letting people save images in RAW format, which permits a greater degree of editing.
34Most cameras use JPEG, which takes less storage space but introduces distortion in the compression.
35One nice feature is the ability to snap a photo by pressing the power button on the back twice, even if the phone's display is off.
36Samsung's S6 launches the camera with the double press of the home button, but the user still has to then take the shot.
37LG also made a dig at Samsung for this, without naming the company.
38As much as LG tries to be different, the layout of the G4's photo gallery resembles the iPhone's.
39In an interview, Cho said LG "felt that kind of experience is important for the customer."
40Few phones have removable batteries these days, in part to keep phones thin.
41LG sacrificed thinness so the battery can be removed and replaced with a spare.
42The G4 also has a slot for a memory card - something lacking in the iPhone and S6 phones.
43LG keeps the slot - especially important if people start storing all their photos in RAW.
