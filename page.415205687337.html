Mukhtar Ablyazov: French court orders wanted former Kazakh minister to be extradited to Russia or Ukraine

1Mr Ablyazov skipped Britain in 2012 after being sentenced to prison for contempt of court.
2After going on the run for over a year, he was eventually tracked down by private investigators who tailed a friend to a villa in which he was hiding near Cannes in the South of France.
3The investigators tipped off the police and Mr Ablyazov was arrested in a dramatic raid by special forces.
4Since then, with the help of a high-profile campaign by his photogenic wife and daughter, he has been fighting extradition to Russia and Ukraine.
5Mr Ablyazov is accused of having embezzled up to $6bn ( 4bn) from the Kazakhstan bank BTA, where he was chairman and a majority shareholder.
6The former Kazakh government minister denies the claims, saying he is being hounded for political reasons due to his opposition to President Nursultan Nazarbayev.
7His wife Alma claims he will be sent to Kazakhstan and be killed if he is extradited.
8Today's decision by France's Cour de Cassation rejected an appeal by Mr Ablyazov and upheld a ruling by a lower court that approved the extradition.
9France does not have an extradition treaty with Kazakhstan, which wants to put Mr Ablyazov on trial, but it does have such deals with Russia and Ukraine.
10"I am extremely disappointed because I think there is a real problem if Mr Ablyazov is sent back to Russia or Ukraine," Mr Ablyazov's lawyer Claire Waquet said.
11Under French law, the extradition must next be approved by governmental decree.
12Multi-billion-dollar black holes were found in BTA's accounts in 2009 and the bank was nationalised with huge losses for lenders around the world including Britain's RBS, Barclays, Standard Chartered and HSBC.
13BTA's receivers pursued his assets in the London High Court, seizing among others, his Carlton House mansion in London's billionaire's row of The Bishop's Avenue.
