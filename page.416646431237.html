Jury in Aurora, Colo., mass shooting trial shown graphic photos

1Marcus Weaver, left, a victim of the 2012 shooting at an Aurora, Colo., movie theater, is embraced after Weaver testified during the third day in the trial of James Holmes, on Wednesday, Apr. 29, 2015.
2CENTENNIAL, Colo.
3-- The bodies lay where they fell, sprawled on steps or wedged between rows of seats, surrounded by spent ammunition, scattered popcorn and shoes left behind in the panic to escape.
4Prosecutors showed jurors graphic crime scene photos Thursday in the Colorado cinema shooting trial of James Holmes, the first time images of the bloodshed were shown in public since the July 20, 2012, attack.
5One juror briefly turned his head after looking at one of the photos displayed on a video screen.
6Most jurors studied the images intently but showed little emotion.
7Some spectators in the gallery wept.
8A woman broke down in sobs and left the courtroom.
9Twelve people were killed and 70 injured in the attack at the Century 16 cinema in the Denver suburb of Aurora.
10Ten died at the theatre, while the other two were pronounced dead at hospitals.
11Defence attorneys say his mind was so distorted by schizophrenia that he didn't know right from wrong.
12If the jury finds he was insane, he would be committed indefinitely to a state mental hospital.
13Prosecutors have described Holmes as calculating and smart, and they say he believed killing others increased his self-worth.
14They are asking jurors to convict him of murder and sentence him to be executed.
15If Holmes -- who has admitted he was the shooter -- felt any reaction to the photos, it wasn't visible.
16He watched from the defence table, where he is tethered to the floor by a harness and cable under his street clothes.
17Holmes' mother, Arlene, gripped her husband Bob's arm and pulled him close as the images appeared.
18He held her hand and looked straight ahead.
19The photos could not be seen on a video feed made available to news organizations, which are sharing it online.
20It was a grim ending to the first short week of the trial, which won't resume until Monday to accommodate plans that one juror made before the case got underway.
21The photos added still more emotional weight to the prosecution's case.
22Since testimony began Tuesday, jurors have heard victims describe the burning pain of gunshot wounds and the agony of watching loved ones collapse before their eyes.
23Police officers -- some so overwhelmed by grief they had to pause to pull themselves together -- described rushing to hospitals with gasping victims in their patrol cars.
24Holmes pleaded not guilty by reason of insanity to multiple counts of murder and attempted murder.
25Police officers testified Thursday that Holmes seemed keenly interested in the attack's aftermath, peering out the window of a squad car as injured victims were treated nearby.
26He first wore a vacant expression and seemed calm and detached -- but sweaty and smelly -- after police handcuffed him in the parking lot behind the theatre, the officers said.
27But when they placed him in the police car near a back door to the theatre, "he would look around like he was taking it all in," Aurora police officer Jason Oviatt said.
28It was a tumultuous scene, with emergency responders treating the wounded and loading them into police cars to be rushed to hospitals because no ambulances had arrived.
29"He would sort of look around whenever a car went speeding past or when there was something else going on, somebody shouting outside the car," Oviatt said.
30Prosecutors called four law-enforcement officers as witnesses Thursday.
31For the first time since testimony began, the defence cross-examined some of them.
32Questioned by defence lawyer Daniel King, the officers said Holmes had disheveled, reddish-orange hair, that his pupils were extremely dilated and that he appeared disoriented and stared off into space when he was first arrested.
33King's questions mirrored an argument the defence made in opening statements -- that Holmes' behaviour was shaped by his mental illness.
34Arlene Holmes attempted to pass a note to her son's attorneys at the defence table from her seat two rows back in the gallery Thursday, but a deputy intercepted it.
35She then left the courtroom with her husband and defence attorney Tamara Brady.
36It wasn't clear what the note said or why they left.
