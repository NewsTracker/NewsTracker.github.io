Baltimore mayor lifts curfew after days of tension

1May 1, 2015: Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake prepares to speak at a media availability at City Hall in Baltimore.
2BALTIMORE - Baltimore's mayor has lifted a citywide curfew six days after the death of Freddie Gray sparked riots in the city.
3The order for residents to stay home after 10 p.m. had been in place since Tuesday, and officials had planned to keep it in place through Sunday.
4Protests since Monday's riots have been peaceful, and the announcement of charges against six officers involved in Gray's arrest eased tensions.
5Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake said in a statement Sunday morning that her goal was not to maintain the curfew any longer than was necessary.
6Gray died after suffering a severe spinal injury while in police custody.
7The six officers face charges ranging from manslaughter to second-degree murder.
