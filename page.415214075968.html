Effort planned to recover mummified body found buried in snow on Mexico's highest peak

1The expedition would begin before dawn, according to the Puebla state press office.
2Climbers found the body Sunday at an elevation of about 17,388 feet (5,300 meters) on the north face of the Pico de Orizaba volcano.
3Investigators requested the help of climbers to recover the body.
4Hilario Aguilar, president of the Mexican Alpine Club in Chalchicomula de Sesma, confirmed the discovery and said Wednesday that the body appeared to be mummified.
5Aguilar said it could be that of a climber but he did not know of any climbers unaccounted for in the past 50 years.
6The volcano on the border of Puebla and Veracruz states in south-central Mexico stands at an elevation of 18,406-foot (5,610-meter), according to Mexico's National Institute of Statistics and Geography.
