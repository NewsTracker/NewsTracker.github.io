Wall Street rallies on Greek deal; tech stocks jump

1(Reuters) - U.S. stocks finished sharply higher on Monday, with the Dow Jones industrial average re-emerging in positive territory for the year, after euro zone leaders reached a tentative deal to bail out Greece.
2The improved European picture led to best three-day run this year for the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite.
3Facebook (FB.O), Netflix (NFLX.O) and Amazon (AMZN.O) all hit record highs, while Apple's (AAPL.O) 1.93 percent rise gave the biggest boost to the Nasdaq.
4Greece won conditional agreement to receive a possible $95 billion over three years, along with an assurance of talks to bridge a funding gap until a bailout is ready.
5The deal is contingent on Greece meeting a tight timetable to enact strict reforms.
6"Headlines out of Greece are going to dissipate a bit and with that the U.S. earnings picture is going to start to emerge as the important factor," said Mike Binger, a portfolio manager at Gradient Investments in Shoreview, Minnesota, with $850 million under management.
7Also making Wall Street more confident, Chinese stocks rose for a third straight day as data showed exports rose while imports slipped in June, a tentative sign global demand might be on the mend.
8Historically high stock valuations may attract fresh attention when U.S. companies post second-quarter results over the next several weeks.
9Wall Street expects a 2.9 percent dip in quarterly earnings, according to Thomson Reuters I/B/E/S.
10Pointing to expectations of calmer trading, the CBOE Volatility index (.VIX) fell 16 percent on Monday.
11Its 29 percent decline in the past two sessions is the largest two-day drop since Jan. 2, 2013.
12The Dow Jones industrial average (.DJI) rose 217.27 points, or 1.22 percent, to end at 17,977.68.
13The S&P 500 (.SPX) gained 22.98 points, or 1.11 percent, to 2,099.6 and the Nasdaq Composite (.IXIC) added 73.82 points, or 1.48 percent, to 5,071.51.
14All of 10 major S&P 500 sectors were higher, led by the technology index (.SPLRCT), up 1.62 percent.
15The financial index (.SPSY) rose 1.09 percent.
16Upcoming quarterly earnings reports from banks are expected to benefit from a recent rise in long-term yields relative to short term yields.
17Crude tumbled on progress toward a nuclear deal that would end sanctions on Iran, allowing more oil onto the market.
18The energy index (.SPNY) stayed positive but has been the worst-performing S&P sector over the last month, falling over 5 percent.
19The oil slide boosted U.S. airline stocks (.DJUSAR).
20American Airlines (AAL.O), United Continental (UAL.N), JetBlue (JBLU.O) and Alaska Air (ALK.N) were all up between 1 and 3 percent.
21Apple (AAPL.O) was up 4.66 percent in the past two days for its best back-to-back run since January.
22Markwest Energy Partners (MWE.N) rose 13.96 percent.
23MPLX (MPLX.N), Marathon Petroleum's (MPC.N) master limited partnership, said it will buy the natural gas processor for about $15.63 billion.
24MPLX (MPLX.N) fell 14.51 while Marathon rose 7.88 percent.
25Advancing issues outnumbered decliners on the NYSE by 2,242 to 830.
26On the Nasdaq, 2,002 issues rose and 808 fell.
27The S&P 500 posted 43 new 52-week highs and 10 new lows; the Nasdaq recorded 149 new highs and 42 new lows.
28About 5.9 billion shares traded on all U.S. platforms, according to BATS exchange data, below the month-to-date average of 6.9 billion.
