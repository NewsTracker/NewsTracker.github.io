Google Maps to include railroad crossing data

1Although the FRA has made contact with multiple mapping software and hardware manufacturers such as MapQuest, TomTom, Garmin and Apple, Google is the first to agree to include data from about 250,000 public and private railroad crossings in its navigation software, Google Maps.
2The information is already available to consumers via the Department of Transportation's Rail Crossing Locator App.
3In addition to providing the location of at-grade crossings, the app can also search and filter different types of railroad crossings and tell the user what to do in case of an emergency or safety concern at a specific crossing.
4The FRA conceded that many drivers crossing unfamiliar areas at night use GPS mapping applications to guide them and that adding the data to such software "just makes sense."
5The FRA estimates that about 270 people died in at-grade crossings in 2014, up for the first time this decade over the previous year, 2013.
6Some 843 people were also injured in 2014.
7Many of these deaths are preventable according to the FRA, since many railroad crossings feature ineffective warning symbols, typically in the form of a single white X.
8The news comes as grade-crossing measures in a Senate bill cleared the Senate Commerce, Science and Transportation Committee last week.
9This legislation, in turn, joins a passenger rail reauthorization bill passed by the House in March that allocates $35 billion to safety improvements at grade crossings.
