U.S. says its troops killed senior Islamic State leader in Syria raid

1WASHINGTON/BEIRUT U.S special forces have carried out a raid inside Syria that killed a man identified as a senior Islamic State leader who helped direct the group's oil, gas and financial operations, U.S. officials said on Saturday.
2The White House said President Barack Obama ordered the raid that killed the Islamic State figure identified as Abu Sayyaf.
3U.S. officials said his wife was captured in the raid.
4It is the first publicly declared special forces operation by U.S. forces in Syria since their failed attempt to rescue American journalist James Foley and other American hostages held by Islamic State last summer.
5Foley was killed by the ultra-radical group, an offshoot of al Qaeda.
6The United States is leading a coalition in a military campaign to roll back the jihadist group whose self-declared caliphate in Syria and Iraq has reshaped the region.
7Islamic State pressed attacks in both countries on Saturday.
8In Iraq, its fighters battled Iraqi security forces in the city of Ramadi, where its black flag was raised over local government headquarters on Friday.
9Were it to fall, Ramadi would be the first major city to be won by the insurgents since an effort to push them back began last year.
10In Syria, they fought Syrian government forces for control of the ancient city of Palmyra, an attack that has raised fears its UNESCO World Heritage site could meet the same fate as monuments destroyed by Islamic State in northern Iraq.
11While Washington is working closely with Iraq in the fight against Islamic State, it has shunned the idea of cooperating with Syrian President Bashar al-Assad, who it says has lost legitimacy to rule and must leave power.
12The United States said it did not warn Assad in advance or coordinate with his officials over the special forces raid.
13Conducted by U.S. personnel based out of Iraq, the raid targeted area called al-Amr in the eastern Deir al-Zor province, an Islamic State stronghold rich in oil that bridges territory the group controls in Syria and Iraq.
14"During the course of the operation, Abu Sayyaf was killed when he engaged U.S. forces," White House National Security Council spokeswoman Bernadette Meehan said.
15The operation was conducted "with the full consent of Iraqi authorities".
16Assad said in February he had been informed about U.S.-led air strikes against Islamic State in Syria via third parties including Iraq, with which his government has close ties.
17A U.S. official said about a dozen fighters were killed in the overnight raid.
18Syrian state TV initially credited the Syrian army with carrying out the raid, saying it killed 40 Islamic State militants and the group's "oil minister", who it identified by a different name.
19The Syrian Observatory for Human Rights, a UK-based organization that tracks the war using sources on the ground, said at least 19 Islamic State members were killed in an air strike in the area which it said was carried out by U.S. forces.
20Twelve of those killed were non-Syrians, it said.
21"WHAT IS THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY DOING?"
22Islamic State, the most powerful insurgent force in Syria, has in recent months launched frequent attacks on government- and rebel-held areas beyond its strongholds in Deir al-Zor and Raqqa provinces.
23Its attack on Palmyra got underway this week.
24The group has seized positions within 1-2 km (about 1 mile) of the city, also known as Tadmur and home to extensive ruins of one of the most important cultural centers of the ancient world.
25The Observatory said on Saturday Islamic State fighters had made further gains in areas around the city.
26A military source said fighting was ongoing but at "a distance far from the city", which he said had been secured by army reinforcements.
27"We are in the fourth or fifth day (of the attack). What is the international community doing? Is it waiting to weep and despair as it did in northern Iraq?"
28Syrian government antiquities chief Maamoun Abdulkarim told Reuters.
29The Observatory reported Islamic State had seized a gas field east of Palmyra - a report denied by the military source.
30The source said Islamic State was keeping up its attack on the city but the fighting was at a lower intensity.
31The Observatory says Islamic State has carried out two mass executions to the north of Palmyra.
32On Friday, it killed 23 people including nine minors and five women, it reported.
33In Ramadi, 100 km (60 miles) west of Baghdad, a speech was delivered in the name of Islamic State in the great city's great mosque, vowing to punish anyone who was deemed to harm Muslims, witnesses said.
34An Iraqi army source said there were heavy clashes between Islamic State militants and members of the security forces in southeastern Ramadi.
35An operations command center was surrounded and being struck by mortars, the army source said.
36The insurgents attacked Ramadi overnight Thursday to Friday, using six suicide car bombs to reach the city center where the Anbar provincial government compound is located.
37A hospital source said seven members of the armed forces and 10 civilians had been killed since Friday.
38Islamic State issued an audio recording on Thursday it said was by its leader, Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi.
39It was his first message since a number of media reports said he was wounded this year in Iraq.
40Some said he was no longer running the group and others suggested he was dead.
