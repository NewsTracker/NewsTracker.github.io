Second blogger, Washikur Rahman, hacked to death in Bangladesh

1Dhaka: A blogger was hacked to death by machete-wielding assailants in the Bangladesh capital Dhaka on Monday, the second attack in five weeks on a critic of religious extremism in the Muslim-majority South Asian nation.
2Washikur Rahman, a secular blogger, was attacked by young religious students on a busy street in the centre of Dhaka on Monday morning, a police official said.
3"Police on duty near the spot caught two attackers red-handed with three machetes as they were fleeing the scene after the incident," police official Humayan Kabir told Reuters.
4A police officer shoots rubber bullets during a clash with Islamic activists in Dhaka in 2013: the government has tried to crack down on hardline Islamist groups.
5The killing comes just weeks after US secular blogger Avijit Roy was hacked to death while returning with his wife from a book fair in Dhaka.
6His wife, Rafida Bonya Ahmed, suffered head injuries and lost a finger in the February 26 attack.
7The attacks come amid a period of political turmoil in the country, with the government and main opposition group locked in a months-long standoff that has created a sense of deepening insecurity across the country.
8In recent years, a string of secular-minded writers have been targeted by religious militants in Bangladesh as the government has tried to crack down on hardline Islamist groups seeking to create a sharia-based state in the nation.
9Blogger Ahmed Rajib Haider was killed in 2013 near his home in Dhaka after he led a protest demanding capital punishment for Islamist leaders convicted of war crimes during Bangladesh's war for independence.
10Another blogger Washikur Rahman Babu was hacked to death today in Bangladesh by Muslim fanatics.
11Bangladesh is not a place for freethinkers.
12In 2004, Humayun Azad, a secular writer and professor at Dhaka University, was also attacked by militants while returning home from a Dhaka book fair.
13He later died in Germany while undergoing treatment.
14Roy's wife, Ahmed, blamed her husband's murder in February on religious fanatics, and accused police on duty of not doing enough to stop the attack.
15Roy's father also told reporters last week that "apparently no progress" had been made in resolving the case.
16Mohammad Habibur Rahman, secretary of the Bangladesh Police Association and Superintendent of Police of Dhaka, said the police had been unfairly criticised over their handling of the crime.
17"There are plenty of examples where our police force come forward to save people, risking their own lives," he said.
18Media group Reporters Without Borders rated Bangladesh 146th among 180 countries in a ranking of press freedom last year.
