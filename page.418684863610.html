Chicago-area man accused of threatening Emanuel, others

1CHICAGO (AP) " A driver for a ride-sharing service used his Facebook page to threaten Chicago Mayor Rahm Emanuel and others, hinting that he hoped to commit an act of violence before traveling to Pakistan, according to a recently unsealed federal complaint.
2Mohammad Waqas Khan, who is around 30 and from the Chicago suburb of Glen Ellyn, should remain behind bars pending trial, a U.S. magistrate judge ruled at a detention hearing Monday in Chicago.
3Khan wrote on May 13 that Emanuel had "led the city of Chicago down the road to perdition," the complaint says.
4He also calls the mayor and unnamed Chicago aldermen "rabid dogs," and adds, "You shall be taught a lesson you will not forget!"
5Khan also allegedly expressed disgust with American and Pakistani politicians, though no one else is mentioned by name in the complaint.
6Defense attorney Ellen Domph told Monday's hearing that Khan's words weren't serious.
7She even suggested they could have been rap lyrics.
8The complaint isn't clear about possible motives.
9It quotes several messages in which Khan seems to suggest he wanted acts of violence that would draw widespread attention.
10On May 7 he wrote, "I'm not killin sum bum on the street. I want a net worth individual to shoot. I want this to be a real human tragedy.
11The 10-page complaint describes federal surveillance of Khan, including as he picked up and drove ride-share customers.
12His arrest in mid-May, the complaint suggests, was hastened by his purchase of a plane ticket to fly from Chicago to Pakistan on June 8.
13He allegedly wrote on Facebook, "I'm not leaving America without getting revenge even if it costs me my life."
14The complaint says the DuPage County Sherriff's Office first arrested Khan on May 14, including on charges he had a loaded semi-automatic pistol in his car in violation of Illinois' concealed-carry law.
15He was later charged federally with transmitting a threatening communication.
16The complaint gives Khan's age as 29, though the Federal Bureau of Prisons record indicates he is 30.
