Pakistan judge orders former CIA lawyer, agent be charged

1Former acting general counsel John A. Rizzo and ex-station chief Jonathan Bank must face charges including murder, conspiracy, waging war against Pakistan and terrorism, Justice Shaukat Aziz Siddiqui of the Islamabad High Court ruled.
2A court clerk and a lawyer involved the case confirmed details of the judge's ruling.
3Rizzo and Bank could not be immediately reached for comment.
4The CIA declined to immediately comment when reached by The Associated Press.
5Bank fled Pakistan in 2010 after his cover was blown when the man named Kareem Khan initially threatened to sue the CIA over the deaths of his brother and his son in a purported 2009 strike.
6The station chief's outing spurred questions at the time of whether Pakistan's spy service might have leaked the information, something the Pakistanis denied.
7The disclosure didn't prevent Bank from landing another sensitive job: He became chief of the Iran operations division at CIA headquarters at Langley.
8He later was removed from that post after CIA officials concluded he created a hostile work environment in the division.
9He has since been detailed to the Pentagon's intelligence arm.
10Rizzo was the CIA's acting general counsel overseeing its drone program.
11He later left the agency.
12The CIA's drone strike program killed Pakistani Taliban fighters and other militants hiding in its tribal regions, but caused major anger across Pakistan over civilian casualties from the strikes.
13Since 2004, the U.S. has carried out nearly 400 suspected drone strikes in the country, according to the New America Foundation's International Security Program, which tracks the American campaign.
