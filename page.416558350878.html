Ukraine eyes EU membership within 5 years

1Ukrainian President Petro Poroshenko, center, joins hands with European Council President Donald Tusk, left, and European Commission President Jean-Claude Juncker, ahead of a EU-Ukraine summit, in Kiev, Ukraine, Monday, April 27, 2015.
2KIEV, Ukraine - Ukraine's president says his country will be able meet conditions to apply for European Union membership within five years.
3Speaking at an EU-Ukraine summit in Kyiv, Petro Poroshenko said Ukraine deserves to be an integral part of the EU and that membership is a key goal for his government.
4Guiding Ukraine toward integration with Europe was at the heart of the movement that culminated in last year's toppling of former President Viktor Yanukovych, who provoked anger by cancelling plans to deepen trade ties with the EU.
5Efforts to reform Ukraine's corruption-ridden economy to become more compliant with European standards have been slow, however, since Poroshenko came to power.
6Numerous top European officials, including EU Commission President Jean-Claude Juncker and European Council President Donald Tusk, are attending the Kyiv talks.
