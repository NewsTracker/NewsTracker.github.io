Fin24.com | Apple rolls out gay emojis

1San Francisco - Users of the Apple iPhone and iPad got a new palate of emoji on Wednesday, including a group of alternative or gay family illustrations.
2The new emoji appeared on an update to Apple's mobile operating software, iOS 8.3, which was rolled out on Wednesday.
3Some test versions of the software had been in use since February.
4The new emoji include illustrations of families with families that include two male parents or two female parents.
5The updated emojis also include characters of various races, as previously announced by the Unicode Consortium which sets international rules for text and characters to insure consistency across platforms.
6Apple made no comment about the update, but earlier this year in a statement to media that it "supports and cares deeply about diversity".
7Apple CEO Tim Cook announced last October he was "proud to be gay", following long speculation about his sexual preference.
