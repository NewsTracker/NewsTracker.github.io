Facebook hooks up with publishers for 'Instant Articles'

1Facebook has teamed up with nine news publishers to launch a feature that will allow them to publish articles directly on the network's mobile news feeds.
2Sharing articles on Facebook's mobile app is more and more common.
3The new feature makes it far faster.
4As it stands, it takes about eight seconds to load articles.
5And this is by far the single slowest content type on Facebook, it said.
6'As more people get their news on mobile devices, we want to make the experience faster and richer on Facebook,' it said.
7The move also makes it easier for publishers to add interactive features.
8'Instant Articles introduces a suite of interactive features that allow publishers to bring their stories to life in new ways.
9'Watch auto-play videos come alive as you scroll through stories.
10Publishers can sell advertisements in their articles and keep the revenue, or use Facebook's Audience Network to monetize unsold inventory.
11Introducing Instant Articles, a new tool for publishers to create fast, interactive articles on Facebook.
12'Fundamentally, this is a tool that enables publishers to provide a better experience for their readers on Facebook' said Facebook Chief Product Officer Chris Cox.
13Publishers will also be able to track data and traffic through comScore and another tools.
14The nine partners are The New York Times, National Geographic, BuzzFeed, NBC, The Atlantic, The Guardian, BBC News, Spiegel and Bild, Facebook said.
