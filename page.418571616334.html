Islamic State punishes 94 it said violated Ramzan fast - Syria monitor

1Islamic State, which controls tracts of territory in Syria and Iraq, has regularly killed and abused people it says have violated its ultra-hardline interpretation of Islam.
2BEIRUT: Islamic State militants have punished at least 94 people including five teenagers, accusing them of violations during the Muslim fasting month of Ramadan, a rights group monitoring the Syrian conflict said on Thursday.
3The people were flogged, hung up by their arms or put in metal cages by the Sunni Muslim militant group in incidents documented since the start of Ramadan last month, the Syrian Observatory for Human Rights said.
4Muslims who observe Ramadan across the world refrain from eating, drinking and smoking during daylight hours.
5Young children are not usually expected to observe the fast.
6All victims survived the punishments which were carried out in Islamic State-held territory in Raqqa, Aleppo and Deir al-Zor provinces, the Britain-based Observatory said, citing its network of contacts on the ground.
7The five teenagers were aged 13 to 16.
8One was put in a metal cage and the other four were hung up crucifixion-style, the Observatory's founder Rami Abdulrahman said.
