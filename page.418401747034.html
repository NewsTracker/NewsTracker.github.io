4NEW YORK (AP) - Facebook wants you to see more of what you want to see.
5New tools will help you weed through the clutter of boring, unwanted information, often from long-forgotten acquaintances, and surface the gems from close friends and interesting pages.
6You'll now be able to choose the friends and pages you want to see on your news feed first.
7To do this, go to the friend's profile.
8Click on the box that says "following" and select "see first."
9Facebook's computer software uses a wide range of information you provide to decide what to show.
10This includes what friends you interact with and how often, or whether you tend to like photos, videos or text updates more.
11In announcing the new tools Thursday, Facebook acknowledged that its automated system isn't perfect, so it wants to give users a way to set their own preferences.
12In addition to selecting who or what page you will see first, you'll still to be able to "unfollow" friends so you won't see them at all.
13This option has been available before to people who don't want to take drastic step of unfriending someone but would rather not read about their lives.
14For the rest, though, Facebook will continue to use its software to choose what to show you.
