Stocks close higher on optimism for Greek deal

1U.S. stocks logged their best day in two months Friday as Greece appeared to move closer to securing a bailout deal that will enable it to avoid bankruptcy and keep the country in the euro.
2Greece and its creditors appeared to be narrowing their differences after Athens offered reform proposals in order to secure a third bailout of around 53 billion euros ($59.5 billion).
3A final decision could be made on Sunday.
4A second day of gains for Chinese stocks also encouraged investors.
5U.S. stocks had fallen sharply on Wednesday, in part on concern that a monthlong slump in China's stock market could crimp growth in the world's second-largest economy.
6"This is a giant collective exhaling," said Kristina Hooper, U.S. investment strategist at Allianz Global Investors.
7The Greek deal "is not done, but we're closer than we have been in a while."
8The Standard & Poor's 500 index rose 25.31 points, or 1.2 percent, to 2,076.62.
9The Dow Jones industrial average climbed 211.79 points, or 1.2 percent, to 17,760.41.
10The Nasdaq composite gained 75.30 points, or 1.5 percent, to 4,997.70.
11The gains pushed the S&P 500 back into positive territory for the year.
12On Friday, stocks in China jumped before the U.S. market opened.
13China's Shanghai Composite Index jumped 4.5 percent, paring its losses for the month to 24 percent.
14The Chinese market is only recovering after the government intervened heavily and about half of the companies listed in mainland China suspended trading in their stocks.
15That's making some investors cautious.
16"The policy makers appear to have some success in stabilizing values, but fifty percent of Chinese stocks are not currently trading," said Jim McDonald, chief investment strategist at Northern Trust.
17Back in the U.S., airline stocks rallied after American Airlines, the nation's largest carrier, signaled that it was cutting back on its growth plans this year amid signs that average fares are declining.
18American said it expects to increase passenger-carrying capacity by 1 percent this year, down from an earlier forecast of 2 percent.
19American Airlines rose $1.54, or 3.9 percent, to $41.21.
20Delta Air Lines jumped $1.91, or 4.7 percent, to $42.46.
21Investors also followed a speech by Federal Reserve Chair Janet Yellen on Friday.
22Speaking in Cleveland, Ohio, Yellen said that the Fed is on track to start raising interest rates later this year, but expressed concerns over headwinds that are still holding back the U.S. economy, in particular lingering weakness in the labor market and new potential threats overseas.
23David Kelly, chief global strategist at JPMorgan Asset Management, said after the speech that he expected the Fed to lift interest rates in September, provided that Greece reaches a deal with its creditors and there were no major financial crises between now and then.
24Investor start focusing on second-quarter earnings next week as the pace of company reporting picks up.
25Among the companies reporting are banks, including JPMorgan, Bank of America and Wells Fargo, as well as Delta, Netflix and Intel.
26"Earnings will be adequate, we don't expect them to blow out the lights," said Allianz's Hooper.
27"It will be a subdued, but mildly positive earnings environment."
28Companies in the S&P 500 are forecast to report that earnings shrank by 4.5 percent on average.
29While that would be the first contraction in earnings in almost six years, the figures are distorted by a big drop in energy company earnings following the collapse in the oil price last year.
30In bond trading, prices fell.
31The yield on the 10-year Treasury note climbed to 2.40 percent from 2.32 percent on Thursday.
32The euro surged against the dollar, climbing 0.7 percent to $1.1151.
33The dollar rose 0.7 percent against the Japanese currency, to 122.80 yen.
34The price of oil declined slightly and ended the week down 7 percent on concerns about economic growth in Europe and China as well as robust production from U.S. drillers.
35Benchmark U.S. crude fell 4 cents to close at $52.74 a barrel in New York.
36It ended last week at $56.93 a barrel.
37Brent crude, a benchmark for international oils used by many U.S. refineries, rose 12 cents Friday to close at $58.73 a barrel in London.
38In metals trading, silver rose 12 cents to $15.47 an ounce.
39Gold dropped $1.30 to $1,157.90 an ounce.
40Copper fell 1.3 cents to $2.55 a pound.
41- Wholesale gasoline fell 2.8 cents to close at $2.017 a gallon.
42- Heating oil fell 0.4 cent to close at $1.740 a gallon.
43- Natural gas rose 4.4 cents to close at $2.770 per 1,000 cubic feet.
