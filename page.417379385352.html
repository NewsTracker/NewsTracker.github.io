Canadian man ticketed for using his Apple Watch while driving on highway

1A Canadian man has spoken out, after he was ticketed Monday when he was driving and using his Apple Watch to change the music in his car at the same time.
2Surete du Quebec police issued Jeffrey Macesin four points on his driver's license and a $120 ticket, CTV News reported.
3Macesin recalled the incident with the officer in an interview with the news outlet.
4$120 Canadian dollars converts to 63, or approximately $96 US dollars.
5Macesin said before the police stop took place, he already knew authorities were on a Highway 20 overpass since his Apple Watch has the traffic app Waze.
6Macesin told CTV News he had been changing music with his Apple Watch while driving near the overpass - but his phone was in a bag and being charged.
7'So I was changing songs like this, with my hand on the steering wheel,' he told the news outlet, gesturing.
8CTV News explained that Macesin's ticket relates to a Quebec Highway Safety Code law.
9Macesin said in the interview 'Going towards Vaudreuil, I - there was a cop car behind me and he didn't have his lights on yet, but then he turned them on and I thought maybe he just wanted me to get out of the way.
10'I pulled over when I got off the bridge there, and sure enough, he got out of his car and came up to me and I asked what the problem was and he said that I was caught using my mobile device,' Macesin told CTV News.
11Masecin said he's submitted a form contesting the ticket form and has a lawyer.
12He said in the interview: 'It's not so much hand-held.
13It's a watch, you know?
14It's on my wrist.
15'I understand their point of view, but the fact is that he thought I was using my phone, and I wasn't using my phone.
16I was using my watch,' Macesin told CTV News.
