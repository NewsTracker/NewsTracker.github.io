Test: McNabb's alcohol level was more than twice legal limit (Yahoo Sports)

1GILBERT, Ariz.
2(AP) -- Former NFL quarterback Donovan McNabb had a blood alcohol level that was more than twice the legal limit when he was arrested on suspicion of DUI in Arizona last month, police said Tuesday.
3Police in the Phoenix suburb of Gilbert said lab tests show McNabb's blood alcohol level was 0.17.
4The legal limit in Arizona is .08 and anything 0.15 or higher qualifies as extreme DUI.
5A message left at a phone number listed for the 38-year-old McNabb was not immediately returned.
6It was the second DUI arrest for McNabb in 18 months.
7He served a day in jail after pleading guilty to a misdemeanor DUI charge resulting from a Dec. 15, 2013, arrest in the Salt River Pima-Maricopa Indian Community east of Phoenix.
8In the latest incident, police said McNabb's vehicle rear-ended one driven by the wife of a Gila River tribal police officer.
9A responding officer said McNabb's eyes were ''very bloodshot and his speech was slurred,'' according to the arrest report.
10In police video of the late-night arrest on June 28, McNabb tells an officer he had just left a sports bar and was driving home.
11He goes on to say he hasn't been drinking and that he's sick.
12''Well first of all, I got a cold.
13So, I've been on cough medicine,'' McNabb says.
14Asked if he had any recent head injuries, McNabb says, ''Listen, I played football.'' The officer then says he is aware of who McNabb is.
15The video was first aired by TMZ.com.
16No injuries were reported in the collision.
17McNabb was cited and released from a police facility.
18McNabb played 13 years in the NFL after being drafted by Philadelphia with the second overall pick in 1999.
19McNabb played for the Washington Redskins in 2010 and Minnesota Vikings in 2011 before retiring.
