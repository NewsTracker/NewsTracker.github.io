Stocks close lower as Greece talks remain stuck

1U.S. stocks are closing lower following losses in Europe as Greece remains at an impasse with its creditors.
2J.M. Smucker fell 4 percent Thursday after the food and coffee maker reported a loss in its latest quarter.
3Dish Network and T-Mobile rose sharply after The Wall Street Journal reported that the two companies are considering a merger.
4The Dow Jones industrial average fell 170 points, or 0.9 percent, to 17,906.
5The Standard & Poor's 500 index lost 18 points, or 0.9 percent, to 2,095.
6The Nasdaq composite declined 40 points, or 0.8 percent, to 5,059.
7Energy stocks had some of the biggest losses as the price of crude oil fell 3 percent.
8Bond prices rose.
9The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell to 2.30 percent.
