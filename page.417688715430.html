World stock markets rise on Greece hopes, Asia rate cuts

1A man look at an electronic stock indicator of a securities firm in Tokyo, Thursday, June 11, 2015.
2Asian shares were mostly higher Thursday after signs of progress in Greece's bailout talks sparked a rally on Wall Street.
3Japan's benchmark Nikkei 225 rose 1.4 percent to 20,335.16 in the morning session.
4Global stocks edged higher on Thursday after signs of progress in Greece's bailout talks, while an interest rate cut in New Zealand sent the country's currency tumbling.
5KEEPING SCORE: France's CAC 40 added 0.5 percent to 4,958.85 while Germany's DAX rose 0.7 percent at 11,340.17.
6Britain's FTSE 100 was up 0.2 percent at 6,846.60.
7U.S. shares were set to open flat, with S&P 500 and Dow futures little changed.
8GREECE DEVELOPMENTS: Hopes rose once again that Greece might be able to reach a deal to get rescue loans and avoid a default this summer.
9Greek stocks jumped 7 percent after officials, including German Chancellor Angela Merkel, said Greece had committed to reaching a compromise in coming days.
10Such optimism has proved unfounded in the past, with the talks dragging on for over four months, but pressure is growing on Greece, which does not have the money to meet debt repayments at the end of June.
11RATE CUTS: South Korea's central bank lowered its key interest rate to a historic low of 1.5 percent, partly because of concerns the outbreak of the deadly MERS virus could slow the economy.
12New Zealand's central bank cut its benchmark interest rate by a quarter point to 3.25 percent as farmers grapple with price drops for milk exports.
13ASIA'S DAY: Japan's benchmark Nikkei 225 rose 1.7 percent to close at 20,382.97.
14South Korea's Kospi added 0.3 percent to2,056.61 and Hong Kong's Hang Seng rose 0.8 percent to 26,902.85.
15The Shanghai Composite inched up 0.3 percent to 5,121.59.
16Australia's S&P/ASX 200 climbed 1.4 percent to 5,556.70.
17CURRENCIES: The U.S. dollar rose to 1.43 New Zealand dollars after the rate cut from 1.40 a day earlier.
18The euro fell to $1.1259 from $1.1348 in late trading Wednesday.
19The dollar recovered to 123.67 yen from 122.86 yen, after sliding Wednesday when the Bank of Japan chief signaled the yen probably won't keep falling.
20ENERGY: Benchmark U.S. crude was down 94 cents to $60.49 a barrel in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
21The contract rose $1.29 to close at $61.43 a barrel in Nymex floor trading on Wednesday after the Energy Department's weekly supply report showed a surprisingly big drop in crude inventories along with rising demand for gasoline.
