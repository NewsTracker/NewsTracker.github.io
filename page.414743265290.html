Official: At least 16 dead in Mexico bus-train crash

1In this image made from Associated Press Television News video emergency responders work at the scene of a bus and freight train collision Friday Feb. 13, 2015.
2The collision at a grade crossing in northern Mexico, killed at least 16 people and injured 22, according to a Mexican official.
3The official said the accident Friday occured in the town of Anahuac, which is Tamaulipas state near the border city of Nuevo Laredo, across from Laredo, Texas.
4MONTERREY, Mexico (AP) - A passenger bus and a freight train collided at a grade crossing in northern Mexico, killing at least 16 people and injuring 22, a Mexican official said.
5The official said the accident occurred Friday in the town of Anahuac, which is Tamaulipas state near the border city of Nuevo Laredo, across from Laredo, Texas.
6The official was not authorized to be quoted by name.
7The train's operator, Kansas City Southern de Mexico, issued a statement confirming there had been "a lamentable accident" between one of its trains and a passenger bus at about 5:25 p.m. The company said its employees reported "an undetermined number of dead and injured at the scene."
8Kansas City Southern did not give a cause for the crash.
9The Mexican official said investigators were looking into whether the bus driver tried to beat the train to the crossing.
10The train was operated by the Transporte Frontera line on a route from Nuevo Laredo to the neighboring state of Coahuila.
