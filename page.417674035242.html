S&P downgrades Greece, says default likely if no debt deal

1NEW YORK (AP) - Standard & Poor's has downgraded Greece's credit rating one notch further into junk territory, saying it's likely the country will default on its commercial debt within a year if it can't strike a deal with its creditors.
2The rating agency said Wednesday Greece has shown it is giving higher priority to pensions and other domestic spending than making debt payments on time.
3It lowered its rating to "CCC" from "CCC+" with a "negative" outlook.
