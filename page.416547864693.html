The Latest: Death toll in Nepal quake more than 3,200

1Nepal's Deputy Inspector General of Police Komal Singh Bam says at least 3,218 people have been confirmed killed in Saturday's earthquake.
2So far 18 people have also been confirmed dead in an avalanche that swept through the Mount Everest base camp in the wake of the earthquake.
3Another 61 people were killed in neighboring India.
4In a western part of Kathmandu, dozens of Nepali soldiers and Indian rescue workers pulled 16 bodies and one survivor from a collapsed green, three-story building that housed a church.
5Officials say a Christian meeting was being held on the second floor when the quake hit on Saturday.
6One Indian national disaster response force, who did not want to be named, said "I really doubt anybody is alive here, but we have to keep trying and look."
7The bodies were laid out, side by side, on a tarp, as crowds of onlookers were kept behind police tape.
8Several generators were set up to power the drills and saws used to dig through the building.
9Several other buildings in the area were damaged, but most appeared fine.
