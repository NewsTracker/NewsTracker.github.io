Is Facebook right to censor user content?

Extra controls in place for storing and importing 1080

1More than 1.4 billion people around the world use facebook every month.
2And the content they post varies just as much as the places they are posting it from.
3Facebook users span different countries, cultures and laws.
4And the company has always had a challenging task of allowing the freedom to post openly, while censoring content it considers offensive or dangerous.
5On Monday, Facebook issued new guidelines on what types of content is allowed on the site.
6And the reasons it bans some material.
7The updated rules cover everything from nudity to bullying and what the social networking site describes as support for 'terrorism'.
8But how effectively can Facebook enforce its rules?
9And do users really want more restrictions on what they post?
1The government has introduced new controls for all workplaces and laboratories using sodium fluoroacetate (1080) from April 10.
2After that date it will be illegal to hold 1080 without having notified the Environmental Protection Authority (EPA) of the quantity held and the supplier.
3Any new imports require an import certificate from the EPA before it can be collected from the New Zealand Customs Service.
4Laboratory managers must also ensure that 1080 is securely locked up when not in use.
5The new measures were introduced by the government last week, in response to a threat made in November to contaminate infant and other formula if 1080 was not withdrawn from use by the end of March.
