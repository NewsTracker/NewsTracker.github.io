4SAN FRANCISCO (AP) — Soon you ll be able to buy an Apple Watch like you d buy most other watches — in a store.
5Two months after Apple began taking online orders for its newest product, the company said Thursday it plans to begin selling some models in its retail stores in two weeks.
6Apple also said it s cutting through a backlog of online orders, with most watches ordered by end of May shipping within two weeks.
7The smartwatch has been on display in Apple stores in the United States and eight other countries, where customers could examine and try them on.
8But they couldn t buy one and wear it out the door.
9Apple has said that was because supplies were limited.
10FILE - In this April 10, 2015, file photo, a customer tries on an Apple Watch at an Apple Store in Hong Kong.
11Just how many watches Apple has sold is up for debate.
12The Cupertino, California, company hasn t released any sales figures, prompting some industry analysts to speculate demand was lower than expected.
13Others cite reports of manufacturing problems in Asia as evidence that supplies were unexpectedly constrained.
14Apple also said Thursday that it will begin selling the watch in more countries later this month, adding Italy, Mexico, Singapore, South Korea, Spain, Switzerland and Taiwan.
15In a statement Thursday, Apple Senior Vice President Jeff Williams said "the response to Apple Watch has surpassed our expectations in every way."
16He did not offer details.
