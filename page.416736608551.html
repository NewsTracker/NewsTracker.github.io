Golden State Warriors star Stephen Curry wins NBA MVP (Yahoo Sports)

1OAKLAND, Calif.
2(AP) -- Golden State Warriors point guard Stephen Curry won the NBA's Most Valuable Player award Monday, beating out Houston's James Harden in a race that turned out not to be that close.
3Curry received 100 of 130 first-place votes for a total of 1,198 points from a panel of 129 writers and broadcasters, along with the fan vote on the NBA's website.
4Harden had 25 first-place votes and 936 points.
5Cleveland's LeBron James, a four-time MVP, got five first-place votes and 552 points.
6Curry carried the top-seeded Warriors to a franchise-record 67 wins, surpassed his own record for most 3-pointers in a season and added to his growing reputation as one of the most entertaining spectacles in sports.
7He's the franchise's first MVP since Wilt Chamberlain in 1960, when the Warriors played in Philadelphia.
8Curry was set to receive the award during an afternoon news conference Monday in Oakland.
9He will be presented with the hardware again during an on-court ceremony when Golden State hosts Memphis in Game 2 of the Western Conference semifinal series Tuesday night.
10Curry, who had 22 points and seven assists in the Warriors' 101-86 victory over the Grizzlies on Sunday, has overcome obstacles at every level.
11He grew up in the shadow of his father, former NBA player Dell Curry.
12Most major colleges didn't offer him a scholarship coming out of high school in Charlotte, North Carolina, believing he was too small.
13And some professional scouts wondered whether his game could transition to the NBA, where point guards are bigger and defenses are tougher.
14Instead, Curry has gone from a shooting guard who dazzled at Davidson during the NCAA Tournament to a polished professional point guard who can shoot, dribble and distribute with the best of them.
15In a game dominated by big men and played by some of the world's greatest athletes, the 6-foot-3, 190-pound Curry controls the flow without physically overpowering defenders.
16But there were times when it seemed Curry's potential might not be reached.
17Two operations on his right ankle in his first three seasons with Golden State fueled questions about his durability.
18He even had to prove his worth to the team that drafted him seventh overall in 2009.
19Curry signed a $44 million, four-year contract extension with the Warriors before the 2012-13 season.
20Back then, the deal looked like a major risk for the Warriors considering Curry's injury history.
21Now?
22Well, Curry is clearly one of basketball's best bargains.
23Curry eclipsed his own record of 272 3-pointers set two years ago, hitting 286 from beyond the arc this season.
24He already owns three of the five most prolific 3-point shooting seasons in NBA history.
25Curry averaged 23.8 points, 7.7 assists, 4.3 rebounds and two steals this season.
26He shot 48.7 percent from the floor and 44.3 percent from 3-point range.
27Off the floor, his popularity is also soaring.
28Curry received more All-Star votes than any player and joined James and NBA Commissioner Adam Silver at the league's biggest marketing events during All-Star weekend in New York, where his face plastered posters in subway stations and televisions in taxi cabs.
29He also delivered by winning his first 3-point contest.
30With the revitalized Warriors winning at a historic pace, Curry's case for MVP echoed around the league as loud as the nightly chants at rowdy Oracle Arena.
