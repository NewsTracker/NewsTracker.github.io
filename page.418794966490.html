Obama returns to Kenya, reunites with father's family

1President Barack Obama hugs his half-sister, Auma Obama, accompanied by Kenya's President Uhuru Kenyatta, right, as he arrives in Nairobi.
2Fulfilling the hopes of millions of Kenyans, Barack Obama returned to his father's homeland Friday for the first time as U.S. president, a long sought visit by a country that considers him a local son.
3The president spent the evening reuniting with his Kenyan family, including his elderly step-grandmother who made the trip to the capital of Nairobi from her rural village.
4U.S. and Kenyan flags lined the main road from Nairobi's airport, and billboards heralding Obama's trip dotted the city.
5President Obama lays a wreath at the memorial commemorating the 1998 bombing of the U.S. Embassy in Nairobi, which killed more than 200 Americans and Kenyans.
6People wave U.S. flags before President Obama's arrival in Nairobi.
7"I don't think that Kenyans think of Obama as African-American. They think of him as Kenyan-American," said EJ Hogendoorn, deputy program director for Africa at the International Crisis Group.
8Obama's link to Kenya is a father he barely knew, but whose influence can nonetheless be seen in his son's presidency.
9Obama has spoken candidly about growing up without his Kenyan-born father and feeling "the weight of that absence."
10A White House initiative to support young men of color who face similar circumstances has become a project dear to Obama, one he plans to continue after leaving the White House.
11In Africa, Obama has used his late father's struggle to overcome government corruption as a way to push leaders to strengthen democracies.
12He's expected to make good governance and democracy-building a centerpiece of his two days of meetings and speeches in Nairobi, as well as a stop next week in Ethiopia.
13"In my father's life, it was partly tribalism and patronage and nepotism in an independent Kenya that for a long stretch derailed his career," Obama said during a 2009 trip to Ghana, his first visit to Africa as president.
14"We know that this kind of corruption is still a daily fact of life for far too many."
15The president's father, Barack Obama, Sr., left Kenya as a young man to study at the University of Hawaii.
16There, he met Stanley Ann Dunham, a white woman from Kansas.
17They would soon marry and have a son, who was named after his father.
18The elder Obama left Hawaii when his son was just 2 years old, first to continue his studies at Harvard, then to return to Kenya.
19The future president and his father would see each other just once more, when the son was 10 years old.
20Obama's father died in a car crash in 1982, at age 46.
21"I didn't have a dad in the house," Obama said last year during a White House event for My Brother's Keeper, his initiative for young men.
22"I was angry about it, even though I didn't necessarily realize it at the time."
23Obama's first trip to Kenya nearly 30 years ago was a quest to fill in the gaps in the story of his father's life.
24In his memoir "Dreams From My Father," Obama wrote that at the time of his death, "my father remained a mystery to me, both more and less than a man."
25What Obama uncovered was a portrait of a talented but troubled man.
26An economist for the Kenyan government, the senior Obama clashed with then-President Jomo Kenyatta over tribal divisions and allegations of corruption.
27He was ultimately fired by the president, sending him into a tailspin of financial problems and heavy drinking.
28The Kenyan leader Obama will meet with this weekend, Uhuru Kenyatta, is the son of the president his father confronted decades ago.
29Obama met most of his Kenyan family for the first time on that initial trip to his father's home country.
30As he stepped off Air Force One Friday, he was greeted by half-sister Auma Obama, pulling her into a warm embrace.
31The siblings then joined about three dozen family members at a restaurant at the president's hotel for a private dinner.
32Logistical constraints and security precautions prevented Obama from visiting Kogelo, the village where his father lived and is buried, on this trip.
33Sarah Obama, the step-grandmother he calls "Granny," still lives in the village.
34Despite the intense focus on the American leader's local roots, the White House has cast the trip as one focused on the relationship between the U.S. and Kenya, not the president and his family.
35Officials say Obama's agenda is heavily focused on trade and economic issues, as well as security and counterterrorism cooperation.
36The president is traveling with nearly two dozen U.S. lawmakers, along with 200 U.S. investors attending the Global Entrepreneurship Summit.
37Michelle Obama and daughters Malia and Sasha did not accompany the president.
38Auma Obama said she believed her late father would be proud to see his son return to Kenya as American president.
39"He'd be extremely proud and say, `Well done,"' she said in an interview with CNN.
40"But then he'd add, `But obviously, you're an Obama."
