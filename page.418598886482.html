Google shares soar as YouTube growth allays fears over Facebook

1Google Inc's (GOOGL.O) shares surged as much as 15.7 percent on Friday, adding nearly $63 billion to its market value, as strong growth in mobile ad revenue allayed concerns its YouTube business could be hurt by Facebook Inc's (FB.O) push into video.
2The surge in the stock, which sent the Nasdaq composite index to a record intraday high, came a day after Google reported better-than-expected revenue and profit for the first time in six quarters.
3Google's share price rise added more to its market value than the combined market capitalization of Twitter Inc (TWTR.N) and LinkedIn Corp (LNKD.N).
4Shares of Google, the world's most valuable company after Apple Inc (AAPL.O), hit a record high of $696, valuing the Web search giant at about $466 billion.
5At least 27 brokerages raised their price targets on Google's stock by as much as $150 to as high as $800, with analysts also welcoming new Chief Financial Officer Ruth Porat's emphasis on disciplined spending.
6At the highest price target, Google will be valued at $545 billion.
7Apple was valued at about $740 billion as of Thursday's close.
8"She (Porat) is known to be tough as nails when it comes to expense management and I think the first quarter off the back, she is delivering," FBN Securities analyst Shebly Seyrafi told Reuters.
9"A lot of investors are comforted by the fact that her first quarter as CFO, reporting, she is delivering."
10Online video ads could be a $17 billion opportunity in the United States alone by 2017 and YouTube looks best positioned to benefit, Jefferies & Co analysts wrote in a note.
11Google said watch time for YouTube rose 60 percent in the second quarter and the video service roped in more viewers aged 18-49 than any U.S. cable network on mobile alone.
12Google and Facebook are trying to woo big companies to market their products via online videos.
13YouTube has an edge over Facebook as it has a more mature monetization platform that seems to attract the best content, Piper Jaffray analyst Gene Munster said.
14Google said the gap was closing between mobile and desktop "cost per click" (CPC), or the average price of online ads.
15Mobile CPCs are likely to exceed desktop CPCs in the long term, Munster said.
16Google's CPCs fell 11 percent in the quarter but that is not the complete story, Jefferies analysts said.
17YouTube TrueView ads, where advertisers pay only when users don't skip an ad, cost less than traditional search ads.
18Google's fast rampup of YouTube monetization is also hurting pricing, they said.
19The company's advertising revenue rose 11 percent to $16.02 billion.
20The number of paid clicks increased 18 percent.
21"This growth has steadily held in the mid-to-high teens since at least Q1:13," RBC analyst Mark Mahaney said.
22"Such consistency is a rarity, especially for a behemoth business..."
23Of 48 analysts covering the stock, 38 have "buy" or higher rating, while 10 have "hold."
24The median price target is $672, according to Thomson Reuters data.
25Google's shares were up 15.5 percent at $695.13 in late morning trading on the Nasdaq.
26Facebook's shares also rose 5 percent to $95.30.
