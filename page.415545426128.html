12Schekun, along with his wife and three sons, are among the roughly 20,000 people who Ukrainian officials say have left Crimea for the mainland since the region was annexed by Russia.
13On March 9, one week before the referendum, plain-clothed policemen detained Schekun at a train station in the Crimean capital of Simferopol, as he awaited the delivery of Ukrainian flags and symbols.
14The group was from a self-described militia charged with ensuring the referendum on joining Russia proceeded smoothly.
15"They thought we wanted to derail their referendum," he said.
16"They thought we were some kind of terrorist group or something."

18Last week, a small group of Crimea residents marked the day of Ukraine's national poet Taras Shevchenko by gathering outside his statue in Simferopol with Ukrainian flags.
19That minor act of defiance earned them 40 hours hard labor.
20The peninsula's Tatar minority, whose leadership resisted Russian annexation, says it too has been subjected to intimidation that has ranged from petty official harassment to beatings, kidnappings and murder.

4At age 15, the London-born Fraser briefly became a member of John Mayall & the Bluesbreakers.
5The group functioned as a training ground for young British rockers including Eric Clapton and Rolling Stones guitarist Mick Taylor.
6Within a year, Fraser became a founding member of Free.
7The band's most prominent member was singer and guitarist Paul Rodgers, who would also go on to front Bad Company and The Firm.
8The band's biggest hit by far was 1970's "All Right Now," which remains one of the defining hits of classic rock radio.
9Fraser also produced the track and plays a bass solo on it.
10The song is also a staple of football stadiums.
11The Stanford University and University of Southern California marching bands each play it at virtually every game.
12Fraser kept playing music for most of his life, but he would never equal the success he had as a teen with Free, which broke up in 1972.
13He and Rodgers took the stage together to play "All Right Now" at Woodstock '94, the reboot of the classic music festival.
