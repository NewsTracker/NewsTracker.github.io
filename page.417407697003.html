Schools reopen in quake-hit areas of Nepal

1KATHMANDU, Nepal (AP) - Thousands of schools across the districts worst hit by two earthquakes in Nepal have reopened.
2With most buildings damaged or unsafe, classes are being held in temporary classrooms that were ordered by the Education Ministry.
3In the worst-hit districts of Gorkha, Sindhupalchok and Nuwakot, it is estimated that more than 90 percent of schools were destroyed.
4The two earthquakes on April 25 and May 12 killed 8,693 people and injured 22,221 others.
5Almost 24,000 classrooms were damaged or destroyed.
6According to UNICEF, Nepal's high dropout rate was already a major concern.
7Around 1.2 million children between the ages 5 and 16 have either never attended school or have dropped out.
