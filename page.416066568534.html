All African nations say they?ll vote for FIFA?s Blatter

1Dutch Football Federation head Michel van Praag, attends the 37th Confederation of African Football (CAF) Ordinary General Assembly on April 7, 2015 in the Egyptian capital Cairo.
2Outgoing FIFA president Sepp Blatter, whose leadership has been plagued by scandal, has headed FIFA since 1998, and is now competing against three rivals for the top world football job -- Jordanian Prince Ali bin Al Hussein, Dutchman Michel van Praag and former Portugal star Luis Figo.
3Sepp Blatter (center) was re-elected FIFA president in 2002, 2007 and 2011.
4Every one of Africa's 54 member countries will vote for Sepp Blatter in next month's FIFA presidential election, the continent's soccer boss said Tuesday, referring to the 79-year-old Swiss as "dear Sepp."
5In front of Blatter's three challengers, Issa Hayatou, president of the Confederation of African Football, promised unanimous support in his speech to open CAF's annual congress in Cairo.
6African soccer leaders applauded his pledge in apparent agreement.
7"Africa is comfortable having you. Africa stays with you," Hayatou said to Blatter, according to a transcript of his comments provided by CAF.
8Blatter was present in his role as FIFA president, and made a speech to the delegates, in which he praised Hayatou as "a pillar" of African soccer, according to CAF.
9"I'm a happy man," Blatter said.
10Africa is the largest of the six continental confederations and has 54 of the 209 FIFA member countries eligible to vote in the presidential election in Zurich on May 29.
11Although the individual countries don't have to follow Hayatou's directive, the continent is expected to be a strong base of support for Blatter when he seeks a fifth term to extend a presidency that began in 1998.
12FIFA vice president Prince Ali bin al-Hussein of Jordan, Dutch FA head Michael van Praag, and former Portugal international Luis Figo are challenging him.
13The three challengers didn't address CAF delegates as they did at UEFA's congress and attended the CAF congress only as "observers," CAF said.
14Blatter backs Israel: The FIFA president says he does not want to see Israel suspended from world soccer for allegedly hampering teams in Palestine and will try to make sure it doesn't happen at FIFA's congress next month.
15Palestine's football federation has accused Israel of restricting the movement of Palestinian players between Gaza and the West Bank and has said it will submit a proposal for Israel's suspension at the FIFA congress in Zurich at the end of next month.
16Blatter said suspending a national federation for any reason "is always something which harms the whole organization."
17Blatter said he was due to meet Palestine football federation President Jibril Rajoub in Cairo later Tuesday.
