Canadian Parliament backs air strikes on Islamic State in Syria

1The House of Commons approved the plan 142-129.
2The result was never in doubt, since the ruling Conservatives have a majority in the chamber.
3The vote also approved the extension of Canada's six-month mission by a year to the end of March 2016.
4Canada has around 70 special forces troops in northern Iraq and six Canadian jets are taking part in U.S.-led bombing attacks against Islamic State in Iraq.
5Prime Minister Stephen Harper, who has made security a main plank of his platform in the run-up to an October election, last week said Canada needed to strike against Islamic State safe havens in Syria.
6Polls show Harper will have a tough time retaining power in October.
7The New Democrats and the Liberals - the two main opposition parties - say Canadian attacks in Syria will only help prop up President Bashar al-Assad.
