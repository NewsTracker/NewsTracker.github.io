Wendell Holmes, singer, songwriter of Holmes Brothers, dies

1Wendell Holmes, the guitarist, pianist and singer and songwriter of the soul and blues band The Holmes Brothers, has died.
2Paul Kahn, Holmes' manager, says the 71-year-old musician died Friday at his home in Rosedale, Maryland.
3Kahn says Holmes died of complications from pulmonary hypertension.
4Holmes and his older brother, bassist Sherman Holmes, played churches in Virginia when they were growing up, and after high school, played professionally in New York.
5Drummer Willie "Popsy" Dixon started working with the brothers and they played the bar circuit.
6The three formed The Holmes Brothers in 1979.
7Dixon died in January.
8Kahn says Wendell Holmes was a natural musical talent, and said his most popular song was the soul ballad "We Meet, We Part, We Remember."
