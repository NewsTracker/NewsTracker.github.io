Health24.com | Breakthrough 15-minute Ebola test gets the nod

1The World Health Organisation has approved the first rapid test for Ebola, it said on Friday.
2The 15-minute test, developed by US firm Corgenix Medical, is less accurate than the standard test but is easy to perform, does not require electricity, and can give results within 15 minutes, WHO spokesperson Tarik Jasarevic said.
3"It's a first rapid test. It's definitely a breakthrough," he said.
4The standard laboratory test has a turnaround time of 12-24 hours.
5While the Corgenix test is not failsafe, it could quickly identify patients who need quarantine and make it much easier to verify rapidly any new outbreaks.
6Procurement and roll-out of the test kits will not begin immediately because the company is still working out costing and needs a week or two more to finish administrative procedures with the US Food and Drug Administration, Jasarevic said.
7The health charity Medecins Sans Frontieres, which has been at the forefront of the fight against Ebola, had expressed an interest, he said.
8The so-called ReEBOV Antigen Rapid Test involves putting a drop of blood on a small paper strip and waiting 15 minutes for a reaction in a test tube.
9It is able to correctly identify about 92 percent of Ebola infected patients and 85 percent of those not infected with the virus, the WHO said.
10Knowing that margin of error is a major help, said Robyn Meurant, from the WHO's department of essential medicines and health products.
11"The big fear has been that the market gets flooded with tests of unknown quality, or unknown performance, and with Ebola you need to know what are the limitations. A false positive has enormous implications. So does a false positive," she said.
12"So this is not a perfect test but... for a rapid test, (it is) not too bad at all."
13It would be especially useful if a cluster of suspected cases flared up, she said.
14"If you had five patients with suspected symptoms and you went ahead and tested them and they were all positive you'd have a high degree of confidence that you've got Ebola."
15Because of the margin of error, the Corgenix test would then need to be followed up by the standard laboratory test.
16The WHO is still assessing four or five other rapid test candidates, but Meurant said it was not possible to say when the next approval might be.
17South African health workers who are on their way to help turn the tide on Ebola in Sierra Leone have only one concern - and that is contracting the virus.
18A second group of South African health workers is packing for Sierra Leone where they'll seek to help bring the Ebola outbreak under control.
19Ebola survivors in Liberia are struggling with many side effects caused by the virus - a condition that doctors are now referring to as "post-Ebola syndrome."
