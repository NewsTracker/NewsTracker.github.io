Egypt policeman killed in clashes with Morsi backers

1One policeman was killed and five others wounded in clashes with supporters of Egypt's ousted Islamist president Mohamed Morsi in a Nile Delta city, police said on Wednesday.
2Clashes erupted late Tuesday when police arrested a group of Morsi supporters, including 13 women, who were demonstrating in a nightime rally in a key square in Damietta, around 50 kilometres (30 miles) west of Port Said.
3Police said an officer who was hit by birdshot during the clashes died later on Tuesday.
4Five other policemen, including an officer, were also wounded in the fighting.
5The health ministry said a total of 22 people were wounded in the clashes.
6Supporters of Morsi have regularly attempted to stage rallies demanding his reinstatement, but their demonstrations have lost momentum amid a deadly government crackdown.
7Hundreds have died and thousands have been jailed in the crackdown targeting Morsi's supporters since his ouster in July 2013.
8Morsi was ousted by then army chief President Abdel Fattah al-Sisi after mass street protests against his sole year of divisive rule.
