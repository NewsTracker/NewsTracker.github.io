Germanwings plane disaster: co-pilot may have practised descent

1Germanwings pilot Andreas Lubitz.
2The co-pilot who crashed his Germanwings plane into the Alps on March 24, killing all 150 on board, may have 'practised' his descent hours earlier.
3According to German newspaper Bild, the co-pilot Andreas Lubitz rehearsed the descent on a flight from Dusseldorf to Barcelona earlier the same day.
4French emergency rescue services work at the site of the crash in March.
5The paper cites information from the French aviation safety authority BEA, recovered from the plane's data recorder.
6In an interim report to be published on Wednesday, the authority found there was "several minutes of controlled flight in a technically unjustified descent" during the A320's flight previous to the one from Barcelona that he crashed.
7The paper said the co-pilot may even have been trying to crash the plane on that previous flight.
8Prosecutors believe 27 year-old Lubitz locked the captain out of the cockpit and steered the plane into a mountain on the second flight back from Barcelona to Dusseldorf.
9German investigators found evidence the co-pilot had suffered depression and had researched suicide plans.
10The BEA report is due to be published in at midday, French time.
