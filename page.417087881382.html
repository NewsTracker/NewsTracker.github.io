CANNES WATCH: Jeff Koons work to be auctioned off for charity

1FILE - In this April 23, 2010 file photo, artist Jeff Koons poses for photographers at the Pinchuk Art Center in Kiev, Ukraine.
2Jeff Koons has agreed to auction off his "Coloring Book" sculpture at the amfAR gala during the 68th Cannes international film festival.
3Jeff Koons is about to generate some major cash for AIDS charity amfAR: He's agreed to auction off his massive "Coloring Book" sculpture for the group's annual gala outside Cannes this week.
4The sculpture, which has been on display in London and in Beverly Hills, is made of mirror-polished stainless steel with transparent color coating.
5It is part of Koons' "Celebration" series, which also includes "Balloon Dog" - sold for $58 million in 2013 as the most expensive artwork by a living artist.
6Organizers at amfAR are hoping "Coloring Book" can generate similar numbers for its Thursday gala at Hotel du Cap-Eden-Roc, just outside of Cannes in d'Antibes.
7The artwork will be on display for the guests, which include top celebrities, in case they want to check it out before they purchase it.
8Last year Damien Hirst's gilded woolly mammoth art piece raised $15 million for the charity.
9The annual amfAR "Cinema Against AIDS" gala is the glitziest event during the Cannes Film Festival.
