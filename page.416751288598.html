Nepal earthquake: Australian Tyronne White last seen in village swallowed by mud

1BBC footage from Nepal shows the almost complete devastation of a remote village in the Langtang region following the 7.8 magnitude earthquake in April.
2Nestled in a valley to the north of Kathmandu, the small village of Langtang was one of the most beautiful places in the Himalayas.
3Now, it no longer exists.
4The first footage taken from the air shows a sea of brown mud, ice and debris has swallowed the 55 hotels and guesthouses, as well as villagers' homes, that once stood on the peaceful valley floor.
5Ten days after the devastating 7.8-magnitude quake rumbled through the mountains, Langtang is thought to be the worst-hit village in Nepal.
6Langtang village, before the earthquake hit.
7And it was in that village that NSW man Tyronne White, 44, was last seen by a fellow traveller, two days before the earthquake struck on April 25.
8The pair shared lunch, and Mr White, originally from Taree, spoke of how he was planning to head further into the mountains, hiking on his own.
9"We're facing facts, and it's not looking good. I'm not sleeping, we're a mess," said Mr White's cousin, Marc Wade Patek, from Sydney.
10Mr White's mother and father, Margaret and Douglas, and his brother, Brett, were sick with worry, and Brett was considering flying to Nepal to start searching for his brother, Mr Patek said.
11Nepali police and local volunteers have found the bodies of about 100 trekkers and villagers buried in the avalanche set off by the earthquake in Langtang.
12They were digging through snow and ice for signs of dozens more missing, officials said overnight.
13The trekkers' bodies were recovered on Saturday and Sunday in the village, 60 kilometres north of Kathmandu, which is on a trekking route popular with westerners.
14"Local volunteers and police personnel are digging through six-feet (deep) snow with shovels looking for more bodies," said Gautam Rimal, assistant chief district officer in the area where Langtang is located.
15It was not clear how many people were in Langtang at the time of the avalanche but other officials said about 120 more people could be buried under the avalanche.
16Mr Patek said he and his family had registered Mr White as missing in Nepal with Australian authorities immediately after the earthquake occurred.
17But he and his family had still not received one phone call from authorities in Australia, Mr Patek said.
18He said Brett had called an Australian consular official in Nepal, and was told the consulate could offer him a tent, a sleeping bag and the phone numbers of two recommended trekkers if he travelled there.
19"Not one person in any authority in Australia has picked up the phone to his mum and dad or me, not one," Mr Patek said.
20"At least one person could have guided us through this. I don't expect a call from the Prime Minister, but at least a call from someone saying: 'We're on it'."
21Worsening the stress for the family, Mr Patek said, was the fact that authorities in Nepal had begun asking foreign teams to wrap up some operations, as hope of finding people alive in the rubble in Langtang receded.
22Rameshwor Dangal, an official at Nepal's home ministry, told Reuters on Monday: "They [search teams] can leave. If they are also specialists in clearing the rubble, they can stay."
23A Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade spokeswoman said on Monday: "There are 1465 Australians, including permanent residents, in Nepal that have now been accounted for. It was believed that virtually every Australian had been accounted for, but we have recently become aware of further information indicating that not all Australians affected by the earthquake have been contacted or located.
24"We are concerned about Australians who may have been in the Langtang area and have deployed two Australian government officials to urgently confirm the welfare of Australians in this region."
25Mr Patek urged any Australians who had returned from Nepal and who had encountered Mr White in their travels, or who had any information about him, to contact authorities.
