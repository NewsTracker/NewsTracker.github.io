1The personal email systems that most of us use, such as Yahoo, Gmail or AOL, are extremely convenient but extremely unsafe.
2I have three email accounts.
3One is my personal email account, the second is my university email account and the third is an account with encryption capability that I use only for federal government consulting work.
4This third system stores all the attachments in my local machine and not on its server.
5I am extremely cautious in using this l system and use all the protection mechanisms I can and do not access the web from the machines I use for the government consulting work.
6As a result, this email system has not been hacked.
7But the personal email system that I use to communicate with friends and relatives has been hacked.
8Why are corporate or government email systems usually more secure?
9They usually have dedicated personnel focusing on security.
10They mandate that we change our passwords, encrypt sensitive emails and carry out penetration testing and monitor the systems for possible attacks.
11These systems also use the latest patches as much as possible.
12Attackers are aware that personal email systems store personal data on their servers.
13They know that the attachments on these systems are often not encrypted.
14Therefore they are in a better position to exploit these vulnerabilities.
15Anyone who sends sensitive messages must use an email system that is secure and provided by a company or the government.
16That goes for Hillary Clinton and for you and me.
