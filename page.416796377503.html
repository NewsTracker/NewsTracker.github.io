Mexico blames drug gangs for string of killings near border

1HERMOSILLO, Mexico (AP) - Authorities in the northern Mexican state of Sonora say drug cartel groups are behind a string of murders in a disputed corridor along the U.S. border.
2At least 11 people have been killed since Friday around the town of Sonoyta, which borders Arizona.
3State Public Security Secretary Ernesto Munro Palacios said Wednesday that the gangs are trying to control the area to run drugs into the United States.
4He did not specify which groups are fighting.
5But handwritten signs were hung on footbridges this week in the state capital, Hermosillo, purporting to name gang leaders who ordered the killings.
6The groups are believed to be affiliated with the Sinaloa cartel.
7Most of the dead have been identified as coming from the state of Sinaloa, which borders Sonora to the south.
