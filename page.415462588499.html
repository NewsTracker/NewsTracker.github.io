Live updates: North Island braces for cyclone Pam

110pm: Despite reports of heavy gusts across the Northland and Whangarei areas, police and the Fire Service have told ONE News they haven't responded to any weather-related incidents.
2Gale force winds are expected to reach Waikato and Hawke's Bay by early Monday morning and police say they're bracing for a rough night ahead.
39.15pm: MetService has issued another severe weather warning for severe southeast gales and heavy rain for northeastern parts of the North Island tonight and Monday.
4The warning is for Bay Of Plenty Rotorua, Gisborne, Hawke's Bay, Northland, Coromandel Peninsula and Auckland.
5Cyclone Pam, currently 500km northeast of Northland, is forecast to track southeastwards tonight and pass east of New Zealand during Monday and Tuesday.
6Strong southeast winds and rain are expected over much of the North Island tonight and Monday, along with extremely large seas about the east coast.
7Around 200mm of rain is expected to accumulate about Gisborne, the ranges of northern Hawke's Bay and eastern Bay of Plenty, with lesser but still significant amounts near the coast of northern Hawke's Bay and the hills and ranges further south.
8Also, around 90mm of rain is expected to accumulate about eastern Northland in an 18 hour period.
9Rivers and streams in these areas will rise rapidly, and localised surface flooding and slips are likely as well as hazardous driving conditions, MetService says.
10Severe southeast gales are likely about Eastern Northland, Great Barrier Island and Coromandel Peninsula until early Monday morning, and about northern Hawke's Bay, Gisborne and Bay of Plenty for much of Monday, with gusts of 120 km/h in exposed places, but 160 km/h or possibly more about the eastern Bay of Plenty and northern parts of Gisborne.
11Winds of this strength are likely to cause damage to trees and powerlines and could lift roofs and make for hazardous driving conditions, MetService says.
128.55pm: Nearly half the population of the tiny Pacific nation of Tuvalu have been severely impacted by the devastation wrought by Cyclone Pam, Prime Minister Enele Sopoaga says.
13While the focus has been on its larger Pacific neighbour Vanuatu, Tuvalu, a grouping of nine coral atolls with a population of less than 11,000, is also struggling to cope, he told Radio New Zealand International, news agency AFP reports.
14"Forty five percent of the population of Tuvalu, most of whom are on the outer islands, have been affected, badly, severely affected," he said.
15Houses and crops were washed away.
16"We are worried about the aftermath in terms of hygiene and supplies of essential materials like food, medicine and water."
177.49pm: Steady rain is now falling in Auckland after light falls since mid-afternoon.
18Winds are slight.
197.40pm: The New Zealand Government is giving a further $1.5 million to help Vanuatu, $1 million of that to non-government aid agencies.
20More than 160 New Zealanders are registered with Foreign Affairs as being in Vanuatu.
21But with power and communications out, checking in with them is difficult.
22Ministry staff are now going to resorts and hotels to find them.
23There are 250,000 people on 65 different islands in Vanuatu and no one knows what impact the cyclone has had in remote areas.
24NZ Foreign Minister Murray McCully says: "We are looking at the prospect of 10,000 homeless."
25Vital supplies and people are taking off in a New Zealand Air Force Hercules to start what aid agencies say will be a massive rebuild effort.
267.23pm: It's estimated up to 90% of housing in Vanuatu's capital, Port Vila, has been seriously damaged by the cyclone.
27It's estimated more than 2000 people are in evacuation centres, but that number is expected to rise as locals have nowhere to go.
28Amongst the damage are hotels many of which have closed their doors.

33"We're expecting to see swells of around eight metres inundating part of the coastal state highway and potentially causing problems," says ONE News reporter Rebecca Edwards.
34"But the good news is most residents we've spoken to today are really heeding the call to be prepared."
35With Northland first in the firing line, residents are preparing to hunker down for a stormy night ahead.
36Further south in Gisborne, locals have been stocking up on emergency supplies - water, batteries and candles at the top of most lists.

38So contractors and engineers are already on standby to deal with whatever Pam may bring.
39"Our key message is first treat all lines as alive at all times, so don't go touching them even if they've fallen down. Leave them alone," says Brent Stewart, Eastland network head.
406.26pm: The Far North has been first to feel the effects of Cyclone Pam.
41ONE NEWS reporter Chris Chang, on the coast at Ruakaka just south of Whangarei, says the wind and rain has really whipped up there over the past few hours.
42"In Kaeo, the wind is nudging up to 117 km/h. Overnight here we're expecting those severe gales could get even stronger - 120km/h. And we're expecting to see 100mm of rain."
43The land in the area is quite dry, meaning there is the potential for some flooding for low lying areas, Chang reports.
44The sea swells could reach six metres, "so they'll be keeping a close eye on high tide which should be around 4.30am."
45The cyclone will pass closest to Gisborne and the East Cape tomorrow.
465.48pm: Spark New Zealand has geared up its emergency preparations in advance of Cyclone Pam affecting parts of New Zealand and has generators on standby to deploy on affected cell sites as quickly as possible.
47Based on advice from Civil Defence, it is anticipated there may be power cuts in the areas most affected by the storm, which will impact both fixed line and mobile services.
48Given the fact that the storm is likely to be at its worst overnight tonight, and that many of the cellsites likely to be affected are in remote, hard to reach locations, Spark says it may not be possible for their field service teams to get generators in place until daylight hours tomorrow.

535.14pm: At least eight people are confirmed dead in Vanuatu after the massive cyclone and the death toll is likely to rise much higher once communications are restored with outlying islands, aid workers say.
54Chloe Morrison, a World Vision emergency communications officer in Port Vila, says officials from Vanuatu's National Disaster Management Office confirmed to her agency that at least eight people died in and around the capital, Port Vila.
55Officials have yet to assess the damage in many of the hard-hit outer islands because communications and power remain cut, she says.
56Ms Morrison says she has heard reports of entire villages being destroyed in more remote areas.
574.45pm: Health is emerging as a major concern in Vanuatu where power outages have hit the main hospital after Cyclone Pam which is feared to be one of the region's worst natural disasters.
58Oxfam Country Director in Port Vila, Colin Collet van Rooyen, says this not only affects patient care but also temperature-sensitive medications and vaccines, which need refrigeration.
59Mr Collet van Rooyen says it was one of the problems that came up at a meeting between the High Commissioners of Australia and New Zealand and Vanuatu's high levels of government at the National Disaster Management Office today.
60Water sanitation and hygiene at the shelters as well as the need for temporary mortuary facilities also emerged as major concerns at the meeting, Mr Collet van Rooyen said.
61Plans around co-ordinated aid and recovery are expected to be announced shortly.
