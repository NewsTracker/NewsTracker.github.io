Cannes Pays Tribute To Ingrid Bergman

1The 2015 Cannes film festival is paying tribute to Hollywood star Ingrid Bergman ahead of what would have been her 100th birthday this year.
2Organisers have unveiled the official poster which shows a black and white photograph of the leading lady of the Golden Age who starred in classics such as Casablanca, Notorious and Spellbound.
3Bergman was president of the Cannes jury in 1973.
4The poster "captures the actress ... in all her beauty, her face lit up by a calm serenity that seems to herald a promising future", festival organisers said in a statement.
5The actress died from breast cancer in 1982, aged 67.
6The festival, which runs from 13-24 May, will be presided over by American writer and director brothers Joel and Ethan Coen.
7Bergman's daughter, actress and director Isabella Rossellini, will head the Un Certain Regard jury, which allows for an alternative forum for films competing for the festival's coveted Palme d'Or prize.
8This year 19 films will be competing for the award.
