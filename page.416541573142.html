Orioles get 20 hits in 18-7 rout of Red Sox (Yahoo Sports)

1BALTIMORE (AP) -- Delmon Young drove in five runs, Chris Davis homered and the Baltimore Orioles received a bounce-back pitching performance from Bud Norris in an 18-7 victory over the Boston Red Sox on Sunday.
2Baltimore took control with a six-run third inning and led 12-0 in the sixth.
3Davis, Jimmy Paredes and Steve Pearce each contributed three RBIs to the Orioles' second straight win following a five-game skid.
4It was Baltimore's most prolific offensive showing since an 18-9 win over Cleveland on April 19, 2006, and the most runs the Orioles ever scored against Boston.
5Baltimore finished with 20 hits, three apiece by Young, Davis, Paredes and Rey Navarro.
6Norris (1-2) allowed three runs and seven hits in 6 2-3 innings.
7The right-hander came in with a 17.42 ERA after allowing 20 earned runs in his first three starts.
8In this one, Norris didn't allow a runner past second base until Pablo Sandoval hit a three-run drive in the seventh, his first home run with Boston since signing as a free agent in November.
9Hanley Ramirez followed Sandoval's long ball with a solo shot off Jason Garcia and added a three-run homer off Garcia in the ninth.
10Boston starter Wade Miley (1-2) gave up six earned runs and five hits in 2 1-3 innings, the second time in four starts this season the left-hander failed to go three innings.
11After scoring an unearned run in the second inning, Baltimore sent 10 batters to the plate in the third.
12Miley issued two straight four-pitch walks to force in a run, then gave up an RBI single to Young before Davis hit a liner that glanced off the glove of center fielder Mookie Betts for a two-run double.
13Pearce chased Miley with a run-scoring bloop single and Paredes capped the uprising with an RBI grounder.
14Young singled in two runs in the fourth to make it 9-0.
15Navarro had an RBI single in the fifth and Davis hit a two-run drive in the sixth.
16Boston closed to 12-4 in the seventh, but Baltimore answered with six runs in the bottom half, the key hit a two-run double by Young.
17Red Sox: RHP Matt Barnes, recalled Saturday after OF Shane Victorino (hamstring) went on the DL, was optioned to Triple-A Pawtucket.
18RHP Heath Hembree was recalled to take his place.
19Orioles: SS J.J. Hardy (left shoulder strain) will need only three or four rehabilitation games after he's ready to go on assignment, manager Buck Showalter said.
20Red Sox: Boston RHP Joe Kelly (1-0, 4.08 ERA) faces his fourth different AL East foe this season, at home against Toronto on Monday night.
21Orioles: Baltimore plays its first game against a non-division opponent, sending Ubaldo Jimenez to the mound Monday night against the visiting Chicago White Sox.
