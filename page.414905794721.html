Christian bishop: I forgive ISIS

White House Looks To Undercut Netanyahu Speech

1Twenty-one Coptic Christians beheaded on a beach in Libya.
2Christians across the world expressed horror and outrage at the mass killing, with Pope Francis calling the victims "martyrs" whose violent deaths should unite the fractured Christian community.
3Bishop Angaelos, general bishop of the Coptic Orthodox Church in the UK.
4Remarkably, though, a leading Coptic Christian bishop says that he has already forgiven the ISIS soldiers who slaughtered his fellow Copts.
5Bishop Angaelos, head of the Coptic Orthodox Church in the United Kingdom, was in Washington on Friday for the swearing-in of the United States' new ambassador-at-large for religious freedom, David Saperstein.
6Angaelos spoke to CNN about why ISIS targets Coptic Christians, and why he forgives them, even as he rejects their horrific acts.
7Q: What was the first thing that came to your mind when you heard about ISIS killing the Coptic Christians?
8A: I suppose I had a sense of the inevitable happening.
9We knew that it was going to happen by the way ISIS was sending out information about the captives, parading them on video.
10It was all very orchestrated.
11It was shocking to see how that could happen today in the 21st century.
12But we also saw strength and bravery in those men, and the number of messages and calls we've received in solidarity, the condolences and support has been overwhelming.
13This crime is not just a crime against Coptic Christians.
14It is a crime against humanity, and if there's anything we should stand for as human beings, first and foremost it's the sanctity of all human life.
15Q: Not long after the video released, you tweeted about the killings, using the hashtag #FatherForgive.
16Did you mean that you forgive ISIS?
17A: Yes.
18It may seem unbelievable to some of your readers, but as a Christian and a Christian minister I have a responsibility to myself and to others to guide them down this path of forgiveness.
19We don't forgive the act because the act is heinous.
20But we do forgive the killers from the depths of our hearts.
21Otherwise, we would become consumed by anger and hatred.
22It becomes a spiral of violence that has no place in this world.
23Q: You and many others had publicly prayed for ISIS to release the Copts and yet they were killed in a brutal manner.
24How do you maintain faith in God or in prayer after such a horrific outcome?
25A: I learned a long time ago that when one prays, one prays for the best outcome, not knowing what that outcome would be.
26Of course, I prayed that they would be safe.
27But I also prayed that, when the moment came, they would have the peace and strength to be able to get through it.
28It doesn't change my view of God that these 21 men died in this way.
29They were sacrificed, but so much has come out of it.
30They brought the imminent dangers to marginalized peoples, not just Christians, but Yazidis and others in the Middle East, to the attention of the whole world.
31Q: This is not the first time ISIS had targeted Coptic Christians.
32Why do you think that is?
33A: They made accusations in the video that Coptic crusaders were forcing women to convert to Christianity, which is totally unfounded and untrue.
34Coptic Christians were not part of the original Crusades and are not part of any crusade now.
35We have lived peacefully and never taken up arms or forcibly converted anyone.
36But violent acts need to be justified and this is the justification they found.
37Q: You're in Washington today meeting with U.S. leaders.
38What should they do about ISIS' persecution of Christians and other religious communities?
39A: I would like to see us all start to work towards human rights generally, because when we're divided into different departments or organizations any change will be fragmented.
40If you look at the rights of every individual, God-given rights, we can all start to work together and safeguard any people who are persecuted anywhere.
41Of course, the vast majority of persecution falls squarely right now on Christians in the Middle East and that needs to be addressed.
42But, as a Christian, I will never be comfortable just safeguarding the rights of Christians.
43We need to help everyone.
