2Q: I'm in the process of outfitting a second home and am considering going TV-free there.
3(Radical, I know.)
4We'll have a desktop computer that we can stream movies to, but we don't want it in the living room, nor do we want to have to watch around a desk.
5Any solutions?
6A: How about using your monitor as a TV in the living room but leaving your computer behind?
7The tidiest way to pull this off is with a tiny streaming video player, like the Roku Streaming Stick, Google Chromecast or Amazon Fire TV Stick.
8These cost between $35 and $50 and stream videos from Netflix, Amazon, HBO Go and other popular services directly to the monitor.
9With Roku and Amazon, you even get a small remote control.
10But you'll need a monitor that has an HDMI input (most newer models do) as well as built-in speakers.
11Although speakers aren't a feature most people look for when shopping for a monitor, they are handy to have.
12The advantage of this setup is that it feels like a TV rather than a computer.
13With a remote in front of you instead of a keyboard, you won't be constantly reminded of all the emails you've left unanswered as you enjoy your favorite flick.
14And the screen will be bigger than a tablet or laptop.
15This self-contained system is also fairly easy to move.
16You can reduce the number of screens in your space by toting the monitor from a desk to the kitchen counter or a coffee table.
17Although most new monitors with speakers would work, there are a couple of models worth highlighting.
18The 34-inch LG 34UC97 ($1,299), for example, has a curved, ultrawide screen that's tailor-made for movie-watching (and working on spreadsheets).
19It has a USB port on the back to power your streaming media player.
20(If your monitor doesn't have a powered USB port, you have to run a cord from the player to an electrical outlet).
21The monitor weighs a manageable 20 pounds.
22If you're looking for something smaller and less expensive, consider the Asus MX279H.
23It has a 27-inch screen and less-powerful speakers than the LG, but it weighs about 9 pounds less, produces a great image and sells for under $299.
