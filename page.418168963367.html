US stocks open higher as hopes rise for late deal on Greece

1U.S. stocks are rising broadly in early trading as investors hope for a last-minute deal on Greece's debt woes.
2The rebound Tuesday came a day after U.S. markets had their biggest plunge of the year.
3The Dow Jones industrial average rose 97 points, or 0.6 percent, to 17,691 as of 9:35 a.m. Eastern time Tuesday.
4The Standard & Poor's 500 index gained 14 points, or 0.7 percent, to 2,071.
5The Nasdaq composite climbed 35 points, or 0.7 percent, to 4,994.
6Energy stocks rose more than the rest of the market as the price of crude oil increased.
7Exxon Mobil rose the most in the Dow.
8European markets were mostly higher.
9Bond prices fell.
10The yield on the 10-year Treasury note rose to 2.37 percent.
