James scores 31, Cavs beat Celts 103-95 for 3-0 series lead (Yahoo Sports)

1BOSTON (AP) -- LeBron James got to the arena early.
2When he left the Cleveland Cavaliers had a chance to put an early end to the series.
3James scored 31 points in his first playoff game in Boston since a masterful performance in the 2012 postseason.
4And Cleveland moved one win away from a sweep in its first playoff series in five years with a 103-95 victory Thursday night.
5James arrived an hour early at the morning shootaround, accompanied by teammate J.R. Smith.
6It was a sign of his leadership, according to coach David Blatt.
7Smith had his best game of the series with 15 points.
8''We were together last night watching playoff games,'' James said.
9Cleveland can complete the sweep of the best-of-seven series Sunday in Boston.
10No NBA team has won a series after trailing 3-0.
11''You want to make sure that we play as well as possible for however many games we have left,'' Celtics coach Brad Stevens said.
12Kevin Love added 23 points for Cleveland, none bigger than two 3-pointers after Boston cut the lead to 95-92 on Evan Turner's 3-pointer with 2:45 left.
13Those shots put Cleveland up 101-93 with 26 seconds to go.
14''Kevin has been great,'' Blatt said.
15''Maybe he didn't score as much in the second game as he did tonight, or in the first game, but he's been engaged at both ends'' of the court.
16Turner had 19 points and Avery Bradley added 18 for Boston while Isaiah Thomas managed just five points and missed seven of nine shots after scoring 22 in the first two games.
17''They're paying him a lot of attention,'' Stevens said.
18It was James' first postseason game in Boston since Game 6 of the 2012 Eastern Conference finals when he was with Miami.
19He had 45 points and 15 rebounds in that game to avoid elimination.
20The Heat won Game 7 at home then beat Oklahoma City for James' first NBA title.
21On Thursday he was booed nearly every time he touched the ball in the first half.
22The Celtics led 48-44 before the Cavaliers closed with a 12-0 run for a 56-44 lead.
23In the last 40 seconds, James had six points and a steal.
24Boston tied it at 56 by scoring the first eight points of the third quarter.
25Then Cleveland went on another spurt and took a 77-66 lead on a 3-pointer by Kyrie Irving with just under 4 minutes left in the period.
26The Cavaliers led 84-76 after three quarters before James opened the fourth by scoring Cleveland's first seven points for a 91-82 lead then fed Tristan Thompson with an alley-oop pass.
27Cleveland is in its first playoff series since 2010 when it lost the Eastern Conference semifinals to Boston in six games.
28Two months later, James left for the Heat and led them to the NBA Finals in all four of his seasons, winning two championships before returning to the Cavaliers this season.
29James finished with 11 rebounds and just two turnovers after committing 11 in the first two games.
30Love had nine rebounds.
