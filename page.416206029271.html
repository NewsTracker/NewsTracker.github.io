Uninsured rate drops again under ObamaCare

1The Gallup-Healthways survey released Monday finds that the rate of people lacking health insurance fell one percentage point, from 12.9 percent, at the end of 2014.
2Since ObamaCare's coverage expansion went into effect at the beginning of 2014, the rate has fallen from 17.1 percent.
3Gallup notes that some of the decrease could also be due to the improving economy, but that the health law appears to be playing a major role.
4The rate is lower than its 14.6 percent mark at the beginning of 2008, which was before the economy crashed.
5The Obama administration has been using the declining numbers to argue that the law is working.
6It touted its estimate in March, based on Gallup data, that 16 million people had gained coverage because of the law.
7"The evidence shows that the Affordable Care Act is working, and families, businesses and taxpayers are better off as a result," HHS Secretary Sylvia Mathews Burwell said then.
8The coverage expansion faces a threat from the Supreme Court, which could gut the law by invalidating the subsidies that help about 7.5 million people afford coverage, in the case of King v.
9The data released Monday shows that the drop in the uninsured rate appears to be slowing.
10In the first enrollment period, in 2014, the rate fell 1.5 percentage points, but in this year's enrollment period, it fell one point.
11The uninsured rate among Hispanic people has dropped at a higher rate, down 8.3 points since the beginning of 2014.
12Hispanics have a higher uninsured rate, at 30.4 percent.
13The uninsured rate for African Americas dropped 7.3 percentage points to 13.6 percent.
14Burwell said in March that for African Americans and Latinos, the statistics are "not probably exactly where you want to be because the numbers are still high."
15She noted that she hit the road to boost enrollment, including a stop at a largely African-American church in Texas.
16The administration has also purchased advertising targeting hispanics, and has put messages on receipts at 7-Elevens.
