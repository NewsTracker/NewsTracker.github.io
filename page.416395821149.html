Israel PM Benjamin Netanyahu given 14 more days to form coalition government

1Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu during a visit of Speaker of the US House of Representatives, John Boehner to Jerusalem.
2Jerusalem: Despite his decisive electoral victory in March, Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu is struggling to form a government and on Monday was granted an extension to complete negotiations for a new ruling coalition.
3Since being tapped to put together the next government, Mr Netanyahu has held talks with the five conservative parties he refers to as his "natural partners."
4However, a month of negotiations has yet to yield an agreement with any of them to join a ruling coalition.
5Mr Netanyahu, who is seeking to form his fourth government since 1996, will have used the 28 days Israeli law allots a candidate for a first stab at securing a ruling coalition later this week.
6On Monday, he asked President Reuven Rivlin for the maximum legal extension of another 14 days, taking him to May 7.
7Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahuat the Wailing Wall in Jerusalem following his party Likud's victory in Israel's general election.
8Failure to form a government after that could result in one of his political rivals being asked to try or even result in new elections, though the latter is far less unlikely.
9Mr Netanyahu is pursuing a government with hawkish, religious and centrist parties that will give him a coalition representing at least 61 members of Israel's 120-seat parliament, or Knesset, though he hopes for the more comfortable backing of 67 lawmakers.
10His Likud party won 30 seats.
11Negotiations focus on reconciling conflicting demands and ideologies on a wide range of issues such as the settlements in the West Bank and East Jerusalem; policies on religion and economy; and the division of coveted appointments and the posts' budgets.
12The deadline pressure on Mr Netanyahu could drive up the political price demanded by each of his potential partners.
13He needs all five for a solid majority of 67 seats that would give his coalition a chance of surviving longer than his recent government, which collapsed after less than two years.
14In theory, the conservative leader still has the option to form a national unity government with opposition leader Isaac Herzog and his dovish Zionist Union party.
15Such a coalition could provide Israel with a more moderate face to show the international community, but both leaders have strongly rejected it as an unrealistic option.
16"We have made progress and are on the way to forming a government," Mr Netanyahu told Mr Rivlin on Monday.
17Mr Rivlin urged the prime minister to form a government as soon as possible to restore political stability.
18The new Knesset elected last month has already been sworn in and Mr Netanyahu's previous cabinet remains in place until it is replaced with a new one approved by the Knesset.
