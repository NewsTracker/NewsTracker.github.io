Greece says Germany owes it compensation for WWII occupation

1ATHENS, Greece (AP) " The radical left-led Greek government insisted Tuesday that the debt-ridden country has never been fully compensated by Germany for its brutal World War II Nazi occupation, linking the issue with Greece's fraught bailout negotiations.
2Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras said a 1960 reparation deal with Germany did not cover key Greek demands, including payments for wrecked infrastructure, war crimes and the return of a forced loan exacted from occupied Greece.
3Germany has repeatedly rejected previous approaches from Athens on further reparations, saying the question was settled by existing agreements.
4Relations between the two countries have soured considerably since Greece's acute financial crisis broke out in late 2009.
5Germany is a major contributor to the international rescue loans that have kept Greece afloat since 2010, and has been a keen proponent of the resented budget and income cuts demanded in exchange to rebuild the country's shattered finances.
6Tsipras told parliament that Greece will honor its obligations to bailout creditors, including Germany, but won't "abandon its irrevocable demands" for World War II reparations.
7His six-week-old government is trying to ease the terms of its rescue loan program.
8"We are not giving lessons in morality, but we will not accept lessons in morality either," Tsipras said during a debate on reviving a special parliamentary committee on demanding German war reparations.
9"We are prepared to offer every political and legal assistance to ensure that this committee's efforts bear fruit," he said.
10Greece has not specified how much compensation it could demand from Germany, and the results of an official report into the issue last year remain classified.
