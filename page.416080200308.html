ICC prosecutor: prospects slight of pursuing IS leaders

1In a statement on Wednesday, prosecutor Fatou Bensouda said she had jurisdiction over crimes committed by fighters who are nationals of ICC member states, but Islamic State's leaders appeared mainly to be from Iraq and Syria, which are not.
2"At this stage, the prospects of my office investigating and prosecuting those most responsible, within the leadership of ISIS, appear limited," she said.
3The court could exercise "personal jurisdiction" over individuals who were citizens of member states, she added.
4Wider jurisdiction could also be referred to The Hague by the U.N. Security Council.
5Many citizens of ICC member states are suspected of committed atrocities while fighting for Islamic State, including "Jihadi John", notorious for beheading hostages, who is believed to be a British citizen.
6Bensouda said the court had received reports of thousands of foreign fighters joining Islamic State, many from ICC member states, including Tunisia, Jordan, France, the United Kingdom, Germany, Belgium, the Netherlands and Australia.
7The reports of crimes sent to the court ranged from mass executions, sexual slavery, rape, torture, forced recruitment of children and even genocide, she said.
8"Some of these individuals (from member states) may have been involved in the commission of crimes against humanity and war crimes," she said.
