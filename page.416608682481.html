2 Separate Shootings Near Ferguson Protests Against Police

1FERGUSON, Mo.
2Looting, fires and gunfire broke out overnight in Ferguson during protests in response to the mysterious death of a black man in police custody in Baltimore.
3Several dozen people gathered Tuesday night on West Florissant Avenue, the site of several protests last summer and fall following the fatal shooting of 18-year-old Michael Brown, who was black and unarmed, by a white Ferguson police officer.
4The protests were more subdued Tuesday evening in Baltimore, where a curfew was imposed after rioting shook the city Monday following the death of Freddie Gray, a 25-year-old black man who died of a spinal-cord injury in police custody.
5In Ferguson, there were reports of two people being shot late Tuesday and early Wednesday, though it wasn't immediately clear if the shootings were linked to the protests.
6In one instance, a man was shot in the leg and bystanders carried him to safety, according to the St. Louis Post-Dispatch.
7Police arrested a suspect and recovered the gun.
8Details about the second shooting were not immediately available.
9Messages seeking comment from Ferguson officials were not returned.
10A gas station was looted in Dellwood, near Ferguson.
11KMOV-TV reported that about 50 people entered a Mobil station about 1:40 a.m. Wednesday.
12Trash cans and a portable toilet were set on fire in the middle of West Florissant.
13Some people threw rocks at police cars.
14There were no reports of officers being injured.
15Tensions have remained high in the St. Louis region in the nearly nine months since Brown was shot.
16A St. Louis County grand jury and the U.S. Justice Department declined to prosecute officer Darren Wilson, who resigned from the police force in November.
17On Monday, several protesters gathered in north St. Louis after police shot a suspect during a stolen car investigation.
18Police Chief Sam Dotson said 41-year-old Alfred Graves threatened to kill an officer before firing shots at police in an alleyway.
19At least three officers returned fire, and Graves was struck in the face, leg and arm.
20His injuries are not believed to be life-threatening.
