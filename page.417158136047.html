Ukraine suspends military cooperation with Russia

1Ukrainian President Petro Poroshenko leaves the presidential office before welcoming his Slovak counterpart in Kyiv, Ukraine, Wednesday, May 20, 2015.
2KIEV, Ukraine - Ukraine's parliament on Thursday voted to suspend military co-operation with Russia in a long-anticipated move signalling a further break in relations between the once-close partners.
3The five co-operation agreements scrapped by the Verkhovna Rada include one giving the Russian military transit rights to reach Moldova, whose territory is partly controlled by a Moscow-supported separatist government.
4Relations between Russia and Ukraine plummeted after the overthrow in February 2014 of Moscow-friendly Ukrainian President Viktor Yanukovych.
5Russia subsequently annexed Ukraine's Crimean Peninsula.
6Kyiv also accuses Moscow of arming and staffing separatist insurgencies in eastern Ukraine.
7In turning away from Russia, Ukraine has increasingly reached out for assistance to NATO, an organization the current government hopes the country will eventually join.
8Russia has about 1,500 troops stationed in Trans-Dniester, a landlocked separatist strip of Moldova that borders Ukraine.
9Rescinding the transit rights for those troops creates a logistical problem for Russia and no solution was immediately apparent.
10"As it now stands, we have to think about it, find a way. We shouldn't toss away Trans-Dniester and Moldova," said Vladimir Komoedov, chairman of the defence committee of the lower house of the Russian parliament, according to the Interfax news agency.
11But he said Russia wouldn't consider retaliatory measures for the time being.
