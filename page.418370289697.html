2Microsoft announced plans Wednesday to restructure the company's phone hardware business and to cut up to 7,800 positions.
3Microsoft (MSFT) announced plans Wednesday cut up to 7,800 positions and take a $7.6 billion write-down on its Nokia (Helsinki Stock Exchange: NOK-FI) business amid efforts to restructure its struggling phone hardware business.
4The technology company also said it would take a restructuring charge of $750 million to $850 million.
5"We are moving from a strategy to grow a standalone phone business to a strategy to grow and create a vibrant Windows ecosystem including our first- party device family," CEO Satya Nadella wrote in an email to employees.
6"In the near-term, we'll run a more effective and focused phone portfolio while retaining capability for long-term reinvention in mobility."
7The job cuts are in addition to the 18,000 positions Microsoft said it planned to cut a year ago.
