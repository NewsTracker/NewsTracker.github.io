German authorities detain Al Jazeera journalist at Egypt's request

1Photo: Al Jazeera journalist Ahmed Mansour was arrested on the request of Egypt while trying to board a flight to Doha.
2A high-profile Al Jazeera journalist has been arrested in Germany at the request of Egypt.
3Ahmed Mansour, a leading talk show host on the channel's Arabic service, has been remanded in custody after an extradition hearing before a German judge, Al Jazeera said.
4Mansour's arrest is the latest in a string of such cases brought by the Egyptian authorities against Al Jazeera journalists.
5Egypt accuses Al Jazeera of being a mouthpiece of the Muslim Brotherhood, the Qatar-backed Islamist movement which president Abdel Fattah al-Sisi toppled in 2013 when he was army chief and accuses of being a terrorist group.
6Both the television channel and the Brotherhood reject the allegations made by Egyptian authorities.
7"The German authorities told me that we are dealing with an international criminal case," Mansour told Al Jazeera by telephone.
8Cairo's criminal court sentenced Mansour, who has dual Egyptian and British citizenship, to 15 years in prison in absentia last year on the charge of torturing a lawyer in 2011 in Tahrir Square, the focus of the popular uprising that toppled veteran leader Hosni Mubarak.
9Al Jazeera said at the time the charge was false and an attempt to silence Mansour, known to viewers across the Arab world.
10Saad Djebbar, a lawyer for Al Jazeera, said Mansour was arrested unexpectedly on Saturday as he tried to board a Qatar Airways flight to Doha.
11"This is a very serious development," Mr Djebbar said.
12"We knew that the Egyptians were going to set such a trap to harass our journalists and that is what has happened."
13A spokesman for the German Federal Police said a 52-year-old man was arrested at Berlin's Tegel airport following an international arrest warrant from the Egyptian authorities.
14Egyptian foreign ministry spokesman Badr Abdelatty said Egypt was coordinating with the German authorities.
15"He is accused of a crime and was sentenced so of course we have called for him to be returned," he said.
16Critics accuse the West of turning a blind eye towards what they say is Egypt's crackdown on dissent and freedom of speech in favour of improved economic ties and regional security cooperation.
17Mr Sisi visited Germany earlier in June at chancellor Angela Merkel's invitation.
18The head of Germany's parliament cancelled a meeting with him, citing rights violations in Egypt.
19Deputy head of the Left Party in Germany's parliament Wolfgang Gehrcke on Sunday said he did not support Mansour's extradition.
20"Everyone knows that the rule of law in Egypt is built on sand - if you can even speak of a rule of law. Of course we can't extradite him. In countries where there is the death penalty, there should be no extraditions in general," he said.
21The Cairo government denies allegations of widespread human rights abuses.
22Egypt released Australian Al Jazeera journalist Peter Greste in February this year after 400 days in prison on charges that included aiding a terrorist group.
23Mohamed Fahmy, a naturalised Canadian who has given up his Egyptian citizenship, and Egyptian Baher Mohamed were released on bail in February after spending more than a year in custody.
24The journalists were originally sentenced to between seven and 10 years in prison on charges including spreading lies to help a terrorist organisation, which they have denied.
25Egypt's High Court ordered a retrial in January.
