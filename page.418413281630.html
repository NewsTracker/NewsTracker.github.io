Thousands stranded in Indonesian as volcano closes airports

1A volcanic eruption on Indonesia's main island of Java has sparked chaos for holidaymakers as airports close and international airlines cancel flights to tourist hotspot Bali, stranding thousands.
2Suantika said lava and ash fall from the 10,930-feet-high mountain on Indonesia's most densely populated island has also resulted in the government calling on people to stay away from a two-mile high-danger zone around the volcano.
3Evacuation of residents living near the volcano is still considered unnecessary, but authorities are urging people to wear masks.
4"Ash can clog engines and harm other parts of the aircraft," said Transport Ministry spokesman Julius Adravida Barata.
5"For the sake of passenger safety, we have to close five airports."
6Airports on the islands of Bali and Lombok as well as airports at Banyuwangi and Jember in East Java have been closed since late Thursday.
7All flights are canceled until late Friday, which could be extended if the ash worsens.
8Barata said thousands of travelers are stranded.
9The volcano has proven particularly problematic for Australians, who flock to Bali during Australia's school holidays.
10Dozens of flights between Australia and Bali's Denpasar airport by Australian carriers Jetstar and Virgin Australia have been cancelled over the past week, with the airlines citing safety concerns.
11Both carriers said they are looking into adding extra flights between Australia and Bali when conditions improve to help clear the backlog.
12Flights within Indonesia were already overbooked as tens of millions of the country's Muslims pour out of major cities to return to their villages during an annual mass exodus to celebrate the end of the Islamic holy month.
13Raung is among about 130 active volcanoes in Indonesia.
14The archipelago is prone to volcanic eruptions and earthquakes because of its location on the Pacific "Ring of Fire," a series of fault lines stretching from the Western Hemisphere through Japan and Southeast Asia.
