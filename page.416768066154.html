Stocks sag, ending a 2-day run; oil rises above $60 a barrel and jitters over Greece return

1NEW YORK - A combination of concerns knocked the U.S. stock market lower Tuesday, snapping a two-day run.
2Crude oil climbed above $60 a barrel for the first time this year, raising expectations for rising inflation and interest rates.
3Greece's government remained in a standoff with its European creditors as a debt payment looms next week.
4"There are some jitters," said Bill Stone, chief investment officer at PNC Asset Management Group.
5"Greece is definitely part of it. The other part is oil prices going up. That implies more inflation."
6Major indexes wavered at the outset of trading, drifted lower throughout the morning, then spent the afternoon slowly ceding ground.
7The Standard & Poor's 500 index lost 25.03 points, or 1.2 percent, to 2,089.46.
8The Dow Jones industrial average dropped 142.20 points, or 0.8 percent, to 17,928.20, while the Nasdaq composite fell 77.60 points, or 1.6 percent, to 4,939.33.
9The price of oil jumped $1.47 to close at $60.40 a barrel, the first time crude has traded that high since early December, following reports that a Libyan oil terminal had closed.
10Brent crude rose $1.07 to $67.52 in London.
11An impasse in talks between Greece and its lenders raised concerns about the country's ability to handle an upcoming debt payment.
12Greece will have to scrounge for cash to make a payment of 750 million euros (the equivalent of $840 million) to the International Monetary Fund due on May 12.
13Among companies turning in results on Tuesday, Kellogg reported that its quarterly earnings slumped 44 percent as a rising U.S. dollar took a bite out of sales.
14The maker of Frosted Flakes and Pop Tarts fell 95 cents, or 1 percent, to $63.18.
15Walt Disney's stock hit an all-time high after it delivered quarterly results that beat Wall Street's estimates, thanks, in part, to rising revenue from its Walt Disney World Resort and other theme parks.
16Last weekend, its "Avengers: Age of Ultron" had the second-biggest domestic opening behind the first "Avengers."
17Disney's stock gave up its early gain and ended the day slightly lower, down 22 cents at $110.81.
18The first-quarter earnings season has given investors little to celebrate, said Tim Dreiling, a senior portfolio manager at a division of U.S. Bank Wealth Management.
19Roughly seven out of every 10 companies in the S&P 500 have reported results that beat analysts' estimates for quarterly profit, according to S&P Capital IQ.
20Yet more than half have fallen short of revenue targets.
21"That's what is concerning," Dreiling said.
22Major markets in Europe slumped.
23France's CAC-40 sank 2.1 percent, while Germany's DAX fell 2.5 percent.
24Britain's FTSE 100 slipped 0.8 percent.
25European government bond prices also fell, shooting government borrowing costs up.
26The Shanghai Composite Index in mainland China had its worst day in months, losing 4.1 percent, in turn dragging down Hong Kong's Hang Seng 1.3 percent.
27Markets in Japan, South Korea and Thailand were shut for holidays.
28Back in the U.S., government bond prices fell, nudging the yield on the 10-year Treasury note up to 2.18 percent from 2.15 percent the day before.
29In commodities trading, gold rose $6.40 to end at $1,193.20 an ounce, while silver picked up 14 cents to $16.58 an ounce.
30Copper added 1 cent to $2.94 a pound.
31- Wholesale gasoline rose 2.9 cents to close at $2.063 a gallon.
32- Heating oil rose 3.6 cents to close at $2.015 a gallon.
33- Natural gas fell 4.1 cents to close at $2.780 per 1,000 cubic feet.
