6Apple (AAPL) is in rare territory, now almost 10 percent below its 50-day moving average, a 3.5 standard deviation (SD) move.
7That is very rare territory.
8It has only been in this range seven times, or 0.1 percent of all trading days since 1990, according to our partners at Kensho.
9In each of the seven times the next move has been higher, reverting to its mean.
10The average time before moving higher over those seven times was 1.9 days.
11Bank of America's downgrade of Apple today (on deceleration of iPhone) may put even more pressure on the stock, but the point is, it is due for a bounce.
12For those looking for a statistics primer: One standard deviation means there is a 68.2 percent chance it will stay within a given range from the mean, in this case the 50-day moving average.
13Two standard deviations means there is a 95 percent chance it will be within a given range; three standard deviations means it is 99.7 percent within a given range, and when you get toward four standard deviations...well, it doesn't happen very often, and that's what gets quants and other statistical types to sit up and take notice.
