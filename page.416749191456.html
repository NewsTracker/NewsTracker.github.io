Chirinos' sacrifice fly in 9th lifts Rangers past Astros (Yahoo Sports)

1HOUSTON (AP) -- Robinson Chirinos hit a tiebreaking sacrifice fly in the ninth inning, and the Texas Rangers snapped Houston's 10-game winning streak with a 2-1 win over the Astros on Monday night.
2Chirinos' sacrifice fly to center scored Adrian Beltre, who led off the inning with a double off Chad Qualls (0-2), moved to third on Kyle Blanks' single and slid in as Jake Marisnick's throw to the plate went to the backstop.
3Keone Kela (2-1) pitched the eighth for the win, and Neftali Feliz pitched the ninth for his third save.
4Houston scored in the first as Jose Altuve and George Springer drew walks, worked a double steal and Altuve scored on Evan Gattis' sacrifice fly to make it 1-0.
5Jake Smolinski tied it in the eighth with a one-out RBI single to center.
6Dallas Keuchel, who was named the AL pitcher of the month for April earlier in the day, allowed one run on five hits with a season-high eight strikeouts in eight innings.
7Keuchel retired nine straight during one stretch, including striking out the side in the second and third innings, and 14 out of 15 batters between the first and fifth innings.
8Other than his struggles in the first inning, Texas starter Ross Detwiler matched Keuchel, allowing one run and four hits with seven strikeouts in seven innings.
9Detwiler had his second consecutive solid outing after allowing 18 runs over 12 1-3 innings in his first three starts.
10Leonys Martin left the game in the fifth with a left wrist sprain and was going for precautionary X-rays.
11Martin injured his wrist while diving for Altuve's double in the third, but after the trainer and manager Jeff Banister came out to look at him, Martin stayed in.
12DeShields replaced Martin in center.
