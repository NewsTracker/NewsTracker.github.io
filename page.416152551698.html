Muslim Brotherhood leader, 13 others receive death sentence in Egypt

1An Egyptian court sentenced Mohamed Badie, leader of the outlawed Muslim Brotherhood, and 13 other senior members of the group to death for inciting chaos and violence, and gave a life term to a U.S.-Egyptian citizen for ties to the Brotherhood.
2The men were among thousands of people detained after freely elected Islamist president Mohammed Morsi was toppled in 2013 by the military under Abdel Fattah al-Sisi, who is now president.
3Sisi describes the Brotherhood as a major security threat.
4The group says it is committed to peaceful activism and had nothing to do with Islamist militant violence in Egypt since Morsi's fall following mass protests against his rule.
5Egypt's mass trials of Brotherhood members and people accused of links to the group, as well as its tough crackdown on Islamist and liberal opposition alike, have drawn international criticism of its judicial system and human rights record.
6The sentences, pronounced at a televised court session on Saturday, can be appealed before Egypt's highest civilian court in a process that could take years to reach a final verdict.
7U.S.-Egyptian citizen Mohamed Soltan was sentenced to life in jail for supporting the veteran Islamist movement and transmitting false news.
8He is the son of Brotherhood preacher Salah Soltan, who was among those sentenced to death.
9Mohamed Abdel-Mawgod, one of the defence lawyers, condemned the verdicts.
10"The court did not differentiate between the defendants and put them all in the same basket," he told reporters at the courthouse.
11None of the defendants were present during the hearing.
12Badie is the Brotherhood's general guide and has already been sentenced to several death and life sentences.
13His deputy Khairat El-Shater was given a life sentence on Saturday.
