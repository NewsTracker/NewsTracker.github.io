Egypt opens borders with Gaza for the first time in months

1RAFAH, Gaza Strip (AP) - Egypt has opened its borders with the Gaza Strip for the first time in months.
2The Rafah border crossing opened Saturday morning and will operate for three days, allowing Palestinians to travel in both directions.
3Maher Abu Sabha, head of Gaza's crossing, says 15,000 people have applied to exit.
4Rafah is Gaza's only gateway to the outside world with no Israeli control.
5Egypt has kept it mostly closed since the militant Hamas group seized control of the coastal strip in 2007.
6The closure worsened after Egypt's military ousted Islamist President Mohammed Morsi, a Muslim Brotherhood member, in 2013.
7Hamas is an offshoot of the Brotherhood.
8Egypt has opened the Rafah crossing in both directions only five days this year.
9Last month, it allowed the return of Palestinians stranded in Egypt.
