Islamic State said to advance further near ancient Palmyra

1BEIRUT, May 16 (Reuters) - Islamic State militants were reported to have gained ground against Syrian troops in fighting near the historic city of Palmyra on Saturday, a target in an offensive by the jihadist group that has raised concern for the U.N. world heritage site.
2The Syrian Observatory for Human Rights, a UK-based group that reports on the war, also said Islamic State militants had executed 23 people on Friday including nine minors and five women in areas seized from state control outside the city.
3Syrian government antiquities chief Maamoun Abdulkarim said fighting continued at 1-2 km (around a mile) from the city, which he said was still "firmly under government control".
4A military source said fighting was ongoing but at "a distance far from the city".
5Abdulkarim, speaking by telephone, added: "We are in the fourth or fifth day (of the attack). What is the international community doing? Is it waiting to weep and despair as it did in northern Iraq?"
6Palmyra, also known as Tadmur, is home to extensive ruins of one of the most important cultural centres of the ancient world.
7It was put on UNESCO's list of World Heritage in danger in 2013.
8Islamic State, which espouses a puritanical Islamist ideology, has destroyed antiquities and ancient monuments in Iraq.
9Palmyra is also of strategic importance, sitting at a highway intersection linking it to the cities of Homs and Damascus, some 240 km (150 miles) to the southwest.
10UNESCO has expressed deep concern over reports of fighting near Palmyra.
11The Syrian government is a pariah to states in the West and in the region that say President Bashar al-Assad has lost legitimacy, complicating any international effort to protect the country's cultural heritage.
12A military source said the army had reinforced the city.
13But the army is under pressure, having lost ground to other insurgents in the northwest and in the south since late March.
14The Observatory reported that Islamic State had seized a gas field to the east of Palmyra - a report denied by the military source.
15The source said Islamic State was keeping up its attack but the fighting on Saturday was at a lower intensity.
16The mass execution reported by the Observatory is the second such killing it has recorded since Islamic State advanced this week into the area.
17In the first, the Observatory said the jihadists had executed 26 men, beheading 10 of them.
18The Syrian military source said there had been one massacre of 30 or more people in that area, including elderly men.
19The Syrian military has been mounting air strikes against Islamic State fighters in the area.
20Rami Abdulrahman, who runs the Observatory, said the sides were fighting near a military intelligence building in Palmyra on Saturday.
21The Islamic State offensive in central Syria has added to the pressures facing government forces that have faced significant setbacks since late March in the four-year-long war.
22Other insurgent groups fighting President Bashar al-Assad have seized control of wide areas of the northwestern province of Idlib since late March.
23Assad has also lost a border crossing with Jordan in the south.
24This week the Syrian army and the allied Lebanese group Hezbollah have driven insurgents from wide areas of the mountainous region to the north of Damascus, shoring up Assad's grip over the border zone between Syria and Lebanon.
