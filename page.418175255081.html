Islamic State beheads female civilians for first time in Syria for 'sorcery': monitor

1A member to the Islamic State in Iraq and the Levant (ISIL) waves an ISIL flag in Raqqa.
2The hardline Islamic State group has beheaded two women in Syria, the first time it has decapitated female civilians, the founder of a group monitoring the war said on Tuesday.
3The beheadings took place in the eastern Deir al-Zor province this week said Rami Abdulrahman, head of the Syrian Observatory for Human Rights, which tracks the conflict using sources on the ground.
4One of the women was beheaded along with her husband in Deir al-Zor city.
5In al-Mayadeen city to the south east, the group beheaded another woman and her husband.
6All of them were accused of sorcery, the monitor said.
7Islamic State has beheaded local and foreign men in Syria, including enemy combatants, aid workers and journalists as well as people it has deemed as violating its hardline interpretation of Islamic law.
8Several of the group's female captives have been stoned to death previously after being accused of adultery and other offences.
9This is the first time it was reported to have beheaded female civilians.
10Islamic State also "crucified" five men in al-Mayadeen for eating during daylight hours of the Muslim fasting month of Ramadan, the Observatory said.
11They were hung up by their limbs on the city wall and children were encouraged to mock them as they suffered, it added.
12Activists say Islamic State uses such public punishments in areas where it is present to control the local population through coercion and fear.
