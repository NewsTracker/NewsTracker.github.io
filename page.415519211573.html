1(CNN)Preliminary exit poll estimates released by Israel's three major broadcasters late Tuesday show an election too close to call.
2Millions of Israelis cast their votes to determine their country's next Prime Minister and the makeup of its parliament.
3Channel 2 Israel reports the Likud party, headed by Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, has a slight lead over the Zionist Union, led by Isaac Herzog -- 28 seats, over 27 seats.
4Channels 10 and 1 show the two tied with 27 seats each.
5The incumbent claimed victory ahead of a final count.
6"Against all odds, we achieved this huge victory," Netanyahu told his cheering supporters.
7"Now we should form a strong and stable government that will be able to take care of the security, safety and welfare of each and every citizen of Israel."
8The Zionist Union responded by saying in a statement that the Likud party "keeps misleading."
9"The rightist bloc has shrunk. Everything is possible until the real results are in, when we can know which parties passed the electoral threshold and which government we can form. All the spins and statements are premature," it read.
10Official results are not expected to be published until next week, and the process of building coalitions could take much longer.
11Gabriel Sassoon, foreign communications adviser for the Herzog campaign, said: "The fat lady has far from sung in this case. We do not know. The parties are neck and neck and it's a matter of coalition negotiations and let me tell you, it's entirely possible to form a center-left coalition at this point. Israel is tired of nine long years of Netanyahu."
12Election ballots are for political parties rather than individual candidates.
13Israel has a proportional representation system, meaning a coalition government is likely to be formed within its 120-seat Knesset.

15CNN's Elise Labott described the early results as a "stunning turnaround" for Netanyahu, who took a sharp turn toward the right during the final days of the hotly-contested campaign.
16"It seems to have worked. He seems to have energized that right-wing base and now he is neck-and-neck with Isaac Herzog, even inching a little bit ahead of him," she said.
17"We'll have to see who's able to form the coalition with the other parties, but it does look as if Prime Minister Netanyahu has fought his way back and has a very good chance of forming this government."
18Before polls closed Tuesday, Netanyahu released a video on his Facebook page urging his supporters to vote, suggesting that leftists are bringing "huge amounts" of Israel's Arab citizens to the polls to vote against his Likud party.
19"The right regime is in danger, the Arab voters are coming in huge amounts to the polls," Netanyahu said.
20"The leftists are bringing them (Arabs) in huge amounts to polls using buses. ... We have an urgent wake-up call."
21Arabs make up about 20% of Israel's population.
22According to the early exit poll estimates, an Arab coalition ranked as the third largest party.
23Moshe Kahlon, a former Likud minister who now heads the Kulanu party, said Netanyahu's statement "clearly shows he is feeling the pressure."
24"These remarks were inappropriate and regrettable," Kahlon said.
25Polls closed at 10 p.m. local time (4 p.m. ET).
26Following the release of exit poll estimates, President Reuven Rivlin said that citizens need a ruling government as soon as possible.
27"The President will work with all the election bodies to start the consultations process ASAP. We hope to start as soon as Sunday," his office said in a statement.

29Herzog comes from Israeli political royalty.
30His grandfather, Rabbi Yitzhak HaLevi Herzog, was the first chief rabbi of the state of Israel.
31His father, Chaim Herzog, was an Army general, an ambassador to the United Nations and the President of Israel.
32Before entering politics, Herzog served with a military intelligence unit of the Israeli Defense Forces, rising to the rank of major, and worked as a lawyer and served as the director of the Israel Anti-Drug Authority.
33He began his political career in 2003, when he first won a seat in the Knesset with the Labor Party.
34He held a variety of ministerial positions, including minister of housing and construction, minister of tourism and minister of welfare and social services before becoming leader of the Labor Party in 2013.
35In those elections, he also became the leader of the opposition, as Netanyahu won another term as Prime Minister.
36Herzog has pegged his bid for the premiership on social reform, vowing to "change the nature of the division of wealth."
37He has also promised to restart stalled peace talks with the Palestinian Authority.
38Netanyahu served for six years in the Israeli Army in an elite commando unit until he was wounded in a rescue operation during an airplane hijacking in 1972.
39In 1976, Netanyahu's brother was killed in a raid to try to free hostages in Uganda from an Air France jet hijacked by pro-Palestinian terrorists.
40Netanyahu set up an anti-terror institute in his name.
41His outspokenness on the threat of terrorism attracted attention, and he was given high-profile diplomatic assignments.
42In 1988, he was elected to the Knesset.
43In 1996, he became Israel's youngest Prime Minister.
44He was defeated three years later after signing an interim peace agreement with then-Palestinian leader Yasser Arafat.
45In 2009, he again became Prime Minister.
46CNN's Labott says Netanyahu's emphasis on security -- particularly speaking out loudly on Iran -- and his counteroffensives against Hamas in Gaza have boosted his popularity in Israel.
47But Labott says peace with the Palestinians remains elusive, and Netanyahu is finding himself increasingly alienated from Western partners.
48As negotiations with Iran near a crucial state, Netanyahu has sided against the White House, telling the U.S. Congress this month that a proposed agreement was "a bad deal" and would not be supported by Israel, which would stand alone if necessary to defend its people.

2Apple has been talking to Walt Disney Co (DIS.N), CBS Corp (CBS.N), and Twenty-First Century Fox Inc (FOXA.O) and other media companies to offer a "skinny" bundle with well-known channels like CBS, ESPN and FX, leaving out the many smaller networks in the standard cable TV package, the Journal said.

6Several media companies are considering joining streaming-only services, or launching their own like HBO and CBS, to attract young people who do not subscribe to traditional pay TV packages.
7But programmers also fear the packages could become so popular that they undercut current, more profitable deals with cable companies.
8In January, Dish Network Corp (DISH.O) unveiled its long-anticipated video streaming service, named Sling TV, targeted at younger consumers who shun pricey cable and satellite subscriptions.
9Dish's $20 a month service, the first from a distributor, will be available through Internet-connected devices such as Amazon Fire TV, Roku and Google Nexus Player for TVs, tablets, computers and smartphones and will include TV programming from ABC, ESPN and Maker Studios, Time Warner's (TWX.N) TNT, CNN, TBS, Cartoon Network and Adult Swim, and Food Network, HGTV and Travel Channel.
10Others like Sony (6758.T) are also rolling out competing services.
