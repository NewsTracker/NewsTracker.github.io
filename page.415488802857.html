1Islamic State fighters traded sniper fire and mortar rounds with Iraqi troops and allied Shi'ite militia forces on Sunday in the city of Tikrit amid further reports the militants had obtained chlorine for possible use as a chemical weapon.
2A military official returned from the front in Tikrit said no major advances were made by either side nearly two weeks into an operation to win back the city IS fighters seized last June.
3Iraqi Kurdish authorities said on Saturday they had proof the radical Islamist militants occupying large parts of the country's north and west used chlorine against Kurdish peshmerga fighters in January in a car bombing attempt west of the city of Mosul.
4The Baghdad government has not issued a statement on the semi-autonomous Kurdish region's announcement.
5But the mayor of a town on the northern edge of Tikrit told Reuters on Sunday that storage containers filled with chlorine were found by troops when they entered al-Alam last week, the day before they fought their way into Tikrit.
6"We found a number of storage units containing chlorine that we think were seized by [the Islamic State] from water purification stations in different parts of Tikrit," Laith al-Jubouri said.
7He said security forces had sealed off the area where the containers were found and alerted Baghdad authorities.
8He also said the security forces had found bombs containing chlorine.
9On Tuesday, when Iraqi forces and allied mainly-Shi'ite militias pushed IS fighters out of the town, a Reuters photographer was present when Iraqi police instructed journalists to stand back and hold their breath as they detonated a roadside bomb.
10When they detonated the bomb, a yellowish plume burst into the air, and as bystanders coughed, officials shouted, "Be careful, it's chlorine!" the Reuters photographer said.
11The statement from Kurdish authorities about Islamic State's use of chlorine referred to footage of "similar attacks" during the recent fighting around Tikrit, though the mayor and another security official only noted the presence of chlorine found in areas recently seized back from IS.
12The military campaign to retake Tikrit has been stalled since Friday, when security officials said Iraqi forces and their militia allies would wait for reinforcements before moving forward.
13Two days later, back-up had not yet arrived, and officials continued to stress the challenges they faced in flushing out militants in street-by-street battles and defusing bombs and booby traps they laid while retreating from parts of the city.
14"There were no new pushes today from either side, only scattered skirmishes in the northern and southern parts of the city," Colonel Mohammed Abid al-Jubouri told Reuters by phone after visiting the southern edge of Tikrit on Sunday.
15Mr. Jubouri said the process of "organizing troops and waiting for reinforcements" was ongoing and did not say when a fresh effort to seize the central districts of the city still held by IS would begin.
16The militants still hold about half the city, which lies about 160 km north of Baghdad.
17Most residents of Tikrit fled in the months after the extremists overran the city in June.
18Fabio Forgione, Head of Mission for Doctors without Borders, said his organization was concerned about the "huge number of civilians" in areas north of Tikrit where fighting could shift after the current offensive.
19The effort to retake Saddam Hussein's home city from the militants has been the biggest offensive yet against the group that has declared an Islamic caliphate on territory it controls in Iraq and neighboring Syria.
20To the southeast near the Shi'ite city of Samarra, a suicide bomber drove a car into a group of Shi'ite militiamen, killing three of them, police and medical sources said.
21The militia groups have been using Samarra as a rear base for the Tikrit offensive.
22Atrocities committed by Islamic State continue to surface.
23More than 50 corpses were found on Sunday near the mainly Shi'ite Turkman town of Bashir near Kirkuk in northern Iraq, said Kamil Amin, spokesman for Iraq's Human Rights ministry.
24He said experts from the Human Rights and Health Ministries also visited three mass graves recently found near the village of Abu Ajeel, east of Tikrit, on Sunday.
25The experts took one corpse as sample to do analysis to determine whether they were the victims of a massacre of Iraqi soldiers of unprecedented scale at Camp Speicher in June.
