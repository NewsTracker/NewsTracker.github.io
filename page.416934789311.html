FIFA introduces more racism surveillance for World Cup

1LONDON (Reuters) - A new system of match observers monitoring incidents of racism and discrimination at 2018 World Cup qualifiers was announced by FIFA on Tuesday.
2The system will be co-ordinated by world soccer's ruling body and implemented in co-operation with the European anti-discrimination organisation FARE.
3Any incidents of discrimination or racism will be reported to FIFA which has pledged to impose disciplinary sanctions on any countries involved.
4The implementation of the new system is particularly apt as racism is rife in soccer in Russia, hosts of the 2018 World Cup.
5More than 200 incidents were committed by Russian fans between 2012 and 2014 according to a recent report by the Sova Center, a Moscow-based racism-monitoring group.
6Last month FIFA president Sepp Blatter said "a lot of work needs to be done" to wipe the scourge of racism and discrimination from Russian soccer and the game in general.
7FIFA and European soccer's governing body UEFA have often imposed relatively small punishments when incidents have occurred in the past.
