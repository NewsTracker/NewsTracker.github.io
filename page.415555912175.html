2SYRACUSE, N.Y. (AP) - Jim Boeheim became the face of Syracuse University over the past five decades, first as player, then as coach - revered for wins and delivering a national title to a struggling city in 2003.
3But the coda to his decorated career has become tinged with undeniable blemishes, laid out in harsh penalties for violations the NCAA says show Boeheim and the university lost control of athletics.
4Boeheim and Syracuse officials acknowledged the unwanted ties Wednesday in announcing he will retire after three more seasons, while insisting they don't agree with parts of the scathing NCAA report and will appeal to try to save scholarships and wins.
5Syracuse University Chancellor Kent Syverud said Boeheim decided to make the announcement to "bring certainty to the team and program in the coming years" and to allow for a smooth transition.
6"Coach Jim Boeheim has been a mainstay at Syracuse University for more than one-third of our entire 144-year history," Syverud said in a statement.
7"He enrolled as a student here in 1962 and has never left. He has been the embodiment of Orange pride."
8When Boeheim turned 70 in November, wife Juli asked him if he was OK with coaching as a septuagenarian and all that goes along with it.
9"I just think one day you're going to have to slow down," Juli said.
10"He doesn't want to, number one, and he feels better than he ever has."
11The violations, lasting more than a decade under Boeheim's watch, involved academic misconduct, extra benefits and the university's drug-testing policy, according to a March 6 report by the NCAA Committee on Infractions.
12Boeheim is already suspended for the first half of the next Atlantic Coast Conference season, a total of nine games.
13Syracuse will also have three scholarships taken away for four seasons and all wins vacated in which an ineligible player participated during five seasons between 2004 and 2012.
14The total wins removed from records could be as high as 108, depending on what happens in the appeal process.
15Syracuse has already vacated 24 wins.
16Athletic director Daryl Gross is also stepping aside, immediately taking another marketing position with the school.
17Pete Sala will serve as interim athletic director.
18Boeheim, who scheduled a news conference for Thursday, has had problems before.
19The NCAA banned the Orange from the 1993 NCAA Tournament for recruiting violations.
20Longtime assistant coach Mike Hopkins, a former star for the Orange, is in line to succeed Boeheim.
21In its report, the NCAA placed Syracuse on probation for five years for breaking with the "most fundamental core values of the NCAA."
22Athletic department officials interfered with academics, making sure star players stayed eligible, the report said.
23The report said the former director of basketball operations, who was picked by Boeheim and whose job primarily consisted of monitoring academic performance of basketball student-athletes, became overly involved.
24He collected and maintained student-athletes' usernames and passwords and provided them to others, including student-athlete support services.
25Members of the support staff routinely accessed and sent emails from student-athletes' accounts and corresponded directly with professors and included attached course work to maintain the required grades for the student-athletes to remain eligible, the report said.
26The 94-page report also said basketball staff encouraged students to develop relationships with a booster, which led to rule violations.
27The booster provided more than $8,000 to three football and two men's basketball students for volunteering at a local YMCA, the report said.
28Additionally, the booster gave money to basketball staff for appearances or assistance at YMCA events, payments that weren't reported to the school as outside income or supplemental pay, as NCAA rules require.
