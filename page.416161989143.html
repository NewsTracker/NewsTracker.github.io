Senators cap sizzling 2nd-half rally with East playoff spot (Yahoo Sports)

1Needing just one point for the playoffs in the season finale, the Senators wrapped up their sizzling second-half rally with a 3-1 win over the Philadelphia Flyers on Saturday and clinched a spot in the Eastern Conference playoffs.
2''Here we are at the end of the year, we made an unbelievable run for the past 30 games,'' Senators captain Erik Karlsson said.
3Headed toward a spot in the NHL draft lottery two months ago, the Senators finished 23-4-4 over their final 31 games to storm into the postseason.
4The Senators hope they can play deep into the postseason behind goalie Andrew Hammond.
5Nicknamed the ''Hamburglar,'' Hammond went from a borderline AHL goalie to posting a 20-1-2 record since he was recalled in the second half.
6''I've never had a stretch of hockey like this in my life,'' Hammond said.
7He stopped 34 shots and made sure the Senators would not need help from other teams to make the playoffs.
8Mark Stone scored twice and Jean-Gabriel Pageau also scored for Ottawa.
9The 27-year-old Hammond entered with a 1.76 goals-against average since his recall from the AHL.
10Not bad for an undrafted rookie.
11Ottawa fans have tossed hamburgers on the ice and the owner of six McDonald's locations in the Ottawa Valley arranged for Hammond to have a card that gives him free McDonald's food for life.
12He led the Senators from 14 points out of a playoff spot on Feb. 10 to contention down the stretch and now they await their playoff destination.
13The Senators mobbed him at the net when the final horn sounded.
14''I don't know if it's completely sunk in yet,'' Hammond said, ''but tomorrow when we wake up, we'll be in the playoffs.
15Stone also has fueled Ottawa's surge, finishing the season with a nine-game points streak.
16He scored an empty-netter in the first period when goalie Steve Mason was wildly out of position off a misplayed shot.
17Stone made it 3-1 in the third with his 26th goal of the season.
18Pageau snapped a 1-1 tie in the second with his 10th goal and third in three games.
19Matt Read scored for the Flyers.
20The Flyers finished 33-31-18 (10-20-11 on the road) and missed the playoffs for the second time in three seasons and this absence could cost coach Craig Berube his job.
21Berube led the Flyers to the playoffs last season when he took over with the team off to an 0-3 start.
22Berube was elevated from an assistant to the head job by former general manager Paul Holmgren.
23Ron Hextall, in his first season calling the shots, could go in a different direction with the Flyers set to have a top-10 draft lottery pick and a potential rebuild ahead.
24Berube said he never thought the finale could be his final game on the bench.
25''We've got some real good franchise players on his hockey team,'' Berube said.
26Jakub Voracek and team captain Claude Giroux both were in the mix most of the season for the league scoring title and Wayne Simmonds led the team in goals despite missing the final two weeks with a foot injury.
27Simmonds hobbled on crutches and posed with fans in the press box during intermission, getting a bird's eye view of a team that never lived up to so much playoff potential.
28''We've got to come with a good attitude next year at camp,'' Giroux said.
29The Flyers have not won a Stanley Cup since hoisting two straight in 1974 and 1975.
30The Flyers have been close - real close to another: Philadelphia lost in the Stanley Cup finals in 1976, 1980, 1985, 1987, 1997 and 2010.
31The Flyers have missed the playoffs two times in a three-season span for the first time since they missed it five straight seasons from 1989-1994.
