Timeline of Events After Fatal Police Shooting in Ferguson

1FERGUSON, Mo.
2A timeline of key events following the fatal police shooting of an unarmed, black 18-year-old in the St. Louis suburb of Ferguson.
3AUG. 9 - Michael Brown and a companion, both black, are confronted by an officer as they walk back to Brown's home from a convenience store.
4Brown and the officer, who is white, are involved in a scuffle, followed by gunshots.
5Brown dies at the scene, and his body remains in the street for four hours in the summer heat.
6Neighbors later lash out at authorities, saying they mistreated the body.
7AUG. 10 - After a candlelight vigil, people protesting Brown's death smash car windows and carry away armloads of looted goods from stores.
8In the first of several nights of violence, looters are seen making off with bags of food, toilet paper and alcohol.
9Some protesters stand atop police cars and taunt officers.
10AUG. 11 - The FBI opens an investigation into Brown's death, and two men who said they saw the shooting tell reporters that Brown had his hands raised when the officer approached with his weapon and fired repeatedly.
11That night, police in riot gear fire tear gas and rubber bullets to try to disperse a crowd.
12AUG. 12 - Ferguson Police Chief Thomas Jackson cancels plans to release the name of the officer who shot Brown, citing death threats against the police department and City Hall.
13AUG. 14 - The Missouri Highway Patrol takes control of security in Ferguson, relieving St. Louis County and local police of their law-enforcement authority following four days of violence.
14The shift in command comes after images from the protests show many officers equipped with military-style gear, including armored vehicles, body armor and assault rifles.
15In scores of photographs that circulate online, officers are seen pointing their weapons at demonstrators.
16AUG. 15 - Police identify the officer who shot Brown as Darren Wilson, 28.
17They also release a video purporting to show Brown robbing a convenience store of almost $50 worth of cigars shortly before he was killed, a move that further inflames protesters.
18AUG. 16 - Missouri Gov. Jay Nixon declares a state of emergency and imposes a curfew in Ferguson.
19AUG. 17- Attorney General Eric Holder orders a federal medical examiner to perform another autopsy on Brown.
20AUG. 18 - Nixon calls the National Guard to Ferguson to help restore order and lifts the curfew.
21AUG. 19 - Nixon says he will not seek the removal of Ferguson prosecutor Bob McCulloch from the investigation into Brown's death.
22Some black leaders questioned whether the prosecutor's deep family connections to police would affect his ability to be impartial.
23McCulloch's father was a police officer who was killed in the line of duty when McCulloch was a child, and he has many relatives who work in law enforcement.
24AUG. 20 - Holder visits Ferguson to offer assurances about the investigation into Brown's death and to meet with investigators and Brown's family.
25A grand jury begins hearing evidence to determine whether Wilson should be charged.
26AUG. 21 - Nixon orders the National Guard to withdraw from Ferguson.
