10But the road to 40-0 won't be easy - Notre Dame and Kansas are among the schools in the Midwest Region.

13The Badgers earned the top seed in the West, opening against Coastal Carolina in a region that includes No.

15Villanova is tops in the East, and Duke tops in the South.
16STAR POWER: Sure, there's no superstar like Andrew Wiggins this season.
17But that hardly means this year's dance is devoid of big-time scorers and dynamic defenders.
18Kentucky has a handful of players who could be headed to the NBA, and the Blue Devils' Jahlil Okafor is the potential No.
191 pick.
20Jerian Grant of Notre Dame proved that seniors can still make headlines in an era of one-and-done prospects, while many believe Wisconsin's Frank Kaminsky deserves the national player of the year award.
21"We have a lot of goals and aspirations in the NCAA Tournament," said Kaminsky, who helped the Badgers to the Final Four a year ago, "so we're going to prepare for our first game and be ready when we tip the ball up and get ready to go."
22CINDERELLA WATCH: These days, with thousands of games televised every season, there are few secrets in college basketball by the time March rolls around.
23But that doesn't mean there aren't a few players who are envisioning their own Bryce Drew moment, or a few teams that can put together an inspired run like George Mason or Butler of years past.
24Want some off-the-radar players to watch?
25Try someone from Drew's own team, Valparaiso, such as 6-foot-10 Jamaican center Vashil Fernandez or high-scoring forward Alex Peters.
26Tyler Harvey of Eastern Washington led the nation in scoring, while Stephen F. Austin's Jacob Parker is one of the best 3-point shooters in the country.
27How about a couple of teams that could make a run?
28Duke icon Bobby Hurley has coached Buffalo to a No.
2912 seed opposite West Virginia in the Midwest region, and everyone knows the history of the 12-5 upset.
30Harvard, the No.
3113 seed in the West, has been to four straight NCAAs and could make fourth-seeded North Carolina a tad uncomfortable.
32INTRIGUING MATCHUPS: The selection committee lined up several interesting matchups early in the tournament - coaches facing former teams, in-state rivals harboring plenty of hatred.
33Take sixth-seeded SMU against No.
3411 seed UCLA in the South.
35Mustangs coach Larry Brown is back in the tournament for the first time since guiding Kansas to the 1988 title, and his opener is against the team he led to the 1980 finals - a runner-up finish later vacated.
36Or, take a potential third-round game in the Midwest.
37If second-seeded Kansas and No.
387 seed Wichita State each win, the schools would meet for the first time since 1993, despite only a few hours of highway separating their campuses.
39The Shockers have been trying to schedule the Jayhawks for years, but Kansas administrators believe they have nothing to gain from the matchup.
