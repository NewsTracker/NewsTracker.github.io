Egypt raises death toll from Nile boat collision to 31

1Egyptian search teams have retrieved 13 more bodies from this week's boat collision on the Nile River, bringing the death toll from the tragedy to 31, the health ministry said Friday.
2Ministry spokesman Hossam Abdel-Ghaffar told The Associated Press that search efforts were still ongoing following the late Wednesday night collision near Cairo, when a passenger boat traveling down the Nile collided with a scow, causing the boat to capsize.
3The scow's captain and his assistant have been detained and accused of manslaughter.
4The online edition of the state-owned Al-Ahram daily reported on Thursday that the families of the missing had blocked a main road in Warraq, the district where the boat sunk, briefly clashing with security forces, which finally broke up the protest.
5Nile traffic has been heavy, especially near Cairo, as Egyptians celebrated the Eid al-Fitr holiday following the holy month of Ramadan.
6Nile rides are among favorite pastimes during the holidays but many city dwellers also use the passenger boats for travel along the Nile.
