Details of Victims in the Crash of Germanwings Flight 9525

1They came from as far away as Australia, Iran, Morocco and Venezuela, and their lives ended together in France's deadliest plane crash in decades.
2The victims included babies and schoolchildren going home from exchange visits, a pair of acclaimed opera singers, three generations of the same family, a vacationing mother and son, people on business trips and others going home.
3Germanwings said 144 passengers and six crew members were on board its plane that crashed Tuesday in the French Alps on its way from Barcelona to Duesseldorf.
4The students and their teachers were returning from an exchange program in Spain.
5Their names have not been released.
6The group was from Haltern, a rural town 80 kilometers (50 miles) northeast of Duesseldorf, where it seemed everyone knew someone who had died.
7The principal of Joseph Koenig High School, Ulrich Wessel, called the loss of his students and two teachers - one who had just married and another who was soon to be - a "tragedy that renders one speechless."
8"I was asked yesterday how many students there are at the high school in Haltern, and I said 1,283 without thinking. Then had to say afterward, unfortunately, 16 fewer since yesterday," he said.
9"That's just terrible."
10Maria Radner, a 34-year-old contralto from Duesseldorf, her husband and infant son were among the victims, Barcelona's Gran Teatre del Liceu said.
11The Spanish opera house said she played the goddess Erda in Richard Wagner's "Siegfried" last weekend.
12Bass baritone Oleg Bryjak, had performed along with Radner in the production of "Siegfried" at Barcelona's Gran Teatre del Liceu, the opera house said.
13Bryjak, 54, had sung the part of Alberich.
14Bryjak was born in Ukraine of Kazakhstani origin and had moved to Germany in 1991 where he was the soloist at the Deutsche Opera am Rhein in Duesseldorf.
15Its director, Christoph Meyer, said that "we have lost a great performer and a great person in Oleg Bryjak."
16Christina Scheppelmann, artistic director of the Liceu, said: "The mood is ominous, like it should be in any kind of workplace facing a tragedy like this one."
17Three generations of one family - a schoolgirl, her mother and grandmother - were on the plane that crashed, according to a statement from the town hall in Sant Cugat del Valles, outside Barcelona.
18The girl was a middle school student, for children aged 10-11, at Santa Isabel school in Sant Cugat, the statement said, without providing the family names.
19"The students are very affected. The teachers are trying to help them any way they can," said a woman who answered the phone at the school.
20She refused to give her name or say more.
21Serrat was export director for Spanish industrial manufacturer INOXPA, the Girona, Spain-based company said.
