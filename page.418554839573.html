3(AP) -- Demaryius Thomas hasn't lost any of his impeccable timing.
4The Denver Broncos' star wide receiver signed a five-year, $70 million contract just before the deadline Wednesday.
5It's the richest contract in franchise history and includes $43.5 million guaranteed.
6Had the sides not hammered out a deal, Thomas would have played this season under the $12,823,000 franchise tag, which still would have been about as much as he'd made in his first five seasons combined.
7''Thrilled to reach a long-term deal with Demaryius,'' general manager John Elway tweeted.
8That was a far different tone than the last time Elway spoke publicly about Peyton Manning's top target.
9It was just before the draft and Elway was asked about Thomas boycotting the team's offseason program, his only perceived leverage under the franchise tag designation.
10The negotiations never really got nasty.
11Manning said sure, he'd prefer Thomas show up but he also wanted him to get every penny he had coming.
12Teammates good-naturedly photo-shopped Thomas into their pictures.
13And Kubiak had his video department send Thomas the new playbook and clips of practice to keep him up to speed.
14The total value of Thomas' deal is similar to the one Dallas Cowboys All-Pro receiver Dez Bryant signed Wednesday.
15Earlier this year, Thomas told The Associated Press he wasn't interested in using Bryant as a barometer for his own contract, insisting, ''I'm just trying to get what I think I deserve.
16Thomas told The AP this spring he also didn't think his absence from the offseason program would hamper his timing with Manning, suggesting their three years together gave him confidence ''we can pick up where we left off'' whenever he signed his deal.
17By signing Thomas, the Broncos won't have to worry about him being a no-show when camp training camp starts in two weeks.
18Nor will they go into the 2016 offseason with two superstars entering free agency.
19Linebacker Von Miller, barring an extension, will be up for a mega deal after the 2015 season.
20Thomas set career highs with 111 catches for 1,619 yards - a franchise record - in 2014, when he scored 11 times.
21Since Manning's arrival in Denver in 2012, Thomas has caught 297 passes for 4,483 yards and 35 TDs.
22In that three-year span, Thomas has ranked first in the league in yards receiving, second to Antonio Brown in catches and second to Dallas' Bryant in touchdowns while making the Pro Bowl each year.
