Smith, James lead Cavs past Hawks 97-89 in Game 1 (Yahoo Sports)

1ATLANTA (AP) -- J.R. Smith knocked down a tough 3-pointer, then another.
2Turning toward the fans, their heckling transformed to groans, he blew off his right index finger and motioned toward his side, like he was holstering his pistol.
3Yep, Smith was that hot.
4While LeBron James led Cleveland with 31 points, it was Smith who ignited the Cavaliers in Game 1 of the Eastern Conference finals.
5He made eight 3-pointers and scored 28 points in a 97-89 victory over the top-seeded Atlanta Hawks on Wednesday night.
6Intent on bringing Cleveland its first NBA title, James sealed the victory with a soaring dunk in the final minute.
7For the Hawks, it was the second straight series in which they've lost the opening game at home.
8Cleveland will look to take command of the series in Game 2 Friday night.
9Making matters worse for Atlanta, DeMarre Carroll went down with a knee injury driving to the basket with 4:59 remaining.
10He was helped to the locker room, not putting any weight on his left leg, and will undergo an MRI on Thursday to determine the seriousness.
11Besides being the main defender on James, Carroll had been the Hawks' leading scorer in the playoffs.
12He was averaging more than 17 points coming into Atlanta's first conference final.
13''DeMarre is a complete player,'' Hawks coach Mike Budenholzer said.
14Jeff Teague led Atlanta with 27 points.
15But Atlanta didn't produce its usual balanced scoring, as two starters - Kyle Korver and Carroll - failed to reach double figures.
16With Smith doing most of the damage, Cleveland outscored the Hawks 22-4 over the final 5 minutes of the third quarter and the first 2 minutes of the fourth.
17During that span, he made five 3-pointers and another jumper from just inside the arc, as well as throwing up a lob that Tristan Thompson dunked.
18Smith has been a huge addition for the Cavaliers since he was acquired in January as part of a three-team trade with New York and Oklahoma City.
19A player once known for his selfishness, he had learned to work with within the confines of a team led by James.
20When the Cavaliers really needed Smith in Game 1, he seemed to make every shot.
21Cleveland scored the first 11 points of the final period - all but two on 3s by Smith - for its biggest lead, 85-67.
22Carroll's injury silenced the raucous crowd and would be a tough blow for the Hawks to overcome.
23While he was being taken off, James came over to say a few words and pat the injured player on the head.
24''We're all a brotherhood,'' James said.
25Even without Carroll, Atlanta closed to 91-87 with about a minute remaining.
26But James got loose from Paul Millsap out near the arc, took off down the lane uncontested and slammed home a dunk that finished off the Hawks.
27Having made their deepest run in the playoffs since 1970, the Hawks carried that momentum to a nine-point lead in the opening quarter.
28Atlanta shot 61 percent and James went to the bench with about 3 minutes remaining in the period after picking up his second foul.
29Cleveland's dominance on the boards kept Atlanta from pulling away.
30Cleveland outrebounded the Hawks 15-6 over the first 12 minutes, actually yanking down more offensive rebounds (seven) than Atlanta's total output.
31James returned in the second quarter and began to take control of the Cleveland offense.
32He scored 14 points in the period on 7-of-9 shooting, taking advantage when the Hawks tried to bait him into a third foul by guarding him with smaller players.
33The game was tied 51-all at the half.
34The Cavaliers finished with a 49-37 rebounding edge, exposing Atlanta's major weakness during the regular season.
35Timofey Mozgov led with 11 rebounds, while Thompson chipped in with 10.
36James also had eight rebounds, giving the Cavaliers three players who had more than anyone on the Hawks.
37Millsap, Kyle Korver and Al Horford each had seven for Atlanta.
38Cavaliers: This was the first time in franchise history that Cleveland won Game 1 of a playoff series on the road.
39The Cavs had been 0-14.
40... Thompson had 14 points, while Mozgov and Iman Shumpert added 10 apiece.
41Hawks: Horford had 16 points, Millsap 13 and Kent Bazemore 10, including a massive dunk in the second half.
42... Bazemore figures to get even more playing time if Carroll is sidelined.
43... Floyd Mayweather Jr. was sitting courtside.
44When he was shown on the video board, most of the crowd booed.
