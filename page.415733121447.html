Demonstrators in Belarus assail Russia's policy, express solidarity with Ukraine

1Demonstrators carried a placard reading "Russia is War," a reference to Moscow's support for a separatist insurgency in eastern Ukraine.
2The rally in Minsk was approved by the authorities and police didn't intervene.
3Belarusian President Alexander Lukashenko has forged close economic, political and military ties with Russia, which has supported the ally with cheap energy and other subsidies.
4Lukashenko has ruled the nation of 10 million with an iron hand for more than 20 years, drawing Western criticism for his relentless crackdown on dissent and independent media.
5But Belarus' ties with the West have improved recently as Minsk hosted crucial Ukraine peace talks, and Lukashenko has openly criticized some of Moscow's moves.
6The Belarusian leader recently accused Moscow of violating the rules of the Eurasian Economic Union, a newly created alliance of Russia, Belarus, Kazakhstan, Armenia and Kyrgyzstan.
7He warned that Belarus could leave the union if the agreements on forming the alliance aren't observed.
8Opposition leader Alexander Milinkevich, who unsuccessfully challenged Lukashenko in 2006, said that the rally reflected fears of Russia's intentions.
9"We are seriously worried and are seeing our historic chance only with the European Union," he said.
10Inga Shakhnovich, a 19-year old student waving Ukrainian flag, said she fears that Belarus could become Russia's next victim.
11"Ukraine is fighting for our independence too," she said.
