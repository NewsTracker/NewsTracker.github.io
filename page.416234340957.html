Israel's Netanyahu speaks to Putin, protests Russian plans to sell advanced missiles to Iran

1Benjamin Netanyahu's office says he spoke with Vladimir Putin Tuesday to protest Russia's planned delivery of advanced S-300 air defense missile systems to the Islamic Republic.
2Israeli military officials consider the Russian arming of Iran an additional obstacle before any potential military strike against Iran's nuclear facilities.
3Netanyahu says he told Putin the move would only increase Iran's aggression in the region and undermine security in the Middle East.
4Israel says the Russian announcement is a direct result of the international community's framework nuclear deal with Iran.
5Israel vehemently opposes any deal with Iran that does not dismantle the military capability of its nuclear program.
