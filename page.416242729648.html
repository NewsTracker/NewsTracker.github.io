1CHICAGO (AP) -- Could Kris Byrant make his Chicago Cubs' debut as early as Friday?
2The Cubs placed infielder Tommy La Stella on the disabled list Tuesday because of right ribcage inflammation and recalled left-hander Zac Rosscup from Triple-A Iowa.
3Chicago manager Joe Maddon said ''it's possible'' the Cubs could add an infielder for Friday's game against San Diego.
4Cubs President of Baseball Operations Theo Epstein didn't discount the possibility another infielder could join the team by the time the homestand ends Sunday.
5''It depends on what's happening with the roster, what's happening with his development,'' Epstein said.

7Since 172 days counts as a year, keeping Bryant in the minor leagues through Thursday ensures Bryant could not be eligible for free agency until after the 2021 season.
8Bryant hit .425 for the Cubs in spring training with nine homers and 15 RBIs in 40 at-bats and entered Tuesday batting .381 at Iowa with two homers and seven RBIs in five game.

10Maddon wouldn't say whether the Cubs considered bringing up an infielder Tuesday, saying ''today was about a pitcher.'' The Chicago bullpen has been taxed to start the season, throwing 21 2-3 innings in six games.
