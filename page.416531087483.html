Malaysia foils attacks in capital by suspected militants

1KUALA LUMPUR, April 26 (Reuters) - Malaysian police have arrested 12 people linked to the militant group Islamic State and seized explosives, foiling a plan to attack several locations in and around the capital city.
2The male suspects, aged 17 to 41, were arrested on Saturday and Sunday from Ulu Langat and Cheras, suburbs near Kuala Lumpur, police chief Khalid Abu Baker said in a statement on Sunday.
3The men were suspected of plotting attacks on "strategic targets and governmental interests around the Klang Valley", he said.
4The plans were in response to calls by Islamic State (IS) to launch terrorist attacks on secular Islamic countries seen as "enemies of IS".
5Authorities said previous arrests of suspected IS militants was another reason behind the intended attacks.
6The explosives seized included 20 kg of ammonium nitrate and 20 kg of potassium nitrate, the statement added.
7Security was heightened in Kuala Lumpur on Sunday as leaders of the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) started arriving in Kuala Lumpur for the ASEAN summit, which opens officially on Monday.
8But it was not clear whether any of the planned attacks were linked to the summit.
9At the summit, Malaysia aims to push for regional cooperation to fight terrorism.
10The Southeast Asian country has not seen any significant militant attacks but has arrested 92 citizens on suspicion of links to IS.
