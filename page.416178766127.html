Soldiers killed in blast in Egypt's North Sinai

1An explosion targeting a military vehicle has killed five Egyptian soldiers near the town of Sheikh Zuweid in North Sinai, Reuters news agency says quoting a security official.
2Security officials said on Sunday that the roadside bomb was planted in an area of the northern Sinai peninsula that is a stronghold of Ansar Beit al-Maqdis, which has pledged allegiance to the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant group.
3Egypt has conducted a long-running campaign in the Sinai Peninsula against fighters from the group formerly known as Ansar Beit al-Maqdis but who are now fighting under the banner of the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant (ISIL) group.
4Ansar Beit al-Maqdis say their attacks targeting security forces are in retaliation for a government crackdown against former President Mohamed Morsi's supporters that left hundreds dead.
5Early in April, a series of ambushes targeting the military killed 15 soldiers and two civilians.
6The army said 15 of the attackers were also killed in the clashes.
