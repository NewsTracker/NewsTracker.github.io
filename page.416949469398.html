Apple CEO meets with Chinese vice premier in Beijing

1BEIJING (AP) - Apple CEO Tim Cook met with a top Chinese official in Beijing while touring a country that has become the tech giant's biggest driver of sales growth, state media reported.
2Cook met with Vice Premier Liu Yandong in the Zhongnanhai leadership compound Tuesday, the official Xinhua News Agency said.
3Xinhua said Cook also visited an elementary school in Beijing earlier Tuesday to talk about innovation.
4A day earlier, Cook announced cooperation between Apple and the environmental group the World Wildlife Fund to responsibly manage Chinese forests and minimize the company's environmental impact.
5After trying to break into the Chinese market, Apple has seen spectacular success over the past year, particularly with iPhone sales in the country.
6China accounts for 30 percent of total Apple revenue, with Chinese sales growing by 71 percent in the last quarter.
