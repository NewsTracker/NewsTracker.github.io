Kenya's president apologizes for past wrongs by his government and previous regimes

1President Uhuru Kenyatta told parliament during his state of the nation address Thursday that time has come to bring closure to the country's painful past.
2He mentioned the 2007-08 postelection violence, the 1984 massacre of hundreds of Kenyan-Somalis and unsolved murders among other historical injustices.
3Kenya's Truth Justice and Reconciliation Commission report had recommended that the president apologize to the public.
4The report names the president and his deputy as being among those suspected of planning and financing Kenya's 2007-08 postelection violence in which more than 1,000 people died.
