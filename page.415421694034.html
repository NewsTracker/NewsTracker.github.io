China political advisers cheer on Winter Olympics bid

1Bethesda - An American health worker who contracted Ebola while volunteering in Africa will be admitted to a hospital at the National Institutes of Health, the agency announced on Thursday.
2The patient was expected to arrive on Friday at the NIH research hospital in Bethesda after being transported to the United States in isolation on a chartered plane.
3The patient's name, age and gender have not been released.
4The patient had been volunteering at an Ebola treatment centre in Sierra Leone.
5The NIH did not release any further details about the patient.
6The agency has one of the few specialised isolation units nationwide.
7Previously, an American nurse was treated there after she contracted Ebola while caring for a Liberian man who died at a Dallas hospital.
8The nurse, Nina Pham, survived and is Ebola-free.
9The treatment facility at the NIH is staffed by specialists in infectious disease and critical care and is designed to prevent the spread of highly contagious viruses, including Ebola.
10The patient will be the 11th person with Ebola to be treated in the US, which has more than 50 hospitals designated as Ebola treatment centres.
11The World Health Organisation estimated on Thursday that the virus has killed more than 10 000 people, mostly in the West African nations of Guinea, Liberia and Sierra Leone.
12The current outbreak is the largest ever for the disease.
13While deaths have slowed dramatically in recent months, the virus appears stubbornly entrenched in parts of Guinea and Sierra Leone.
