Romania: Italian foreign minister discusses Greece, Ukraine

1BUCHAREST, Romania (AP) - Italy's foreign minister has discussed the situations in Greece, Moldova and Ukraine with Romanian officials.
2Paolo Gentiloni met President Klaus Iohannis Thursday for talks about problems in the region, including migration and Greece.
3Romania's Foreign Minister Bogdan Aurescu said migrants who have traveled to Europe in recent months from the Middle East should be accepted by EU countries "taking into account the economic and social realities of member countries."
4Gentiloni said "we shouldn't raise walls, but should have shared responsibility," thanking Romania for sharing the same view.
5Iohannis and Gentiloni also talked about Romania's eastern neighbors, Moldova and Ukraine, which have both signed EU association agreements.
6Romania supports both countries joining the EU.
7Joint trade between Romania and Italy was about 12.6 billion euros ($14 billion) last year and Italy is Romania's second biggest trading partner, the foreign ministry said.
8Italy is the most popular country for Romanians to emigrate to, due to linguistic and historical links.
9Hundreds of thousands of Romanians live and work there.
