Microsoft to lay off 7,800 from Nokia handset business

1Microsoft said on Wednesday it would cut 7,800 jobs and write down about $7.6 billion related to its Nokia handset business, which it acquired in 2013.
2Most of the job cuts will be in the phone hardware business, underscoring the company's shift from hardware to its core software business.
3The Redmond, Wash., company was widely expected to write off all or part of the $7.2 billion it paid for Nokia's handset unit, a deal that left the company with a money-losing business and only 3 percent of the smartphone market.
4Microsoft's shares, after opening up about 1 percent, slipped to about break-even, at $44.29, in early morning trading on Wednesday.
5The latest round of job cuts is on top of the 18,000 job cuts the company announced last year.
6Microsoft, which had more than 118,000 employees worldwide as of March 31, said it would take a restructuring charge of about $750 million-to-$850 million.
7The company said it would record the charge in the fourth quarter.
