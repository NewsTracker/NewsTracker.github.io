Kenya Red Cross: 54 Killed in Cattle Raid Attacks

1Fifty-four people have been killed in Kenya's northern region in tribal fighting that police say was started by a cattle raid, the Kenya Red Cross said Wednesday.
2Five others were critically wounded in the attacks in Turkana county and 350 people have been displaced from their homes by the violence, the agency said.
3Police said the attacks began Monday when armed raiders believed to be Pokots attacked a Turkana village and stole animals, killing seven people.
4Turkana gunmen retaliated that night.
5Livestock-keeping nomadic tribes in Kenya's north routinely engage in traditional cattle raids, which experts say have become more deadly because the tribes have switched from using spears and arrows to guns.
6These nomadic tribes have also resisted police attempts to arrest those involved in the cattle raids.
7In November, 22 police officers were killed as they pursued cattle thieves.
8In November 2012, 42 police officers were killed pursuing herds of stolen cattle.
9Kenya's interior minister said Wednesday he is deeply disappointed over the killings that happened even after regional politicians signed a peace accord in January.
10Minister Joseph Nkaissery said the government had launched an operation to disarm all communities in the region.
