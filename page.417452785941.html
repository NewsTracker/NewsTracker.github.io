Asian stocks mixed as markets weigh Greece, US growth

1Asian stocks were mixed Tuesday as markets weighed a possible rebound in U.S. growth against doubts about Greece's ability to make debt payments and stay in the euro currency bloc.
2KEEPING SCORE: Japan's Nikkei 225 was up 0.1 percent at 20,584.75 and China's Shanghai Composite added 0.8 percent to 4,866.10.
3Hong Kong's Hang Seng shed 0.3 percent to 27,503.61.
4Australia's S&P/ASX 200 dropped 0.9 percent to 5,683.80 ahead of a central bank interest rate decision.
5Seoul's Kospi was down 1.1 percent to 2,078.85.
6GREEK DEADLINE: Greece's cash-strapped government faced fresh dissent from within the ruling Syriza party after failing to deliver on a promise to reach an agreement with rescue lenders over the weekend.
7Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras is struggling to reach a deal with bailout creditors to get more bailout loans, without which it cannot afford debt repayments it owes the IMF this month, starting Friday.
8But Tsipras faces opposition within his own party to any agreement that extends austerity measures that creditors have been demanding.
9THE QUOTE: "The risks in global markets are simmering under the surface: Grexit, increases to the Fed funds rate, China's equity valuations, Japan's QE program to 'nowhere,' ECB's QE program and Australia's inability to pull up the slowdown in the economy," said IG strategist Evan Lucas in a market commentary.
10WALL STREET: The biggest gainer in the S&P 500 was chip designer Altera, the target of a $17 billion cash offer by giant chip-maker Intel.
11Altera jumped $2.83 to $51.68, a 6 percent gain.
12Companies have been combining at a rapid clip, helping to boost stocks in the seventh year of the bull market.
13The S&P 500 rose 4.34 points, or 0.2 percent, to 2,111.73.
14The Dow Jones industrial average rose 29.69 points, or 0.2 percent, to 18,040.37.
15The Nasdaq composite climbed 12.90 points, or 0.3 percent, to 5,082.93.
