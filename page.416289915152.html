Pharmacy expert: Problem with Georgia execution drug caused by storage at low temperature

1State officials on March 2 called off the scheduled execution of Kelly Renee Gissendaner, saying the lethal injection drug they intended to use appeared "cloudy."
2The state Department of Corrections on Thursday released lab reports, a sworn statement from a pharmaceutical expert hired by the state and a short video showing a syringe of clear liquid with chunks of a white solid floating in the solution.
3Gissendaner's attorneys did not have a comment on the analysis.
4Gissendaner would have been the first woman executed by the state in 70 years.
