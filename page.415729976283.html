Boko Haram Kidnaps Hundreds of Nigerian Civilians

1Nigerian troops patrol in the northeastern Nigeria city of Mubi, some 20 kms (14 miles) west of the Cameroon border earlier this month.
2ABUJA, Nigeria (AP) - Hundreds of civilians, including many children, have been kidnapped and are being used as human shields by Boko Haram extremists, a top Nigerian official confirmed Wednesday.
3Several hundred people were abducted by the Islamic militants as they retreated earlier this month from Damasak in northeastern Nigeria, Mike Omeri, the Nigerian spokesman for the fight against Boko Haram, told The Associated Press Wednesday.
4He said he could not specify how many were kidnapped but local reports say as many as 500 people were taken.
5The Islamic rebels went to Damasak's primary schools and rounded up students and teachers and then retreated, said Omeri.
6Troops from Chad and Niger recaptured Damasak, near the border with Niger, from Boko Haram on March 16.
7The mass kidnapping happened as the extremists were fleeing the advancing troops and information about the abductions has only been confirmed now.
8The soldiers who recaptured Damasak found the town largely deserted.
9Damasak had been held for months by Boko Haram, who used the trading town as an administrative center.
10The troops from Chad and Niger who now hold Damasak have discovered evidence of a mass grave, Chad's ambassador to the U.N. Mahamat Zene Cherif confirmed Wednesday.
11International assistance is needed for the thousands of Nigerian refugees who have fled the violence, said the head of the U.N. refugee agency.
12Some 74,000 Nigerians have fled to neighboring Cameroon, according to the agency.
13Over 100,000 more have flooded into Chad and Niger.
14Troops from the three countries are now helping Nigeria to combat the militants and win back Nigerian towns.
15The refugee agency will funnel more resources to Cameroon, said U.N. High Commissioner for Refugees Antonio Guterres on Wednesday while visiting Maroua, the capital of Cameroon's Far North region.
16He stressed that additional assistance is needed.
17"Cameroon is today not only a very important protection space for refugees, but it is in the first line of defense of the international community," he said.
18The U.N. agency says the Nigerian crisis is one of the most underfunded in the world.
19In February, the agency asked for $71 million to assist displaced people in Nigeria and neighboring countries; already that figure appears to be too low, it said this week.
20Thus far, it has received only $6.8 million in donations, he said.
