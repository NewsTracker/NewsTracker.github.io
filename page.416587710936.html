Google commits millions to European news organizations

1BERLIN -- Google says it will give European publishers more than $162 million to help them adapt to the challenge of selling news online.
2The Internet giant has had a rocky relationship with the European publishing industry, which accuses Google of profiting from its content without sharing the revenue.
3Google said in a statement Tuesday it is establishing the Digital News Initiative together with eight leading publishers, including the Financial Times and the Guardian.
4The fund is open to other publishers and will focus on developing new distribution channels, ways to make consumers pay for news, and training for journalists.
5In this Tuesday, March 23, 2010 file photo, the Google logo is seen at the Google headquarters in Brussels.
6Last year, Google blocked Spanish publishers' reports from more than 70 Google News international editions due to a new Spanish law requiring aggregators to pay to link content.
