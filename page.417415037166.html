News24.com | $100 000 cheque awaits mystery recycler of rare Apple 1 computer

1Washington - A $100 000 cheque is waiting for a mystery woman who donated a rare Apple 1 computer to a Silicon Valley recycling firm.
2CleanBayArea in Milpitas, California, said on its web site that a woman in her 60s dropped off some electronic goods in April, when she was cleaning out the garage after her husband died.
3The boxes of computer parts contained a 1976 Apple 1, which the recycling firm sold for $200 000 in a private auction.
4The recycler's policy is to split the proceeds 50-50 with the person who donated the equipment.
5Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak built the computers in 1976 and sold them for $666.66 each.
6Only a few dozen of the ground breaking home computers are known to still exist.
7"We thought it was fake. It was real," CleanBayArea Vice President Victor Gichun told NBC news.
8He said he remembers what the donor looks like and all she has to do is show up.
9"Tell this lady to please come over to our warehouse in Milpitas again," Gichun said.
10"And we'll give her a cheque for $100 000."
