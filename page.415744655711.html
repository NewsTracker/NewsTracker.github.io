Bali nine: Joko Widodo too busy to talk to Tony Abbott about pair on death row

1Indonesian President Joko Widodo has been too busy to return Tony Abbott's phone call about the fate of the Bali nine pair on death row, Jakarta's envoy in Australia has said.
2Indonesian ambassador Nadjib Riphat Kesoema said in Canberra on Thursday that Mr Joko had taken Mr Abbott's initial call pressing for clemency for Andrew Chan and Myuran Sukumaran, but had been too busy travelling to talk to him since then.
3"The second and the third time - the President was so busy," he said.
4"As you know, the first program of the President is going to his people, to the provinces, not only in Java, in Kalimanta or Sumatra, but also in Papua. So there are many visits that he should make."
5Australian Prime Minister Tony Abbott and Indonesian President Joko Widodo.
6Mr Abbott sought a phone conversation with Mr Joko in early March to press Australia's case that the men had reformed and should be spared death by firing squad.
7Three weeks later, the conversation still has not taken place and Mr Joko is standing firm on his refusal to grant clemency for the pair.
8Mr Kesoema reiterated that the issue was not damaging relations between the countries.
9"Perhaps it will add some dynamcis of our reations but I don't believe that this case will harm the relations between the two countries because our foundation is very strong. We have common interests, so many things, in politics, in culture, in economy."
10Mr Kesoema insisted Indonesia would not see any cut in aid as retaliation by Australia if the executions go ahead.
11"We don't see it as retaliation. We never ask you for money. If you give it [to] us, it's, 'Thankyou so much' because you help our people ... It's the big heart of Australia."
