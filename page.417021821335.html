Bill, Hillary Clinton make $25M from 100 speeches: official

1WASHINGTON - For Bill and Hillary Clinton, silence isn't golden - speeches are.
2The couple raked in a whopping $25 million from about 100 paid speeches since January 2014, a Clinton campaign official said Friday.
3That's a long way from "dead broke" - Hillary Clinton's characterization of their finances when they left the White House in 2000.
4Though the total fees come to an average of about $250,000 a speech, the actual fees vary depending on the speaking engagement.
5The information is contained in disclosure reports the campaign is filing with the Federal Election Commission.
6Candidates are required to disclose their income to the FEC within 30 days of declaring their candidacy.
7Both Clintons have traveled widely to give paid addresses to corporate groups and others, routinely receiving six-figure fees.
8Bill Clinton recently rejected criticism of his huge speaking fees by saying: "I gotta pay our bills."
9Hillary Clinton also earned more than $5 million for her 2014 book, "Hard Choices," according to her campaign.
10The campaign said the couple paid an effective federal tax rate of 30% in 2014.
11The Clintons had no investments that earned capital gains.
