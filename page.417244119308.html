Refugee drama 'Dheepan' named best film at Cannes

1French director Jacques Audiard's refugee drama "Dheepan" won the Palme d'Or for best film at the Cannes Film Festival Sunday.
2The film is about a Tamil Tiger fighter called Dheepan who flees Sri Lanka to make his way to France, where he lands a job working as a caretaker in the crime-ridden outer suburbs of Paris.
3The movie was one of 19 competing for the Palme d'Or, which is one of the most prestigious awards in cinema.
4"It is not a documentary about Sri Lanka or social housing," Audiard saud at the premiere in Cannes last week.
5"The background is like wallpaper."
6"The film is a more love story than a thriller," he said, with Dheepan travelling to France with a woman and a young girl he meets in a refugee camp.
7They form a fake family in the hope of improving their chances of securing asylum in Europe.
8However, Dheepan soon finds he needs the skills he honed as a fighter in Sri Lanka to protect his new family in the war zone of Paris' tough suburbs.
9The Cannes jury awarded this year's Grand Prix to Hungarian director Laszlo Nemes for his graphic portrayal of Nazi Germany's death machine in "Saul Fia" (Son of Saul).
10The Grand Prix is the festival's second prize, after the Palme d'Or.
11Cannes' third prize, the jury prize, went to Greek director Yorgos Lanthimos for his offbeat social satire, "The Lobster."
12The best director prize was awarded to Taiwan's Hou Hsiao-Hsien for the martial arts movie "Nie Yinniang" (The Assassin), about a female assassin in 9th century China.
13France's Vincent Lindon won the award for best actor for his role as a man struggling with the humiliations of looking for work in the film "La Loi du Marche" (The Measure of Man) from French director Stephane Brize.
14Rooney Mara from the United States and France's Emmanuelle Bercot shared the award for best actress.
15Bercot starred in French director Maiwenn's movie "Mon Roi", about a woman recovering from a difficult relationship with her father, while Mara starred in U.S. director Todd Haynes' lesbian love story "Carol."
16Mexican director Michel Franco won the best screenplay award for his movie "Chronic," about a troubled nurse who cares for terminally ill patients.
