2 Al-Jazeera journalists freed from prison in Egypt

1CAIRO (AP) - Two Al-Jazeera English journalists were released from an Egyptian prison on Friday, after spending more than a year behind bars on terror-related charges in a case denounced as a sham by rights groups and the international community.
2The two - Mohammed Fahmy and Baher Mohammed - were freed pending a retrial.
3The network said by midday that they have been reunited with their families in Cairo.
4A third co-worker, Australian Peter Greste, was freed two weeks ago and deported to his home-country, Australia.
5Baher was initially sentenced to 10 years in prison and Fahmy to seven years but Egypt's top appeals court subsequently ordered a retrial after overturning the initial ruling, citing "flawed evidence."
6On the first session of the retrial on Thursday, the judge ordered their release.
7Now that both are out, their next court hearing is due Feb. 23 and they have to check in at a police station every day until then.
8Early on Friday, Fahmy's brother tweeted that he posted $33,000 bail to grant his brother's release, following a court decision on Thursday.
9Mohammed's wife, Jehane Rashed, told The Associated Press that her husband arrived home around 7 a.m. Photos on Facebook and video aired by Al-Jazeera showed him hugging and playing with his children - one of whom was born during his detention - and wearing clothes with the tag, "Free Press."
10"I will continue fighting for the freedom of expression, and I will not back off," he said in the video.
11He was freed without bail.
12The Al-Jazeera reporters spent over 400 days in prison after being caught in the bitter feud between Egypt and Qatar, main backer of Egypt's Muslim Brotherhood group, from which ousted President Mohammed Morsi hails.
13Thursday's decision indicated the court was moving ahead with a retrial of Fahmy and Mohammed, but several scenarios are possible.
14The court could eventually throw out the case, acquit them, convict them but sentence them to time served, or impose more prison time, with the possibility of a pardon from President Abdel-Fattah el-Sissi.
15Like Australian Greste, Fahmy, who is Canadian, could also be deported but prospects for Baher, who only has Egyptian citizenship, remain murkier.
16Cairo has signaled it wants to resolve the case and end the criticism ahead of a major economic conference next month to drum up international investment.
17Egypt's staggered parliamentary elections are also due to start in March.
18The three, who worked for Al-Jazeera's English-language channel, were arrested in December 2013 and accused of belonging to the Brotherhood, which was branded a terrorist organization after Morsi's removal from power earlier that year.
19Since the ouster, Egypt has been cracking down heavily on Morsi's supporters, and the journalists were accused of being mouthpieces for the Brotherhood and falsifying footage to suggest that Egypt faces civil war.
20They rejected the charges against them, saying they were simply reporting the news.
21They were convicted by a lower court on terrorism-related charges and sentenced to at least seven years in prison.
22The Court of Cassation, the country's highest appeals court, also said in ordering the retrial that the initial proceedings were marred by violations of the defendants' rights.
