Arab-Israeli political leaders reject Netanyahu's apology

1FILE - In this March 18, 2015, file photo, Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu greets supporters at the party's election headquarters in Tel Aviv.
2Netanyahu apologized to Israel's Arab citizens on Monday, March 23, 2015, for remarks he made during last week's parliament election that offended members of the community.
3JERUSALEM (AP) - Arab political leaders in Israel on Tuesday rejected Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's apology for comments he made in last week's national elections that offended members of the Arab community and said his words made him unsuitable to return for a third consecutive term on the job.
4The spat has touched on longstanding claims of discrimination by Israel's Arab minority, which makes up 20 percent of the Jewish state, and signaled that the rift will not be healed anytime soon.
5An Arab advocacy center in Israel said the country's national elections brought an "unprecedented level of racist incitement" against the minority community.
6In the heat of a close race last week, Netanyahu posted a video on his Facebook page where he implored his hard-line supporters to head to the polls, saying that "left-wing organizations" were bussing Arabs to the polls "in droves."
7The comments drew accusations of racism from Arab voters and a White House rebuke.
8Netanyahu told a gathering of Arab dignitaries Monday, "I know that what I said a few days ago offended some of Israel's citizens, offended Israeli Arabs. I had no intention of doing so. I am sorry for this."
9But Arab politicians from the Joint List - a new coalition of mostly Arab parties - said they were not invited to the gathering at the prime minister's residence.
10"Why didn't he call us? Why didn't he invite us?" said Ayman Odeh, head of the Joint List.
11There was no immediate comment from Netanyahu's Likud party.
12Arab politicians said his election victory was illegitimate, claiming Netanyahu won the vote by pandering to anti-Arab fears.
13"We call on Netanyahu to return the mandate he received on the basis of incitement and fear-mongering," the Joint List said in a statement.
14Netanyahu has secured a majority of backers in the new parliament and is expected to be formally tapped on Wednesday to form the next government.
15Opinion polls conducted on the eve of elections showed Netanyahu's conservative Likud party trailing behind his main challenger, the center-left Zionist Union.
16His sudden turnaround victory came as Netanyahu made his remark about Arab voters in the final hours of voting.
17His comments on Arabs, along with a declaration that he would not allow the establishment of a Palestinian state on his watch, have infuriated the White House.
18And his attempts to backtrack on both positions have been greeted with skepticism.
19"We just don't know what to believe at this point," State Department spokeswoman Marie Harf said Monday.
20The controversy comes amid the backdrop of the Arab-Israeli community's newfound political might.
21The Joint List made unprecedented gains in the March 17 election, earning enough votes to make it the third-largest party in Israel's 120-seat parliament.
22The Joint List said election results showed that more than 90 percent of Arab-Israeli voters supported the coalition in the elections.
23Arab-Israeli politician Ahmed Tibi told Israel Radio Tuesday that Netanyahu's attempt to defuse the dispute was "not an honest apology."
24Emilie Moatti, a spokeswoman for the Joint List, said the Arab dignitaries who gathered at Netanyahu's home were all Likud supporters, with no Joint List representatives invited.
25Yunis Meri, chair of the municipal council in the Arab-Israeli village of Fureidis, and a Likud supporter, said he hugged the prime minister at the gathering.
26"What he said before the elections, he put it behind him," Meri said.
27He said his support for Likud has paid off for his village in the form of easier access to government channels to advance local building projects.
28"If you know them, you open the door and you get an answer. That is my advantage," he said.
29In the Arab village of Abu Ghosh, a symbol of coexistence with its famous hummus restaurants packed with Jewish visitors, Arab residents were not impressed by Netanyahu's kind words.
