NFL player Ray McDonald arrested in domestic violence case (Yahoo Sports)

1SANTA CLARA, Calif.
2(AP) -- Police say Chicago Bears defensive end Ray McDonald has been accused of domestic violence in Northern California, the latest arrest for the former San Francisco 49er.
3Santa Clara police Lt. Kurt Clarke tells the San Francisco Chronicle (http://bit.ly/1cWyGwZ ) that McDonald was taken into custody at 7 a.m. Monday on suspicion of domestic violence and child endangerment.
4Clarke didn't immediately return calls or an email from The Associated Press.
5The Bears did not respond to a message seeking comment Monday.
6Chicago signed McDonald to a one-year contract in late March knowing it was a gamble given his history of legal issues.
7Chairman George McCaskey even acknowledged at the time that he initially told new general manager Ryan Pace not to go after him.
8But McCaskey came away impressed from a face-to-face conversation that he described as ''very candid, very forthright'' and ''difficult'' after McDonald paid his way to Chicago.
9His release from the team came just a month after the Santa Clara County district attorney's office announced it had declined to file charges against McDonald in a separate domestic violence investigation stemming from an arrest on Aug. 31 while celebrating his 30th birthday at his Northern California home.
10Prosecutors cited conflicting versions of what happened, a lack of verifiable eyewitnesses and a lack of cooperation by the alleged victim, McDonald's fiancee, in explaining their decision not to pursue charges in the domestic violence investigation.
11In March, McDonald filed a defamation lawsuit against a woman who had accused him of rape.
12McDonald says security camera footage will show a consensual sexual encounter occurred in his hot tub.
13Police say the woman doesn't recall any sexual encounters and reported blacking out after drinking alcohol and falling at McDonald's home.
14She said she went to police after waking up naked next to McDonald.
15The Santa Clara County district attorney is considering whether to file criminal charges.
16McDonald had been signed by the 49ers through the 2015 season.
17He had three sacks in 14 games last year.
18McDonald had 19 1/2 sacks in eight seasons with the 49ers.
