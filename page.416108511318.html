US STOCKS-Wall St searches for direction with data, earnings eyed

1The market has lately struggled to digest economic data, since strong numbers would back the idea that the Federal Reserve should begin raising interest rates.
2Weak data, on the other hand, could mean the economic slowdown in the first quarter was not just weather related.
3The energy sector helped buoy the market with a 0.6 percent gain on the back of a rebound in crude futures prices.
4Stocks are struggling to find direction because investors are caught between the angst of losing the support Fed policy has given the market and having few other options regarding where to put their money, said Tim Rudderow, president and chief investment officer at Mount Lucas Management in Newtown, Pennsylvania.
5"It's a very difficult argument to make that I should take my money out of the stock market," he said, adding that stocks will likely tread water even through earnings season.
6Alcoa shares fell 4.9 percent to $13.00 after revenue lagged analysts' estimates in the first quarter.
7The Dow Jones industrial average fell 47.58 points, or 0.27 percent, to 17,854.93, the S&P 500 lost 4.17 points, or 0.2 percent, to 2,077.73 and the Nasdaq Composite dropped 9.05 points, or 0.18 percent, to 4,941.77.
8Initial claims for unemployment benefits rose 14,000 to a seasonally adjusted 281,000.
9Economists polled by Reuters had forecast claims rising to 285,000.
10Altera shares fell 1 percent to $41.57 after CNBC, citing sources, reported that Intel dropped its takeover offer after the two companies could not agree on a price.
11Intel shares were down less than 0.1 percent.
12Walgreen shares gained 3 percent to $90.33 after the company reported a better-than-expected quarterly profit and said it would close about 200 stores in the United States as part of a restructuring plan aimed at cutting costs and streamlining its businesses.
13Shares of Zynga lost 14.5 percent to $2.48 after announcing on Wednesday that Chief Executive Don Mattrick will step down and founder Mark Pincus will return to head the company.
14U.S.-traded shares of Novogen jumped 28.2 percent to $5.78 after the Australian drug company said studies confirmed a potential drug to treat a type of skin cancer.
15Taser added 7.4 percent to $27.03 after the body-worn camera manufacturer said it received its first significant order from the London police department.
