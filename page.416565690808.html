Germanwings victims remembered in Barcelona memorial service

1Madrid (AFP) - Some 1,500 people attended a memorial service Monday at Barcelona's iconic Sagrada Familia Cathedral, and paid homage to the 150 people killed in last month's Germanwings plane crash.
2The March 24 budget flight took off from Barcelona but never made it to its Duesseldorf destination.
3It crashed in the French Alps, killing everyone on board, including 72 Germans and 50 Spaniards.
4Spain's King Felipe VI, Queen Letizia, Prime Minister Mariano Rajoy and French State Secretary for European Affairs Harlem Desir were among the mourners on Monday.
5The ceremony was held in several languages.
6Students from a college near Barcelona, which had hosted 16 German teenagers before their tragic deaths, held a solemn march.
7They lit up 150 candles, one for each victim, placing them at the altar of the impressive cathedral, which was designed by Catalan modernist architect Antonio Gaudi.
8Families of the 50 Spanish victims accompanied dozens of the Germans' relatives at the ceremony.
9Lufthansa chief executive Carsten Spohr and Germanwings managing director Thomas Winkelmann were also present.
10On April 17, grieving relatives already joined political and religious leaders at a sombre memorial service in Cologne, Germany.
