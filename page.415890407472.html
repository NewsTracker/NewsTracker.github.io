Mark Wahlberg to produce Boston Marathon bombing movie

1LOS ANGELES - Mark Wahlberg, Scott Stuber, Dylan Clark, Stephen Levinson and Michael Radutzky will produce "Patriots' Day," a movie chronicling the events of the 2013 Boston Marathon bombing, for CBS Films.
2No director or actor is attached yet, but a source indicated that Wahlberg is considering coming on board for one of the roles.
3"Patriots' Day" is based on the account of Boston Police commissioner Ed Davis.
4Matt Charman ("Bridge of Spies") is writing the screenplay and Nicholas Nesbitt will executive produce.
5Davis worked with the FBI, Watertown Police Department, Boston Police Department, Massachusetts State Police and local first responders to track, identify and apprehend the suspected bombers.
6The announcement came Tuesday as the defense rested its case in the trial of Dzhokhar Tsarnaev, who is accused of the April 15, 2013 attack.
7Juror deliberations will begin Monday after closing arguments.
8Radutzky is a "60 Minutes" senior producer who leads the broadcast's creative development unit, 60 Minutes Productions.
9CBS Films has secured Davis' life rights as well as the information in connection with the "60 Minutes" story in which he appeared.
10Davis was appointed the police commissioner of the Boston Police Department in 2006, serving seven years before announcing his retirement in 2013.
11He serves as the security analyst on WBZ News Boston.
12Wahlberg is a Boston native.
13He portrayed Bostonians in "The Departed," "The Fighter" and "Ted."
