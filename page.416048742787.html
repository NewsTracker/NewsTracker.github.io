Opposition Strike Shuts Down Nepal; Protesters, Police Clash

1Opposition protesters clashed with police and set vehicles on fire in Nepal's capital on Tuesday, as they enforced a nationwide general strike that crippled life throughout the country.
2The alliance of 30 opposition parties is demanding the Himalayan nation's new constitution draft be passed by a national consensus.
3The ruling coalition has been attempting to get the draft approved through a vote in parliament after negotiations with the opposition failed.
4Protesters and police clashed in Kathmandu, the capital, on the first day of the three-day strike.
5Riot police fired tear gas shells, while the protesters retaliated by throwing stones.
6The demonstrators also set a taxi and a motorcycle on fire.
7Taxi driver Niraj Vaidya told reporters that the protesters poured gasoline on his taxi and set it on fire while he was still inside the vehicle.
8He was able to escape, but said he lost his taxi in the fire.
9General strikes are a common tactic for the opposition in Nepal, and it is common for protesters to attack vehicles, shops and businesses that defy the calls to strike.
10Schools were closed and markets shuttered across the country.
11Highways were deserted, with protesters allowing only emergency vehicles on the roads.
