Town evacuated in Chile as volcano erupts

1Around 1,500 people have been evacuated after the Calbuco volcano in southern Chile erupted for the first time in 43 years.
2The evacuation of the town of Ensenada was a precaution, authorities in Chile said.
3Trevor Moffat, who lives in Ensenada, some 10 km from the volcano, said he and his family fled when the volcano erupted.
4Among Chile’s most active volcanoes Calbuco, which is 620 miles south of Santiago, last erupted in 1972.
5Apart from Indonesia, Chile has the largest chain of volcanoes in the world including 500 which are potentially active.
