Islamic State said to battle Syrian army near ancient Palmyra citadel

1BEIRUT, May 16 (Reuters) - Fighting between Islamic State militants and the Syrian army was reported on Saturday near an ancient citadel in the historic city of Palmyra, the target of a big offensive by the jihadist group that has raised concern for the U.N. world heritage site.
2The Syrian Observatory for Human Rights, a UK-based group that reports on the war, also said Islamic State militants had executed 23 people on Friday including nine minors and five women in areas seized from state control outside the city.
3A Syrian military source said Islamic State was keeping up its attack but at "a far distance" from the city which had been secured with army reinforcements.
4Fighting continued on Saturday but at a lower intensity.
5Palmyra, also known as Tadmur, is home to extensive ruins of one of the most important cultural centres of the ancient world.
6It was put on UNESCO's list of World Heritage in danger in 2013.
7Islamic State, which espouses a puritanical Islamist ideology, has destroyed antiquities and ancient monuments in Iraq.
8The government's antiquities chief has said the jihadists will destroy Palmyra's ruins too if they take the area.
9Palmyra is also of strategic importance, sitting at a highway intersection linking it to the cities of Homs and Damascus, some 240 km (150 miles) to the southwest.
10The mass execution reported by the Observatory is the second such killing it has recorded since Islamic State advanced this week into the area.
11In the first, the Observatory said the jihadists had executed 26 men, beheading 10 of them.
12The Syrian military source said there had been one massacre of 30 or more people in that area, including elderly men.
13The Syrian military has been mounting air strikes against Islamic State fighters in the area.
14Rami Abdulrahman, who runs the Observatory, said the sides were fighting near a military intelligence building in Palmyra on Saturday.
15Fighting was also reported at a gas field to the east of Palmyra.
16A statement posted by Islamic State supporters on Twitter said the group had taken large parts of the gas field.
17The military source said that attack was also repelled.
18The Islamic State offensive in central Syria has added to the pressures facing government forces that have faced significant setbacks since late March in the four-year-long war.
19Other insurgent groups fighting President Bashar al-Assad have seized control of wide areas of the northwestern province of Idlib since late March.
20Assad has also lost a border crossing with Jordan in the south.
21This week the Syrian army and the allied Lebanese group Hezbollah have driven insurgents from wide areas of the mountainous region to the north of Damascus, shoring up Assad's grip over the border zone between Syria and Lebanon.
