Warriors beat Pelicans 97-87 to take 2-0 series lead (Yahoo Sports)

1OAKLAND, Calif.
2(AP) -- Golden State Warriors coach Steve Kerr likes to say his team teeters on ''explosive and careless'' basketball, pulling off an astonishing play one moment and a perplexing one the next.
3Kerr saw both extremes Monday night.
4And the better half was just good enough to defend home court.
5Klay Thompson scored 26 points, Stephen Curry had 22 points and six assists and the Warriors regrouped from an early deficit to beat the New Orleans Pelicans 97-87 and take a 2-0 lead in their first-round playoff series.
6''We still get excited at times and do some crazy things.
7I kind of like the fact that we walk that line,'' Kerr said.
8The top-seeded Warriors fell behind by 13 points in the first quarter after a strong start by Anthony Davis and Eric Gordon quieted an announced sellout crowd of 19,596 wearing golden yellow shirts.
9But a big burst before halftime pushed the Warriors ahead, and their defense clamped down in the closing moments to put away the pesky Pelicans.
10Game 3 of the best-of-seven series is Thursday night in New Orleans.
11Davis had 26 points and 10 rebounds, and Gordon scored 23 points for a Pelicans team that played with more poise and passion than it did in the series opener.
12''We just have to stick with it,'' Gordon said.
13''We're playing the best team in the league and we're fighting tooth and nail,'' Pelicans coach Monty Williams added.
14In the end, the Warriors were just better when it mattered most.
15Golden State turned up the NBA's top-rated defense late, holding the Pelicans to 35 points in the second half.
16New Orleans shot just 37.8 for the game.
17The Pelicans still pulled within one in the final minutes before Draymond Green and center Andrew Bogut - Golden State's defensive stoppers - helped the Warriors shut down Davis and New Orleans again.
18Green finished with 14 points, 12 rebounds and five assists.
19Thompson also converted a running bank shot over Gordon to start a three-point play, and Bogut followed with a two-handed slam to give Golden State a 97-86 lead with 1:02 left, fending off a tougher-than-expected fight from the West's eighth seed.
20''We know we're going to have turnovers or lapses.
21But it's the defense that won us the game,'' Thompson said.
22The Warriors have won 20 straight games and 41 of 43 at home this season.
23Their last loss at Oracle Arena came against Chicago in overtime on Jan. 27.
24New Orleans shook of all those marks - and all the pregame chatter - to nearly pull off a playoff surprise.
25Williams riled up the Warriors' vocal fan base before the morning shootaround, saying the decibel level at Oracle Arena might not be legal.
26Williams later said he meant it more as a compliment and didn't mean to suggest the Warriors were breaking any rules.
27Fans seemed to pounce on Williams' words.
28Chants of ''War-ri-ors!'' drowned out the Pelicans during pregame introductions, but they figured out a way to control the crowd: get off to a fast start.
29New Orleans made five of its first six shots and got big contributions from Gordon and Davis to go ahead 28-17 after the first quarter.
30Reserve guard Leandro Barbosa (12 points) and the backups brought the Warriors back in the second quarter, though Pelicans super sub Norris Cole countered with some big shots of his own.
31Of course, the Pelicans struggled to corral Curry and Thompson in the key moments.
32They helped the Warriors outscore New Orleans 38-24 in the second quarter, with Curry capping the run with a deep 3-pointer to give Golden State a 55-52 halftime lead.
33The Warriors went up by nine early in the third quarter, and the teams were tied 71-all entering the fourth.
34''We know that we can beat this team,'' Davis said.
35''We were right there.
36Cole played heavier minutes at backup point guard in place of Jrue Holiday, who sat out with soreness in his lower right leg.
37He helped spell starter Tyreke Evans, who played with a bone bruise in his left knee.
38Evans had 16 points, 10 rebounds and seven assists.
39Cole had 11 points in 28 minutes.
