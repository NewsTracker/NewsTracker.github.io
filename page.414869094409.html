Justice Department May Sue Ferguson Police Over Racial Bias

Oscars 2015 diary

1While the protests over the shooting of Michael Brown have mostly died down, the Justice Department is still conducting two investigations related to the case, and it looks like we'll hear the results shortly.
2In the fall, the department launched probes into both the shooting and police department operations.
3On Tuesday, Attorney General Eric Holder said he's "satisfied with the progress" that's been made and "comfortable" saying he'll be able to announce the findings before he steps down in several weeks.
4Now sources tell CNN that, as expected, charges will not be brought against former officer Darren Wilson, but the Justice Department did find a pattern of racial discrimination in the department's tactics, and is prepared to sue if the force does not implement changes.
5Earlier this month, 15 people from the St. Louis region filed lawsuits against Ferguson and nearby Jennings, alleging the cities essentially created debtors' prisons by targeting poor black residents for tickets over minor offenses.
6They claim they were thrown in unsanitary and overcrowded jails when they were unable to pay the fines, all in an effort to generate revenue for the town.
7A law enforcement official told Politico that the Justice Department has been "seriously examining" these allegations and meeting with lawyers from groups working with the plaintiffs.
8"All discussions [with the Justice Department] have focused on what we believe to be a connection between the allegations of police misconduct and the erosion of trust between members of communities of color and their government," said Thomas Harvey, executive director of Arch City Defenders.
9Even if the Justice Department concludes that Ferguson police "engaged in systemic violations," the department may be able to avoid a lawsuit.
10Under Holder, dozens of police departments have been investigated for civil-rights abuses, which often end with the police agreeing to make specific reforms and submitting to federal monitoring.
11So far, Ferguson's police chief, Thomas Jackson, has suggested that he's willing to work with the Justice Department.
12He told CNN, "Everything they suggested in the past has been reasonable and we have tried to comply."

44From Baftas to Oscars?
45Much of the attention at the Oscars is about the A-list stars on the red carpet.
46But the majority of the nominees are off-screen talent such as cinematographers, costume designers, film editors and musicians who help breathe life into a film.
47On Friday I was at the Great British Film Reception in Hollywood which celebrates the UK's films and talent that have earned 39 nominations across 19 categories at the Oscars.
48It was a chance to catch up with Ben Wilkins (see diary entry from 3 February) who is nominated for sound mixing for Whiplash.
49He won a Bafta two weeks ago and admits: "It's been surreal, but a good sort of surreal. Sunday is a big deal".
50His interest in all things sonic goes back to a BBC sound effects album that he owned as a child.
51"I still have it," he admits, "An LP of horror and death sound effects. My favourite is spooky church bells - with crows cawing in the background."
52Another Bafta winner was Michael Lennox, whose Boogaloo and Graham is up for live action short at the Oscars.
53Set in 1970s Belfast, it is about two young boys who discover the facts of life with the help of their pet chickens.
54The film won a Bafta and has been in demand at film festivals around the world.
55"We call it our wee chicken movie," Lennox told me.
56"It's a comedy, and it's translated amazingly. People respond to the boys and their performances. It's a film with a big heart."
57Lennox is already working on a feature film, in which the same boys from Boogaloo and Graham get to meet Father Christmas.
58Its title?
59The Night I Got Shot by Santa.
60"I've never been to the Oscars, I've been to few pre Oscar parties," Stephen Fry tells me at the Oscar Wilde awards in Santa Monica, where he is presenting an honour to Star Wars actress Carrie Fisher.
61But what does the host of the Baftas think of his Oscars counterpart Neil Patrick Harris?
62"He is a really good idea for a host - such a good song and dance man. At the Oscars you do expect there to be big numbers. It's a shameless and unembarrassed celebration of the film year."
63Is he backing Birdman or Boyhood for best picture?
64"Film-makers admire the enterprise of Boyhood, but Birdman has an originality and a charm and a confidence. I would go for Birdman over Boyhood only because I'm shamelessly a child of my generation who finds anything above an hour and three quarters too much for a film!"
65Also on the Oscar Wilde green carpet was US satirist Stephen Colbert who described Neil Patrick Harris as "a very attractive whippet-thin man".
66"He's going to be a perfect Oscars host - he looks like a statuette."
67Celebrity chef Wolfgang Puck has revealed what the stars will be eating at the traditional Governors Ball immediately after the Oscars ceremony.
68"All of our guests will go the theatre hungry because they don't eat all day," Puck said.
69"This is day to eat and drink whether you win or lose."
70The Austrian-born chef is creating the gala party menu for the 21st year in a row.
71"I saw Barbra Streisand a couple of weeks ago and she said she was coming to the Oscars but I have to make the chicken pot pie," he said.
72Sweets include a chocolate cake in the form of Charlie Chaplin's hat and 5,000 chocolate Oscars covered in 24 carat gold.
73The chef told me he actually prepares enough food to cater for 2,000 people, but none of it goes to waste.
74"We always have more than enough, but we have the trucks ready for afterwards and we bring all the leftovers to the homeless downtown.
75"So nothing gets wasted and even the homeless can eat like George Clooney."

80Meanwhile, in the run-up to the Oscars the irreverent "Toscars" have taken place.
81The annual event - run by British expats in Los Angeles - features low-budget parodies of this year's Oscar hopefuls made in just three weeks.
82This year Big BirdMan swept the board with six Toscars, including best film.
83Other parodies included With-Lash, BoyWood, The Immigration Game, American Snapper and (wince) The Great Rudapest Motel.
84What's it like having a small role in a film which is up for best picture?
85That's what I asked actress Maxine Peake when I caught up with her new play How to Hold Your Breath at the Royal Court in London.
86In Oscar-nominated biopic The Theory of Everything, the Bolton-born actress plays Professor Stephen Hawking's nurse and second wife, Elaine Mason.
87"I was in it for a couple of scenes," she tells me.
88"I got the scripts and I rocked up and I was made to feel so welcome."
89"It's nice to be attached to something like that. It's so lovely when it does well."
90And Peake hopes her role in such a successful film might lead to more movie roles.
91"Normally I'm in films that are made for 5,000 and are all cobbled together - not that there's anything wrong with that.
92"Because I'm attached to things like Theory of Everything, then maybe I'll get other nice little parts in bigger budget films."

150USA Today says there's a little history being made in the original score category.
151"For the first time at the Academy Awards, no American composer was nominated," it points out.

153If you watched the Bafta Film Awards last night, you might have noticed that neither Richard Linklater nor Wes Anderson were on hand to collect awards for Boyhood and The Grand Budapest Hotel respectively.
154That was because their presence was required elsewhere - by the Directors Guild of America (DGA), who handed out their own shiny baubles on Saturday night in Los Angeles.
155Ironically enough, the evening's top award went to neither film-maker - a fact that may well have ramifications for the Academy Awards in two weeks' time.
156Instead it was Birdman director Alejandro Gonzalez Inarritu (pictured) who took home the DGA's award for Outstanding Directorial Achievement in Feature Film, upping his chances of landing the best director Oscar on 22 February.
157The DGA's award for feature film director has correctly predicted the best director Academy Award for 10 of the past 11 years and is consequently seen as a very reliable indicator of Oscars success.
158So will Inarritu be crowned best director at the Academy Awards, or will it be Bafta's choice, Linklater?
159Right now, it's too close to call.
160Speaking backstage after his win, Inarritu revealed he was wearing a tie previously owned by the late Billy Wilder and a shirt previously worn by Raymond Carver, the US author referenced in Birdman.
161"I'm just a humble guy wearing those masters," said the Mexican film-maker.
162Eyebrows were raised last month when Ava DuVernay failed to get a best director Oscar nod for her Martin Luther King drama, Selma.
163The former film publicist would have been the first African-American woman nominated in that category.
164But the lack of recognition came as no surprise to her.
165"There was shock by a lot of people but not a shock by me," she told me on a visit to London ahead of the film's UK release.
166"I'd been telling people since October it wasn't going to happen. No one listened to me.
167"I've worked on Oscar campaigns - I know how they work. I know that awards are awarded by branch and I know that people vote for what they know, who they've met and what appeals to them.
168"This is a film that's outside of that bracket. I know maybe two people out of 300 in my branch - I wasn't going round shaking hands and meeting folk. I was out promoting the movie.
169"And there's no precedent for it. Why would it happen for me and not for all the other black women who have made beautiful things before?"
170Selma did get nominated for best picture and best song.
171But DuVernay said she was disappointed David Oyelowo missed out on a best actor nod for his role as civil rights leader King.
172"I didn't need anyone to tell me it was the best performance of the year."
173The Academy Award for best foreign language film doesn't get a lot of attention on Oscar night - and some films remain little seen even when they win.
174But for some recent releases, such as Amour, winning the Oscar helped bring mainstream success.

176Many Oscar watchers have commented this year's best picture nominees are dominated by independents over big studio movies.
177But just how true is that?
178This fascinating article by Yannis Tzioumakis, senior lecturer in communication and media at the University of Liverpool, argues this year's Oscars don't suggest a shift in the Academy's tastes.
179"Instead, they demonstrate that the Hollywood majors are ever trying to copy and assimilate the independent film sector through an increasingly extensive use of their vast resources," he says.
