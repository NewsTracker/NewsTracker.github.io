Hurricane Andres Strengthens Far off Coast of Mexico

1Hurricane Andres is getting stronger as it spins far off the coast of Mexico.
2The first named storm of the eastern Pacific hurricane season poses no threat to land.
3Andres had maximum sustained winds of 85 mph (140 kph) Friday night.
4The U.S. National Hurricane Center in Miami says some additional strengthening is expected Saturday, but the storm should gradually weaken by Sunday.
5Andres is centered 740 miles (1,195 kms) off the southern tip of Baja California, Mexico, and is moving northwest at 7 mph (11 kph).
