Death toll in Mexico gas tanker explosion rises to 17

1MEXICO CITY -- The death toll from a March 26 gasoline tanker truck explosion in southern Mexico has risen to 17 after more victims died of burn injuries at hospitals.
2The explosion occurred in the Gulf coast state of Tabasco when villagers tried to take gasoline from a tanker truck that had crashed.
3The Tabasco state Health Department said in its Twitter account that 12 patients remain hospitalized in Tabasco and three others are being treated at a burn centre in Galveston, Texas.
4The state prosecutor's office said the 17th victim was a 45-year-old man.
5State officials are still investigating what ignited the gasoline.
6Prosecutors say inhabitants broke through a police perimeter and then chased off firefighters so they could steal gasoline.
