Small plane crashes after NCAA title game, killing ISU coach (Yahoo Sports)

1Wichita State won the game 70-55.
2BLOOMINGTON, Ill.
3(AP) -- A private plane returning from the NCAA basketball tournament in Indianapolis crashed in a central Illinois field on Tuesday, killing all seven people on board, including Illinois State University's associate head basketball coach and a deputy athletics director.
4Rescue personnel found no survivors at the site near the city of Bloomington, and a coroner pronounced the seven occupants dead, McLean County Sheriff Jon Sandage said at a news conference.
5The plane went down in a soybean field 2 1/2 miles from the Bloomington airport.
6All seven victims, who were found strapped in their seats, died from blunt force trauma resulting from the crash, said Coroner Kathleen Davis.
7The journey to Monday night's championship game began with a phone conversation.
8Scott Bittner, a 42-year-old business owner, got a call from sports bar owner Terry Stralow, 64, asking if he wanted to go to the game.
9''He said he had an extra ticket and asked him to go,'' said Terry Wertz, who worked alongside Bittner at a meat processing plant.
10They took off for the game in a plane that Bittner used regularly for business trips, owned by his father.
11It was not clear exactly how they were connected with the others on board, but local broadcasters talked about the group as if many of them were well acquainted with one another.
12Bittner and Stralow were two of the seven killed.
13Illinois State University President Larry Dietz confirmed in an email to students, faculty and staff that associate head basketball coach Torrey Ward, 36, and Aaron Leetch, 37, the athletic department's deputy director for external relations, also were killed in the early-morning crash.
14The email was released to media.
15''Words cannot fully express the grief that is felt in the wake of such a tragedy,'' Dietz wrote, adding that both men were well-respected and much-loved in the athletics department.
16Several players and staff carried through with an optional practice Tuesday afternoon at Redbird Arena.
17A spokesman said they would not make players or coaches available for comment.
18The Cessna 414 twin-engine aircraft took off from Indianapolis and crashed just short of the Central Illinois Regional Airport in Bloomington after midnight, the Federal Aviation Administration said.
19The National Transportation Safety Board was investigating, but there was no initial word on the cause of the crash.
20News photos from near the scene showed dense fog.
21The airport was open and all systems, including its runway lighting, were functioning, though the tower had closed several hours earlier and handed responsibility to an air traffic control facility in Peoria.
22Radar contact was lost moments before the crash and a search was launched when the pilot failed to close out his flight plan.
23It took about three hours to find the wreckage.
24NTSB investigator Todd Fox said the plane was cleared to land in fog and rain but appears to have made a turn away from approaching the runway before crashing.
25It isn't clear why, he said.
26Fox said he doesn't know if the plane was having problems or the pilot radioed in distress.
27Its engines caught fire on impact, Fox said.
28The pilot had flown about 12,000 hours and held an air transport license, which allows a pilot to fly commercial airliners, Fox said.
29He said the full NTSB investigation is expected to take a year to 18 months.
30The other victims included Jason Jones, 45, a former basketball and baseball player for Illinois Wesleyan University who worked at Wells Fargo Advisors.
31''He loved his children and his family more than anything in the world, wonderful man.
32That's really all I can say right now,'' said his wife, Lyndsey Jones.
33The McLean County coroner's office identified the other victims as Stralow, pilot Thomas Hileman, 51, and Andrew Butler, 40, an employee of Sprint.
34Bittner lived with his wife and two children in Towanda, a small village just outside Bloomington, his co-worker Wertz told The Associated Press.
35He owned a meat processing plant in Eureka, Illinois, carrying on the family's line of work after his dad established another plant in the small city of Chenoa, where Bittner grew up.
36''He always told me that he wasn't my boss, that I didn't work for him, I worked with him,'' said Wertz, who has worked at Bittner's Meat Co. for 15 years.
37''If I needed anything, he'd do anything for you,'' Wertz said through tears.
38The plant butchers livestock, including beef, pork, lambs and goats.
39The aircraft was registered to Make it Happen Aviation LLC of Towanda, Illinois.
