3Here are the facts in the case against Boston Marathon Bombing suspect Dzhokhar Tsarnaev.
4Closing arguments concluded Monday in the guilt-or-innocence phase of the trial with prosecutors ratcheting up their case that Tsarnaev, 21, is not the "passive, go-along-to-get-along guy" they say the defense made him out to be.
5Three people died and more than 260 were injured in twin blasts April 15, 2013 near the finish line of the marathon.
6If Tsarnaev is convicted -- and his lawyer has not denied Tsarnaev's involvement -- a hearing would be held to determine if he is executed or is sentenced to life in prison.
7Prosecutors described Tsarnaev as a true believer in the cause of radical, violent jihad to avenge what he saw as harm by the U.S. against Muslims.
8"The plan was to make this bombing as memorable as it could possibly be," said Assistant U.S. Attorney William Weinreb.
9The two sides clashed over the meaning of Tsarnaev's writings in the backyard boat where he was found after a shootout with police.
10The shootout ended when he fled in a stolen Mercedes SUV, running over his injured brother, Tamerlan, 26, who died.
11Assistant U.S. Attorney Aloke Chakravarty spoke at length about Dzhokhar Tsarnaev's secret life of recorded lectures, magazine articles and music of the violent jihad movement.
12From that life came the boat writings that reflected Tsarnaev's true motives, he said.
13"The U.S. government is killing our innocent civilians," Chakravarty read from what was scrawled in the blood-stained boat.
14"As a Muslim," Tsarnaev had written, "I can't stand see to such evil go unpunished."
15Defense attorney Judy Clarke said the writings did not represent Tsarnaev's "message to the world."
16If he had been a true jihadi, he would have penned "Death to America!" she said.
17Instead, he wrote what she termed "a 19-year-old's attempt to tell why they did what they did."
18"He knew all along it was wrong to take innocent life," Clarke said.
19"But he expressed the very twisted belief ... that his actions would make a difference."
