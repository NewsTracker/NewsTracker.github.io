Philadelphia Woman Arrested For Trying To Support ISIS

1Thirty-year-old Keonna Thomas, also known as Fatayat Al Khilafah and YoungLioness, was charged with trying to provide material support and resources, including herself as a fighter, to a foreign terrorist organization, the department said.
2Prosecutors allege that Thomas communicated with an Islamic State fighter in Syria, who asked her whether she wanted to take part in what was termed a martyrdom operation, to which she responded: "that would be amazing... a girl can only wish."
3The criminal complaint also alleges that Thomas did online research on indirect travel routes to Turkey and bought an electronic visa to the country.
4Turkey is a common transit point for people traveling from Europe to enter Syria and join Islamic State.
5The woman then purchased a plane ticket for a flight overseas, according to the allegations.
6Thomas does not yet have an attorney, a spokeswoman for the U.S. Attorney's Office in Philadelphia said.
7If convicted, Thomas faces a maximum sentence of 15 years in prison, according to the Justice Department.
