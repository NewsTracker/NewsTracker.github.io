Fighting surges in east Ukraine

1Ukrainian servicemen in a military truck near Krasnoarmiisk, Donetsk region, in eastern Ukraine on Tuesday.
2Kiev: Pro-Russian rebels have mounted one of the heaviest attacks on government troops since a February peace accord, Ukraine said, as the two sides blamed each other for a surge in fighting in the country's east.
3Eight government soldiers were killed and 16 wounded over the last 24 hours, said Ukrainian military spokesman Andriy Lysenko..
4Two rebels died and four were wounded in the same period, the separatist-controlled DAN news service reported, citing Eduard Basurin, deputy commander of the Donetsk People's Republic.
5In this photo taken Tuesday, Ukrainian soldiers on board armoured vehicle in the Donetsk region on Tuesday.
6"There's no sign that some massive military campaign has begun," said Yuriy Yakymenko, an analyst at the Razumkov Centre.
7"One day they fight, another day it's quiet, and then they attack again."
8While the fighting in Ukraine between rebels and government forces has subsided since the truce was signed in Minsk, the cease-fire remains fragile amid tensions over implementation of the agreements and accusations from both sides that the other is breaching the deal.
9Ukraine is seeking to regain control of its industrial heartland from the rebels, who insist on constitutional reform that may grant them vast autonomy from the administration in Kiev.
10The separatists' actions "mark another attempt by Russia and its puppets to completely ruin the Minsk agreements and renew active fighting," said Oleksandr Turchynov, head of Ukraine's National Defence and Security Council.
11Rebels attacked "in at least three important directions across the front line," he added.
12Manouvering in the Donetsk region of Ukraine, where fighting has surged in recent days.
13The insurgents violated the truce 100 times in the past 24 hours, according to Lysenko.
14Ukrainian troops breached it 86 times, DAN said citing the Defense Ministry of the self-declared republic.
15Five of the Ukrainian soldiers were killed when a mine exploded in the village of Bolotenne, northeast of the city of Luhansk, on Tuesday, according to the Interior Ministry.
16A group including negotiators from Ukraine, Russia, the rebels, and the Organisation for Security and Cooperation in Europe is set to meet on July 21.
17The OSCE hopes to see "some concrete steps" toward deescalation and weapon pullback during the meeting, said representative Martin Sajdik.
18At least 6,764 people have been killed in the 16-month conflict, according to the United Nations.
19The fighting has damaged Ukraine's economy and drained its reserves, forcing the government to seek debt relief from its foreign lenders to avert financial ruin.
