Serena wins 8th Key Biscayne title by beating Suarez Navarro (Yahoo Sports)

1(AP) -- Serena Williams won her eighth Key Biscayne title Saturday and remained unbeaten this year by drubbing Carla Suarez Navarro 6-2, 6-0 in the final of the Miami Open.
2Williams became the fourth woman to win the same WTA event at least eight times.
3Martina Navratilova won Chicago 12 times, the most titles by a woman at any tournament.
4Navratilova was on hand to present the top-ranked Williams with her latest trophy.
5Williams claimed her second title this year.
6She won her 19th Grand Slam championship at the Australian Open in January, and has now won 12 consecutive finals.
7Williams improved to 18-0 this year.
8She withdrew before the Indian Wells semifinal last month with a knee injury but quickly recovered.
9Williams was erratic at times as she navigated this year's draw, and was jeered for angrily pounding the court with her racket in an early-round match.
10But the sun-splashed crowd was firmly behind her in the final.
11A roar erupted when she entered the stadium, and she had them cheering her winners from the first point.
12Suarez Navarro looked nervous, and while she managed to hold in her first two service games, Williams pulled away from there.
13Williams' grunts grew louder as she closed out the first set, and she won the final 10 games, including 25 of 30 points in the second set.
14Mixing pace and direction, Williams won 21 of 22 points on her first serve, and she totaled 29 winners to three for Suarez Navarro.
15Williams won with more than power, coming forward to put away a volley on the first point, hitting delicate slice backhands and giving Suarez Navarro fits with her slice serve.
16Williams' demeanor was subdued for most of the match, which was 30 minutes old before her first fist pump.
17But she wore a big grin as she accepted the championship trophy from Navratilova.
18Williams improved to 5-0 against Suarez Navarro and has lost a total of 12 games in those matches.
19Now 73-7 at Key Biscayne, Williams has won her past 18 matches in the tournament.
20She considers it her home event because she lives 90 minutes up Interstate 95 in Palm Beach Gardens.
21She has gone home with the championship trophy eight of the 15 times she has entered, with her first title in 2002.
22The 26-year-old Suarez Navarro was playing in the biggest final of her career, and she'll crack the top 10 next week for the first time.
