Ex-FBI agent to get psychiatric exam after arrest at CIA

1ALEXANDRIA, Va.
2(AP) - A federal magistrate has ordered a psychiatric evaluation for an ex-FBI agent arrested at CIA headquarters after a confrontation with security officers.
3Tunisia Davis of Alexandria was charged with interfering with a federal officer, a misdemeanor, in federal court in Alexandria last week.
4After a combative court appearance Friday, U.S. Magistrate Theresa Carroll Buchanan ordered Davis held for a psychiatric evaluation.
5According to court records, Davis on Thursday morning visited NSA headquarters at Fort Meade, Maryland, then drove to CIA headquarters at Langley.
6She was arrested after refusing to follow CIA security officers' instructions.
7One officer said she tried to reach for an officer's gun while partially handcuffed and shouted that the CIA could be bombed.
8Court records say she was an FBI special agent from 2004 to 2010.
