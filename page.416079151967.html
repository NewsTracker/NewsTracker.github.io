Greek PM Tsipras in Moscow for talks

1Russian President Vladimir Putin has called for improved trade ties with Greece, during a visit to Moscow by Greek Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras.
2Mr Putin called the visit by Mr Tsipras "very timely" and hailed the two countries' "common spiritual roots".
3Greek officials have previously pointed to Russia as a possible alternative source of financial assistance to alleviate the country's debt crisis.
4But analysts say Russia's own economic woes mean any help would be limited.
5Greece's new government is embroiled in negotiations with the EU and IMF to unblock a bailout package and could run out of funds within weeks.
6Russia was among Greece's leading trade partners before sanctions on its energy industry and Greece's own economic woes dropped trade between the two countries by 40%.
7Mr Tsipras received a warm welcome in Moscow, which has seen its ties with the EU strained over Russia's actions in Ukraine.
8The Russian president said the two leaders would "analyse what the two of us could do to restore the previous rate of growth" in trade.
9Mr Tsipras said he aimed to "restart relations for the good of both our people".
10Russia is not in a position to solve Greece's economic issues however, said Constantinos Filis from the Institute of International Relations.
11"Russia is not and cannot be a (EU) substitute for Greece. It can only be a supplementary option," he said.
12Mr Putin and Mr Tsipras are also expected to discuss ties between the EU and Russia in the wake of the Ukraine crisis.
13Before his arrival, Mr Tsipras described the sanctions imposed by the EU and US on Russia in the wake of its annexation of the Crimea as "a road to nowhere".
14The European Parliament President, Martin Schulz, said Mr Tsipras should not break with the EU line on sanctions.
15"Greece demands and gets a lot of solidarity from the EU. We can therefore also ask for solidarity from Greece and for this solidarity not to be ended unilaterally by pulling out of joint measures," he told a regional German newspaper, the Muenchner Merkur.
16Russia imposed a ban on many western food imports in retaliation, but Agriculture Minister Nikolai Fyodorov has said the government could consider removing three countries, including Greece, from the embargo, Russian state media reported.
17The Greek government may play up cultural ties with Russia based on moments of shared history and a common religion.
18But the truth is that Greece's memberships of Nato and the EU are central to its modern identity and crucial to its economic survival.
19In theory a deal could be constructed where Greece could threaten to veto those EU sanctions in return for serious and sustained economic help, but in truth neither side is probably in a position to make such a deal even if it wanted to.
20Russia has too many economic problems of its own to rescue Greece even if it were minded to.
21The Greek authorities are not in a position to alienate the rest of the EU by undermining a shared position on Ukraine.
22Some sort of improvement in trade ties to bring Greek soft fruits back to the Russian market seems much more likely.
23Mr Tsipras came to power pledging to end austerity, but his plans have met resistance from Greece's EU/IMF creditors, who lent the country billions to help it avoid bankruptcy.
24Greece has not received bailout funds since August last year, with the EU and IMF dissatisfied with the pace of Greek reforms.
25A Greek repayment of 448m to the IMF is due this Thursday.
26On Tuesday, the Greek government said Germany owed Greece nearly 279bn ( 204bn; $303bn) in war reparations for the Nazi occupation during World War Two.
27It is the first time Greece has calculated what Germany allegedly owes.
28But Germany says the matter was resolved legally years ago, and reacting to the claim Economy Minister Sigmar Gabriel said it was "dumb" to link Greece's bailout with the question of war reparations.
