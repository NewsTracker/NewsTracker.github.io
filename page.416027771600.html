John Oliver grills Edward Snowden over potentially harmful NSA leaks

1John Oliver sat down with National Security Agency contractor-turned-whistleblower Edward Snowden on HBO's "Last Week Tonight" for a wide-ranging interview that was funny and, at times, surprisingly tense.
2Oliver, who flew to Moscow to conduct the interview, grilled Snowden on the leaked documents.
3"How many of those documents have you actually read?"
4Oliver asked Snowden.
5"I have evaluated all of the documents that are in the archive," the 29-year-old former CIA systems administrator said.
6"I do understand what I turned over."
7"There's a difference between understanding what's in the documents and reading what's in the documents," Oliver countered.
8"Because when you're handing over thousands of NSA documents the last thing you'd want to do is read them."
9The comedian and former "Daily Show" correspondent pointed out that Snowden's actions led to the disclosure of sensitive information beyond the NSA's controversial government surveillance program, including the revelation that the United States was monitoring al Qaeda in northern Iraq.
10"The New York Times took a slide, didn't redact it properly, and in the end it was possible for people to see that something was being used in Mosul on al Qaeda," Oliver said.
11"That is a problem," Snowden conceded.
12"Well, that's a f--- up," Oliver declared.
13"Right. But you have to own that then," Oliver said.
14"You're giving documents with information you know could be harmful, which could get out there."
15Snowden appeared to pause before offering a response: "Yes."
16The interview had plenty of lighter moments, too.
17Oliver asked Snowden - who has been living in asylum in Russia for more than 18 months - if he misses America, specifically Hot Pockets and "the entire state of Florida."
18"Yes, I miss Hot Pockets very much," Snowden replied.
19(Florida? Not so much.)
20Later, Oliver asked Snowden if the spy agency was collecting racy photos of Americans' private parts.
21"The good news is there's no program named 'The D--- Pic program,'" Snowden said.
22"The bad news is they're still collecting everybody's information - including your d--- pics."
