Google data on driverless car accidents made public

1Google said it would give monthly updates of accidents involving its driverless cars.
2The report for May showed Google cars had been involved in 12 accidents since the company first began testing its self-driving cars in 2009, mostly involving rear-ending.
3Google said one of its vehicles was rear-ended at a stoplight in California on Thursday, bringing the total count to 13 accidents.
4"That could mean that the vehicles tend to stop more quickly than human drivers expect," public interest group Consumer Watchdog said.
5A Google spokeswoman said the consumer watchdog conclusion was erroneous because most of the rear-end accidents occurred when the vehicle was stopped.
6Consumer Watchdog called for more details on the accidents, including statements from witnesses and other drivers.
7None of these accidents were caused by a fault with the car, Google said.
8Google's self-driving-car program director, Chris Urmson, said in May that the cars have been involved in 11 accidents.
9The accidents mostly involved Google's self-driving Lexus RX450h sport utility vehicles.
10Google is about to take its pod-like prototype vehicles off the test track and on to Mountain View, California, roads this summer.
11The company described the two-seater car as "the world's first fully self-driving vehicle."
