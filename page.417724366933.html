Greek Jitters Upset US, European Stock Markets

1A setback in talks between Greece and its creditors helped knock the stock market lower on Friday, amid renewed concerns that the country could default on its debts.
2Despite the drop, the Standard & Poor's 500 index managed to eke out a 1-point gain for the week, snapping a two-week slump.
3An unexpected decision by the International Monetary Fund to walk away from talks with Greece spurred the selling.
4At a summit meeting in Brussels late Thursday, the IMF pulled its negotiators out of talks with Greece, saying there had been no progress and that major differences remained on key issues.
5Without a deal by the end of the month, Greece faces the prospect of going bankrupt and dropping the euro currency.
6Markets are likely to make sudden turns until Greece and its creditors reach a deal, said Ninh Chung, head of investment strategy at SVB Asset Management.
7Earlier this week, stocks on both sides of the Atlantic rallied on reports of progress in the talks.
8"There had been optimism over Greece," Chung said, "and now it seems like we've had a complete 180."
9The S&P 500 slipped 14.75 points, or 0.7 percent, to close at 2,094.11.
10The Dow Jones industrial average fell 140.53 points, or 0.8 percent, to 17,898.84, and the Nasdaq composite lost 31.41 points, or 0.6 percent, to 5,051.10.
11The losses were modest but broad: All 30 companies in the Dow and all 10 industries in the S&P 500 finished with losses.
12Speculation over Greece's fate and the Federal Reserve's first interest rate increase have weighed on markets over recent weeks.
13Many think an improving U.S. economy will push the Fed to raise its benchmark interest rate later this year for the first time since the Great Recession.
14The Fed's ultra-cheap interest rates have helped fuel the six-year bull market in stocks.
15"I'm not sure the downside risk with Greece is as big as investors believe, but it's caught investors' attention," said Jack Ablin, chief investment officer at BMO Private Bank.
16"It's the same with the Fed tightening."
17Major indexes in Europe slumped on Friday, wiping out gains from earlier in the week.
18Greece's market fell the most, with the main Athens index down 6 percent.
19Germany's DAX sank 1.2 percent, and the CAC-40 in France finished with a loss of 1.4 percent.
20Britain's FTSE 100 lost 1 percent.
21In Asia, Japan's Nikkei 225 inched up 0.1 percent.
22In China, the Shanghai composite index advanced 0.9 percent, and Hong Kong's Hang Seng rose 1.4 percent.
23Back in the U.S., shares of Wingstop vaulted 61 percent in their first day of trading as a public company.
24The chicken-wings chain raised $110.2 million in its initial public offering late Thursday, selling shares at $19 each.
25Wingstop soared $11.59 to $30.59.
26Government bond prices wavered, finishing the day slightly lower.
27The yield on the10 -year Treasury inched up to 2.39 percent from 2.38 percent the day before.
28In metals trading, gold fell $1.20 to settle at $1,179.20 an ounce, while silver slipped 14 cents to $15.83 an ounce.
29Copper picked up a penny to $2.68 a pound.
30Oil fell for a second straight day after reaching a high for the year on Wednesday.
31Benchmark crude oil sank 81 cents to settle at $59.96 a barrel.
32Brent crude, an international benchmark, dropped $1.24 to $63.87 a barrel.
33- Natural gas dropped 7.5 cents to $2.75 per 1,000 cubic feet.
