Gov. Wolf renews case for his agenda in Facebook session

1Hundreds of written questions expressing viewpoints about tax reform and other issues were posted on Facebook before the event, but they were distilled into about a dozen questions that Wolf answered while sitting at a table with deputy press secretary Megan Healey, a former local TV journalist.
2In contrast, last month's Twitter Town Hall was faster-paced and allowed residents and interest groups to interact with him directly while tweeted questions scrolled on the screen.
3Wolf's spokesman, Jeff Sheridan, said the two formats require different approaches but are equally useful in getting out his boss's message.
4"We'll continue to do both," Sheridan said after the roughly 20-minute program.
5Among other things, the governor defended his proposal to increase the state sales tax from 6 percent to 6.6 percent and extend it to many transactions that are now exempt.
6The plan would generate more than $3 billion a year to finance much of his plan to slash school property taxes and provide additional aid to prekindergarten programs and public schools.
7He also has proposed a new tax on natural gas drilling and a reduction in the corporate net income tax.
8In a statement, the state Republican Party noted that Wolf is holding social media events while boosting a proposal that would make computer purchases more expensive.
9If the tax were increased, "many Pennsylvanians won't be able to afford to participate in one of his social media town halls next year," said GOP spokeswoman Megan Sweeney.
10Wolf said he hopes to build bipartisan support for a budget agreement in the GOP-controlled Legislature.
11The current state budget expires June 30.
12"Legislators, Republican and Democrat, heard the same thing I did from the voters of Pennsylvania. They want property tax relief. My budget has that in a big way," he said.
