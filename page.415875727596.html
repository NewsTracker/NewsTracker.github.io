1TIKRIT, Iraq (AP) - Iraqi forces battled Islamic State militants Tuesday holed up in downtown Tikrit as the country's prime minister announced security forces had reached the city's center.
2In a statement Tuesday, Prime Minister Haider al-Abadi said security forces already have retaken neighborhoods on the southern and western edges of Saddam Hussein's hometown and are moving to control the entire city.
3But Abdul-Wahab al-Saadi, the commander of the Salahuddin operation, said his forces fighting in from the west were still 300 meters (325 yards) from the center of Tikrit.
4Street-to-street fighting raged into Tuesday afternoon and estimates on how much of this strategic city on the banks of the Tigris River that Iraqi forces held widely differed.
5Iraqi army Lt. Gen. Talib Shaghati said at least 75 percent of the city had been recaptured.
6Ammar Hikmat, deputy governor of Salahuddin province, said more than 40 percent of Tikrit was under Iraqi control.

8"Our security forces are now pushing forward toward the presidential complex and have already entered parts of it," Hikmat said.
9"I think the whole city will be retaken within the coming 24 hours."
10An Associated Press reporter embedded with Iraqi security forces saw soldiers surround the iconic presidential palace in Tikrit, as well as surround the provincial government headquarters there.
11The Islamic State group seized the Sunni city last summer during its lightning advance across northern and western Iraq.
12The battle for Tikrit is seen as a key step toward eventually driving the extremists from Iraq's second-largest city Mosul, which is further north.

14Gen. Saad Maan Ibrahim said at least 40 militants were killed during the Tikrit offensive Tuesday as Iraqi forces dismantled 300 roadside bombs.
15He said that the federal police, backed by allied militias, were able to clear the government compound, the Tikrit provincial council headquarters, its security headquarters and the presidential palace.
16The leader of Iraq's Popular Mobilization Units, a collaborative force made up mostly of Shiite militias, also said Tuesday his fighters had rejoined the Tikrit operation, less than a week after announcing a boycott over U.S. involvement.
17Abu Mahdi al-Mohandis told the AP that his fighters participated in the southern offensive Tuesday and would be joining the offensive on the northern and western fronts shortly.
18Iranian military advisers have been providing significant support since the Tikrit offensive began on March 2, arming and training the Iraqi Shiite militias, which have played a prominent role on the battlefield.
19Militiamen make up more than two-thirds of the force fighting the Islamic State group in Tikrit.
20But the operation stalled until U.S. forces joined the offensive by launching airstrikes on March 25.
21Since then, Iraqi allied forces have moved in on the city, though they have been slowed by snipers and hidden bombs.
22The Iraqi military has struggled to recover from its collapse in June when facing the Islamic State group.
23In the face of the advance, commanders disappeared.
24Pleas for more ammunition went unanswered.
25In some cases, soldiers stripped off their uniforms and ran.
26Retaking Tikrit would be the biggest win so far for Baghdad's Shiite-led government.
27The city lies about 130 kilometers (80 miles) north of Baghdad and lies on the road connecting the capital to Mosul.
28Retaking it will help Iraqi forces have a major supply link for any future operation to retake Mosul.
