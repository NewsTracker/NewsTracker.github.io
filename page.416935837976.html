Nepal earthquake kills dozens, triggers panic [Photos] [Video]

1KATHMANDU - Nineteen people were killed in Nepal and 981 injured in an earthquake on Tuesday, home ministry official Laxmi Prasad Dhakal told Reuters, giving a first national estimate.
2The quake, with a magnitude of 7.3, struck less than three weeks after a 7.8 temblor killed more than 8,000 people and damaged hundreds of thousands of homes in the Himalayan nation.
3At least four people were killed in Chautara town in Sindhupalchowk district, north of the Nepali capital Kathmandu, a spokesman for the International Organization for Migration said.
4Police in the Himalayan nation confirmed at least three deaths, as the government struggled with patchy phone lines to gather information.
5It wasn't immediately clear if they were referring to the same people.
6Four people were also killed in Indian states bordering Nepal, one in Uttar Pradesh and three in Bihar, officials said.
7One person died in Tibet after rocks fell on a car, according to Chinese state media.
8The U.S. Geological Survey said Tuesday's earthquake was centred 68 km (about 42 miles) west of the town of Namche Bazaar, close to Mount Everest and the border with Tibet.
9A magnitude 7.3 quake, it was felt as far apart as New Delhi and Dhaka, the capital of Bangladesh.
10The quake was followed by at least half a dozen aftershocks, including one 6.3 magnitude quake.
11The temblor, which struck around 12:30 p.m. local time, came just weeks after a 7.8 quake killed more than 8,000 people and damaged hundreds of thousands of buildings in Nepal.
12The epicentre of Tuesday's quake was close to Everest Base Camp, which was evacuated after an avalanche triggered by last month's quake killed 18 climbers.
13Mountaineers seeking to scale the world's tallest peak have called off this year's Everest season.
14Nepal is still picking up the pieces from the devastation caused by last month's earthquake, the worst in more than 80 years in the country.
15Hundreds of thousands of buildings, including many ancient sites, were destroyed and many more were damaged.
16The latest earthquake unleashed panic in Kathmandu, Reuters witnesses said.
17Parents could be seen clutching children tightly and hundreds of people were frantically trying to call relatives on their cellphones.
18Shopkeepers closed their shops and the streets were jammed with people rushing to check on their families.
19"I'm heading straight home," said Bishal Rai, a man in his 20s, who said he was trying to contact his family in the north of the capital.
20Medics and volunteers formed a human chain at a Kathmandu hospital to keep a path open for ambulances.
21A volunteer at the hospital said five or six injured people had been brought in, two on stretchers.
22So far, few ambulances had arrived, he said.
23Some aid teams which were on their way back from Nepal were considering whether to return to the country to help.
24Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu, speaking at a welcoming ceremony for a military rescue delegation after it landed back in Tel Aviv from Nepal, said they were prepared to go back if needed.
25De Wojtek Wilk, CEO of the Polish Center for International Aid, said he could send his team of medics "back straight from the airport."
26"There is a huge concern for the people who may be living in structures that did not prove earthquake sound," he said.
27Indian Air Force spokesman Simranpal Singh Birdi said one MiG 17 aircraft stationed in Kathmandu has been sent to Namche Bazaar to assess the damage.
28"Our team is conducting an aerial survey before we decide to launch rescue operations," he said.
29In Sindhupalchowk district, which suffered the heaviest death toll in last month's quake, the new temblor also triggered at least three big landslides in the district.
30A district official there said 12 people had been injured.
31"The latest earthquake has left us shaken. I am still trembling," the official, Diwakar Koirala, said, when reached soon after the quake.
32Rhita Doma Sherpa, a nurse with the Mountain Medicine Center in Namche Bazaar near the quake's epicentre, said the quake caused cracks in several buildings, including a school, but she had not seen major damage.
33Residents in the Indian town of Siliguri, near the border with Nepal, said chunks of concrete fell off one or two buildings.
34Last month's quake killed at least 8,046 people and injured more than 17,800.
35It was recorded at 7.8 magnitude, almost six times stronger than Tuesday's quake.
36But a 7.3 magnitude earthquake has the potential to cause significant damage and landslides.
