Houston arson investigators probe blaze at Islamic center

1No one was injured in the early Friday blaze at the Quba Islamic Institute, fire officials said.
2The fire destroyed one of three buildings at the center, which remains fully operational.
3Arson investigators with the Houston Fire Department were working to pinpoint the cause of the blaze, but no official determination has been made, fire officials said.
4In a video on the institute's Facebook page, Ahsan Zahid, son of the imam, said fire officials told him the fire appeared to be arson.
5He also urged the Islamic community and supporters not to assign blame until the investigation concluded.
6Zahid told Reuters a series of odd events preceded the blaze.
7A fire official said it could be several days before the investigation into the cause of the blaze would be concluded.
8The Texas office of the Council on American-Islamic Relations called for authorities to investigate a possible bias motive if the blaze is determined to be the result of arson.
9Zahid said the fire caused more than $100,000 in damages.
10The Houston fire comes days after a gunman shot dead three young Muslims near the University of North Carolina, riling Muslim activists who have demanded that U.S. state and federal authorities investigate the detained suspect for possible hate crimes.
11Javid Sultan, the president at the University of Houston's Muslim Student Association said students on campus were stunned to hear about the fire at the Islamic center.
