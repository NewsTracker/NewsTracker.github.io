Recap: Kansas vs. New Mexico St

Glenn Moore: Rise of Kane shows loaning out is increasingly the way to develop young players

1After an 0-3 showing by the conference on Thursday, Kansas provided a lift with a 75-56 win Friday over New Mexico State.
2Frank Mason III scored 17 points and grabbed nine rebounds for the Jayhawks (27-8), who were coming off a loss to Iowa State in the Big 12 championship game last Saturday.
3Five other players scored at least eight points in the balanced attack for Kansas, which shot 54.0 percent.
4Perry Ellis scored nine points for the Jayhawks, while Brannen Greene, Kelly Oubre Jr., Jamari Traylor and Devonte' Graham each chipped in eight.
5"It was nice to see the ball go in the basket, gave us confidence as the game went on, and we were happy that we knocked down shots," said Mason.
6Next up for Kansas, seeded second in the Midwest Region, is a third-round matchup Sunday with Wichita State.
7The Jayhawks and Shockers, despite their proximity, haven't played since Jan. 6, 1993.
8DK Eldridge led New Mexico State with 11 points, while leading scorer Remi Barry added 10.
9Barry, who entered the contest making 46.3 percent of his shots and 44.6 percent from 3-point range, hit just 2-of-6 from beyond the arc and 4-of-13 overall on Friday.
10The Aggies (23-11) had won 13 straight games, including the WAC championship for a fourth consecutive year, but have now lost nine straight NCAA Tournament games.
11"We ran into the real Kansas Jayhawks tonight," said New Mexico State coach Marvin Menzies.
12Kansas never trailed and blew it open early on Thursday.
13Landen Lucas snapped a 4-4 tie with a bucket, igniting a run of seven straight points.
14The lead reached double digits for the first time on Wayne Selden Jr.'s dunk for a 19-9 edge nine minutes into the contest.
15The Jayhawks opened some breathing room with a 10-0 run.
16Graham buried threes on consecutive possessions during the burst and Traylor's basket made it 34-16 with just over three minutes left in the first half.
17New Mexico State cut it 36-23 at the intermission and got as close as 11 early in the second half.
18The Aggies were still within 55-43 with just under 10 minutes remaining, but the Jayhawks followed with 13 of the next 15 points to put it away.
19Greene drilled back-to-back 3-pointers to fuel the surge and Mason scored the other seven for Kansas.
20Mason's triple capped the run and gave the Jayhawks a comfortable 68-45 cushion with just under six minutes to play.
21"We did a lot of good things today; we did some things not so well, but for the most part we were pretty consistent and never allowed a really good team to make a serious run there in the second half," said Kansas coach Bill Self.
1But Leyton Orient, Millwall, Norwich and Leicester can also claim some credit for Kane being Roy Hodgson's choice.
2He was on loan to these clubs and while some loans were more successful than others, Kane says he learned from the difficult times at Carrow Road as well as the good ones at The Den.
3This is the route to the top now for young footballers.
4There was a time when a player either came through the reserves at a big club or made his way up the divisions.
5That still happens, but increasingly rarely.
6It can be a long path to glory, taking in such footballing bywaters as Brackley and Welling, Cheltenham and Accrington, Southend and Northampton.
7All those clubs have helped develop, via the loan system, players in the England Under-21 squad Kane was promoted from on Thursday.
8Indeed, seven of Gareth Southgate's squad named this week to play the Czech Republic on Thursday are currently on loan and three quarters of the 24-man party have been farmed out at some stage in their fledgling careers.
9Many of Kane's new team-mates in the seniors also had loan periods, notably Andros Townsend with nine and Kyle Walker with four.
10Kane, Walker and Townsend are all Spurs players; the club has been a keen advocate of the loan system, feeling it provides a competitive edge not present in the Premier League Under-21 competition.
11Les Ferdinand, now head of football operations at QPR but previously involved in Spurs' development programme, said this season: "When you're playing for the Under-21s the environment is quite nice, it's quite cosy. When you go and play in the Football League, people are playing to pay their mortgage. There is a different emphasis."
12Huw Jennings, Fulham's academy director, recalls sending a youngster down two divisions to a relegation-threatened club.
13"They were 2-0 up when a player was sent off 10 minutes before the break. They went in at 2-1 and the rest of the team absolutely pilloried the player who was sent off. It was an eye-opener for our lad. You can't replicate that at Under-21 level."
14But Southampton, whose youth programme was judged Europe's most profitable in a recent report by the CIES Football Observatory, are reluctant to use the loan system.
15The view of Les Reed, the club's executive director, is that it is more beneficial for players to develop within their own first-team environment.
16Three of the six England Under-21 players who have not been on loan are or were with Saints: Calum Chambers, Matt Targett and James Ward-Prowse.
17Similarly Theo Walcott, Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain and Luke Shaw were not loaned out when at the club.
18"I don't think the lower league environment is the best way to nurture your young players," said Reed.
19"There's a theory of 'rough them up' but I don't agree with that. Our lads gain their mental and physical strength at Southampton, nowhere else. Luke and James did not go on loan, neither did Calum; they made the transition into the Premier League with little difficulty."
20A rival in youth development, an enthusiast for the loan system, points out that when Adam Lallana, Walcott and Oxlade-Chamberlain came through, "Saints were in League One, then the Championship; players had that opportunity to develop in the lower leagues".
21However, while Southampton do now have a quartet of young players on loan, Targett, Ward-Prowse, Harrison Reed and Sam Gallagher are getting their education in the first team.
22Southampton's method works because the manager is prepared to play youngsters in the first team (and is told that is the club's policy when he takes the job).
23One reason Spurs loaned young players out is that in the past that was not necessarily the case.
24"Foreign managers won't consider players unless they have been in the Premier League," said one coach, making the argument for Kyle Walker needing to be loaned to Aston Villa before he was given a run at White Hart Lane.
25It also helps Saints that, at present, finishing in the top 10 is success, higher is a bonus.
26That does not apply to the other clubs in the top seven.
27Even ones with faith in youngsters prefer them with loan experience - the breakthrough players at Arsenal, Liverpool and Manchester United, Hector Bellerin, Jordon Ibe and Tyler Blackett, have all been on loan.
28There are a few who have come through the old way.
29Of the Under-21s, Danny Ings and Callum Wilson have, though even they had loan spells in non-League.
30But until the Under-21 League is upgraded, or Saints' managerial model more widely followed, most clubs will continue to send their players to other clubs to develop.
31However closely clubs monitor their loanees, it does seem a haphazard way to develop your own.
32It can work, but for every Kane there is a Josh McEachran.
33Once the coming man at Chelsea he is now, at 22 and on his fifth loan, a fringe player at Vitesse Arnhem.
34The science of youth development still needs refining.
