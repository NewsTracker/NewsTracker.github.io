Migrants hope to re-start protest convoy in Mexico

1MEXICO CITY (AP) - Activists for migrants and rights groups in Mexico say about 200 Central American migrants hope to restart their journey to hold a "Viacrucis" protest in Mexico City that has been stymied by highway immigration checkpoints.
2For several years, migrants in Mexico have carried wooden crosses in marches around Easter week to protest suffering at the hands of criminals and corrupt police.
3The migrant group had hoped to travel by buses last week from the city of Ixtepec in Oaxaca state.
4But the Rev.
5Alejandro Solalinde, who runs a shelter for migrants, said authorities threatened to arrest owners of buses rented to carry the protesters.
6Solalinde's shelter says the migrants now plan to set out Wednesday.
7Amnesty International is urging Mexican authorities to guarantee security for the migrants on their trip.
