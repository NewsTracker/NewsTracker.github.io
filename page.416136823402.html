Mining company director: We have 'good relationship' with cartels to maintain operations

1Rob McEwen of Canada-based McEwen Mining Inc. said in an interview Thursday with Canada's Business News Network that company employees ask the drug cartels for permission before they explore.
2"Generally we've had a good relationship with them (the cartels)," McEwen said.
3He said the company had not suffered any other crime beyond the robbery of the gold.
4McEwen said the mining company operates in the foothills, and more problems occur higher in the mountains.
5The theft of 900 kilograms (1,984 pounds) of gold-bearing concentrate containing 7,000 ounces of gold occurred Tuesday at El Gallo 1 mine in the western state of Sinaloa, which is controlled by a powerful cartel of the same name.
6At no time, however, did McEwen say the company had paid criminal organizations, a common suspicion in areas where anyone doing business is often a target of extortion.
7Authorities in Sinaloa have said that so far the theft suggests the involvement of employees or former workers who understand the company's operations.
8Sinaloa's deputy attorney general, Martin Robles Armenta, told reporters that one reason investigators suspect an inside job is that the door to vault where the material was stored had been left open since the previous day.
9Also, the theft was not immediately reported to police.
10The thieves overpowered two employees initially who had keys to various parts of the site, Robles said.
11He added that they used at least two vehicles to remove the material.
12McEwen called it "very well planned" and said a month's worth of production was taken.
13The Toronto-based company has operations in Mexico, Nevada and Argentina.
14It is the third hit against mining interests in Mexico in recent months.
15In February, suspected drug traffickers kidnapped some workers from a highway leading to the Media Luna mine owned by Torex Gold Resources in the southern state of Guerrero.
16The workers were later released.
17After the kidnapping, community police in the town of Nuevo Balsas demanded increased security, both for the mine and the entire community.
18In March, the Mexican Mining Chamber said that three miners from the Los Filos mine, also in Guerrero, had been killed after being kidnapped.
19The mine is operated by the Canada-based GoldCorp.
20The company said the victims were employees, but were not working or traveling to the mine when they were taken hostage.
