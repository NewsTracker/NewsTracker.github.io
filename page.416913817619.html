U.S. supporter of Islamic State gets 4-1/2 years in prison

1WASHINGTON, May 11 (Reuters) - A Virginia woman was sentenced to 4-1/2 years in prison on Monday for lying to U.S. investigators about using social media to promote and recruit for the militant Islamic State group.
2Heather Elizabeth Coffman, 29, of Glen Allen, Virginia, pleaded guilty to the charge in January.
3She admitted that between approximately June and November last year she used several Facebook accounts to promote the group and claimed to have Islamic State contacts in Syria.
4During that time she was in a relationship with an unidentified foreign national outside the United States, whom she tried to help travel to Syria to fight for Islamic State.
5They communicated almost daily on Facebook until he backed out of the trip after their relationship ended in September.
6Coffman listed "jihad for Allah's sake" under her "work and education" on Facebook, according to the FBI, and was in contact online with individuals she believed were ISIS facilitators in Syria.
7An FBI undercover agent posing as an Islamic State sympathizer began meeting with Coffman last July.
8When she was questioned by federal investigators in November 2014, she lied about her relationship with the foreign national and her activities in support of the militant group.
9A lawyer for Coffman did not immediately respond to a request for comment.
