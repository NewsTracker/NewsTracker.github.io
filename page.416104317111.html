Your guide to trying on, ordering Apple Watch

19, 2014 file photo, the new Apple Watch is modeled during a media event in Cupertino, Calif.
2Consumers will have their first chance to see and hold the Apple Watch on Friday, April 10, 2015, as Apple stores in the U.S. and eight other markets start previews of Apple's latest product.
3NEW YORK (AP) - Buying an Apple Watch won't be as simple as walking into an Apple store and handing over your credit card.
4The smartwatch is Apple's first new product category in five years and comes in 54 configurations - more than any other Apple gadget.
5So starting Friday, Apple stores will have staff trained to help you choose.
6Even after the watch starts shipping on April 24, you'll still have to book a try-on visit to buy the watch in stores and wait for it to arrive.
7There's no walking in and walking out with a watch.
8Of course, you can bypass all this by ordering online.
9No smartwatch has gotten as much attention as the Apple Watch, given Apple's devoted fan base and the company's knack for designing products consumers can't live without.
10By insisting on customers ordering ahead of time, Apple can manage inventory at its stores.
11Prices range from $349 to $17,000 depending on the watch case, band and size.
12A standard model with the cheapest band costs $549 for the 38-millimeter model and $599 for the 42-millimeter version, as measured on the watch face from top to bottom.
13Here's what you need to know about buying one.
14If you know the specific configuration you want, you're best off ordering it online, starting at 12:01 a.m. PDT Friday (3:01 a.m. EDT).
15They will start to ship on April 24.
16Popular models might not be available right away if you're not among the first to order.
17Your watch will get shipped to you.
18There won't be an in-store pick-up option.
19Besides the United States, the watch is available to customers in Australia, Canada, China, France, Germany, Hong Kong, Japan and the United Kingdom.
20Starting Friday, Apple Store staff will be able to help you choose from the array of options.
21Apple recommends that you make a reservation to see a sales representative.
22Apple will start taking reservations through its website at 12:01 a.m. PDT Friday as well.
23Appointments will be in 15-minute increments, though you can take more time at the store if you need.
24You might be able to walk in and try one out, but initially there will likely be a long wait.
25Once you choose, you'll still need to place the order online, though you can do that at a Web terminal inside the store.
26Again, your only option is to have the watch shipped, at least for the foreseeable future.
27For the most part, watches will be available for viewing when stores open Friday, so many overseas stores will actually show the watch before orders are accepted.
28Apple's 24-hour store on New York's Fifth Avenue will close temporarily to set up the displays; it will reopen at 9 a.m. Friday.
29Only some stores will carry the 18-karat gold luxury models known as Edition.
30When you make the try-on reservation, you'll have to specify whether you're looking for these models or the cheaper ones.
31Specialists will handle these luxury appointments.
32If you're just interested in seeing them, all stores will have them available for viewing in glass cases.
33You won't be able to visit Best Buy or your wireless carrier.
34Apple will handle most of the sales directly.
35However, the watch will be available to try on at Galeries Lafayette in Paris, Isetan in Tokyo, Selfridges in London and some Apple resellers in China and Japan starting Friday.
36Once the watch goes on sale on April 24, it will be available in additional retail outlets, such as select department stores and boutiques, including Maxfield in Los Angeles.
37It's not known whether those stores will have similar requirements to order ahead of time.
38If you need help, you can come back to the store for staff to walk you through the setup and answer any other questions.
39You'll need an iPhone 5 or later with at least iOS 8.2, which was released in March.
40The latest, iOS 8.3, is recommended.
41Apple typically allows 14 days for returns, but many items are excluded.
42Expect some restrictions on the luxury models.
43Until you know for sure, don't count on "borrowing" a $17,000 watch for a weekend party and expecting a full refund.
