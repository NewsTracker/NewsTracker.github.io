Apple Granted New Trial Over Damages in iTunes Patent Case

1Apple pointed to this case as an example of why patent reform is needed.
2A federal judge threw out a $532.9 million jury award against Apple for infringing on three patents of a closely held Texas software company with its iTunes service.
3U.S. District Judge Rodney Gilstrap on Tuesday ordered a retrial on the damage award after Apple argued the jurors miscalculated the royalties.
4The ruling nullified a Feb. 24 award to Smartflash.
5Smartflash, which makes no products, claimed Apple violated its patents connected to storing data and managing payments.
6The Tyler, Texas, company said Smartflash founder Patrick Racz had shared his plans with Augustin Farrugia, who later became an Apple senior director.
7Smartflash said Apple used its technology to manage payments for apps in its iTunes store, specifically "Coin Dozer Pro" and "Grub Guardian."
8Initially Smartflash also sued the manufacturers of those games, but they were dismissed from the case.
9In its appeal, Apple said jurors inflated the royalties the company owed because they failed to differentiate between patented and unpatented features.
10Apple shares were down 1.65% to $123.66 a share in afternoon trading.
11Rachel Wolf, a spokeswoman for Apple, declined to comment on the news of the retrial.
12She reiterated a statement from February which accused Smartflash of exploiting the patent system.
13Apple has said the case highlights the need for patent reform.
14Both the House and Senate are considering legislation that would limit so-called patent trolls, which exploit broad patents to collect royalties.
15Smartflash and Bradley Caldwell, a Dallas-based trial lawyer representing the company, did not immediately respond to requests for comment.
16Earlier this year, Racz wrote a column in The Huffington Post saying he is an inventor, not a troll.
17Smartflash originally sought damages of $852 million in U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Texas, a popular location for patent cases.
18The new trial on damages will begin September 14.
19Smartflash also has filed suits over the same technology against Amazon, Google and Samsung Electronics.
