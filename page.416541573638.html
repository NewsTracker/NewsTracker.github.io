No power, comms for Nepal quake rescuers

1An Australian woman believed to have been on her first expedition to Mount Everest had "no chance" when an avalanche washed away her tent at base camp on the world's highest mountain.
2Renu Fotedar, 48, is among at least 19 people killed at the Everest base camp, with some 200 Australians believed to be still missing in Nepal after an 7.8 magnitude earthquake struck on Saturday.
3A Dreamers Destination Trek and Expeditions representative said Ms Fotedar's body had been recovered and was being taken to the Nepalese capital.
4"She's on the way to Kathmandu right now," he told AAP from Nepal on Monday.
5"She's from Australia."
6The trekking company's managing director Temba Tsheri said Ms Fotedar was inside a tent in base camp when the quake hit.
7"A big avalanche came and a lot of people were washed away. There was no time," he told News Corp Australia.
8"There was a big sound. They didn't get the chance to run away. There was no place to hide."
9Ms Fotedar was a holistic and transpersonal counsellor, with her website noting that she was to be on a 59-day Everest expedition.
10Ms Fotedar's LinkedIn profile says she earned a degree from the University of Wollongong in 1999 and ran businesses in Victoria from 2005 to 2012.
11A worried cousin told The Times of India that she was last contacted at the Makalu Base Camp and was to climb to the Mt Everest Base Camp 1 on Friday.
12"She has two young boys 17 and 15 years old who are anxious to hear some news about her," Ashish Kaul said on Sunday.
13Foreign Minister Julie Bishop says more than 850 Australians are confirmed safe in Nepal.
14More than 3600 people have died in the quake with about 6000 injured, buildings destroyed and morgues and hospitals overflowing in Kathmandu, as powerful aftershocks continue and people camp outside in cold and wet conditions.
15"It's apparent that these figures are likely to rise," Ms Bishop said.
16Hundreds of foreign mountaineers were at Mount Everest at the start of the annual climbing season, including 18-year-old Australian Zachary Sheridan.
17Actor Hugh Sheridan was heading to Nepal, and then base camp, to search for his younger brother.
18"Stay positive everyone, I feel optimistic that he is alive and stuck," Sheridan posted on social media on Monday.
19Australian man Glenn Azar said his climber daughter Alyssa, 18, was among the lucky ones.
20She was away from base camp when the avalanche struck and wants to get off the mountain as soon as possible.
21After initially fearing the worst, Perth man Michael Penson was relieved late on Monday when he heard from his 34-year-old son Blake who was at Everest Base Camp 2.
22Blake contacted his family following the initial quake but gave them an anxious wait when they hadn't heard from him after the second tremor.
23Victorian Camille Thomas had to pull herself out after being buried under snow in the village of Langtang, just north of the capital Kathmandu.
24"From where we were, after it there was nothing you could see. All the villages were gone," the 18-year-old told 3AW.
25Confederation of Australian Motorsport president Andrew Papadopoulos, who was in a Kathmandu hotel when the quake struck, said it was horrible and scary.
26"You see the movies on earthquakes and we all think they're exaggerated but I can tell you being in the middle of it. It was very surreal," he told 2UE.
27Australian embassy officials in Kathmandu are trying to confirm the welfare of all Australians in affected areas including India and Bangladesh.
28A nine-person Australian crisis response team arrived in Nepal on Monday.
