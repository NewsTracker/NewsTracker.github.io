We will eradicate Boko Haram within a months, says Nigeria president

1Nigeria's military hopes to recapture all towns seized by Boko Haram within a month, said Goodluck Jonathan, Nigeria's president, on Friday.
2Predicting what would be a swift victory after six years of bloody conflict, Mr Jonathan, who is seeking re-election on March 28, said Boko Haram was "getting weaker and weaker every day".
3Nigeria has claimed major gains against the Islamists with the help of coalition partners Cameroon, Chad and Niger, achieving in just over one month what for years it had failed to on its own.
4Two out of three of the worst-hit northeast states - Yobe and Adamawa - have been declared "cleared" while the third, Borno, is expected to be liberated "soon", the military said this week.
5Major towns such as Bama and Dikwa are among some 36 localities recaptured, with just three said to be still in rebel hands.
6National security advisor Mike Omeri said on Wednesday that "the final onslaught" was under way.
7More than 13,000 people have lost their lives in the conflict and the main opposition candidate, former military ruler Muhammadu Buhari, has campaigned hard on the government's security record.
8Mark Schroeder, vice-president for Africa analysis at security risk consultants Stratfor, said announcing victory before March 28 made political sense for Jonathan as part of the election campaign.
9"The risk he runs, however, is that the insurgency is not really defeated, only disrupted temporarily and for political posturing," he told AFP.
10"It would be akin to the 'Mission Accomplished' declaration by President Bush in 2003 that was a premature symbol of victory in Iraq.
11"Clearly, Iraq is still today struggling with an Islamist insurgency."
12Until this year, Nigerian troops had appeared unable - even unwilling - to tackle the better-armed militants, who have allied themselves with the Islamic State group in Syria and Iraq.
13Jonathan acknowledged to the BBC that a lack of resources was to blame.
14Nnamdi Obasi, senior Nigeria researcher at the International Crisis Group, attributed the sense of urgency to the prospect of defeat by Buhari.
15"That urgency facilitated the delivery and deployment of new military hardware, including assets more relevant to counter-insurgency operations," he said.
16Improvement had been seen in command and deployment structures, including the use of senior officers to lead combat operations, special forces and co-operation with local vigilantes.
17The involvement of foreign military contractors, many of them South Africans, to provide technical expertise may also have made a difference, he said.
18"Offensives by the military forces of neighbouring countries have helped to overstretch the insurgents and thus undermine their ability to withstand Nigerian military offensives," he added.
19A Boko Haram attack on the border town of Gamboru on Wednesday and Thursday killed 11 civilians and gave an indication of the difficulties in bringing a definitive end to the violence.
20The incident highlighted possible co-ordination problems among the allies, as Chadian troops, who had recaptured the town, had withdrawn but were not replaced, according to residents.
21The Nigeria Security Network of analysts on Thursday meanwhile warned that Boko Haram would revert to its guerrilla campaign of bombings and suicide attacks in the face of military pressure.
22Fears remain about the safety of voters on polling day, after a spate of suicide attacks in the north in recent weeks and after Boko Haram leader Abubakar Shekau vowed to disrupt the election.
23In the short-term shattered lives and infrastructure needed to be rebuilt, while longer term, Nigeria needs to tackle the root causes of the insurgency to prevent it flaring again, experts said.
24The Borno Elders Forum on Wednesday warned that it was "too early to even start talking about any of the (1.5 million) displaced going back to any of these reclaimed territories".
25The forum's chairman, Usman Gaji Galtimari said many areas appear to have been mined, while there have still been repeated attacks in areas said to have been recaptured.
