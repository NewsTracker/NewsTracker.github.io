Fin24.com | Facebook sued over data privacy

1Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg speaks during a conference at the Mobile World Congress in Barcelona.
2Vienna - An Austrian law graduate spearheading a class action case against Facebook for alleged privacy breaches officially filed the suit in a Vienna court on Thursday.
3In a closely-watched case, Max Schrems and 25 000 other users are suing the social media giant for various rights violations, ranging from the "illegal" tracking of their data under EU law to Facebook's involvement in the Prism surveillance programme of the US National Security Agency (NSA).
4"Basically we are asking Facebook to stop mass surveillance, to (have) a proper privacy policy that people can understand, but also to stop collecting data of people that are not even Facebook users," 27-year-old Schrems said in an interview this week.
5The case has been brought against Facebook's European headquarters in Dublin, which registers all accounts outside the US and Canada - making up some 80% of Facebook's 1.35 billion users.
6Schrems was able to file his action against the Irish subsidiary at a civil court in Vienna because under EU law, all member states have to enforce court rulings from any other member state.
7Among other issues, judges will have to rule on Facebook's objection that the class action is inadmissible under Austrian law - an objection dismissed by Schrems' lawyer as lacking "any substance".
8So far, the social media company has not been available for comment on the matter.
