CANNES WATCH: Watts into feathers; Coens not into TV

1CANNES, France (AP) - Premieres, parties, pretty people and paparazzi.
2All come together at the annual Cannes Film Festival on the French Riviera.
3Feathers helped the Cannes Film Festival take flight on the opening-night red carpet.
4Naomi Watts, who appears in Gus van Sant's festival entry "The Sea of Trees," wore a powder blue gown from Elie Saab Couture's spring-summer 2015 collection, its top festooned with sequins and pearls above a flowing feathered skirt.
5Not to be outdone, Julianne Moore wore a silk gauze column dress by Georgio Armani Prive, adorned with aqua and black Swarovski embroidery and paired with a black feathered top.
6The flame-haired actress, who won Cannes' best-actress prize last year for "Maps to the Stars," offset the look with bold emerald earrings.
7Natalie Portman, Jake Gyllenhaal and Sienna Miller also walked the Riviera red carpet ahead of Wednesday's gala screening of Emmanuelle Bercot's "Standing Tall."
8Don't ask the Coen brothers about TV.
9They're not watching.
10Joel and Ethan Coen are presidents of the jury that will decide the Cannes Film Festival top honor, the Palme d'Or.
11Introducing their jury, which includes Jake Gyllenhaal and Guillermo del Toro, the filmmaking brothers confessed Wednesday they remain outsiders to the much-celebrated rise of TV dramas.
12"It's not that I don't like TV," said Ethan Coen.
13"It's alien to me. I haven't watched a television show in decades."
14Joel suggested he was no less familiar, despite his wife, Frances McDormand, recently starring in the acclaimed HBO miniseries "Olive Kitteridge."
15''You're going to get me in trouble," he said.
16The Coens' 1996 film "Fargo" was turned into the FX series, but with little input from them.
17On the increase of new digital avenues like Netflix for movie watching, Joel wasn't much more enthusiastic.
18"How do we feel about people watching 'Lawrence of Arabia' on their iPhone?" he said, archly rephrasing a more general question about digital media.
19"There's something special about sitting with a big crowd of people watching a movie on a big 80-foot screen."
20French acting icon Catherine Deneuve is a cinema supernova.
21And she says they don't make stars like they used to.
22Deneuve told reporters in Cannes Wednesday that glamour needs mystery, and that's hard to achieve now that social media has blurred the boundary between private and public.
23Deneuve said the all-revealing nature of celebrity culture doesn't "let people dream anymore" about celebrities.
24"The private lives of actors and actresses are displayed around the globe," she said.
25"People even post up their own private photos on these social networks, and I find that a pity.
26Deneuve's message fits with the mood of a Cannes festival that is trying to eradicate selfies - another symbol of our oversharing age - from the red carpet.
27Deneuve made her Cannes debut in 1964 in multicolored musical "The Umbrellas of Cherbourg."
28The 71-year-old star plays a family-court judge trying to tame a teenage tearaway in this year's festival opener, Emmanuelle Bercot's "Standing Tall."
29The 68th Cannes Film Festival runs to May 24.
