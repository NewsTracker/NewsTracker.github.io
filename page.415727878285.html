Germanwings crash: Air traffic controllers 'tried three times to radio pilots with no response'

1The German newspaper Bild claims to have obtained a leaked report from French air traffic controllers, which had been passed to the German authorities, detailing the three unsuccessful attempts to raise the pilots during the aircraft's eight-minute descent to disaster.
2Bild published this timeline of what it says happened to the Airbus (times are local, one hour ahead of GMT).
310:31:02 Flight 4U9525 leaves its assigned cruising altitude without approval and begins to descend.
4Radar observes an average descent rate of approximately 17.8 metres per second (3,500 feet per minute).
510:35 French air traffic control declares an international normalized emergency according to international norms.
6French search and rescue services are informed.
7Flight 4U9525 passes through an altitude of around 7600m (25,000 feet).
810:36:47 French air traffic control tries one last time to contact Flight 4U9525 German Wings on the international distress frequency.
910:40 Flight 4U9525 disappears from radar.
1010:42 French air traffic control informs the search and rescue national control centre of the loss of radar contact.
1110:49 Two military search and rescue helicopters head towards the location of Flight 4U9525's final radar contact.
12Aviation experts said that while the lack of communication could point to the pilots being incapacitated, they may have been simply too busy trying to get the aircraft under control, as they are taught to "aviate, navigate, communicate", in that order.
13One of the plane's black boxes has been found, but it was unclear whether it was the flight data recorder or the cockpit voice recorder.
14Investigators will continue searching for the second black box.
15Weather did not appear to be a factor in the crash, with conditions calm at the time, French weather officials said.
16Lufthansa, the parent company of Germanwings, said it was working on the assumption the crash was an "accident".
17"Anything else would be speculation," Lufthansa vice president Heike Birlenbach told reporters in Barcelona.

19The arduous search for the 150 victims of the worst aviation disaster on French soil in decades resumed at dawn on Wednesday.
20The accident's cause remains a mystery but authorities have recovered a black box from the Airbus A320 at the crash site, where debris was believed to be scattered over four acres of remote and inaccessible mountainous terrain, hampering rescue efforts.
21More than 300 policemen and 380 firefighters have been mobilised.
22Lieutenant Colonel Jean-Marc Menichini said a squad of 30 mountain rescue police would resume attempts to reach the crash site by helicopter at dawn Wednesday, while a further 65 police were seeking access on foot.
23Five investigators had spent the night at the site.
24It would take "at least a week" to search the remote site, he said, and "at least several days" to repatriate the bodies.
25Video images from a government helicopter on Tuesday showed a desolate snow-flecked moonscape, with steep ravines covered in scree.
26Debris was strewn across the mountainside, pieces of twisted metal smashed into tiny bits.
27The plane was "totally destroyed", a local MP who flew over the site said, describing the scene as "horrendous".
28A crisis cell has been set up in the area between Barcelonnette and Digne-les-Bains along with an emergency flight control centre to coordinate chopper flights to the crash site.
29French President Francois Hollande, his German counterpart Angela Merkel and Spanish Prime Minister Mariano Rajoy were expected to reach the scene around 2:00 pm local time.
30The 144 passengers were mainly German and Spanish.
31The high school in the small German town of Haltern attended by the 16 students on the plane was set to hold an event Wednesday to honour the victims.
