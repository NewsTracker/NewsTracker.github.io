Analyst downgrades Apple shares as smartwatch goes on sale

1Analyst Tavis McCourt downgraded the shares to "Market Perform" from "Outperform," saying fiscal 2016 is shaping up to be difficult year for the company compared to this year.
2While sales of the Apple Watch won't be a big part of the company's earnings for some time, McCourt said reviews have not been great and investors may start to worry that the company is going to struggle with launching new products.
3"Early reviews on Apple Watch suggest it will fall far short of the 'insanely great' benchmark, at least in this first iteration," he said.
4While Apple has launched several new versions of the iPhone in Cook's nearly four-year tenure as CEO, the Apple Watch is its first entirely new product in that time.
5McCourt raised his profit and revenue forecasts because he thinks iPhones are selling well in China and expects an increased stock buyback by the company.
6He set a price target of $124 on the stock in January and expects shares to stay around their current prices.
7Shares of Apple Inc. have climbed 69 percent over the last 12 months.
8They reached an all-time high of $133.60 in February and lost 29 cents to $126.27 in midday trading.
9Consumers were allowed to pre-order the Apple Watch starting at 3 a.m. Eastern time on Friday.
10They can also make an appointment to come to Apple stores to try on the watch and test out some of its features.
11The first people who pre-ordered the device are expected to receive it in June.
12Some analysts expect the company to sell 10 million to 20 million watches in the first year alone, but others are less optimistic.
13Based on a Raymond James survey, McCourt said around 22 million iPhone users plan to buy the product.
14That's a small percentage out of around 400 million iPhone users, he said, but it's a large number of buyers.
15The Cupertino, California-based company is scheduled to report its fiscal second-quarter results after the market closes on April 27.
