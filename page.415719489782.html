7He described the crash as a ”tragic incident” in a statement released on Tuesday night, and sent his condolences to those who have lost family or friends.
8“I don't want to speculate on numbers of British nationals involved until we have completed our checks on all the passenger information,” he said.

11American, German, Spanish, Australian, Colombian, Belgian, Danish and Turkish citizens are also thought to be among the dead.
12The arduous search for the 150 victims of the worst aviation disaster on French soil in decades was set to resume at dawn Wednesday.
13The accident's cause remains a mystery but authorities have recovered a black box from the Airbus A320 at the crash site, where debris was believed to be scattered over four acres of remote and inaccessible mountainous terrain, hampering rescue efforts.
14More than 300 policemen and 380 firefighters have been mobilised.
15Lieutenant Colonel Jean-Marc Menichini said a squad of 30 mountain rescue police would resume attempts to reach the crash site by helicopter at dawn Wednesday, while a further 65 police were seeking access on foot.
16Five investigators had spent the night at the site.
17It would take "at least a week" to search the remote site, he said, and "at least several days" to repatriate the bodies.

19Debris was strewn across the mountainside, pieces of twisted metal smashed into tiny bits.
20The plane was "totally destroyed", a local MP who flew over the site said, describing the scene as "horrendous".
21A crisis cell has been set up in the area between Barcelonnette and Digne-les-Bains along with an emergency flight control centre to coordinate chopper flights to the crash site.
22French President Francois Hollande, his German counterpart Angela Merkel and Spanish Prime Minister Mariano Rajoy were expected to reach the scene around 2:00 pm local time.
