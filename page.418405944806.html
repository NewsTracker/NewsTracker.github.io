4NEW YORK (AP) - T-Mobile will now let its U.S. customers call, text, use apps and browse the Internet within North America without paying extra charges.
5T-Mobile US Inc. said this will mean savings for customers who travel to Canada or Mexico or call people there frequently.
6WHY IS THIS SIGNIFICANT?
7Canada and Mexico account for a significant chunk of international travel by Americans, and they are the destinations for many of the international calls from the U.S. Customers with friends or family in Mexico or Canada or who live in border states will benefit the most from this deal.
8T-Mobile isn't alone in eyeing Mexico.
9In recent years, AT&T bought two Mexican telecommunications companies.
10But AT&T says its growth is more about adding subscribers in Mexico than offering service there for traveling Americans.
11The company aims to cover 100 million Mexicans on its network by the end of 2018.
12WHO IS ELIGIBLE AND HOW DO I SIGN UP?
13The service is free for most existing and new T-Mobile customers.
14T-Mobile said customers with certain older or budget-oriented plans are exempt.
15Customers must sign up online or at a store to take advantage of the deal.
16There's no charge.
17The new plan takes effect next Wednesday.
18WHAT DOES IT INCLUDE?
19T-Mobile customers will be able to call and text Mexican and Canadian phone numbers with their regular U.S. minutes.
20Those on unlimited plans will have unlimited calls or texts to those numbers, too.
21When traveling in those countries, customers will be able to call U.S., Mexican and Canadian numbers with their minutes.
22"We're just making Canada and Mexico part of your home territory," says T-Mobile CEO John Legere said on a conference call.
23Previously, T-Mobile charged 20 cents per minute for calls made while traveling in Canada and Mexico.
24Texting was already free.
25Data service also was free, but only at the slower, 2G speeds.
26That can be adequate for email, but it's painfully slow for Web surfing.
27For calls from the U.S. to those countries, T-Mobile customers previously had to buy an add-on global calling plan or pay 49 cents per minute to call Canada and 69 cents to call Mexico.
28Customers of other major wireless carriers also need to pay extra, as T-Mobile previously required.
29For instance, AT&T Inc. sells packages of minutes good for calls made while traveling in Mexico and Canada.
30They start at $30 for 80 minutes per month.
31AT&T also sells monthly global calling plans that include free calls to Mexico.
32Various amounts of data are included in the global calling plans.
33Verizon Wireless offers a $10-a-month plan for travel in Canada in Mexico.
34It offers 100 megabytes of data and some texting privileges, while $5 more per month adds 100 minutes of calls and expanded texting capabilities.
35HOW IS T-MOBILE ABLE TO OFFER THIS?
36The company says it has struck deals with some of the top carriers in Canada and Mexico, allowing customers to use their networks while traveling.
37In exchange, some of those carriers will get to use T-Mobile's networks.
38It's similar to deals U.S. carriers make with each other to let customers roam for free on a rival network when the customer's own network has no coverage.
39T-Mobile wouldn't provide details on the deal or say whether they are exclusive to T-Mobile.
40As a result, it's unclear whether other major U.S. carriers will be able to broker deals of their own.
