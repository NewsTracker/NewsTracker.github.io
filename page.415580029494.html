Tesla updating Model S to ease range anxiety, improve safety

1It praised the two attackers whom the recording said were "knights of the Islamic State" who were armed with machineguns and bombs.
1Tesla's latest update - which will be beamed to owners automatically in about 10 days - will map out the best route to a driver's destination based on the location of charging stations and will guide the car to available spots to charge.
2The car will also warn drivers if battery power is low before they drive beyond an area where they can charge.
3It will take into account elevation, wind speed, air conditioning use and other factors that can deplete the battery.
4"You don't need to think ahead and make any calculations," Tesla CEO Elon Musk said during a conference call to announce the update.
5"The car will always take care of you."
6Musk said Tesla is also working on an automatic steering feature on test drives between San Francisco and Seattle.
7That feature - which could be added with another software update about three months from now - would allow automatic, hands-free driving on a highway with well-marked lanes.
8The Model S can travel up to 270 miles on a single battery charge, which is more than double the range of other electric cars on the market, such as the Nissan Leaf.
9Musk said owners generally find that's enough to get them where they need to go.
10But range remains a concern for many potential buyers.
11"Range is generally one of the issues people have about electric cars, and it is an important thing to address really emphatically," Musk said.
12Tesla will update all Model S sedans globally for free.
13There are about 56,000 Model S sedans on the road.
14The company's Model X SUV, which will go on sale later this year, will also have the new software.
15Musk said the software will give preference to the company's Supercharger stations - which can almost fully charge a Model S in 30 minutes - but it will also point out other charging points along a route.
16Currently, 90 percent of the U.S. population is within 175 miles of a Supercharger.
17Europe, Canada and parts of China will soon have similar coverage, Musk said.
18The update will also add several safety features, including automatic emergency braking, blind spot detection and a side collision warning system.
19Many of those features are already standard in other luxury cars.
20Musk caused a frenzy last Sunday when he tweeted that Tesla was "about to end range anxiety" with a software update.
21But he might have raised investors' expectations a little too much.
22Tesla's shares fell $2.80, or 1.4 percent, to $197.91 in afternoon trading after the announcement.
23Karl Brauer, a senior analyst with Kelley Blue Book, says the update won't fully eliminate the anxiety over range.
24"One could argue such aggressive warnings will only remind drivers how critical it is to get to a charging stating before draining the battery pack," he said.
