Nepal quake: Boy, woman rescued in Kathmandu rubble after five days

1Krishna Devi Khadka is carried on a stretcher after being rescued from a building that collapsed in Saturday's earthquake in Kathmandu, Nepal.
2Kathmandu: Hundreds cheered as rescuers toiling amid rubble left by Nepal's earthquake pulled a boy and a woman to safety on Thursday after they had been trapped for five days.
3A 23-year-old maid, Krishna Devi Khadka, was found lying along with the bodies of three other people, police said.
4Members of a rescue team from Hungary and Nepal armed police personnel search for earthquake survivors under the debris of a collapsed building, in Kathmandu.
5Officials said the chances of finding more survivors were fading as the death toll reached 5858.
6But Nepal's Armed Police Force managed to save 15-year-old Pema Lama from the collapsed ruins of Kathmandu's Hilton Hotel.
7"I saw the police drilling for four hours to remove mounds of debris before they could pull him out," said Ambar Giri, a medical worker at the scene.
8The news comes after rescuers pulled a 15-year-old boy alive from rubble on the same day.
9A villager sits on his collapsed home in the destroyed village of Jalingi, near the epicentre of Saturday's massive earthquake, in the Gorkha District of Nepal.
10Away from the capital, aid was finally reaching some of Nepal's remote towns and villages nestled among mountains and foothills, where the extent of the damage and loss of life has yet to be properly assessed.
11From an army helicopter flying from Chautara, northeast of Kathmandu, towards the Tibet border, a witness estimated 70 to 80 per cent of buildings had been severely damaged.
12In a remote village, an army medical team treated injured locals and soldiers supervised the unloading of goods on a muddy expanse of ground next to a school that served as a helipad.
13An elderly woman waits for relief aid in her destroyed village of Balua, near the epicentre of Saturday's massive earthquake.
14In Chautara itself, a few people cleared rubble from the upper floors of their houses and mixed cement by the roadside, as the long rebuilding process began.
15Many Nepalis have been sleeping in the open since Saturday's quake.
16According to the United Nations, 600,000 houses have been destroyed or damaged.
17It said eight million people had been affected, with at least two million in need of tents, water, food and medicines over the next three months.
18Nepali security personnel carry relief material from an Indian Air Force helicopter in Dhading, Nepal.
19An official from Nepal's home ministry said the number of confirmed deaths from the 7.8 magnitude earthquake had risen to 5858 by Thursday afternoon, and almost 13,800 were injured.
20Anger over the pace of the rescue has flared in some areas, with Nepalis accusing the government of being too slow to distribute international aid that flooded into the country.
21It has yet to reach many in need, particularly in areas hard to reach given the quake damage and poor weather.
22A woman mourns the death of a family member after the body was recovered from a collapsed house during the cremation ceremony, after Saturday's earthquake, in Bhaktapur, Nepal.
23Tensions between foreigners and Nepalis desperate to be evacuated have also surfaced.
24In Langtang valley, where 150 people are feared trapped, a helicopter pilot was taken hostage by locals demanding to be evacuated first, one report said.
25In Ashrang village in Gorkha, one of the worst-hit districts about four hours by road west of Kathmandu, hundreds of Nepali villagers were living outdoors with little food and water despite boxes of biscuits, juices and sacks of rice and wheat being stored in a nearby government office.
26Pashmina Ghaley, 10, left, and her friend Ramina Bhujel, 10, pal around in the destroyed village of Balua.
27Police commandos shut the high iron gates of the building, refusing people access while they counted the relief supplies.
28"We told them we can manage without their help," said Mohammad Ishaq, a school teacher, who had been offered four plastic sheets.
29"It is as if we are doing everything on our own, feeding our people, tending to the sick."
30But district facilitator Dipendra Shrestha said the local administration was doing what it could to get aid to victims and help foreign teams offering rescue and medical support.
31"Owners are refusing to rent out vehicles," he said.
32"We only have 20 at the moment. We need many more."
33A German search and rescue team in the area was shifting focus to medical work, because villagers had managed to dig bodies and survivors from the remains of mud and brick homes themselves.
34In the capital, a 28-year-old man pulled from a collapsed apartment block on Tuesday after spending around 80 hours trapped with three dead bodies said his rescue was a mixed blessing.
35"I don't even have the money to buy a wheelchair now," Rishi Khanal told Reuters on Thursday, a day after he had one of his legs amputated.
36"How will I spend the rest of my life and support my family?"
37In another tale of escape, a young girl worshipped by many as a living goddess survived Saturday's earthquake near one of the royal palaces in Kathmandu where most other buildings were flattened.
38"Her temple stands intact because of her divine powers," Pratap Man Shakya, the girl's father, told Reuters.
39Nepal is appealing to foreign governments for more helicopters.
40There are currently about 20 Nepali army, private and Indian army helicopters involved in rescue operations, according to Laxmi Prasad Dhakal, a home ministry official.
41China is expected to send helicopters on Thursday, he said.
42Prime Minister Sushil Koirala told Reuters earlier this week the death toll from the quake could reach 10,000.
43That would surpass the 8500 who died in a 1934 earthquake, the last disaster on this scale to hit the nation of 28 million people sandwiched between India and China.
44In Kathmandu and other cities, hospitals overflowed with injured, with many being treated out in the open or not at all.
45"The new waves of patients are those who survived the quake, but are sick because they were living in the open and drinking contaminated water," said Binay Pandey, a doctor at the government-run Bir Hospital in the capital.
46In the Himalayas, climbing is set to reopen on Mount Everest next week after damage caused by avalanches triggered by the quake is repaired.
47A massive avalanche wiped out a swath of Everest base camp, killing 18 climbers and sherpa mountain guides on Saturday.
48Many climbers have abandoned their ascent of Everest, the world's tallest peak.
