Egypt imposes life jail penalty for cross-border tunnel use

1Egypt declared a state of emergency in the border area last year after at least 33 security personnel were killed in attacks in the Sinai Peninsula, a remote but strategic region bordering Israel, Gaza and the Suez Canal.
2There were several militant attacks in the Sinai on Sunday.
3The amendments to the penal code were passed by presidential decree, MENA said.
4It also said people who used illegal crossings to transfer goods or equipment could also face a life sentence, as well as people with knowledge of them who failed to report them to the authorities.
5It said the government had the right seize buildings on top of the tunnels or any of the tools used to make them.
6Residents of Sinai, who complain they have long been neglected by the state, say they rely on smuggling trade through tunnels for their living.
7Egyptian authorities see the tunnels as a threat and regularly destroy them.
8In November Egypt said it would deepen its buffer zone with Gaza after finding local tunnels, a move which stoked local resentment.
