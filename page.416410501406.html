Quebec filmmaker Xavier Dolan joins jury at Cannes film festival

1Quebec filmmaker Xavier Dolan joins jury at Cannes film festival Add to...
2Xavier Dolan is heading back to the Cannes Film Festival, this time as a jury member.
3The Quebec filmmaker joins U.S. actor Jake Gyllenhaal and Mexican director Guillermo del Toro as members of the panel, tasked with picking various prize winners including the coveted Palme d'or.
4The other jury members are: Spanish actress Rossy de Palma, French actress/director Sophie Marceau, U.K. actress Sienna Miller and Malian musician Rokia Traore.
5Dolan returns to Cannes a year after his film "Mommy" earned the jury prize at the prestigious festival.
6The festival opens May 13 with French director Emmanuelle Bercot's drama "La Tete Haute."
7Dolan has been a Cannes darling since he debuted his first feature film, "I Killed My Mother," at age 20 in 2009, when it won several awards.
8New movies by Quebec filmmaker Denis Villeneuve, U.S. director Gus Van Sant and French writer/director Jacques Audiard are among the films competing for the festival's top prize, the Palme d'Or.
9The grand prize will be announced during a festival closing ceremony May 24.
