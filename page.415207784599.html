Bishop offers Bali Nine prisoner swap

1Foreign Ministry spokesman Arrmanatha Nasir told the BBC that Indonesia had no law to provide for such an exchange.
2Earlier Australia's Foreign Minister Julie Bishop confirmed the swap offer was among options being explored.
3Andrew Chan and Myuran Sukumara, from the 'Bali Nine' drug-smuggling group, are due to be executed within days.
4The men are due to be shot by firing squad.
5A spokesman for Indonesia's attorney general said the execution date may be decided in a few days.
6Ms Bishop had announced the offer to repatriate three Indonesian convicts in return for the two Australian men in a joint news conference with Prime Minister Tony Abbott.
7"What we are seeking to do is have an opportunity to talk about options that might be available in the area of prisoner transfer or prisoner swap," she said.
8The offer was made to Ms Bishop's Indonesian counterpart Retno Marsudi, who "undertook to provide that information to the president", Ms Bishop said.
9The Australian government was exploring "every avenue that might be available to save the lives of these two men", she added.
10Mr Abbott said that he was seeking another telephone call with Indonesian President Joko Widodo to attempt to persuade him to show the men mercy.
11"I've put in a request. I can't guarantee that the request will be met, but I've certainly put in a request," said Mr Abbott.
12"We respect Indonesia, we honour the friendship that we have with Indonesia, but we stand up for our values and we stand up for our citizens and these are Australian citizens in extremis."
13Mr Abbott and Ms Bishop were joined on Thursday morning by MPs and members of the public for a candlelit vigil for the men outside the parliament building in Canberra.
14Chan and Sukumaran were convicted in 2005 after being caught attempting to smuggle heroin from Bali to Australia.
15Indonesia has some of the toughest drug laws in the world and ended a four-year moratorium on executions in 2013.
16President Joko Widodo has said the drugs trade destroys lives in Indonesia and has adopted a hard line on convicted dealers.
17Chan and Sukumaran left Kerobokan jail in Bali early on Wednesday morning and were flown to Nusakambangan, the high-security prison island where Indonesia conducts executions.
18The date of their execution has not been announced but was expected to be this week.
19Al-Jazeera's Indonesia correspondent Step Vaessen said on Wednesday Mr Widodo told her the execution would not happen this week.
20The men are scheduled to be executed alongside citizens from countries including France, Brazil, Ghana and Nigeria.
21A woman from the Philippines also facing execution has appealed for a judicial review.
22Although it is not clear when the executions will take place, the authorities must legally give the convicts 72 hours' notice.
23Chan and Sukumaran's relatives and supporters have pleaded for their lives to be spared, arguing that they have been rehabilitated while in jail.
