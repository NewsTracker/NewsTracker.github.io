Markets fall as hopes low for Greece bailout deal at meeting

1Greece's Finance Minister Yanis Varoufakis arrives at prime minister's office to take part in a cabinet meeting in Athens, on Sunday, May 10, 2015.
2Eurozone finance ministers will meet on Monday amid slow-moving talks on a deal with Greece's creditors.
3Greece's Finance Minister Yanis Varoufakis, left, and EU Commissioner for Economic and Financial Affairs, Taxation and Customs Pierre Moscovici arrive for a debate on structural reforms at the European Business Summit in Brussels on Thursday, May 7, 2015.
4France says talks between Greece and its bailout creditors are going the right way and hopes that Monday's eurozone meeting will see the sides narrow their differences enough to have a deal "within hand's grasp."
5Hopes for a deal between Greece and its European creditors at a key meeting Monday are slim, weighing on the region's stock markets as the country struggles to make upcoming debt repayments.
6Ahead of a meeting with his counterparts in the 19-country eurozone, Greek Finance Minister Yanis Varoufakis said Greece and its creditors have a "moral and political responsibility" to swiftly reach a deal.
7Athens will not, however, budge from its key negotiating positions, he said.
8Stock markets fell in the eurozone, led by Athens' index, which dropped 2.8 percent.
9Despite three months of talks, Greece and its creditors have failed to agree on further reforms and savings Athens needs to qualify for a 7.2 billion euro ($8 billion) loan installment.
10Without the cash, Greece could go bankrupt within weeks.
11Without more bailout loans, Greece will have trouble paying pensions and state salaries at the end of the month, as well as more debt obligations in July and August.
12In a sign of how the pressure is growing, Varoufakis is scheduled to meet German Finance Minister Wolfgang Schaeuble - one of the toughest negotiators in the talks with Greece - ahead of the wider meeting on Monday.
13Varoufakis says Greece's left-led government will reject any deal that doesn't guarantee a credible prospect of ending Greece's economic crisis.
14The Greek government came to power in January on a pledge to end budget cuts that it blames for focusing too much on reducing debt, at the expense of the economy.
15Monday's meeting in Brussels will include representatives from international creditor institutions - the European Commission, the European Central Bank and the International Monetary Fund.
16The ministers will take stock of the institutions' assessment of whether Athens has made enough progress on its reform plans to secure the sorely-needed payment.
17After a shaky start, talks between Greece and the institutions have improved but no final agreement to secure the bailout money is likely on Monday, and it remains unclear whether one will come before June 30, when Greece's European bailout program expires.
18Varoufakis said in a radio interview that Greece has done "everything humanly possible" in offering concessions for a deal.
19"The common ground is there, a mutually beneficial solution is visible, so long as (Greece's creditors) have the political will to take the necessary steps," he told pro-government Sto Kokkino radio.
20Greece last received a bailout payment in August 2014.
21Greece's Finance Minister Yanis Varoufakis talks during a debate on structural reforms at the European Business Summit in Brussels on Thursday, May 7, 2015.
