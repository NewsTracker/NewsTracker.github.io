Saudi Arabia announces arrest of 93 in foiled, Islamic State-linked attack on U.S. Embassy, residences

1RIYADH - Saudi Arabia on Tuesday announced the arrest of 93 suspects with ties to the Islamic State group who it says were planning multipronged attacks on the U.S. Embassy, security forces and residential compounds where foreigners live.
2The list of targets recalls a wave of attacks launched by al-Qaida inside the kingdom from 2004 to 2007, which killed dozens of people, including foreigners, and threatened the stability of one of the world's most important oil-producing nations.
3Saudi Arabia is also home to Islam's holiest sites, in Mecca and Medina.
4Interior Ministry spokesman Maj.
5Gen. Mansour al-Turki told The Associated Press that Saudi Arabia's security forces are better prepared than ever to fight back against the Islamic State group.
6The kingdom is part of a U.S.-led coalition bombing the group in Iraq and Syria.
7He said there have been five Islamic State-related attacks across the kingdom in recent months that have killed 15 civilians and security personnel.
8But he said Saudis have largely ignored the Islamic State group's calls to take up arms against their government and attack the Shiite minority, security forces and foreigners living in the kingdom.
9"We do have a number of people who do respond to such calls and do try to carry out such terrorist organizations' orders, but these people do not represent the Saudi population, do not represent the 20 million Saudis," he said in remarks to AP a day before the announcement of the arrests, which took place over several months.
10Al-Turki said the security raids included a cell of 65 people arrested in March who were involved in a plan to target residential compounds and prisons.
11They also allegedly planned to carry out attacks aimed at creating sectarian strife.
12All but two in the cell were Saudi citizens.
13Authorities also disrupted a plot for a suicide car bomber to attack the U.S. Embassy in Riyadh after receiving information about the plan in mid-March, he said.
14Two Syrians and a Saudi citizen were arrested in relation to the plot.
15The timing of the alleged attack coincides with a U.S. decision to halt all consular services for a week starting March 15 at the embassy and diplomatic missions in Jiddah and Dhahran over security fears.
16The U.S. Embassy in Riyadh is located in a large and heavily guarded compound with other embassies.
17The embassy is surrounded by fortified barriers and guarded by police, and a U.S. Marine checks visitor passes inside.
18Last week, Saudi Arabia increased security around shopping centers and oil installations for a few days, also in response to security threats.
19Al-Turki said another group of nine Saudis, including one woman, were arrested on suspicion they tried to use social media to lure a military officer into a trap and assassinate him.
20Other arrests involving alleged Islamic State operatives included a cell of 15 Saudis broken up around New Year's.
21That group, which called itself "Soldiers of the Land of the Two Holy Mosques" in reference to sites in Mecca and Medina, trained in desolate areas in the central ultraconservative region of al-Qassim, al-Turki said.
22Earlier in the day, al-Turki said police arrested a suspected Islamic State operative wanted for the killing of two police officers in Riyadh, who were shot dead April 8 while on patrol.
23Nawaf al-Enezi, a 29-year-old Saudi citizen, was taken into custody early Tuesday morning after callers tipped the police off, he said.
24Al-Enezi was found in a hideout about 100 km (60 miles) east of Riyadh and was wounded by police gunfire during the arrest.
25The Interior Ministry last week announced a $267,000 reward for information leading to his arrest.
26Islamic State group members have previously been blamed for an attack on Saudi border guards near Iraq that killed three security personnel in January and a shooting attack on Shiite worshippers in al-Ahsa in the Eastern Province in November that killed eight people.
27Two police officers died in raids that led to the arrests of suspects behind the al-Ahsa shooting.
28Other Islamic State attacks in the kingdom in recent months include the shooting and wounding of a Danish citizen in Riyadh and a separate shooting directed at police in the city that did not kill anyone.
29Al-Turki told AP that a total of seven Islamic State-linked suspects have been killed by police in raids.
