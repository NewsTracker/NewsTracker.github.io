1(AP) - On his first day back from a mandatory leave for shooting and wounding a knife-wielding man earlier this month, a Wisconsin police officer shot and killed an armed suspect after confronting him following a chase, authorities said.
2Kenosha police officer Pablo Torres returned from leave Saturday, 10 days after shooting a man who advanced on police armed with knives, the department said.
3While that March 4 shooting was investigated, Torres was placed on administrative leave by department policy in police-involved shootings.
4He also attended annual in-service training before returning to work last weekend, police said.
5On Saturday morning, police chased a car driven by 26-year-old Aaron Siler, who was wanted on a felony probation and parole warrant, Lt. Brad Hetlet said in a statement.
6Siler crashed at around 9:30 a.m. and took off running.
7When Torres confronted Siler, Siler "armed himself with a weapon" and Torres fatally shot him, Hetlet said.
8Wisconsin online court records show a man with the same name and birthdate as Siler was charged in 2011 in Kenosha County with strangulation, false imprisonment, battery and disorderly conduct.
9He pleaded no contest to strangulation with the other charges dismissed and was sentenced in 2013 to four years of probation on condition he serve one year in jail.
10In 2011, the same man pleaded guilty to bail jumping and was sentenced in May 2013 to credit served for 563 days he spent in jail.
11A theft charge was dismissed.
12In the earlier officer-involved shooting, Torres, an 18-year veteran of the police force, was among three officers and a recruit who went to a home after a woman called to say her husband had gone into the garage to kill himself.
13Police say the man was armed with two knives and was seated in a running vehicle.
14When the man refused to drop the knives, two officers shot him with Tasers.
15When the man began advancing on police, Torres shot him once in the stomach.
16The man is expected to survive.
17Hetlet said police had no additional information to release about the shooting of Siler.
18Kenosha is in southeastern Wisconsin between Milwaukee and Chicago.
