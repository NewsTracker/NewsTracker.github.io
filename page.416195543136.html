Clinton takes road trip to Iowa for first campaign event

1Clinton left Sunday on a road trip from her home in Chappaqua, New York, in a van headed for Iowa, home of the nation's first presidential caucuses.
2The former secretary of state announced her Democratic presidential bid Sunday and will hold her first campaign event on Tuesday in eastern Iowa.
3The road trip was Clinton's idea, aides said.
4"When Hillary first told us that she was ready to hit the road for Iowa, we literally looked at her and said, 'Seriously?' And she said, 'Seriously,'" said longtime aide Huma Abedin in a conference call with Clinton alumni.
5"This was her idea, and she's been really excited about it since she came up with it."
6Abedin said Clinton's van stopped at a gas station in Pennsylvania, where the former first lady and New York senator met a family from Michigan.
7"I think it's safe to say she surprised quite a few people who had just happened to stop for gas at the same time she did," she said.
8A Clinton aide said the van is nicknamed "Scooby" after the Mystery Machine van in the 1970s animated television show, "The Scooby Doo Show."
9The aide said Clinton was a passenger and the van was driven by the Secret Service.
10The trip will cover roughly 1,000 miles between Clinton's home in New York City's suburbs and Iowa.
11Clinton expects to arrive in the state on Monday and is appearing at a community college in Monticello, Iowa, on Tuesday.
