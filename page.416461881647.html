Kerry urges Russia to remove forces from Ukraine

1WASHINGTON (AP) - Secretary of State John Kerry has urged Russia to remove forces from eastern Ukraine and press Ukrainian separatists into observing a cease-fire.
2The State Department says Kerry and Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov (SEHR'-gay LAHV'-rahf) spoke by telephone Wednesday.
3Kerry insisted Russia pull troops from eastern Ukraine.
4The U.S. says Russians provide heavy weaponry and training to Ukraine's rebels, and operate surface-to-air missile sites.
5The department didn't provide Lavrov's response.
6Russia says it has no troops in Ukraine.
7Kerry also sought help in ensuring international monitors can access areas under separatist control.
8The pair also discussed arms control, Iran, Yemen, Syria and an upcoming meeting of Arctic nations.
9On Wednesday, State Department spokeswoman Marie Harf accused "combined Russian-separatist" forces of violating the latest cease-fire agreed with Ukraine's government in February.
