Recent actions by the world's central banks, at a glance

As Ferguson manhunt grinds on, leaders aim to defuse tension

9Russia's central bank cut its key rate Friday by 1 percentage point to 14 percent to try to energize the economy, which appears to be sliding into a brutal recession.
10It was the second rate cut in as many months as officials reverse some of the sharp rate increases made in December " when they hiked them from 10.5 percent to 17 percent " to support a collapsing ruble.
11Higher rates tend to bolster a currency but hurt the economy by making borrowing more expensive.
12The central bank predicts that Russia's economy will shrink by between 3.5 percent and 4 percent this year.
13India's central bank last week unexpectedly cut a key rate by a quarter percentage point, the second such reduction this year to lend support to government efforts to bolster the economy.
14The latest cut lowers the rate at which commercial banks can borrow from the Reserve Bank of India.
15India's economic growth averaged about 8 percent a year in the decade ending in 2010 but slumped to about 5 percent in the following years.
16The move comes days after India's Finance Minister Arun Jaitley announced in the new government's first annual budget that it would boost spending to try to jump-start Asia's third-largest economy.
17South Korea unexpectedly lowered its benchmark rate Thursday to a record low of 1.75 percent in an effort to revitalize sluggish economic growth.
18The quarter percentage point cut was the first cut by the Bank of Korea in five months.
19The central bank said South Korea's economic growth and inflation this year will be lower than expected.
20There are also concerns that the country could fall into deflation.
21Interest rates: The European Central Bank kept its benchmark rate unchanged at 0.05 percent last week.
22Other policies: The ECB has unleashed its 1.1 trillion euro ($1.2 trillion) stimulus program and said after its meeting last week that its actions were already boosting the eurozone economy.
23The central bank raised its eurozone growth forecast for this year to 1.5 percent from 1.0 percent amid signs that credit is flowing more easily.
24On Monday, the ECB began buying 60 billion euros ($67 billion) a month in government and corporate bonds.
25The purchases, with newly printed money, are intended to lower borrowing rates, stimulate lending and growth and raise the inflation rate, which is dangerously low at minus 0.3 percent.
26Chinese authorities have generally been cautious in tweaking monetary policy.
27But the People's Bank of China has lowered its benchmark rates twice since November to try to energize a decelerating economy and ward off a politically dangerous spike in joblessness.
28In February, the central bank cut the rate on a one-year loan by commercial banks by 0.25 percentage point to 5.35 percent.
29China has set an economic growth target for this year of about 7 percent, which Premier Li Keqiang described as in line with efforts to create a "moderately prosperous society."
30That's even slower than last year's growth of 7.4 percent, its weakest performance in nearly a quarter-century.
31The central bank is spending trillions of yen (tens of billions of dollars) a month on asset purchases, seeking to spur inflation and finally end the chronic deflation that has long hampered growth.
32Government data this week showed that the world's third-largest economy escaped recession in the final quarter of 2014 but grew less than first estimated as demand and wages remained weak.
33Extreme monetary easing has been the linchpin of the "Abenomics" economic revival strategy, along with strong government spending and sweeping reforms.
34Brazil's central bank last week lifted its benchmark interest rate to 12.75 percent to try to control soaring prices.
35The central bank's inflation target is 4.5 percent, with an upper limit of 6.5 percent.
36But consumer prices over the past year have been stuck above that ceiling.
37The rate hike is expected to further pressure South America's biggest economy, which is struggling to stay out of recession.

14Activists have pressed for more reforms to address what the report called a "toxic environment" for the city's mostly African-American residents.
15Chaunte Williams, 13, holds up three candles as she takes part in a vigil on Thursday in Ferguson.

18"But there was no excuse for criminal acts, and whoever fired those shots shouldn't detract from the issue. They're criminals. They need to be arrested," Obama said.
19The shooting of the officers followed by less than three months the killing of two New York City patrolmen by a troubled man who said he was seeking to avenge the deaths of Brown and an unarmed black man in New York killed by police.
20Activists, condemning the wounding of the officers, held a candle-light prayer vigil for peace in Ferguson late on Thursday.
21About 100 people then held a boisterous but peaceful protest outside the police station in light rain.
22The crowd blocked traffic at times but there were no arrests and the demonstration passed without incident.
