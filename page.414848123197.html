Greece to submit extension request on Thursday

1Greece is expected to submit a request on Thursday to extend a "loan agreement" by up to six months.
2The new Greek government has gone into overdrive in hopes of resolving Greece's debt crisis on its own terms.
3Athens is to submit a formal request for a loan extension on Thursday, according to Greek officials.
4The application is also expected to ask for tough austerity conditions to be removed.
5Greece's current 240-billion-euro ($273-billion) bailout program will expire on February 28.
6The country says an extension is the only way to avoid a possible default, and eurozone exit.
7The nation has received two major bailouts since 2010.
8Repealing the conditions attached to these, including harsh budget cuts, was the centerpiece of the newly-installed leftist government's campaign.
9Reports circulating through the media have suggested that Greece may accept so-called "sensible conditionalities," although it is unclear what those may be.
10Valdis Dombrovskis, the European Commission's vice president, told reporters on Wednesday at a press conference that both the eurozone and Greece were open to negotiating.
11"The best way forward is to extend the existing program with its conditionality," he said.
12European Commission President Jean-Claude Juncker echoed this, saying Athens must honor its contractual obligations.
13Germany, who has been a vocal critic of increasing aid to Greece, said it was not open to making changes.
14"Simply saying, 'we need more money again and we're not going to do anything any more,' and then insulting people, that absolutely won't work," Finance Minister Wolfgang Schauble said on German television on Tuesday.
15"Of course Greece would like to continue receiving credit".
16Despite this, government spokesman Gabriel Sakellarides told Greek broadcaster ANT1 they were staying positive.
17"We are coming to the table to find a solution. We want to believe we are on a good path and all the deliberations will find a common ground," he said.
18Any changes to the current bailout program would have to first be approved by various European parliaments, including Germany.
19Without an extension Greece's cash reserves are expected to begin running dry on February 24.
20Months of negotiations have failed to bring about an agreement.
21At a meeting in Brussels on Monday, EU finance ministers set conditions for its continued financial support of Athens, including Greece leaving reforms in place.
22Greece has signaled its desire to overturn many of these measures, including raising the minimum wage.
23But Germany's Schauble said doing this would ruin any chance of Greece getting its requested delay granted.
24"It can't and won't be the case that you go for an extension without the agreed reforms," he said on Tuesday.
25The European Central Bank (ECB) is also debating whether or not it will continue proving emergency lending assistance to the country's crippled banks.
26US Treasury Secretary Jacob Lew has also pushed Greece to "find a constructive path forward in partnership with Europe and the IMF," saying time is of the essence.
27Greek Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras' government has gone into overdrive in hopes of resolving Greece's debt crisis on its own terms.
28German Finance Minister Schauble said they were "insulting" their Eurogroup colleagues.
29Talks between Greece and its eurozone partners on how to manage Greek debt have broken down after Athens rejected a eurozone demand to extend its bailout.
30No date for further talks has been set.
31The far-left Syriza party is set to form Greece's next government after sweeping to victory in Sunday's election.
32Syriza plans to renegotiate the country's international bailout, leading to fears for the eurozone.
33The new government in Athens is holding on to its position that it wouldn't seek an extension of the country's bailout program.
34Experts say this stance could lead to Greece's bankruptcy and its exit from the eurozone.
