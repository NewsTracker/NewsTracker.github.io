16Yet serving during the Nixon administration also took a heavy toll.
17"To live through the intense two years of Watergate," Euphemia Davis said, "he had to maintain an absolute belief that the president did not know, that it had to be his underlings who had done this. If he thought otherwise, he would lose his internal compass."
18In the end, though, even Warren could not deny the president's guilt.
19"To find out that he had been used that way," Davis said, "it was devastating to him."
20He returned to journalism in 1975 as editor of the San Diego Union, overseeing the paper through its merger with the Evening Tribune.
21When he stepped down in 1995, he described his career in journalism as "a great ride."
22Warren moved with his second wife, Viviane Pratt, to Middleburg, Virginia, and devoted much of his time to the Episcopal Church.
23"The fact that he worked for two presidents is sort of what makes him known," Johnson, his daughter, said.
24"Dad would say that his later life, with his connections to his faith and learning, are what he is most proud of."
