Pro-Russian journalist gunned down in Ukraine

1Police watch as activists demand an end to corruption in front of the parliament building in Kyiv, Ukraine on Tuesday, April 7, 2015.
2MOSCOW -- A Ukrainian journalist known for his pro-Russian views was gunned down in broad daylight in Kyiv a day after a pro-Russian lawmaker was found dead, local police said Thursday.
3Oles Buzyna, 45, who worked as editor-in-chief of a leading daily until March, was killed early Thursday afternoon by two masked men shooting from a passing car, the Ukrainian interior ministry said in a statement.
4Buzyna, who ran for a seat in parliament on the Russian Bloc ticket in 2012 and was known for his pro-Russian views, was largely perceived as more of an activist than a journalist.
5He had a three-month stint as the editor-in-chief of Segodnya, a newspaper owned by Ukraine's Russia-leaning richest man, before he resigned in March citing pressure for censorship.
6The killing is particularly ominous following another high-profile murder on Wednesday.
7Oleh Kalashnikov, a lawmaker from ex-President Viktor Yanukovych's party, was found dead on the landing of his home in Kyiv with a gunshot wound.
8Police would not immediately say whether he was murdered or killed himself.
9Anton Herashchenko, an adviser to the interior minister, said in a Facebook post that Buzyna -- like Kalashnikov -- was a key witness in a criminal case related to pro-Russian activists who in early 2014 attacked the pro-Western protests on Kyiv's main square.
10The protests eventually led to the ouster of the Kremlin-friendly Yanukovych, who fled the country in February last year.
11Herashchenko called on all witnesses in the case or anyone who might have information about it to contact the police if they fear for their safety.
12The minister said the killings "could be" orchestrated by Russia in order to "destabilize the situation in Ukraine from within."
13Speaking at his annual call-in show, Russian President Vladimir Putin described Buzyna's death as a political murder.
