Israel's Netanyahu scrambles to form governing coalition

1JERUSALEM (AP) - Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu is scrambling to get his governing coalition together ahead of a deadline Wednesday.
2Netanyahu has had a tough time striking deals with potential partners to form a majority coalition in the 120-seat parliament.
3If he fails by the end of day Wednesday, President Reuven Rivlin will task someone else with the job.
4Netanyahu's efforts hit a snag Monday when a key ally, Foreign Minister Avigdor Lieberman, unexpectedly resigned and announced his party is joining the opposition.
5Later that night, Netanyahu teamed up with Shas, an ultra-Orthodox party.
6Last week, the center-right Kulanu party and another ultra-Orthodox party joined.
7But he still was negotiating Tuesday with the nationalist Jewish Home party.
8A deal would give him a narrow majority, setting the stage for an unstable government.
