1OUJA, Iraq - The tomb of Iraq's late dictator Saddam Hussein was virtually leveled in heavy clashes between militants from the Islamic State group and Iraqi security forces in a fight for control of the city of Tikrit.
2Fighting intensified to the north and south of Saddam's hometown Sunday as Iraqi security forces vowed to reach the center of Tikrit within 48 hours.
3Associated Press video from the village of Ouja, just south of Tikrit, shows all that remains of Saddam's once-lavish tomb are the support columns that held up the roof.
4Poster-size pictures of the late Sunni dictator, which once covered the mausoleum, are now nowhere to be seen amid the mountains of concrete rubble.
5Instead, Shiite militia flags and photos of militia leaders mark the predominantly Sunni village, including that of Maj.

13The extremist Islamic State group has controlled Tikrit since June, when it waged its lightning offensive that saw Iraq's second-largest city, Mosul, come under their control.
14The group claimed in August that the tomb had been destroyed, but local officials said it was just ransacked and burned, but suffered only minor damage.
15Saddam's body has been kept in the mausoleum in his birthplace, Ouja, since 2007.
