Germanwings Airbus crashes in French Alps, 150 feared dead

1LA JAVIE, France - An Airbus operated by Lufthansa's Germanwings budget airline crashed in a remote snowy area of the French Alps on Tuesday and all 150 on board were feared dead.
2French President Francois Hollande said he believed none of those on board the A320 had survived, while the head of Lufthansa spoke of a dark day for the German airline.
3Germanwings confirmed its flight 4U9525 from Barcelona to Duesseldorf crashed in the French Alps with 144 passengers and six crew members on board.
4Hollande said: "The conditions of the accident, which have not yet been clarified, lead us to think there are no survivors."
5Officials said the plane issued a distress call at 0947 GMT, about 52 minutes after take-off.
6The accident happened in a snow-clad alpine area that is hard for rescue services to reach.
7Hollande said there were likely to be significant numbers of Germans on the flight.
8Spain's deputy prime minister said 45 passengers had Spanish names.
9It was the first crash of a large passenger jet on French soil since the Concorde disaster just outside Paris in July 2000.
10The A320 is a workhorse of worldwide aviation fleets.
11They are the world's most used passenger jets and have a good though not unblemished safety record.
12Lufthansa Chief Executive Carsten Spohr spoke of a "dark day" for the airline.
13"We do not yet know what has happened to flight 4U9525. My deepest sympathy goes to the families and friends of our passengers and crew," Lufthansa said on Twitter, citing Spohr.
14"If our fears are confirmed, this is a dark day for Lufthansa. We hope to find survivors," it said.
15A spokesman for France's DGAC aviation authority said the airliner crashed near the town of Barcelonnette about 100 km (65 miles) north of the French Riviera city of Nice.
16French and German accident investigators were heading for the crash site in Meolans-Revel, a remote and sparsely inhabited commune in the foothills of the French Alps.
17German Transport Minister Alexander Dobrindt would also travel to the crash site, a ministry spokesman said.
18Airbus said it was aware of reports of the crash.
19"We are aware of the media reports," Airbus said on Twitter.
20"All efforts are now going towards assessing the situation. We will provide further information as soon as available."
21Officials at Barcelona airport said the flight took off at 0855 GMT.
22Families of those on board the plane were gathering at a specially prepared building there.
23The crashed A320 is 24 years old -- at the upper end of useful life of an aircraft in first-tier airlines -- and has been with the parent Lufthansa group since 1991, according to online database airfleets.net.
