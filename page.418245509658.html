World stocks drift lower ahead of Greek vote; China plunges

1World stock markets mostly drifted lower Friday ahead of Greece's weekend referendum, while China's main stock benchmark plunged as government efforts failed to reassure panicky investors.
2KEEPING SCORE: European stocks were mixed, with France's CAC 40 falling 1 percent to 4,787.
3Germany's DAX fell 0.7 percent to 11,020.
4Britain's FTSE 100 dropped 0.8 percent to 6,577.
5U.S. markets were closed in observance of Independence Day.
6GREECE VOTES: Investors are awaiting the outcome of a weekend referendum in Greece on whether to accept more budget cuts in exchange for new bailout loans.
7The government says a "No" vote will put it in a better bargaining position for new terms, while European officials and the opposition say a rejection could lead to Greece's exit from the euro.
8Markets in Asia will get the first chance to react to the result of Sunday's vote.
9CHINA SELL-OFF: A Chinese market rout deepened as investors dumped shares in spite of government measures this week aimed at restoring confidence, such as cutting fees and easing rules on borrowing money for trading.
10The China Securities Regulatory Commission, the market watchdog, said late Thursday that it's launching an investigation into suspected stock market manipulation, state media reported, in an indication of Beijing's efforts to halt the market slide.
11ANALYST VIEWPOINT: "Policies take time to work their way through the system before sentiments can be more permanently altered," Bernard Aw of IG Markets in Singapore wrote in a commentary.
12"For now, the mood is verging on panic. It is extremely hard to calm a bear who is in a rage - not impossible, but tough."
13ASIA: The Shanghai Composite Index ended down 5.8 percent at 3,686.92.
14The index has plunged 29 percent since hitting a peak of 5,166.35 June 12.
15Hong Kong's Hang Seng fell 0.8 percent to close at 26,064.11, while Japan's Nikkei 225 edged up 0.1 percent to 20,539.79.
16South Korea's Kospi slipped 0.1 percent to 2,104.41 and Australia's S&P/ASX 200 retreated 1.1 percent to 5,538.30.
17ENERGY: Benchmark U.S. crude fell 39 cents to $56.54 a barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
18Brent crude, a benchmark for international oils used by many U.S. refineries, fell 39 cents to $61.68 a barrel in London.
19CURRENCIES: The dollar fell to 122.65 yen from 123.09 late Thursday.
20The euro rose to $1.1097 from $1.1083.
