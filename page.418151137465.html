Escapees? ride to Mexico a no-show so pair tried to walk to Canada: Cuomo

1PLATTSBURGH, NEW YORK - Two convicted murderers who eluded a massive manhunt for three weeks planned to drive to Mexico after escaping prison but ended up walking toward Canada when their ride backed out - finally splitting up in their final days of freedom, New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo said Monday.
2David Sweat, 35, was hospitalized in serious condition after being shot twice in the torso by a state trooper and captured Sunday near the Canadian border.
3Cuomo said Sweat has begun providing information about his audacious escape from the maximum-security Clinton Correctional Facility on June 6 with Richard Matt and their weeks on the lam.
4Matt was killed by officers on Friday.
5Prosecutors have previously said prison tailor shop employee Joyce Mitchell got close to the men and agreed to be their getaway driver but backed out because she felt guilty.
6Authorities also have said they discussed killing Mitchell's husband, matching the newly detailed account provided by Cuomo on the Capitol Pressroom radio program.
7"They would kill Mitchell's husband and then get in the car and drive to Mexico on the theory that Mitchell was in love with one or both of them," the governor said.
8"When Mitchell doesn't show up, the Mexico plan gets foiled, and they head north toward Canada."
9Matt had previously fled to Mexico after killing and dismembering his former boss in 1997.
10Matt and Sweat apparently spent more than two weeks together roaming the thick northern New York woods.
11Authorities believe they traveled mostly at night and managed to procure food, a gun and other supplies from hunting camps and seasonal cabins.
12Cuomo said the two men split up about five days ago.
13Matt had blisters on his feet - searchers found his bloody socks - and Sweat thought his 49-year-old escape partner was holding him back.
14"Sweat felt that Matt was slowing him down," Cuomo said.
15Sweat was captured Sunday in the town of Constable, about 30 miles (48 km) northwest of the prison, after New York State Police Sgt. Jay Cook spotted him while on routine patrol.
16Cook shot Sweat as he fled toward a stand of trees.
17Sweat had a bag containing maps, tools, bug repellent and Pop Tarts when he was shot by Cook.
18Sweat was unarmed at the time, authorities said.
19Sweat was airlifted to Albany Medical Center, where he was upgraded from critical to serious condition after doctors determined overnight that he didn't need immediate surgery.
20He is expected to stay at the hospital for a few days while his condition stabilizes, according to hospital officials.
21Mitchell and corrections officer Gene Palmer have been charged in connection with the escape.
22Mitchell pleaded not guilty June 15 to charges including felony promoting prison contraband.
23Palmer, who is charged with promoting prison contraband, tampering with physical evidence and official misconduct, was due in court Monday.
24Palmer told investigators he did not know of the escape plans.
25His attorney has said he will plead not guilty.
26Sweat had been serving a sentence of life without parole in the killing of a sheriff's deputy in Broome County in 2002.
27Matt was serving 25 years to life for the killing and dismembering of his former boss in western New York.
