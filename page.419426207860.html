German parliament overwhelmingly approves Greek bailout

2German Chancellor Angela Merkel, center, stands between lawmakers after she casts her vote on a bailout package for Greece, in the German parliament Bundestag in Berlin, Wednesday, Aug. 19, 2015.
3(AP Photo/Markus Schreiber).
4Germany's parliament overwhelmingly approved a third bailout for Greece on Wednesday, removing a key hurdle to providing new loans to the country and keeping it from defaulting on its debts in as little as 24 hours.
5The vote's result also dispelled speculation that Chancellor Angela Merkel would have difficulty getting her conservative bloc to sign on.
6Lawmakers voted 453-113 in favor, with 18 abstentions.
7The approval is among the last due from parliaments across Europe, with the Dutch scheduled to vote later Wednesday, after which Greece is expected to get the first installment of its new 86 billion euro ($95 billion) loans package.
8The country needs the cash to make a debt repayment Thursday.
9The board of the European bailout fund that will disburse the money will hold a teleconference Wednesday night to discuss the matter.
10The German approval was never in doubt but in a similar vote last month, 60 members of Merkel's conservative bloc voted against.
11Some local media had speculated that as many as double that could rebel this time as Germans are increasingly skeptical about giving Greece more money, but in the end only 63 from her bloc of 311 voted against.
12German Finance Minister Wolfgang Schaeuble, a senior member of Merkel's Christian Democratic Party who has been one of the harshest critics of Greece, may have helped the cause as he lobbied hard ahead of the vote for the passage of Greek's third bailout in five years.
13Schaeuble told lawmakers that approval of the three-year loan package is "in the interest of Greece and the interest of Europe."
14He noted that the Greek government has taken big steps over the past few weeks to restore trust with its creditors.
15Schaeuble conceded that voting in favor of the bailout wasn't an easy one for him, but that "it would be irresponsible to not use the chance for a new beginning in Greece" in light of the fact that the Greek parliament has already backed a large chunk of reform measures demanded by creditors.
16Germany is the largest single contributor to the bailouts and many in Schaeuble's party remain skeptical.
17Merkel's coalition partner, the Social Democrats, and the opposition Greens also backed the deal.
18In the Netherlands, lawmakers interrupted their summer recess to debate the Dutch government's support for the Greek bailout.
19Geert Wilders, the anti-Islam lawmaker who is also a staunch opponent of the European Union and financial support for Greece, opened the debate by calling Prime Minister Mark Rutter "the Pinocchio of the Low Countries" for breaking an election pledge not to approve another bailout.
20Rutte does not formally need Parliament's support to sign off on the bailout, but is unlikely to do so without support of a majority of lawmakers.
21Ahead of the debate, Rutte's own party said it supports - grudgingly - the bailout, as does coalition partner the Labor Party and pro-European party Democrats 66, giving the government a comfortable majority in the 150-seat legislature.
22Under the terms of the deal, Greece has to make further spending cuts and tax increases and implement big reforms to its economy.
23Schaeuble laid out his hope that the bailout will help turn the Greek economy around.
24Greece has spent much of the past six years in recession.
25Its economy is around a quarter smaller than it was and that's pushed unemployment and poverty rates sharply higher.
26"If Greece stands by its obligations and the program is completely and resolutely implemented, then the Greek economy can grow again," Schaeuble said.
27"The opportunity is there. Whether it will be used, only the Greeks can decide."
28Greek Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras is mulling whether to call a vote of confidence in his government after a big rebellion among his radical left Syriza party over the bailout.
29There's also growing talk in Greece that Tsipras may opt for early elections as soon as next month now that the bailout deal is in place.
30Tsipras' Syriza topped January's election on a promise to bring an end to hated austerity measures but after months of tortuous discussions with creditors, he was forced into a U-turn so the country could get the rescue money that maintains Greece's place in the 19-country eurozone.
