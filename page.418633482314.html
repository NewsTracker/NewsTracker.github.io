Intensified shelling in east Ukraine kills four civilians, soldier

1Local residents remove debris inside a damaged building, which according to locals was caused by recent shelling, in Avdiivka in Donetsk region, Ukraine, July 18, 2015.

3Ukraine and pro-Russian rebels accused each other on Saturday of shelling residential areas despite a ceasefire, reporting four civilians and one soldier killed following a week of the deadliest fighting in over a month.
4Separatists said one man was killed by shelling from Ukrainian forces in central Donetsk.
5A Reuters witness reported seeing the body of man in a burning building, though it was unclear if it was the same person.
6More than 6,500 people have been killed since the conflict broke out in eastern Ukraine in April last year.
7Attacks have lessened since a peace agreement was brokered in Minsk, Belarus, five months ago, but both sides accuse each other of violations.
8"The vast majority of shelling and armed attacks by the rebels take place at night to avoid attracting the attention of international observers," Ukrainian military spokesman Andriy Lysenko said.
9He said separatists had focused their attacks on residential areas north-west of rebel-held Donetsk city and that one serviceman, a woman, her granddaughter and a 49-year-old man had been killed.
10Rebels meanwhile accused Ukraine of intensifying their assault.
11The sounds of incoming and outgoing artillery strikes could be heard in the center of separatist-held Donetsk on Saturday evening, according to the Reuters witness.
