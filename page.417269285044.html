Hundreds of Taxi Drivers March in Mexico Against Uber

1Hundreds of taxi drivers marched through Mexico City chanting "Uber Out!" and demanding city authorities ban the online ride service.
2Some drove their pink-and-white cabs in ranks, snarling traffic along the city's main boulevard.
3The drivers say that Uber and other ride-sharing services evade the tax, registration and safety laws that regular cabs are subject to.
4Taxi drivers' leader Eleazar Romero told the rally that "we are not against technology. We just want a level playing field, we want everyone to follow the same tax rules we do."
5Uber responded on its blog by offering Mexico City commuters free rides on Monday, with a hashtag that roughly translates as "If Mexico won't stop, Uber won't stop."
6It said commuters could take up to two rides worth about $10 for free.
