Blatter to visit US in 2016, rejects talk of avoiding FBI

1FILE - In this March 20, 2015 file photo FIFA President Joseph Blatter speaks to journalists following the FIFA Executive Committee meeting in Zurich, Switzerland.
2Blatter told Tuesday's May 12, 2015 edition of free Swiss daily 20 Minutes that his presidency benefited from a boom in soccer's finances and development fueled by television rights deals.
3ZURICH (AP) - Sepp Blatter says he has no fear of setting foot in the United States - no matter what the FBI might be doing.
4The FIFA president insisted Friday he is not avoiding the U.S. despite a reported probe implicating two of his former FIFA colleagues in financial corruption.
5In fact, Blatter plans an American visit in June 2016 for the Copa America tournament.
6"I will be there if elected," said Blatter, who is widely expected to win another term in the FIFA presidential vote on May 29.
7"Even if not elected, they will invite me I am sure."
8Just two weeks before the election that would extend his 17-year reign in world soccer's top job, Blatter is pursuing a low-key campaign.
9Blatter talked about his latest soccer peacemaking mission to Israel and the Palestinian territories; hinted at changing how nations qualify to play in and bid to host the World Cup; and sent a letter to 209 voting federations explaining his priorities.
10The single page document - including the slogan "Develop the game, touch the world and build a better future" - is as near as Blatter has come to a campaign manifesto for a fifth presidential term.
11Fighting discrimination and match-fixing and helping professional clubs would be key.
12Still, three days after ESPN aired a documentary profile of him, Blatter was pressed on a central claim.
13He denied avoiding the United States amid reports that the FBI is investigating former leaders of CONCACAF, the U.S.-based regional soccer body which gets millions of dollars each year from FIFA.
14Blatter said he was aware that Jack Warner of Trinidad and Tobago and Chuck Blazer of the United States, both one-time FIFA executive committee members, were under scrutiny.
15"I know, and this everybody knows, that in the U.S. there is an investigation against former people (that) have been in my government," Blatter said.
16"But it is nothing against me."
17Blatter visited the U.S. in May 2011 for a CONCACAF meeting in Miami and returned weeks later for a Gold Cup semifinal match in Houston.
18ESPN claimed he has purposely stayed away since.
19On Friday, Blatter said he "can go there" and noted there are international channels for authorities "if they want to speak with me."
20But his next visit to the U.S. could be for the centenary Copa America, the South American championship being hosted next year as a one-off by the U.S. Soccer Federation.
21The U.S. also hopes to stage the 2026 World Cup and got a boost Friday.
22Blatter said FIFA's executive committee could pass new rules this month that would block a continent that hosts the World Cup from bidding for the next two editions.
23With Russia staging the 2018 tournament, Europe would then be ineligible to host the World Cup again until 2030.
24"It should be this way," Blatter said Friday, adding that the proposal is "more than an option."
25The Americans could compete against CONCACAF neighbors Canada and Mexico.
26African countries could also join, and Morocco has expressed interest.
27FIFA will choose the 2026 host in a May 2017 vote.
28It will be the first since December 2010 when a controversial 2018-2022 joint process chose Russia and Qatar, which edged the U.S. in a final round of voting.
29The executive committee meets on May 24-25 and again May 30 to confirm the allocation of 2018 World Cup qualifying slots open to each continent.
30Blatter has long encouraged smaller regions to seek more places, potentially taking spots from Europe's 13 qualifying slots in the 32-team lineup.
31On Friday, Blatter hinted at expanding the intercontinental playoffs, which currently do not involve European or African teams.
32"That is a good idea and you are not so far away," Blatter said.
33"This gives a little bit more incentive."
