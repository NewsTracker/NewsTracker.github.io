Burial set for American killed fighting Islamic State

1Kurdish people waving flags flash the V-sign and applaud while lining the road, as the convoy carrying the body of U.S. citizen Keith Broomfield, killed in fighting with the militants of the Islamic State group in Kobani, Syria, is driven by through Suruc, on the Turkey-Syria border, Thursday, June 11, 2015.
2Broomfield, 20 from Massachusetts, died June 3 in battle in a Syrian village near Kobani, making him likely the first U.S. citizen to die fighting alongside Kurds against the Islamic State group.
3BOSTON (AP) - Funeral arrangements for an American killed in Syria while fighting the Islamic State group have been set for later this week.
4Keith Broomfield, who had no military training, is believed to be the first U.S. citizen to die fighting alongside Kurdish forces against ISIS.
5Broomfield, 36, of Westminster, will be buried Wednesday at a private ceremony following a morning funeral at Grace Baptist Church in Hudson.
6A wake will be held at the church Tuesday evening.
7Broomfield, a plant manager at his family's Bolton-based business, Broomfield Labs Inc., died June 3.
8His body arrived in Boston's Logan Airport on Saturday via a Turkish Airlines plane and was taken to Tighe Hamilton Funeral Home in Hudson.
9His obituary said he died "in defense of those being persecuted for their Christian faith" and that "his heart was for the protection of the innocence of Kurdish women and children."
10He is survived by his parents and five siblings.
11Kurdish officials have said Broomfield died in battle in a Syrian village near Kobani.
12Hundreds of people came out to bid farewell to Broomfield before his body was handed over to his family at a Turkish border crossing.
13Broomfield had joined the People's Protection Units known as the YPG on Feb. 24 under the nom de guerre Gelhat Rumet.
14The YPG are the main Kurdish guerrillas battling the Islamic State group in Syria.
15Broomfield's family has said he felt compelled to fight against what he considered "evil."
16The YPG on posted a video online in which Broomfield says he was in Syria "to do what I can to help Kurdistan. With everything that's been going on, it seems like the right thing to do."
