Forget the watch: Apple's iPhones are still the main event

1SAN FRANCISCO (AP) " The iPhone is still the engine behind Apple's phenomenal success, even as attention turned to its new smartwatch in recent weeks.
2While skeptics question whether the company's future is tied too much to one product, the iPhone's popularity was the reason Apple turned in another blow-out financial report Monday.
3The results far surpassed most analysts' expectations for the first three months of the year, when sales traditionally fall from their holiday-season peak.
4Apple sold more than 61 million iPhones in the quarter, accounting for more than two-thirds of its $58 billion in revenue for the quarter and the lion's share of its $13.6 billion in profit.
5As expected, the numbers were down from the previous quarter, when holiday shoppers bought a record 74 million of Apple's new iPhone 6, 6 Plus and older models.
6But the 61 million was a 40 percent increase over the number of iPhones sold in the first three months of 2014.
7"We're seeing great results all over the world," Apple chief financial officer Luca Maestri told The Associated Press, adding that iPhone sales grew 72 percent in China, where the company has big hopes for expansion.
8Other products played a much smaller role.
9Revenue from Mac computers rose 2 percent from a year earlier, to $5.6 billion, while iPad revenue fell 29 percent, to $5.4 billion " continuing a steady decline in tablet sales.
10Apple didn't report any results for the new Apple Watch, which it began selling this month, after the quarter ended.
11Maestri said customer response had been "positive."
12Analysts estimate about 2 million have sold to date, suggesting early demand is healthy but not of blockbuster proportions.
13The iPhone is another story.
14Since it began offering models with bigger screens last fall, Apple has vied with South Korea's Samsung for the No.
151 position in the global smartphone market.
16By some estimates, Apple outsold Samsung in the quarter that ended in December, and analysts will be watching closely when Samsung reports its latest results this week.
17Apple also announced an expansion of its effort to return more of its sizable cash war chest to investors.
18The company said it will raise its quarterly dividend by 11 percent, to 52 cents a share, and has increased a $90 billion stock buyback program to $140 billion.
19In total, the company said the program will return $200 billion to investors by the end of March 2017.
20As iPhone sales have surged, so has Apple's stock.
21Apple shares have gained more than 50 percent over the last year, making it the world's most valuable company.
22The stock closed Monday at $132.65, up 1.8 percent for the day, and was rising in late trading.
23The iPhone isn't just Apple's "dominant product," said Frank Gillett, a tech industry analyst at Forrester Research.
24"It's more than anything else what's driving the success of their company."
25Market researchers, however, expect smartphone growth to slow down worldwide this year, particularly at the higher price range where Apple competes, as most consumers in industrialized countries have already bought one.
26That could make it difficult for Apple to maintain its recent pace.
27"They're extremely dependent on the iPhone," said investment Colin Gillis at BGC Partners.
28"At some point, the market dynamics change," he said, adding that "the question is what could replace the iPhone" if sales begin to slow.
29Apple CEO Tim Cook has said he's optimistic about new markets such as China, where Apple has made a strong showing against Samsung and China's Xiaomi.
30And even if Apple is increasingly selling new iPhones to people who are simply upgrading older models, "that's still a pretty healthy market," said Gartner analyst Van Baker, noting that more than 700 million iPhones have been sold since the first model was introduced in 2007.
31Maestri also stressed the potential for new products like Apple Watch and Apple Pay, the company's mobile payment service.
32While these currently provide minimal revenue, analysts say they have big potential.
33And they are designed to work closely with the iPhone, which means each may bolster the other's popularity in the future, Gillett said.
