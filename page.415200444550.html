7The suspect is the brother of Miguel Angel Trevino Morales, described as the most bloodthirsty leader of Mexico's most violent cartel.
8Miguel Angel was arrested in July 2013, almost a year after Mexican marines killed the Zetas' other biggest leader, Heriberto Lazcano "El Lazca."
9Rubido said Omar, 38, took over leadership of the Zetas after his brother's arrest and immediately became a target of their investigation.
10The hunt for him caught a break in February when authorities observed someone matching Trevino's description at one of the houses around Monterrey where Jimenez Encinas, the alleged finance chief, was known to hold meetings, Rubido said.
11Surveillance was increased until Trevino's identity was confirmed.
12Authorities discovered he was moving with a smaller than usual security detail to draw less attention.
13Just after 3 a.m. Wednesday, federal police and soldiers moved on both locations, Rubido said.
14Four additional men were also arrested.
15All six were walked across the tarmac from a military plane at Mexico City's airport and loaded into armored personnel carriers.
16The Zetas was originally a gang formed by deserters from an elite army unit and left a trail of brutality, bloodshed and mutilated bodies across northern Mexico during turf battles with the rival Gulf cartel.
17But much of the violence along Mexico's northeast border now is due to internal battles among Gulf cartel factions.
18"The truth is that this is a group that has been fragmented," Javier Oliva, an expert on the drug war at Mexico's National Autonomous University, said of the Zetas.
19"Their influence is now more on a local level, in townships" rather than the broad, multi-state trafficking corridor they once controlled.
20But Oliva said that doesn't mean the Zetas and their internal fight to determine a replacement for Trevino Morales won't turn bloody.
21He said gunmen or second-tier operators who were active under Trevino Morales may now seek to replace him.
22"One would hope that the federal and state governments have taken precautions to avoid this arrest from causing more violence," Oliva said.
23The capture of Omar Trevino Morales follows Friday's arrest of another big cartel leader, Servando Gomez, known as "La Tuta."
24Gomez allegedly led the Knights Templar, a pseudo-religious drug gang that built up control of many sectors of the economy in the western state of Michoacan before it was weakened by an uprising of citizen vigilante groups and a stepped-up federal security campaign.
25The two arrests provided much-needed good news for the administration of President Enrique Pena Nieto, who has been battered by a series of scandals in recent months.
26"The government needed to show that some of its policies were successful," said Raul Benitez, a security expert at the National Autonomous University.
27"And in this case, without doubt the strategy against the drug cartels is going well."
28Like Servando Gomez, Omar Trevino Morales was captured without any shots being fired.
29The Trevino Morales brothers took proceeds from their U.S. drug sales and laundered them by purchasing American quarter horses.
30That scheme was led by Jose Trevino Morales, a third brother.
31A jury in Texas found Morales guilty in May of investing $16 million of drug money in the buying, training and racing of horses across the Southwest United States.

10But a border guard scheme is contentious.
11Many countries are reluctant to surrender control of their borders to an EU agency, and not all members function in the same way.
12The coastguard in Malta " another country on the migration front-line " is run by the military, which could complicate any effort to have a common EU border guard force.
