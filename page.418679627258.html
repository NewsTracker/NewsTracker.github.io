Suspected Islamic State suicide bomber kills 30 in Suruc, Turkey

1The aftermath of an explosion that killed dozens of people and injured scores of others in the Turkish town of Suruc near the Syrian border on Monday.
2Suruc: A suspected Islamic State suicide bomber killed at least 30 people, mostly young students, in an attack on a Turkish town near the Syrian border on Monday.
3Bodies lay beneath trees after the blast outside a cultural centre in the mostly Kurdish town of Suruc in south-eastern Turkey, some 10 km from the Syrian town of Kobane, where Kurdish fighters have been battling Islamic State.
4Helping the students injured in the explosion that killed dozens in the Turkish town of Suruc.
5Video footage showed young men and women standing behind a banner declaring support for Kobane, some holding up small red flags.
6Suddenly there was a huge explosion, apparently from within the crowd, sending up a column of flame.
7It tore through a group of mostly university-aged students from an activist group as they gathered to make a statement to the local press about a trip they were planning to help rebuild Kobani.
8The students from the Federation of Socialist Youth Associations had been planning a trip to Kobani to build a library, plant a forest and build a playground, said Fatma Edemen, a member of the group wounded in the blast.
9Rushing to the assistance of the students wounded in the blast in Suruc.
10"I was behind a banner so I couldn't see the attacker, but we understand it was a suicide attack. I was thrown to the ground...I jumped up and began running before I even realised I was hurt," said Edemen, a 22-year-old journalism student at Ankara University.
11One witness, giving his name as Mehmet, said he saw more than 20 bodies.
12"It was a huge explosion, we all shook."
13Turkey's NATO allies have been seeking tighter controls on a porous border with Syria that runs alongside Islamic State-held territories.
14But monitoring is difficult with 1.8 million Syrian refugees now on the Turkish side and smuggling rife.
15An injured student in Suruc on Monday.
16The US, which has an air base at Incirlik in southern Turkey, though it is not being used for its air attacks on IS forces, called the bombing a "heinous terror attack".
17Prime Minister Ahmet Davutoglu told a news conference in Ankara 30 people had been killed.
18"It is...most probably a suicide bombing".
19The Hurriyet newspaper said the attacker was an 18-year old woman, but there was no confirmation.
20Demonstrators run from tear gas used by riot police in Istanbul during a protest in central Istanbul, which erupted after the Suruc blast.
21Protesters blamed the government for not preventing the bombing.
22"Turkey has taken and will continue to take all necessary measures against the Islamic State", Mr Davutoglu said, without giving details.
23"Measures on our border with Syria...will be increased."
24The Suruc attack comes weeks after Turkey deployed additional troops and equipment along parts of its border with Syria, concerned about the risk of spillover as fighting between Kurdish forces, rebel groups, Syrian government troops and Islamic State militants intensifies.
25"Terror has no religion, no country, no race," President Tayyip Erdogan said of the bloodiest such attack in Turkey since at least 50 people were killed in the town of Reyhanli near the border in 2013.
26Tear gas used by riot police dispersed demonstrators in Istanbul.
27The protesters blamed the Suruc blast on lax government action on Islamic State.
28Turkey's Kurds have been enraged by what they see as the AKP party government's failure to do more to stop Islamic State.
29The PKK held Ankara responsible for Monday's attack, saying it had "supported and cultivated" Islamic State against the Kurds.
30Police in Istanbul fired tear gas and water cannon after a demonstration by several hundred pro-Kurdish protesters on the central Taksim Square turned violent.
31"Murderer Islamic State, collaborator Erdogan and AKP", ran one slogan.
32Kobani was the site of one of the biggest battles against Islamic State last year and was secured by Syrian Kurdish fighters last month after repeated assaults.
33The YPG drove the Islamist militants back from the town with the help of US air strikes after months of fighting and siege.
34The blast in Suruc came after a series of attacks on the Kurdish HDP party in the run-up to a June 7 election in Turkey, including two small bombs at a political rally in the city of Diyarbakir, which the party blamed on Islamic State sympathisers.
35"What gives us pause about this attack is that while the others were haphazard and sloppy ... the size of this explosion suggests something more sophisticated," said Aaron Stein, an Atlantic Council fellow who specialises on Turkey and Syria.
