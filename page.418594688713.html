EU task force seeks mental exams, drug tests for pilots

1BRUSSELS (AP) - A European Union task force is recommending pre-employment psychological evaluations and random drug and alcohol tests for pilots to prevent a repeat of the Germanwings disaster.
2The panel led by the European Aviation Safety Agency issued its recommendations Friday.
3It was formed in response to the March 24 crash of a German airliner in France that killed all 150 people onboard.
4Experts say the plane's co-pilot, Andreas Lubitz, who reportedly had a history of mental illness, deliberately flew the jet into a mountain after locking the pilot out of the cockpit.
5The task force's recommendations also call for keeping two people in the cockpit at all times.
6Its advice will now be considered by the European Commission and may lead to new EU-wide aviation safety regulations.
