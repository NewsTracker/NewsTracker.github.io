Mexico hit by tornado, floods leaving 13 dead

1A car lies destroyed in front of a house after a tornado struck in Ciudad Acuna, northern Mexico on Monday.
2Mexico City: A tornado ripped into a town in northern Mexico, killing at least 13 people and flattening hundreds of homes in a deadly six-second blast of carnage.
3The savage twister roared through the Mexican border town of Ciudad Acuna at dawn on Monday, tossing cars and big rig trucks into the air before they smashed into houses and buildings.
4Images from the aftermath showed several crumpled vehicles resting against walls and roofs as shellshocked inhabitants of the town of 136,000 struggled to make sense of the devastation.
5People collect their belongings after a tornado ripped into Ciudad Acuna in northern Mexico, killing at least 13 people on Monday.
6The tornado came as ferocious weather battered swaths of Mexico and the southern United States, where rescuers hunted for 12 people missing in flash floods across Texas and Oklahoma that left three people dead.
7In Mexico, national civil protection coordinator Luis Felipe Puente told Foro TV that "more than 1000 homes" had been affected.
8The tornado swept in at "impressive speed" of 270-300km/h and lasted only six seconds, Mr Puente said.
9A damaged car sits on a road in Ciudad Acuna, northern Mexico after a powerful tornado touched down.
10Coahuila state Interior Secretary Victor Zamora said the death toll rose from 11 to 13.
11The victims include 10 adults and three children.
12Almost 230 people were hurt after getting hit or cut by objects thrown by the wind, Mr Zamora told Milenio television.
13Of those, 88 were still getting treatment in the afternoon.
14Ciudad Acuna Mayor Evaristo Lenin Perez said a seven-year-old boy was missing, while rescuers combed through the rubble to look for any more victims or survivors.
15Most of the fatalities were people who were out on the street.
16"We have hundreds of houses that are practically destroyed," Mr Perez said, appealing for water, canned food and clothing for the victims.
17Authorities opened eight temporary shelters for those left homeless.
18The tornado whipped up as heavy rains pounded the city, with hail falling in some areas.
