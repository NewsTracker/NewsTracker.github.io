Kenya launches airstrikes on Islamic extremists in Somalia after massacre

1Kenya launched air strikes against Islamic militants in Somalia following an extremist attack on a Kenyan college that killed 148 people, a military spokesman said Monday.
2Warplanes attacked positions of the al-Shabab militant group on Sunday afternoon and early Monday morning, said Col.
3David Obonyo of the Kenyan military.
4Al-Shabab, which is based in Somalia, claimed responsibility for the college attack Thursday in the Kenyan town of Garissa.
5Four gunmen died in the assault.
6The air strikes occurred in the Gedo region of Somalia, Obonyo said.
7"This is part of continuing operations, not just in response to Garissa," he said.
8Kenyan President Uhuru Kenyatta had vowed harsh measures against al-Shabab following the slaughter at Garissa, which is near the border with Somalia.
9Kenya has troops in Somalia as part of an African Union force to attack al-Shabab and shore up the beleaguered Somali government.
10Kenya has carried out air strikes before.
11Al-Shabab said it attacked students at Garissa College University as a reprisal for Kenya sending troops into Somalia.
