Morrison's tiebreaking double helps Mariners beat Rangers

1Seattle Mariners' Nelson Cruz passes third base after he hit a solo-home run against the Texas Rangers during second inning of a baseball game, Wednesday, April 29, 2015, in Arlington, Texas.
2Logan Morrison had a tiebreaking, two-run double to back Felix Hernandez and lead the Seattle Mariners to a 5-2 victory over the Texas Rangers on Wednesday night.
3With the score tied 2-2 and the bases loaded in the fifth inning, Morrison hit a ground ball past first base against Texas starter Wandy Rodriguez (0-1).
4Morrison finished 4 for 5, including two hits against Rodriguez to improve to 7 for 14 in his career against the left-hander.
5Hernandez allowed five hits, two runs and one walk in 6 2-3 innings to improve to 4-0 for the first time since 2009.
6Fernando Rodney pitched a perfect ninth inning for his seventh save in eight opportunities.
7He saved all three games as the Mariners swept the series for their first three-game winning streak this season.
8Seattle's Nelson Cruz was 2 for 4, including his 10th home run to lead off the second inning.
9Each team scored two runs in the second.
10Cruz homered into the club level in left field.
11Only 19 homers have reached that level in 22 seasons, and Cruz has hit three of those.
12A walk and two singles loaded the bases, but Rodriguez gave up only one more run when Mike Zunino grounded into a double play.
13In the bottom of the second, Adrian Beltre worked a walk from Hernandez.
14Beltre scored on a double by Carlos Peguero, who had been in an 0-for-15 slump, and Peguero scored on Carlos Corporan's two-out single.
15Justin Ruggiano led off the Mariners' sixth with a double.
16After two walks loaded the bases with one out, pitching coach Mike Maddux visited Rodriguez, but he stayed in the game.
17Morrison then bounced a grounder that eluded the reach of first baseman Kyle Blanks and went into the right-field corner as two runs scored.
18Seattle added an unearned run in the ninth on Keone Kela's wild pitch.
19Hernandez threw a season-high 114 pitches.
20He is 14-21 against Texas in his career, but 2-0 in 2015.
21Mariners: 3B Kyle Seager was not in the starting lineup because of a sore thumb that has been bothering since he jammed it while swinging at an inside pitch last week.
22Seager entered the game as a defensive replacement in the eighth to keep alive a streak of 147 straight games played.
23Rangers: 1B Mitch Moreland underwent surgery Wednesday to remove a loose particle from his left elbow.
24He went on the disabled list retroactive to Tuesday, and is expected back next month.
25Mariners: A 10-game trip continues on Thursday with the first of four games at Houston.
26LHP James Paxton (0-2, 6.86) is scheduled to make his first start against the Astros.
27Rangers: They'll be at home on Friday against Oakland.
28RHP Colby Lewis (1-2, 3.75) is 7-1 with a 2.86 ERA in his last 12 starts vs. the Athletics dating back to 2010.
