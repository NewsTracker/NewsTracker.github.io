Ex-cyclone to cause wild weather in NT

1After two evacuations in one month, a Goulburn Island resident is calling on the Northern Territory government to invest in a cyclone shelter in the community.
2Located 300km east of Darwin off the remote coast of the Northern Territory, there are no buildings on the island that can withstand a category 3 storm.
3All 427 of the island's residents were flown to Darwin for four days in February as category 4 Cyclone Lam bore down, and again for two days this week as category 2 Cyclone Nathan approached, which was forecast to intensify to a category 3.
4On Wednesday morning the residents were heading home from the emergency cyclone shelter at Darwin Showgrounds where they had spent the last two nights.
5"I hope and pray this will be the last one," said Goulburn Island resident Bunug Galaminda.
6"What I'm trying to say to the government now: give us a helping hand by building a proper multi-purpose hall.
7"Especially the old people and children being moved away, it's not good, so we need to house people and have a proper shelter for our community."
8He said the money spent on the two evacuations could have been put towards the construction of a shelter.
9"Taxpayers' money can be used for something else, for the benefit of the community or for the benefit of the NT," Mr Galaminda said.
10The government has not yet released figures on the cost of the operation, but Chief Minister Adam Giles said it was planning to find a suitable location and determine what type of storm shelter was needed for the island.
11"It's just a community that's never had a cyclone shelter and never had a coded building which can accommodate the 400-odd resident population," he told the ABC.
12"We'd already started talking about it before Cyclone Nathan turned up, but we'll see what we can put in place."
