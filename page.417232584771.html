At Cannes, Michel Franco's 'Chronic' looks death in the face

1Actor Tim Roth, left, and director Michel Franco pose for photographers during a photo call for the film Chronic, at the 68th international film festival, Cannes, southern France, Friday, May 22, 2015.
2CANNES, France (AP) - Death is something we'd all like to avoid.
3But director Michel Franco thinks a reluctance to look at the end of life is unhealthy.
4The Mexican filmmaker's Cannes Film Festival entry "Chronic" stars Tim Roth as a homecare nurse to the dying.
5It's an unflinching film - no music, sparse dialogue - that plants the camera in bathrooms and bedrooms where Roth's character, David, shares his patients' most intimate moments.
6"When I watch a film, the minute I sense that I am being manipulated, I step back," Franco said at a Cannes hotel poolside.
7"I was avoiding (trying) to manipulate the audience. You just show it. I think that's the most powerful, the purest, way to approach this material."
8Many reviews of the film have included the word "challenging."
9Variety noted that the film asks "audiences to do the heavy lifting required to arrive at a meaningful emotional response."
10Not everyone will embrace the film, but 35-year-old Franco has the firm loyalty of his crew.
11Sarah Sutherland, who plays the estranged daughter of Roth's character, said she knew from her first audition that this would not be a run-of-the-mill job.
12"Immediately afterwards, I got an email saying 'We'd like you to come back. Please don't brush your hair, take off your makeup, wear flat shoes,'" said Sutherland, best known as the daughter of Julia Louis-Dreyfus' politician on the HBO comedy "Veep."
13"That's when I knew that Michel and I would really click," she added.
14Roth praised Franco for looking where many others would flinch.
15"Death is the last taboo," Roth said.
16"Nobody wants to talk about it. ... We have no vocabulary. We do not know what to do. We are helpless, floundering around."
17Roth is in almost every shot of the film, and critics have praised the British actor's intense depiction of a man who has buried layers of emotional pain.
18He is a strong candidate for best actor at Cannes' award ceremony on Sunday.
19The actor and director met at Cannes in 2012, when a jury headed by Roth awarded a prize to Franco's "After Lucia."
20At the time, Franco was writing a script inspired by the nurse who had cared for his grandmother after she had a stroke.
21"He asked me what was my next movie," Franco said.
22The role is a change of pace for Roth from his work in the high-energy films of Quentin Tarantino.
23Roth was in "Reservoir Dogs" and "Pulp Fiction," and has just finished filming Tarantino's upcoming Western, "The Hateful Eight."
24Franco said before he met Roth, "maybe it wouldn't have occurred to me that Tim was perfect for the role."
25"Chronic" is Franco's English-language debut, shot in Los Angeles with a mostly American cast.
26The director said working in the U.S. film industry meant resisting the urge to supersize his small, simple filmmaking style.
27"I am used to doing things pretty much my own way," Franco said.
28"I like working with a tiny, tiny crew. When you're trying to portray intimacy onscreen, it's better to have an intimate crew as well.
