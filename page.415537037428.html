6*TOKYO - After years of scoffing at the threat from smartphones, Nintendo Co. is doing an about-face and entering an alliance with Japanese mobile game company DeNa Co. to develop games for mobile devices.
7The announcement Tuesday means that Nintendo's trademark game characters such as Super Mario and Pokemon could finally be featured on smartphones and tablets.
8Such characters have been fiercely protected by Nintendo, appearing only on Nintendo platforms such as the Wii home console and 3DS mobile machines.
9Both sides said they will build a global membership service for various devices including personal computers, smartphones and Nintendo machines.
10The service is set to be introduced in the fall.
11They said the mobile games won't be mere adaptations of the games already out for the Nintendo machines but developed especially for the smartphone experience.
12Nintendo promised that it will not abandon the business of making its own games hardware, saying that it had a new game platform under development.
13The company's president, Satoru Iwata, said Nintendo will share more details about the platform, code-named NX, next year.
14The alliance will be combining Nintendo's intellectual property and game development skills with DeNa's expertise in mobile games, the announcement said.
15Under the alliance, Nintendo will acquire about 15 million DeNa shares or a 10 percent stake.
16In return, DeNa will acquire about 1.759 million Nintendo shares, or 1.24 percent of the company.
17Both acquisitions are worth $182 million.
18The payment is due April 2, they said.
19Nintendo has run into trouble in the past few years as people increasingly turned to mobile phones to play games and spend time on social networks.
20Most mobile games are cheaper than Nintendo games, especially the free-to-play games that dominate mobile entertainment.
21The company is expecting to turn a profit for the fiscal year that ends this month, a turnaround from the red ink posted in the previous year.
22DeNa, founded in 1999, has a reputation as an innovator and is one of the most successful of a new generation of Japanese technology companies.
23It develops and operates a broad range of mobile and online services, including games and e-commerce.
