In an IS training camp, children told: Behead the doll

1In this April 19, 2015 picture, Syrian boy Ahmad, 7, whose parents went missing in Syria, attends a class of religious lessons at an Islamic teaching center designed to counter Islamic State group indoctrination, near the Turkish-Syrian border city of Sanliurfa, southern Turkey.
2"They are planting extremism and terrorism in young people's minds," said Abu Hafs Naqshabandi, who runs the classes.
3"I am terribly worried about future generations."
4SANLIURFA, Turkey (AP) - The children had all been shown videos of beheadings and told by their trainers with the Islamic State group that they would perform one someday.
5First, they had to practice technique.
6The more than 120 boys were each given a doll and a sword and told, cut off its head.
7A 14-year-old who was among the boys, all abducted from Iraq's Yazidi religious minority, said he couldn't cut it right.
8He chopped once, twice, three times.
9"Then they taught me how to hold the sword, and they told me how to hit. They told me it was the head of the infidels," the boy, renamed Yahya by his IS captors, told The Associated Press last week in northern Iraq, where he fled after escaping the IS training camp.
10When Islamic State extremists overran Yazidi towns in northern Iraq last year, they butchered older men and enslaved many of the women and girls.
11Dozens of young Yazidi boys like Yahya had a different fate: The IS sought to re-educate them.
12They forced them to convert to Islam from their ancient faith and tried to turn them into jihadi fighters.
13It is part of a concerted effort by the extremists to build a new generation of militants, according to AP interviews with residents who fled or still live under IS in Syria and Iraq.
14The group is recruiting teens and children using gifts, threats and brainwashing.
15Boys have been turned into killers and suicide bombers.
16An IS video issued last week showed a boy beheading a Syrian soldier under an adult militant's supervision.
17Last month, a video showed 25 children unflinchingly shooting 25 captured Syrian soldiers in the head.
18In schools and mosques, militants infuse children with extremist doctrine, often turning them against their own parents.
19Fighters in the street befriend children with toys.
20IS training camps churn out the Ashbal, Arabic for "lion cubs," child fighters for the "caliphate" that IS declared across its territory.
21The caliphate is a historic form of Islamic rule that the group claims to be reviving with its own radical interpretation, though the vast majority of Muslims reject its claims.
22"I am terribly worried about future generations," said Abu Hafs Naqshabandi, a Syrian sheikh who runs religion classes for refugees in the Turkish city of Sanliurfa to counter IS ideology.
23The indoctrination mainly targets Sunni Muslim children.
24In IS-held towns, militants show young people videos at street booths.
25They hold outdoor events for children, distributing soft drinks and candy - and propaganda.
26They tell adults, "We have given up on you, we care about the new generation," said an anti-IS activist who fled the Syrian city of Raqqa, the extremists' de facto capital.
27He spoke on condition of anonymity to preserve the safety of relatives under IS rule.
28With the Yazidis, whom IS considers heretics ripe for slaughter, the group sought to take another community's youth, erase their past and replace it with radicalism.
29Yahya, his little brother, their mother and hundreds of Yazidis were captured when IS seized the Iraqi town of Sulagh in August.
30They were taken to Raqqa, where the brothers and other Yazidi boys aged 8 to 15 were put in the Farouq training camp.
31They were given Muslim Arabic names to replace their Kurdish names.
32Yahya asked that AP not use his real name for his and his family's safety.
33He spent nearly five months there, training eight to 10 hours a day, including exercises, weapons drills and Quranic studies.
34They told him Yazidis are "dirty" and should be killed, he said.
35They showed him how to shoot someone from close range.
36The boys hit each other in some exercises.
37Yahya punched his 10-year-old brother, knocking out a tooth.
38The trainer "said if I didn't do it, he'd shoot me," Yahya said.
39"They ... told us it would make us tougher. They beat us everywhere."
