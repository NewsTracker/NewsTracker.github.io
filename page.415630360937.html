Football: Enigma of Bale and Beckham

Real Madrid players in high spirits as Los Blancos prepare for La Liga title showdown with rivals BarcelonaÂ 

1Tomorrow morning (NZT), a very different British ambassador for football will run out at the Nou Camp as Real Madrid endeavour to avoid falling four points behind Barcelona in the title race.
2It remains a mystery why supporters of the 10-times European Cup holders have still not taken to Gareth Bale as they did to Beckham just over a decade ago -- especially when you consider that Beckham won a solitary league title in his fourth and final season, and Bale has already won, and scored decisive goals in, the Copa del Rey, the Champions League, and Club World Cup.
3As well as just having had the best first season of any Brit abroad, and being the only top British player currently plying his trade in a major European League, Bale is also now the last remaining Briton left in the Champions League.
4A solitary figure is also how he has been depicted in Madrid.
5He didn't go to Cristiano Ronaldo's 30th birthday party last month, he still doesn't speak Spanish, and even on the pitch analysts have him pegged as the man stood out on the wing, neither tracking back to bolster a recently porous defence, nor involving himself in the team's build-up play.
6A lot less is made of those three historic goals - his header in Lisbon in the Champions League final, the sprint from the halfway line in the last minutes of the Copa del Rey final, and the second goal against San Lorenzo in December that made Real Madrid world club champions.
7That win in Marrakesh was the team's 22nd consecutive victory but what followed was defeat in the league to Valencia in the first game of the new year, a quarter-final exit from the Copa del Rey, a 4-0 derby defeat to Atletico Madrid, the defeat to Athletic Bilbao that surrendered the lead at the top of the table to Barcelona, and a 4-3 home reverse to Schalke in the Champions League that meant, despite going through, the team was whistled off the pitch.
8All this would have been even worse had Bale not won and scored a late penalty away to Cordoba after Ronaldo had been sent off during a game at the end of January, but not much is made of that either, as he bears the brunt of much of the criticism for the monumental 2015 dip.
9His goal celebration last week - covering his ears as he ran to kick the corner flag - was the first public show of any annoyance with the puzzling treatment.
10Perhaps it's Real Madrid supporters' hankering for a more local hero that has led to their deification of Malaga-born Isco - a talented attacking midfielder, but far from the complete player he is being portrayed as.
11Last season Bale, Karim Benzema and Ronaldo did very little back-tracking as Luca Modric and Angel Di Maria covered the kilometres either side of Xabi Alonso.
12This season's team has lacked that defensive balance, especially when Modric missed three months of the season with a thigh injury.
13He returned last weekend against Levante and Bale immediately looked more comfortable on the pitch with a friend, an English speaker, and a midfielder capable of defending.
14In the dressing room, he also gets on well with left back Marcelo and there is a healthy relationship with both Ancelotti and his assistant, Paul Clement.
15But Bale has an even more highly placed sponsor in the club's president, Florentino Perez.
16It was Perez who sanctioned the 100 million outlay on Bale.
17The gamble paid out in shiny Champions League medals and Bale then went off on a promotional tour of Indonesia in the summer, before being the brightest light on an otherwise flat Real Madrid tour of the US.
18It would be inaccurate to say there is any great animosity between Bale and Ronaldo but, neither does there seem to be any great bond.
19Tomorrow they will need to be united to get the better of Barcelona.
20If Beckham is in attendance maybe he can take the time out post-match to explain the complexities of these most fickle supporters to the man who now runs up and down that same right wing, has won far more in far less time, but is still struggling to gain anywhere near the same affection.
1Gareth Bale's brace in a 2-0 win over Levante last weekend arrested a run of three games without victory for Los Blancos, but the game against Barca will undoubtedly represent a far sterner test.
2Madrid boss Carlo Ancelotti has been under intense pressure in recent weeks from the fans and board alike and the Italian will know that a victory against Barca would go along way towards assuaging those concerns.
3Meanwhile, world-record signing Bale, will be keen to carry on the form he showed against Levante, which marked his first two goals in nine games.
4The forward had also been on the receiving end of some stick from the crowd.
