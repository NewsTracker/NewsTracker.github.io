Liberia quarantines community where corpse tested for Ebola

1FILE - In this file photo taken on Wednesday, March 11, 2015, health workers carry a body of a person that they suspected died form the Ebola virus at a new graveyard on the outskirts of Monrovia, Liberia.
2The corpse of a 17-year-old man has tested positive for Ebola in Liberia, but no other cases have been reported, the country's deputy health minister said late Monday, June 29, 2015.
3MONROVIA, Liberia (AP) - Liberian authorities have quarantined the Nedowein area where the corpse of a 17-year-old boy tested positive for Ebola, sparking fears the country could face another outbreak of the disease nearly two months after being declared Ebola-free.
4Deputy health minister and head of Liberia's Ebola response team, Tolbert Nyenswah, told The Associated Press Tuesday that the boy died at his home and was buried safely to avoid spread.
5He said teams are investigating how the boy became infected.
6The area is not near the borders with Sierra Leone and Guinea, which still have Ebola cases.
7Nyenswah said "Liberia has got a re-infection of Ebola."
8The World Health Organization declared Liberia Ebola-free on May 9, after going 42 days without a new case.
9More than 4,800 people died in Liberia from Ebola.
