The Latest on Nepal Quake: Death toll 4,438 in 3 countries

1A Nepal police official says at least 4,352 bodies have so far been recovered after last week's massive earthquake that struck just outside of capital Kathmandu.
2Deputy Inspector General of Police Komal Singh Bam says the toll includes 1,176 bodies recovered in Sindhupalchuk district, just northeast of the capital.
3He says 8,063 people have been injured.
4Another 18 people were also killed in a quake-triggered avalanche that swept the Everest base camp.
5In neighboring India 61 people were killed and China's official Xinhua News Agency reported 25 dead in Tibet.
6A local seismologist says major aftershocks are now unlikely to occur as the 72-hour mark after Saturday's devastating earthquake approaches.
7Lok Bijaya Adhikari, chief of Nepal's National Seismological Center, says the number and strength of aftershocks have been receding.
8There have been more than 100 aftershocks since Saturday's magnitude 7.8 temblor that left more than 4,000 people confirmed dead so far.
9The largest of these was magnitude 6.7 on Sunday.
10The ground shook Tuesday morning at 5 a.m. but measured only magnitude 4.5.
11Smaller aftershocks are expected to continue for a month and Kathmandu residents could continue to feel tremors because the epicenter is close to the city.
12Health workers fear a major health crisis among the survivors of Saturday's massive earthquake who are living in the open or in crowded tents with no access to sanitation or clean water.
13Baburam Marasini, director of Nepal's Epidemiology and Disease Control Division, says their main concern is making sure people get clean water.
14He says "we fear diseases."
15He says the department is asking people to take precautions such as eating with a clean spoon and not with their hands as most people here normally do.
16He says people are also being asked to drink clean water.
17Attempts are also being made to reach rural areas quickly where a clear picture of the death toll - now more than 4,000 - is still not available.
18A government official says business owners are being asked to open their shops amid anxiety among locals about dwindling food and medical supplies in the wake of Saturday's earthquake that has left more than 4,000 people dead.
19Naindra Prasad Upadhaya, an official at the Commerce and Supplies Ministry says the government has made arrangements to pick up food and supplies directly from factories and distribute them free in areas where necessary.
20Water has been the big issue.
21There will be more tankers bringing water to the areas where people are camped out in Kathmandu and surrounding areas, he says.
22Food will also be sent to the rural areas on helicopters, he says.
23Police are on the lookout for businesses that are overcharging to take advantage of demand and scarcity, and such people will be arrested and punished, he said.
