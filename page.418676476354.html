6Smartphone sales may be slowing for some tech companies, but not for Apple.
7Analysts expect another powerhouse performance from the California tech giant when it reports quarterly financial results Tuesday.
8Wall Street analysts estimate Apple will report a hefty $10.3 billion in profit after selling $49 billion worth of iPhones, iPads, Mac computers and other products during the April-June quarter.
9That's an increase of more than 30 percent in both revenue and profit from the same period a year earlier.
10Apple has said it won't release sales figures for the new Apple Watch, though some analysts believe demand has fallen since Apple introduced the wearable gadget three months ago.
11But it almost doesn't matter: As in other recent quarters, Apple will reap its biggest rewards from its smartphones, especially the new, big-screen iPhone 6 and 6 Plus models introduced last fall.
12People use smartphones for social networking, playing games, looking at pictures - far more than they use them to make calls.
13And for those activities, many users prefer a bigger screen.
14Several analysts say their studies confirm Apple CEO Tim Cook's claim that many recent iPhone buyers are people who switched from Android phones.
15Some consumers were drawn to Android devices in recent years because they had bigger screens, but Apple's decision to increase the iPhone's screen size is persuading Android owners to defect, said Ryan Reith, who tracks smartphone sales for the research firm IDC.
16Apple likely sold about 47 million iPhones during the most recent quarter, or 34 percent more than a year earlier, according to analysts polled by FactSet.
17At an average selling price of more than $600, before carrier subsidies, Apple's phones are more expensive than many competing Android models, but they also provide more profit.
18While Apple doesn't break out the numbers, Canaccord Genuity analyst Michael Walkley estimates that during the first three months of 2015, Apple reaped 92 percent of the $16.6 billion in operating profit generated from smartphones by companies around the world.
19Samsung sold more phones than Apple during that period, but many of them were lower-priced models, giving Samsung only about 15 percent of the industry's operating profits, according to Walkley.
20He estimates other companies operated their smartphone business at a loss or broke even.
21Apple sold about 18.2 percent of all the smartphones worldwide in the first quarter of 2015, while Samsung had 24.5 percent of the market, according to IDC.
22But Samsung has said it expects a drop in profit for the April-June quarter, apparently because sales of its new Galaxy S6 models fell short of expectations.
23HTC has also lowered its revenue forecast for the quarter, as it blamed weak sales in China and lower demand for high-end Android phones.
24Microsoft, meanwhile, is cutting 7,600 jobs and writing down the value of its Nokia phone division by $7.6 billion, essentially acknowledging that its effort to build a business selling Windows smartphones had failed.
25Microsoft also reports earnings Tuesday.
26Although each company has its own issues, experts say smartphone sales are slowing in many developed countries because most people already own one.
27IDC is forecasting an 11 percent increase in global smartphone sales this year, but that's down from 28 percent in 2014.
28Phone makers using Google's Android operating system will see even slower growth of about 8.5 percent.
29But IDC predicts iPhone sales will grow by 23 percent this year.
30Most experts say iPhone sales will get another bump when Apple releases new models this fall.
31Analysts say a majority of iPhone owners are still using older models, such as the iPhone 4 or 5.
32As a result, they say, those users are likely to buy new iPhones when their two-year wireless contracts expire.
33The picture isn't quite as rosy for some other Apple products.
34Sales of the once-vaunted iPad tablet computer have been declining for several quarters.
35And while the company says demand for the Apple Watch is strong, analysts have produced estimates that range from bullish to lackluster.
36Though Apple has often trumpeted sales results for new products, Cook has said he doesn't want to reveal Apple Watch figures for competitive reasons.
37Instead, Apple will lump revenue from the watch into its "Other Products" category, which includes the iPod media player, Apple TV streaming device and Beats headphones.
