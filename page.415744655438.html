Which way will One Direction go after Malik's departure?

1LOS ANGELES (AP) - David Lee Roth and Van Halen.
2Noel Gallagher and Oasis.
3And now Zayn Malik and One Direction.
4Malik said Wednesday he is leaving the chart-topping British boy band "to be a normal 22-year-old who is able to relax and have some private time out of the spotlight."
5His name immediately became a top trending topic online - #AlwaysInOurHeartsZaynMalik - as the band announced it would continue its world tour, "On the Road Again," without Malik.
6Plans also remained in place for the now-foursome to record an album later this year.
7"For kids now, it's truly the equivalent of if one of the Beatles would have left at the height of their success," said Rolling Stone Senior Writer Brian Hiatt of Malik's departure.
8But while it may be sad news for Malik's fans, his departure isn't necessarily bad for the band - at least not immediately.
9The remaining four members have concert dates scheduled through October and they promise a future for the group.
10Unlike Van Halen, which started over when it replaced Roth with a new lead singer (Sammy Hagar); or Oasis, which essentially disbanded when Gallagher left, One Direction is all vocalists, making it easier to absorb a loss.
11The band's only choice now is to continue with four, Hiatt said: "I can't imagine, under any circumstances, them replacing him."
12One Direction formed in 2010 after five teenagers - Harry Styles, Liam Payne, Niall Horan, Louis Tomlinson and Malik - auditioned individually for the British TV talent show "The X Factor."
13Simon Cowell had the idea of putting them together as a boy band.
14They didn't win the competition, but they went on to score hits and win young hearts around the world with their perky pop and cheeky personalities.
15Each member has his own following, with Styles and Malik arguably the most popular.
16Cowell said in a statement Wednesday that One Direction fans "can rest assured that Niall, Liam, Harry and Louis are hugely excited about the future of the band."
17Another British boy band faced a similar challenge in the 1990s, with unsettled results.
18Take That started as a singing, dancing group of five.
19When one of its members left, the band finished its world tour, released a single, then broke up.
20It reunited as a foursome almost a decade later and found new success.
21The fifth member briefly returned, then left again, followed by another member's departure.
22The group now records and tours as a trio and just released a single this month.
23It's too soon to say which way One Direction, or Malik, might go, but making a clean split was a good first step, writes Jason Lipshutz, an associate editor at Billboard.
24"Zayn's exit from One Direction is painful because it means something has ended, but it would have been vastly more painful to approach the next few months with a sense of unsteadiness," he says.
25"Now, Zayn is gone, and One Direction can move on."
