Timeline of unrest in Tunisia since the popular revolt

1An Australian was among the 17 tourists gunned down at the Bardo National Museum in Tunis.
2The attack prompted Tunisian president Beji Caid Essebsi to declare a merciless "war against terrorism".
3The country that gave birth to the Arab Spring has seen a sharp rise in Islamist militancy which has left 60 police and military personnel dead.
4December 17: A young university graduate who has only been able to find work as a fruit seller burns himself to death to protest against police harassment and unemployment in the central town of Sidi Bouzid.
5His act, from which he dies on January 4, unleashes bloody rioting against unemployment and the cost of living, which spreads across the country and takes on a political dimension.
6Some 338 are killed in the unrest.
7January 14: Thousands of demonstrators gather in Tunis and in the regions calling on president Ben Ali to step down.
8He flees to Saudi Arabia, after 23 years in power, becoming the first Arab leader to quit under popular pressure.
9February 25: Police stations are attacked as anti-government demonstrations force Ben Ali's last prime minister, Mohamed Ghannouchi, to resign.
10He is replaced by veteran politician Beji Caid Essebsi.
11October 27-28: Violence erupts in Sidi Bouzid after results of Tunisia's first free election are announced, in which the Islamist Ennahda party wins most seats in a constituent assembly.
12Photo: A Tunisian couple show their ink stained fingers after voting in the country's elections on October 23, 2011.
13June 11-12: Unrest is triggered by an art exhibition of work deemed offensive to Islam.
14The government blames hardline Salafists and old regime loyalists.
15September 14: Four attackers are killed in clashes at the US embassy amid protests over an anti-Islam film.
16November 27- December 1: 300 people are hurt in clashes between police and protesters in Siliana, south-west of Tunis.
17Photo: A police car burns after it was set on fire by protesters demonstrating against the reopening of a rubbish dump in Guellala, on island of Djerba.
18February 6: Prominent opposition leader Chokri Belaid is shot dead, triggering deadly protests and a political crisis that brings down Islamist prime minister Hamadi Jebali.
19On July 25, opposition leader Mohamed Brahmi is shot dead.
20In December 2014 jihadists claim both killings.
21July 29: Eight soldiers are killed in the Mount Chaambi area near Algeria where Tunisian forces have been hunting an Al Qaeda-linked group since December.
22On August 2, the army announces a major operation against Islamist militants in the area.
23October 30: A suicide bomber blows himself up on a beach in the resort town of Sousse, leaving no victims, while security forces foil another planned attack nearby.
24Photo: Mr Chokri Belaid, a senior leader in Tunisia's left-leaning opposition Democratic Patriots party, was shot dead on February 6, 2013.
25January 26: Tunisia's lawmakers adopt a new constitution after two years of political turmoil which exposed a deep rift between Ennahda and the secular opposition.
26A government of independents, led by Mehdi Jomaa and tasked with steering Tunisia to fresh elections, is sworn in, replacing the Islamist-led administration.
27February 4: The suspected Islamist assassin of Belaid is killed in a police raid, one of seven heavily armed terrorists slain in an operation launched at a house in a Tunis suburb.
28June 13: Al Qaeda in the Islamic Maghreb says it is responsible for an attack on the interior minister's home that killed four policemen, the first such claim in the country.
29July 16: Suspected jihadists kill 15 soldiers in the Mount Chaambi region, in the deadliest such attack in the army's history.
30October 24: Police kill six suspected militants, five of them women, in a raid on a suburban house after a 28-hour standoff, fanning tensions ahead of parliamentary polls.
31December 21: Essebsi wins Tunisia's first free presidential election.
32Photo: Beji Caid Essebsi (centre), leader of Tunisia's secular Nidaa Tounes party and a presidential candidate, gestures after casting his vote at a polling station in Tunis.
33March 18: Seventeen tourists from Poland, Italy, Germany and Spain are among 22 people killed as gunmen attack a Tunis museum, according to the country's prime minister.
