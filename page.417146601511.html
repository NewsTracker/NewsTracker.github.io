Toronto Maple Leafs hire Mike Babcock as new head coach (Yahoo Sports)

1Mike Babcock moved from one Original Six franchise to another Wednesday, agreeing to an eight-year, $50 million deal to coach the Toronto Maple Leafs.
2The Maple Leafs announced the hiring to put to an end months of speculation about Babcock's future with the Detroit Red Wings, where he spent the past 10 seasons and won a Stanley Cup in 2008.
3Babcock will be introduced at a news conference Thursday.
4Though Babcock was under contract with Detroit through June 30, the Red Wings granted him permission to speak to other teams on May 8.
5The deal makes Babcock the NHL's highest-paid coach.
6He will reportedly receive a large signing bonus and make between $5 million and $6 million a season.
7By hiring Babcock, the Leafs must send a third-round pick to Detroit as compensation.
8Landing Babcock is a major coup for Brendan Shanahan, who in the past 13 months since taking over as president has fired general manager Dave Nonis, coach Randy Carlyle, interim Peter Horachek and several assistants and scouts.
9''I'm proud of Shanny, I'm proud that he dreamt big,'' Maple Leaf Sports & Entertainment President and CEO Tim Leiweke said.
10''He got the big whale.
11''It should give everyone great hope about the future of this organization.
12Detroit general manager Ken Holland said Babcock told him of his decision Wednesday morning.
13''My offers last June were a four-year term.
14Again in January, it was a four-year term,'' Holland said.
15''As we sat yesterday morning, I said, 'Mike, the best I can do is five years.' When you've been in the same city as long as I have, and as long as Mike has, you don't get much longer term than four and five years.
16In selecting Toronto, Babcock goes from a Red Wings franchise that has reached the playoffs for an NHL-leading 24 consecutive seasons to a team that has struggled competitively.
17Toronto missed the playoffs last season for the ninth time in 10 years.
18The Maple Leafs have also not won a Stanley Cup since 1967, a season before the NHL expanded from its original six franchises.
19The Red Wings, by comparison, have won the championship four times since 1997.
20The 52-year-old Babcock also coached Canada to Olympic gold medals in 2010 and '14.
21He brings stability and a high-profile face to a franchise that is on its fourth full-time coach since Paul Maurice took over in 2006.
22The Maple Leafs had a vacancy after Carlyle was fired in January, and replaced on an interim basis by assistant Horachek.
23Babcock's decision comes as a slight surprise, given that he indicated last week that he hated losing and wanted to be part of a team that had an opportunity to win immediately.
24The Maple Leafs have an over-priced and aging roster, and questions at goaltending.
25The team unraveled last season.
26After holding a 19-9-3 record in mid-December, Toronto closed the season going 11-35-8 to finish 15th in the 16-team Eastern Conference standings.
27On the bright side, Babcock is reunited with Shanahan, who took over as the Maple Leafs president last year.
28Shanahan played for Babcock in Detroit.
29Babcock earned $2 million per season in his previous four-year contract and the corporately owned Maple Leafs are one of the NHL's most profitable franchises.
30They were last valued by Forbes as being worth an NHL-high $1.3 billion.
31Babcock had not ruled out staying in Detroit, and also interviewed with the Buffalo Sabres.
32The Sabres had held contract negotiations with Babcock as recently as Tuesday, but were informed they were out of the running on Wednesday, two people familiar with the discussions told The Associated Press.
33The two people spoke on the condition of anonymity because the Sabres have not publicly discussed their coaching search to replace Ted Nolan, who was fired a day after the end of the season.
34The Sabres must now turn to Plan B. Potential candidates include former NHL coaches Dan Bylsma and Paul MacLean, and minor-league coach Luke Richardson.
35The Red Wings, who were eliminated by Tampa Bay in Game 7 of their first-round playoff series last month, have an obvious coaching candidate in their system.
36Jeff Blashill is coach of Detroit's minor-league affiliate in Grand Rapids and was honored as the American Hockey League's outstanding coach in 2013-14.
37Holland said he wants to spend some time with Blashill shortly, although he doesn't want to disrupt Grand Rapids' preparation in the AHL playoffs.
38Babcock began his NHL head coaching career with a flourish in 2002-03, when he took Anaheim to within one win of a Stanley Cup title.
39He began coaching the Red Wings after the lockout that wiped out the 2004-05 season.
40Babcock was the first coach to win at least 50 games in each of his first four seasons with an NHL team - a deceiving stat in the shootout era but an impressive one nonetheless - when he did it with the Red Wings.
41With 458 wins for Detroit, he ranks No.
421 on the franchise's career list, ahead of luminaries like Scotty Bowman and Jack Adams.
43AP Sports Writer Noah Trister in Detroit contributed to this report.
