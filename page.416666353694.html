7 killed among violence in Jalisco, Mexico

1GUADALAJARA, Mexico (AP) - Flames and gunfire erupted around the western Mexican state of Jalisco on Friday as a military operation targeting a violent drug cartel was launched at the start of a three-day holiday weekend.
2Suspected cartel members stopped buses and trucks across key highways in the state capital of Guadalajara and other cities, snarling traffic on a day Mexicans took to the road in droves.
3Gunmen fired on a military helicopter, killing three soldiers and forcing it to make an emergency landing about 150 miles (250 kilometers) southwest of Guadalajara.
4Twelve others - 10 soldiers and two federal police officers - were also injured and three soldiers remained missing, according to a statement from Mexico's defense ministry.
5The shell of a torched passenger bus is parked on the side of a road after it was extinguished by firefighters in Guadalajara, Mexico, Friday, May 1, 2015.
6Authorities in western Mexico are asking residents to stay at home as they scramble to extinguish burning vehicles blocking roads in various parts of Guadalajara.
7Such blockades are a common cartel response to the arrest of important members or are used to foil police and military operations.
8Jalisco Gov. Aristoteles Sandoval said in an afternoon news conference that the violence was a reaction to the federal government's Operation Jalisco, "an operation to get to the bottom of and to be able to arrest all the leaders of this cartel, of this organization."
9Sandoval did not name the cartel, but authorities have been locked in an increasingly bloody battle with the Jalisco New Generation cartel.
10Last month, cartel gunmen killed 15 state police officers in an ambush.
11It was the bloodiest single attack on Mexican authorities in recent memory.
12One week earlier, cartel gunmen attempted to assassinate the state security commissioner and on March 19, they killed five federal police officers.
13The security commissioner had said the attacks were revenge for state forces killing a cartel leader.
14On Friday, Sandoval said Operation Jalisco, which had been announced by federal authorities earlier in the day, had the goal of arresting all members of the cartel.
15Sandoval said there had been a total of seven deaths Friday, but did not specify the circumstances or the victims.
16He counted 29 blockades around the state affecting 19 municipalities.
17He said 11 banks and five gas stations had been attacked and the state remained under a "code red" protocol.
18Videos circulating on social media showed unidentified men lighting businesses on fire in broad daylight.
19Fifteen people had been arrested and there were four armed confrontations, he said.
20Jalisco state spokesman Gonzalo Sanchez told Milenio TV that a police officer was killed in one of the clashes around the state in the community of Autlan, about 120 miles southwest of Guadalajara.
21President Enrique Pena Nieto said through his Twitter account that he lamented the soldiers' deaths in the course of their work in Jalisco.
22The defense department said the helicopter that took fire was participating in Operation Jalisco between Villa Purificacion and Casimiro Castillo.
23The state prosecutor's office used its Twitter account to ask residents of Guadalajara to remain calm Friday morning as authorities responded to the blockades.
24It said authorities were coordinating to extinguish fires and regain calm in other parts of the state's interior.
