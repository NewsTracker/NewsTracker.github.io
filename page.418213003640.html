US requests extradition of FIFA officials

1The United States has sent Switzerland extradition requests for seven soccer officials arrested in May on suspicion of racketeering in a corruption probe, the Swiss Federal Office of Justice (FOJ) has said.
2The officials were detained in a dawn raid on 27 May on a luxury Zurich hotel two days before FIFA's annual congress, throwing soccer's governing body into turmoil.
3They have since been jailed around the Zurich region.
4Zurich's local police will now give the seven officials a hearing on the extradition requests, the FOJ said in a statement.
5The FOJ will then grant the officials or their lawyers 14 days to respond to the request.
6This may be extended by a further 14 days.
7A ruling was due within weeks, it added.
8A statement from the Federal Office of Justice said: "The USA has asked Switzerland to extradite the seven FIFA officials arrested in Zurich. The formal extradition requests were submitted to the Federal Office of Justice (FOJ) yesterday evening.
9"The seven FIFA officials were arrested in Zurich on 27 May 2015 on the basis of a request from the USA, and detained pending extradition.
10"On 1st July 2015, the US embassy in Bern submitted the formal extradition requests within the deadline laid down in the bilateral extradition treaty.
11"The requests are based on the arrest warrants issued on 20 May 2015 by the United States Attorney's Office for the Eastern District of New York, which is investigating the high-ranking FIFA officers on suspicion of taking bribes worth over 100 million dollars."
12It is alleged that in return for the money, the officials awarded contracts for media, marketing and sponsorship rights to football tournaments in the United States and Latin America.
13The payments are said to have been routed through American banks.
14In total, the US Department of Justice has indicted 18 people as part of its investigation.
15The Swiss authorities are leading a separate investigation into the awarding of the 2018 and 2022 World Cups.
16Days after the arrests, Sepp Blatter was elected to a fifth term as FIFA president, but less than a week later he announced he would lay down his mandate at an extraordinary congress which is expected to take place early next year.
17Blatter is reportedly under investigation himself although that has not been confirmed.
18The seven FIFA officials facing extradition will now be given a hearing by the Swiss authorities regarding their case, after which they will have 14 days to prepare a response.
19They can request an additional 14 days "if sufficient grounds exist", and may ultimately appeal to both the Swiss Federal Criminal Court and the Federal Supreme Court if they so choose.
