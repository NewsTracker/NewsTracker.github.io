2Billie Holiday had one of the most distinctive voices of all time.
3Her intensely personal style, on songs like Strange Fruit, Lover Man and Don't Explain, inspired everyone from Janis Joplin to Joni Mitchell.
4One of the first singers to employ the subtleties of jazz phrasing in a serious manner, she danced around the beat and the melody, often adding harmonies inspired by her favourite horn players Louis Armstrong and Lester Young.
5But she is also remembered for her turbulent personal life - born to teenage parents who couldn't cope, jailed for prostitution in her own teenage years, and dying, aged 44, while under arrest in her hospital bed for possession of narcotics.
6Dozens of events are planned to celebrate the centenary of her birth this year.
7Among those paying tribute is Rebecca Ferguson, whose third album is a collection of Billie Holiday covers.
8It was recorded after a turbulent year, which began with the pop star collapsing on live television.
9She later found out she was pregnant - at which point the father of her unborn child walked away.
10All the pain was channelled into recording Lady Sings the Blues, which she made at the iconic Capitol Studios in Hollywood.
11She tells the BBC that revisiting Holiday's back catalogue taught her discipline and humility.
12How much of Billie Holiday's story did you know before you made this record?
13Actually, I'd done a project on her at college.
14I remember sitting in the library having to read through book after book.
15And I had to get her death certificate and other pieces of information.
16A lot of her life frustrated me and upset me, to be honest.
17I remember looking at a picture and seeing all these terrible men round her, and I was thinking, "What are you doing? How do you not know you're Billie Holiday?"
18But this project has made me delve into her story even more - and I've come out respecting her as a woman.
19I've listened to her interviews and she was actually really strong.
20As well as having addictions and making a lot of bad decisions, she was a fighter.
21She got up on that stage and she'd sing, no matter what.
22She encountered a lot of prejudice in her early days.
23She was the only black person in those clubs sometimes.
24The band would all be able to have drinks at the bar, and she'd just be hiding at the back.
25That's so sad because, as a singer, you already feel vulnerable.
26But she was a tough cookie.
27When Bob Dylan released his album of Frank Sinatra standards last month, he said he had to ignore the originals to be able to inhabit the songs himself.
28Do you agree?
29Absolutely - you can't think about the originals.
30I said to them, "Play me it twice and that's it."
31I had to go in and interpret it in my own way.
32If you over-listen to the song, you'll just sound like a silly mimic.
33But some of her lyrics were quite dark.
34In My Man, she sings: "I love him. I don't know why I should. He isn't good, he isn't true. He beats me, too."
35But what I loved with Billie Holiday is she had a good way of parlaying the sorrow with a positive musical twist.
36Discipline.
37Working with people who played with Frank Sinatra, Sammy Davis Jr, Tony Some of them are 80 years of age and they just knew what they were doing.
38Was it a quick album to record?
39I imagine the arrangements were done and you came in and put your vocals on top.
40No, it's hard work.
41It's harder than writing your own music, because they're classics and you're conscious of the fact that you've got to draw out your own emotional connection to the song.
42The band had to play around me, but I was lucky in that respect.
43The musicians were that good that I didn't have anything to argue about.
44They'd sit and play and then they'd ask me, "What did you think of that, Bex?"
45And I'd always go, "It sounds amazing."
46One of them actually said to me, "You're a cool cat, Rebecca, you're really chilled."
47But that's been their field of work for 50 years.
48I'm not going to come in and tell them to do it differently.
49How intimidating was it to sing in front of those people?
50I was just sitting there like a big kid!
51I kept trying to get stories out of them all.
52One told me a story about Sammy Davis Jr and a time all the lights went off while he was performing.
53They had just one spotlight and he had to entertain the audience for an hour - and all he did was tap dance.
54How's your tap dancing?
55Oh, me tap dancing's terrible!
56I'd just walk off.
57I'd be like, "Send them all home!"
58What's the biggest disaster you've ever had on stage?
59My first ever date on my first tour, the sound completely cut out.
60So I had to go on and just shout loudly to the audience.
61Around this time last year, you passed out while performing on Loose Women.
62That must have been pretty awful, too.
63That was the pregnancy.
64I didn't know [I was pregnant] at the time - and that was what made me go and get a test.
65My body was doing all kinds of crazy things.
66I had quite a hard pregnancy.
67I was vomiting constantly, it was very draining.
68Luckily she [third child Arabella] is absolutely fabulous and worth it all.
69You've said quite openly that 2014 was "a year of loss, a year of sadness and drama".
70Did that all feed into the recording?
71It definitely did.
72Certain songs, like Embraceable You, I sung that to my baby.
73It was a very bad year.
74I think at one point when I'm older I might open up a bit more about that, but out of respect for my daughter and my children I don't want to talk about it.
75But it was very hard.
76You recorded the album during your pregnancy.
77Did that change your voice at all?
78Do you know what?
79I found I sang better.
80It makes no sense because it's supposed to affect it.
