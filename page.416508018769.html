Islamic State takes military barracks, dam in Iraq's Anbar -sources

1BAGHDAD, April 25 (Reuters) - Islamic State militants in Iraq took partial control of a water dam and military barracks guarding it in the western Anbar province after fierce fighting through the night that continued on Saturday, security sources and witnesses said.
2The Iraqi government announced a new offensive this month to recapture parts of Anbar, Iraq's Sunni Muslim heartland, from Islamic State, in an attempt to build on an earlier victory against the group in the central city of Tikrit.
3But the hardline Sunni militants struck back by attacking Ramadi, the provincial capital of Anbar, prompting thousands of families to flee.
4They also hit Baiji, Iraq's largest refinery last week.
5Late on Friday, the insurgents attacked the security perimeter at al-Thirthar dam with explosive-laden vehicles and then battled army forces in clashes that continued on Saturday, police and army sources told Reuters.
6Dozens of Iraqi troops were killed in the fighting, but poor communications in the area made it difficult to confirm a precise figure, said Athal al-Fahdawi, an Anbar provincial council member.
7Army sources said two senior officers were among the dead.
8Officials from Iraq's defence and interior ministries could not immediately be reached for comment.
9Security forces and Shi'ite paramilitaries have regained some ground in Iraq since Islamic State proclaimed a caliphate straddling the border of Syria and Iraq, but core Sunni territories such as Anbar remain under Islamic State control.
10A video posted online on Saturday by Islamic State's official media organisation appeared to show its fighters moving about freely at al-Thirthar dam, which serves as a flood control for Baghdad and other cities.
11A black flag commonly used by the militants is seen flying from a radio tower.
12Two bodies apparently belonging to Iraqi forces also appear in the footage.
