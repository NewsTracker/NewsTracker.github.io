Apple launches new iPod Touch

1This composite product image provided by Apple shows varieties of the new iPod Touch, available for sale on Wednesday, July 15, 2015.
2NEW YORK -- Apple is updating its iPod Touch music player for the first time in nearly three years, as the company seeks to make music a central part of its devices once again.
3The new Touch has a faster processor and better cameras.
4It also has a software update that enables Apple Music, a US$10-a-month service that offers unlimited playback of millions of songs.
5Apple Music launched June 30 as music fans increasingly embrace subscriptions over pay-per-song services such as Apple's industry-leading iTunes.
6The move comes as iPod's popularity has waned.
7The iPod was Apple's first successful foray beyond personal computers, and it paved the way for Apple to release the iPhone six years later, in 2007.
8Although music players existed before, the iPod was the first to simplify syncing with digital music collections on personal computers.
9In recent years, though, many people use smartphones instead to listen to music.
10The last time Apple gave figures for iPod sales, for the July-September quarter last year, Apple sold 2.6 million iPods, a 24 per cent decline from the previous year and a fraction of the 39 million iPhones sold that quarter.
11As demand has slowed, so has Apple's updates to the iPod: Apple no longer updates its iPods annually like it usually does with its top sellers.
12But the latest move shows Apple isn't giving up on the iPod.
13The Touch is essentially an iPhone without cellular capabilities, as it runs the same iOS operating system and most of the same apps.
14It extends Apple Music and iPhone apps to teens and other younger customers who might not need cellphones or cannot afford cellular service.
15Beyond music, the iPod Touch might get new customers hooked on Apple's apps, video and other content -- such that they'll turn to an iPhone, iPad or Mac when they are ready to buy a device that does more.
16Prices start at $199, the same as an iPhone 6 with a two-year service contract, but the iPod Touch requires no monthly service fees for voice, text and data.
17Calling is possible through FaceTime video conferencing over Wi-Fi.
18-- The processor now matches that in the iPhone 6 and 6 Plus from last fall, rather than one from the iPhone 4s in 2011.
19Apple says the new chip is six times faster for general tasks and 10 times faster for graphics.
20-- The rear camera can take sharper pictures, at 8 megapixels.
21That matches recent iPhones and exceeds the 5 megapixels in the previous Touch.
22The front and rear cameras can now take 10 shots per second and slow-motion video at 120 frames per second.
23"Customers can experience next-level gameplay, take even more beautiful photos and enjoy their favourite music, TV shows and movies," said Greg Joswiak, Apple's vice-president of iPhone, iPod and iOS product marketing.
24The new Touch won't have all of the iPhone's sensors.
25There's no barometer to measure steps climbed and no fingerprint ID to unlock the device.
26There's also no Apple Pay, the technology for making payments at retail stores.
27The screen remains at 4 inches, as measured diagonally, even though iPhones have gotten bigger at 4.7 and 5.5 inches.
28The Touch ships with iOS 8.4, which includes a new app for Apple Music.
29Although people can still buy songs individually through iTunes, the subscription offers most of Apple's music library at a set price.
30Users lose access, though, once they cancel.
31Owners of older Touch models can manually upgrade the operating system for Apple Music.
32The colour choices are silver, grey and gold, matching Apple's other products, plus pink, blue and red.
33Apple is introducing a new model with 128 gigabytes for $399.
34The base model has 16 gigabytes, enough for thousands of songs, though the iPod Touch is meant to hold photos, video and apps, too.
35The $49 iPod Shuffle and the $149 Nano are getting the same colours, but what's inside isn't changing.
36Neither model can run Apple Music or the various iPhone apps.
