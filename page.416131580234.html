Walker says he expects to meet with Netanyahu in Israel

1(AP) - Wisconsin Gov. Scott Walker "absolutely" expects to meet with Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu during his first visit to Israel next month, the Republican said as he prepared to depart for a western European trade mission.
2The likely presidential candidate told The Associated Press in an interview Thursday that the Netanyahu meeting in mid-May is not yet confirmed, but that it's his "hope and expectation" it will happen.
3A spokeswoman for Walker's political group, Our American Revival, did not immediately confirm Friday whether the meeting has been set up.
4Walker met with British Prime Minister David Cameron during a trade mission to London in February.
5Walker departs Friday for visits to Germany, France and Spain.
6Walker originally planned for his Israel trip to be a taxpayer-funded trade mission, but he determined that was impossible because the "overwhelming majority" of those who wanted to meet with him in Israel had a political interest.
7His political arm is footing the bill.
8Walker said his goal is to meet with Netanyahu and other members of the Israeli government to get an understanding of the issues facing the Middle East.
9Walker has recently hired foreign policy experts to help bolster his credentials, and the Israel trip will be his third trip overseas in four months as he prepares for a likely White House bid.
