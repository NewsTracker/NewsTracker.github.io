Petraeus OKs guilty plea in data leak to mistress

1David Petraeus, the former CIA director and top Army general whose affair with his biographer brought down what many considered a bright political future, has agreed to plead guilty to mishandling classified materials.
2The plea agreement was filed Tuesday in U.S. District Court in Charlotte, North Carolina.
3Petraeus was charged with one count of unauthorized removal and retention of classified material, which carries a possible penalty of up to a year in prison, $100,000 fine and five years of probation.
4Since 2013, Petraeus has held a senior position at Kohlberg Kravis Roberts.
5"We look forward to working with him as he continues to add value as Chairman of the KKR Global Institute," the private equity firm said in a statement Tuesday.
6The case was filed in Charlotte, the hometown of Paula Broadwell, the general's biographer and former mistress.
7According to court documents, Petraeus gave Broadwell binders of classified material from Afghanistan while she was writing her book.
8The binders were known as "black books" and were seized by the FBI in a search of Petraeus home, according to court documents.
9He also lied about providing them to Broadwell, according to court documents.
10There were eight binders in all, each containing classified information about Petraeus' time as the top general in Afghanistan.
11The material included his daily schedule and notes he took during official briefings.
12The books were top secret and also included notes about Petraeus' discussions with the president.
13Petraeus retained the black books in his home even after he left the Defense Department.
14On Aug. 28, 2011, he delivered them to a private residence in Washington where Broadwell was staying.
15Petraeus left the books with her so she could use them as source material for the biography on Petraeus that she wrote, which was released in 2012.
16On Sept.
171, Petraeus brought the books back to his home in Arlington, Virginia.
18When Petraeus resigned from the CIA, he signed a security exit form indicating he had no classified material in his possession.
19However, he still had the black books in his home at that time.
20On April 5, 2013, the FBI searched his home and seized the black books from an unlocked desk drawer in a first-floor study.
21When interviewed by the FBI in 2012, Petraeus said he never provided classified information to his biographer.
22Prosecutors say that was false, and that Petraeus lied to federal investigators.
23Petraeus' lawyers David Kendall and Robert Barnett in Washington declined to comment.
24CORRECTION: This updates an earlier version by Reuters to correct that Petraeus has agreed to plead guilty but has not actually done so.
