American who contracted Ebola arrives at US hospital

1BETHESDA, Maryland (AP) " An American healthcare worker who contracted Ebola while volunteering in a Sierra Leone treatment unit arrived safely at the National Institutes of Health's hospital in Maryland, officials said Friday.
2Doctors evaluated the patient, and the person is in serious condition after being flown in isolation from Sierra Leone on a chartered plane and admitted at 4:44 a.m., NIH officials said in a statement.
3The patient's name, age and gender were not released.
4The patient is the 11th person with Ebola to be treated in the U.S. and the second admitted to the NIH Clinical Center.
5It has one of the few specialized isolation units nationwide set up to treat Ebola patients.
6The center's Special Clinical Studies Unit is staffed by specialists in infectious disease and critical care and is designed to prevent the spread of highly contagious viruses, including Ebola.
7Previously, an American nurse was treated there after she contracted Ebola while caring for a patient in a Dallas hospital.
8The nurse, Nina Pham, survived and is Ebola-free.
9The World Health Organization estimated Thursday that the virus has killed more than 10,000 people, mostly in the West African nations of Guinea, Liberia and Sierra Leone.
10The current outbreak is the largest ever for the disease.
11While deaths have slowed dramatically in recent months, the virus appears stubbornly entrenched in parts of Guinea and Sierra Leone.
