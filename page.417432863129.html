Islamic State threatens to seize Syrian border town from other rebels

1Beirut: Islamic State militants are reported to be closing in on a strategic border town in north-western Syria's Aleppo province, near the Turkish frontier, after routing rival rebel groups.
2The attack appeared to signal another territorial gain for the breakaway al-Qaeda faction, which has recently seized the central Syrian city of Palmyra and the western Iraqi city of Ramadi.
3Pro-opposition activists hostile to IS said on Sunday that the militants in their latest thrust had overrun the village of Soran, east of the Syrian border town of Azaz, and nearby areas.
4IS forces were widely expected to assault Azaz, which has been in the hands of insurgents hostile to Islamic State for more than a year.
5The town is a strategic prize because of its proximity to the giant Bab al-Salam border crossing into Turkey, a major transit point for goods and supplies entering northern Syria.
6Turkey, which shares a more than 800-kilometre border with Syria, is a vital logistics and resupply hub for a broad range of armed Syrian opposition groups.
7Since Syria's conflict began in 2011, rebel factions in control of border posts have been positioned both to supply their forces expeditiously and to collect fees from truckers and others entering Syria.
8Fierce resistance from Kurdish-led forces aided by US bombing early this year helped thwart Islamic State's bid to seize the Syrian border town of Kobane, about 140 kilometres north-east of Azaz.
9Kurdish and allied forces are also reported to be fighting to evict IS from the crucial Tal Abyad border crossing, about 65 kilometres south-east of Kobane.
10On Sunday, the IS advance toward Azaz triggered widespread alarm among rival rebel factions on social media.
11The Aleppo Revolutionaries Union, a pro-opposition body hostile to Islamic State, issued a statement calling on all its allies to confront the militant group after "its vicious attack".
12The rebels arrayed against Islamic State in north-western Syria include Islamist and Western-backed factions.
13Prominent among them are the al-Qaeda-linked Nusra Front and Ahrar al-Sham - both Islamist groups that are fierce rivals of IS.
14Many predicted that the IS thrust would push back opposition plans for a renewed offensive against government-held areas in the city of Aleppo, which has long been divided between opposition and loyalist forces.
15"All the preparation work for the liberation of Aleppo has now been sabotaged by the attack," an Ahrar al-Sham spokesman who goes by the name Abu Yusuf al-Muhajer said via Skype from Turkey.
16IS appears to be seeking to recapture territory that it lost early last year, when it was expelled from Azaz and much of Aleppo province in fighting.
