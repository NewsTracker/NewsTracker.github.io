Yahoo to live-stream Bills-Jaguars game from London

1Yahoo Inc will broadcast the Oct. 25 National Football League game between the Buffalo Bills and Jacksonville Jaguars in London in the first live-stream of an NFL game.
2The International Series game will be streamed exclusively free of charge on Yahoo sites including Yahoo Sports, Yahoo Screen and Tumblr, Yahoo and the NFL said on Wednesday.
3Yahoo and the NFL did not disclose terms of the deal.
4Technology news site Re/code, however, reported that Yahoo paid at least $20 million for the streaming rights, citing industry executives familiar with the negotiations.
5Yahoo will have exclusive advertising rights to the game, which will also be available in a 30-minute on-demand version, Senior Vice President of product and engineering Adam Cahan said in an interview.
6The on-demand version will include commercials that viewers would not be able to skip.
7Yahoo was hopeful of more such partnerships in the future, Cahan said.
8The company has been trying to fight stalling revenue growth by turning towards video ads as its once-hot Web portal and email service lag those of rivals such as Google Inc.
9It bought automated advertising service BrightRoll for $640 million in November, saying the deal would make its video ad platform the largest in the United States.
10The Oct. 25 game will also be telecast in the Buffalo (WIVB-TV) and Jacksonville (WTEV-TV) markets.
11A decision on whether the NFL will partner with streaming service providers for future games has not been made, spokesman Alex Reithmiller said.
