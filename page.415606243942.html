Bail Set at $25 Million for 'Suge' Knight in Murder Case

1Los Angeles prosecutors are asking a judge to set bail in a murder case against the former rap music mogul Knight at $25 million citing his violent history.
2The motion filed by Deputy District Attorney Cynthia Barnes on Thursday, March 19, 2015, cites 31 incidents in which Knight is accused of threatening others or using violence.
3Former rap music mogul Marion "Suge" Knight collapsed in a courtroom Friday shortly after a judge ordered him held on $25 million bail in a murder case.
4Bailiffs cleared the courtroom, paramedics arrived with a stretcher a few minutes later and an ambulance was seen leaving the courthouse.
5Defense attorney Matthew Fletcher said Knight was unconscious when the lawyer left the courtroom and an update on his condition was not immediately available.
6Fletcher said his client, who is diabetic and has a blood clot, previously told him that he hadn't received any medication since Thursday.
7Knight hit his head on a chair when he fell after the bail hearing, Fletcher said.
8The 6-foot-4-inch tall Knight collapsed while deputies were bringing him back into the courtroom after Fletcher asked a judge to order that Knight be given his medication.
9The attorney said Knight was being kept in solitary confinement in jail without proper access to medication.
10"He's being treated worse than Charles Manson," Fletcher said.
11The collapse marked the fourth time that Knight has been taken by ambulance from a courthouse since he was charged with killing Terry Carter, 55, in early February.
12Los Angeles Superior Court Judge Ronald S. Coen set bail on Friday at $25 million for Knight, who is accused of running down and killing Carter with his truck.
13Coen made his decision after Deputy District Attorney Cynthia Barnes noted that Knight was on bail in a robbery case when he struck Carter and another man in a parking lot in Compton.
14"He escalated his behavior and committed murder," Barnes told the judge.
15Fletcher argued that Knight's bail should be set at $2 million, but Coen said the higher amount was warranted.
16The attorney said before the hearing that his client likely could not post bail if it was set at $25 million.
17Knight, the 49-year-old co-founder of Death Row Records, has pleaded not guilty to murder, attempted murder and hit-and-run charges.
18Barnes filed a motion Thursday accusing Knight of being part of a scheme that has extorted more than $10 million from up-and-coming and established rappers in recent years.
19Fletcher countered that prosecutors should file charges involving those extortion claims if they have enough evidence.
20Fletcher also said phone records and other evidence in the murder case would show that Knight was lured to the location where the men were hit by the truck.
21Knight was attacked when he arrived and hit the two men while trying to flee the scene, the lawyer said.
22Fletcher said Cle "Bone" Sloan acknowledged to sheriff's investigators that he attacked Knight.
23The attorney argued his client wasn't required to remain at the scene and endure the attack, and that Sloan should be charged.
24"If his name wasn't 'Suge' Knight, they wouldn't have filed this case," Fletcher said.
25Knight was a key player in the gangster rap scene that flourished in the 1990s, his label once listing Dr. Dre, Tupac Shakur and Snoop Dogg among its artists.
26Knight lost control of the company after it was forced into bankruptcy.
