Landslide hits 6 villages in Nepal; 15 feared dead

1KATHMANDU, Nepal (AP) - A landslide triggered by heavy rainfall buried six villages in Nepal's mountainous northeast, and at least 15 people sleeping in the homes are believed to have been killed, officials said Thursday.
2The landslide hit during the night in Taplejung district, about 500 kilometers (310 miles) east of the capital, Kathmandu, said government administrator Surendra Bhattarai.
3At least 15 people are feared to have died and several are missing, but details are sketchy because of the remoteness of the area.
4The nearest town is at least five hours' away on foot when the weather is good, but getting to the site was taking much longer on Thursday because of rains and fog that prevented a rescue helicopter from landing, said Home Ministry official Laxmi Dhakal in Kathmandu.
5There are no government offices or police stations in the area.
6Landslides are common in mountainous Nepal during the rainy monsoon season, which began in June and ends in September.
7The Himalayan nation is still recovering from earthquakes in April and May that killed more than 8,700 people and caused massive damage, with many of the roads cut off by landslides.
