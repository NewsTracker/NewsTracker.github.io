Earthquake of 7.5 Magnitude Shakes Nepal, Tremors Felt in India

1A 7.5 magnitude earthquake struck Nepal, centred 77 kilometres north-west of Kathmandu, the US Geological Survey said.
2There are reports of damage in the Himalayan nation, Associated Press reports.
3The temblor was 11.9 kilometres deep and hit at 11:41am local time.
4Tremors were felt at least as far away as India's capital New Delhi.
5The south Asia region has a history of catastrophic earthquakes because the tectonic plate that carries the Indian subcontinent is pushing northward into the main Asian plate.
6About 60 per cent of the India's land area faces moderate to severe seismic hazard, according to its National Disaster Management Authority.
7Iran and Pakistan in April 2013 were hit by a major earthquake measuring 7.8 that was centred on the region straddling their common border.
8The ground shook as far away as Dubai and New Delhi.
9A 6.9-magnitude quake in September 2011 jolted India's eastern Sikkim state, killing more than 80 people.
10The 2005 Kashmir quake killed more than 70,000 in Pakistan.
11A major temblor in western Gujarat state in 2001 killed about 25,000 people and caused more than $US4 billion dollars of damage.
12A 1934 earthquake centred in Nepal, just west of Sikkim, killed more than 16,000 people.
