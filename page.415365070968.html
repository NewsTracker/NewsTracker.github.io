French boy, 13, youngest foreigner to die for Islamic State

1A Twitter picture of French boy Abu Bakr al-Faransi, 13, who is reported to be the youngest foreign fighter to die for Islamic State in Syria.
2London: A 13-year-old French boy has become the youngest known foreigner to die fighting for the so-called Islamic State.
3Abu Bakr al-Faransi ("Abu Bakr the French") was reportedly killed two months ago defending a frontier post against the Syrian army in the region of Homs, Radio France International said.
4His two brothers are also thought to have been killed in Syria.
5The boy, who was pictured on social media brandishing an AK-47 and holding up his index finger in an Islamist salute, was originally from the troubled Strasbourg district of Ampere, Europe 1 reported.
6He arrived in Syria in August 2014 with his parents, his brother Nadir and sisters.
7The family had reportedly travelled there in a camper van to recover the body of Mohamed, Abu Bakr's other brother, who had left in March that year but was dead four months later.
8By October 2014, Nadir, aged in his 20s, had also died.
9The fate of the sisters and parents is unknown.
10Meanwhile, an IS defector has reportedly revealed why the murdered Western hostages - Britons David Haines and Alan Henning and Americans James Foley, Steven Sotloff and Peter Kassig - appeared to show no fear during the recordings of their deaths.
11The junior IS jailer told Sky News that he was told by a superior: "'Say to them 'no problem, only video, we don't kill you, we want from your government [to] stop attacking Syria. We don't have any problem with you; you are only our visitors'.
12"So they don't worry. Always I say to them 'don't worry, doesn't matter, nothing dangerous for you'. But at the end I was sure [they would die]."
