Barcelona slip up opens door for Real Madrid

1Barcelona gave Real Madrid new hope in the Spanish league title race by stumbling to a draw despite spectacular goals from Lionel Messi and Neymar.
2Barcelona squandered a two-goal lead to draw 2-2 at Sevilla, letting Real Madrid pull within two points of the Spanish La Liga leader after easing past Eibar 3-0 with Cristiano Ronaldo scoring his competition-high 38th goal of the season.
3Barcelona couldn't have asked for a better start when Messi curled in a shot for the 14th-minute opener before Neymar left Sevilla goalkeeper Sergio Rico flat-footed with a superb free kick in the 31st.
4But Ever Banega pulled one back in the 38th, and substitute Kevin Gameiro capped a counterattack with six minutes to play to keep Sevilla undefeated at home in 32 straight games.
5In Madrid, Ronaldo's free kick was followed by strikes from Javier Hernandez and Jese Rodriguez.
6But even Ronaldo was upstaged by Celta Vigo's 19-year-old Santi Mina, who found the net four times in a 6-1 demolition of Rayo Vallecano.
7Third-place Atletico Madrid also dropped two points at Malaga in a 2-2 draw, three days before the defending league champions host Madrid in the Champions League quarterfinals.
8England: Arsenal earned its eighth straight Premier League victory as Aaron Ramsey scored in a 1-0 win at Burnley, putting the Gunners four points behind Chelsea - although Jose Mourinho's side still has two games in hand.
9Burnley goalkeeper Tom Heaton conceded his first goal at home since Feb. 28 in the 12th minute after Ramsey sent a powerful strike into the top corner.
10More Europe: In France, Zlatan Ibrahimovic and substitute Edinson Cavani both scored twice as Paris Saint-Germain retained the League Cup with a 4-0 win over 10-man Bastia.
11PSG is chasing an unprecedented quadruple, as Laurent Blanc's side also made it to the French Cup final and leads the French league standings.
12It plays Barcelona in the Champions League quarterfinals.
13... In Germany, Robert Lewandowski scored twice as Bayern Munich moved a step closer to the Bundesliga title with a 3-0 win against Eintracht Frankfurt.
14... In Italy, last-place Parma pulled off the biggest surprise of the season as the crisis-hit club beat runaway leader Juventus 1-0.
15Parma was declared bankrupt last month and its players have not been paid all season.
16MLS: Vincent Nogueira scored in stoppage time and Philadelphia beat New York City FC 2-1 for the host Union's first victory of the season.
17... Bobby Shuttleworth had his third shutout in six games and New England played to a scoreless draw with visiting Columbus.
