Robinson says Ferguson's ready for Raiders

1Sydney Roosters star Blake Ferguson is ready to face his former club Canberra for the first time since they sacked him in 2013.
2Sydney Roosters coach Trent Robinson has challenged born-again Blake Ferguson to back up his two-try haul with another defining performance against Canberra on Sunday.
3Fresh from his match-winning heroics in Monday night's 20-12 victory over Penrith, Ferguson confronts his past when he faces the Raiders for the first time since being sacked by the NRL club in 2013.
4The one-time NSW State of Origin star is certain to attract extra attention at Allianz Stadium but Robinson is confident Ferguson is big enough and mature enough to cope.
5"Now it's time for him to go and prove that," Robinson said on Friday.
6"It's easy to find a fight every week, a reason to do well, but his pressure's internal and he's got to make sure that he performs each week based on his standards.
7"If you're rising and falling based on who you're playing, then you're in trouble.
8"So I'm more than confident that Blake will have a really strong game again."
9Ferguson gets the chance to upstage his ex-teammates just four rounds into his NRL comeback.
10Robinson is hoping the Roosters deliver a similar performance to that when Sonny Bill Williams inspired the tricolours to a 38-0 win over the Bulldogs in the Kiwi superstar's much-hyped first outing against the club that sacked him five years earlier.
11"Whenever it was going to be, there was always going to be a talking point around Blake playing Canberra," Robinson said.
12"I still remember when Sonny played Canterbury, it was a huge talking point and people were focused on Sonny versus Canterbury and Sonny focused on the Roosters versus Canterbury and that's the way to handle it.
13"That's the way that Blake needs to handle it and that's the way that we need to as well.
14Ferguson last week spoke of the love he feels at the Roosters after being handed a career lifeline after a two-year exile following his indecent assault conviction.
15"It's a bit of a scary word for footy players to say, but that's what it is," Robinson said.
16"You want to make people feel comfortable. We've tried to create a home for him and the other players, and that's a by-product of that.
17"It's an important thing to feel."
18Robinson said the Roosters and Ferguson had worked hard on helping the 25-year-old turn his life around and that it was a delight to see the backline talent making progress.
19"The structures are often really easy (to put in place)," he said.
20"We've taken a bit of a different approach. We've tried to help him on a daily basis. How to train and prepare and also off the field on how to think about the way he lives his life.
21"That's the best way. He makes better choices. It's not based on what the club says or does. It's based on what he thinks is right."
