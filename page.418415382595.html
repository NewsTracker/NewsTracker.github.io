Tunisia defends efforts to protect British tourists

1FILE - In this Friday, June 26, 2015 file photo, bodies are covered on a Tunisian beach, in Sousse.
2The British government on Thursday, July 9, 2015 told all U.K. tourists to leave Tunisia because a terrorist attack is "highly likely" and the North African country's government has not done enough to enhance security.
3TUNIS, Tunisia (AP) - Tunisia's prime minister says that his country has done "everything in our power" to protect British and other foreign tourists, as British travelers flock to Tunisian airports under orders from their government to flee amid risks of terror attacks.
4Britain on Thursday told all U.K. tourists to leave Tunisia, saying the North African country hasn't done enough to enhance security.
5British tourists made up most of the 38 victims of a massacre at a Tunisian beach resort last month by an Islamic extremist.
6Tunisian Prime Minister Habib Essid said early Friday that his government would help evacuate British citizens, and that he would speak to British counterpart David Cameron later in the day about repercussions of the decision.
7Essid was speaking to Parliament during a debate on security.
