Exhumation Begins At Suspected Mass Graves In Tikrit

1Iraqi forensic teams have begun excavating 12 suspected mass grave sites thought to hold the bodies of up to 1,700 soldiers killed by Islamic State (IS) militants in the city of Tikrit.
2The exhumations started on April 6, days after government security forces, backed by Shi'ite paramilitaries, drove the militants from the city following a monthlong siege.
3Some of the burial sites are in late dictator Saddam Hussein's presidential compound.
4The mostly Shi'ite soldiers from the former U.S. Army base of Camp Speicher were killed in June 2014 by IS militants as the extremist group swept across northern Iraq.
