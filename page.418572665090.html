Islamic State claims rocket attack on Egypt navy vessel left ablaze after shootout

1Egypt's Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant (Isil) affiliate said on Thursday it fired a rocket at an Egyptian naval vessel in the Mediterranean Sea near the coast of Israel and the Gaza Strip.
2Such incidents at sea are rare, though Egypt is battling an increasingly brazen Islamist insurgency in the peninsula that lies between Israel, the Gaza Strip and the Suez Canal.
3Sinai Province, the most lethal militant group in Egypt, last year pledged loyalty to Isil, which controls large tracts of territory in Syria and Iraq and has a presence in Egypt's neighbour Libya.
4It has recently carried out high profile attacks which prompted the drafting of a sweeping counter-terrorism law.
5On July 1, 100 militants and at least 17 members of the security forces were killed in a single day of clashes and attacks claimed by Sinai Province, authorities said.
6A Reuters witness in Gaza saw a plume of dark grey smoke rising from a boat off the coast.
