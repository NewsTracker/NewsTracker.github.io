Still Got It: Facebook Mobile Ad Revenue Continues to Surge

1Nearly three-quarters of Facebook's advertising revenue now comes from mobile ads, as many more users access the social network on smartphones and other handheld gadgets.
2Facebook, which bought the popular photo-sharing app Instagram and the messaging service WhatsApp, has been coming out with its own stand-alone mobile apps to capture more of the time people spend on phones.
3Besides its Messenger app, though, Facebook home-grown efforts have had limited success.
4On Wednesday, the company introduced Hello, a voice-calling app designed for Android phones.
5In essence, it's meant to replace the voice dialer on your phone.
6But based on Facebook's failure to take over the home screen with its Home app for Android, users may not be too keen on replacing existing smartphone tools with Facebook-branded versions.
7Facebook finance chief David Wehner said mobile "continues to be a key driver" of Facebook's growth.
8He called Creative Labs an experimental platform to launch apps and said Facebook will continue to experiment.
9For now, the money-making focus is on Facebook itself and, slowly, on popular, established apps such as Instagram.
10In the previous quarter, mobile represented 69 percent of total advertising revenue.
11That percentage has grown steadily since Facebook began showing mobile ads in 2012.
12The company reported adjusted earnings of 42 cents per share in the January-March quarter, above the 41 cents per share that analysts polled by FactSet were expecting.
13Revenue increased 46 percent to $3.54 billion, from $2.5 billion a year earlier.
14Analysts had expected $3.56 billion.
15Net income declined as Facebook's expenses grew 83 percent from a year earlier.
16The company earned $512 million, or 18 cents per share, down 20 percent from $642 million, or 25 cents per share.
17Facebook, which is based in Menlo Park, California, had 1.44 billion monthly active users as of March, up 13 percent from a year earlier.
18The number of users who accessed Facebook on mobile devices at least once a month grew 24 percent to 1.25 billion.
