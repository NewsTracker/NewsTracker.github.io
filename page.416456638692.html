Timeline of events after fatal police shooting in Ferguson

1A timeline of key events following the fatal police shooting of an unarmed, black 18-year-old in the St. Louis suburb of Ferguson.
2AUG. 9 - Michael Brown and a companion, both black, are confronted by an officer as they walk back to Brown's home from a convenience store.
3Brown and the officer, who is white, are involved in a scuffle, followed by gunshots.
4Brown dies at the scene, and his body remains in the street for four hours in the summer heat.
5Neighbors later lash out at authorities, saying they mistreated the body.
6AUG. 10 - After a candlelight vigil, people protesting Brown's death smash car windows and carry away armloads of looted goods from stores.
7In the first of several nights of violence, looters are seen making off with bags of food, toilet paper and alcohol.
8Some protesters stand atop police cars and taunt officers.
9AUG. 11 - The FBI opens an investigation into Brown's death, and two men who said they saw the shooting tell reporters that Brown had his hands raised when the officer approached with his weapon and fired repeatedly.
10That night, police in riot gear fire tear gas and rubber bullets to try to disperse a crowd.
11AUG. 12 - Ferguson Police Chief Thomas Jackson cancels plans to release the name of the officer who shot Brown, citing death threats against the police department and City Hall.
12AUG. 14 - The Missouri Highway Patrol takes control of security in Ferguson, relieving St. Louis County and local police of their law-enforcement authority following four days of violence.
13The shift in command comes after images from the protests show many officers equipped with military-style gear, including armored vehicles, body armor and assault rifles.
14In scores of photographs that circulate online, officers are seen pointing their weapons at demonstrators.
15AUG. 15 - Police identify the officer who shot Brown as Darren Wilson, 28.
16They also release a video purporting to show Brown robbing a convenience store of almost $50 worth of cigars shortly before he was killed, a move that further inflames protesters.
17AUG. 16 - Missouri Gov. Jay Nixon declares a state of emergency and imposes a curfew in Ferguson.
18AUG. 17- Attorney General Eric Holder orders a federal medical examiner to perform another autopsy on Brown.
19AUG. 18 - Nixon calls the National Guard to Ferguson to help restore order and lifts the curfew.
20AUG. 19 - Nixon says he will not seek the removal of Ferguson prosecutor Bob McCulloch from the investigation into Brown's death.
21Some black leaders questioned whether the prosecutor's deep family connections to police would affect his ability to be impartial.
22McCulloch's father was a police officer who was killed in the line of duty when McCulloch was a child, and he has many relatives who work in law enforcement.
23AUG. 20 - Holder visits Ferguson to offer assurances about the investigation into Brown's death and to meet with investigators and Brown's family.
24A grand jury begins hearing evidence to determine whether Wilson should be charged.
25AUG. 21 - Nixon orders the National Guard to withdraw from Ferguson.
26SEPT.
2725 - Holder announces his resignation but says he plans to remain in office until his successor is confirmed.
2825 - Ferguson Chief Tom Jackson releases a videotaped apology to Brown's family and attempts to march in solidarity with protesters, a move that backfires when Ferguson officers scuffle with demonstrators and arrest one person moments after Jackson joins the group.
29OCT. 10 - Protesters from across the country descend on the St. Louis region for "Ferguson October," four days of coordinated and spontaneous protests.
30A weekend march and rally in downtown St. Louis draws several thousand participants.
31OCT. 21 - Nixon pledges to create an independent Ferguson Commission to examine race relations, failing schools and other broader social and economic issues in the aftermath of Brown's death.
32NOV. 17 - The Democratic governor declares a state of emergency and activates the National Guard again ahead of a decision from a grand jury.
33He places the Ferguson Police Department in charge of security in Ferguson, with orders to work as a unified command with St. Louis city police and the Missouri Highway Patrol.
34NOV. 18 - Nixon names 16 people to the Ferguson Commission, selecting a diverse group that includes the owner of construction-supply company, two pastors, two attorneys, a university professor, a 20-year-old community activist and a police detective.
35Nine of its members are black.
36Seven are white.
37NOV. 24 - The St. Louis County prosecutor announces that the grand jury has decided not to indict Wilson.
38During ensuing protests, at least a dozen buildings and multiple police cars are burned, officers are hit by rocks and batteries and reports of gunfire force some St. Louis-bound flights to be diverted.
39MARCH 4 - The U.S. Justice Department announces that it will not prosecute Wilson in Brown's death but releases a scathing report that faults the city and its law enforcement for racial bias.
40The report said blacks in Ferguson are disproportionately subject to excessive police force, baseless traffic stops and citations for infractions as petty as walking down the middle of the street.
41MARCH 11 - Ferguson police chief Thomas Jackson resigns, effective March 19.
42He is the sixth employee to resign or be fired in light of the Justice Department's report.
43MARCH 12 - Two St. Louis-area police officers are shot in front of the Ferguson Police Department during a demonstration by protesters.
44MARCH 15 - St. Louis County officials charge 20-year-old man with first-degree assault in shootings of the two St. Louis-area officers.
