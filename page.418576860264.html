Ex-FIFA Executive Jeffrey Webb Extradited to U.S.

3Jeffrey Webb, a former top official at FIFA, has agreed to extradition and is currently in the United States, a person who has been briefed on the investigation confirmed Thursday.
4Mr. Webb is the first of the 14 soccer officials and marketing executives indicted in May to be sent to the United States from abroad.
5The Swiss Justice Department said last week that one of the seven FIFA officials arrested and detained in Switzerland had agreed to be extradited in a simplified procedure, but did not name the official.
6Until his indictment, Mr. Webb was president of Concacaf, the regional confederation representing Central and North America and the Caribbean, since 2012.
7He was also a vice president of FIFA, and he was known for calling for reform within the organization.
8The indictment charged that he asked for bribes in return for the lucrative television deals regarding soccer games.
9It is unclear when Mr. Webb will make his first United States court appearance.
10His lawyer could not immediately be reached for comment.
11The racketeering and corruption case against the 14 officials and executives, brought in May, sent shock waves through the soccer world.
12Though many had complained of corruption within FIFA's ranks, and questioned how it awarded the hosting rights to the World Cup, United States prosecutors bringing a case against so many officials at once startled soccer fans and executives.
13The arrests, several made in the early-morning hours at a Swiss lakeside hotel where soccer officials had gathered for FIFA's annual congress, only added to the drama.
14The case led to the June announcement by Sepp Blatter, FIFA's powerful longtime president, that he would step down.
15Blatter has not been charged.
