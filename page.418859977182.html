Mexico finds cocaine diluted in fruit shipment from Colombia

1MEXICO CITY (AP) " Customs and law enforcement personnel in Mexico say they found an undetermined amount of cocaine dissolved in fruit pulp imported from Colombia.
2The drug was detected among 24 metric tons of red berries packed into 120 fifty-five-gallon drums of frozen fruit pulp.
3The berries were thawed, and tests revealed that 16 of the drums contained cocaine mixed into the fruit.
4Mexico's customs and tax authority said Monday that it was a "sophisticated" and "unprecedented" smuggling attempt.
