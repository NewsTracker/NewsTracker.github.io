Prosecutors launch criminal probe into FIFA?s 2018 and 2022 World Cups just hours after U.S. raid, seven arrests

1ZURICH - Swiss prosecutors opened criminal proceedings into FIFA's awarding of the 2018 and 2022 World Cups, only hours after seven soccer officials were arrested Wednesday pending extradition to the U.S. in a separate probe of "rampant, systemic, and deep-rooted" corruption.
2FIFA, meanwhile, said Friday's presidential election would go ahead as planned with Sepp Blatter going for a fifth term.
3Blatter was not named in either investigation.
4FIFA also ruled out a revote of the World Cups won by Russia in 2018 and Qatar in 2022.
5The Swiss prosecutors' office said in a statement they seized "electronic data and documents" at FIFA's headquarters on Wednesday as part of their probe.
6And Swiss police said they will question 10 FIFA executive committee members who took part in the World Cup votes in December 2010.
7The Swiss investigation against "persons unknown on suspicion of criminal mismanagement and of money laundering" again throws into the doubt the integrity of the voting.
8"FIFA is fully co-operating with the investigation and is supporting the collection of evidence in this regard," FIFA said in a statement..
9The Swiss announcement came only hours after 14 people were indicted in the U.S. for corruption.
10Seven of them were arrested and detained by Swiss police at the request of U.S. authorities after a raid at a luxury hotel in Zurich.
11The U.S. Department of Justice said in a statement that two current FIFA vice-presidents were among those arrested and indicted, Jeffrey Webb of the Cayman Islands and Eugenio Figueredo of Uruguay.
12The others are Eduardo Li of Costa Rica, Julio Rocha of Nicaragua, Costas Takkas of the Cayman Islands, Rafael Esquivel of Venezuela and Jose Maria Marin of Brazil.
13All seven are connected with the regional confederations of North and South America and face up to 20 years in prison if convicted.
14"The indictment alleges corruption that is rampant, systemic, and deep-rooted both abroad and here in the United States," Attorney General Loretta E. Lynch said in the statement.
15"It spans at least two generations of soccer officials who, as alleged, have abused their positions of trust to acquire millions of dollars in bribes and kickbacks."
16Nine of the 14 that were indicted by the Justice Department are soccer officials, while four are sports marketing executives and another works in broadcasting.
17Jack Warner, a former FIFA vice-president from Trinidad and Tobago, was among those indicted.
18The Swiss prosecutors' office said the U.S. probe was separate from its investigation but that authorities were working together.
19The votes to award the World Cups to Russia and Qatar have been surrounded in controversy and accusations of corruption.
20Qatar, a tiny Gulf nation with little soccer tradition, was criticized from the start for its extreme summer heat.
21FIFA has since been forced to move the tournament to November-December instead of the usual June-July time slot.
22FIFA also hired U.S. attorney Michael Garcia to investigate the 2018 and 2022 bid process.
23His findings were never fully released and both Russia and Qatar were confirmed as hosts.
24Garcia's full report was turned over to Swiss authorities in November, prompting Wednesday's raid on FIFA headquarters.
25Russian Sports Minister Vitaly Mutko, who is also a FIFA executive committee member, told The Associated Press "we've got nothing to hide."
26"We're prepared to show everything," Mutko said in a telephone interview.
27"We've always acted within the law."
28Qatari soccer officials declined to comment.
29The U.S. case involves bribes "totalling more than $100 million" linked to commercial deals dating back to the 1990s for soccer tournaments in the United States and Latin America, the Swiss Federal Office of Justice said.
30The Justice Department said the corruption is linked to World Cup qualifying matches and the Copa America - South America's continental championship.
31U.S. prosecutors in New York said they had uncovered a dozen different schemes during its investigation, and some involved the awarding of the 2010 World Cup.
32South Africa, with the backing of Nelson Mandela, beat rival bids from Morocco and Egypt to host the tournament in 2010, four years after narrowly losing out to Germany for the previous tournament.
33Dozens of soccer officials are in Switzerland for the FIFA congress and presidential election, where Blatter is widely expected to win re-election at the helm of the governing body of world soccer.
34Blatter had been scheduled to attend a meeting of the Confederation of African Football in a different downtown Zurich hotel, but he cancelled his appearance.
35He later cancelled his plans to attend a meeting of the South American confederation.
36Blatter's only opponent in Friday's presidential election, Prince Ali bin al-Hussein of Jordan, seized on the situation to push his candidacy.
37"We cannot continue with the crisis in FIFA, a crisis that has been ongoing and is not just relevant to the events of today," Prince Ali said in a statement.
38"FIFA needs leadership that governs, guides and protects our national associations. & Leadership that restores confidence in the hundreds of millions of football fans around the world."
39The arrests were made at the lakeside Baur au Lac Hotel in downtown Zurich, long favoured as a place for senior FIFA officials to stay.
40It was the stage for intense lobbying for votes ahead of the 2018 and 2022 World Cup hosting decisions.
41In Florida, a small group of agents from the FBI and IRS executed search warrants at CONCACAF headquarters in Miami Beach.
42Neither agency offered comment on the investigation.
43The North American regional body, known as CONCACAF, reported itself to U.S. tax authorities in 2012.
44Then based in New York, the organization had not paid taxes over several years when its president was Warner and secretary general was Chuck Blazer of the United States.
45Warner left soccer in 2011 to avoid FIFA sanctions in a bribery scandal during that year's presidential election.
46Blazer left in 2013 and has pleaded guilty to charges, the Justice Department said in Wednesday's statement.
47Warner's successor as CONCACAF leader and FIFA vice-president is Webb, who was staying at the Baur au Lac this week.
48The Swiss Federal Office of Justice said in its statement that U.S. authorities suspect the arrested officials of having received or paid bribes totalling millions of dollars and that the crimes were agreed to and prepared in the U.S., and payments carried out via U.S. banks.
49"The bribery suspects - representatives of sports media and sports promotion firms - are alleged to have been involved in schemes to make payments to the soccer functionaries (FIFA delegates) and other functionaries of FIFA sub-organizations - totalling more than USD 100 million," the FOJ statement said.
