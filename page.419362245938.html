Kenseth in control at Michigan, wins 3rd Cup race of season (Yahoo Sports)

1(AP) -- Matt Kenseth had little to say about how NASCAR's rules package affected his race at Michigan International Speedway.
2''I didn't see much of the race, which was totally fine with me,'' he said.
3Kenseth started in the lead, stayed there for most of the race and finished with his third Sprint Cup victory of the season Sunday, beating Kevin Harvick by 1.7 seconds.
4It was the second race under NASCAR's high-drag aerodynamic package, which was also in place at Indianapolis last month in an effort to improve passing.
5At Michigan, Kenseth won while leading 146 of 200 laps - so whatever excitement there was occurred further back in the pack.
6''Cars could really, at the end of the straightaway, gain on others, two or three car lengths,'' said Jason Ratcliff, Kenseth's crew chief.

10... I'd say eighth place back, it was fun to watch.

1320 Toyota.
14It was his 34th career victory, and Joe Gibbs Racing has won five of the last six Cup races - two by Kenseth and three by Kyle Busch.
15''You really need to enjoy it, because about 10 races back, we were struggling, trying to get there,'' Gibbs said.
16''You just hope now that we'll be able to hold some momentum here and head into the Chase, but it's very hard to do.

19Martin Truex Jr. finished behind Harvick in third.

22NASCAR used a special high-drag aerodynamic package for this race and last month's at Indianapolis Motor Speedway.

24Kenseth led for the first 22 laps, and there were 16 lead changes after that.
