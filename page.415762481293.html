Yahoo ups share buyback by $2 billion

1NEW YORK (AP) -- Yahoo says it will buy back $2 billion in company stock as it prepares to spin off its stake in Chinese e-commerce company Alibaba.
2Investors were pushing Yahoo to use some of the proceeds from the Alibaba spinoff to buy back stock, and the company had said it would keep buying back stock to return cash to shareholders.
3Shares of Yahoo rose 93 cents, or 2 percent, to $45.40 in aftermarket trading.
4The stock repurchase program will expire March 31, 2018.
5Yahoo has $726 million remaining on previous stock buyback plan, which was approved in 2013 and expires at the end of 2016.
6In January, Yahoo Inc. said it will spin off its stake in Alibaba Group Holding Ltd. later this year.
7The move will allow the new entity to pay lower taxes on Alibaba stock sales than Yahoo would have.
8Yahoo owns 384 million shares of Alibaba, and as of Thursday's close its stake is worth about $32 billion.
9Activist investor Starboard Value pushed for the spinoff in 2014.
10More recently it has called for Yahoo to spin off its Japanese business, cut costs, and get cash for its intellectual property and real estate holdings.
11It proposed an even larger stock repurchase of up to $4 billion.
