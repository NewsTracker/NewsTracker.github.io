Your guide to trying on, ordering Apple Watch

1The smartwatch is Apple's first new product category in five years and comes in 54 configurations - more than any other Apple gadget.
2So starting Friday, Apple stores will have staff trained to help you choose.
3Even after the watch starts shipping on April 24, you'll still have to book a try-on visit to buy the watch in stores and wait for it to arrive.
4There's no walking in and walking out with a watch.
5Of course, you can bypass all this by ordering online.
6No smartwatch has gotten as much attention as the Apple Watch, given Apple's devoted fan base and the company's knack for designing products consumers can't live without.
7By insisting on customers ordering ahead of time, Apple can manage inventory at its stores.
8Prices range from $349 to $17,000 depending on the watch case, band and size.
9A standard model with the cheapest band costs $549 for the 38-millimeter model and $599 for the 42-millimeter version, as measured on the watch face from top to bottom.
10Here's what you need to know about buying one.
11If you know the specific configuration you want, you're best off ordering it online, starting at 12:01 a.m. PDT Friday (3:01 a.m. EDT).
12They will start to ship on April 24.
13Popular models might not be available right away if you're not among the first to order.
14Your watch will get shipped to you.
15There won't be an in-store pick-up option.
16Besides the United States, the watch is available to customers in Australia, Canada, China, France, Germany, Hong Kong, Japan and the United Kingdom.
17Starting Friday, Apple Store staff will be able to help you choose from the array of options.
18Apple recommends that you make a reservation to see a sales representative.
19Apple will start taking reservations through its website at 12:01 a.m. PDT Friday as well.
20Appointments will be in 15-minute increments, though you can take more time at the store if you need.
21You might be able to walk in and try one out, but initially there will likely be a long wait.
22Once you choose, you'll still need to place the order online, though you can do that at a Web terminal inside the store.
23Again, your only option is to have the watch shipped, at least for the foreseeable future.
24For the most part, watches will be available for viewing when stores open Friday, so many overseas stores will actually show the watch before orders are accepted.
25Apple's 24-hour store on New York's Fifth Avenue will close temporarily to set up the displays; it will reopen at 9 a.m. Friday.
26Only some stores will carry the 18-karat gold luxury models known as Edition.
27When you make the try-on reservation, you'll have to specify whether you're looking for these models or the cheaper ones.
28Specialists will handle these luxury appointments.
29If you're just interested in seeing them, all stores will have them available for viewing in glass cases.
