Recap: Boston vs. Baltimore

1Boston, MA (SportsNetwork.com) - Matt Wieters clubbed a two-run homer and J.J. Hardy also knocked in a pair of runs as Baltimore topped Boston by an 8-6 count in the rubber match of a three-game series.
2Nolan Reimold collected two hits and scored twice for the Orioles, while Steve Pearce doubled twice among his three hits and crossed home once.
3Chris Davis, Ryan Flaherty and Manny Machado also contributed an RBI apiece for the win.
4Activated from the 15-day disabled list to make his start, Miguel Gonzalez (6-4) worked five-plus innings and gave up four runs on eight hits with four strikeouts.
5Zach Britton worked a scoreless ninth for his 21st save.
6Baltimore has taken nine of its last 12 series in Boston dating back to 2011, losing a series only once in that span.
7Alejandro De Aza hit a three-run homer for the Red Sox, with Blake Swihart and Xander Bogaerts adding two hits and an RBI each in defeat.
8Eduardo Rodriguez (3-2) was tagged for six runs on seven hits despite five strikeouts and no walks over 3 2/3 frames.
9Swihart's single plated Mike Napoli in the home first, then the O's responded with six in the fourth to take the lead for good.
10Chris Parmelee doubled with one down and scored on a Reimond single compounded by a Rodriguez throwing error followed by a two-run shot into right from Wieters.
11Delmon Young and Davis added base hits and Hardy doubled them both in for a 5-1 game.
12After Pearce singled, a fly to center by Flaherty brought in Hardy and Alexi Ogando ended the rally by retiring Machado.
13The Sox trimmed that deficit to two in the home half, as Pablo Sandoval and Napoli both singled ahead of a De Aza shot which landed inside the Pesky Pole in short right field.
14It was 7-4 for Baltimore on a Machado two-out single to plate Pearce in the sixth, then the O's edge stretched to four in the seventh as Reimold hit a leadoff double and scored on a Davis double.
15Boston got two back in the home half as Mookie Betts singled and scored on a Bogaerts double while the latter crossed home on a groundout and wild pitch.
16Tommy Hunter set down the Sox in order in the eighth and Britton stranded a runner in the ninth.
