11European plane maker Airbus is providing helicopters, planes and satellite imagery for relief efforts in Nepal.
12Airbus Helicopters is offering aircraft for French rescue missions to locate and retrieve anyone trapped in difficult-to-reach areas, according to the French Foreign Ministry.
13Airbus' defense and space business is offering satellite imagery to help assess damage and help rescuers target and deliver aid.
14France-based Airbus and its charitable arm plan to use a new Nepal Airlines A320 to bring medical supplies and aid workers to Kathmandu.
15In quake-wracked Nepal, everyone who is able is joining the aid effort.
16The 32-year-oid Narayan Thapa was working at a tire factory in Gorkha town when the earthquake destroyed his village of Bungkot, around 45 kilometers (28 miles) away.
17"Everything is destroyed. Not even one home is standing. We think there is still one person dead under the rubble," Thapa said.
18Thapa and his friends are scrambling to find a four-wheel vehicle to carry the medicine and food they've cobbled together to the village.
19"People are hungry there, they are suffering. I'm doing whatever I can to help," he said.
20Among those in desperate need of supplies in Bungkot, with a population of about 300, are Thapa's parents, wife and daughter.
21"My friends are helping me. Everyone is doing whatever they can. We managed to send a little bit yesterday. My family - they're relying on me. There is no food. They are in trouble there," Thapa said.
22Despite a steady morning drizzle, hundreds of people are lining up in central Kathmandu hoping to get on government-run bus service so they can visit their homes in remote parts of the quake-hit country.
23"I have to get out of here, I have to get home. It has already been so many days," said Shanti Kumari, a housewife, heading to join the line with her 7-year-old daughter.
24Kumari, a Kathmandu resident, said she was desperate to check on her family in her village in eastern Nepal.
25"I want to get at least a night of peace," she said.
26Over the past few days, the government has been running school buses free of charge for people wishing to travel to remote villages, worst-hit by Saturday's magnitude 7.8 earthquake.
27Five days after the quake, tents at the Tudikhel grounds, in the heart of Kathmandu, thinned out by Thursday morning.
28Overnight rainfall forced people to return to their homes, many of which were damaged in the quake.
29Nepal's weather office predicted rain all day Thursday, with the weather was expected to improve later in the day or Friday.
30Police in Nepal say the death toll from Saturday's earthquake has topped 5,500.
31The Kathmandu police say 5,489 have died and another 11,440 people have been injured in Nepal.
32The quake that was centered just outside Kathmandu also triggered an avalanche that killed at least 19 people at the Everest base camp.
33Another 61 were killed in neighboring India and Bangladesh, and China's official Xinhua News Agency reported 25 dead in Tibet.
34Chinese authorities have closed all climbing on the Tibetan side of Mount Everest for the spring.
35Meanwhile, some climbers in Nepal have announced plans to try to return to the mountain, a move that was criticized by others in the climbing community.
36No one climbing on the north side was injured following Saturday's deadly quake.
37But climbing guide Adrian Ballinger says Chinese officials announced their decision to end the climbing season.
38Ballinger said two reasons were cited: Safety concerns over possible additional earthquakes and solidarity with Nepal and the Sherpas.
39China reported has 25 deaths in Tibet from the quake.
40President Barack Obama has spoken to Prime Minister Sushil Koirala of Nepal to express sympathy over the thousands of deaths and vast destruction.
41White House spokesman Josh Earnest says the two leaders discussed U.S. military and civilian efforts already underway to help Nepal and international aid groups in their emergency rescue and response.
42They discussed the ongoing efforts by U.S. civilian and military personnel to assist the government of Nepal and international organizations in the disaster response efforts, including provision of rescue and logistics support.
43The Nepal Mountaineering Association has increased to 19 the number of fatalities from the avalanche on Mount Everest.
44It said Wednesday that five of the dead were foreign climbers and 14 were Nepalese Sherpa guides.
45The avalanche triggered by Saturday's 7.8-magnitude quake also injured dozens of people when it swept through base camp, knocking over tents and people.
46The association said 10 Sherpas have been identified.
47The Americans were Daniel Fredinburg and Marisa Eve Girawong, and the other foreigners were Ge Zhenfang from China, Renu Fotedar from Australia and Hiroshi Yamagata from Japan.
48All climbers have now left Everest, ending the climbing season early for the second year.
49Last year, climbs were cancelled after 16 Sherpa guides were killed in an avalanche while hauling gear between camps.
50Hundreds of people attempt to scale the world's tallest mountain each year, and Nepal earns significant income from the permit fees.
51The tiny hamlets clinging to the mountainsides of Gorkha District were ravaged by the earthquake.
52But lives were spared.
53Entire clusters of homes are piles of stone and splintered wood.
54Orange plastic tarps now dot the cliffs and terraced rice paddies carved into the Himalayan land.
55But the death toll in villages like Gumda was far lower than feared.
56The quake on Saturday struck at midday, and many people were working outdoors in the rural region in spring.
57Of Gumda's 1,300 residents, five were killed in the quake and 20 more were injured.
58The official overall toll as of Wednesday evening exceeded 5,100 in Nepal, India and Tibet.
59The United Nations is appealing for $415 million to provide for vital needs in Nepal over the next three months.
60It intends to support government efforts in provide shelter, water and sanitation, emergency health, food, and protection.
61The U.N.'s humanitarian coordinator for Nepal, Jamie McGoldrick, said the response to date was encouraging, but those efforts need to be maintained, especially in remote areas.
62He noted that the coming monsoon season would like add a logistical challenge to relief efforts, adding to the urgency.
63Helicopters have rescued some 210 foreign trekkers and local villagers stranded in the Lantang area north of Nepal's capital.
64The international trekkers were stranded in Langtang, a popular trekking route bordering Tibet, since the earthquake on Saturday that killed more than 5,000 people.
65Government administrator Gautam Rimal said the trekkers and villagers were flown to the nearby town of Dhunche.
66But with landslides cutting off the roads from there to Kathmandu, the trekkers will have to trudge for at least four hours before they can board a bus to the Nepalese capital.
67The number of people missing after a mudslide struck a village in central Nepal appears to be lower than first thought.
68The government had said Tuesday that 250 people were missing after a mudslide and avalanche hit the village of Ghodatabela near the quake epicenter.
69Government administrator Gautam Rimal said on Wednesday, however, "We believe now there are only a few people who were swept by the mudslide."
70Authorities withdrew the figure of 250 after getting more information from local residents, but Rimal didn't have an amended figure.
71Nepalese rescue helicopters are taking advantage of breaks in the rain to bring out the injured from remote mountain villages where aid is only beginning to trickle in, four days after the massive earthquake.
72A rescue mission on Wednesday landed in the village of Darkha, about 100 kilometers (60 miles) northwest of the capital, Kathmandu, and unloaded boxes of aid supplies.
73Nepalese soldiers disembarked and carried back on a stretcher the 69-year-old Ek Bahadur Thapa and others in need of treatment.
74The government says more than 10,000 people were injured.
75The first 44 Spaniards who were stranded in Nepal have returned home on an air force plane with Foreign Minister Jose Manuel Garcia Margallo accompanying them.
76The group is the first of some 127 to be evacuated by the government.
77About 500 Spaniards were affected by the earthquake and 103 have yet to be located.
78More than 20 were able to return by their own means.
79A second Spanish plane was due to bring back another group of Spaniards as well as other European and Latin American citizens later Wednesday.
80The quake occurred while Margallo was on an official visit to New Delhi.
81In another sign of life inching back to normal, banks in Kathmandu opened for few hours Wednesday and stuffed their ATMs with cash.
82At the Standard Chartered Bank in the city, people are lining up.
83"I needed money in cash so I can take my family out of Kathmandu. I want to be out of here for at least a few days," said Suraj Shrestha.
84He wasn't sure if ATMs outside the city were dispensing cash and wanted to carry "as much as possible."
85About 200 people have blocked traffic in Kathmandu to protest the slow pace of aid delivery.
86The protesters faced off with police and there were minor scuffles but no arrests were made.
87One protester says they haven't received any relief.
88"We are hungry, we haven't had anything to drink. We haven't been able to sleep. I have a 7-year-old child who is sleeping in the open. It's getting cold and people are getting pneumonia," he said.
89He accused the government of not doing enough.
90The death toll has climbed to 5,093 and more than 8 million people have been affected.
