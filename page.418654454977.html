Greek banks to reopen for first time in three weeks

1Greek banks will reopen on Monday morning for the first time in three weeks, with queues expected to form outside branches as customers rush to empty deposit boxes they have been unable to access.
2Withdrawals will still be restricted, but in a slight relaxation of capital controls, Greeks will be able to withdraw a weekly allowance of 420 ( 290) in one transaction, rather than being limited to 60 a day.
3Restrictions on sending money abroad and other controls remain in place.
4Deposit boxes are not affected by the capital controls and customers can take whatever they want from them, bank officials said.
5"We are expecting queues in our branches in the first two or three days. Many people will ask to open their safe deposit boxes," said an official at EFG Eurobank Ergasias, the country's third largest bank.
6The Greek government made the order over the weekend for lenders to pull up their shutters.
7They were ordered to close on 29 June to prevent the financial system collapsing, as withdrawals skyrocketed over worries that the country's debt crisis would see it ejected from the euro.
8In the days leading up to the closure, Greeks had been taking out as much as 1.6bn a day in cash.
9On Sunday Angela Merkel said the government of Alexis Tsipras would have to move fast to agree new bailout terms.
10"That is not a normal life so we have to negotiate quickly," said the German chancellor in an interview with German broadcaster ARD.
11Merkel said Berlin would "negotiate hard" to ensure Athens stuck to reform agreements.
12"There are things that we have discussed with all of the Greek governments since 2010 that have never been done, but that have been done in other countries like Portugal and Ireland," she said.
13She said Germany would be prepared to grant Greece some flexibility in delaying debt repayments or reducing interest rates, but there could be no wholesale debt forgiveness - or "haircut" - and no action at all before bailout talks were concluded.
14"Greece has already been given relief. We had a voluntary haircut among the private creditors and we then extended maturities once and reduced interest rates," said Merkel in the interview.
15"And we can now talk about such possibilities again | once the first successful review of the programme to be negotiated has been completed then exactly this question will be discussed - not now but then."

17The head of Greece's banking association, Louka Katseli, urged Greeks to return cash they have been storing at home to their banks.
18"When the banks reopen and normality is restored, let's all help our economy. If we take our money out of chests and from our homes - where they are not safe in any case - and we deposit them in the banks, we will strengthen the liquidity of the economy," she told Skai television.
19The chancellor's remarks came as her counterpart in Paris, Francois Hollande, called for the creation of what he called a "a government of the eurozone", reviving an idea originally put forward by former European commission chief Jacques Delors.
20Writing in the Journal du Dimanche, the French president proposed a government for the single currency "with a specific budget as well as a parliament to ensure its democratic control".
21He added: "What threatens us is not an excess of Europe, but its insufficiency."
22In Greece, the cautious reopening of the banks, and an increase in VAT on restaurant food and public transport, are aimed at restoring trust inside and outside the country after an aid-for-reforms deal last week averted bankruptcy.
23Tsipras is trying to turn a corner after reluctantly agreeing to negotiate on a third bailout, which could be worth 86bn, but will have stringent conditions attached.
24It prompted a rebellion in his leftist Syriza party, but Tsipras sacked party rebels in a government reshuffle on Friday and is seeking a swift start to talks on the bailout accord with European partners and the International Monetary Fund, before elections that the interior minister, Nikos Voutsis, said were likely in September or October.
25Some in Greece were sceptical that the move to reopen banks would change much.
26"The banks opening ... won't change anything for me," 31-year-old hotel worker Joanna Arvanitaki told Reuters.
27"I never used to withdraw 60 a day - 60 is what I had a week."
