Mexico Prison Boss Sacked After El Chapo Escape

1The country's interior minister says the escape "must" have been an inside job, as a 60 million peso reward is offered.
2A maximum security prison boss has been sacked after Mexico's interior minister said the escape of one of the country's most feared drug lords must have been an inside job.
3Miguel Angel Osorio Chong announced the dismissal of three prison officials as Joaquin "El Chapo" Guzman's family made public pictures appearing to show the Sinaloa cartel chief enjoying his freedom.
4Mr Osorio Chong denied that Mexican authorities were ever informed by the United States of a potential escape plot after drug agents received information as far back as March 2014.
5Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA) documents revealed that Guzman's family and associates were exploring the possibility of a jailbreak and a US official told AP that Mexican authorities had been alerted.
6Guzman, whose nickname means "Shorty", escaped from a maximum security jail for a second time through a 1.5km (1 mile) tunnel connected to an elaborate hatch in the shower area of his cell.
7The tunnel had its own ventilation and light system, along with a motorcycle on a rail system that is thought to have been used to transport tools and remove earth.
8Despite the huge bounty, Guzman appeared ready to taunt the authorities, with a blog that monitors Mexico's drug war publishing photos purportedly showing him flying in a plane and enjoying a beer since his escape.
9The photos, sent by one of the kingpin's sons, were allegedly taken two days after he fled the jail but have not been verified.
10In the images, Guzman seems to be sporting a full head of hair and a moustache, while in a recent prison photo released by authorities, he has a shaved head and no facial hair.
11Police probing the escape have questioned 49 people, including 32 prison employees.
