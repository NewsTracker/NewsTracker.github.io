Nigeria: Pre-election 'final onslaught' on Boko Haram

1Nigeria has begun its "final onslaught" against Boko Haram, with the group now ousted from the town of Bama.
2Top government officials are now debating how to ensure security during Nigeria's upcoming general elections.
3Boko Haram has a foothold in just three local government areas in Nigeria, according to officials.
4National security spokesman Mike Omeri said forces made "significant strategic military successes and gains," including taking back Borno state's second biggest town on Monday after six months under Boko Haram's control.
5"Bama was retaken yesterday and we have Abadam, Gwoza and Askira as part of the remaining areas where we still have this presence," Omeri told AFP on Tuesday.
6"Soldiers are still out there working hard and we're en route to the final onslaught because it has started already from Bama," he added.
7Nigeria's government has recaptured three dozen towns, according to officials.
8Borno is the state worst hit after six years of Boko Haram's uprising.
9The insurgency has left more than 13,000 people dead since 2009.
10Backed by Cameroon and Chad - and reportedly employing the services of foreign mercenaries - Nigeria's operation aims to reduce the threat posed by Boko Haram, which pledged allegiance to the "Islamic State," and to secure the northeast for elections.
11Officials had rescheduled the February 14 election for March 28 over fears that, with an active counteroffensive under way, soldiers could not provide security on polling day.
12In an interview published on March 11, Goodluck Jonathan, the threatened presidential incumbent, had promised to free Borno state from Boko Haram within three weeks, or just in time for the election.
13Jonathan faces a stiff challenge from the main opposition candidate, Muhammadu Buhari, a former military ruler who has attacked the president's record on tackling Boko Haram.
14Buhari, who headed his autocratic government for 20 months starting in December 1983, has accused Jonathan of consistently failing to provide leadership.
15The retired general and his party have complained that the government has not provided enough support and equipment to soldiers - an issue apparently only recently rectified.
16"We are confident there will be a level of security to enable citizens to vote," Omeri said on Tuesday.
17The government had Boko Haram "running with their tails between their legs," he added.
18"Boko Haram are not being pushed into neighboring countries," the national security spokesman said.
19"We are pushing them to an area where we are finding a solution to their menace."
20Less than two weeks before Nigerians vote, the military says it has captured at least 40 towns previously occupied by Boko Haram Islamists.
21The sudden victories are a sharp contrast to the defeats of the past six years.
22Nigeria's army has said its troops have ousted Islamist group Boko Haram from the northeastern state of Yobe.
23The military has also reclaimed Bama, close to the Borno state capital of Maiduguri.
24"Islamic State" militants have accepted a pledge of allegiance from Nigerian Islamists Boko Haram.
25Although both groups are on the defensive, the new alliance has the potential to worsen the conflict in northern Nigeria.
26Nigeria has reportedly recaptured 36 towns from Boko Haram.
27The successes come after the start of a four-nation military offensive with Chad, Cameroon and Niger.
28Nigeria's elections have been postponed for six weeks.
29Officially, army and police are too busy fighting Boko Haram to keep voters safe.
30Some welcome the delay; others suspect a government stratagem to stay in power.
31Islamist militants have killed several people in an attack in northeast Nigeria.
32Nigeria's army says it has liberated many territories previously held by Boko Haram.
33Leaders of West and Central Africa are planning an April summit to discuss a common strategy to combat the Islamists.
