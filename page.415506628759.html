15Plaskett carefully explains that he doesn't consciously set out to have any type of sound, it's just that Nova Scotia naturally influences his songs.
16"I'm most influenced by my contemporary musicians on the east coast," says Plaskett.
17"That's not to say other Canadian music doesn't have an effect on me, it does. But I'm right there in the heart of it and I feel as though I have a connection there.
18"It's not like I'm trying to channel that, it just is who I am. I recognize that when I'm travelling, I write about where I'm from, and when I come home, I write about where I've been."
19When asked about turning the big 4-0, Plaskett puts on his philosophy hat to explain the important day.
20"I'm comfortable with getting older and gaining experience," Plaskett says.
21"I may have a crisis in three or four years, but I don't fear it like some people do. If I'm going to leave my seven-year-old boy with any knowledge, I have to get older in order to do that."
22Plaskett is overjoyed when the conversation turns toward his old band Thrush Hermit, and he even tells a story about a time when the group passed up the chance to be on a huge film's soundtrack.
23"We turned down some stuff that we thought would be lame, so we didn't seize the opportunity," Plaskett laments.
