11Action Bronson (Tuesday and Wednesday) This professional chef-cum-rapper from Queens redirects his voracity toward the fleet rhyme composition of the Wu-Tang Clan and the foodie jargon (not to mention the irascibility) of Anthony Bourdain.
12His full-length 2011 debut, "Dr. Lecter," shows where his heart is in the tracks "Brunch," "Shiraz" and "Jerk Chicken."

15Neil Diamond (Thursday) This "Sweet Caroline" crooner's easygoing humor has informed his career for the past 50-odd years, and he continues to be a youthful presence onstage.

18Fifth Harmony (Monday) A girl group forged by the "X Factor" impresario Simon Cowell, Fifth Harmony has drawn many early comparisons to One Direction, which sprang from the same origins.
19The surefooted R&B, pop effervescence and satiny harmonies on "Reflection," the group's debut studio album, indicate a globe-trotting future success all its own.
20With Jacob Whitesides, Jasmine V and Mahogany LOX.
21At 7 p.m., Best Buy Theater, 1515 Broadway, at 44th Street, 888-929-7849, bestbuytheater.com.
22Brandon Flowers (Tuesday) Before this erstwhile frontman of the Killers releases his second solo album, "The Desired Effect," in May - which, from its short first trailers, suggests that Mr. Flowers, a longtime New Wave enthusiast, will also be indulging arena-rock predilections - he will perform this one-off gig at Webster Hall.
23On March 30, he will duet with Brian Wilson at the Fonda Theater in Los Angeles as part of "Brian Fest," a celebration of Mr. Wilson's music.

26Ariana Grande (Friday and Saturday) This young pop singer leapt from the Disney Channel into full, unfettered pop stardom last year with the release of her second album, "My Everything."
27She deserved it after her white-knuckle job in 2013: opening for Justin Bieber.
28Expect her to flip her formidable ponytail through peppy, melismatic renditions of her Top 10 hits "Break Free," "Problem" and "Love Me Harder."

30Debbie Harry (Fridays, Saturdays, and Tuesdays through Thursdays, through April 4) In the 1970s, when Ms. Harry and her band Blondie first trod CBGB's sticky boards, she was the prototype of the glamorous punk: a woman full of righteous conviction who also radiated poise.
31Now, one year after the band celebrated its 40th anniversary and released the album "Ghosts of Download," she takes to the regal Cafe Carlyle on the Upper East Side for a short residency.
32It's a long way from the East Village, but a fitting place for a rock eminence.
33Fridays at 8:45 p.m. and 10:45 p.m.; Saturdays, and Tuesdays through Thursdays, at 8:45 p.m.; Cafe Carlyle, 35 East 76th Street, Manhattan, 212-744-1600, thecarlyle.com.
34Ms. Lauryn Hill (Friday, Saturday and Wednesday) Ms. Hill seems to be warming to local showmanship; in recent years, she has had a handful of concerts at small clubs.
35Now she'll perform a few acoustic sets at City Winery and Highline Ballroom, which may be more ideal settings for this talented singer and rapper: In his review of one of her outings last year, Jon Pareles lamented that she "was often buried in the mix of her band's hyperactive funk."

38Hozier (Sunday) Something's in the air in Ireland: two of its musical representatives have blatant religious fixations.
39The brooding singer Hozier, whose real name is Andrew Hozier-Byrne, broke into American radio and graced the stage of "Saturday Night Live" with his first single, "Take Me to Church," a sultry, bracing appraisal of love and the hypocrisy of religion.
40The song shouldn't be confused with his countrywoman Sinead O'Connor's identically titled track, a yearning for redemptive love.
41With George Ezra.
42At 8 p.m., Hammerstein Ballroom, 311 West 34th Street, Manhattan, 800-745-3000, ticketmaster.com.
43Ibeyi (Wednesday) These 20-year-old French-Cuban twins are the daughters of Anga Diaz, a prominent percussionist in Cuba who performed with the Buena Vista Social Club.
44Their music is an ecstatic whirl of Yoruba chants, French and English purrs, sparse electronic beats, Cajon drums and a lot of preternatural tenacity.
45The duo's self-titled debut album, which was recently released on XL, is a remarkable first outing.
46With Flo Morrissey.
47At 8:30 p.m., Music Hall of Williamsburg, 66 North Sixth Street, Brooklyn, 718-486-5400, musichallofwilliamsburg.com.
48Tobias Jesso Jr. (Thursday) Another member of the current '70s-inflected folk-pop surge, along with Father John Misty and Patrick Watson, Mr. Jesso is capturing national attention fast for "Goon," his debut solo album.

56Laura Marling (Monday) This nu-folk singer-songwriter and 2011 Brit Award-winner for best female solo artist is an old, serene soul: her intricate guitar strums and flinty intonation meld contemporary influences with the hearth-warmed acoustics of Britain's 1960s and '70s folk revival.

59The Month in Cabaret (through April 4) The most anticipated event of cabaret's high season is Debbie Harry's already sold-out engagement at Cafe Carlyle, from Tuesday through April 4.
60On the heels of Buster Poindexter's run there, it is proof positive that downtown has crashed the gates of uptown and that the punk-rock revolution, absorbed by the mainstream, now qualifies as rock nostalgia.
61Before that, Herb Alpert and Lani Hall are at the Cafe through Saturday with a mixture of light pop-jazz built around Mr. Alpert's burbling trumpet and Ms. Hall's sultry voice.
62(Friday at 8:45 p.m. and Saturday at 8:45 and 10:45 p.m., 35 East 76th Street, Manhattan, 212-744-1600, thecarlyle.com.)
63The Music of David Byrne and Talking Heads (Monday) The New Wave eccentrics of Talking Heads and their endlessly inventive frontman, David Byrne, will be celebrated in a series of lavish covers during this installment of the 11-years-strong concert series for music education programs.

67Shakey Graves (Thursday) Although he is occasionally given the artless "hobo folk" handle for his scraped howl and twangy guitar fingerpicking, the singer-songwriter Alejandro Rose-Garcia transcends that label with his lo-fi Americana.
68Performing under the name Shakey Graves, this musician and actor from Austin, Tex., is worldly but not jaded for his 20-something years, energetic yet minimally produced with many aching riffs.
69His most recent album, "And the War Came," has freak-folk moments that beg for further exploration.
70With Nikki Lane.

72Songhoy Blues (Monday) The Malian desert blues band is making a snazzy debut in America this month with several shows in New York City.

10The extremists now rule a vast area straddling the Syria-Iraq border in which they have imposed a harsh version of Islamic law and beheaded and massacred their opponents.
11The U.N. report, published by the Human Rights Office, draws on the testimony of 100 people who survived attacks by the militant group in Iraq between June 2014 and last month.
12The accounts detail killings, torture, rape and sexual slavery, forced religious conversions and the conscription of children.
13The report also details alleged crimes committed against Iraq's minority Yazidi community, viewed as apostates by the Islamic State extremists.
14The report said that when the militants captured Yazidis they killed the men and enslaved women and children.
15Witnesses said militants raped children as young as 6, and women interviewed by the U.N. said they were raped, impregnated and given abortions by Islamic State doctors.
16Iraqi and Kurdish officials say the extremist group has captured hundreds of Yazidi women.
17In a recent issue of the Islamic State group's online magazine the militants boasted of enslaving them.
18In the once-diverse city of Mosul, the Islamic State group has forced Christians and other religious minorities to convert to Islam, pay special taxes or die, causing tens of thousands to flee.
19The U.N. report also details killings, torture and abductions allegedly carried out by Iraqi security forces and Shiite militias battling the Islamic State group.
20The report said fleeing Iraqi forces allegedly set fire to an army base in the eastern Diyala province where 43 Sunnis were held prisoner.
21It also included witness accounts of Sunni civilians being forced from their homes at gunpoint.
22The U.N. called on the Iraqi government to ensure that all accusations are investigated in line with international human rights standards and to publicize the results.
