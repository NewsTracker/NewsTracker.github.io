2On Friday, Mark Bryan's "Live at the Charleston Music Hall" kicks off its television and radio run with performances by Edwin McCain and Sam Bush.
3Future acts haven't been announced, but Bryan, of Hootie & the Blowfish fame, says the show will have a regular run on both public television and radio channels.
4This weekend's shows are airing on public radio and television throughout South Carolina, and shows later this year will be broadcast nationwide.
1Chadian military spokesman Azem Bermandoa Agouna also confirmed the pre-dawn attack on Ngouboua, a village across the water from the Nigerian town of Baga.
2The insurgents struck around 3:00am (2:00am Irish time) after crossing the lake from Baga -- scene of a Boko Haram massacre last month -- in boats.
3"They started firing on everything that moved. The military retaliated," the spokesman said on national radio, adding the fighters then fled back towards Nigeria with troops "in hot pursuit".
4A security source speaking on condition of anonymity told AFP news agency four civilians, including the village chief, and one soldier were killed.
5The authorities gave a toll of one civilian (the village chief) and one soldier killed, and four troops wounded and claimed two dead and five injured in Boko Haram's ranks.
6Chadian aircraft were scrambled to help repel the offensive, destroying the attackers' vessels with strikes, the security source added.
7Two-thirds of Ngouboua, which lies on a peninsula about 18km from the border and had become a sanctuary for thousands of Nigerians fleeing the militants' attacks, was torched in the onslaught, the source said.
8Boko Haram has stepped up attacks since Nigeria's neighbours decided last month to muster a 7,500-strong five-country force to take on the extremists.
9The planned contingent was later boosted to 8,700.
10But while the insurgents have made several forays into northern Cameroon, and more recently southeastern Niger, Chad - the first of Nigeria's neighbours to send troops across the border to fight the militants - had, until now, been spared attack.
11News of the attack in Chad coincided with reports of further attacks by the insurgents within Nigeria, where general elections initially due on Saturday have been postponed by six weeks over the violence, until 28 March.
12At least 21 people were killed in two separate attacks yesterday on northeastern villages near the major city of Maiduguri, a community leader and a witness said.
13"They (Boko Haram) killed 12 people in Akida village and nine others in Mbuta village during a raid," said community leader Mustapha Abbagini.
14A witness to the attack in Mbuta gave the same death toll.
15The attackers first struck Akida, 25km from Maiduguri, while villagers were sleeping.
16After setting homes and businesses on fire, the insurgents left and attacked Mbuta, Mr Abbagini said.
17Mbuta resident Hamidu Bukar said the attackers accused the villagers of "spying for military authorities".
18Also yesterday a female suicide bomber blew herself up at a crowded market in the town of Biu, in the south of Borno state, of which Maiduguri is the capital.
19The city has a population that has swollen to roughly two million because of displaced people seeking safety.
20A civilian vigilante helping the Nigerian army in the counter-insurgency and a source at the town's hospital both said the death toll had risen from seven to 11 after the blast.
21"Four more people, all of them adults, died at the hospital here in Biu while receiving treatment," said the vigilante, Abor Kabiru.
22A hospital source confirmed that 11 bodies had been identified.
23The death toll could increase further, with health officials working to establish the identities of at least two other people blown apart by the blast.
24Boko Haram, which already controls vast swathes of northeast Nigeria, has ramped up its bloody, six-year insurgency in the past few months.
25The conflict has killed more than 13,000 since 2009 and become an increasing regional threat.
26In mid-January, Chad's President Idriss Deby Itno sent soldiers to Cameroon to help counter the threat from the group.
27Chadian aircraft carried out several days of airstrikes against Boko Haram's positions in the Nigerian border town of Gamboru before sending thousands of troops into the town to rout the militants.
28The Chadians succeeded in wresting the town from Boko Haram but were left reeling a day later after the Islamists took the fight back across the border into Cameroon, killing 19 Chadian and Cameroonian troops.
29The retaliatory attack on the Cameroonian village of Fotokol also claimed the lives of 81 civilians, according to Cameroonian authorities.
30Last week, the Islamists also carried out their first deadly raids in Niger to Nigeria's north.
31The militants struck the border town of Diffa five times, after Niger announced it planned to send 750 troops into Nigeria.
