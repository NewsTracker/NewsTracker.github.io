National Briefing | Midwest: Missouri: Arizona Commander Will Lead Ferguson Police

1Andre Anderson was introduced Wednesday as the new interim police chief in Ferguson, with the job of building trust between residents of the predominantly black St. Louis suburb and its mostly white officers nearly a year after Michael Brown's killing roiled racial tensions.
2Chief Anderson, 50, who is black and has nearly 25 years in law enforcement, starts in Ferguson on Thursday, essentially on six-month loan from the Phoenix suburb of Glendale, Ariz., where he is a police commander.
3Known for having a knack for community outreach in policing, Mr. Anderson arrives as Ferguson is still regrouping after a scathing Department of Justice report in March cited racial bias and profiling in policing as well as a profit-driven municipal court system that frequently targeted black residents.
