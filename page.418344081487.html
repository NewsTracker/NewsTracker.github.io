Can Apple Music win the hearts of Spotify's fans?

1Can Apple Music win the hearts of Spotify's fans?
2Is there enough going for Apple Music to make you switch from Spotify?
3Apple's new streaming service launched on Tuesday last week and now critics are weighing in on whether it does too much or too little - but not many seem to think the Cupertino-based company got it just right.
4Of course, critics had many complaints against the iPod, the iPhone, the iPad and, most recently, the Apple Watch, none of which affected the products' stunning popularity.
5Fans appear to be singing a different tune about Apple Music - many were won over by the service in a matter of days.
6Apple's new Beats 1 radio service was a popular topic on Twitter over the holiday weekend, according to Fortune.
7Most tech-savvy consumers already stream music, but is there enough buzz around Apple Music to get them to make the switch?
8Several critics likened the new service to to Ping, an Apple social networking feature launched back in 2010 that aimed to let artists to connect with fans.
9Ping tanked due to its partnership with Facebook- the social media network pulled support just before Ping's debut.
10Apple moved forward on Ping without Facebook and the feature never quite panned out.
11Unlike Ping, Apple Music is completely owned by Apple and incorporates Beats technology, which it acquired in 2014 for $3 billion.
12The service also uses Apple's in-house iTunes features and iCloud services.
13But is it better than Spotify?
14Critics noted that the user experience is a bit overwhelming.
15It can be a challenge to figure out how to save songs offline or a disappointment to find that you can't follow friends.
16Variety's critic pointed out that the app itself has too much going on within it.
17Users can access their own local music files, the subscription catalog, personal recommendations and the existing iTunes download store.
18Also included in the app is a section for home movies and a section for movie rentals and purchases.
19On top of that is access to audio books, ringtones and iOS apps.
20On the plus side, Apple Music, like Spotify, has about 30 million songs users can access.
21Apple has a unique visual setup system that helps the service curate music for the user and it's also very easy to launch if the user is already on an iPhone.
22The "For You" section is winning over fans because of its targeted recommendation features, which appear to go beyond what Spotify can do, according to Business Insider.
23The radio feature, with curated lists from popular DJs and artists, is also winning rave reviews from users.
24Have a hard time getting up in the morning?
25Apple Music will let you stream any song as your alarm to help you get out of bed.
26Siri can also act as your personal music curator.
27Just by asking, Siri can select songs similar to the one you are listening to, or she can create a playlist from a particular year.
28Users can also assign photos to playlists to quickly identify a list visually.
29None of these features are game changers, but they are nice little perks on top of the regular streaming service.
