Kelly Clarkson Fires Back at Fat Shamer

News of robust hiring in US month sends bonds lower, dollar higher; Apple gains, will join Dow

1Kelly Clarkson doesn't care what anyone thinks about her weight, not even Britain's "most hated woman."
2Journalist and reality star Katie Hopkins, known as "the most hated woman in Britain," blasted Clarkson's size on Twitter.
3"What happened to Kelly Clarkson?"
4Hopkins wrote on Feb. 20.
5"Did she eat all of her backing singers? Happily I have wide-screen."
6She went on: "Darling, if you had a baby a year ago, that is not baby weight. It is fat. Quit calling it cute names to make yourself feel better."
7Hopkins, who gained her reputation as the villain on "The Apprentice UK," didn't leave it there.
8In an interview with "Entertainment Tonight" on Wednesday, she said, "Kelly Clarkson is now a chunky monkey, if we're putting it kindly. She does look like she's eaten her backup singers. My advice: she needs to get out there with her stroller and do some pushing and get some of that weight off."
9Clarkson, who gave birth to daughter River Rose last June, was unfazed -- even puzzled -- by the fat-shaming comments when first asked about them by Heat magazine.
10"I don't have a clue what you're talking about," she told the magazine.
11"Someone tweeted something about me?"
12After the reporter explained who Hopkins is and what she'd said, Clarkson laughed.
13"Oh, and she's tweeted something nasty about me? That's because she doesn't know me," she said.
14"I'm awesome! It doesn't bother me. It's a free world. Say what you will."
15Added Clarkson, "I've just never cared what people think. It's more if I'm happy and I'm confident and feeling good. That's always been my thing. And more so now, since having a family -- I don't seek out any other acceptance."
1Apple rose 1 percent early Friday after it was announced that the company would be joining the Dow Jones industrial average, replacing AT&T.
2AT&T fell 1 percent.
3The Dow Jones industrial average was down 107 points, or 0.6 percent, to 18,024 as of 9:35 a.m. Eastern time.
4The Standard & Poor's 500 lost nine points, or 0.4 percent, to 2,091.
5The Nasdaq fell 10 points, or 0.2 percent, to 4,971.
6Bond prices fell, sending yields higher, after the Labor Department reported that U.S. employers extended a healthy streak of hiring in February.
7That could give the Federal Reserve room to raise rates.
