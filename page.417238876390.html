6CANNES, France (AP) - Jacques Audiard's Sri Lankan refugee drama "Dheepan" on Sunday won the Palme d'Or, the top honor of the Cannes Film Festival.
7The choice, as selected by a jury led by Joel and Ethan Coen, was unexpected.
8"Dheepan" is about a trio of Sri Lankans who pretend to be a family in order to flee their war-torn country for a housing project in France.
9While the dapper Audiard has drawn widespread acclaim for films such as "A Prophet" and "Rust and Bone," many criticized "Dheepan" for the thriller-like conclusion of its otherwise patient depiction of immigrant adjustment.
10"To receive a prize from the Coen brothers is exceptional," said Audiard.
11"There's only the Dardenne brothers (that could match it)."
12The runner-up prize, the Grand Prix, went to "Son of Saul," a grim Holocaust drama by first-time Hungarian director Laszlo Nemes.
13Some expected Nemes' horrifying plunge into the life of an Auschwitz worker to take the top award, but it's been 26 years since a debut film (Steven Soderbergh's "Sex, Lies, and Videotape") was given the Palme.
14Hou Hsiao-Hsien, the masterful 68-year-old Taiwanese filmmaker, won best director for his first feature in eight years: "The Assassin," a lushly painterly martial arts drama.
15The best actress prize was split but not the way some expected.
16It was given to both Rooney Mara, half of the romantic pair of Todd Haynes' '50s lesbian drama "Carol," and Emmanuelle Bercot, the French star of the roller-coaster marriage drama "My King."
17(Bercot also directed the festival opener, "Standing Tall," about a delinquent teenager.)
18Many expected Mara might share the prize with her "Carol" co-star, Cate Blanchett.
19Best actor was awarded to Vincent Lindon, the veteran French actor of Stephane Brize's "The Measure of a Man."
20In it, he plays a man struggling to make a living after a long period of unemployment.
21Yorgos Lanthimos, a Greek filmmaker working in English for the first time, took the jury prize for "The Lobster," a deadpan dystopian comedy, starring Colin Farrell and Rachel Weisz, about a near-future where unmarried singles are turned into the animals of their choice.
22"Chronic," an understated drama about a home-care nurse (Tim Roth) for the terminally ill, took best screenplay for Mexican writer-director Michel Franco.
23Franco and Roth met three years ago when Roth, on the Un Certain Regard jury, helped award Franco the Un Certain prize.
24"It's a Cannes story," said Franco.
25The Camera d'Or, Cannes award for best first feature film, went to "La Tierra Y la Sombra."
26Cesar Augusto Acevedo's debut, which played in the Critics Week section, is about an old farmer returning home to tend to his gravely ill son.
27The Palme was decided by a jury headed by the Coen brothers, who themselves took the coveted award in 1991 for "Barton Fink."
28The others on the Cannes jury were: Guillermo del Toro, Jake Gyllenhaal, Sienna Miller, Sophie Marceau, Xavier Dolan, Rokia Traore and Rossy de Palma.
29The last two Cannes winners have been three-hour art-house epics: the glacial Turkish drama "Winter Sleep," chosen last year by Jane Campion's jury, and "Blue is the Warmest Color," as picked by Steven Spielberg's jury.
30But no film in competition this year was much longer than two hours.
31This year's competition slate of 19 films left some critics calling it a somewhat down year for Cannes.
32Some of the films that drew the biggest raves - "Mad Max: Fury Road," Pixar's "Inside Out" - played out of competition.
33The festival was dominated by discussion about gender equality with many - from Cate Blanchett to Jane Fonda - speaking about female opportunity in the movie business.
34"You hope it's not just the year, it's not some sort of fashionable moment," said Blanchett.
35On Sunday, an honorary Palme d'Or was given to French filmmaker Agnes Varda, the first woman to receive one and only the fourth director after Woody Allen, Clint Eastwood and Bernardo Bertolucci.
36But the festival was overrun by an unlikely scandal when several women were turned away from the formal premiere of Todd Haynes' "Carol" for wearing flat shoes, rather than high heels.
37The festival insisted it was the mistake of overzealous security guards and not part of Cannes' notoriously strict dress code.
38Emily Blunt, star of the drug war thriller "Sicario" called the situation "very disappointing."
39On Saturday, "Rams," a drama set among farmers and their sheep in a remote Icelandic valley, won the Un Certain Regard, a sidebar competition at Cannes.
