Bills McCoy says he's done talking about ex-coach Chip Kelly (Yahoo Sports)

1ORCHARD PARK, N.Y. (AP) -- Buffalo Bills running LeSean McCoy is done talking about Chip Kelly, and sees no reason to explain what he meant in suggesting his former coach might have shown a racial bias when parting ways with several Philadelphia Eagles star players.
2''I don't have to explain myself to no one,'' McCoy said after a voluntary practice on Wednesday.
3''I said what I said.
4It's how I felt at the time.
5McCoy spoke for the first time since creating a stir early last month, when he was quoted in ESPN The Magazine as questioning Kelly's motivations in retooling the Eagles roster.
6''You see how he got rid of all the good players, especially all the good black players.
7He Got rid of them the fastest,'' McCoy, who is black, told the magazine.
8''It's hard to explain with him.
9McCoy did not explain his reasoning to the magazine.
10Aside from trading McCoy to Buffalo in March after six seasons in Philadelphia where he was the NFL's 2013 rushing leader, the Eagles also released star receiver DeSean Jackson, who is black, in 2013.
11The team kept receiver Riley Cooper, who is white, even though Cooper had to leave training camp for a few days in 2013 after a video surfaced on the Internet of him using the N-word.
12''Everybody can make their own thoughts and assumptions about it, but I'm done talking about it,'' McCoy said Wednesday.
13McCoy threatened to end the interview after being asked a fourth consecutive question about Kelly.
14''Yo, are you all listening to me?'' McCoy said.
15''Can I say it any other way?
16McCoy, however, did broach Kelly's name on his own later, when favorably comparing the Eagles and Bills offense under new coordinator Greg Roman.
17He referred to Kelly and Roman as ''geniuses'' for how they're able to get the ball to running backs in open spaces.
18''As a coach, you can't say nothing negative about him, X's and O's,'' McCoy said about Kelly.
19''I don't regret nothing I say, never,'' McCoy said.
20Last week, Kelly said it was wrong for McCoy to suggest he favors white players over black players.
21''I've got great respect for LeSean.
22However, in that situation, I think he's wrong,'' Kelly said.
23The Eagles replaced McCoy by signing DeMarco Murray and Ryan Mathews in free agency.
24McCoy previously acknowledged he was initially upset when informed of being traded to Buffalo in exchange for linebacker Kiko Alonso, who played for Kelly at Oregon.
25This marks the first time McCoy is playing for a team outside of Pennsylvania.
26He's from Harrisburg, played college at Pittsburgh and was the Eagles' second-round pick in the 2009 draft.
27McCoy quickly warmed to the move to Buffalo.
28He negotiated a new five-year, $40 million contract, with $26.5 million of that guaranteed.
29Roman and new coach Rex Ryan are installing an offense that heavily emphasizes the running attack.
30Though McCoy's comments made numerous headlines last month, he didn't believe he created a distraction with his new team.
31''I think it's something small,'' McCoy said.
32NOTES: New owner Terry Pegula and his dog, Sidney, attended practice.
33... QB Matt Simms and LB Michael Buchanan both practiced after being acquired by Buffalo earlier this week.
34... Ryan spoke highly of how rookie third-round draft pick John Miller is fitting at the starting right guard spot.
