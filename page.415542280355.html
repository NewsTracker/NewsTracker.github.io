13Her death is believed by many to be a suicide.
14Durst has claimed he witnessed his mother's fall.
15His brother Douglas disputes that.
16But even Douglas has been quoted as saying that the scene was something almost beyond imagination-that he and his three siblings were suddenly awakened and whisked out of the house the night the death occurred.
17Let's take the last fact, to start with.
18Just imagine the unconscious mental gymnastics a boy has to attempt in order to function after his mother, young and alive that very day, inhabiting the same dwelling as he, is suddenly dead, perhaps having caused her own death.
19When a 7-year-old loses a parent in such awful circumstances, it can shake his belief in the reality of anything and everything-love, friendship, the future, even his own, core identity.
20And it can make a child bury the loose ends of his grief and rage very deeply.
21Was Robert Durst's mother just pretending to be alive and well hours before her death, his 7-year-old mind might have unconsciously wondered?
22Was she pretending to love him, if she then left him?
23Were others pretending she was dead?
24What can be trusted if one's mother can fall off a roof, to her death, while one is warm, tucked into bed, dreaming?
25And what if, even worse, Robert Durst did witness the death-or believes deeply that he did?
26Not everyone can do what Mr. Durst did decades later-disappear from one state, disguised as someone of the opposite gender, pretending to have no voice, living as though impoverished, in a down-and-out apartment.
27But having to pretend, at 7, to comprehend a mother's alleged suicide, then eventually go to school and play with friends, is a kind of tragic training in disappearing.
28And only a person who has experience with walling off very powerful feelings-not feeling-could dismember a body as Mr. Durst did, put the remains in trash bags, then cast them adrift.
29Could such a person also kill a wife, or kill a friend, or both?
30What, after all, does having a wife mean to such a person?
31What does having a friend mean to such a person?
32Could it mean something powerful at one moment, then be utterly meaningless the next?
33If a mother's love can disappear, can't any emotion?
34If a mother can disappear, can't anyone?
35Maybe the full, human version of Robert Durst, for all intents and purposes, disappeared at age 7, along with his mother.
36Maybe that's why he can assume an alternate identity so completely.
37Maybe that's why he can cut up a body and throw it away.
38And maybe that's why he can tell investigators that his wife-now missing for so long-simply boarded a train all those years ago, never to be seen, again.
