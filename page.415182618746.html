Key figures in fatal police shooting in Ferguson, Missouri

1(AP) - A look at some of the key figures in the case of Michael Brown, the black 18-year-old who was shot and killed by a white police officer in August.
2His death stirred weeks of violent unrest in the St. Louis suburb after the shooting and again in November, after a grand jury decided not to indict the officer.
3Michael Brown graduated from Normandy High School last spring and was preparing to attend Vatterott College, where he planned to study to become a heating and air conditioning technician.
4Friends say he eventually wanted to go into business for himself.
5Relatives and friends described Brown, who grew up in a tough neighborhood, as a quiet, gentle giant who stood around 6-foot-3 and weighed nearly 300 pounds.
6He was unarmed on the day he was killed.
7Police said later that he was a suspect in the "strong-arm" robbery of a convenience store moments before the shooting.
8A family attorney said Brown may have made mistakes but did not deserve to die.
9Some descriptions of Ferguson police officer Darren Wilson are similar to those of Brown.
10Both men have been described as gentle and quiet.
11Police Chief Thomas Jackson said Wilson had no previous complaints against him and a good career record.
12"He's devastated," the chief said after naming Wilson as the shooter back in August.
13"He never intended for this to happen. He is, and has been, an excellent police officer."
14Wilson began his career in nearby Jennings before moving to the Ferguson job several years ago.
15He was placed on paid administrative leave after the shooting and resigned in November.
16Thomas Jackson was a police veteran long before he came to Ferguson.
17He spent more than 30 years with the St. Louis County Police Department, at one point serving as commander of a drug task force.
18Before that, he was a SWAT team supervisor, undercover detective and hostage negotiator.
19He heads a department with 53 officers, only three of them black, in a town where nearly 70 percent of the 21,000 residents are African-American.
20"I'm constantly trying to recruit African-Americans and other minorities," Jackson has said.
21"But it's an uphill battle. The minority makeup of this police department is not where I want it to be."
22Some of Jackson's actions in the wake of the shooting have drawn criticism, including his decision to announce that Brown was a suspect in the convenience store robbery, a move that stirred anger in Ferguson's black community.
23Since his election in 1991, Bob McCulloch has been the top prosecutor in St. Louis County.
24A Democrat with a reputation for being tough on crime, he comes from a law-enforcement family.
25He was 12 when his father, a police officer, was shot and killed by a black suspect in 1964.
26Some critics, including St. Louis County Executive Charlie Dooley, questioned McCulloch's ability to be objective in the Ferguson case.
27They wondered if the loss of his father in such circumstances creates a built-in bias.
28MISSOURI STATE HIGHWAY PATROL CAPT.
29During a 27-year career, Capt.
30Ron Johnson rose from patrolman to chief of the 11-county division of the Missouri State Highway Patrol that includes St. Louis and its suburbs.
31Back in August, Gov. Jay Nixon appointed Johnson to take command of security in Ferguson.
32That decision came after complaints that authorities were too heavy-handed with protesters when St. Louis County police were in charge.
33Johnson's calm but commanding presence drew high praise from many observers.
34When Johnson, who is black, walked down the streets of Ferguson with protesters, many demonstrators shook his hand or posed for photos with him.
35He reminded locals of his Ferguson roots and suggested that he, too, had lessons to learn from the case.
36"We all ought to be thanking the Browns for Michael, because Michael's going to make it better for our sons, so they can be better black men," he said in August.
37He also apologized to Brown's family.
38Gov. Jay Nixon, a Democrat who served four terms as attorney general, drew some criticism in the days immediately after the shooting for keeping a low profile, but he soon moved to the forefront, putting state police in charge of security and then calling in the National Guard to help quell the violence.
39Benjamin Crump became a national figure when he represented the family of Trayvon Martin, the Florida teenager fatally shot by a neighborhood-watch organizer in 2012.
40Now he is back in the spotlight, representing Brown's family in another racially charged death.
41Crump was born in North Carolina, one of nine children.
42Now based in Tallahassee, Florida, he seems to fight back his own emotions as he talks about the loss suffered by Brown's parents.
43"I don't want to sugarcoat it," Crump said in August.
44Brown "was executed in broad daylight."
45Almost from the outset, Attorney General Eric Holder showed strong interest in Brown's death.
46Two days after the shooting, Holder said the case deserved a full review and dispatched a Justice Department team to Ferguson to try to calm tensions.
47The department soon launched its own civil rights investigation.
48Holder ordered a federal medical examiner to perform a third autopsy on Brown and called the Brown family to express his condolences.
49He said aggressively pursuing these types of investigations is "critical for preserving trust between law enforcement and the communities they serve."
