Islamic State issues audio of Baghdadi calling supporters to join him

1Thought to be wounded and incapacitated, this is an image of the militant leader of Islamic State, Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi, taken from a propaganda video.
2Beirut: Islamic State has issued an audio recording that it said was by its leader Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi, calling on supporters around the world to join the fight in Syria and Iraq or to take up arms wherever they live.
3It was Baghdadi's first message since a number of media reports said that he was wounded earlier this year in Iraq.
4Some said that he was no longer running the group and others suggested he had even died.
5An image of the militant thought to be at one time, and perhaps still, the leader of Islamic State, Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi, taken from a propaganda video.
6"Joining (its fight) is a duty on every Muslim. We are calling on you either join or carry weapons (to fight) wherever you are."
7The authenticity of the audio or its date could not be verified.
8An Islamic State militant holding a pistol in an image from a propaganda video released in April.
9In the message, Baghdadi tells the rulers of Saudi Arabia that "their end is near", adding that Gulf rulers felt threatened by the growing popularity of his group among Sunni Muslims.
10"They have lost their so called legitimacy ... when they felt that their masters, the Crusaders and the Jews, have replaced them with the Rafeda they launched their so-called war against Rafeda in Yemen," he said, using a derogatory term for Shiite Muslims.
11Saudi Arabia heads a coalition of Gulf and Arab nations fighting Shiite Houthi rebels in Yemen.
12"They claim that they are defending the Sunnis ... they are lying. It is a desperate attempt to deter Muslims from joining the Islamic State."
13Islamic State considers all those who oppose it as infidels.
14The group has faced military setbacks in Iraq when Iraqi forces including Shi'ite paramilitaries retook the city of Tikrit in the first big counter-offensive since Islamic State rapidly seized swathes of territory last year.
15But the militants have struck back at the oil-producing town of Baiji and in the western province of Anbar where its move on the provincial capital of Ramadi has displaced thousands of people.
16Baghdadi called on those who left Ramadi to return saying: "You are our people, we will defend you ... we want your safety and security."
17With the United States conducting air strikes against Islamic State positions, rumours have circulated that Baghdadi had been seriously wounded and had handed leadership over to a deputy.
