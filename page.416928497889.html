Nepal army rescues 117 people stranded in trekking villages after quake

1USGS says an earthquake measuring 7.4 magnitude and only 19 km deep stroke 68 km west of Namche Bazar.
2A magnitude 7.4 earthquake struck Nepal on Tuesday, the U.S. Geological Survey said, causing people to run out of buildings in the capital Kathmandu and shaking structures in the Indian capital.
3The quake, which struck near the base camp for Everest, was measured at a shallow depth of about 19 km.
4A 7.8-magnitude earthquake struck on April 25, killing atleast 8,046 people and injuring more than 17,800.
5The Nepali army has rescued 117 people from three remote mountain villages north of the capital Kathmandu, including two US citizens who had been searching for a missing relative, a military official said.
6The 115 Nepalis and two Americans were evacuated on Monday from Syanjen, Kenjing and Langtang Village, where hundreds of people were killed in a huge landslide and avalanche triggered by last month's earthquake.
7All of those rescued were stranded in the three popular trekking villages in Rasuwa district, about 60 km (35 miles) north of Kathmandu, after the 7.8 earthquake struck on April 25, killing at least 8,046 people and injured more than 17,800.
8A series of avalanches and bad weather in the area had slowed the military's efforts to reach those cut off.
9"The entire Langtang Valley has turned into a rocky and snowy ruin due to avalanches," Uddhav Bhattarai, Rasuwa's district administrator, told Reuters.
10On Sunday, the continuing avalanche risk led officials to suspend their search for some 200 bodies believed to be buried beneath the rock and snow in Langtang Village.
11As of Monday, 128 bodies had been recovered there.
12"Rescuers have been unable to go there to look for bodies since the weekend because avalanches continue to fall and make it dangerous," Bhattarai said.
13The US citizens rescued on Monday were with a Nepali guide, a military official said, and were brought to the district capital of Dhunche along with the Nepalis who were rescued.
14Two Adelaide mothers have travelled to Nepal to set up tents and distribute medical supplies to an orphanage that was heavily damaged in last month's devastating earthquake.
15Lynne Rawson and Jo Bourchier arrived in Kathmandu earlier this week, where they were reunited with Ishwor Ghimire, a Nepali teenager they both hosted as part of a program run by Adelaide's Pulteney Grammar School.
16Ghimire had helped bring more than 50 children to safety during the earthquake, which caused huge cracks in its walls making it too dangerous to live in.
17The children are now forced to sleep outside.
18Ghimire said it has been difficult to keep them warm and healthy living under nothing more than plastic covering.
19"The earthquake's destroyed the lives of people and if people start thinking negative, they will destroy themselves," he said.
20"So this is the time that we really need to work together hand-in-hand and move forward."
21Rawson, who is a mother of four, said she wanted to offer love and support to the children.
22"In a good world, everyone is equal but our world isn't always like that," she said.
23"I think these children are as special as any other child. They are as special as a child in Australia.
24"I think we need to look after every child and they should be treated equally. So, I would like to see that these kids get as much help as anybody else."
25She and her friend Jo Bourchier have vowed to stay and help at the orphanage for at least a month.
26The orphanage is run by Rajan Bishwokarma, known locally as Mother Rajan.
27Ms Bourchier said the orphanage is yet to received any help from the government or other organisations.
28"We're just hoping that we can lighten Mother Rajan's load," she said.
29"She has been under so much strain and I think she felt very alone and because they haven't had any help."
30The earthquake flattened or damaged more than 600,000 houses, with eight million of Nepal's 28 million people were affected by the quake, according to the United Nations.
31At least two million people are expected to be in need of tents, water, food and medicine over the next three months.
32Many countries have pledged money that will be necessary to rebuild homes, hospitals and historic buildings.
33The death toll has reached at least 7,759, and more than 16,400 people were injured.
34Body parts are strewn on the slopes of a mountain in Nepal, and up to 300 people, many of them foreigners, are believed buried there by an avalanche set off by last month's devastating earthquake, an official said on Thursday.
35Rescuers pulled out six bodies from the Langtang village area, 60 km north of Kathmandu, on Wednesday but operations were being hampered by bad weather, said Gautam Rimal, assistant district administrator in the area.
36About 100 bodies were recovered in the area on Saturday and Sunday and Rimal had previously said about 120 more were buried there.
37But on Thursday he said the number could be as high as 300, including 110 foreigners.
38The government has said 7,759 people were killed in the April 25 earthquake and more than 16,000 injured.
39Langtang is on a trekking route popular with Westerners and the village had 55 guesthouses catering for visitors.
40The village was wiped out by the avalanche but it was not clear how many people were there at the time.
41Bad weather has cut links with a remote village in Nepal where dozens of villagers and trekkers are believed to be buried under an avalanche set off by last month's devastating earthquake, officials said on Tuesday.
42The death toll from the April 25 earthquake in the Himalayan nation has reached 7,557, the government said.
43About 100 bodies were recovered on Saturday and Sunday at Langtang village, 60 km north of Kathmandu, which is on a trekking route popular with Westerners.
44The entire village, which includes 55 guesthouses for trekkers, was wiped out by the avalanche and rescuers are digging in the snow for signs of about 120 others believed buried.
45Gautam Rimal, assistant chief district officer in the area where Langtang is located, said authorities had not made contact with Langtang for more 24 hours because of bad weather.
46"We hope to send a rescue helicopter today (Tuesday) to the area," he said.
47"We'll know the situation then."
48The dead include at least seven foreigners, but only two had been identified, he said.
49The government has begun asking foreign teams to wrap up search and rescue operations as hopes of finding people alive in the rubble receded.
50"They can leave. If they are also specialists in clearing the rubble, they can stay," Rameshwor Dangal, an official at Nepal's home ministry, told Reuters on Monday.
51A European Union source said only about 60 citizens from the 28-nation bloc were still unaccounted for.
52Last week a senior EU official had estimated around 1,000 EU citizens were missing after the quake.
53The number is "going down by the hour" as rescue teams reach remoter areas, the EU source said.
54A US State Department spokesman said helicopters chartered by the embassy in Kathmandu had rescued 17 US citizens in total from remote areas hit by the quake.
55The United States has provided $14.2 million in humanitarian aid since the quake.
56The government has said it has not closed Mount Everest, the world's highest peak, to climbers, although the route to the summit has been damaged by the earthquake.
57At least 18 people were killed on Everest when the earthquake struck.
58Climbers pay $11,000 each to climb Everest, and 357 were registered for this climbing season.
59Last year, the government extended permits when teams abandoned their expeditions after an avalanche killed 16 Sherpa mountain guides.
60The United Nations has said 8 million of Nepal's 28 million people were affected by the quake, with at least 2 million needing tents, water, food and medicines over the next three months.
61The United Nations Children's Fund said more than half a million children were being vaccinated to prevent measles outbreaks.
62Around 1.7 million children remain in urgent need of humanitarian aid in the worst-hit areas, it added.
63The Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade is deploying a team to search for an Australian missing in Nepal, more than a week after a deadly earthquake rocked the country.
64In a statement issued to SBS, a DFAT spokeswoman said the department was concerned for the welfare of Tyronne White, who is currently unaccounted for in Nepal.
65"Officials in Canberra and Nepal have been providing consular assistance to Mr White's family in Australia," she said.
66"We are deploying a team of Australian officials to travel to Langtang village, Mr White's last known location, today which will supplement the two Australian officials already in the region.
67"We are liaising closely with Nepalese authorities and with officials from other countries, including the US, UK, Canada and Spain, to broaden and coordinate our ongoing efforts to confirm the welfare of Mr White."
68Brett White told SBS he's still waiting to hear from his brother Tyronne, who is believed to be trekking when the quake hit.
69He said in early April, his brother Tyronne told him via a Facebook message that he was setting out on a three-week trek between Kathmandu and the Chinese border.
70"I've tried to call everyone and anyone," he said.
71"I've called them [DFAT] frequently and they say they're working with the Americans and also the British and couple of other countries about handing his information over and they'll try to actually look for him."
72Brett White said he is considering flying over to Nepal but doesn't know if he will be much help.
73"I'm considering it but there are so many things happening over there, I'm not actually sure if I would be able to help or not," he said.
74Nepali police and local volunteers found the bodies of about 100 trekkers and villagers buried in an avalanche set off by last month's devastating earthquake and were digging through snow and ice for signs of dozens more missing, officials said on Monday.
75The bodies were recovered on Saturday and Sunday at the Langtang village, 60 kilometres(40 miles) north of Kathmandu, which is on a trekking route popular with Westerners.
76The entire village, which includes 55 guesthouses for trekkers, was wiped out by the avalanche, officials said.
77"Local volunteers and police personnel are digging through six-feet (deep) snow with shovels looking for more bodies," said Gautam Rimal, assistant chief district officer in the area where Langtang is located.
78The dead include at least 7 foreigners but only two had been identified, he said.
79It was not clear how many people were in Langtang at the time of the avalanche but other officials said about 120 more people could be buried under the snow.
80"We had not been able to reach the area earlier because of rains and cloudy weather," Uddhav Bhattarai, the district's senior bureaucrat, said by telephone on Sunday.
81The April 25 earthquake has killed 7,276 people and wounded over 14,300, Nepal's government said.
82In other parts of the Himalayan nation, three people were pulled alive from the rubble of their home on Sunday, eight days after the earthquake, while several media outlets reported that a 101-year-old man was found alive in the rubble on Saturday.
83US military aircraft and personnel arrived in Nepal on Sunday and were due to begin helping ferry relief supplies to stricken areas outside the capital, a US Marines spokeswoman said.
84The contingent comprised eight aircraft, including one Huey and two C-130s, and between 100 and 120 personnel, spokeswoman Captain Cassandra Gesecki said.
85The Huey was expected to leave on an assessment mission early on Monday, and it was up to the U.S. Agency for International Development (USAID) when the other aircraft would be deployed.
86"They're the ones telling us what to take and where," she said.
87On Sunday, the government restricted the landing of large cargo aircraft at the airport to limit damage to the stressed runway, said a UN official who declined to be named.
88A 101-year-old man has been pulled alive from the rubble of his house in Nepal, seven days after it collapsed in a deadly earthquake.
89The man was rescued on Saturday and is now in hospital, according to Arun Kumar Singh, a local police official.
90On Sunday two women and a man were rescued alive in the country's north-east.
91"Kanchan Khatri, Gyan Kumari Khatri, and Dhan Kumari Khatri were rescued by security forces (on Sunday) at Kerabari village in the Syauli area," Ram Bahadur Nepali, a police officer in the north-eastern district of Sindhupalchowk, told DPA.
92He said two of them were buried under a collapsed mud house, while one was pulled from under the earth after being swept by a landslide triggered by the earthquake.
93They have been evacuated to the district hospital.
