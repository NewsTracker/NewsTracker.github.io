Millionaire Robert Durst returning to court in New Orleans

1Durst was arrested at the J.W. Marriott hotel in New Orleans, where he had registered under the name Everette Ward and was lying low while HBO aired the final chapters of his life story.
2FBI agents recovered his passport and birth certificate, an apparently fake Texas ID, stacks of $100 bills, bags of marijuana, a .38-caliber revolver, a map folded to show Louisiana and Cuba, and a flesh-toned latex mask with salt-and-pepper hair.
3"This was not a mask for Halloween;" it was a disguise extending down to the chest, Assistant District Attorney Mark Burton said.
4Durst stopped using his cellphone after HBO aired the next-to-last episode of the "The Jinx," its six-part documentary about him, and bought but apparently never activated a new cellphone, Burton added.
5Magistrate Harry Cantrell ordered Durst held without bond on weapons charges in Louisiana, and set a preliminary hearing for April 2, delaying his transfer to California to face murder charges.
6The 71-year-old millionaire sat beside his lawyers with his hands shackled to his sides in padded cuffs.
7He has been in a prison's mental health unit for nearly a week.
8Jail officials have called him a suicide risk.
9In Vermont on Monday, authorities said they were investigating the link between Durst and a Middlebury College student who disappeared in 1971.
10An estranged member of the wealthy New York real estate family that runs 1 World Trade Center, Durst is accused of killing Susan Berman in 2000 before she could speak with former New York prosecutor Jeanine Pirro's investigators about the disappearance of Durst's first wife, Kathleen, in 1982.
11FBI agents caught up with Durst the day before HBO's finale, after Los Angeles police said he had used a hotel phone to check his email.
12FBI agents found him in the lobby, asked for his ID, and escorted him to his room, where he told them he had his passport, testified Jim O'Herne, an Orleans Parish district attorney investigator.
13An FBI agent listed everything Durst had in the room to protect both the agency and Durst, who was arrested on the murder warrant later that evening, O'Herne said.
14O'Herne said he was able to wake up a judge about 1:30 or 2:30 a.m. the next morning to sign a search warrant for Los Angeles police.
15Defense attorney Dick DeGuerin said the timing of the agent's inventory proves the search was illegal.
16"That's an improper search," he told the judge.
17Burton said the legality of the arrest was outside the scope of Monday's hearing.
18Outside court, DeGuerin said he never expected the magistrate to set bond.
19"We were able to get a lot of information we didn't have before," DeGuerin said.
20"I think all in all we had a very good day."
