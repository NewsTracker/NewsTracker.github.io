Israeli leader gets 2-week extension to form coalition

1Netanyahu told the Israeli president he has made progress in negotiations with potential partners but needs more time to form a stable coalition.
2The Israeli leader has held weeks of negotiations with his nationalist, religious and ultra-Orthodox Jewish allies.
3Netanyahu looks close to finalizing deals with two ultra-Orthodox parties and the centrist, economics-focused Kulanu party.
4But large gaps remain with two nationalist parties led by long-time Netanyahu associates.
5Israeli President Reuven Rivlin granted Netanyahu the extension Monday.
6Under Israeli election rules, if Netanyahu fails to form a coalition after that time, Rivlin can task someone else to do so.
