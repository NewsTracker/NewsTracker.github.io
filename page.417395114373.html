Nigerian army repels Boko Haram as new president starts term

1Nigeria's military on Saturday repelled a Boko Haram attack on the key north-eastern city of Maiduguri, hours after President Muhammadu Buhari took office vowing to crush the Islamist group.
2"RPGs kept flying and falling on homes," he said.
3While some residents described watching bodies being recovered from targeted homes, no-one was able to provide a precise death toll.
4In his inaugural speech after taking the oath of office on Friday, Mr Buhari described Nigeria's Islamist rebels as a "mindless" and "godless" group that would ultimately be destroyed.
5He announced plans to reinforce Maiduguri with a new command and control centre to better co-ordinate the counter-insurgency effort, a move analysts said signalled his commitment to intensifying the fight.
6It was not clear if Boko Haram's fresh assault on the strategically crucial city was timed to coincide with the inauguration.
7But the new president will likely be tested repeatedly in the coming months by a militant group that has proved resilient over its six-year uprising.
8Shortly after midnight, residents in Dala woke to the sound of RPGs being fired in succession, resident Modu Karumi said, in an account supported by several others.
9Witnesses said hundreds of Islamist gunmen were trying to advance on the city, which is now home to hundreds of thousands of people displaced by unrest in other parts of Borno state.
10"Five houses were destroyed," and Islamist gunmen fired indiscriminately in Dala and two neighbouring suburbs, said Babagana Bulunkutu, who describes himself as a vigilante.
11An AFP reporter who lives in the area said he heard what sounded like armoured personnel carriers deploying to the southern edge of the city to face the rebel advance.
12Three senior security sources in Maiduguri who were not authorised to speak publicly said the attack had been repelled.
13"All is under control. There is no cause for alarm," one of those sources told AFP.
14Analysts doubt that Boko Haram currently has the capacity to seize Maiduguri, but a major attack inside the city would likely be disastrous for civilians.
15The Islamist rebels have been flushed out of several Borno state towns they controlled in an offensive launched in February by Nigeria with backing from neighbouring Cameroon, Chad and Niger.
16But there are signs of the militants regrouping, particularly in the remote parts of eastern Borno near the Cameroon border.
17Mr Buhari in his inaugural speech noted the successes of the four-nation offensive but said Boko Haram would not be defeated until operational command was shifted from the capital Abuja to Maiduguri.
18"This denotes a more hands-on approach to the fight against Boko Haram," said Yan St-Pierre, head of the Modern Security Consulting Group, describing the move as "very sound."
19Mr Buhari's predecessor, Goodluck Jonathan, was heavily criticised for his performance against the militants, with the conflict killing more than 15,000 people since 2009 and forcing another 1.5 million from their homes.
