Egypt steps up security after foiling suicide bomb attack in Luxor

1The scene of the foiled suicide attack outside the Temple of Karnak in Luxor.
2Egyptian police said they foiled an attempted suicide bomb attack Wednesday on one of the country's most popular tourist destinations in Luxor, killing two militants.
3Police said one attacker detonated a bomb and two other assailants died in a firefight close to the famed Karnak temple in Luxor, a town 700 kilometres south of Cairo.
4The Temple of Karnak in Luxor.
5A source at the interior ministry said no tourists were hurt.
6Luxor police said officers opened fire on three men after they had had refused to undergo security screening at a checkpoint outside the temple, shooting two of them dead.
7The third attacker managed to detonate a bomb he was carrying, according to officers on the scene.
8Local health ministry official Nahed Mohamed told AFP there were "two militants killed and one wounded" in the foiled attack, adding that two civilians and two policemen were also wounded.
9The police kept visitors at the temple inside the site during the operation, a senior antiquities ministry official told AFP.
10"Security forces in Luxor foiled a terrorist operation ... Two terrorists killed and a third was wounded," the police said in a statement.
11Jihadists have claimed regular bomb attacks in Egypt since the army's ouster of Islamist president Mohamed Mursi in July 2013 and a brutal crackdown on his supporters.
12Tourists have been largely spared from attacks although a suicide bombing last year on a tour bus in the restive Sinai Peninsula, home of the local branch of the jihadist Islamic State group, killed three South Koreans and their Egyptian driver.
13Karnak was built on the ruins of Thebes, one of the capitals of ancient Egypt.
14Its huge temple is dedicated to the god Amon.
15Tourism in Egypt has faltered since early 2011, when a popular uprising toppled longtime strongman Hosni Mubarak after three decades in power.
16Years of instability and a rising tide of attacks claimed by jihadists have scared off would-be visitors, damaging the economy and sending Egypt's foreign currency reserves plunging.
17Luxor has had to deal with numerous blows, including a deadly hot air balloon crash in 2013 that killed 19 tourists.
18The town took several years to recover from a 1997 massacre when Islamist gunmen opened fire on tourists at an ancient temple complex, killing 58 foreigners and their four Egyptian guards.
