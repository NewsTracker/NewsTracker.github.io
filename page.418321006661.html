Kuwait detains 26 over Islamic State suicide attack on mosque -newspaper

1KUWAIT: Kuwait has detained 26 people suspected of involvement in a suicide bombing on a Shia Muslim mosque last month that killed 27 people, a local newspaper reported on Monday, quoting the public prosecutor.
2The June 26 attack by Islamic State militants jolted Kuwait, raising the spectre of sectarian strife in the Gulf Arab oil exporting state.
3The attack prompted the government to declare it was at war with Islamist militants and that it would strike at cells believed to be on its soil.
4The prosecutor, Dherar al-Asousi, said four women were among the 26 suspects detained for possible links to the attack, the Kuwaiti Arabic-language al-Qabs newspaper said.
5Asousi said the suspects had been detained for 10 days.
6He said other suspects, including fighters with Islamic State abroad, have been identified and that some of their relatives inside Kuwait have been charged in connection with the blast.
7Asousi gave no numbers and provided no further details.
8Al-Rai newspaper, another Arabic-language Kuwaiti daily, said last week that 10 suspects, among them Saudis, Kuwaitis and stateless residents of Kuwait, had been referred to the public prosecution, a move that indicates a criminal case has been opened.
9Among those 10 are five principal suspects accused of helping the suicide bomber, a Saudi, to carry out the attack, the newspaper said.
10Kuwaiti officials have said the attack was aimed at stirring up sectarian conflict in the majority Sunni Muslim emirate, where the two sects have traditionally coexisted in peace.
11As authorities tightened security, parliament last Wednesday approved a law put forward by the interior ministry to create a DNA registry of Kuwaiti nationals and residents living in the Gulf state.
