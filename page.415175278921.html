Archaeologists in Egypt discover 3000-year-old tomb in Luxor, beautifully painted

1Tuesday's statement says the tomb, found near the southern city of Luxor, dates back to the New Kingdom of the 18th Dynasty - the most famous of ancient Egypt dynasties.
2The nobleman's name was Amenhotep.
3Minister of Antiquities Mamdouh el-Damaty says the tomb contains "stunning scenes with bright colors" painted on the walls.
4He says the scenes depict the tomb owner and his wife in front of an offering table, as well as scenes of daily life, such as hunting.
5The ministry says the tomb was discovered by the American Research Center's team.
6It gave no date for the discovery.
