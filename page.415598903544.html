Zayn Malik flies home from One Direction tour with stress

1In this Nov. 23, 2014 file photo Liam Payne, from left, Niall Horan, Louis Tomlinson, Zayn Malik and Harry Styles of the musical group One Direction arrive at the 42nd annual American Music Awards at Nokia Theatre L.A. Live in Los Angeles.
2LONDON (AP) - One Direction is one man down on its world tour.
3The boy band's publicist says a stressed Zayn Malik is flying home to Britain.
4Publicist Simon Jones said Thursday that "Zayn has been signed off with stress and is flying back to the U.K. to recuperate."
5He said the band will continue with upcoming performances in Manila, Philippines, and Jakarta, Indonesia, as part of the "On the Road Again" tour.
6It was not clear whether Malik would rejoin the tour for three scheduled dates in South Africa starting March 28.
7Malik's departure comes a day after he hit back on Twitter at rumors of trouble in his relationship with fiancee Perrie Edwards of the band Little Mix after he was photographed with another woman.
8He tweeted: "I'm 22 years old ... I love a girl named Perrie Edwards."
9Many fans in the Philippines were heartbroken Malik would not be in Manila.
10Patrisha Yacob, a 14-year-old high school student who spent 15,000 pesos ($335) - more than the country's monthly minimum wage - for a concert ticket, told The Associated Press on Friday that she cried when she found out Malik will not be performing.
11"My sunshine is not complete. I thought I will see them all but now I will only see only four members," added Yacob.
12Another fan, Rachel Marcelino, a graduating college student who could not afford a ticket, believes concert producers should give ticket buyers a discount because the band is incomplete.
13She also expressed disappointment with Malik's "shallow" reason for not coming to Manila.
14"Some artist when they get sick, they still perform," she added.
15The Philippines earlier this week imposed a $5,000 "weed bond" on Malik and fellow band member Louis Tomlinson, with the money to be forfeited if either of them was caught using or promoting illegal drugs while in the country.
16A video that circulated online last year appeared to show Malik and Tomlinson smoking cannabis and joking about drug use.
17Christine Ching, spokeswoman of the local Anti-Drugs Advocate group that petitioned authorities to strictly scrutinize the band's concert permits because of the video, said "we were very disappointed and saddened" that Malik is not arriving.
18But she said that he's skipping Manila may be more likely due to issues with his girlfriend, not the drugs controversy.
