Islamic State 'controls Palmyra'

1Islamic State militants are in full control of the famed archaeological site in the ancient town of Palmyra in central Syria, according to activists.
2The extremists overran the site to the south-west of the town shortly after midnight, said Rami Abdurrahman of the Britain-based Syrian Observatory for Human Rights.
3Bebars al-Talawy, an activist in the central province of Homs, also said IS controls the ruins at Palmyra, a Unesco world heritage site famous for its 2,000-year-old Roman-era colonnades and other ruins and priceless artefacts.
4Both activists said IS has not damaged the ruins so far.
5There are fears the extremists will destroy the ruins as they did major archaeological sites in Iraq.
