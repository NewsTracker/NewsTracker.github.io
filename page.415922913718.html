Wife of imprisoned Greek ex-defense minister flees hospital

1The wife of an imprisoned former Greek defense minister escaped from a psychiatric hospital where she was serving her sentence on the same corruption charges as her husband, police said Thursday.
2Vicky Stamati, wife of former Socialist Party stalwart Akis Tsochatzopoulos, was found missing from her guarded cell in the Dromokaitio state psychiatric institution in the early hours of Thursday morning.
3Police said they were searching for the woman in Athens, and an investigation was ordered into how Stamati escaped.
4Her escape came the day after another of her multiple appeals to be released from prison was rejected, one of the lawyers who represented her, Alexis Cougias, told local media.
5Stamati was initially imprisoned in 2012 and was convicted in 2013 along with her husband, step-daughter and more than a dozen others in a military procurement corruption case against Tsochatzopoulos.
6Stamati was deemed to have known about bribes her husband accepted.
7She was sentenced to 12 years for money laundering and accepting illegal funds.
8An appeals trial has not yet been completed.
9In poor health, she underwent a double mastectomy shortly after the trial while imprisoned.
10She was transferred to the psychiatric hospital in December 2013 after suffering a breakdown, attempting suicide and being found to be suffering from severe depression.
11Stamati has appealed five times to judicial authorities to be released from prison, citing health grounds and the grounds that her young child is now being brought up by her elderly mother.
12All appeals have so far been rejected.
13The trial against Tsochatzopoulos was Greece's biggest corruption trial in decades.
14The ex-minister, once one of the most powerful men in the then dominant socialist PASOK party, served as defense minister from 1996-2001.
15He was initially sentenced to the maximum 20 years in prison.
16His appeals trial is ongoing.
