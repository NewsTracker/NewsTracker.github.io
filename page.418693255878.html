Funeral held for F1 driver Bianchi

1The funeral of Jules Bianchi is being held in his hometown of Nice as tributes to the French Formula 1 driver continue to be paid.
2Family, friends and fellow drivers are attending the service at the city's cathedral while fans of the 25-year-old have gathered outside.
3He died on Friday from result of head injuries in a crash at the 2014 Japanese Grand Prix.
4His car number, 17, is being retired from F1 in his honour.
5Bianchi had been in a coma since crashing his Marussia car into a recovery vehicle at a rain-hit race last October.
6He is the first F1 driver to die from injuries sustained in a grand prix since Brazilian triple world champion Ayrton Senna was killed at the San Marino Grand Prix in 1994.
7Tributes are expected to be paid at Hungarian Grand Prix this weekend.
8A number of Formula 1 colleagues travelled to Nice to pay their respects on Tuesday.
9Large posters of Bianchi were draped outside the cathedral entrance.
10"Direction Nice to say goodbye to our colleague, friend Jules," Lotus driver Romain Grosjean posted on Twitter.
11Other drivers attending included Lewis Hamilton, Nico Rosberg, Felipe Massa and Olivier Panis.
12Announcing his death on Saturday, Bianchi's family said: "Jules fought right to the very end, as he always did, but today his battle came to an end."
13Former world champion Jenson Button led tributes from the world of motorsport.
14"Last night we lost a truly great guy and a real fighter. RIP Jules, my sincerest condolences to his family and friends," the McLaren driver said on Saturday.
15The Marussia team, now known as Manor, said Bianchi had left an "indelible mark on all our lives".
16The accident happened when Bianchi's car slid off the wet Suzuka track and into a crane picking up the Sauber of German driver Adrian Sutil, who had crashed at the same spot one lap earlier.
17He suffered massive head injuries and was treated in intensive care in Japan before being transferred to a hospital in Nice in November.
18The 25-year-old competed in 34 races during the 2013 and 2014 seasons, scoring the first-ever championship points for the Manor-Marussia team by finishing ninth at last year's Monaco Grand Prix.
