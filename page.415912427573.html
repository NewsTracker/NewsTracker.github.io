Tikrit victory boosts Iraq fight to defeat IS

1Tikrit (Iraq) (AFP) - Iraq vowed Wednesday to reclaim the entire country from jihadists after retaking Tikrit from the Islamic State group, as its forces fought to consolidate control of the city.
2Speaking from a newly-recaptured area of central Tikrit after a month-long operation that was Baghdad's biggest success yet against IS, Prime Minister Haider al-Abadi said the government was "determined to liberate every inch of Iraqi land".
3But Tikrit is far from secure, with various officials saying that IS militants were still present in the city, while bomb-rigged houses and buildings also pose a major threat.
4Interior Minister Mohammed al-Ghaban told journalists in Tikrit that there were "a few pockets (of IS fighters) remaining in some of the neighbourhoods".
5And a commander from the Ketaeb Imam Ali militia, one of the paramilitary forces fighting alongside police and soldiers, reported clashes with IS in the city's north in the morning.
6They "tried to advance on the university", Rasul al-Abadi told AFP, referring to Tikrit University on the northern edge of the city that has served as a base for pro-government forces.
7Ghaban said IS fighters were trying to cross the Tigris river, east of the city, to escape.
8Supporting that assertion, a paramilitary commander said the jihadists launched an attack Wednesday from a mountain hideout northeast of Tikrit in an attempt to open a safe passage for fleeing militants.
9There has been concern that Iraq does not have enough specialised ordnance clearance teams to handle the large quantity of traps left by IS fighters.
10Ghaban said security forces had so far found 185 rigged houses and about 900 other bombs.
11Exploding bombs periodically sent clouds of dust rising over Tikrit, apparently detonated intentionally by forces working to clear the city.
12One militiaman said he fired a rocket-propelled grenade to dispose of a suspected car bomb in central Tikrit.
13Columns of black smoke rose from various burning houses and buildings in the city.
14Some of the dozens of palaces in dictator Saddam Hussein's massive complex on the eastern side of the city were smashed by shelling or air strikes, but other areas of Tikrit were comparatively lightly damaged.
15Militiamen have quickly set about spray painting the names of their groups on walls and windows, and also looted items including clothes, shampoo and shaving cream from shops in central Tikrit.
16There is still evidence of IS's almost 10-month presence in the city, including the group's black flag painted on walls and writing identifying some buildings as "properties" of the jihadists' self-declared state.
17On Tuesday, Abadi claimed the city was retaken but the US-led coalition supporting Baghdad from the air said IS still held parts of Tikrit.
18Defence Minister Khaled al-Obeidi vowed Wednesday to press on with offensive operations.
19"We are coming, Anbar. We are coming, Nineveh," he said in a recorded address, referring to the last two provinces still largely controlled by IS.
20The loss of Tikrit further isolates Mosul, the capital of Nineveh and the main IS hub in Iraq, with Baghdad's forces now poised to push north while Kurdish forces close in from the three other directions.
21Zaid al-Ali, author of "The Struggle For Iraq's Future", said however that the fighting in Tikrit was made easier because the city was largely emptied of its population even before the operation began on March 2.
22"Mosul still has a large civilian population, which will make things very complicated," the analyst said.
23The government has provided no information on how many of its fighters were killed, wounded or captured in the battle, but Baghdad's forces are believed to have suffered significant casualties.
24The army and police, as well as pro-government paramilitary forces dominated by Iran-backed Shiite militias, completely surrounded Tikrit within two weeks of launching the operation, but it then stalled and Abadi requested coalition strikes, which began on March 25.
25Iran was Baghdad's top foreign partner for much of the operation -- involvement evidenced by graffiti in Persian in the city.
26The Iraqi government had tried and failed several times to retake Tikrit, but the latest operation was much larger and better organised.
27When Iraqi forces retook Saddam's palace complex, they also found sites believed to contain the bodies of mainly-Shiite recruits executed by IS last year in a massacre that stoked widespread anger and helped rally support for the battle against the jihadists.
