Germanwings plane crashes in southern France - police

1Two Australians are confirmed as being among 150 people killed when an Airbus operated by Lufthansa's Germanwings budget airline crashed in a remote snowy area of the French Alps on Tuesday.
2Carol Friday and her son Greig Friday, from Melbourne, were named by Foreign Affairs Minister Julie Bishop on Wednesday afternoon.
3"Sadly, I can confirm that there were two Australian citizens on board, a mother and her adult son from Victoria," she said on Wednesday morning.
4The pulverised wreckage of flight 4U9525, which was travelling from Barcelona in Spain to Dusseldorf in Germany with 144 passengers and six crew, has now been located in the Alps by emergency workers.
5The crash did not appear to have been caused by a terrorist attack, the US White House said, while Lufthansa confirmed it was working on the assumption that the tragedy had been an accident, adding that any other theory would be speculation.
6A rescue helicopter flies over debris of the Germanwings passenger jet, scattered on the mountainside, near Seyne-les-Alpes in the French Alps.
7Emergency workers at the scene found no survivors, and fragments of the plane "the size of a car".
8"We saw an aircraft that had literally been ripped apart; the bodies are in a state of destruction, there is not one intact piece of wing or fuselage," Bruce Robin, prosecutor for the city of Marseilles, told Reuters in Seyne-les-Alpes after flying over the crash zone in a helicopter.
9Aerial footage of the crash site, near the town of Barcelonnette about 100 kilometres north of the French Riviera city of Nice, showed fragments of debris scattered over a large area on the sides of mountains.
10Smashed remnants of the Germanwings passenger jet scattered near Seyne-les-Alpes, French Alps.
11"It is going to take days to recover the victims, then the debris," senior police officer Jean-Paul Bloy told Reuters.
12Prime Minister Tony Abbott said consular officials were doing what they could for the families.
13"Our thoughts and prayers are with the friends and families of all those killed, particularly with the loved ones of the two Australians who have lost their lives," he said.
14Family members of people involved in the Germanwings plane crash comfort each other as they arrive at Barcelona airport in Spain.
15Foreign Minister Julie Bishop said Australia would help in the recovery effort.
16The disaster is one of several international air crashes to kill Australians in just 18 months.
17The location of the Germanwings flight 4U9525 crash in the French Alps.
18French Interior Minister Bernard Cazeneuve said one of the black boxes from the plane had been found.
19"A black box that we found a few hours after the crash will immediately be examined to help the investigation move forward quickly," he said.
20After night fell in southern France on Tuesday, emergency services stopped helicopter flights to the crash site, due to safety concerns.
21A rescue helicopter from the French Securite Civile flies over the French Alps following the crash of an Airbus A320.
22Police said search teams would stay overnight at altitude.
23"We are still searching. It's unlikely any bodies will be airlifted until Wednesday," regional police chief David Galtier told Reuters.
24Rescue and crash investigation work will begin again at first light.
25Ms Bishop said Australian consular officials would set up a mobile office in the French town of Gap, near the crash site, to help with the identification and recovery of the bodies.
26Thomas Winkelmann, managing director of Germanwings, speaks during a news conference in Cologne, Germany, on Tuesday.
27Photo: Bloomberg.
28"We will provide all consular assistance, we will be involved in the recovery effort, we are setting up a mobile office at a location near the crash site," she said.
29Details of the flight's short journey are now beginning to emerge, though no cause has yet been identified.
30Investigations will focus on why the plane did not issue a distress call before it began a descent and eventually crashed.
31Students light candles at the Joseph-Koenig Gymnasium in Haltern, Germany.
32Sixteen school children and two teachers from Haltern are among the victims.
33Flight tracking data shows that, half an hour into the flight, about 9.30am GMT, the 24-year-old plane rapidly lost altitude.
34Germanwings said the plane started descending one minute after reaching its cruising height and continued losing altitude for eight minutes.
35"The aircraft's contact with French radar, French air traffic controllers ended at 10.53am at an altitude of about 6000 feet [1.8 kilometres]. The plane then crashed," Germanwings' managing director Thomas Winkelmann told journalists.
36German Chancellor Angela Merkel addresses the media in Berlin following the plane crash on Tuesday.
37First image of Germanwings crash site revealed.
38Experts said that that, while the Airbus had descended rapidly, its rate of descent did not suggest it had simply fallen out of the sky.
39France's DGAC aviation authority said air traffic controllers initiated distress procedures after they lost contact with the Airbus.
40"The aircraft did not itself make a distress call but it was the combination of the loss of radio contact and the aircraft's descent which led the controller to implement the distress phase," a DGAC spokesman said.
41In emergencies, pilots are trained to try to fly the aircraft as their first priority, then to pay attention to navigation and only then to communicate with the ground.
42The Airbus 320 had been in continuous operation with Lufthansa and then Germanwings since 1991.
43It had a routine check in Dusseldorf the day before the crash, and a regular extensive inspection in mid-2013.
44Nothing irregular had been identified - although the airlines will now review their inspection logbooks.
45The captain had more than 10 years' experience, including 6000 flight-hours on Airbus models.
46The A320 is a workhorse of worldwide aviation fleets.
47It is the world's most used passenger jet and has a good, though not unblemished, safety record.
48Mr Winkelmann also said that routine maintenance of the aircraft was performed by Lufthansa Technik on Monday.
49Ms Bishop addressed the Australian media on Wednesday morning to confirm that at least two Australians were among the dead, but declined to provide further personal information.
50"It would not be appropriate to disclose further details about our citizens at this stage, due to the privacy considerations of the family," she said.
51Ms Bishop said her department was now working to confirm whether there were any other citizens, dual nationals or Australian residents on board the flight.
52Also among the victims were 16 children and two teachers from the Joseph-Koenig-Gymnasium high school in the town of Haltern am See in north-west Germany, who were returning from a short exchange program in Spain.
53Students and teachers at the school broke down in tears after receiving the news on Tuesday, and many attended a candlelight vigil in the afternoon.
54Opera stars bass-baritone Oleg Bryjak, 54, and contralto Maria Radner, 33, were also on board, flying to their home city of Duesseldorf after starring in Richard Wagner's Siegfried at Barcelona's opera house, the Gran Teatre del Liceu.
55Radner was travelling with her husband and baby, the BBC reported.
56Lufthansa vice-president Heike Birlenbach said the biggest priorities for the airline were to support relatives and friends of the victims, and to focus on investigating the crash.
57The airline was looking at whether it was feasible to fly relatives to the scene of the accident, and they would be told on Wednesday whether it was possible.
58She could not give the nationalities of the passengers, she said, because the airline didn't want to "just assume by name" whether they were Spanish, German or another nationality.
59As it was a flight within Europe, passengers did not necessarily need to produce their passports to board the flight.
60French President Francois Hollande said he could not be sure if there were French passengers on board the flight, describing the crash as a "terrible event".
61"We are in mourning," Mr Hollande said.
62The accident is the first air disaster in France since the Concorde tragedy in 2000 in which 113 died.
63In 2009, Air France flight 447 crashed into the Atlantic Ocean, killing all 228 on board.
64In a press conference, German Chancellor Angela Merkel said: "We are shocked by the extent of the accident, which has brought disaster to so many people.
65"We are going through hard hours."
66Her thoughts and sympathies went to all those affected, families and friends of the victims.
67She had spoken to Mr Hollande and the Spanish Prime Minister, Mariano Rajoy, who were "doing their utmost to investigate the cause of the accident", Ms Merkel said.
68The German foreign and transport ministers were on their way to the crash area.
69"This is a time and an hour of great sorrow, of great grief," she said.
70"We should be thinking about the people who have lost relatives and friends."
71US President Barack Obama expressed his sympathy for the people of Europe during a press conference in Washington on Tuesday afternoon.
72"It is particularly heartbreaking because it apparently includes the loss of so many children, some of them infants," he said.
73Mr Obama said America stood with Germany and Spain in this moment of sorrow, and said he would offer any assistance needed.
74Le Parisien reported a witness from the area who said she "heard a big thud and then I saw a column of smoke".
75The woman from the town of Vernet, near the crash scene, said the noise was a little like the sound of dynamite sometimes used to trigger avalanches.
76"Around noon I began to see a column of smoke rise into the air."
77A resident of the Pra Loup ski resort told the BBC her husband had seen a plane flying low over the mountains and she had heard a sound like a plane "going down very fast".
78She said she did not hear any explosion.
79A witness who was skiing near the crash site told a French television channel he "heard an enormous noise" about the time of the disaster.
80A French police helicopter dispatched to the site of the crash reported spotting debris in a mountain range known as Les Trois Eveches, which reaches an altitude of 1400 metres.
81High winds and snow are forecast for the area over the next two days, adding to the challenge for rescue workers and crash investigators.
82Also, this year has seen a particularly dangerous avalanche season in the Alps.
83According to the Aviation Safety Network, which collects data on fatal crashes of aircraft carrying more than 14 passengers, there were 20 crashes last year, making it the safest year to fly since 1942, when ASN's dataset starts.
84The figures don't include Malaysia Airlines Flight 17, shot down over eastern Ukraine as the 298 victims are regarded as casualties of war.
85The International Air Transport Association reported a total of 73 airline accidents, including 12 fatal accidents, in 2014, down from 81 accidents, 16 of which were fatal, in 2013, according to the Los Angeles Times.
86Equivalent of one accident per 4.4 million flights.
87The previous lowest accident rate was 2012 when it was one accident per 3.6 million flights.
88Although the accident rate dropped to a record low, the number of fatalities rose significantly to 641 last year, compared with 210 fatalities in 2013, the report said.
