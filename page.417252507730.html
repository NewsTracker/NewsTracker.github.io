Carl Edwards races to first victory for new JGR team (Yahoo Sports)

1CONCORD, N.C. (AP) -- Even a master strategist like Joe Gibbs wasn't sure his team made the right call.
2The Super Bowl-winning coach, owner of Joe Gibbs Racing, had followed driver Denny Hamlin who was on top down the stretch in the Coca-Cola 600.
3But when Hamlin went to the pits to fix a wheel vibration, Gibbs saw team newcomer Carl Edwards was out front as his fuel was dwindling down.
4''I turned around with nine to go and said, 'We can't make this, can we?''' Gibbs said.
5Yes, they could.
6Edwards outlasted Greg Biffle over the final 20 laps to win the Coca-Cola 600 and take his first victory since joining JGR this season.
7''I just had this feeling that we were gearing up for some really great stuff,'' Edwards said.
8Edwards is the third Gibbs racer with a victory this season, joining Matt Kenseth and Denny Hamlin as likely entrants for the 10-race, end-of-the-season championship chase.
9Still, it took crew chief Darian Grubb's call to pit earlier than most and stay out on the track while others scrambled to tank up and get fresh tires.
10''We were not going to win this race on pure speed.
11That was obvious the first 550 miles or so,'' Edwards said.
12''So (Grubb) put us in a position to win.
13''It may be a fuel mileage win, but it's still whoever gets from the start-finish line to the start-finish line over 600 miles the fastest,'' Grubb said.
14Edwards hadn't won in 31 races since June 2014 at Sonoma.
15He ended his long association with Roush Fenway Racing this past offseason, hoping for bigger things with JGR.
16He got it at Charlotte Motor Speedway after shuffling in front as most of his competitors needed green-flag pit stops close to the end.
17Most figured Edwards would have to stop, too, but he remained on the track and cruised to his first victory at Charlotte.
18Greg Biffle was second, followed by Dale Earnhardt Jr., pole-sitter Matt Kenseth and Martin Truex Jr., who had his 11th top-10 finish in 12 events this season.
19Edwards parked his car, did his signature back flip and rushed into the waiting arms of his crew, all jumping around in celebration.
20He climbed into the stands to grab the checkered flag before heading to victory lane.
21The win gave JGR a Charlotte sweep after Denny Hamlin won the All-Star Race on May 16th.
22Hamlin looked like he might gain the sweep on his own, leading the pack with less than 50 laps remaining.
23But Hamlin felt a vibration in a wheel and had no choice but to head to the pits to straighten out - costing him his chance at the victory.
24Hamlin finished eighth, then went to the care center to get checked out of complaining of migraine headaches.
25He returned to congratulate Edwards, feeling better after taking fluids.
26The fourth Joe Gibbs racer, new father Kyle Busch, was 11th in his first points race since breaking his right leg and left in a crash at Daytona three months ago.
27Edwards capped racing's biggest day.
28It began when Nico Rosberg got his third straight win at the Monaco Grand Prix.
29It continued in Indianapolis when one-time NASCAR driver Juan Pablo Montoya won the Indy 500 for the second time.
30Edwards joins Hamlin and Kenseth with wins this season, all figuring to make the Sprint Cup season-ending chase for the championship.
31Biffle had his highest finish of the year after struggling to crack the top 10 all season.
32He said he tracked down Edwards despite his crew telling him to save fuel.
33''I got pretty close to him with two to go, but then the fuel light came on that the pressure was low,'' Biffle said.
34Earnhardt had gambled on fuel again as he had here in 2011 when he famously ran out of juice on the final lap as Kevin Harvick passed him for the win.
35''I was hoping the guys in front of me would run out of gas,'' Earnhardt said.
36''We tried to go for a win.
37We didn't have a fast enough car so we had to do something different.
38Truex, of the one-horse Furniture Row Racing operation, led the most laps with 131.
39He, too, got shuffled back after having to pit late.
40''They gave me an awesome race car.
41I'm so proud of them.
42It hurts to come home fifth and run that hard and lead that many laps,'' he said.
43''But at the same time it's pretty awesome to run like that.
44Ryan Newman was sixth, followed by Brad Keselowski, Hamlin and points leader Harvick.
45Kurt Busch, who dominated the early part of the race with 118 laps led, ended up 10th.
46Jeff Gordon had hoped for a strong showing in his final Coca-Cola 600, yet could not find a rhythm at the end of a long day that began in Indianapolis as he drove the pace car for that race.
47Gordon's hopes faded quickly as he got shuffled back to 29th and never found the right groove to get back into contention.
48He finished 15th.
49Gordon's teammate, six-time Sprint Cup champion Jimmie Johnson, is accustomed to waving his arms in celebration at 1 1/2-mile layouts - he'd won three of the four previous events at that distance this season - instead of watching the action from the pits.
50But the defending Coca-Cola 600 champion couldn't overcome a racecar that had not handled to his liking much of the night.
51He spun out while racing alone on lap 90 and survived that as he slid without hitting anything.
52He wasn't so lucky later on, sliding near the same spot and this time striking head on with the inside pit wall.
53Johnson took the No.
5448 car quickly to the garage for major repairs.
