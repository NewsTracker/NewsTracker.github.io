PICTURED: Frantic bid to save woman after whale crashed into side of boat in Mexico

1The Canadian tourist Jennifer Karren, 35, was part of a group of nine visitors on a snorkel tour in Cabo San Lucas, Mexico, when the sudden collision took place.
2Cabo Adventures, who operated the tour, said that the captain tried to turn when the whale first appeared but it quickly struck the vessel.
3A statement describes how force of the crash threw Canadian Jennifer Karren, 35, overboard before she died in the water from a head trauma.
4It also said that she was pulled back into the boat and immediately received mouth-to-mouth resuscitation form another tourist, who happened to be a qualified nurse, before naval rescue paramedics took her to the hospital.
5Another two tourists were also taken to hospital, but one was released and the other's injuries were not life-threatening.
6Officials identified the creature as a grey whale, but a whale specialist from the Baja California State University believes that it was almost certainly a humpback.
7He said: "This is the first time in 30 years of studying whales that I have heard about an accident like this, in which passengers are pitched into the sea and one dies."
8However collisions between whales and boats are not unheard of in Mexico, where whales come to breed in coastal lagoons during the winter.
9In January 2014 a humpback whale crashed into a boat off the coast of Baja California, injuring a US tourist and three other people on board.
