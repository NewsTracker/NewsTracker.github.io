Mayoral candidate for Mexico's governing coalition reported slain in troubled Guerrero state

1MEXICO CITY - The legislature in the southern Mexican state of Guerrero has condemned the killing of a local mayoral candidate.
2A statement late Friday from Guerrero's congress reported the killing of Ulises Fabian Quiroz, who was a candidate for mayor of Chilapa for the governing Institutional Revolutionary Party-Green party coalition.
3He was apparently shot Friday afternoon in the community of Atzacoaloya.
4Guerrero has been the site of intense violence as multiple crime groups battle for control of drug cultivation and smuggling routes.
5In September, 43 students from a teachers college disappeared in the Guerrero city of Iguala.
6The government says they were killed and incinerated by a drug gang collaborating with local police.
