Airline official: Germanwings probe shouldn't set precedent

1WASHINGTON (AP) - The head of a trade association representing the global airline industry says the Germanwings air crash investigation shouldn't set a precedent for future crashes because it assigned blame before the probe was complete and could jeopardize future airline cooperation.
2Tony Tyler, director general of the International Air Transport Association, told reporters that global aviation has long-established procedures for investigating crashes that put identifying and correcting safety risks ahead of assigning blame.
3He said investigating with the intent to punish risks a loss of transparency and openness.
