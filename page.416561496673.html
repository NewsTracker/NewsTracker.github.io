Thousands attend funeral for Freddie Gray in Baltimore

1The six Baltimore police officers suspended after a man suffered serious spinal injuries while in custody should tell the public what happened, an attorney for the man's family said at his funeral Monday.
2Bill Murphy's remarks about the officers drew a standing ovation at the funeral of Freddie Gray.
3Gray died April 19, days after his encounter with police.
4The 2,500-capacity New Shiloh Baptist church was filled with mourners, many of whom filed past Gray's casket before the service began.
5"This is our moment to get at truth. This is our moment to get it right," Murphy said.
6While the funeral was underway, police said in a news release that the department had received a "credible threat" that three notoriously violent gangs are now working together to "take out" law enforcement officers.
7Gray's death has heightened tensions between residents and the police, with weekend protests at times turning violent.
8A small group of mourners started lining up about two hours ahead of Monday's funeral.
9As they began filing into the church, the white casket with Gray's body was opened, flanked by floral arrangements.
10A rope was placed in front of the casket to prevent people from getting too close.
11One person used a cellphone to take a photo of the body.
12Placed atop Gray's body was a white pillow with a screened picture of him.
13A projector aimed at two screens on the walls showed the words "Black Lives Matter & All Lives Matter."
14Erica Garner, 24, the daughter of Eric Garner, who died in New York police custody, attended Gray's funeral.
15She said she came after seeing video of Gray's arrest, which she said reminded her of her father's shouts that he could not breathe when he was being arrested on a city street.
16Gray's death has prompted near-daily demonstrations.
17Gray was arrested one week before he died when officers chased him through a West Baltimore neighborhood and dragged him into a police van.
18Police said Gray was arrested after he made eye contact with officers and ran away.
19Officers held him down, handcuffed him and loaded him into the van.
20While inside, he became irate and leg cuffs were put on him, police have said.
21Gray asked for medical help several times, beginning before he was placed in the van.
22After a 30-minute ride that included three stops, paramedics were called.
23Authorities have not explained how or when Gray's spine was injured.
24Police acknowledged Friday that Gray should have received medical attention on the spot where he was arrested - before he was put inside a police transport van handcuffed and without a seat belt, a violation of the police department's policy.
25Rep. Elijah Cummings, D-Md., center, speaks in front of faith and community leaders at a news conference calling for peace in response to a Freddie Gray protest that turned violent, Sunday, April 26, 2015, at the Bethel AME Church in Baltimore.
26Gray died from spinal injuries about a week after he was arrested and transported in a police van.
