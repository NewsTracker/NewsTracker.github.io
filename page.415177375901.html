Ex-CIA chief Petraeus admits mishandling classified information

1Former CIA Director David Patraeus has agreed to plead guilty to mishandling classified materials.
2David Petraeus, the former CIA director and top Army general whose affair with his biographer brought down what many considered a bright political future, has agreed to plead guilty to mishandling classified materials.
3The plea agreement was filed Tuesday in U.S. District Court in Charlotte, North Carolina.
4Petraeus was charged with one count of unauthorized removal and retention of classified material, which carries a possible penalty of up to a year in prison, $100,000 US fine and five years of probation.
5According to court documents, Petraeus gave his biographer, Paula Broadwell, binders of classified material from Afghanistan while she was writing her book.
6Petraeus admitted having an affair with Broadwell when he resigned from his position in November 2012.
7Both have publicly apologized and said their romantic relationship began only after he retired from the military and started at the CIA.
8The binders were known as "black books" and were seized by the FBI in a search of Petraeus's home, according to court documents.
9He also lied about providing them to Broadwell, according to court documents.
10There were eight binders in all, each containing classified information about Petraeus' time as the top general in Afghanistan.
11The material included his daily schedule and notes he took during official briefings.
12The books were top secret and also included notes about Petraeus's discussions with the U.S. president.
13Petraeus retained the black books in his home even after he left the Defence Department.
14On Aug. 28, 2011, he delivered them to a private residence in Washington where Broadwell was staying.
15Petraeus left the books with her so she could use them as source material for the biography on Petraeus that she wrote, which was released in 2012.

171, Petraeus brought the books back to his home in Arlington, Virginia.
18When Petraeus resigned from the CIA, he signed a security exit form indicating he had no classified material in his possession.
19However, he still had the black books in his home at that time.
20On April 5, 2013, the FBI searched his home and seized the black books from an unlocked desk drawer in a first-floor study.
21When interviewed by the FBI in 2012, Petraeus said he never provided classified information to his biographer.
22Prosecutors say that was false, and that Petraeus lied to federal investigators.
23Petraeus's lawyers David Kendall and Robert Barnett in Washington declined to comment.
