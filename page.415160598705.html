Ebola survivor leaves US after recovery to return to Africa

1CHARLOTTE, N.C. (AP) - An American missionary who survived the Ebola virus is returning to the West African country where she was infected last year.
2A spokesman for the North Carolina-based charity SIM said Monday that Nancy Writebol is returning to Liberia with her husband.
3SIM spokesman George Salloum says David Writebol is taking over as the charity's country director.
4He says the Charlotte couple are due to arrive in Liberia in a couple of weeks after attending a conference in Thailand.
5Nancy Writebol last summer became the second American infected with the Ebola virus while working at a hospital SIM supports in the Liberian capital of Monrovia.
6She and fellow American Dr. Kent Brantly recovered from the highly fatal virus at Emory University Hospital in Atlanta.
