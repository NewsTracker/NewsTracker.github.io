EU urges refugee action as over 1,000 migrants reach Greece

1A migrants receives medical care after he was disembark from the Swedish Coast Guard ship KBV 001 Poseidon at Messina harbor, Italy, Tuesday, June 9, 2015.
2Over the last weekend, nearly 6,000 migrants were rescued by an array of European military vessels, including 2,371 who were saved on Sunday from 15 boats that ran into difficulty shortly after smugglers set off with them from Libyan shores, the Italian coast guard said.
3BRUSSELS (AP) - The European Union urged its 28 nations Wednesday to swiftly endorse its plan to relocate 40,000 migrants as 1,000 more migrants arrived in Greece in just one day.
4The plan to relocate the tens of thousands arriving in Italy and Greece with their EU partners is part of efforts to cope with the massive influx of migrants that has already seen more than 100,000 people cross the Mediterranean into Europe this year.
5Around 2,000 have died or are missing.
6Still, a number of EU nations have objected to the plan.
7"We need to act now and not in four months," EU migration spokeswoman Natasha Bertaud told reporters in Brussels.
8Greek authorities said Wednesday that more than 1,000 people had entered the country by sea, bringing its five-month total to about 50,000 - well above the number who arrived in Greece for all of 2014.
9Greece is a close second to Italy in migrant arrivals this year.
10The coast guard says 670 people were rescued since early Tuesday in the eastern Aegean Sea and south of Crete, where 263 people were found in one small vessel.
11Another 361 were found on the Greek island of Lesbos.
12About nine in 10 arrivals this year came from war-torn parts of the Middle East - principally Syria - and Africa.
13EU interior ministers are to discuss the relocation plan next Tuesday at talks in Luxembourg.
14Many object to the EU's calculation for sharing the refugees - mainly Syrians and Eritreans - saying it doesn't take into account efforts they already make to host migrants.
15"We put an ambitious proposal on the table for a relocation mechanism. We knew it would not win any prizes but we do expect the ministers ... to take their responsibility," Bertaud said.
16The number of migrants arriving in Europe by sea from Turkey and Libya is expected to grow further during the traditional June-to-September high season for water crossings.
17Italian Foreign Minister Paolo Gentiloni warned that "it would be a huge political failure for Europe" if the EU migrant plan was not quickly endorsed.
18Italy is fighting to implement the plan "not because we think it magically resolves all the questions but because it is a politically important choice," he said in Brussels.
