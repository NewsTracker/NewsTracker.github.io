Greinke's shutout streak on hold for birth of 1st child (Yahoo Sports)

1NEW YORK (AP) -- Zack Greinke's shutout streak is on hold while the Los Angeles Dodgers ace and his wife await the birth of their first child.
2Greinke has pitched 43 2/3 scoreless innings, and was set to start Friday night against the New York Mets at Citi Field.
3He is approaching the big league record of 59 straight scoreless innings set by Orel Hershiser in 1988 for the Dodgers.
4Dodgers manager Don Mattingly said before Thursday night's game against the Mets that Greinke had returned to Los Angeles early in the morning to join wife Emily.
5Mattingly received a call in his hotel room at 5 a.m. from Greinke, who caught a flight a couple of hours later.
6''To get back to pitch tomorrow would be, I'd say unrealistic, but we do feel like there's a good chance he'd pitch this weekend,'' Mattingly said before the game.
7Mattingly said after the game left-handed reliever Ian Thomas (0-1) would take Greinke's start Friday.
8The Mets look like a good matchup for a pitcher chasing a scoreless innings streak record.
9New York had scored the second-fewest runs in baseball and had the lowest team batting average entering Thursday night's game against Clayton Kershaw, who pitched a three-hitter in a 3-0 win.
10The Dodgers can fill Greinke's roster spot Friday if necessary and put him on three days' paternity leave.
11The team is off Monday and returns to Los Angeles for a series against Oakland that starts Tuesday.
12Greinke leads the majors with a 1.30 ERA.
13He is 9-2 in 19 starts for the NL West leaders.
14Mattingly said he has not noticed a drastic difference in how Greinke has pitched this season compared to the right-hander's last two with the Dodgers.
15''Since we've had him, he's been really, really good,'' Mattingly said.
