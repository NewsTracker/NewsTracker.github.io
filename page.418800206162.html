Hillary Clinton to testify before Benghazi panel in October: campaign

1Democratic presidential hopeful Hillary Rodham Clinton delivers a speech at the New York University Leonard N. Stern School of Business in New York on Friday, July 24, 2015.
2WASHINGTON -- Hillary Rodham Clinton will testify Oct. 22 before the House committee investigating the deaths of four Americans in the 2012 attacks in Benghazi, Libya, her presidential campaign said Saturday.
3The committee did not immediately confirm the date for the former secretary of state's appearance.
4Campaign spokesman Nick Merrill said the Democratic candidate's testimony will be public.
5Clinton's lawyers and the Republican-led committee have been negotiating over the terms under which she might appeal before the committee.
6The chairman, Rep. Trey Gowdy, R-S.C., initially requested a private interview.
7The testimony, in the months leading up to the first presidential nominating contests in Iowa and New Hampshire, is likely to reverberate through the 2016 race.
8Gowdy's committee is investigating the deaths of U.S. ambassador and three other Americans in the attack on the diplomatic facility in Libya.
