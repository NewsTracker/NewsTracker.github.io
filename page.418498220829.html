Nigeria investigating Boko Haram attack in remote northeast

1At least 43 people were killed in attacks by Nigeria's Boko Haram Islamic extremists in a remote region in the country's northeast, according to police and residents.
2The attacks happened Friday on the villages of Kalwa, Misala and Gwollam and people were either shot dead or stabbed, said Aderemi Opadokun, Borno state police commissioner.
3At least 43 people were killed in the villages Friday night, said Haruna Bukar, a civilian security agent in the area.
4Officials say more such killings may be unreported because poor communication links to the remote parts of Borno state make it difficult for authorities to get news from the area.
