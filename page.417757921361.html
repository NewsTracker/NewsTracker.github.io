Fabricio Werdum shocks Cain Velasquez, Mexico City crowd at UFC 188

1MEXICO CITY - Cain Velasquez had so thoroughly dominated the pre-fight conversation that it seemed like almost a given that he'd dominate Saturday night's UFC heavyweight championship bout.
2Interim champion Fabricio Werdum had other ideas.
3The Brazilian shocked fight fans on Saturday night, silencing an emotional Mexico City crowd by submitting Velasquez with a guillotine in the third round.
4It was a fight Mexican fans had waited to see for years.
5Velasquez is one of the country's most beloved athletes, and after almost two years spent sidelined by injuries, his first-ever fight in Mexico City was treated almost as a coronation.
6For the first-round, it looked to be just that.
7Velasquez landed a number of big shots and Werdum appeared to be gassing out by the end of the opening frame.
8The noise in the crowd was deafening and a Velasquez victory began to feel almost like a foregone conclusion.
9Then, Werdum came out for the second round and appeared to tap into some previously unseen energy source.
10His strikes were landing flush, his movement appeared faster, and Velasquez was fading right as his opponent found a rhythm.
11In the third it was more of the same, and when Velasquez went for a takedown - he'd been avoiding engaging with Werdum on the ground all fight - it seemed almost desperate.
12Once they got to the mat, the fight didn't last long.
13Within seconds, Werdum's superior jiu-jitsu skills took over and Velasquez was tapping his out just 2:15 into the third round.
14"I'm sorry to everyone here, my dream was to fight here," Velasquez said.
15"I'll come back and I'll win that title again."
