Saudi Arabia accuses 93 of terrorist and Islamic State links

1Saudi Interior Ministry spokesman Major-General Mansour al-Turki listens to questions at a press conference in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, last week.
2Beirut: Saudi Arabia has arrested 93 people accused of plotting terrorist attacks since December, including a group described as having sought to strike the United States embassy with a car bomb, according to government officials.
3The Saudi Interior Ministry said in a statement that the suspects were accused of recruiting and training new members; testing explosives; gathering firearms; and plotting to attack residential areas and security facilities.
4They were arrested in six groups, the ministry said, adding that most of the suspects had ties to the so-called Islamic State extremist group and nearly all were Saudi citizens.
5One of the 93 suspects is a woman.
6Abdulrahman Al-Banyan, second right, Saudi Arabia's Chief of Staff, arrives at the Arab League headquarters last week.
7Saudi Arabia has joined a US-led coalition that is bombing Islamic State in Iraq and Syria, where the group has seized territory.
8The Saudis are also leading an Arab coalition carrying out air strikes in Yemen.
9For their part, Islamic State's leaders have called on their supporters to mount attacks inside Saudi Arabia.
10There has been a series of mainly small-scale attacks in recent months.
11Saudi officials said last week that a pair of men accused of killing two policemen in a drive-by shooting had received support from Islamic State.
12One of those suspects has been arrested; the other is at large.
13Abdulrahman Al-Banyan at the Arab defence ministers meeting at the Arab League headquarters in Cairo last week.
14It was unclear why Saudi authorities revealed the 93 arrests on Tuesday.
15They did not give the names of any suspects or provide any information about how or when the suspects would be tried.
16A spokesman for the US embassy in Riyadh declined to comment about the assertion that one group among those arrested was planning a car bombing at the embassy.
17The embassy suspended consular services in Riyadh and at consulates elsewhere in the country on March 15 because of security concerns; the services were resumed a week later, but Americans were urged to take precautions.
18Another group consisted of 65 people, including two Palestinians and a Yemeni, who had ties to the Islamic State and sought to spread its ideology and set up training sites for new recruits, the ministry statement said.
19Major-General Mansour al-Turki tells journalists a Saudi man arrested on suspicion of shooting dead two policemen and wounding two others in separate attacks in Riyadh in March and April was acting on instructions from Islamic State militants in Syria.
