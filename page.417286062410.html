Teenager in Austrian 'Playstation' terrorism case gets two years

1VIENNA (Reuters) - A 14-year-old boy from Austria who downloaded bomb-making plans onto his Playstation games console was sentenced to a two-year jail term on Tuesday after pleading guilty to terrorism charges, a court spokeswoman said.
2As well as researching how to build a bomb, the boy made contact with militants supporting the Islamic State jihadist group in Syria, prosecutors said ahead of the trial.
3Sixteen months of the sentence were suspended.
4The boy, a Turkish national, will serve what remains of the eight-month custodial term in a juvenile detention center, the spokeswoman for the regional court in Sankt Poelten said.
5He had been briefly placed in investigative custody in October on suspicion of terrorism-related activity, before being conditionally released.
6He was detained for a second time in January.
7He had faced up to five years in jail for supporting a terrorist organization and planning an attack.
8Those charges were based on data found on the boy's Playstation, including bomb-building plans downloaded from the internet, prosecutors said.
9More than 200 people have left Austria to fight in the Middle East, some 30 of whom have been killed while around 70 have returned, according to the interior ministry.
10A court in Vienna is currently hearing the case of a 16-year-old girl accused of preparing to join a terrorist organization.
