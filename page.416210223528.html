'La Tete Haute' to open Cannes Film Festival

1NEW YORK (AP) - For the first time, a female director will kick off the Cannes Film Festival.
2The festival announced Monday that "La Tete Haute," a French drama directed by Emmanuelle Bercot, will open the 68th Cannes Film Festival on May 13.
3The film is about the upbringing of a juvenile delinquent and stars Catherine Deneuve.
4The French Riviera festival has sometimes been criticized for not programming more female-directed films.
5Cannes head Thierry Fremaux said the choice "is a clear reflection of our desire to see the festival start with a different piece, which is both bold and moving."
6This year's jury is being presided over by Joel and Ethan Coen.
7The film slate will be announced Thursday, adding to the already announced out-of-competition entry "Mad Max: Fury Road."
8Cannes runs May 13-24.
