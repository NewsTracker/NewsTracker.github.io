Indian groom rejected at altar by bride who tested his maths

As Ferguson manhunt grinds on, leaders aim to defuse tension

1'The groom's family kept us in the dark about his poor education,' said Mohar Singh, the bride's father.
2Most marriages in India are arranged by the families of the bride and groom.

10Mr Singh, who was wearing jeans and a leather jacket, was caught off guard, but agreed to go ahead with the wedding.
11After the ceremony, Mr Kishore returned to the venue after being treated in hospital to find that his fiancee had married someone else, it was reported by The Times of India.

2Law enforcement authorities were tight-lipped about any progress they may have made in tracking down whoever was responsible for wounding the officers during a volatile demonstration in front of police headquarters.

4So far authorities have scheduled no media briefings for Friday.
5Demonstrators protest in front of the police station on Wednesday night before two police were shot.

9"The community and the police can come together."
10A police officer's helmet lies on the ground splashed with blood in Ferguson.
11He was one of two officers shot on Thursday morning in the troubled Missouri city.
12Thursday's shooting took place a week after the release of a US Justice Department report that appeared to vindicate long-standing complaints of pervasive racial bias leveled against Ferguson's mostly white police force.
13The report, initiated after Brown's death, led to the resignation this week of the city's police chief and other officials, but demonstrations, such as the rally where the two officers were shot, have persisted.
14Activists have pressed for more reforms to address what the report called a "toxic environment" for the city's mostly African-American residents.
15Chaunte Williams, 13, holds up three candles as she takes part in a vigil on Thursday in Ferguson.
16President Barack Obama said such protests were warranted in the light of events in the city but said whoever shot the police officials must be brought to justice.

19The shooting of the officers followed by less than three months the killing of two New York City patrolmen by a troubled man who said he was seeking to avenge the deaths of Brown and an unarmed black man in New York killed by police.

22The crowd blocked traffic at times but there were no arrests and the demonstration passed without incident.

25Crime Stoppers, a nationwide organisation that works to prevent and solve crimes, has offered a $US10,000 ($13,098) reward for information leading to the culprit, and two Missouri Congressmen have added $US3000 ($3929).
