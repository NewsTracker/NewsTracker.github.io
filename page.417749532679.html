Scientists emerge from isolated dome on Hawaii volcano slope

1HONOLULU (AP) - Six scientists who were living under a dome on the slopes of a dormant Hawaii volcano for eight months to simulate life on Mars have emerged from isolation.
2The crew stepped outside the dome that's 8,000 feet up the slopes of Mauna Loa to feel fresh air on their skin Saturday.
3It was the first time they left without donning a space suit.
4The scientists are part of a human performance study funded by NASA that tracked how they worked together as a team.
5They have been monitored by surveillance cameras, body movement trackers and electronic surveys.
6Crew member Jocelyn Dunn said it was awesome to feel the sensation of wind on her skin.
7"When we first walked out the door, it was scary not to have a suit on," said Dunn, 27, a doctoral candidate at Purdue University.
8"We've been pretending for so long."
9The dome's volcanic location, silence and its simulated airlock seal provided an atmosphere similar to space.
10Looking out the dome's porthole windows, all the scientists could see were lava fields and mountains, said University of Hawaii professor Kim Binsted, principal investigator for the study, in a statement.
11Spending eight months in a confined space with six people had its challenges, but crew members relieved stress doing team workouts and yoga.
12They were able to use a solar-powered treadmill and stationary bike, but only in the afternoons on sunny days.
13"When you're having a good day its fine, it's fun. You have friends around to share in the enjoyment of a good day," Dunn said.
14"But if you have a bad day, it's really tough to be in a confined environment. You can't get out and go for a walk ... it's constantly witnessed by everyone."
15The hardest part was being far away from family and missing events like her sister's wedding, for which she delivered a toast via video, Dunn said.
16"I'm glad I was able to be there in that way, but ... I just always dreamed of being there to help," she said.
17The first thing crew members did when they emerged from the dome was to chow down on foods they've been craving - juicy watermelon, deviled eggs, peaches and croissants, which was a step up from the freeze dried chili they'd been eating.
18Next on Dunn's list: going for a swim.
19Showers in the isolated environment were limited to six minutes per week, she said.
20"To be able to just submerge myself in water for as long as I want, to feel the sun, will be amazing," Dunn said.
21"I feel like a ghost."
