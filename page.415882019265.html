Bali Nine final appeal to wrap up

1The state administrative court last month threw out a challenge against the clemency rejections for the Bali Nine pair, deciding it didn't have the authority to examine the decrees by President Joko Widodo.
2The men sentenced for heroin smuggling in 2006 have no other legal avenues to spare them from the firing squad and have been transferred to the island where Indonesia will execute them when they and other prisoners have exhausted court action.
3Their appeals will wrap up in Jakarta on Wednesday, with summary arguments from both sides.
4On Monday, legal expert Otong Rosadi of Ekasakti University told the court he supported the challenge.
5In his opinion, all "products of the law" can be reviewed, even clemency.
6Lawyers for the state argue clemency is a matter of presidential prerogative, and have supplied expert evidence supporting that view in writing.
7Meanwhile on Tuesday, Australia's new ambassador to Indonesia Paul Grigson met Indonesia's Vice President Jusuf Kalla for the first time since presenting his credentials.
8The vice president's chief of staff, Dewi Fortuna Anwar, told reporters afterwards that the pair only discussed the case briefly.
9The ambassador thanked the vice president for accepting a phone call from Foreign Minister Julie Bishop on the issue, she said.
10"The vice president also said that he understands the Australian government's position in trying to save its citizens from the death penalty, that's the government's duty," she said, as quoted by the Indonesian website merdeka.com.
11"The vice president also said the Indonesian government would fight to the end, too, to save its citizens facing the death penalty, like in Saudi Arabia for example.
12"But this is a legal process. Certainly each country must respect legal process."
13Mr Joko and Prime Minister Tony Abbott were photographed speaking on the sidelines of the state funeral for Lee Kuan Yew in Singapore on Sunday.
14Mr Abbott says he would like another chance to speak with the president regarding Chan and Sukumaran, whom he believes have fully rehabilitated.
15A spokesman for Indonesia's Foreign Ministry has said they did not have time to meet in Singapore due to the president's tight schedule.
