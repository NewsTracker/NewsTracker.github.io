Officials probe release of felon accused of killing officer

1ALBUQUERQUE, N.M. (AP) - New Mexico's attorney general is investigating the circumstances that led to the release of a repeat offender who prosecutors say went on to kill a police officer.
2Attorney General Hector Balderas this week is summoning about a dozen criminal justice and social service providers who came in contact with 28-year-old Andrew Romero.
3Prosecutors have charged Romero with first-degree murder in the Memorial Day shooting that killed Rio Rancho police officer Gregg "Nigel" Benner.
4Romero has yet to enter a plea, and his public defender didn't immediately return a message seeking comment.
5Balderas says the judicial system failed by not keeping Romero behind bars.
6Romero is a convicted felon who was put on probation when he could have received up to 13 years in jail for possessing illegal drugs, a stolen car and a weapon.
