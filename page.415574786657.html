1The Philippine Bureau of Immigration is requiring two members of the English-Irish band One Direction to post bonds worth nearly $5,000 each, to be forfeited if they are caught using or impliedly promoting illegal drugs while in Manila for their concerts during the weekend of March 21-22, 2015.
2MANILA, Philippines (AP) - The Philippines is imposing a "weed bond" on One Direction.
3The Bureau of Immigration has asked Zayn Malik and Louis Tomlinson to post bonds worth nearly $5,000 each, which will be forfeited if they are caught using or impliedly promoting illegal drugs during a concert in Manila this weekend.
4It follows a request by the local Anti-Drugs Advocate group to the bureau to strictly scrutinize the band's concert permit following a video that circulated on the Internet of Malik and Tomlinson purportedly smoking marijuana.
5Malik and Tomlinson are required to post 200,000 pesos ($4,470) bond and 20,000 pesos ($447) processing fee each through their producer, bureau spokeswoman Elaine Tan said Thursday.
6The five-member band will perform at the seaside Mall of Asia Concert Grounds as part of their "On The Road Again" tour.
7"The condition is intended to protect the public interest should the band members commit any violation during their stay in the Philippines," Tan said.
8She said the band will be prevented from performing if the producer fails to post the bond before the concert.
9The producer went to the Immigration Bureau Thursday afternoon to post the bond and fee, said Christine Ching, a spokeswoman of Anti-Drugs Advocate.
10The Filipino group is also awaiting a response to their petition asking a local court to direct government agencies to ensure that the band members are drug-free.
11It also urged the Philippine Drug Enforcement Agency to send officers to monitor the band.
12The group is concerned about the popular band's influence on Filipino youth, Ching added.
13"We want to see their pure, raw, untainted talent," without need for them to take drugs, she said.
14She said the concert producers, during a meeting with her group, expressed willingness to cooperate and give drug enforcers and Anti-Drugs Advocate access passes to allow them to monitor the band members.
15"To be honest, there are a lot of videos that are coming out showing people doing drugs. ... If this group is coming here to perform, will they take drugs while they are performing? Of course not," the college freshman said.
16Following the leak of the video last year, One Direction's Liam Payne twitted an apology, saying "I love my boys and maybe things have gone a little sideways. I apologize for that."
17He said: "We are only in our 20's and we all do stupid things at this age."
18After Manila, One Direction is scheduled to perform in Jakarta, Indonesia, March 25.
