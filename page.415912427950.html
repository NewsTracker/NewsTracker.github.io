Mark Wahlberg eyes role in Boston Marathon bombing film

1Boston-bred Mark Wahlberg may star in a film that recounts one of the most tragic events in his hometown's history: the 2013 Boston Marathon bombing.
2Deadline Hollywood said Wahlberg is looking to play Boston Police Commissioner Ed Davis in the film, "Patriots' Day," which will be based on Davis' account of the five-day manhunt for Dzhokhar Tsarnaev.
3Closing arguments begin Monday in the trial of Tsarnaev, 21, who is facing the death penalty for his role in the bombing that killed three people and injured more than 260 others.
4This is the second major feature film in development about the bombing and the citywide manhunt that also led to the death of Tsarnaev's older brother and alleged co-conspirator, Tamerlan.
5In this Nov. 10, 2014 file photo, Mark Wahlberg arrives at the 2014 AFI Fest "The Gambler, " in Los Angeles.
6Wahlberg will coproduce "Patriots' Day, " a film chronicling the events surrounding the 2013 Boston Marathon Bombing, based on the first-hand account of Boston Police Commissioner Ed Davis.
7The script for "Patriots' Day" is nearly complete, and CBS Films has fast-tracked its production.
8The company announced that Wahlberg is signed on to coproduce the film, Entertainment Weekly said.
9Other producers include Bluegrass Films and "60 Minutes" senior producer Michael Radutzky.
