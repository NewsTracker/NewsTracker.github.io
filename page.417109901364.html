Ghana defender Jonathan Mensah banned 4 months by FIFA

1Ghana defender Jonathan Mensah has been banned for four months by FIFA in a breach-of-contract case.
2The Court of Arbitration for Sport said its appeal panel upheld Mensah's suspension imposed by FIFA last July in a case dating to 2011.
3He is also liable for compensation to former club Udinese.
4However, FIFA said Tuesday that Mensah - who played at the past two World Cup tournaments - is eligible to play for Ghana next month.
5It hosts Mauritius in the opening match of a 2017 African Cup of Nations qualifying group.
6FIFA judged that Mensah was still contracted to Udinese when he joined French club Evian Thonon Gaillard four years ago claiming to be a free agent.
7Under FIFA rules, Mensah will serve about one month of the ban until the French season ends.
8The sanction is not spent during the offseason and does not apply to national team friendlies or qualifiers.
9Mensah will become a free agent and is unlikely to stay with Evian, which has been relegated from the top division.
10He will serve the final three months of the ban from the official start of his next club's new season.
11The 24-year-old Mensah played in all three Ghana matches in Brazil last June, including a 2-1 loss to the United States.
12He was a member of the Under-20 World Cup-winning team in 2009.
13CAS said the case has not ended for Evian, which FIFA could order to compensate Udinese and ban from signing players.
14"The sporting sanctions that were imposed on Evian Thonon Gaillard ... are suspended and referred back to the (FIFA) Dispute Resolution Chamber," the court said.
