Ivan Rakitic helped beat Man City... but who is the midfielder who almost joined Chelsea at 16?

U.S. has flown 2,320 strikes against Islamic State at a cost of $1.83 billion: official

1Of all the players Manchester City might have expected to drive the final nail into their Champions League coffin Ivan Rakitic was a long way down the list, but it was the former Sevilla midfielder who got the goal on Wednesday night.
2Here MailSport takes a closer look at the Croatian midfielder more than proving his worth in his first season at the Nou Camp.
3When he signed in the summer what was the reaction?
4There was no hysteria but it was considered a welcome addition for two reasons - firstly they need midfielders after selling Cesc Fabregas to Chelsea and Thiago to Bayern Munich, and second Rakitic was, along with his fellow countryman Luka Modric, the best midfielder in La Liga last season.
5Is he an improvement on the aforementioned Fabregas and Thiago?
6Thiago has the potential to be the best of the three but appalling luck with injuries has stalled his career.
7Is Rakitic better than Cesc?
8Probably not but he has many of the same characteristics so that has made him a worthy replacement.
9And those characteristics are?
10He is versatile in as much as he can play behind the striker and score goals as he did regularly for his former club Sevilla and he can play deep in front of the centre-back as he also did, although less so for his former team, and as he did for Barca last Saturday away at Eibar.
11Much like his compatriot at Real Madrid Modric, he can tackle, pass, and shoot.
12He's a very complete midfielder.
13Who brought him to Spain?
14Sevilla signed him from Schalke for 2.5m.
15It was another work of genius from the club's Sporting Director Monchi because within a very short space of time it was clear Rakitic was destined for bigger things and would make the club a tidy profit.
16His wife, Raquel Mauri, is a hairdresser from Sevilla, and so he preferred to stay in Spain.
17When Barcelona came calling it was a no-brainer.
18He was close to going to Chelsea as a 16-year-old but his father advised him to move to Schalke instead.
19The language was not going to be a problem wherever he went because the Swiss born Croatian international speaks five languages.
20And his battle with Modric will be one to look out for on Sunday?
21It will.
22And even more so because Rakitic was denied the chance to face his international team-mate in the first Clasico of the season because he was left out of the starting eleven.
23How did that work out for Luis Enrique?
24Badly.
25Many had tipped the coach to resist the temptation of giving Luis Suarez his debut after so long out, move Iniesta forward to the front three and bring in Xavi alongside Rakitic.
26Instead Luis Enrique dropped Rakitic despite the fact that he had been playing well and lined Xavi up alongside Andres Iniesta.
27'They are great players but they are not getting any younger,' Paul Clement told me after the game.
28Barca started well but lost control of midfielder and ultimately the match.
29Without Rakitic they were completely out-run in the middle of the park.
30When he is at his best, he is their engine.
31Now he's back in the team for good?
32He didn't start against Malaga and Barca lost 1-0.
33He then played and scored against Granada.
34He is becoming a leader on the pitch and has bought into the ideas of sacrifice and generosity at Barcelona having been very much the talisman of the team at Sevilla.

7Coalition strikes have hit 73 tanks, some of them U.S.-made Iraqi M1A1 Abrams tanks seized when Islamic State militants overran northwestern parts Iraq last year.

12The Pentagon has said that as a result of the strikes oil sales are no longer the primary source of revenue for Islamic State.
13As of March 12, the Pentagon had spent about $1.83 billion on the strikes, an average of about $8.5 million daily, Warren said.

15U.S. forces believed the device was essentially a model aircraft that would have been commercially available to anyone, Warren said.

17He said it was unclear how the pilot had been using the drone or if it was carrying devices such as a camera to conduct reconnaissance.
18"To my knowledge this is the first time we've observed ISIL using these types of equipment," Warren said, using an acronym for the group.
