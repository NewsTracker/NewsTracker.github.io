3WASHINGTON - The United States began conducting airstrikes around Tikrit Wednesday, joining an offensive that is being largely carried out by Iranian-backed Shiite militia, the Pentagon announced.
4The airstrikes came at the request of the Iraqi government, the U.S. military said, and come as an offensive to root out Islamic State militants has stalled after weeks of fighting.
5"This will further enable Iraqi forces under Iraqi command to maneuver and defeat ISIL in the vicinity of Tikrit," said Lt. Gen. James Terry, using an acronym for the Islamic State.
6As many as 20 targets were hit, including barricades, in the initial wave of attacks.
7Pilots were also looking for Islamic State troop barracks.
8The offensive in Tikrit, a town about 80 miles north of Baghdad that was the hometown of Saddam Hussein, has put the United States in an awkward position.
9The bulk of the Iraqi force attacking into the city, about 20,000 fighters, is made up of Shiite militias who are supported by Iranian advisers and artillery and rocket support.
10The Pentagon has taken pains to say it is not coordinating directly with the Iranians, but are working instead with Iraq's armed forces.
11The U.S. support could help break a stalemate in the city by allowing the Iraqi forces to root out the remaining defenders who are inside the city in well-defended positions.
12The offensive has been underway for weeks but the initial success has slowed in recent days.
13Urban warfare of this nature requires precise firepower, which Iraq's armed forces or the Iranians are unable to provide.
14"These strikes are intended to destroy ISIL strongholds with precision, thereby saving innocent Iraqi lives while minimizing collateral damage to infrastructure," Terry said in a statement.
15Earlier this week, the United States began providing surveillance support to the Iraqi forces operating in Tikrit.
16"Iraqi Security Forces have ISIL in Tikrit encircled," according to the statement.
17"Renewed efforts on the ground supported by the coalition are aimed at dislodging ISIL fighting elements from Tikrit and once again placing the town under the government of Iraq control."
