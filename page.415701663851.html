Official: US-led force flying over Islamic State-held Tikrit

1The flights and intelligence sharing, which began Saturday, mark the first time the coalition has been involved in the offensive, which up to now largely has been supported by Iranian advisers including Gen. Qassem Soleimani, commander of the powerful Revolutionary Guard Quds Force.
2The coalition official, who spoke on condition of anonymity as he was not authorized to speak to journalists about the matter, said the support began at the request of the Iraqi government.
3He declined to comment on whether they are providing airstrikes, saying he cannot discuss current or future operations.
4He also declined to discuss whether U.S. forces were directly communicating with Iranians on the ground there.
5Iraqi security forces, backed by Iranian-supported Shiite militias, launched a large-scale offensive to recapture Tikrit earlier this month.
6The U.S. previously had said that the Iraqi government never asked them to participate in the campaign.
7A senior Iraqi military official said that coalition is not providing airstrikes in support of the Tikrit operation at this time, but launched airstrikes in the nearby oil refinery town of Beiji on Tuesday.
8He added that Soleimani has just left Tikrit after providing front-line assistance and advising since the start of the operation.
9"He will come back if we need him to," said the Iraqi official, who spoke on condition of anonymity because he's not authorized to brief journalists.
10Tikrit, the capital of Salahuddin province, lies about 130 kilometers (80 miles) north of Baghdad.
11It is one of the largest cities held by Islamic State militants and lies on the road connecting Baghdad to Mosul.
12Retaking it will help Iraqi forces have a major supply link for any future operation to retake Mosul, the country's second-largest city.
13U.S. military officials have that said a coordinated military mission to retake Mosul likely will begin in April or May and involve up to 25,000 Iraqi troops.
14But the Americans have cautioned that if the Iraqis are not ready, the offensive could be delayed.
15The U.S. and other coalition nations have been providing weapons and training to Iraqi and Kurdish forces in the north in an effort to make them ready for the battle ahead.
16The U.S.-led airstrike campaign, first launched by the U.S. in August, nearly has halted the Islamic State rampage across Iraq.
17The Tikrit operation is the largest to date and requires careful air and ground coordination so not to risk civilian casualties or damage the city's infrastructure.
18The mission to retake Tikrit had progressed earlier this month, with Iraqi security forces and the supporting militias surrounding the city and entering some northern and southern neighborhoods of the city.
19But the offensive has stalled in recent weeks, with Iraqi officials saying they will not rush a final assault.
