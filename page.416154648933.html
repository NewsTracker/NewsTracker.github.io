Review: Many choices, indecision with Apple Watch

1NEW YORK (AP) - Of the 13 Apple Watch models I tried over two sessions, the one I liked most cost $15,000.
2Both sessions at the Apple store began with indecision: What size do I want?
3Which band do I prefer?
4I didn't have good answers as my Apple Watch try-on visits started - at first modestly, with models that cost about the same as an iPhone.
5The try-on session typically begins with making an appointment online.
6If you're looking for a luxury "Edition" made of 18-karat gold alloy, you specify that.
7Only some stores offer those, including the one I visited on New York's Upper West Side.
8I bypassed the appointments because Apple's media reps in Cupertino, California, had arranged my sessions.
9Stores will try to accommodate walk-ins, but appointments are encouraged.
10As someone who can't decide what to eat for lunch, I expected to be told which watch I wanted.
11Sensing my indecision, the employees in both cases picked one just to get started.
12I began with a smaller version of the stainless steel case, with a brown leather band called Modern Buckle ($749).
13The band looks like a leather strap you buckle, but has magnetic clasps.
14It felt loose, even at the tightest setting.
15I was told not every band is going to fit every wrist.
16I then tried a large stainless steel case with a black leather loop ($699) - also clasped magnetically, but without the holes you normally see with a buckle.
17The band comes in two sizes, but only the large one was available for trying.
18It was too long.
19It turns out stores don't have all 54 configurations available for trying out.
20Most stations have 18 watches to choose from, locked in a drawer that requires a tap from the employee's security device.
21You get a feel, but not necessarily in your color or length.
22I tried the Modern Buckle again, this time in black.
23Still loose.
24That was followed by large and small cases with a classic buckle.
25The fit was much better.
26I also tried models with a synthetic-rubber sports band and a stainless steel link bracelet.
27Bands are easily interchangeable by pressing a release button, and some are sold separately.
28I can use the sports band for running and a classic buckle for regular wear.
29Likewise, individual links on the bracelet can be removed without special tools.
30But the try-on visits aren't set up for that.
31Nor are they set up for lefties; everything will appear upside down in the watches' demonstration mode.
32You can change that - once you buy it.
33I returned several hours later to meet with an Edition specialist.
34I wasn't feted with chocolate and champagne, but I did get a more personalized experience.
35You're taken to a private room in some stores, but mine didn't have one, so luxury appointments are done on the main floor, to the side.
36As the specialist assisted me, another employee went into a backroom to bring out specific models on request - in nice boxes that give the watch a luxury feel, while doubling as a charger (there's a charging port in the back).
37The process wasn't as quick as I expected, but what's the rush when you're ready to spend $10,000 to $17,000?
