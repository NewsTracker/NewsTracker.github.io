Asia Stocks Dampened by Greek Bailout Saga, Weak Wall Street

1Asian stock markets were mostly lower Wednesday as Greece's bailout woes and a stagnant Wall Street performance dampened investor sentiment.
2KEEPING SCORE: Japan's benchmark Nikkei 225, which had been on a bull streak recently, edged down 0.3 percent to 20,476.71.
3South Korea's Kospi fell 0.2 percent to 2,073.50.
4Hong Kong's Hang Seng added 0.6 percent to 27,639.79 while the Shanghai Composite slipped 1 percent to 4,860.58.
5Southeast Asian stocks were mixed.
6EUROPEAN AGENDA: Concerns about Greece's ability to meet debt payments to its bailout creditors have weighed on markets.
7If Greece defaults on its debt, that may mean a departure from the euro zone.
8Greece's prime minister was set to meet European Commission President Jean-Claude Juncker in Brussels to discuss his proposal to secure a vital, long-overdue agreement with the country's bailout lenders.
9Separately, the European Central Bank meets later Wednesday.
10THE QUOTE: Markets are closely watching for any "rhetoric" that comes out of the European Central Bank, although no change is expected to its new policy of quantitative monetary easing, Mizuho Bank said in a market commentary.
11"The expected pick-up in growth momentum also reduces the need for extended policy stimulus," Mizuho said.
12"Any rhetoric on Greece ... will also be scrutinized."
13WALL STREET: The Standard & Poor's 500 fell 2.13 points, or 0.1 percent, to 2,109.60 on Tuesday.
14The index has gained 0.1 percent in the past month.
15The Dow Jones industrial average dropped 28.43 points, or 0.2 percent, to 18,011.94.
16The Nasdaq composite fell 6.40 points, or 0.1 percent, to 5,076.52.
17ENERGY: Benchmark U.S. crude was down 42 cents to $60.84 a barrel in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
18The futures contract rose $1.06 to close at $61.26 a barrel in Nymex floor trading on Tuesday.
19Brent crude, a benchmark for international oil used by many U.S. refineries, was down 38 cents to $65.11 a barrel in London.
20CURRENCIES: The dollar slipped to 123.95 yen from 124.03 yen Tuesday.
21The euro rose to $1.1174 from $1.1152.
