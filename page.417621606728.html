Greek police say they're banned from parliament bathrooms

1ATHENS, Greece (AP) - Forget bailouts or austerity riots.
2The hot topic now in Greece is toilets.
3Police guarding Greece's parliament say they are no longer being allowed inside the building to use its bathrooms.
4The Police Officers Association in Athens said in a statement Monday that uniformed officers have been recently banned from entering parliament.
5It urged the Public Order Ministry to intervene to stop police from being treated as "third-class citizens" in the building they have protected from numerous violent protests.
6Greece's governing left-wing Syriza party, which came to power in January, often sided with anti-austerity demonstrations outside parliament while condemning any violence.
7Parliament refused to comment on the complaint.
8It later issued guidelines saying officers providing security at the building would in the future be dressed in civilian clothes with badges.
