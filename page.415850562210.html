Malaysia proposes anti-terror laws to curb Islamic militants

1The government has detained scores of Malaysians suspected of supporting the Islamic State militant group.
2Critics however, slammed the laws as a revival of a controversial security law that was repealed in 2012 and warned it could severely curtail civil liberties.
3The Prevention of Anti-Terror bill introduced Monday needs to be approved by Parliament for it to become law.
4It would allow authorities to detain suspects indefinitely without trial.
5Another new law gives power to authorities to seize the travel documents of any citizen or foreigners suspected to be engaged in or support of terrorist acts.
