Colorado movie massacre gunman found guilty of multiple murders

5CENTENNIAL, Colo.
6Colorado movie massacre gunman James Holmes was found guilty on Thursday of multiple counts of first degree murder, a verdict that enables prosecutors to seek the death penalty for the former graduate student who killed a dozen people and wounded 70 at a midnight premiere of a Batman film in 2012.
7After a three-month trial in which they were presented with thousands of pieces of evidence and testimony from hundreds of witnesses, jurors deliberated for about a day and a half, then found Holmes guilty on all 165 counts against him.
8The panel of nine women and three men rejected the defense's claim that Holmes was legally insane.
9Before the jury was called in at around 4:00 p.m. local time (1800 EDT), Arapahoe County District Court Judge Carlos Samour warned the packed public gallery to refrain from emotional outbursts.
10In a hushed courtroom, he began reading the guilty verdicts and did not finish until more than an hour later.
11Holmes showed no reaction.
12Wearing a blue, long-sleeved shirt and tan slacks, and tethered to the floor, the gunman stood beside his court-appointed attorneys, looking straight ahead with his hands in his pockets.
13The trial now enters the punishment phase, when the jury must determine whether Holmes, 27, should be put to death or serve a mandatory life sentence with no possibility of parole.
14That process is expected to last until late August, with both sides bringing a fresh round of witnesses.
15Prosecutors are likely to call survivors or relatives of those killed, some of whom had called for Holmes to be executed and hugged members of the prosecution when testimony ended.
16The defense team could call witnesses including more mental health professionals, and possibly even Holmes' parents, Arlene and Bob, who have attended court for most of the trial.
17The defense had conceded that Holmes was the shooter, but presented expert witnesses who testified that the former neuroscience student was not in control of his actions because he suffered from schizophrenia and heard voices ordering him to kill.
18The prosecution, meanwhile, called two court-appointed psychiatrists who concluded Holmes was legally sane when he plotted and carried out the July 2012 rampage at a multiplex in the Denver suburb of Aurora.
19District Attorney George Brauchler said the gunman was unusually intelligent but socially inept, and harbored a long-standing hatred of humanity.
20Brauchler said Holmes could not take it when he did poorly on exams at the University of Colorado, and broke up with the only girlfriend he had ever been intimate with.
21The prosecution argued that Holmes' detailed preparations for the attack showed he knew what he was doing, and knew it was wrong.
22They presented evidence about his purchases of guns, tear gas and body armor.
23They also showed how he conducted online research into bomb-making so he could booby-trap his apartment before he left for the cinema.
24Holmes rigged the bombs and turned loud music on the stereo, hoping someone would open the door and trigger a deadly blast.
25The devices were later defused by a police bomb squad.
26During the trial as dozens of wounded survivors testified about hiding behind plastic chairs from the hail of bullets, and stumbling over the bodies of loved ones as they fled the theater.
27Brauchler showed photos of the dead during his closing argument.
28His voice broke and he wiped his eyes.
29"That guy, sitting right there," he said, pointing at Holmes.
30"He did this."
31When he went to Aurora's Century 16 multiplex, Holmes was dressed head to toe in a gas mask, helmet and body armor.
32He lobbed a teargas canister into the screening, then opened fire with a semiautomatic rifle, pump action shotgun and pistol.
33He was listening to loud techno music on headphones at the time, "to block out the screams," the prosecution had said.
34Holmes declined to testify in his own defense, but jurors did watch more than 22 hours of a videotaped sanity examination conducted by one of the two court-appointed psychiatrists.
35In the video, Holmes confirmed most of the details of the mass shooting, including his purchase of the weapons and his plan to draw police and other first responders away from the theater by blowing up his apartment.
36Brauchler, during his closing argument this week, read to the jury mundane emails which Holmes sent to his parents discussing everyday topics, including the weather and a savings account, all while he was steadily amassing "overwhelming" firepower, steel-penetrating rounds, and bomb-making materials.
37Holmes, who graduated with honors from the University of California, Riverside, had no previous criminal record.
38He had been courted by neuroscience doctoral programs, but had been seeing a school psychiatrist and dropped out of a graduate program at the University of Colorado's Anschutz Medical Campus in Aurora just weeks before the attack.
39Days after the rampage, the defendant first appeared in the same small, windowless courtroom in Centennial, another Denver suburb.
40At that time, he looked wide-eyed and disoriented, and with his hair dyed red.
41He has put on weight since then, and his hair has returned to its natural brown color.
