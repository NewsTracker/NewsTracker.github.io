Egypt begins demolishing Mubarak party HQ

1Egypt on Sunday began tearing down the headquarters of the once-dominant party of ex-president Hosni Mubarak that was torched during the 2011 uprising that toppled him.
2Demolition workers, using cranes and bulldozers, began razing the four-storey headquarters of Mubarak's National Democratic Party (NDP) that overlooks the Nile in central Cairo.
3An adjacent 14-storey tower that once housed offices of state-run organisations was also being dismantled.
4In early 2011, angry protesters demanding the ouster of Mubarak had ransacked and set fire to the NDP headquarters and the adjacent tower -- seen as symbols of his autocratic rule.
5Last month the government approved plans to demolish the buildings.
6Both buildings are located close to the famed building of the Egyptian Museum of Antiquities near Cairo's iconic Tahrir Square -- epicentre of the anti-Mubarak revolt.
7"I was requested to demolish the buildings, including the foundations, in three months. After we finish this, we will start the second phase to convert it into a park," said contractor Ragab Hafez.
8He said about 200 workers would be involved in the operation.
9Mubarak's NDP party was ordered officially dissolved in 2011 but a court in July lifted a ban on former members running in elections.
10A court in November dropped murder charges against Mubarak, 87, in connection with the deaths of some 800 protesters during the uprising.
