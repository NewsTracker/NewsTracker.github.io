Silicon Valley's Google turns to Wall Street's Morgan Stanley for CFO role

1Morgan Stanley's chief financial officer Ruth Porat is leaving the bank to join internet giant Google in fresh evidence of the shift of power from Wall Street to Silicon Valley.
2Mr Pichette announced his retirement from Google on 10 March.
3At the time the internet company said that it would find a replacement within six months.
4Ms Porat, considered the most senior woman on Wall Street, will leave the bank in April, Morgan Stanley said and will be replaced by Jonathan Pruzan, co-head of the global financial institutions group at the investment bank.
5Morgan Stanley shares dropped by 0.3pc on the back of the news to $36.24 while Google shares added 1.88pc to $576.

8She also has experience with Silicon Valley as lead banker on technology financing rounds for firms including Amazon and eBay.
9The US search giant has gone through a string of changes to senior management.
