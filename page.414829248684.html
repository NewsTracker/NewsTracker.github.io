"American Sniper" trial jury considers suspect's rambling confession

1What they heard may have a big impact on whether they believe Routh's insanity defense, reports CBS News correspondent Manuel Bojorquez.
2Prosecutors used Routh's own words against him as jurors heard Routh admit several times to killing the two men and later apologizing to their families.
3Following his arrest, Routh, still in handcuffs, was interviewed by a Texas Ranger in an interrogation room.
4Navy SEAL Chris Kyle and his friend Chad Littlefield had taken the veteran, allegedly suffering from post-traumatic stress disorder, to a shooting range earlier in the day.
5The tape was played for jurors, but the judge is not allowing the audio to be released to the media.
6Routh told his interrogator after meeting Kyle and Littlefield, "I imagine they're headhunters, trying to hunt everybody down."
7He said the man he shot first was Kyle, "the one he could clearly identify."
8"If I did not take down his soul, he was going to take down mine," Routh said in the interrogation.
9"He believed that he had to pull the trigger, that it was his life or theirs," legal expert Gloria Browne-Marshall said.
10"He might have been in a state in which he thought, at that point in time, he had to do what he did."
11Routh admits to shooting the two men, but has pleaded not guilty by reason of insanity.
12On the witness stand Monday, the Ranger who interviewed Routh told jurors, "He stated that he knew it was wrong to kill them, that he wished he hadn't done it, that if he could apologize to the families he would."
13Asked if he knows the difference between right and wrong, Routh responded, "Yes, sir."
14Asked what he'd say to the families of the men he killed, he said, "I would tell them I'm so sorry for what I've done."
15The confession also included rambling statements that were more difficult to decipher, including "the warlords aren't very happy with me" and "I've got tons of people eating on my soul right now."
16"The statements that were made were sounding crazy. However, he had also been drinking as well as smoking marijuana," Browne-Marshall said.
17"Did that confused state of mind rise to the level of insanity? And that's what the jury is wrestling with."
18In the interrogation video, Routh asked if his parents had arrived at the station.
19He said, "I'd like to see them ... at least talk to my mom one last time."
