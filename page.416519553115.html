Bali Nine legal processes not done: Labor

1The federal opposition says the legal processes for the two Australians facing imminent execution in Indonesia should be allowed to run their course.
2Convicted drug smugglers Myuran Sukumaran and Andrew Chan have been told they may be executed as soon as Tuesday after being given the minimum of 72 hours notice on Saturday.
3"It is particularly concerning given there are legal processes still underway. These should be allowed to run their course," Labor's foreign affairs spokeswoman Tanya Plibersek said in a statement on Sunday.
4The Chan and Sukumaran families are making their way to Indonesia and it's understood they will be allowed to visit the men on Sunday.
5Foreign Minister Julie Bishop says the government will continue to seek clemency from Indonesian President Widodo for both men.
6UN chief Ban Ki-moon has asked Indonesia to spare the Australian pair and eight others in their group from impending execution.
7The prime minister is in Europe for the Anzac Day commemorations.
8"I think it has come down to the last minute and I would encourage the prime minister to intervene at the highest level," she told Sky News.
9Junior minister Simon Birmingham fears the worse but said the government will continue work and "hope for the best" outcomes.
10"We have left no stone unturned to date and ... we believe it is an appalling miscarriage of justice for Indonesia to proceed with the execution of these men," Senator Birmingham told Sky News.
