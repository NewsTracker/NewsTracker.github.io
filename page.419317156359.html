Cambodia senator arrested, accused of treason for Facebook comment on border dispute

1PHNOM PENH, Cambodia - Cambodian authorities arrested an opposition senator Saturday, two days after Prime Minister Hun Sen accused him of treason for comments posted on Facebook.
2Armed police handcuffed Hong Sok Hour, who was the subject of a two-day manhunt as a result of the comments that criticized a 36-year-old border agreement with Vietnam.
3Interior Ministry spokesman Gen. Khieu Sopheak said the senator was arrested in an early morning raid at the home of an opposition lawmaker where he was hiding and was taken to police headquarters for questioning before heading to a court to face charges.
4The arrest was the latest in a string of attacks on Hun Sen's political opponents.
5In recent weeks, Hun Sen has used his public speeches to deliver what amounts to arrest orders, which are generally carried out quickly.
6On Thursday, Hun Sen accused Hong Sok Hour of posting material about the 1979 border agreement.
7Hun Sen was foreign minister at that time in a government installed by a Vietnamese occupation force that invaded Cambodia to oust the murderous Khmer Rouge regime.
8"This is a crime that amounts to treason," Hun Sen said during a speech at a graduation ceremony.
9"An act of treason like this cannot be tolerated."
10The opposition Cambodia National Rescue Party has been seeking political benefit by accusing Vietnam of encroaching on Cambodian soil - a politically sensitive topic that has ramped up tension at the border.
11The opposition says the courts, under Hun Sen's influence, are pressuring them to drop the border issue by jailing their activists on insurrection charges in connection with a violent protest last year.
12Hun Sen has been in power for almost three decades, and while Cambodia is formally democratic, his government is authoritarian and known for intimidating opponents.
13An opposition spokesman said that Saturday's arrest was illegal since Hong Sok Hour is protected by parliamentary immunity.
