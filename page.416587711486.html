Baltimore riots force 2nd postponed game between Orioles, White Sox

1After a pair of postponements caused by rioting in Baltimore, the Orioles and Chicago White Sox will play Wednesday at Camden Yards in what is believed to be the first game without fans in Major League Baseball's history.
2"It's all about what's best for the city and the safety of our people," Orioles manager Buck Showalter said in a telephone interview with The Associated Press.
3"The last thing you want to do is put the fans in harm's way. You have to err on the side of safety."
4Wednesday's O's-White Sox game will be closed to public.
5Schools were closed Tuesday and the mayor imposed a 10 p.m.-to-5 a.m. curfew.
6The announcement of the unique closed-doors game came late Tuesday afternoon, after the Orioles postponed games against Chicago on Monday and Tuesday.
7"We were just trying to respond to the wishes of the public officials and protect the integrity of the schedule," Dan Duquette, the Orioles executive vice-president of baseball operations, said in a phone interview.
8The Baseball Hall of Fame and John Thorn, MLB's official historian, said they did not think there ever had been a closed-doors big league game, although there have been instances in the minor leagues.
