Chief Netanyahu rival won't join coalition, claims PM's campaign 'touched on racism'

1Isaac Herzog said his Zionist Union, which finished second in Tuesday's vote, would sit in the opposition as a counterweight to the nationalist right-wing governing coalition that Netanyahu is poised to form.
2The vote, Herzog told Israeli Army Radio, showed that "the nation wants an extreme right-wing government."
3"We will challenge it," he added, referring to his role in the opposition.
4The resounding victory by Netanyahu's right-wing Likud Party was a stunning turnaround after a tight race that had put his lengthy rule in jeopardy.
5The latest tally from Tuesday's vote shows the Zionist Union got 24 seats, compared to Likud's 30.
6In a last-ditch attempt by Netanyahu to spur supporters to the polls, he claimed of a worldwide effort to oust him and warned that Arab citizens were voting "in droves" and endangering years of Likud rule.
7The comments drew accusations of racism from Israeli Arabs and a White House rebuke which called the rhetoric "deeply concerning."
8"What Netanyahu did recently touched on racism," Herzog said of the prime minister's remarks.
9It "destroyed a deep relationship with our allies in the world. The American reaction is not at all easy."
10Herzog also accused Netanyahu of running a campaign based on "lies, fear-mongering, hostility" and said the premier's earlier claim that foreign governments were working to oust him was a "complete lie."
11He also warned of "a deep intimidation campaign that played on the deepest fears of the Israeli public," citing text messages sent to Israeli voters in the countdown to the election that warned of a high Arab voter turnout.
