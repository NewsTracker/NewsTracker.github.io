17That's close to a record low of 3.31 percent and compares with an average rate of 5.9 percent a decade ago.
18Greg McBride, chief financial analyst at Bankrate.com, thinks homeowners ought to lock in mortgage rates as long as they remain below 4 percent.

20Home loans won't hinge on the Fed's next move, though.
21Mortgage rates are closely tied to long-term interest rates, specifically the 10-year Treasury note.
22These rates are tethered to the Fed's benchmark yet have plenty of wiggle room.
23The 10-year yield has actually been falling over the past year.
24The reason?
25The Treasury market is dominated by global players.
26So when Europe's economy runs into trouble, for example, traders around the world look for safety in the Treasury market, buying U.S. government bonds and pushing yields down.
27Another factor: The Fed is keeping a lid on yields by sitting on trillions of dollars of Treasurys following a huge bond-buying program that ended last year.
