American in UAE Facebook Case May Have Charges Dismissed

1An American helicopter mechanic whose Facebook post complaining about his employer in the United Arab Emirates got him arrested said Tuesday he has been told the company is seeking to dismiss the charges against him.
2Ryan Pate, 30, appeared in an Abu Dhabi court Tuesday to present his defense on charges he defamed his Emirati employer, Global Aerospace Logistics.
3The Belleair Bluffs, Florida native posted about the company during a visit stateside following a dispute with his employer over sick leave.
4When he returned to Abu Dhabi, the oil-rich Emirati capital, he was told to report to a police station and was arrested for breaking local defamation laws.
5Pate's Facebook post has since been deleted.
6He says he can't remember exactly what he wrote, but he knows he called his bosses "backstabbers" and warned others not to work for them.
7In an interview after his hearing, Pate said his lawyer informed him that GAL has submitted paperwork seeking to dismiss the charges.
8He was cautiously optimistic that step will end his legal ordeal, though he was still waiting for final confirmation from the court.
9"I'm so grateful to the company for doing that. That was a huge thing for them to do," said Pate, adding that he regrets letting his emotions get the best of him.
10Pate says he spent about 10 days in jail before being released pending a verdict in the case.
11Authorities are holding his passport, a customary step for defendants in the Emirates, meaning he cannot leave the Gulf nation.
12Pate's congressman, Rep.
13David Jolly, has lobbied the U.S. State Department and Emirates officials for help.
14Jolly welcomed the news in a statement thanking those who helped Pate, including U.S. Ambassador Barbara Leaf, and saying he believed his constituent's comments were protected by the U.S. Constitution's first amendment because they were made on American soil.
15"I now look forward to Ryan being reunited with his family and friends back home in Florida," he said.
16GAL did not immediately return a call seeking comment.
