30 new Ebola cases, lowest weekly figure in nearly a year: WHO

1"This is the lowest weekly total since the third week of May 2014," the WHO said in its latest update.
2The virus is receding in Liberia, which reported no cases in the week to April 5, and in Sierra Leone, which reported nine, its fifth consecutive weekly decrease, it said.
3But the picture was "mixed" in Guinea, which had 21 new infections, the WHO said.
4Unsafe burials of bodies continued in Guinea and "unknown chains of transmission could be a source of new infections in the coming weeks", it warned.
5The Ebola epidemic, which began in December 2013, has claimed 10,572 lives among 25,515 known cases, according to the United Nations health agency.
6The WHO has called a meeting of independent experts this week to review whether West Africa's outbreak still constitutes a "public health emergency of international concern", which it declared in August 2014.
7A decision is due on Friday.
8Ebola is still spreading in western Guinea, including the capital Conakry, but detection of the disease has improved, the WHO said.
9"A three-day door-to-door campaign to improve community participation in surveillance activities and identify suspected cases will begin on 10 April (Friday)", it added.
10The last known Ebola case in Liberia was a patient who died on March 27, and whose 332 contacts are being monitored but none have shown symptoms so far, it said.
11"Heightened vigilance is being maintained throughout the country," the WHO said.
12"In the context of falling case incidence and a receding zone of transmission, treatment capacity exceeds demand in Liberia and Sierra Leone."
13Each country would retain a core capacity of high-quality Ebola treatment centres, strategically located to ensure complete geographic coverage, with additional rapid-response capacity held in reserve.
