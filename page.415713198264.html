Boko Haram kidnap 500 women, children from Nigerian town, residents say

1Boko Haram militants have kidnapped more than 500 women and children from the northern Nigerian town of Damasak that was reportedly freed this month by troops from Niger and Chad, residents claim.
2"They took 506 young women and children," Damasak trader Souleymane Ali said.
3"They killed about 50 of them before leaving.
4"We don't know if they killed others after leaving, but they took the rest with them."
5Mohamed Ousmane, another trader, said the militants took his two wives and three of their children.
6A 40-year-old resident who gave her name as Fana said fighters had rounded up captives in the main mosque before taking them out of town.
7She said she saved her two children by hiding them in her house.
8There was no immediate official confirmation of the kidnap figure, but the Islamist group has previously carried out mass kidnappings in northern Nigeria, where it wants to carve out a caliphate.
9Boko Haram's abduction last April of nearly 300 schoolgirls in the region stirred international outrage and drew global attention to the group's six-year insurgency.
10Troops from Niger and Chad last week found the bodies of at least 70 people in an apparent execution site under a bridge leading out of Damasak, where the streets remain strewn with debris and burnt-out cars after the fighting.
11Nigerian, Chadian and Niger forces have driven militants out of a string of towns in simultaneous offensives over the past month.
12Nigeria said all but three of the 20 local government areas occupied at the beginning of the year had been freed.
13Nigeria's rearranged election is now due to take place on Saturday after a sharp increase in violence forced a delay in planned elections last month.
14Souleymane Ali, the Damasak trader, said he was just hoping life would return to normal.
15"They killed all our friends, our family members, so we just submitted ourselves to God."
