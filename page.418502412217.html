Apple Pay UK launch: Everything you need to know

1YOUR iPhone is about to replace your wallet.
2And it starts across the UK tomorrow.
3Apple Pay - which was announced at WWDC - will hit UK stores tomorrow morning.
4As soon as the new payment system goes live, iPhone and Apple Watch owners will be able to use their Apple-branded gadgets instead of debit and credit cards at checkout.
5Apple Pay will allow iPhone users to "tap-and-pay" with their smartphones for the first time.
6The UK launch also marks the first time the software will be available outside the US.
7Starting tomorrow, Apple iPhone 6, iPhone 6 Plus and Apple Watch owners will be able to use the new payment system in more than 250,000 locations across the UK.
8Express.co.uk has rounded up some of the most common questions around the launch - and answered them.
9Which Apple devices do I need to use Apple Pay?
10Those who want to replace their humble debit card with Apple Pay will need either an iPhone 6, iPhone 6 Plus or Apple Watch.
11These are the only devices which support tap-to-pay because they are the only Apple devices with the NFC chip.
12However - Apple Pay also works online across a number of retailers.
13This feature - which secures the online transaction with your fingerprint - works across a slightly larger spread of Apple devices, including the iPhone 6, 6 Plus, iPad Air 2, iPad mini 3 and the Apple Watch.
14Some reports have suggested older phones may work in the future but, for now, anyone without the latest Apple iPhone will have to stick to good old fashioned chip-and-pin.
15Apple Pay uses Near Field Communication antenna technology, dubbed NFC.
16This system - which is also available in a range of Android devices and used for rival payment solutions - allows the iPhone to communicate wirelessly with the contactless payment machines.
17Once the system has gone live, Apple iPhone owners will be able to hold their smartphone close to a contactless reader while keeping their finger on the Touch ID fingerprint scanner.
18This authorises the payment.
19The smartphone will vibrate to let you know the payment has been approved.
20Apple Pay will automatically pay using the customers default card, although you can cycle through the available credit and debit cards at checkout on the smartphone or Watch display.
21Credit, debit and loyalty cards can all be kept within the iPhone's preinstalled passbook app.
22Cards can be added by taking a photograph.
23Will my bank let me use Apple Pay?
24Eight British banks will support the new mobile payment system at launch.
25American Express, First Direct, HSBC, Nationwide, Natwest, Royal Bank of Scotland, Santander and Ulster Bank customers will all be able to use the system in the UK.
26Bank of Scotland, Halifax, Lloyds Bank, M&S Bank, MBNA and TSB have all announced plans to support Apple Pay - although a final date has not been released.
27Barclays - which recently refreshed its own wearable payment system - will not support the iPhone payment system.
28Where can I shop with Apple Pay?
29Apple claims a staggering 250,000 stores - as well as the entire Transport for London underground, bus and rail network - will accept Apple Pay as soon as the system goes live.
30Shops already signed up to support the system include Starbucks, Costa, M&S, McDonald's, KFC, Pret A Manger, Boots, JD Sports, Nando's, Lidl and Waitrose.
31Apple claims more will be added "every day."
32Is Apple Pay secure?
33Apple says privacy and security is a key part of Apple Pay.
34Once you have added a credit or debit card to your device, the card numbers are never shared again.
35Apple does not upload them to its servers - nor does it even reveal your card details to the merchant you are paying.
36Instead the privacy-minded firm creates an entirely new "unique Device Account Number".
37This is "assigned, encrypted and securely stored in the Secure Element, a dedicated chip in iPhone, iPad and Apple Watch."
38Since every device has a separate Secure Element, card details have to be entered separately on each Apple device.
39"And when you make a purchase, the Device Account Number, along with a transaction-specific dynamic security code, is used to process your payment," Apple explains.
40"So your actual credit or debit card numbers are never shared by Apple with merchants or transmitted with payment."
41Apple say their service is far more secure than current payment methods since every time you hand over your physical credit or debit card to pay - your card number, details and identity are visible.
42Each time you pay you'll need to use the Touch ID fingerprint scanner - this allows your iPhone and iPad to identify you as the shopper and adds a layer of biometric security.
43If your device is stolen you can immediately revoke the ability to make payments using your Apple account via the iCloud website.
