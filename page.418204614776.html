New Mexico governor denounces Trump's comments

1New Mexico Gov. Susana Martinez speaks about new laws expanding the state's Amber Alert and fighting child prostitution Wednesday, July 1, 2015, at a New Mexico Police State station in Albuquerque N.M. The nation's only Latina governor denounced fellow Republican and GOP presidential hopeful Donald Trump for his comments about Mexican immigrants.
2New Mexico Gov. Susana Martinez denounced GOP presidential hopeful Donald Trump's recent remarks about Mexican immigrants and called his comments "horrible."
3After a news conference Wednesday on new state laws aimed at protecting abused children, the nation's only Latina governor said she strongly disagreed with Trump's comments that have drawn criticism across the country.
4Macy's, for example, said in a statement the retailer is disappointed and distressed by Trump's remarks and it would no longer carry a Trump menswear line.
5"I think those are horrible things to say about anyone or any culture ... anyone of any ethnicity," said Martinez, a Republican.
6"I mean, that is uncalled for ... completely."
7Martinez said she hadn't been keeping up with Trump, but expressed shock after learning about comments he made during his presidential campaign announcement.
8The outspoken Trump asserted that some Mexican immigrants bring drugs and crime to the U.S. and are rapists.
9The comment struck many as insensitive, and Univision's decision last week to back out of televising Miss USA and break off its business ties with Trump led to a cascade of others following suit, including the Macy's department store chain.
10The PGA Tour, LPGA Tour, USGA and PGA of America also on Wednesday distanced themselves from Trump in a statement and said his remarks were "inconsistent with our strong commitment to an inclusive and welcoming environment in the game of golf."
11Martinez, a rising star within the Republican Party, has been vocal about candidates toning down rhetoric on immigration reform.
12She is actively involved in efforts to recruit more women and minority candidates to run for office under the GOP.
13In addition, Martinez has gone on a number of trade missions to Mexico and has been pushing for more infrastructure at the booming border town of Santa Teresa.
14Last year, she won re-election in blue-leaning New Mexico, the state with the largest number of Hispanic residents, by a wide margin.
15Martinez has faced criticism from immigrant advocates for her efforts to repeal a state law that allows immigrants in the country illegally to obtain a state driver's license.
