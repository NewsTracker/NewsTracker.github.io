4March 2011: Russian FSB intelligence security service gives FBI information that Tamerlan Tsarnaev, of Cambridge, Massachusetts, is a follower of radical Islam.
5June 2011: FBI closes investigation after finding nothing to link Tamerlan Tsarnaev to terrorism.
6Sept. 12, 2011: Bodies of three men are found in Waltham, Massachusetts, with their throats slit and marijuana sprinkled over them.
7Late 2011: U.S. officials add the Tsarnaevs' mother to a federal terrorism database after Russia contacts CIA with concerns they were religious militants about to travel to Russia.
8She later says she has no links to terrorism.
9January 2012: Tamerlan arrives in Russia, where he spends time in two predominantly Muslim provinces, Dagestan and Chechnya.
10July 2012: Officials in Dagestan say Tamerlan applies for a new passport but never picks it up.
11Russian officials say they have him under surveillance but lose track of him after the death of a Canadian man who had joined an Islamic insurgency in the region.
12July 17, 2012: Tamerlan returns to U.S.
13November 2012: Islamic Society of Boston Cultural Center in Cambridge says Tamerlan has an outburst that interrupts a sermon about it being acceptable for Muslims to celebrate American holidays.
14January 2013: Islamic Society says Tamerlan has a second outburst after a sermon that includes praise for the Rev.
15Martin Luther King Jr.
16April 15, 2013: Bombs go off at the finish line of the Boston Marathon, killing three people and injuring more than 260 others.
17April 16, 2013: Federal agents say the bombs were made from pressure cookers packed with explosives, nails and other shrapnel, but they still don't know who detonated them or why.
18April 17, 2013: President Barack Obama signs emergency declaration for Massachusetts and orders federal aid to supplement local response.
19April 18, 2013: Investigators release photos and video of two suspects and ask for public's help identifying them.
20Later that night, Massachusetts Institute of Technology police officer Sean Collier is shot to death in his cruiser, allegedly by Tamerlan and Dzhokhar Tsarnaev.
21Prosecutors say they steal an SUV at gunpoint from a Cambridge gas station.
22The driver is held for about a half-hour, then released unharmed.
23April 19, 2013: Tsarnaevs have an early morning gunbattle with authorities who have tracked them to Watertown.
24Tamerlan, who is run over by his younger brother, dies.
25Dzhokhar escapes, and at around 6 a.m., authorities tell residents of Boston and surrounding communities to stay indoors.
26All mass transit is shut down.
27That order is lifted around 6:30 p.m., just before authorities trace Dzhokhar to a Watertown backyard, where he is found hiding in a boat and taken into custody.
28April 22, 2013: Dzhokhar Tsarnaev, injured in the shootout, is charged in his hospital room with using and conspiring to use a weapon of mass destruction.
29April 30, 2013: Two friends of Dzhokhar's are charged with attempting to destroy evidence by disposing of a backpack and laptop computer taken from his room after they found he was a suspect in the bombing.
30Another is charged with lying to investigators.
31May 9, 2013: Tamerlan Tsarnaev is secretly buried in Virginia after a weeklong search for a cemetery willing to take the body.
32May 22, 2013: An FBI agent in Orlando, Florida, fatally shoots Ibragim Todashev, a friend of Tamerlan's, after he lunges at law enforcement officials questioning him about the Waltham killings.
33Officials say that before he died, he had agreed to give a statement about his involvement.
34July 10, 2013: Dzhokhar Tsarnaev pleads not guilty to 30 federal charges.
35July 23, 2013: Marc Fucarile is the last survivor of the bombings to leave the hospital.
36Jan. 30, 2014: Prosecutors announce they will seek the death penalty against Dzhokhar.
37April 15, 2014: Ceremonies and events mark the anniversary of the attacks.
38April 21, 2014: The 2014 Boston Marathon features a field of 36,000 runners, 9,000 more than 2013 and the second-biggest field in history.
39May 30, 2014: Khairullozhon Matanov, 23, is arrested on charges of obstructing the investigation by deleting information from his computer and lying to investigators.
40June 18, 2014: Tsarnaev's lawyers file first of several requests to move the trial to Washington, D.C.
41July 21, 2014: Azamat Tazhayakov, a college friend of Dzhokhar's, is convicted of obstruction of justice and conspiracy for agreeing with another friend to get rid of a backpack and disabled fireworks they took from his dorm room three days after the attack.
42July 22, 2014: Stephen Silva, believed to have provided the gun used by the Tsarnaevs to kill Collier, is arrested on drug and weapons charges.
43Aug. 22, 2014: Dias Kadyrbayev, 20, pleads guilty to impeding the investigation by removing incriminating evidence from Dzhokhar's dorm room.
44Sept. 24, 2014: Judge grants delay and pushes start of trial to Jan. 5, 2015.
45Oct. 28, 2014: Robel Phillipos, 21, is convicted of lying to federal agents about being in Dzhokhar's room.
46Nov. 25, 2014: Federal judge rejects a request from lawyers for Tsarnaev to order prosecutors to turn over evidence about his older brother's possible participation in the Waltham slayings.
47Dec. 18, 2014: Tsarnaev appears in court for first time since his July 2013 arraignment.
48Jan. 5, 2015: Jury selection begins in Tsarnaev's trial.
49March 4, 2015: Tsarnaev's lead defense attorney, Judy Clarke, declares in opening statements: ``It was him.''
50April 6, 2015: Prosecutors and defense present closing statements.
51April 7, 2015: Jury begins deliberating verdicts.
52April 8, 2015: Jury convicts Tsarnaev; will weigh possible death sentence in forthcoming penalty phase of trial.
