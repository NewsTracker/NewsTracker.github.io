After a Cannes drubbing, Van Sant reflects on 'Sea of Trees'

1CANNES, France (AP) - The full-throated passions of the Cannes Film Festival can elevate, just as they can humble.
2As the Palme d'Or winner in 2003 for his school-shooting drama "Elephant," Gus Van Sant has basked in the festival's sunny glow, just as he has, this year, experienced its cold shoulder.
3Van Sant's "The Sea of Trees," which stars Matthew McConaughey as a man lost in Japan's Aokigahara "suicide" forest, received emphatic boos at its premiere to press members.
4Though its official red-carpet premiere on Saturday went down better, the Cannes fate of "The Sea of Trees" was already sealed as (at least thus far) the festival's most rudely received film.
5"I was, as I always am, cautious," Van Sant said in an interview about showing "The Sea of Trees" at Cannes.
6Speaking Sunday on a windy, seaside pier, Van Sant sounded sanguine about the stormy waters of Cannes.
7"Who knows what people thought they were going to see, but they could easily get really mad because it is very sentimental," said Van Sant.
8"What better movie to get angry at than something that's too schmaltzy for you? I can see that."
9In "The Sea of Trees," McConaughey plays a man racked with guilt over his marriage (Naomi Watts plays his wife) who travels to Japan to kill himself.
10In the forest, he meets another man (Ken Watanabe) and the movie plays out as part spiritual soul-searching, part survival drama.
11Plenty of films at Cannes have been received negatively this year.
12But usually the loudest booing is reserved only for strongly polarizing movies (even Francis Ford Coppola's "Apocalypse Now" was booed in its Cannes debut) or maybe if the hype has so exceeded the movie's merits.
13Such was the case last year when Ryan Gosling's highly anticipated directorial debut "Lost River" was booed and slammed by critics.
14"I think maybe because it's McConaughey, there's a lot of stock in him at the moment and his Lincoln ads are parodied," said Van Sant.
15"And it's me. It was sort of an illustrious screenplay. It had a really good reaction among the agents. ... If it was anticipated, I could easily see myself disappointed. If I thought it was a certain type of movie, and it was just a different movie."
16Van Sant says that festival director Thierry Fremaux called to say he wanted the film for Cannes before seeing it.
17"Then it went to competition and I was like: 'OK, wow. This is scary,'" said Van Sant.
18"But it doesn't matter what you've made. It's always going to be scary."
19But sunnier days could still shine on "The Sea of Trees."
20It was picked up for U.S. distribution by Lionsgate and Roadside Attractions ahead of the festival.
21The movie-going public generally has less of an issue with sentimentality than Cannes critics, and McConaughey is one of the most popular stars in film right now.
22A release date hasn't yet been announced, but sometime next year is likely.
23"Every film you do," Van Sant says, "has its possibility of hitting just the right note at the right time."
