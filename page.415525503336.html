1Nearly 400 years after his death, the quest to find Cervantes had led Spanish scientists deep into the sub-soil of a 17th century convent.

10Investigators began their search almost a year ago in the cloistered convent, still home to a dozen elderly nuns.
11They recently found bone fragments and as well as a dilapidated piece of a wooden coffin with the letters "M" and "C" on it.
12None of the bones showed signs of the injuries Cervantes was known to have sustained during his life, including lesions to his left arm when he fought in the Battle of Lepanto in 1571, the experts said.
