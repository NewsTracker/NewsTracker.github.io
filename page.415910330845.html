Massive fire engulfs Mexican oil rig, 4 dead

1The fire broke out overnight at the Abkatun Permanente platform, located in the Campeche Sound, near the coast of the Mexican states of Campeche and Tabasco.
2MEXICO CITY (AP) - A massive ball of flames engulfed an oil platform in the Gulf of Mexico on Wednesday, killing four people and sending terrified workers jumping into the sea.
3State-run oil company Petroleos Mexicanos, or Pemex, said there was no evidence yet of a major oil spill following the blast, which injured 16 workers, two seriously, and forced the evacuation of 300.
4Fleets of helicopters ferried workers with bandaged hands and faces, and burn marks on their overalls to the nearby city of Ciudad del Carmen, where crowds of concerned relatives of oil workers thronged outside hospitals.
5A survivor of the blaze on the shallow-water Abkatun Permanente platform in the Campeche Sound said workers "jumped into the sea out of desperation and panic."
6"There was nothing you could do but run," said Roger Arias Sanchez, an employee of Pemex contractor Cotemar who escaped the burning platform in an evacuation boat.
7Eight firefighting boats were trying to extinguish the fire, said Pemex.
8Mexico's Energy Security Agency said the fire "is being extinguished."
9On its Twitter account Wednesday afternoon, Pemex raised the death toll from one to four.
10Many of the injured appeared to be Cotemar employees.
11Pemex's media office said it was unclear whether any significant amount of oil had spilled from the Abkatun Permanente platform, which largely serves to separate gas, oil and other petroleum products, and pump them to refineries onshore.
12Previous spills from Mexican facilities had usually occurred at active offshore wells, not processing stations such as Abkatun.
13The Abkatun platform lies off the coast of the states of Campeche and Tabasco.
14It is further out to sea than the platform involved in the last severe fire in the area, the 2007 fire at the Kab 121 offshore rig.
15That accident was caused by high waves that hit the rig, sending a boom crashing into an oil platform's valve assembly.
16The blaze killed at least 21 workers and the rig spilled crude and natural gas for almost two months.
17Mexico's worst major spill in the Gulf was in June 1979, when an offshore drilling rig in Mexican waters - the Ixtoc I - blew up, releasing 140 million gallons of oil.
18It took Pemex and a series of U.S. contractors nearly nine months to cap the well, and a great deal of the oil contaminated Mexican and U.S. waters.
