American Arrested, 1 Dead After Police Foil Mass Killing Plot

Both sides claim violations after Ukraine cease-fire starts

1The man was a suspect in a Valentine's Day mass murder plot, Feb. 13, 2015.
2Police in Canada say they foiled a plot by an American woman and a Canadian man who allegedly planned to go to a public place in Halifax on Valentine's Day and kill as many people as possible before committing suicide.
3The Nova Scotia Royal Canadian Mounted Police identified the woman as Lindsay Kantha Souvannarath, of Geneva, Illinois.
4She was arrested at Halifax Stanfield International Airport early Friday.
5Souvannarath, 23, and Randall Steven Shepherd, 20, were charged with conspiracy to commit murder, police said.
6"This is a reminder that this type of incident can happen anywhere," Halifax Regional Police Chief Jean-Michel Blais said.
7A 19-year-old Canadian man who was believed to be linked to the plot died from a self-inflicted gunshot wound after officers surrounded his home, police said.
8Officials said authorities received a tip that Souvannarath and the 19-year-old Canadian "had access to firearms, and it was their intention to go to a public venue in the Halifax region on February 14 with the goal of opening fire to kill citizens and then themselves."
9Police said that "evidence also supports" that Shepherd was involved in the plot, but they did not release further details.
10"It would have been devastating," Canadian Minister of Justice Peter Mackay said Saturday.
11Mackay did not say what public venue was targeted, but said extra security was in place at the site.
12Mackay said Souvannarath and the 19-year-old Canadian were chatting online.
13An official told The Associated Press that the two apparently were obsessed with death and had many photos of mass killings.
14A 17-year-old who was arrested Friday was later released by police.
15Police in Geneva, Illinois, who are assisting with the investigation, said today they never had any contact with Souvannarath and there is "no indication at all that there was any involvement locally."
16The Geneva police added that her family has been "extremely cooperative."
17Souvannarath and Shepherd are scheduled to appear in Halifax Provincial Court on Tuesday.

8Meanwhile, rebels accused the Ukrainians of deploying artillery shortly after midnight.

12Speaking to his Russian counterpart Sergey Lavrov by telephone Saturday, U.S. Secretary of State John Kerry expressed concern about what he called efforts by Russia and the separatists to cut off Debaltseve in advance of the cease-fire.

15An undated satellite image issued by the Ukrainian government showed a five-kilometer- (three-mile-) long cloud of black smoke hovering above Debaltseve, evidence of what it said was the scale of rebel shelling in the hours before the scheduled start of the cease-fire.
16Entire swathes of the war-stricken regions of Donetsk and Luhansk were caught up in artillery duels over the weekend.

18Mariupol is on the Azov Sea and concerns are strong that the Russian-backed separatists aim to seize it to create a land corridor that runs between mainland Russia and the Crimean Peninsula, which Russia annexed 11 months ago.
19Towns distant from the front line were not spared.
20Projectiles rained down Saturday afternoon on the government-held town of Artemivsk, 40 kilometers (25 miles) north of Debaltseve, striking a school, which rapidly burned to the ground.
21Russia has repeatedly denied Western claims that it has sent troops and equipment to aid the rebels.
22But on Saturday, the U.S. ambassador to Ukraine, Geoffrey Pyatt, posted on Twitter what he said were satellite photos showing Russian artillery systems near the town of Lomuvatka, 20 kilometers (12 miles) northeast of Debaltseve.
23The images could not immediately be verified.
24In a telephone call with Poroshenko hours before the start of the cease-fire period, President Barack Obama expressed his "deep concern about the ongoing violence, particularly in and around Debaltseve."
25The White House said in a statement that the two leaders "emphasized the pressing need" for all parties to implement the cease-fire and agreed to remain in contact in the coming days.
26Obama also spoke with German Chancellor Angela Merkel who took a lead role in negotiating the cease-fire agreement.
27The deal was hammered out in a marathon talks this week between Merkel and her counterparts from Ukraine, Russia and France in the Belarusian capital, Minsk.
28German Foreign Minister Frank-Walter Steinmeier said failure to maintain the peace would cost the region and the people of eastern Ukraine "a high price."
29"If we miss this opportunity, the chance to defuse the serious conflict in eastern Ukraine by way of negotiations will be gone for a long time," Steinmeier said.
30Speaking in Kiev on Saturday afternoon, Poroshenko said his government would take urgent measures if the Minsk agreement collapsed.
31"If there is no peace, we may have to take the decision to impose martial law," he said.
32"In this event, martial law will be imposed not only in the Donetsk and Luhansk (regions), but across the whole country."
33Cessation of hostilities is only the first in a series of planned steps agreed to in Minsk.
34Withdrawals of heavy weaponry from the front line, creating a zone roughly 30-85 miles wide, depending on the caliber of the weapons, are to begin Monday and be completed in two weeks.
35No provisions are envisioned for the withdrawal of troops.
36The peace plan also requires the Ukrainian government to resume paying pensions and state benefits to citizens in rebel-held territory.
37Ukraine's financial blockade against the rebels has led to a catastrophic collapse in living standards in eastern Ukraine, depriving the poorest of any immediate means of support.
38The fighting started in April after armed pro-Russian separatists took control of towns and official buildings in the Donetsk and Luhansk regions.
39The seizures began after Russia-friendly president Viktor Yanukovych was driven from power in the wake of months of protests in the capital, Kiev.
40The separatists claim the new Ukrainian authorities are fascist-inspired and aim to suppress the heavily ethnic Russian population in the east.
