20BALTIMORE (AP) - A protest over the death of Freddie Gray, who was critically injured in police custody, started peacefully with thousands marching through downtown streets before the demonstration turned violent and volatile.
21The chaotic scene Saturday night prompted the first public remarks from Freddie Gray's twin sister, who pleaded for peace at a news conference alongside the mayor.
22"My family wants to say, can you all please, please stop the violence?"
23Fredricka Gray said.
24"Freddie Gray would not want this."
25Just before nightfall, groups of protesters marched from City Hall to the Camden Yards baseball stadium, where the Baltimore Orioles played the Boston Red Sox.
26Fans were told to briefly stay inside the stadium until the police were able to clear an intersection outside of the venue.
27Meanwhile, a smaller "splinter group" looted a convenience store and threw tables and chairs through storefront windows, shattering the glass.
28One group smashed the window of a department store inside a downtown mall and, at one point, a protester tossed a flaming metal garbage can toward a line of police officers in riot gear as they tried to push back the crowd.
29Earlier, a group of protesters smashed the windows of at least three police cars and got into fights with baseball fans outside a bar.
30Police Commissioner Anthony Batts said roughly 1,200 officers were deployed downtown and across the city to try and keep the peace.
31At least five officers were injured and 12 people were arrested.
32Batts said he believes the "very violent agitators" are not from Baltimore.
33"I'm proud of our residents," Batts said.
34"The majority of the people here did a great job."
35But Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake said she was "profoundly disappointed."
36"Unfortunately a small group of agitators turned what was otherwise a peaceful demonstration into a violent protest. This is something that's unacceptable to me and everyone who lives in Baltimore," she said.
37Fire officials said roughly 1,200 protesters gathered at City Hall Saturday afternoon to protest Gray's death, which has prompted near-daily demonstrations since his death on April 19.
38Gray was arrested one week before that when officers chased him through a West Baltimore neighborhood and dragged him into a police van.
39Police acknowledged Friday that Gray, 25, should have received medical attention at the spot where he was arrested - before he was put inside a police transport van handcuffed and without a seat belt, a violation of the Police Department's policy.
40Gray, who is black, was arrested after he made eye contact with officers and ran away, police said.
41Officers held him down, handcuffed him and loaded him into the van.
42While inside, he became irate and leg cuffs were put on him, police have said.
43Gray asked for medical help several times, beginning before he was placed in the van.
44After a 30-minute ride that included three stops, paramedics were called.
45Authorities have not explained how or when Gray's spine was injured.
46On Saturday afternoon, protesters gathered at the site of Gray's arrest in the Sandtown neighborhood of West Baltimore before making their way to City Hall.
47Leonard Patterson, 56, said he drove from Manassas, Virginia, to be a part of the protest.
48Patterson said he decided to come after thinking about his college-aged daughter.
49"I'm trying to do everything in my limbs, everything in my power, to make this a better world for her," he said as he held a drawing of Gray being hoisted from a police van to heaven by two angels.
50"I'm here to do what I can. Police brutality is as old as the 1950s, 1960s. It's still here."
51Wearing a sign around his neck that said "I am Freddie Gray," 33-year-old Dante Acree joined thousands of others outside City Hall.
52Acree said he came out to the protest because "it could have been one of my kids."
