Bad weather, bad golf: Tiger Woods misses another major cut (Yahoo Sports)

1ST. ANDREWS, Scotland (AP) -- Tiger Woods at least enjoyed that familiar walk over the Swilcan Bridge and up the 18th fairway Saturday at St. Andrews, the gray old town in the background and a crowd six deep lining the road and watching from windows.
2Not so familiar was his score.
3He hasn't had 7-over par next to his name at St. Andrews since he was a 19-year-old amateur, and that was the final round.
4This was only the second round, and he was headed home early for the third time in the last four majors.
5''It's one of the things I was talking about with Jason (Day).
6We were coming up 18, I said, 'It's the greatest walk in golf,''' Woods said after a 75 to finish two miserable days at 151, his worst 36-hole score in the British Open.
7''He said, 'Yeah, it's nice when you have an eight-shot lead, too,'' Woods added with a smile.
8Woods won by eight shots in 2000 to complete the career Grand Slam.
9He won by five shots in 2005 to establish anew his dominance in the game.
10Those days seem so long ago.
11The former No.
121 player in the world and 14-time major champion looked tired, beaten and stressed as he made his way around one of his favorite courses.
13He had only three birdies over 36 holes and said he has no idea why his shots didn't penetrate in the wind and why he couldn't get it close enough to the hole.
14''The golf course wasn't playing that hard,'' Woods said.
15That's been the story all year.
16For the first time, he missed the cut in the U.S. Open and the British Open in the same season, and neither was close.
17In those two championships he has won a combined six times, Woods beat only four players at Chambers Bay and seven players at St. Andrews.
18And so the mystery continues.
19Woods will fall out of the top 250 when the next world ranking is published after starting the year at No.
2032. He already has had three rounds in the 80s this year after having just one for his entire career.
21He had hope coming off The Greenbrier, where he tied for 32nd and was six shots behind.
22He looked lost on the Old Course.
23''I felt like I was playing well enough to win this event,'' Woods said.
24He arrived on Saturday to do a junior clinic for Nike - Woods typically is all about preparations at the majors - and then after practice rounds on Sunday and Monday, he didn't play another practice round on the Old Course until the championship started.
25Woods said he knew the course, practiced in both wind directions and wanted to conserve energy for what usually is a long week.
26Except for the weather - rain on Friday, wind on Saturday - that made the second round take 24 hours to complete, this was a short week.
27Woods plays in two weeks at his Quicken Loans National, and then he most likely closes his 2014-15 season at the PGA Championship.
28He said he still had designs on winning his tournament to become eligible for the Bridgestone Invitational.
29He won't see St. Andrews again until the British Open returns, likely in 2021.
30''I'll probably have less hair then,'' Woods said, ''and hopefully a little better game.''
