Russia urges Germany, France to safeguard peace in Ukraine

1Paris and Berlin helped mediate a peace deal in the Belarussian capital Minsk on Feb. 12 to try to end fighting between government forces and pro-Russian separatists in eastern Ukraine but the truce remains fragile.
2In an interview with Russian television, Russian Foreign Minister Sergei Lavrov said he was concerned Kiev might stage "provocations" to try to persuade the United States that it should aid Kiev by sending it lethal weapons.
3"Provocateurs in Kiev ... could try to 'whip something up' in the expectation that this will influence the world public and weapons will flow into Ukraine," he told the new program Vesti on Saturday with Sergei Brilev.
4"I am convinced that Berlin and Paris, as the most important players ..., should prevent such a turn of events."
5Lavrov also repeated Russia's opposition to United Nations peacekeepers being sent to the east.
6The United States has been considering whether to provide lethal weapons to Ukraine but has taken no decision on this yet.
7Kiev accuses Moscow of not carrying out the terms of the Minsk agreements.
8It and the West say Russia backs the separatists in east Ukraine with weapons and troops but Moscow denies this.
9More than 6,000 people have been killed in almost one year of fighting in the east.
10The truce there is fragile, with Moscow and Kiev clashing publicly over who is to blame for the failure to carry out all the steps outlined in the Minsk agreements.
11The European Union and the United States have imposed economic sanctions on Russia over the Ukraine crisis, which has caused the worst strains in relations between the West and Moscow since the end of the Cold War.
12EU leaders decided on Thursday that the sanctions would stay in place until the peace deal is fully implemented, effectively extending them to the end of the year if need be.
