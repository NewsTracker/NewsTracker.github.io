Cabrera hits 400th homer, Tigers outlast Cards 4-3 in 10 (Yahoo Sports)

1ST. LOUIS (AP) -- Miguel Cabrera's bat gave the Detroit Tigers a smashing start.
2His under-rated defense helped save them late.
3Cabrera hit his 400th career home run and turned a nifty double play with a catch near the first base stands followed by an on-target throw in a 4-3, 10-inning victory over the St. Louis Cardinals on Saturday.
4''He's definitely the best hitter in baseball and he's hands down the smartest player in baseball as well,'' starter David Price said.
5Cabrera's milestone shot gave him the most homers by a Venezuelan-born player, passing Andres Galarraga.
6The solo shot came in the first inning just before a rain delay of 1 hour, 20 minutes.
7''That means a lot to me,'' Cabrera said about the milestone.
8Nearly five hours later, Jose Iglesias had an RBI single for the go-ahead hit.
9Cabrera has hit four homers in three games, 10 overall and 262 with Detroit, tying him with Willie Horton for fourth on the franchise list.
10Cabrera said he wasn't sure if he got the 400th home run ball back.
11Adrian Beltre of Texas hit his 400th homer Friday.
12Cabrera tied Beltre for 52nd place on the career list, with Duke Snider next at 407.
13The Cardinals had a man on second with none out in the eighth when Cabrera caught a foul popup near the stands.
14He wheeled and threw out pinch runner Pete Kozma, who was trying to tag up and advance to third.
15''It was a crazy play,'' Cabrera said.
16Price threw only a couple of pitches before the rain delay.
17He surrendered three homers, matching the total he'd allowed his first seven starts, but all were with the bases empty on changeups.
18''It was some good swings on some pitches down the middle of the plate, and that's what good hitters do,'' Price said.
19Matt Carpenter, Peter Bourjos and Jhonny Peralta connected, but the Cardinals made three costly base-running goofs in the final three innings.
20Blaine Hardy (1-0) struck out two with a walk in the ninth and Joakim Soria finished for his 12th save in 12 chances.
21Detroit, which pasted St. Louis 10-4 in the opener, will go for a three-game interleague sweep on Sunday.
22Iglesias, the eighth-place hitter, flied out with the bases loaded to end the third and popped out with two on to finish the fifth.
23He came through with two outs in the 10th against Matt Belisle (1-1).
24J.D. Martinez drew a leadoff walk and Yoenis Cespedes followed with a single.
25Two outs later, Iglesias singled.
26The Cardinals blew a chance to take the lead in the seventh.
27Jason Heyward stayed near second base, waiting to tag up, and could only reach third when Randal Grichuk's flyball hit the wall for a double.
28''I looked at the ball, checked the outfielder and I was thinking to myself 'This is so high I think it's either going to be gone or caught at the wall,'' Heyward added.
29With the Cardinals down a run, Matt Holliday was out trying to stretch a single to two bases leading off the 10th.
30Carpenter's seventh homer tied it in the bottom of the first, on just the second at-bat after play resumed following the delay.
31Bourjos had been 1 for 11 against Price before putting the Cardinals ahead in the second with his first homer.
32Tigers: Alfredo Simon (4-1, 3.05) was 2-0 in three starts against St. Louis last year while with Cincinnati.
33Cardinals: Lance Lynn (2-3, 3.27) is 28-13 with a 2.83 ERA at home.
34Tigers: Pitcher Shane Greene underwent an MRI exam and Tigers manager Brad Ausmus said ''concern is way down.'' On Saturday, he was removed due to numbness in his pitching hand.
35Cespedes bent his glove thumb attempting a diving catch in the 10th.
36Cardinals: Pitcher Jaime Garcia (shoulder) was back in St. Louis a day after throwing six innings in his second rehab start and could be close to rejoining the rotation.
