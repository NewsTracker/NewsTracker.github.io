Mexico reports bird flu outbreaks in Puebla and Oaxaca states

1Following a notification by the owners of an increase in sudden death in birds, 139 birds were found infected by the virus, the Mexican agriculture ministry said in a report posted on the website of the Paris-based organization.
2"The official veterinary services launched the necessary epidemiological investigation and the presence of highly pathogenic avian influenza virus subtype H7N3 was confirmed," it said.
3Both outbreaks were confirmed as positive for avian influenza on March 9.
4So far, no epidemiological link had been identified between them, it said.
