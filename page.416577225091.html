Bali nine executions: Myuran Sukumaran's haunting final paintings

1In this 2013 interview, Myuran Sukumaran explains how he pursued redemption through art.
2A painting of a human heart by Myuran Sukumaran has been brought back from the penal island on which he will be put to death.
3The painting, which is signed by all nine prisoners facing execution, is inscribed "Satu hati satu rasa didalam cinta - (one heart, one feeling in love)".
4Myuran Sukumaran's painting of a human heart.
5It is the latest in an extraordinary series of death row paintings with haunting inscriptions such as "72 hours just started", "A BAD SLEEP LAST NIGHT" and "Self portrait: Time is ticking".
6One prisoner signed the painting with "God bless Indonesia".
7Another, Filipino mother Mary Jane Veloso, called on people to "keep smiling".
8On Monday the Bali nine pair's Australian lawyer Julian McMahon brought back a self portrait of Sukumaran's torso with a black hole over the heart.
9Lawyer Julian McMahon carries Myuran Sukumaran's self portrait of his upper body, with a black hole over his heart.
10Meanwhile, the Indonesian government has appointed two Indonesian spiritual counsellors to be with Sukumaran and Andrew Chan in their final hours.
11In what Chan's brother Michael described as "the last bit of dignity denied," the Attorney-General's office denied the men their choice of religious guide.
12This is despite reassuring the Australian embassy two days earlier that they would be permitted to choose who would witness their deaths.
13Paintings by Myuran Sukumaran, and signed by all nine awaiting execution are displayed by Sukumaran's family at Wijaya Pura in Cilacap on the day of the execution.
14Chan had chosen Salvation Army Minister and old family friend David Soper, who presided at his and Febyanti Herewila's wedding, and Sukumaran had asked for Bayside Church pastor Christie Buckingham.
15However the Indonesian authorities have appointed pastor Tuhu Santoso for Sukumaran, and they have yet to allocate a pastor for Chan.
16Pastor Karina De Vega was supposed to be with Chan for the execution, but has been reassigned to Nigerian Okwudili Oyatanze.
17Paintings by Myuran Sukumaran have been signed by all nine awaiting execution.
18On website trustwomenconf.com, De Vega is described as a mother, poet, song writer, novelist, preacher, activist and human trafficking survivor.
19In 1990 she was apparently drugged by a human trafficker and woke up in a shipping container bound for Yemen.
20It says she escaped from a brothel and was found by an Australian expatriate who arranged for her return home.
21Muslim cleric Hasan Makirim, who attended the executions of Namaona Denis from Malawi and Rani Adriani from Indonesia on January 18, told Fairfax Media in January that it is always easier when a religious counsellor already has a relationship with the condemned prisoner.
22The bodies of Andrew Chan and Myuran Sukumaran will be returned to Australia following their execution.
23"Because of that the person can go into the isolation room calmer," he said.
24To offer succour through someone's last days and at the moment of death requires enormous mental preparation.
25"I pray before the execution, on the execution day and when I have to see the convict for the last time. I say a lot of prayers at the mosque because I need strength to do my job," Hasan said in January.
26"I pray the person I have to meet can also be strengthened."
27Tony Spontana, spokesman for the Attorney-General, said the bodies of Chan and Sukumaran would be flown back to Australia after being driven to Jakarta in an ambulance.
