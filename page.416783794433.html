'Doubtful' Microsoft will buy Salesforce: Analysts

1Despite reports that Microsoft (NASDAQ: MSFT) is interested in buying Salesforce.com (NYSE: CRM), Oracle (NYSE: ORCL) is still the most likely buyer of the cloud services firm if reports of buyout talks are indeed true, analysts said Wednesday.
2Shares of Salesforce shot higher Tuesday afternoon amid reports that Microsoft was considering a bid for the company.
3Microsoft declined to comment.
4Salesforce said it does not comment on rumors or speculation.
5"Whether Microsoft would make a serious bid, I'm more doubtful. I think Oracle probably makes the most sense," Rick Sherlund, director of technology research at Nomura Securities, said on CNBC's "Squawk Box."
6No deal is imminent, according to Bloomberg.
7Last week, Bloomberg reported that Salesforce hired financial advisors after being approached by potential buyers.
8Industry watchers have since speculated that Oracle is the company behind the initial reported bid.
9Sherlund ticked off a list of reasons why Oracle is the more likely suitor: It's a better strategic fit, Salesforce founder Marc Benioff used to work for Oracle Executive Chairman Larry Ellison, and Oracle has excess sales capacity that would help it do a deal.
10FBR Capital Markets' Samad Samana also believes Oracle is the most likely bidder.
11However, he and Sherlund said a deal would make sense for both Oracle and Microsoft.
12"There's not any type of transformative acquisition in the cloud that you can do that's bigger and more marquee than Salesforce," Samana told "Squawk Box."
13Microsoft has placed increased emphasis on cloud computing during CEO Satya Nadella's tenure.
14The company reported last month that its cloud business grew 106 percent year over year in its most recent full quarter.
15Nadella last week projected that Microsoft could hit $20 billion in cloud revenue by 2018, a leap from the current annualized rate of $6.3 billion.
16"Satya Nadella, he has a vision for the cloud," Samana said.
17"They've given analysts a peg out there, and what better way to get there than acquiring Salesforce.com, which would make them an even more robust enterprise application provider?"
18The acquisition would also block Amazon (NASDAQ: AMZN), the dominant player in cloud computing infrastructure, from consolidating its lead through a purchase of Salesforce.com's platform.
19Trading in Salesforce was briefly halted around 3 p.m. EDT Tuesday with the stock up 6 percent after Bloomberg reported that the software giant was evaluating a bid.
20Another company previously approached Salesforce about a potential bid, the news service said, citing sources.
21Benioff may not necessarily be ready to sell the company he founded, Samana said.
22"I think Marc Benioff has a vision of what he wants to accomplish, and I don't think he's quite done cementing what he wants to do," Samana said.
23Salesforce has a market cap of about $48 billion, compared with Microsoft's roughly $385 billion.
24In December, the companies tightened their partnership, making it easier to use Salesforce on Windows Mobile phones and in Office.
25That close relationship, as well as Benioff's focus on customer experience, could influence his decision on whether to accept a deal, Samana said.
26"If they can be more productive and more valuable with his customers being with Microsoft and more tightly integrated, I think it's something he would certainly consider because at the end of the day, they're a customer company," he said.
27Salesforce shares ended Tuesday up 1.6 percent, while Microsoft fell 1.3 percent.
28Shares of Salesforce were up more than 1.5 2 percent in premarket trading Wednesday.
