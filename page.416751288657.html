Costa Rica international airport to reopen Tuesday after volcano ash cleared

1Empty ticket counters are seen at Juan Santamaria international airport in Alajuela April 23, 2015.
2SAN JOSE (Reuters) - Costa Rica's international airport is set to reopen Tuesday morning after the eruption of a nearby volcano blanketed runways with ash, forcing its closure overnight.
3The airport, the largest in the tourist hot spot, will resume operations at 6:00 a.m. local time, said airport official Silvia Chaves, adding that 13 flights had been delayed, canceled or rerouted.
4The Turrialba volcano began erupting Monday afternoon, spewing thick black clouds of ash more than 8,200 feet (2,500 meters) into the sky.
5Winds pushed the ash towards the capital San Jose, some 31 miles (50 km) away.
6Ash can affect the safety of flights during takeoff and landing as well as damage engines.
