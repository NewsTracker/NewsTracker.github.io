US stocks fall in early trade as Greece debt woes escalate

1NEW YORK (AP) - Stocks are falling in early trading in the U.S., but not as much as in Europe as Greece's debt woes deepen.
2The Dow Jones industrial average fell 138 points, or 0.8 percent, to 17,804 as of 9:35 a.m. Eastern time Monday.
3The Standard & Poor's 500 index gave up 16 points, or 0.8 percent, to 2,085.
4The Nasdaq composite fell 47 points, or 0.9 percent, to 5,033.
5The declines were steeper in Europe.
6Benchmark indexes were down 2 percent in Germany and 3 percent in France.
7Nine of the 10 industry sectors in the S&P 500 index fell.
8The only one that rose was utilities, a traditional safe-haven play.
9Bond prices rose.
10The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell to 2.39 percent.
