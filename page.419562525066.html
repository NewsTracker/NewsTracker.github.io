O'Brien names Hoyer the Texans' starting quarterback (Yahoo Sports)

1Houston Texans' Brian Hoyer (7) throw prior to an NFL preseason football game between the Denver Broncos and Houston Texans, Saturday, Aug. 22, 2015, in Houston.
2HOUSTON (AP) -- Now that he's been named Houston's starting quarterback Brian Hoyer knows he'll have to continue to perform to keep that job.
3Hoyer had been competing with Ryan Mallett for the job.
4Coach Bill O'Brien announced the decision before practice on Monday.
5''The whole competition pushed us to be better,'' Hoyer said.
6''We competed very hard and I know that just because I'm named the starter today doesn't mean if we go out there and lose a bunch of games that I'm going to still be the starter.
7Hoyer started 13 games for the Cleveland Browns last season before signing with the Texans in the offseason.
8''I thought that Brian showed me the most consistency, the most composure in certain situations, the experience in our system, all those different things that go into the fact that I think he's ready to be our starter,'' O'Brien said.
9Some have questioned whether either of these quarterbacks are the long-term solution for this team.
10On Monday he addressed that question without being asked about it.
11''We all need to give Brian Hoyer a chance,'' O'Brien said.
12''He's got an opportunity here.
13He's got an opportunity to go in there as a starter and make the most of his opportunity ... I believe in him.
14I believe in his ability to run our offense efficiently.
15I believe in his experience in our offense.
16The 29-year-old Hoyer was 7-6 as a starter last season and finished with 3,326 yards passing with 12 touchdowns and 13 interceptions.
17''Being the new guy in this organization I want to go out there and earn the respect of my teammates and I think the only way you really do that is by winning football games,'' Hoyer said.
18Hoyer went undrafted out of Michigan State before signing as a free agent with the Patriots, where he worked as Tom Brady's backup until he was released in 2012.
19He was also released by the Steelers and the Cardinals before signing with the Browns.
20He started three games for Cleveland in 2013 before tearing his ACL.
21''I've always kind of had that chip on my shoulder,'' Hoyer said.
22''I've been doubted my entire career whether it was not getting drafted, cut, hurt, whatever it might be.
23Mallett started two games for Houston last season before having season-ending surgery for a torn pectoral muscle.
24O'Brien said he reminded Mallett that the Texans had to use four quarterbacks last season because of injuries when he spoke to him about what he called a ''very difficult'' decision.
25''You have to prepare every single week like you're the starter.
26You have to be ready to play at a moment's notice.'' O'Brien said.
27''I believe that he understands that.
