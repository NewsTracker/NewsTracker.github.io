Mexico tops Costa Rica 1-0 in Gold Cup on late penalty

1Costa Rica's goalkeeper Esteban Alvarado (23) reacts as he watches the ball shot by Mexico's Andres Guardado (18) go in for the game winning goal during the overtime period of a CONCACAF Gold Cup soccer match Sunday, July 19, 2015, at MetLife stadium in East Rutherford, N.J. Mexico won 1-0.
2Andres Guardado scored on a penalty kick in the final minute of stoppage time in extra time to give Mexico a 1-0 victory over Costa Rica in the CONCACAF Gold Cup quarterfinals Sunday night.
3Roy Miller was whistled for a questionable foul on Oribe Peralta with the game seconds from going to penalty kicks.
4Guardado converted to send six-time champion Mexico to the semifinals, where it will face Panama on Wednesday night in Atlanta.
5Mexico had dominated play but was unable to score until the final moments.
6Angry Costa Rica fans - heavily outnumbered by Mexico fans in the crowd of 74,187 at MetLife Stadium - threw bottles toward the field in protest.
7Peralta was leaping to try to head in a cross in front of the goal and Miller appeared to make little contact with him.
8The Mexico-Costa Rica matchup looked like a potential final at the start of the tournament, with Costa Rica coming off a World Cup quarterfinal appearance last year.
9But both teams were sluggish in the group stage to each finish second and set up the quarterfinal meeting.
10Mexico repeatedly broke down Costa Rica's defense to set up good looks only to miss high or wide.
11Carlos Esquivel nearly put Mexico ahead in the 63th minute, but goalkeeper Esteban Alvarado got the tips of his fingers on the ball while leaning the other way to deflect it off the crossbar.
12Panama also went to extra time in the first game Sunday.
13That game made it to penalty kicks with the score tied 1-1, and Panama won 6-5.
