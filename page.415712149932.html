What to expect from Zuckerberg at Facebook's F8

1This year the developer conference will be about just how broad and wide-reaching Facebook's family of products has become.
2In a sign of Zuckerberg's commitment to his strategy of building out a portfolio of apps, it's also featuring speakers and giving updates on Oculus, WhatsApp, LIveRail, Parse and Instagram.
3For the first time the conference will be two days instead of one, with more than 2,000 developers coming in from around the world.
4Here are some key topics that could make headlines-game changers for developers, and potentially investors as well.
51) Ad tech will be in the spotlight; Facebook could launch a new mobile ad platform to compete directly with Twitter's MoPub.
6Last year Facebook acquired start-up LiveRail, a tool for publishers to sell video ad space.
7There's been a lot of speculation that it would be a natural extension for LiveRail to start auctioning off ad space for other companies.
8That would put it in direct competition with Twitter's MoPub as well as Google's ad exchange.
9This would effectively allow Facebook's technology to own the entire life cycle of the ad-buying process.
10In addition to buying ads on Facebook and measuring their impact with Facebook's Atlas, marketers could buy across the Web through LiveRail.
11The big keynote Thursday is about virtual reality and Facebook's Oculus platform.
12The key question there is whether the company will provide any updates on when the platform will be ready to hit the market.
13Facebook is in talks with several media organizations to host articles within the social network, according to the New York Times.
14On the heels of announcing the ability to send money to friends through Messenger, we could hear more about Facebook's plans to turn the app into a more of a platform.
15In addition to enhancing the instant messaging with more info about callers, Facebook could also introduce ways to incorporate news and other content or services into the service.
16Facebook has been making a big push for video content companies, like Buzzfeed, to publish videos directly into Facebook's video player.
17Now we could hear details on plans to integrate news content directly into Facebook, instead of making users click outside the site to NYTimes.com or BuzzFeed.com to finish an article.
18The New York Times laid out this proposition in an article published Monday.
19The question is how Facebook lures publishers over to its site-and what kind of ad revenue split, or promotion it promises.
20Facebook will have to make it worth publishers' while to justify ceding control of the user experience, and their ownership over ad and subscription revenue.
