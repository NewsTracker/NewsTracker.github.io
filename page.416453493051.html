Facebook's quarterly revenue growth slows

1Facebook posted its slowest growth in quarterly revenue in two years and higher spending on research and development ate into profits, according to its latest quarterly results released last night.
2Facebook has warned of heavy investments in 2015 as it steps up efforts to expand a collection of products.
3These include messaging service WhatsApp, photo-sharing service Instagram and virtual reality headset maker Oculus Rift.
4As a result, Facebook's operating expenses rose 83% in the first quarter as R&D costs jumped 133% and marketing and sales spending nearly doubled.
5However, Facebook said monthly active users grew 13% over a year ago to 1.44 billion, with 87% of them accessing its service on smartphones and other mobile devices.
6The company offered assurances that it has a handle on spending, saying it now expects expenses to increase by about 55-65% this year, a slight decrease at the top end from its previous forecast of 55-70%.
7It said foreign exchange rates due to the strong US dollar would likely cut revenue in the current quarter by more than the 10% it saw in the last quarter.
8Facebook's net income fell to $509m, or 18 cents per share, in the three months ended March 31, from $639m, or 25 cents per share, a year earlier.
9Excluding items, the company earned 42 cents per share.
10Revenues rose to $3.54 billion from $2.50 billion.
11Revenue from advertising was $3.32 billion, up 46% from a year earlier.
12Analysts on average had expected a profit of 40 cents per share and revenue of $3.56 billion.
