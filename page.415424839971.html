2"We will protest until we see everything in our favor. This movement has legs," Derrick Robinson, a protest organizer, declared Friday.
3"We're out here fighting for justice and equality, and that's what we'll continue to fight for."
4Part of the movement has also been channeled into legislative change.
5On Wednesday, about two dozen people from the Don't Shoot Coalition and the American Civil Liberties Union traveled to the Missouri Capitol in support of the "Fair and Impartial Policing Act," a measure that would strengthen state laws about racial profiling by police and require law officers to undergo "anti-bias" training.
6The Justice Department fueled the sense of achievement among activists, announcing in a scathing report last week that it had found widespread racial bias in the city's policing and in a municipal court system driven by profit extracted from mostly black and low-income residents.
7That same report also cleared former Ferguson officer Darren Wilson in the Aug. 9 death of Brown, the unarmed 18-year-old who for protesters became a symbol of unjustified use of force and unfair treatment of minorities by police.
8The Justice Department's conclusions drew a muted response among activists.
9Only a few dozen protesters gathered that night outside Ferguson's police station - the nexus of many demonstrations.
10That was in sharp contrast to the throngs that turned out there and across the nation in the days after Brown's death and in November, when a Missouri grand jury declined to indict Wilson.
11The comparatively sparse turnout raised questions about whether the movement had lost momentum.
12Organizers said the federal government's conclusions had been expected after some of the findings were leaked in the weeks before the formal announcements.
13Rasheen Aldridge, a 20-year-old regular at the protests, considers Ferguson his generation's civil-rights movement.
14That idea, he said, seemed to gain resonance in the last week after the killing of an unarmed biracial man by a white officer in Madison, Wisconsin, and the suspension of a University of Oklahoma fraternity chapter that was caught on video singing a racist chant.
15Aldridge, head of Young Activists United St. Louis, said he did not expect the protest movement "to grow into the energy that it has, empowering young people not to be silent anymore and to take action and fix it."
16"This is the new time to make changes," added Aldridge, also a member of the Ferguson Commission, a group tasked by Missouri's governor to address underlying social and economic problems.
17"This is our time."
18In the months after Brown's death, some demonstrations were marred by looting and arson fires that targeted businesses.
19Organizers blamed those incidents on outside agitators.
20That was the case again Thursday, when two police officers helping monitor protests outside the police department were shot in an attack that was still under investigation.
21The officers were later released from the hospital.
22On Friday, St. Louis County Police Chief Jon Belmar said detectives were working around the clock to crack that case.
23The department's top priority, he said, was to continue "a tempo of service and protection and relationships" that ensure Ferguson does not "have a regression from all that we've done since last fall."
24The gunfire drew instant, broad condemnation from activists.
25Dozens of protesters gathered again Thursday night in Ferguson, expressing sympathy for the wounded officers and praying for peace during a candlelight vigil.
26"We cannot afford these kinds of incidents happening. That gets us absolutely nowhere," said St. Louis activist John Gaskin III.
27He called the gunfire "disgraceful and cowardly" and said people "need to be working for reforms and justice, not revenge."
28Robinson said the wounding of the officers "definitely set us back."
29But he believes protesters can regain what they lost.
30"Even last night as we protested, police were very gracious to us," he said Friday.
31"That shows a lot of trust to us."
32Gaskin said protesters should take stock in the Ferguson house cleaning they helped achieve.
