Border Control discover TWO sophisticated underground tunnels from California to Mexico and stop a $700K shipment of methamphetamine entering the US

1U.S. Border Patrol agents say they have seized two sophisticated tunneling systems stretching beneath the U.S.-Mexico border.
2The first tunnel was discovered Monday in Calexico, southern California.
3The secret passageway measured a total of 230 feet - 70 meters - and burrowed into Mexicali.
4It was about four feet high and four feet wide and was fitted with a complex lighting and ventilation system, UT San Diego reported.
5Seized: Agents came across the tunnel after intercepting a major shipment of methamphetamine nearby.
6The entrance of the tunnel was inside a residence in Mexicali.
7It was discovered by members of the patrol's Border Search Trauma and Rescue unit.
8The discovery was precipitated by the seizure of 25 vacuum-sealed packages containing 69 pounds of methamphetamine.
9A group of men were caught with the haul - worth $694,000 - trying to cross the All-American Canal.
10Three of the men fled back to Mexico.
11Then on Tuesday an incomplete tunnel was found on the Tijuana-San Diego border.
12The tunnel was under construction, but had a rail system with a cart, as well as lighting.
13It was tall enough to walk through but had no drugs or contraband inside.
14The entrance was within a home in Tijuana, but the US exit had not been dug out yet, authorities said.
15The residence is apparently located near the U.S. border fence in Colonia Castillo.
16U.S. federal authorities have found more than 80 cross-border smuggling tunnels since 2006, according to UT San Diego.
17Most of the systems are in California and Arizona.
18Drugs are the primary reason for the tunnels.
