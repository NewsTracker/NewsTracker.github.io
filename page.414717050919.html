Al Jazeera journalists freed in Egypt

1A Cairo court on Thursday ordered the release of Canadian Mohamed Fahmy and Egyptian Baher Mohamed, who face retrial on charges of supporting the black-listed Muslim Brotherhood.
2"We finished the procedures for the release of my brother a short while ago," Mohamed's brother Assem said on Friday.
3"He is at home for the first time in more than a year."
4Fahmy's brother Adel posted on Twitter: "My brother has been released from the police station! I am going on holiday before they arrest him again!"
5UN Secretary General Ban Ki-moon welcomed their release, urging authorities to free other journalists "in accordance with Egypt's international obligations to protect the freedoms of expression and association".
6Fahmy, Mohamed and Australian Peter Greste were arrested in December 2013 and sentenced to between seven and 10 years for aiding the Muslim Brotherhood of ousted president Mohamed Morsi.
7Greste was deported on February 1 under a decree signed by President Abdel Fattah al-Sisi allowing foreigners to face trial or serve their sentences in their home countries.
8The Australian said on Friday he was overjoyed at his two colleagues' release but said that it was too soon to celebrate with a retrial pending.
9"The trial is ongoing, and nobody has yet been acquitted," he said in comments carried by Australian Associated Press news agency.
