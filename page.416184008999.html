White House Brief: Things to know about Hillary Clinton

1She won a Senate seat representing New York in 2000 and ran for the president in 2008, losing the nomination to Barack Obama.
2She was his secretary of state for four years.
3No woman has been a major party's presidential nominee or been elected president.
4Lawyer, senator, diplomat.
5In Arkansas, she was a lawyer at a top firm while Bill Clinton was governor.
6She advised her husband after he won the White House in 1992.
7In the Senate, she struck a bipartisan tone at times.
8Her Senate vote for the 2002 Iraq invasion became a point of contention in 2008; Obama had spoken out against the "dumb war."
9At the State Department, she was a hawkish member of Obama's national security team.
10She helped set the foundation for nuclear talks with Iran.
11The daughter of a small-business owner and homemaker, Clinton grew up in suburban Chicago.
12As a senior at Wellesley College, she delivered a 1969 commencement speech that earned national attention.
13At Yale Law School, she met Bill Clinton.
14After working as a child advocate, Clinton followed her future husband back to Arkansas, where he launched his political career.
15The couple's 35-year-old daughter, Chelsea Clinton, gave birth to her first child, Charlotte, in September.
16A 1995 address in Beijing and her final campaign event in 2008 are signature moments.
17As first lady, Clinton declared in a speech at a U.N. conference on women that "human rights are women's rights and women's rights are human rights."
18The speech challenged human rights abuses of women and helped set the tone for Clinton's work years later in the State Department.
19Her 2008 speech, delivered after Obama locked up the nomination, told supporters they had made "18 million cracks" in the glass ceiling, denoting the number of primary votes she won.
20It left the impression of unfinished business and the potential for a woman eventually to win the White House.
21Her critics remember her for blaming her husband's scandals on a "vast right wing conspiracy."
22Clinton has signaled that she intends to make a major push in the Iowa caucuses, won by Obama in 2008.
23Her team has hired a former top aide to Agriculture Secretary Tom Vilsack to lead her Iowa campaign.
24Her ties to New Hampshire are much stronger.
25State Democrats remember Bill Clinton's surprising second-place finish in the 1992 primary that helped him overcome charges of draft dodging and womanizing.
26Hillary Clinton surprised Obama by winning the 2008 New Hampshire primary.
27Clinton wrote "Hard Choices," about her time as secretary of state, and promoted the book around the country in 2014.
28The book generated mediocre sales and Clinton stumbled at times during the book tour, saying in one interview that she and her husband were "dead broke" when they left the White House.
29While they faced large legal bills from the Whitewater investigation, the couple made millions after Bill Clinton's presidency; the comments were considered tone-deaf.
30Clinton already was a publishing powerhouse at that point.
31Her 2003 book, "Living History," sold more than 1 million copies.
32During her husband's presidency, she released "It Takes a Village" in 1996, a book that discussed her work in child advocacy and steps to help children become productive adults.
33Other books: "Dear Socks, Dear Buddy: Kids' Letters to the First Pets," in 1998, and "An Invitation to the White House: At Home with History," in 2000.
