Yahoo says IRS statement won?t stop its spin-off

1Yahoo said Wednesday it will move forward with its plans to spin off its small business division, despite the IRS announcing it will reexamine its rules for spin-offs.
2Yahoo said its understanding is that the statement from the IRS was "not specific" to its spin-off plans and "reflects no change in applicable law and does not affect previously filed ruling requests."
3Yahoo filed its request for the spin-off in the first quarter and plans to separate its small business division, along with shares in Alibaba, into a new company.
4The move would allow Yahoo to avoid paying $16 billion in tax liabilities.
5Some investors were skittish on Tuesday after the IRS statement, which would apply to new spin-off requests.
6Yahoo's stock plunged nearly 8 percent to $40.98 at the market's close Tuesday, its lowest stock price since last October.
7By Wednesday midday, Yahoo stock was up about 5 percent, trading at $42.98 a share.
8Meanwhile, Yahoo has been hiring more engineers for its small business division and working with a strategic branding company on the spin-off's name.
