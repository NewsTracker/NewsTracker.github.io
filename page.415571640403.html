Defeated Herzog vows not to join new Israeli government, accuses Netanyahu of racism

1The centrist front-runner defeated in Israel's parliamentary election this week says his party will not join the next government, and accused Benjamin Netanyahu of racism.
2Isaac Herzog says his Zionist Union, which finished second in the vote, would sit in the opposition and "challenge a narrow right-wing government."
3Herzog spoke Thursday to Army Radio.
4The latest tally from Tuesday's vote shows the Zionist Union got 24 seats, compared to Likud's 30.
5Herzog criticized Netanyahu's campaign, saying it "touched on racism" and was based on "lies, hostility and fear-mongering."
6In a last-ditch attempt to spur supporters to the polls, Netanyahu said Arab citizens were voting "in droves" and were endangering years of rule by his Likud Party.
7The comments drew accusations of racism from Israeli Arabs and a White House rebuke.
