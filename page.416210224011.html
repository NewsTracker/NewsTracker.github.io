Iraq exhumes remains of 164 from mass graves in Saddam Hussein's palace compound in Tikrit

1Members of the Iraqi security forces work at the site of a mass grave containing the remains of people believed to have been slain by Islamic State (IS) jihadists in Tikrit, on April 12.
2Baghdad: Iraq has exhumed the remains of 164 people believed to have been massacred by jihadists from mass graves in Tikrit, the Human Rights ministry said Monday.
3"Search teams have discovered the remains of 164 (victims) so far in four mass graves during work over the past week," ministry spokesman Kamel Amin said.
4He said documents and mobile phones that have been found indicate the dead are victims of the infamous Speicher massacre, named for the military base near which up to 1700 mostly Shiite recruits were abducted by the Islamic State (IS) jihadist group last year.
5An Iraqi forensic worker excavates human remains in a mass grave, believed to contain the bodies of Iraqi soldiers killed by Islamic State militants when they overran Camp Speicher military base last June, in Tikrit.
6But DNA testing is required to confirm their identities, Amin said.
7The remains were discovered inside former president Saddam Hussein's palace compound in Tikrit, which officials say holds 10 mass graves, while three more are located outside it.
8The killing of the recruits - which IS documented in photos and videos posted online - stoked widespread anger and helped rally support for the battle against the jihadist group.
9The mass grave sites were discovered after Iraqi forces retook the northern city of Tikrit earlier this month in their biggest victory so far against IS.
10IS led an offensive last June that overran large areas north and west of Baghdad, but Iraqi security forces and allied paramilitaries have succeeded in regaining significant ground.
