HSBC drops out of Apple Pay UK Launch and people are very ANGRY

1APPLE has debuted Apple Pay to customers across the UK - but those who bank with HSBC will have to wait a little longer than expected.
2Apple Pay - the contactless payment solution built into the iPhone 6, iPhone 6 Plus and Apple Watch - has launched in the UK.
3The system means iPhone users can "tap-to-pay" using their smartphones for the first time, and many have been testing out the new system for the first time this morning.
4However - HSBC's 16 million UK customers have been forced to wait for the new payment solution after the bank missed the launch.
5HSBC, which leaked the Apple Pay release date in a tweet this week, was initially confirmed to be amongst the first banks to offer the Apple-branded contactless solution.
6But the British banking firm was quietly moved from the "participating bank" to "coming soon" on the Apple Pay website.
7HSBC said it would allow customers to use Apply Pay by the end of July.
8Thousands of angry customers have taken to Twitter to complain about the sudden change of plan.
9A spokesperson for the bank told the BBC: "We are working hard to bring this to our customers by the end of July."
10Those who bank with First Direct - a subsidiary of HSBC - will also have to wait.
11HSBC and First Direct customers are not the only people unable to add their credit and debit cards to Apple Pay this morning.
12Bank of Scotland, Halifax, Lloyds Bank, M&S Bank, MBNA and TSB have all announced plans to support Apple Pay later this summer.
13Barclays - which has its own wearable payment solution - today changed its turn and announced support for Apple's iPhone payment system "in the future".
