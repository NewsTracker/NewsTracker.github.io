Google Inc shopping for patents in experiment to limit lawsuits

1Google Inc. is shopping for patents.
2The largest Internet search company said it's taking applications next month from patent owners willing to sell.
3After reviewing submissions, Google will pick the patents it wants, negotiate a sale and shell out the money by August.
4The program is an experiment to see if Google can obtain patents that would otherwise fall in the hands of licensing firms that may use them in litigation against tech companies.
5While ownership also would free Google to use the technology itself, there's no indication that the Mountain View, California-based company is looking to be pitched ideas for new products.
6Instead, it is designed to "remove friction from the patent market," according to the blog posting Monday by Allen Lo, Google's deputy general counsel for patents.
7Patent litigation has become a sore spot for tech companies and retailers who say they are too often targeted by patent owners of relatively trivial ideas looking for big payouts.
8They are pushing Congress to raise the bar for making such demands.
9In many cases, the patents were first obtained by individual inventors who were unable to commercialize their ideas.
10They sold off their patents to licensing firms that specialize more in litigation than production.
11There have been some nascent efforts to help inventors start their own companies.
12Groups like 1776 in Washington and various angel investors are starting to act as sort of "idea labs" for small businesses.
