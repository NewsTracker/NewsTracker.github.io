1So far, 11 families have received $5,000 each, said Tolbert Nyenswah, who leads the government's Ebola response; dozens more payments are in the works.
2Ebola has killed 180 health workers in the country, according to the World Health Organization.
3The World Bank is providing the funding for the stipends, according to state radio.
4Ebola, often called the caregivers' disease, has decimated the ranks of doctors and nurses in the three countries hardest hit in this outbreak: Liberia, Guinea and Sierra Leone.
5It is transmitted through bodily fluids.
6In the early days of the outbreak, many health workers did not know how to protect themselves or lacked the equipment to do so.
7Liberia currently has no Ebola cases, but it must still wait several weeks before it is officially declared free of the disease.
