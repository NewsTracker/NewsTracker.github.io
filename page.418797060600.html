Greek bailout talks expected to go ahead on Monday after delay

1Talks between Greece and its international creditors over a new bailout package should go ahead after logistical issues that delayed meetings this week are resolved, a Greek official has said.
2The meetings with officials from the European commission, European Central Bank and International Monetary Fund had been expected to start on Friday but were delayed by organisational issues including the location of talks and security.
3The finance ministry official said on Saturday that talks were now expected to get formally underway on Monday after the logistical issues had been resolved.
4The official denied that the government was trying to keep the lenders' team away from government departments.
5"We don't have any problem with them visiting the general accounting office," the official said.
6Greeks have viewed inspection visits by lenders in Athens as a violation of the country's sovereignty and six months of acrimonious negotiations with EU partners took place in Brussels at the government's request.
7Asked if the government would now allow EU, IMF and ECB mission chiefs to visit Athens for talks on a new loan, state minister Alekos Flambouraris said: "If the agreement says that they should visit a ministry, we have to accept that."
8The confusion around the expected start to the talks on Friday underlined the challenges ahead if negotiations are to be wrapped up in time for a bailout worth up to 86bn ( 60bn) to be approved in parliament by 20 August, as Greece intends.
9Already, the Greek prime minister, Alexis Tsipras, is struggling to contain a rebellion in his leftwing Syriza party that made his government dependent on votes from pro-European opposition parties to get the tough bailout terms approved in parliament.
10One of Tsipras's closest aides warned that the understanding with the opposition parties could not last long and said clear solutions were needed, underlining widespread expectations that new elections may come as soon as September or October.
11"The country cannot go on with a minority government for long. We need clear, strong solutions," state minister Nikos Pappas said.
12Tsipras, who is by far the most popular politician in Greece according to opinion polls, has said his priority is to secure the bailout package before dealing with the political fallout from the Syriza party rebellion.
13According to a poll by Metron Analysis for Parapolitika newspaper on Saturday, 61% of Greeks had a positive view of Tsipras, compared with 36% who disapproved.
14An overwhelming majority - 78% - still wanted Greece to stay in the eurozone against 19% in favour of going back to the drachma.
15Tsipras insists there is no viable alternative to the bailout but has been wary of striking out against his party opponents in a bid to keep it together, at least while talks proceed.
16Flambouraris called on Syriza rebels to drop their opposition.
17"They are still my comrades and I urge them to get back to their senses even at the last moment," he said.
18"They should realise that the left movement is now in power. It's not an opposition party. Now we have to discuss the new landscape."
