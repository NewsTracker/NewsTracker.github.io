Nigerian army rescues 234 girls

1Nigeria's military says it has rescued 234 more girls and women from a Boko Haram forest stronghold in the country's north-east.
2The announcement by the Nigerian Defence Headquarters brings the number declared rescued this week to more than 677.
3It comes as the army deployed ground troops following air raids on Sambisa Forest camps said to be the last holdout of the Islamic extremists.
4There have been reports that some women fought the troops, with Boko Haram using them as an armed human shield.
5It is not known how many girls, women, boys and men Boko Haram has kidnapped over its six-year-old rebellion.
6President Goodluck Jonathan, whose term ends this month, said on Thursday that the Sambisa Forest is the last holdout of the Islamic militants and he pledged to "hand over a Nigeria completely free of terrorist strongholds".
7The military says it is screening the girls and women to find out what villages they came from.
8Some women the soldiers tried to rescue even shot at their rescuers, a military spokesman has said, indicating that some might now identify with Boko Haram after months of captivity and forced marriages.
9It also remains unclear if some of the women had willingly joined Boko Haram, or are family members of fighters.
10The Nigerian military released photos of some of the girls and women they said were taken between Tuesday and Thursday in the Sambisa Forest.
11The photos show 20 or so women, children and babies looking generally healthy physically.
12But at least one child looks emaciated and some of the children have the orange-coloured hair signalling severe malnutrition.
13There has been no announcement yet on whether any of those rescued are the students who were kidnapped from the Chibok school a year ago, a mass kidnapping that outraged much of the world.
14Some photos were taken in an open courtyard with a high wall and leafy trees beyond.
15A military man in a flight suit, an assault rifle held by his side, stands among them.
16A young military medic with blue rubber gloves and a surgical mask dangling from his ears appears to be checking several of the children.
17Muhammad Gavi, a spokesman for a self-defence group that fights Boko Haram, said some of the hundreds of women and girls who were freed are pregnant, citing information he got from some group members who have seen the females.
