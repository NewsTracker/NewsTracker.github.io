Boko Haram bombs kill dozens at mosque and restaurant in Nigeria

1A burned-out car sits in central Jos, Nigeria, on July 6, the day after two bombs killed 51 people.
2JOS, Nigeria - Two bombs blamed on the Islamic extremist group Boko Haram exploded at a crowded mosque and a posh Muslim restaurant in Nigeria's central city of Jos, killing 51 people, officials said Monday.
3The blasts on Sunday night came hours after a woman suicide bomber blew up at a crowded evangelical Christian church service in the northeastern city of Potiskum, killing six people, according to witnesses.
4Also Sunday, extremists returned to northeastern villages attacked three days earlier, killing nine villagers and burning down 32 churches and about 300 homes, said Stephen Apagu, chairman of a self-defense group in Borno state's Askira-Uba local government area.
5He said the militia killed three militants.
6Sunday's attacks are the latest in a string blamed on Boko Haram that have now killed about 300 people in the past week.
7In the most deadly, more than 140 people were killed, most men and boys as they prayed in mosques in northeastern Kukawa town on Wednesday.
8The attacks may correspond to an Islamic State group order for more mayhem during the Muslim holy month of Ramadan.
9Boko Haram became the Islamic State group's West Africa franchise earlier this year.
10In Jos, 51 people died and were buried Monday morning, Muslim community lawyer Ahmed Garba told a news conference.
11Another 67 people were wounded, according to Abdussalam Mohammed, the National Emergency Management Agency coordinator.
12Police confirmed the explosions but said a final toll must wait as they are still excavating rubble.
13The explosion at the Yantaya Mosque came as leading cleric Sani Yahaya of the Jama'atu Izalatul Bidia organization, which preaches peaceful co-existence of all religions, was addressing a crowd, according to survivors.
14Gunmen opened fire on the mosque from three directions, said Garba, the lawyer.
15Survivor Danladi Sani said he saw a man robed in white open fire at Yahaya, and then blow himself up.
16Yahaya was unharmed, he said.
17"He is a great Islamic scholar who has spoken out against Boko Haram, and that is why we believe he was the target," Sani told the Associated Press.
18Another bomb exploded at Shagalinku, a restaurant patronized by state governors and other elite politicians seeking specialties from Nigeria's mainly Muslim north, witnesses said.
19Sabi'u Bako bought take-out and then heard a massive blast as he walked away with friends.
20"The restaurant was destroyed and we saw many people covered in blood," he said.
21"We can't believe that we escaped."
22Jos is a hot spot for violent religious confrontations, located in the center of the country where Nigeria's majority Muslim north and mainly Christian south collide.
23The city has been targeted in the past by bomb blasts claimed by Boko Haram extremists that have killed hundreds of people.
24Nigeria's President Muhammadu Buhari condemned Sunday's attacks on places of worship and said the government will defend Nigerians' right to worship freely.
25The United States also condemned recent attacks and "continues to provide counterterrorism assistance to help Nigerian authorities & combat the threat posed by Boko Haram," said a statement Monday from State Department spokesman John Kirby.
26Boko Haram took over a large swath of northeastern Nigeria last year and stepped up cross-border raids.
27A multinational army from Nigeria and its neighbors forced the militants out of towns, but bombings and village attacks increased in recent weeks.
