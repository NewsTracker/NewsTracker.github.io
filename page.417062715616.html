Suicide car bombs kill 15 Iraqi troops in besieged Ramadi

1Displaced Iraqis from Ramadi cross the Bzebiz bridge after spending the night walking towards Baghdad, as they flee their hometown, 65 km west of Baghdad, Iraq, Saturday, May 16, 2015.
2Islamic State militants seized the center of Ramadi in western Iraq and raised their black flag over the government compound, local officials said.
3Suicide car bomb attacks killed 15 members of Iraqi security forces Sunday in Ramadi, which now is largely held by the Islamic State group, authorities said.
4Police and army officials said four nearly simultaneous bombings targeted police officers defending the Malaab district in southern Ramadi, killing 10 and wounding 15.
5Among the dead was Col.
6Muthana al-Jabri, the chief of the Malaab police station, they said.
7Later on, police said three suicide bombers drove their explosive-laden cars into the gate of the Anbar Operation Command, the military headquarters for the province, killing five soldiers and wounding 12.
8Fierce clashes erupted between security forces and Islamic State militants following the attacks.
9Subsequently, Islamic State militants seized the Malaab area after government forces withdrew.
10A police officer who was in Malaab said retreating forces left behind about 30 army vehicles and weapons that included artillery and assault rifles.
11He said some two dozen policemen also went missing during the fighting.
12The officials spoke on condition of anonymity as they weren't authorized to talk to journalists.
13The new setback came only a day after Baghdad's decision to send reinforcements to help its battered forces in Ramadi.
14Meanwhile, Iraqi warplanes launched airstrikes on Islamic State positions inside Ramadi on Sunday, the Iraqi Defense Ministry said, without elaborating.
15Last week, the militants swept through Ramadi, seizing the main government headquarters and other key parts of the city.
16It marked a major setback for the Iraqi government's efforts to drive the militants out of areas they seized last year.
