URGENT-Google rolls out new U.S. cell phone service

1NEW YORK, April 22 (Reuters) - Google Inc on Wednesday launched a new U.S. wireless service that will enable customers to pay only for the data they use and use Wi-Fi networks to curb data use and keep phone bills low.
2The service, Google's first entry into the wireless industry, will work only on the company's Nexus 6 phones and will be hosted through Sprint Corp and T-Mobile's networks, Google said in a statement.
3The phones will also be able to switch between the two networks, depending on which signal is stronger.
4The network, called Project Fi, will cost $20 a month plus $10 per gigabyte of data used.
