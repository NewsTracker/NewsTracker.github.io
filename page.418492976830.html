Apple Pay about to launch in the UK

1Apple Pay is about to go live in the UK, with shops' signs being updated for the launch and HSBC's official account apparently confirming the date.
2The company is gearing up to launch the new service on July 14, the leaks indicate.
3That fits with previous rumours, based on information shared by shops that were gearing up for the launch.
4HSBC's official Twitter feed posted, and then deleted, a message confirming that Apple Pay was about to launch.
5Elsewhere, shops have been adding stickers to confirm that they accept Apple Pay alongside other payment technologies.
6It's unclear whether Apple will have to send out an update for users to download to allow the technology to work, or whether it will just be switched on.
7Apple Pay works by letting people load their card details into their phone.
8They can then touch their iPhone 6 or later, or Apple Watch, to the contactless payment machine and pay.
9Apple Pay will be supported by all the major banks, apart from Barclays.
10It will also be supported by all shops that have contactless payment systems.
11Initially, purchases will be limited to 20, but that is expected to rise in the autumn.
12The maximum charge for contactless payment cards is also 20, but is going up to 30 in September - it's likely that the Apple Pay limit will be upgraded at the same time.
13Apple Pay works online as well as in shops.
14The company claims that its online payment system is more safe than normal purchases, because it keeps the shops from seeing their details.
