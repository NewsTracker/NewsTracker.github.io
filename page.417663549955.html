Suicide bomber attacks tourist site in Luxor, four Egyptians wounded

1Police and people stand near the scene of a foiled suicide attack in Luxor, Egypt, June 10, 2015.
2CAIRO (Reuters) - A suicide bomber blew himself up near Egypt's ancient Karnak temple in the southern city of Luxor on Wednesday, security sources and witnesses said, the second attack in just over one week targeting Egypt's vital tourism industry.
3No group immediately claimed responsibility for the attack in which four Egyptians were wounded, the health ministry said.
4Islamist militants have killed hundreds of police and soldiers over the past two years in a campaign to topple the Egyptian government, but there have recently been attacks on softer tourist and economic targets.
5Gunmen on a motorcycle shot dead two members of Egypt's tourism and antiquities police force on a road near the Giza pyramids last week.
6The Interior Ministry said in a statement it had "foiled a terrorist attack" at Karnak and that two assailants had been killed and a third wounded.
7It did not give further details.
8Security sources said the casualties at Karnak included bazaar shop owners and two policemen.
9No tourists were wounded, according to government officials.
10Images from the scene of the explosion showed what appeared to be body parts on the ground in front of a tourist shop and atop a public restroom.
11Uniformed and plainclothes police gathered nearby, and ambulances were parked beside tourist buses.
12Antiquities Minister Mamdouh al-Damaty said he had, in coordination with the Interior Ministry, ordered security to be bolstered at ancient sites across Egypt.
13Tourism is one of the top sources of income and foreign currency earnings for the Arab world's most populous country, and the sector has made an effective target for militants.
14In 1997, 58 tourists and four Egyptians were killed in an attack in Luxor at a woman pharaoh's temple.
15It took Hosni Mubarak, the veteran autocrat ousted in a 2011 uprising, several years to crush an Islamist insurgency in the 1990s.
16There were conflicting reports on the details of Wednesday's attack at Karnak, around 2 kilometers (1.6 miles) from the center of Luxor, although all sources said three attackers were involved.
17A senior Interior Ministry security source told MENA the attackers tried to target a tourist bus before security forces engaged them, while security sources and witnesses said the men had tried to storm a barricade to enter the site.
18One blew himself up, while the others engaged in the gunfight in which both were badly wounded.
19One died on the way to hospital.
20The latest attack could impact Egypt's efforts to win back foreign tourists scared away by political and economic turmoil since the 2011 uprising.
21North Sinai is the epicenter of an Islamist insurgency but there have also been repeated security incidents in Cairo that have raised concern over the effectiveness of security forces.
22Security sources said two small roadside bombs in the Cairo area were discovered by security forces.
23Separately, a soldier was killed by gunmen in the North Sinai town of Rafah, bordering Israel and the Gaza Strip, security and medical sources said.
