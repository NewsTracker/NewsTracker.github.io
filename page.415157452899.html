Mireille Enos to Star in ABC's Shondaland Drama 'The Catch'

Chadian troops seize Nigerian town of Dikwa from Boko Haram

1The Killing alum is returning to the small screen with the starring role in ABC drama pilot The Catch, The Hollywood Reporter has learned.
2The Catch is described as a thriller about a woman about to get married and about to get conned.
3Unbeknownst to her slippery fiance, the heroine - who investigates fraud for a living - is not all she claims to be.
4And when the fiance's expertly planned con collides with her perfectly constructed lies, they plunge into a dangerous game of cat and mouse.
5The show is based on the novel by Kate Atkinson.
6Enos will star as Alice, a feisty woman with a nervy spirit.
7Alice is getting married to Kieran, yet feels uneasy about whether she should marrying him.
8She is clean and a minimalist.
9She is also a tough, competent woman who is run over by worry and exhaustion.
10Her entire life is dedicated to work as a forensic accountant in Chicago.
11Hannibal's Jennifer Schur will pen the script for ABC Studios and executive produce alongside Shondaland's Shonda Rhimes and Betsy Beers.
12Helen Gregory and Atkinson will co-executive produce.
13JulieAnne Robinson will also exec produce and direct the pilot.
14Enos is best known for her starring turn as Sarah Linden on AMC/Netflix's The Killing, which earned her Golden Globe, Emmy and Critics Choice Award nominations.
15The Catch marks her follow-up TV role since The Killing, which wrapped its fourth and final run on Netflix in August.
16Between seasons of The Killing, Enos became an in-demand leading lady on the feature side, with roles in World War Z opposite Brad Pitt, Gangster Squad, The Captive and If I Stay.
17She's repped by CAA, Gartner/Green Entertainment and attorney Mitch Smelkinson.
18Should The Catch go to series, Enos would join fellow Shondaland leading ladies Ellen Pompeo (Grey's Anatomy), Kerry Washington (Scandal) and Viola Davis (How to Get Away With Murder).
19Enos joins a cast that also includes Jacky Ido as an FBI agent.
1Chad has deployed thousands of soldiers in strategic positions around Lake Chad in an effort to contain the radical jihadist group within Nigeria, sometimes chasing them across the border.
2Its efforts have intensified since Boko Haram attacked the Chadian village of Ngouboua last month, killing several people in the first known lethal attack in the country.
3"We have total control of the town," said Colonel Azem Bermandoua.
4He added that many Boko Haram fighters had also been killed in the clashes on Monday in northeastern Nigeria, the Islamist group's stronghold.
5A Reuters reporter on the scene said black and white Boko Haram flags still flew in a town deserted of residents after several weeks of occupation.
6In a separate incident, Niger, whose army is also fighting the militants, said it had killed six Boko Haram fighters on Saturday near the village of Bossi.
7Two of their vehicles were then destroyed by Chad's air forces, the army said.
8Boko Haram has killed thousands of people and kidnapped hundreds in its attempt to create an Islamic caliphate in Nigeria, although the tide appears to be turning.
9Nigeria's army has won significant battles against them in recent weeks, raising hopes that the country's delayed presidential election will go ahead peacefully on March 28.
