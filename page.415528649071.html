Foreigners train Nigerian troops as "final onslaught" hits Boko Haram

Move over, Julie. A look at some other Marias

1According to security and diplomatic sources, Nigeria has brought in hundreds of mercenaries to give its offensive against the Islamist militant group a shot in the arm ahead of the March 28 presidential elections.
2But government spokesman Mike Omeri said foreigners on the ground were only engaged in training Nigerian troops.
3"There are trainers on the ground to assist in the handling of equipment," Omeri told Reuters on the sidelines of a news conference in London.
4"They simulate, they teach. These are the things they do."
5Asked if they were directly involved in fighting, he said: "I am not aware of that."
6Africa's most populous nation and top energy producer has been plagued by the Boko Haram insurgency since 2009, when insurgents intensified efforts to establish an Islamic caliphate in the northeastern Borno state.
7However, this year Nigeria and its neighbors have launched a series of offensives to recapture territory, turning the tide against Boko Haram in the run-up to the hotly contested presidential poll.
8Omeri would not confirm how many foreigners were involved but said they had come from the same countries that had provided military equipment.
9He cited South Africa, Russia and South Korea.
10"Acquisition of recruitment and military hardware is done through a number of processes. There are government-to-government exchanges and there are those who also come through contractors," he said.
11The contractors' stay in Nigeria would end when local troops had become proficient at handling the equipment, he added.
12"This is training on site and maybe this is why the people on the ground have been described as mercenaries."
13He declined to predict how long it would take for the military to regain full control.
1MARY MARTIN: Martin won a Tony Award for playing Maria seven years before Andrews played her on-screen.
2FLORENCE HENDERSON: "The Brady Bunch" star played Maria in the national touring company of "The Sound of Music" in 1961 and she belted out a version of "The Hills Are Alive" at the 1971 Tony Awards.
3PETULA CLARK: The British singer played Maria starting at age 49 in London for over a year, setting box office records in 1981 and having the largest advance sale in the history of British theater at the time.
4The real Maria von Trapp called her the best Maria ever.
5SHIRLEY JONES: The matriarch of "The Partridge Family" was considered for the role of Maria in the 1965 film but lost out to Andrews.
6The next year she sang the part in summer stock at the Westbury Music Fair on Long Island.
7MARIE OSMOND: The always-wholesome Osmond played Maria in a national touring company in the mid-1990s.
8Osmond's real-life son Stephen Blosil played the youngest boy Kurt and her three other kids went on the road with her, too.
9DEBBY BOONE: The "You Light Up My Life" singer tried her hand at Maria with the New York City Opera in 1990.
10The New York Times said she "brings her own winsomeness and vulnerability to the part."
11LAURA BENANTI: The "Nashville" star was only 19 and playing a nun and a Maria understudy when she was asked to take over the role on Broadway in 1999 from Rebecca Luker.
12She played Baroness Elsa von Schraeder in 2013 when NBC did a live version.
13CONNIE FISHER: A 2006 London revival of "The Sound of Music" was led by this actress, who was chosen by the public who voted on candidates in the reality-show competition "How Do You Solve a Problem Like Maria?"
14CARRIE UNDERWOOD: The country star starred as Maria in a three-hour NBC live broadcast in 2013 that attracted more than 18 million viewers.
15Her Maria was well sung but most critics felt the acting was flat.
