Tekmira suspends work on Ebola drug, will change name

1NEW YORK (AP) " One of the companies that had been studying a potential treatment for the Ebola virus said Monday it will suspend its research on Ebola drugs and change its name.
2Tekmira Pharmaceuticals Corp. said last month that an experimental Ebola treatment that it was developing would probably fail in a clinical trial.
3Tekmira said the new name reflects its combination with OnCore BioPharma, which it bought in March.
4The company says the name change will take effect by Aug. 3.
5Its ticker symbol will become "ABUS."
6The company does not have any approved drugs.
7It says it wants to have at least four drugs in clinical development in early 2016 and intends to acquire other drugs so it can study them in combinations.
8Tekmira's experimental drugs were used in at least one patient in 2014 as an Ebola epidemic spread through several West African nations and the search for more effective treatments kicked into high gear.
9Other companies in the field included NewLink Genetics Corp., Inovio Pharmaceuticals Inc., GlaxoSmithKline PLC and Allergan PLC's MAPP Pharmaceuticals unit.
10More than 11,000 people have died in the Ebola outbreak, which was first reported in March 2014.
11Tekmira said it will suspend work on its drug TKM-Ebola and is re-evaluating its development contract with the U.S. Department of Defense.
12The company said in June that a related drug, TKM-Ebola-Guinea, would probably fail in a mid-stage clinical trial.
13The drug is designed to block genes that help the virus reproduce and spread.
14Shares of Tekmira jumped after the OnCore deal was announced in January, but since the end of that month they have fallen 56 percent.
15Its U.S. shares fell 10 cents to close at $11.18 on Monday.
16Its shares have risen more than 19 percent over the past year.
