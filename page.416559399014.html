The Latest on Nepal Quake: Aid worker in epicenter district

17:15 p.m. (1330 GMT, 9:30 a.m.)
2World Vision aid worker Matt Darvas reached Nepal's Gorkha district, the epicenter of Saturday's powerful quake, early Monday afternoon.
3He said almost no aid had reached there ahead of him.
4He told the AP by telephone: "It does not seem aid is reaching here very quickly."
5An Indian Air Force person walks carrying a Nepalese child, wounded in s earthquake, to a waiting ambulance as the mother rushes to join after they were evacuated from a remote area at the airport in Kathmandu, Nepal, Monday, April 27, 2015.
6The death toll from Nepal's earthquake is expected to rise depended largely on the condition of vulnerable mountain villages that rescue workers were still struggling to reach two days after the disaster.
7Landslides and other destruction delayed attempts to reach the district earlier, but Gorkha is feared to have extensive damage.
8Darvas says most of the newer concrete buildings were intact after the quake but remote mountainside villages were reportedly devastated.
9He says, "Further north from here the reports are very disturbing."
10He says up to 75 percent of the buildings in Singla may have collapsed and the village, a two-days walk away, has been out of contact since Saturday night.
11Local officials lost contact with military and police who set out for Singla, and Darvas says helicopters have had to turn back because of clouds.
12He says a few SUVs with foreign tourists bringing basic aid supplies had begun to reach Gorkha by early evening.

205:45 p.m. (1200 GMT, 8 a.m.)
21All mountaineering on the Chinese side of Mount Everest was cancelled after Saturday's earthquake.
22China's official Xinhua News Agency says more than 400 climbers from 20-plus countries were on the northern side of the world's highest mountain and were reported safe after they descended to lower elevations.
23Xinhua quoted an official with the Tibetan bureau of sports as saying that an avalanche at 7,000 meters (23,000 feet) and the possibility of further aftershocks was considered to have made climbing too dangerous.
24There was no word on when the ban would be lifted.
25The Chinese side of Everest is less popular with climbers, in part because a special permit is required to enter Tibet.
26But it is an alternative to the heavily trafficked Nepalese side, and it is growing popular especially with Chinese climbers.

28Doctors Without Borders is sending eight teams to provide medical aid and other relief in Nepal after Saturday's earthquake.
29The group says four of the teams were trying to crossing the border from India's Bihar state, a team from New Delhi is heading to Kathmandu and a team from Japan is heading to the Kathmandu Valley.

34Lila Mani Poudyal, the government's chief secretary and the rescue coordinator, appealed for more help from the international community, saying Nepal was short of everything from paramedics to electricity.
35"We are appealing for tents, dry goods, blankets, mattresses, and 80 different medicines ... that we desperately need now," he told reporters.

46World Vision says its aid workers reached Gorkha district, the epicenter, but gathering information from the villages remains a challenge.

48"These remote areas don't have any search or rescue operations assistance as of this time. In some of the remote areas staff heading out for assessments are finding both the road and the trails blocked by landslides, making access extremely difficult," World Vision said by email.

502.30 p.m. (0845 GMT, 4:45 a.m.)
51A respected consultancy says the long-term cost of reconstruction after Saturday's earthquake could be more than $5 billion, or about 20 percent of Nepal's GDP.
52Rajiv Biswas, Asia-Pacific Chief Economist for the Colorado-based consultancy services IHS, says Nepal has extremely limited capacity to finance relief efforts and reconstruction from its own resources.
53"The total long-term cost of reconstruction in Nepal using appropriate building standards for regions vulnerable to severe earthquakes could exceed $5 billion, which is around 20 percent of Nepal's GDP," he says.
54Nepal's annual per capita GDP is only $1,000, and the average family lives in poverty.
55"Massive international disaster relief and rescue efforts will be needed urgently, as well as large-scale international financial and technical assistance for long-term reconstruction of the economy," says Biswas.

58In addition, 6,515 people were injured nationwide, the police department said in a tweet.
