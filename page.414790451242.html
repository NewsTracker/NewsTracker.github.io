Hong Kong ferry captain sentenced to 8 years for deadly 2012 collision

Egypt strikes IS group in Libya after video of mass beheading surfaces

1HONG KONG -- A Hong Kong ferry captain was sentenced Monday to eight years in prison for the deaths of 39 people in a 2012 accident that was the southern Chinese coastal city's biggest maritime disaster in decades.
2A judge handed down the sentence to Lai Sai-ming after his conviction Saturday on charges of manslaughter and endangering the safety of others at sea, according to a Department of Justice spokeswoman.
3The captain of the other boat involved in the collision, Chow Chi-wai, was given a nine-month sentence.
4He was cleared by a jury of manslaughter but convicted of endangering others at sea.
5All of those killed, including eight children, and most of the nearly 100 injured were aboard Chow's boat.
6The two captains had blamed each other for the Oct. 1, 2012, collision, which came as a shock to the former British colony, renowned for efficient and safe public transport networks.
7The verdicts came down after a 60-day trial and four days of deliberations.
8Lai's commuter ferry was heading from Hong Kong Island to the outlying island of Lamma when the collision occurred.
9The smaller boat, owned by the Hong Kong Electric Co., was taking employees of the company on a harbour excursion to watch the Chinese National Day fireworks display.
10Fleets of ferries form the backbone of the city's transportation network, running frequently to outlying islands, the Chinese mainland and the gambling enclave of Macau.

2The statement said the warplanes targeted weapons caches and training camps before returning safely.
3It said the strikes were "to avenge the bloodshed and to seek retribution from the killers. Let those far and near know that Egyptians have a shield that protects them."
4This image made from a video released on February 15 by militants in Libya claiming loyalty to the Islamic State group purportedly shows Egyptian Coptic Christians in orange jumpsuits being led along a beach, each accompanied by a masked militant.
5Later in the video, the men are made to kneel and one militant addresses the camera in English before the men are simultaneously beheaded.
6The video is still not verified.
7Libya's air force, meanwhile, announced it had launched strikes in the eastern city of Darna, which was taken over by an Islamic State affiliate in 2014.
8The announcement, on the Facebook page of the Air Force Chief of Staff, did not provide further details.
9The video purporting to show the mass beheading of Coptic Christian hostages was released late on Sunday by militants in Libya affiliated with the Islamic State group.
10The killings raise the possibility that the extremist group - which controls about a third of Syria and Iraq in a self-declared caliphate - has established a direct affiliate less than 500 miles (800 kilometers) from the southern tip of Italy.
11One of the militants in the video makes direct reference to that possibility, saying the group now plans to "conquer Rome."
12The militants had been holding 21 Egyptian Coptic Christian laborers rounded up from the city of Sirte in December and January.
13It was not clear from the video whether all 21 hostages were killed.
14It was one of the first such beheading videos from an Islamic State group affiliate to come from outside the group's core territory in Syria and Iraq.
15Libya in recent months has seen the worst unrest since the 2011 uprising that toppled and killed longtime dictator Moammar Gadhafi, which will complicate any efforts to combat the country's many Islamic extremist groups.
16The internationally recognized government has been confined to the country's far east since Islamist-allied militias seized the capital Tripoli in 2014, and Islamist politicians have reconstituted a previous government and Parliament.
17Egypt has strongly backed the internationally recognized government, and US officials have said both Egypt and the United Arab Emirates have taken part in a series of mysterious airstrikes targeting Islamist-allied forces.
18The Egyptian government declared a seven-day mourning period after the release of the video and President Abdel Fattah el-Sissi addressed the nation late Sunday night, pledging resilience in a fight against terrorism.
19"These cowardly actions will not undermine our determination" said el-Sissi, who also banned all travel to Libya by Egyptian citizens.
20"Egypt and the whole world are in a fierce battle with extremist groups carrying extremist ideology and sharing the same goals."
21The UN Security Council "strongly condemned the heinous and cowardly apparent murder in Libya of 21 Egyptian Coptic Christians by an affiliate of the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant."
