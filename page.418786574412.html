Australian Islamic State group medic arrested on return home

1An Australian nurse who says he was forced by Islamic State militants to work as a medic in Syria was arrested after returning home and faces potential terrorism-related charges.
2Adam Brookman, 39, was arrested at Sydney International Airport on Friday night on a Victoria state warrant relating to his alleged involvement in the conflict in Syria, Australian Federal Police said in a statement.
3He appeared from a police cell by video link in the Parramatta Bail Court on Saturday, where a magistrate granted an application by the Melbourne Joint Counter Terrorism Team to extradite him to Victoria.
4He is to appear in a Melbourne court no later than Monday morning.
5The court heard a warrant for Brookman's arrest was issued on Friday.
6Police did not detail the charges he could face.
7Brookman did not speak during his brief appearance.
8He surrendered to Turkish officials in Turkey on Tuesday.
9He voluntarily flew back to Australia with a police escort.
10Brookman, a Muslim convert and father of five children who live in Melbourne, told Fairfax Media in May that he went to Syria last year to do humanitarian work for civilians caught in the war.
11He said he was innocent of any crime.
12Brookman said he was forced to join Islamic State militants after being injured in an airstrike and taken to a hospital controlled by the group.
13"After I recovered, they wouldn't let me leave," he told Fairfax.
14He won the militants' trust by working as a medic and was able to escape to Turkey in December.
15Brookman told Fairfax that he opposed the violent and extreme actions of the militants, including the beheading of their captives.
16"Of course there will be an investigation. That is fine. Hopefully things don't look that bad," Brookman told Fairfax.
17It is not clear whether Brookman was still in Syria on Dec. 4, when Australia made a presence in the Islamic State stronghold of al-Raqqa province in Syria a crime punishable by 10 years in prison.
18If charged, the onus would be on Brookman to prove he had a legitimate reason to be in the terrorist hotspot.
19Supporting a terrorist organization is a crime that carries a maximum penalty of 25 years in prison.
