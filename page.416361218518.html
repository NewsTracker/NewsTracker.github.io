Islamic State shoots and beheads 30 Ethiopian Christians in Libya: video

1Reuters was not able to verify the authenticity of the video but the killings resemble past violence carried out by Islamic State, an ultra-hardline group which has expanded its reach from strongholds in Iraq and Syria to conflict-ridden Libya.
2The video, in which militants call Christians "crusaders" who are out to kill Muslims, showed about 15 men being beheaded on a beach and another group of the same size, in an area of shrubland, being shot in the head.
3Both groups of men are referred to in a subtitle as "worshippers of the cross belonging to the hostile Ethiopian church".
4Libyan officials were not immediately available for comment.
5Ethiopia said it had not been able to verify whether the people shown in the video were its citizens.
6"Nonetheless, the Ethiopian government condemns the atrocious act," government spokesman Redwan Hussein said.
7He said Ethiopia, which does not have an embassy in Libya, would help repatriate Ethiopians if they wanted to leave Libya.
8Militants professing loyalty to Islamic State have claimed several high-profile attacks on foreigners in Libya this year, including an assault on the Corinthia Hotel in Tripoli and the beheading of 21 Egyptian Christians in February.
9The killing of the Egyptians prompted Egyptian President Abdel Fattah al-Sisi to order air strikes on Islamic State targets in Libya.
10In the latest video, a man dressed in black clutching a pistol stood behind some of the victims.
11"Muslim blood shed under the hands of your religions is not cheap," he says, looking at the camera.
12"To the nation of the cross: We are now back again."
13The video concludes with a warning that Christians will not be safe unless they embrace Islam or pay protection money.
14Islamic State controls large parts of Iraq and Syria and wants to redraw the map of the Middle East.
15It is not clear how many fighters it has in Libya, an oil-producing nation.
16Egyptian security officials estimate that thousands of militants who share Islamic State's ideology moved from the Sinai Peninsula to Libya after the army toppled President Mohamed Mursi of the Muslim Brotherhood in 2013.
