Google plans to equip automated cars with external airbags

1Airbags are usually located inside cars, but Google wants to change that on its driverless cars.
2Google acquired a new patent that illustrates the idea of equipping automated cars with external airbags to protect nearby pedestrians in case of an accident.
3The patent, which is called "System for pedestrian leg protection in vehicle impact," shows several illustrations in which the air bags are located on the front bumper of the automated car and it extends from side to side.
4The bumper airbags would deploy upon impact and create a buffer for pedestrians.
5"A system for protecting a pedestrian during impact with a vehicle, the system having a bumper adapted for attachment to an end of the vehicle, wherein the bumper is comprised of a plurality of air sacs," reads the patent.
6"The plurality of air sacs stretch and then burst during impact between the bumper and a pedestrian wherein the bursting of some of the plurality of air sacs reduces spring back of the bumper on the pedestrian."
7Google's driverless cars are meant to reduce car accidents and so far the cars have only been in two recorded accidents.
8The first was a read-end incident and the other was an accident that occurred while somebody was driving the car.
9Google wants to make sure its cars include as many safety features possible since automated cars make a lot of people nervous.
10Google is not the only company working on external airbags.
11Volvo has also been working on equipping its cars with pedestrian-sensing airbags.
12It's still unclear whether these bumpers will be installed on automated cars.
13Google has yet to comment on the patent.
