New York Fashion Week: Naomi Campbell wows Zac Posen crowd

Man arrested for making bomb threat at Islamic centre in Texas

1What does a runway queen wear?
2A ruby glitter ballgown, of course.
3Naomi Campbell had Zac Posen's crowd collectively uttering that fashion word of all words - wow - when she closed his Monday night show in the flocked taffeta bustier number against the grandeur of Vanderbilt Hall in Grand Central Terminal.
4Compared to a sea of muted grays, blues and blacks on other runways - and the gloom of winter's deep freeze - Posen's show of color was a welcome sight.
5It included a range of reds, emerald green, plum, burnt orange and sparkly disco silver in a column gown done up in bugle beads that glistened under the hall's stately chandeliers.
6And Campbell wasn't the only royalty in the house.
7Rihanna selfied with Lee Daniels and sat front row with Mary J. Blige, while Christina Hendricks and Coco Rocha beamed from their prime seats.
8Uzo Aduba and Katie Holmes were also among Posen's guests.
9Of the runway, Posen said in a backstage interview he was looking to mix and match his muses for fall.
10He worked in stretch jersey, included more day looks and ran with a '70s vibe in sparkle.
11And there was mink, in scarves tied close to the neck, a coat and a bright orange top paired with a long loose skirt of a similar shade.
12"We wanted more fluidity on the runway. I was feeling the glamour of Grace Kelly meets the spice of Chaka Khan. I draped most of the collection myself, on weekends. In my quiet moments," the affable designer said.
13So what's on Posen's mind for the Oscars?
14It's not all about the red carpet.
15"I don't believe in pushing hard for red carpets. Getting something on a red carpet shouldn't have to be a notch every awards show," he said.
16What else might be up for Posen?
17There's a documentary in the works about his life.
18And the avid home chef who delights followers on Instagram with his recipes and hashtag of CookingwithZac may just do a cookbook.
19He likes thinking up new recipes in the same way he likes taking fabric to a mannequin.
20"Finding your ingredients is like finding a great fabric," he said.
21"Both are sensual in the same way."
22Let no one underestimate the power of a cold beer and some spicy nuts to raise the spirits of a weary Fashion Week crowd.
23Often an 8 p.m. show feels like one too many on a freezing day, but the mood inside Rag & Bone's Fall/Winter show Monday night felt truly jovial.
24Did we mention they were offering beer?
25As for the clothes, designers David Neville and Marcus Wainwright channeled a '90s vibe with lots of layering, including interesting combos like a slip dress over skintight trousers with a huge parka on top.
26Colors were bright - especially a nice "spicy orange" - and there was a refreshing "anything goes with anything" vibe.
27Especially noticeable were satiny slips (silk charmeuse, actually) in all sorts of styles - slip dresses, slip skirts, even a slip jumpsuit.
28These came in luscious colors like licorice and chocolate.
29The designers, whose guests included actor Dylan McDermott, dancer Lil Buck and of course Vogue editor Anna Winter, also used innovative projections on the room's massive walls, covering up windows on one side and showing constantly alternating angles of the show in progress.
30There were close-up runway shots, long-distance shots, and most enticingly, inside-the-wings peeks at models getting primped just before being ushered onto the catwalk.
31Shoes came in fun colors like bright yellow and orange, and included Mary Janes and comfy loafers.
32Parkas were huge and inviting, especially given the weather.
33All in all a very user-friendly show - and, did we mention the beer?
