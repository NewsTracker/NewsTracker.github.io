Mexican radio station fires journalist who exposed presidential scandal

1Mexican radio station fires journalist who exposed presidential scandal Add to...
2One of Mexico's most prominent journalists, whose team revealed a conflict-of-interest scandal ensnaring President Enrique Pena Nieto last year, has been fired, her employer MVS Radio said on Sunday.
3Late last year, reporter Carmen Aristegui exposed that Pena Nieto's wife, Angelica Rivera, was in the process of acquiring a luxury house from a government contractor that won millions of dollars in state business.
4It later emerged that both Pena Nieto and his finance minister had also purchased houses from government contractors.
5The revelations damaged Pena Nieto's reputation, compounding a deep political crisis triggered by his government's handling of a probe into the disappearance and apparent massacre of 43 trainee teachers in September.
6Last week, MVS Radio and Aristegui publicly locked horns over a brand-new platform for investigative journalism called Mexicoleaks.
7The company contended that Aristegui and her team offered MVS's name and funding for Mexicoleaks without authorization.
8MVS subsequently fired two of her star investigative reporters due to "loss of confidence", prompting Aristegui to insist that reinstatement of her reporters was an "absolute condition".
9"Instead of firing them, we should be giving them prizes," Aristegui said on her morning radio show.
10The events have inspired a torrent of Tweets supporting Aristegui under the hashtag #EndefensadeAristegui (in defense of Aristegui).
11In its statement on Sunday, MVS Radio said "as a company, we can't accept conditions and ultimatums from our collaborators."
