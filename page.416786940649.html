Apple Dives Back into Bond Market

1Apple Inc. is back in the bond market.
2The tech giant is looking to complete a large bond deal Wednesday, though no size has been set yet.
3The company says it will use the proceeds to help pay for share buybacks and dividends, part of an expanded capital-return program for shareholders the company announced last month.
4Apple is offering the bonds in up to seven parts, with maturities ranging from two to 30 years.
5Investors have said previously that they were expecting Apple to tap the market, given that the company has sold bonds around this time the past two years.
6Apple's bond sale comes after a number of large bond deals from highly rated companies, as issuers take advantage of the continued low-interest rate environment.
7On Tuesday, AbbVie Inc. sold $16.7 billion, and last month, AT&T Inc. sold $17.5 billion.
8Earlier in the year, Actavis PLC placed a $21 billion deal.
9Apple is coming off a strong quarter.
10The company reported a 33% increase in profit for the quarter ended March 28, with net profit totaling $13.6 billion, compared with $10.2 billion in the year-earlier period.
11Strong iPhone sales helped boost the company's performance.
12Some outstanding Apple bonds traded slightly lower in price, and higher in yield, on Wednesday morning, as some investors sold in anticipation of the new deal.
13A 2018 bond traded to yield 1.286%, compared with 1.237% on Tuesday, according to data from MarketAxess.
