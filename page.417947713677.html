Egypt's arrest of Al-Jazeera journalist poses political problem for Germany

1Ahmed Mansour is a prominent journalist with the Qatar-based Al-Jazeera broadcaster's Arabic service.
2Cairo: A high-profile Al-Jazeera journalist arrested in Berlin at the request of Egypt said he expected to soon face a judge who would decide whether to extradite him, in a case that could embarrass Germany and renew questions about Cairo's crackdown on dissent.
3Ahmed Mansour, a leading talk show host on the Qatari channel's Arabic service, was arrested at a Berlin airport on Saturday, the latest Al-Jazeera journalist to be pursued by the Egyptian authorities.
4A protest rally in Berlin on Sunday demanded the release of Ahmed Mansour.
5Egypt accuses Al-Jazeera of being a mouthpiece of the Muslim Brotherhood, the Islamist movement that President Abdel Fattah al-Sisi removed from power in 2013 when he was army chief.
6Egypt called the Muslim Brotherhood a terrorist group.
7Both the television channel and the Brotherhood reject the allegations made by Egyptian authorities.
8Mansour told Al-Jazeera by telephone: "The German authorities told me that we are dealing with an international criminal case" and a judge would decide whether he should be extradited to Egypt.
9A Cairo court sentenced Mansour, who has dual Egyptian and British citizenship, to 15 years in prison in absentia last year on a charge of torturing a lawyer in 2011 in Tahrir Square, the focus of the uprising that toppled veteran autocrat Hosni Mubarak.
10Al-Jazeera said at the time the charge was false and an attempt to silence Mansour, known to viewers across the Arab world.
11Saad Djebbar, a lawyer for Al-Jazeera, said Mansour was arrested on Saturday as he tried to board a Qatar Airways flight to Doha.
12Egyptian Foreign Ministry spokesman Badr Abdelatty said Cairo was liaising with the German authorities.
13"He is accused of a crime and was sentenced so of course we have called for him to be returned," he told Reuters.
14Critics accuse the West of turning a blind eye towards what they say is Egypt's crackdown on dissent and freedom of speech in favour of improved economic ties and security cooperation.
15Mansour's arrest may bring to a head Germany's divisions over how to deal with Egypt, a country that is a valuable political ally and business partner, but also accused of widespread human rights abuses.
16Sisi visited Germany this month at Chancellor Angela Merkel's invitation, but he was snubbed by the speaker of Germany's parliament who cancelled a meeting with him, citing rights violations in Egypt.
17Also during the visit, German company Siemens signed a multi-billion euro deal with Egypt to supply gas and wind power plants.
18"The arrest of (Mansour) throws up many questions which need to be cleared up as soon as possible since Germany must not be a henchman of Egypt's politically controlled justice system," Niels Annen, foreign policy spokesman for the Social Democrats in the German parliament, told Spiegel Online.
19Egypt released Australian Al-Jazeera journalist Peter Greste in February this year after 400 days in prison on charges that included aiding a terrorist group.
20Mohamed Fahmy, a naturalised Canadian who has given up his Egyptian citizenship, and Egyptian Baher Mohamed were released on bail in February after spending more than a year in custody.
21The journalists were originally sentenced to between seven and 10 years in prison on charges including spreading lies to help a terrorist organisation, which they have denied.
22Egypt's high court ordered a retrial in January.
