82Scientists know that Ceres once had an icy ocean at its core and probably still does.
83But it's also possible that radioactivity inside the planet melted some of the ice, creating a lake or a sea.
84Dawn will look for a liquid ocean.

87Just because Ceres is intriguing doesn't mean it was easy to mount an expedition there.
88NASA canceled Dawn twice, recalls Russell, one of the mission's founders.

95When it starts to orbit Ceres, Dawn will become the first spacecraft in history to orbit two extraterrestrial objects, a feat impossible without its high-tech propulsion system.
96Like the TIE fighters in "Star Wars," Dawn is powered by so-called ion engines, which rely on electrically charged atoms called ions to push the craft ahead.
97The engines are far more efficient than conventional rocket engines, allowing the spacecraft to cover some 3 billion miles on a very small amount of fuel.
