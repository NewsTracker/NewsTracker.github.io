Ferguson shooting's witness denied bond-reduction request

1ST. LOUIS (AP) - A judge has refused to lower the bond of a key witness of the Michael Brown shooting death who is accused of resisting arrest during a recent confrontation with police.
2St. Louis Associate Circuit Judge Michael Noble denied a defense request to reduce the $1,000 cash-only bond for 23-year-old Dorian Johnson.
3Johnson was charged last week with resisting arrest or interfering with a lawful stop or detention.
4He's accused of trying to hinder the arrest of his younger brother the previous day.
5Johnson was a prominent witness of the shooting death last August of Brown, a black, unarmed 18-year-old who was killed by white Ferguson officer Darren Wilson during a confrontation in a street.
6Brown's death led to sometimes-violent protests in Ferguson and other U.S. cities.
