13About 200 people trapped by one fire were plucked off a beach by rescue boats.
14In all, firefighters were battling 59 fire fronts, mainly in southern Greece and near the capital.
15Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras said Greece had requested help from his country's army and air force, as well as the European emergency response center, to fight the blazes.
16France was sending two Canadair firefighting aircraft and a Beechcraft reconnaissance plane along with expert teams, French Interior Minister Bernard Cazeneuve said.
17The aircraft and teams were to fly to Greece on Saturday.
18France helped Greece fight forest fires in August 2014.
19Tsipras was holding an emergency meeting Friday night with the head of the armed forces, the defense and interior ministers, the head of the civil protection agency and the head of Greece's secret services.
20Defense Minister Panos Kammenos said it was likely that at least some of the fires were the result of arson, and ordered the armed forces to patrol forests and woodlands across the country as a preventative measure.
21"All forces are on alert," Tsipras said, noting that the hot, dry and windy conditions were making firefighting more difficult.
22The most severe fire was a massive blaze in the Lakonia area in the Peloponnese, southern Greece, where at least three villages were evacuated.
23Rescue boats battled gale-force winds to reach about 200 people who had taken refuge in a bay, ferrying them to safety, the coast guard said.
24Twelve other people were rescued from a separate beach, it said.
25Two helicopters, 120 firefighters and nearly 30 soldiers were fighting the Lakonia blaze, which local media said burned through electric pylons, cutting off electricity to the southern island of Kythira.
26Two pilots were slightly injured when their firefighting plane had to make an emergency landing.
27Another major fire burned across a large swathe of Mount Ymittos on the outskirts of the Greek capital, blanketing parts of southern Athens in smoke and threatening houses in outlying neighborhoods.
28Strong winds fanned the flames across the mountainside, hampering efforts by water-dropping planes and helicopters that continued flying sorties as night fell.
29A third major fire broke out Friday afternoon north of the capital in Malakasa, where more than 120 children were being evacuated from summer camps, mayor Thomas Rousis told state television.
30Firefighters were also tackling at least three other blazes, one near Athens, another in the southern area of Argolida in the Peloponnese, and a third on the island of Evia, northwest of the capital.
31Forest and brush fires are common during Greece's hot, dry summers.
32In 2007, massive forest fires ravaged southern Greece, devastating dozens of villages and killing about 60 people.
