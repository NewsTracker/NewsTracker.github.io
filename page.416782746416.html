Greece makes IMF payment but another, bigger one looms

215, 2011 file photo, a worker cleans the paint-splattered by protesters entrance of the bank of Greece, in central Athens.
3Wednesday May 6 marks five years since Greece voted in its first bailout deal in the face of violent popular protests that left three dead.
4The day was followed by years of turmoil in which the country tried to overhaul its economy in the midst of a downturn matched in depth and length only by the Great Depression.
5ATHENS, Greece (AP) - With Greece's cash flow problems growing by the day and negotiations with its creditors on the ropes, Athens managed to make a 200 million euro ($222 million) repayment to the International Monetary Fund on Wednesday.
6But another, much larger repayment looms next week that Greece will struggle to manage.
7The country once more faces the possibility of having to default on its debt, which could set off a chain of events jeopardizing its membership in Europe's joint currency.
8The country's left-wing government has been locked in troubled negotiations with its creditors and the institutions overseeing its bailout - the IMF, European Central Bank and European Commission - for the past three months.
9Finance Minister Yanis Varoufakis was in Rome to discuss the issue with his Italian counterpart Pier Carlo Padoan, while he heads to Madrid on Friday to meet Spanish Finance Minister Luis de Guindos.
10Both sides hope to make progress by May 11, when eurozone finance ministers are to meet.
11But expectations that a deal will be reached by then have faded.
12On Tuesday, a government official in Athens attributed the latest snag in negotiations to disagreements between the IMF and European Commission on how to handle the Greek crisis.
13The country is desperate for the creditors to unlock the final 7.2 billion euro installment from its 240 billion euro bailout.
14Locked out of the international bond market due to high interest rates that reflect investor fears of a default, the bailout funds are the only significant source of financing Greece has.
15The final installment has been pending since last summer, and Athens is now running out of options for funds needed both to repay its international loans and to ensure pensions and salaries are paid.
16It has already ordered state enterprises, including municipalities and schools, to transfer their reserves into a Bank of Greece account where they can be used as a loan by the state.
17Wednesday's repayment of interest on IMF's loans was made without any problems.
18"The payment is proceeding normally," a finance ministry official said, on condition of anonymity in line with government regulations.
19But on May 12, Greece must repay 750 million euros to the IMF - and it is not clear whether it will be able to meet both that payment and pay some pensions and salaries due mid-month.
