She did it again! Mexico's glamorous First Lady is the star of the show as she dazzles in diamonds at a lavish London banquet

US finds racist, profit-driven practices in Ferguson

1Last night saw Mexican First Lady Angelica Rivera on dazzling form for the second day in a row as she dressed up in a floor-length black dress and diamonds for a banquet hosted by the Lord Mayor of London.
2Ms Rivera, 45, and her husband Enrique Pena Nieto, 48, are currently on a state visit to the UK, which began with a ceremonial welcome at Horse Guards Parade.
3There, the glamorous political wife was spotted winking at the royals as she arrived with her husband, before smiling and laughing her way through the ceremony - indulged by an equally cheerful Queen.
4Ms Rivera, a former soap star, was then squired on a tour of the Royal Collection by a clearly charmed Duke of Edinburgh, with the pair seen deep in conversation as they inspected a saddle presented to Prince Philip during a visit to Mexico in 1975.
5Her whirlwind tour also included a solo visit to the set of Downton Abbey alongside the Countess of Wessex yesterday, with her husband spending much of the day at Downing Street instead.
6But it was for the final evening of her stay that Ms Rivera really pulled out all the stops, arriving at London's Guildhall in a spectacular floor-length black dress, teamed with a pair of diamond drop earrings and a matching bracelet.
7The banquet, which was hosted by the Lord Mayor of London, Alderman Alan Yarrow, and the City of London Corporation, is a state visit tradition, with invitations extended to all visiting heads of state.
8Ms Rivera, who was presented with a pretty bunch of spring flowers as she arrived, spent much of the evening chatting happily to the Duke and Duchess of Gloucester, who were also at the event, and Mrs Gilly Yarrow, the wife of the Lord Mayor.
9On arrival, the couple were welcomed with a Royal Salute fired from the cannons at the Tower of London during which a total of 62 shots were fired - the basic 21-gun Royal Salute, plus another 20 because the Tower is a royal palace and a further 21 for the City of London.
10The Mexican National Anthem was then played and President Pena Nieto was invited to inspect a Guard of Honour before making his way inside.
11At the end of the banquet, both President Pena Nieto and Lord Mayor Yarrow made speeches - in President Pena Nieto's case, for the second night in a row.
1While the Department of Justice cleared Officer Darren Wilson of federal civil rights charges in the August death of Michael Brown, it also called for sweeping changes in a city where officers trade racist emails, issue tickets mostly to black drivers that generate millions of dollars in revenue, and routinely use what investigators called excessive force on people stopped for minor or non-existent offenses.
2Attorney General Eric Holder said Wednesday that the department "found a community that was deeply polarized; a community where deep distrust and hostility often characterized interactions between police and area residents."
3Ferguson Mayor James Knowles III said steps are already being taken to correct problems.
4"We must do better not only as a city, but as a state and a country," Knowles said.
5The shooting of Brown sparked a national dialogue on race and law enforcement.
6Separate federal investigations into the shooting and the police department began soon after Brown was killed.
7In pairing the announcements on the investigations' results, the Obama administration sought to offset community disappointment over the conclusion that the shooting was legally justified with a message of hope for Ferguson's majority-black citizens.
8Officials announced 26 recommendations, including training officers in how to de-escalate confrontations and banning the use of ticketing and arrest quotas.
9Wilson was cleared in November by a state grand jury, a decision that set off protests, looting and fires.
10The federal report concurred that there was no evidence to disprove Wilson's testimony that he feared for his safety.
11Nor were there reliable witness accounts to establish that Brown had his hands up in surrender when he was shot, Justice Department lawyers said.
12An attorney for Wilson, Neil Bruntrager, described the officer as "very happy" with the outcome.
13Ben Crump, the attorney for Brown's parents, said the family was "extremely disappointed. This underscores the need for change and reform when there is continued use of excessive deadly force on people of color by police officers."
14About 30 protesters braved sub-freezing weather Wednesday night to gather outside Ferguson's police station.
15Four of them who ignored police warnings to clear the road were handcuffed and taken into custody.
16By 10:30 p.m., most of the protesters had dispersed.
17While the federal government declined to prosecute Wilson, it raised grave concerns about the operation of Ferguson's police department and municipal court.
18Though about two-thirds of the city's 21,000 residents are black, only four of 54 commissioned officers are African-American.
19That lack of diversity undermines community trust, the Justice Department's report said.
20It also found Ferguson relies heavily on fines for petty offenses, such as jaywalking, to raise revenue.
21Holder said the city collected more than $1.3 million in fines and court fees in 2010, but $3 million more is projected for the current fiscal year.
22"Our review of the evidence, and our conversations with police officers, have shown that significant pressure is brought to bear on law enforcement personnel to deliver on these revenue increases," Holder said.
23He cited a 2007 case where a woman received two parking tickets costing $152.
24Because of court fees and other expenses, she has paid $550 so far, spent six days in jail, and still owes $541.
25Another woman, Tiffany Tunstall, 34, told The Associated Press that she received "threatening" letters for nearly two years after paying off traffic tickets through an installment plan.
26"I didn't want to have anything to do with the city I was raised in," she said.
27"I felt disrespected."
28Activist John Gaskin III, a member of the national NAACP board of directors, said lines outside of municipal court are often long.
29"You'd think we're buying tickets to a Beyonce concert," Gaskin said.
30"The common theme is: Everyone there is African-American."
31Federal investigators found many other examples of discrimination.
32A lawful protest was broken up with a police warning of "everybody here's going to jail."
33And a black man sitting in a car with tinted windows was accused without cause of being a pedophile by an officer who pointed a gun at his head.
34Between 2012 and 2014, black drivers were more than twice as likely as others to be searched during routine traffic stops, but 26 percent less likely to be carrying contraband.
35The report also included seven racially tinged emails that did not result in punishment.
36The writer of one 2008 email stated that President Barack Obama would not be in office for long because "what black man holds a steady job for four years."
37Knowles said three employees were responsible for those emails.
38One was fired Wednesday, and the other two are on administrative leave pending an investigation, he said.
39He did not take questions, and Police Chief Tom Jackson was not at the news conference where the mayor spoke.
40The report's recommendations, if accepted by city officials, could lead to an overhaul of basic practices by police officers and court officials.
41Those include improving officer supervision, better recruiting, hiring and promotion and new mechanisms for responding to misconduct complaints.
42Federal officials described Ferguson leaders as cooperative and open to change and said there were already signs of improvement.
43The city, for example, no longer issues failure-to-appear warrants, has eliminated a fee for towing cars and rescinded warrants for nearly 600 defendants.
44Under Holder, the Justice Department has investigated roughly 20 police departments over alleged civil rights violations.
45Some have led to the appointment of independent monitors and have been resolved with agreements in which police commit to major changes.
46"It's quite evident that change is coming down the pike. This is encouraging," Gaskin said.
47"It's so unfortunate that Michael Brown had to be killed. But in spite of that, I feel justice is coming."
