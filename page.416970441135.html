Official says Ecuador and Mexico talking about DNA bank

1QUITO, Ecuador (AP) - A high-ranking Ecuadorean official said Wednesday his government is talking with Mexico about establishing a bank of DNA material from some of the migrants from his country who have disappeared trying to reach the U.S. illegally.
2Humberto Cordero, undersecretary for the Community of Returned Ecuadorean Migrants, told The Associated Press the genetic material could help determine if Ecuadoreans are among unidentified remains found in Mexico.
3Cordero says Ecuadorean police have sent some DNA to Mexican authorities in the past in the hopes of identifying the remains of migrants.
4Mexican authorities did not immediately respond to a call seeking comment.
5Associated Press writer Maria Verza in Mexico City contributed to this report.
