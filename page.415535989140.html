Reports: HPD searching Robert Durst's Houston home

1Authorities on Tuesday carried away two boxes after searching the Houston home of real estate millionaire Robert Durst after his weekend arrest in New Orleans on a murder warrant.
2Durst, 71, is charged with murder in a killing in California 15 years ago, and has been suspected - but never charged - in the disappearance of his first wife in New York.
3In 2003, he was acquitted of murder in a dismemberment death in Galveston.
4His arrest in New Orleans came shortly before the finale of an HBO series about his links to the three killings.
5Seven officers spent hours Tuesday searching Durst's home in a 17-story condominium building in Houston's posh Rice Village neighborhood.
6About 8:30 p.m. the officers, including one wearing a Los Angeles Police Department badge, carried two white cardboard document boxes to six vehicles and left.
7As one of them got into what seemed to be an FBI vehicle, a Chronicle reporter asked if there was any comment.
8A man responded, "We're all done now. You can leave. "
9FBI spokeswoman Shauna Dunlap confirmed that FBI agents participated in the operation at the request of the Los Angeles Police Department.
10Harris County district attorney's office spokesman Jeff McShan said the Los Angeles Police Department contacted his office last week.
11Neither Dunlap nor McShan would elaborate on what was happening inside the building and referred questions to Los Angeles police.
12LAPD spokesman Sgt. Barry Montgomery said the department is not commenting until Durst is in its custody.
13Durst is being held in Louisiana, where he faces drug and weapons charges, and it's not clear how soon he will be returned to California.
14"This is an investigation that's being handled by multi-jurisdictions," Montgomery said.
15"At this juncture the only thing the LAPD is doing is waiting for the extradition. We are just waiting on him to make it into our custody."
16Late Tuesday, KTRK and KPRC reported that Durst was transferred to the Elayn Hunt Correctional Center, in St. Gabriel, La.
17He is receiving mental health treatment there, KTRK said.
18Durst, 71, was charged Monday in Los Angeles with first-degree murder in the shooting of Susan Berman, his confidante and the daughter of a prominent Las Vegas mobster, in Beverly Hills.
19He could face the death penalty under special circumstances that allege he ambushed her and murdered a witness to a crime.
20Dick DeGuerin, Durst's longtime Houston defense attorney, said he was baffled by what investigators could be looking for in the condo building.
21"I think it's a publicity stunt. I'm not surprised by it, but I would really be surprised if they found anything of any evidentiary value," he said.
22In the documentary "The Jinx: The Life and Deaths of Robert Durst" that just wrapped up, Durst mumbled about how he "killed them all," providing a dramatic kick to the end of the series.
23But a law enforcement official said his arrest on the murder charge was based on words he wrote.
24Analysis linked a letter Durst wrote to Berman a year before her killing with one that pointed police to her body, and that was the key new evidence in the long-dormant investigation into the 2000 killing, the official, not authorized to speak publicly, told the Associated Press on the condition of anonymity because the investigation was ongoing.
25Carol Martin, who has lived in the same Houston condominium building as Durst for a little more than a year, said she often saw him in the elevator, lobby or neighborhood with his backpack.
26Durst waived extradition in New Orleans, but authorities there charged him with being a felon in possession of a gun and with having marijuana.
27Assistant District Mark Burton said investigators found more than a quarter-pound of pot and a revolver in his hotel room.
28DeGuerin said he wants a hearing in Louisiana as soon as possible to contest the arrest.
29"The warrant we believe is based on a television show and not on actual fact," he said.
30"We want a hearing as quickly as possible so Mr. Durst can go to California and face trial as quickly as possible."
31The judge in New Orleans scheduled another hearing for next Monday.
32Contributing to this report are: Chronicle reporter Anita Hassan and Associated Press writers Tami Abdollah and Brian Melley in Los Angeles, Janet McConnaughey in New Orleans and Emily Wagster in Jackson, Miss.
