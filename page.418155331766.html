Chobani selected as Greek yogurt provider for school lunches

4Public schools across America will soon offer Greek yogurt as a meat substitute in school lunches beginning this fall.
5Chobani, a manufacturer of Greek yogurt, officials announced Monday it had been selected by the U.S. Department of Agriculture to supply the yogurt as part of the federal school lunch program.
6The USDA decided in April to include Greek yogurt as a permanent option in its school lunch program after classifying it as an approved meat substitute in 2013.
7Chobani was selected as the exclusive provider after it successfully led a Greek yogurt pilot program over the past year, expanding the program from four to 12 states.
8During the first three months of the pilot program, schools in Idaho, New York, Arizona and Tennessee consumed 200,000 pounds of Chobani Greek yogurt.
9By the time the program was expanded, schools were ordering 700,000 pounds of yogurt.
10Company officials did not disclose the value of its USDA contract.
11Chobani, which is based in New York and opened a one of the biggest yogurt processing plant in the world in Idaho nearly three years ago, leads the U.S. in Greek yogurt production.
12Greek yogurt is a thicker style yogurt that has twice the protein of traditional yogurts and uses hormone-free milk.
13"Chobani is a nutrient-intense food and getting children the best nutrition early in life is very important to us," said Robert Post, senior director of Chobani's nutrition and regulatory affairs, in a prepared statement.
14"Since the very beginning, Chobani has sourced milk from cows not treated with artificial growth hormones."
