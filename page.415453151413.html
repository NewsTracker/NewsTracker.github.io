Iraqi troops pause battle for Tikrit, await reinforcements

1BAGHDAD, Iraq - Iraqi forces and mainly Shia militiamen battling to wrest full control of the city of Tikrit from Islamic State militants paused their offensive for a second day on Saturday as they awaited reinforcements, a military source said.
2More than 20,000 troops and Iranian-backed Shia fighters entered Tikrit on Wednesday, having retaken areas to the north and south in a campaign launched almost two weeks ago, Iraq's biggest offensive against the militants yet.
3Islamic State fighters still hold about half the city and have booby-trapped buildings and laid improvised explosive devices and roadside bombs, the source in the local military command center told Reuters.
4More "well-trained forces" were needed for the street-by-street battles that recapturing the whole city would require, the source said, speaking by phone from Tikrit.
5He did not give a timeline for their arrival.
6Victory for Iraq's Shia-led government in Tikrit against the Sunni insurgents would set the tone for a broader confrontation in Mosul, the largest city in the north.
7"We do not need a large number, just one or two thousand. We need professional personnel and soldiers," the source told Reuters.
8Military commanders had "reached a decision to halt the operation until a suitable, carefully set plan is in place" to break into central Tikrit, the source said.
9Islamic State overran Iraq's weakened army last year, seizing large amounts of territory where they have declared a caliphate and imposed brutal rule.
10In Tikrit, which lies about 160 km (100 miles) north of Baghdad, the extremist group still controls central districts and a complex of palaces built by Saddam Hussein, the executed former Iraqi leader.
11Iraqi security expert Hisham al-Hashemi doubted the city could be retaken with ground forces only, saying airpower was required to clear the many buildings which the IS fighters had rigged with explosives.
12As the army and Shia militias, who are known as Hashid Shaabi (Popular Mobilization), recaptured IS-held towns near Tikrit, a small number of residents who had fled the militants' advance began to return home.
13Nearly 2 million Iraqis were displaced last year and officials have said that securing their return is a priority.
14On Friday, some 200 families went home to the town of al-Alam with a security personnel escort from the government-held town of Dhuluiya, police sources there said.
15While the offensive on Tikrit has been put on hold, clashes in the environs continue.
16In the town to the north of al-Malha, near the Beiji oil refinery, IS fighters were fighting police and Hashid Shaabi forces, local police said.
17Iraqi forces were advancing toward the strategically important al-Fatha bridge, which was blown up by the militants this week, Interior Ministry spokesman Brig.
18Gen. Saad Maan told Reuters.
19Kurdish peshmerga forces, backed by Shia militia fighters, have been attacking Islamic State-held towns and villages south and west of the oil-rich city of Kirkuk, peshmerga sources said.
20Their advances, unlike the Tikrit battle to the southwest, have been backed by sustained air strikes from a US-led coalition.
21Islamic State also carried out attacks in Ramadi, about 90 km west of Baghdad.
22Two suicide car bombers attacked security personnel positions, killing two policemen, a police source said.
23The attacks were followed by clashes between IS fighters and Iraqi security forces in the city center, he added.
24And on the outskirts of Samarra, a sacred Shia city being used as a rear base for the Tikrit offensive further north, militants attacked an Iraqi army unit on Friday, two security officials said.
25One said 11 soldiers had been captured by the militants while the second said some soldiers had gone missing.
26A bomb near a busy market killed 4 people and wounded 11 in the Sab al-Bour neighborhood of northern Baghdad, police and medics said.
27No one immediately claimed responsibility for the attacks.
