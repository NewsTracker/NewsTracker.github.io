Republicans feast on crow, saying food stamps for poor like feeding park animals

1OKLAHOMA CITY - A regional branch of the Republican Party is apologizing for saying on Facebook that giving low-income Americans food vouchers is akin to feeding animals in national parks.
2Randy Brogdon, chairman of the Oklahoma Republican Party, said Tuesday on Facebook that the post was intended to illustrate the cycle of government dependency.
3He apologized "for any misconceptions that were created."
4The original message, posted Monday, says 46 million Americans participate in the national food assistance program, commonly referred to as food stamps.
5The post then says the National Park Service encourages people not to feed wild animals because they "will grow dependent on handouts and will not learn to take care of themselves."
6The post concludes: "Thus ends today's lesson in irony."
