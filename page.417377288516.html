Falcons LB Shembo waived after animal cruelty charge (Yahoo Sports)

1FILE - This June 15, 2014, file photo, Atlanta Falcons linebacker Prince Shembo.
2LAWRENCEVILLE, Ga.
3(AP) -- The Atlanta Falcons waived linebacker Prince Shembo on Friday, shortly after he was charged with aggravated animal cruelty.
4Police in suburban Gwinnett County said Shembo was charged after his then-girlfriend's dog died from blunt force trauma last month.
5The Falcons moved quickly to cut ties with the player.
6''We are aware of the charges that have been filed against Prince Shembo,'' the team said in a statement.
7''We are extremely disappointed that one of our players is involved in something like this.
8The case was especially sensitive to a franchise that lost star quarterback Michael Vick after he was charged with running a dogfighting ring in 2007.
9Vick wound up serving federal prison time and never played again for the Falcons.
10Denicia Williams called police on April 19 to report that her ex-boyfriend had killed her dog, police said in a news release.
11Williams told investigators she had taken her Yorkie, Dior, to Shembo's apartment on April 15.
12At some point, she left the 6-foot-2, 254-pound Shembo alone with the dog, and when she found the dog later he was unresponsive.
13Williams took the animal to an animal hospital where he died shortly afterward.
14Shembo told her over the phone the next day that he had kicked the dog, and she ended their relationship, Williams told police.
15Shembo surrendered at the Gwinnett County jail Friday night and his bond was set at $16,700 according to county records.
16Attorney Jerry Froelich told reporters that Shembo said he was putting the dog in its cage and he kicked it after it bit his hand.
17''When you're a small dog it doesn't take much with the size he is,'' Froelich said, adding that the dog's death was accidental.
18Shembo, 23, was a fourth-round pick in 2014 out of Notre Dame.
19He got significant time at inside linebacker as a rookie, playing in all 16 games with three starts and making 59 tackles.
20New coach Dan Quinn planned to move Shembo to outside linebacker and give him a shot at a regular role in the pass rush rotation.
21Now, the Falcons will have to come up with another option.
22A necropsy on the dog's body was done April 21 and tissue samples were sent to the University of Georgia for further testing.
23Police got the final results on Thursday.
24While waiting for those results, a police investigator spoke to Shembo several times by phone.
25Police decided to take out a warrant for Shembo's arrest because of inconsistencies in his account of what happened and because of the necropsy results, the release said.
26The dog suffered extensive injuries, including a fractured rib, abdominal hemorrhage, head trauma, eye injuries and extensive bruising in the muscles of his front leg and shoulders.
27Police said an investigator contacted the Falcons to let them know about the warrant.
28Froelich said he hadn't seen the police report as of Friday night.
