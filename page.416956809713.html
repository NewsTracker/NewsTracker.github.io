Islamic State attacks army-held areas in central Syria

1BEIRUT, May 13 (Reuters) - Islamic State fighters launched an attack on Syrian army-held areas in Homs province overnight, a Syrian military source and an organisation tracking the war said, part of the group's efforts to expand beyond its strongholds in the east.
2Syrian troops repelled the attack in places and were still fighting in others, the military source said.
3The British-based monitoring group, the Syrian Observatory for Human Rights, said there were large numbers of dead but had no toll.
4Islamic State's targets included the town of al-Sukhna, some 220 km (140 miles) east of Homs city, the Observatory said.
5The military source said, "(The army) repelled the attack in areas, there are areas where it is still ongoing."
6Islamic State has in recent months launched attacks in both the Syrian government-held as well as rebel-held areas far beyond areas of northern and eastern Syria where it is most firmly entrenched.
7The attacks have added to pressure on the army and allied militia that have faced significant setbacks recently, notably in the northwestern province of Idlib where rebels have seized the provincial capital and the nearby town of Jisr al-Shughour.
8The Lebanese group Hezbollah, a crucial ally of President Bashar al-Assad, and the Syrian military have meanwhile launched a big push to drive insurgents from the strategically important border zone between Lebanon and Syria.
9Media run by Hezbollah, an Iranian-backed Shi'ite group with a powerful militia, on Wednesday reported further progress in the offensive to drive insurgents including members of the al Qaeda-linked Nusra Front from the Qalamoun mountain range.
10The offensive is focused on areas about 50 km (30 miles) north of Assad's seat of power in Damascus that are seen as a top priority as he and his allies try to shore up control over over heavily populated parts of western Syria.
11Hezbollah's media arm, al-Manar, said the group's fighters and the Syrian army were bombarding a hill known as Tel Moussa - seen as the most important target in the offensive due to its high elevation overlooking the entire area.
