Chile volcano remains unstable

1The Chilean authorities have warned of further eruptions at the Calbuco volcano in southern Chile, which erupted twice this week.
2During a lull in its activity, most airports in Chile and neighbouring Argentina reopened.
3They were closed because of the tons of fine volcanic ash thrown into the atmosphere.
4More than 6,000 people living close to the volcano have been evacuated.
5Scientists say they are also concerned that rain expected to fall in the next few days could mix with the ash to form dangerous mudslides.
6Calbuco is estimated to have spewed out 210 million cubic metres (7,420m cubic feet) of ash which has coated the land for around in dust as light as flour or talcum powder.
7In some towns, local TV stations showed people wearing masks clearing the heavy ash off the roofs of their houses and nearby roads.
8On Saturday, the Chilean government announced it would provide aid to cover huge financial losses incurred by local farmers and to help evacuate thousands of farm animals.
9International flights were delayed or cancelled because of concerns over the ash cloud created by the volcano.
10The eruption on Thursday created a cloud of ash that rose 20km (12 miles) into the air.
11Volcanic ash can be extremely dangerous to aircraft as the fine particles can damage engines.
12In March, the Villarrica volcano, 200km (125 miles) north of Calbuco, erupted in the early hours of the morning, spewing ash and lava into the sky.
13Calbuco is located in Los Lagos, a region popular with tourists because of its mountain landscapes of volcanoes and lakes.
14Chile has about 90 active volcanoes.
