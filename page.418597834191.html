Google Soars on 2Q Results, YouTube Growth

1Google Inc's (GOOGL) shares surged as much as 14.5 percent on Friday, adding nearly $60 billion to its market value, as strong growth in mobile ad revenue allayed concerns its YouTube business could be hurt by Facebook Inc's push into video.
2The surge in the stock, which sent the Nasdaq composite index to a record intraday high, came a day after Google reported better-than-expected revenue and profit for the first time in six quarters.
3Shares of Google, the world's most valuable company after Apple Inc , hit a record high of $688.81, valuing the Web search giant at about $460 billion.
4At least 24 brokerages raised their price targets on Google's stock by as much as $150 to as high as $800, with analysts also welcoming new Chief Financial Officer Ruth Porat's emphasis on disciplined spending.
5Online video ads could be a $17 billion opportunity in the United States alone by 2017 and YouTube looks best positioned to benefit, Jefferies & Co analysts wrote in a note.
6"Second, new CFO Ruth Porat will take a more disciplined approach to expense management and capital allocation - welcome news," they wrote.
7Google said watch time for YouTube rose 60 percent in the second quarter and the video service roped in more viewers aged 18-49 than any U.S. cable network on mobile alone.
8Google and Facebook are trying to woo big companies to market their products via online videos.
9YouTube has an edge over Facebook as it has a more mature monetization platform that seems to attract the best content, Piper Jaffray analyst Gene Munster said.
10Google said the gap was closing between mobile and desktop "cost per click" (CPC), or the average price of online ads.
11Mobile CPCs are likely to exceed desktop CPCs in the long term, Munster said.
12Google's CPCs fell 11 percent in the quarter but that is not the complete story, Jefferies analysts said.
13YouTube TrueView ads, where advertisers pay only when users don't skip an ad, cost less than traditional search ads.
14Google's fast rampup of YouTube monetization is also hurting pricing, they said.
15The company's advertising revenue rose 11 percent to $16.02 billion.
16The number of paid clicks increased 18 percent.
17"This growth has steadily held in the mid-to-high teens since at least Q1:13," RBC analyst Mark Mahaney said.
18"Such consistency is a rarity, especially for a behemoth business..."
19Of 48 analysts covering the stock, 38 have "buy" or higher rating, while 10 have "hold."
20The median price target is $672, according to Thomson Reuters data.
21Google's shares were up about 14 percent at $685.52 after 20 minutes of trading on the Nasdaq.
