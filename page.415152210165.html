No smiles from Kerry, Russian FM as Ukraine festers

1GENEVA -- U.S. Secretary of State John Kerry met Monday with his Russian counterpart in what appeared to be less than amicable talks amid continuing tensions over Ukraine and American calls for a full probe into the murder of a prominent opposition figure in Moscow.
2Kerry and Russia's Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov spoke for roughly 80 minutes at a Geneva hotel, according to the State Department, which did not immediately provide details of the discussion.
3Kerry said in a tweet that they talked about Ukraine, Syria and ongoing negotiations with Iran over its nuclear program.
4But in a change from their usual cordial encounters for cameras, neither man smiled or spoke substantively as they shook hands at the start of the talks that took place less than a week after Kerry told Congress that Russian officials have lied to his face about Moscow's role in Ukraine.
5That comment drew a rebuke from the Russian foreign ministry.
6U.S. officials have pointed out that Kerry did not specifically accuse Lavrov of lying to him.
7They say he was referring to public statements and media reports, although Lavrov is the only Russian official Kerry is known to have met face-to-face in recent months.
8The pair last met in February on the sidelines of an international security conference in Munich shortly before a new Ukraine ceasefire agreement was agreed upon.
9Both Ukraine and the Russian-backed rebels have accused each other of violating the truce and the meeting comes as the U.S. and its European allies contemplate additional sanctions on Russia for its actions in Ukraine.
10In his congressional testimony last week, Kerry said that fresh U.S. sanctions had already been prepared and suggested they could be implemented soon.
11However, U.S. officials have said since that they will wait to see if the ceasefire and follow-on commitments to end the violence in Ukraine's east are complied with before putting the sanctions in place.
12As the two men met, the Geneva-based U.N. human rights office said that more than 6,000 people have died in eastern Ukraine since the start of the conflict almost a year ago.
13It said the fighting has led to a "merciless devastation of civilian lives and infrastructure."
14Hundreds of civilians and military personnel have been killed in recent weeks alone after an upswing in fighting particularly near Donetsk airport and in the Debaltseve area, the body said in a report covering the period from December to February.
15The strategic railroad town of Debaltseve was captured from Ukrainian government forces last month by pro-Russian separatists.
16While Russia denies its troops are fighting in Ukraine, the U.N. cited "credible reports (that) indicate a continuing flow of heavy weaponry and foreign fighters" from Russia.
17Kerry and Lavrov are to address the U.N. Human Rights Council separately later Monday.
18The Kerry-Lavrov meeting also followed Friday's murder near the Kremlin of Russian opposition leader Boris Nemtsov.
19Kerry and other U.S. and western officials have called on Russia to conduct a prompt, thorough, transparent and credible investigation into the slaying.
20Russian President Vladimir Putin has said he will take personal charge of the probe.
21U.S. officials traveling with Kerry would not say what they thought of Putin's move and said they would withhold comment until the investigation is complete.
