12Eretz Nehederet's producers say the first episode of the latest season drew in nearly 30 percent of television audiences, a viewing figure most candidates could only dream of attracting.
13The show has become so popular that real-life politicians are starting to get in on the parody act.
14Netanyahu released a wry campaign video in which he sits on a sofa eating popcorn as he takes care of a young couple's children, portraying himself as the nation's "Bibi-sitter".

16While the show's writers admit to being impressed by the self-deprecation of some of Israel's most senior politicians, they aren't concerned about losing their jobs.
17"Don't worry; we're still better than them," says producer Muli Segev.
18"Firstly, we're more fun. And you can't forget that their primary role is to run the country. Unlike us, they haven't really done their job in recent years," he adds.

21During a visit to Jerusalem in 2013, US President Barack Obama dismissed reports of a dispute between him and his "friend Bibi" as "a plot to find something to feed Eretz Nehederet".
22Back in the studio, nothing is sacred, with the set including replicas of the concrete wall separating Israel from the West Bank, a plastic anti-missile system and mock-ups of smuggling tunnels in Gaza -- all set up around the talk show table.
