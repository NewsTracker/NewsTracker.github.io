Campaigns halt ahead of Greek referendum

1Campaigns have halted in Greece on the eve of a closely watched bailout referendum - with voters in a dead heat over whether to defy creditors and push for better repayment terms or essentially seek new political leadership to find a compromise.
2Political rallies and publication of new opinion polls are banned 24 hours before Sunday's referendum called by left-wing Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras, who has promised to ease austerity after six years of recession.
3Rival rallies took place a half-mile apart in central Athens late Friday, and Tsipras made his final pitch on a stage set up for a campaign rock concert outside parliament.
4"This is not a protest. It is a celebration to overcome fear and blackmail," he told a crowd of 30,000 as they roared oxi, oxi - "no, no."
5The 40-year-old Tsipras is gambling the future of his new left wing government on Sunday's snap poll - insisting a "no" vote will strengthen his hand to negotiate a third bailout with better terms.
6If he loses, Tsipras has strongly indicated he would step aside.
7Athens' high-stakes stand-off with lenders saw Greece default on debts this week, close banks to avoid their collapse, and lose access to billions of euros after an existing bailout deal expired.
8The EFSF, the Eurozone's rescue fund and Greece's largest creditor, said Friday it considered the country to be in default.
9At Friday's "no" rally, Athens resident Maria Antoniou held a handmade sign, reading "oxi".
10"We have to strengthen Tsipras. It's not his fault we are bankrupt," she said.
11That is a message the "yes" voters refuse to believe.
12Police said about 22,000 people gathered outside the nearby Panathenian stadium for the "yes" rally, waving Greek and European Union flags and chanting "Greece, Europe, Democracy."
13Evgenia Bouzala, a Greek born in Germany, said she was considering shutting down her olive oil export business because of the financial turmoil.
14Rallies for both campaigns were also held in 10 other Greek cities on Friday, and the drama remained high in the final hours of campaigning.
15The country's top court stayed in session till the late afternoon before rejecting a petition to declare the referendum illegal.
16A series of polls published on Friday at the end of a frantic week-long campaign showed the two sides in a dead heat, with an incremental lead of the "yes" vote well within the margin of error.
17They also showed an overwhelming majority of people - about 75 per cent - want Greece to remain in the euro currency.
