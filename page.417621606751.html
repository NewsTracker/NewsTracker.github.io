Apple Music is CONFIRMED, Apple Pay to hit UK and iPhone to get THREE hour battery boost

1APPLE has announced some major updates to its iPhone, Mac and watchOS at its annual developer conference - WWDC.
2Apple has unveiled a new music service to rival Spotify as well as more tools to help developers create richer apps for the Apple Watch.
3The Cupertino firm also announced updates to its Mac OS X operating system as well as giving a sneak peak at iOS 9.
4Both new operating systems will be released later in the year.
5Express.co.uk has rounded up everything you need to know about the iPhone manufacturer's latest announcements and upcoming releases.
6Apple started its iOS 9 talk by focusing on its voice activated assistant, Siri.
7The firm claims the accuracy has become "industry leading".
8Siri has also been given a visual overhaul - which keeps it in-line with the interface rolled out with the Apple Watch earlier this year.
9The voice assistant boasts a number of pro-active features.
10It will search for unknown numbers in your email and suggest contacts when you get an incoming phone call.
11Siri learns your habits and will adapt its behaviour to suit you.
12If you listen to music when you jog every morning, your iPhone will jump straight into the music when you connect your headphones at the start of your day.
13It is also more context-aware than ever before.
14If you are in Messages looking at a text message, you can tell Siri to remind you "of this" and the app will know what you are referring to on-screen.
15Apple has also updated the Spotlight Search in iOS 9.
16Siri now makes suggestions for people to contact - based on the time of day and your location.
17Users will be able to watch videos from YouTube within the Spotlight search.
18The software's search feature has also been improved, now enabling users to see and jump straight into apps from search results.
19Siri can also now be used to voice search for photos on you device.
20Apple has also added some clever machine learning to its photos app, which means you can ask Siri to find photos "of me in San Francisco last year".
21The firm is calling the whole update "Intelligence".
22Mr Federighi also made a point on privacy by saying that Apple is "not interested" in user data and that Apple doesn't mine user data.
23"It stays on your device, under your control," he said.
24Last week, Tim Cook accused Google of invading user privacy by using their photos via a new app to mine data on users.
25Apple is taking a stand on battery life.
26Simply by updating to iOS 9, Apple claims it will add an extra hour of battery life to your iOS device.
27The latest mobile update also includes a battery saver mode which "pulls switches you didn't even know existed" to give iPhone and iPad users an extra three hours of battery.
28For the first time, Apple will be offering the new update as a public beta, coming in July.
29OS 9 will also introduce multi-tasking to the iPad.
30Users will now be able to use a feature called "slide-over" that will enable you to bring a second app on-screen while using the internet.
31A split view will enable users to interact with both apps and windows at the same time.
32The feature will also support picture-in-picture, so users can watch videos on the corner of the screen while also using another app on their iPad.
33The number of space needed to make an iOS update - a common user problem - has also been greatly reduced from 4.6Gb to 1.8Gb.
34The internet of things was also addressed, with Apple adding support for window shades, motion sensors and security systems, as well as remote access to these appliances from wherever you are via iCloud.
35Mr Cook returned to the stage to announced that the Apple App Store has surpassed 100 billion downloads in the seven years since it launched.
36Apple has finally confirmed its hugely anticipated music streaming service - dubbed Apple Music.
37It allows customers to stream the entire iTunes music library as well as music videos.
38Apple Music also boasts a 24 hour, seven-day live radio station called "Beats 1" hosted from London, New York and LA by former BBC Radio 1 host Zane Lowe and other popular DJs.
39The app will also give artists a forum called "Connect" to allow them to interact with fans by releasing demos, playlists, soundbites, videos and songs.
40Legendary music industry figure Jimmy Iovine, who introduced Apple Music at WWDC, called it a "revolutionary music service".
41Iovine claims the app brings together elements of the music world that are confusing and currently spread across many apps.
42Apple Music will launch in more than 100 different countries on June 30th.
43An Android version of the app will be coming in the autumn, Apple confirmed on-stage.
44The music streaming service will be free for the first three months and will then be priced at $9.99.
45Apple is also offering what it calls a "family plan" for $14.99 a month for up to six family members.
46Apple Music will be available via an update on iPhone, iPad, Mac and Windows.
47And Android version of Apple Music was also confirmed, although this will be released later this year.
48Apple bought the Beats headphone maker and music streaming service for $3 billion last year.
49Since then, Apple has been quietly putting together a team for the relaunch of the Beats streaming service.
50The Cupertino company hired popular DJ Zane Lowe, who has hosted the BBC Radio 1 evening show since 2002, earlier this year.
51It also poached James Bursey - who worked as a producer on Zane Lowe's show.
52In addition to its hires from the BBC, Apple also hired head of editorial at Deezer, James Foley.
53Before it was sold to Apple, the Beats Music streaming service had 303,000 US subscribers.
54In contrast, market leader Spotify has some 4.7 million customers.
55Apple is launching its touch-to-pay system in the UK next month.
56Apple Pay will enable users to pay for items by touching their iPhone to contactless payment points.
57"We will launch with eight of the most popular banks, more coming this fall," said Apple's Jennifer Bailey.
58It confirmed that a number of stores, including Boots and Marks & Spencers, will support the new contactless system.
59The Cupertino firm confirmed that it has signed up some of the biggest banks - which will allow 70 per cent of users to pay with their iPhones this summer.
60More than 250,000 locations will be accepting Apple Pay in UK, next month.
61Customers will be also able to pay with the system across the London Underground network, starting in July.
62The long-rumoured introduction of rewards cards into Apple Pay was also confirmed, with the Passbook app being renamed Wallet, and now housing credit cards and reward cards in one place.
63Apple will offer a new app for news, with a personalised feed based on your interests and choices.
64The app draws in text, photos and video from a variety of sources.
65Stories that use Apple's new News format will look best on the app.
66Features include the ability to save articles to read later and to get suggestions on new publishers and topics by selecting "explore."
67It's not immediately clear how Apple will handle news sources that require subscriptions.
68Apple says it has worked with leading news organisationsto bring stories to the app.
69The app will initially be offered in the US, the UK and Australia.
70The iPhone manufacturer is finally bringing public transit support to its native map application, Apple Maps.
71It was one of the biggest gaps when Apple infamously replaced Google Maps as the default map app on iPhones and iPads in 2012.
72Tapping on a station will show you all the lines that run through it, along with upcoming departure times.
73You can also get step-by-step directions - that include the walk to the station.
74The transit feature will be available in select cities around the world initially.
75Apple unveiled the successor to OS X Yosemite, dubbed OS X El Capitan.
76The update keeps the same minimalist and refreshed visual style Apple introduced to its desktop products last year.
77OS X El Capitan will work across Apple's desktop and laptop range.
78It appears to be a more measured and focused update compared to the complete overhaul unveiled this time last year.
79The desktop update adds a number of new features as well as improvements to stability.
80OS X El Capitan has been updated to better handle the native fullscreen apps, Apple first introduced with OS X Lion.
81Users can now run fullscreen apps side-by-side, which the firm has dubbed Split View.
82Apple said the aim is to boost productivity for every day users.
83El Capitan is the name of a mountain in Yosemite National Park in California, Yosemite having been the name of last year's update.
84The iPhone maker has adopted the tradition of naming its software after landmarks in California in recent years.
85Apple claims that updating to OS X El Capitan will give customers a speed bump, thanks to a number of under-the-bonnet improvements.
86It claims overall performance on the latest desktop OS is almost one-and-a-half times faster than the previous release.
87OS X El Capitan also offers better battery life to Apple laptops and brings the successful Metal gaming architecture introduced for iOS this time last year.
88Craig Federighi confirmed that a public beta of El Capitan would become available in July, before becoming available as a free upgrade in the autumn.
89"We believe in technology designed for the wrist", said Mr Cook, discussing the new version of Apple Watch software, which the company calls watchOS.
90Apple's Kevin Lynch took to the stage to show off the new features of watchOS, including new customisable watch faces, which can be made up of images for the first time.
91Timelapse photos shown as live, based on the time of day, can also be used.
92Mr Lynch also showed off "Time Travel" - a feature that will enable users to scroll forward and back to see the different parts of their schedule.
93It will reel through appointments throughout the day, clocks in different time zones and will even predict the charge of your electric vehicle - all from the watch face.
94"Flux Capacitor sold separately," said the message on-screen.
95The drawings in Digital Touch can now also be done in a range of colours.
96The Apple Watch will also support short video playback on the watch face, including from apps such as Vine.
97Siri can now also be used to control aspects of your smart home, asking it to change lighting for example, as well as ask the digital assistant to show the Glance notifications that run on Apple Watch.
98An Apple Watch was also shown controlling the temperature inside a connected car, as well as using voice to reply to emails.
99The new watchOS will launch in the autumn, it was confirmed.
