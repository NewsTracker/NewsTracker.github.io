Bianchi death overshadows F1 title tussle

1The championship tussle between Lewis Hamilton and Nico Rosberg will take a back seat at the Hungarian Grand Prix this weekend, with Formula One still coming to terms with the death of Jules Bianchi.
2The 25-year-old Bianchi died in a hospital in his hometown of Nice on Friday from injuries suffered in a head-on crash during last October's Japanese GP.
3The outpouring of sympathy and support for Bianchi's family reflected just how popular he was.
4He was also highly rated, and made enough of an impression in his 34 GPs for many observers to predict he would be a future star.
5Bianchi had been expected to replace Kimi Raikkonen at Ferrari.
6Hamilton, Rosberg, Brazilian driver Felipe Massa and French driver Romain Grosjean attended Bianchi's funeral at the Sainte Reparate Cathedral in Nice on Tuesday, along with four-time F1 champions Alain Prost and Sebastian Vettel and FIA president Jean Todt.
7"Saying goodbye to Jules was incredibly hard for everyone," Hamilton said.
8"For myself, I wished I had known him better. But from what I knew of him, he was a kind heart with a great spirit and a bright future."
9Nice is about 30km from the track where Bianchi sealed the best performance of his career.
10Driving for Marussia - now known as Manor Marussia - he finished ninth at the Monaco GP last year and gave the unheralded team its first points in F1.
11Bianchi made his GP debut for Marussia in 2013.
12The previous year, he drove for the Force India team in several practice sessions.
13"We had the pleasure of working with Jules during 2012 and he left a strong impression on everyone," Force India team principal Vijay Mallya said.
14"An outstanding young man and a tremendous talent who was destined for great things in Formula One. The world has lost a true racer and we have all lost a friend."
15Bianchi's accident occurred at the end of the race at the Suzuka circuit.
16In rainy, gloomy conditions, his car slid off the track and ploughed into a crane picking up the Sauber of German driver Adrian Sutil, who crashed one lap earlier.
17The accident prompted F1 to alter its rules, allowing a "virtual safety car" in which stewards can neutralise a race, forcing all cars to proceed slowly into the pit lane rather than continuing to lap the circuit.
18The start times of some races were also moved forward to avoid them continuing in dim light conditions.
19Governing body FIA will retire the number 17 - Bianchi's racing car number last year - in his honour, and it will be an emotional weekend at the Hungaroring circuit.
