Freddie Gray case: Six Baltimore police officers face murder, false imprisonment charges in death of black man

1Baltimore state attorney Marilyn Mosby announces there is 'probable cause to file criminal charges in the Freddie Gray case' against police officers involved in the arrest of the black man who died in police custody.
2Baltimore: Six Baltimore police officers will face criminal charges, including second-degree murder and manslaughter, in the death of a black man who was arrested and suffered fatal injuries while riding in a moving police van, the city's chief prosecutor said during an unexpected announcement on on Friday.
3Marilyn Mosby, the state's attorney for Baltimore City, said Gray, who died a week after his April 12 arrest, suffered a critical neck injury as a result of being handcuffed inside the van.
4She said officers failed to provide medical attention to Gray even though he asked for it on at least two occasions.
5Renay Battle, left, and Charvae Day, right, react to the news from State's Attorney Marilyn Mosby that there are probable causes to charge those involved in the police-custody death of Freddie Gray.
6In a news conference on Friday, Mosby said that the death of Gray had been ruled a homicide and the police had been negligent in his death.
7"We have probable cause to file criminal charges," Mosby said.
8As Mosby spoke outside the War Memorial here, dozens of police officers dressed in riot gear stood nearby.
9Freddie Gray was found to have a legal pocket knife and the police had no reason to arrest him, Mosby said.
10Protesters march after a rally at City Hall in Philadelphia on Thursday.
11The event in Philadelphia follows days of unrest in Baltimore amid Freddie Gray's police-custody death.
12She described repeated mistreatment of Gray.
13She said that time and again police mistreated Gray, arresting him with no grounds, violating police procedure by putting him in cuffs and leg cuffs in the van without seat belting him and then repeatedly failing to get him medical attention.
14She said that when he was removed from the wagon, "Mr. Gray was no longer breathing at all."
15Warrants have been issued for the arrest of the officers charged in the case, she said.
16In addition to murder and manslaughter, charges include assault, misconduct and false imprisonment.
17In a dramatic news conference, Mosby said the Maryland chief medical examiner ruled Gray's death a homicide and that his spinal injuries occurred while he was riding without restraints in the van.
18Representatives for the police union and Gray's family were not immediately available for comment.
19"We put all our resources to make sure we were pursuing and leading where the facts took us in this case, which was to pursue justice," Mosby said, a day after the Baltimore Police Department turned over findings from its internal investigation.
20Mosby said the Maryland chief medical examiner ruled Gray's death a homicide.
21The 25-year-old Gray was no longer breathing when he was finally removed from the van, Mosby said.
22A crowd of people who gathered to listen to the prosecutor's announcement broke into applause and drivers honked their car horns after she finished speaking.
23Some of the onlookers chanted: "Justice!"
24"Justice for Freddie!"
25"Thank you Ms Mosby."
26"The people of America give me hope. People like this district attorney give me hope," said Jay Morrison, a youth leader who attended the news conference.
27Mosby, a 35-year-old African-American who took office in January, is facing the biggest test of her short career in deciding to bring charges against the officers.
28In Ferguson and New York last year, grand juries decided against charging officers who were involved in the deaths of two unarmed black men.
29The news triggered rioting in the St. Louis suburb and days of protest marches in New York and other cities.
30Gray's death on April 19, a week after his arrest, is the latest flashpoint in a national outcry over the treatment of African-Americans and other minority groups by US law enforcement.
31After a night of rioting in Baltimore on Monday, protests spread to other major cities in a reprise of demonstrations last year set off by police killings of unarmed black men in Ferguson, Missouri, New York and elsewhere.
