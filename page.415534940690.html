10The U.S., according to Price, is also threatened by lackluster economic growth.
11"The economy is not working for many Americans. A lot of people are struggling to keep up or are being left behind Our economy is not reaching its full potential."
12The culprits here are "out-of-control spending, higher taxes, and burdensome regulations," according to Price.

All the things Americans want to do are being harmed and made more difficult by the president's policies."
14The budget calls for comprehensive tax reform, the repeal of restrictive (including much of the Dodd-Frank Wall Street reform bill signed into law after the financial crisis) and a major increase in U.S. energy extraction.
15The budget says that would reduce spending and bring down prices for average Americans.
16Another assumption is that the Affordable Care Act is a mortal threat to the health care system in the U.S. Again, many would dispute that, but it is a driving element of how the budget deals with health care spending.
17In this worldview, unless Obamacare is eliminated root and branch, no other repairs to the health care system are possible.
18"Obamacare is not working for America's families, doctors or employers," it reads.
19Once Obamacare is gone, the plan would shift Medicare into a "premium support" system.
20(Many economists see that as vouchers by another name.)
21It would transform Medicaid into a kind of modified block grant program managed mostly by the states.
22The budget does not directly address how millions of Americans newly covered under Medicaid and plans sold on the ACA's health care exchanges would find coverage.
23But it praises "patient-centered health care" and the market's power.
24"The goal is to empower patients - whether we are talking about increasing competition and transparency in the health insurance market or allowing individuals to join together voluntarily to pool risk so no person is priced out of the market even if they have a pre-existing condition."
25The budget also assumes the U.S. is under direct threat of attack from various bad actors around the world - and that we must increase our defense budget, though it's already larger than the combined defense budgets of the next 10 biggest spenders.
26"Today, our nation, our allies and our interests at home and around the world are threatened by radical Islamic terrorists as well as unstable and oppressive regimes," it finds.
27As a result, the Price budget hikes defense spending sharply, in part by using a vehicle called the Overseas Contingency Operations fund.
28The OCO is off-budget spending, meaning it doesn't technically add to the deficit.
29It is supposed to be used to fund the ongoing military operations in Iraq and Afghanistan.
30Yet under the Price budget, it appears a portion of the money would go to the Pentagon to fund programs normally accounted for in the budget.
31Price's budget sees a U.S. in which the social safety net is failing those it's supposed to help, largely because of the hubris of the federal bureaucrats running it.
32There's a place for safety net programs, he allows.
33However: "Our charge is to address these challenges in a way that is compassionate and constructive - mindful of the fact that Washington does not hold the answer to every question. Government must respect its limits."
34The Price budget consolidates many existing social programs, pushing them down in large part to the states.
35Most notably, the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program, commonly known as Food Stamps, becomes a state-level program funded by federal grants.
36Finally, in Price's view (though he's hardly the first to say this), Washington is a hotbed of waste, fraud and abuse, enabled by an unaccountable government.
37"All of Washington ought to face scrutiny - every department, agency and program," the proposal says.
38"And once tax dollars are sent out the door, there ought to be in place a system to better track the efficiency and effectiveness of the initiatives being funded. More accountability from departments and agencies means a government that's more respectful and responsive to the needs of its people."
39Price's Democratic counterpart on the Budget Committee, ranking member Chris Van Hollen (D-MD), said in a statement Tuesday, "Congressional Republicans have been quick to pay lip service to wanting to help working families - but this budget means that Americans who are working harder than ever will be getting even less.

41"Students will see deep education cuts and college will be less affordable. And this budget takes away the tools that allow people to climb the ladder of opportunity."

11Organization officials did not respond to calls and emails from The Associated Press on Monday or Tuesday.

13The Ebola virus is spread through contact with a sick patient's blood or bodily fluids.
14The World Health Organization estimates the virus has killed more than 10,000 people, mostly in Guinea, Liberia and Sierra Leone.
15While deaths have slowed dramatically in recent months, the virus appears stubbornly entrenched in parts of Guinea and Sierra Leone.
