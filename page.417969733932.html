Mexico deports 49 percent more minors in first 5 months

1MEXICO CITY (AP) - While the wave of child and teen migrants has dropped off at the U.S. border, detentions of Central American minors are up sharply in Mexico this year.
2Mexico's National Immigration Institute says detentions of Central American minors have risen 49 percent compared to the similar period last year.
3It says about half of the 11,893 underage migrants detained between January and May were travelling alone or with a smuggler.
4Two-thirds were between 12 and 17.
5One third were 11 or younger.
6The institute said Monday it was caring for the increased number of children through the country's child welfare agencies before they are deported.
7But as in the 2014 U.S. immigration crisis, there are accusations of inadequate facilities to handle all the minors detained.
