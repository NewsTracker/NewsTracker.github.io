Boko Haram-Like Attack Kills 30 in Nigeria

1Attack at crowded mosque by two young female suicide bombers in northeast Nigeria kills 30.
2BAUCHI, Nigeria (AP) - Two girls blew themselves up on Monday near a crowded mosque in northeast Nigeria's biggest city, killing about 30 people, witnesses said.
3It is the fourth suicide bombing this month in Maiduguri, which is the birthplace of the Boko Haram Islamic extremist group.
4Fishmonger Idi Idrisa said one teenager exploded as she approached the mosque crowded with people from the nearby Baga Road fish market, performing afternoon prayers during the holy month of Ramadan.
5The second teen appeared to run away and blew up further away, killing only herself, he said.
6Civilian defense fighter, Sama Ila Abu, said he counted at least 30 corpses as he helped to collect the dead.
7Boko Haram has kidnapped hundreds and hundreds of girls and women and the numbers of female suicide bombers has raised fears that it is using the captives in its campaign.
8A military bomb disposal expert has told the AP that most bombs carried by girls and women have remote detonation devices, meaning the carrier cannot control the explosion.
9Boko Haram has stepped up attacks since Nigeria's new President Muhammadu Buhari announced the military command center is moving from the capital Abuja to Maiduguri in Borno State.
10The attacks come as Nigeria and its neighbors are preparing to strengthen a multinational army that this year drove Boko Haram out of the towns and villages where it had set up a so-called Islamic caliphate.
11But bombings and hit-and-run attacks have continued, along with cross-border raids.
12On Thursday, a group of the extremists attacked two towns in neighboring Niger, killing at least 40 people, the government said.
13In its first attack on Chad, suicide bombers a week ago simultaneously attacked two buildings including the national police academy in N'Djamena, the Chadian capital, killing at least 33 people.
