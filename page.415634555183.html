NT residents warned to 'take shelter now' from Cyclone Nathan

1Tropical cyclone Nathan is lashing the Northern Territory and winds of up to 150km/h are hitting some areas.
2The cyclone, which has intensified to a "high end" category 2, crossed the coast between Nhulunbuy and Cape Shield on Sunday morning.
3It's west of Nhulunbuy, moving northwest at 14km/h over the Gove Peninsula towards Elcho Island, which was severely damaged by Cyclone Lam in late February.
4Nhulunbuy cafe owner Paul King, who is sheltering at his business with his family, says the power has been out for about two hours.
5"One tree has gone on someone's home, just a friend of ours - we've seen it on Facebook.
6"They're actually not at home, they're actually on holidays so they're lucky."
7Bureau of Meteorology senior meteorologist Adam Morgan said winds at Nhulunbuy reached up to 98km/h.
8Destructive winds with gusts up to 150km/h are being experienced over the Gove Peninsula.
9Elcho Island residents could see "low end destructive winds" when Nathan reaches them at about 5-6pm, Mr Morgan said.
10Coastal residents between Groote Eylandt and Nhulunbuy have been warned of a dangerous storm tide with damaging waves and dangerous flooding.
11They have been advised by emergency services to take shelter.
12Nathan is expected to head back out to sea on Sunday night before weakening as it moves westward parallel to the Top End north coast on Monday.
