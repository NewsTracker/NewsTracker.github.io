Boston police captain's son charged in Islamic State terror plot

1BOSTON -- The son of a Boston police captain has been arrested in an FBI sting and accused of plotting to commit terrorist acts in support of the Islamic State group, including the setting off of pressure-cooker bombs at an unidentified university and the slaughter of students live online.
2Alexander Ciccolo's own father alerted authorities last fall that the younger man had a long history of mental illness and was talking about joining the Islamic State, according to two law enforcement officials who spoke on condition of anonymity because they were not authorized to release details.
3Ciccolo, 23, of Adams, was charged in a criminal complaint unsealed Monday with illegal possession of a firearm for receiving four guns July 4 from a person co-operating with the Western Massachusetts Joint Terrorism Task Force.
4Because of a drunken driving conviction, Ciccolo was barred from having a gun.
5Ciccolo's father, Robert Ciccolo, is a 27-year veteran of the Boston police force.
6"While we were saddened and disappointed to learn or our son's intentions, we are grateful that authorities were able to prevent any loss of life or harm to others," Ciccolo's parents said in a statement.
7In court papers, the FBI said Ciccolo, also known as Abu Ali al Amriki, had talked with the co-operating witness in recorded conversations in June about his plans to commit acts inspired by the Islamic State.
8Ciccolo initially talked about killing civilians, police officers and members of the U.S. military but later said he wanted instead to attack a state university outside Massachusetts because more people would be there, according to the FBI.
9The FBI said the attack would include executions of students, broadcast live over the Internet.
10The day before his arrest, agents watched Ciccolo at Wal-Mart buying a pressure cooker similar to those used in the 2013 Boston Marathon bombings, the FBI said.
11Ciccolo first came to the attention of the terrorism task force on Sept.
1211 last year, when a close acquaintance -- identified by the two law enforcement officials as Ciccolo's father -- told the FBI that Ciccolo had expressed a desire to go overseas and fight for the Islamic State, according to court papers.
13The acquaintance told the FBI that Ciccolo had a long history of mental illness and in the last 18 months "had become obsessed with Islam."
14The person also told the FBI he had received texts from Ciccolo in which he said America was "Satan" and Americans were disgusting.
15The FBI said Ciccolo told the co-operating witness he planned to attack the university with assault rifles and explosives, focusing on dorms and the cafeteria during lunch because it would be packed with people.
16He also allegedly said that if a student was a Muslim, "he would be permitted to help, sit tight or leave."
17Ciccolo told the witness that the Boston Marathon bombing gave him the idea of using pressure-cooker bombs, the FBI said.
18The FBI said it found several partially constructed Molotov cocktails, two machetes and a long curved knife during a search of his apartment.
19After Ciccolo's arrest, he grabbed a pen during a jailhouse medical screening and stabbed a nurse in the head, according to the FBI.
20The FBI and Department of Homeland Security had warned recently of a heightened terror threat tied to the Fourth of July.
21FBI Director James Comey said last week that the FBI believes it stopped acts of violence in the month before the holiday with the arrests of more than 10 people, but he gave no details.
22Ciccolo is scheduled to appear in federal court in Springfield on Tuesday for a bail hearing.
