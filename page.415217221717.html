Jurors hear graphic accounts of Boston Marathon bombing

1This undated photo provided by Bill Richard shows his son, Martin Richard, in Boston.
2Martin Richard was one of three people killed in the bombing near the finish line of the race on April 15, 2013.
3The boy's younger sister, 6-year-old Jane, had a leg blown off, while their older brother, Henry, suffered minor injuries.
4Their father, testifying at Tsarnaev's federal death penalty trial, spoke in a slow, halting voice but remained largely composed as he described the chaos and confusion.
5He said he scooped Jane up in one arm and took Henry in the other and "tried to shield both of their eyes" from the carnage around them as he took them away.
6Richard took the stand as federal prosecutors continued trying to drive home the consequences of the attack in such heartbreaking detail that Tsarnaev's lawyers objected - and were overruled.
7Some of the women on the jury appeared to wince at times during his testimony.
8Spectators in the courtroom could be heard crying quietly, including Rebekah Gregory, who lost a leg in the bombing.
9As Richard testified, the jury watched a video of the father rushing to help his children and a grievously wounded Jane struggling to get up, only to fall down.
10A prosecutor showed Richard a photo and circled a face - a young man in a white baseball cap worn backward- who could be seen just a few feet behind Jane and Martin as the youngsters stood on a metal barricade, watching the race.
11It was Tsarnaev, shortly before the two pressure-cooker bombs went off.
12Richard said he himself suffered shrapnel injuries, burns on his legs and two perforated eardrums.
13His wife, Denise, was blinded in one eye and had other injuries.
14Earlier Thursday, Roseann Sdoia testified that she saw two flashes of white light at her feet near the finish line, looked down, and for a split second thought to herself: I'm wearing strappy sandals.
15She quickly realized she was looking at her foot dangling from her mangled leg.
16"Someone came running over to me and told me I had to get out of there. I told them I couldn't get up. I didn't have a leg," a sobbing Sdoia told the jury.
17She walked to the witness stand on an artificial leg, plainly visible below the hemline of her skirt.
18Sdoia, who was at the race as a spectator, said she saw wounded people all around her, including someone covered with soot, dazed and "walking around like a zombie."
19Prosecutors also showed the jury a grisly photo of her shredded leg.
20Tsarnaev's lawyer has admitted the former college student took part in the bombings.
21But in a bid to save Tsarnaev from a death sentence, she argued that he was influenced by his older brother, Tamerlan, who was killed in a getaway attempt days after the bombing.
22Also Thursday, Jeff Bauman - who lost both legs in the attack and was photographed being wheeled away that day in one of the most widely seen images of the tragedy - testified that he locked eyes with one of the bombers shortly before the twin blasts.
23"He was alone. He wasn't watching the race," said Bauman, who walked slowly into court on two prosthetic legs.
24"I looked at him, and he just kind of looked down at me. I just thought it was odd."
25Later, from his hospital bed, Bauman remembered the man's face clearly enough to give the FBI a description of someone authorities say turned out to be Tamerlan Tsarnaev.
26Before testimony began Thursday, Tsarnaev's lawyers complained to the judge that the survivors' testimony from the previous day went into too much detail about the effect on their lives.
27They said that kind of testimony should be reserved for the punishment phase of the trial.
28U.S. District Judge George O'Toole Jr. said the testimony did not go too far.
