Cars of OSCE observers vandalized in eastern Ukraine

1A demonstrator uses spray-paint to deface the windshield of a car of the OSCE and the International Committee of the Red Cross during a protest in Donetsk, eastern Ukraine, Thursday, July 23, 2015.
2Observers at the Organization for Security and Cooperation and Security in Europe say protesters in rebel-controlled Donetsk vandalized their cars on Thursday showed several dozen protesters outside the hotel where OSCE observers were staying.
3Some of them, young men wearing sunglasses, spray-painted the cars of the OSCE and the International Committee of the Red Cross in the parking lot.
4Observers at the Organization for Security and Cooperation and Security in Europe say protesters in rebel-controlled Donetsk have vandalized their cars.
5Russian television on Thursday showed several dozen protesters outside the hotel where OSCE observers were staying.
6Some of them, mostly young men wearing sunglasses, spray-painted the cars of the OSCE and the International Committee of the Red Cross in the parking lot.
7Alexander Hug, deputy head of the OSCE monitoring mission in Ukraine, said late Thursday 30 cars owned by the OSCE and other international organizations have been damaged with spray paint and are temporarily out of service, making travel difficult for inspectors.
