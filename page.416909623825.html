Kerry to meet with Putin this week for talks, first trip to Russia since Ukraine crisis

1WASHINGTON - Secretary of State John Kerry will travel to Russia this week for talks with Russian President Vladimir Putin, Kerry's first visit to Russia since the Ukraine crisis began, the State Department announced Monday.
2Relations between Moscow and the West have been badly damaged over Ukraine, with Western nations accusing Russia of supporting separatists there with arms and manpower - a charge Moscow denies.
3Russia, meanwhile, has bristled at Washington's pledge to provide Ukraine with military assistance in the form of hardware and training.
4The State Department said Kerry will meet Putin and Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov at the Black Sea resort of Sochi on Tuesday.
5It's Kerry's second trip to Russia since taking office.
6The announcement said Kerry will "discuss a full range of bilateral and regional issues, including Iran, Syria, and Ukraine."
7The trip is part of an ongoing U.S. effort "to maintain direct lines of communication with senior Russian officials and to ensure U.S. views are clearly conveyed," the State Department said.
8After his brief stop in Russia, Kerry will travel to Turkey for a meeting of NATO foreign ministers and return to Washington for a summit of Gulf Arab leaders that President Barack Obama is hosting at Camp David.
9Kerry last visited Russia in May 2013.
