Boston Marathon bombing trial enters 3rd week following testimony about injuries, deaths

1BOSTON - Three police officers on Monday described an onslaught of gunfire and bombs thrown during a violent confrontation with the Boston Marathon bombers days after the deadly attack and one suspect's furious escape in a stolen car that ended with him dragging his brother's body through the street.
2The testimony by Watertown officers came hours after jurors in the federal death penalty trial of Dzhokhar Tsarnaev went to see the bullet-ridden boat he was found hiding in the evening of April 19, 2013.
3Earlier that day, shortly after midnight, one officer spotted a carjacked Mercedes SUV on a quiet residential street.
4That set off a frenzied clash in which Dzhokhar and his older brother, Tamerlan, hurled explosives at police, including two pipe bombs and one pressure-cooker bomb similar to those used near the marathon finish line that killed 3 people and injured more than 260 others, the officers testified.
5Joseph Reynolds, the first officer at the scene, said he "locked eyes" with the SUV driver, who was later identified as Tamerlan Tsarnaev.
6Reynolds said Tamerlan got out of the car and began firing at him.
7The shots continued as other officers began to arrive, Reynolds said.
8He saw a wick and a lighter and then watched as an object flew through the air, landed in the street and exploded, he said, shaking him to his knees.
9Sgt. Jeffrey Pugliese said he cut through backyards to get closer to the bombers, and he eventually could see their feet illuminated by car headlights.
10He said he aimed at the ground near their feet in the hope that the bullets would ricochet into their ankles.
11But Tamerlan saw him and came charging up the street, firing at him, said Pugliese, who fired back.
12The two men came face to face, with only 6 to 8 feet separating them, Pugliese said.
13But Tamerlan had a problem with his pistol, the officer said.
14"He kind of looked at his gun. He looked at me. We looked at each other," Pugliese said.
15"I think out of frustration, he threw his gun at me."
16Pugliese said Tamerlan began to run away, but he tackled him.
17Tamerlan struggled as three officers tried to handcuff him, even though he was wounded and bleeding, Pugliese said.
18That's when they saw the stolen car speeding toward them with Dzhokhar behind the wheel, Pugliese said.
19The officer stepped out of the witness box to demonstrate how he tried to pull Tamerlan out of the way and how he rolled over to get himself out of the car's path.
20"The black SUV, it was right in my face," he said.
21"I looked down, and I saw the front wheels were over Tamerlan."
22Dzhokhar ran over his brother and dragged his body 25 to 30 feet, Pugliese said.
23He also struck a police car before he made his escape.
24Tamerlan Tsarnaev died of gunshot wounds and the injuries he received when being hit by the car.
25After a massive manhunt and door-to-door search, Dzhokhar was found hiding in a boat parked in a backyard in Watertown.
