Guardado lifts Mexico past angry Panama, 2-1, in Gold Cup

1Panama's Erick Davis (15) is restrained as tempers flare during the second half of a CONCACAF Gold Cup soccer semifinal between Panama and Mexico on Wednesday, July 22, 2015, in Atlanta.
2Mexico won 2-1 in extra time.
3ATLANTA (AP) - A night of conflict and controversy ended with referees surrounded by security and another win by Mexico surrounded by suspicion.
4Andres Guardado scored two goals on penalty kicks, including the tiebreaker in extra time, and six-time champion Mexico beat short-handed and angered Panama 2-1 in the Gold Cup semifinals Wednesday night.
5Turmoil on the field incited fans - among the 70,511 at the Georgia Dome - to hurl drinks and other debris onto the field on two occasions.
6Tensions rose after a foul was called on Panama's Roman Torres for touching the ball with his hand in the penalty area late in the second half when Panama was protecting a 1-0 lead.
7There was a delay as cups were thrown from the stands.
8Players from both teams argued on the sideline and appeared to be on the verge of an all-out melee before being separated.
9The call set up Guardado's first penalty kick in stoppage time.
10Panama coach Hernan Gomez called it a "stolen goal" and said he was left with "immense sadness."
11"I ask myself why did this happen?"
12Gomez said through a translator.
13"We were doing everything well. It is very sad.
14"We are people of football and I still can't believe this happened and I wonder if this really happened."
15Mexico will play Jamaica in the final Sunday in Philadelphia.
16Jamaica upset defending champion United States 2-1 in the first semifinal and will be appearing in its first final.
17"This is not the ideal situation," Mexico coach Miguel Herrera said.
18"It wasn't the ideal way to win."
19Panama's Roman Torres scored on a header early in the second half, and the 1-0 lead held until Guardado's penalty kick in stoppage time.
20The penalty kick, which followed a 10-minute delay as tempers flared, set up the extra time.
21Panama played at a disadvantage after forward Luis Tejada drew a red card and was ejected for his contact with Mexico's Francisco Rodriguez about 25 minutes into the match.
22Similar controversy surrounded Mexico's 1-0 win over Costa Rica in the quarterfinals Sunday night.
23The win came on Guardado's penalty kick in the final minute of extra time after Roy Miller was called for a questionable foul on Oribe Peralta.
24"We didn't produce football," Herrera said.
25"We had nothing to do with that."
26Panama was denied its second straight final.
27It lost to the U.S. in the 2013 final after beating Mexico in the semifinals.
28A win by Panama's win would have been an upset, but not on the level of Jamaica's shocker over the U.S. Mexico is No.
2940 in the world rankings and has appeared in seven of 12 finals in the CONCACAF tournament.
30Panama is 62nd.
31A header by Torres early in the second half broke the scoreless tie.
32Torres was in position to intercept a corner kick from Erick Davis in front of the goal, and his header to the ground bounced past goal keeper Guillermo Ochoa.
33Torres jumped in glee, his fists clinched, as Panama took its fifth straight 1-0 lead in the tournament.
34In each of its first four games, Panama took 1-0 leads only to allow the equalizer.
35This time, its defense held firm.
36In the 25th minute, Tejada jumped to attempt a header when he hit Rodriguez with his arm and hand, knocking the defender to the ground.
37Tejada protested and had to be restrained by his teammates when the red card was pulled.
38There was tension from both teams as Mexico players pushed Panama's players away from Rodriguez, who remained on the ground.
39Tejada was very slow to leave the field, stopping and circling back as fans hurled cups and plastic bottles in his direction.
40Tejada's teammate Adolfo Machado attempted to serve as an escort off the field but finally gave up when Tejada continued to linger.
41Finally, Tejada departed into the tunnel, leaving Panama with the difficult task of taking on Mexico with 10 players.
42Mexico's Miguel Layun cleared the debris from the field, picking up the cups and bottles, so play could resume.
