HBO will launch online streaming service in April for $15

Graphic novel 'Intergalactic Nemesis? invades the stage

1HBO will launch its standalone streaming service in April and it'll cost $15, according to the International Business Times.
2HBO is looking to launch HBO Now close to the premiere of season five of "Game of Thrones," but it wants to make sure that there are no issues upon launch.
3Last year, HBO Go users couldn't watch the premiere of George R.R. Martin's fantasy show because the servers were saturated.
4Apple will be behind the launch of HBO Now, according to the Times.
5By Apple securing its place as a launch partner, HBO Now could potentially be available on Apple TV through a standalone app.
6Apple TV started with a weak selection of channels, but over the last the two years it has been adding a lot.
7HBO Now would make the product more appealing.
8HBO Now's availability would be cross platform-similar to Hulu, Netflix and Amazon Prime.
9The service would be accessible from Roku, Amazon Fire Stick, Xbox One and PlayStation 4.
10There was no mention of the service being available of computers or mobile devices.
11It's still unclear as to whether HBO Now will offer everything HBO Go offers, which is all the live programming a cable user would originally get.
12Standalone subscription services have been increasing in popularity.
13NBC, CBS and Nickelodeon have all announced the release of a possible standalone online streaming services, ideal for cable cutters.
14Cable companies have also been launching their own online streaming services, without contract.
15Dish recently released Sling TV, which offers channels like ESPN, AMC, Food Network, TNT and CBS for $20 a month.

6Two years ago, Jason Neulander's "live-action graphic novel" "Intergalactic Nemesis" made its Bay Area debut, courtesy of Berkeley's Cal Performances.
7That installment, "Book One: Target Earth," introduced local audiences to the concept of three actors, a sound effects guy and a musician performing a 1940s-style radio show, while vividly colored panels of a comic book filled the screen above them.
8That Bay Area visit by this enterprise born in Austin, Texas, was successful, so book two is on its way.
9No word yet if "Book Three: Twin Infinity," which premiered last fall, will make a Bay Area stop - but Neulander, speaking from his Austin home, did have some thoughts on book two and his "Intergalactic" empire.
10Q: Do you need to have seen the first installment to enjoy "Robot Planet Rising"?
11A: Absolutely not.
12No previous "Intergalactic" experience necessary.
13The story stands alone.
14Book two is, like any good middle part in a trilogy, a little bit darker.
15It has more characters and is more complex.
16I've never been more satisfied with the final product of my storytelling than I have been with this.
17It's sophisticated and goofy and fun.
18It's kind of an edge-of-your-seat whodunit that leads to a supersonic adventure story.
19And it's hysterically funny.
20Q: Given that you're essentially doing a live radio show and a slide show, why do you think modern audiences, so used to high-tech entertainment, have embraced this form?
21A: I have no idea.
22I created the project to push my own personal entertainment buttons.
23I've always believed the best theater creates an experience in which the whole is greater than the sum of the parts, and I had that idea in mind when we were developing the idea of a live-action graphic novel.
24I love this kind of storytelling - being taken on a journey - and I guess other people feel similarly.
25Sometimes less is more.
26Q: You've completed the "Intergalactic" trilogy, and you're working on a crime novel, "Salt," a spin-off of book one.
27Any plans for another live-action graphic novel?
28A: Yes, though nothing "Intergalactic"-related.
29We're just in the beginning stages of looking to adapt a really outstanding, classic, public-domain adventure book.
30I can't say more, but one of the entities engaged in this discussion is Cal Performances.
