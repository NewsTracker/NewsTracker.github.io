US stocks open lower after Greek 'no' vote on bailout

1NEW YORK (AP) - U.S. stocks are opening lower after Greeks voted overwhelmingly to reject the terms of the country's latest bailout.
2Aetna sank 6 percent in early trading.
3The company agreed last week to buy rival health insurer Humana for $35 billion.
4The Dow Jones industrial average fell 126 points, or 0.7 percent, to 17,601 as of 9:35 a.m. Eastern time.
5The Standard & Poor's 500 index gave up 15 points, or 0.8 percent, to 2,061.
6The Nasdaq composite fell 38 points, also 0.8 percent, to 4,971.
7The price of oil sank 5 percent.
8The yield on the 10-year Treasury note fell to 2.29 percent.
