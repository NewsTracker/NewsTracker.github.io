The Latest: Baltimore Police Officers Face Hearing May 27

1The six Baltimore police officers charged in Freddie Gray's death are scheduled to appear in court at the end of the month.
2Lt. Brian Rice, Sgt. Alicia White, and officers Caesar Goodson, Garrett Miller, Edward Nero and William Porter have a preliminary hearing together on May 27 in Baltimore District Court, according to online court records.
3If the judge finds there is probable cause to support the charges, the case will move to circuit court to begin preparations for trial.
4Defendants can, and often do, waive preliminary hearings and the case is transferred to circuit court.
5Also, a prosecutor can seek an indictment from a grand jury, which would make a preliminary hearing unnecessary.
6Demonstrators hold a moment of silence at the intersection of Pennsylvania and North avenues in Baltimore for Freddie Gray, a 25-year-old black man who died after being seriously injured while in police custody last month.
7The moment of silence comes as Baltimore braces for a mass demonstration and rally in memory of Gray.
8Six police officers were charged Friday with felonies ranging from assault to murder in Gray's death.
9In announcing the charges, State's Attorney Marilyn Mosby said police had no reason to stop or chase Gray in the first place when they confronted him on April 12.
10He died of injuries on April 19.
11Heavily armed police and National Guard troops are making a show of force at key intersections and government buildings in Baltimore in preparation for Saturday's mass protest marches.
12Barricades have been erected to block vehicle traffic from the blocks around City Hall, the destination of the largest planned march.
13Armored military vehicles were parked at the checkpoints, and there were large numbers of police and troops donning body armor, many carrying assault rifles.
14The marches were called earlier this week to protest the death of Freddie Gray, a black man injured while in police custody April 12.
15But after the arrest of six Baltimore police officers in the case on Friday, the mood of Saturday's marches are expected to be more celebratory than tense.
16Police spokesman Sgt. Jarron Jackson says there were 38 protest-related arrests and 15 curfew-violation arrests Friday night.
17Demonstrators took to the streets Friday to celebrate the announcement that six police officers were being charged in the death of Freddie Gray, who died of a spinal injury received while in police custody earlier this month.
18A group of about 15 protesters marched through the downtown streets chanting "No justice, no peace" - to nobody in particular.
19Police vans and armored National Guard vehicles drove by the group, but took no action, despite the protesters' clear defiance of a 10 p.m. curfew.
