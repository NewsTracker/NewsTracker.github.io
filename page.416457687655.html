Calbuco volcano erupts anew; ash causing concern

1The Calbuco volcano erupts near Puerto Varas, Chile, Thursday, April 23, 2015.
2SANTIAGO, Chile (AP) - Twin blasts from the Calbuco volcano in southern Chile have sent vast clouds of ash into the sky, increasing concerns that it could contaminate water, cause respiratory illnesses and ground flights.
3The volcano erupted Wednesday afternoon for the first time in more than four decades, then had another outburst early Thursday.
4Authorities have evacuated 4,000 people and closed access to the area around the volcano, which lies near the cities of Puerto Varas and Puerto Montt, some 1,000 kilometers (620 miles) south of Santiago.
5LATAM airlines cancelled flights to and from both cities because airborne ash can damage jet engines.
