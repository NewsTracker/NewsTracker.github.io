Russia cuts off power supplies to Ukraine rebels: Kiev

1Russia had cut off electricity to Ukraine's rebel-run regions, Kiev said on Tuesday.
2KIEV: Kiev said on Tuesday that Russia had cut off electricity to Ukraine's rebel-run regions in what appears to be another sign that Moscow is losing interest in plans to splinter its neighbour.
3Russian President Vladimir Putin has gone through an extended spell without peppering his speeches with mentions of a "New Russia" (Novorossiya) made up of Ukrainian lands that were once under tsarist control.
4The veteran Kremlin leader had fervently promoted the project while the separatists were gaining ground along Ukraine's eastern industrial heartland toward the end of last year.
5But Ukrainian insurgents who claimed to be in charge of re-creating a part of the old Russian empire have in recent weeks complained of a sudden lack of interest from the Kremlin and a dire shortage of funds.
6Ukraine's Energy Minister Volodymyr Demchyshyn said Tuesday that energy-rich Russia had recently also stopped supplying electricity to the militia-run regions of Lugansk and Donetsk because bills were not being paid.
7"We held fairly productive negotiations with the Russians," Interfax-Ukraine quoted Demchyshyn as telling an energy ministry meeting.
8"We have been able to switch off four lines that ran from Russia to territories outside our control."
9The energy minister said the cables supplied an allowance of $15 million (14 million euros) of power a month.
10"That money was not being paid," Demchyshyn said.
11There was no immediate response to his comments from either Moscow or the rebels themselves.
12Their 15-month revolt against Kiev's new pro-Western position saw the rebels carve out a region about the size of Wales that is home to 3.5 million people and shares a 500-kilometre (300-mile) border with Russia.
13But much of the region remains a ghost of its proud image as the economic driving engine of the former Soviet Union's second-largest state.
14Most of the coal mines and steel mills have been either shuttered or cannot operate because of chronic shortages of electricity.
15Kiev policy hawks such as Ukrainian Prime Minister Arseniy Yatsenyuk have also pushed through a raft of measures designed to erect a punitive "economic blockade" of rebel-run territory.
16Yatsenyuk ordered Ukraine's power suppliers to halt all deliveries to the separatists on May 1.
17Natural gas flows from the rest of Ukraine to the east have also been significantly lowered.
18Russia had earlier promised to make up for the shortage by building extensions and branches to existing gas pipelines.
19It remains unclear where those plans stand on Tuesday.
20Demchyshyn's comments came ahead of a new round of negotiations Tuesday in the Belarussian capital Minsk aimed at ending a conflict that has claimed more than 6,500 lives.
21Efforts to stop the fighting seem locked in stalemate.
22The EU-mediated meeting will see Ukrainian President Petro Poroshenko's personal envoy speak to the Moscow-appointed negotiator in the presence of separatist representatives who are not formally part of the talks.
