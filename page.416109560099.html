Dutch hostage rescued from Islamic militants in Mali thanks French troops

1Sjaak Rijke also thanked the French special forces troops who rescued him on Monday in northern Mali, according to a written statement distributed by the Dutch foreign ministry.
2Rijke is now getting reacquainted with his partner, Tilly, at an undisclosed location.
3He said his years in captivity had been physically and mentally grueling, but that he had generally been treated well "apart from a few incidents."
4He did not elaborate.
5"Life in the desert is tough," he said.
6"Food is often scarce and therefore not varied. I don't want to see pasta again for a while."
7He said he lost a couple of kilograms (pounds) while in captivity but was in generally good physical shape thanks to working out when he could.
8It remains unclear who was holding Rijke, but the Dutchman appeared in a video posted in November by al-Qaida in the Islamic Maghreb.
9The statement made no mention of who his captors were or what demands, if any, they made.
10Rijke said his ordeal got tougher mentally the longer it dragged on.
11"I don't know how much longer I could have endured it," he said.
12"So I want to express my huge gratitude to the French elite troops who freed me."
13Rijke, who was abducted from a hostel in Timbuktu in November 2011, said his rescue early Monday "was a very hectic and scary moment, but I'm alive and I'm free!"
14His statement made no mention of Swede Johan Gustafsson and South African Stephen Malcolm who were abducted along with him.
15A German died in the attack.
