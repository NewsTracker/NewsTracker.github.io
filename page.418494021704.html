Vanuatu volcano puts on primal show for those daring to visit

1A man watches as Yasur volcano erupts on Tanna Island in Vanuatu on June 1, 2015 photo.
2TANNA ISLAND, Vanuatu -- It's constantly hissing, fussing and belching, but every half hour or so Yasur volcano sends up a tremendous spurt of lava and a boom so loud it is deafening on the crater rim and can be heard for miles.
3At dusk, the explosions begin to resemble fireworks, the lava glittering as it falls from the sky.
4At just 361 meters elevation, the volcano on the Pacific archipelago of Vanuatu is particularly accessible, and spectacularly active.
5Some have dubbed it the world's friendliest volcano, although its primal ferocity and occasionally dangerous eruptions of lava and gas would seem to defy that description.
6Getting there is an adventure.
7Yasur is on Vanuatu's southern Tanna island, which doesn't have paved roads and where many of its 30,000 people continue to live as the islanders have for centuries, in simple thatch huts with pigs and chickens running freely.
8Legend has it that the volcano is a god.
9Kelson Hosea, who owns the rustic Jungle Oasis bungalows near the base of Yasur, said the story goes that Yasur returned home one day to find his two wives and four sons still at the beach, and in anger turned himself into a pig, before falling asleep.
10"When the boys came back home with their mother, they saw the pig and they got angry," he said.
11"They got angry and they didn't realize that it was their father. They thought it was a pig. And they killed the pig with a stick. And the pig exploded."
12He said the four distinct craters in the volcano represent the four sons.
13From the Tanna airport, the main transport across the island's rugged dirt roads is by truck.
14To enter the volcano grounds, there is a 3,350 vatu ($31) fee.
15From the base, it's about a 90 minute hike to the crater rim.
16There's also an access road, and, for a fee, local drivers will take tourists up, leaving just a 15-minute walk across a rugged, barren moonscape to the rim.
17There's a lonely postal box near the top marked the "Volcano Post" where tourists can mail postcards.
18Authorities periodically close Yasur when the eruptions get too violent and it becomes too dangerous to stand near the crater rim.
19Yoram Teitler, a geologist who was recently a research fellow at the University of Western Australia, said the volcano has been active for perhaps 2,000 years.
20It is constantly bubbling, with larger eruptions coming once or twice an hour.
21Observers can stand about 150 metres from the crater floor.
22"It's quite rare to get such a kind of activity in a volcano," he said.
23"There are just a few examples in the world, and it's requiring the combination of very specific conditions. You need to have a specific viscosity of the lava, a specific amount of gases within the magma. And as well, a proper plumbing system to trigger such regular explosions."
24Teitler said that once people arrive on the rim, it's important to avoid the poisonous sulfurous gases coming from the craters and some side vents by taking a position upwind of them.
25He said that if the lava does explode beyond the rim, rather than running blindly away it's better to watch where the hunks are falling so you can avoid them.
26Vanuatu President Baldwin Lonsdale said the volcano has become a popular tourist attraction and a place where locals are able to make some money.
27"But people have to be very careful when they walk up the volcano," he said.
28"Because it's very dangerous there."
