Blatter rejects demands from UEFA for him to resign

1FIFA President Sepp Blatter says he intends to help restore trust in world soccer after a pair of corruption investigations brought "shame and humiliation" on the sport.
2Blatter spoke publicly for the first time since the corruption scandals broke Wednesday and threw FIFA into crisis ahead of Friday's presidential election.
3Blatter blamed the "actions of individuals" and said corruption "has to be stopped now" to prevent FIFA's reputation from being "dragged through the mud."
4Blatter rejected demands from European association UEFA that he resign.
5He is expected to win a fifth term in Friday's election against Prince Ali of Jordan.
6American and Swiss authorities are pursuing separate federal investigations into racketeering and the 2018 and 2022 World Cup votes.
7UEFA President Michel Platini says some European countries might pull out of FIFA and the World Cup if Sepp Blatter wins a fifth term as FIFA president.
8Speaking at a news conference in Zurich, Platini said through a translator that the European football body will be "open to all options" but insisted that he opposes a boycott of FIFA - even if longtime president Blatter beats Prince Ali of Jordan in Friday's election.
9Platini says he expects that a majority of European countries - at least 45 - will vote for the Jordanian royal in the 209-nation election after UEFA backed down from a threat to boycott the FIFA Congress.
10Platini said he told Blatter directly in a meeting on Thursday morning to stand down before the ballot - but the FIFA chief refused.
11Platini lamented "too many scandals" and said "FIFA doesn't deserve to be treated that way."
12With FIFA engulfed by a corruption scandal prompted by an investigation by U.S. authorities announced Wednesday, Platini said that Ali "doesn't need money because he is a prince."
