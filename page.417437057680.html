$100,000 check awaits mystery recycler of rare Apple 1 computer

1$100,000 check awaits mystery recycler of rare Apple 1 computer Add to...
2A $100,000 check is waiting for a mystery woman who donated a rare Apple 1 computer to a Silicon Valley recycling firm.
3CleanBayArea in Milpitas, California, is trying to track down a woman in her 60s who dropped off some electronic goods in April, when she was cleaning out the garage after her husband died.
4In one of the boxes, buried under worthless keyboards, personal computer pieces and wires, was a 1976 Apple 1, a groundbreaking home computer.
5Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak designed and hand-built the computers and sold them for $666.66 each.
6Only a few dozen are known still to exist.
7The recycling firm sold the Apple for $200,000 in a private auction.
8Its policy is to split the proceeds 50-50 with the person who donated the equipment.
9"The body was made out of wood. I've never seen anything like that. My first reaction was it was a fake. Then we started looking at it," said Victor Gichun, vice president of marketing for CleanBayArea.
10Gichun declined to say who bought the Apple 1, only that it was a private collector.
11He's not sure whether the Apple is still operational.
12He said he will recognize the woman, who he believes is local, when he sees her and will write her out a check for $100,000.
13The boxes sat in the company's warehouse on a pallet for a couple of weeks because they didn't expect to find anything valuable, Gichun said.
