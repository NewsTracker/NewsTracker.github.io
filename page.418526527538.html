Klinsmann shakes up US roster for Gold Cup knockout rounds (Yahoo Sports)

1(AP) -- Jurgen Klinsmann spouted optimism after the United States played Panama to a 1-1 draw to wrap up the group stage of the Gold Cup, reasoning that despite a trio of sluggish performances the Americans had still survived unbeaten.
2That didn't stop Klinsmann from shaking up his roster less than 24 hours later.
3The coach chose to add DaMarcus Beasley, Joe Corona and Alan Gordon for the knockout rounds, which begin with the quarterfinals Saturday in Baltimore.
4Taking a seat are Greg Garza, Alfredo Morales and Jozy Altidore, who has been hampered by an ailing hamstring.
5Teams could make up to six roster changes using their 35-player preliminary list.
6Altidore started the first two games in the group stage but was substituted out of both.
7He was left on the bench against Panama on Monday night.
8''We believe that Jozy's just not there yet.
9Jozy never really got into this tournament and never really picked up the rhythm,'' Klinsmann said in announcing the changes Tuesday.
10Klinsmann said he knew that Altidore might not be match fit when he selected him for the Gold Cup, and that's why Gordon was on standby from the beginning.
11The Los Angeles Galaxy striker will make his 2015 debut for the national team after playing in the Gold Cup two years ago.
12Klinsmann said that the addition of the 33-year-old Beasley will provide leadership and experience.
