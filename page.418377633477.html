Baltimore mayor fires police commissioner amid spike in homicides

1In this April 30, 2015, file photo, Baltimore Police Department Commissioner Anthony Batts listens as Deputy Commissioner Kevin Davis speaks at a news conference, in Baltimore.
2Baltimore Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake announced the firing of Batts in a news release on Wednesday, July 8.
3Baltimore Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake fired the troubled city's police commissioner Wednesday, saying that a recent spike in homicide rates weeks after a black man died of injuries in police custody required a change in leadership.
4Rawlings-Blake thanked Police Commissioner Anthony Batts for his service and announced that she was appointing Deputy Police Commissioner Kevin Davis as interim commissioner.
5"We need a change," Rawlings-Blake told a news conference.
6"This was not an easy decision but it is one that is in the best interest of Baltimore. The people of Baltimore deserve better and we're going to get better."
7The firing comes 2 1/2 months after the city broke out into riots following the death of Freddie Gray, who died in April of injuries he received in police custody.
8Six police officers have been criminally charged in Gray's death.
9After the violence, arrests in the city plummeted and the city's homicide rate spiked.
10The most recent violence happened Tuesday night, when gunmen jumped out of two vans and fired at a group of people a few blocks from an urban university campus, killing three people.
11A fourth person sought treatment for a gunshot wound to the buttocks and was in stable condition.
12Police said Wednesday that the shooting wasn't random, but no arrests have been made.
13On Tuesday, the police department announced that an outside organization would review the department's response to the civil unrest that followed Gray's death.
14Most of the unrest took place on April 27, prompted by Gray's death on April 19.
15In the meantime, the U.S. Justice Department is conducting a civil rights review of the department.
16The Baltimore police union has criticized Batts for his handling of the unrest.
17Rawlings-Blake appointed Batts as police commissioner in September 2012.
18He has had more than three decades of experience in law enforcement, according to his biography on the Baltimore Police Department website, including serving as chief of police in Long Beach, California, and Oakland, California.
19He started his career in 1982 as an officer with the Long Beach department, working his way up to chief over a 20-year span.
20He also served as city manager of Long Beach for four months and taught at California State University, Long Beach.
21He was chief in Oakland for two years, from 2009 to 2011.
22Batts earned a doctorate in public administration from the University of La Verne, a master's in business management from the University of Redlands, and a bachelor's in law enforcement administration from California State University, Long Beach.
