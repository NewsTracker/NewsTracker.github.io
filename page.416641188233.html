Microsoft thinks it can guess your age

1Upload a selfie or type your name into the search bar at how-old.net to do Bing image search, and Microsoft's "How Old Do I Look?" tool takes a stab at guessing your age and gender.
2Microsoft (MSFT, Tech30) is playing a dangerous game.
3Guess too high and people are offended.
4Guess too low and they think the tool doesn't work.
5Guess right on the nose and they'll probably wish it had low-balled by a few years.
6The company announced the site at its Microsoft Build developer's conference in San Francisco on Thursday.
7It's meant to show off the power of Microsoft's face detection API tools and its Azure computing platform for developers.
8Part of Microsoft's Project Oxford, the Face Detection engine can also detect the same person in multiple photos.
9(You can test its face-matching tool here.)
10Third-party developers can use these features in their own apps, say for photo editing or sorting through a library of images.
11When Microsoft originally sent an email asking people to test the tool, it didn't predict that it would go viral.
12But vanity is a funny thing, and in a few hours, more than 35,000 people had used the site.
13After its shout-out onstage at the conference Thursday, it quickly spread on social media where many people (including CNNMoney reporters) were shocked to find that Microsoft overestimated their age, sometimes by several decades.
