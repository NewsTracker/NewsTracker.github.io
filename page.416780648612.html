Engineers inspecting quake-damaged homes in Nepal's capital

1KATHMANDU, Nepal (AP) - More than a thousand engineers are checking damaged houses in Nepal's capital and advising people about whether they are safe.
2Nepal Engineers Association General Secretary Kishore Kumar Jha said Wednesday that about 13,000 families have asked his organization to inspect their homes after the massive magnitude-7.8 earthquake near Kathmandu on April 25.
3It is still unclear how many houses were damaged in the capital and how many are repairable.
4Jha said about 40 percent of the damaged houses they have inspected so far were considered safe.
5Police say about one-third of Kathmandu's population has left the city since the earthquake.
6Many others have moved in with relatives, and some are staying in tents in open areas.
