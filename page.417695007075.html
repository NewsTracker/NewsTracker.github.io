Jim Morrison poem found in Paris hotel room being auctioned

1Written on the last page torn out of a thick notebook, the double-sided document is the highlight of an online auction that went live Thursday.
2Auctioneer Paddle8 said bidding through June 25 is expected to reach between $60,000 and $80,000.
3The lead singer and songwriter of the 1960s rock group the Doors died at age 27.
4The exact cause of death was never determined and speculation has abounded.
5Whether the poem's words were a foretelling of what was to come is anyone's guess.
6The flamboyant rocker famous for such iconic tunes as "Light My Fire," ''L.A. Woman" and "Riders on the Storm" was known for his darkly poetic lyrics and alcohol and drug use.
7"He was kind of obsessed" by the theme of death from the very first album, said Simeon Lipman, a specialist who works on Paddle8's "Legendary" series of music memorabilia auctions.
8The notebook was originally intact and then broken apart and sold in pieces over several years, he said.
9The page being sold is numbered but not dated.
10The "Last Words" poem, which also contains the line "I have drunk the drug of forgetfulness," appears on page 152.
11"What stands out is the fact that the one on page 152 was indeed the last page of the notebook," said Lipman.
12"His writings can be taken so many different ways. Personally I like to visualize what he's saying. ... That's what makes his songs and poems so relevant today because people can envision them," he added.
13The notebook the page came from was found inside Morrison's "Fascination Trunk," which contained other personal belongings including books and other notebooks.
14Morrison moved to Paris in 1971 to take a break from performing and to focus on his writing.
15He died a few months later.
16French police ruled out foul play and an autopsy was never performed.
17Morrison's grave in Paris attracts tourists from around the world.
18"He is the prototype of that kind of brooding, brilliant rock star," said Lipman.
19"Because he died young, he's timeless like James Dean and Marilyn Monroe."
20What makes the document special is that it "combines a lot of different threads," he said.
21It's written in Morrison's own hand, deals with the themes of death and snakes, and was found in the hotel where he died.
22Paddle8 is a four-year-old company that targets new and veteran collectors with items of contemporary art, design and memorabilia estimated at $100,000 and under.
