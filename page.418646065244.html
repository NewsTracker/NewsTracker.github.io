Iraq restructures Diyala police after Islamic State bombing

1Civilians inspect the aftermath of a suicide car bombing at a busy market in Khan Bani Saad in the Diyala province, about 20 miles (30 kilometers) northeast of Baghdad, Iraq, Saturday, July 18, 2015.
2A suicide car bombing in Iraq's eastern Diyala province killed at least 80 people gathered at a marketplace to mark the end of the holy month of Ramadan on Friday.
3BAGHDAD (AP) - Two police officials in Iraq's eastern Diyala province say authorities are restructuring a town's police department after an Islamic State-claimed truck bombing there killed 115 people.
4The officials say the police chief in Khan Beni Saad and three officers have been fired in the wake of the attack on a crowded market as residents began to mark the end of the Muslim holy month of Ramadan.
5They say two other officers are being investigated.
6The officials spoke on condition of anonymity as they weren't authorized to talk to journalists.
7At least 115 people, including women and children, were killed in Friday night's attack in the mostly Shiite town and more than 170 were wounded.
8It was one of the deadliest attacks to strike Iraq since U.S. troops left in 2011.
