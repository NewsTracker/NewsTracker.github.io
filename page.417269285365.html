Islamic State probe: IT chief at Bangladesh Coca-Cola unit arrested as suspect

1Islamic State's influence is extending into south Asia.
2Dhaka: An information technology manager at a subsidiary of Coca-Cola Co was one of two men arrested in Bangladesh on suspicion of planning to fight for Islamic State in Syria, police and company sources said on Monday.
3The pair were detained during a raid in the capital Dhaka on Sunday night, Sheikh Nazmul Alam, a senior official of the police detective branch, said.
4One man, Aminul Islam, was the IT head of a multinational company and worked as a regional co-ordinator for IS, while the other, Sakib Bin Kamal, was a teacher at a school in Dhaka, he said.
5A police official and a company source said Mr Islam worked at International Beverages Private Ltd, a Coca-Cola unit.
6The company source, who requested anonymity because of the sensitivity of the case, confirmed the arrested man was the head of IT, and said he had been absent from work for several days.
7In a statement, the subsidiary said it was aware of media reports that the employee, whom it named as Aminul Islam Baig, had been arrested.
8"We will fully co-operate with the law enforcement agencies as required," it said.
9Mr Alam said the suspects had admitted persuading at least 25 students to join IS, the militant group that has captured large parts of Syria and Iraq.
10At least 12 people have been arrested in Bangladesh in recent months for suspected involvement with IS, and reports of its growing influence have raised fears across south Asia.
11Bangladesh, whose population is about 90 per cent Muslim, is already on alert after three secular bloggers, including a US citizen, Avijit Roy, were killed by radical Islamists since February.
12In a separate development on Monday, the interior ministry banned Ansarullah Bangla Team, a group that has claimed responsibility for the killings, as an extremist militant organisation.
