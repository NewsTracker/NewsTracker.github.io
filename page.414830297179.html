Greece planning to seek loan agreement extension

1ATHENS, Greece (AP) " Greece is considering requesting an extension to its loan agreement, government officials said Tuesday, in an effort to reach a last-minute deal with the country's European creditors and avoid the danger of a euro exit.
2The country would submit the request before Friday, said the officials, who spoke on condition of anonymity pending the formal announcement.
3They stressed the request would not be for an extension of Greece's bailout agreement, which includes strict cost-cutting measures as conditions for rescue loans.
4The move follows an ultimatum issued by eurozone finance ministers after their meeting to find a solution for Greece collapsed Monday night.
5They have said Greece must ask for an extension to its current bailout by the end of the week in order to reconvene a meeting.
6It was unclear whether the Greek request would meet the requirements of the country's creditors, but the news was greeted with some market optimism.
7U.S. stocks moved slightly higher, and the euro also gained some lost ground against the dollar.
8"Greece's new government wants more independence, but it doesn't want that independence at all costs," said Anastasia Amoroso, a global market strategist at JPMorgan Funds.
9"The extension is good news because it buys Greece and the eurozone time to reach a long-term resolution."
10Greece's new left-wing government won elections last month on a promise to renegotiate the country's bailout agreement.
11That deal provided 240 billion euros in rescue loans from other EU countries and the International Monetary Fund, but came on the condition that Athens imposed draconian austerity measures in return.
12Time is running out for the two sides to reach an agreement.
13The European part of Greece's bailout program expires on Feb. 28, after which the country will struggle to service its debts.
14Earlier Tuesday, Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras sounded a defiant note, saying Greece will not compromise in talks with its European creditors, but that it is working for an "honest and mutually beneficial agreement."
15The failure in Monday's meeting of eurozone finance ministers has increased fears Greece may be forced out of the joint euro currency, although investors seem to believe the two sides will eventually reach an agreement.
16Athens insists it cannot ask for the continuation of a program it considers wrong.
17Instead, it has asked for a "bridging agreement" that would ensure it doesn't face any cash liquidity problems while it negotiates a new deal.
18"Salvation will not come by extending the mistake," Tsipras said during a speech to his party lawmakers in parliament.
19Anything else, the prime minister said, "is not an agreement but a surrender that would complete the euthanasia of our country."
20Germany, the largest single contributor to the Greek bailout, has been most vocal in insisting Greece stick to its commitments.
21German Finance Minister Wolfgang Schaeuble, speaking in Brussels, said it was unclear what Greece wanted.
22"A lot of colleagues were asking: 'What do they really want? Do they have a plan?'" Schaeuble said, adding: "I don't know."
23Investors are not panicking despite the breakdown in talks.
24European stocks were steady, while the Athens index fell 2.5 percent.
25Greece's government borrowing rates rose, however, a sign investors are more wary of a potential bankruptcy.
