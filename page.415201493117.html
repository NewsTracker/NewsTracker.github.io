4BRUSSELS (AP) - German Chancellor Angela Merkel is warning Russia to live up to the Ukraine peace agreement it backed in Minsk last month or face tougher European Union sanctions.
5Merkel told reporters on Wednesday that the immediate task at hand was to stop the bloodletting in eastern Ukraine, where a fragile cease-fire is largely holding despite some violations since the Feb. 12 Minsk agreement.
6Merkel said that "if Minsk doesn't work, then the member states and the European Commission are quite prepared to take tougher sanctions."
7European Commission President Jean-Claude Juncker ruled out any plans to hold an EU-Russia summit in the near future.
1TOKYO (AP) - The world's oldest person says 117 years doesn't seem like such a long time.
2Misao Okawa, the daughter of a kimono maker, made the comment Wednesday, at a celebration a day before her 117th birthday.
3Appropriately, she was wearing a pink kimono decorated with cherry blossom prints.
4Okawa, born in Osaka on March 5, 1898, was recognized as the world's oldest person by Guinness World Records in 2013.
5"It seemed rather short," she said after Osaka government official Takehiro Ogura, who brought her a big bouquet, asked how she felt about living for 117 years.
6Okawa, her hair decorated with a pink daisy pin, looked up from her wheelchair and said she was "very happy" to be that age.
7Japan has the most centenarians in the world, with more than 58,000, according to the government.
8About 87 percent of them are women.
9Okawa has slowed in recent months and has trouble hearing but she eats well and is in good health, according to her Osaka nursing home, where Wednesday's televised celebration was held.
10Okawa married her husband, Yukio, in 1919, and they had three children - two daughters and a son.
11She now has four grandchildren and six great-grandchildren.
12Her husband died in 1931.
