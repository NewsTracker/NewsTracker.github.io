Mexico reports bird flu outbreaks in Puebla, Oaxaca states

1Following a notification by the homeowners of an increase in the sudden death of birds, 139 birds were found infected by the virus, the Mexican agriculture ministry said in a report posted on the website of the Paris-based organisation.
2"The official veterinary services launched the necessary epidemiological investigation and the presence of highly pathogenic avian influenza virus subtype H7N3 was confirmed," it said.
3Both outbreaks were confirmed as positive for avian influenza on March 9.
4The report said that so far, no epidemiological link had been identified between them.
5Mexico's agriculture ministry said in a statement on Thursday that the reported cases were immediately controlled and that animal health authorities had not found any other infected birds.
6No traces of the virus were found within a 10 kilometer radius of the area, the ministry said.
7The ministry also said poultry companies in the areas had not been affected.
