Steinmeier urges diplomatic solution for Ukraine at Visegrad meeting

1Frank-Walter Steinmeier met with four of his central European counterparts in Slovakia's capital Bratislava.
2The parties involved in the Ukraine conflict must avoid any steps which could lead to a new escalation of tensions, German Foreign Minster Frank-Walter Steinmeier said Monday, at a meeting of foreign ministers of the four : Poland, the Czech Republic, Hungary and Slovakia.
3Steinmeier urged for the continuation of the Minsk peace process.
4"One thing is clear I think to all of us: there is no, and there cannot be, a military solution to the crisis in Ukraine," Steinmeier told reporters.
5Slovakian foreign minister Miroslav Lajcak expressed the support of the central European countries for Germany in its role as a key mediator in Ukraine.
6"We all very much appreciate Germany's leadership in this matter," he said.
7According to news agency Reuters, Steinmeier also said senior officials from Germany, Ukraine and Russia would meet in Paris on Wednesday to discuss oversight mechanisms for the ceasefire in eastern Ukraine.
8German Foreign Minister Frank-Walter Steinmeier met for talks with his counterpart in Washington on a three-day visit.
9Both sides announced more aid for Ukraine.
10Though the two countries agree on a diplomatic solution, they differ on just what kind of support should be given.
11A Romanian foreign ministry spokeswoman has lost her job over a gaffe in which the German foreign minister was given a brochure that featured a map of France, not Germany, by mistake.
12German Foreign Minister Frank-Walter Steinmeier has begun a three-day visit to the United States.
13He met first with his American counterpart, John Kerry, to discuss the Ukraine crisis and ongoing nuclear talks with Iran.
