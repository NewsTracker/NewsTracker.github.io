Some women pictured nude or seminude on US fraternity's Facebook page are talking to police

1STATE COLLEGE, Pennsylvania - Some women pictured nude or seminude in photos posted to a Penn State fraternity's secret Facebook page have come forward, and some members of the male student social group are now cooperating with an ongoing criminal investigation, police said.
2State College police Lt. Keith Robb said some of the women were able to identify themselves in screenshots taken from the site, and Penn State's Office of Student Conduct has helped students come forward in a way that makes them feel safe and comfortable, the Centre Daily Times reported late Wednesday.
3The Kappa Delta Rho fraternity has been suspended for a year and is being ordered by its national office to reorganize.
4Penn State President Eric Barron said the university has also begun a wide review of the role fraternities and sororities play at the school.
5The police investigation became public last week after information about a search warrant was reported.
6The warrant targeted an invitation-only Facebook page run by the fraternity members that showed pictures of women, some apparently passed out or sleeping, nude or partially dressed, and screenshots of text messages discussing sex with the women, among other things.
7Police have said anyone who posted such pictures might be subject to criminal charges, including invasion of privacy.
8The investigation has prompted demonstrations on campus and outside the fraternity house.
9On Wednesday, protesters marched to the house and called for the school to shut down the fraternity permanently.
