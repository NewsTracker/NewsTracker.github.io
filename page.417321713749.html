6By the end of the year, nearly every major automaker will begin offering Apple's CarPlay or Google's Android Auto, systems that effectively turn a car's dashboard screen into a smartphone.
7General Motors made the biggest move so far Wednesday, announcing that both systems will be available in seven 2016 Chevrolet models starting in the summer.
8The Apple system will appear in seven additional Chevy models.
9Hyundai said this week that the Android system will be in the Sonata midsize car.
10Experts say the move to systems devised in Silicon Valley is an admission by the automakers that people favor the way their smartphones work over the automakers' touch-screens and voice commands, which have been prone to glitches.
11People are familiar with smartphones and have music libraries, podcasts, social media contacts and other personal items on them, said Tim Bajarin, president of Creative Strategies, a San Jose technology research firm.
12Automakers, he said, have realized that most drivers can't be bothered learning a whole new system.
13"We just want familiar. We want our content, our services that we already own on our phone. We just want the car to have the representation of that on demand," Bajarin said.
14To get the systems, GM customers will have to purchase a Chevrolet equipped with the brand's MyLink touch-screens, which are available on many entry-level models.
15Once people plug into the car's USB port, the system will convert the screen to resemble the phone.
16The system will then be able to play a person's music library, log on to music apps with a touch, send and receive text messages by voice, and even call up Apple or Google Maps for navigation.
17The number of available apps will be limited to avoid driver distraction, GM officials said.
18Many can be controlled by voice commands, and video won't be supported by the car system.
19To work with MyLink, Android phones must have at least the Lollipop 5.0 operating system, while Apple CarPlay requires an iPhone 5 or newer model.
20GM will provide CarPlay or Android Car for no additional cost.
21Although this might cause some drivers to shun MyLink, GM believes that ultimately giving buyers the choice will help it sell more cars, said Saejin Park, the company's director of innovation and portfolio planning.
22Mark Boyadjis, senior analyst for infotainment at IHS Automotive, said the decision doesn't necessarily mean the death of MyLink, Ford's Sync and other systems.
23The automakers' systems have specific information about the car that Apple or Google can't duplicate - engine diagnostics, heating and air conditioning controls or even the ability to set up service appointments with dealerships, Boyadjis said.
24GM officials also noted that some car owners might drive in areas without the cellular telephone coverage needed to run the Apple or Google systems.
25And others prefer not to plug anything in to their cars.
26Initially both systems will be offered in the 2016 Spark mini car, Cruze compact, Malibu midisze car, Camaro and Camaro convertible and the Silverado pickup, both regular and heavy-duty models.
27If something goes wrong with the system under warranty, Apple and Google would be responsible if the problem originates with their software.
28GM would handle any problems on its end such as the MyLink hardware, GM said.
29Apple and Android combined control about 95 percent of the world's smartphone market, so having both systems is necessary.
30International Data Corp. is forecasting Android will have a 79 percent share of the smartphone market this year with Apple (iOS) a distant second at 16 percent.
