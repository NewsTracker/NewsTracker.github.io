'Suge' Knight taken to hospital after ruling in murder case

1Knight's condition and the cause of his latest health problem were not immediately known, but it was the fifth time the Death Row Records co-founder had been taken from a courthouse to a hospital during proceedings in the case.
2The latest trip came after Superior Court Judge Ronald Coen rejected Knight's argument that he acted in self-defense when he was viciously attacked by men outside a Compton burger stand.
3FILE - In this March 9, 2015 file photo, Marion "Suge" Knight, center, arrives in court for a hearing about evidence in his murder case, in Los Angeles.
4Knight will stand trial on murder and attempted-murder charges after the former rap music mogul struck two men with his pickup truck in January, killing one and seriously injuring the other.
5Superior Court Judge Ronald Coen made the ruling Thursday after concluding a hearing that focused heavily on testimony from Cle "Bone" Sloan, who was hit outside a Compton burger stand.
6The judge also reduced bail from $25 million to $10 million.
7Authorities contend Knight intentionally hit the men, killing Terry Carter, 55, and injuring Cle "Bone" Sloan.
8Knight's attorney Matt Fletcher argued that his client was ambushed and was trying to escape from Sloan when Knight ran down the men in the attack captured on security video.
9"There's no intent to kill," Fletcher said in court while asking the judge to dismiss the case.
10"There's an intent to survive."
11Coen, however, said it appeared Knight, 49, wasn't attempting to flee but instead trying to kill someone when he accelerated forward in the truck.
12The judge did throw out one of two hit-and-run counts, saying the charge applies to one event even if there are multiple victims.
13He also reduced bail from $25 million to $10 million.
14Knight appeared to be OK as he looked at members of his family in the gallery and shuffled out of the courtroom in ankle shackles and handcuffs.
15He was later taken to the hospital.
16Knight has diabetes and a blood clot near his lung, his lawyer previously said.
17Before word of his hospitalization was public, his fiancee, Toi Kelly, said outside court that Knight's health had been improving.
18Fletcher said Thursday evening that he did not have any information about his client's health.
19The ruling came at the end of a hearing that focused heavily on testimony from Sloan, a former gang member who told detectives he attacked Knight before getting run over.
20Sloan said he didn't remember the fight and did not want to be a snitch.
21Prosecutors played a statement by Sloan to police that offered a lucid, detailed account of the events on Jan. 29 that led to the deadly encounter.
22Sloan's testimony demonstrated the difficulty in prosecuting Knight, who has gang ties and a reputation for intimidating witnesses.
23"I will not be used to send Suge Knight to prison," Sloan testified, adding that he was on the witness stand only because he was subpoenaed.
24He has known Knight for decades.
25Prosecutors granted Sloan immunity from prosecution after he said he intended to invoke his Fifth Amendment right to avoid self-incrimination.
26Knight was a key player in the gangster rap scene that flourished in the 1990s, and his label once listed Dr. Dre, Tupac Shakur and Snoop Dogg among its artists.
27Knight lost control of the company after it was forced into bankruptcy.
28Sloan, an adviser on the upcoming film "Straight Outta Compton" about rap music, testified that he was trying not to think about the crash in which he suffered two fractured ankles, a serious cut to his head, two torn knee ligaments and a shoulder injury.
29"Every day, I try to forget it," Sloan said.
30"I just know, I screwed up, and Terry's dead."
31The judge also heard from the lead detective in the case and watched the security footage of the crash.
32The camera caught a limited view of the parking lot, but it shows Knight struggling with Sloan through the window of the truck.
33Knight then put the vehicle in reverse, striking Sloan twice before running over Carter while fleeing the scene.
34Deputy District Attorney Cynthia Barnes said Knight gave up his right to claim self-defense when he used his car as a deadly weapon in response to a fistfight.
35Knight could face life in prison if convicted in the case.
36He has prior felony convictions for armed robbery and assault with a gun.
37Knight pleaded no contest in a 1995 assault on two rap entertainers and was sentenced to five years' probation.
38He was sentenced to prison in February 1997 for violating terms of the probation by taking part in a fight at a Las Vegas hotel hours before Shakur was fatally wounded in a drive-by attack as he rode in Knight's car.
39Shakur's slaying remains unsolved.
