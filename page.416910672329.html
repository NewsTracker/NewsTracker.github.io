Panel questions why WHO response slow to Ebola

1DAKAR, Senegal (AP) - An independent panel of experts studying what went wrong in the World Health Organization's response to the Ebola crisis says it's still not clear why it took so long for the body to take action.
2The Ebola epidemic that has now killed more than 11,000 people first became known in March 2014.
3In the panel's first report issued Monday, it said it was still unclear why the early warnings "did not result in an effective and adequate response."
4In particular the experts say that the World Health Organization needs "deep and substantial organizational change."
5Ebola continues to spread in the West African nations of Guinea and Sierra Leone, while Liberia was declared Ebola-free on Saturday.
