TV man to experiment on his blood

More Russian tanks, equipment cross Ukraine border: U.S. official

1Dr Mosley, who in a previous show on parasites deliberately infested himself with tapeworms, is taking an in-depth look at blood in a new BBC4 programme.
2He will carry out six "bold" experiments on his own blood, including injecting it with snake venom to see how it can clot within seconds and starving it of oxygen.
3The Wonderful World Of Blood, featuring specialist photography to "look at blood as never seen before", is one of several new BBC science shows announced today.
4Other programmes include a year-long examination of a single oak tree and a look at what happens to life in the womb.
5In Oak Tree on BBC4, viewers will see explorer and TV presenter Dr George McGavin inspect a 350-year-old oak tree in Wytham Woods, near Oxford, in "meticulous and forensic detail" over a whole year.
6The film aims to give viewers a "new appreciation of and insight into Britain's best loved tree".
7Dr Mosley also presents Nine Months That Made You on BBC2, a look at the "minute-by-minute, hour-by-hour" development of a baby in the first 280 days of life from a single cell.
8The BBC screened a Horizon documentary, The Nine Months That Made You, in 2011, which looked at how much of a human being's health has been determined by the time we get out of the womb.
9Other programmes include Natural Born Hustlers presented by Chris Packham, on how animals deploy optical illusions, camouflage and mimicry to stay alive.
10The documentary will feature "cross-dressing birds, a lizard which fakes its own death and two-timing monkeys".
1The comments by U.S. Assistant Secretary of State for Europe, Victoria Nuland, follow accusations by Ukraine and Western governments that Russia continues to send troops and weapons to support separatists in eastern Ukraine despite the Minsk ceasefire deal, a charge the Kremlin has denied.
2Eastern Ukraine and Crimea, the Black Sea peninsula annexed by Russia a year ago, was "suffering a reign of terror," Nuland told a congressional hearing on Ukraine, adding that the White House was still considering whether to arm Ukraine's military.
3The last few days have seen new transfers of Russian tanks, armored vehicles, heavy artillery and rocket equipment over the border to the separatists, Nuland told the Senate Foreign Relations Committee.
4She said "hundreds and hundreds" of Russian troops had been killed during the conflict in eastern Ukraine.
5"In the coming days, days not weeks, we need to see a complete ceasefire," Nuland said.
6"Pressure is going to have to increase if Minsk is not implemented."
7She said there was a "spirited" debate within the administration on whether to send arms to Ukraine.
8Brian McKeon, the U.S. principal deputy under secretary of defense, told lawmakers 11 Russian battalion tactical teams were in Russia's Rostov region across the Ukrainian border.
9The United States pledged $118 million in non-lethal assistance to Ukraine but has delivered only about half of that, he said.
10"It's a case of finding it in the stocks of the United States military, and in the case of some equipment we're purchasing it off the production line."
11The Senate and House of Representatives unanimously passed legislation in December that authorized sending arms to Kiev.
12Obama signed the measure into law but it gave him leeway over whether or when to send the arms.
13In a letter to U.S. President Barack Obama on Monday, senators Bob Corker, a Republican from Tennessee and Robert Menendez, a Democrat from New Jersey, called on Obama to immediately submit a report to Congress on plans for providing arms to Ukraine.
14The lawmakers said the report, required under the Ukraine Freedom Support Act, had been due on Feb. 15.
15It was unclear when the report would be submitted.
