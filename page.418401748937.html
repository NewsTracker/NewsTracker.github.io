U.K. tells tourists to leave Tunisia, says attack 'highly likely'

1The British government on Thursday told all U.K. tourists to leave Tunisia because a terrorist attack is "highly likely."
2U.K. tells tourists to leave Tunisia, says attack 'highly likely' The British government on Thursday told all U.K. tourists to leave Tunisia because a terrorist attack is "highly likely."
3British Foreign Secretary Philip Hammond warned thousands of British tourists in Tunisia on Thursday that a terrorist attack there is 'highly likely" and urged them to return home immediately.
4The official travel advisory was later updated to advise against all but essential travel to Tunisia "for the time being."
5An attack last month on the Tunisian beach resort of Sousse by a suspected Islamic State terrorist left 38 people dead, including 30 British vacationers.
6Abu Yahya al-Qayrawani, the alleged gunman, was killed by police during the attack.
7The Foreign Office estimates there are up to 3,000 British tourists currently in Tunisia, and a few hundred British residents live there, the BBC reports.
8"While we do not have any information suggesting a specific or imminent threat, since the attack in Sousse the intelligence and threat picture has developed considerably, leading us to the view that a further terrorist attack is highly likely," Hammond said.
9The foreign office warned of attacks "by individuals unknown to the authorities whose actions may be inspired by terrorist groups via social media."
10"If you're in Tunisia and you don't have an essential need to remain you should leave by commercial means," the statement said.
11Tour operators were arranging extra flights to help get the travelers home, Hammond said.
12The Tunisian government said Wednesday it would build a 100-mile wall along its border with Libya to stop Islamic State terrorists from crossing into the country.
13Tunisian Prime Minister Habib Essid said the barrier would stretch inland from the Mediterranean coast.
14The foreign office said, however, that it did not believe measures taken by Tunisia to counter such attacks would provide "adequate protection."
15Germany's Foreign Ministry said it was observing the situation closely and adjusting travel advice regularly, the Associated Press reported.
16As of now, however, there was no immediate change to its travel advice last updated five days ago.
17Two Germans were killed in the Sousse attack.
