Baltimore police fatally shoot unarmed black man

1Less than two months after tensions between police and those protesting against the death Freddie Gray (as depicted in this photo) hit a boiling point, an unarmed black man has been killed by police on June 25, 2015 in Baltimore, Maryland.
2OWINGS MILLS, Md.
3-- An unarmed black man was fatally shot Thursday by officers in suburban Baltimore who thought he had a weapon when they were called to a home from which he was barred by protective order, the police chief said.
4The death follows that of a 25-year-old black man who died in April from an injury suffered in the custody of Baltimore city police, which prompted protests and rioting.
5And it comes amid a national debate over the treatment of African-Americans at the hands of police, after high-profile deaths of black men in Ferguson, Missouri, and New York City.
6Spencer Lee McCain, 41, was shot about 1 a.m. at the home in Owings Mills while a woman who said he threatened to beat her and her two young children were there, Baltimore County Police Chief Jim Johnson said at a news conference.
7Three officers were on the scene, and all fired as McCain was in a "defensive position" and they thought he had a weapon, Johnson said.
8But he said no weapon was found.
9Two of the officers are white, and one is black, police said.
10All are on administrative leave.
11Police said the incident began when one of the children in the home called his grandmother, who called 911 and asked police to go there.
12An officer who arrived heard screams and called for backup, Johnson said.
13He said 19 casings were found at the scene, but it wasn't clear how many times McCain was hit.
14Police said the woman had head injuries, cuts, bruises and swelling.
15Johnson said she told detectives that McCain said to her, "You're going to get the beating you deserve."
16Sixteen to 20 domestic violence calls had been made to that home address since 2012, police spokesman Cpl.
17John Wachter said.
18According to 2013 census figures, 27.5% of Baltimore County's population is black and 64.1% is white.
19The police chief and county executive are white.
20As of February, black people made up less than 13% of the county's 1,868-member police force.
21A medical examiner ruled this week that the death of Freddie Gray was a homicide, after he suffered a "high-energy injury," most likely caused when a Baltimore city police van he was riding in suddenly slowed down.
22A grand jury indicted six officers on various charges; one officer faces the most serious charge of second-degree "depraved-heart" murder.
23They have pleaded not guilty.
24The report said Freddie Gray's death could not be ruled an accident and is instead a homicide because officers didn't follow safety procedures "through acts of omission."
25Police and an attorney for the Gray family have said previously that Gray suffered a severe spine injury.
