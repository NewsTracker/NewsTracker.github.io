1NEW YORK (AP) - The world's richest person got even richer this year.
2And a basketball superstar-turned-owner made the list for the first time.
3Forbes said Monday that Bill Gates's net worth rose to $79.2 billion in 2015 from $76 billion last year.
4That put him at the top of the magazine's list of the world's billionaires for the second consecutive year.
5The co-founder of Microsoft Corp. has topped the list for 16 of the last 21 years.
6In second place is Mexican telecommunications mogul Carlos Slim Helu, with a net worth of $77.1 billion.
7He had topped the list in 2013.
8Next is investor Warren Buffett, who moved up one slot this year with a net worth of $72.7 billion.
9In fourth place was Amancio Ortega, the Spanish co-founder of clothing retail chain Zara, with a net worth of $64.5 billion.
10Rounding out the top five was Larry Ellison, founder of technology company Oracle Corp., with $54.3 billion.
11Forbes said there were 1,826 billionaires on its list this year, up from 1,645 in 2014.
12Added together, they were worth a combined $7.05 trillion, up from $6.4 trillion last year.
13Most of those on the list were men.
14But there were 197 women, up from 172 a year ago.
15The highest-ranking woman was Christy Walton, the widow of John Walton, a son of the founder of Wal-Mart Stores Inc. She has a net worth of $41.7 billion, according to Forbes.
16The world's youngest billionaire was 24-year-old Evan Spiegel, the CEO and co-founder of mobile messaging company Snapchat, with a net worth of $1.5 billion.
17Snapchat's other co-founder, 25-year-old Bobby Murphy, had the same net worth as Spiegel.
18Other tech billionaire newcomers were two co-founders of taxi-ordering app Uber and one of its executives.
19Three co-founders of Airbnb, the vacation-home rental website, also made the list.
20Basketball legend Michael Jordan joined the list for the first time this year, thanks to his ownership in basketball team Charlotte Hornets and payouts form his Nike brand.
21Jordan had a net worth of $1 billion, the magazine said.
22This is the 29th year that Forbes has released its billionaires list.
23The magazine said it calculated each person's wealth based on stock prices and exchange rates on Feb. 13, 2015.

5He warned lawyers who protest about cuts to legal aid that it was 'no good complaining that public funds are available only for X and not for Y if money available for X is being squandered'.
6Sir James, president of the High Court's Family Division, said it was only necessary for the father to be able to read 51 pages of documents.
7But translating even the slimmed-down bundle of legal and social work papers is still likely to cost taxpayers around 2,000.
8The case will decide the future of three children of a Warwickshire woman who are in state care.
9The oldest is the daughter of the Slovenian man, but Sir James said 'little of the documentation relates to or refers to him'.
10However, a district judge in Coventry, who was not named in Sir James's ruling, decided in a hearing in November that the father could not make his views known in court 'until he has had the opportunity of considering the papers'.
11The district judge ruled that the father should be able to read more than half of the nearly 1,000 pages.
12But Sir James said the 591 pages to be translated included 23 pages of Warwickshire council's legal applications; 16 of court orders; 17 of immigration reports on another person; 32 of transcripts of recordings of the children; 131 of statements by the mother; and an index.
13'I could go on,' he said, adding: 'Why are these pages in the bundle at all?' He criticised lawyers who let files of court documents grow to the length of a novel, and said they should be no longer than 350 pages.
14The High Court judge warned lawyers who put bigger files before the court that they may have their fees docked, their papers destroyed, or be ordered to start the work again.
15'Public funds, whether those under the control of the Legal Aid Agency or those under the control of other public bodies are limited,' he said.
16Last month, Sir James attacked the failure of the legal aid system to provide a lawyer for two parents with learning difficulties from Swindon trying to stop their three-year-old son being adopted.
17He said the refusal to grant aid, on grounds of the father's 800-a-month earnings, was 'neither compassionate nor even humane'.
