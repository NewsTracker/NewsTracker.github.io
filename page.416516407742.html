Google executive Dan Fredinburg among hundreds dead from Nepal earthquake

1July 31, 2013: Sophia Bush, left, and Dan Fredinburg arrive at the Do Something Awards at the Avalon in Los Angeles.
2Fredinburg, a Google executive who described himself as an adventurer, was among the hundreds who died in a massive earthquake that struck Nepal.
3NEW YORK - Dan Fredinburg, a Google executive who described himself as an adventurer, was among the hundreds who died in a massive earthquake that struck Nepal on Saturday.
4Google confirmed his death.
5Lawrence You, the company's director of privacy, posted online that Fredinburg was in Nepal with three other Google employees climbing Mount Everest.
6The other three, he added, are safe.
7Google would not give further details.
8According to the technology blog Re/Code, Fredinburg was an experienced climber who co-founded, in his spare time, Google Adventure.
9The project aims to "translate the Google Street View concept into extreme, exotic locations like the summit of Mount Everest or the Great Barrier Reef off Australia," according to Startup Grind, a global startup community.
10Fredinburg also helped start Save the Ice, an organization dedicated to raising awareness about global warming "through adventurous campaigns and events around the world," according to its website.
11Fredinburg started at Google in 2007.
12He served as product manager and the head of privacy at Google X, the company's secretive arm best known for "moonshot" projects such as the self-driving car.
13Google said it has launched a "person finder" tool for Nepal to help people find loved ones in the aftermath of the quake and "is working to get updated satellite imagery to aid in the recovery effort."
14Google says it is committing $1 million to the quake response.
15The actress Sophia Bush, who has appeared in photos with Fredinburg posted by entertainment outlets, called him "one-of-a-kind" in a post on Instagram.
