Romanian parliament approves new spy chief

1Lawmakers voted 498-15 in favor of installing Eduard Hellvig to the post.
2The previous chief resigned after criticizing the country's top court for rejecting security laws that allowed the government to collect data on people without court approval.
3Hellvig, 40, on Monday spoke of "the tense geostrategic situation" in eastern Europe and said NATO member Romania is providing cyber security for Ukraine.
