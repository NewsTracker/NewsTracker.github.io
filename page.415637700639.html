Obama takes Netanyahu 'at his word' on Palestinian state

1Netanyahu has backpedaled since he stunned the U.S. and the international community by announcing that dramatic policy reversal on the eve of his re-election Tuesday.
2But in his first public comments on the election outcome, Obama suggested that he does not believe the Israeli leader's softer position on the Palestinian state issue.
3"We take him at his word when he said that it wouldn't happen during his prime ministership, and so that's why we've got to evaluate what other options are available to make sure that we don't see a chaotic situation in the region," Obama told The Huffington Post in an interview released Saturday.
4Obama, who placed a congratulatory telephone call to Netanyahu on Thursday, said he indicated to the prime minister that the U.S. remains committed to a two-state solution as the only way to keep Israel secure.
5"And I indicated to him that given his statements prior to the election, it is going to be hard to find a path where people are seriously believing that negotiations are possible," Obama said.
6Tensions between the Obama administration and Netanyahu escalated as the March 17 Israeli elections neared.
7The White House was particularly annoyed when Netanyahu accepted an invitation from Republican House Speaker John Boehner to address lawmakers earlier this month.
8Boehner had not consulted the administration before extending the invitation, which an infuriated White House said was a break from standard practice.
9Netanyahu sharply criticized a deal being negotiated among the U.S., Iran and other world powers over Iran's nuclear program.
10Obama said U.S.-Israeli military and security cooperation would remain unchanged, regardless of disagreements on policy.
11"But we are going to continue to insist that, from our point of view, the status quo is unsustainable," he said.
12"And that while taking into complete account Israel's security, we can't just in perpetuity maintain the status quo, expand settlements. That's not a recipe for stability in the region."
13Obama also criticized Netanyahu for saying as the election neared that Arab voters were heading to the polls "in droves."
14Obama's spokesman Josh Earnest previously denounced the rhetoric as a "cynical Election Day tactic" and a "pretty transparent effort to marginalize Arab Israeli votes."
15"We indicated that that kind of rhetoric was contrary to what is the best of Israel's traditions," Obama said, adding that Israeli democracy is premised on everyone being treated equally and fairly.
16"If that is lost, then I think that not only does it give ammunition to folks who don't believe in a Jewish state, but it also, I think, starts to erode the name of democracy in the country."
17White House chief of staff Denis McDonough is scheduled on Monday to address J Street, an Israeli advocacy group that opposes Netanyahu.
