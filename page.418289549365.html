Hillary Clinton, Ellen DeGeneres offer online support for gay child

1Photo posted on the Humans of New York Facebook page.
2Hillary Clinton and Ellen DeGeneres have come to the support of a young boy online, after he expressed fears for his future due to his homosexuality in a comment to a popular New York social media site.
3"I'm homosexual and I'm afraid about what my future will be and that people won't like me," he is captioned as saying, alongside a photograph posted on Saturday to the Humans of New York Facebook page, which features photographs and quotes from people across the city.
4Among an outpouring of supportive comments, two have stood out.
5Ellen Degeneres came out publicly on the cover of Time magazine.
6"Not only will people like you, they'll love you. I just heard of you and I love you already," stated a comment posted from the official account of The Ellen DeGeneres Show, a popular American talk show.
7Presidential candidate Hillary Clinton also commented from her official account in a post tagged with her initial: "Prediction from a grown-up: Your future is going to be amazing. You will surprise yourself with what you're capable of and the incredible things you go on to do. Find the people who love and believe in you - there will be lots of them. -H."
8The Facebook post has garnered more than 51,000 comments, including some with the hashtag #lovewins, which has been used across social media after the US Supreme Court's recent decision outlawing state bans on same-sex marriage.
9Hillary Clinton, a Democrat candidate for the US presidency.
