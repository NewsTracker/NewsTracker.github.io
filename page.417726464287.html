Interpol suspends decade-long partnership with FIFA amid corruption investigations

1Interpol has suspended its 10-year, 20million partnership with FIFA over match-fixing amid ongoing investigations into alleged corruption at world football's governing body.
2The international police organisation has announced it will 'freeze the use of financial contributions from FIFA' for its Integrity in Sport programme.
3Interpol secretary general Jurgen Stock said: 'In light of the current context surrounding FIFA, while Interpol is still committed to developing our Integrity in Sport programme, I have decided to suspend the agreement.
4A statement from FIFA read: 'We are disappointed to learn of the decision by Interpol to suspend co-operation in the fight against match-fixing.
