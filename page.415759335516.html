Air strikes trigger final push on Iraq's Tikrit

1Wary of being seen as cosying up to Iran's proxies, Washington conditioned its intervention on an enhanced role for regular government forces in the fight against the Islamic State group.
2The Tehran-backed paramilitary groups that had done the heavy lifting in the Tikrit operation so far threatened to pull out of it completely, accusing the US of "hijacking victory".
3The operation to retake Tikrit was launched on March 2 but had failed to dislodge a relatively small number of IS fighters who have hemmed themselves in with thousands of bombs for a last stand in the city centre.
4"The assault on the last (IS-held) pocket of Tikrit started from the southern front, in Awja," a brigadier general in the Tikrit area told AFP.
5He said Iraqi forces were also attacking from the west and north and were repairing a bridge over the Tigris that IS recently blew up to pile further pressure from the east.
6The forces involved in the fighting include the volunteer Popular Mobilisation units, Shiite militia groups, the army's counter-terrorism force as well as interior ministry units.
7Iraqi air strikes had, by Baghdad's own admission, not been good enough to break the back of IS resistance.
8"Now the operation to take Tikrit really begins," one US defence official told AFP.
9The Pentagon has said its technological edge would allow for precision strikes that can spare civilian lives and infrastructure.
10The exact number of civilians trapped inside Tikrit is unclear but a Red Crescent spokesman last week said "no more than 30,000, probably quite a bit less".
11Iraqi Defence Minister Khaled al-Obeidi said Iraqi Sukhoi fighter jets also pounded Tikrit Thursday.
12And a spokesman in Paris announced the French air force took part in the coalition raids that began late Wednesday.
13"The ongoing Iraqi and coalition air strikes are setting the conditions for offensive action to be conducted by Iraqi forces currently surrounding Tikrit," Terry said.
14The US military said the first wave of coalition air raids consisted of 17 strikes that struck buildings, bridges, checkpoints, staging areas, berms and a command post.
15Washington had expressed strong reservations over the leading role played in the Tikrit operation by Shiite militia groups, some of which have been accused of serious abuses.
16Iran's top commander in charge of external operations, Qassem Suleimani, has been ubiquitous on the Salaheddin front lines and is perceived by many Iraqis as the brain behind Iraq's ground operations.
17But the Pentagon insisted Washington remained Baghdad's most precious partner in the war to reclaim the vast regions of Iraq IS conquered last summer.
18"Reliable, professional, advanced military capabilities are something that very clearly and very squarely reside with the coalition," spokesman Colonel Steven Warren said Wednesday.
19Lloyd Austin, a top US general, said militia groups had already "pulled back" but it was not immediately clear from sources on the ground where.
20The militias vehemently criticised Baghdad's decision to call fort US help and held meetings among themselves to decide on the future of their involvement in the Tikrit assault.
21"This operation (US-led strikes) has negative results," said Jassem al-Jazaeri, a top political official from Kataeb Hezbollah, one of the main militia groups in Iraq.
22"The government might come to realise this in the coming days. We hope they will reconsider and not submit to foreign pressure," he said, speaking to his group's Etejah TV channel.
23One group threatened to turn against Americans while another said it would "boycott" any operation in which the United States is involved.
24Tikrit holds both strategic and symbolic importance.
25It was the hometown of executed dictator Saddam Hussein, the remnants of whose Baath party collaborated with IS last summer.
26And it is seen as a key stepping stone to recapturing Iraq's second city Mosul, the jihadists' largest hub.
27The International Crisis Group warned that, so far, advances against IS paradoxically risked undermining what is left of the Iraqi state.
28In a brief entitled "Defeating the Iraqi state, one victory at a time", the think tank said it was crucial to re-empower local elites, build credible local forces and provide services in recently reconquered areas.
