?On the Twentieth Century' review: Blissful Broadway revival

1The official, who insisted on anonymity because he wasn't authorized to speak with the media, said the victims were believed to be non-Muslims.
2The attack comes two days after gunmen attacked the convoy of Mandera Gov. Ali Roba, killing four people including two policemen.
3Roba survived the attack.
4Adan Duale, a Kenyan Somali who is majority leader in parliament, was quoted on the BBC Somali Service on Saturday telling al-Shabab to leave Kenya alone.
5"If you wish to, we shall send you elders, religious leaders and politicians. We shall come, tell us your whereabouts. Leave Kenya alone," Duale said in a statement that many in Kenya interpreted as a call for negotiations with al-Shabab.
6But Duale said Sunday on his Twitter account that Kenya will never negotiate with "terrorists."
7Al-Shabab, based in Somalia, has vowed to inflict violent attacks on Kenya because it has contributed troops to the African Union force supporting the Western-backed government in Somalia.
8Kenya has experienced a string of attacks since it sent troops into Somalia in October 2011.
9Police said last month that 312 people have been killed in extremist attacks since 2012.
10In October, Roba's convoy was hit by an improvised explosive device as he was being driven to work, injuring one of his guards.
11In November, al-Shabab fighters hijacked a bus near Mandera, singled out non-Muslims and non-Somalis, and shot dead 28 passengers.
12Twenty-two of those killed were teachers.
13Ten days later, al-Shabab massacred 36 quarry workers who were non-Muslims.
14Hundreds of teachers in parts of northern Kenya that border Somalia have refused to return to work, fearing attacks despite government reassurance of increased security.
1That's where the Roundabout revival of "On the Twentieth Century," directed with verve by Scott Ellis, takes you.
2But that's nothing compared to the practically nuclear glow that comes off Kristin Chenoweth, whose singular talent and skills are tailor-made for a role originated on Broadway in 1978 by Madeline Kahn.
3Chenoweth is a stick of blond dynamite, a virtuoso comedian and singer.
4She uses her petite body, ample bosom and middle finger for a laugh.
5She hits every high C in the joyous and eclectic score that pushes the plot along expertly.
6As Mildred Plotka, Chenoweth is a nobody, an audition pianist plunking out a living.
7She morphs into Lily Garland, an Oscar-winning glamour girl ever-fetching in stunning gowns - trimmed with lace and wit by William Ivey Long.
8She gets there with help from the powerful theater producer and sometime lover Oscar Jaffe.
9Now Oscar, played by Peter Gallagher with just the right egomaniacal spin and scary glint in his eye, is broke.
10He needs Lily for his next project about Mary Magdalene, so he books a neighboring compartment on the train.
11But a rival producer, Max Jacobs (James Moye) wants Lily for his project about high society.
12Which will Lily choose?
13That sets up one of a few elaborate and hysterical production numbers ingeniously choreographed by Warren Carlyle.
14Four tap-dancing porters add their own percussive and precise delight.
15Lending sterling support as Oscar's right-hand "musketeers" are Mark Linn-Baker and Michael McGrath.
16Mary Louise Wilson lets her inner rascal out as a fanatical, off-kilter millionaire who promises to back Oscar's movie.
17And Andy Karl (remember him as the best thing about "Rocky"?
18In the show's title song, it comes out that the Twentieth Century famously gives passengers "nothing but the best."
19This production, fizzy and dizzy entertainment, does likewise.
