10Here are five other new devices and services targeting your wallet.
11Apple's new laptop, simply called MacBook, debuts Friday.
12It's the company's lightest and thinnest laptop so far, making the ultra-thin MacBook Air appear bulky by comparison.
13Apple borrowed many design techniques from the iPad, including eliminating the fan.
14The new MacBook comes in silver, gold or gray - the first time there has been a color choice since Apple ditched plastic for metal on its laptops.
15The base model is $1,299.
16You get double the storage and a faster processor for $1,599.
17Samsung's Galaxy S6 phones and the HTC One M9 make their general U.S. debut in retail stores.
18(Online orders have been accepted for a few weeks).

20It also improved the camera and software interface.
21HTC also improved its camera and has added home-screen personalization based on location.
22Prices vary by carrier.
23No-contract versions generally start at about $650.
24In time for the return of "Game of Thrones" this Sunday, HBO launched its stand-alone streaming service, HBO Now, priced at $15 a month.
25Initially, it's available only to owners of an Apple TV, iPhone or iPad, or to Cablevision's Internet-access subscribers.
26HBO Now offers the same content as HBO Go, the companion app to HBO's cable channels.
27The difference is you don't need a cable or satellite TV subscription.
28You could also pay $15 a month to watch HBO shows online through Dish's Sling TV service.
29That's on top of Sling TV's $20 monthly fee for nearly two dozen other cable channels, including ESPN and CNN.
30Sling TV plans to debut HBO no later than Saturday as part of an app upgrade.

32CBS' $6-a-month streaming service, All Access, is now available on Roku - the first streaming TV device for the network.
33The subscription offers live feeds of CBS stations in some markets, along with a bigger library of on-demand content than what's available for free.
34Meanwhile, the Roku 3 player now has a new remote control with a voice search button that customers can use to see what's available across multiple streaming services such as Netflix.
35Another feature tracks when a movie you're interested in becomes available for streaming or gets a price cut.
36Attention cord-cutters and cord-nevers - you'll be able to watch free live local TV stations like CBS, Fox, NBC and PBS through Microsoft's Xbox One.
37You'll need an $80 dongle that connects your digital TV antenna to the game console's USB port.
38Shows will show up in the Xbox's TV guide and be integrated into the console's dual-screen "Snap" mode so users can play games and watch TV at the same time.
39With the Hauppage WinTV-955Q dongle, gamers can also stream the local feed to a tablet computer and pause live shows.
40The feature is rolling out now to Xbox One Preview members in the U.S. and Canada, and will be available to all Xbox gamers there over the next few months.
41OnLive, which streams high-end video games over an Internet connection (like a Netflix for games), is shutting down April 30 following Sony Corp.'s purchase of "important parts" of the company.
