US beats defending champ Russia 4-2 at ice hockey worlds (Yahoo Sports)

1PRAGUE (AP) -- The United States beat defending champion Russia 4-2 Monday for its third straight win at the ice hockey world championship.
2Sweden also made it three victories from three games with an 8-1 rout of Latvia.
3The young U.S. team leads Group B in Ostrava with nine points, three more than Russia, and plays Belarus next on Thursday.
4In a matchup of two unbeaten teams, Torey Krug used a two-man advantage in the second period to roof a wrist shot for the 2-1 go-ahead goal and Mark Arcobello made it 3-1 in the third.
5Sergei Plotnikov cut it to 3-2 when the puck deflected into the goal off defenseman Seth Jones' stick with 3:49 to go, but Brock Nelson scored into an empty net with 9 seconds left.
6U.S. goaltender Jack Campbell made 15 saves, including two big stops to deny Plotnikov in the opening period.
7''It's a fast game, a lot of great players,'' Campbell said.
8In Group A in Prague, Boston Bruins forward Loui Eriksson scored a hat trick for Sweden and Nashville Predators forward Filip Forsberg netted a penalty shot for his fourth goal of the tournament.
9''It's always fun to score a lot of goals,'' Forsberg said.
10Sweden faces the star-studded Canadian team on Wednesday.
11Later Monday, high-scoring Canada faces host Czech Republic in the day's other big matchup and last year's runner-up Finland plays Norway.
