Focus on Islamic State and Libya as NATO foreign ministers meet

1ANTALYA, Turkey, May 12 (Reuters) - Preoccupied for more than a year by the Ukraine crisis, NATO foreign ministers meeting in Turkey this week will focus on instability on the alliance's southern flank, ranging from Islamic State in Iraq and Syria to turmoil in Libya.
2By meeting in Turkey, which shares a 1,200 km (750-mile) border with Iraq and Syria, NATO hopes to show it is responsive to the concerns of its southern members, as well as reinforcing eastern European allies worried by Russia's actions in Ukraine.
3"You've got, to the east, to the southeast and to the south, pretty fundamental security challenges for NATO," U.S. Ambassador to NATO Douglas Lute told reporters ahead of Wednesday and Thursday's meetings in Antalya.
4Lute spoke of an "arc of instability" around the east and south of the alliance, where the "maybe failed state of Libya" served as a funnel for illegal immigration from countries such as northern Nigeria, Mali, Niger and Somalia.
5Libya has descended into lawlessness since rebels overthrew strongman Muammar Gaddafi in 2011 with the help of a NATO bombing campaign.
6NATO as an organisation has not been highly active militarily in the south recently, although all of its member nations are part of the U.S.-led coalition against Islamic State.
7It also sent Patriot anti-missile systems to defend Turkey from possible attack from war-torn Syria.
8The alliance is considering a request from Iraq for help training its army.
9But it says the security situation in Libya must improve before it could help train Libyan security forces.
10EU foreign policy chief Federica Mogherini is expected to brief the NATO ministers on the EU's proposals for a military mission to capture and destroy smugglers' boats used to ferry migrants on perilous Mediterranean crossings from Libya.
11NATO Secretary General Jens Stoltenberg said NATO had not been asked to play a military role in the mission.
12But Lute said information-sharing between NATO and the EU could be possible.
13Accurate intelligence pinpointing smugglers' vessels will be key to the success of the operation.
14Ministers will discuss the situation in Ukraine and will also talk about whether NATO should keep a presence in Afghanistan after the current NATO-led training mission ends, probably at the end of 2016.
15The most likely outcome is that NATO will then retain a small civilian-led mission, including both soldiers and civilians, a NATO source said.
