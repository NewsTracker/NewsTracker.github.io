Stocks rise as Greece gets bailout deal with creditors

1This Monday, July 6, 2015 photo shows a sign for Wall Street carved into the side of a building, in New York.
2Hopes that Greece will be able to forge a deal with creditors this weekend that will secure its euro future helped stock markets around the world and Europe's single currency itself post big gains on Friday, July 10.
3Global stock markets bounced higher Monday after Greece and its European creditors agreed on a bailout deal, substantially reducing the uncertainty about Greece's future in the euro common currency.
4KEEPING SCORE: France's CAC 40 added 1.9 percent to 4,998.33 and Germany's DAX rose 1.3 percent to 11,457.69.
5Britain's FTSE 100 edged up 0.8 percent to 6,723.91.
6U.S. shares were set to open higher.
7Dow and S&P 500 futures both rose 0.7 percent.
8GREECE FACTOR: A summit of European leaders reached a tentative agreement with Greece, removing an immediate threat that the country could collapse financially and leave the euro.
9Nine hours after a self-imposed deadline passed, the leaders announced the breakthrough early Monday.
10If the talks had failed, Greece could have faced bankruptcy and a possible exit from the euro.
11The deal needs approval from Greece's parliament, among others.
12The country will have to accept tough budget conditions in exchange for a three-year rescue program.
13ANALYST'S TAKE: "The euro area summit managed to reach an agreement with Greece, with the latter pretty much backtracking on all its pre-election demands. This should support risk appetite," said Vasileios Gkionakis, global head of forex strategy at UniCredit Bank in London.
14ASIA'S DAY: Japan's Nikkei 225 gained 1.6 percent to 20,089.77.
15South Korea's Kospi climbed 1.5 percent to 2,061.52.
16Hong Kong's Hang Seng rose 1.3 percent to 25,224.01.
17The Shanghai Composite added 2.4 percent to 3,970.39, bouncing back after a slew of government measures to halt a dramatic slide.
18Australia's S&P/ASX 200 was little changed, slipping 0.3 percent to 5,473.20.
19Other regional markets were also higher, including Taiwan, Singapore, Thailand and Indonesia.
20ENERGY: Benchmark U.S. crude was down 83 cents to $52.30 a barrel in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
21Oil prices have dropped as expectations grow that a provisional agreement may be reached soon on the Iran nuclear talks to curb the country's atomic program in return for tens of billions of dollars in sanctions relief.
22CURRENCIES: The euro initially spiked higher on the Greece bailout news then dropped to $1.1070 compared with $1.1157 on Friday.
