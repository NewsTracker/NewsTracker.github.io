Kerry: Decision soon on unfreezing US military aid to Egypt

Sierra Leone's vice president asks U.S. for asylum

1Egypt has been trying to get F-16s fighter jets, tanks and other materiel to combat the extremist threat spilling over from Libya and in the Sinai Peninsula.
2But the U.S. must decide whether to certify Egyptian progress on human rights, democracy and the rule of law, or issue a declaration that such aid is in the interests of U.S. national security.
3The aid was suspended after the military's 2013 takeover of the government.
4Kerry spoke at a news conference Saturday in Egypt.
1FREETOWN, Sierra Leone (AP) - Sierra Leone's vice president sought asylum on the United States on Saturday, saying he no longer felt safe in the country after soldiers disarmed the security team at his residence.
2"I don't feel safe this morning as vice president," Samuel Sam-Sumana told The Associated Press by phone.
3He said he was not at his residence and declined to disclose his whereabouts.
4After hearing that soldiers were heading to his home Saturday morning, Sam-Sumana said he tried and failed to contact President Ernest Bai Koroma.
5Instead, he said he reached top officials at the presidential guard who informed him his security team was being disarmed on orders from the president.
6Sam-Sumana said he then called U.S. Ambassador John Hoover and requested asylum.
7"They're having a meeting and they will get back to me," the vice president said.
8U.S. embassy spokeswoman Hollyn Green said Saturday that embassy officials, including Hoover, had "seen the news" but could not provide any reaction.
9"There is no comment at the moment and there is no action on our part," Green said.
10Sam-Sumana was expelled from the ruling All People's Congress political party last week, accused of "orchestrating political violence" and trying to form a new party in his home district of Kono, located in the country's Eastern Province.
11An announcement read out on state media also accused Sam-Sumana of "harboring a group of thugs."
12"The decision was taken to preserve the integrity of the party and discourage political violence in the country," the statement said.
13Sam-Sumana is also currently under Ebola quarantine after a member of his security team died of the disease, which has ravaged Sierra Leone.
14Despite his expulsion from the party and his quarantine, he officially remained vice president, meaning responsibility for running the country falls to him if Koroma travels abroad.
