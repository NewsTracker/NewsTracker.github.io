Nemtsov's shaken girlfriend says she didn't see killer

Boris Nemtsov case: Video reports conflict in probe of slaying of Putin critic

4MOSCOW (Reuters) - Boris Nemtsov's girlfriend has broken her public silence on the murder of the Russian opposition activist, saying she did not see the killer who gunned him down as they strolled across a bridge near the Kremlin.
5Speaking largely without emotion in deadened monosyllables, Ukrainian fashion model Anna Duritskaya said she had little recollection of what happened in the moments after Nemtsov was shot dead on Friday night.
6She told online news channel Dozhd that she had not noticed anything suspicious as the couple dined at a restaurant overlooking Red Square.
7It had not occurred to her that someone might be following them as they headed across the river towards Nemtsov's apartment.
8"I don't want to answer questions about what happened on the bridge. I don't want to talk about this," she said.
9"I am in a very difficult psychological condition and I cannot talk about this any more now. I feel bad ... I saw no one. I don't know where he came from, he was behind my back," she said of the killer or killers.
10Nemtsov, 55, was shot several times and killed instantly, becoming the most prominent opposition figure to be murdered during President Vladimir Putin's 15-year rule.
11Tens of thousands of Russians marched through Moscow on Sunday to honor the former deputy prime minister and anti-corruption fighter.
12Duritskaya, who is 23 or 24, said she had been under constant guard since the murder and would probably be unable to attend Nemtsov's funeral on Tuesday.
13All she wanted to do was to go home to her mother in Ukraine.
14"I have every right to leave the territory of the Russian Federation. I am not a suspect. I am a witness who gave full testimony and did everything possible to assist the investigation," she said.
15"They are physically not allowing me to go anywhere without them," Duritskaya said, referring to law enforcement officials.
16"They have explained to me that this is for security reasons."
17Nemtsov, an opposition politician whose murder has deepened a divide between liberals and Putin supporters, will be buried on Tuesday at the Troekurovskoye cemetery in western Moscow, far from where Russia's top politicians are usually buried.
18"I cannot go out. Most likely I will not go," Duritskaya said when asked if she would attend.
19She said she was now staying at a friend's apartment in Moscow.
20Investigators had questioned her several times, gone through her belongings and taken data from her mobile phones.
21She said she was initially questioned for several hours without a lawyer or representative from the Ukrainian embassy.
22Nemtsov had criticized Putin's annexation of Crimea and his support for pro-Russian separatists in the east of Ukraine.
23He and Duritskaya had been dating for three years, she said.
24The investigators say they are following several lines of inquiry and have come up with a number of possible motives.
25One is that Nemtsov may have been the victim of a jealous former lover, a version Duritskaya ruled out.
26"I don't think anything. I cannot think at the moment. I don't know who did it," she said.
1No suspects have been arrested since Nemtsov was shot dead Friday night on a Moscow bridge, a slaying that came just hours after a radio interview in which he denounced Putin's "mad, aggressive policy" in Ukraine.
2According to Russian investigators, Nemtsov was walking near the Kremlin on his way home with a woman when he was shot four times by an assailant, who then escaped in a light-colored car.
3It was not clear what CCTV footage may have captured his slaying, even though the area is one of the most highly secure parts of the Russian capital.
4The business daily newspaper Kommersant quoted anonymous sources in the Interior Ministry as saying there was no CCTV footage of his killing because the cameras in question were not working at the time.
5However, Yelena Novikova, a spokeswoman for Moscow's information technology department, which oversees surveillance cameras in the city, said Monday that all cameras "belonging to the city" were operating correctly on the night of Nemtsov's death.
6She said federal authorities also had surveillance cameras near the Kremlin that are not under her organization's control.
7The failure to dig up CCTV footage in such a public place as central Moscow has deepened the frustration of those who supported opposition leader Nemtsov, many of whom believe the government is responsible for his killing.
8In Geneva, Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov called the Nemtsov killing a "heinous crime which will be fully investigated."
9He told the U.N. Human Rights Council on Monday that Russian President Vladimir Putin had "immediately handed down all instructions and is ensuring special control over this investigation."
10At the same time he rebuffed outside interference in the probe, saying any "attempt to use the heinous killing of Boris Nemtsov for political purposes is despicable."
11Investigators said they are looking into several possible motives for the slaying and have offered 3 million rubles (nearly $50,000) for information about it, but so far there have been few leads.
12The one witness is the Ukrainian woman who was accompanying Nemtsov when he was shot, 23-year-old Anna Duritskaya.
13She has been under police guard for questioning since early Saturday, but in an interview Monday with the independent Russian TV channel Dozhd she said she had not seen Nemtsov's attacker.
14"I didn't see anything," she told Dozhd.
15"I turned around and all I saw was a light-colored car. I saw neither the brand nor the license plate of the car when it was driving away."
16Duritskaya said she had known Nemtsov for three years.
17She said she had been questioned exhaustively by police and wants to leave the country but Russian investigators would not let her.
18She is now staying at a friend's apartment in Moscow.
19Meanwhile, TV Center, a station controlled by the Moscow city government, broadcast a poor-resolution video from one of its web cameras that it said shows Nemtsov and his date shortly before the killing.
20The station, which superimposed its own time code on the footage, circled figures that it said were Nemtsov and the woman walking across the bridge on a rainy night.
21A vehicle that TVC identified as a snowplow moved slowly behind the couple, obscuring the view of the shooting.
22TV Center then circled what it said was the suspected killer jumping into a passing car.
