Flooding, destructive winds as Cyclone Pam bears down on Vanuatu

1Photo: The eye of Cyclone Pam moves south-west placing several islands of Vanuatu in its path.
2A category five tropical cyclone is battering Vanuatu, packing winds of up to 250 kilometres per hour and triggering flooding in parts of the country.
3The Vanuatu Meteorological Service said red alerts were in place for Sanma, Penama, Tafea, Malampa and Shefa provinces.
4There are unconfirmed reports 44 people have died as a result of the storm.
5The centre of Tropical Cyclone Pam cyclone has passed Port Vila and is moving in a southerly direction at about 27 kilometres per hour.
6The island of Erromango took a direct hit as the cyclone tracked south.
7Vanuatu's Meteorological Services said "very destructive hurricane force winds" of 250 kilometres per hour were affecting Shefa and Tafea provinces.
8It warned of torrential rainfalls, flash flooding, landslides and storm surge.
9There have been reports the cyclone caused power and water outages in the capital, Port Vila.
10Nichola Krey, head of the humanitarian affairs at Save the Children, was in contact with staff in Vanuatu before communications were cut to the area.
11She said there were reports of complete and utter devastation in the capital of Port Vila.
12"Temporary dwellings, which most Vanuatu people live in, corrugated iron houses have been completely annihilated," she said.
13"Nothing left in terms of local housing. Some of the larger structures are also gone as well. We are expecting a fairly chaotic scene this morning."
14She said no-one would have any shelter over their heads for the next couple of weeks.
15Oxfam spokeswoman Helen Szoke said the organisation had been unable to contact staff on the ground since last night.
16But before communications went down, she said local staff were giving harrowing reports of the impact of Cyclone Pam.
17"Our staff in Port Vila, they're saying the noise was incredible, just the strength of the wind," she said.
18"It's going to be some days before we know the full extent to the damage that's been done."
19Speaking from a hotel shelter on Friday, program director for CARE Australia in Vanuatu, Inga Mepham, told ABC's The World program that people had heeded warnings to take shelter.
20"It's a thundery noise coming through and obviously things are starting to fly around. We can feel things hitting us at different times," she said.
21"I think people would still be in disbelief tonight ... certainly we are."
22In Fiji, head of the United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA) for the Pacific, Sune Gudnitz, said the storm was the "worst-case scenario".
23"The most vulnerable will certainly be the people that are living or residing closer to the coastline, which in a place like Vanuatu is a lot of people," he said.
24"Very few structures I think will be able to withstand a category five cyclone of the magnitude that we are seeing.
25"So we are looking at potentially total destruction of many shelters, residences, especially in the islands south and outside of Port Vila."
26Vanuatu's northern islands were the first to feel the destructive force of Pam, the strongest storm to hit the nation of 270,000 people in nearly 30 years.
27Aid agencies said many people living in flimsy slum accommodation were particularly at risk, as well as those in remote outlying islands.
28Authorities in Vanuatu advised thousands of residents to shelter in evacuation centres ahead of the storm.
29UNICEF spokeswoman Alice Clements said the capital resembled a "ghost town" as people battened down.
30"The winds have definitely increased, the palm trees are blowing around like crazy, you're starting to get that kind of howling wind coming through," the official with the UN children's agency said.
31Meteorologist Neville Koop, from Fiji's Nadraki Weather Service, said Pam's winds were capable of bringing down even well-built structures.
32He said they could be more destructive than Cyclone Uma, which killed at least 30 people when it sank two ferries off Port Vila.
33"Pam has winds which are much stronger than Vanuatu experienced (in 1987)", he said.
34Australian Foreign Affairs Minister Julie Bishop issued a statement on Friday urging citizens "to reconsider your need to travel" to Vanuatu.
35"We are monitoring the humanitarian impact closely and are preparing a potential response to this emergency," Ms Bishop said.
36Vanuatu's meteorological service warned more bad weather was likely to follow Pam as forecast models showed a separate tropical cyclone heading its way.
37Cyclone Nathan, a category two storm located off the coast of Queensland, was expected to intensify and possibly hit Vanuatu next week, the service said.
38The UN's OCHA said Papua New Guinea and Solomon Islands could also be affected by Cyclone Nathan.
39"This could have devastating effects for communities living in areas that have already experienced prolonged rains and other impacts from Tropical Cyclone Pam," OCHA said.
40The bad weather being generated by Cyclone Pam has been felt right across the region.
41In Kiribati, the combination of heavy rain, strong winds and high tides has damaged causeways connecting islets in the most populated area South Tarawa.
42The Solomon Islands National Disaster Management Office said it had received reports of damage to houses and food gardens in Temotu province.
