Tunisia delays reopening of besieged museum

1TUNIS, Tunisia -- The curator of the Tunisian museum targeted by a terrorist attack said Tuesday that the reopening was being postponed for logistical and security reasons.
2The National Bardo Museum in Tunis was scheduled to reopen Tuesday for the first time since gunmen opened fire on tourists March 18.
3The attack killed 21 people, and two gunmen were killed by police.
4Curator Moncef Ben Moussa said Tuesday morning on Radio Shems that the museum was delaying the reopening, and it would probably reopen Sunday instead.
5He did not elaborate on what prompted the last-minute change, only citing logistics and security.
6Authorities have arrested several people in the attack, the worst in Tunisia in 13 years, and are still hunting for the suspected mastermind.
7Officials have said none of the museum's major archaeological treasures suffered damage.
