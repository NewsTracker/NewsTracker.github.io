Apple, A123 close to settling battery hiring lawsuit

1The Apple logo is seen at the flagship Apple retail store in San Francisco, California April 27, 2015.
2SAN FRANCISCO (Reuters) - Apple Inc (AAPL.O) and electric battery maker A123 Systems are close to settling a legal dispute over claims that the smartphone and computer maker, which is also looking into building an electric vehicle, poached A123 employees to build a large-scale battery unit.
3A123, which makes batteries that are used in electric cars, sued Apple in February in Massachusetts federal court, claiming that around June 2014 Apple began aggressively poaching A123 engineers tasked with leading some of the company's most critical projects.
4Apple denied the claims.
5The Waltham, Mass.-based battery maker disclosed in court papers Monday that the parties have reached an agreement to settle the case and are working out the final details.
6Neither company immediately responded to requests for comment.
7Lawyers for Apple did not immediately respond, while A123 lawyer Michael Rosen declined to comment.
8A123 Systems is a pioneering industrial lithium-ion battery maker, which was backed by a $249 million U.S. government grant.
9It filed for bankruptcy in 2012 and has been selling off assets.
10The case in U.S. District Court, District of Massachusetts is A123 Systems LLC vs. Apple Inc et al., 15-10438.
