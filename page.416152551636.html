Fin24.com | Samsung and Apple clash with new products

1Seoul - Samsung rolled out a new smartphone on Friday in a bid to regain market dominance as arch-rival Apple, eyeing the next generation of wearable technology, opened pre-sales for its first watch.
2Samsung's Galaxy S6 and its curved-edge variant, the Galaxy S6 Edge, went on sale in South Korea, home of the electronics giant, as well as Europe, the United States and markets in the Asia-Pacific such as Australia, Singapore and India.
3Samsung, labouring under successive quarters of plunging profits and booming sales of Apple's iPhone 6, is hoping the new phone will reverse its fortunes.
4The range of S6 models has won strong reviews from tech pundits, with the Wall Street Journal calling them "the most beautiful phones Samsung has ever made".
5"Given the response from the market and clients... we expect the S6 to set a sales record for all Galaxy models," Lee Sang-Chul, the vice head of Samsung's mobile unit, told reporters.
6Joo Seung-Bin was one of the first in line to buy an S6 in Seoul on Friday morning.
7"It's a great design, and it's just got a great feel," the 23-year-old said.
8"It's not cheap, but then I'm not one of those people who upgrades their phone every year. There's been a lot of buzz about this model, so I thought I'd check it out," Joo said.
9The S6 retails at around 858,000 won ($800) in South Korea, while the S6 Edge comes in at 979,000 won, broadly in line with their Apple counterparts.
10Both models are powered by Google's Android operating platform and, in a break from their-plastic-backed predecessors, feature metal and glass bodies.
11However, the S6 had to contend on launch day with the Apple Watch, which will connect wirelessly to a user's iPhone to facilitate messaging, calls and apps - especially ones geared toward health and fitness.
12The first generation of smart watches from the likes of Motorola, LG and Samsung itself failed to gain much traction with consumers.
13But experts say the Apple Watch may have an edge given its design quality and universe of apps.
14The US company on Friday started taking pre-orders for the watch both in selected territories and online.
15First deliveries will begin in nine countries on April 24.
16Would-be early buyers queued for a "trial fitting" at the Apple store in Tokyo's chic Omotesando area.
17"I'm very keen to buy it," said Kazuki Miura, a 43-year-old technology writer, as he slipped the device onto his wrist.
18Apple Watch prices will start at $349, with a limited-edition gold version costing $10 000.
19Jan Dawson at Jackdaw Research expects Apple to sell around 20 million of the devices this year, which will "catalyse the overall smart watch market and help other vendors even as Apple comes to enjoy levels of market share it hasn't had since the iPod".
20Tech firms are betting heavily on wearables as the next frontier in consumer electronics.
21Luxury Swiss watchmaker TAG Heuer announced last month it was joining forces with Google and Intel to develop a rival to the Apple Watch.
22But, despite fierce competition on various market fronts, there are signs that Samsung is beginning to fight back.
23Earlier this week it released a better-than-expected profit estimate for the first quarter.
24Samsung suffered a dramatic slide in profits last year, hit by slowing demand in an increasingly saturated and competitive smartphone market that it had largely dominated since 2011.
25The company struggled to fend off a double challenge from Apple in the high-end market and rising Chinese firms such as Lenovo and Xiaomi in the fast-growing mid and low-end markets.
26Samsung rarely discloses handset sales figures but analysts say the Galaxy S4, released in 2013, set the firm's sales record of 70 million units globally.
