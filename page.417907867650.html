A-Rod homers for 3,000th hit; 29th player to reach milestone (Yahoo Sports)

1NEW YORK (AP) -- Alex Rodriguez homered for his 3,000th career hit and smiled all the way around the bases Friday night, the highlight so far in what's become a resurgent season for the disgraced slugger.
2The New York Yankees star wasted no time, connecting in the first inning on a first-pitch, 95 mph fastball from Detroit ace Justin Verlander.
3Out of baseball last year while serving a drug suspension, Rodriguez became the 29th player in major league history to reach 3,000 hits.
4He was the first to do it since Yankees great Derek Jeter homered from the very same batter's box in 2011.
5''It's a magical number,'' Rodriguez said after the final out of New York's 7-2 victory.
6With the crowd at Yankee Stadium standing in anticipation, Rodriguez sent a high drive to right field.
7He held onto the bat as he took a few steps toward first base, and outfielder J.D. Martinez bumped into the wall as he backed up.
8Fans roared as the ball sailed a half-dozen rows into the seats.
9Rodriguez pointed to the crowd a couple of times and blew a kiss to someone behind the backstop after crossing the plate.
10He was greeted by Mark Teixeira and other teammates between the plate and the dugout, and got a big hug from manager Joe Girardi.
11The only other players to hit a homer for No.
123,000 were Jeter and Wade Boggs.
13Retrieving the souvenir ball might be harder for Rodriguez than hitting it.
14Zack Hample, a 37-year-old who describes himself as a professional home run ball catcher, wound up with the prized keepsake.
15Hample originally told the Yankees he had no intention of giving it back.
16He watched the ninth inning with team president Randy Levine and chief operating officer Lonn Trost, and said he's considering what to do.
17Rodriguez turns 40 next month.
18The three-time AL MVP launched his 13th homer of the season and has enjoyed a productive year as a designated hitter after returning from his suspension.
19Rodriguez hit his 667th career home run after beginning the day with a .299 lifetime average in 21 seasons.
20He joined Hank Aaron and Willie Mays as the only players with 600 homers and 3,000 hits.
21Earlier this year, Rodriguez passed Mays for fourth place on the all-time homer list and eclipsed Barry Bonds for second on the official RBIs chart.
22''I'm enjoying this season as much as any,'' he said.
23Unlike with home run No.
24660 that tied Mays, there was no marketing agreement between Rodriguez and the Yankees regarding this milestone - and thus, no potential squabble over $6 million.
25The team even tweeted updates throughout his pursuit, using the hashtag AROD3K.
26This hit was quite different from his first one - a four-hop, infield single on July 9, 1994, at Fenway Park against Boston's Sergio Valdez.
27Back then, Rodriguez was an 18-year-old batting last for the Seattle Mariners, a young man with a big future.
28Friday night's plate umpire, Ed Hickox, coincidentally was behind the plate for A-Rod's first hit, too.
29''Is that right - 21 years apart?'' Hickox said an hour before the first pitch.
30For all his achievements, A-Rod has been a polarizing lightning rod in recent years.
31While the crowd in the Bronx cheered him, there are many fans who will forever attach an asterisk to anything Rodriguez accomplishes.
32Such is the baseball world in the wake of performance-enhancing drugs, where RBIs, ERAs and other huge numbers have been tainted by PEDs.
33Bonds, Roger Clemens and Mark McGwire are among the many with Cooperstown-caliber credentials who haven't come close to being elected to the Hall of Fame because of drug scandals.
34High school student Claire Campo, from Mission Viejo, California, bought a $135 Rodriguez jersey in the souvenir store an hour before the game.
35''I love Alex Rodriguez,'' she said.
36''People need to get off his case.
37He's done everything you could ask for on the field.
38What he's done off the field, he apologized and paid the price.
39Her grandfather, Larry Vastola of Morristown, New Jersey, said he completely agreed with her.
40But he said his wife - Campo's grandmother - had a different view.
41Rodriguez was the No.
421 pick in the 1993 draft.
43He became a star in Seattle, later joined Texas and was traded to the Yankees after the 2003 season.
44His 3,000th hit was his 1,465th with the Yankees.
45As Rodriguez ascended to becoming a perennial All-Star, many looked at him as a player who could help restore credibility to the record books while stars such as Manny Ramirez, Sammy Sosa and Rafael Palmeiro were caught up in drug scandals during the late 1990s and 2000s.
46But in 2009, Rodriguez admitted to using steroids while with Texas.
47His involvement in the Biogenesis drug investigation near Miami, where he lives in the offseason, was a low point and it got him suspended for a year after an acrimonious appeal.
48After suing MLB and the Yankees - all lawsuits have been dropped - Rodriguez returned to baseball with trepidation.
49But he has won back some fans in New York by being a productive player on the field and a model citizen off the diamond.
50Rodriguez homered on the birthday of late Yankees hero Lou Gehrig, and connected a day before Yogi Berra, Whitey Ford, Reggie Jackson, Boggs and other New York stars return for Old-Timers' Day.
51Jeter finished with 3,465 hits.
52His milestone day was indeed monumental, when he went 5 for 5 with the deciding hit.
53The buzz leading up to his 3,000th became the talk of baseball and when he homered off Tampa Bay's David Price - now with the Tigers - the crowd responded with a 4-minute ovation.
54As Jeter rounded the bases, first baseman Casey Kotchman doffed his hat and several Tampa Bay players came out of the dugout to applaud.
55Rodriguez came out for a curtain call after his homer, but there was nothing close to the outpouring of love that Jeter received.
56Cleveland Cavaliers star Kevin Love and New York Knicks executive Phil Jackson were among those in attendance.
57Rodriguez homered off Verlander for the fourth straight game, and popped up and grounded out in his next two at-bats.
58A-Rod has tagged a lot of pitchers over the years, getting 26 hits off knuckleballer Tim Wakefield and 25 off David Wells.
59The pitcher who has held Rodriguez hitless the longest?
60Arizona lefty Oliver Perez, against whom Rodriguez is 0 for 11.
61Rodriguez tied the late Roberto Clemente with 3,000 hits, and can rapidly move up the list.
62Craig Biggio is 21st and reachable this year with 3,060, and Cal Ripken is 15th at 3,184.
63Pete Rose owns the career record with 4,256.
64As for who will next reach 3,000, it's hard to say.
65Ichiro Suzuki, now a part-time player for the Marlins, is closest to Rodriguez and began the day with 2,886 hits.
66Adrian Beltre followed with 2,657 and Albert Pujols had 2,585.
67Tigers star Miguel Cabrera had 2,266 and is 32.
68Before Jeter, no player had gotten his 3,000th hit in a Yankees uniform.
69Now, the last two to accomplish the feat did so wearing pinstripes.
