Williams wins Wimbledon to secure another Serena Slam (Yahoo Sports)

1LONDON (AP) -- Game.
2Set. Serena Slam.
3Serena Williams overcame a slow start, eight double-faults and a nervy finish to beat Garbine Muguruza 6-4, 6-4 on Saturday for her sixth Wimbledon title, fourth Grand Slam championship in a row and 21st major overall.
4The win means Williams holds all four Grand Slam titles at once - completing the second ''Serena Slam'' of her career.
5What's more, she secured the third leg of a calendar-year Grand Slam and, if she wins the U.S. Open, will become the first player to sweep all four majors in the same season since Steffi Graf in 1988.
6Williams, winning her 28th straight Grand Slam match, is now just one major title behind Graf on the Open era list and two behind all-time leader Margaret Court.
7At 33, Williams is also the oldest woman to win a Grand Slam title in the Open era.
8She has now won eight major championships in her 30s.
9Williams was broken in the opening game by the 21-year-old Spaniard, who was playing in her first Grand Slam final, and fell behind 4-2.
10But Williams began playing more aggressively and ran off five straight games to take the set and go up 1-0 in the second.
11Williams pulled out to a 5-1 lead and looked ready to finish Muguruza off quickly.
12But she seemed to get tight, and was broken twice while serving for the match.
13Muguruza saved a match point and converted on her fifth break point to draw within 5-4.
14But Williams then broke at love in the next game to close out the match, which finished in anticlimactic fashion when Muguruza hit a forehand wide.
15Neither player seemed to realize the match was over, and there was an awkward delay before Williams covered her face with both hands, then smiled and raised her arms in the air.
16After the two players hugged at the net, Williams hopped up and down on the side of the court with her arms above her head.
17She later accepted the winner's trophy - the Venus Rosewater Dish - from the Duke of Kent.
18Williams hasn't lost in a Grand Slam since falling in the third round to Alize Cornet at Wimbledon last year.
19Since then, she won last year's U.S. Open and this year's Australian and French Opens before completing the set at Wimbledon.
20Williams extended her winning streak in Grand Slam finals to 10 and improved her overall record in major finals to 21-4, including 6-2 at Wimbledon.
21Williams was the last player to win four majors in a row, when she achieved her first ''Serena Slam'' by taking the 2002 French Open, Wimbledon and U.S. Open and the 2003 Australian Open.
22Apart from Graf, only two women have completed a calendar-year Grand Slam - Maureen Connolly in 1953 and Court in 1970.
23Martina Navratilova also won four straight majors in 1983-84.
24Williams' sixth Wimbledon singles title puts her third on the Open era list behind Navratilova with nine and Graf with seven.
25Sunday's men's final pits defending champion Novak Djokovic against seven-time winner Roger Federer.
26It's a repeat of last year's championship match, won by Djokovic in five sets.
27Federer is aiming for a record eighth Wimbledon trophy and an 18th Grand Slam title, while Djokovic is chasing a third title at the All England Club and ninth major.
