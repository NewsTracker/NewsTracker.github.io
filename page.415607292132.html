Italy hosts meeting to combat Islamic State group financing

1The two-day meeting was co-chaired by the United States, Saudi Arabia and Italy.
2It ended Friday with an action plan that the Italian foreign ministry said aimed to "to drain the sources of income, capacity to transfer funds and, more in general, the economic stability" of the group.
3A U.S. Treasury Department statement said key steps include cracking down on unregulated money remitters and countering the Islamic State group's exploitation of cash, oil, cultural property and other resources.
4The Islamic State group has taken control of parts of Syria and Iraq, and some extremists based in other countries are affiliated or sympathetic to it.
