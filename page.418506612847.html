Facebook close sets speed record for $250 billion market cap

1Facebook stumbled after its IPO, but the company's stock has thrived in the last couple of years.
2It seems like just yesterday that Facebook Inc. went public.
3Now its market value has topped $250 billion.
4The social network company's 2.4 percent climb to a record close on Monday made it the first company in the Standard & Poor's 500 index to breach that market cap so quickly.
5The previous record holder was Google Inc., which took about eight years.
6Facebook's rapid ascent may indicate investor confidence that the company will continue to increase mobile-advertising sales on its application and others.
7To some degree, the gain also reflects froth in tech stocks; the Nasdaq Internet index has almost doubled since Facebook went public.
8Its shares trade at 87 times earnings, almost five times the average in the S&P 500.
9Companies in the Nasdaq Internet Index trade at a price-earnings ratio of 27.
10"When you see stocks with these high multiples, it shows you the market's comfort in the longer-term growth story," said Paul Sweeney, an analyst at Bloomberg Intelligence.
11"Investors think Facebook is more valuable than the average Nasdaq stock."
12The company's quick rise to $90.10 a share on Monday is even more remarkable because the stock lost more than half its value in the four months after its IPO in May 2012.
13Facebook had a market value of $104.2 billion at its IPO, so the company didn't have to climb as far as some other companies did to reach $250 billion.
14With a market value of $253 billion, Facebook is now the ninth-biggest company in the S&P 500 - bigger than Wal-Mart Stores Inc. and Procter & Gamble Co., which took decades to grow as valuable.
15Facebook's revenue from advertising - from which the company gets more than 90 percent of its sales - increased 46 percent to $3.32 billion in the first quarter from a year earlier.
16More than two-thirds of that came from mobile ad sales.
17Analysts estimate that sales rose 37 in the second quarter.
18Longer-term, the company plans to serve ads on other applications and websites and to make money from its rapidly growing Instagram, WhatsApp and Messenger apps.
19Facebook also is betting on its $2 billion purchase last year of Oculus VR Inc., a virtual-reality headset maker, and efforts to expand Internet connections in developing countries.
