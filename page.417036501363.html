CANNES WATCH: Winehouse documentary shows fame's dark side

1CANNES, France (AP) - Celebrity-worshipping Cannes got a sobering reminder of fame's dark side Saturday with "Amy," a powerful documentary about the late singer Amy Winehouse.
2"I don't think I'm going to be at all famous," a teenage Winehouse says at one point in the film by British director Asif Kapadia.
3"I don't think I could handle it."
4Winehouse was a global celebrity when she died in July 2011 of accidental alcohol poisoning at age 27.
5The sense that the singer foresaw her fate lends extra poignancy to the film, which is getting its world premiere at a Cannes midnight screening.
6Kapadia, who made acclaimed documentary "Senna" about Formula 1 driver Ayrton Senna - another star who died young - interviewed 80 of Winehouse's friends, family and collaborators for the film, layering their words over footage of the musician from childhood onwards.
7Winehouse emerges as a vibrant personality whose soulful voice and intensely personal lyrics made her a star, but whose struggles with depression, bulimia, drugs and alcohol were worsened by the pressures of sudden fame.
8Some journalists at a press screening shuffled uncomfortably in their seats at the sight of the media frenzy that surrounded Winehouse after her second album, "Back to Black," made her a star - and her erratic behavior made her tabloid news.
9Winehouse's parents appear in the film, which is due for release in the U.S. in July.
10But they have also recently criticized it, calling it "unbalanced" and saying it suggests family members did too little to help the singer overcome her addictions.
11A family spokesman said Winehouse's parents did not plan to attend Saturday's premiere.
12But no one emerges as an outright villain in the film, which shows how people who cared about Winehouse tried, and failed, to curb her demons.
13One of the final shots is of Tony Bennett ranking Winehouse in the pantheon of singers alongside Ella Fitzgerald and Billie Holiday.
14It's a moving end to a sad, cautionary tale.
