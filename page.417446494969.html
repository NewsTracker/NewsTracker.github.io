Apple's streaming music service may come next week

1LOS ANGELES - Apple is getting ready to announce its answer to Spotify at its Worldwide Developer Conference (WWDC) in San Francisco next week, according to a Wall Street Journal report.
2Much like its competitor, Apple's service will be priced US$10 a month for unlimited streaming; however, it looks like there won't be any ad-supported on-demand tier.
3Instead, Apple is looking to attract users unwilling to pay for music with a revamped version of its iTunes Radio service, which reportedly will feature stations programmed by musicians like Q-Tip, Drake and Dr. Dre.
4The Journal notes that Apple hasn't finalized deals for its subscription service with the major record labels, so there is a chance that an announcement may be postponed until the ink on those deals is dry.
5Apple long ignored music subscription services, instead focusing on its iTunes digital download store.
6But with digital download sales declining and services, such as Spotify, gaining ground, the company is looking to change course.
7It acquired Beats Electronics for $3 billion a year ago, and as part of the deal also got its hand on the company's Beats Music subscription service.
8Beats Music is expected to be rolled into Apple's own service when it launches.
