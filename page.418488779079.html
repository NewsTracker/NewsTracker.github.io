Nintendo Boss Satoru Iwata Dies Of Cancer

1The 55-year-old, who led the Japanese video game company through years of growth, has died from bile duct cancer.
2Nintendo has said its president, Satoru Iwata, has died of cancer at a hospital in Kyoto at the age of 55.
3In a brief statement, the Japanese video game company said: "Nintendo Co, Ltd deeply regrets to announce that President Satoru Iwata passed away on July 11, 2015, due to a bile duct growth."
4Mr Iwata, who started off as a programmer, took the top post at Nintendo in 2002 - just two years after joining the firm.
5He led the company through years of growth with its Pokemon and Super Mario franchises, its hit Wii home console, and also through its recent woes caused by the booming popularity of smartphones.
6Last year, Mr Iwata said he would slash his salary in half for several months to atone for the firm's downturn, which has struggled as rivals Sony and Microsoft outpaced it in console sales.
7Mr Iwata had argued that venturing into the overpopulated world of smartphones and tablets risked hollowing out the core business and cannibalising the hard-fought value of their game creations.
8But he later acknowledged Nintendo had to move into new areas, and launched a push into the smartphone games market.
9"The world is changing, so any company that is not coping with the change will fall into decline," he said at the time.
10Mr Iwata announced his illness last year and did not attend the firm's shareholder meeting in June 2014, although the severity of his illness was unclear at the time.
