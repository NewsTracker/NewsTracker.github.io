423 for the trial of Christian Englander.
5Englander, 31, who is white, has been charged with disturbing the peace and battery after the fruit-throwing disturbance in March while Chappelle was onstage at the Lensic Performing Arts Center in Santa Fe.
6Englander had acknowledged throwing a peel from a banana he had eaten earlier because he took offence to jokes that Chappelle, who is African-American, made about his companion, according to police.
7The friend reportedly was drawing in a sketchbook during the show when Chappelle noticed and asked the man his name, which he gave as "Johnny Appleseed."
8Chappelle then made jokes about the moniker, and police say that's why Englander got upset.
9"If I can, I would like to avoid a trial," Englander said Monday following a brief hearing before Judge Donita Sena in Magistrate Court.
10"I'm not afraid of the end result, but it's a waste of human resources."
11Englander said there are "real criminals out there that they can try. If there is a plea offering, I'll take it."
12Englander's attorney, Roderick Thompson, said while there is always the possibility of a plea bargain, he could not say more about it.
13The case has taken on racial overtones and attracted widespread attention after Chappelle said throwing the banana peel was a racist act.
14Englander said Monday that after the police apprehended him on March 30, Chappelle came to the police station to interrogate him.
15There Chappelle asked him, "Don't you know bananas are racist?"
16Englander said he told Chappelle, 41, he did not agree and that Chappelle then responded with profanity-laced insults about Englander and his mother.
17Lt. Andrea Dobyns of the Santa Fe Police Department confirmed that Chappelle appeared at the police station that night to talk to Englander.
18A representative of the Gersh Agency, which represents Chappelle, declined comment Monday.
