9The government has even deployed school buses to supplement the overstretched service.
10In another sign of life inching back to normal, banks in Kathmandu opened for few hours Wednesday and stuffed their ATMs with cash.
11At the Standard Chartered Bank in the city, people are lining up.
12"I needed money in cash so I can take my family out of Kathmandu. I want to be out of here for at least a few days," said Suraj Shrestha.
13He wasn't sure if ATMs outside the city were dispensing cash and wanted to carry "as much as possible."
14About 200 people have blocked traffic in Kathmandu to protest the slow pace of aid delivery.
15The protesters faced off with police and there were minor scuffles but no arrests were made.
16One protester says they haven't received any relief.
17"We are hungry, we haven't had anything to drink. We haven't been able to sleep. I have a 7-year-old child who is sleeping in the open. It's getting cold and people are getting pneumonia," he said.
18He accused the government of not doing enough.
19The death toll has climbed to 5,093 and more than 8 million people have been affected.
20Police in Nepal say the death toll from Saturday's earthquake has topped 5,000.
21The Kathmandu police say 4,989 have died and another 10,260 people have been injured in Nepal.
22The quake that was centered just outside Kathmandu also triggered an avalanche that killed at least 18 people at the Everest base camp.
23Another 61 were killed in neighboring India and Bangladesh, and China's official Xinhua News Agency reported 25 dead in Tibet.
24Police have arrested dozens of people on suspicion of looting abandoned homes as well as causing panic by spreading rumors of another big quake.
25Police official Bigyan Raj Sharma says 27 have been detained for stealing from homes whose owners moved out following Saturday's quake and powerful aftershocks.
26The aftershocks have waned but people are still anxious, many preferring to stay out in the open.
27Sharma says another four people were arrested for spreading false rumors of an impending quake through social media and text messages.
28The first aid shipments have reached Dhading district, close to the epicenter of the devastating earthquake in Nepal.
29U.N. food agency emergency officer Geoff Pinnock says the distribution in remote quake-hit villages will start Wednesday, but cautions it would take time.
30In Gorkha, the neighboring district to the west, five cargo trucks filled with rice, cooking oil and sugar stood on a grassy field in Majuwa village waiting for a helicopter from Kathmandu to take the supplies to the hardest-hit areas of that district.
31The World Food Program also expects the delivery of high-energy biscuits, which will be send out to areas without enough water for cooking.

33Rishi Khanal tells The Associated Press that he had given up all hope of rescue as his lips cracked and his nails turned white.
34There were dead bodies around him and a terrible smell.
35But he kept banging on the rubble all around him and eventually this brought a French rescue team that extracted him after an operation lasting many hours.
36He's now being treated for leg injuries at a hospital in the capital the day after French rescuers found him and brought him out from a collapsed hotel.

39Municipal workers on Wednesday began cleaning the streets, and the "kalimati bazaar" - the vegetable market - has reopened.
40Before first light, truckloads of fresh produce were unloaded and customers began to arrive.

44Others are simply scared of staying on so close to the quake's epicenter.

50Phillip Ewert, Operations Director for World Vision in Nepal, says, "we know the clock is ticking for those impacted by the earthquake in some of the most remote areas - aid is a matter of life or death for many at this point."
