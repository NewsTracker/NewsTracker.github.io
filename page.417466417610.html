Copter chartered by aid group crashes in Nepal, killing 4

1KATHMANDU, Nepal (AP) - A helicopter chartered by Doctors Without Borders crashed in quake-damaged northeast Nepal on Tuesday and four people on board were killed, police and the aid group said.
2The helicopter was returning to Kathmandu when it crashed on Yamuna Danda hill near Balefi village, Nepal Police spokesman Kamal Singh Bam said.
3The bodies of three men and a woman were recovered and police rescuers were trying to return the bodies to Kathmandu, he said.
4The crash occurred in a severely earthquake-damaged region, and the government and aid agencies have used helicopters to carry relief materials to mountainous regions where damage or the lack of existing roads has made delivery of aid all but impossible.
5The website of the aid group confirmed it had chartered the flight and was working to confirm the victim's identities.
6The group's Dutch branch said one victim was Dutch but did not release the person's identity.
7"Our thoughts go out to the families and friends of all the people who lost their lives in this terrible accident," the Dutch organization said.
8A U.S. Marines UH-1 "Huey" helicopter crashed last month in a nearby district while on a rescue mission, killing six Marines and two Nepalese soldiers.
9The earthquakes on April 25 and May 12 in Nepal have already killed more than 8,600 people.
