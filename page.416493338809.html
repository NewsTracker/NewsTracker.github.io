Man Charged With Making Threats in Islamic State Group Case

1A Minneapolis man has been charged with threatening law enforcement officials after authorities charged six men earlier this week with trying to travel to Syria to join the Islamic State group.
2Mahamed Abukar Said (sy-EED') appeared in federal court in St. Paul on Friday.
3Assistant U.S. Attorney Tracy Perzel says Said tweeted threats.
4He's accused of tweeting one threat that said if the men weren't freed, there would be a "massacre."
5He is also accused of tweeting a picture of the FBI informant in the case.
6Said's attorney, Shannon Elkins, didn't immediately return a phone message from The Associated Press.
7A judge on Thursday had ordered four of the men accused of trying to join the Islamic State group to remain in custody as their case proceeds.
