52Apple's early advertising promoted the watch as a fashion accessory, with elegant design and numerous options in watch faces, bands and other features.
53But the company is also emphasizing the new technology it's developed, including the "tapping" feature that signals alerts and messages, and new apps specifically designed for the watch's relatively small screen.
54Software apps and Internet services were a vital part of the iPhone's success, and Apple has made sure the new watch will have a wide range of apps available from the start.
55Along with a host of Apple-designed apps, such as Maps, Mail, Siri and Apple Pay, watch owners will be able to download numerous apps developed by outside companies.
56Several media companies have developed apps to provide headlines and quick news updates for the Apple Watch.
57These include news outlets like CNN, NPR and the New York Times, along with the sports-focused ESPN and MLB.com.
58The Times promises one-sentence articles "crafted specially" for the watch, along with photographs and "short, bulleted summaries" of news developments.
59Travel and transportation information will be available through apps developed by the car-hailing service Uber, mapping services like CityMapper and SafeMap, and travel booking services such as TripAdvisor and Expedia.
60Travelers can check their flights on an American Airlines app or unlock their door with a Starwood Hotels app that turns the watch into a wireless room key.
61An app from Babbel promises to help wearers learn new words in foreign languages.
62Not surprisingly, given the popularity of fitness wristbands, a number of apps are promising to help watch-owners track their health and exercise routines.
63Apps from Nike, Strava and Runtastic are all designed to measure workouts and physical exertion.
64An app called Tensio will help owners monitor their blood pressure.

66If you don't like your watch, the standard 14-day return policy applies in most cases.
67Most Apple Watches come with Apple's standard warranty - one year for hardware, plus 90 days of free telephone support.
68For luxury editions, you get two years of hardware repairs and telephone support.
69Extended coverage is available through AppleCare - $49 for the cheapest "Sport" version, $69 for the regular edition and $1,500 for the luxury version, known as Edition.
70For Sport and regular editions, the coverage gets you two years of repairs and support, including what comes with the warranty.
71With the luxury version, it's three years.
72In all cases, you're also protected from two incidents of accidental damage, though you're charged a service fee each time - $69 for Sport, $79 for the regular version and $1,000 for the luxury edition.
73The regular warranty typically doesn't cover repairs when it's your fault.
74You have 60 days after buying the watch to sign up for AppleCare.

127Why is Apple insisting on online orders, even for those who come to a store to try on the watch?
128Inventory management seems to be a big reason.
129Angela Ahrendts, Apple's senior vice president for its stores, said the company expects "strong customer demand will exceed our supply at launch. To provide the best experience and selection to as many customers as we can, we will be taking orders for Apple Watch exclusively online during the initial launch period."
130The try-on visit "gives the air of concierge service and something extra to the process, while operationally it's pretty smart for them," says Anne Zybowski, vice president for retail insights at the consulting firm Kantar Retail in Boston.
131It's not unprecedented in retail to have a sales representative walk you through your options before buying.
132Think wedding dresses and home furnishings.
133These visits are "more for people who are on the fence and want to explore what it is," says Ben Bajarin, principal analyst at Creative Strategies.
