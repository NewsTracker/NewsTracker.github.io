Microsoft increases paid leave for new parents

1SAN FRANCISCO - Microsoft says it will offer more paid leave to new parents and increase other benefits for U.S. employees, a day after Netflix announced generous new-parent benefits for its workers.
2Under its new policy, Microsoft Corp. will increase total paid leave for new mothers to 20 weeks, from a current 12 weeks paid and eight weeks of unpaid leave.
3New fathers will get 12 paid weeks, instead of four paid and eight unpaid.
4Microsoft is also increasing its contribution to employees' 401k plans.
5The move follows Netflix's offer of up to a year of paid parental leave.
6Many tech companies offer generous benefits as they compete to attract talented engineers and other workers.
7Netflix has about 2,000 workers, while about half of Microsoft's 110,000 workers are based in the United States.
