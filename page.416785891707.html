Greece makes IMF payment amid signs of bailout progress

1FILE - In this Thursday, Sept.
215, 2011 file photo, a worker cleans the paint-splattered by protesters entrance of the bank of Greece, in central Athens.
3Wednesday May 6 marks five years since Greece voted in its first bailout deal in the face of violent popular protests that left three dead.
4The day was followed by years of turmoil in which the country tried to overhaul its economy in the midst of a downturn matched in depth and length only by the Great Depression.
5Cash-strapped Greece managed to make a 200 million-euro ($222 million) repayment to the International Monetary Fund on Wednesday, as its long-stalled bailout negotiations appeared to make some progress.
6The country has a much larger repayment due next week that it will struggle to manage.
7On May 12, Greece must repay about 770 million euros to the IMF - and it is not clear whether it will be able to meet both that payment and pay some pensions and salaries due mid-month.
8Greece once more faces the possibility of defaulting on its debts in the next few weeks, which could set off a chain reaction that jeopardizes its membership in Europe's joint currency.
9The country's left-wing government has been locked in negotiations with its creditors and the institutions overseeing its bailout - the IMF, European Central Bank and European Commission - for the past three months over reforms required to unlock the remaining 7.2 billion euro installment of its 240 billion euro bailout.
10With no other significant external source of funding, Greece will not be able to continue meeting its debt repayments and pay for day-to-day spending without the rescue loans.
11In Brussels, technical talks that started last week were extended beyond Wednesday amid hopes for a breakthrough.
12A eurozone official, who asked not to be identified because the negotiations were ongoing, confirmed that there was now visible progress after the talks had been bogged down for weeks.
13Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras spoke by phone with European Commission President Jean-Claude Juncker Wednesday, and both said that "constructive talks should continue."
14They issued a joint statement saying the two discussed "progress made in the talks ... over the last few days" and spoke of details of the reforms Greece needs to implement.
15According to the statement, those include modernizing the pension system "so that it is fair, fiscally sustainable and effective in averting old-age poverty."
16They also discussed "the need for wage developments and labor market institutions to be supportive of job creation, competitiveness and social cohesion."
17Finance Minister Yanis Varoufakis was in Rome to discuss the issue with his Italian counterpart Pier Carlo Padoan, before heading to Madrid on Friday to meet his peer Luis de Guindos.
18Both sides hope to make progress by May 11, when eurozone finance ministers are due to meet.
19But expectations of a deal being reached by then have faded.
20On Tuesday, a government official in Athens attributed the latest snag in negotiations to disagreements between the IMF and European Commission on how to handle the Greek crisis.
21Facing an acute liquidity problem, the government has already ordered state enterprises, including municipalities and schools, to transfer their reserves into a Bank of Greece account where they can be used as a loan by the state.
22Wednesday's repayment of interest on IMF's loans was made without any problems.
23"The payment is proceeding normally," a finance ministry official said, on condition of anonymity in line with government regulations.
