Thousands stranded in Indonesian as volcano closes airports

1A volcanic eruption on Indonesia's main island of Java has sparked chaos for holidaymakers as airports close and international airlines cancel flights to tourist hotspot Bali, stranding thousands.
2Mount Raung in East Java province about 90 miles from Bali's international airport has been rumbling for several weeks.
3The level of activity increased in the past week and on Friday it blasted ash and debris 12,500 feet into the air.
4Government volcanologist Gede Suantika said the eruption has forced authorities to close five airports due to the risks posed by volcanic ash.
5The transport ministry has told airlines to avoid routes near the mountain.
6"For the sake of passenger safety, we have to close five airports."
7Airports on the islands of Bali and Lombok as well as airports at Banyuwangi and Jember in East Java have been closed since late Thursday.
8All flights are canceled until late Friday, which could be extended if the ash worsens.
9Barata said thousands of travelers are stranded.
10The volcano has proven particularly problematic for Australians, who flock to Bali during Australia's school holidays.
11Dozens of flights between Australia and Bali's Denpasar airport by Australian carriers Jetstar and Virgin Australia have been cancelled over the past week, with the airlines citing safety concerns.
12Both carriers said they are looking into adding extra flights between Australia and Bali when conditions improve to help clear the backlog.
