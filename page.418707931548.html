Apple gives weak forecast, shares fall nearly 7 percent

1BENGALURU/SAN FRANCISCO Apple Inc (AAPL.O) shares slumped nearly 7 percent in after-hours trading as its fourth-quarter revenue forecast fell short of estimates and it missed some targets for iPhone sales.
2The shares dropped to $121 after the company released its results on Tuesday, from $130.75 at the close.
3The forecast overshadowed Apple's strong sales in China, which more than doubled to $13.23 billion from a year earlier.
4Apple Chief Financial Officer Luca Maestri said in an interview the results in China were "spectacular" during the quarter and noted plans to open 40 stores there over the next 12 months.
5Without disclosing specific figures, Maestri said sales of the Apple Watch beat the company's expectations.
6He noted that in the nine weeks since its launch in late April, the device has sold better than either iPhones or iPads over a similar period after their launch.
7Apple said on Tuesday it sold 47.5 million iPhones in the third quarter, up 35 percent from a year ago.
8But some analysts had expected around 49 million.
9Colin Gillis, an analyst for BGC Partners, said the results highlighted the vulnerability of Apple's dependence on the iPhone and the Chinese market's growing importance to the company.
10"Where are you going to find growth in the world?" he said.
11"You've done an amazing job sucking all the smartphone profits into your balance sheet, but smartphone sales are slowing. What's going to happen when the industry matures, just like PCs did?"
12The company forecast revenue of $49 billion to $51 billion, missing analysts' average estimate of $51.13 billion according to Thomson Reuters I/B/E/S.
13The company's iPhone 6 and 6 Plus, which shattered iPhone sales records when they were launched, are already 10 months old.
14Apple had a troubled day, with its App Store, Apple Music, iTunes Store and some other services suffering disruption for more than three hours before results were released.
15The company said net income rose to $10.68 billion, or $1.85 per share, from $7.75 billion, or $1.28 per share, a year earlier.
16Analysts had expected earnings of $1.81 per share, according to Thomson Reuters I/B/E/S.
17Revenue rose 32.5 percent to $49.61 billion from a year earlier, beating Wall Street's expectations of $49.43 billion.
