7The box box that has housed the Wu-Tang Clan's secret album for much of the past year recently arrived at MoMA PS1 in Queens, carried by a white-gloved porter and flanked by security guards-but it almost didn't make it through customs.
8That was just one of the takeaways from an event at MoMA last night where album producer Cilvaringz joined Wu-Tang ringleader RZA to publicly discuss the album and play a 13 minute sampler in front of a crowd of journalists, art aficionados and lucky fans, all of whom were required to check phones at the door and pass through airport-level security.
9The two producers explained this would likely be the last time any part of the album was played in public for at least 88 years, which-as FORBES was the first to report-is the period of time during which its eventual buyer will be forbidden from releasing the work commercially.
10After reiterating that they decided to release just one copy of the album (dubbed The Wu - Once Upon A Time In Shaolin) in order to make a statement about the intrinsic value of music, the duo gave way to what associate curator Jenny Schlenzka dubbed "the first, the last, the only public listening session" for the record.
11Packed with pounding beats and the urgent, gritty rhymes that characterized much Wu-Tang's early work, the music fulfilled Cilvaringz's promise that the album would embody the spirit of the group's heyday.
12There were, of course, a few updates, namely the addition of guests including Cher.
13One detail that struck an odd chord on the recording, though, were the rappers' exhortations to their expected audience-the sort of "this is for my [insert group here]" lyrics that have long been a hip-hop hallmark.
14Once Upon A Time In Shaolin, after all, is really just for the lone person who purchases it.
15The idea behind the one-version release, however, is aimed at a broader audience.
16" The end goal is actually the exact opposite of elitism ," said RZA.
17"Yes, the album in itself will only be owned by someone who can afford it. Only one man can hold the scepter. But look beyond the prize and prestige of owning this album and focus on the statement it carries and you'll see that we're trying to re-attach values to music for the benefit of all kinds of musicians."
18The MoMA event concluded with a discussion between RZA, Cilvaringz and Genius executive editor Sasha Frere-Jones that bounced back and forth from topics including the group's goals for the album and the unusual logistical concerns associated with transporting a giant silver box from Morocco to New York on a commercial airliner.
19According to Cilvaringz, the latter nearly didn't happen.
20In the midst of his journey, airport security agents became concerned about the mysterious silver box he was hand-carrying to the U.S. When they asked him to open it, he declined-and had no choice, because the key used to open the box was in Marrakech.
21Cilvaringz said it took three hours before he made it through security-with the help of some calls from the team at Paddle8, the upstart auction house that will sell Once Upon A Time In Shaolin.
22Though the last backup copies of the album have been destroyed, seizure by airport security likely wouldn't have impacted the eventual sale of Once Upon A Time In Shaolin too greatly: the lone disc sits safely in a vault at the Royal Mansour hotel in Morocco.
