Apple Watch delivery slowed by faulty part, report says

1Apple Watches are displayed at an Apple Inc. store in New York, U.S., on Friday, April 10, 2015.
2Shipments of the highly anticipated watch are reportedly being slowed by a faulty component manufactured by one of Apple's suppliers in China.
3Shipments of the highly anticipated Apple Watch are reportedly being slowed by a faulty component manufactured by one of Apple's suppliers in China.
4The Wall Street Journal said the problem centers on a part called the taptic engine, a component designed to mimic being tapped on the wrist.
5The engine powers a small rod back and forth to alert users in lieu of a vibration or a ringer.
6Apple discovered that some taptic engines supplied by AAC Technologies Holdings Inc. in the southern factory hub of Shenzhen began to falter, according to the Journal report.
7Watches with the broken parts were being scrapped and Apple is hoping to increase production at the company's second supplier, Nidec Corp. of Japan.
8Apple is also looking at adding new suppliers such as Hon Hai Precision Industry Co., better known as Foxconn, the report said.
9Apple told the Los Angeles Times it won't comment on "rumor and speculation."
10The setback complicates the rollout of the Apple Watch, which is only being offered online and in a handful of special boutiques.
11It is not available in Apple retail stores, the result of limited supplies and the need to market the watch differently from other Apple products.
12"Apple Watch is also our most personal product yet, with multiple case and band options because it's an object of self-expression," wrote Angela Ahrendts, Apple's head of retail, in a memo to employees.
13"Given the high interest and initial supply at launch, we will be able to get customers the model they want earlier and faster by taking orders online."
14Analysts expect Apple to sell between eight and 10 million watches in the first year.
15A problem with the watch's components would follow a supplier issue with Apple's larger-screened iPhone 6 and 6 Plus introduced last year.
16The phones were expected to have display screens made of sapphire, but Apple's supplier GT Advanced couldn't meet the requirements.
17The supplier filed for bankruptcy in October.
