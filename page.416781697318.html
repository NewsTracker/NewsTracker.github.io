Rolling Stones' Jagger, Wood record Nepal quake charity single

1Keith Richards, Mick Jagger (C) and Ronnie Wood (L) of the Rolling Stones perform during their '14 on Fire' concert at the Hayarkon Park in Tel Aviv June 4, 2014.
2Singer Beverly Knight arrives for the Elle Style Awards at the Atlantis Gallery in east London February 20, 2006.
3LONDON (Reuters) - The Rolling Stones' Mick Jagger and Ronnie Wood have joined forces with British soul singer Beverley Knight to record a charity single in aid of victims of last month's earthquake in Nepal.
4The track, entitled "Look into Your Heart" will raise funds for charity Save The Children's Nepal Earthquake Appeal.
5Wood said he was "delighted" to be able to take part in the recording, which features Knight on lead vocals and Jagger on backing vocals.
6They are accompanied by a 100-strong assembled choir of the charity's supporters.
7"The terrible events in Nepal show just how vulnerable our fellow man can be in the face of nature," Knight said.
8"My hope is that the proceeds of this song will go some way to help raise urgently needed funds to repair homes and restore lives."
