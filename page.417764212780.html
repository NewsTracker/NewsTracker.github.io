Kurds nearing key Islamic State-held Syrian border town

10The mass displacement of Syrians came as Kurdish fighters announced they are making headway toward the border town of Tal Abyad, a stronghold of the extremist group near the Turkish border.
11The loss of Tal Abyad would be a major blow to the Islamic State group as it is a major avenue for commerce for the extremist group.
12AKCAKALE, Turkey (AP) - Syrian Kurdish fighters closed in on the outskirts of a strategic Islamic State-held town on the Turkish border Sunday, Kurdish officials and an activist group said, potentially cutting off a key supply line for the extremists' nearby de facto capital.
13Taking Tal Abyad, some 80 kilometers (50 miles) from the Islamic State stronghold of Raqqa, would mean the group wouldn't have a direct route to bring in new foreign militants or supplies.
14The Kurdish advance, coming under the cover of intense U.S.-led coalition airstrikes in the area, also would link their two fronts and put even more pressure on Raqqa as Iraqi forces struggle to contain the group in their country.
15Meanwhile, hundreds trying to flee the offensive pressed against the barbed wire fences separating the town from Turkey, as masked militants likely belonging to the Islamic State group could be seen as Turkish soldiers watched from the other side.
16On Sunday, Kurdish official Idriss Naasan said that Islamic State fighters have fled from Suluk, a few kilometers (miles) southwest of Tal Abyad, and that Kurds now hold the town.
17The Britain-based Syrian Observatory for Human Rights also said Islamic State fighters had withdrawn.
18The Observatory said the Kurds are about 5 kilometers (3 miles) from Tal Abyad.
19"It's only a matter of time before this area is liberated," Naasan told The Associated Press by telephone from northern Syria, saying the Kurds surround Tal Abyad from the east, west and south.
20The Turkish border - and the soldiers there - now hem the extremists in from the north.
21However, Rami Abdurrahman, who heads the Observatory, said Islamic State fighters still control the road linking the Turkish border with Raqqa.
22Since the beginning of May, members of the main Syrian Kurdish force, the People's Protection Units, or YPG, have taken more than 200 Kurdish and Christian towns in northeastern Syria, as well as strategic mountains seized earlier by the Islamic State group.
23They have pushed into Raqqa province, a stronghold of the Islamic State group.
24Along the way, they have picked up ammunition, weapons and vehicles left behind by the jihadis, almost mirroring the way the extremists overran Iraqi positions last year in their sweep across a third of that country.
25The Islamic State group has declared areas of Syria and Iraq is holds as part of its self-declared "caliphate," demanding the loyalty of the world's Muslims.
26Their gruesome propaganda videos of mass killings have drawn in foreign fighters, many coming in over the border from Syria.
27In recent weeks, the Islamic State group has relied more and more on foreign fighters to carry out the coordinated suicide bombings that have pushed out Iraqi forces.
28Even if Kurds cut off Tal Abyad from Raqqa, the Islamic State group still could bring in fighters across the border in Syria's Aleppo province, where they still hold ground.
29However, that would be a round-about route that could expose them to other fighting amid the long Syrian civil war against President Bashar Assad.
30In Syria, a country now split mostly between Islamic militants and forces loyal to Assad, the U.S. has found a reliable partner in the YPG, the country's strongest Kurdish militia.
31They are moderate, mostly secular fighters, driven by revolutionary fervor and a desire to eventually have a nation of their own carved out in the region.
32U.S. airstrikes continued Sunday in the area, as an Associated Press journalist on the Turkish side of the border from Tal Abyad saw one strike east of the town.
33Nasser Haj Mansour, a defense official in Syria's Kurdish region, said YPG officials are coordinating with the U.S.-led coalition regarding a possible attack on Tal Abyad.
34He added that the aerial coverage prevented the Islamic State group from brining reinforcements to the area.
