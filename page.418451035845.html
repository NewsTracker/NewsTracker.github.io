At least two dead in Ukraine sports centre attack

1At least two people were killed on Saturday in a gun and grenade attack in a western Ukraine city carried out by Right Sector, a far-right nationalist militia.
2The violence in the city of Mukhachevo, near the Polish and Hungarian borders, broke out at a sports facility reportedly controlled by a national parliament member from a faction opposed to Right Sector.
3Anton Gerashchenko, an adviser to the interior ministry, said three were killed and that three policemen and four civilians were wounded.
4He also said the attackers destroyed two police cars with grenade launchers.
5The Right Sector statement claimed its members were attacked by "bandits" connected to Mikhail Lano, who controlled the sports facility, which local media said included a gym and swimming pool.
6Mustafa Nayyem, a Ukrainian journalist and member of parliament who arrived on the scene this afternoon, said locals told him the fight was about control of the contraband cigarette market.
7Right Sector was one of the most militant factions in the massive protests in Ukraine's capital that led to Viktor Yanukovych, the pro-Russia president, fleeing the country in February 2014.
8After war broke out in eastern Ukraine between government forces and pro-Russia separatists several months later, Right Sector has fought on the government side and Amnesty International has accused the group of holding civilians as prisoners and torturing them.
