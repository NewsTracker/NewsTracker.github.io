1California Pacific Medical Center surgeon Dr. William Bry said the "kidney paired donations" will be possible thanks to an altruistic donor who started a chain of donations, KGO-TV reported Wednesday.
2Zully Broussard of Sacramento agreed to give a kidney to a man who has a relative willing to donate a kidney but was not compatible with him.
3That man's relative, in turn, will donate a kidney to a patient who also has a relative or friend willing to donate but unable to because of compatibility issues.
4This chain of donations continues until the remaining four people needing transplants get kidneys.
5Broussard said her son died of cancer 13 years ago and her husband passed away 14 months ago, also from cancer.
6"I know what it feels like to want an extra day," she said when asked why she volunteered to donate a kidney to a stranger.
7Three transplants are planned for Thursday and the remaining three on Friday.
8Most of those involved are from the San Francisco Bay Area.
9"I thought I was going to help this one person who I don't know, but the fact that so many people can have a life extension, that's pretty big," Broussard said.
10This paired kidney exchange is achieved thanks to a software called MatchGrid, developed by David Jacobs, who is a kidney recipient.
