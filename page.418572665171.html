Islamic State affiliate Sinai Province claims attack on Egypt navy vessel

1This image posted on a militant social media account affiliated with the Egyptian branch of Islamic State shows a fireball rising from an Egyptian Navy vessel.
2Cairo: Egypt's Islamic State affiliate said on Thursday it fired a rocket at an Egyptian naval vessel in the Mediterranean Sea near the coast of Israel and the Gaza Strip.
3The militant group Sinai Province has focused mainly on attacking Egyptian soldiers and police in the Sinai peninsula, killing hundreds since the army toppled Islamist president Mohamed Morsi in 2013 after mass protests against his rule.
4Smokes rises from the Egyptian Navy vessel on the coast of northern Sinai.
5Photographs distributed online by the group appeared to show a rocket heading towards a ship and setting it ablaze on impact, though the militants' version of events could not be verified.
6The Egyptian military said in a statement a coastguard launch had exchanged shots with "terrorist elements", causing the vessel to catch fire.
7It said no one was killed in the attack.
8A witness in Gaza saw a plume of dark grey smoke rising from a boat off the coast.
9Other witnesses in the Palestinian enclave said they heard explosions and gunfire.
10An Israeli military spokeswoman said Israel was not involved in the incident and had not been asked to assist.
11Military sources said the suspected militants had fled after firing on the vessel.
12Such incidents at sea are rare, though Egypt is battling an increasingly brazen Islamist insurgency in the peninsula that lies between Israel, the Gaza Strip and the Suez Canal.
13Sinai Province, the most lethal militant group in Egypt, last year pledged loyalty to Islamic State, which controls large tracts of territory in Syria and Iraq and has a presence in Egypt's neighbour Libya.
14It has recently carried out high profile attacks which prompted the drafting of a sweeping counter-terrorism law.
15On July 1, 100 militants and at least 17 members of the security forces were killed in a single day of clashes and attacks claimed by Sinai Province, authorities said.
