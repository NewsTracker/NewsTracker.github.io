7KIEV, Ukraine - The Ukrainian government and the separatist rebels blamed each other Friday for violating a fragile cease-fire dozens of times, sparking fears of wider hostilities in war-torn eastern Ukraine.
8Amid the tensions, Moscow sternly warned cash-strapped Ukraine that its payment for Russian gas would only last for a few more days, raising the specter of another gas war with potential repercussions for Europe.
9A Ukrainian military spokesman said that the Russia-backed rebels fired on Ukrainian positions nearly 50 times in the past 24 hours and that Russia sent more tanks into Ukraine despite a cease-fire that was supposed to begin Sunday.
10The rebels, meanwhile, claimed that Ukrainian forces had violated the cease-fire more than 20 times Friday.
11The government claims, which followed the rebel seizure of the key rail hub of Debaltseve, raised the question of whether weeks of high-level diplomacy aimed at producing a cease-fire and a peace plan had simply allowed the rebels to redouble efforts to grab more territory.
12The village of Kurakhovo, west of the rebel stronghold of Donetsk, was hit by Grad rockets and the village of Berdyansk, near the key port city of Mariupol, was hit overnight by artillery and mortar fire, Lt. Col.
13Anatoliy Stelmakh, a Ukrainian military spokesman, told reporters.
14Russia is still moving military equipment into Ukraine, including 10 tanks brought into Novoazovsk, near Mariupol, he added.
15French President Francois Hollande, who brokered the peace deal last week together with German Chancellor Angela Merkel, said Friday that he did not have confirmation about Russian tanks entering Ukraine.
