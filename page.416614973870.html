The Latest on Nepal: In ravaged hamlets, lives were spared

1Chinese authorities have closed all climbing on the Tibetan side of Mount Everest for the spring.
2Meanwhile, some climbers in Nepal have announced plans to try to return to the mountain, a move that was criticized by others in the climbing community.
3No one climbing on the north side was injured following Saturday's deadly quake.
4But climbing guide Adrian Ballinger says Chinese officials announced their decision to end the climbing season.
5Ballinger said two reasons were cited: Safety concerns over possible additional earthquakes and solidarity with Nepal and the Sherpas.
6China reported has 25 deaths in Tibet from the quake.
7President Barack Obama has spoken to Prime Minister Sushil Koirala of Nepal to express sympathy over the thousands of deaths and vast destruction.
8White House spokesman Josh Earnest says the two leaders discussed U.S. military and civilian efforts already underway to help Nepal and international aid groups in their emergency rescue and response.
9They discussed the ongoing efforts by U.S. civilian and military personnel to assist the government of Nepal and international organizations in the disaster response efforts, including provision of rescue and logistics support.
10The Nepal Mountaineering Association has increased to 19 the number of fatalities from the avalanche on Mount Everest.
11It said Wednesday that five of the dead were foreign climbers and 14 were Nepalese Sherpa guides.
12The avalanche triggered by Saturday's 7.8-magnitude quake also injured dozens of people when it swept through base camp, knocking over tents and people.
13All climbers have now left Everest, ending the climbing season early for the second year.
14Last year, climbs were cancelled after 16 Sherpa guides were killed in an avalanche while hauling gear between camps.
15Hundreds of people attempt to scale the world's tallest mountain each year, and Nepal earns significant income from the permit fees.
16The tiny hamlets clinging to the mountainsides of Gorkha District were ravaged by the earthquake.
17But lives were spared.
18Entire clusters of homes are piles of stone and splintered wood.
19Orange plastic tarps now dot the cliffs and terraced rice paddies carved into the Himalayan land.
20But the death toll in villages like Gumda was far lower than feared.
21The quake on Saturday struck at midday, and many people were working outdoors in the rural region in spring.
22Of Gumda's 1,300 residents, five were killed in the quake and 20 more were injured.
23The official overall toll as of Wednesday evening exceeded 5,100 in Nepal, India and Tibet.
24The United Nations is appealing for $415 million to provide for vital needs in Nepal over the next three months.
25It intends to support government efforts in provide shelter, water and sanitation, emergency health, food, and protection.
26The U.N.'s humanitarian coordinator for Nepal, Jamie McGoldrick, said the response to date was encouraging, but those efforts need to be maintained, especially in remote areas.
27He noted that the coming monsoon season would like add a logistical challenge to relief efforts, adding to the urgency.
28Helicopters have rescued some 210 foreign trekkers and local villagers stranded in the Lantang area north of Nepal's capital.
29The international trekkers were stranded in Langtang, a popular trekking route bordering Tibet, since the earthquake on Saturday that killed more than 5,000 people.
30Government administrator Gautam Rimal said the trekkers and villagers were flown to the nearby town of Dhunche.
31But with landslides cutting off the roads from there to Kathmandu, the trekkers will have to trudge for at least four hours before they can board a bus to the Nepalese capital.
32The number of people missing after a mudslide struck a village in central Nepal appears to be lower than first thought.
33The government had said Tuesday that 250 people were missing after a mudslide and avalanche hit the village of Ghodatabela near the quake epicenter.
34Government administrator Gautam Rimal said on Wednesday, however, "We believe now there are only a few people who were swept by the mudslide."
35Authorities withdrew the figure of 250 after getting more information from local residents, but Rimal didn't have an amended figure.
36Nepalese rescue helicopters are taking advantage of breaks in the rain to bring out the injured from remote mountain villages where aid is only beginning to trickle in, four days after the massive earthquake.
37A rescue mission on Wednesday landed in the village of Darkha, about 100 kilometers (60 miles) northwest of the capital, Kathmandu, and unloaded boxes of aid supplies.
38Nepalese soldiers disembarked and carried back on a stretcher the 69-year-old Ek Bahadur Thapa and others in need of treatment.
39The government says more than 10,000 people were injured.
40The first 44 Spaniards who were stranded in Nepal have returned home on an air force plane with Foreign Minister Jose Manuel Garcia Margallo accompanying them.
41The group is the first of some 127 to be evacuated by the government.
42About 500 Spaniards were affected by the earthquake and 103 have yet to be located.
43More than 20 were able to return by their own means.
44A second Spanish plane was due to bring back another group of Spaniards as well as other European and Latin American citizens later Wednesday.
45The quake occurred while Margallo was on an official visit to New Delhi.
46In another sign of life inching back to normal, banks in Kathmandu opened for few hours Wednesday and stuffed their ATMs with cash.
47At the Standard Chartered Bank in the city, people are lining up.
48"I needed money in cash so I can take my family out of Kathmandu. I want to be out of here for at least a few days," said Suraj Shrestha.
49He wasn't sure if ATMs outside the city were dispensing cash and wanted to carry "as much as possible."
50About 200 people have blocked traffic in Kathmandu to protest the slow pace of aid delivery.
51The protesters faced off with police and there were minor scuffles but no arrests were made.
52One protester says they haven't received any relief.
53"We are hungry, we haven't had anything to drink. We haven't been able to sleep. I have a 7-year-old child who is sleeping in the open. It's getting cold and people are getting pneumonia," he said.
54He accused the government of not doing enough.
55The death toll has climbed to 5,093 and more than 8 million people have been affected.
56Police in Nepal say the death toll from Saturday's earthquake has topped 5,000.
57The Kathmandu police say 4,989 have died and another 10,260 people have been injured in Nepal.
58The quake that was centered just outside Kathmandu also triggered an avalanche that killed at least 18 people at the Everest base camp.
59Another 61 were killed in neighboring India and Bangladesh, and China's official Xinhua News Agency reported 25 dead in Tibet.
60Police have arrested dozens of people on suspicion of looting abandoned homes as well as causing panic by spreading rumors of another big quake.
61Police official Bigyan Raj Sharma says 27 have been detained for stealing from homes whose owners moved out following Saturday's quake and powerful aftershocks.
62The aftershocks have waned but people are still anxious, many preferring to stay out in the open.
63Sharma says another four people were arrested for spreading false rumors of an impending quake through social media and text messages.
