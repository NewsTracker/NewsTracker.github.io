Rescuers pull survivor from rubble 5 days after Nepal quake

1KATHMANDU, Nepal (AP) " Crowds cheered Thursday as a teenage boy was pulled, dazed and dusty, from the wreckage of a seven-story Kathmandu building that collapsed around him five days ago when an enormous earthquake shook Nepal.
2Pemba Tamang was carried out on a stretcher, his face was covered in dust.
3Medics had put an IV drip into his arm and a blue brace had been placed around his neck.
4He appeared stunned, and his eyes blinked in the sunlight.
5Nepalese rescuers, with support from an American disaster response team, had been working for hours to free Tamang, who police said was 18 years old.
6L.B. Basnet, the police officer who crawled into a gap in the rubble to reach Tamang, said he was surprisingly responsive.
7"He thanked me when I first approached him," said Basnet.
8"He told me his name, his address, and I gave him some water. I assured him we were near to him."
9Rescuers eventually used jacks to lift the concrete slabs that had wedged him in, said Basnet.
10An American disaster response team had been helping the Nepalese.
11"He's not too far down, but the floors have collapsed and he'd pancaked between them," Andrew Olvera, who is heading the team from the U.S. Agency for International Development, said shortly before the boy was freed.
12Twisted ropes of steel reinforcing rods were all that stopped huge concrete slabs from falling onto the scene.
13Two concrete floors hung down in front of the building like curtains.
14"The whole operation is dangerous," Olvera said.
15"But it's risk versus gain. To save a human life, we'll risk almost anything."
16The rescue was a rare bit of good news in a city that has known little but despair since the earthquake hit Saturday, leaving more than 5,500 people dead across this poverty-wracked Himalayan nation.
17Asked how Tamang had lasted for so many days, Basnet replied: "He survived by good faith."
