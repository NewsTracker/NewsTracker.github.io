Bali nine executions: Indonesia can't be sure it will be this month as other drug felons appeal

1The executions of 10 drug felons on death row in Indonesia are waiting on the outcomes of legal appeals.
2The Attorney-General H.M. Prasetyo has previously been adamant that all legal avenues had been exhausted for the 10 prisoners - including Australians Andrew Chan and Myuran Sukumaran - after the Indonesian president rejected their clemency pleas.
3However in a major departure, the Attorney-General's spokesman Tony Spontana said one of the felons was requesting a judicial review of her case and the government "had to respect the ongoing legal process".
4Mr Spontana said he couldn't be sure the prisoners would be executed this month.
5Filipino woman Mary Jane Fiesta Veloso, who was caught smuggling 2.6 kilograms of heroin into Yogyakarta, has had her application for a judicial review referred to the Supreme Court.
6Frenchman Serge Atloui, who was arrested at an ecstasy laboratory in Tangerang in 2005, has also requested a judicial review of his case which will be held on March 11.
7Sources told Fairfax Media that reports the executions would be delayed indefinitely were inaccurate and the Indonesian government was merely waiting on the outcome of court cases scheduled for next week.
8Meanwhile Chan and Sukumaran have spent three lonely days at Nusakambangan, with their families so far blocked from visiting them on the penal island where Indonesia's government plans to execute them.
9Refusing to show any latitude to the emotionally battered families, Indonesian officials are saying they can only see the pair between 9am and 1pm on Mondays and Wednesdays.
10Australian consular staff are negotiating for some leniency but the head of the Central Java justice office, Mirza Zukarnain, said there could be no compromise.
11"No exception. We can't make allowances for certain families only. It won't be fair for the others," he said.
12The stand-off comes as Indonesian President Joko Widodo insisted there were "no problems" with its relationship with Australia, despite outrage at the extraordinary military and police operation to transfer the reformed drug smugglers to Nusakambangan.
13"We are still good friends, good neighbours but the death penalty is our legal sovereignty," Mr Joko told reporters in East Java.
14Foreign Affairs Minister Julie Bishop described the "disproportionate" transfer of Chan and Sukumaran and the release of photos showing them with a smiling Bali police chief as "undignified and degrading" on Friday.
15"It seems that our citizens have been singled out for treatment designed to maximise publicity that was certainly at odds with the treatment of other citizens of other countries in the same position," she said.
16Dozens of heavily armed police and military personnel accompanied Chan and Sukumaran duo during the transfer, while hundreds more secured roads and airports.
17Sukhoi fighters jets shadowed the plane that carried them from Bali to Central Java.
18Other drug felons due to be executed were transferred in a far more low key fashion, often in just a single prison van.
19Australian government ministers have described their treatment as "macabre".
20Mr Spontana defended the militarised operation, saying it was required "to ensure a smooth transfer".
21Foreign Affairs deputy secretary Greg Moriarty, who was Australia's ambassador in Jakarta until last year, spoke with ambassador Nadjib Riphat Kesoema on Thursday night to register Australia's displeasure.
22Ms Bishop intends to meet with the ambassador next week.
23Chan and Sukumaran are being held in isolation cells on Besi prison, or iron prison, one of seven jails on Nusakambangan.
24They are incarcerated in separate cells but can call out to each other.
25They have no contact with other inmates at the prison other than Nigerian Raheem Agbaje Salami, who is also due to be slated for death by firing squad.
26On Friday morning, the men's lawyer, Julian McMahon, and Australia's consul-general to Bali, Majell Hind, pushed through a frenzied media mob and anti-drugs protesters to board the ferry to Nusakambangan.
27It is understood Chan and Sukumaran are being treated well and are in good spirits.
28They are reportedly looking forward to their family's visit on Monday.
29Anti-drug demonstrators marched to the ferry dock singing the Indonesian national anthem and carrying a grisly post wrapped in sheets with a face scrawled with coloured pen, made to resemble a corpse wrapped in a traditional Muslim shroud for burial.
30The shroud, known as kain kafan in Indonesian, is the prescribed length of cloth used in Muslim burials to wrap the body of a dead person.
31They urged that the Australians be shot and carried signs saying "We love Indonesia. Save Indonesia generation" and "Go to hell".
32The scenes came as Indonesia flagged a delay in the duo's pair's execution of up to 10 days.
33The pause should give their lawyers time to pursue a legal appeal lodged on Monday with the state administrative court in Jakarta.
34The action is the second attempt to challenge the rejection of Bali nine drug smugglers clemency pleas by Mr Joko.
35Lawyers for the pair say Mr Joko rebuffed Chan and Sukumaran's petition for mercy without properly considering their specific circumstances.
36With the guidance of prison staff, the pair transformed Kerobokan penitentiary in Bali into a model of rehabilitation.
37On December 9 last year, Mr Joko said: "The clemency requests are not on my table yet. But I guarantee that there will be no clemency for convicts who committed narcotics-related crimes."
38Mr Spontana, said "we have to respect the ongoing legal process", a reversal of the position the Attorney-General elucidated on Tuesday.
39Indonesia's Foreign Minister Retno Marsudi stressed on Friday that Indonesia was determined to "maintain a good relationship with Australia, with all countries".
40She said a United Nations account of a human rights dialogue quoting Indonesian diplomats saying the country could introduce a moratorium on the death penalty was a misrepresentation.
41"We protested because we never stated it and the uploaded version from the Office of the UN Human Rights High Commissioner has been revised in accordance to what was stated by the Indonesian delegation," she said.
42At the time of writing, the UN had not changed the document.
43Bali police spokesman Hery Wiyanto has revealed that an investigation will be launched into the media leak of a photo of a smiling Denpasar police chief with his arm on Chan's shoulder.
44Mr Hery said Senior Commissioner Djoko Hari Utomo did not violate any rules and would not be investigated.
45"It wasn't a selfie moment, it wasn't a pose," Mr Hery said.
46"He was talking to and encouraging the boys."
47However, he said intelligence officers and the internal affairs division of the police force were investigating who took the photo and how it reached the media.
48Disciplinary action would be taken if the investigation discovered the photo had been sold to the media.
