Sports in Nepal not spared by the earthquakes

1A revered national sports coach is among the thousands of dead, trapped in his rented house when devastating earthquakes rocked Nepal.
2The main football stadium was damaged, before becoming a makeshift refuge for displaced survivors of the quakes.
3The national cricket team's preparations for a major international tournament next are in disarray.
4The Asian weightlifting championships, scheduled for Kathmandu in July, are destined to be held somewhere else.
5The two recent earthquakes killed more than 8,500 people, and have had a devastating impact on all aspects of life in Nepal, including sports.
6Nepal Volleyball Association general secretary Jeetendra Bahadur Chand said coach Keshav Lal Shrestha's death in Kathmandu was "irreparable".
7"He was a dedicated and experienced coach," Chand said of Shrestha, who played in the 1982 Asian Games at New Delhi and was coaching for almost 30 years.
8"He was a dedicated and experienced coach whose demise is a great loss for sports in Nepal."
9Shrestha was among eight people associated with sports who were killed in the quakes, according to the National Sports Council's Deepak Bista, the member secretary of the players' welfare fund.
10Paralympian Jeet Bahadur Khadka and prominent sports writer Suman Bhomjan are among those to have died.
11The small but resilient national sports fraternity is determined to get things back on track.
12Pubundu Dassanayake is trying to prepare for the national cricket team for qualifiers for the World Twenty20 tournament.
13"We're not going to give up," he said.
14"The tournament is vital for the future of Nepal's cricket and we plan to go to India for preparations."
15Nepal qualified for the last World Twenty20 in Bangladesh in 2014 and is due to participate in the July 9-26 qualifying competition in Ireland and Scotland, hoping to secure a spot at the 2016 edition in India.
16"None of our players are harmed physically but they have suffered mental scars just like any other person," said Dassanayake.
17The Tribhuvan University ground's indoor cricket facility has sustained obvious damage, while the walls surrounding the Dasharath Stadium, which hosted the recent World Cup football qualifier against India, have collapsed.
18Both venues are being used as shelters for people displaced by the earthquake.
19The national football league has been suspended indefinitely.
20Nepal may also lose the rights to host the 2015 Asian weightlifting championships, which were due to be held in Kathmandu from July 24-30.
21The Asian weightlifting federation lists the championships as "to be confirmed" and has encouraged the Nepal organisers to host the 2016 championships instead.
22Battling power cuts, underfunding and unrest, Nepal's young team makes impressive debut in Bangladesh tournament.
