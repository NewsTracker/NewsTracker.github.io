Cash Transfer For Facebook Messenger

Myanmar Journalists Jailed

1Users of Facebook Messenger will soon be able to send and receive money between friends.
2The new feature will begin to be rolled out in the US over "the coming months", and will be free to use, the social network said.
3A dollar sign icon will be added below the text entry box, next to where users tap to add audio notes, images or emojis.
4Once the icon has been tapped, users will be able to enter the amount they wish to send and transfer it via their debit card.
5It is not known yet if the feature will be extended outside the US.
6Facebook has moved to reassure users that the system will be secure, pointing out that some financial transactions have been taking place on the site for years.
7A spokesman said: "A dependable and trusted payments processor for game players and advertisers since 2007, Facebook processes more than one million transactions daily on the site and also handles all the payments processed on Messenger.
8"Incorporating security best practices into our payments business has always been a top priority.
9"We use secure systems that encrypt the connection between you and Facebook as well as your card information when you ask us to store it for you.
10"These payment systems are kept in a secured environment that is separate from other parts of the Facebook network and that receive additional monitoring and control.
11The first time users access the service they will have to add a debit or credit card to their Facebook account.
12Users will then be able to create and use a pin code to complete transfers.
13Facebook confirmed that iPhone users will be able to use the Touch ID fingerprint scanner to send money.
14The feature will also be accessible on desktop computers.

2YANGON, Myanmar-A Myanmar court sentenced two reporters to two months in prison on charges of defamation, in the latest ruling that raises concerns about press freedoms as the country emerges from military rule.
3The Myanmar Post's chief editor Than Htaik Thu and reporter Hsan Moe Tun were charged in February 2014, after their weekly newspaper quoted a military lawmaker purportedly making comments about his colleagues in the parliament having low education.
4The lawmaker, Maj.
5Thein Zaw, from the Mon state, denied saying this and filed the defamation case against the pair.
6"The decision is a threat to media freedom," the journal's editor in chief Zin Thaw Naing said Thursday.
7"Myanmar's judicial system is not independent and is under pressure from the administration."
8This is the first time journalists have been convicted of defamation since President Thein Sein 's government took office in 2011.
9The charges carry a penalty of up to two years imprisonment.
10Ten other journalists are serving prison terms for other offenses and nearly 20 are facing trials.
11Under a half-century of dictatorship, Myanmar regularly banned dissent and imprisoned journalists and other critics of the ruling junta.
12Despite dramatic changes since 2011, when a nominally civilian government came to power, journalists are increasingly being arrested and imprisoned.
13Many in the media viewed the ruling as a message ahead of general elections scheduled for later this year.
14"Jailing journalists with defamation before the 2015 general elections is a clear indication the government is determined to restrict freedom of press. They are determined to silence our critical voices," said Wai Phyo, chief editor of the influential private newspaper Daily Eleven.
15He and four colleagues including the paper's chief executive are facing a similar charge.
16The government is also facing strong criticism from the local and international community for arresting and beating journalists during a crackdown on a recent protest.
17An international media organization, the International Press Institute, which will hold its annual congress in Yangon this month, called on the government to investigate the arrests and said the cycle of impunity needs to stop.
