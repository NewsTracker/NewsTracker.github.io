Baltimore racked by violence; governor calls out National Guard

1Firefighters battle flames that broke out during riots in Baltimore.
2A firefighter responds to a building front that caught fire as protests in Baltimore continue over the death of Freddie Gray.
3Police and firefighters respond to a burning building as protests over the death of Freddie Gray continue in Baltimore.
4A firefighter works to put out flames set by rioters in Baltimore late Monday.
5Family of Freddie Gray, who died after a fatal injury in police custody, pray inside a church after a news conference in Baltimore on Monday.
6The streets of this city erupted in violence Monday hours after the funeral of a black man who suffered a mortal injury in police custody, with hundreds of rioters setting police cars and businesses ablaze, throwing rocks, looting stores and injuring at least 15 officers, six of them seriously.
7Maryland Gov. Larry Hogan declared a state of emergency and called out the National Guard.
8Police from nearby towns converged to help restore order after a week of protests turned violent over the weekend, culminating Monday in running street battles.
9The governor of Maryland declared a state of emergency Monday after riots broke out in Baltimore in response to the death of Freddie Gray, a black man who died after suffering a severed spine while in police custody.
10Hours after the funeral of Freddie Gray - a man who died after being taken into police custody - Baltimore police and protesters face off.
11Rioters in the streets of Baltimore are looting stores.
12A Baltimore Orioles game at Camden Yards was abruptly canceled, sending hundreds of exiting fans streaming into the streets near downtown.
13Police in riot gear erected barricades around City Hall and the inner harbor tourist area as a string of structure fires burned in various parts of the city.
14"Too many people have spent a generation building up this city for it to be destroyed by thugs who in a very senseless way are trying to tear down what so many have fought for," Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake said as she declared a citywide curfew beginning 10 p.m. Tuesday.
15She said city schools would be closed Tuesday.
16The unrest was touched off by the April 19 death of Freddie Gray, 25, who had been arrested a week earlier and injured while in custody.
17His spine was partially severed, and how that happened is under investigation.
18Police are scheduled to release the results of their investigation Friday.
19On Monday, Baltimore police announced they had received a "credible threat" that three violent gangs - the Black Guerrilla Family, the Bloods and the Crips - were working together to "take out" law enforcement officers.
20"This is a group of lawless individuals with no regard for people," said police Capt.
21Eric Kowalczyk.
22"We don't know who is out there. We do know they are criminals and have attacked officers without provocation."
23U.S. Atty.
24Gen. Loretta Lynch - the first black woman to serve as the nation's top law enforcement officer - condemned the violence.
25She promised to work with community leaders to "protect the security and civil rights of all residents" as federal officials conduct an independent investigation into Gray's death.
26The unrest began in the afternoon when a few people confronted a phalanx of officers.
27Police Commissioner Anthony Batts said a call had gone out to high school students on social media urging demonstrators to gather at the Mondawmin Mall, then move toward City Hall.
28Batts said officials had information that "a large purge of high school students" would be there.
29He said 250 to 300 officers were at the mall, and they were outnumbered.
30"Yes, we planned for it," he said.
31"That wasn't the issue. We just had too many people out there" for the officers to handle.
32Fifteen officers were injured, including six seriously, Batts said.
33He called for parents to step up, citing a mother who had stopped her son.
34"I wish I had more parents that took charge of the kids out there tonight," he said.
35A 2013 movie called "The Purge" was based on the idea of a temporary suspension of all law.
36James Eldredge, 25, of Baltimore saw people throwing rocks near the mall.
37A nearby corner was littered with bricks and chunks of rock.
38"It's kind of sad to see what's going on here," he said.
39"It's not a movie, it's not Ferguson. This is home."
40Donte Reives, 24, who works security at the University of Maryland, watched the melee from the mall parking lot.
41"You hate to see your city like this," he said.
42"We're better than this.... This ain't for Freddie."
43A few blocks away, helicopters hovered overhead as crowds of people milled around.
44A firefighter standing by a hydrant said rioters had tried to cut fire hoses.
45George Roy, 49, a surgical technician, lives about six blocks from where Gray was arrested, and about a block from a CVS pharmacy that was set afire Monday.
46All the businesses up and down his block have been looted, he said, calling it "outright crazy."
47"They destroyed the city and they didn't wait for the investigation to occur," he said.
48"Now we don't have stores to go to. We don't have fast food we can eat at, because they've destroyed it all," Roy said.
49The violence came about two weeks after Gray's arrest.
50Gray and a friend were walking in West Baltimore on April 12 when, officials have said, Gray made eye contact with an officer and fled.
51Police gave chase.
52Videos of the arrest show Gray being put into a police van, dragging his feet, with his hands cuffed behind him.
53He can be heard screaming.
54During the trip to police headquarters, Gray asked for medical help, including for an inhaler, but did not receive it.
55The van stopped at least twice.
56At one stop, captured by another video, Gray was taken out of the van and placed on the ground.
57His legs were put in irons and he was returned to the van.
58At the second stop, another prisoner was put inside, separated from Gray by a metal barrier.
59There is no camera in the van.
60When officers reached headquarters and took Gray out of the van, he could not speak and was not breathing.
61Gray slipped into a coma, underwent surgery and died a week later.
62Gray died of a severed spine, officials have confirmed.
63The family says his voice box was crushed and his neck snapped.
64Demonstrations over Gray's death had been largely peaceful until Saturday, when 35 people were arrested and six officers injured.
65Afterward, Gray's twin sister, Fredericka Gray, called for peace.
66"My family wants to say, 'Can y'all please, please stop the violence?'" she said.
67"Freddie Gray would not want this. Freddie's father and mother do not want any violence. Violence does not get justice."
68The Gray family repeated that plea at a news conference late Monday.
69Earlier in the day, thousands of mourners gathered for Gray's televised funeral at a church.
70Rawlings-Blake attended, as did a representative of the White House, the Rev.
71Jesse Jackson, comedian and activist Dick Gregory, Rep.
72John Sarbanes (D-Md.)
73and Kweisi Mfume, formerly an NAACP leader and U.S representative from Maryland.
74Screens on the church walls showed the words, "Black Lives Matter & All Lives Matter," which have become slogans at demonstrations around the country since a white police officer shot to death an unarmed black man in Ferguson, Mo., in August.
75That case was followed by demonstrations over the deaths of African Americans at the hands of police in New York City; Cleveland; Tulsa, Okla.; and South Carolina.
76"The eyes of this country are all on us, because they want to see whether we have the stuff to make this right," William Murphy Jr., a lawyer who is representing the Gray family, told the mourners.
77"We need justice not just for Freddie Gray, for the Freddie Grays to come," he said.
78Another mourner was Erica Garner, 24, daughter of Eric Garner, who died in New York police custody while gasping, "I can't breathe."
79His arrest was videotaped by a bystander.
80Erica Garner said she came to Baltimore after seeing video of Gray's arrest.
81"It's like there is no accountability, no justice," she said.
82"It's like we're back in the '50s, back in the Martin Luther King days. When is our day to be free going to come?"
83Hours later, on the city's bitter streets, looting erupted at an East Baltimore shopping center called Church Square.
84Throngs of looters, mostly teenagers and young men, dashed into stores with an air of celebration and emerged with boxes of sneakers and armfuls of clothing.
85Nearly an hour passed before police arrived.
86Substitute teacher Loren Braswell urged restraint.
87"We got to think about what we're doing here," said Braswell, 53.
88"I love my city of Baltimore, but people got to think rationally and they're not doing that right now."
89But if the officers who caused Gray's death are not punished, Braswell said, "I think the city's going to burn down."
