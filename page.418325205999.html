6MOSCOW (AP) - In a story July 1 about Gazprom halting gas supplies to Ukraine, The Associated Press reported erroneously that Russia offered Ukraine a price of $247 per cubic meter.
7The price offered was per thousand cubic meters.
8MOSCOW - The Russian gas company Gazprom halted supplies to neighboring Ukraine on Wednesday after the collapse of pricing talks, a company official said - but an EU official said the dispute would not affect the flow of Russian gas to Europe.
9Gazprom CEO Alexei Miller said Russia stopped deliveries at 10 a.m. because Ukraine didn't make an advance payment for July's gas.
10"Gazprom is not going to send gas to Ukraine at any price without the advance payment," Miller said in comments carried by Russian news agencies.
11The latest energy dispute comes as Russian support to separatist rebels in eastern Ukraine has strained the relations between Russia and Ukraine.
12Still, the two nations have had years of disputes over Russian gas going to Ukraine and heading onto Europe through Ukrainian pipelines.
13These disputes have even left other European nations without heat in the dead of winter.
14This time, Ukraine has been buying less and less gas directly from Russia.
15Russian and Ukrainian energy ministers failed to reach an agreement Tuesday on future supplies at talks mediated by EU officials.
16Russia offered Ukraine a price of $247 per thousand cubic meters, a $40 discount.
17Ukraine wanted a $100 discount.
18Russian Energy Minister Alexander Novak insisted the discount that Ukraine is asking for would put the cost of supplying Ukraine below the market rate.
19But Ukrainian Energy Minister Volodymyr Demchyshyn says the Russian discount isn't enough and that Ukraine will buy gas from other nations starting July 1 until an agreement is reached with Russia.
20Gas consumption is low at this time of year, allowing Ukraine to bargain for better terms.
21"We are being offered, categorically, a price which is higher than that of the reserve supply from Europe," Demchyshyn said in a statement Wednesday.
22"Russian gas at the eastern border should be cheaper because (the price) does not include the cost of transit."
23Ukraine has said it has found cheaper supplies from other European nations, which resell the gas they have gotten from Russia.
24Moscow has described these so-called reverse flows as illegal.
25Ukraine imported a third of its total gas from Russia in the first six months of this year, the national pipeline operator said Wednesday.
26Ukraine, which is struggling to balance its budget amid protracted fighting against Russia-backed separatists in the east, relies heavily on gas for its metals and machinery industries.
27The European Commission Vice-President Maros Sefcovic, who mediated the Russia-Ukraine gas talks, said gas supplies to the EU and Ukraine are "not in danger."
28"(Today) we are much better prepared in Europe for any problems with energy supplies," he said - due to the increased ability of European countries to move gas where it's needed via "reverse flows."
29Sefcovic said both Russia and Ukraine have asked the EU to come up with further proposals.
30He said technical experts would meet again in late August, followed by political talks in September.
