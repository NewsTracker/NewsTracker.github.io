Who's Who in music streaming: Apple, Spotify, Pandora & more

1SAN FRANCISCO (AP) - Apple shook up the music world more than a decade ago when it offered an easy way to buy songs digitally for 99 cents each.
2Now, it's trying to do it again with a subscription music streaming service.
3Several services charge $10 a month to let you play any of millions of tracks on mobile devices and even download them for offline listening.
4Once you stop paying, though, you lose all your songs, even ones you've already downloaded.
5Some offer free versions with ads and other restrictions, such as song selection only on PCs.
6- With last year's purchase of headphones maker Beats, Apple got Beats Music, which offers unlimited listening and offline playback for $10 a month.
7That is evolving into Apple Music and will have a prominent spot on iPhones and other Apple devices, as well as a unique integration with Siri, Apple's voice-activated digital assistant.
8Apple Music, set to debut June 30, will also be $10 a month, after a three-month free trial.
9The whole family can sign up for $15.
10- Spotify is one of Apple Music's biggest competitors in this space, with 60 million active users worldwide, and a quarter of them paying subscribers.
11It also offers the ability to pick and play tracks on PCs for free with ads; on mobile devices, non-paying users can play albums, artists or playlists they want but with tracks in random order, also with ads.
12- Rhapsody, operating as Napster in some countries, offers unlimited music for $10, along with a hybrid service called "unRadio" for $5 a month.
13It's basically ad-free Internet radio with the ability to save up to 25 favorites for playback anytime.
14- Tidal and Deezer also offer high-fidelity CD-quality song streaming, which audiophiles tend to prefer over MP3s and other formats that reduce quality in the compression process.
15Tidal offers music video and curated playlists from experts.
16Both charge $10 a month for standard sound quality and $20 for high-fidelity, although discounts can reduce this price.
17- Rdio offers full on-demand listening for $10, or a $4 hybrid service that allows 25 tracks to be picked and played on mobile devices.
18- Besides the main on-demand features, Slacker's twist on a hybrid plan is a $4-per-month "Radio Plus" that allows for Internet radio stations to be listened to offline.
19- Google Play Music service also offers unlimited on-demand listening for $10 a month, with no free option.
20Google also offers a service called YouTube Music Key for selected music videos, free of ads, for $10.
21Paying for one gets you the other, too.
22YouTube itself, which is free with ads, is possibly the world's favorite free option for music, whether you watch the video or not.
23- Amazon offers on-demand unlimited listening with a $99-a-year Prime membership, though the song selection is very limited compared with rivals.
