Google has until August 31 to reply to EU antitrust charges

1A woman hovers a mouse over the Google and European Union logos in Sarajevo, in this April 15, 2015 photo illustration.
2BRUSSELS Google Inc (GOOGL.O) has been given an extra two weeks to respond to European Union charges of abusing its market power in a dozen EU countries and stave off a possible billion-euro fine.
3The European Commission has extended the deadline for replying to the charges until Aug. 31, a Google spokesman said on Thursday.
4The Internet company was previously given until Aug. 17 to respond to the Commission's charge sheet, or statement of objections.
5The Commission, which in April accused Google of distorting web search results to favor its shopping service, confirmed it had accepted the company's request for additional time to reply.
6"In line with normal practice, the Commission analyzed the reasons for the request. As a result, it has granted an extension allowing Google to fully exercise its rights of defence," Commission spokesman Ricardo Cardoso said.
7European Competition Commissioner Margrethe Vestager's charges in April escalated a five-year investigation which Google previously tried to settle three times, without success.
