11"If you get good governance and good leadership, you make good decisions, and those good decisions will lead to the right outcomes," Gulati said, "whether it's where World Cups are played or how many teams are in World Cups or anything else. So for us, this is a vote for good governance."
12Gulati also says Canadian Soccer Association President Victor Montagliani told him Canada will vote for Ali.
13Former FIFA vice president Jack Warner has left a jail in Trinidad by ambulance a day after he was charged in a U.S. corruption case that has led to the arrests of more than a dozen international soccer officials and businessman.
14Judicial officer Abraham Ali said Warner had complained of exhaustion and was not able to face questions from reporters gathered outside the jail.
15Warner surrendered to authorities Wednesday and was granted a $395,000 bond but spent the night in jail.
16He is facing eight counts against him including conspiracy to defraud and engage in racketeering.
17He has not entered a plea and is expected to appear in court in July.
18Warner was forced out of FIFA in 2011 over a bribery scandal.
19He has denied any wrongdoing.
20The head of the Brazilian soccer confederation, FIFA executive committee member Marco Polo Del Nero, has left Zurich on the eve of the governing body's presidential election to return home.
21FIFA spokeswoman Delia Fischer confirmed that Del Nero had left Switzerland on Thursday to fly home to Brazil.
22As an executive committee member, he was set to attend Friday's FIFA Congress and oversee Brazil's vote in the election between incumbent Sepp Blatter and challenger Prince Ali of Jordan.
23Fischer did not provide a reason for Del Nero's departure.
24The FIFA corruption scandal is having wide-ranging implications in soccer-loving Brazil.
25The country's justice minister, Jose Eduardo Cardozo, has asked federal police to see if the U.S. probe into FIFA could help identify crimes that may have been committed by Brazilian sport officials and businessmen.
26After being visited by police agents, the Rio de Janeiro-based Klefer Sports Marketing company said it had turned over all the documents requested pertaining to its contract with the Brazilian soccer confederation for marketing rights to the domestic soccer championship.
27Klefer signed the contract together with Traffic, a Brazilian sports marketing company that American officials are investigating.
28Meanwhile, the Brazilian Senate on Thursday approved the request made by former soccer star and now Senator Romario for the creation of a congressional panel to investigate corruption in soccer.
29In Rio de Janeiro, the Brazilian confederation ordered the removal of the name of Jose Maria Marin from its lavish building.
30The 83-year-old Marin, a former confederation president, is one of 14 senior sports officials and executives charged with taking part in the FIFA bribery and kickback scheme.
31He was arrested in Zurich on Wednesday.
32Following a U.S. extradition request, a judge in Buenos Aires has ordered the arrest of three Argentine businessmen in connection with the FIFA corruption scandal.
33Judge Marcelo Martinez de Giorgi told local radio Thursday that he has issued arrest orders for Alejandro Burzaco, the president of Argentine sports marketing firm Torneos y Competencias, as well as Mariano and Hugo Jinkis, the owners of Argentina-based sports media business Full Play.
34The judge says Interpol "is working" on his request.
35The three men are accused of bribing officials at the Conmebol and CONCACAF confederations for TV and marketing rights of continental tournaments.
36IOC President Thomas Bach says Sepp Blatter and FIFA can learn from the way the Olympic body cleaned itself up after the Salt Lake City scandal.
37Bach urged Blatter - who is also an IOC member - to "take all necessary measures" following the U.S. and Swiss corruption investigations that rocked FIFA this week ahead of Friday's presidential election.
38Bach cited the "sad and difficult days for FIFA" in his speech Thursday at the opening ceremony of the FIFA congress in Zurich.
39"In the IOC we know from experience 15 years ago that this fight is challenging and painful," Bach said, referring to the vote-buying scandal involving Salt Lake City's winning bid for the 2002 Winter Olympics.
40Ten International Olympic Committee members resigned or were expelled for accepting cash, gifts and other inducements.
41The IOC also approved a raft of reforms that included tighter ethics rules and a ban on member visits to bid cities.
42Bach encouraged Blatter to "strengthen your cooperation with the relevant authorities, to shed full light on all the concerned matters and to take all the necessary measures by means of your new structures which you recently created in order to properly address such grave allegations."
43A Kuwaiti sheikh who is set to join FIFA's executive committee on Friday has criticized the "Hollywood-style" raid by Swiss police on the FIFA hotel in Zurich as part of a U.S. investigation into corruption.
44Sheikh Ahmad Al-Fahad Al-Sabah, already a powerful Olympic powerbroker, questioned whether the American probe stems from the country losing out on the 2022 World Cup to Qatar and its tense relationship with 2018 host Russia over the Ukraine conflict.
45"Is it because they (the U.S) want to kill FIFA? Is it because they (US) didn't win the election?"
46Sheikh Ahmad told The Associated Press, referencing the 2010 vote on World Cup hosts.
47"Is it related to the Ukraine-Russian war? A lot of questions (are being) raised ... maybe not a lot of people can say it."
48Sheikh Ahmad, who heads the Association of National Olympic Committees, endorsed Sepp Blatter to win a fifth term as FIFA president in the election on Friday.
49"He is the best from the names we are seeing on the list of who is running for the leadership of this organization," Sheikh Ahmad said.
50"He is the most qualified one to continue."
51CONCACAF says it has provisionally banned its president, Jeffrey Webb, after he was detained by police in Zurich as part of a U.S. investigation into soccer corruption.
52Alfredo Hawit from Honduras, who was named president of the North American confederation, said in a statement that it had become "the victim of fraud" and was cooperating with government investigators.
53Webb was provisionally suspended on Wednesday from his role as FIFA vice president and all soccer activities.
54Soccer officials have come under increasing pressure amid U.S. and Swiss federal investigations into high-level corruption tearing at FIFA and the world's most popular sport.
55Switzerland's sports minister said the country was doing its duty arresting FIFA officials for an American probe of corruption in soccer.
56Ueli Maurer told the FIFA congress on Thursday that Switzerland "fulfills its international treaty obligations" to provide legal assistance.
57He said Switzerland "strongly condemns any form of corruption" and is committed to fighting it.
58Two FIFA vice presidents and two presidents of FIFA member federations were among seven men arrested Wednesday at the request of United States federal agencies.
59They are suspected a wide-ranging case of bribery, money laundering and wire fraud linked to marketing rights worth hundreds of millions of dollars for tournaments in North and South America.
60A separate Swiss federal probe into possible "criminal mismanagement" in the 2018-2022 World Cup votes must proceed in due course, Maurer said.
61Swiss police seized evidence from FIFA headquarters in a raid early Wednesday.
62Swiss authorities say that a soccer official who had agreed to be extradited to the United States has changed his mind and now plans to fight the U.S. request.
63The unnamed soccer official was detained Wednesday together with six others while visiting Switzerland for a meeting of the game's governing body FIFA.
64Unlike the others, he had initially agreed to a simplified extradition procedure that would have allowed him to be handed over within days to the U.S., to face corruption charges.
65Swiss Justice Ministry spokesman Folco Galli said the seventh official "declared in a second hearing (Thursday) that he plans to resist the extradition."
66The United States has 40 days to submit a formal extradition request for the men, who continue to be held in detention.
67FIFA President Sepp Blatter says an American federal probe into soccer corruption brings "shame and humiliation" upon the sport.
68Blatter blamed the "actions of individuals" and said "it has to be stopped now" to prevent FIFA's reputation from being "dragged through the mud."
69Though Blatter told FIFA's 209 member federations Thursday that events "demand change from us all," he said he intends to continue leading FIFA through its problems.
70American and Swiss authorities are pursuing separate federal investigations, respectively, into racketeering and the 2018 and 2022 World Cup hosting votes.
71"I know many people hold me ultimately responsible for the actions and reputation of the global football community, whether it's a decision for the hosting of a World Cup or a corruption scandal," Blatter said.
72"We, or I, cannot monitor everyone all of the time. If people want to do wrong, they will also try to hide it."
73He said he had a responsibility "for the reputation and well-being of our organization - and to find the way forward to fix things."
74Blatter says he expects more bad news to follow.
75The FIFA chief tells his voters ahead of Friday's presidential election that soccer officials work "not for greed, not for exploit(ation), not for power but because of love for the game."

78Brazil's football confederation is now re-evaluating its contracts with its commercial partners, and a congressional probe into how local soccer is run is in the works.
