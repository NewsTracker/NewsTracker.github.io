J.B. Holmes wins Houston Open in 3-player playoff

1HOUSTON (AP) " J.B. Holmes used a strong start Sunday for a Houston Open victory in a three-way playoff against Jordan Spieth and Johnson Wagner.
2The 32-year-old Holmes birdied the first five holes and nine of the first 12 after starting the day six shots back of Jordan Spieth.
3He had a 64, the day's low round, and won on the second playoff hole for his fourth PGA Tour victory and the $1,188,000 check.
4Although Holmes' missed birdie putt had given Wagner another chance, his short putt for par lipped out.
5"I knew I had to play a really low round and I started out great," Holmes said.
6"It worked out for me."
7Spieth, the hottest player on the PGA Tour heading into the Masters, was trying to win his second playoff in three weeks, sandwiched around a runner-up finish in the Texas Open last Sunday.
8But he bowed out on the first playoff hole after his approach shot landed in a bunker and, lying two, he couldn't get close to the cup on his chip.
9Wagner, who got into the field only through a sponsor's exemption, was trying to become only the second player to get into the Masters on the weekend before it starts, having done it previously by winning Houston in 2008.
10Holmes' final-day rally was the biggest on the PGA Tour since Matt Jones also came from six down to beat Matt Kuchar in a playoff last April.
11The Houston Open has featured more playoffs than any tournament on the circuit.
12Wagner and Holmes hit perfect drives on the first playoff hole, while Spieth almost landed in the water, then wound up buried in the sand.
13"I'm not sure what happened," he said.
14"I heard something or maybe it was just me. It's not an excuse. I got down in the sand and caught it fat and didn't give myself a chance to continue in the playoff."

17He wound up with a steep downhill lie and a bunker to clear.
18However, he chipped within 11 feet, then calmly made the putt.

20He also could have passed Jimmy Walker, who pulled out of the tournament before it began citing illness, for the lead in the current season's points standing.
21Still, Spieth thinks he's well positioned to contend this week in the Masters.
22He finished second to Bubba Watson last year.
23Holmes began the day as an after-thought, tied for 18th place.
