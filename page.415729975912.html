US has discussed possible Tikrit airstrikes with Iraq

1WASHINGTON (AP) - American officials say Washington has been in discussions with the Iraqi government on the possibility of U.S. airstrikes to support the Iraqi ground offensive against a dug-in Islamic State group in Tikrit.
2The talks raise the highly sensitive issue of U.S. air support for an offensive spearheaded by Iranian-backed Iraqi Shiite militias.
3The Pentagon on Tuesday said the Iraqi offensive had "stalled."
4Two U.S. officials confirmed the talks about possible U.S. airstrikes.
5They were not authorized to discuss the matter publicly on the record and so were granted anonymity.
6On Tuesday, other officials said that at Baghdad's request the U.S.-led coalition had begun aerial surveillance over Tikrit and is sharing the collected intelligence with the Iraqi government.
