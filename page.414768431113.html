Gunmen kill policeman in Egypt's Nile Delta

Billionaire Nutella magnate dead at 89: reports

1MENA said the gunmen fired on police from a flyover early Sunday before fleeing, adding that a police car was damaged.
2It said an investigation has been launched to determine the identity of the attackers.
1Michele Ferrero, the visionary behind the famous chocolate hazelnut spread, the Tic Tac, Ferrero Rocher and the Kinder brand, died in Monte Carlo surrounded by family after several months of illness, according to The Associated Press.
2Ferrero and his father created the Nutella formula in the Piedmont region of Italy in the 1940s, as cocoa rationing during World War Two limited the supply of chocolate, a history on the company's USA website says.
3The company introduced Kinder chocolates in 1968, the Tic Tac in 1969 and Ferrero Rocher in 1982, AP reported.
4As the company grew, so did Ferrero's vast fortunes.
5He registers higher than any other Italian on the Bloomberg Billionaires Index at number 31, with a net worth of $22.3 billion, according to Bloomberg Business.
6Ferrero was "always ahead of his time thanks to innovative products and his tenacious and reserved work," Italian President Sergio Mattarella said in a statement.
7Ferrero's wife, Maria Franca, and his son Giovanni, the current CEO of Ferrero Group, survive him, AP reported.
8The company operates 20 production plants and employs 24,797 people, its website says.
9On Sunday, the front page of the firm's site displayed a picture of Ferrero with a simple message for visitors: "We are proud of you. Thank you Michele."
