Athlete's Foot owner buys Dr Martens, Vans

At SXSW, folk-roots mother-son duo build buzz

1With one foot in a Doc Martens boot and the other in a Vans sneaker, the owner of The Athlete's Foot is stepping into new territory.
2RCG Corporation will pay up to $200 million for New Zealand's Accent Group, which owns sneaker chain Platypus Shoes and exclusive distribution rights to popular youth and street brands Vans, Dr Martens and Timberland.
3The deal will increase RCG's store numbers by 97 to 269, and lift total sales from $267 million to $450 million.
4Accent's brands, which also includes Skechers and Palladium, will open up RCG to new customers, offering a point of difference to the runners and sports shoes sold through its major chain The Athlete's Foot.
5"That was obviously the appeal for us," RCG chief executive Hilton Brett.
6"It provides us with a complimentary business - a complimentary portfolio of brands - rather than one that cannibalise your business."
7The move has expanded RCG's brand portfolio from six to 13 licenses.
8Mr Brett said this would improve the company's ability to respond to changes in a competitive retail market.
9It also means RCG and Accent can sell each other's brands and make the most of their retail coverage.
10"We think that Skechers is an opportunity to drive further growth through The Athlete's Foot," he said.
11RCG brands Caterpillar, Sperry and Saucony may also soon appear on shelves in Platypus Shoes.
12The timing for the acquisition was right for RCG, Mr Brett said, after a new board helped build its market value.
13"We've been looking over the years for the right opportunity," he said.
14As part of the deal, three of Accent's four shareholders will join the RCG board, subject to shareholder approval.
15RCG will also aim to raise $125 million through a capital raising with investors and shareholders to help fund the acquisition.
1Madisen Ward and the Mama Bear consist of 26-year-old Madisen and his mother, Ruth.
2The duo has been building buzz at coffee houses for nearly six years in their hometown of Kansas City, Missouri with their striking, folk-roots sound.
3It led to a recording contact, signed in October, with Glassnote Records - the home label to Mumford & Sons and Phoenix.
4They've already performed on "Late Show with David Letterman" and will release their debut album on May 19.
5"When we're playing together, I don't look at Madisen, my son, I look at my fellow musician," Ruth said in an interview Wednesday.
6"He can get mad at me and it won't affect me - well, a little bit," she adds, as Madisen laughs and says, "And vice versa."
7"I hate to say it - it's kind of not really mother and son that much whenever we play music. And then as soon as we come off, we're right back," Madisen said.
8"When it comes to the business, we're being business people."
9Madisen is the chief songwriter and lead vocalist of the group.
10Ruth, who sings as well, edits the songs and vocals with her son to round out their sound.
11And they both play guitar.
12"We are hard on each other though," he said after taping a Daytrotter session at Danny's Studios in Austin, Texas - one of many performances slated around the South by Southwest festival.
13Their honesty - and directness - pierces throughout the 12-track "Skeleton Crew," which they recorded in Nashville this winter with producer Jim Abbiss (Adele, Arctic Monkeys).
14Ruth, a veteran performer, has recorded albums, played in bands and sang solo since she was 19.
15She said she initially grew up listening to Motown, but that changed when her brother introduced her to acts like Crosby, Stills, Nash & Young, Simon & Garfunkel and Peter, Paul and Mary.
16Madisen, the youngest of her three children, grew up watching his mother perform.
17And as he got older, he would join her onstage.
18"I'm sure she thought this was a dream that was far gone, not necessarily something she was going to pursue again, and for it to come back to life and know that I can be a part of that, I think about that often," Madisen said.
19"I think it's incredible."
20"I am very proud of my son ...and sometimes I can get choked up when I think about it," Ruth said, looking at Madisen.
21"I just see him becoming who he is. ...I'm blessed to have him as my son."
