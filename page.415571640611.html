Erroneous LAPD handwriting analysis set back Robert Durst probe, warrant claims

1Durst, a member of a wealthy New York real estate family, was arrested in New Orleans over the weekend and charged with murder in California for the December 2000 shooting death of Susan Berman.
2Durst's arrest came shortly before the finale of an HBO series about his links to three killings - that of his first wife, who disappeared in New York in 1982; Berman, the daughter a mobster and a close friend who acted as his spokeswoman after his wife disappeared; and a 71-year-old neighbor in Texas whose chopped up body was found floating in Galveston Bay in 2001.
3Durst has only ever been tried for the Texas killing, and he was acquitted of murder.
4Authorities on Tuesday searched Durst's Houston home, and the warrant released Wednesday details the timeline of the Berman case.
5It shows that police focused in on Durst's handwriting early on after a note received by police tipped them to Berman's death.
6The envelope and note, written in block letters, misspelled "Beverley Hills Police" and included the word "cadaver" along with Berman's Beverly Hills address.
7In 2001, Los Angeles Police Department document examiner William Leaver concluded it was "highly probable" that the envelope and note were written by Nyle Brenner, Berman's manager and friend.
8The assessment was "rubber stamped" by Leaver's supervisor, who told detectives this past September that she had not performed her own technical review.
9Investigators had not initially analyzed examples of Durst's handwriting, and it wasn't until eight months later that a comparison was made to Durst's limited sample writings.
10Leaver said "similarities" were enough to request more exemplar writings, which police eventually obtained.
11In June 2003, Leaver concluded the handwriting was likely Durst's.
12By then, Durst had been charged in Texas with murder for the death and dismemberment of Morris Black.
13The conflicting assessments meant valuable time lost.
14Eventually, the envelope, letter and writing samples were sent to the California Department of Justice and an investigator determined Durst as "probably the author of the cadaver letter and note."
15The Los Angeles case went dormant and wasn't revived until 2012, when law enforcement learned of the HBO documentary.
16The filmmakers say they shared tape with police months ago of interviews with Durst, including his reactions to handwriting analysis and what appears to be a confession to Berman's killing.
17In October and November, the LAPD had two independent examiners analyze the documents and both concluded Durst was the author.
18The LAPD has declined comment until Durst is in its custody.
19He's being held in Louisiana on gun charges.
20Durst's attorney, Dick DeGuerin, said the conflicting determinations on the handwriting "shows you what junk science really is."
21He also questioned what evidence police expected to find in Durst's Houston home.
22Durst himself may have pointed to the home, in a recording made while talking to himself after a tense interview with the makers of the six-part HBO documentary "The Jinx: The Life and Deaths of Robert Durst," which concluded Sunday.
23Just before saying he "killed them all," he says, "I don't know what's in the house!"
24The search warrant affidavit showed authorities were concerned Durst, who has an estimated net worth of $100 million, might be preparing to flee the country.
25He had withdrawn large sums of money from multiple bank accounts, including daily amounts of $9,000 for 35 days starting in October.
26Durst was under FBI surveillance, and agents arrested him in a New Orleans hotel under the name "Everette Ward," finding nearly $43,000 in cash, a gun and a rubber mask that could cover his head and neck in his room, the affidavit says.
27A law enforcement official with knowledge of the investigation told The Associated Press Wednesday that it was a kind of "old man mask" and investigators were looking at whether he had been using it to travel around.
28The official spoke on condition of anonymity because the person wasn't authorized to speak publicly about the ongoing investigation.
29Durst could face the death penalty if convicted of killing Berman.
