World stocks uneven as Europe's rally fades while Asian shares rise, led by China jump

1HONG KONG - World stock markets were uneven Friday as Europe's rally faded while most Asian indexes advanced, led by a jump in Chinese shares, after strong U.S. earnings.
2KEEPING SCORE: European stocks were mixed in early trading, with France's CAC 40 up less than 0.1 percent to 5,124.73.
3Germany's DAX slipped 0.1 percent to 11,705.67.
4Britain's FTSE 100 edged 0.1 percent lower to 6,788.61.
5U.S. stocks were poised to open lower, with Dow futures down 0.1 percent to 17,997.00.
6Broader S&P 500 futures dipped 0.1 percent to 2,115.70.
7GLOBAL OUTLOOK: With bailout lenders pledging support for Greece as it moves to re-open banks, investors' fears about a Greek exit from the euro quickly faded and they were now examining other parts of the global economy.
8U.S. companies have posted strong second quarter results this week, offsetting fears about the weakening Chinese economy and the Fed's signal it will raise rates by the end of the year.
9ANALYST INSIGHT: "Greece is on the way to becoming just background noise - at least for a while anyway," said Shane Oliver, head of investment strategy at AMP Capital.
10He said that declines in European and Asian stock markets give investors the chance to increase their exposure.
11"Some volatility is likely to remain though as we are still going through a seasonally weak period of the year for shares and a likely Fed interest rate hike lies ahead for later this year."
12ASIA'S SCORECARD: Markets were boosted by a slew of strong earnings from U.S. companies the day before.
13Japan's benchmark Nikkei 225 index added 0.3 percent to close at 20,650.92 while South Korea's Kospi lost 0.5 percent to 2,076.79.
14Hong Kong's Hang Seng rose 1 percent to 25,415.27 and the Shanghai Composite Index in mainland China, which has been highly volatile for weeks, jumped 3.5 percent to 3,957.35.
15Australia's S&P/ASX 200 was practically unchanged at 5,670.10.
16SAMSUNG VOTE: Shareholder approval of a highly contested merger of two companies in the vast Samsung empire weighed on South Korean market sentiment.
17The vote to allow construction firm Samsung C&T's takeover by Cheil Industries clears a crucial hurdle for a once-in-a-generation leadership shift at Samsung, a conglomerate with dozens of businesses but best known globally for its Samsung Electronics brand.
18The takeover had met unexpected strong opposition led by a U.S. hedge fund.
19Critics of the deal said it undervalued C&T and was designed to benefit Samsung's founding family.
20AVOID KOREA?
21There was strong criticism of weak shareholder rights in South Korea after the Samsung vote.
22The global funds "who are looking to seek investment opportunities in Asia will divert their way from Korea even faster to China where there are more exciting growth opportunities," said Thomas Choi, founder of Third Square Investments, which does not own any shares in Cheil or Samsung C&T.
23"Foreign investors would not want to take inherent management risk by investing in Korean companies with such poor corporate governance that lead to shareholders' value being exploited by the founding family members for their own benefit not shareholders," he said.
24ENERGY: Benchmark U.S. crude fell 2 cents to $50.89 a barrel in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
25The contract lost 50 cents to close at $50.91 a barrel in New York on Thursday.
26Brent crude, a benchmark for international oils used by many U.S. refineries, rose 9 cents to $57.02 a barrel in London.
27CURRENCIES: The dollar eased to 124.13 yen from 124.18 yen in late trading Thursday.
28The euro rose to $1.0891 from $1.0879.
