EU was looking into airline safety regulations prior to Germanwings crash in the French Alps

1BRUSSELS - European Union authorities were looking into German airline safety procedures on "a number of issues" well ahead of the March 24 Germanwings crash after a standard inspection by its aviation safety agency questioned some procedures.
2An EU Commission official, who asked not be named because of the sensitivity of the situation, said Wednesday the issues included health checks of pilots but could not elaborate.
3The official said that already in November EU authorities "had asked for clarification to make sure all airlines actively observe rules."
