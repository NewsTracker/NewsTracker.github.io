Google, Microsoft battle drives down prices for PCs, tablets

1SAN FRANCISCO (AP) - Google is releasing its cheapest Chromebook laptops yet, two versions priced at $149 aimed at undercutting Microsoft's Windows franchise and gaining ground in even more classrooms.
2Various PC manufacturers have been working with Google to design lightweight laptops running on the Chrome operating system since 2011.
3The newest versions are made by Hisense and Haier.
4Hisense's Chromebook can be ordered beginning Tuesday at Walmart.com and Haier's version can be bought at Amazon.com.
5Their arrival coincides with Microsoft's rollout of a lower-priced Surface tablet in an effort to reach students and budget-conscious families.
6Pre-orders for that device began Tuesday, too.
7As the prices for tablets and smartphones have been declining, it has forced on PC makers to lower their prices, said International Data Corp. analyst Jay Chou.
8The success of the Chromebook line is intensifying the PC pricing pressure.
9"It has been good news for consumers, but not so good for vendors," Chou said.
10The cheaper version of the Surface Pro 3 sells for $499, compared with $799 to $1,949 for the higher-end models.
11The discounted version has a slightly smaller screen - 10.8 inches rather than 12 - a slower processor, and less flexible kickstand - just three angles rather than unlimited positions.
12The Chromebook has served a dual purpose for Google.
13Like the company's Android software for mobile devices, the Chrome system is set up so users will automatically begin using Google's search engine and other services, such as Gmail and YouTube.
14Google has used the Chromebooks as a prod to bring down the prices of all PCs, something the company wanted to do because it has more opportunities to show the digital ads that bring in most of its revenue when more people can afford to buy an Internet-connected device.
15"We cannot be happier that Microsoft is helping drive down the prices of PCs," said Caesar Sengupta, Google's vice president of product management for Chromebooks.
16"If Microsoft is reacting to (Chromebook's low prices), that's fantastic. We love it."
17Unlike most computers, Chromebooks don't have a hard drive.
18Instead, they function as terminals dependent on an Internet connection to get most work done.
19Despite those limitations, Chromebooks have been steadily gaining in popularity, particularly in schools, as more applications and services made available over Internet connections - a phenomenon known as "cloud computing" that has reduced the need for hard drives.
20About 6 million Chromebooks were sold worldwide last year, more than doubling from 2.7 million in 2013, according to IDC.
21In contrast, sales in the overall PC market slipped 2 percent last year, marking the third consecutive annual decline.
22IDC is projecting 8 million Chromebooks will be sold this year.
23Besides the $149 laptops, Google also is attacking Microsoft on other fronts.
24Later this spring, Google and Asus are releasing a hybrid Chromebook that can use used as either a laptop or tablet with a 10-inch screen.
25Called the Chromebook Flip, it will sell for $249.
26In the summer, Asus and Google will start selling a new Chrome device that will provide people a cheaper way to upgrade an old PC.
27The device, called the Chromebit, is a stick loaded with an entire operating system that can be plugged into any HDMI port.
28The Chromebit's price hasn't been set, Google says it will cost less than $100.
29Graphic artists, engineers and finance professionals needing to run complex software might still want the company's higher-end Pro 3, said Dennis Meinhardt, director of program management for Surface.
30But the Surface 3 should be good for everyday tasks, he said, and brings the Pro 3's premium feel to a device that will be affordable to more people.
31The new Surface model is thinner and lighter, partly because it gets rid of the fan - similar to Apple's new MacBook laptop.
32At 214 pixels per inch, the screen resolution is comparable to the Pro 3's.
33Battery life is promised at 10 hours for video playback.
34The Surface 3 runs the regular version of Windows 8.1, just like the Pro 3.
35In the past, Microsoft has used a lightweight version called RT in its cheaper tablets.
36The Surface 3 also has a USB port, a feature that distinguishes the Surface line from most rival tablets.
37Both the Hisense and Haier models have 11.6 inch screens, 2 gigabits of memory and run on Rockchip processors.
38The Hisense machine promises up to 8.5 hours of battery power per charge while Haier is touting up to 10 hours of power per charge on a battery that can be removed for easier replacement.
