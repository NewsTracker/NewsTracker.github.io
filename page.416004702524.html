Ronaldo scores 5 in Madrid's 9-1 rout of Granada

1Ronaldo followed Gareth Bale's opener by netting his first three goals in eight minutes in the first half at the Santiago Bernabeu.
2The Ballon d'Or holder added two more goals after halftime as Karim Benzema also netted a pair in one of the club's most lopsided wins.
3Madrid hadn't scored nine goals in a league match in 47 years.
4Its biggest league victory remains an 11-2 thrashing of Elche in 1960.
5Madrid and the Spanish league said this was the first time Ronaldo had scored five goals in a game and it came after a two-month stretch when the Portugal star was merely extremely good after his spectacular year in 2014.
6That recent dip in form had even earned him unexpected criticism as Lionel Messi took over as the league's most in-form scorer.
7Usually protective of his players, Madrid coach Carlo Ancelotti said on Saturday that Ronaldo "had lowered his productivity" in the past two months as Madrid lost its league lead to Barcelona.
8Ronaldo responded in emphatic fashion by netting his 43rd, 44th, 45th, 46th and 47th goals in his 41st game of the season in all competitions.
9Ancelotti was almost left without words after the match.
10"What more can be said?"
11Ancelotti asked.
12Ronaldo's outburst moved him ahead of Lionel Messi in their individual duel to lead the scoring this season with 36 goals to the Barcelona forward's 32.
13Madrid's win came one round after losing at Barcelona and pulled it to within one point of the pacesetter before it visits Celta Vigo later.
14In other games, Valencia needs to beat Villarreal to recover third place and Deportivo La Coruna visits Getafe.
15Granada goalkeeper Oier Olazabal's long day began in the 25th minute when Bale won Toni Kroos' throughball and was all alone to dribble around Olazabal and poke the ball home.
16Five minutes later James Rodriguez marked his first game in two months after recovering from a broken right foot with an exquisite pass to set up Ronaldo for his first goal.
17Olazabal was partly at fault for Ronaldo's next two goals.
18Ronaldo got his second when he blasted in a short clearance by Olazabal in the 36th before he drove a long strike through Olazabal's hands in the 38th for his hat trick.
19Ronado's treble was not the fastest of the season.
20Rayo Vallecano's Alberto Bueno notched three goals in six minutes before scoring a fourth in a 4-2 win over Levante in February.
21Benzema got into the act with a goal in the 52nd.
22Ronaldo headed in Bale's pass two minutes later, and Benzema's deflected shot found the net in the 56th.
23Roberto Ibanez scored for Granada in the 74th.
24But Diego Mainz scored an own goal with seven minutes left before Ronaldo got his final goal against Granada's demoralized defense in the 90th.
25"This is the biggest blow I have ever had, either as a player or a coach," said Granada's veteran manager Abel Resino, whose team was left in the relegation zone.
