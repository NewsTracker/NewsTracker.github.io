Islamic State Militants Attack 2 Cities in Northern Syria

1After weeks of setbacks, militants from the Islamic State group launched swift counteroffensives Thursday on predominantly Kurdish areas of northern Syria, killing and wounding dozens and setting off car bombs, activists and officials said.
2The two-pronged attack on the northeastern city of Hassakeh and the border town of Kobani came two days after an Islamic State spokesman acknowledged that the group might lose some battles but would not be defeated.
3The spokesman, Abu Muhammad al-Adnani, had urged militants to strike back at their foes during the Muslim holy month of Ramadan and "shake the ground beneath them."
4The early morning assault by IS captured part of Hassakeh, which has long been divided between Syrian Kurds and the military forces of President Bashar Assad.
5The militants also hit Kobani, a northern town on Syria's border with Turkey that had become a symbol of Kurdish resistance against the Islamic State extremists.
6The Kurdish forces, backed by a campaign of U.S.-led airstrikes, drove the militants from Kobani and surrounding villages in January.
7Thursday's fighting in Kobani killed 35 civilians and Kurdish fighters, and 14 extremists, according to the Britain-based Syrian Observatory for Human Rights.
8It was the first time in six months the militants, who set off three car bombs, had managed to enter the town, the group said.
9The IS militants, wearing Syrian rebel uniforms and carrying flags of the mainstream Free Syrian Army to deceive the Kurdish defenders, launched their attack from areas to the south and west of Kobani, said Redur Khalil, a spokesman for the Kurdish People's Protection Units, or YPG.
10Ghalia Nehme, a commander with the Kurdish Women's Protection Units, told The Associated Press by telephone from Kobani that its fighters were defending a position in the town.
11Another Kurdish official in Kobani, Idriss Naasan, said the fighting was intense in the morning but sporadic at midday.
12"We hear cracks of gunfire every now and then," Naasan said around noon, adding that he had heard explosions of unknown origin.
13Kobani-based activist Mustafa Bali said in the evening that IS fighters were still in the city and held several buildings, but that YPG fighters were trying to surround them.
14He said some IS militants were using civilians as human shields.
15Turkey's Deputy Prime Minister Numan Kurtulmus said on Twitter that four people were killed and 96 people had been wounded in Kobani.
16A suicide bomber detonated his car near the border gate, according to two Turkish officials who spoke on condition of anonymity because they were not authorized to talk to reporters.
17Syrian state TV said the extremists crossed into Kobani from Turkey, but Kurtulmus dismissed such allegations as untrue, according to Turkey's Anadolu news agency.
18IS fighters entered the village of Barkh Botan near Kobani, opening fire on civilians and killing 20 people, the Observatory said.
19Syria's state news agency SANA said 22 people were killed, including women and children.
20The differing casualty figures from Turkish, Kurdish and Syrian sources could not be reconciled in the immediate aftermath of the violence.
21Natasha Underhill, an expert on Middle East terrorism from Britain's Nottingham Trent University, said the re-entry of Islamic State forces in Kobani "comes as a warning to the strength of the group."
