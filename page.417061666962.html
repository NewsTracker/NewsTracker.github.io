US, Nepal officials identify the bodies of 6 US Marines, 2 Nepalese soldiers in chopper crash

1KATHMANDU, Nepal - The bodies of six U.S. Marines and two Nepalese soldiers who were aboard a Marine helicopter that crashed during a relief mission in earthquake-hit Nepal have been identified, officials said Sunday.
2The wreckage of the UH-1 "Huey" was found Friday following days of intense searching in the mountains northeast of Kathmandu, Nepal's capital.
3The first three charred bodies were retrieved Friday by Nepalese and U.S. military teams, and the rest were found Saturday.
4The U.S. Marines who were killed were Capt.
5Dustin R. Lukasiewicz, from Nebraska, Capt.
6Christopher L. Norgren, from Kansas, Sgt. Ward M. Johnson IV, from Florida, Sgt. Eric M. Seaman, from California, Cpl.
7Sara A. Medina, from Illinois, and Lance Cpl.
8Jacob A. Hug, from Arizona, according to a statement from the U.S. military joint task force in Okinawa, Japan.
9Nepal's army identified its soldiers as Tapendra Rawal and Basanta Titara, and said all eight bodies have been flown to Kathmandu.
10The U.S. relief mission was deployed after a magnitude-7.8 earthquake hit Nepal on April 25, killing more than 8,200 people.
11A magnitude-7.3 quake struck the country on Tuesday, killing at least 117 people and injuring about 2,800.
12The helicopter went missing Tuesday while delivering rice and tarps in Charikot, the area worst hit by that day's quake.
13It had dropped off supplies in one location and was en route to a second site when contact was lost.
14The cause of the crash has not been determined.
15U.S. military officials have said that an Indian helicopter in the air nearby heard radio chatter from the Huey aircraft about a possible fuel problem.
