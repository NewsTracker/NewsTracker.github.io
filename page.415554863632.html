Canada prime minister to extend military mission in Iraq

Air Force vet pleads not guilty to federal terrorism charges

1Prime Minister Stephen Harper said Wednesday his Conservative government will introduce in Parliament a request for an "extension and expansion" of the mission against the Islamic State militant group.
2It was due to expire at the end of March.
3Canada has 69 special forces soldiers with Kurdish peshmerga fighters.
4They were sent to help train Kurdish fighters last September in a mission that was billed as noncombat with the troops working far behind the front lines.
5The fact that they've have been training and assisting on the front lines and directing airstrikes has stirred controversy in Canada.
6A Canadian soldier was killed in a friendly fire incident earlier this month.
7The mission also includes six CF-18 fighter jets, a refueling tanker aircraft, two surveillance planes and one airlift aircraft.
8About 600 airmen and airwomen are based in Kuwait.
9Canada has launched airstrikes in Iraq following a request from the U.S. last year.
10Canada's military is not involved in Syria, but Harper said the proposal will deal with that issue.
11He said he would provide more details next week.
12Although the mission doesn't need parliamentary approval, the government is expected to submit it to a vote to show consensus.
13Any motion would pass because Harper's Conservative Party has a majority in Parliament.
1A bearded Tairod Nathan Webster Pugh, wearing prison-issued khaki pants and a blue short-sleeved shirt, repeated his full name when asked but said nothing else before Judge Nicholas Garaufis in a New York federal courthouse.
2His attorney, Michael K. Schneider, entered the plea on his behalf.
3He declined to address reporters after the brief court appearance.
4Pugh, 47, of Neptune, New Jersey, was indicted Tuesday on charges of attempting to provide material support to a terrorist group and obstructing justice.
5He was stopped at a Turkish airport in January carrying a laptop containing information on Turkey-Syria border crossing points as well as 180 jihadist propaganda videos, including one featuring an Islamic State prisoner beheading, according to an indictment.
6In a letter addressed to a woman investigators believe is Pugh's Egyptian wife, Pugh declared: "I will use the talents and skills given to me by Allah to establish and defend the Islamic States," according to court papers.
7"There is only two possible outcomes for me," said the letter, which was recovered from his computer.
8"Victory or martyr."
9The computer, as well as thumb drive data-storage devices and other recovered equipment, appeared to have been intentionally destroyed to deny investigators access, the indictment said.
10Garaufis scheduled a May 8 status conference to review prosecutors' evidence and discuss any possible plea negotiations.
11Schneider said in court he would need time for his own forensic expert to review the data seized by federal authorities and to coordinate interviews with potential witnesses in Turkey, Egypt and elsewhere.
12Pugh has been living overseas for the past year and a half, most recently in Egypt, the court papers show.
13Pugh served in the Air Force from 1986 to 1990 and was trained in installing and maintaining aircraft engines and navigation and weapons systems.
14The airman first class was first assigned in July 1987 to the Woodbridge Air Base in England and then to the Davis-Monthan Air Force Base in Arizona in July 1989, the Air Force said.
15The FBI got a 2001 tip about Pugh from a co-worker at American Airlines who said Pugh expressed sympathy for Osama bin Laden, according to court papers.
16The airline said he left in early 2000 after a few months at American.
17In 2002, an associate of Pugh's again told the FBI that Pugh was interested in traveling to Chechnya to wage war, the indictment said.
18Pugh was stopped by Turkish authorities on Jan. 10, and returned to the U.S. five days later by way of Egypt.
19He told investigators he was in Turkey on vacation and to look for a job and had no intention of crossing into Syria, the indictment said.
