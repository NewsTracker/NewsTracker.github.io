German Parliament approves plan for new Greek bailout

1BERLIN - German lawmakers have given their overwhelming approval to opening detailed negotiations with Greece on a new bailout package.
2Lawmakers voted 439-119 Friday in favor of the commencement of discussions.
3There were 40 abstentions.
4Chancellor Angela Merkel needed Parliament's approval to launch talks thrashing out details of the package.
5On Monday, Merkel and other eurozone leaders struck a preliminary deal for a three-year bailout of Greece that's expected to be worth 85 billion euros ($93 billion).
6Germany has been the largest single contributor to Greece's bailouts.
7It has taken a hard line, insisting on stringent spending cuts and tax hikes in return.
8Merkel acknowledged concerns over the chances of a successful bailout but said the alternative would be chaos in Greece.
