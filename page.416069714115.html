2 black candidates elected to Ferguson City Council

1Rich Baranowski, left, and Tracy Hardy vote in Ferguson's municipal election Tuesday, April 7, 2015, in Ferguson, Mo.
2Three of the six city council seats are up for grabs in the St. Louis County town where 18-year-old Michael Brown, who was black, was fatally shot by a white Ferguson police officer in August.
3FERGUSON, Mo.
4(AP) - Two black candidates are among three people elected to the Ferguson City Council, substantially increasing African-American representation in the St. Louis suburb at the center of a national debate about how police interact with minority residents.
5Unofficial results on Tuesday showed that Wesley Bell, a black man, won in the 3rd Ward and Ella Jones, a black woman, won in the 1st Ward.
6Brian Fletcher, a former mayor who is white, won a 2nd Ward race against another white candidate.
7Until Tuesday, the mayor and five of six City Council members were white.
8Mayor James Knowles III and three council members, including Dwayne James, who is black, were not up for election this year.
