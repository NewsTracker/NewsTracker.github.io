Google announces newer, cheaper Chromebooks

1Google unveils new cheaper Chromebooks and a device called Chromebit that converts any display into a Chrome computer.
2Google announces newer, cheaper Chromebooks Google unveils new cheaper Chromebooks and a device called Chromebit that converts any display into a Chrome computer.
3NEW YORK-A few weeks ago, Google unveiled its latest Chromebook Pixel laptop at a starting price of $999.
4To a lot of people the imposing sum suggested that this is a machine for those other guys, the folks who can afford a pricey premium notebook that's meant to live (most of the time) in the cloud.
5But most Chromebooks in fact are priced for buyers that have to keep a close eye on their budgets.
6Many are sell in the $250 range and some are even less expensive than that.
7The price is about to drop even further.
8Google announced Tuesday that two new models are on the way, the Haier Chromebook 11 that will be sold through Amazon and the Hisense Chromebook that will be available from Walmart.
9The computers cost $149 each.
10Google claims both are fast, light and boast an all-day battery but I haven't gotten a chance to check either model out yet.
11Meantime, Asus will be bringing out a new product in the summer called Chromebit.
12Priced at less than $100 and smaller, Google says, than a candy bar, you'll be able to plug it into the HDMI port on any display to turn that display into a Chrome computer.
