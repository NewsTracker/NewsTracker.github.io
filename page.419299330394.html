Apple says hired 65 percent more women in past year

1Apple Store employees fill orders of the new iPhone 6 in Palo Alto, California.
2Apple said it had hired over 11,000 women globally in the past year-65 percent more than the previous year-to boost employee diversity.
3"In the first 6 months of this year, nearly 50 percent of the people we've hired in the United States are women, Black, Hispanic, or Native American," Chief Executive Tim Cook said in a message on the company's website.
4Cook maintained there was "a lot" more work to be done.
5Apple had 92,600 full-time employees as of September 2014.
6Diversity reports in the recent past have spurred a national debate about the lack of diversity at Silicon Valley's tech companies and how to improve gender and race ratios.
7Google said in June that 21 percent of tech hires last year were women, boosting overall number of women in technical roles by 1 percent, as part of efforts to increase diversity.
