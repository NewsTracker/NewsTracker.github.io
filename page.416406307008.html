Iraqi troops retake some Ramadi districts from Islamic State

1Smoke rises from the Haouz neighborhood during clashes with Islamic State group militants, in central Ramadi, 70 miles (115 kilometers) west of Baghdad, Iraq, Monday, April 20, 2015.
2BAGHDAD (AP) - Iraq security forces recaptured areas lost earlier to the Islamic State group in and around the battleground city of Ramadi, security officials said on Tuesday.
3Police Major Omar al-Alawni said that government forces regained control of the city's Pediatric and Maternity Hospital and the surrounding neighborhood late Monday night after fierce clashes with IS militants.
4The hospital is located about 500 meters from a complex of government offices.
5Police Colonel Mahdi Abbas said Iraqi troops were engaged in intense clashes Tuesday in an offensive to regain control of Soufiya, one of three villages that fell into the hands of the Islamic State group last week.
6Both officials said the battles turned in favor of government forces after the arrival of reinforcements and weapons from Baghdad.
7At least 12 militants were killed in the clashes overnight, they said.
8Footage obtained by The Associated Press showed military black Humvees advancing in a residential area in Ramadi and Iraqi soldiers firing their rifles while taking shelter behind a wall.
9The security situation in Ramadi, the capital of Anbar province, sharply deteriorated after IS seized Soufiya and two other villages, Sjariyah and Albu-Ghanim, forcing thousands of civilians to flee their homes.
