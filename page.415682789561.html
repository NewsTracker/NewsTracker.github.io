Robert Durst denied bail, called flight risk by judge

1The deranged 71-year-old heir of the wealthy New York real estate family will be held on weapons charges as authorities arrange his extradition to Los Angeles.
2On March 14 he was arrested in the Big Easy for the murder of his confidante, Susan Berman, in California in 2000.
3He has been held in a prison's mental health unit for nearly a week because he is a suicide risk, officials say.
4During the bail hearing before Magistrate Harry Cantrell, Durst sat beside his lawyers with his hands shackled to his sides in padded handcuffs.
5Durst's attorney, Dick DeGuerin, said the hearing had gone as he expected.
6"We will not contest bail until we get to California. That's where we want to be," he said.
7DeGuerin signaled he plans to argue his client was railroaded due to media attention surrounding the HBO miniseries, "The Jinx: The Life and Deaths of Robert Durst."
8The attorney singled out prosecutor-turned-TV-host Jeanine Pirro, who featured prominently in the show.
9"She's here because she's been participating in the dogging of Mr. Durst for years," DeGuerin said, asking a judge to remove her from the courtroom because she is a potential witness.
10DeGuerin said he wanted to question Pirro.
11But after a brief recess Cantrell ruled that Pirro would not testify.
12The documentary series revisited the circumstances surrounding the disappearance of Durst's first wife in 1982, Berman's murder and Durst's dismemberment of his 71-year-old neighbor in 2011.
13Durst was acquitted of murdering the neighbor, whose body parts were found floating in Galveston Bay.
14Durst's March 14 arrest in New Orleans Berman's shooting death came one day before the show's finale.
15The jaw-dropping conclusion featured Durst mumbling to himself in a bathroom that he "killed them all, of course" on a hot mic.
16Durst was arrested March 14 at the J.W. Marriott Hotel.
