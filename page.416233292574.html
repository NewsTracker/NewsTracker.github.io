Canada rushing 200 troops to Ukraine to help counter ‘Putin regime’s ongoing aggression,’ Stephen Harper says

1Ottawa - Canada will send 200 troops to Ukraine for the next two years to help train military forces in that country, Prime Minister Stephen Harper announced Tuesday.
2Harper made the announcement at National Defence Headquarters and was joined by Defence Minister Jason Kenney and Gen. Tom Lawson, Chief of the Defence Staff.
3The development was the latest move by the federal Conservative government in assisting Ukraine since the takeover last year of Crimea by Russian president Vladimir Putin.
4Russia also backs rebels in the eastern part of Ukraine.
5It marks a significant escalation in Canada's military involvement in the region and could spark questions about whether Canadian troops are being drawn into a dangerous conflict with Russia.
6"Canada continues to stand with the people of Ukraine in the face of the Putin regime's ongoing aggression," Harper said in a written statement accompanying the announcement.
7"The Canadian military contribution being announced today will help Ukrainian forces' personnel to better defend their country's sovereignty and territorial integrity."
8The training, intended to begin this summer, comes after consultation with "key allies," said the government.
9The training will take place primarily at a NATO centre in Yavoriv, in western Ukraine close to the Polish border.
10As well, some of the training will occur at the Ukrainian Ministry of Defence Demining Centre in Kamyanets-Podilsky, in western Ukraine.
11According to a news release issued Tuesday, Canada will deploy about 200 military personnel to Ukraine until March 31, 2017, "to develop and deliver military training and capacity-building programs for Ukrainian forces personnel."
12The government said the Canadian Armed Forces members will be deployed "on both a sustained and periodic basis" - depending on the type of training being conducted.
13Their activities will include: explosive ordnance disposal and improvised explosive device disposal training, military police training, medical training, flight safety training, and logistics system modernization training.
14As well, Canadian troops will work alongside United States personnel to provide "individual and unit tactics training" to the Ukrainian National Guard.
15"Canada's latest military contribution, which addresses a number of requests from the Government of Ukraine, is being provided to help the country in its efforts to maintain sovereignty, security and stability in the face of Russian aggression," said the news released issued Tuesday.
16"Canada's position on Ukraine has been clear since the outset: we recognize the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Ukraine and will never recognize the illegal Russian occupation of Crimea or any part of that country."
17The decision to send Canadian troops into Ukraine follows previous announcements in which non-lethal military supplies were provided.
18Canada will also be sending 22 Harris high-frequency radios, 238 pairs of PVS-7 night vision goggles, 1,134 tactical medical kits, a mobile field hospital structure and some explosive ordnance disposal equipment to Ukraine.
