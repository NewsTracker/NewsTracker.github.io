1The 25 suspects were found dead in their cell one day after being arrested in December, Communications Minister Issa Tchiroma Bakary said in a press conference Friday evening.
2They were part of a group of 56 detainees, and the cause of their deaths is still being determined, he said.
3"I can already state here that at this stage of investigation, no evidence has been established to corroborate that these persons were deliberately killed," Bakary said.
4He added that a senior military official had been removed from his post and was being investigated over the deaths, though he did not identify this person by name.
5In January, the Network of Human Rights Defenders in Central Africa, based in the capital, Yaounde, cited "reliable sources" in claiming that 50 Boko Haram suspects had suffocated to death in their cells before being buried in a mass grave.
6The group's report accused the army of engaging in rights violations including torture "with impunity."
7Boko Haram has been fighting a nearly 6-year insurgency against the Nigerian government, killing thousands and carrying out mass kidnappings, including of schoolgirls.
8The conflict has taken on an increasingly international dimension in recent months.
9Cameroon's northern region was repeatedly targeted by Boko Haram before the country signed up to participate in a multinational force that has dislodged the group from a score of northeastern Nigerian towns in recent weeks.
10Nigeria acknowledged on Thursday that it is getting help from regional security operatives amid reports that South Africa and other foreign contractors are involved in the fight against Boko Haram.
11Also on Thursday, Islamic State militants in the Middle East accepted a pledge of allegiance from Boko Haram, though it is unclear how the groups might be able to cooperate effectively.

2Panos Kammenos, Greece's defense minister, spoke to German newspaper "Bild" on Saturday, saying his country's leaving the euro could precede an exit by Italy and Spain, followed by Germany in the future.
3"If Greece explodes, Spain and Italy will be next and then at some point, Germany. We therefore need to find a way within the eurozone, but this way cannot be that the Greeks keep on having to pay," Kammenos told Bild.
4Instead of a bailout, Greece needed a debt "haircut" like the one Germany's creditors had to accept in 1953, Kammenos proposed.
5He also argued that Berlin should pay World War II reparations to Athens.
6"All European countries have been compensated for crimes committed by Nazis, except for Greece," Kammenos said, referring to the gold Nazi soldiers brought back from Athens during the war.
7The defense minister also accused Germany of "interfering" in its domestic affairs.
8His criticism was aimed at German Finance Minister Schauble, who earlier warned of a "Grexident" which could push Athens out of the euro.
9"I don't understand why he turns against Greece every day in new statements. It's like a psychological war and Schauble is poisoning the relationship between the two countries through that," he said.
10Talking to Bild, Kammenos warned that his country would not take any more refugees in case of an exit from the eurozone, potentially creating a refugee crisis at Europe's frontiers.
11He also said his country was facing severe problems after the EU and the US imposed sanctions on Russia following its alleged role in the Ukraine war.
12European officials meanwhile criticized Athens' attitude.
13"In Greece, too much blame for Greece's problems is laid outside Greece and Germany is now the preferred victim," Eurogroup chief and Dutch Finance Minister Jeroen Dijsselbloem told Dutch state television NOS.
14EU Commission chief Jean Claude Juncker, however, pleaded for a breakthrough, saying he did not want a failure and would like Europeans to stick together.
15Greece and the other eurozone countries have been engaged in a drawn-out discussion on finalizing a bailout plan for Greece by April 1.
16But the process has stalled, with EU member countries, especially Germany, asking Greece to impose more austerity measures and present a concrete plan to cut government expenditure.
17However, Greek's Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras's Syriza party maintains that the EU's previous bailouts and rescue plans have impoverished the population.
18"People have not benefited from the so-called aid packages, which have reached only the bank. People don't have any work, but prices continue to rise," Kammenos said in his interview.
