Taylor Swift Explains Why She Won't Put New Album on Apple Music

1Taylor Swift and Apple used to be mad love.
2The singer posted an open letter to the company on her site Sunday in which she explained why she will not be making her latest album, 1989, available on the new Apple Music streaming service.
3Swift explained that Apple's planned three-month free trial for the service, during which labels will not be paid for music sales, was keeping her from providing the album.
4"I find [the free trial] to be shocking, disappointing and completely unlike this historically progressive and generous company," she wrote of the trial period.
5"We know how astronomically successful Apple has been, and we know that this incredible company has the money to pay artists, writers and producers for the three-month trial period ... even if it is free for the fans trying it out."
6She added that she is able to support herself and her team on live performances, but she is looking out for newer artists, songwriters and producers who aren't yet as financially successful.
7"These are not the complaints of a spoiled, petulant child," Swift wrote.
8"These are the echoed sentiments of every artist, writer and producer in my social circles who are afraid to speak up publicly because we admire and respect Apple so much. We simply do not respect this particular call."
9Swift encouraged Apple that it is not late to change this decision, adding that she respects the company and its "truly ingenious" employees.
10"We don't ask you for free iPhones," she continued.
11"Please don't ask us to provide you with our music for no compensation."
12This is the latest development in Swift's complicated relationship with streaming services.
13She has withheld her albums from Spotify, saying that the service doesn't pay artists fairly, but her albums are available on Tidal, with the exception of 1989.
14Her full message to Apple can be read below.
15I write this to explain why I'll be holding back my album, 1989, from the new streaming service, Apple Music.
16I feel this deserves an explanation because Apple has been and will continue to be one of my best partners in selling music and creating ways for me to connect with my fans.
17I respect the company and the truly ingenious minds that have created a legacy based on innovation and pushing the right boundaries.
18I'm sure you are aware that Apple Music will be offering a free 3 month trial to anyone who signs up for the service.
19I'm not sure you know that Apple Music will not be paying writers, producers, or artists for those three months.
20I find it to be shocking, disappointing, and completely unlike this historically progressive and generous company.
21This is not about me.
22Thankfully I am on my fifth album and can support myself, my band, crew, and entire management team by playing live shows.
23This is about the new artist or band that has just released their first single and will not be paid for its success.
24This is about the young songwriter who just got his or her first cut and thought that the royalties from that would get them out of debt.
25This is about the producer who works tirelessly to innovate and create, just like the innovators and creators at Apple are pioneering in their field but will not get paid for a quarter of a year's worth of plays on his or her songs.
26These are not the complaints of a spoiled, petulant child.
27These are the echoed sentiments of every artist, writer and producer in my social circles who are afraid to speak up publicly because we admire and respect Apple so much.
28We simply do not respect this particular call.
29I realize that Apple is working towards a goal of paid streaming.
30I think that is beautiful progress.
31We know how astronomically successful Apple has been and we know that this incredible company has the money to pay artists, writers and producers for the 3 month trial period even if it is free for the fans trying it out.
32Three months is a long time to go unpaid, and it is unfair to ask anyone to work for nothing.
33I say this with love, reverence, and admiration for everything else Apple has done.
34I hope that soon I can join them in the progression towards a streaming model that seems fair to those who create this music.
35I think this could be the platform that gets it right.
36But I say to Apple with all due respect, it's not too late to change this policy and change the minds of those in the music industry who will be deeply and gravely affected by this.
37We don't ask you for free iPhones.
38Please don't ask us to provide you with our music for no compensation.
