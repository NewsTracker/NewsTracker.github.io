'Nature seems to be against us': rain hampers Nepal rescue teams

1Rescue teams toiled in pouring rain in the debris left by last week's devastating earthquake in Nepal, but officials said the chance of finding any more survivors was bleak as the death toll neared 5,500.
2Rescue teams toiled in pouring rain on Thursday in the debris left by last week's devastating earthquake in Nepal, but officials said the chances of finding any more survivors was bleak as the death toll neared 5,500.
3While rescue teams were out in the capital Kathmandu despite the rain, helicopters could not fly to the worst-hit areas in the countryside of the impoverished Himalayan nation.
4"There may not be any more survivors," said Rameshwor Dandal, chief of the disaster management centre at Nepal's home ministry.
5"The rain is adding to the problems. Nature seems to be against us."
6Anger over the slow pace of the rescue flared on Wednesday with protests outside parliament.
7In the interior, villagers blocked trucks carrying supplies, demanding the government do more to hasten the distribution of aid that has flooded into the country but has been slow to reach those in need.
8An official from Nepal's home ministry said the number of confirmed deaths from Saturday's 7.8 magnitude earthquake had risen to 5,489 by Thursday morning.
9Almost 11,000 were injured, and more than 80 were also killed in neighbouring India and Tibet.
10Many people have been sleeping in the open after the quake - the United Nations has said 600,000 houses were destroyed or damaged.
11It has said eight million people have been affected, with at least two million in need of tents, water, food and medicines over the next three months.
12Dandal said foreign rescue teams had told him that their work is almost done because there is little chance of finding any survivors.
13A Nepali-French rescue team pulled a 28-year-old man, Rishi Khanal, from a collapsed apartment block in Kathmandu on Tuesday after he had spent around 80 hours trapped in a room with three dead bodies.
14However, doctors amputated one of his legs on Wednesday because of damage from prolonged internal bleeding.
15Nepal is appealing to foreign governments for more helicopters.
16There are currently about 20 Nepali army, private and Indian army helicopters involved in rescue operations, according to Laxmi Prasad Dhakal, a home ministry official.
17China is expected to send helicopters on Thursday, he said.
18Prime Minister Sushil Koirala has told Reuters the death toll could reach 10,000, with information on casualties and damage from far-flung villages and towns yet to come in.
19That would surpass the 8,500 who died in a 1934 earthquake, the last disaster on this scale to hit the nation of 28 million people sandwiched between India and China.
20In Kathmandu and other cities, hospitals quickly overflowed with injured soon after the quake, with many being treated out in the open or not at all.
21Guna Raj, who works for a Kathmandu-based NGO specialising in providing sanitation, said there have been outbreaks of diarrhoea in relief camps because of a shortage of toilets and clean water.
22"In the next few days or weeks I am sure there will be an outbreak of epidemics," said Raj, who is involved in the relief effort.
23Tensions between foreigners and Nepalis desperate for relief surfaced, rescuers said, as fresh avalanches were reported in several areas.
24Members of Israeli search-and-rescue group Magnus said hundreds of tourists, including about 100 Israelis, were airlifted out of Langtang in Rasuwa district, a popular trekking area north of Kathmandu hit by an avalanche on Tuesday.
25But at least two foreigners, whose nationalities were as yet unknown, were found dead, the home ministry said.
26Fights had broken out there because of food shortages, Magnus team member Amit Rubin said.
27One of the trekkers said there had also been scuffles over places on the rescue helicopters.
28The quake also triggered an avalanche on Mount Everest that killed at least 18 climbers and guides, including four foreigners, the worst disaster on the world's highest peak.
29Nepali villagers blocked trucks carrying supplies for earthquake victims on Wednesday, demanding the government do more to help after last week's disaster left more than 5,200 people dead and tens of thousands homeless and short of food and water.
30In the capital Kathmandu, about 200 people protested outside parliament, asking for more buses to go to their homes in remote parts of the Himalayan nation and to hasten the distribution of aid that has flooded into the country but been slow to reach those in need.
31In Sangachowk village in one of the worst-hit districts, about three hours by road from the capital, scores of angry villagers blocked the road with tires.
32They stopped two trucks headed for the district capital with rice, noodles and biscuits.
33Later they blocked a convoy of three army trucks with relief supplies, leading to a tense standoff with armed soldiers.
34"We have been given no food by the government," said Udhav Giri, 34.
35"Trucks carrying rice go past and don't stop. The district headquarters is getting all the food."
36The government was struggling to fully assess the devastation wrought by Saturday's 7.8 magnitude quake.
37"This is a disaster on an unprecedented scale. There have been some weaknesses in managing the relief operation," Nepal's Communication Minister Minendra Rijal said late on Tuesday.
38An official from Nepal's home ministry said the number of confirmed deaths had risen to 5,238 by Wednesday night.
39Almost 10,350 were injured in Nepal, and more than 80 were also killed in India and Tibet.
40That would surpass the 8,500 who died in a 1934 earthquake, the last disaster on this scale to hit the Himalayan nation of 28 million people located between India and China.
41Guna Raj, who works for a Kathmandu-based NGO specializing in providing sanitation, said there have been outbreaks of diarrhea in relief camps because of a shortage of toilets and clean water.
42Foreign Secretary Shanker Das Bairagi appealed for specialist doctors from overseas, as well as for search-and-rescue teams despite earlier suggestions from officials that Nepal did not need more such assistance.
43"Our top priority is for relief and rescue teams. We need neurologists, orthopedic surgeons and trauma surgeons," Bairagi said.
44Experts from a Polish NGO that has an 87-strong team in Nepal have said the chances of finding people alive in the ruins five days after the quake were "next to zero".
45The number of people killed in Nepal by the Himalayan country's worst earthquake in eight decades has risen to 5,006, a home ministry official said on Wednesday.
46That number represented a jump of 300 from earlier on Wednesday.
47Nepal's Prime Minister Sushil Koirala told Reuters on Tuesday the toll from the 7.9 magnitude quake could reach 10,000.
48Four young men from Perth are the last of the West Australians missing in Nepal to make contact with family back home.
49While hundreds of people are still missing in Nepal, all the climbers who had been stranded on Mount Everest have now been rescued.
50People stranded in remote villages and towns across Nepal were still waiting for aid and relief to arrive on Tuesday, four days after a devastating earthquake destroyed buildings and roads and killed more than 4,600 people.
51The government has yet to assess the full scale of the damage wrought by Saturday's 7.9 magnitude quake, unable to reach many mountainous areas despite aid supplies and personnel pouring in from around the world.
52Prime Minister Sushil Koirala told Reuters the death toll could reach 10,000, as information on damage from far-flung villages and towns has yet to come in.
53That would surpass the 8,500 who died in a 1934 earthquake, the last disaster on this scale to hit the Himalayan nation.
54"The government is doing all it can for rescue and relief on a war footing," Koirala said.
55"It is a challenge and a very difficult hour for Nepal."
56Nepal told aid agencies it did not need more foreign rescue teams to help search for survivors, because its government and military could cope, the national head of the United Nations Development Programme told Reuters.
57Experts said the chance of finding people alive in the ruins was slim more than four days after disaster struck.
58"After the first 72 hours the survival rate drops dramatically and we are on day four," said Wojtek Wilk of the Polish Center for International Aid, an NGO which has six medical staff and 81 firefighters in Nepal.
59"On the fifth day it's next to zero."
60In a rare glimmer of hope, a Nepali-French rescue team pulled a 28-year-old man, Rishi Khanal, from a collapsed apartment block in Kathmandu after he had spent around 80 hours trapped in a room with three dead bodies.
61In Jharibar, a village in the hilly Gorkha district of Nepal close to the quake's epicentre, Sunthalia was not so lucky.
62Her husband away in India and with no help in sight, she dug for hours in the rubble of her collapsed home on Saturday to recover the bodies of two of her children, a 10-year-old daughter and eight-year-old son.
63Another son aged four miraculously survived.
64In Barpak, further north, rescue helicopters were unable to find a place to land.
65On Tuesday, soldiers had started to make their way overland, first by bus, then by foot.
66Army helicopters also circled over Laprak, another village in the district best known as the home of Gurkha soldiers.
67A local health official estimated that 1,600 of the 1,700 houses there had been razed.
68Helicopters dropped food packets in the hope that survivors could gather them up.
69In Sindhupalchowk, about 3.5 hours by road northeast of Kathmandu, the earthquake was followed by landslides, killing 1,182 people and seriously injuring 376.
70A local official said he feared many more were trapped and more aid was needed.
71"There are hundreds of houses where our people have not been able to reach yet," said Krishna Pokharel, the district administrator.
72"There is a shortage of fuel, the weather is bad and there is not enough help coming in from Kathmandu."
73International aid has begun arriving in Nepal, but disbursement has been slow, partly because aftershocks have sporadically closed the airport.
74According to the home (interior) ministry, the confirmed death toll stands at 4,682, with more than 9,240 injured.
75The United Nations said 8 million people were affected by the quake and that 1.4 million people were in need of food.
76Nepal's most deadly quake in 81 years also triggered a huge avalanche on Mount Everest that killed at least 18 climbers and guides, including four foreigners, the worst single disaster on the world's highest peak.
77All the climbers who had been stranded at camps high up on Everest had been flown by helicopters to safety, mountaineers reported on Tuesday.
78Up to 250 people were missing after an avalanche hit a village on Tuesday in Rasuwa district, a popular trekking area to the north of Kathmandu, district governor Uddhav Bhattarai said.
79Congestion at Kathmandu's Tribhuvan International Airport has forced several international rescue teams to either fly home or land at regional hubs such as Bangkok.
80The country's sole international airport, which can only service eight large aircraft at a time, has had to deal with incoming commercial, humanitarian and rescue flights that severely strain its capacity.
81Photos on social media show severely crowded terminals filled with travellers waiting to fly out of Nepal after the huge earthquake on the weekend.
82A plane carrying a Japanese search and rescue team that departed from Thailand was put into a holding pattern on Monday for several hours, before the pilot was forced to divert and land in Kolkata, India to refuel.
83A second attempt to land in Kathmandu was unsuccessful and the plane was forced to return to Thailand.
84Thai Airways officials said the aircraft did not stay overnight in Kolkata because hotel rooms near the airport were filled with passengers from other diverted flights to Kathmandu.
85A spokesman from the Indian Ministry of Defence also confirmed that an Air Force cargo plane was forced to return to India due to airport congestion.
86He said the military would reschedule flights to "low density time" to avoid congestion at Kathmandu.
