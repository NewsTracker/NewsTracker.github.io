Candidate's comparison of Fort Wayne to Ferguson draws heat

1"My whole intent was to show dissatisfaction with the city administration and how they dealt with a single situation," candidate Richard Stevenson said in a telephone interview Wednesday, a day after meeting with a group of police officers.
2"I apologized to officers if anything was misconstrued that I'm against the police department."
3Stevenson, the Wayne Township trustee who's challenging incumbent Mayor Tom Henry in the Democratic primary, cited the 2007 shooting of a motorist by a white police officer in declaring "there is a lot of similarity between Fort Wayne and Ferguson" during a candidates' forum Saturday at the Fort Wayne Urban League.
4"The Fort Wayne Police Department needs some direction. They need to be cleaned up," Stevenson said.
5"How do you kill a person like that and send him back out to beat up somebody else."
6He was referring to the 2007 shooting of Baudilio Lemus-Rodriguez by white officer James Arnold after Lemus-Rodriguez had been stopped for a traffic violation and then tried to ram officers with his car.
7A private consultant hired by the city to review the case ruled the shooting "objectively reasonable," and Arnold was allowed to remain on duty.
8Five years later, Arnold was suspended for 30 days for using excessive force in a different case.
9Jon Bonar, president of the 370-member Patrolmen's Benevolent Association, said a group of officers met with Stevenson on Tuesday and replayed the video.
10"He didn't recant or apologize. Comparing us to Ferguson is ridiculous," Bonar said.
11"It's not a good thing to say. There's no merit behind it and I think it's going to have some ramifications for him in terms of the election," Bonar said.
12"Quite frankly, if it was on the ballot between him and a can of peaches, I'll vote for the can of peaches."
13City Safety Director Rusty York, Fort Wayne's former police chief, called any comparison between Fort Wayne and Ferguson "reckless and unfortunate."
