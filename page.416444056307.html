Chile volcano erupts for first time in decades

1Puerto Varas (Chile) (AFP) - A volcano in Chile that had been inactive for over 50 years suddenly erupted twice in the space of hours, blasting huge clouds of ash into the air and forcing the evacuation of around 5,000 people.
2There were no immediate reports of injuries after the eruptions from the Calbuco volcano in southern Chile Wednesday evening and then again seven hours later, early Thursday.
3A state of emergency was declared after the first eruption, and air traffic was disrupted.
4Chilean TV aired spectacular footage of ash, bright orange flames and flaming rocks belching from the mouth of the volcano.
5The first eruption spewed a giant mushroom of ash 10 kilometers (six miles) into the sky.
6Ash reached neighboring Argentina, where officials started taking emergency measures.
7Calbuco had been dormant for 54 years, officials said.
8Officials ordered an evacuation for a 20-kilometer radius around the volcano and the interior ministry rushed in the army to temporarily take control of the province of Llanquihue and the town of Puerto Octay.
9The National Geology and Mining Service said the volcano might start oozing lava, raising the possibility of mountain-top snow and ice melting, causing floods and raising water levels in rivers.
10Smoke and ash rise from the Calbuco volcano as seen from the city of Puerto Montt, April 22, 2015.
11It said a third eruption was likely over the next few hours.
12President Michelle Bachelet said she would travel to the affected area Thursday along with several ministers.
13"The ash might damage crops, animal feed, bridges, roads, people's work routines, tourism and especially their health," Bachelet said.
14The first eruption lasted nearly 90 minutes, vulcanologist Gabriel Orozco of the geology service said.
15On a 0-8 scale measuring volcano eruption strength, the first one came in at four or five, Interior Minister Rodrigo Penailillo said.
16The second one was just about as powerful, the ministry said.
17It said there were no immediate reports of people hurt or missing.
18Until minutes right before the blast, volcano eruption monitoring systems had picked up nothing.
19In fact, volcano watchers in Chile had been watching another one, Villarica also in the south, for a possible eruption.
20Emergency measures were also in place in Argentina, where the picturesque city of Bariloche -- about 100 kilometers from the volcano -- said it was anticipating the arrival of ash clouds and warned people to stay at home.
21Classes in Bariloche were cancelled for Thursday.
22At hospitals, staff shifts were rearranged to deal particularly with ash from Chile.
23The Chilean geology service that much of the ash from the volcano was in fact headed for Argentina.
24Puerto Montt, over the border from Bariloche and the largest Chilean city in the area, was already blanketed in a cloud of ash.
25"People are very, very frightened," said Gervoy Paredes, mayor of Puerto Montt.
26"The situation is pretty complicated," he added, saying reports indicated the Blanco river was flooding because of ice melt triggered by the eruption.
27Airlines canceled flights as the towering ash cloud rose from Calbuco's snowy peak and schools also suspended classes in the region.
28Television footage showed large traffic jams and long lines at gas stations in Puerto Montt, where a red alert was declared, along with nearby Puerto Varas.
29"I would like to call on the population to remain calm and stay informed," Penailillo said.
30Police called on residents to keep roads as clear as possible to aid the evacuation.
31It was a "rather explosive eruption," said Gabriel Orozco, a volcanologist at the National Geology and Mining Service.
32He said the 10-kilometer-high ash cloud risked collapsing, and warned: "River beds are very dangerous at the moment" because of the risk of ice and snow melting and causing floods.
33The 2,000-meter (6,500-foot) volcano is located in the Los Lagos region, some 1,400 kilometers south of the capital Santiago.
34It is the second volcano in southern Chile to have a substantial eruption since March 3, when the Villarrica volcano emitted a brief but fiery burst of ash and lava.
35That eruption caused authorities to evacuate more than 3,500 people.
36No lava was seen in the latest eruption, which began around 6:00 pm (2100 GMT).
37But the ash cloud, which could be seen from 20 kilometers away, loomed over the crater, turning hues of pink and yellow as the sun set over the area.
38Chile has about 90 active volcanoes, of which Calbuco is considered one of the most dangerous.
