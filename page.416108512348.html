Durst pleads not guilty to gun charges in New Orleans

1A frail-looking Robert Durst pleaded not guilty Thursday to felony gun possession charges in Louisiana Criminal Court, nearly a month after his arrest in the Big Easy.
2Durst, 71, wearing a neon yellow jumpsuit, had to be held up by two of his lawyers as he moved toward the podium and had a hard time hearing as he was arraigned.
3"I didn't hear," Durst said in a high-pitched voice when Orleans Parish Criminal District Court Judge Franz Zilbich asked him how he wanted to plead.
4After lead defense lawyer Dick DeGuerin whispered in his ear, Durst finally said, "I am not guilty, your honor."
5Before his arraignment, Durst, who appeared to be disoriented, peered over his shoulder at the crowd of reporters seated in the gallery.
6The millionaire real estate heir was indicted Wednesday on charges of possession of a weapon and possession of a weapon in the presence of a controlled dangerous substance stemming from a search of his New Orleans hotel room March 14.
7He had been picked up on a murder warrant out of Los Angeles, where he is accused of murdering a longtime female friend.
8His lawyers have argued that two FBI agents conducted the search illegally without a proper arrest warrant in hand.
9The agents were subpoenaed to testify at a hearing last week but were advised not to show up because they weren't prepared.
10DeGuerin said in court Thursday that he wants both agents present at an evidentiary hearing in criminal court May 7.
11"We ran into a stunt," said DeGuerin.
12Durst will also appear in New Orleans federal court later Thursday.
13He is being held without bail and will have to answer to charges there before he is extradited to Los Angeles for the murder rap.
14Jamie Landry-Zimmer reported from New Orleans.
