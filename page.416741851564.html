Mexico state official says SurveyMonkey CEO died of severe head trauma in exercise accident

1MEXICO CITY - SurveyMonkey CEO David Goldberg died of severe head trauma in an exercise accident in the Mexican resort town of Punta Mita, a Mexican state official said Monday.
2Goldberg was found lying next to a treadmill on Friday at the Four Seasons Resort Punta Mita near Puerto Vallarta.
3The official said he left his room at about 4 p.m. to exercise, and family members went to look for him after he didn't return.
4He was found at about 6:30 p.m. in one of the resort's gymnasiums lying by a treadmill in a pool of blood, with a blow to the lower back of his head.
5He apparently had slipped on the treadmill and hit the machine, said the official, who spoke on condition of anonymity because the person was not authorized to speak to the press.
6The official said Goldberg still had vital signs when he was discovered, but later died at a hospital in Nuevo Vallarta.
7The official said the cause of death was severe head trauma and hypovolemic shock, or bleeding.
8The family had checked in on April 30.
