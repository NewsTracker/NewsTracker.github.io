European lawmakers urge FIFA president Blatter to go now

1STRASBOURG, France (AP) - European lawmakers have urged FIFA President Sepp Blatter to step down immediately, and for an interim leader to be appointed.
2Members of the European Parliament passed their resolution by a show of hands after debating the corruption allegations which have plunged soccer's governing body into crisis.
3FIFA is not obliged to heed the parliament and has previously dismissed calls for action made by lawmaker groups, including the Council of Europe.
4Blatter announced his resignation plans last week and promised an election to replace him would be held within nine months.
5FIFA rules require senior vice president Issa Hayatou of Cameroon to step up as interim president if Blatter leaves before the election.
