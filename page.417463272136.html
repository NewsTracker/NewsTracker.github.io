Egypt court postpones final ruling on Morsi death sentence until June 16

1Ousted Egyptian president Mohamed Morsi gestures from the defendants cage as he attends his trial at the police academy on the outskirts of the capital Cairo on June 2, 2015.
2The Egyptian court postponed its final ruling on Morsi, who was sentenced to death along with dozens more over a mass jailbreak during the 2011 uprising.
3An Egyptian court on Tuesday set a date later this month for its final ruling on the death sentence recently handed down to ousted president Mohamed Morsi, a member of the Muslim Brotherhood group.
4The new date for the ruling, which was originally scheduled for June 2, was handed down just hours after the arrest of two high-ranking Brotherhood leaders.
5The court set June 16 as the date to decide on Morsi's death sentence, after it receives an opinion in the matter from Shawki Allam, Egypt's grand mufti.
6According to Egyptian law, all death sentences are referred to the top cleric.
7Morsi entered the courtroom for the hearing on his death sentence Tuesday as fellow defendants chanted, "Down, down with the military."
8Late Monday, authorities arrested Abdel-Rahman el-Bar, the Muslim Brotherhood's top religious cleric, and Mahmoud Ghozlan, the group's former spokesman and a member of its top decision-making body.
9The two were hiding in Giza, a suburb outside Cairo, according to a security official, who spoke on condition of anonymity to The Associated Press because he was not authorized to talk to reporters.
10The arrests were the latest in an ongoing crackdown on the Brotherhood, which has been banned by Egypt.
11Cairo considers the group a "terrorist" organization and accuses it of orchestrating attacks on Egyptian policemen, judges, army officers and public figures.
12The crackdown was launched after the military's ouster of Morsi in July 2013.
13Morsi, Egypt's first freely elected president, was overthrown after millions staged demonstrations demanding he step down.
14Dozens of Brotherhood leaders have been put on trial, and many were given death sentences.
15Scores of other Brotherhood members have gone into hiding.
16The Brotherhood, founded in 1928, was dissolved by Egypt's military rulers in 1954, but remained active within Egyptian society largely underground.
17After the overthrow of longtime dictator Hosni Mubarak in 2011, the group legally registered the Freedom and Justice Party and fielded candidate Morsi for president.
18He won in 2012.
19With Morsi's victory, the Muslim Brotherhood appeared to cement itself in the heart of power for years to come.
20However, accusations that his administration was incompetent and intent on monopolizing power tarnished the group's reputation.
21The 87-year-old Brotherhood has been shaken by nearly two years of constant pressure and persecution by authorities.
22Mohammed Montassir, a Brotherhood spokesman, described the latest arrests as a "failed attempt ... to disrupt the revolutionaries across the nation," in a post on his Facebook page.
23Separately, Egyptian state TV on Monday night announced that the police have disrupted what it called several Brotherhood "plots" to assault symbols of the government such as the police, army and judges, as well as media, political and public figures.
24Morsi was sentenced to death in May over a mass prison break during Egypt's 2011 uprising.
25That case was one of several legal cases against him, for which he was tried concurrently.
