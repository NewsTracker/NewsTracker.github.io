Google Maps Now Shows You Everywhere You've Been

1This article, Google Maps now shows you everywhere you ve been, originally appeared on CNET.com.
2Google can show you a map of everywhere you ve been.
3There s maybe a handful of people that know everywhere you go.
4Maybe it s your husband or wife, or your kid, if you regularly talk about your schedules.
5Oh and there s Google -- depending on how much you rely on its services, like Maps and its digital assistant Google Now.
6Google on Tuesday announced a feature called Your Timeline that shows you everywhere you ve been on a map, tracking it by time and date.
7The company is rolling out the feature gradually, first on the Web and for phones running Google s Android operating system.
8The company said the new feature is useful for remembering the places you ve been, like a fun bar you visited or the road trips you ve taken.
9"Your Timeline allows you to visualize your real-world routines," Gerard Sanz, a Google project manager, wrote in a blog post Tuesday.
10But the feature is also a reminder of how much data Google collects on its users.
11The company, which makes the bulk of its money on advertising, lives on knowing personal information about the people who use its services -- like where they ve been or what they ve searched for online.
12Privacy advocates have criticized the company for already knowing too much about its users.
13Google is quick to point to privacy controls for the feature.
14A user can delete anything from their timeline, including a day s worth of location data, as well as the entire timeline itself.
15People can also edit and personalize names of places on the map (like Mom s house), or turn off location tracking services altogether.
