Mets' Mejia banned 80 games, 4th MLB positive within 16 days (Yahoo Sports)

1ATLANTA (AP) -- Injured New York Mets closer Jenrry Mejia was suspended for 80 games Saturday, the fourth player in 16 days banned under the major league drug program following a positive test for the steroid Stanozolol.
2Minnesota pitcher Ervin Santana, Seattle pitcher David Rollins and Atlanta pitcher Arodys Vizcaino also have been disciplined in recent weeks for Stanozolol, which is sold under the name Winstrol and is popular with body builders.
3A person familiar with the penalties told The Associated Press that MLB has not yet found any links to the four positive tests.
4The person spoke on condition of anonymity because the probe is ongoing.
5Mejia will lose $1,134,426 of his $2,595,000 salary because of the suspension.
6''It's disappointing not only for him and his career but for us as a team,'' Dillon Gee (0-1) said after taking the loss in Saturday's 5-3 defeat to Atlanta.
7Mejia began the season as New York's closer while Bobby Parnell completes his recovery from Tommy John surgery, but Mejia couldn't get loose while warming up during Monday's opener at Washington.
8He was placed on the 15-day disabled list Wednesday with posterior elbow inflammation, without having pitched in a game.
9His time on the DL counts toward suspension.
10''We're very disappointed, extremely disappointed,'' Mets manager Terry Collins said.
11Mejia apologized to the organization, his teammates, fans and family while also maintaining his innocence.
12''I have been through a lot in my young career and missed time due to injury,'' Mejia said.
13The Mets issued a boilerplate statement of support for MLB's drug program.
14Mejia began 2014 as a starter but moved to the closer role about six weeks after Parnell tore an elbow ligament in the first game of the season.
15The right-hander from the Dominican Republic had 28 saves, 18 after the All-Star break to tie for second-most in the NL.
16''I'm going to be honest - I love this kid,'' Collins said.
17''We challenged this guy last year and he stepped up for us and did a great job for us.
18Parnell is expected to return next month.
19''The one thing we aren't going to do - we aren't going to rush him back,'' Collins said of Parnell.
20Jeurys Familia is the team's closer for now.
21''The guys who are here are going to have to pick it up and get the job done,'' Collins said.
