Mexico President Leaves Hospital After Gallbladder Surgery

1Mexican President Enrique Pena Nieto left the hospital on Sunday after undergoing surgery to remove his gallbladder.
2Pena Nieto was released from the Central Military Hospital at midday, telling the press he is feeling "very good" and thanking doctors and nurses for their professional care.
3He said he is maintaining his normal schedule, including receiving a visit by Spanish King Felipe VI on Monday.
4The 48-year-old president had the operation on Friday after experiencing "intense discomfort."
5Doctors said it was the only option.
6They said he was fully healthy and in charge after the surgery.
7Pena Nieto said he has been advised to lower his fat intake going forward and to limit exercise over the next few weeks.
8The sudden illness forced Pena Nieto to cancel plans to travel to Guatemala on Friday for a regional summit of national leaders.
9It was his second surgery since taking office in late 2012.
10In July 2013, he underwent surgery to remove a thyroid nodule, which showed no signs of malignancy.
