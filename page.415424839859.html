Lily James is living a fairytale life with starring role in 'Cinderella'

American who contracted Ebola arrives at Maryland hospital

1The British actress had spent most of her young acting life in smaller roles, from films like "Wrath of the Titans" and the TV shows "Secret Diary of a Call Girl" and "Just William."
2Then two big breaks came her way.
3She was cast as the spunky Lady Rose in "Downton Abbey," a series that has been a massive hit for the BBC and PBS.
4In the middle of all that attention, she landed a film role that has turned her into a movie star.
5She plays "Cinderella" in the new live-action film about a young girl with a good heart, a stepmother with a lust for power and a prince with a shoe obsession.
6"I'm here, in L.A., with my mum and my brothers. Tonight, there's this Hollywood premiere and so all of that really, truly feels like a fairytale," James says.
7"A role like this has all this magic around it because it's Disney, it's a princess, it's a fairytale. I feel so, so lucky."
8She credits director Kenneth Branagh.
9He could have made the story cheesy with its mice, transforming lizards and a fairy godmother who is a few handfuls of pixie dust short of a spell.
10But he made the film a sweet love story.
11Branagh feels lucky to have found James.
12He had a long list of qualities he wanted in Cinderella: She had to be witty, smart and sharp, but not cruel.
13Inner and outer beauty were musts.
14He saw all of that when James auditioned.
15She sees "Cinderella" as a story about two people from different backgrounds who meet and fall in love.
16She laughs and says it was easy to be lovestruck by Richard Madden, who is both as charming as his prince and "a little bit naughty."
17The pair got along from the first day.
18James was impressed that when she burped in his face after drinking a warm soda too fast, he was not offended.
19"I think when people are doing a love story the work can get complicated. But, this was so uncomplicated," James says.
20"We were just good friends and that made it easy to do the scenes."
21Another thing that made the work so easy was the beautiful wardrobe.
22James' favorite dress was the pink gown Cinderella sews from one of her mother's gowns because of the sentimental attachment.
23Although she considers Cinderella a role of a lifetime, the princess was not her favorite growing up.
24She identified with Belle, from "Beauty and the Beast."
25"I also liked Belle's spirit of adventure and wanting more. Growing up, I liked the characters who dreamed more and wanted more. The animated version of Cinderella was more about the Prince saving her," James says.
26But, she didn't limit herself to Belle.
27Her grandmother would make her princess outfits to wear around the house.
28The greatest challenge of playing Cinderella ended up being the pressure she put on herself.
29"It wasn't just a fear of ruining Cinderella for everyone in the world, because everybody has such a clear image of her whether it's from the storybook world, their imagination or the animation," James says.
30"I think every little girl wants to be a princess but the reality of actually doing it, and believing you can do it, that was the challenge.
31"I ended up not thinking of her as a princess, but simply as Ella."
1(AP) - An American healthcare worker who contracted Ebola while volunteering in a Sierra Leone treatment unit arrived safely at the National Institutes of Health's hospital in Maryland, officials said Friday.
2Doctors evaluated the patient, and the person is in serious condition after being flown in isolation from Sierra Leone on a chartered plane and admitted at 4:44 a.m., NIH officials said in a statement.
3The patient's name, age and gender were not released.
4The patient is the 11th person with Ebola to be treated in the U.S. and the second admitted to the NIH Clinical Center.
5It has one of the few specialized isolation units nationwide set up to treat Ebola patients.
6The center's Special Clinical Studies Unit is staffed by specialists in infectious disease and critical care and is designed to prevent the spread of highly contagious viruses, including Ebola.
7Previously, an American nurse was treated there after she contracted Ebola while caring for a patient in a Dallas hospital.
8The nurse, Nina Pham, survived and is Ebola-free.
9The World Health Organization estimated Thursday that the virus has killed more than 10,000 people, mostly in the West African nations of Guinea, Liberia and Sierra Leone.
10The current outbreak is the largest ever for the disease.
11While deaths have slowed dramatically in recent months, the virus appears stubbornly entrenched in parts of Guinea and Sierra Leone.
