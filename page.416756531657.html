Nine foreign trekkers among dead found at Nepal village buried by earthquake

1Nine victims recovered from an earthquake-triggered mudslide in Nepal that wiped out the entire village of Langtang were foreign trekkers, rescuers said on Tuesday.
2The valley and its little village of Langtang are about 35 miles north of Nepal's capital, Kathmandu.
3It was a popular stop for trekkers because of its scenic views of the Himalayas.
4"The entire village was wiped out by the mudslide. There were some 60 houses there, but they were all buried under rubble. It will be impossible to recover all the bodies," said Gautam Rimal, the top government official in the Rasuwa district.
5The village is now about a two-day hike from the nearest town because the landslide has blocked area roads.
6While helicopters allow easy access, they remain in short supply because of aid missions across the quake-affected parts of Nepal.
7The still-rising death toll from the quake, Nepal's worst in more than 80 years, has reached more than 7,500.
8In Kathmandu, authorities say up to one-third of the city's residents have left since the quake.
9In the first days, bus stations were jammed with people fearing aftershocks or trying to get home to relatives in devastated villages.
10Authorities do not know how many of those people have returned to the capital, but on Tuesday there were still people waiting for buses to leave.
11"I stayed back here to help out my neighbours and clean up the neighbourhood," said Surya Singh, who was at a large bus station.
12But now he wants to see the damage in his home village – though with many roads still blocked by landslides he was unsure if he could get all the way by bus.
13Kathmandu police say nearly 900,000 people have left in the past 10 days.
14The population of Kathmandu valley – including the city of Kathmandu and smaller towns of Lalitpur and Bhaktapur – is 2.5 million people.
15Life has been slowly returning to normal in Kathmandu.
16Schools are to remain closed until May 14 but some markets are open and trucks have been bringing in fresh food and vegetables every day.
