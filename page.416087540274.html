Police: Pacers' Copeland stabbed outside NYC nightclub (Yahoo Sports)

1NEW YORK (AP) -- Indiana Pacers forward Chris Copeland, his girlfriend and another woman were stabbed early Wednesday following an argument on the street near a Manhattan nightclub that also led to the arrest of two Atlanta Hawks players, authorities said.
2The violence broke out just before 4 a.m. outside 1Oak Club, a trendy Chelsea spot where celebs like Justin Bieber and Snoop Dogg mingle with partygoers, police said.
3The couple was arguing on the street as the attacker eavesdropped and started to interfere, according to police.
4The dispute escalated until the 22-year-old suspect pulled out a knife and started slashing, police said.
5The suspect, Shezoy Bleary, was in custody, authorities said.
6Police said charges were pending, and it wasn't clear whether Bleary had an attorney who could comment on the charges.
7Copeland, 31, a former member of the New York Knicks, was stabbed in the left elbow and abdomen and his girlfriend, Katrine Saltara, was slashed in the arm and across the breast.
8The second woman, who was believed to be with the attacker, was slashed in the abdomen.
9The victims were hospitalized and in stable condition, police said.
10A knife was recovered at the scene.
11Atlanta Hawks players Pero Antic, 33 and Thabo Sefolosha, 30, who were not with Copeland but were also at the club, were arrested after police said they blocked officers from trying to start their investigation.
12The teammates were in town to play the Brooklyn Nets on Wednesday night.
13The team arrived late Tuesday, after beating the Phoenix Suns in Atlanta.
14Antic and Sefolosha were released without bail after a brief court appearance where they were charged with obstructing governmental authority and disorderly conduct.
15Their attorney, Alex Spiro, said they did not commit any crime.
16''They should not have been arrested.
17We fully expect the case to be dismissed,'' he said.
18Hawks officials did not indicate whether the two would play Wednesday night.
19Sefolosha was limping during his appearance and Spiro said he had been injured during the arrest.
20The two are reserves, averaging less than 6 points per game on a team that has locked up the top playoff seed in the Eastern Conference.
21Garin Narain, the Hawks vice president of public relations, said in a statement to The Associated Press on Wednesday morning that the team is investigating the matter.
22''We are aware of the situation involving Pero Antic and Thabo Sefolosha this morning,'' Narain said.
23The Pacers were in town to play the Knicks; Copeland played with New York during the 2012-13 season.
24Pacers President of Basketball Operations Larry Bird said the team was aware that Copeland had been injured.
25''We are still gathering information and will update when we know more.
26Our thoughts are with Chris and those injured,'' Bird said.
27Copeland has played infrequently, missing 21 games since Jan. 31.
28With All-Star Paul George making his season debut Sunday, Copeland's already limited minutes were expected to get cut.
29Police initially said Saltara was his wife, but Copeland's agent said the two are not married.
30According to Saltara's Facebook page, they have been engaged for several months.
31In a statement, the club disputed the police account.
32It said the altercation stemmed from a dispute at a public housing project about two blocks away from the nightspot and that Copeland's driver grabbed the suspect, who was later arrested.
33''The incident occurred beyond the view of our security.
34Once alerted the venue provided all assistance possible,'' the statement read.
35The club did not say how Copeland got back to the front of the club on West 17th Street, where the street was splattered with blood from the altercation.
36The nightclub had been cited at least 10 times for altercations and four times for noise in 2014, according to data obtained by the New York Post from the State Liquor Authority.
37Associated Press writers Jeff Martin in Atlanta and Rick Callahan in Indianapolis contributed to this report.
