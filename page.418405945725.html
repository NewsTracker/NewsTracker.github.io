After terror attacks, Tunisia erects fence on Libya border

1TUNIS, Tunisia - Tunisia's army and contractors are building a barrier along part of the country's border with Libya to keep out extremists in the wake of attacks claimed by the Islamic State group.
2Prime Minister Habib Essid said the goal is to render the border "impassable" by jihadist fighters and vehicles, and construction should be finished this year.
3Essid told Tunisian TV the barrier will cover 105 miles - about one-third of the border - and will include fencing, a sand wall, trenches and surveillance posts.
4Tunisia's government declared a state of emergency days after a gunman killed 38 tourists, mostly Britons, on June 26 in the coastal resort of Sousse.
5In March, two attackers fired on tourists and others at Tunis' National Bardo Museum, killing 22 people.
