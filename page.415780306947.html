Nigerian troops recapture Boko Haram 'headquarters'

1Abuja, Nigeria: Nigerian troops have retaken the town of Gwoza from Boko Haram, from which the group declared its caliphate last year.
2"Troops this morning captured Gwoza destroying the Headquarters of the Terrorists self-styled Caliphate," Defence Headquarters in Abuja said on Twitter on Friday.
3"Several terrorists died while many are captured. Mopping up of entire Gwoza and her suburbs is ongoing," it added in a separate message.
4Nigerian special forces prepare to fight Boko Haram in Diffa on Wednesday.
5Nigerian forces have retaken the Boko Haram stronghold of Gwoza.
6Earlier this month, residents who fled the town in Borno state said militants had been massing in Gwoza and killing local people who were unable to flee.
7That led to speculation the group, which has been pushed out of a number of towns in three northeast states in recent weeks, was preparing for a final assault.
8Boko Haram leader Abubakar Shekau declared on August 24 last year Gwoza was "part of the Islamic caliphate", adding to speculation the militants were imitating the Islamic State group.
9An image still from a video released by Boko Haram in July, 2014.
10Shekau had the previous month praised IS leader Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi but stopped short of pledging allegiance.
11He has since formally allied himself to the group in Syria and Iraq.
12Nigeria's national security spokesman Mike Omeri said last week troops had begun the "final onslaught" against Boko Haram, saying Gwoza was one of three areas yet to be retaken.
13A four-nation coalition of Nigeria, Niger, Chad and Cameroon has claimed a number of successes since the turn of the year to end the insurgency which has claimed more than 13,000 lives since 2009.
14The ongoing operation was cited as a reason to delay Nigeria's general election on February 14 to this Saturday, as soldiers would not be able to provide security nationwide.
15In a televised address broadcast on Friday, President Goodluck Jonathan hailed troops for having "successfully stemmed the seizure of Nigerian territories".
