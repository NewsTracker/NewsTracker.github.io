Egypt bombs IS Libya targets after killings

1In a statement read out on Egyptian State Television, the armed forces confirmed airstrikes were conducted in the early morning hours against "Daesh (IS) terrorist targets" in Libya.
2"The goals were achieved with precision. And the Eagles of our air forces returned to their bases".
3A graphic video was circulated on various websites purportedly showing the beheading of the Egyptians in the city of Sirte along the Libyan coast.
4The footage, entitled "A Message with Blood to the Nation of the Cross" used advanced production techniques and victims were made to wear orange jumpsuits, both common features of previous IS videos.
5The killings have been condemned by governments around the world, including the by U.S. and the U.K..
6Italy went as far as to close its embassy in Libya, citing a "deteriorating situation", and further pressed its case for a United Nations mission.
7Bernardino Leon, the UN Special Representative to Libya, has so far failed to broker a lasting agreement between the warring factions.
8One of the masked militants in the video, speaking in English fluently, reiterated the intention of the group to expand, saying it "will conquer Rome".
9"There is now increasing urgency in Arab and EU capitals on the need to do something to reverse the descent of order in Libya and the increasing dangers to the neighboring states," Angus Blair, founder of the Signet Institute, a Cairo-based think tank, told CNBC.
10Libya has been embroiled in a civil war since the ousting of Muammar Gaddafi in 2011.
11The internationally-recognized government has fled the capital Tripoli, while oil production has fallen further to 325,000 barrels per day (bpd) in January, from 900,000 bpd in October.
