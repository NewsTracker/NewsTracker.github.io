US stocks down as Shanghai sinks, Greece deadline looms

1U.S. stocks were modestly lower in morning trading Thursday, following a steep sell-off in the Chinese stock market and the approaching deadline for cash-starved Greece to make a debt payment.
2KEEPING SCORE: The Dow Jones industrial average lost 66 points, or 0.4 percent, to 18,096 as of 10:55 a.m. Eastern.
3The Standard & Poor's 500 index lost seven points, or 0.3 percent, to 2,116 and the Nasdaq composite lost 14 points, or 0.3 percent, to 5,092.
4MORE M&A: Chipmakers Avago Technologies and Broadcom agreed to merge in a $37 billion deal.
5Avago was down $1.84, or 1 percent, to $139.70.
6Broadcom was down $1.72, or 3 percent, to $55.45.
7However both of those stocks jumped sharply Wednesday - Broadcom by 22 percent and Avago by 8 percent - on rumors those companies were in merger talks.
8CHINA DIVE: The Shanghai Composite sank 6.5 percent in another volatile move.
9Stock market commentator Hexun attributed the fall to several factors including brokerages tightening margin lending, selling by speculators and a Chinese sovereign wealth fund selling shares in two state banks.
10Despite a deepening economic slowdown, the index has gained 40 percent in the past three months alone.
11Chinese leaders have tried to tap brakes on the stock market boom, fearing it could run out of control and disrupt economic reform plans.
12GREECE FACTOR: Traders continue to get mixed messages about the progress of talks between Greece and its creditors.
13Greece said Wednesday it expected to reach a deal to get more bailout loans in time for it to make a key debt repayment on June 5.
14But its creditors, however, were quick to temper expectations.
15Greek stocks were down 1.7 percent.
16Germany's DAX lost 1.2 percent, France's CAC-40 lost 1.5 percent and the U.K.'s FTSE 100 index was down 0.3 percent.
17THE QUOTE: "With the situation in Greece rapidly coming to a head and election results showing disenchantment with the status quo and the establishment across the continent, the pressure to reach a solution on Greece has grown rapidly," said Nicholas Teo, analyst with CMC in Singapore, in a market commentary.
18"Developments on this front may continue to drive trading action in the coming days."
19BONDS, CURRENCIES: U.S. government bond prices didn't move much.
20The yield on the 10-year Treasury note held steady at 2.14 percent.
21The euro rose to $1.0914 from $1.0899.
22The dollar rose to 124.23 yen from 123.75 yen.
