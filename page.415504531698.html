7Yet it is unusual for diversified funds like Burnham's to hold more than 10% of their portfolios in one company, said Todd Rosenbluth, director of mutual fund research at S&P Capital IQ.
8"Investors may not realize that their fund manager is taking on a higher amount of risk," he said.
9Most fund managers are inclined to take profits when a stock hits 7% of a portfolio, yet there are few set mandates set down by fund firms, Rosenbluth said.
10He said that he can't think of any fund families that have to sell if holdings exceed guidelines as a result of appreciation.
11Diversified mutual funds are allowed by law to add shares of a company as long as its total weight is below 24.9%of their portfolios overall, but do not have to sell shares if they appreciate above that level, said Jay Baris, an attorney at Morrison & Foerster in New York.
