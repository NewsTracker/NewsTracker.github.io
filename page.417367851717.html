News24.com | FIFA presidential vote goes to second round

1Zurich -Sepp Blatter unexpectedly failed to win the votes he needed to become president of FIFA for a fifth term on Friday and there will be a second ballot.
2Blatter had been expected to win despite demands that he quit in the light of a major bribery and corruption scandal engulfing world soccer's governing body.
3Neither Blatter nor Jordanian challenger Prince Ali bin Al Hussein got the necessary two thirds of the vote.
4Blatter secured 133 votes and Prince Ali won 73 at FIFA's annual Congress in Switzerland.
5Speaking just before the vote, Blatter, who joined FIFA in 1975, said he felt that he had only been with the organisation for a short time and wanted to stay longer.
6"What is time anyway. I find that the time I have spent at FIFA is very short," he said.
7"The more one ages the more time flies by quickly. I am with you, and I would like to stay with you," he said to applause.
8Prince Ali, in his pitch for votes, had pledged an open, more democratic FIFA, saying: "We have heard in recent days, voices which described our FIFA as an avaricious body which feeds on the game that the world loves.
9While Asian, African and Latin American states had been expected to rally around Blatter, Europe, which accounts for all but three of the countries that have ever made it to a World Cup's final match, had been keen for him to step aside.
10On a visit to Berlin, British Prime Minister David Cameron told Blatter to go "the sooner the better".
11Chancellor Angela Merkel said the dirty side of soccer must be cleaned up.
12US authorities have accused top FIFA figures and sports executives of corruption, while Switzerland is investigating the award of the next World Cup finals to Russia and Qatar.
13The scandal widened on Friday when Britain's Serious Fraud Office said it was examining possible corruption at FIFA.
14A judge in Argentina has ordered the arrest of three businessmen accused of using bribery to obtain soccer media rights, and the Brazilian Senate moved to open a formal inquiry into soccer bribery allegations.
15Marco Polo Del Nero, the president of the Brazilian Football Confederation, dramatically flew back to Brazil from the FIFA Congress shortly before the election.
16His abrupt departure followed the arrest of nine senior FIFA officials including former Brazilian soccer chief Jose Maria Marin.
17Del Nero told a press conference on Friday he did not plan to resign and "had nothing to do" with corruption.
18FIFA takes in billions of dollars in revenue from television marketing rights and sponsorships, making it one of the wealthiest and most powerful sports bodies in the world.
19It has been dogged by corruption scandals for decades, mostly investigating itself and avoiding scrutiny by criminal courts.
20Russia and Qatar deny wrongdoing in their bids to host the cup.
21Russian President Vladimir Putin has accused the United States of meddling in an effort to force Blatter out.
22Qatar on Friday issued a further defence of its bid and said it would carry on with plans to stage the event.
23The decision to host the world's biggest soccer tournament in a small desert state where daytime summer temperatures rarely fall below 40 degrees Celsius startled many in global sport.
24Many of Blatter's opponents have spoken of steps they can take against him.
25English Football Association chairman Greg Dyke said England could back a possible boycott of the 2018 World Cup if Blatter stays in office.
26Other European soccer officials have also alluded to the prospect of a boycott, but that is still seen as unlikely given the tournament's importance to the global game.
27Most of the developing world in Africa, Asia and parts of Central America and the Caribbean are happy that FIFA under Blatter has guaranteed them annual grants and bonus payments in World Cup years.
