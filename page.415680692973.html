Nationals name Max Scherzer opening day starter (Yahoo Sports)

1(AP) -- Max Scherzer will start the Washington Nationals' opener on April 6 against the New York Mets, not Stephen Strasburg.
2''It's going to be real exciting,'' Scherzer said Monday.
3''It's a great honor to be able to start opening day, especially for this club, and especially on this staff.
4But at the end of the day, it's just game one.
5Starting a $210 million, seven-year contract, Scherzer will be making his first opening-day start.
6Strasburg had pitched the last three for the Nationals.
7Manager Matt Williams said there were ''lots of reasons'' for picking Scherzer.
8''He's a really good pitcher,'' Williams said.
9''He's confident.
10Strasburg could slot in for game 3 if the team stays to its spring training rotation of Scherzer, Jordan Zimmermann, Strasburg, Doug Fister and Gio Gonzalez.
11Williams said he spoken with Strasburg about the opening-day start, approaching him with ''honesty'' and making sure that Strasburg understood the team had confidence in him, too.
12''I can tell you this, to a man: The guys who are actually throwing the baseball, they don't much care about it,'' Williams said.
13Scherzer went 18-5 with a 3.15 ERA for Detroit last season and won the AL Cy Young Award in 2013.
14He did not get the opening day start last year, taken by Justin Verlander.
15Scherzer said pitching the opener was secondary to making the postseason.
16''In my experiences, the playoffs are more intense than anything I've ever seen in the regular season,'' Scherzer said.
17''I've started Game 1 of the playoffs.
