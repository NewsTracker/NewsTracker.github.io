Home runs by Cruz, Morrison lead Mariners to win over Angels

1So if this is going to be a thing for Nelson Cruz and Logan Morrison, maybe they should have some runners on base when it happens to maximize the effect.
2For the third time this season, Nelson Cruz and Logan Morrison homered in the same inning - belting solo shots in the seventh inning of Seattle's 3-2 victory Monday over the Los Angeles Angels.
3The duo did it twice during the Mariners' miserable series against the Houston Astros, but unlike the defeats in Houston, the Mariners prevailed Monday, snapping a four-game losing streak.
4On a night when the Mariners were just fighting to get a runner on base against Angels starter Matt Shoemaker, the solos would suffice.
5Shoemaker had been cruising along for six innings, allowing just one hit - a Kyle Seager single to left in the second inning.
6Shoemaker was up 1-2 on Cruz, but he left a changeup over the inside part of the plate belt-high.
7Cruz yanked a line drive well inside the left-field foul pole for his 14th homer of the season.
8Cruz is just one of five players in MLB history to hit 14 homers in his first 26 games of the season.
9Two batters later, Morrison jumped all over a 1-0 changeup from Shoemaker, depositing it into the right field seats to make it 2-0.
10A 2-0 lead seemed like plenty for Mariners' starter Felix Hernandez.
11Heck, he's used to working with less.
12The Seattle ace let his lead get trimmed to 2-1 when he left a fastball over the plate on a 3-1 count to Matt Joyce for a solo homer to right center.
13But the Mariners answered Joyce's solo shot with another of their own when Seth Smith hit a homer to right center to make it 3-1.
14The Mariners have hit 34 homers this season and 27 have come with the bases empty.
15That one run became very important because closer Fernando Rodney made things interesting in the ninth, giving up a solo home run to David Freese to make it 3-2.
16He then walked Joyce to put the tying run on first.
17But the game ended moments later when pinch runner Collin Cowgill attempted to steal second base on the first pitch.
18Instead, Mike Zunino fired a perfect throw to second to get Cowgill with ease for the final out of the game.
19Rodney got his eighth save of the season.
20Hernandez improved to 5-0 on the season working seven innings, giving up one run on six hits with a walk and eight strikeouts to lower his ERA to 1.73.
21He wasn't invincible.
22The Angels had some scoring chances against him.
23In the fourth inning, they had runners on first and second with two outs, but Joyce's hard fly ball to right field was run down by Cruz.
24In the sixth inning, Mike Trout led off with a single on a poorly located 0-2 changeup from Hernandez.
25But any potential trouble was averted when the Mariners doubled off a stealing Trout at first base.
26Albert Pujols hit a liner to right field, which was easily caught by Cruz.
27Trout tried to retreat and get back safely, but Cruz's strong throw was gloved on the short hop by Morrison for the double play.
28Threat averted.
