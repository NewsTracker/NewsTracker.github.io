Baltimore police open probe into new shooting of unarmed black man

1WASHINGTON Police have opened an investigation into the killing of an unarmed black man by law enforcement officers outside Baltimore, authorities said on Saturday, two months after the city was rocked by protests over the death of another African-American who was taken into custody.
2The latest incident unfolded in the Baltimore suburb of Owing Mills, where three officers fired at least 19 rounds and killed Spencer Lee McCain, 41, during a domestic disturbance on Thursday.
3The Baltimore County police's homicide unit is investigating, police said in a statement Saturday.
4Police will conduct an internal review to determine compliance with departmental policy, rules and regulations, the department said.
5It will send the findings to the Baltimore County State's Attorney's Office for review.
6The investigation comes at time when U.S. law enforcement is under close scrutiny over the use of lethal force, especially in confrontations with African-American men.
7In April, 25-year-old Freddie Gray died from a spinal injury suffered in custody of Baltimore police.
8His death triggered protests and rioting on the day of his funeral, drawing national attention to the city.
9Six Baltimore police officers are now facing criminal charges in his death.
10A statement by the Baltimore County police identified the officers involved in the latest incident as Wilkes, Besaw and Stargel, providing only their last names.
11A police spokesman would not provide first names or ages, saying he was prohibited from doing so by union rules.
12Baltimore County Police Department salary records show Shenell Wilkes, Jonathan Besaw and Shannon Stargel as the only employees with those last names.
13Wilkes is a six year veteran, Besaw an eight year veteran, and Stargel is a five year veteran, police said.
14Officers were called to a home in Owings Mills just after 1 a.m. on Thursday.
15They forced their way inside when they heard a disturbance, police said.
16Three officers shot McCain, fearing he had a weapon, but no firearms were found.
17Investigators found 19 shell casings, but the exact number of rounds fired was unknown, police said.
18McCain was taken to a hospital, where he was pronounced dead.
19The officers have been placed on administrative leave.
20A woman and two children were also in the home and the woman was injured, the police statement said.
21McCain was under a protective order barring him from having contact with the woman and the children and from going to the home.
22Police had been called to the address about 17 times since January 2012, police said.
