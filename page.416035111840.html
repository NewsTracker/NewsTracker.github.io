Braves designate Quinten for assignment; Grilli new closer (Yahoo Sports)

1The Nationals won 9-8.
2MIAMI (AP) -- Hours before Monday's season opener, Atlanta Braves manager Fredi Gonzalez assessed his team's latest moves and didn't try to spin them as an upgrade.
3''It's a big boy's world,'' Gonzalez said.
4On the eve of the season, the Braves traded All-Star closer Craig Kimbel and outfielder Melvin Upton Jr. to the San Diego Padres for outfielders Cameron Maybin and Carlos Quinten, two minor leaguers and a draft pick.
5Quinten never joined the Braves, and the two-time All-Star was designated for assignment.
6Maybin arrived before the opener against the Miami Marlins but wasn't in the starting lineup.
7Veteran right-hander Jason Grilli was given the closer job, with right-hander Jim Johnson his backup.
8Grilli has 50 career saves, including 33 with the Pittsburgh Pirates in 2013.
9''We feel like if the team gives us a lead, these guys will be able to shut it down for us,'' Gonzalez said.
10Maybin will play some against left-handers and be used as a late-inning defensive replacement in center field, Gonzalez said.
11The Braves' motive for the trade was to unload the remaining $46.35 million on the underachieving Upton's contract.
12Even so, the loss of Kimbrel was a blow.
13He has had at least 45 saves each of the past four seasons.
14The trade was the latest in a series of rebuilding moves by the Braves since a late-season collapse that cost them a 2014 playoff berth.
15They have only six players who were on the opening day roster a year ago, the fewest in the majors.
16The objective this year hasn't changed, however, Gonzalez said.
