The latest on Egypt attack: 4 wounded in shootout

1The governor of the southern Egyptian city of Luxor, Mohammed Sayed Badr, says that no tourists were hurt in the suicide bombing outside the famed Karnak temple.
2He told The Associated Press over the phone that the attack was "an attempt to break into the temple of Karnak. They didn't make it in."
3Badr says three suspected militants carrying bags got out of a car in the temple's parking lot.
4Police were immediately suspicious and ordered them to stop.
5One of the three then began running, so the police fired at him and an explosive belt he was wearing blew up.
6A second man had a gun and started firing at the police before he was shot dead.
7The governor says the third man was arrested by an undercover policeman after he was wounded in the exchange of fire.
8Badr says the nationalities of the three men have yet to be determined.
9Egyptian officials say police killed two suspected Islamic militants in a shootout outside the ancient Karnak temple in the southern city of Luxor as a suicide bomber struck meters away from the famed tourist attraction, without killing anyone.
10The Health Ministry says four people, including two policemen, were wounded in the gunbattle Wednesday.
11The officials say there were only a handful of tourists and Egyptians inside the temple at the time of the late morning attack.
12The officials spoke on condition of anonymity because they were not authorized to brief reporters.
13Luxor is home to some of Egypt's most famous ancient temples and pharaonic tombs, including that of King Tutankhamun.
14The city has been hit hard by a downturn in visitors since Egypt's 2011 uprising.
15Associated Press writer Haggag Salama in Luxor, Egypt contributed to this report.
