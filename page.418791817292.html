Turkish jets strike Kurds in Iraq, Islamic State in Syria

1Image from aircraft cockpit video released by Turkey's state-run agency Anadolu Friday, July 24, 2015, of what they report to be Turkish warplanes striking Islamic State group targets across the border in Syria.
2Earlier a government official said three F-16 jets took off from Diyarbakir airbase in southeast Turkey early Friday and used smart bombs to hit three IS targets across the Turkish border province from Kilis.
3The official, who spoke on condition of anonymity because of government rules requiring prior authorisation for comment, said the targets were two command centres and a gathering point of IS supporters.
4The official said the Turkish planes had not violated Syrian airspace.
5ANKARA, Turkey (AP) - Turkish jets struck camps belonging to Kurdish militants in northern Iraq, authorities said Saturday, the first strike since a peace deal was announced in 2013, as Ankara also bombed Islamic State positions in Syria for a second straight night.
6The strikes in Iraq targeted the Kurdistan Workers' Party, or PKK, whose affiliates have been effective in battling the Islamic State group.
7The strikes further complicate the U.S.-led war against the extremists, which has relied on Kurdish forces making gains in both Iraq and Syria.
8A spokesman in Iraq for the PKK, which has been fighting Turkey for autonomy since 1984 and is considered a terrorist organization by Ankara and its allies, said the strikes likely spelled the end of the peace agreement.
9"Turkey has basically ended the cease-fire," Zagros Hiwa told The Associated Press, declining to elaborate further.
10He said the PKK was still assessing the damage caused by the strikes, though they didn't appear to cause casualties.
11The jets hit PKK shelters, bunkers, caves, storages facilities and other "logistical points," a statement from the Turkish prime minister's office said.
12It said five areas were targeted, including the Qandil mountains, where the PKK's command is based.
13The statement did not specify Islamic State targets that were struck in Syria in a second night of bombings, but described the airstrikes in both Syria and Iraq as being "effective."
14Hiwa said the jets struck villages on Qandil although the PKK base was not hit.
15Turkey's military also shelled Islamic State and PKK positions in Syria from across the Turkish border, the government said.
16It vowed to press ahead with operations against the PKK and IS, saying it was "determined to take all steps to ensure peace and security for our people."
17Turkish police meanwhile proceeded with a major operation against the Islamic State, the PKK and the far-left DHKP-C for a second day running.
18More than 300 people were detained in raids in Istanbul and 21 provinces, the government statement said.
19Tensions have flared with Kurds in recent days after an Islamic State suicide bombing in the southeastern Turkish city of Suruc on Monday killed 32 people.
20Kurdish groups held the Turkish government responsible for the blast, saying it had not been aggressive in battling the Islamic State group.
21On Wednesday, the PKK claimed responsibility for the killing of two Turkish police officers near the Kurdish majority city of Sanliurfa, near the Syrian border.
22In other attacks, seven police officers were wounded after suspected PKK militants hurled a small bomb at a police station in the town of Bismil, near the mainly Kurdish city of Diyarbakir, the Dogan news agency reported Friday.
23Assailants also hurled a small bomb at officers inside a police vehicle in the town of Semdinli, near the border with Iraq, the agency said.
24Kurdish rebels also kidnapped a police officer in Lice, the state-run Anadolu Agency reported.
25Another group of rebels raided a privately owned thermal power plant near the Iraqi border, destroying vehicles and abducting 15 workers, Dogan reported.
26Turkey started attacking Islamic State positions after the suicide bombing and an extremist ambush that killed a Turkish soldier.
27On Friday, three F-16 jets struck Islamic State targets that included two command centers and a gathering point near the Turkish border in Syria.
28The Syrian Observatory for Human Rights said nine Islamic State militants were killed in the raids.
29The extremists have yet to comment on the strikes.
30Turkey also announced Friday that it was allowing its air bases to be used by the U.S.-led coalition forces for operations against Islamic State extremists.
31Turkey until now had been reluctant to take part in U.S.-led coalition airstrikes against the Islamic State group.
32Associated Press writer Bram Janssen in Irbil, Iraq contributed to this report.
