Google Doodle celebrates 126th Anniversary of Eiffel Tower's first public opening

1GOOGLE is celebrating the 126th Anniversary of the Eiffel Towers first ever public opening with a Doodle on its homepage to mark the occasion.
2The Doodle shows six workmen on iron girders - nonchalantly putting the final touches to the iron structure - despite being over 1,000ft in the air.
3Secured to the structure by safety lines which spell out the word Google, the workmen don't seem unnerved at the enormous task ahead of them.
4One of the men can be seen wearing a traditional French beret, while two others have chosen to opt for a handlebar moustache and full bushy beard.
5The Eiffel Tower, located on the Champ de Mars, Paris, was created by French civil engineer and architect Alexandre Gustave Eiffel in 1887 - serving as the entrance arch to the 1889 World's Fair - to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French revolution.
6Boasting three floors, the iron lattice tower is the tallest structure in Paris - attracting around seven million visitors to the iconic landmark each year.
