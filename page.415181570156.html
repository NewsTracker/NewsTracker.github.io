Firefighters wake up to smoke in station, then battle blaze

WIRELESS SHOW HIGHLIGHTS: Eyes on Apple, BlackBerry

1Nine Salt Lake City firefighters who escaped the fire that erupted at their own station were taken to a hospital to be checked for smoke inhalation.
2Fire Department spokesman Jasen Asay says in a statement that the nine firefighters fled the building and returned with a truck to fight the blaze.
3SALT LAKE CITY (AP) - Firefighters are used to alarms in the middle of the night, but the smoke doesn't usually come to their front door.
4A group of sleeping Salt Lake City firefighters cracked open the door of their upstairs dorm room early Tuesday to find smoke pouring inside from a fire that erupted at their own station.
5They closed the door and jumped on the room's fire pole to flee the flames, then quickly returned to the building with a firetruck to battle the blaze.
6"They wake up, and within a minute, they're fighting a fire in their second home," he said.
7Two dozen additional firefighters soon joined them.
8When the fire was extinguished, nine firefighters were taken to a hospital to be evaluated for smoke inhalation and then released several hours later.
9"They love their station and needed to react quickly, and they did a good job of that," he said.
10Still, it was an emotional morning, he said.
11Crews usually have a few minutes to prepare themselves before they start putting out a fire, but that was impossible Tuesday as the blaze spread in a place with special meaning for many of the city's firefighters.
12The station is one of Salt Lake City's oldest, and most of the department's 325 firefighters have worked there at some point, Asay said.
13"It means a lot to the entire department, especially to the nine firefighters who were there this morning," he said.
14Investigators are trying to determine what caused the accidental blaze, which left the second floor of the building with heavy damage.
15It started in a utility room with a soda machine where linens and rags are stored and spread to the kitchen and the building's vent system.
16Smoke woke up two captains sleeping on the first floor about 1:30 a.m., and they called to the other seven firefighters upstairs.
17All the firefighters based at the damaged building are working out of other stations until their facility gets repaired.
