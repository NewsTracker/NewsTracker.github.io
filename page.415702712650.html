Ruth Porat to become Google CFO

1Morgan Stanley chief financial officer and executive vice president Ruth Porat participates in a panel discussion at the Brookings Institution March 2, 2015 in Washington, DC.
2Morgan Stanley chief financial officer Ruth Porat is leaving the bank to join Google Inc in the same position, the companies said on Tuesday.
3Porat, 57, will replace Patrick Pichette, who said on March 10 that he planned to retire.
4Jonathan Pruzan, co-head of Morgan Stanley's global financial institutions group in investment banking, will become CFO after Porat leaves on April 30.
5Porat, who joined Morgan Stanley in 1987, is no stranger to the technology industry, having led the bank's technology franchise during the internet boom.
6Porat will join Google on May 26, the company said in a blog post.
7Pruzan joined Morgan Stanley in 1994 and he has been working with its financial institution clients.
8"He understands the current regulatory environment, having shepherded clients through the Fed's stress test," chief executive James Gorman said in a memo to staff.
9Google shares were up 0.7% at $570.45 in early trading on the Nasdaq, while Morgan Stanley shares were down slightly at $36.16 on the New York Stock Exchange.
