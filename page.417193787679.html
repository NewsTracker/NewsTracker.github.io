4MONTREAL (AP) -- Michael Sam has found a pro football job.
5In Canada.
6Sam, the first openly gay player drafted in the NFL, signed a two-year contract Friday with the Montreal Alouettes of the Canadian Football league.
7General manager Jim Popp considered Sam's sexuality a nonissue.
8''Michael Sam is a very good football player, and that's the reason we signed him,'' Popp said.
9He also was unemployed after failing to stick with two NFL teams last season.
10Sam came out before last year's NFL draft.
11He was selected in the seventh round by the St. Louis Rams and cut in training camp.
12The Dallas Cowboys signed him to their practice squad but released him in October.
13Sam then made another unsuccessful attempt at an NFL job at this year's veterans combine.
14''I cannot wait to put on the pads, get back on the field and work hard each and every day with my teammates to bring a Grey Cup to the great fans here in Montreal,'' Sam said in a statement.
15The 25-year-old defensive end will be introduced at a news conference Tuesday in Montreal and report to training camp the following day.
16The regular season opens June 25 when Montreal hosts Ottawa.
17Sam starred at Missouri in college, earning SEC Defensive Player of the Year honors in 2013.
18After he came out, his NFL jersey was an instant best-seller and President Barack Obama publicly congratulated Sam and the Rams.
19CFL Commissioner Jeffrey Orridge did the same Friday.
20''Congratulations to the Montreal Alouettes on the signing of Michael Sam,'' Orridge said in a statement.

25''Each time he was let go, there were discussions,'' Popp said.
26Popp said Sam was considered a ''tweener'' by NFL clubs, not quite the right body type to be a defensive end or an outside linebacker for that league.
27But he may fit perfectly in the CFL.
28Popp believes Sam can follow a path similar to that of Cam Wake, who was converted from linebacker to end when he joined the B.C. Lions in 2007.
29Wake was the CFL Defensive Player of the Year in each of his two seasons before signing with the Miami Dolphins, where he has had a successful NFL career.
