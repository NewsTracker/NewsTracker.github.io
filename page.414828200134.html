Iraq tells United Nations that Islamic State committed genocide

1The statement came the same day as reports that Islamic State fighters had burned 45 people to death in Iraq.
2Iraq told the United Nations Security Council on Tuesday that Islamic State militants have committed genocide.
3"These terrorist groups have desecrated all human values. They have committed the most heinous criminal terrorist acts against the Iraqi people whether Shi'ite, Sunni, Christians, Turkmen, Shabak or Yazidis," Iraq's UN Ambassador Mohamed Ali Alhakim said.
4"These are in fact crimes of genocide committed against humanity that must be held accountable before international justice," he told the UN Security Council.

6Alhakim did not give further details of the crimes.
7The country's Shia-led government, backed by US-led airstrikes, has been trying to push back the Islamic State since it swept through mainly Sunni Muslim provinces of northern Iraq in June.
8At the time, the militants met virtually no resistance.
9Alhakim said Iraq needed more help to liberate all areas under Islamic State control.
10Victims of the Islamic State onslaught have previously claimed the terror group committed genocide in their towns and villages.
11The US began its air campaign against the Islamic State last August with President Barack Obama citing fears that genocide against minority Yazidis would take place.
12BBC: Islamic State fighters have burned to death 45 people in western Iraqi town of al-Baghdadi http://t.co/ufM2zowAWR.
13UN Assistant Secretary-General for human rights Ivan Simonovic said in October that the campaign of Islamic State militants against Iraq's Yazidi minority may be attempted genocide.
14The UN envoy to Iraq, Nikolay Mladenov, said he was gravely concerned about the safety of Iraq's diverse ethnic and religious communities in areas under Islamic State control, particularly thousands of women and children still in captivity.
15"Almost daily terrorist attacks continue to deliberately target all Iraqis, most notably the Shi'ite community, as well as ethnic and religious minorities, across the country," he told the Security Council.
16"Equally worrying is the increasing number of reports of revenge attacks committed particularly against members of the Sunni community in areas liberated from (Islamic State) control," he said.
17Mladenov urged the Iraqi government to quickly provide military and financial help it had pledged to local leaders and tribal fighters to take on Islamic State, but warned against just a military solution.
18"For Iraq to move forward, it is crucial that this fragile process of inclusion also expands to the political sphere," Mladenov said.
19"An exclusively military solution to the problem of (Islamic State) is impossible, indeed it would be counterproductive."
