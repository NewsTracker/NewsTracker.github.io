Islamic State seizes town near Ramadi in Iraq, planting black flag with 'bodies lying in the street'

1Smoke rises from a bomb attack in clashes between Iraqi security forces and Islamic State militants on the outskirts of Ramadi.
2Baghdad: Islamic State militants have gained ground in western Iraq, overrunning another village near the capital of Anbar province in a dawn raid, police sources and local officials said.
3Hundreds of families fled Albu Ghanim after security forces came under attack from the militants overnight and withdrew from the area, around five kilometres north-east of the provincial capital Ramadi.
4Sunni fighters stand guard near a school used as a shelter for displaced people in Ramadi, before IS took the village.
5Abu Jasim, who left Albu Ghanim soon after it fell on Wednesday, said the insurgents had set up a checkpoint at the main entrance to the village and planted their black flag there.
6"IS stopped us and said we have came to liberate you from these Safavids and rejectionists," Abu Jasim said.
7Safavid and rejectionist are derogatory terms used by hardline Sunni Islamists to refer to Shi'ites.
8"We told them we were leaving because the kids were terrified. They let us go, and we saw bodies lying in the streets, some police and others civilians."
9Refugees sit in the school in Ramadi.
10The militants have been making inroads on Ramadi's northern periphery since the government announced the start of a new offensive last week to recapture the Sunni heartland of Anbar.
11Two federal police battalions arrived in Ramadi on Wednesday to reinforce the beleaguered forces, according to a colonel and a policeman.
12Another resident who left Albu Ghanim said the jihadists had declared their victory via loudspeaker in the village mosque.
13Abu Amar said his son, a policeman, was missing, and he had heard the militants had a list of conscripts whom they had already begun executing.
14Large parts of Anbar had slipped from the government's grasp even before Islamic State seized the northern city of Mosul last June and proclaimed a caliphate straddling the border between Iraq and Syria.
15Security forces and Shi'ite paramilitaries have since regained some ground in Iraq, although core Sunni territories remain under Islamic State control, including Nineveh province, of which Mosul is capital, and most of Anbar.
16The new Anbar campaign was intended to build on a victory in the city of Tikrit, which Iraqi security forces and Shi'ite paramilitaries retook this month.
17But the Sunni jihadists have struck back in Anbar as well as Baiji, where they blasted through the security perimeter around Iraq's largest refinery several days ago.
18The operations command for Salahuddin province in which Baiji is located said skirmishes between security forces and the militants continued on Wednesday inside the refinery compound.
19The US-led coalition said it had conducted air strikes in support of Iraqi forces in both Baiji and around Ramadi.
