Microsoft profit, revenue beats Wall Street view; shares up

1(Reuters) - Microsoft Corp on Thursday reported revenue and profit above Wall Street expectations, as sales of its hardware and cloud-computing services helped to offset a decline in the company's core Windows business.
2Shares rose 3.1 percent to $44.70 in after-hours trading.
3"The company beat across the board," said FBR Capital markets analyst Daniel Ives.
4"The Street will cheer these results as it appears Microsoft is back on the right track after a head-scratching performance last quarter."
5Sales of Windows to computer manufacturers to install on new PCs fell 19 percent in the quarter, reflecting a sharp dip from a year ago when Windows got a brief boost from consumers rushing to buy new machines after Microsoft stopped support for the 14-year-old XP operating system.
6That decline was offset by higher revenue from its Surface tablet, back-end server software and cloud-related offerings such as its online Office 365 suite of applications.
7The company said its commercial cloud-related revenue for the quarter more than doubled, and was now running at $6.3 billion a year.
8Amazon.com Inc said on Thursday its quarterly cloud revenue rose almost 50 percent to $1.57 billion, suggesting a similar annual number.
9Microsoft's overall revenue rose 6 percent to $21.7 billion, above Wall Street's average forecast of $21.1 billion, according to Thomson Reuters I/B/E/S.
10Taking out the effects of the strong U.S. dollar on currency rates, Microsoft said revenue would have risen 9 percent.
11Earnings per share declined to 61 cents per share from 68 cents in the year-ago quarter.
12Analysts had expected 51 cents, on average.
