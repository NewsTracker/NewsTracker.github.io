Sheryl Sandberg joins SurveyMonkey's board to contribute to late husband's legacy

1Sheryl Sandberg, chief operating officer of Facebook Inc., is joining the board of SurveyMonkey, the company her late husband, Dave Goldberg, used to run.
2Sheryl Sandberg took a seat Monday on the board of Palo Alto-based SurveyMonkey, as the online polling company previously run by her late husband, Dave Goldberg, also named a top Hewlett-Packard executive as its new CEO.
3Bill Veghte, the head of HP's Enterprise Group and its former chief operating officer, is the new CEO, effective on Aug. 3.
4He is also joining SurveyMonkey's board along with Sandberg and Lyra Health Chief Executive David Ebersman.
5Veghte and Goldberg were friends going back to their college days together and Re/code reported that Goldberg had urged Veghte to join SurveyMonkey's board before he died this year.
6Now, he will join the board and serve alongside Sandberg.
7Goldberg died in an accident during a vacation in May when he collapsed while exercising.
8Here's how Sandberg will continue to contribute to SurveyMonkey.
9Although Sandberg is busy with her roles as the chief operating officer at Facebook Inc. and author of the book "Lean In," she felt it was important to contribute to Goldberg's legacy.
10"Ever since Dave joined SurveyMonkey six years ago, I watched him and his team execute on their mission to help people make better decisions," Sandberg said in a press release.
11"I'm looking forward to working with the board and this amazing team and help to realize Dave's vision of building a lasting company that will impact the way we all do business for years to come."
12Sandberg took to Facebook in early May to express her feelings for Goldberg, who she said was her "rock."
13"We had 11 truly joyful years of the deepest love, happiest marriage and truest partnership that I could imagine," Sandberg wrote.
14"He gave me the experience of being deeply understood, truly supported and completely and utterly loved - and I will carry that with me always."
