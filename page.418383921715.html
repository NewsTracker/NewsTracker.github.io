Reds' Frazier, rookies Bryant, Pederson in Home Run Derby (Yahoo Sports)

1NEW YORK (AP) -- The Reds' Todd Frazier is paired with two-time champion Prince Fielder of Texas in the first round of Monday's All-Star Home Run Derby in Cincinnati.
2In this year's new format, Chicago Cubs rookie Kris Bryant is in a pairing with the Los Angeles Angels' Albert Pujols, Toronto's Josh Donaldson is in a bracket with the Chicago Cubs' Anthony Rizzo, and Los Angeles Dodgers rookie Joc Pederson takes on Baltimore's Manny Machado.
3The Frazier-Fielder winner faces Donaldson or Rizzo, and the Bryant-Pujols winner goes against Pederson or Machado.
4Brackets were based on season home run totals through Tuesday.
5Each batter gets five minutes per round, and home runs in each batter's final minute stop the clock.
6Players can earn up to 1 minute, 30 seconds of bonus time based on home run distance.
7''Swinging for five minutes, that's going to be a really long time.
8Really long,'' Pederson said.
9Pederson has selected Triple-A coach Johnny Washington to pitch to him.
10''He's been with me since Day 1,'' he said.
11Bryant said his father, Mike, will throw to him.
12Mike Bryant taught Kris to hit at a young age, passing along the lessons he learned from Ted Williams during the two spring trainings he was a Boston Red Sox farmhand in the early 1980s.
13''I'm probably doing it more for him so he can experience it with me,'' Kris Bryant said Wednesday.
14Bryant thinks Rizzo may have an advantage at Great American Ball Park.
15''Cincinnati's a pretty good left-handed hitter's ballpark,'' Bryant said.
16Fielder won the derby in 2009 and 2012.
17He will be participating for the sixth time, matching Barry Bonds and Sammy Sosa, and trailing only Ken Griffey Jr. and Mark McGwire.
