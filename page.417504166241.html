Asian stocks drift higher on Greek deal hopes, ECB comments

1HONG KONG (AP) - Most Asian stock markets were higher Thursday on hopes Greece can reach a deal with creditors to save the country from financial disaster and signs that Europe's stimulus program is working.
2KEEPING SCORE: Japan's benchmark Nikkei 225 index gained 0.4 percent to 20,546.25 while South Korea's Kospi rose 0.7 percent to 2,078.44.
3Hong Kong's Hang Seng climbed 0.4 percent to 27,776.11 while the Shanghai Composite Index in mainland China added 0.4 percent to 4,933.23.
4Australia's S&P/ASX 200 fell 0.4 percent to 5,561.80.
5GREEK TALKS: Key creditors are meeting with Greece's prime minister in Brussels this week as part of diplomatic efforts to resolve negotiations over budget reforms it needs to carry out in order to get access to bailout loans.
6Investor hopes were rising that both sides would be able to come to a deal and avert financial meltdown for the debt-stricken country.
7EUROPE'S RECOVERY: European Central Bank chief Mario Draghi said the 1.1 trillion euro stimulus program is supporting the modest recovery in the eurozone.
8He also raised the ECB's inflation 2015 forecast to 0.3 percent from zero previously, signaling there's less risk of a long-term drop in prices that can cause economic stagnation.
9WALL STREET: Major U.S. benchmarks rose on encouraging economic news.
10The S&P 500 edged up 0.2 percent to close at 2,114.07 while the Dow Jones industrial average rose 0.4 percent to 18,076.27.
11The Nasdaq composite climbed 0.5 percent to 5,099.23.
12ENERGY: Benchmark U.S. crude slipped 9 cents to $59.55 a barrel in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
13The contract fell $1.62 to close at $59.64 a barrel on Wednesday.
14Brent crude, a benchmark for international oil used by many U.S. refineries, lost 14 cents to $63.66 in London.
15CURRENCIES: The dollar rose to 124.47 yen from 124.35 yen on Wednesday.
16The euro rose to $1.1275 from $1.1262.
