Prince Ali hints he might pull out of FIFA election campaign

1DEAD SEA, Jordan (Reuters) - Prince Ali Bin Al Hussein said on Monday he would consider withdrawing from the FIFA presidential election race to allow a single candidate to challenge Sepp Blatter when the votes are cast at the FIFA Congress on May 29.
2The 39-year-old Jordanian royal, who is president of the Jordan FA and a member of the FIFA executive committee is standing against Blatter along with Michael van Praag, the president of the Dutch FA and former Portugal World Player of the Year Luis Figo.
3"The important thing is that we support the person who has the best chance of making a change in FIFA," he said.
4Prince Ali has privately spoken of the need to launch the strongest possible challenge to Blatter and said at the Soccerex Asia Forum, which he is hosting at the King Hussein convention centre some 60kms from Amman, that the time had come to change FIFA for the better.
5The 79-year-old Blatter, who has been president since 1998, is seeking a fifth term in office and is expected to get it when FIFA's 209 members vote at their Congress in Zurich.
6Unless he wins the presidential election, Prince Ali will leave the FIFA executive in May because of changes to the way the Asian confederation selects its members for the board.
7He told the BBC that it might be better for delegates at the Congress to have a straight choice between two candidates rather than four.
8"We all have our different approaches but I think we have one common goal and that's about the need for change in FIFA.
9"It's never about one person and that's been the case for me from the very beginning."
10Asked if he would consider withdrawing he replied: "Of course, everything is there -- but it has to be for the candidate with the best possibilities of winning."
