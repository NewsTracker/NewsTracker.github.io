British PM asks for drones, troops in fight against Islamic State

1LONDON -- Prime Minister David Cameron wants Britain's military to invest more in drones and elite troops to help counter the threat from the Islamic State group.
2Cameron says he's asking defence chiefs to consider boosting quick-response counterterrorist capabilities such as "spy planes, drones and special forces."
3On Monday, Cameron is visiting an air force base that is home to Britain's fleet of Reaper drones, which have flown surveillance missions over Iraq and Syria as part of the U.S.-led campaign against IS.
4British warplanes have struck targets in Iraq, and Cameron has suggested the mission could be expanded to Syria.
5Britain's military has faced a budget squeeze in recent years as Cameron's government cut public spending.
6But Treasury chief George Osborne has committed to spending 2 per cent of national income on defence.
