Ukraine's president says truce not working, urges more Russia sanctions: newspaper

Astros’ Brett Oberholtzer sidelined at least a week

1FRANKFURT (Reuters) - Ukraine's president accused Russian-backed separatists in the east of failing to respect a ceasefire with Ukrainian troops and called for further sanctions on Russia in comments to a German newspaper to be published on Monday.
2Attacks have become less frequent since the latest ceasefire came into force in mid-February, but both sides accuse each other of violations.
3Ukraine's military said last week that pro-Russian rebels were using the truce to amass heavy weapons.
4An agreement signed in Minsk in September called with the withdrawal of heavy weapons from the front line.
5"Ukraine has fulfilled every single point of the Minsk agreement. The ceasefire has been implemented immediately on our part, but the Russian fighters have done the exact opposite," Petro Poroshenko told the Bild newspaper.
6"Every day, there is shooting from the Russian side, often more than 60 times a day. In total, the ceasefire has been broken 1,100 times," he said.
7"The truth is that the agreement is not working."
8Poroshenko also called for additional sanctions against Russia for breaking the ceasefire.
9At a summit in Brussels next Thursday and Friday, European Union leaders will discuss the sanctions imposed on Russia's financial, energy and defense sectors last July over Russia's annexation of Crimea and supporting the separatists in Ukraine.
10Germany and other EU member countries want to push for a declaration that will not allow sanctions to be eased unless Moscow complies with the Minsk agreement, two officials said on Saturday.
1- Astros lefthander Brett Oberholtzer, who entered camp as a near lock for the starting rotation, has been ordered off the mound for a week because of a mild left lat strain.
2Oberholtzer was scheduled to pitch in a minor-league game Sunday, but an MRI exam Friday revealed the lat strain.
3He initially felt lat discomfort after making a pickoff throw during his outing Tuesday.
4"I went in and got an MRI and it's just a little tightness, nothing to worry about," he said.
5"I'm still on my throwing program, but just taking a little break off the mound for about a week or so."
6Oberholtzer and Astros manager A.J. Hinch both appear optimistic about having the lefthander ready soon.
7"This is mild from what we know, but again we got to keep evaluating with them," Hinch said.
8"We're at a time in camp where we want to make sure these guys are feeling good as these guys are extended out into their outings.
9"We'll lose a few days with Obie right now. When we reconvene after the split-squad and into (Wednesday's) off-day we'll reevaluate him."
10Oberholtzer, 25, is 0-1 with a 3.60 ERA this spring while giving up three runs (two earned) over five innings through two outings.
11He was 5-13 with a 4.39 ERA last year over 24 starts in the majors while shuttling between Houston and Class AAA Oklahoma City.
12He was the Astros' Pitcher of the Year in 2013 after posting a 4-5 record and 2.76 ERA as a rookie.
13Lat strains have proved problematic for the Astros in the last two spring training camps.
14Righthanded prospect Asher Wojciechowski didn't pitch last spring because of a lat strain he suffered before camp.
15Hard-throwing righthander Vince Velasquez, one of top pitching prospects in the organization, has not gotten on the mound this spring because he still hasn't recovered from the lat strain he suffered in early November during the Arizona Fall League.
16"It feels great throwing, but it's spring training," Oberholtzer said.
17"It's still early. I wanted to nip it in the bud and take care of it while I can."
