NFL DRAFT: Jets grab QB Petty in trade (Yahoo Sports)

1CHICAGO (AP) -- Quarterbacks Bryce Petty and Brett Hundley found their way into the NFL on Day 3 of the draft Saturday - heading to entirely different circumstances.
2Baylor's Petty went to the New York Jets, who've had inconsistency at the position for years.
3UCLA's Hundley will journey to Green Bay, where hardly anyone can remember the last time the Packers had questions behind center.
4The Jets made an early splash in the fourth round after a night of contemplating who remained on the board.
5New York moved up one spot in a deal with Jacksonville to get the prolific passer who operated a spread offense in college.
6Petty will have plenty to learn in the pros, and will have Geno Smith, Ryan Fitzpatrick and Matt Simms ahead on the depth chart.
7But the Jets have not truly been set at quarterback since the days of Chad Pennington, and new coach Todd Bowles has no ties to the other QBs.
8As for the big transition, Petty was confident he can do it smoothly.
9''What I was asked to do in college, it was different, but it's not because I couldn't do a pro-style offense or West Coast offense or whatever,'' he said.
10Hundley, who went in the first round of some mock drafts, instead had to wait until No.
11147 overall.
12His next stop is Lambeau Field, hardly a quarterbacking abyss.
13''I think Green Bay is the perfect organization to learn as much as possible, and they have the best coaches to help me do that,'' Hundley said.
14Another quarterback went in the fourth round - well a former QB, Blake Bell of Oklahoma.
15The ''Belldozer'' is now a tight end and wound up San Francisco.
16''In your eyes, you're kind of just, 'Hey, I played quarterback before.
17So, it's I can see the coverage, man, zone, what the corner's doing, linebackers, D-ends, stuff like that,' '' he said.
