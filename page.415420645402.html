Residents Seek Shelter as Cyclone Pam Bears Down on Vanuatu

TV View: Clarkson drama enough to drive you to despair

1The U.N. emergency relief agency UNICEF warns that 260,000 people are in the storm's path and red alerts have been issued for the major northern provinces of Torba, Sanma, Penama and Malampa, reports the Guardian.
2Cyclone Pam is expected to reach the archipelago's east coast on Friday night and residents have been urged to seek shelter immediately.
3Though the storm won't make landfall, its hurricane-force winds will bring with it "phenomenal seas" and the potential for storm surges, flash flooding and landslides.
4The Vanuatu Meteorological Service says Cyclone Pam is the biggest storm ever to hit the island nation.
5"As far as I can remember, this is the first [category five] cyclone to come very close to Vanuatu," acting director David Gibson told the Guardian.
6"This is obviously a once-in-a-lifetime experience for most of us."

24etc etc... Neighbours is 30 years old.
25While I haven't watched it since the giddy glory days of Bouncer's dream, crazy Henry and Mike the student (who went on to become Guy Pearce), it still exerts a curious hold on folks of my generation.
26Maybe it's something to do with the fact that it was the first time (the late eighties), any of us had seen contemporary Australian life on prime-time TV.
27Sure, there was Sons and Daughters - whose theme tune I can, in theory, still sing - and Prisoner Cell Block H, but Ramsay Street was a place you could imagine living in.
28I remember, as luck would have it, being off school sick for the first ever episode shown over here.
29It was possibly the only time I've been in at the birth of a cultural phenomenon.
30Des had just had his stag do, and the stripper Daphne had woken up in his house the morning after.
31It's an unlikely premise for one of the enduring romances of soapsville, but nevertheless, millions tuned in to see them getting hitched.
32Even more saw Scott and Charlene tie the knot.
33I remember dozens of us eschewing football in the playground to see what unlikely mishaps had befallen the folks of Erinsborough.
34The most unlikely thing of all perhaps was that, for a time, my school actually screened Neighbours at lunchtime.
35That was a long time ago, and it's since moved to Channel 5.
36But you were good Neighbours.
37And everybody needs that, once in a while.
38Being James Galway suprised me.
39It presumably doesn't surprise James Galway, who's been James Galway for 75 years now.
40But watching this affectionate programme about his life and musical legacy reminded me that he's still playing, still very much alive.
41"The man with the golden flute" turned out to be a literal description as he showed off is 24-carat gold flute amidst an array of instruments in what looked like an extremely fancy cutlery drawer.
42The most surprising thing, however, was the realisation that the Belfast man was such an accomplished and respected musician.
43Growing up, Galway's music to my culturally Stalinist mind was the stuff of musical wallpaper.
44Admittedly it was our musical wallpaper - a local take on the 'pan pipe moods goes pop' and the like - but it was a revelation to find out just what a seminal artist he was.
45The likes of Melvyn Bragg marvelled at his natural ability with the instrument and leant some highbrow wellie to the tale.
46He may have lived in Switzerland for 40 years now, but this was a warming reminder of ostensibly decent and hugely gifted man from the streets of Belfast.
