Germanwings plane crash: Lufthansa to require two people in cockpit at all times

1Protocal overhaul: Lufthansa CEO Carsten Spohr says the Lufthansa Group, which includes Germanwings, Austrian Airlines, Swiss Air and Eurowings, will require two crew members to be in the cockpit at all times.
2Berlin: Lufthansa said it will introduce new rules requiring two crew members to be in the cockpit at all times after one of the pilots at its Germanwings unit crashed a plane in the French Alps.
3Prosecutors believe Andreas Lubitz, 27, locked himself alone in the cockpit of the Airbus A320 on Tuesday and deliberately steered it into a mountain, killing all 150 people on board.
4German co-pilot Andreas Lubitz,27, is believed to have used the cockpit door lock to prevent the captain from returning to the cockpit so he could crash Germanwings Flight 4U9525.
5Lufthansa had said on Thursday that it did not see any reason to hastily change its procedures, but many other airlines swiftly changed their own rules.
6"The passenger airlines of the Lufthansa Group will put this new rule into place as soon as possible in agreement with the relevant authorities," Lufthansa said in a statement on Friday.
7The Lufthansa Group also includes Germanwings, Austrian Airlines, Swiss Air and Eurowings.
8The rule has been agreed by all German airlines, aviation association BDL said in a statement.
9Air Berlin had announced plans on Thursday to introduce the new procedure.
10Scandinavian airline SAS also said on Friday that it had changed procedures to ensure two people are in the cockpit at all times.
11Lufthansa also said it was creating a new role of group safety pilot, who will check and develop flight safety procedures and report directly to Chief Executive Carsten Spohr.
12Currently, each airline has its own safety pilot.
13The group safety pilot role will be held by Werner Maas, currently the safety pilot for the Lufthansa brand.
