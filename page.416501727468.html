Earthquake of 7.5 Magnitude Shakes Nepal, Tremors Felt in India

1People wait at a school after a 7.9 magnitude earthquake struck, in Kathmandu, Nepal.
2Kathmandu: A 7.9 magnitude earthquake struck Nepal, causing some damage and injury in the Himalayan nation and shaking buildings in India and Bangladesh.
3"Possibly hundreds of people have died in various parts of the country, particularly in Kathmandu and Pokhara."
4A collapsed building is seen in Nepal's capital Kathmandu.
5The earthquake was centred 77 kilometres north-west of Kathmandu, the US Geological Survey said.
6The Dharara Tower, built in 1832, has collapsed in Kathmandu after the quake, with several bodies seen at the site of the disaster, witnesses and television footage showed.
7Around a dozen bodies were taken away from the ruins of the Dharahara tower in the centre of the capital, according to an AFP photographer at the scene.
8People stand on the runway outside the International Terminal after a earthquake hit, at Tribhuvan International Airport, Kathmandu, Nepal.
9Nepal media has reported that 50 people were trapped by the rubble.
10One body was removed from the tower and a second lay further up the road, a Reuters witness said.
11A collapsed building in Nepal's capital Kathmandu after a strong earthquake shook Nepal's capital and the densely populated Kathmandu Valley before noon Saturday, causing extensive damage with toppled walls and collapsed buildings.
12At the main hospital in Kathmandu, people with broken limbs and arms were being rushed in for treatment.
13It was unclear how many people were injured.
14A girl died after a statue fell on her in a park in Kathmandu, a witness said, while another died in India when her house collapsed.
15A Reuters reporter in Kathmandu said he had seen some buildings collapse and walls of several houses reduced to rubble.
16"Everyone is out in the streets, people are rushing to the hospital," the reporter said.
17The city is home to ancient, wooden Hindu temples.
18Photographs posted online showed buildings left in rubble, large cracks along roads and residents sitting in the street holding babies.
19Mountaineer Alex Gavan tweeted from Mount Pumori, about 8 kilometres west of Mount Everest, that the quake had caused an avalanche on Everest.
20"Everest base camp huge earthquake then huge avalanche from Pumori. Running for life from my tent. Unhurt. Many many people up the mountain," tweeted Gavan.
21Tremors were felt as far away as New Delhi and other northern cities in India, with reports of tremors lasting almost one minute.
22"Massive tremors have been felt here in Delhi and several other parts of India," said a newsreader on NDTV in Delhi.
23"You can see pictures of our Delhi studios, where the windows rattled and everything shook for a very long time, for a minute perhaps or longer," she said as footage showed studio ceiling camera lights shaking.
24A police officer in the control room of neighbouring Indian state of Bihar said the phone lines were jammed with callers from across the heavily populated state.
25"We don't know about the casualties, we are flooded with calls."
26The U.S. Geological Survey said the quake, initially measured at 7.7 but upgraded to 7.9 magnitude, struck 80 kilometres east of Pokhara.
27It was only two kilometres deep.
28"We are in the process of finding more information and are working to reach out to those affected, both at home & in Nepal," tweeted Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi.
29Modi convened a high-level meeting with ministers and top officials to assess the situation.
30There were no preliminary reports of damage anywhere in India, Home Minister Rajnath Singh told the Press Trust of India.
31The temblor was centred 81 kilometers northwest of Kathmandu, was 15 kilometers deep and hit at 11.41 am local time.
32People living in Nepal's capital of Kathmandu fled their homes when the initial temblor hit, and remained out in the open as powerful aftershocks continued to rock the city.
33"It was so powerful and the entire house was shaking, so we got out," said Sila Gurung, 28, who lives in a three-storey home with her mother in Kathmandu's Nakhipot district, close to the popular tourist site Patan Durbar Square.
34"It's still shaking now. Everyone is very scared, and no one knows when it will be safe to go back home."
35The south Asia region has a history of catastrophic earthquakes because the tectonic plate that carries the Indian subcontinent is pushing northward into the main Asian plate.
36About 60 per cent of the India's land area faces moderate to severe seismic hazard, according to its National Disaster Management Authority.
