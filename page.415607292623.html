Liberia officials: New patient tests positive for Ebola

1Liberia has seen the most deaths in the West African Ebola outbreak, which has killed more than 10,000 people.
2But since it discharged its last case on March 5, it was counting down the 42 days countries must wait before being declared Ebola-free.
3Now, a new patient has tested positive, said Dr. Francis Kateh, the acting head of the country's Ebola Incident Management Team.
4Tolbert Nyenswah, who runs Liberia's Ebola response, also said he had been told the test result was positive.
5The woman went to the emergency room of Monrovia's Redemption Hospital on Thursday night, according to Elizabeth Hamann of the International Rescue Committee, which is helping the hospital reopen amid the outbreak.
6She was identified as a suspected Ebola case and put in the hospital's isolation unit while awaiting test results.
7She is now at a treatment center.
8In a worrying sign, it is not clear where the woman became infected.
9She doesn't seem to be linked to any of the people on an Ebola contacts list, and she has said that she did not travel to an infected country, Kateh said.
10He said authorities were considering the possibility that she had a visitor from outside Liberia who infected her or that she had sex with a survivor.
11The Ebola virus can be found in the semen of survivors for up to three months, and health authorities recommend that survivors abstain from sex during that period.
12An emergency meeting will be held Saturday to discuss the case.
13Although hopes were high that Liberia had beaten Ebola, officials knew that until two neighboring countries " Sierra Leone and Guinea " also beat the disease, Liberia would remain at risk.
