Egypt's former president Mohammed Morsi sentenced to 20 years

1Egypt's former president Mohammed Morsi has been sentenced to 20 years for ordering the arrest and torture of protesters in 2012.
2But a court in the country's capital Cairo acquitted the 63-year-old leader of charges that would have seen him face the death penalty.
3Fourteen others were convicted on the same charges, with most also sentenced to 20 years in jail.
4But the court cleared the defendants on charges of inciting murder over the deaths of a journalist and two protesters during the December 5, 2012 clashes outside the presidential palace in Cairo.
5Morsi, Egypt's first freely elected leader, came to power following the 2011 ouster of Hosni Mubarak in a popular uprising.
6But after just a year in power, Morsi was himself toppled by then-army chief and now President Abdel Fattah al-Sisi following mass street protests.
7Morsi's Muslim Brotherhood has been blacklisted and targeted in a government crackdown that has seen hundreds killed and thousands thrown in jail.
8Other Brotherhood leaders have been sentenced to death and Tuesday's decision to acquit Morsi on the incitement to murder charge was unexpected.
9'This is a surprise... We were expecting them to be convicted of murder,' Ramy Ghanem, a lawyer for an anti-Morsi protester who was wounded in the clashes, told AFP.
10'But the sentences are not bad.
11We were expecting life in prison, but then 20 years is not very different,' he said.
12A life sentence in Egypt is 25 years.
13Hundreds of Morsi supporters have been sentenced to death in speedy mass trials which the United Nations has called 'unprecedented in recent history'.
14Earlier this month, a Cairo court confirmed death sentences for Muslim Brotherhood leader Mohamed Badie and 13 others over protest violence.
15The Brotherhood had called for protests in support of Morsi on Tuesday.
16'The coup commander is exploiting the judiciary,' it said in a statement ahead of the verdict, referring to Sisi.
17Morsi is facing two other trials in which he could face the death penalty, including one that sees him accused of spying for foreign powers.
18Verdicts in those two cases are due on May 16.
19Sisi has vowed to 'eradicate' the Brotherhood, an 85-year-old movement that topped successive polls between Mubarak's fall and Morsi's presidential election victory in May 2012.
20The authorities designated it a 'terrorist group' in December 2013, making even verbal expressions of support punishable by stiff jail terms.
21The crackdown sparked a fierce backlash including from jihadist groups, who have claimed a string of deadly attacks on security forces.
22Officials say more than 500 police and soldiers have been killed in attacks since Morsi was overthrown.
23Sisi was elected president in May last year, but he faced no serious challengers and - in a country where the army has been in power for decades - his victory dashed hopes raised by Mubarak's ouster of a civilian democracy.
24Sisi's regime is popular among many Egyptians tired of political turmoil and seeking stability and economic growth.
25But Sisi has been accused of repression and his crackdown has also targeted secular and liberal activists who spearheaded the 2011 uprising against Mubarak.
26In November, a court dropped murder charges against Mubarak in his own trial over the deaths of hundreds of protesters in 2011.
