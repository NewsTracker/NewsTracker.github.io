Government: Radical militants kill 4 Tunisian National Guard

1Ministry spokesman Mohammed Ali Aroui said 20 militants from the Oqba Ibn Nafaa radical group ambushed a night patrol around 20 kilometers (12 miles) from the Kasserine city around midnight.
2"Our response will be strong and violent," he said, adding that a sweep of the area was underway to catch the attackers.
3The attack took place near Mount Chaambi, a national park on the Algerian border that has provided refuge to al-Qaida linked Islamist radicals blamed for attacks against army and politicians in Tunisia for the past two years.
4The attack comes as Tunisian forces on the Libyan border are on high alert in the wake of the murder of 21 Egyptian Christians in Libya by another radical Islamic group.
5Since Tunisians overthrew their secular dictator in 2011, kicking off the region-wide Arab Spring, they have come increasingly under attack by Islamic militants.
6Despite the efforts of Tunisia's small army, attacks have continued around Mount Chaambi.
7The Oqba bin Nafaa group describes itself as the military wing of Ansar al-Sharia, whose leaders fled to Libya in 2013.
8They have been implicated in the murder two politicians in 2013, the massacre of 15 soldiers last July and an attack on the home of the former interior minister.
9Aroui said the attack must be in revenge for a series of police operations against jihadi "sleeper cells" that have resulted in the arrest of dozens of suspected militant supporters.
10In one sweep earlier this month, 32 suspects were arrested for planning attacks on the Interior Ministry and National Guard bases.
11Algerian and Tunisian forces are attempting to coordinate their efforts to control the rugged region, which straddles the border between the two countries.
12On Monday, Algerian forces killed a militant carrying explosives near Tebessa, just across the border from Kasserine.
