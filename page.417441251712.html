Blatter disgraced FIFA, says former vice-president

1No one brought more shame on FIFA than its president Sepp Blatter and he should step aside for a younger leader, the organisation's former vice-president Jack Warner has said.
2Warner, who has been accused of soliciting bribes as part of a US-led corruption case against senior FIFA officials, left jail in Trinidad and Tobago on Thursday after he was granted bail, according to local media.
3"Why are there no investigations in Asia, or in Europe?"
4Warner told German magazine Stern.
5Why are there no investigations into Sepp Blatter?
6"Why are there no investigations into Sepp Blatter? No other person has brought so much shame and disgrace on FIFA."
7Warner is among nine FIFA officials and five corporate executives charged by the U.S. Department of Justice with running a criminal enterprise that involved more than $150m in bribes.
8"I only know this: he was elected FIFA boss five times in succession. Is he corrupt? I don't know.
9"If I were his age... I'd retire and pass the presidency of FIFA on to someone younger. But everyone has different ideas of what to do with their lives."
10Once one of the most powerful men in FIFA, Warner surrendered to authorities on Wednesday after US officials sought his extradition.
11Prosecutors say Warner solicited bribes worth $10m from the South African government for them to host the 2010 World Cup and diverted bribes for personal use.
12South Africa, meanwhile, have confirmed a payment was made but denied it was a bribe.
13Warner issued a statement protesting his innocence on Wednesday as FIFA reeled from police raids in Switzerland and the U.S. and a second investigation opened by the Swiss authorities into the awarding of the 2018 and 2022 World Cups.
14Warner, who faces 12 charges, including racketeering and bribery, said on Wednesday he was innocent and noted he had left football activities four years ago.
15The 72-year-old resigned from FIFA after ethics investigations were begun into a meeting he held with former Asian Football Confederation chief Mohammed Bin Hammam where payments were made to Caribbean soccer officials ahead of the election for FIFA president in 2011.
16A defiant Blatter, who won his fifth term as FIFA president on Friday, has come out fighting against allegations.
