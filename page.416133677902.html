Germany to bring back 100 mothballed tanks, dealing with new needs following Ukraine crisis

1BERLIN - The German military plans to bring 100 mothballed tanks back into service as it deals with increased demands from the Ukraine crisis.
2Defense Ministry spokesman Jens Flosdorff said Friday that the military will pay 22 million euros ($23.6 million) to buy back the Leopard 2 main battle tanks from the defense industry, which has kept them in storage.
3The tanks will then be modernized starting in 2017.
4The decision to equip the Bundeswehr with a maximum 328 tanks nearly reverses a four-year-old decision to cut the total from 350 to 225.
