Nepal's opposition cuts short crippling strike as constitution impasse drags on

1KATHMANDU, Nepal - Life has returned to normal in Nepal after an opposition alliance cut short a crippling general strike over an impasse about how to approve the Himalayan nation's constitution.
2Schools and markets opened and vehicles returned to the streets on Wednesday, a day after police clashed with protesters on the first day of the strike.
3A meeting of the opposition late Tuesday decided to cut short the three-day protest action, citing inconveniences to the public.
4The alliance of 30 opposition parties is demanding a constitutional draft be passed by an agreement involving all political parties inside and outside parliament.
5The ruling coalition has been attempting to get the draft approved through a vote in parliament after negotiations with the opposition failed.
6Nepal has been without a constitution since 2008.
