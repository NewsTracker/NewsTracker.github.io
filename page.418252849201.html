Rockets land in Israel, Egypt's IS affiliate claims responsibility

1JERUSALEM/CAIRO Militants in Egypt's Sinai peninsula fired rockets into southern Israel on Friday in an incident that caused no casualties but appeared to be linked to fighting between Islamist insurgents and Egyptian security forces.
2Islamic State's Egypt affiliate, Sinai Province, said in a statement posted on Twitter by supporters that it had launched three Grad rockets toward "occupied Palestine".
3Reuters could not immediately verify the authenticity of the statement.
4An Israeli military source earlier said the rockets had been fired from Sinai, which borders Israel, the Gaza Strip and the Suez Canal.
5Israeli police said they had so far found remnants of two rockets in an open area.
6No damage or casualties were reported.
7It was possible the launch was linked to the fighting in Sinai, the military source said, where militants of the Sinai Province group launched their biggest assault in years against Egyptian security forces on Wednesday.
8Dozens of people have been killed in the past few days of clashes and air strikes in the desert region.
9The Israeli military on Friday closed a southern highway, part of which runs along the Egyptian border, as a safety precaution.
10Egyptian security sources earlier told Reuters they were investigating the reports and said there was no immediate evidence a rocket was launched from Egyptian territory.
11Militants, including those from Sinai Province - formerly known as Ansar Bayt al-Maqdis - have fired rockets into Israel from Sinai on several occasions in the past.
12Israel has accused Hamas, the Islamist group which controls Gaza, of helping Islamic State in Sinai, an allegation Hamas denies.
13Hamas, an Islamist group that seized power in Gaza in 2007, has faced its own threats from Salafist and Islamic State-linked militants.
14On Tuesday Islamic State militants released a video threatening to turn Gaza into another "fiefdom", as in parts of Iraq and Syria.
15It also said in the video that it will uproot "the state of the Jews" (Israel) and secular Palestinian movement Fatah, headed by Palestinian President Mahmoud Abbas.
