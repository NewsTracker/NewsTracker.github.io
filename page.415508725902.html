14In recent days Likud has been on a get-out-the-vote blitz, with Netanyahu stepping up his nationalist rhetoric, saying a dovish government would spell disaster for the country and complaining of an international conspiracy to oust him.
15Netanyahu, who rarely speaks to the media, has given a series of interviews to Israeli media in recent days and on Sunday night, he addressed an outdoor rally before tens of thousands of hard-line supporters in Tel Aviv.
16On his final day of campaigning Monday, Netanyahu visited Har Homa, a Jewish development in east Jerusalem that is viewed as an illegal settlement by the Palestinians and the international community.
17The Palestinians claim east Jerusalem, captured by Israel in 1967, as their capital.
18"We will preserve Jerusalem's unity in all its parts. We will continue to build and fortify Jerusalem so that its division won't be possible and it will stay united forever," he said.
19"Likud's victory is the only thing that can ensure the continuation of a national leadership and will prevent the establishment of a left-wing government."
20Herzog has been surging in the polls on a campaign that promises to repair ties with the Palestinians and the international community and also bring relief to the country's struggling middle class.
21Visiting his party headquarters, an upbeat Herzog talked about a "crucial" vote for the country and warned against splitting the anti-Netanyahu vote among the various centrist parties, including charismatic leader Yair Lapid's Yesh Atid Party.
22"Whoever wants Lapid, whoever wants Yesh Atid, in the government has to vote for us. They have no other choice," he said.
23"Whoever wants an upheaval has to vote for us."
24Lapid himself got a warm welcome in the coastal city of Netanya, where people stopped him in the street to take selfies.
25He accused both Netanyahu and Herzog of working outside deals with special interest groups and said that only he was tackling the real issues facing the Israeli middle class.
26Lapid has so far refused to commit to either Herzog or Netanyahu, though he is widely seen as a logical ally of Herzog's in a future coalition.
27Under Israel's electoral system, no party has ever won an outright majority in the 120-member parliament.
28Instead, the party with the best chance of forming a coalition - usually the largest party - is given the chance to form a coalition.
29Since neither Likud nor the Zionist Union is expected to earn more than a quarter of the votes, the election will likely be followed by a lengthy period of negotiations over the next coalition government.
30A potential kingmaker could be found in the new centrist party of Moshe Kahlon, who is running on an economic platform that deals almost exclusively with bread-and-butter issues while putting Israel's diplomatic challenges on the back burner.
31Kahlon is demanding to become finance minister in the next government and could tip the scales in favor of either Netanyahu or Herzog.
32The son of Libyan immigrants, Kahlon is popular with working class Israelis thanks to his Middle Eastern background, his modest upbringing and for reforming the local mobile-phone market.
