Apple, Google and Microsoft audited by ATO

1Three major tech companies giving evidence at a Senate inquiry into tax avoidance have confirmed they are being audited by the Australian Taxation Office.
2Executives from Apple, Google and Microsoft, under questioning at the hearing in Sydney on Wednesday, confirmed they were being investigated.
3Google Australia managing director Maile Carnegie said the company made $58 million in revenue in 2013, and profits of just more than $46 million, but paid $7.1 million in tax.
4Earlier, Tax Commissioner Chris Jordan continued to refuse to reveal the identities of a number of companies suspected of avoiding tax by shifting billions of dollars offshore.
5Committee chairman Sam Dastyari had written to Mr Jordan on Tuesday requesting he reveal the names.
6But Mr Jordan refused, and in a response sent to Senator Dastyari and tabled at the hearing on Wednesday, defended the decision on the grounds that naming the companies would undermine public confidence in the tax system.
