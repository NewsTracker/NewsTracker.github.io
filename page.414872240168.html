1Ukrainian President Petro Poroshenko has called for U.N. peacekeepers to be deployed to monitor a cease-fire in war-weary east Ukraine, a proposal that pro-Russia rebels swiftly said would be in breach of a peace deal.
2At an emergency meeting late on Wednesday, Ukraine's national security and defense council approved Poroshenko's request, a clear sign of Ukraine's concerns after pro-Russian rebels seized a strategic town after a cease-fire took effect on Sunday.
3"The best format for us is a police mission of the EU," Poroshenko said according to a statement on his website.
4"It will be the most efficient guarantor of security in the situation when the word of peace is not observed either by Russia or by those who are supported by it."
5Ukraine pulled thousands of troops out of the east Ukrainian town of Debaltseve on Wednesday after failing to stop an offensive by Moscow-backed rebels who said the railway hub was not part of last week's cease-fire.
6On Thursday, Kiev said 90 of its troops had been captured in the town with 82 still missing.
7As rebel fighters celebrated in the streets of Debaltseve on Thursday, there were hopes that a decisive victory there would finally give the cease-fire a chance to hold.
8The idea of a U.N. peacekeeping force has been floated before, and many feel it is not workable.
9An international force posted along Ukraine's separatist fault line might only serve to further entrench the armed divide and hamper Kiev's efforts to reunite the country.
10For their part, the rebels denounced Poroshenko's call for peacekeepers, saying their presence would "violate" the cease-fire, negotiated in marathon talks by France, Germany, Russia and Ukraine in the Belarussian capital Minsk late last week.
11"This is an actual violation of the package of measures to implement the Minsk agreement," Denis Pushilin, a senior separatist figure, was quoted as saying by Russia's RIA news agency.
12"And so we have a very negative attitude to this. Moreover we are ready to address the heads of state who were guarantors that Ukraine would meet its commitments."
13Russia's ambassador to the United Nations was also quoted as saying that Poroshenko's proposal put into doubt Ukraine's resolve to fulfill the Minsk agreements.
14Meanwhile in Paris, French President Francois Hollande said he and German Chancellor Angela Merkel spoke Thursday with the leaders of Ukraine and Russia about cease-fire violations and their consequences.
15He did not elaborate.
