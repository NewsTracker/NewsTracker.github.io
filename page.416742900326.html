Israel's Foreign Minister Avigdor Lieberman snubs Netanyahu coalition invite

1Tel Aviv, Israel: Israeli Foreign Minister Avigdor Lieberman announced on Monday that he will not join Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's government, dealing a blow to the incumbent's emerging coalition in the 11th hour of negotiations.
2"We are going to serve the people from the opposition," he said of his six-seat hardline right-wing Israel Beiteinu party.
3Defying predictions, Mr Netanyahu's right-wing, nationalist Likud won 30 seats in the March 17 election, paving the way for him to serve a fourth term in office as premier.
4The incumbent must present his government by midnight on Wednesday.
5Mr Lieberman's ultra-nationalist Beiteinu has long been considered a natural ally for Mr Netanyahu's Likud party in coalition negotiations, but Mr Lieberman's rejection means Mr Netanyahu may be reduced to a slim majority of 61 seats in Israel's 120-seat parliament, the Knesset.
6Mr Lieberman told reporters in Jerusalem that he will also resign from his post as foreign minister.
7Mr Netanyahu's Likud party signed its first two coalition agreements on Wednesday last week, with Kulanu and United Torah Judaism (UTJ).
8Mr Lieberman cited promises made in those agreements as grounds for not joining the coalition.
9The coalition Mr Netanyahu was building with two ultra-Orthodox parties, another right-wing party and the centre-right Kulanu party of finance minister-designate Moshe Kahlon "is not to our taste, to say the least", Mr Lieberman said.
10"This government has no intention of uprooting Hamas," he said of the Islamist Palestinian movement in de facto control of the Gaza Strip, mentioning one reason of his dissatisfaction.
11The next Netanyahu government would not be a nationalist one, "but the personification of opportunism", he charged.
12According to recent local media reports, Mr Lieberman holds a grudge against Mr Netanyahu, blaming him for an ongoing police investigation into allegations of corruption by senior members of his party.
13The Likud is also negotiating with another ultra-Orthodox party, Shas (seven seats) and with the pro-settler, right-wing Jewish Home (eight seats).
14Mr Netanyahu still has a good chance of presenting his fourth government by the deadline.
15But if he fails, President Reuven Rivlin can appoint another lawmaker to the task of forming a government.
16That would then likely be Labor Party leader Isaac Herzog.
17As part of the agreement signed last week with UTJ, a social reform program introduced with much fanfare during the previous government, will be cancelled.
18The program had included criminal sanctions for ultra-Orthodox Torah students who refuse to report for compulsory military service.
19It had also included a cancellation of other privileges and financial benefits for the ultra-Orthodox population in Israel.
