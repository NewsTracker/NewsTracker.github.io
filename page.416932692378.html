Russian soldiers 'died in Ukraine'

1A report containing material compiled by murdered Russian opposition leader Boris Nemtsov says at least 220 Russian soldiers died in two battles in eastern Ukraine in the past year.
2The Russian Defence Ministry denies that any of its soldiers are fighting in Ukraine, saying that Russians who have joined the separatists are volunteers.
3The report claims the soldiers were released from their duties with the Russian military and listed as volunteers.
4The 64-page report was compiled from witness reports in media accounts and information gathered by Mr Nemtsov, including from relatives and other representatives of the dead soldiers.
5Nemtsov was shot dead on February 27 in central Moscow.
6Five suspects, including a Chechen police officer, were arrested but investigators have not named a suspected mastermind and the motive remains unclear.
7The Russian Defence Ministry promised to pay compensation if the soldiers were killed or wounded, but failed to live up to its commitments, the report said.
8The report was presented to the public by opposition figures from the political party Mr Nemtsov co-chaired.
9According to Mr Nemtsov's sources, at least 150 Russian soldiers died in August.
10Their relatives received two million rubles (now worth about 25,000) in compensation and signed non-disclosure documents.
11Around 70 Russian soldiers died in January and February in fighting around the city of Debaltseve, the report said, adding that family members were unable to receive compensation and appealed to Mr Nemtsov for help.
