Separate bus crashes in Egypt kill 23, injure 17 others

1CAIRO - Officials say two separate road accidents in Egypt Sunday have killed 23 Egyptians and injured 17 others.
2Husam Gamil, director of emergency services for the Red Sea governorate, says one accident occurred when a passenger bus flipped over near the resort city of Hurghada, killing 11 people and injuring 11 others.
3Maj. Gen. Mohamed Ismail, a local security director, said 12 people were killed and another six injured when their minibus collided with a truck carrying construction materials near Wadi Natrun, some 125 kilometers (78 miles) northwest of Cairo.
4Deadly road accidents claim thousands of lives in Egypt every year.
5They are mostly caused by speeding, bad roads or lack of regular vehicle maintenance.
6Road accidents killed some 14,400 people in Egypt last year.
