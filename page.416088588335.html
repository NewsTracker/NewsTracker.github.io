Hillary Clinton hires Google's Stephanie Hannon for attracting voters

1Hillary Clinton is deadly serious about attracting young and tech-savvy voters in 2016, hiring a new digital guru who hails from the world's most powerful online brand.
2Stephanie Hannon, who was the tech giant's director of productmanagement for civic innovation and social impact, will serve as her presidential campaign's chief technology officer.
3In that role she will supervise a sprawling effort to develop websites, mobile apps and other vehicles for pushing the former secretary of state's brand through the 2016 elections.
4Hannon previously worked on Gmail and Google Maps.
5She also spent some time at Facebook, and also worked for the event-organizing website Eventbrite, according to her LinkedIn profile.
6Seasoned digital talent is in high demand as nearly 20 potential White House hopefuls plan for a 2016 campaign season that will rely heavily on clicks, likes, shares and other indications of a broad reach.
7Every major party candidate will have to navigate the brave new world of online fundraising, too, which President Barack Obama's team mastered in 2008 en route to his victory - including a stunning Democratic primary upset of Clinton.
8Kentucky Republican Sen. Rand Paul, who announced his candidacy on Tuesday in Louisville, poached his tech leader from rival candidate Ted Cruz late last year.
9Vincent Harris, who also helped manicure Kentucky Sen. Mitch McConnell's online image during a bruising re-election fight in 2014, runs his own Texas consulting firm with a staff of 20.
10His team was responsible for McConnell's breakthrough video 'What rhymes with Alison Lundergan Grimes,' a brief online ad that launched a year-long slap-fest directed at McConnell's unsuccessful challenger.
11The Washington Post was first to report Clinton's latest hire.
12The onetime first lady and former U.S. senator hasn't yet launched an official campaign, but an announcement is expected in the next 10 days.
13Her campaign-in-waiting has already signed a lease for two floors of a Brooklyn, New York, office building, starting a 15-day countdown for a formal declaration of her candidacy set by the Federal Election Commission.
14Clinton's staff also includes several key members who came over from the White House.
15Kristina Schake was first lady Michelle Obama's aide responsible for recasting her as a fun-loving 'everywoman' through TV appearances on pop-culture shows.
16She will help transform Clinton into a softer, more accessible public figure.
17White House Communications Director Jen Palmieri stepped down this month to helm Clinton's campaign communications team.
18Palmieri has been replaced by Jen Psaki, the former State Department chief spokeswoman.
