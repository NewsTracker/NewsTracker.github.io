Father of slain U.S. blogger says no progress in Bangladesh probe

1Bangladeshi-born Avijit Roy was murdered on Feb. 26 outside a book fair in the capital Dhaka.
2His father Ajoy Roy, who met police earlier this week, told reporters on Thursday that "apparently no progress has been achieved".
3There has been a spate of assaults on secular bloggers in the Muslim-majority nation over the past few years.
4Media group Reporters Without Borders rated Bangladesh 146th among 180 countries in a ranking of press freedom last year.
5Police have come under intense pressure over the case, with the dead man's wife accusing officers of standing by and failing to stop the attack.
6The wife, Rafida Bonya Ahmed, suffered head injuries and lost a finger.
7She later returned to the United States for treatment, from where she has blamed the killing on "religious fanatics".
8The dead man's father said he had handed police photographs taken at a meeting attended by his son earlier on the day of the killing.
9Krishna Pada Roy, a deputy police commissioner, told Reuters that an academic who was present at that meeting had been questioned by police for several hours on Wednesday.
10The man had been told not to try to leave the country, and to report for further questioning if required.
11The U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation is helping Bangladeshi authorities.
12A suspect accused of ties to militant group Hizbut Tahrir, Farabi Shafiur Rahman, has been arrested.
13Police are also seeking Redwanul Azad Rana, a member of an Islamist militant group called the Ansarullah Bangla Team.
14Mohammad Habibur Rahman, secretary of the Bangladesh Police Association and Superintendent of Police of Dhaka, said the police had been unfairly criticized over the incident.
15"There are plenty of examples where our police force come forward to save people, risking their own lives," he told Reuters.
