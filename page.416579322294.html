Bali nine families make final desperate pleas after saying goodbye to Andrew Chan and Myuran Sukumaran

1Myuran Sukumaran's sister, Brintha, is overcome on her way to Nusakambangan island.
2In heartbreaking scenes played out live on television, the families of Myuran Sukumaran and Andrew Chan last night begged Indonesian President Joko Widodo to spare the lives of their loved ones, following their final goodbyes.
3With the Bali nine ringleaders due to face the firing squad about midnight (3am AEST time), Sukumaran's sister Brintha wailed with grief, begging for her brother's life.
4Raji Sukumaran breaks down while pleading for her son's life on Tuesday afternoon.
5"Indonesian people, show mercy. Please don't do this to my brother. Please, please Mr President, Joko Widodo, I beg you please. Please don't take my brother from me," she said.
6Between anguished cries, Sukumaran's mother, Raji, told journalists of her last moments with her son.
7"I just had to say goodbye to my son and I won't see him again," she said through tears after her return to the port of Cilacap.
8"They are going to take him at midnight and shoot him.
9"He is healthy and he is beautiful ... I'm asking the government please don't kill him today. Please don't hold the execution. Please don't kill my son."
10"I won't see him again. They are going to take him at midnight and shoot him," she said.
11"He is beautiful and has a lot of compassion for other people.
12"I'm asking the government please don't kill him today. Please don't hold the execution. Please don't kill my son. Please don't."
13In a brief statement as they disembarked at Cilacap port, Chan's brother Michael said his family were extremely upset at the men's ordeal.
14"We are very upset as a family to have to go through this. No human being should ever have to go through this. It's torture.
15"Anyone with a heart would forgive these guys for what they've done and show mercy."
16Late on Tuesday night, Indonesia still had not formally notified the Australian government of the impending executions as a former ambassador warned the two countries were facing their most serious rift since the East Timor crisis.
17High-level ministerial meetings with Indonesia will be cancelled and senior government sources say all aspects of the diplomatic relationship are "on the table" in the fallout of the Bali nine case.
18Fairfax Media has also confirmed that the Abbott government will now almost certainly withdraw Australia's ambassador.
19Earlier the families had arrived at the port for their last visit.
20It was the day Myuran Sukumaran has been painting.
21Paintings of hearts broken, literally and figuratively.
22Self-portraits of the artist with gaping black holes over his heart and lines of black blood, paintings of the life draining out of him in whooshes of thick paint.
23And on Tuesday, the day of final goodbyes, a portrait of a human heart.
24Inscribed was "Satu hati satu rasa didalam cinta" - one heart, one feeling in love.
25It was signed by all nine of those on death row whose hearts will be ripped apart by bullets.
26It seemed that Tuesday could not get any any worse for Sukumaran and Andrew Chan and their loved ones.
27But it did.
28At the eleventh hour the families learned that the men had been denied their choice of a spiritual counsellor who stays with the condemned until the bitter end.
29As Michael Chan told Fairfax Media in an SMS: "Last bit of dignity denied."
30Chan and Sukumaran had asked for Salvation Army minister David Soper and Bayside Church Senior Pastor Christie Buckingham to accompany them until their deaths.
31Both are old friends, both know the men intimately and their journey over the past decade since their 2005 arrest for smuggling heroin out of Indonesia.
32Both have witnessed their transformation from stupid, selfish young men to the painter and the pastor.
33The reasons for the last minute denial seem inexplicable: just two days earlier the Australian Embassy had been assured it would be ok.
34After frantic and frustrating negotiations throughout day, the two ministers will be allowed to return to Nusakambangan for a few hours.
35But they will not be permitted to comfort them in their last minutes on earth or witness their execution by firing squad.
36On learning this final request had been refused the families were forced to walk the gauntlet of a frenzied media scrum.
37In past days they had been driven into the ferry dock, but on Tuesday had to walk the dusty narrow road to the harbour.
38The Sukumaran family became trapped inside a feverish media pack as they boarded the ferry to Nusakambangan - now referred to simply as "execution island" - for the last time.
39It was impossible for them to move.
40Brintha Sukumaran collapsed.
41A wild-eyed male relative screamed in fright.
42Police dogs snapped and snarled at ankles.
43News Limited journalist Cindy Wockner was bitten on the leg.
44After an eternity the family made it to the port.
45We could hear the anguished wails long after they disappeared from sight.
46A planned press conference was cancelled.
47Chan is now a pastor, and as of Monday, a married man.
48He and his wife Febyanti Herewila had reportedly dreamed of setting up a community centre on Sabu Island, west of Timor, to hold music classes and provide a place for young people to play sport and learn.
49Sukumaran is an artist who this year was awarded an associate degree in fine arts from Curtin University.
50His legacy - the art and other classes he established at Bali's Kerobokan jail - have changed the trajectories of other prisoners' lives.
51"Myu is ok. He is ready for what is to come," Sukumaran's friend and mentor Ben Quilty wrote on his Facebook page.
52"But with his passing my world will take a dark plunge backwards."
53Quilty said "Myu" had inadvertently brought together compassionate and forgiving people, around the world.
54"Together we will all ensure that compassion continues to flourish."
55"Myu made terrible mistakes," Quilty said.
56"So did I, but his family and his friends will pay a dreadful price for his youthful self-indulgence. And so will Myu."
