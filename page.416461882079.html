Letters signal Bali Nine execution close

1Embassies representing prisoners on death row with Andrew Chan and Myuran Sukumaran have been summoned to a meeting that could signal the executions are imminent.
2Utomo Karim, lawyer for Raheem Salami, said on Thursday afternoon the Nigerian embassy received a letter asking it to go to Cilacap, the port closest to Nusakambangan, on Saturday.
3"Based on experience from the previous execution, they're going to tell them the date for the execution," he told AAP.
4A lawyer for Rodrigo Gularte says the Brazilian embassy revived the same letter from Indonesia's foreign ministry.
5"It's a letter from the foreign ministry to the embassy, asking representatives from the embassy to come to Cilacap for a meeting," Ricky Gunawan told AAP.
6The Department of Foreign Affairs was contacted for comment.
7Indonesia is required to give 72 hours' notice of the executions.
8Chan and Sukumaran are in the same cell block as Salami on Nusakambangan, where they and six other drug offenders await execution.
9Mr Utomo says based on the experience of the Nigerian embassy in January, when six people were executed, the Saturday meeting means the date is drawing near.
10"Last time, when we were asked to gather in the district prosecutor's office, we were then taken to Nusakambangan to tell the convict about the execution time," he said.
11"And three days after that, they were executed. I don't know whether other embassies have been notified at the same."
12The Attorney-General's spokesman Tony Spontana confirmed reports on Thursday that his office has sent letters to prosecutors advising them to prepare for the executions.
13Asked if a date was set he replied: "up to tonight, not yet".
14The letters asked prosecutors to make preparations, and were not the notification letters for the convicts themselves, he said.
15Lawyers for Chan and Sukumaran have lodged a new appeal in the constitutional court but the attorney-general says the Bali Nine pair have exhausted their legal avenues and he won't recognise the latest action.
16A tenth prisoner, Filipina Mary Jane Veloso, is also in line for execution but has yet to be moved to Nusakambangan.
17Her lawyers were planning to file for a judicial review on Monday, with new evidence pointing to her being a victim of human trafficking, rather than a drug trafficker.
18Her family has arrived in Indonesia from the Philippines and was due to visit her on Friday.
19The only Indonesian on death row, Zainal Abidin, still has an application for a judicial review pending in the Supreme Court.
20Mr Spontana told Indonesian website detik.com the date hadn't been set because of his case.
21"We're still waiting for Zainal Abidin," he reportedly said.
22"When it's rejected then it will be complete, 10 convicts."
23Chan, 31, and Sukumaran, 34, were arrested 10 years ago in a plot to take heroin out of Indonesia, which has so far rejected diplomatic and legal efforts for mercy.
