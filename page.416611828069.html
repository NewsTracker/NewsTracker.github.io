Calvin Peete, winner of 12 PGA Tour events, dies (Yahoo Sports)

1ATLANTA (AP) -- Calvin Peete, who taught himself how to play golf at 24 and became the most successful black player on the PGA Tour before the arrival of Tiger Woods, died Wednesday morning, the PGA Tour said.
2He was 71.
3The tour did not have a cause of death.
4Murray Brothers Funeral Home confirmed it was handling the arrangements but did not release additional information.
5Peete won 12 times on the PGA Tour, mainly on the strength of his uncanny accuracy off the tee.
6Peete led the PGA Tour in driving accuracy for 10 straight years starting in 1981, and he captured the Vardon Trophy over Jack Nicklaus in 1984.
7He also played on two Ryder Cup teams.
8More impressive than his record, however, was the journey to compete - and beat - the best in golf.
9Born July 18, 1943, in Detroit, Peete used to pick beans and corn in the fields in Florida to make money for his family.
10He eventually financed a station wagon from which he sold vegetables and other goods to migrants.
11He wasn't interested in golf, even at the prodding of friends.
12Peete had spent enough time in the hot sun.
13According to a 1983 profile in The New York Times, Peete became interested when he saw how much money golfers were making.
14He first took up the game in Rochester, New York, when he was 24.
15Within six months he was breaking 80, and a year later he was breaking par.
16Even more remarkable is that Peete had a left arm he couldn't fully extend.
17He had fallen from a tree and broken his elbow as a kid, and it was never properly set.
18That didn't keep him from developing a repeatable swing that always seemed to find the fairway.
19''Some of the players still drive farther than I do,'' Peete told The Times in the 1983 profile.
20Peete became the fourth black player to win on the PGA Tour, joining pioneers like Pete Brown, Charlie Sifford and Lee Elder.
21Four years after finally earning a card, he won the Greater Milwaukee Open in 1979 by five shots over Lee Trevino, Victor Regalado and Jim Simons.
22He finished 27th on the money list that year and cracked the $100,000 mark ($122,481) for the first time.
23Peete won four times in 1982, tied for the most titles on the PGA Tour that year with Tom Watson and Craig Stadler.
24He won the Vardon Trophy for the lowest stroke average in 1984, and his biggest victory was a year later when he captured The Players Championship.
25''I can still remember watching Calvin hit drive after drive, straight down the middle of the fairway, an amazing display of talent he possessed despite some of his physical limitations,'' PGA Tour Commissioner Tim Finchem said.
26''Calvin will always be remembered as a great champion and an individual who consistently gave back to the game.
27Peete finished his career with over $3.2 million in earnings from the PGA Tour and Champions Tour.
28He was inducted into the African-American Ethnic Sports Hall of Fame in 2002.
29He also had a degree.
30A few years after his first PGA Tour victory, he returned to Detroit to take the General Equivalency Diploma test.
31''I love this game,'' Peete said in the Times profile.
32Two years later, the season he won The Players Championship and the Phoenix Open, he earned $384,489 to finish fourth on the money list.
33Peete is survived by his wife, Pepper, and seven children.
