Saban: NFL draft evaluations affected Alabama chemistry (Yahoo Sports)

1(AP) -- Alabama coach Nick Saban thinks something happened to his team's chemistry between the Southeastern Conference championship game and the national semifinals against Ohio State.
2Saban said Wednesday at SEC media days one factor was that six juniors received their evaluations for the NFL draft during that period.
3He wants that date pushed back until after the final game to prevent such potential distractions.
4''We had six guys in this situation this past year and 11 the year before,'' Saban said.
5Whatever the reason, a defense that shut down Missouri was manhandled by Ohio State in the College Football Playoff.
6The potent offense made some costly mistakes - including three turnovers - and for the second straight year the Crimson Tide fell short of a national title.
7Receiver Amari Cooper was Alabama's only first-round pick last season, though tailback T.J. Yeldon and safety Landon Collins were early second-rounders.
8All three were underclassmen.
9It is more of an issue with Alabama than most programs, with an annual early exodus of star players to the NFL.
10''I'd say guys got distracted by people in their ear,'' said linebacker Reggie Ragland, who opted to return for his senior season.
11''With me, the people that were in my ear, I wouldn't answer the phone.
12I was just worried about playing football.
13''A lot of guys got sidetracked by that, but I think this year coming up we've got a lot of guys that are not worried about the NFL.
14Ohio State beat Alabama 42-35 on its way to the national title.
15Ragland said the Buckeyes were ''a formidable opponent'' but feels the Tide could have at least shown up with a better performance.
16Alabama's defense, typically among the nation's best, has been victimized late in back-to-back seasons.
17Ragland thinks teams aren't as intimidated as they once were when facing the Tide.
18''Guys aren't scared to play us anymore,'' he said.
19''That's a fact.
20Guys are coming in very happy and excited to play us.
21I used to see teams break down in the first half and just give up playing.
22Now, the Tide must replace nine offensive starters, including quarterback Blake Sims.
23Sims' backup, Jake Coker, and David Cornwell are vying to be the starting quarterback, and Saban said he has no timetable for making that call.
24But those are preseason concerns.
25Postseason was the main topic Wednesday.
26Ultimately, Alabama is hoping to be in position to contend for titles and come through at the end this time after a stretch when the Tide won three national championships in four years.
