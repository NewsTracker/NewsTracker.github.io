6 Baltimore police officers suspended amid investigation of suspect's fatal spine injury

1April 19, 2015: Citizens and groups such as the Justice League of NYC protest against the police following the death of Freddie Gray in Baltimore.
2Gray, 25, of Baltimore, died Sunday at a hospital, a week after he was hurt following an arrest.
3Baltimore's mayor, police officials, and prosecutor sought to calm their city Monday, while six officers were suspended as authorities investigated how a suspect suffered a fatal spine injury in police custody last week.
4A week after Freddie Gray, 25, was pulled off the street and into a police van, authorities don't have any videos or other evidence explaining what happened to cause the "medical emergency" an arresting officer said Gray suffered while being taken to the local police station, Deputy Commissioner Jerry Rodriguez said.
5The Gray family's lawyer, Billy Murphy, had said that Gray's "spine was 80 percent severed at his neck."
6Autopsy results returned Monday show that Gray "did suffer a significant spinal injury that led to his death," Rodriguez said.
7"What we don't know is how he suffered that injury."
8In a police report obtained by The Baltimore Sun, Officer Garrett Miller wrote that police on bike patrol stopped Gray April 12 after he was seen fleeing "unprovoked upon noticing police presence."
9Police said a knife was found clipped to Gray's pants pocket and he was arrested on a weapons charge.
10The video taken by a bystander of the arrest does not show the injury occurring, but did capture four police officers dragging a screaming Gray to a police van.
11On Monday, police released a more detailed timeline that revealed that Gray was placed in leg irons after an officer felt he was becoming "irate," and that the van stopped on its way to the police station, even picking up another prisoner in an unrelated case, while Gray repeatedly asked for medical attention.
12"During transport to Western District via wagon transport the defendant suffered a medical emergency and was immediately transported to Shock Trauma via medic," Miller wrote in the report.
13Police Commissioner Anthony Batts said that Gray asked first for an inhaler, and then several times during his transport for medical care.
14"There were several times he made a medical request," Batts said.
15"He asked for an inhaler, and at one or two of the stops it was noticed that he was having trouble breathing and we probably should have asked for paramedics."
16"When Mr. Gray was put in that van, he could talk, he was upset. And when he was taken out of that van, he could not talk and he could not breathe," Rodriguez said.
17Batts also said it is still unclear why Gray was stopped in the first place, saying only that officers "made eye contact" with Gray and another man, and the two took off running.
18"That's part of the question we have to dig into," Batts said, "if there's more than just running. There is no law against running."
19Mayor Stephanie Rawlings-Blake said she too is "angry that we are here again" after trying to overcome decades of distrust between police and citizens in Baltimore's inner city.
20"Our community is experiencing a great deal of trauma," Rawlings-Blake said.
21"This is a very, very tense time for Baltimore City."
22Batts said he is ordering that police review and rewrite "effective immediately" its policies on moving prisoners and providing them with medical attention.
23"We are a community on edge right now. We hear, I hear, the outrage. I hear the concern and I hear the fear," Batts said, asking for calm.
24"We are on edge as a city, and I need your help to make sure we get this out in the proper way."
25But Billy Murphy, an attorney representing Gray's family, said Monday that the news conference left him with more questions than answers.
26"They were vague about how his spine was injured. We'll have to wait to see the autopsy that they admitted they worked closely with the medical examiner's office to develop," Murphy said.
27"So tell us what it says. I have even more questions than I did before. Who did it? How did they do it and why did they do it? Why all these stops? What were the police doing during those stops? What did they see?"
28Officer Miller's official request for a criminal charge against Gray, a 25-year-old black man who was only 5-foot-8 inches tall and 145 pounds, said that he had been arrested "without force or incident."
29Miller sought a charge of carrying a switchblade, punishable by a year in prison and a $500 fine, according to court records obtained Monday by The Associated Press.
30"We have no confidence that the city or the police department is going to fairly and objectively investigate this case," Murphy said.
31Activists protesting excessive use of force and even Baltimore city officials say they have more questions than answers.
32About 50 people marched from City Hall to police headquarters Monday, carrying signs reading "Black lives matter" and "Jobs, not police killings."
33They unfurled a yellow banner reading "Stop police terror."
34"This is just one of the most egregious cases I've ever seen," said Colleen Davidson of the Baltimore People's Power Assembly, which she said organized the rally at the request of Gray's family.
35"We felt the need to be out here and make it known that we will not stand and watch things like this happen."
36Rodriguez said his investigators will hand everything they find over to the office of State's Attorney Marilyn Mosby by May 1.
37She too appealed Monday for anyone with information to contact her office.
