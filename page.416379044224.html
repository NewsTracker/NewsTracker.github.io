North Carolina man's obituary: 'Do not vote for Hillary Clinton in 2016'

1And in his obituary, his family made just two requests: please donate to a children's hospital, and please don't vote for Clinton.
2Upright, a staunch Republican, died Monday at a North Carolina hospital.
3He was 81.
4"In lieu of flowers, memorials may be sent to Shriners Hospital for Children," his obituary reads.
5"Also, the family respectfully asks that you do not vote for Hillary Clinton in 2016. R.I.P. Grandaddy."
6Daughter Jill McLain said it was natural to put that line in her father's tribute.
7"He was very passionate about politics and probably passed a little of that on," she told CNN affiliate WSOC.
8Son Michael Upright said he knew his father would enjoy the request.
9"We know he's up there giggling right now, just laughing out loud," he told WSOC.
10Upright's passions included much more than just politics.
11His greatest joy was his family, and he was a former Shriner of the Year at the Cabarrus Shrine Club, according to his obituary.
12After retiring from the building automation industry, he became an avid golfer.
13But it's the last few lines of his obituary that is spurred comments from across the country.
14"You have my solemn promise I will not waste a vote on Hillary Clinton," Marina Shear of Dallas wrote in the obituary's online guestbook.
15"You were a wise man, Larry Upright," said Ron Renno of Forsyth, Georgia.
16"I will remember your request in November 2016. Rest in peace, Sir."
17Nigel Dufont of Kannapolis, North Carolina, offered his condolences but said he wouldn't budge on his support of Clinton.
18"So sorry to hear about Larry, but I am still voting for Hilary!" he wrote.
19"May you rest in peace. I will vote for Hillary if she is the best candidate."
