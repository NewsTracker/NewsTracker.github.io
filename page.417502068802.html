Mariners get Trumbo from Diamondbacks in 6-player trade (Yahoo Sports)

1SEATTLE (AP) -- In need of a spark for its struggling offense, the Seattle Mariners took a significant step in trying to avoid completely falling out of the AL West race.
2Seattle acquired slugger Mark Trumbo from the Arizona Diamondbacks on Wednesday as part of a six-player trade, pulling the trigger on the first major trade of the season with the underachieving Mariners already facing a near double-digit deficit in their division and coming off a fifth straight loss.
3The Mariners announced the trade after a 3- 1 loss to the New York Yankees where the lack of pop at the plate was on display again.
4Seattle entered Wednesday with the lowest batting average and second-worst on-base percentage in the American League.
5''You look at where we were offensively and we're not doing exactly what I thought we would do, or most people thought we would do and Trumbo is a guy that brings an awful lot to the table,'' Seattle general manager Jack Zduriencik said.
6''He's a big power hitter that will bat somewhere in the middle of our lineup.
7Arizona also sent left-handed pitcher Vidal Nuno to the Mariners, while catcher Welington Castillo, right-handed reliever Dominic Leone and minor leaguers Gabby Guerrero and Jack Reinheimer are going to the Diamondbacks.
8Nuno will start in Seattle's bullpen, Zduriencik said, while Castillo provides stability at catcher after Tuffy Gosewisch was lost for the season to an ACL tear.
9The trade also makes room for third baseman Jake Lamb's return from the disabled list with Yasmany Tomas playing more in the outfield.
10Castillo was acquired by Seattle on May 19 to be an offensive upgrade as the Mariners backup catcher.
11But Arizona has a dire catching situation with Gosewisch done for the season.
12Jordan Pacheco and Jarrod Saltalamacchia have handled the catching duties since Gosewisch was injured last week.
13''Not that Salty's not the answer, but we really wanted to address that area.
14(Jake) Lamb's going to be coming off the disabled list pretty soon, so it's just going to create more of a logjam for us,'' Arizona GM Dave Stewart said.
15Trumbo is hitting .259 with nine home runs and 23 RBIs for the Diamondbacks, playing 40 of his 46 games in right field.
16He gives the Mariners a right-handed power bat to complement Nelson Cruz and help balance their lefty-heavy lineup.
17Trumbo's addition should also provide an opportunity for Seattle to use Cruz at designated hitter more often.
18Trumbo did not play in Arizona's 9-8 win over Atlanta on Wednesday and stood stunned at his locker after being told of the deal.
19''I knew it was a possibility so I guess it's not something that catches me completely off guard but it's a bit of a shock,'' Trumbo said.
20Seattle was expected to be a contender in the AL West but is 24-29 after starting an 11-game homestand 1-6.
21The Mariners have been over .500 for one day all season and while the problem in the first couple of weeks was starting pitching, the problems of the last month have been more at the plate.
22Five of Seattle's regular starters are hitting under .250, including Robinson Cano, who is 4-for-23 on the homestand and hitting .211 since May 13.
23Trumbo doesn't solve all of Seattle's offensive problems, but he does lengthen the lineup and give manager Lloyd McClendon a few more options.
24''I think there's probably an exclamation point here doing the deal now and not waiting and trying to get some immediate help now to add to this lineup,'' Zduriencik said.
25Nuno was a starter at Triple-A this season and made three relief appearances with the Diamondbacks.
26Most of his time in the majors has been as a starter.
27''To bring a pitcher like him who has started in the past, who's also been in the bullpen, who is versatile kid, tough, has five years of control, we think it's an asset to the organization,'' Zduriencik said.
