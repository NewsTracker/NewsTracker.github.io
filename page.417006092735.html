Nepal rescuers find 3 bodies near crashed U.S. Marine helicopter

1Nepalese army soldiers prepare to leave for a rescue mission to the site where the suspected wreckage of a U.S. Marine helicopter was spotted on May 15, 2015.
2KATHMANDU, Nepal -- Nepalese rescuers on Friday found three bodies near the wreckage of a U.S. Marine helicopter that disappeared this week during a relief mission in the earthquake-hit Himalayan nation, and officials said it was unlikely there were any survivors from the crash.
3"The wreckage of the helicopter was found in pieces, and there are no chances of any survivors," Nepal's defence secretary, Iswori Poudyal said.
4He did not give the nationalities of the three victims, only saying their remains were charred.
5The helicopter was carrying six Marines and two Nepalese army soldiers.
6A separate team sent by the U.S. Marines said they identified the wreckage as the missing helicopter, the UH-1 "Huey."
7Lt. Gen. John Wissler, commander of the Marine-led joint task force, told reporters in Kathmandu that his team could not immediately identify the cause of the crash or identify the bodies found.
8"It was very severe crash, and based on what we saw in the condition of the aircraft, we believe there were no survivors," he said.
9He said extreme weather and difficult terrain hampered his team's efforts to work at the crash site.
10"Due to the extremely difficult terrain of the site of the mishap, below-freezing temperatures and violent winds and thunderstorms, I made the decision to cease the recovery efforts for this evening," he said.
11"We cannot afford to put U.S. or Nepalese service members at any further risk."
12The recovery mission will resume at first light Saturday.
13The wreckage was found about 24 kilometres (15 miles) from the town of Charikot, near where the aircraft went missing on Tuesday while delivering humanitarian aid to villages hit by two deadly earthquakes, according to the U.S. military joint task force in Okinawa, Japan.
14The area is near Gothali village in the district of Dolakha, about 80 kilometres (50 miles) northeast of Nepal's capital, Kathmandu.
15The discovery of the wreckage, first spotted by Nepalese ground troops and two army helicopters Friday, followed days of intense search involving U.S. and Nepalese aircraft and even U.S. satellites.
16The U.S. relief mission was deployed soon after a magnitude-7.8 quake hit April 25, killing more than 8,200 people.
17It was followed by another magnitude-7.3 quake on Tuesday that killed 117 people and injured 2,800.
18The helicopter had been delivering rice and tarps in Charikot, the area worst hit by Tuesday's quake.
19It had dropped off supplies in one location and was en route to a second site when contact was lost.
20U.S. military officials said earlier this week that an Indian helicopter in the air nearby had heard radio chatter from the Huey aircraft about a possible fuel problem.
21A total of 300 U.S. military personnel have been supporting the aid mission in Nepal, which includes three Hueys, four Marine MV-22B Ospreys, two KC-130 Hercules and four Air Force C-17 Globemaster heavy-lift aircraft.
22The Huey helicopter that crashed was from Marine Light Attack Helicopter squadron 469 based at Camp Pendleton, California.
