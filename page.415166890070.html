Final jury to be chosen in Boston Marathon bombing trial

1But starting Wednesday, a day after a jury was selected, the focus will shift dramatically from the legal process to the harsh reality of what happened that day: the explosions, the screams, the chaos and the blood.
2Prosecutors are expected to present graphic images of the carnage caused by the bombs, including a surveillance video that authorities say shows Tsarnaev placing a backpack just feet from 8-year-old Martin Richard and his family.
3The boy died in the explosion.
4The bombs set April 15, 2013, killed three people and injured more than 260.
5At least 16 people lost limbs.
6"When people start streaming into that courthouse - many with missing limbs - and the prosecutors get up off their chairs and start talking about this again, people are going to relive the enormity and the awful nature of this," said Gerry Leone, a former state and federal prosecutor who led the prosecution of shoe bomber Richard Reid but is not involved in the Tsarnaev case.
7"It wouldn't surprise me to see the young boy's parents as the first witnesses. Oftentimes, in a homicide case, you humanize the victims right away, and you're brought right back to that day," Leone said.
8A blast from the second bomb killed Martin and tore off his 7-year-old sister's left leg.
9Lingzi Lu, 23, a Boston University graduate student, was also killed by that blast.
10Krystal Campbell, a 29-year-old restaurant manager from Medford, was killed by the first bomb.
11Authorities say Tsarnaev, then 19, and his older brother, Tamerlan, 26- ethnic Chechens who had lived in the former Soviet republic of Kyrgyzstan and the volatile Dagestan region of Russia - carried out the bombings to retaliate against the U.S. for wars in Iraq and Afghanistan.
12The brothers came to the U.S. with their parents and two sisters about a decade before the bombings.
13Tamerlan Tsarnaev died following a shootout with police several days after the bombings.
14Dzhokhar Tsarnaev, now 21, faces 30 federal charges in the bombings and in the fatal shooting of a Massachusetts Institute of Technology police officer days later.
15Seventeen of the charges carry the possibility of the death penalty.
16Some bombing survivors have said they plan to attend the trial; others say they have no desire to be there.
17"It's not something I feel I need to do," said Jarrod Clowery, who suffered burns and shrapnel wounds.
18"I have closure in my life. I'm happy. I have a second chance at life, and I'm living it."
19Clowery was watching the marathon with his friends, Paul and J.P. Norden, when the bombs exploded.
20The Nordens each lost a leg.
21The Norden brothers also plan to stay away from the trial, but their mother, Liz, plans to be there every day.
22The trial is expected to last three to four months.
23"It's important to me. I take it personally, what happened to my family," she said.
24In addition to the video, prosecutors also plan to show jurors what they call a confession Tsarnaev scrawled inside the boat he was hiding in.
25"The US Government is killing our innocent civilians but most of you already know that. ... I can't stand to see such evil go unpunished. We Muslims are one body, you hurt one you hurt us all," the note allegedly says.
26"Stop killing our innocent people and we will stop."
27Tsarnaev's lawyers have made it clear that they plan to depict Tamerlan Tsarnaev as the mastermind of the attack and a powerful force in his brother's life.
28Legal analysts say portraying Tamerlan as a coercive influence will likely not be enough to win Dzhokhar an acquittal but could be a significant piece of the defense argument against the death penalty.
29After the guilt phase of the trial, the same jury will decide whether he should be sentenced to life in prison without parole or the death penalty.
30The jury was chosen Tuesday after nearly two months of jury selection.
31The all-white panel consists of 10 women and eight men, including a self-employed house painter, an air traffic controller, a former emergency room nurse and an executive assistant at a law firm.
32Tsarnaev may also get some help from his family.
33One of his sisters, Ailina Tsarnaeva, plans to attend at least part of the trial.
34Liz Norden said she can't quite put her finger on why she feels such a strong need to be there.
