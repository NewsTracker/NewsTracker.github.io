57James Holmes, who has pleaded not guilty by reason of insanity, faces 165 charges in the July 2012 shooting that left 12 people dead and 70 injured.
58Jurors reached a verdict Thursday.
59It is expected to be read at 6:15 p.m. ET, a court spokesman said.
60The verdict was reached in almost hours: The jury began deliberations Wednesday morning.
61Thursday afternoon, survivors and friends and family of the victims began arriving in court to await the reading of the verdict.
62By virtue of his insanity plea, the now 27-year-old Holmes has never denied he was behind the killings.
63But given his mental state, his lawyers have argued that he should not be found culpable.
64"The evidence is clear that he could not control his thoughts, ... he could not control his actions, and he could not control his perceptions," defense attorney Dan King said during closing arguments.
65King told the court psychosis had obscured Holmes' ability to think about things the way a rational person does.
66"Only the mental illness caused this to happen and nothing else."
67Prosecutors -- who called more than 200 witnesses to the stand, among them investigators, students who knew Holmes and his ex-girlfriend -- insisted the shooter knew well what he was doing.
68He acted deliberately to deliver pain and his mental issues shouldn't excuse him from paying the price, they argued.
69"Look at the evidence, then hold this man accountable," Arapahoe County District Attorney George Brauchler said.
70Having bought a ticket 12 days earlier, Holmes on July 19, 2012, walked into the theater No.
719 screening of "The Dark Knight Rises" like other patrons.
72He then walked out through a rear door, which he left propped open.
73Just after midnight, some 18 minutes after the movie began, he returned wearing a ballistic helmet, a gas mask, black gloves and protective gear for his legs, throat and groin.
74A tear gas canister exploded in the theater, then gunfire erupted from an AR-15 rifle, a 12-gauge shotgun and at least one .40 caliber handgun.
75The shooting stopped with Holmes' arrest outside the theater about seven minutes after the first 911 calls were made to police.
76If jurors decide to convict Holmes on multiple murder charges, the next question would be what price he'll pay.
77In 2013, the prosecution signaled it would seek the death penalty.
78The shooter's parents, Robert and Arlene Holmes, were regulars in court during their son's trial.
79They have not talked to reporters.
80But they have written two open letters and published a prayer book detailing the family's internal struggle and pleading for their son's life.
81In a December 2014 letter published in the Denver Post, the couple said, "We have spent every moment for more than two years thinking about those who were injured, and the families and friends of the deceased who were killed, in the theater shooting in Aurora.
82"We are always praying for everyone in Aurora. We wish that July 20, 2012, never happened."
83Still, while they don't deny James Holmes was behind the carnage, the parents said they didn't think he should have been put on trial, much less be convicted and possibly face the death penalty, given his mental state.
84"(James Holmes) is not a monster. He is a human being gripped by a severe mental illness," his parents wrote.
85"We believe that the death penalty is morally wrong, especially when the condemned is mentally ill."
