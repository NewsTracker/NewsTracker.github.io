Smith: Bad Boys 3 still in the air

Boko Haram Fight Intensifies In West Africa

1The terror group appears to be mounting more attacks especially on neighbouring border towns along the country's frontier.
2Authorities who put back the elections by six weeks because of the threat from the extremist group, have vowed to crush all Boko Haram camps between now and polling day on March 28.
3On Saturday, Nigeria's military repelled an attack by scores of Boko Haram militants in the northeastern town of Gombe.
4As the insurgents retreated, they warned residents not to take part in the elections.
5A five-Nation group of West African countries are now involved in the fight against the extremists.
6Nigeria has been joined by Cameroon, Niger, Chad and Benin.
7The Sky News team of me, cameraman Garwen McLuckie and producer Nick Ludlam gained rare access to Cameroon's elite Rapid Intervention Battalion - known as BIR - and joined their troops in the far north of the country.
8We travelled with them to the town of Fotokol, which continues to be the focus of fighting.
9Parts of Fotokol have been torn apart - with rows of stalls, homes and vehicles torched.
10Survivors talk of men being sprayed with bullets as they rose from praying in one of the mosques.
11We saw the walls of the mosque, peppered with holes.
12One of those who carried the bodies out said 37 men were killed there.
13Fotokol is just one of the towns on the border with Nigeria to be invaded and terrorised by the Boko Haram extremist group - and its inhabitants are traumatised.
14One inhabitant told us he had been too scared to leave his household since the attack five days earlier.
15Nine members of his household had been killed shortly after early morning prayers.
16The stakes are high for the Cameroon military posted along the frontier with their much richer, bigger, more powerful Nigerian neighbour.
17They have now been bolstered by troops from Chad, but the battle with the militants is just as fierce.
18And if they fail, the consequences are devastating - as Fotokol found out.
19As we arrived in the area, more Chadian troops were preparing to advance into Nigeria, telling us there was a battle still raging over the bridge separating Cameroon from their neighbour.
20One Chadian soldier who spoke to us told us the fighting was "very dangerous and hard".
21"The fighting goes on through the night even."
22The Cameroon soldiers are fiercely dedicated to their task.
23"We will not let Boko Haram enter our country," said one commander.
24Abubakar Shekau is the leader of Boko Haram.
