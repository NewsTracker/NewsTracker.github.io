Scientists emerge from isolated dome on Hawaii volcano slope

1Six scientists who have been living under a dome on the slopes of a dormant Hawaii volcano for eight months to simulate life on Mars have emerged from isolation.
2The crew stepped outside the dome that's 2,400 metres up the slopes of Mauna Loa to feel fresh air on their skin Saturday.
3It was the first time they left without donning a space suit.
4The scientists are part of a human performance study funded by NASA that's tracking how they work together as a team.
5Crew member Jocelyn Dunn says it feels awesome to have the sensation of wind on her skin.
6She says the first thing she wants to do is to go for a swim.
