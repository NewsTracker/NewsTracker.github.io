Alexandre Lamfalussy, helped found euro currency, dies at 86

1FILE - In this Jan. 10, 1997 file photo, President of the European Monetary Institute (EMI), Alexandre Lamfalussy attends a press conference in Frankfurt.
2Lamfalussy, who died on Monday, May 11, 2015 at the age of 86, headed the Frankfurt-based European Monetary Institute from 1994-97, laying the groundwork for the introduction of the euro and a supra-national central bank to manage the currency.
3FRANKFURT, Germany (AP) - Belgian economist Alexandre Lamfalussy, who headed the institution that became the European Central Bank and was credited as one of the founders of the shared euro currency, has died at age 86.
4The ECB said in a statement Monday that "core building stones" of the shared currency were put in place under Lamfalussy's stewardship.
5It called him "one of the euro's founding fathers."
6Lamfalussy headed the Frankfurt-based European Monetary Institute from 1994-97.
7The institute laid the groundwork for the creation of a supra-national central bank to manage the new currency.
8The institute became the European Central Bank in July 1998, under its first president, Wim Duisenberg.
9The euro was introduced on Jan. 1, 1999, with 11 member countries.
10It now has 19, after Lithuania joined in January.
11Member countries have struggled recently to overcome excessive debt and poor growth.
12As head of the EMI, Lamfalussy helped to define the bank's monetary policy strategy and to devise the technical means to implement it across multiple countries.
13The ECB sets interest rates for the countries that use the euro currency.
14Lamfalussy was born in Hungary in 1929 and studied economics at the Catholic University of Louvain in Belgium and at Oxford University's Nuffield College, according to a biography on the website of the Catholic University of Louvain.
15He served as general manager of the Bank for International Settlements, an important forum for the world's central banks, in Basel from 1985 to 1993.
16Details on when Lamfalussy died and the cause of death were not provided in the ECB statement.
