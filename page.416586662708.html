Facebook Messenger App Now Has Free Video Calling

1Facebook has announced that its Messenger app now has a video calling feature so that you can have face-to-face conversations with your family and friends.
2You can quickly start a video call from Facebook Messenger with just one tap.
3There is a video icon at the top right corner of the screen that lets you start a video call from within an existing Messenger conversation.
4When you start the video call, the camera will start in selfie mode and you can switch to the rear camera to change the view mode.
5Facebook Messenger's video calling feature uses a small window that shows what your camera is pointing at, which can be dragged around.
6There is also an option to cut off the video feed on your end while still running the audio of the conversation.
7This feature comes in handy if the signal is repeatedly interrupted.
8Before the video calling feature was announced, Facebook Messenger already offered the ability to make voice calls with your family and friends.
9In fact, Facebook chief executive and co-founder Mark Zuckerberg said last week that Messenger accounts for "more than 10% of all mobile VoIP calls globally."
10Video calling simply enhances your ability to have real-time conversations with your family and friends through Facebook.
11Facebook said that its video calling service works between iOS and Android.
12Video calling on Facebook Messenger works through a mobile data connection and WiFi.
13This means that someone with a strong LTE signal on their iPhone in the U.S. can still have a video chat with someone in a third-world country that has a weaker 3G signal on their Android device.
14Facebook is also currently experimenting with group video calling and video stabilization, according to TechCrunch.
15This is not the first time that Facebook experimented with video calling.
16Facebook offered video calling through a partnership with Skype in 2011.
17Rather than depending on a third-party for a video calling feature, Facebook decided to invest in its own video communication infrastructure.
18The video calling feature definitely makes Facebook Messenger a worthy adversary against services like Apple Apple FaceTime, Google Google Hangouts and Skype.
19Ever since Facebook separated Messenger from its main app, several new features have been added to the mobile messaging app.
20For example, Facebook Messenger now has the ability to send money to your friends.
21Earlier this month, Facebook created a dedicated website for Messenger called Messenger.com.
22Facebook also recently released an Android app called Hello - which shows you who is calling if their phone number is registered on the social network.
23Third party developers can now create apps that utilize Facebook Messenger through the Messenger Platform.
24And Facebook plans to launch an enterprise messaging service called Businesses on Messenger soon.
25Zuckerberg has a personal goal of connecting people around the world through an initiative called Internet.org.

28In the last couple months, WhatsApp integrated a voice calling feature into its Android and iOS apps.
29The video calling feature for Facebook Messenger is currently available for iOS and Android users in Belgium, Canada, Croatia, Denmark, France, Greece, Ireland, Laos, Lithuania, Mexico, Nigeria, Norway, Oman, Poland, Portugal, the U.K., the U.S. and Uruguay.
30It will gradually roll out to other countries over the next several weeks.
