Facebook COO '30 years sadder' after husband's death

1Sheryl Sandberg, chief operating officer (COO) of Facebook, arrives with her husband David Goldberg, CEO of SurveyMonkey, for the first day of the Allen and Co. media conference in Sun Valley, Idaho in this July 9, 2014, file photo.
2SAN FRANCISCO - Facebook's chief operating officer Sheryl Sandberg took to the social media site on Wednesday to say she felt "thirty years sadder" after the 30-day mourning period she observed following her husband's sudden death.
3Sandberg's husband, SurveyMonkey chief executive Dave Goldberg, died on May 1 after falling off a treadmill during a vacation in Mexico.
4Sandberg posted an online tribute four days after the death of Goldberg, who built a company valued at US$2 billion.
5She has also periodically shared posts about her mourning and pictures of Goldberg.
6Her 1,731-word post Wednesday marked the end of sheloshim, the traditional Jewish 30-day period of mourning for a spouse.
7"I have lived thirty years in these thirty days," Sandberg wrote.
8"I am thirty years sadder. I feel like I am thirty years wiser."
9Sandberg also said Goldberg's death helped her gain a "more profound understanding of what it is to be a mother," and that she rejoiced that her children were alive each day.
10She ended the post with lyrics from Bono, who sang at Goldberg's Silicon Valley memorial service last month.
11"'There is no end to grief ... and there is no end to love,'" she posted.
12"I love you, Dave."
13Today is the end of sheloshim for my beloved husband—the first thirty days.
