12According to a 2012 LA Times study, Oscar voters are nearly 94 percent white and 77 percent male, with a median age of 62.

14April Reign, a lawyer who created the hashtag #OscarsSoWhite, doesn't blame Hollywood's overwhelming whiteness solely on the Academy, but rather the structure in place that allows for it.
15"The Oscars are white and male because the movies are white and male. The movies are white and male because the directors and producers are white and male. The directors and producers are white and male because the studio heads are white and male," she told The Huffington Post.
16If we want to see different faces on movie posters, and a diverse crowd of people on stage Sunday night during the Oscar ceremony itself, "The mindset about who should play these parts, about who should direct these films, about who should write the screenplays, must be changed," Reign said.
17In a scathing December op-ed for The Hollywood Reporter, comedian Chris Rock elaborated on how that current framework enables Hollywood to remain mostly white and mostly male.
18"When it comes to casting, Hollywood pretty much decides to cast a black guy or they don't. We're never on the 'short list.' We're never 'in the mix.' ... I never heard anyone go, 'Is it going to be Amy Adams or Gabrielle Union?'" he wrote.
19"You can go to whole movies and not see one black woman... I go to the movies almost every week, and I can go a month and not see a black woman having an actual speaking part in a movie. That's the truth."
20Of course, jobs, and therefore nominations, should be based on merit.
21But until opportunities for the former, and subsequently the latter, include faces of all types, audiences who long for more diversity will continue to be disappointed.
