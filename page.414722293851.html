U.S. says 15 more airstrikes launched against Islamic State

Red-tailed hawk gets trapped in SUV grille

1In a statement on Friday, the Combined Joint Task Force leading the air operations said the strikes near al Raqqah, Kobani and Dayr az Zawr in Syria hit numerous armored vehicles used by the militant group as well as two units of fighters, among other targets.
2In Iraq, the air strikes near al Asad, Mosul and Sinjar hit five units of Islamic State fighters, three buildings, three fighting positions and other targets, according to the statement.
1Andy Somerville from Texas was driving home when he saw a flash in front of him and felt a bump on the front of his vehicle.
2However, he assumed he'd hit something and it had bounced off and didn't think anything more of it.
3But the next morning on further inspection, he discovered that a red-tailed hawk had got lodged in his bumper.
4Miraculously, the animal was still alive.
5Somerville immediately called the Wildlife Center of Texas who came out to rescue the animal.
6Photographs of the trapped hawk show it hanging out of the grill by its wings.
7Somerville said the bird's body was the engine compartment and he was just 'freaking out' when he found it.
8He said he could imagine anything surviving the collision as he was doing about 70mph at the time.
9Just over a week later, vets say the hawk is on the road to recovery.
10He didn't sustain any broken bones and has slight bruising from where he was hanging.
11Red-tailed hawks can live up to 21 years in the wild with a maximum wing span of around 1.4 meters.
