DNA confirms that girl in Mexico is US woman's daughter

1MORELIA, Mexico (AP) - A Mexican prosecutor says DNA tests on Alondra Diaz match the U.S. mother who has been searching for her for eight years.
2Jose Martin Godoy Castro says tests show that Alondra is the daughter of Houston woman Dorotea Garcia, who has searched for the girl since Alondra's father took her to Mexico without her consent.
3Godoy spoke late Thursday.
4Garcia's search gained international attention last month after Judge Cinthia Elodia Mercado ruled that another teenager, 14-year-old Alondra Luna, was the missing girl and ordered her released to Garcia.
5Video recordings circulated widely of Alondra Luna screaming and desperately resisting police after the court decision.
6Garcia reunited with her real daughter late Tuesday in a courtroom in the western state of Michoacan.
7But this time the judge waited for DNA confirmation.
