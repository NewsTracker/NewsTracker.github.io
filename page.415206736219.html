Man dies in Washington state jail cell hours after drug arrest

Feds: Evidence backs Ferguson officer's account in shooting

1The Pierce County Medical Examiner's Office in Tacoma is working to determine whether the suspect, 32-year-old Demetrius Dixon, died of a self-inflicted drug overdose, an investigator said.
2Results of the investigation could be ready later on Wednesday.
3Dixon was arrested Tuesday in the city of Lakewood, between Seattle and Olympia, when he was found to be in possession of cocaine, prescription drugs and illegal quantities of marijuana during a traffic stop, Lakewood police spokesman John Unfred said.Dixon resisted "slightly" during his arrest, and the officer believed he saw him ingest a white powder as he was taken into custody, Unfred said.
4"He denied several times that he had ingested anything," Unfred said.
5The officer called for assistance from emergency medical officials, who determined the suspect had a slightly elevated heart rate, police said.
6He was then taken to St. Clare Hospital in Lakewood where a nurse cleared him, police said.
7Dixon was later booked into Pierce County Jail on charges including drug possession with intent to distribute.
8He was found dead, alone in his cell, about four hours later, authorities said.
9Pierce County Detective Ed Troyer said Dixon had not complained of feeling ill.
10"He is very versed in the criminal justice system and had plenty of opportunity to say something was wrong with him even after the hospital cleared him," Troyer said.
11The suspect had 12 felony arrests and six felony convictions for drug dealing, weapons, burglary and assault charges, Troyer said.
12Dixon's family could not immediately be located for comment.
13A spokesman for the Lakewood hospital where police said a nurse cleared him said it was checking into the report.
1The Department of Justice released an 86-page report Wednesday summarizing its reasons for not charging former officer Darren Wilson, who is white, with a federal civil-rights violation for the Aug. 9 shooting that sparked protests in the St. Louis area and across the nation.
2"Multiple credible witnesses corroborate virtually every material aspect of Wilson's account and are consistent with the physical evidence," the report said.
3It added: "Based on this investigation, the Department has concluded that Darren Wilson's actions do not constitute prosecutable violations."
4That mirrors the determination of a St. Louis County grand jury, which decided in November not to bring state criminal charges against Wilson.
5Wilson first encountered Brown and his friend, Dorian Johnson, walking in the middle of a Ferguson street, just minutes after a report of a robbery at a nearby convenience store.
6According to Wilson's testimony, after he told the two to move out of the street, he realized they matched the description of the robbery suspects.
7The Justice Department report says that police dispatch recordings and Wilson's radio transmissions show that he was aware of the theft and had a description of the suspects as he encountered Brown, who was carrying stolen cigarillos.
8After the initial encounter, Wilson backed up his police vehicle and confronted Brown.
9A struggle ensued.
10The Justice Department report says that Wilson and other witnesses stated that Brown reached into the police SUV through an open window "and punched and grabbed Wilson."
11The report says that is "corroborated by bruising on Wilson's jaw and scratches on his neck, the presence of Brown's DNA on Wilson's collar, shirt and pants, and Wilson's DNA on Brown's palm."
12Wilson testified that Brown reached for his gun during the struggle in his vehicle.
13The report says Wilson fired two shots from the vehicle.
14Brown was shot in his hand.
15It says autopsy results and the trajectory of the bullets back Wilson's account.
16The report says skin from Brown's palm was found on the outside of the police SUV door, and Brown's DNA was also found on the inside of the driver's door.
17That would "corroborate Wilson's account that during the struggle, Brown used his right hand to grab and attempt to control Wilson's gun," the report says.
18It adds: "The evidence establishes that the shots fired by Wilson while he was seated in his SUV were in self-defense."
19After the initial shots, Brown ran away from Wilson's vehicle.
20The Justice Department report says Brown was not shot in the back, because there were no entrance wounds there.
21At some point, Brown stopped and turned around.
22Some witnesses have said Brown had his hands up in a sign of surrender- an assertion that became popular among protesters.
23The federal report notes that there are varying accounts of what Brown was doing with his hands.
24But the report concludes: "There are no credible witness accounts that state that Brown was clearly attempting to surrender when Wilson shot him."
25Wilson testified that after Brown stopped running, he turned around and began charging back toward the officer.
26Wilson said he feared for his life as he then fatally shot Brown.
27The federal report says all the credible witness accounts "establish that Brown was moving toward Wilson when Wilson shot him."
28The report says the physical evidence - namely, blood stains on the road - also confirms Brown moved toward Wilson.
29It adds: "The evidence establishes that the shots fired by Wilson after Brown turned around were in self-defense."
30Protesters upset about Brown's shooting have emphasized that he did not have a gun.
31But the Justice Department says that alone is not a reason to charge Wilson.
32The report notes the struggle at the vehicle and Brown's subsequent movement back toward Wilson.
33"Although, with hindsight, we know that Brown was not armed with a gun or other weapon, this fact does not render Wilson's use of deadly force objectively unreasonable," the report says.
34"Sufficient credible evidence supports Wilson's claim that he reasonably perceived Brown to be posing a deadly threat."
35The report describes eight witnesses who it says gave credible accounts corroborating Wilson's assertion that he acted in self-defense.
36By contrast, the report says there were no credible witnesses who incriminated Wilson and whose accounts were consistent with prior statements, physical evidence and other witnesses.
37It says portions of Johnson's testimony about his friend's shooting were inconsistent with prior statements and physical and forensic evidence.
38The Justice Department says some witnesses were reluctant to come forward because of their personal safety.
39One woman finally agreed to meet investigators in a library parking lot.
40Another man refused to comply with a grand jury subpoena or give a formal statement, citing a fear of reprisal should the neighborhood learn that his testimony corroborated Wilson's account instead of the "hands up" narrative.
41Yet another woman blocked her door with a couch in an attempt to avoid being served with a grand jury subpoena.
