4Said Musalam said IS lured his 19-year-old son Mohamed with promises of women, money and cars.
5"They promised a lot of things and then I came here and there is nothing," Musalam recalled his son saying in a phone call home from Syria.

7The father spoke to The Associated Press from the family's east Jerusalem home, a day after IS released a video purporting to show the killing of a captive identified as Mohamed Said Ismail Musalam.
8In the 13-minute video posted online, a militant and a boy dressed in camouflage stand behind a man in an orange jumpsuit kneeling on the ground.
9The fighter makes a brief statement in French accusing the man of working for the Mossad before the boy steps forward and shoots the captive in the head.
10The AP could not independently verify the video but it was released by the IS group's Furqan media arm and corresponds to previous IS videos.
11The Israeli Foreign Ministry declined comment while Israel's Shin Bet internal security agency said it announced earlier this year that Mohamed Musalam had left for Turkey to fight with IS.
12The elder Musalam said he was the only one in the family to watch the video.
13He said he saw his son dressed in orange, knew that he was marked for death, and could not watch it through to the end.
14His wife cried and she and other family members were too distraught to watch, he added.
15The family said it had begun a customary three-day mourning period, though there will not be a mourning tent because they live in a Jewish area of east Jerusalem.
16Musalam said he wished the same fate upon the family of Islamic State group's leader Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi.
17"I will leave it to God and what happened to my son Mohamed I hope God will do the same for you, your sons and your family," Musalam said in Arabic, addressing al-Baghdadi.
18"God knows that one day we'll meet, whether in paradise or in hell, and we'll settle the accounts."
19After the video was released on Tuesday, Musalam told the AP that his son left for Syria four months ago without telling his family and later told his brother that he was going to fight with IS.
20More than a month ago, the family received a call from an unidentified person who said Musalam had fled IS, was caught at a Turkish checkpoint, and was put in IS jail.
21"They did not want to let him leave because if he comes back, he might be caught by the Israelis and tell them what he had seen. So they wanted to get rid of him," the father said.
22"I know my son. I raised him well. I am sure he's not working for the Mossad."
