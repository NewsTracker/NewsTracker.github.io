The Netherlands remembers MH17 victims

1An emotional service has been held in the Netherlands to commemorate the 298 people who died when flight MH17 was shot down over east Ukraine last year.
2The Netherlands remembered on Friday the 298 people who died when flight MH17 was shot downover east Ukraine last year.
3About 2,000 friends and relatives attended the ceremony, one year after the attack.

5Kiev and the West blame pro-Russian separatists for the tragedy, and have called for a UN tribunal to try those responsible.
6Ukraine's president accused Russia's military on Friday of ordering the missile strike that brought down Malaysia Airlines flight MH17 one year ago, as relatives gathered from the Netherlands to Australia to remember the 298 passengers and crew.
7"MH17, where 298 victims were killed for no reason, was shot down by a Russian BUK missile, launched by a Russian professional crew under an order and an instruction from the Russian military," President Petro Poroshenko said on the first anniversary of the disaster.
8Ukraine has consistently blamed Russia, but Poroshenko's comments were his most direct to date.
9Russia denies responsibility and in turn has pointed the finger at Ukraine, whose forces are fighting pro-Russian separatist rebels in the region of eastern Ukraine where the plane crashed to earth.
10Poroshenko did not say what evidence he had for accusing the Russian military.
11In comments later on Friday, he used a different form of words, blaming the tragedy on Russian-trained "terrorists" - a description often used by the Ukrainian government to describe the separatists.
12In rebel-held eastern Ukraine, a church service was held and residents joined a procession to a gravestone near the charred area where twisted metal and body parts came crashing down on July 17 last year.
13"To the memory of the dead - 298 innocent victims of civil war," was written on the gravestone in the village of Hrabove, where a Russian Orthodox priest and an imam said prayers.
14About 300 people brought flowers and flags of the victims' countries, some with black ribbons attached, and released white balloons into the sky.
15"The memory of these people will always be in our hearts ...Pray for their souls," said a priest who gave his name as Oleg.
16Of the victims, 196 were from the Netherlands, where 1,500 relatives gathered for a ceremony of music and dance at which the names of the victims were read out.
17"Today is a hard day, a day of reliving," Prime Minister Mark Rutte told mourners.
18"The 298 unique people we remember today are for always bound with your lives and those of thousands of others."
19The wife of the airliner's first officer took the stage to read out the passenger announcement her husband was never able to make.
20"Salam alaikum and good evening," she said, her voice trembling.
21"We will begin our descent shortly."
22At Amsterdam's Schiphol Airport, from where flight MH17 departed, and at towns across the country, sunflowers were laid like those that grow at the crash site.
23Flags flew at half-mast.
24Western governments believe that pro-Russian rebels shot the plane out of the sky with a Russian-supplied BUK missile.
25Britain and Ukraine marked the anniversary with new appeals for an international tribunal to prosecute suspects, an option also favoured by Malaysia, Australia, the Netherlands and Belgium.
26Russian President Vladimir Putin dismissed those calls as premature and counter-productive on Thursday in a phone call with Rutte and criticised "politicised" versions of the incident "planted" in foreign media.
27Western diplomats say they are open to the prospect of a tribunal that is not backed by the U.N. if Russia wields its Security Council veto.
28Thirty-nine of the victims were from Australia, which held a memorial service.
29"Their passing leaves a void than can never be filled and a pain that still throbs," Australian Prime Minister Tony Abbott said.
30Malaysia remembered the victims, 43 of whom were Malaysian, at a ceremony on July 11.
