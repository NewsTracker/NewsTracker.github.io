1An estimated 3,400 people were evacuated from the area around Villarica, including parts of the towns Pucon and Conaripe.
2Chilean authorities issued a red alert and sealed off roads surrounding the volcano, located in a tourist enclave outside the city of Temuco.
3The president, Michelle Bachelet, announced an emergency visit to the zone.
4Government planes flew over the crater in an attempt to gather data following the 20-minute pre-dawn eruption.
5The eruption followed weeks of increased activity in and around the volcano, including small earthquakes.
6The snow-covered peak has a crater 180 metres across and a pool of lava estimated at 120 metres (400ft) deep.
7Considered one of Latin America's most active volcanoes, Villarica has erupted on average once every 10 years.
8A major eruption in 1971 created a cloud of poisonous gases that killed approximately a dozen people.
9Authorities are concerned that lava and heat from the latest eruption could melt glaciers and snow on the flanks of the volcano and cause mudslides.
10The Rio Turbio river was reported to have risen 15ft.
11Schools were shut in the four districts closest to the volcano.
12Police patrols to the area were increased to prevent looting.
13In 2010 Bachelet was widely criticised for her response to an earthquake.
14The emergency services agency known as the Onemi was reorganised and is now coordinated by a former army general, Ricardo Toro, who for years managed international humanitarian operations in Haiti.
15If the volcano shows signs of increased activity, Onemi will next organise the evacuation of an estimated 20,000 more people from Pucon and the surrounding area.
