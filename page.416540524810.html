Pentagon: US military sending aid to quake-ravaged Nepal

1WASHINGTON (AP) - The Pentagon says a U.S. military plane has departed from the Dover Air Force Base in Delaware bound for earthquake-stricken Nepal.
2Onboard are 70 personnel, including a U.S. Agency for International Development disaster assistance response team, a Virginia-based search and rescue team and 45 tons of cargo to provide assistance to areas hit by Saturday's massive quake that left more than 2,500 dead.
3The Pentagon says the flight is expected to arrive at Kathmandu on Monday.
4More than two dozen Defense Department personnel and a transport plane are currently in Nepal on a training exercise.
5All personnel are accounted for.
