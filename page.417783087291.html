Asian stock markets slip, euro slides as Greek debt talks appear to go nowhere

1HONG KONG -- Asian stocks slipped and the euro sank Monday as Greece inched closer to a possible default after weekend talks with international creditors over freeing up the rest of its bailout funds went nowhere.
2Japan's benchmark Nikkei 225 index lost 0.4 per cent to 20,335.88 and South Korea's Kospi shed 0.5 per cent to 2,041.82.
3Hong Kong's Hang Seng slumped 1.3 per cent to 26,926.47.
4The Shanghai Composite Index in mainland China dropped 1.3 per cent to 5,100.46.
5Australia's S&P/ASX 200 retreated 0.4 per cent to 5,526.10.
6Benchmarks in Singapore, Taiwan and New Zealand also fell.
7Weekend negotiations on Greece's bailout terms produced no breakthroughs and Greek officials and creditors remained far apart as a deadline for a debt payment nears.
8Greek leaders want to get access to the final 7.2 billion euros ($8.2 billion) of the 240 billion euro bailout that's needed to repay debts and avoid a possible default that could trigger an exit from the euro; the bailout package expires at the end of the month.
9But investors are growing nervous at signs of scant progress in talks with creditors, including fellow eurozone states, the European Central Bank and the International Monetary Fund, on new economic reforms required before the money is doled out.
10"We have always argued that a Greek deal would only occur at one minute to midnight," Michael Every, Rabobank's head of Asia-Pacific financial market research, wrote in a commentary.
11"Yet it now looks like 23:58 and counting."
12The euro faltered on news of the Greek debt impasse, falling 0.5 per cent to $1.1204 from $1.1260 in late trading Friday.
13The dollar strengthened to 123.49 yen from 123.39 yen.
14Moves by regulators to tighten up on margin financing sent Chinese shares lower.
15The country's securities regulator issued draft rules Friday limiting the amount investors can borrow from brokerages for trading to four times their net capital, according to a report by the official Xinhua news agency.
16Authorities are clamping down as they try to prevent a crash in China's overheated stock markets, which have soared in the past year in a boom fueled partly by borrowed money.
17Major U.S. stock indexes closed lower on Friday on the European uncertainty.
18The Dow Jones industrial average fell 0.8 per cent to 17,898.84 and the S&P 500 slipped 0.7 per cent to 2,094.11.
19The Nasdaq composite lost 0.6 per cent to 5,051.10.
20Benchmark U.S. crude oil extended losses, falling 22 cents to $59.74 a barrel in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
21The contract hit a high for the year on Wednesday but has been falling since then and on Friday it sank 81 cents to settle at $59.96 a barrel.
22Brent crude, an international benchmark, dropped 25 cents to $64.39 a barrel.
