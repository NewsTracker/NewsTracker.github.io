Maryland governor declares state of emergency over Baltimore violence

1April 27 (Reuters) - Maryland Governor Larry Hogan declared a state of emergency and activated the National Guard to address the violence in Baltimore, his office said on Monday.
2The governor's office said the actions came at the request of the city of Baltimore.
3It added that Hogan would hold a news conference at 8:30 p.m. to discuss the violence following the funeral of a 25-year-old black man who died after he was injured in police custody.
