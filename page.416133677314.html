Maradona says election chance to kick Blatter in 'rear end'

1Diego Maradona says the upcoming FIFA election offers a chance "to kick (Sepp) Blatter in the rear end."
2Maradona is in Colombia to play an exhibition game called the "Game for Peace," but he could not resist weighing in on Blatter's bid for his fifth consecutive term to head soccer's governing body.
3Maradona, who has had a long-running feud with Blatter, says he is supporting FIFA vice president Prince Ali bin al-Hussein of Jordan.
4Dutch soccer association chairman Michael van Praag and former Portugal international Luis Figo are also running against Blatter.
5Argentina's soccer legend Diego Armando Maradona, left, blows kisses towards the press as Colombian former senator Piedad Cordoba, smiles sitting next to him during a press conference in Bogota, Colombia, Thursday, April 9, 2015.
6Maradona will play Friday in an exhibition soccer match in support of peace talks between the Colombian government and legist rebels.
