Before Tennessee rampage, suspect texted friend link to Islamic verse

1CHATTANOOGA, Tenn.
2Hours before the Tennessee shooting that killed five U.S. servicemen, the suspected gunman texted a close friend a link to an Islamic verse that included the line: "Whosoever shows enmity to a friend of Mine, then I have declared war against him."
3The gunman's family said in a statement late on Saturday he had suffered from depression, and that they had experienced shock and horror over the violence.
4The suspect's friend said he thought nothing of the text message at the time, but now wonders if it was a clue to Thursday's rampage in Chattanooga, which has re-ignited concerns about the radicalization of young Muslim men.
5"I didn't see it as a hint at the time, but it may have been his way of telling me something," the friend told Reuters on Saturday.
6He requested anonymity for fear of a backlash.
7The suspect, Mohammod Youssuf Abdulazeez, a Kuwaiti-born naturalized U.S. citizen, was killed in a gunfight with police.
8The Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) is investigating the attack as an act of terrorism, but said it was premature to speculate on the gunman's motive.
9Abdulazeez's family offered condolences to the families of the "victims of the shooting our son committed on Thursday."
10"There are no words to describe our shock, horror and grief," they said in a statement.
11"The person who committed this horrible crime was not the son we knew and loved," they said, adding "for many years, our son suffered from depression," and that "his pain found its expression in this heinous act."
12The family said they were cooperating with authorities and added that now was not the time to say anything publicly beyond expressing sorrow for the victims and their families.
13While a firm connection between the 24-year-old suspect and radical Islam has not been established, the shooting follows a series of attacks or thwarted attacks in the United States and other countries by Muslims claiming to be inspired by Islamic State or other militant groups.
14Abdulazeez returned from a trip to Jordan in 2014 concerned about conflicts in the Middle East and the reluctance of the United States and other countries to intervene, according to two friends who knew him since elementary school.
15"That trip was eye-opening for him. He learned a lot about the traditions and culture of the Middle East," said one of the two friends, the person who received the text message.
16Abdulazeez was upset about the 2014 Israeli bombing campaign in Gaza and the civil war in Syria, he said.
17"He felt Jordan, Qatar, Saudi Arabia were not doing enough to help, and that they were heavily influenced by the United States."
18The other friend said Abdulazeez had always talked about the Middle East, "but I'd say his level of understanding and awareness really rose after he came back."
19U.S. authorities said Abdulazeez sprayed gunfire at a military recruiting center in a strip mall in Chattanooga, then drove to a Naval Reserve Center about 6 miles (10 km) away, where he killed four Marines before he himself was shot dead.
20Three people were wounded, including a sailor who died on Saturday.
21The U.S. Navy identified the sailor as Navy Petty Officer 2nd Class Randall Smith of Paulding, Ohio, who left behind a wife and three young daughters.
22"He was an awesome young man," said Smith's step-grandmother, Darlene Proxmire.
23"He loved his wife and children. He loved the Navy."
24Abdulazeez, an engineer, went to the Middle East in 2010 and visited several countries, said the first friend.
25He then went to Jordan in 2014 to work for his uncle, and lived with his uncle and his grandparents there, he said.
26After Abdulazeez returned, he seemed more mellow, less interested in partying.
27"That is part of what drew us closer. He was a guy who wanted to settle down and get his life going. That connected us," he said.
28The night before the attack, just after 10 p.m., the friend received a text message from Abdulazeez with a link to a Hadith, or Islamic teaching: sunnah.com/nawawi40/38.
29He showed Reuters the text message on his phone.
30The friend said he had been asking Abdulazeez for job advice, and continued to text him that evening and into Thursday but did not get a reply.
31The friend said he was interviewed by the FBI.
32The FBI declined to comment on Saturday.
33For jihadists and ultraconservative Salafist Sunni Muslims, the Hadith "is usually understood within the context of al-wala wa-l-bara (or) love for Islam and hatred for its enemies," said David Cook, an associate professor who specializes in Islam at Rice University in Texas.
34According to Abdulazeez's friends, he purchased three guns on Armslist.com after returning from Jordan, including an AK-74, an AR-15, and a Saiga 12.
35They said he also owned 9mm and .22-caliber handguns.
36Armslist.com is an online listing site on which individuals can buy and sell firearms through private transactions.
37Critics say it enables people to obtain weapons without background checks, though the site has a disclaimer that it requires users to agree to follow all state and federal firearms laws.
38Emails seeking comment from Armlist.com and Armslist Legal Defense Fund, created to defend Armslist against lawsuits, were not immediately answered.
39Abdulazeez's friends said he occasionally smoked marijuana and drank alcohol, and struggled to reconcile that with his faith in Islam.
40At one point, in 2012 or 2013, he received therapy for drugs and alcohol use, they said.
41"He used it to de-stress, when things were difficult at home, or whatever," the first friend said, adding that tensions between Abdulazeez and his Palestinian parents had also upset him.
42His parents nearly got divorced in 2009, according to court records.
43In April, Abdulazeez was charged with driving under the influence and had faced a July 30 court date.
44Abdulazeez also had problems with local youths that sometimes took on a religious and racial tone, his friend said.
45"There were rednecks, ignorant people, who sometimes would cause problems. Mo never fought, but he used to get worked up and yell and stuff," he said.
46"Afterwards he would calm down, and just say it doesn't matter."
47Over the past few months, Abdulazeez and his friends practiced shooting in the Prentice Cooper State Forest near Chattanooga, sometimes two or three times a week.
48"He was always interested in guns, since he was young. He started with a BB gun and paintball, and went on from there. We would go out shooting quite often," said the friend.
49Abdulazeez also liked driving fast in the hills surrounding Chattanooga.
50Two nights before the attack, he and some friends went joyriding in Abdulazeez's rented gray convertible Ford Mustang, passing through the towns of Whitwell, Dayton and Jasper.
51"Fast car on a rainy night. We were flying, doing tight turns and drifting," said the friend, adding that they returned home at about 3 o'clock in the morning.
52He said Abdulazeez had a well-paid job and many plans for his life, including possibly starting a computer sales business in Chattanooga.
53"He wanted to buy a car. He wanted a video console, to make a man cave - every guy's dream."
54He said it was difficult to understand how his friend became the suspect in the rampage.
55"The signs just weren't there," he said.
56"The only thing I can think of is that it was a combination of things - what is happening overseas, his family problems, maybe some of the issues with the less educated people here. I don't know."
