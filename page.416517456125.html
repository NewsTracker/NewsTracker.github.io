Thousands take to the streets for Freddie Gray rally

1Protestors rally at Oriole Park at Camden Yards during a march for Freddie Gray, Saturday, April 25, 2015.
2BALTIMORE (AP) - Thousands of protesters took to the streets Saturday to demand answers in the case of Freddie Gray, the largest rally since the 25-year-old black man died in police custody.
3After a few hours of peaceful demonstrations, scores of rowdy protesters crowded outside of Camden Yards, where the Baltimore Orioles were set to play the Boston Red Sox.
4One protester threw something at a police car, and kicked the windshield, cracking it, while others threw cans and plastic bottles in the direction of police officers.
5At least one protester jumped on a police car.
6Another car's front windshield was also shattered.
7From inside the stadium, fans watched the protesters gather.
8Before the protest turned tense, demonstrators held signs with slogans such as "Jail Killer Police!" and "Unite Here!"
9They filled two city blocks and marched 2 miles to City Hall, where the crowd overtook the grassy plaza adjacent from the building.
10Tanya Peacher, a 36-year-old Baltimore resident, said she'd never attended a protest in the city before, but watching a video of Gray's arrest motivated her.
11Late Friday, Deputy Commissioner Kevin Davis said Gray should have received medical attention at the spot where he was arrested - before he was put inside a police transport van handcuffed and without a seat belt, a violation of the department's policy.
12Gray died April 19 after suffering a fatal spinal injury while in custody.
13Authorities have not explained how or when Gray's spine was injured.
14Video showed him being dragged into a police van and police have said he rode in it for about 30 minutes before paramedics were called.
15On Saturday, residents young and old, from Baltimore and beyond, voiced their anger at how the department and the city's officials are handling the investigation into Gray's death.
16At one point, the crowd paused for a moment of silence in front of Shock Trauma, the hospital where Gray died.
17The marchers then migrated to Camden Yards, where the Baltimore Orioles are scheduled to play the Boston Red Sox on Saturday evening.
18At a downtown intersection, a dozen marchers laid down in the street during an impromptu "die-in."
19Wearing a sign around his neck that said "I am Freddie Gray," 33-year-old Dante Acree joined thousands of others outside City Hall.
20Acree said he came out to the protest because "it could have been one of my kids."
21Leonard Patterson, 56, said he drove from Manassas, Virginia, to be a part of the protest.
22Patterson said he decided to come after thinking about his college-aged daughter.
23"I'm trying to do everything in my limbs, everything in my power, to make this a better world for her," said Patterson, holding up his black and white drawing of Freddie Gray.
24The drawing shows Gray being hoisted from a police van to heaven by two angels.
25"I'm here to do what I can. Police brutality is as old as the 1950s, the 1960s. It's still here," he said.
