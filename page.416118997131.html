What you need to know about buying the Apple Watch

1pple CEO Tim Cook announces the Apple Watch during an Apple special event at the Yerba Buena Center for the Arts on March 9, 2015 in San Francisco, California.
2After a lot of hype, the Apple Watch is (almost) here and fans are already drooling at the possibility of getting their hands on the U.S. tech giant's latest piece of must-have tech.
3Apple's wearable device is available for pre-order on Friday April 10 and will be released on April 24.
4But unlike the iPhone launch you won't be able to just pop into the store and try it out.
5Here's what you'll have to do.
6If you want to try an Apple Watch on, you'll need an appointment.
7Those can be booked online through the Apple Store or iOS Apple Store App starting from April 10 after 12:01am PDT.
8Different styles of the Apple Watch will be displayed in a table covered with glass.
9Unlike other devices such as iPhones and iPads, you won't be able to play with them.
10Once you are happy with your choice of Watch, employees will be able to order the device for you via their in-store system.
11This can be picked up at the store or can be delivered to your home on April 24th.
12Alternatively, users can order a watch online.
13This will be delivered straight to their home.
14A leaked memo from Apple's retail chief Angela Ahrendts to Business Insider told staff to encourage shoppers to buy the Watch online rather than in store, to force a "significant change in mindset" of customers who are accustomed to lining up for hours for the latest product.
15The Apple Watch is available in three models: Apple Watch Sport, starting at $349, Apple Watch, available from $549, and the 18 karat gold Apple Watch Edition, starting at $10,000.
16People booking an appointment to buy the 18-karat gold Apple Watch Edition, which starts at $10,000, will have a longer and private appointment.
17Customers buying the high-end wearable will have a special try-on station as well as the ability to skip any lines, according to a report by 9To5Mac.
18On April 10, it will be available for pre-order in Australia, Canada, China, France, Germany, Hong Kong, Japan, the U.K. and the U.S.
19Customers will begin receiving the product on April 24, the official release date for the product.
20Customers who purchase online or in-store from Apple will be offered a personal setup to customise and pair the Apple Watch with their iPhone.
21When the Apple Watch is available from April 24, customers will still need a reservation or can order online.
22That's the million dollar question and one we won't know until Apple releases sales data.
23A range of estimates have flooded in with JPMorgan predicting Apple shipping 26.3 million units in calendar 2015, and Bernstein projecting 7.5 million units to be sold in the second half of 2015 and 20 million sold in 2016.
24And consumer studies are not pointing to the hype that Apple would have us believe.
25One survey by ACCENT Marketing of 1,000 people that were planning on buying a wearable this year, found that four out of five were going to buy the Apple Watch.
