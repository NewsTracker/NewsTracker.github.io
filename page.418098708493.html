Marlins' slugger Stanton out 4-6 weeks with broken hand (Yahoo Sports)

1MIAMI (AP) -- For the second year in a row, Giancarlo Stanton's chances of winning the major league home run title have been hurt by an injury.
2The Miami Marlins slugger has a broken left hand, which is expected to sideline him four to six weeks.
3He was placed on the 15-day disabled list on Saturday and will meet with a hand specialist.
4Stanton, who grimaced as he swung and missed for strike three Friday night in the ninth inning of the Marlins' 7-1 loss to the Los Angeles Dodgers, said the injury became worse as the game progressed.
5''We're hoping that it will be the quickest course possible, but certainly not great news when you lose a guy that means what he's meant to this ballclub and to baseball,'' Marlins manager Dan Jennings said before Saturday's game.
6Stanton led the majors with 27 homers and 67 RBIs when sidelined.
7Last year he was in the thick of the home run race when a beaning ended his season on Sept.
811, and he finished tied for second in the majors with 37 homers.
9The latest injury means Stanton will miss the All-Star Game, and it's a big blow to a team that lost its fifth game in a row Friday to fall a season-worst 15 games below .500.
10While Stanton took lusty cuts in the ninth, he said the injury occurred earlier in the game.
11''On a swing, my bat dug into my hand a little bit and didn't feel the greatest,'' he said Friday after the loss.
12Stanton's hand appeared swollen, and he said it hurt where he grips the bat.
13He went 0 for 4 on the night, which included a broken bat.
14He ground out in the first inning and struck out in the fourth, sixth and ninth innings.
15Stanton said he didn't think the broken bat affected him.
16''No, I don't think it was that at-bat,'' he said.
17Stanton has 12 homers in June, tying the franchise record for any month.
18Despite his power surge, the Marlins had been woeful lately, batting .201 and averaging two runs a game over the past nine games through Friday.
19''Now it's upon us to pick it up and we can't feel sorry for ourselves,'' Jennings said.
20His injury comes as the team anticipates the return Thursday of ace Jose Fernandez, who has been sidelined more than a year recovering from Tommy John surgery.
21Stanton signed a record $325 million, 13-year contract last November.
22At age 25 he has 181 career homers, but because of injuries has never played more than 150 games in a season.
