Islamic State battles Syrian army near world heritage site

1Islamic State militants were reported on Thursday to be advancing in a government held area near one of Syria's world heritage sites, part of a major offensive in central regions by the jihadist group that has destroyed antiquities in Iraq.
2Islamic State fighters reached online said the group was shelling an air base near Tadmur, an oasis in the desert north-east of Damascus also known as Palmyra.
3The Syrian Observatory for Human Rights monitoring group said the militants had taken up positions outside the town, which is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage site, after capturing al-Sukhna, 75 kilometres to the northeast, on Wednesday.
4An army source said fighting continued in al-Sukhna for a second day on Thursday and troops were still in their positions.
5The Syrian military and allied Lebanese militants have been pushed back in the northwest and south recently, but this week cleared insurgents from mountains near the Syria-Lebanon border north of Damascus in a notable advance.
6The Observatory reported heavy casualties on both sides in fighting near al-Sukhna on Wednesday, with at least 70 troops and 40 Islamic State fighters killed.
7It would be the first town taken by Islamic State in direct fighting with government forces.
8Others seized by the group in the north and east were taken from other insurgents.
9Islamic State has in recent months mounted frequent attacks on government- and rebel-held areas to the west of its strongholds in Deir al-Zor and Raqqa provinces, where its fighters have been facing air strikes by a U.S.-led coalition.
10Tadmur, which has previously been a frontline in the four-year-long Syrian conflict, was included in UNESCO's list of World Heritage in danger in 2013, according to the UNESCO web site.
11It contains the monumental ruins of a city that was one of the most important cultural centres of the ancient world.
12The ultra-radical Islamist group has systematically destroyed antiquities in Iraqi territory it seized last year.
13The military source said Syrian forces had inflicted heavy losses on what it said were many Islamic State fighters targeting al-Sukhna.
14"They are attacking in large numbers like cattle with heads facing the ground," the source said.
15The army had yet to confirm losses in its ranks, the source added.
16Syrian state media said Islamic State fighters were being hit by Syrian army air strikes, adding that an Islamic State convoy had been destroyed to the east of al-Sukhna.
17Since March, the Syrian army and allied militia have lost control of wide areas of the northwestern province of Idlib, and also a crossing at the border with Jordan.
