Coney Island Cyclone Roller Coaster Gets Stuck On Opening Day

1The Cyclone, the famed wooden roller coaster at Coney Island in Brooklyn, New York, got stuck on its first operating day of the season on Sunday.
2The coaster at Luna Park got stuck near the top of its first big hill, and about a dozen riders were escorted off by foot and walked down along the tracks.
3"Eric Adams, the borough president came down and christened the ride, the dignitaries took their ride, everything went fine and then us general public went for a nice ride, and we had a little belt slippage, and they shut it down," Eric Napp, who is known as "Mr. Cyclone" and has been on the coaster more than 1,000 times, told ABC 7.
4"The locks were in place, so the ride didn't slide back down, and then staff came up and safely escorted us off the ride."
5But not everyone took the evacuation in stride.
6"It was terrifying, because I was up there and everything was spinning," Gabriella Centeno told NBC New York.
7"I didn't know what to do."
8"That thing was the scariest thing I ever did," David Zubin told amNewYork.
9"The bricks, walking down, looked like it was going to break when you stepped on it."
10Luna Park, which operates the ride, posted on Facebook that no one was injured.
11"This was an isolated issue and it is quickly being resolved," the statement said.
12"Those on the ride were safely evacuated according to our safety training procedures and will be able to ride again for free."
13The ride is expected to reopen on Saturday, the next day the park is open, amNewYork reported.
