16A strong magnitude earthquake shook Nepal on Saturday devastating the region and leaving some thousands shell-shocked and displaced.
17Most of the people who were sleeping outside their homes for fear of aftershocks have moved back indoors.
18There were only a few dozen tents left at the Tudikhel grounds in the heart of Kathmandu, with less than a thousand people still there.
19The area used to be crowded with more than 30,000 people in the first days after the quake hit.
20Almost all the gas stations are open Friday in Kathmandu and there are no more lines.
21The popular Hermann's bakery at Lalitpur, a Kathmandu suburb, is selling fresh bread and croissants, which were quickly snapped up.
22Krishna Maharjan, who brought green onions and cauliflower from his farm on a bicycle, says he and his fellow farmers on the outskirts of Kathmandu are now delivering produce to the city.
23"We are trying to get as much fresh food to the people as possible. I feel it is our small contribution, but that's what we can do and every little bit helps," he says.
24Farmers who miss the planting season that is expected to start late May onwards will be unable to harvest rice - the country's staple food - again until late 2016.
25The U.N. Food and Agriculture Organization says this, together with likely losses of food stocks and wheat and maize harvests, would severely limit food supplies and incomes in the South Asian country, where around two-thirds of people rely on agriculture for their livelihood.
26The U.N. humanitarian chief, Valerie Amos, is in Nepal for a three-day visit to meet victims and local leaders.
27She plans to visit areas outside the Kathmandu Valley, according to the deputy spokesman for the U.N. secretary-general, Farhan Haq.
28The U.N. humanitarian office says about 24,000 people are living in 13 camps in Kathmandu.
29More than 130,000 houses are reportedly destroyed.
30The main challenges are the "inaccessibility of some remote areas, the lack of helicopters, poor communication and security concerns."
31Police say a woman in her 20s has been rescued five days after Nepal's massive earthquake.
32A police official identified the woman as Krishna Devi Khadka and said she was rescued Thursday evening in an area near Kathmandu's main bus terminal.
33The official asked not to be identified because he wasn't authorized to speak to the media.
34Earlier Thursday, a 15-year-old boy, Pemba Tamang, was pulled, dazed and dusty, from the wreckage of a seven-story building elsewhere in Kathmandu.
35An Indian seismologist says more than 70 aftershocks have been recorded in the Himalayan region since Saturday's earthquake.
36J.L. Gautam says the 73 aftershocks recorded as of Thursday morning by India's monitoring stations ranged from magnitude 3.2 to 6.9, the strongest occurring Sunday.
37Gautam is the director of seismology at the Indian Meteorological Department in New Delhi.
38India monitors seismic activity from stations in the Himalayan foothills in northern India in the towns of Gangtok, Dehradun and Shimla and others in Shillong and Guwahati in northeastern India.
39Gautam said the aftershocks will continue for "a few weeks, or months, or even years."
40He says, "These aftershocks are quite normal after a powerful earthquake of such magnitude."
41France has announced the death of a third French citizen in the quake.
42The first government flight carrying French nationals from Nepal arrived in Paris overnight.
43According to EU officials, some 9,000 EU citizens are still in Nepal following Saturday's earthquake.
44More than 1,000 of them have not been reached yet.
45The official death toll in Nepal has reached 5,489.
46It does not include the 19 people killed at Mount Everest - five foreign climbers and 14 Nepalese Sherpa guides - when the quake caused an avalanche at base camp.
47Relief aid group Oxfam says congestion at Kathmandu airport, blocked roads, fuel shortages and difficult terrain are slowing down the pace of aid delivery.
48The group says it is looking at ways to transport essential goods overland from India.

52Over 70,000 homes are believed to be destroyed and another 530,000 damaged in 39 out of the country's 75 districts.

81In quake-wracked Nepal, everyone who is able is joining the aid effort.
82The 32-year-oid Narayan Thapa was working at a tire factory in Gorkha town when the earthquake destroyed his village of Bungkot, around 45 kilometers (28 miles) away.

91"I want to get at least a night of peace," she said.
