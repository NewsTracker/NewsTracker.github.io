Kenya attack survivor says gunmen had scouted the campus

1GARISSA, Kenya: The Islamic extremists who slaughtered 147 people at a college in Kenya as they shouted "God is great" appeared to have planned extensively, even targeting a site where Christians had gone to pray, survivors said on Friday.
2Police on Friday were at the campus of Garissa University College, taking fingerprints from the bodies of the four assailants and of the students and security officials who died, for thorough identification purposes.
3The northeastern Kenyan town lacks the facilities to store all the bodies.
4In Nairobi, Kenya's capital, family members were lining up at a morgue where about 20 bodies had already been airlifted from Garissa, victims of the worst attack in Kenya since the 1998 bombing of the U.S. Embassy by al-Qaida which killed more than 200 people.
5Screaming and crying relatives of the victims were assisted by Kenyan Red Cross staffers, who tried to console them.
6One of the first things that the al-Shabaab gunmen did when they assaulted the campus early Thursday, survivor Helen Titus said, was to head for a lecture hall where Christians were in early morning prayer.
7Al-Shabaab is a Somalia-based extremist group with ties to al-Qaida.
8"They investigated our area. They knew everything," Helen Titus told The Associated Press at a hospital in Garissa where she was being treated for a bullet wound to the wrist.
9Officials said 79 people were wounded.
10Titus, a 21-year-old English literature student, said she covered her face and hair with the blood of classmates and lay still at one point during al-Shabaab's deadliest attack on Kenyan soil in hopes the Islamic extremist gunmen would think she was dead.
11The gunmen also told students hiding in dormitories to come out, assuring them that they would not be killed, said Titus, who wore a patient's gown as she sat on a bench in the hospital yard.
12"We just wondered whether to come out or not," she said.
13Many students did, whereupon the gunmen started shooting men, saying they would not kill "ladies," Titus said.
14But they also shot women and targeted Christians, said Titus, who is a Christian.
15Esther Wanjiru said she was awake at the time of the attack.
16Asked if she lost anyone, she said: "My best friend."
17Another survivor, Nina Kozel, said she was woken up by screaming and that many students escaped by sprinting to the fences and jumping over them.
18Some suffered bruises, she said.
19Many men were unable to escape, and hid in vain under beds and in closets in their rooms, according to Kozel.
20"They were shot there and then," she said.
21Those who surrendered were either selected for killing, or freed in some cases, apparently because they were Muslim, she said.
22The killers shouted "God is great" in Arabic as they proceeded with the slaughter, she said.
23Security forces stood guard Friday at the gate of the school.
24School slogans on the wall outside said "Oasis of Innovation" and "A World Class University of Technological Processes and Development."
25At one point, a group approached the college gate and was blocked by soldiers.
26Several women began shrieking and collapsed in apparent grief in the dust for several minutes.
27A bystander said the son of one of the women had died in the attack.
28A small group of male demonstrators walked down a main road in Garissa with signs that read "We are against the killing of innocent Kenyans!!!! We are tired!!" and "Enough is enough. No more killing!! We are with you, our fellow Kenyans."
29"We feel very sorry for them and we condemn the attack," demonstrator Abdullahi Muktar said of the victims.
30Some surviving students awaited evacuation to Nairobi by plane from a nearby airstrip.
31The masked attackers - strapped with explosives and armed with AK-47s - singled out non-Muslim students and then gunned them down without mercy, survivors said.
32The gunmen took dozens of hostages in a dormitory as they battled troops and police before the operation ended after about 13 hours, witnesses said.
33Al-Shabaab spokesman Ali Mohamud Rage said fighters the group was responsible for the attack.
34The al-Qaida-linked group has been blamed for a series of attacks in Kenya, including the siege at the Westgate Mall in Nairobi in 2013 that killed 67 people, as well as other violence in the north.
35The group has vowed to retaliate against Kenya for sending troops to Somalia in 2011 to fight the militants staging cross-border attacks and kidnappings.
36Somali President Said Hassan Sheikh Mohamud called for stronger collaboration between Somalia and Kenya to defeat al-Shabaab.
