Hawks' Thabo Sefolosha hurt in arrest, to miss playoffs (Yahoo Sports)

1ATLANTA (AP) -- Atlanta forward Thabo Sefolosha will miss the Hawks' final four regular-season games and the postseason because of a fractured right fibula and ligament damage suffered when he and teammate Pero Antic were arrested early Wednesday in New York.
2The Hawks confirmed Sefolosha's injury Thursday and said he will need surgery.
3The Swiss player was limping following the arrest.
4''This is a very difficult situation for Thabo and our thoughts and support will be with him during his recovery,'' Hawks coach Mike Budenholzer said in a statement.
5''We know that his approach and dedication will serve him well in his rehabilitation.
6Sefolosha's attorney, Alex Spiro, said Wednesday the forward was injured during the arrest, which followed the stabbings of Indiana Pacers forward Chris Copeland, his girlfriend and another woman on the street near a Manhattan nightclub.
7Sefolosha and Antic were released without bail after they were charged with obstructing governmental authority and disorderly conduct.
8Spiro said the two did not commit a crime and he expects the charges to be dismissed.
9The Hawks held out Sefolosha and Antic in Wednesday night's 114-111 win at the Brooklyn Nets and haven't said when Antic will return.
10''We will continue to look into the facts of the incident but with the information and foundation that we currently have established, we will support them through this process,'' Budenholzer said in a statement.
11In the joint statement, the players said they would contest the charges.
12''As members of the Atlanta Hawks, we hold ourselves to a high standard and take our roles as professionals very seriously,'' the statement said.
13The Hawks said Sefolosha's injuries were disclosed in examinations that included an X-ray at Barclays Center on Wednesday and an MRI in Atlanta on Thursday.
14In 52 games, including seven starts, the 6-foot-7 Sefolosha has averaged 5.3 points and 4.3 rebounds.
15He is especially valuable in Budenholzer's system for his defense and size on the perimeter.
16The Hawks have ample experience without the ninth-year veteran.
17Sefolosha returned on March 25 after missing 23 games with a right calf injury.
18The Hawks (59-19) have clinched the No.
191 seed in the Eastern Conference playoffs and will play Charlotte on Friday night.
