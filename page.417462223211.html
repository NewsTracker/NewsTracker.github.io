Greek uncertainty continues to weigh on global markets

1KEEPING SCORE: In Europe, France's CAC 40 dropped 0.1 percent to 5,020 while Germany's DAX shed 0.7 percent to 11,356.
2Britain's FTSE 100 fell 0.5 percent to 6,921.
3U.S. stocks were set for modest losses at the open with Dow futures and the broader S&P 500 futures down 0.1 percent.
4GREEK IN FOCUS: A day after an emergency mini-summit of Greece's international creditors, the country submitted a proposal it hopes will help break the logjam and secure a deal that will unlock desperately needed rescue money.
5Greek Prime Minister Alexis Tspiras said it was now up to the leaders of Europe, who are Greece's main creditors, to accept a deal or risk potentially disastrous consequences for the region.
6For four months, Greece and its creditors have been locked in a standoff over what reforms the country needs to make to get more loans.
7Tsipras gave no specifics of what was included in the proposal included, which was submitted Monday night to the European Central Bank, International Monetary Fund and European Commission, the EU's executive.
8ANALYST TAKE: "There are some small signs of progress being made although that is doing little to settle the markets, with most European equity indices in the red," said Craig Erlam, market analyst at OANDA.
9EUROZONE INFLATION: While most of the focus has been on developments surrounding Greece, there was some interest in the news that consumer prices in the 19-country eurozone are rising for the first time since last November.
10Official figures Tuesday from the European Union's statistics agency, Eurostat, showed that consumer prices across the single currency bloc were up 0.3 percent in May from the year before.
11That's up from the flat reading in April and is the first positive reading since November.
12It's also above market expectations for a more modest 0.2 percent rise and suggests the eurozone is past its bout of falling prices, which can become a long-term threat to growth.
13The inflation figures helped shore up the euro, which rose 1.2 percent to $1.1050.
14US PAYROLLS LOOM: Another big focus this week will be the state of the U.S. economy.
15A slew of data ends Friday with May payroll figures.
16They can set the market tone for a week or two after their release, especially at a time when investors are trying to work out when the Federal Reserve will start raising interest rates.
17Recent figures have been mixed.
18ASIA SCORECARD: Japan's Nikkei 225 closed down 0.1 percent at 20,543.19 while China's Shanghai Composite rose 1.1 percent to 4,910.53..
19Hong Kong's Hang Seng shed 0.5 percent to 27,466.72.
20Australia's S&P/ASX 200 dropped 1.7 percent to 5,636.00 ahead of a central bank interest rate decision.
21Seoul's Kospi sank 1.1 percent to 2,078.64.
