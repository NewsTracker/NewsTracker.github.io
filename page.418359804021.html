China Leads Dive in Asia Stocks as Greece Rattles Investors

1Chinese stocks led a slump in Asian markets on Wednesday as the sell-off in Shanghai intensified and Greece's future in the euro remained highly uncertain.
2KEEPING SCORE: China's Shanghai Composite Index tumbled 4.7 percent to 3,553.47 and Hong Kong's Hang Seng dived 4.3 percent to 23,907.41.
3Japan's Nikkei 225 lost 1.5 percent to 20,067.59 as the yen strengthened on safe haven buying.
4South Korea's Kospi was down 1.3 percent at 2,014.22.
5Other regional markets were also down, including Taiwan, Singapore, Australia and the Philippines.
6CHINA SELL-OFF: A slew of official measures to provide support to Chinese stock markets have failed to halt a recent sell-off.
7The Shanghai benchmark is down more than 30 percent since peaking June 12 after a sizzling yearlong rally.
8Millions of novice investors piled into the market when it was at its most frothy.
9Some made big profits but the slump has left many with shares worth less than they paid and hoping for a rebound so they can sell.
10ANALYST TAKE: "There is clearly a lot of work to be done and the latest developments only seek to dent investor confidence," Bernard Aw, market strategist at IG in Singapore, said of the Chinese situation.
11"I feel that trading halts are a stop-gap measure, and the authorities will need to come up with stronger initiatives to restore confidence."
12GREECE CRISIS: Frustrated European leaders fearing for the future of their common currency gave the Greek prime minister a last-minute chance Tuesday to finally come up with a viable proposal on how to save his country from financial ruin.
13Overcoming their surprise when Alexis Tsipras failed to present them with a detailed reform blueprint, the leaders reluctantly agreed to a final summit on Sunday.
14Indebted Greece needs a financial lifeline from its European creditors who have been pressing for more austerity in exchange for bailout funds.
15Without that it's expected to default, paving the way for its exit from the euro currency.
16WALL STREET: U.S. shares rose moderately on domestic factors.
17The Standard & Poor's 500 rose 12.58 points, or 0.6 percent, to 2,081.34.
18The Dow Jones industrial average climbed 93.33 points, or 0.5 percent, to 17,776.91.
19The Nasdaq composite inched up 5.52 points, or 0.1 percent, to 4,997.46.
20ENERGY: Benchmark U.S. crude was down 24 cents to $52.09 a barrel in electronic trading on the New York Mercantile Exchange.
21The contract fell 20 cents to close at $52.33 in New York on Tuesday.
22Brent crude, the international benchmark, slipped 35 cents to $56.50 a barrel in London.
23CURRENCIES: The euro inched down to $1.0987 from $1.1040.
24The dollar fell to 122.18 yen from 122.68.
