US Open singles champs to earn record $3.3 million (Yahoo Sports)

1WHITE PLAINS, N.Y. (AP) -- This year's U.S. Open men's and women's singles champions will each earn a record $3.3 million, up 10 percent from 2014.
2The U.S. Tennis Association announced Tuesday the total tournament purse will be $42.3 million, a 10.5 percent increase from a year ago.
3The payout for each round in singles is rising by at least 10 percent.
4The runners-up will receive $1.6 million while a player who loses in the first round will make $39,500.
5The winning doubles teams get $570,000.
6The year's last Grand Slam tournament starts Aug. 31 at Flushing Meadows.
7The singles champions won about $2.5 million at this year's Australian Open, $2 million at the French Open and $2.9 million at Wimbledon.
