Ranchers sue over rule giving feds authority on state water

1Ranchers in New Mexico, California and Washington state are challenging a new Obama administration rule giving federal agencies authority to protect some streams and wetlands.
2The Sacramento, California-based Pacific Legal Foundation announced this week it has filed a federal lawsuit in U.S. District Court in Minnesota on behalf of the ranchers over a recent expansion of the Clean Water Act.
3The rule is a response to calls from the U.S. Supreme Court and Congress for the Environmental Protection Agency to clarify which smaller waterways are protected under the law.
4But the foundation argues the rule is unconstitutional because it sets no limit on the Clean Water Act's reach.
5EPA spokeswoman Monica Lee declined to comment on the lawsuit.
6In June, 13 states led by North Dakota filed a lawsuit over the same rule change.
