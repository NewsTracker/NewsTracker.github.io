13Strangers from two Greek Orthodox churches in the city's Queens borough came to the rescue, giving them survival cash until their flight home to Greece on Friday.
14The couple's U.S. adventure started after their June 6 wedding in Volos, Greece, a port city several hours north of Athens.
15Their coast-to-coast U.S. trip that took in Los Angeles and a Caribbean cruise "was the dream trip of our lives," Limnioti said.
16They had saved for a whole year to pre-pay for flights and hotels, with enough cash left for both necessities and pleasures.
17Two Greek banks issued them cards before the trip - a Visa credit card and a debit card.
18"Everything was all right - then 'boom!' in New York," Limnioti said.
19Their midtown Manhattan hotel asked them to pay a $45 surcharge.
20That's when their cards bounced.
21They paid with their dwindling funds.
22Within days, the couple ran out of cash and "we couldn't withdraw any money - zero," Limnioti said.
23On Tuesday, in despair, they reached out to the New York-based Greek Orthodox Archdiocese of America, which contacted the churches in Queens' Astoria neighborhood.
24The honeymooners were offered about $350 from the St. Demetrios Greek Orthodox church and another nearby one, St. Irene Chrysovalantou.
25"I said to them, 'Don't worry, that's why we're here,' " said the Rev.
26Vasilios Louros of St. Demetrios.
27"This is the church of Christ and we always help people."
28The money was withdrawn from the church's bank account, "and that was it," he said.
29In addition, an undisclosed amount came from a New York-based Greek journalist who hails from Volos.
30The couple insisted they'd pay back the money but were told it was a gift, said Limnioti, speaking on her cellphone Wednesday from the American Museum of Natural History.
31She said relatives in Greece told them other Greeks abroad also were left penniless, including some patients in U.S. hospitals who cannot pay for medical care.
32She said she's speaking out "because we Greeks are a proud people, and I want the world to know that we are not in this situation because we're lazy or did something wrong."
33Their financial woes won't be over once they get home.
34With banks closed, Greece faces a deep financial crisis.
35Greeks will vote Sunday in a referendum on whether to back more spending cuts, more tax increases and more negotiations with European creditors.
36A rejection of such draconian measures could trigger a Greek exit from the Eurozone.
37Limnioti, 36, is unemployed after the small business for which she worked failed.
38Her 39-year-old husband still has his job as a helicopter engineer for the Greek military.
39But in every sense, the couple's honeymoon is over, with a financial sword of Damocles looming over their country.
40"There are only three things saving us now: our families, our friends and our God," Limnioti said.
