Icelandic film 'Rams' wins Cannes Un Certain Regard prize

1CANNES, France (AP) - "Rams," a drama set among farmers and their sheep in a remote Icelandic valley, won the Cannes Film Festival's Un Certain Regard competition on Saturday.
2Jury president Isabella Rossellini said Grimur Hakonarson's film was being honored for "treating in a masterful, tragicomic way the undeniable bond that links all humans to animals."
3There were 19 films in the Un Certain Regard competition, which honors new directors and more offbeat films than those up for Cannes' main Palme d'Or prize.
4The second-place Jury Prize went to Croatian director Dalibor Matanic for "Zvizdan" ("The High Sun"), which explores love and ethnic hatred in the Balkans.
5Matanic dedicated the prize to his producer, his wife and his soon-to-be-born daughter.
6The jury bestowed the directing prize on Kiyoshi Kurosawa for "Journey to the Shore," and also gave awards to "Treasure," by Romania's Corneliu Porumboiu, "Nahid" by Iranian director Ida Panahandeh and "Masaan" by India's Neeraj Ghaywan.
7Actress-director Rossellini said serving on the jury had been "like taking a flight over the planet and seeing all its inhabitants and their emotions."
