Bus runs over landmine in Ukraine, four dead

1ARTEMIVSK, Ukraine, March 25 (UPI) -- Four people died Wednesday near the Ukrainian town of Artemivsk after a passenger bus they were riding on ran over a landmine while allegedly evading a government checkpoint.
2Many regions in contested areas of Ukraine are littered with mines, interior ministry official Vyacheslav Abroskin said.
3The bus driver is accused of attempting to skirt checkpoint guards by leaving the main road and driving through what turned out to be a minefield.
4One of the vehicle's rear wheels struck the incendiary anti-personnel device.
5At least four people aboard the bus died, officials said, and nearly a dozen others were injured.
6The Kuwait News Agency said the bus had been carrying more than two dozen passengers.
7Photos of the torn bus were posted by Denmark's DW news service.
8A Ukrainian government website posted photos of the damaged bus on its Twitter channel Wednesday.
9Artemivsk is a government-controlled town in Ukraine's Donetsk region, which has been a combat zone for months as Czech government troops battle pro-Russia rebels there.
10More than 6,000 people have been killed in fighting so far.
11A ceasefire, reached last month, has largely eliminated ongoing arms battles but landmines remain to be a deadly hidden danger throughout the area.
12The United States and NATO have accused the Russian government of assisting the rebels financially and logistically, but Moscow has maintained that pro-Russia separatists are unilaterally acting on their own.
13A year ago, the Russian government heightened tensions in the region when it annexed Ukraine's southern Crimea peninsula -- an act that was widely condemned worldwide.
14Rebels seeking unification with Russia have continued the fight since.
