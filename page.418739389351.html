Jamaica stuns U.S. in Gold Cup soccer

1ATLANTA, GA - JULY 22: Darren Mattocks #11 of Jamaica celebrates scoring the opening goal against the United States of America with Michael Hector #3 during the 2015 CONCACAF Golf Cup Semifinal match between Jamaica and the United States at Georgia Dome on July 22, 2015 in Atlanta, Georgia.
2ATLANTA -- The United States had everything on its side.
3History. A raucous home crowd.
4And, supposedly, the better team.
5None of it prevented Jamaica from handing the Americans their biggest upset defeat.
6The Reggae Boyz stunned the U.S. team with a pair of first-half goals, one off a blunder by goalkeeper Brad Guzan, and held on for a 2-1 victory in the CONCACAF Gold Cup semifinals Wednesday night.
7Instead of heading on to an expected berth in Sunday's title game at Philadelphia, the Americans will play a day earlier for third place following their first home loss to a Caribbean nation since a 1969 defeat to Haiti.
8"Obviously the team is disappointed. The fans are disappointed," U.S. coach Jurgen Klinsmann said.
9"We wanted to play in the big final Sunday."
10The ball gets past United States goalkeeper Brad Guzan for a Jamaica goal during the first half of a CONCACAF Gold Cup soccer semifinal.
11Wednesday, July 22, 2015, in Atlanta.
12Darren Mattocks, who plays for the Vancouver Whitecaps in Major League Soccer, put Jamaica ahead with a 31st-minute header directly off a throw-in.
13The Houston Dynamo's Giles Barnes followed five minutes later with a goal on an 18-yard free kick after Guzan was caught outside the penalty area on a routine throw.
14Other than those two set plays, the Americans largely dominated.
15They finished with a 10-3 edge in shots on goal -- including eight in the second half, as they furiously charged at Jamaican goalkeeper Ryan Thompson, who plays for the Pittsburgh Riverhounds in the third-tier United Soccer League.
16Thompson was up to the task, turning aside every shot but Michael Bradley's goal in the 48th minute.
17"My phone hasn't stopped, as you can imagine," said Barnes, savoring one of his country's greatest victories.
18"Back in Jamaica, there's got to be a party going on. Everybody knows how we are."
19Jamaica, ranked 76th in the world, became the first Caribbean nation to reach a Gold Cup final and will face Mexico, a 2-1 winner over Panama in the wild second semifinal.
20The Jamaicans want more, said their German coach, Winfried Schaefer.
21"We have one more match to play," he said.
22"Bob Marley is for after the match."
23The 34th-ranked Americans, who had played in five straight Gold Cup finals and were the defending champion, will face Panama.
24They also must meet the Gold Cup winner in a playoff for the North and Central American and Caribbean berth in the 2017 Confederations Cup.
25Andres Guardado scored two goals on penalty kicks, including the tiebreaker in extra time, and six-time champion Mexico beat short-handed Panama.
26Panama's Roman Torres scored on a header early in the second half, and the 1-0 lead held until Guardado's penalty kick in stoppage time.
27Panama played at a disadvantage after forward Luis Tejada drew a red card and was ejected for his contact with Mexico's Francisco Rodriguez about 25 minutes into the match.
28Howard plans return: Goalkeeper Tim Howard is ready to make himself available for the U.S. national team again after taking a break since the 2014 World Cup.
29The 36-year-old Howard, who plays for Everton in the English Premier League, was allowed a sabbatical from international soccer in August last year.
