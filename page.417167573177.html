Gaspar Noe's explicit 'Love' sets Cannes atwitter

1CANNES, France (AP) - The movies are in love with love, but they're often squeamish about sex.
2That's not something that can be said about director Gaspar Noe, whose new film "Love" brings graphic sex - in myriad positions and permutations, in twosomes, threesomes and groups - to the Cannes Film Festival.
3A depiction of turbulent youthful relationships, it's extremely explicit - and it's in 3-D.
4"Love" is full frontal, in-your-face filmmaking.
5"I want to make movies out of blood, sperm and tears," said the central male character, an American film student in Paris.
6That could be Noe's motto, too.
7The director wants to smash the convention that keeps graphic eroticism out of mainstream cinema.
8"I was making a film about love," Noe told reporters in Cannes Thursday.
9"It wasn't a film about Swiss banks or Scientology."
10"What everyone has in common is their love of making love," he said.
11"Love" stars American actor Karl Glusman and European performers Aomi Muyock and Klara Kristin as the members of a turbulent love triangle in Paris.
12Noe has a cameo as a wig-wearing art gallery owner.
13The actors said they were initially uncomfortable with the film's all-baring intimacy, though they came to embrace it.
14"The first day shooting, Gaspar decided to start us off with a close-up of my genitals," Glusman said.
15"I was in the bathroom beforehand looking in the mirror, thinking I should escape to the airport and run back to the United States."
16Glusman said he was proud of the finished movie - and so was his mother, "especially because the film is playing in such a prestigious festival."
17Noe, an Argentina-born French director, has a history of cinematic provocations.
18His 2002 film "Irreversible" contained a scene so violent that some audience members bolted for the exits.
19"Enter the Void," which played at Cannes in 2009, was a hallucinatory drug odyssey that left some entranced and others baffled.
20"Love" offers an eye-catching mix of sex and 3-D, a technology Noe says he used because it was a new challenge.
21"There is something childish about 3-D," Noe said.
22"It's like a game. ... I thought what's the next game I've never played that might be fun?"
23"Love" was - unsurprisingly - one of the most anticipated films at Cannes, where it is playing as an out-of-competition entry.
24Lines for screenings stretched around the block, and scores of journalists were turned away from a packed press viewing.
25The Hollywood Reporter said that if you strip out the sex, the film is "a wistful, some might say sappy story about heartbreak, made with impressive cinematic elan but somewhat shallow emotional depth."
26Whatever the critics say, sex will sell "Love" - and also limit its commercial prospects.
27The film should be screened uncut in much of Europe, where audiences and censors are tolerant of explicit, nonviolent sex scenes.
28But it is likely to fall foul of film censors in many countries and will probably have a limited release in the United States.
29"The Americans, when it comes to film distribution, they can be very square," Noe said.
