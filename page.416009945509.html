Ronaldo scores 5 in Madrid's 9-1 rout of Granada

1Ronaldo followed Gareth Bale's opener by netting his first three goals in a stunning eight-minute span to decide the match before halftime at the Santiago Bernabeu.
2The Ballon d'Or holder added two more goals after the break as Karim Benzema also netted a pair in one of the club's most lopsided wins.
3Madrid hadn't scored nine goals in a league match in 47 years.
4Its biggest league victory remains an 11-2 thrashing of Elche in 1960.
5"Cristiano is a phenomenon," Benzema said.
6"He is always on the lookout for goals and ways of helping the team."
7Madrid and the Spanish league said this was the first time Ronaldo had scored five goals in a match and it came after a two-month stretch when the Portugal star was merely extremely good after his spectacular year in 2014.
8That recent dip in form had even earned him unexpected criticism as Lionel Messi took over as the league's most in-form scorer.
9Usually protective of his players, Madrid coach Carlo Ancelotti said on Saturday that Ronaldo "had lowered his productivity" in the past two months as Madrid lost its league lead to Barcelona.
10Ronaldo responded in emphatic fashion by netting his 43rd, 44th, 45th, 46th and 47th goals in his 41st game of the season in all competitions.
11His performance left his coach almost as awe-struck as Granada's helpless defenders.
12"What more can be said?"
13Ancelotti said.
14"(Ronaldo) has improved just as the team has. This is the first time he has scored five goals in a game and that is good news for everyone, for him, for the team and in terms of the upcoming games."
15Ronaldo's outburst moved him ahead of Messi in their individual duel to lead the scoring this season with 36 goals to the Barcelona forward's 32.
16Ronaldo's 28th game with three or more goals for Madrid increased his career tally with the club to 300 goals in 287 games.
17Only Alfredo Di Stefano with 308 goals in 396 games and Raul Gonzalez with 323 in 741 have more goals in Madrid's history.
18Madrid's win came one round after losing at Barcelona and pulled it to within one point of the pacesetter before it visits Celta Vigo later.
19Elsewhere, Valencia remained in fourth place and Villarreal in sixth after they finished 0-0 at Mestalla Stadium.
20The stalemate means defending champion Atletico Madrid is third after its win at Cordoba on Saturday.
21Getafe also beat Deportivo La Coruna 2-1 to edge away from the relegation zone.
22Granada goalkeeper Oier Olazabal's long day began in the 25th minute when Bale won Toni Kroos' throughball and was all alone to dribble around Olazabal and poke the ball home.
23Five minutes later James Rodriguez marked his first game in two months after recovering from a broken right foot with an exquisite pass to set up Ronaldo for his first goal.
24Olazabal was partly at fault for Ronaldo's next two goals.
25Ronaldo got his second when he blasted in a short clearance by Olazabal in the 36th before he drove a long strike through Olazabal's hands in the 38th for his hat trick.
26Ronaldo's treble was not the fastest of the season.
27Rayo Vallecano's Alberto Bueno notched three goals in six minutes before scoring a fourth in a 4-2 win over Levante in February.
28Benzema got into the act with a goal in the 52nd.
29Ronaldo headed in Bale's pass two minutes later, and Benzema's deflected shot found the net in the 56th.
30Robert Ibanez scored for Granada in the 74th.
31But Diego Mainz scored an own goal with seven minutes left before Ronaldo got his final goal against Granada's demoralized defense in the 90th.
32"This is the biggest blow I have ever had, either as a player or a coach," said Granada's veteran manager Abel Resino, whose team was left in danger of the drop.
