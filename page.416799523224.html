Philippine volcano ejects ash, but no violent eruption

1MANILA, Philippines (AP) - A Philippine volcano has spewed ash for the second time in less than a week but there were no signs of any eminent violent eruption, the country's chief volcano expert said Thursday.
2Mount Bulusan in Sorsogon province southeast of Manila ejected ash 250 meters (825 feet) above the 1,565-meter (5,165-foot) volcano late Wednesday, said Renato Solidum, director of the Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology.
3The agency asked local officials to keep people away from a 4-kilometer (2.5-mile) hazardous zone around the crater, he said.
4He said aircraft should not fly close to the mountain to avoid any sudden explosions and ash clouds.
5Solidum said the explosion was due to ground water coming into contact with hot rocks and not rising magma.
6He says such "phreatic" explosions are typical of Bulusan.
7The ash explosion lasted about 3-1/2 minutes, shorter than the 5-minute explosion recorded May 1.
8A large explosion in February 2011 that shot an ash plume about 2 kilometers (more than a mile) into the sky prompted about 1,200 villagers to flee to shelters.
9Bulusan is one 23 volcanoes considered active by the volcanology agency, but it has not had a violent eruption since in 1918.
