'He won't take this lying down': Condemned drug smuggler Myuran Sukumaran vows to stare down firing squad... as Bali Nine duo choose execution witnesses

1Condemned Bali Nine drug smuggler Myuran Sukumaran has pledged to stare down the Indonesian firing squad who will drag him to a jungle clearing and kill him shortly after midnight Wednesday.
2That's according close supporter and artist Ben Quilty, who has taken to the airwaves in a desperate final bid to stop Sukumaran and fellow Australian inmate Andrew Chan's execution.
3'Myuran always said to me he would never take this lying down,' Mr Quilty told Radio 2GB.
4As a 'realistic' Mr Quilty pledged to fight the death penalty 'with everything I have for the rest of my life', the Australian duo selected the spiritual advisers who will accompany them to their execution.
5Fairfax Media reported Sukumaran has nominated his friend Christie Buckingham, a senior pastor at Melbourne's Bayside Church.
6Meanwhile, Chan has asked Salvation Army minister David Soper - a family friend - to accompany him to the clearing known as Death Valley.
7The Chan and Sukumaran families took a boat from Cilacap to the island of Nusakambangan on Monday morning for some of their last hours with their loved ones.
8They initially left the Cilicap port followed by Australia's consul-general to Bali, Majell Hind, and lawyer Julian McMahon after it was believed they were told the visitation rules had changed.
9The families have now received notice they will be required to make their final goodbyes on Tuesday afternoon, around 2pm local time.
10The Australians will be executed just after the stroke of midnight local time on Wednesday (3am AEST).
11Chan and Sukumaran both refused to sign their execution warrants when they were given 72 hours notice of their deaths during official proceedings on Saturday.
12The pair said they believed it would be unjust to kill them.
13Mr McMahon told Radio 3AW the pair were 'magnificently' calm when they were told of their executions.
14Chan has requested fresh air and to attend a church service with his family prior to the end.
15As for Sukumaran, he has asked for as much time to paint as possible.
16Mr Quilty said he was confident 'Myu' will face death with 'strength and dignity'.
17'But he has never been able to forgive himself for what he put his mum through, and his little sister, and his little brother.
18'He will face it with strength and dignity.
19I know that about him.
20Because he won't want his mother to think he's a weeping mess at the end.
21'He'll want his mum to think he's brave and strong and dignified.
22Bribery claims around the death sentences of Myuran Sukumaran and Andrew Chan must be investigated urgently as the pair await their execution within days, their lawyer is arguing.
23Muhammad Rifan, the lawyer who represented the Bali Nine pair when they were sentenced to death in 2006, has told Fairfax Media judges asked for more than $130,000 for a lighter sentence.
24Mr Rifan raised the claims months ago - without putting a sum on the bribe - and the men's current lawyer, Todung Mulya Lubis, has already lodged a complaint with the judicial commission, which appears to have stalled.
25Mr Lubis says the commission has an obligation to finish its probe and it's not too late to stop the firing squad, which is set for Tuesday.
26'We appeal to the attorney-general, we appeal to the president in the name of due process of law, in the name of fairness and justice, not to do the executions,' he said.
27Mr Lubis says claims the men face execution because of a corrupt system must be urgently investigated.
28'I know that we are counting the hours,' Mr Lubis told reporters at Cilacap before visiting the Australians.
