Chaos at Kathmandu airport as Indians fight to leave Nepal after earthquake

1Chaos broke out this afternoon at an airport in Kathmandu as hundreds of Indian nationals fought to return home to safety following the devastating earthquake in Nepal.
2As rescue efforts continued throughout the Nepalese capital, more than 1,000 passengers joined a haphazard 1km-long queue outside the nearby airport in a desperate attempt to flee the stricken city.
3Despite plummeting temperatures and rain, crowds had started to form outside Tribhuwan International Airport as darkness fell on Saturday night.
4Even more passengers flocked to the airport this morning, including families huddled together beneath blankets.
5Many visitors had already booked return journeys but had crowded outside the airport in the hope of boarding the next available flight back to India.
6Others had been left stranded after some flights out of the capital city were cancelled.
7As the departure lounges grew busier, security guards were unable to control the sheer number of passengers vying for a coveted place in the queue.
8The airport's parking lot was converted into a makeshift waiting area to accommodate travellers.
9Mohammad Pervez, one of the passengers outside the airport, told NDTV: 'There is no water here or food.
10I have been sitting with my wife and child since early morning.
11Lu Hee, who was playing cards with friends in the waiting area, said: 'I had come only two days ago.
12But now, I want to head back to my home in Tibet.
13But she added: 'I don't mind waiting here.
14The earthquake, which struck on Saturday, came at a busy time of year, with an estimated 300,000 foreign tourists in the country.
15The economy of Nepal, a nation of 27.8 million people, relies heavily on tourism, principally trekking and Himalayan mountain climbing.
16The death toll has already hit 2,500 people, with the historic city Kathmandu - and all its famous sites - now reduced to rubble.
17The Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu had been briefly shut down yesterday due to a powerful aftershock from the 7.9 magnitude quake.
18According to reports, the impact was so great that planes had to turn around mid-flight.
19India's budget SpiceJet said it was forced to recall its plane to New Delhi after the air traffic control centre at Kathmandu's airport was evacuated.
20Meanwhile, two Air India flights - carrying 135 and 45 passengers - were also turned back after the evacuation on the ground, according to the Press Trust of India news agency.
21The Indian Air Force evacuated several hundred stranded tourists but commercial flights were resumed just one hour later.
22The temporary shutdown highlights the difficulty facing Nepal's authorities, who are struggling to rescue and treat thousands of people in the capital.
23As well as killing more than 2,000 people nationwide, the quake toppled whole buildings and ripped apart roads and other infrastructure, cutting off electricity and communication networks.
