Police: Half of Those Arrested During Riots Released

1Baltimore police say more than half of the people detained during Monday's riots have been released without charges.
2Capt. Eric Kowalczyk, a police spokesman, says 201 people were arrested during the riots and 106 of them were subsequently released after 48 hours because specific charges couldn't be filed.
3He says police are reviewing surveillance footage and expect to charge many of those people once their identities have been confirmed.
4Kowalczyk also says 98 officers have been injured since Monday.
5Of those, 43 required treatment and 13 have been unable to return to work, while another 15 are on limited duty.
6Earlier Thursday, police delivered a report to city prosecutors detailing evidence in the death of Freddie Gray in custody.
7Six officers involved in Gray's arrest have been suspended.
