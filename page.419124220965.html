Jihadists attack hotel in central Mali

1BAMAKO, Mali (AP) " Islamic extremists attacked a hotel in central Mali near an army camp in Sevare, an army spokesman said Friday.
2The jihadists exchanged fire with the army near the Hotel Debo, said Col.
3Souleymane Maiga.
4Smoke was coming from the hotel, said a resident who spoke on condition of anonymity for fear of his safety.
5"This morning as I was leaving for work I heard shots and saw smoke coming from the Hotel Debo. The area is surrounded by soldiers who told us to return to our homes," he said.
6Attacks by extremists on Malian army positions have moved into central and southern Mali this year.
7On Saturday, two Malian soldiers were killed and five were injured when their convoy was ambushed in the center of the country on the road between the towns of Nampala and Diabaly.
8In March, a masked gunman opened fire at a restaurant popular with foreigners in Bamako, the capital, killing five people.
9And in late June, gunmen killed three soldiers in a village near the border with Mauritania, then the next day briefly occupied a village near the border with Ivory Coast.
10The Ansar Dine extremist group later said it was behind those attacks.
11Mali's north fell under the control of Islamic extremists following a military coup in 2012.
12A French-led military intervention in 2013 scattered the extremists.
