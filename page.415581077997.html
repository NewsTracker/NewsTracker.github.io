UK military trainers in Ukraine for medical, tactical drills

UK Military Trainers in Ukraine for Medical, Tactical Drills

1Britain's defense ministry said it aims to boost Ukrainian armed forces' defensive capability in their armed campaign against Russian-backed separatists in the east.
2The fragile cease-fire in place since mid-February has largely held, although sporadic small arms clashes continue to be reported daily.
3Separatist leaders have warned they could abandon the truce over what they say is Ukraine's failure to live up to a guarantee to grant autonomy to eastern regions.
4British trainers will run a series of combat first aid courses at bases across the country, said Ukrainian defense ministry spokesman Oleksandr Motuzianik.
5Assistance is being aimed at preventing further Ukrainian fatalities and casualties, Britain's defense ministry said in a statement.
6It is unclear what tactical insight instructors will be providing, but the British defense ministry said it would "help improve situational awareness on the ground."
7"Training will complement what we have already been providing - night vision goggles, body (armor), winter fuel, medical kits, winter clothing and sleeping bags - to the Ukrainian Armed Forces over the last year," the ministry said.
8Motuzianik said British instructors will also give courses on providing psychological rehabilitation to troops returning from combat.
9The British defense ministry said the number of British trainers in Ukraine could rise to 75, depending on the training program that is developed.
10Motuzianik said training is being rolled out over a two-month period at bases in the regions of Zhytomyr, Mykolaiv and Rivne.
11Ukraine has lobbied hard for international military support.
12The Pentagon announced Thursday that 290 soldiers from the 173rd Airborne Brigade, based in Italy, will go to Ukraine next month to conduct training with the Ukrainian National Guard.
13The training was announced last year but has been under extensive review amid uncertainty about the Ukraine crisis.
14Russia has bristled at plans announced by various countries to provide military assistance to Ukraine, saying it won't help end the conflict.
