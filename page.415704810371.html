Germanwings airliner 4U 9525 crashes in French Alps - 150 reported dead

1The Germanwings A320 had 144 passengers and six crew on board.
2Flight 4U 9525 was flying from Barcelona to Dusseldorf when it crashed at an altitude of 6,500ft in the mountainous zone of Meolans-Revels, near Digne.
3Among the passengers were 67 Germans and 45 Spanish citizens.
4German newspaper Bild is reporting that 16 teenagers and two teachers from Haltern, in western Germany, are among the dead.
5France's Transport Ministry confirmed on Tuesday afternoon there were no survivors.
6French prime minister Manuel Valls said a helicopter has managed to land near the crash site.
7Gilbert Sauvan, an official with the local council, told Les Echos newspaper: "The plane is disintegrated.
8Interior Ministry spokesman Pierre-Henry Brandet said he expects "an extremely long and extremely difficult" search and rescue operation because the area is so remote.
9It is surrounded by mountains and there are few clear trails to the snow-covered area.
10The plane sent out a distress signal at 10.45am local time (9.45am GMT).
11The boss of Germanwings said the plane went into a long descent before it crashed.
12Germanwings CEO Thomas Winkelmann said the plane began descending again shortly after it reached its cruising height following take-off from Barcelona Airport.
13The descent lasted eight minutes, he told reporters in Cologne.
14Radar and air traffic control contact broke off at 10.53am.
15He said the pilot had more than 10 years' experience working for Germanwings and its parent airline Lufthansa.
16Airbus said the A320 was delivered to Lufthansa in 1991.
17Germanwings said the passenger manifest included two babies.
18Officials believe there were 67 German nationals on board.
19The owner of a campground near the crash site, Pierre Polizzi, said he heard the plane making curious noises shortly before it crashed.
20"At 11.30, I heard a series of loud noises in the air. There are often fighter jets flying over, so I thought it sounded just like that. I looked outside, but I couldn't see any fighter planes," he said.
21"The noise I heard was long - like eight seconds - as if the plane was going more slowly than a military plane. There was another long noise after about 30 seconds."
22Mr Polizzi said the plane crashed about 3 to 11 miles (5 to 8km) from his place, which is closed for the season.
23"It's going to be very difficult to get there. The mountain is snowy and very hostile," he said.
24The municipal sports hall of Seyne-les-Alpes, six miles (10 kilometres) from the Val d'Allos ski resort, was being set up to take bodies or survivors from the crash, according to Sandrine Julien of the town hall.
25There was no obvious weather reason why the plane went down.
26Captain Benoit Zeisser of the nearby Digne-le-Bains police said there were some clouds but the cloud ceiling was not low.
27In addition, the safest part of a flight is when the plane is at cruising elevation.
28Just 10% of fatal accidents occur at that point, according to a safety analysis by Boeing.
29In contrast, take-off and the initial climb accounts for 14% of crashes and final approach and landing accounts for 47%.
30Germanwings official Oliver Wagner told German television that Germanwings flight 9525 carried 144 passengers and six crew members.
31He did not give a breakdown of nationalities on board.
32Germanwings is a lower-cost unit of Lufthansa, Germany's biggest airline.
33The Germanwings logo, normally maroon and yellow, was blacked out on its Twitter feed.
34Family members arriving at Dusseldorf airport were taken from the main terminal to a nearby building, which airport employees partially covered with sheets for privacy.
35At Barcelona airport, police escorted several crying women to a part of the airport away from the media.
36One woman held a jacket over the head of another woman, who was sobbing.
37In a live briefing, the French President Francois Hollande said it was likely that a number of the victims were German.
38"It's a tragedy on our soil," he said.
39The last time a passenger jet crashed in France was the 2000 Concorde accident, which left 113 dead - 109 in the plane and four on the ground.
40German chancellor Angela Merkel spoke to both Mr Hollande and Spanish prime minister Mariano Rajoy about the crash, immediately cancelling all other appointments.
41At a briefing in Berlin, Ms Merkel told reporters she would travel to the crash site region tomorrow, and that Germany's foreign and transport ministers were already en route.
42She said her thoughts were "with those people who so suddenly lost their lives, among them many compatriots".
43"The crash of the German plane with more than 140 people on board is a shock that plunges us in Germany, the French and the Spanish into deep sorrow," Ms Merkel said.
44She reminded everyone that the cause was not known.
45"We still don't know much beyond the bare information on the flight, and there should be no speculation on the cause of the crash," she said.
46"All that will be investigated thoroughly."
47The A320 plane is a workhorse of modern aviation.
48Similar to the Boeing 737, the single-aisle, twin-engine jet is used to connect cities between one and five hours apart.
49Worldwide, 3,606 A320s are in operation, according to Airbus.
50The A320 is certified to fly up to 39,000 feet but it can begin to experience problems as low as 37,000 feet, depending on temperature and weight, including fuel, cargo and passengers.
51The A320 family also has a good safety record, with just 0.14 fatal accidents per million take-offs, according to a Boeing safety analysis.
52The German Federal Bureau of Aircraft Accident Investigation was sending three people to France to join the investigation.
53French interior minister Bernard Cazeneuve, the country's top security official, and the German ambassador in Paris were among those heading to the crash site.
54In Madrid, Mr Rajoy suspended his agenda to preside over an emergency government meeting about the crash.
55"Like everyone, I lament this incredibly sad and dramatic accident," Mr Rajoy said.
56Antonio San Jose, spokesman for the Spanish airport authority AENA, said his agency was working with Germanwings to reach out to relatives of the victims.
57Spain's king and queen, in Paris today, cancelled their previously planned state visit and offered their condolences to all who lost a loved one in the crash.
