Employment hits fresh high but wage growth disappoints

Ukraine separatist rebels make new threat to abandon cease-fire

1Higher employment and a lower claimant count kept unemployment rate at a six year low of 5.7pc.
2Although this was slightly higher than economists' forecasts for a fall to 5.6pc, it was down from 6pc in the previous three months and matched the six-year low seen in the three months to December.
3Real pay also continued to strengthen, although overall pay growth also fell short of expectations.
4Average weekly earnings, including bonuses, grew by 1.8pc in the three months to January, from 2.1pc in the quarter to December, when workers enjoyed a bumper rise in bonus payments.
5Economists had expected wages to grow by 2.2pc.
6By comparison, inflation fell to a record low of 0.3pc in January, and is on course to fall further in the coming months.
7Regular pay grew by 1.6pc over the same period, from 1.7pc in the three months to December.
8Regular real pay growth is now at its highest since before the financial crisis.
9However, the pound fell against the dollar and euro as growth fell short of expectations and minutes of the Bank of England's latest interest rate meeting showed policymakers were concerned that sterling's recent strength could spark a deflationary episode.
