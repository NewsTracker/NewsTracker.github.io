Egypt strikes ISIL group in Libya after video of mass beheading

1Egypt's army warplanes carried out airstrikes in neighboring Libya against areas claimed by affiliates of Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant (ISIL) on Monday in Derna, Libya.
2Egyptian jets bombed Islamic State in Iraq and the Levant (ISIL) targets in Libya on Monday, a day after the release of a video showing the beheading of 21 Egyptian Christians, drawing Cairo directly into the conflict across its border.
3Sources told Al Jazeera on Monday that at least seven civilians, including three children, were killed by the blasts.
4Libya's air force also participated in Monday's attack on Derna - an eastern coastal city seen as a base for ISIL fighters in the oil-rich nation.
5While Cairo is believed to have provided clandestine support to a Libyan general fighting a rogue government in Tripoli, the mass killings pushed Egypt's President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi into open action, expanding his battle against radical groups.
6Cairo is also calling for international intervention in Libya against ISIL.
7Loyalists of the Syria- and Iraq-based group have risen to dominate several cities in the chaos-riven North African nation, just across the Mediterranean Sea from Italy.
8After the release of the beheading video Sunday night, the tiny Christian-majority home village of more than half of the 21 Egyptians was gutted by grief.
9Inside the village church, relatives wept and shouted the names of the dead in shock.
10"What will be a relief to me is to take a hold of his murderer, tear him apart, eat up his flesh and liver," said Bushra Fawzi in el-Aour village, as he wept over the loss of his 22-year-old son Shenouda.
11"I want his body back. If they dumped it in the sea, I want it back. If they set fire to it, I want its dust."
12The 21 victims - mainly young men from impoverished families - had traveled to Libya for work and were kidnapped in two groups in December and January from the coastal city of Sirte.
13In the video, the group is marched onto what is purported to be a Libyan beach before masked figures with knives behead them.
14The killing of at least a dozen of them is clearly visible, though it is not clear from the video whether all 21 hostages were killed.
15On Monday morning, an Egyptian armed forces spokesman announced the strikes over state radio, marking the first time Cairo has publicly acknowledged taking military action in neighboring Libya.
16The statement said the warplanes targeted weapons caches and training camps before returning safely.
17Libya's air force commander, Saqr al-Joroushi, told Egyptian state TV that the air strikes were coordinated with the Libyan side and that they killed about 50 ISIL-linked fighters.
18The strikes hit four positions in the eastern Libyan city of Derna, taken over by an ISIL affiliate last year, a Libyan security official told The Associated Press.
19"The airstrikes hit their targets precisely, and the falcons of our air forces returned safely to their base," said an Egyptian military statement issued shortly after the strikes.
20Libya's air force said it had carried out its own strikes in Derna, without providing further details.
21It said the "intense strikes" were "to avenge the bloodshed and to seek retribution from the killers."
22After the strikes, Sisi spoke with France's president and Italy's prime minister about the Libya situation.
23He sent his foreign minister, Sameh Shukri, to New York to hold consultations with United Nations officials and Security Council members ahead of a conference opening Wednesday in Washington.
24"What is happening in Libya is a threat to international peace and security," said Sisi, who also banned all travel to Libya by Egyptian citizens.
25Egypt's Foreign Ministry said the international community must adopt "immediate and effective" moves against such groups in Libya.
26"Leaving things in Libya as they are without decisive intervention to suppress these terror groups constitutes a clear danger to international peace and security," the statement said.
27It also called on the U.S.-led coalition staging airstrikes against ISIL in Iraq and Syria to offer Egypt political and material support to counter the threat posed by the group's affiliates in Libya.
