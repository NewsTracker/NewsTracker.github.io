Lawyer who sued after Ferguson wants special prosecutor in South Carolina man's shooting death

1NORTH CHARLESTON, S.C. - The attorney who brought a $40 million lawsuit following the fatal shooting of a black man by a white officer in Ferguson, Missouri, last year wants a special prosecutor to investigate a similar case in South Carolina.
2Malik Shabazz, the president of Black Lawyers for Justice, says his group also will conduct its own investigation into the slaying of 50-year-old Walter Scott by a white officer April 4.
3He also plans a nationwide town hall meeting next weekend to explore the issue of race and police practices.
4Shabazz attended a rally at North Charleston City Hall attended by about 75 people on Monday, where he said it isn't enough that officer Michael Slager has been charged with murder.
5"A charge to us doesn't mean anything. We want to see that the charges stick," he said.
6The shooting was captured on a cellphone video recorded by an eyewitness who was walking nearby.
7The footage shows Slager firing eight times as Scott is running away, then shows Scott falling to the ground.
8The video has been seen by millions and resulted in the charge against Slager.
9"We have reached the point and the tragic point that if you don't have video showing what happened," a shooting "is justifiable," Shabazz said.
10"We're not going to be hunted down like deer."
11He added that "when we see dead bodies on the ground, we are at war," and said he was encouraging protesters to "stir the pot" to bring change.
12But he said he was not advocating violence.
13"Of course not, I'm an attorney," he said.
14Black Lawyers for Justice, founded by Shabazz, brought a lawsuit alleging Ferguson, Missouri, and St. Louis County used excessive force and falsely arrested bystanders to quell unrest after the fatal shooting of a black 18-year-old man by a white officer there last year.
15Shabazz said whether a lawsuit is brought in South Carolina depends on what his investigation turns up.
16The response to the North Charleston shooting has been subdued, generally attributed to authorities immediately charging Slager with murder after the cellphone video surfaced.
17The State Law Enforcement Division released almost 13 hours of video from the cruisers of the five officers who responded to the scene of the shooting.
18On one, Slager can be heard wondering aloud to an officer identified as his supervisor why Scott ran away.
19Scott was behind $18,000 in his child support payments and family members have said he may have run because he was worried about going back to jail.
20A warrant had been issued for his arrest.
21On the video, Slager is heard answering a call on his cellphone.
22"Everything's OK, OK?" he tells the caller.
23"I just shot somebody."
24He also tells the caller: "He grabbed my Taser, yeah. He was running from me."
25Slager initially said after the shooting that Scott had tried to take his Taser.
26The man with the cellphone said he started recording after noticing a scuffle.
