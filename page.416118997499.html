5In a scrawling, handwritten letter to The Times, murder suspect Robert Durst doesn't speak of the serious charges he now faces but instead writes wistfully of his days in Los Angeles.
6The real estate scion muses about watching the city lights, the hopeless traffic congestion, spending time at a Sunset Boulevard coffee shop and his love of attending the opera.
7Robert Durst's attorney Dick DeGuerin speaks outside the courthouse in New Orleans on April 2 after a hearing was continued for a week.
8"I loved watching the traffic come up La Cienega and mush into Santa Monica," he wrote in his two-page letter, postmarked nearly two weeks after his arrest in the mysterious murder of author Susan Berman, found shot in the head at her Benedict Canyon home 15 years ago.
9Durst blames L.A.'s political and business leaders for the absence of professional football in Los Angeles, ridicules a performance of Wagner's "Ring Cycle" as being a "true example of Hollywood gone berserk" and ruminates on his own medical condition.
10"I have said nothing about charges, crimes or trials," he adds.
11"I'm sure you know what your abilities are to visit me when I get to L.A."
12But it seems unlikely Durst will be coming to California anytime soon.
13Nearly after a month after his arrest, the 71-year-old subject of the HBO documentary "The Jinx" was in Orleans Parish Criminal District Court Thursday to plead not guilty to state weapons charges, a case that has delayed his extradition to California.
14In court, Durst greeted his three attorneys with a "Good morning," but looked feeble.
15He shuffled in wearing a yellow jail jumpsuit and plastic sandals, shackled and with his head shaved, to hear a judge read the charges against him - including a list more than 10 aliases - and ask how he pleaded.
16Durst leaned toward his lead attorney, Dick DeGuerin, who whispered in his ear before he replied.
17"I am not guilty, your honor," Durst said, his voice thin and even.
18"On both counts?"
19Judge Franz Zibilich asked.
20Durst again paused to consult DeGuerin.
21Durst was arrested March 14 when FBI agents stopped him on a California murder warrant and found a .38 revolver and marijuana in his hotel room.
22Agents had tracked the millionaire from his home in Houston and feared he had a plan to escape to Cuba.
23His attorneys have been clear that they see the weapons charges as a distraction and would rather be in L.A. fighting the 2000 murder case.
24"I feel like we're being tag-teamed," DeGuerin said after Thursday's hearing.
25"And I feel like we need to be in California where the main case is so we can try the case."
26Durst was indicted on state charges of possessing a firearm as a convicted felon and possessing a firearm with a controlled substance, and now faces similar federal weapons charges.
27His attorneys have waived extradition and attempted to subpoena the FBI agents who picked him up to testify about the arrest and searches so that they can get the charges dismissed.
28But federal prosecutors ordered the agents not to appear at a hearing last week, and persuaded the magistrate to continue the hearing.
29On Thursday, DeGuerin reiterated that at Durst's next hearing "it will be necessary to have two, maybe one of the federal agents there.... We run into a stump with that because although we followed all the procedures to have them appear at a hearing last week, they filed a removal" to federal court.
