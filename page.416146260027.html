Felton lifts Mavs to 144-143 win over Nuggets in 2 OTs (Yahoo Sports)

1DENVER (AP) -- Raymond Felton hit a layup with 1.5 seconds remaining in double overtime, then blocked a last-second shot to help the Dallas Mavericks beat the Denver Nuggets 144-143 on Friday night.
2Felton drove past Randy Foye and banked in a shot high off the glass.
3He then got a piece of Kenneth Faried's jumper at the buzzer, causing the ball to wind up short.
4Danilo Gallinari scored a career-high 47 points, including eight in the first overtime and nine in the second.
5Denver had a chance to win in regulation, but Faried's jumper rolled off the rim.
6Dallas had a shot to claim victory at the end of the first overtime, only to have Monta Ellis' long shot clang off the iron.
7Dirk Nowitzki had 25 points and Richard Jefferson added a season-high 24.
8Mavericks coach Rick Carlisle pulled most of his starters for the second overtime, relying on a fresher unit.
9The strategy worked.
10The Mavericks rested guard Rajon Rondo with the team locked into the seventh seed.
11They're still uncertain who they will face in the first round of the Western Conference playoffs.
12Ty Lawson had 19 points and 18 assists for the Nuggets (29-50), who lost 50 games in a season for first time since 2002-03.
13Interim coach Melvin Hunt fell to 9-11 since stepping in after the firing of Brian Shaw last month.
14Denver led by as many as four points in the first overtime, only to have Dallas tie it on a layup by Ellis.
15Dallas appeared headed toward a rout early in this game, leading by as many as 23 points in the first half.
16Denver climbed back with a 37-20 third quarter.
17The Nuggets took their first lead on a dunk by Faried with 2:45 remaining in regulation.
18They increased it to 119-114 on back-to-back 3-pointers by Wilson Chandler and Lawson.
19But the Mavericks tied the game at 121 with 29.5 left on Jefferson's layup.
20Chandler wasn't too pleased with Jusuf Nurkic late in the second quarter, glaring at the Nuggets rookie after he pushed Chandler from behind on a dunk.
21The officials assessed Nurkic a flagrant-1 foul.
22The Mavericks were comfortably up 73-52 at halftime, tying the most points they've scored in the opening half this season.
23They shot nearly 63 percent from the field and had 20 assists.
24Carlisle said he plans on closely monitoring the minutes of his players.
25Still, he wants them to head into the postseason with an edge, so with Denver charging late, he couldn't afford to rest his regulars.
