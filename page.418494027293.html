Iraq announces a large-scale operation to oust Islamic State militants from Anbar province

1BAGHDAD - The Iraqi government began on Monday a long-awaited large-scale military operation to dislodge Islamic State militants from Iraq's western Anbar province, a military spokesman announced.
2The spokesman for the Joint Operations Command, Brig.
3Gen. Yahya Rasool, said in a televised statement that the operation started at dawn Monday and that government forces are backed by Shiite and Sunni pro-government fighters.
4Rasool didn't clarify whether the U.S.-led international coalition is taking part.
5This is not the first time the Iraqi government has announced an operation to retake Anbar - where several key towns, including the provincial capital Ramadi, remain under IS control.
6In May, authorities announced an operation to retake Ramadi, but there has not been any major progress on the ground since then.
7In a brief statement, Iraq's Prime Minister, Haider al-Abadi, vowed to "take revenge from Daesh criminals on the battlefield... and their cowardly crimes against unarmed civilians will only increase our determination to chase them and to expel them from the land of Iraq."
8The Islamic State group, also known by the Arabic acronym Daesh, seized large parts of Anbar in early 2014 and captured Ramadi in May. Iraqi forces, which had been making steady progress against the extremists in recent months with the help of the air campaign, scored a major victory in recapturing Saddam Hussein's hometown of Tikrit last month.
9During the past few weeks, the troops have been moving to cut the militants' supply routes and to surround and isolate Ramadi and Fallujah.
10Rasool didn't provide any further details on the ongoing operations.
11By noon, the country's state TV reported government forces recapturing villages and areas around Fallujah.
12Hours after the announcement of the military operation, Iraq's Defense Ministry announced the arrival of four F-16 fighter jets from the United States to Balad air base north of Baghdad.
13They are part of 36 F-16s purchased by the Iraqi government.
14The new fighter jets will boost Iraq's Air Force, which depends only on several Russian-made secondhand Sukhoi jets.
15Last week, a Sukhoi fighter jet accidentally dropped a bomb over a Baghdad neighborhood and killed at least 12 people.
16Meanwhile Monday, the IS group claimed responsibility for Sunday's series of bombings in Shiite areas of the capital, Baghdad, that killed at least 29 people and wounded 81 others, according to the IS-affiliated Aamaq news agency.
17Iraq is going through its worst crisis since the 2011 withdrawal of U.S. troops.
18The Islamic State group controls large swaths of the country's north and west after capturing Iraq's second-largest city of Mosul and the majority of Anbar province.
