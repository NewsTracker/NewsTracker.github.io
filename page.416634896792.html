More than 100 arrested in Baltimore overnight

1BALTIMORE - Residents of Baltimore and activists across the United States waited on Thursday for official word on the cause of a black man's death in police custody earlier this month, as protests over the case spread to other cities.
2More than 100 people were arrested in New York overnight on Wednesday during protests against police violence, while Baltimore saw a largely peaceful night as a curfew held after rioting over the death of 25-year-old Freddie Gray.
3Thousands of National Guard troops and police remained in the mostly black city of Baltimore on Thursday morning to maintain order and enforce a 10 p.m. curfew set after unrest on Monday saw buildings and cars burned, stores looted and 20 officers hurt.
4Cities across the country also held demonstrations on Wednesday as part of a movement against racial profiling and police use of lethal force sparked by the deaths of African-American men in Cleveland; Ferguson, Mo.; New York and elsewhere over the past year.
5Baltimore officials tried to tamp down expectations that there would be a quick answer on the fate of the six police officers involved in the arrest that preceded Gray's death.
6While police plan to turn their report on the incident over to the Baltimore City state's attorney on Friday, they cautioned that it would not immediately be made public.
7"We are confident that our state's attorney is going to do what needs to be done," said State Senator Catherine Pugh, who has been a frequent presence on Baltimore's streets the last two nights, referring to Marilyn Mosby, state's attorney for the county.
8"We have to give her time and give her office time to wade through the papers they will receive," Pugh said.
9New York City police said early on Thursday they arrested more than 100 people, mostly for disorderly conduct, as protesters weaved in separate groups through Manhattan and blocked traffic in a few areas.
10The demonstrations from Union Square to Times Square and elsewhere, were reminiscent of protests in December after a grand jury decided against charges in the case of Eric Garner, an unarmed black man who died after a police officer put him in a chokehold.
11Smaller protests were held in Boston, Houston, Ferguson, Washington, D.C., and Seattle, and a handful of demonstrators were arrested in Denver on Wednesday.
12Gray was arrested on April 12 after running from nearby police.
13He was transported to a police station in a van, with no seat restraint, and suffered a spinal injury that led to his death a week later.
14A lawyer for Gray's family says his spine was 80% severed at the neck while in custody.
15Six officers have been suspended, and the U.S. Justice Department is investigating the incident for possible civil rights violations.
16The Washington Post reported on Wednesday night that a prisoner who was in the same van as Gray, but who could not see him, heard him banging against the walls and believed he was intentionally trying to injure himself.
17The newspaper cited a document written by a police investigator.
18Police have arrested close to 270 people since Monday's violence, 18 of them on Wednesday.
19Police Commissioner Anthony Batts said more than 100 people had been released without being charged, because officials could not keep up with the paperwork, but he said charges would be brought later.
20The unrest in Baltimore prompted national figures - from the new U.S. Attorney General Loretta Lynch to Democratic presidential candidate Hillary Clinton - to vow to work on improving law enforcement and criminal justice in minority communities nationwide.
