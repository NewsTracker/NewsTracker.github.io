7A passenger jet carrying 150 people as it flew from Barcelona to Duesseldorf went into an eight-minute descent Tuesday before it crashed in the French Alps, authorities said.
8French Prime Minister Manuel Valls says a helicopter has managed to land near the crash site and has found no survivors.
9The weather in the area deteriorated Tuesday afternoon, with a chilly rain falling.
10Gilbert Sauvan, of the local council, told Les Echos newspaper, "The plane is disintegrated."
11The crash site was at Meolans-Revels, near the popular ski resort of Pra Loup, according to Eric Ciotti, the head of the regional council in southeast France.
12The site is 430 miles south-southeast of Paris.
13People gathered at Germany's Duesseldorf airport shortly after news that a jetliner carrying at least 150 people had crashed in a remote area of the French Alps as it was flying from Barcelona, Spain to Duesseldorf.
14The CEO of Lufthansa's Germanwings budget airline confirmed on Tuesday (March 24) that its flight 4U9525 from Barcelona to Duesseldorf crashed.
15French President Francois Hollande said he believed none of the 148 people on board the Germanwings plane that crashed on Tuesday (March 24) had survived.
16Following a plane crash in the south of France, Britain's Deputy Prime Minister says his "heart goes out" to the families of the victims.
17The Germanwings Airbus A320 plane left Barcelona at 9:55 a.m., sent out a distress signal at 10:45 a.m., then crashed in a mountainous zone in France at an altitude of about 6,550 feet, said Pierre-Henry Brandet, the French Interior Ministry spokesman.
18Brandet told BFM television he expected "an extremely long and extremely difficult" search-and-rescue operation because of the area's remoteness.
19Germanwings CEO Thomas Winkelmann said the plane began descending again shortly after it reached its cruising height following takeoff from Barcelona Airport.
20The descent lasted eight minutes, he told reporters in Cologne.
21He said the pilot had more than 10 years' experience working for Germanwings and its parent airline Lufthansa.
22Airbus said the A320 was delivered to Lufthansa in 1991.
23Germanwings official Oliver Wagner told German television that Germanwings Flight 9525 carried 144 passengers and 6 crew members.
24Two of the passengers on board were babies, the airline said.
25The airline is a lower-cost unit of Lufthansa, Germany's biggest airline.
26It has been operating since 2002, part of traditional national carriers' response from rising European budget carriers.
27It serves mainly European destinations.
28The Germanwings logo, normally maroon and yellow, was blacked out on its Twitter feed.
29Family members arriving at Duesseldorf airport were taken from the main terminal to a nearby building, which airport employees partially covered with sheets for privacy.
30At Barcelona airport, police escorted several crying women to a part of the airport away from the media.
31One woman held a jacket over the head of another woman, who was sobbing.
32The owner of a campground near the crash site, Pierre Polizzi, said he heard the plane making curious noises shortly before it crashed.
33"At 11.30, I heard a series of loud noises in the air. There are often fighter jets flying over, so I thought it sounded just like that. I looked outside, but I couldn't see any fighter planes," he told The Associated Press in a phone interview.
34"The noise I heard was long - like 8 seconds - as if the plane was going more slowly than a military plane. There was another long noise after about 30 seconds."
35Polizzi said the plane crashed about 3-to-11 miles from his place, which is closed for the season.
36The municipal sports hall of Seyne-les-Alpes, 6 miles from the Val d'Allos ski resort, was being set up to take bodies or survivors from the crash, according to Sandrine Julien of the town hall.
37There was no obvious weather reason Tuesday why the plane went down.
38Capt. Benoit Zeisser of the nearby Digne-le-Bains police said there were some clouds but the cloud ceiling was not low.
39In addition, the safest part of a flight is when the plane is at cruising elevation.
40Just 10 percent of fatal accidents occur at that point, according to a safety analysis by Boeing.
41In contrast, takeoff and the initial climb accounts for 14 percent of crashes and final approach and landing accounts for 47 percent.
42In a live briefing Tuesday, Hollande said it was likely that a number of the victims were German.
43The last time a passenger jet crashed in France was the 2000 Concorde accident, which left 113 dead - 109 in the plane and four on the ground.
44German Chancellor Angela Merkel spoke with both Hollande and Spanish Prime Minister Mariano Rajoy about the crash, immediately cancelling all other appointments.
45At a briefing in Berlin, Merkel told reporters she would travel to the crash site region on Wednesday, and that Germany's foreign and transport ministers were already en route.
46She said her thoughts were "with those people who so suddenly lost their lives, among them many compatriots."
47"The crash of the German plane with more than 140 people on board is a shock that plunges us in Germany, the French and the Spanish into deep sorrow," Merkel said.
48"We still don't know much beyond the bare information on the flight, and there should be no speculation on the cause of the crash," she said.
49"All that will be investigated thoroughly."
50The A320 plane is a workhorse of modern aviation.
51Similar to the Boeing 737, the single-aisle, twin-engine jet is used to connect cities between one and five hours apart.
52Worldwide, 3,606 A320s are in operation, according to Airbus.
53The A320 is certified to fly up to 39,000 feet but it can begin to experience problems as low as 37,000 feet, depending on temperature and weight, including fuel, cargo and passengers.
54The A320 family also has a good safety record, with just 0.14 fatal accidents per million takeoffs, according to a Boeing safety analysis.
55The German Federal Bureau of Aircraft Accident Investigation was sending three people to France to join the investigation.
56French Interior Minister Bernard Cazeneuve, the country's top security official, and the German ambassador in Paris were among those heading to the crash site.
57In Madrid, Rajoy suspended his agenda to preside over an emergency government meeting about the crash.
58"Like everyone, I lament this incredibly sad and dramatic accident," Rajoy said.
59Antonio San Jose, spokesman for the Spanish airport authority AENA, said his agency was working with Germanwings to reach out to relatives of the victims.
60Spain's king and queen, in Paris on Tuesday, canceled their previously planned state visit and offered their condolences to all who lost a loved one in the crash.
