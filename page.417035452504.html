Turkey criticizes death sentence for Egypt's ousted leader

1ANKARA, Turkey (AP) - Turkish President Recep Tayyip Erdogan has criticized Egypt for sentencing ousted President Mohammed Morsi to death, saying the country was returning to the "old Egypt" by rolling back democracy.
2Erdogan on Saturday also criticized western nations that he accused of not speaking out against Egyptian President Abdel-Fattah el-Sissi, who ousted Morsi, or against death sentences being handed down to Morsi's Muslim Brotherhood group.
3Turkey's government strongly supports the Muslim Brotherhood.
4Relations between Egypt and Turkey soured after Morsi's ouster by el-Sissi.
5On Saturday, an Egyptian court sentenced Morsi to death over his part in a mass prison break that took place during the 2011 uprising that toppled Egypt's long-time leader Hosni Mubarak.
