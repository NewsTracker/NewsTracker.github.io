Ukraine says radiation levels not affected by Chernobyl fire

1KIEV, Ukraine (AP) - Ukrainian authorities say there has been no change in background radiation levels in the capital as a result of fires in woodlands in the exclusion zone around the destroyed Chernobyl nuclear power plant.
2The emergency services agency said in a statement Wednesday that isotope levels in surface soil and water are within standard levels.
3Kiev, the capital, lies less than 100 kilometers (60 miles) downriver from the zone around Chernobyl, which was heavily contaminated by radiation from the 1986 reactor explosion and fire.
4A 30-kilometer (18.6-mile) zone around the plant is off-limits to most people except for workers constructing a new shelter to cover the destroyed reactor's building, and to visitors on short trips.
5Authorities said Tuesday evening's fire swept through 400 hectares (1.5 square miles) of woodland.
