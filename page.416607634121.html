1st meeting of split Cyprus' rival leaders meeting postponed

1NICOSIA, Cyprus (AP) - A Cypriot government official says the first meeting between the country's Greek Cypriot president Nicos Anastasiades and the newly elected Turkish Cypriot leader Mustafa Akinci has been postponed until next week.
2Government spokesman Nicos Christodoulides said Tuesday that the meeting, which had been tentatively set for Saturday, said the reasons for the postponement are "not considered of substance."
3Akinci, a left-wing moderate with a long track record of reaching out to Greek Cypriots, easily defeated the hard-line incumbent in Sunday's vote in the breakaway Turkish Cypriot north of divided Cyprus.
4The meeting is seen as preparing the ground for the resumption of stalled negotiations aimed at reunifying the country, split in 1974 when Turkey invaded after a coup by supporters of union with Greece.
