News24.com | Plane skids off runway during snowstorm in NY

Emirates forges special relationship with el-Sissi's Egypt

1None of the 127 passengers and five crew members were seriously injured as Delta flight 1086 from Atlanta skidded on the tarmac at about 11:00 EST (16:00 GMT).
2Images on local media showed the plane's nose had pushed through a chain-link fence and was jutting out over an embankment.
3LaGuardia, the smallest of the New York area's three major airports, was immediately closed after the mishap.
4By the time its runways started to reopen at about 2:30 EST, air traffic was snarled throughout the US East Coast and beyond.
5Hundreds of flights were canceled at LaGuardia, compounding weather-related travel disruptions in many parts of the country.
6"I think the pilot did everything he could to slow the aircraft down," said Patrick Foye, executive director of the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey, the airport's operator.
7"Obviously, the pilot's and the co-pilot's good efforts were reflected in the fact that there were only minor injuries."
8After exit slides failed to deploy properly, passengers were forced to disembark by climbing over a wing with the help of emergency responders, Foye said at a news conference.
9There were only two minor injuries reported, he said, though that number may change.
10One of those on board was Larry Donnell, a professional football player with the New York Giants.
11"We were all shocked and alarmed when the plane started to skid, but most importantly, as far as I know, all of the passengers and flight crew were able to exit the plane safely," the tight end said in a statement released by the team.
12Even though the aircraft landed in a driving snowstorm, Foye said two aircraft that touched down minutes before the incident had reported "good braking action."
13Officials did not make any statement about a possible cause, and experts from the National Transportation Safety Board were en route to the airport to begin an investigation.
14A spokesman for the NTSB declined further comment.
15Delta, which operates a hub at LaGuardia, said in a statement that it would work with all authorities to look into what happened.
16Its shares were down 1.9 percent at $44.28 in Thursday afternoon trading.
17The airline said it had taken delivery of the MD-88 involved in the incident in 1987, and that it last had a major maintenance overhaul in December 2010.
18The plane had a routine service check on Tuesday, it said, but declined further comment.
19LaGuardia Airport, located in the New York City borough of Queens, has in recent history been the scene of two previous crashes involving wintry weather.
20On March 2, 1994, Continental Airlines flight 705 bound for Denver aborted takeoff during a snowstorm and slid off the runway into a ditch, injuring 29 people.
21Two years earlier, USAir Flight 405 headed for Cleveland crashed into Flushing Bay during a snowy takeoff at LaGuardia, killing 27 people of the 51 on board.
22The crash was later blamed on icing on its wings.

6DUBAI, United Arab Emirates (AP) - This small Gulf nation, known for its soaring skyscrapers and mercantile bent, is making itself into the most stalwart ally of the Arab world's biggest country.
7In their deepening relationship, an economically exhausted Egypt benefits from the UAE's finances, and the U.S.-allied Emirates gets a heavyweight with extensive manpower on its side in a region deeply unstable with threats of militant violence and Iranian expansion.
8"We are among the vanguards calling for stability in Egypt, whose security represents a cornerstone of Arab world security," Emirati Foreign Minister Sheikh Abdullah bin Zayed Al Nahyan told a full house during his speech at a governance conference held in Dubai last month.
9"We cannot keep our fingers crossed about achieving stability and development in Egypt."
10That conference put the UAE-Egypt relationship front and center among the 80 nations attending.
11A slick, museum-worthy display outside the vast conference hall spotlighted the countries' close ties, declaring in a logo, "Among its many brotherly neighbors, Egypt holds a special place with the United Arab Emirates."
12Egypt's prime minister, Ibrahim Mahlab, had a prime speaking spot, which he used to praise the Emirates' "wise leadership" and the two nations' friendship.
13The Emirates is also a key organizer of an economic development conference aimed at enticing investors to Egypt and reviving its economy, to be held next week in the Sinai resort of Sharm el-Sheikh.
14The Egypt the Future investment conference's speakers list presents a who's who of the Emirati corporate elite.
15Executives from Western blue-chip names like General Electric and Coca Cola will share the stage with UAE-based heads of telecom Etisalat Group, private equity firm Abraaj Capital, and the real estate developer behind the world's tallest skyscraper, Emaar Properties.
16Executives from the Gulf nation's aviation, banking and energy industries will be there too.
17Emirati construction giant Arabtec Holding has ambitious plans for a $40 billion project to build 1 million middle-income homes across Egypt.
18It has said the project will create more than a million jobs in Egypt.
19Arabtec helped raise Dubai's record-breaking Burj Khalifa skyscraper.
20Its biggest shareholder is Aabar Investments, which is controlled by the government of the oil-rich Emirati capital, Abu Dhabi.
21Minister of State Sultan al-Jaber, an Emirati official with one of the few seats on the conference's steering committee, promised the event will be "a first step in the implementation of a larger strategy that will ensure Egypt's economic revival."
22Egypt's economy has been gutted by turmoil since the 2011 uprising that ousted autocrat Hosni Mubarak.
23Foreign investment and tourism revenues dried up, depleting foreign currency reserves and driving up unemployment, and frequent rounds of violence in the country have frustrated attempts to rebuild.
24The Emirates, Saudi Arabia and Kuwait pumped billions of dollars into Egypt following the military's ouster of Islamist President Mohammed Morsi in July 2013.
25Like Saudi Arabia and new Egyptian President Abdel-Fattah el-Sissi, the Emirates sees Morsi's Muslim Brotherhood as a threat and moved aggressively to stamp out sympathizers of the group in the wake of the Arab Spring.
26It followed Cairo's and Riyadh's lead in branding the group a terrorist organization in November.
27A UAE government report shows that Egypt is by far the biggest single recipient of donor cash from the Emirates, with $4.6 billion distributed just in 2013, the most recent year for which data is available.
28Egypt's aid share was 29 times more than that of the next largest recipient, Jordan.
29In addition to providing direct cash aid, the OPEC member has been building schools, clinics, wheat silos and tens of thousands of homes as part of at least $10 billion earmarked for Egypt since Morsi's ouster.
30In October 2013 it opened a hospital named after the federation's founder, Sheikh Zayed Al Nahyan, in the Manshiyat Nasser slum on the outskirts of Cairo.
31In contrast, before Morsi's fall, Egypt had been in talks for several years with the International Monetary Fund for a $4.8 billion loan, but the talks never reached a deal.
32For Egypt, the benefit of an enthusiastic, deep-pocketed patron is obvious.
33But the Emirates stands to gain, too.
34The seven-state federation sees Egypt as central to regional security given its location, the size of its population and its outsize historical and cultural role.
35As the Arab world's second biggest economy - after Saudi Arabia - the Emirates has plenty to spend on its defense budget and boasts one of the region's best-equipped militaries.
36What it doesn't have is Egypt's manpower.
37The UAE is home to only about 9 million people, fewer than one in five of them citizens with the foreign workers and their families making up the rest - compared to Egypt's 90 million people.
38"Egypt is the No. 1 strategic partner for the UAE outside the Gulf itself. It's partly because they share a very similar vision for the region, and a very similar threat perception," said Jane Kinninmont, a Middle East expert at British think tank Chatham House.
39"Their defense and security establishments really see eye to eye," she added.
40"Muslim Brotherhood concerns have brought them closer together."
41The growing ties were on display in a series of bilateral military exercises last year.
42Photos released following one set of drills showed el-Sissi, clad in fatigues, peering off the deck of a warship, with Emirati Prime Minister Sheik Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum and Abu Dhabi's powerful crown prince, Sheikh Mohammed bin Zayed Al Nahyan, at his side.
43The training may well have paid off when, according to American officials, the two nations teamed up in August to bomb Islamic militants in Libya.
44Egyptian officials have said Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait and UAE were in talks late last year over creating a military pact to face militants.
45Now el-Sissi is looking to formalize the bonds.
46Last week, in the wake of the beheading in Libya of a group of Egyptian Christians by Islamic State militants, the soldier-turned-politician called for the creation of a joint Arab military force.
47Two countries, he added, had separately offered to deploy military forces to help Egypt: Jordan and the United Arab Emirates.
