Simeone signs contract extension with Atletico Madrid

1Although he was linked with other clubs, including Manchester City as a possible replacement for Manuel Pellegrini, Simeone said he would "stay where I am" because he was sure the club would continue to grow.
2Simeone said he aims "to finish third in the league for three more seasons and to establish financial stability."
3He acknowledged it would not be easy because rivals such as Sevilla, Valencia, and Villarreal were also "growing and working well."
4The 44-year-old former Argentina and Atletico midfielder took over as coach in December 2011.
5"What we give to the club as coaches is passion, as we work with great enthusiasm, and we love this club," he said.
6Atletico has advanced to the quarterfinals of the Champions League, and will next face Real Madrid.
7The team is also fourth in the Spanish league, nine behind leader Barcelona.
8Last season's league title was Atletico's first since 1996.
9During his tenure, Atletico will move from its aging Vicente Calderon Stadium.
10The team was supposed to move to its new Peineta Stadium on the eastern outskirts of Madrid in the summer of 2016, but the club said in November that the change will be delayed by a year.

12The former Argentina and Atletico midfielder, who took over as coach in December 2011, has renewed his contract with the Spanish champions for another five seasons, keeping him at the club until 2020.
