What Apple Watch pre-orders tell us about their buyers

1Most people ordered the blander colors of the Apple Watch and got the watch in the larger size.
2Apple Watch is appealing to early adopters who are (no surprise) big fans of the Apple brand.
3Apple received about 1 million pre-orders for the wearable tech last Friday, according to consumer spending research firm Slice Intelligence.
4Friday marked the first day the watch was available to order online.
5Apple expects to start shipping on April 24.
6So what can the initial orders tell us about the Apple Watch consumer?
7Nearly 72 percent of Apple Watch customers bought an Apple product within the past two years.
8Of course, that makes perfect sense considering the watch works best in conjunction with the iPhone.
9And for many fans it was in for a dime, in for a dollar.
10Almost 43 percent of those ordering Apple's latest MacBook also bought an Apple Watch.
11Nearly two-thirds of the pre-orders were for the least expensive model, the aluminum Sport line starting at $349.
12Almost one-third of the orders were for the stainless steel Apple Watch, starting at $549.
13The Edition gold watch series, with its $10,000 price tag, didn't register enough buyers to make a dent in the report.
14Apple has two stores in Orlando and one in Altamonte Springs.
