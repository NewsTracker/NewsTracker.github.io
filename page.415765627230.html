Germanwings plane crash: how the cockpit door lock works

1Evidence from the Germanwings cockpit voice recorder indicates that the co-pilot locked the captain out of the cockpit.
2Here is how the aircraft crew is supposed to lock and unlock the cockpit door, based on a video by Airbus, the manufacturer of the plane.
3The cockpit door locks by default when it is closed.
4If a crew member wants to enter the cockpit, he or she must first call the pilot using an intercom.
5The crew member then presses the pound key on the codepad, setting off a buzzer inside the cockpit.
6On the cockpit pedestal next to the pilot is a door-control toggle.
7The pilot sets the toggle to the "unlock" position to let the crew member in.
8If the pilot is incapacitated, the door lock can be overridden using a special emergency code on the keypad.
9However, the pilot, or someone else present in the cockpit, can prevent access by moving the door toggle to the "lock" position, which disables emergency access for five minutes.
