1CAIRO - A car bomb killed Egypt's top prosecutor on Monday, ripping through his convoy in a Cairo neighborhood, in the first assassination of a top official in the country in a quarter century, marking an apparent escalation by Islamic militants in their campaign of revenge attacks for a 2-year-old crackdown on the Muslim Brotherhood.
2Hisham Barakat led the widescale prosecution against figures from the Brotherhood and other Islamists, including former President Mohammed Morsi, who was ousted by the military in July 2013.
3The crackdown against the group has seen the courts handing down mass death sentences against Morsi and other Islamists.
4Militants who for years had been fighting in Egypt's Sinai Peninsula, widened their insurgency after the military's ouster of Morsi, which was prompted by massive protests against his rule.
5Egyptian authorities accuse the Brotherhood of involvement in the violence and have branded it a terror group, a claim the group denies.
6Militant attacks have focused on police and the military, but in recent months have turned to target the judiciary, with the killings of several judges in Sinai earlier this year.
7The killing also recalled one of Egypt's darkest chapters in 1990s when Islamic militant groups and state security apparatus were engaged in score-settling killings for nearly a decade.
8In 1990, militants gunned down then-parliament speaker Rifaat el-Mahgoub in downtown Cairo, the last time a senior official was assassinated, though they made multiple subsequent attempts against other ministers until the insurgency was crushed in the late 1990s.
9Monday's attack took place around 10:00 a.m. when a car laden with large amount of explosives was detonated by a remote control as Barakat's car and his entourage passed through the eastern Cairo district of Heliopolis.
10The 65-year-old Barakat received multiple shrapnel wounds to the shoulder, chest and liver, according to a medical official at the nearby Nozha hospital.
11His two guards and five other people were also wounded by the explosion, officials said.
12Hours after the attack, he was announced dead after undergoing a critical surgery, according to Egypt's state news agency MENA.
13Footage from the scene of the blast showed cars charred and wrecked from the explosion, as black smoke rose from the site.
14Several trees had caught fire and firefighters were dousing the area to extinguish the flames.
15At the scene of the bombing, an Associated Reporter saw rubble and twisted metal strewn across an entire city block.
16Several charred cars blocked the street and a dozen others were wrecked or damaged.
17In nearby buildings, ground-floor shops, apartment balconies and windows were shattered several stories up from the street.
18Residents, some sobbing, were going through the debris.
19Security forces cordoned off the area shortly after the blast.
20A senior security official said that initial investigation showed that Islamic militants along with the Muslim Brotherhood group are behind the attack.
21Pro-government TV networks immediately blamed the Brotherhood.
22All the officials spoke on condition of anonymity because they were not authorized to talk to the media.
23Hours later, a lesser-known Egyptian militant group calling itself "Popular Resistance in Giza" claimed responsibility for the attack in an online statement posted on its Facebook, with photographs from the site of the bombing.
24It said it had planted an explosive device placed under Barakat's car.
25The claim could not be independently verified.
26Security officials said the bomb was not under the prosecutor's car.
27Several groups with the "Popular Resistance" name have claimed responsibility for smaller attacks, mainly targeting the police and power stations.
28The attack came at a time Egyptian security forces were already on high alert on the eve of the second anniversary of massive anti-Islamist demonstrations that paved the way, days later, for the military's ouster of Morsi.
29Two years ago on June 30, millions of Egyptians took to the streets, demanding that the Islamist Morsi step down over allegations that he abused power in favor of his Muslim Brotherhood group.
30The rallies continued until days later, the military stepped in, removed Morsi from power and jailed him at an unknown location.
31Morsi's supporters responded with a campaign of street protests that frequently descended into violence.

33Since then, authorities have carried out mass trials and handed down death sentences to hundreds of suspects.
34This has brought the Egyptian judiciary and officials under international criticism.
35They have also been targeted in attacks by Islamic militants.
36The attack on Barakat is the first major assassination attempt on a high government official since the 2013 suicide bombing targeting the then-Interior Minister Mohammed Ibrahim.
37Egypt's main Islamic militant group Ansar Beit al-Maqdis - which has pledged allegiance to the Islamic State group fighting in Iraq and Syria - has claimed responsibility for that attack.
38Now calling itself the Islamic State group's Sinai Province, the group has claimed responsibility for most of the country's major suicide bombings and assassinations.
39On Sunday, it released a video of a May attack that killed three judges in the northern Sinai Peninsula city of el-Arish - the group's main base.
