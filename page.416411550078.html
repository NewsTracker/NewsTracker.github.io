Critical-thinking counseling sought in Islamic State case

1CHICAGO (AP) - A lawyer for a suburban teenager accused of trying to join the Islamic State group has told a federal judge his client isn't dangerous and should be released to get counseling on critical thinking.
2Mohammed Hamzah Khan's attorney told a Tuesday hearing in Chicago that a psychiatrist determined Khan isn't mentally ill.
3Thomas Durkin says it's "more of an issue of education and critical thinking."
4Durkin later told reporters the irrationality the 19-year-old displayed in assessing Islamic State is a feature of his age group.
5He says that "doesn't make them crazy, it just makes them teenagers."
6A defense filing also complains Khan has received no counseling "or even a kind word" since his October arrest.
7Khan's denied attempting to provide material support to foreign terrorists.
8Prosecutors declined comment Tuesday.
