Islamic State accepts Boko Haram pledge of allegiance

1Boko Haram, meanwhile, has been weakened by a multinational force that has dislodged it from a score of northeastern Nigerian towns.
2But its new Twitter account, increasingly slick and more frequent video messages, and a new media arm all were considered signs that the group is now being helped by Isil propagandists.
3Then on Saturday, Boko Haram leader Abubakar Shekau posted an audio recording online that pledged allegiance to Isil.
4On Thursday, the Islamic State group’s media arm Al-Furqan, in an audio recording by Abu Mohammed al-Adnani, a spokesman, said that Boko Haram’s pledge of allegiance has been accepted, claiming the caliphate has now expanded to west Africa.
5Al-Adnani had urged foreign fighters from around the world to migrate and join Boko Haram.
6“We announce our allegiance to the Caliph of the Muslims ... and will hear and obey in times of difficulty and prosperity, in hardship and ease, and to endure being discriminated against, and not to dispute about rule with those in power, except in case of evident infidelity regarding that which there is a proof from Allah,” said the message.
7J. Peter Pham, director of the Africa Centre at the Atlantic Council, a think tank in Washington, noted Isil’s quick acceptance of Boko Haram’s allegiance and said that the bond highlights a new risk.
8“Militants finding it increasingly harder to get to Syria and Iraq may choose instead to go to northeastern Nigeria and internationalise that conflict,” he said.
9In the past – as was the case with Isil affiliates in Egypt, Yemen and Libya – it took weeks for the Islamic State to respond to a pledge of allegiance.
10“The prompt – one might even say ‘fast-tracked’ – acceptance by the so-called Islamic State of Boko Haram’s pledge of allegiance” underscores that both needed the propaganda boost from the affiliation, Mr Pham added.
11The Boko Haram’s pledge comes as the militants reportedly were massing in the northeastern Nigerian town of Gwoza, considered their headquarters, for a showdown with the Chadian-led multinational force.
12Boko Haram killed an estimated 10,000 people last year, and it is blamed for last April’s abduction of more than 275 schoolgirls.
13Thousands of Nigerians have fled to neighbouring Chad.
14The group is waging a nearly 6-year insurgency to impose Islamic law, or Sharia, in Nigeria.
15It began launching attacks across the border into Cameroon last year, and this year its fighters struck in Niger and Chad in retaliation to their agreement to form a multinational force to fight the militants.
16Boko Haram followed the lead of Isil in August by declaring an Islamic caliphate in northeast Nigeria that grew to cover an area the size of Belgium.
17After their blitz last year, Islamic State extremists declared a caliphate in the vast swathes of territory they control in Iraq and Syria and imposed their harsh interpretation of Islamic law.
18The Nigerian group has also followed Isil in publishing videos of beheadings.
19The latest one, published March 2, borrowed certain elements from Isil productions, such as the sound of a beating heart and heavy breathing immediately before the execution, according to SITE Intelligence Group.
