Carlos weakens off Mexico, but heavy rains and strengthening seen

1Boats damaged by winds and waves caused by hurricane Carlos are seen at a marina in Acapulco, June 14, 2015.
2MEXICO CITY Tropical storm Carlos threatened the Mexican Pacific beach resort of Acapulco with heavy rains on Sunday, and is likely to become a hurricane again as it barrels toward the coast, the U.S. National Hurricane Center said.
3Bracing for high winds, strong waves and downpours, Acapulco, the biggest city in the southwestern state of Guerrero, closed its port, and school classes were suspended in the state for Monday, local government authorities said.
4Shelters for people at risk from the rainfall were opened, and the advancing weather front has already knocked down trees and fences in parts of the state, authorities added.
5Meanwhile on the other side of the country, a low pressure system in the Gulf of Mexico was producing showers on the coast of Guatemala, Belize, and the Yucatan peninsula, and it could become a tropical storm in the next two days, the NHC said.
6On Sunday morning, Carlos was 75 miles (121 km) south-southwest of Acapulco, blowing maximum sustained winds of 70 miles per hour (113 km/h) with higher gusts, and moving to the northwest at around 2 mph (3 km/h), the Miami-based NHC said.
7The storm's forward speed is forecast to increase and Carlos should later turn toward the west-northwest, the center said.
8Carlos was a category 1 hurricane on Saturday, but was downgraded to a tropical storm after weakening overnight.
9By Monday, Carlos is expected to become a hurricane again.
10The NHC projections showed it could hit the coast near the industrial port of Manzanillo by Tuesday morning, before moving inland into the state of Jalisco the following day.
11Rain from Carlos is expected to fall in the states of Oaxaca, Guerrero, Michoacan, Colima and Jalisco, with 15 inches (38cm) possible in some areas through Tuesday, the center said.
12A hurricane watch was in effect from Punta San Telmo to Manzanillo, with storm warnings also out in Guerrero.
