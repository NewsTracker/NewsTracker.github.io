4-way tie at Memorial as Woods struggles to stay in game (Yahoo Sports)

1DUBLIN, Ohio (AP) -- Tiger Woods stuck with a swing that wasn't working and settled into a long week at the Memorial.
2Woods had to scramble on the front nine to salvage a 1-over 73 in the opening round Thursday at Muirfield Village, a strong fight and reasonable score though still nowhere near the four-way tie for the lead among the early starters.
3Harris English, Ryan Moore, Ken Duke and Brendon Todd each had a 67.
4The large group at 68 included Jordan Spieth, who saved the start of his round with a world-class short game and then made one big mistake that turned a birdie into a bogey late in his round.
5Woods, playing for only the second time since his return at the Masters, was all over the course where he has won five times.
6He made bogey on his opening hole of a PGA Tour event for the eighth time in his past nine events.
7He blocked a tee shot so far right on the 18th that it went out of bounds, and then hit a duck-hook with a 3-wood on the first hole and scrambled for par.
8Woods, at age 39, is making more changes to his swing and was sticking to them regardless of the scores on his card.
9''I was just trying to stay committed to what we're working on, to what we're doing,'' he said.
10''I hit it awful, yeah.
11So what?
12I was going to go through this phase and stick with it, keep sticking with it.
13And some of the shots I hit were really, really good.
14But then I also had some really bad shots, too.
15With an overcast sky, relatively soft conditions and not much wind, Muirfield Village was ripe for good scoring.
16English, at No.
1770 in the world and in dire need of a big week to avoid U.S. Open qualifying on Monday, bogeyed two of his opening three holes and that was the end of his mistakes.
18He ran off three straight birdies on the back nine.
19Moore did his damage early with a hybrid into 5 feet for eagle on the par-5 fifth hole, followed by a pair of birdies.
20Duke and Todd each plodded their way around.
21No one went too low, but plenty were right there.
22Francesco Molinari, Keegan Bradley and Justin Rose joined Spieth in the group at 68.
23Justin Thomas and Patrick Rodgers, roommates in south Florida and this week (along with Spieth), were in the group at 69.
24Rodgers was at Muirfield Village last year to accept the Jack Nicklaus Award as the best player in college.
25Now he's playing and has a reasonable chance of doing well enough to earn unlimited exemptions the rest of the year in a bid to earn his card next year.
26Woods grabbed the most attention for two reasons.
27He's Tiger Woods, still enough to attract the biggest gallery.
28And there remains a mystery about the game of a 79-time PGA Tour winner who has plunged to No.
29172 in the world.
30Woods has worked with Chris Como since last November, and said they implemented more changes since The Players.
31Most intriguing about his assessment of Thursday's round was a stubbornness to see the changes to a conclusion, no matter how long that takes.
32Como is the fourth instructor he has used as a pro, and this would be his fifth swing change (two under Butch Harmon).
33Previous changes have taken as long as 18 months for Woods to figure it out.
34Time is no longer on his side, however, not at his age and with five surgeries behind him.
35''I've gone through phases like this, rounds like this, where yeah, it's easy to revert back and go ahead and hit some old pattern,'' he said.
36''But it doesn't do you any good going forward.
37And I've done it.
38Sometimes it's taken me about a year and then it kicked in and I did pretty good after that.
39... If you believe in it, do it.
40And eventually it will start turning.
41The competition has never been stronger, starting with the 21-year-old Spieth.
42The Masters champion salvaged his round early.
43He didn't hit a fairway or a green until his fifth hole, yet he saved par each time, including a tough wedge with his back against a tree from 50 yards out to 7 feet to save par.
44He was tied for the lead when he played hybrid for his second shot to the par-5 seventh, even though he felt the wind shift.
45Spieth misplayed a tough wedge into a bunker and wound up making bogey.
46''Kind of a tough finish to swallow after such a great round of golf,'' Spieth said.
47Woods could have been a lot worse off except for a bogey-free front nine with three birdies.
48It was hard work for one day, not to lead but to hang on.
49''Physically, I feel good.
