Bali Nine duo executions set for 3am

1The executions of Myuran Sukumaran, Andrew Chan and seven others will be held after midnight local time, (0300 AEST Wednesday), the Attorney-General HM Prasetyo has confirmed.
2"Nine death row convicts will be executed tonight, later after midnight," he told reporters in Jakarta on Tuesday night.
3Earlier on Tuesday, Mr Prasetyo had told reporters he didn't want to give a time for the executions for "strategic" reasons.
4Salvation Army minister David Soper and minister Christie Buckingham will give Chan and Sukumaran solace in their last moments and give them their last rites.
5They will not witness the execution by firing squad, in which the Sydney men can elect to stand, sit or kneel.
6Australia's Consul General to Bali, Majell Hind, and lawyer Julian McMahon are also on Nusakambangan for official duties.
7Ms Hind will receive the bodies of Chan and Sukumaran and take legal responsibility for them on the island.
8They will be driven to Jakarta by local ambulance with an Australian consular officer following the execution.
9The Australians' bodies will then be flown back to Sydney.
10Asked about a joint statement by the EU, France and Australia urging Indonesia to cease its executions, Mr Prasetyo cited Indonesia's legal sovereignty.
11"What we fight is the serious crime of drugs."
12Cancelling the executions would show that Indonesia was soft on drugs, he said.
