Police carry 2 boxes from home of millionaire Robert Durst

1Durst, 71, is charged with murder in a killing in California 15 years ago, and has been suspected - but never charged - in the disappearance of his first wife in New York.
2In 2003, he was acquitted of murder in a dismemberment death in Texas.
3His arrest in New Orleans came shortly before the finale of an HBO series about his links to the three killings.
4Seven officers spent hours Tuesday searching Durst's home in a 17-story condominium building in a posh Houston neighborhood.
5About 8:30 p.m. the officers, including one wearing a Los Angeles Police Department badge, carried two white cardboard document boxes to six vehicles and left.
6Robert Durst is transported from Orleans Parish Criminal District Court to the Orleans Parish Prison after his arraignment in New Orleans, Tuesday, March 17, 2015.
7Durst was rebooked on charges of being a convicted felon in possession of a firearm, and possession of a weapon with a controlled dangerous substance, a small amount of marijuana.
8The officers declined to comment, except for one who confirmed they were done.
9FBI spokeswoman Shauna Dunlap confirmed that FBI agents participated in the operation at the request of the Los Angeles Police Department.
10Harris County district attorney's office spokesman Jeff McShan said the Los Angeles Police Department contacted his office last week.
11Neither Dunlap nor McShan would elaborate on what was happening inside the building and referred questions to Los Angeles police.
12LAPD spokesman Sgt. Barry Montgomery said the department is not commenting until Durst is in its custody.
13Durst is being held in Louisiana, where he faces drug and weapons charges, and it's not clear how soon he will be returned to California.
14"This is an investigation that's being handled by multi-jurisdictions," Montgomery said.
15"At this juncture the only thing the LAPD is doing is waiting for the extradition. We are just waiting on him to make it into our custody."
16Durst, 71, was charged Monday in Los Angeles with first-degree murder in the shooting of Susan Berman, his confidante and the daughter of a prominent Las Vegas mobster, in Beverly Hills.
17He could face the death penalty under special circumstances that allege he ambushed her and murdered a witness to a crime.
18Dick DeGuerin, Durst's longtime Houston defense attorney, said he was baffled by what investigators could be looking for in the condo building.
19In the documentary "The Jinx: The Life and Deaths of Robert Durst" that just wrapped up, Durst mumbled about how he "killed them all," providing a dramatic kick to the end of the series.
20But a law enforcement official said his arrest on the murder charge was based on words he wrote.
21Analysis linked a letter Durst wrote to Berman a year before her killing with one that pointed police to her body, and that was the key new evidence in the long-dormant investigation into the 2000 killing, the official not authorized to speak publicly told The Associated Press on the condition of anonymity because the investigation was ongoing.
22Bob Martin, who has lived with his wife, Carol, in the Houston building for 1 years, said he would occasionally say hello to Durst and discuss the real estate market with him.
23He described Durst as no quirkier than anyone else in the building.
24He added that Durst would occasionally attend the building's homeowners' association meetings and that the building staff reported Durst was courteous to them, as well.
25Durst waived extradition in New Orleans, but authorities there charged him with being a felon in possession of a gun and with having marijuana.
26Assistant District Mark Burton said investigators found more than a quarter-pound of pot and a revolver in his hotel room.
27DeGuerin said he wants a hearing in Louisiana as soon as possible to contest the arrest.
28"The warrant we believe is based on a television show and not on actual fact," he said.
29"We want a hearing as quickly as possible so Mr. Durst can go to California and face trial as quickly as possible."
30The judge in New Orleans scheduled another hearing for next Monday.
