Jason Brown of New York arrested again after having license suspended 41 times

Egypt parliamentary poll set for delay after court ruling

1A Suffolk County police officer pulled Jason Brown over on Saturday afternoon in Yaphank after noticing the suspect's minivan was being driven erratically.
2Police say even though Brown gave a fake name to Highway Patrol Officer Matthew DeWitt, the officer determined the suspect's New York State driver's license had been suspended 41 times.
3Officer DeWitt also 'observed marijuana in plain view within the van'.
4At that point, Brown, 36, was taken to the 5th Precinct in Patchogue for processing.
5During processing, Officer DeWitt 'discovered Brown had hidden on his person quantities of heroin and cocaine'.
6Brown, of Mastic Beach, was charged with aggravated unlicensed operation of a motor vehicle, criminal impersonation, unlawful possession of marijuana and two counts of criminal possession of a controlled substance.
7After being held at the precinct overnight, Brown was arraigned at First District Court in Central Islip.
1Cairo: Egypt's parliamentary poll looks set to be delayed after a court ruled part of an election law was unconstitutional and the main election committee said it was working on a new timetable for the long-awaited vote.
2President Abdel Fattah al-Sisi directed his government to amend the law within one month.
3The first phase of the poll is due to start on March 22.
4The election is the final step in a political roadmap the army announced in July 2013.
5Another court ruling later this month will decide definitively whether or not the elections would be delayed.
6The latest development highlights Egypt's rocky path towards democracy since the ousting of Islamist president Mohamed Morsi in 2013 by the army after mass protests against the rule of Egypt's first democratically elected leader.
7Egypt has been without a parliament since June 2012 when a court dissolved the democratically elected main chamber, reversing a major accomplishment of the 2011 uprising that toppled autocrat Hosni Mubarak.
8"The committee will prepare a new timetable for [election] measures after the legislative amendments are issued," the Supreme Election Committee said.
9Earlier, the Supreme Constitutional Court said an article in a law defining electoral districts was unconstitutional.
10Zaid al-Ali, a senior adviser with intergovernmental organisation International IDEA, said there was "little question" the elections would be delayed.
11Drafters of the legislation would need time to understand the ruling, prepare a new version of the law, allow time for review and for any new legal challenges before the election.
12"If they decide not to wait, then they will run the risk of the new version of the law being declared unconstitutional after the elections, which would lead to yet another dissolution, which would be a disaster," Mr Ali said.
13Egyptian leaders say the election shows their commitment to democracy.
14Critics say Mr Sisi, who as army chief toppled Dr Morsi, has undermined freedoms gained after the uprising that ended Mr Mubarak's 30-year rule.
15The People's Assembly is made up of 567 seats, with 420 elected as individuals and 120 through winner-takes-all lists with quotas for women, Christians and youth.
16The remaining seats are appointed by the president.
17Several opposition political parties had announced they would boycott the election.
18Some say the emphasis on individuals was a throwback to Mubarak-era politics, which often favoured candidates with wealth and family connections.
