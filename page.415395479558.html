Two officers shot outside Ferguson police HQ

1Two police officers were shot during a protest outside the Ferguson, Missouri police headquarters early on Thursday.
2This comes hours after the city's police chief Thomas Jackson quit following a damning justice report into his department.
3St. Louis County Police Chief Jon Belmar told reporters that a 41-year-old officer from his department was struck in the shoulder and a 32-year-old officer from the nearby Webster Groves Police Department was hit in the face around midnight as the crowd was starting to break up.
4He said he did not know the conditions of the officers, whom he did not identify, but said they were both conscious and being treated at a local hospital.
5"These police officers were standing there and they were shot, just because they were police officers," Belmar said.
6The St. Louis County Police Department Twitter account had earlier said both of the shot officers were from their department.
7A few dozen demonstrators scrambled following the sound of gunfire around midnight, with some screaming.
8"They hit a cop," according to a Reuters photographer at the scene.
9Protesters had called for Jackson's removal since the fatal shooting of unarmed black 18-year-old Michael Brown by white Ferguson police officer Darren Wilson on August 9, 2014.
10The killing triggered nationwide protests and drew scrutiny to police use of deadly force, especially against black men.
11Neither a grand jury nor the federal probe led to charges against Wilson.
12Jackson's departure was the latest in a string of officials who have stepped down following a scathing report from the US Justice Department that found widespread racially biased abuses in the city's police department and municipal court.
