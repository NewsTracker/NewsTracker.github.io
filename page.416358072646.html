Jordan, at center of fight against Islamic militants, hosts competition of anti-terror squads

1Jordan is hosting a competition of elite anti-terrorism squads from 18 countries, including fellow members of the military coalitions fighting rebels in Yemen and Islamic State extremists in Iraq and Syria.
2The competition opened Sunday with a drill by Jordanian special forces rescuing hostages from a plane and rappelling from a helicopter.
3AMMAN, Jordan - Jordan on Sunday launched a competition among elite anti-terrorism squads from 18 countries, including fellow members of military coalitions fighting rebels in Yemen and Islamic State extremists in Iraq and Syria.
4The 7th annual Warrior Competition is being held at a time when Jordan is stepping up its military role in the region, as part of a U.S.-led campaign against Islamic State and a Saudi-led battle against Shiite rebels in Yemen.
5The five-day competition offers a chance for team leaders to "meet and to know each other, as the terrorists have become international," said Col.
6Khaled Abu Hamad, a spokesman for the King Abdullah Special Operations Training Center on the outskirts of Amman, where the event is being held.
7The competition opened with a drill by Jordanian special forces who rescued mock-hostages from a passenger plane and rappelled from a helicopter into a five-story building to chase militants.
8The opening ceremony also included skydivers and snipers.
9Teams compete in 10 events, including shooting.
10Among those participating are the U.S., Russia, Saudi Arabia, Afghanistan, Bahrain and China - which won the counter-terrorism competition in 2014.
11Jordan assumed a more high-profile role in the coalition against the Islamic State group after the militants released a video in February showing a captured Jordanian pilot being immolated while trapped in a cage.
12Jordan's army chief, Brig.
13Gen. Mashal Zaben, said Sunday that the killing of the pilot "unmasked the ugly faces of those outlaws."
