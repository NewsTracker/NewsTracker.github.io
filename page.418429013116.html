Facebook seeking deal to add music videos

1Over the past few years, Facebook has slowly encroached on YouTube's territory as a go-to outlet for online video, showing popular content from publishers like BuzzFeed and Vice, and a growing number of user-loaded clips.
2Now Facebook is considering going after another piece of YouTube's signature turf: music videos.
3In recent weeks, Facebook has held preliminary discussions with the major record companies, seeking licensing deals to insert music videos into Facebook users' feeds, according to four people briefed on the talks who spoke on the condition of anonymity.
4According to these people, Facebook has proposed adding a limited number of music videos, all of which would be chosen by the labels, to the feeds.
5Facebook has told the labels that it wants to begin adding the videos in the next few months and monitor their performance through the end of the year, the sources said.
6Facebook would share advertising revenue with the labels according to terms that have not yet been set.
7Facebook declined to comment on its video plans, first reported in detail by Billboard.
8But the Menlo Park company directly denied another report from Music Ally, a news site, that said Facebook was working on a streaming service comparable to Apple Music or Spotify.
9"We have no plans to go into music streaming," a Facebook representative said.
10Facebook's plans are said to still be in an early stage.
11But its talks with record companies highlight the company's eagerness to expand its video offerings and compete more aggressively with YouTube, whose most popular content is music videos.
12Given Facebook's vast size - it has 1.4 billion users - it is one of the few online outlets that could seriously challenge YouTube's dominance, media executives say.
13Facebook has offered the music labels better revenue-sharing deals than YouTube, according to one of the people briefed on the talks.
14Facebook is also promising to police the platform more thoroughly for unauthorized content, this person added.
15Music is just the latest video-related frontier for Facebook.
16For months, the company has been urging publishers like BuzzFeed, Vice, CNN and ESPN to publish original videos directly to its platform, and it has been satisfied with letting those publishers post the clips on sites like YouTube and Vimeo and then link to them from Facebook.
17It has also encouraged its users to upload their own videos.
18But by building up its video platform, Facebook could keep users within its system and capture more advertising revenue.
19The strategy seems to be working: Facebook had an estimated 315 billion video views in the first quarter of 2015, according to Ampere Analysis, an independent video analytics firm.
20If Facebook were able to build a stronger presence for music videos, it would put pressure on YouTube, which is owned by Google and has long had a complex relationship with the music industry.
21YouTube, by most accounts, is the most popular online outlet for young people to consume music.
22Yet its royalty rates are considered low, and record executives have frequently complained that by making virtually all music available free, YouTube - along with Spotify - has made it more difficult for paid services to grow.
23By hosting music videos, Facebook would also compete somewhat with Vevo, a company set up by Universal and Sony for their music videos and other official content, which is accessible through YouTube and other platforms.
24By offering only professionally made videos, Vevo is able to reap higher ad rates than YouTube can for its typical videos, but most of Vevo's viewers still reach it through YouTube.
25Warner Music, the third major music label, is not a part of Vevo.
26Universal, Sony and Warner Music declined to talk about the discussions with Facebook.
27Although Facebook plays a powerful role in the music world as a promotional outlet for artists, it has not been a major outlet for music itself.
28The company has considered various approaches to this problem for years, according to people involved in Facebook's internal discussions, vacillating between ideas such as potentially acquiring Spotify to forming partnerships with these companies.
29Four years ago, Facebook teamed with online media services like Spotify and Netflix, letting those companies' customers use their Facebook accounts to alert friends to what they were watching or listening to.
30The integration helped the outlets grow, but also drew complaints from Facebook users about the resulting river of notifications.
