Kenya: Survivor found in school attack which killed 148

1Cynthia Cheroitich, 19, told The Associated Press from her hospital gurney that she hid in a large cupboard and covered herself with clothes, refusing to emerge even when some of her classmates came out of hiding at the demands of the gunmen from the al-Shabab group.
2She was rescued shortly before 10 a.m., according to Kenyan officials.
3Cheroitich said she didn't believe that rescuers urging her to come out of her hiding place were there to help, suspecting at first that they were militants.
4"How do I know that you are the Kenyan police?" she said she asked them.
5Only when Kenyan security forces had one of her teachers appeal to her did she come out, she said.
6"I was just praying to my God," Cheroitich, a Christian, said of her ordeal.
7Cheroitich appeared tired and thirsty, sipping on yoghurt and a soft drink, but otherwise seemed in good health.
8She said she drank a lotion because she was so thirsty and hungry while in hiding.
9The good news that Cheroitich survived the attack came as grieving Kenyans gathered in Nairobi to view the bodies of family members killed in the Garissa attack.
10Five people have been arrested on suspicion of involvement in the Garissa attack, a Kenyan official said.
11Kenyan security agencies arrested three people trying to cross into Somalia, said Interior Ministry spokesman Mwenda Njoka in a Twitter post.
12He said the three are associates of Mohamed Mohamud, also known as Dulyadin Gamadhere, a former teacher at a Kenyan Madrassa Islamic school who authorities say coordinated the Garissa attack.
13Kenyan authorities have put a $220,000 bounty for information leading to Gamadhere's arrest.
14The three arrests brings to five the number of suspects arrested in relation to the Garissa attack as two suspects were arrested at the college.
15The Islamic militant group issued a statement which said the attack on Garissa college was in retaliation for killings carried out by Kenyan troops fighting the rebels in Somalia.
16The attack on the college in northeastern Kenya on Thursday killed 148 people.
17"No amount of precaution of safety measures will be able to guarantee your safety, thwart another attack or prevent another bloodbath," said the statement.
18"Kenyan cities will run red with blood ... This will be a long, gruesome war of which you, the Kenyan public, are its first casualties," said the statement, issued on Shabab affiliated webites and Twitter accounts.
