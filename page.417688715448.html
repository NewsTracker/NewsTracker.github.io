EU lawmakers urge FIFA's Blatter to go now

1Sepp Blatter addresses a news conference at the FIFA headquarters in Zurich, Switzerland June 2, 2015.
2BRUSSELS EU lawmakers called on FIFA President Sepp Blatter on Thursday to step down immediately, saying that his failure to do so would stall urgent reforms to rid soccer's governing body of its "rampant, systemic and deep-rooted" corruption.
3The European Parliament voted by a clear show of hands in favor of an eight-page resolution with a series of demands to clean up the sport.
4The resolution said that the parliament welcomed Blatter's decision to resign last week, but expressed "serious concern" that he might stay on for a further nine months before a new leader is appointed.
5Urgent reforms to root out corruption and restore FIFA's credibility could not begin in earnest until then, it said.
6"The European Parliament... therefore calls on FIFA to select, in a transparent and inclusive way, an appropriate interim leader to replace Joseph Blatter forthwith."
7The parliament said that corruption within FIFA had seriously damaged the integrity of global football, with a "devastating impact" from the top level of professional soccer to amateur grassroots clubs.
8It is also calling for FIFA to publish in full the report produced by former U.S. prosecutor Michael Garcia into the 2018 and 2022 World Cup bidding processes.
9The parliament said it welcomed comments from the chairman of FIFA's audit and compliance committee, Domenico Scala, that Russia and Qatar could be stripped of World Cup hosting rights if evidence emerged of bribery in the bidding process.
10Host countries, it said, should abide by international standards in respect of fundamental rights.
11During a debate on Wednesday, British Labour MEP Neena Gill said she had little confidence that any reforms in FIFA would actually happen.
