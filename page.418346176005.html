Microsoft's Xbox Music Renamed Groove Music in Upcoming Windows 10

1This article, Microsoft s Xbox Music renamed Groove in upcoming Windows 10, originally appeared on CNET.com.
2Microsoft has changed the name of Xbox Music to Groove.
3Microsoft is rebranding its current Xbox Music service to Groove, and will start rolling it out with Windows 10 PCs as of July 29.
4Those currently subscribing to Xbox Music Pass don t need to do anything; they ll automatically get Groove Music Pass.
5The renamed Microsoft Groove service costs $9.99 a month, or $99 per year.
6Windows 10, the upcoming version of Microsoft s operating system, is set to start rolling out at the end of this month.
7Xbox Video, as Windows Insiders already know, also is being renamed to Movies & TV.
8Windows Insiders will be able to test Groove once Microsoft releases the next Windows 10 preview build for PCs, according to a Microsoft blog post Monday.
9After Twitter user @TimN_FL discovered a new page that referred to Xbox Music Pass as Groove Music Pass earlier Monday, a number of us Microsoft watchers were wondering what was in the works.
10After all, there s already a Windows Phone/Windows 8 music app in the Store called Groove: Smart Music Player.
11That app, developed by Canadian startup Zikera, is also available for iOS.
12The Groove player app from Zikera that exists today is powered by Last.fm.
13(Last.fm is a part of CBS, which also owns CNET.)
14But it seems the new Microsoft Groove and Groove Music Pass have nothing to do with Zikera.
15When I asked if Microsoft had bought Zikera, a Microsoft spokesperson said "we have nothing more to share at this time."
16There s nothing on Zikera s site that indicates they have been bought by Microsoft.
17This story originally posted as "Microsoft rebrands Xbox Music as Groove" on ZDNet.
