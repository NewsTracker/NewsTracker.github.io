Busch grabs elusive Indy victory as comeback continues (Yahoo Sports)

1INDIANAPOLIS (AP) -- There was no celebratory burnout - Kyle Busch has done plenty of those the last few weeks - just another big, fat kiss at Indianapolis Motor Speedway.
2Busch's triumphant return following a horrible crash the day before the season-opening Daytona 500 continued Sunday with one of the biggest wins of his career.
3He grabbed an elusive victory at Indy, where Busch won the Brickyard 400 for a weekend sweep at the historic track.
4Busch missed the first 11 races of the season with a broken right leg and broken left foot.
5He returned in late May and has won three consecutive Sprint Cup Series races and four of the last five.
6''Being away for 11 weeks, it was tough.
7It was pretty hard times,'' Busch said.
8''It was tough to fight through all those things that I had to go through, Samantha had to go through while she was pregnant.
9She was helping me.
10All the steps that we went through to get back into the race car were quite challenging.
11''But once we've been back, I felt like I just continued right on my stride.
12This has been a phenomenal return.
13Now he has a prestigious Brickyard victory to give him one of NASCAR's elusive crown jewels.
14His Indianapolis victory ranks alongside his Southern 500 win at Darlington Raceway as the biggest of his career.
15''Maybe I found my happy place,'' Busch said in victory lane when asked if he has found a new perspective since he was injured in a crash the day before the season-opening Daytona.
16Busch, who also won the second-tier Xfinity Series race Saturday at Indianapolis, moved 23 points away from cracking the top 30 in the standings.
17NASCAR granted him a waiver that will make him eligible for the Chase for the Sprint Cup championship should he be ranked inside the top 30 in points.
18''We're a championship contending team, we just need to be championship eligible,'' Busch said.
19The win for Joe Gibbs Racing was the first Sprint Cup Series victory at Indianapolis for Toyota, which has now won at all 23 active tracks.
20Toyota has won the last six national series races dating back to Busch's win at Kentucky, and JGR has won four consecutive poles and four consecutive races.
21Chevrolet had entered the race on a 12-year winning streak and had won 16 of the 21 Brickyards.
22The manufacturer also won the Indianapolis 500 in May with Roger Penske driver Juan Pablo Montoya.
23But Penske was denied his first Brickyard 400 win when Joey Logano finished second to Busch on Sunday in a Ford.
24''Geez, I guess Kyle's back,'' Daytona 500 winner Logano said.
25''It's just so frustrating running second at the Brickyard.
26Busch knows that all too well.
27He finished second in two of the previous three Brickyards.
28Kevin Harvick was third in a Chevrolet, followed by Martin Truex Jr., Busch teammate Denny Hamlin and Clint Bowyer.
29Matt Kenseth, another Gibbs driver, was seventh, followed by Kurt Busch and Kyle Larson.
30Penske driver Brad Keselowski rounded out the top 10.
31Jeff Gordon's final Brickyard 400 was a huge bust as an early spin caused considerable damage that eventually sent him to the garage for repairs.
32Gordon, who has a record five victories at Indianapolis, finished 42nd.
33He's retiring at the end of the year.
34''It was disappointing,'' Gordon said.
35''I have had an amazing career here in Indy.
36The fans have been spectacular.
37Two-time Brickyard winner Tony Stewart was strong early but strategy backfired on the Indiana native and he finished 28th.
38NASCAR used a track-specific rules package Sunday that was designed to improve the racing on a track that has proven to be incredibly hard to pass at since the heavy stock cars began racing on the Brickyard.
39But the low-drag package didn't appear to be much of an improvement as drivers complained all weekend that the turbulence was too strong around their car when they'd close in on another.
40In the end, there were 16 lead changes among six drivers and Harvick, who called Sunday's race ''a science project,'' led a race-high 75 laps.
41''I think everybody put in a lot of effort to really try to make everything a lot better, spent a lot of money, but I don't know that we accomplished everything that we were looking to accomplish,'' Harvick said.
42Aside from the lack of improved competition, the cars were extremely hot inside the cockpit because the aerodynamic package was trapping air inside.
43Busch was one of many drivers physically drained following Saturday's race, and Sunday's race seemed to have the same consequence.
44This same rules package is scheduled to be used next month at Michigan.
45''I got a huge blister on my foot,'' Logano said.
46''I think the extended bumper cover on the back of these things doesn't let the airflow underneath the car like it used to without it, and I think it builds the inside temperature up.
47I've got sweat in my eyes.
48''It's just too hot inside those cars.''
