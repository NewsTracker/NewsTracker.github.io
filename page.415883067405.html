Patriots owner: Aaron Hernandez told me he was innocent (Yahoo Sports)

1(AP) -- New England Patriots team owner Robert Kraft testified Tuesday that his former star tight end Aaron Hernandez told him he was innocent when asked if he was involved in a 2013 killing.
2Kraft was called by the prosecution in Hernandez's murder trial over the June 17, 2013, slaying of Odin Lloyd, who was dating the sister of Hernandez's fiancee.
3He at times seemed uncomfortable on the stand, even when he was asked where he worked.
4He first said 1 Patriot Place, the address of Gillette Stadium, where the Patriots play.
5When asked what he did at work, he replied, ''Whatever they ask me to do.'' Then, asked if he ran a business, he replied: ''We're a packaging and paper business and private equity, and we have two sports teams.'' He first listed the New England Revolution soccer team, then the Patriots.
6Kraft was asked about the events of June 19, two days after the killing.
7By then, Kraft said, there was a strong media presence at Gillette Stadium, including helicopters, which were covering the investigation.
8Kraft said he found Hernandez in a weight room working out and pulled him into an adjacent office for a private talk.
9''I understood there was an incident that had transpired, and I wanted to know whether he was involved, and if he was, any player that comes into our system, I consider part of our extended family, and I wanted to get him help,'' he said.
10''What did he say?'' prosecutor William McCauley asked.
11''He said he was not involved.
12That he was innocent and that he hoped that the time of the murder incident came out because he said he was in a club,'' Kraft said.
13Prosecutors have said Hernandez was at a bar earlier in the evening, then drove to Boston with two friends, picked up Lloyd and killed him in an industrial park.
14Kraft said his conversation with Hernandez lasted five to 10 minutes.
15Later, he saw him one last time at the stadium.
16''He hugged and kissed me and thanked me for my concern,'' Kraft said.
17Hernandez signed a $40 million contract with the Patriots in 2012, but as defense lawyer Michael Fee questioned him, Kraft said he couldn't remember whether it ran through the 2018 season.
18Hernandez watched closely during Kraft's testimony, which lasted a little over 30 minutes.
19Next to the stand was the Patriots' director of security, Mark Briggs.
20He said he also had a conversation with Hernandez on June 19.
21The judge struck the comment from the record and instructed the jury that citizens do not have any obligation to speak with police.
22Briggs said Hernandez told him that he had been with Lloyd at a club and they went their separate ways, so Hernandez gave him keys to a vehicle.
23He told him those keys were found in Lloyd's pocket, Briggs said.
24Investigators did find keys to an SUV Hernandez rented in Lloyd's pocket.
25Briggs said he asked Hernandez if he was involved in Lloyd's killing, and Hernandez replied no.
26He said he then looked in his eyes and asked if he was telling the truth.
27''He swore on his baby's life that he was telling the truth,'' Briggs said.
28Briggs said the following day, Hernandez showed up at Gillette Stadium, and Briggs asked him to leave, which Hernandez did.
29''You asked him to leave the stadium because his presence there was bad for business?'' Fee asked.
30''That is correct,'' Briggs replied.
31Hernandez was arrested June 26 in Lloyd's slaying.
32Less than two hours later, he was cut from the team.
33Also Tuesday, lawyers said they expect to wrap up their cases next week.
34McCauley told the judge he expected to rest Thursday.
35Hernandez lawyer James Sultan then asked the judge to hear arguments on some pending issues Friday and said he expected to put on witnesses and finish Monday.
36Both sides will also deliver closing arguments, then deliberations will begin.
