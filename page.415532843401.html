Facebook to Let Messenger Users Send Cash to Friends

1Facebook is letting users of its Messenger app send money to friends.
2Facebook is giving the more than 500 million users of its Messenger app the ability to send money to each other, becoming the latest mobile platform to incorporate a payments service.
3Once users link a Visa or Mastercard debit card to their Messenger account, they can send their friends money by tapping a dollar sign in the chat box.
4Facebook said the feature will become available to U.S. users in the coming months.
5The service will be free, and Facebook said it has no plans to monetize it as this time.
6The feature isn't exactly a surprise.
7Last summer, PayPal chief David Marcus joined Facebook to become vice president of messaging products.
8A few months later, technology blog TechCrunch posted what it reported were hacked screenshots of a payments feature within Facebook Messenger.
9Facebook has processed payments on its larger main site for years, but it doesn't let users send money to each other.
10The company's WhatsApp messenger app, with more than 700 million users, also doesn't have a payments feature.
11There are now a host of options to pay by text or email, however.
12Mobile-payments company Square has a Cash feature that allows users to send money to others for free over email with a debit card.
13Ephemeral messaging service Snapchat introduced a service in November called Snapcash that lets users 18 years or older to send money to anyone using the app free of charge.
14That tool uses Square to transmit the funds.
15EBay, through its PayPal subsidiary, has been offering consumer-to-consumer payment methods for years, including via email and through its app.
16Its Venmo unit, which it acquired in the $800 million deal for payment firm Braintree in late 2013, has become a popular option for transferring funds among college students.
17Venmo's service is free, though it takes a fee for transfers using a credit card.
18Facebook said it will handle all the payments processed on Messenger.
19The social networking giant said it currently processes more than one million transactions daily for game players and advertisers.
