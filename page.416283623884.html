Microsoft, Yahoo amend search deal

1The 10-year search partnership, crafted by former Microsoft CEO Steve Ballmer and former Yahoo CEO Carol Bartz, allowed the companies to amend or terminate it after five years.
2Last month, the companies extended by 30 days the deadline to renegotiate the deal.
3Earlier, Microsoft controlled how Bing displayed search results on Yahoo websites accessed on PCs.
4Microsoft will now own the ads delivered from its own Bing Ads platform.
5Yahoo was responsible for sales for Bing search ads.
6Microsoft and Yahoo plan to transition sales responsibilities starting this summer.
7Originally, the deal also included a revenue-sharing agreement where Microsoft paid Yahoo a percentage of Bing ads revenue delivered from Yahoo searches.
8This structure would remain intact, the companies said.
9Yahoo and Microsoft shares were down marginally in late-morning trading.
