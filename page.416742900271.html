Baltimore Police: Man Arrested Near Scene of Recent Riots

1Days after riots crippled Baltimore, tensions again escalated after police said they arrested a man who appeared to be armed in a scuffle that drew a crowd of protesters who chanted "black community control the police!"
2Lt. Col.
3Melvin Russell said police pursued a man who was spotted on surveillance cameras and appeared to be armed with a handgun.
4Police said the man was taken into custody after a brief chase, during which a gunshot was heard.
5Russell said that police never fired their weapons and that no one was shot.
6Police recovered a handgun loaded with three rounds, one of which was spent.
7The suspect was not injured and did not want to be transported to a hospital but was taken to the hospital anyway in an ambulance, he said.
8Live television coverage and photos tweeted from the scene showed medics putting a man in an ambulance and a large police presence, with officers lining up to apparently block one street.
9The arrest happened in the same area where police first spotted Freddie Gray on April 12.
10He was arrested and fatally injured in police custody.
11Six officers were charged Friday in Gray's death.
12A few dozen police officers wearing helmets and carrying shields formed a line across North Avenue as crowds gathered across the street, occasionally shouting "control the police" at the officers.
13Minutes after police briefed reporters, the line of officers left the street and police began to allow traffic to get through the intersection.
14Meanwhile, about a dozen protesters briefly blocked traffic while chanting "Kill the killer cop!" and "Black community control the police!"
15State Sen. Catherine Pugh, who was nearby and arrived at the scene shortly after the incident, said she was trying to sort through conflicting narratives of what happened.
16She said witnesses insisted that police had shot someone, which police denied.
17She said police did a good job defusing tensions after the incident by quickly breaking down a barricade and scaling back the number of officers on the scene.
18But she said police should consider using different enforcement tactics while tensions are still high.
19"I think we need to keep police presence down at this time, especially in this area," said Pugh, a Democrat who represents west Baltimore.
20"I think we ought to just dial it back a little. Let's give the community a chance to heal."
21James Carter, manager of Metro PCS cellphone store, said the incident happened about 5 feet from his shop on North Avenue.
22He heard a single gunshot and waited to hear another, but didn't.
23Carter said he looked outside and saw no sign of a gun on the sidewalk.
24He went back into his shop, came back out and then saw a gun on the ground.
25He said he also saw a young African-American man lying on the ground.
26Carter said he saw no blood.
27He said the young man yelled as he was put into the ambulance.
28Carters said people are "extremely" jumpy.
29This is the biggest police presence at Pennsylvania and North avenues since Saturday, the last night of the curfew, he said.
