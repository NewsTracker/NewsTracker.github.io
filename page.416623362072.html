24To those on the outside, Baltimore is best known for crab shacks on the Chesapeake, the cobblestone walkways of Fells Point, a vintage baseball stadium, the retro weirdness of John Waters.
25But there is another side of Baltimore that is far less charming.
26And on Monday, April 27, 2015, that side burned.
27Vast swaths of east and west Baltimore were consumed by flames and anger.
28With tempers still smoldering in riot-torn Baltimore and nearby neighborhoods clearing out rubble and debris, the Orioles played a baseball game Wednesday.
29This wasn't for the fans, because there weren't any at Camden Yards.
30The game was held behind closed doors, and the Orioles and Chicago White Sox played because it was considered to be the best way to fill out the schedule for both teams.
31The timing worked for baseball, not so much for Baltimore.
32It was an unusual move by Major League Baseball, which usually errs on the side of caution in the wake of tragedy.
33Baseball games were canceled after riots ignited in Los Angeles and terrorists attacked New York and Washington.
34Baseball put off the World Series in 1989 after an earthquake hit San Francisco.
35In Baltimore, after a drug store was set on fire about four miles from the ballpark and the National Guard was summoned to restore order, they played a game because this was the only planned visit by this season by the White Sox.
36The postponed games Monday and Tuesday were to be made up as part of a doubleheader on May 28, but there was seemingly nowhere to go on the schedule with Wednesday's game.
37So they moved up the starting time by five hours to 2:05 p.m. to beat the 10 o'clock curfew and had the teams go at it before 47,000 empty seats.
38Baltimore won 8-2.
39"We have a schedule so we've got to get games in," Chicago second baseman Gordon Beckham said.
40"We can't just miss all three games and expect to make them up down the line. I mean we'll have no off days for the rest of the year.
41Just about everyone who put on a uniform understood the circumstances.
42Freddie Gray, a 25-year-old black man, died of spinal cord and other injuries while in police custody.
43The protests that followed included looting, violence and rioting.
44Though no property around the ballpark was damaged, the city was hurting and here they were, playing a baseball game to preserve the integrity of the schedule.
45"It is strange. The commissioner of baseball made a decision," said Gov. Larry Hogan.
46"I'm glad that we don't have tens of thousands of fans down there. Even though things appear to be calm, having tens of thousands of people in that part of the city, that's where our command center is operating."
47On a scale of what was significant to Baltimore on this day, the Orioles' 20th game of the season wasn't exactly at the top of the list.
48"It makes you realize how unimportant really in a lot of ways this is compared to some things that are going on," Orioles manager Buck Showalter said.
49"You try to keep that mind and look at things realistically, where this fits in the scheme of things. You prioritize what's important and we tried to do that."
50Athletes are paid well to play when they're told, no matter what the sport.
51"There's nothing those guys can do about it. They're a team," said 49ers tight end Vernon Davis, who played in college at Maryland.
52"They have no control over what just happened. This is just how the world is. It has to be like this. They're going to have to play no matter what."
53Showalter hopes this game can serve as the first step toward getting Baltimore back on its feet.
54"We've made quite a statement as a city, some good and some bad," he said.
55"Now, let's get on with taking the statements we've made and create a positive. I want to be a rallying force for our city."
56Playing the game without any fans in attendance was both a good and a bad thing.
57The team didn't divert any police from doing their job around the city, but the people of Baltimore didn't get a chance to turn the page by watching the home team play at Camden Yards.
58"Sports brings people together - black, white, or any different," Orioles center fielder Adam Jones said.
59"For those three hours, you can have beers, nachos and some Boog's (barbecue) and forget about our daily lives.
60"But today, we're just going to have to play a Major League Baseball game without fans. I think that's first time in history."
61Must the game go on?
62"We're doing the right thing," Orioles first baseman Chris Davis said.
63"I'm not real happy about playing in an empty stadium. That's one of the reasons that we look forward to coming home so much, playing in front of our fans. But we also understand that there's a bigger picture here."
64That's true, but the bigger question is whether they should have been playing at all.
65"There are a lot more important issues going on outside the stadium," Orioles left-hander Zach Britton said.
66"It kind of makes you realize how small baseball is compared to some of the other issues in the U.S. and around the world."
