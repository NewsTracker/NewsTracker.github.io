Hillary Clinton Officially Running For President

1A top adviser to the former First Lady and Secretary of State made her 2016 plans official with an email message sent to veterans of her first presidential campaign.
2Minutes later, Mrs Clinton announced the details on her campaign website.
3She said in a video, uploaded to her new campaign website, that "everyday Americans need a champion. I want to be that champion".
4"I'm getting ready to do something too, I'm running for President," she said.
5"Americans have fought their way back from tough economic times but the deck is still stacked in favour of those at the top.
6"Everyday Americans need a champion, and I want to be that champion, so you can do more than just get by, you can get ahead, and stay ahead, because when families are strong, America is strong.
7Mrs Clinton is aiming to be the first female president.
8She enters the Democratic primaries in a strong position to succeed her rival from the 2008 campaign, President Barack Obama.
9Following Sunday's announcement, she's expected to travel soon to early voting states, including Iowa and New Hampshire.
10Mrs Clinton has signalled that she intends to run on strengthening economic security for the middle class and expanding opportunities for working families.
11Republicans are already trying to link Mrs Clinton to President Obama.
12They say electing her would be no different from giving Mr Obama a third term.
13Republican presidential contender Randy Paul said Mrs Clinton was a poor choice.
14He said: "Hillary Clinton represents the worst of the Washington machine - the arrogance of power, corruption and cover-up, conflicts of interest and failed leadership with tragic consequences."
15Sky's US correspondent Greg Milam, who is in Iowa ahead of an expected speech from Mrs Clinton, said: "She has kept us waiting for it and it didn't come out as expected with an announcement on social media from Hillary Clinton.
16"It came in an email from John Podesta, a veteran of the Obama White House and now part of Hillary Clinton's team.
17"It's interesting that it was him reaching out to donors, the people who will raise the expected $1bn for her campaign, getting them on side."
