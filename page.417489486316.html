Inspiration and Wisdom From Greece With the Challenge of ALS

1Greek Orthodox monk living by the Grace of God with ALS at Governeto Monastery, Chania, Crete.
2My name is Father Sophronios and I am a Greek Orthodox monk living with (Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis) ALS for the past five years on the island of Crete in Greece.
3I have great love and respect for people living with ALS (PALS) and for everyone in the wider ALS community.
4I want to share with you some of my experiences.
5Living with ALS is not easy -- that's probably the understatement of the year!
6But I have learned much from the disease.
7I am writing to you using a special program that allows me to write with my eyes.
8I am on a respirator and have a trach.
9I also have a feeding tube.
10I can't walk, talk, nor use my arms.
11So all the things I feared when I first received my diagnosis five years ago, I now live with.
12But I want you to know that I am happy and am very much alive.
13I don't consider myself exceptionally brave or strong; but, my happiness comes from a number of things, which I now share with you.
14First and foremost is my Christian Orthodox faith.
15Any strength that I have comes from God.
16And my belief in the eternal life gives me the courage to endure the difficulties I face today.
17Prayer allows me to commune with God and to look beyond the temporary condition and to contemplate the eternal condition.
18I thank God for many other things that sustain me.
19I share these with you knowing that I may be more fortunate than others, but encourage you, whether newly diagnosed or living with ALS for years, to adopt or cultivate some of things which I have found helpful.
20A supportive environment: I live in a unique environment.
21I became a monk late in life, yielding to a calling to serve God and my fellow man.
22Shortly after becoming a monk, I was diagnosed with ALS.
23I live in a peaceful community of prayer.
24The 700-year-old monastery is perched high on cliffs overlooking the blue waters of the Aegean on the North-western peninsula of Crete.
25My brother monks care for me with love, compassion, understanding and patience.
26I urge you, if possible, to create a good supportive environment.
27Loving family and friends: When I first learned of my diagnosis, I kept it inside of me for quite some time, not wanting to worry my mother, brother and friends -- what a foolish mistake!
28Once I opened up to my loved ones, the outpouring of love and affection has been a buoy which has kept me afloat ever since.
29I recommend you communicate often and early with loved ones and friends.
30Exceptional caregivers: My brother monks are not trained nurses; however, they have learned all the essentials of caring for a person with ALS.
31In addition to my brother monks, I am blessed to have a wonderful nurse who has trained my other caregivers and has provided me with exceptional care.
32At first, caring for someone may seem daunting; especially in the later stages when machines are in place, but the practical aspects of care are learnable and the key ingredient of all for exceptional care is LOVE.
33If possible, find caregivers who have compassion for others.
34Compassionate physicians: When I was at the early stages of ALS, I was able to travel and met with some of the best ALS doctors in America and England.
35I was grateful for their expertise, time,and patience.
36Here on Crete there may not be the same experience with ALS, but I have been blessed with very caring doctors.
37These doctors drive up the winding mountain roads to visit me at the monastery.
38I am grateful to all for the sensitivity, compassion and interest in making me as comfortable as possible and for meeting all my medical needs.
39Please seek out physicians who will go beyond the typical protocols and see you as a person first.
40Excellent technology: I have had the blessing to be able to have access to some great technology: a very good wheel chair; a comfortable air mattress; a wonderful computer system; and all the necessary machines to keep me breathing comfortably.
41Plan early and anticipate your future needs.
42Great nutrition: Living on Crete, I follow what is called a Mediterranean diet.
43I have mostly fresh, seasonal fruits, vegetables, lots of olive oil, yogurt from goat's milk, free-range chicken, eggs, fish and herbal teas.
44And now that I have a feeding tube, I maintain the same diet; all is well blended and easily passes through my feeding tube.
45I chose to forego the ready-made food supplements.
46I eat real foods and digest them well.
47I have regular blood work with normal, healthy results.
48I hope you will consider the importance of good nutrition.
49The beauty of nature: I am not as mobile as I used to be, but I am blessed to be in a wonderful natural setting.
50I drink up as much of the nature that surrounds me.
51I take joy in the sun's rays, the blue sky, the chirping of birds, and the view of the mountains, the sound of rainfall, and many other gifts that God presents us in His creation.
52All of these wonders take on new meaning for me.
53Many of my friends send me photographs that fill my screen with beautiful images of nature; this brings me much joy.
54Whether it be nature, music, or favorite pets surround yourself with things which give you joy.
55Communication: Although I may have lost my voice, I have not lost my ability to communicate.
56Thanks to my computer system I am in regular communication with friends and loved ones.
57I feel alive through this communication.
58I have meaning and purpose.
59I don't feel closed in or cut off.
60Please don't shut down; open up to others -- communicate.
61Prayer: I believe in the power of prayer.
62It lifts me up and allows me to be connected to God.
63I pray for others.
64I pray for all who suffer from ALS.
65I pray for their families.
66I pray for the doctors, caregivers, and the many committed to finding a cure, such as the Byers.
67Although I suffer with this disease, I know that there are many who suffer more than I and I pray for them.
68I hope your faith and beliefs bring you comfort and allow you to go beyond the self.

70Have a sense of purpose: One of the things I am most grateful for is that the disease has not affected my mind.
71Most who are stricken with ALS remain extremely cogent.
72I believe, therefore, that we can use that mind to remain stimulated, to help others, and to have a sense of purpose.
73Please don't look at what you can't do, but what you can do.
74I also want to thank ALS Worldwide for their heroic dedication in supporting all of us.
75ALS Worldwide is a non-profit organization based in the United States that advances promising research and provides free personal support via videoconference, email, phone and in-person visits to people living with ALS and their loved ones in more than 85 countries.
76Stephen and Barbara Byer founded ALS Worldwide in 2008 shortly after their son Ben, a documentary filmmaker, passed away from the disease at the age of 37.
77I had the good pleasure of being introduced to the Byer family through our mutual friend here in Greece, Anthony Papoulias, whose late wife and always beloved PAL, Annie can be seen with Ben in his significant film, Indestructible.
78I especially want to thank Sarah Byer, ALS Worldwide's Director of Outreach and Counseling, who is always there to answer any question I might have.
79I encourage PALS and their loved ones to visit ALS Worldwide online at alsworldwide.org and on Facebook and Twitter to learn more about the organization that has helped me so much.

82Communicate with friends and loved ones when you suspect something is wrong with your health and certainly when you receive an ALS diagnosis.
83I regret I didn't do that from the beginning.
84I delayed in getting a feeding tube and I waited too long.
85I lost too much weight and became very weak; my weakness caused complications to my respiratory system and most likely contributed to my needing a trach.
86Don't neglect physical therapy.
87I was not regular with my physical therapy and this resulted in my having some aches, pains, and discomfort I could have avoided.
88My heart goes out to all of you.
89I hope that something I have shared may be helpful.
90If you would like to communicate with me, you may email me sophrony@ymail.com.
91God bless you all.
92Stay strong; share your love with others, and pray for a cure.
