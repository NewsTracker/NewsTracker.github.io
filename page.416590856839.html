Nigerian army says it has rescued hundreds of girls from Boko Haram

1Nigerian army says it has rescued hundreds of girls from Boko Haram Add to...
2Nigeria's army has rescued 200 girls and 93 women during a military operation to wrest back the Sambisa Forest from the Boko Haram Islamist militant group, it said on Tuesday.
3Boko Haram kidnapped more than 200 schoolgirls near the northern village of Chibok in April, 2014, causing an international outcry.
4The six-year insurgency has seen thousands killed and many more abducted.
5"Troops this afternoon rescued 200 girls and 93 women from Sambisa Forest. We cannot confirm if the Chibok girls are in this group," army spokesman Major-General Chris Olukolade said, adding Nigerian troops had also destroyed three camps run by the militants there.
6Diplomats and intelligence officials say they believed at least some of the girls were being held in the forest about 100 kilometres (60 miles) from Chibok, although U.S. reconnaissance drones failed to find them.
7The girls and women will be screened on Wednesday to determine whether they had been abducted or if they were married to the militants, one intelligence source told Reuters.
8"Now they are excited about their freedom," he said.
9"Tomorrow there will be screenings to determine whether they are Boko Haram wives, whether they are from Chibok, how long they have been in the camps, and if they have children."
10Some of the girls were injured, and some of the militants killed, he said without giving more details.
11The group was rescued on Tuesday afternoon from camps, "discovered near or on the way to Sambisa," one army official said.
12Nigerian forces backed by warplanes invaded the vast former colonial game reserve late last week as part of a push to win back territory from the group.
13The group, notorious for violence against civilians, controlled an area roughly the size of Belgium at the start of the year but has since been beaten back by Nigerian troops, backed by Chad, Niger and Cameroon.
14While the Nigerian army maintains the group is now hemmed in Sambisa Forest, militants have managed to launch attacks in the neighborhood including chasing soldiers out of Marte town and an island on Lake Chad.
