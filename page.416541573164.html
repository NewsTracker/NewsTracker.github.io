Chaos mars Nepal quake relief operations

1Thousands of displaced residents in the Nepali capital have expressed anger towards the government, as they face food and water shortages, a day after a magnitude 7.8 quake hit the country and killed more than 2,500 people.
2As rescuers continue to dig through the rubble on Sunday, the densely-populated capital Kathmandu faces a "chaotic situation" with hospitals running out of medical supply, and thousands of people, who are camped in open air areas, are left hungry and thirsty, according to Al Jazeera reporters on the ground.
3"A lot of people taking shelter outside in open spaces are without food or water," Al Jazeera's Subina Shrestha, who is in Kathmandu, said.
4"People are very angry with the government for being left in the lurch."
5Al Jazeer's Andrew Simmons, who is also reporting from Kathmandu, said "there's a great worry about how people are going to get by", with many instructure destroyed and the power out.
6He also reported that the frequent aftershocks, including one at magnitude 6.7 on Sunday morning, have rattled the already jittery survivors.
7Kathmandu "is going through an absolute trauma of extraordinary proportions" following the worst disaster to hit the Himalayan nation in more than 80 years.
8At least 1,152 were reported killed in the capital.
9Relief agencies have already warned that as many as six million people might be affected in Nepal by Saturday's disaster.
10Hospitals in the Kathmandu Valley, the quake-affected region with 2.5 million people, were overcrowded, running out of emergency supplies and space to store corpses, the UN said in a statement.
11At overstretched hospitals, where medics were also treating patients in hastily erected tents, staff were forced to flee from buildings for fear of further collapses.
12There was a little more order on Sunday as rescue teams fanned out across Kathmandu.
13Al Jazeera's Simmons also reported that rescuers and medical staff are also making their way to other parts of the country, but the congestion at the airport has slowed down their deployment.
14International aid groups and governments have sent emergency crews to reinforce those trying to find survivors in Kathmandu, and in rural areas cut off by blocked roads and patchy phone networks.
15On Sunday, planeloads of supplies have begun to arrive in the capital, along with doctors and relief workers.
16The Red Cross said it was concerned about the fate of rural villages close to the epicentre of the quake northwest of Kathmandu.
17Among the dead are 17 who were struck by an avalanche on Mount Everest that buried part of the base camp packed with foreign climbers preparing to make their summit attempts.
18At least 5,000 people were injured across Nepal.
19The quake destroyed expanses of the oldest neighbourhoods of Kathmandu, and was strong enough to be felt all across parts of India, Bangladesh, China's region of Tibet and Pakistan.
20At least 50 people were killed in India mostly in eastern Bihar state.
21Al Jazeera's Maher Sattar, reporting from Dhaka, said at least three people were killed in Bangladesh, including one who was killed following a stampede arising from the quake.
22Kathmandu's historic nine-storey Dharahara tower, a major tourist attraction and a UNESCO-recognised historical monument, was among the buildings toppled by Saturday's earthquake.
23The disaster is likely to put a huge strain on the resources of this poor country best known for Everest, the highest mountain in the world.
24The economy of Nepal, a nation of 27.8 million people, relies heavily on tourism.
25The world reacted quickly to the disaster, offering money, relief materials, equipment, expertise and rescue teams.
26Among the first to move in was Nepal's neighbour India, with which it has close political, cultural and religious ties.
27Indian air force planes landed on Sunday with 43 tonnes of relief material, including tents and food, and nearly 200 rescuers, Vikas Swarup, India's External Affairs Ministry spokesperson, said.
28Weather forecasters warned that rain was on the way, with dark clouds looming over Kathmandu that promised more misery for displaced survivors.
29The US Geological Survey said the quake hit at 11:56am local time (06:11 GMT) on Saturday.
