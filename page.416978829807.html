Rangers head to NHL conference finals to face Lightning (Yahoo Sports)

1NEW YORK (AP) -- It would seem there are two certainties ahead for the New York Rangers.
2First, they will see some very familiar faces in the lineup of their opponent in the Eastern Conference finals: Tampa Bay's Brian Boyle, Anton Stralman and, if he recovers quickly enough from an appendectomy, former captain Ryan Callahan.
3Second, they will play lots of one-goal games.
4The series opens Saturday at Madison Square Garden, which former Lightning players Martin St. Louis, Dan Boyle and Dominic now call home.
5New York has played 12 postseason games this spring and all were decided by the slimmest of margins, four in overtime, including Wednesday night's 2-1 victory in Game 7 against Washington.
6The Rangers also had two one-goal outcomes at the end of the 2014 Stanley Cup finals loss to Los Angeles; the 14 straight is a record.
7''You have to be smart,'' captain Ryan McDonagh said after New York capped a comeback from a 3-1 hole in the series with Washington.
8''We talked about it ... to make sure shifts are short and you don't get overextended out there.
9It helps to have an all-world goalie like Henrik Lundqvist, who made 35 saves before Derek Stepan's goal eliminated the Capitals.
10It helps to have no fear of overtime, in which the Rangers are 4-0 this spring, all by 2-1 scores.
11And it doesn't hurt to know you can handle the toughest situations.
12The Rangers, who had the NHL's best record this season, are the only team to win a series after trailing 3-1 in successive years, doing the same thing to Pittsburgh in the second round in 2014.
13New York also has won six straight Game 7s.
14''We have a good group that keeps their wits about them and keeps calm,'' said Stepan, who was mobbed by his teammates along the glass as Madison Square Garden shook from the cheers.
15''There is no real panic.
16We just go out and play.
17They haven't played Tampa Bay since December, and the Lightning swept all three meetings.
18Boyle, a center and penalty-killing fiend, and Stralman, one of the NHL's most underrated defensemen, joined Tampa Bay as free agents last offseason.
19Callahan was dealt for St. Louis at the trading deadline last year.
20Lundqvist was having few, if any, thoughts about his former teammates, and Steven Stamkos and Tyler Johnson, the Lightning's top scorers.
21''I am going to enjoy this tonight and maybe tomorrow; I don't even know when we start,'' said the third goalie with six Game 7 victories, joining Patrick Roy and Martin Brodeur.
22''It's a team that we obviously had some problems with during the season.
23The Lightning eliminated Montreal is six games.
24They finished five points behind New York in the overall standings.
25''I think our guys stay in the moment,'' said Alain Vigneault, a finalist for NHL Coach of the Year honors.
26''They stay in the present and focus on what needs to be done.
27Indeed, the Rangers weren't a dominant home team this season.
28The Lightning were, posting the league's best home mark at 32-8-1.
29But New York had the best road record, 28-11-2.
30Home-ice advantage barely has existed in this postseason.
31So it's on to the final four for the Rangers.
32''You tell your players to focus on their job and what they need to do and you (as coaches) are doing the same thing,'' Vigneault said.
33''There was a lot of atmosphere, there was obviously a tense moment, but the guys just play.
