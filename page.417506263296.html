Former FIFA VP Warner makes televised address in Trinidad

1Former FIFA vice president Jack Warner hold a copy of a check while he speaks at a political rally in Marabella, Trinidad and Tobago, Wednesday, June 3, 2015.
2Warner made a televised address Wednesday night, saying he will prove a link between soccer's governing body and his nation's elections in 2010.
3Warner said he has documents and checks that link FIFA officials, including embattled President Sepp Blatter, to the 2010 election in Trinidad and Tobago.
4Former FIFA vice president Jack Warner made a televised address in Trinidad on Wednesday night, saying he will prove a link between soccer's governing body and his nation's elections in 2010.
5"I will no longer keep secrets for them who actively seek to destroy the country," Warner said.
6Warner also said in the address, which was a paid political advertisement, that "I reasonably actually fear for my life."
7Still, a half-hour after the speech aired, Warner appeared at his Independent Liberal Party's rally held under a canopy on a residential street.
8At least a couple hundred people were present when Warner spoke, many of them having not seen the televised remarks.
9"I apologize for not disclosing my knowledge of these events before," Warner said.
10At his rally, a half-hour into his remarks, Warner - mopping sweat from his forehead several times - told supporters that he will not hold back in his newfound plan to expose scandal.
11He said he has compiled reams of documents and is delivering them to his attorneys, for them to disseminate as they see.
12"Not even death will stop the avalanche that is coming," Warner said as his supporters cheered in celebration.
13"The die is cast. There can be no turning back. Let the chips fall where they fall."
14Warner also said that when he heard Blatter was stepping down, he wrote him to urge his immediate departure from FIFA.
15"Blatter knows why he fell. And if anyone else knows, I do," Warner said.
