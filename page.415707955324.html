Euro stocks get data lift as dollar rebounds

1NEW YORK, March 24 (Reuters) - European stocks rose to near-record highs on Tuesday on signs the euro zone economy was gaining momentum, while the dollar recovered from recent losses and oil fell.
2U.S. stocks traded tightly amid worries about the dollar's gains before turning lower.
3Europe's FTSEurofirst 300 index of top shares closed up 0.26 percent at 1,604.36, near a recent 7-1/2-year high, after a stronger-than-expected survey showed euro zone manufacturing at a four-year high.
4Subsequent strong data in the United States, however, shifted sentiment in favor of the greenback, which may drag on U.S. corporate profits.
5The Dow Jones industrial average was down 63.12 points, or 0.35 percent, at 18,052.92.
6The Standard & Poor's 500 Index was down 6.95 points, or 0.33 percent, at 2,097.47.
7The Nasdaq Composite Index was down 4.70 points, or 0.09 percent, at 5,006.28.
8The euro topped $1.10 early in the global session but was last down 0.30 percent at 1.092.
9The dollar has been on a roll in the last 12 months, rising more than 20 percent against a basket of major currencies on expectation of higher U.S. interest rates.
10"Any positive surprises from the euro area are further adding to this euro/dollar rally; however we think this is temporary," said Nikolaos Sgouropoulos, foreign exchange strategist at Barclays in London.
11"We still believe in the dollar strength trend going into the second half of the year."
12The greenback's gains have been tempered somewhat after the Federal Reserve last week cut its forecasts for U.S. inflation and growth, making investors reconsider when the Fed might start raising rates.
13The dollar index was last up 0.1 percent at 97.135, below its 12-year peak of 100.390 struck on March 13.
14San Francisco Fed President John Williams said on Tuesday the strong dollar would drag on growth this year, though the U.S. economy was strong enough to handle it.
15Bond yields fell with inflation remaining low.
16Reports showed core U.S. consumer inflation running at a 1.7 percent year-over-year rate and U.K. inflation was unchanged on an annual basis.
17Benchmark 10-year U.S. Treasury notes were last up 10/32 in price, the yield falling to 1.88 percent.
18The China flash HSBC/Markit Purchasing Managers Index dipped to 49.2 in March, below the 50 level separating expansion from contraction.
19Economists polled by Reuters had forecast a reading of 50.6, slightly weaker than February's 50.7.
20The Chinese data weighed on oil prices.
21Brent crude fell under $55 a barrel and was last off 0.4 percent at $55.69, as Saudi Arabia said its production was close to an all-time high.
22Japan's Nikkei stock average slipped 0.2 percent, pulling away from the previous session's 15-year highs.
23A manufacturing survey added to concerns that Japan's slowly recovering economy may be losing momentum, with activity expanding at a much slower clip as domestic orders contracted.
