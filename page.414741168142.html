'Laugh-In' announcer, voiceover vet Gary Owens dies at 80

Bus and train in fatal Mexico crash

1He was 80.
2The veteran voiceover star died Thursday at his Los Angeles-area home, his son, producer Scott Owens, said Friday.
3Gary Owens had struggled with complications from diabetes, which he had since childhood, family spokeswoman Vicki Greenleaf said Friday.
4Owens hosted thousands of radio programs in his long career, appeared in more than a dozen movies and on scores of TV shows, including Lucille Ball and Bob Hope specials.
5He also voiced hundreds of animated characters, was part of dozens of comedy albums and wrote books.
6On "Laugh-In," the 1968-73 sketch show starring Dan Rowan and Dick Martin, Owens was shown on camera in a parody of an old-school announcer, with his hand cupped firmly over his ear.
7But his voice was always the real thing, rich and authoritative.
8Owens had "such a great voice, so smooth. That was his real voice, even if he was ordering in a restaurant," said Tom Kenny, the "SpongeBob SquarePants" voice actor who worked with Owens on cartoons including "Dexter's Laboratory."
9"Laugh-In" creator and producer George Schlatter, who knew Owens but said he hired him for the show after hearing his voice boom through a restaurant restroom, called him a "lovely, lovely man."
10"He had a whimsical, fey sense of humor and he brought a lot to 'Laugh-In' in the way of thoughts, words and jokes," Schlatter said.
11Given Owens' jam-packed resume, was he a workaholic?
12"Gary did not work. Gary played," Schlatter said.
13"He was a very charming, creative, witty guy who had a good time."
14Owens, a native of Plankinton, South Dakota, was inducted into the National Radio Hall of Fame in Washington D.C. in 1995 and into the National Television Hall of Fame in 2001.
15In his early career, Owens hopscotched from radio jobs in states including South Dakota and Colorado.
16He arrived in Los Angeles in 1961.
17He was heard on a variety of Los Angeles radio stations including KMPC, KFI and KIIS FM, and hosted a national show on The Music of Your Life Network.
18He was part of Armed Forces Radio for 10 years and host of the syndicated "Soundtrack of the 60s."
19The catchphrase "beautiful downtown Burbank," employed by Owens on his KMPC show, became a favorite of Johnny Carson on NBC's "Tonight Show."
20"National Lampoon's European Vacation," ''The Green Hornet" and "Neil Simon's Prisoner of Second Avenue" were among his film credits.
21Besides son Scott, Owens is survived by his wife of 57 years, Arleta, and their other son, Chris, a musician and producer.
22A memorial service was pending, the family said.
