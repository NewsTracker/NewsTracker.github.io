'No deal' with Greece as talks fail

1The latest round of talks between Greek and EU officials in Brussels has failed to reach an agreement, despite Greece submitting a counter-proposal.
2A European Commission spokesman said while that progress was made on Sunday, "significant gaps" remained.
3Europe wants Greece to make spending cuts worth 2bn ( 1.44bn), to secure a deal that will unlock bailout funds.
4A Greek official said that Athens would not agree to cutting wages or pensions, or raising VAT on electricity.
5"The Greek delegation remains ready to complete the negotiations and reach a mutually beneficial solution," he said.
6The cash-strapped nation is trying to agree a funding deal with the European Union and IMF before the end of June to avoid a default.
7Eurozone finance ministers will discuss Greece when they meet on Thursday.
8The gathering is regarded as Greece's last chance to strike a deal.
9The talks come as Germany ramps up pressure on Greece.
10Vice-chancellor Sigmar Gabriel said on Sunday that European nations were losing patience with Greece.
11Germany wanted to keep Greece in the eurozone, but writing in Bild he warned that "not only is time running out, but so too is patience across Europe".
12Mr Sigmar is also economy minister and head of junior coalition partners the Social Democrats.
13His article is seen as a warning, particularly as his party has been more sympathetic to Greece in the past.
14"Everywhere in Europe, the sentiment is growing that enough is enough," he wrote.
15Greece is seeking to avoid defaulting on a 1.5bn debt repayment to the IMF due by the end of the month.
16Creditors have demanded cuts in spending in return for another tranche of bailout funds.
17But Greece's ruling left-wing Syriza party, led by Alexis Tsipras, was elected in January on promises to ease up on the highly unpopular austerity measures, increase the minimum monthly wage and create more jobs.
18However, on Saturday Mr Tsipras warned the Greek people to prepare for a "difficult compromise".
