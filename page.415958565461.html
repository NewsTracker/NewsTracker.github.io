Netanyahu: Iran must recognize Israel's right to exist

1(CNN)Slamming world powers' framework nuclear deal with Iran, Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu on Friday demanded that any final deal include a "clear and unambiguous Iranian recognition of Israel's right to exist."
2Netanyahu has been perhaps the most vocal critic of nuclear talks with Iran, insisting again and again it can't be trusted.
3He took his case to the U.S. Congress last month, at a time before negotiators had hammered out any sort of agreement, over the objections of President Barack Obama.
4There's never been any dispute about his take, but the Prime Minister has sharpened his rhetoric in recent days, saying the deal increases the risk of a "horrific war."
5Netanyahu has lobbied hard for a deal that dismantles and disassembles Iran's nuclear infrastructure -- rather than limiting its usage or repurposing Iran's facilities -- but such stipulations were not part of the framework agreement.
6In the absence of both elements, Netanyahu is calling the emerging deal a historic mistake.
7After an emergency Cabinet meeting Friday afternoon hours before the Jewish holiday of Passover, Netanyahu said the agreement "would pose a grave danger to the region and to the world and would threaten the very survival of the state of Israel."
8" ... I want to make clear to all: The survival of Israel is non-negotiable," Netanyahu said.
9"Israel will not accept an agreement which allows a country that vows to annihilate us to develop nuclear weapons, period."
10Obama called Netanyahu after Thursday's announcement of the framework agreement to reassure him that Israel's security will remain a prime concern in the ensuing negotiations and the final deal, which faces a June 30 deadline.
11"There is no daylight when it comes to our support for Israel's security," Obama said, reaffirming the American commitment to Israel's security.
12But Netanyahu said he isn't convinced Israel is safe from Iran.
13"Just two days ago, Iran said that 'the destruction of Israel is non-negotiable.' And in these fateful days Iran is accelerating the arming of its terror proxies to attack Israel."
14The Israeli leader added that "this deal would legitimize Iran's nuclear program, bolster Iran's economy and increase Iran's aggression and terror throughout the Middle East and beyond."
