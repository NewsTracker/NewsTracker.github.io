Islamic State pushes back rival Syria insurgents near Turkey

1BEIRUT, May 31 (Reuters) - Islamic State fighters advanced against rival insurgents in northern Syria on Sunday, capturing areas close to a border crossing with Turkey, the Syrian Observatory for Human Rights said.
2The fighters captured the town of Soran Azaz and two nearby villages after clashes with other rebels, the Observatory said.
3This meant the group could now move along a road leading north to the Bab al-Salam border crossing between the Syrian province of Aleppo and the Turkish province of Kilis.
4The next stop would be Syria's Azaz city, 10 km (6 miles) further north, Islamic State a gateway to the border crossing close by, the Observatory added.
5Islamic State has seized tracts of territory across Syria and Iraq.
6It had advanced rapidly in other parts of Syria in recent weeks, capturing the central city of Palmyra and the last border crossing between Syria and Iraq in the east.
7The group is fighting both rival insurgents, the Syrian military and Kurdish forces in the four-year-old conflict.
8Further east in Hasaka province, a fire in a clinic caused by a gas explosion killed at least 24 people including children in Qamishli city, the Observatory said.
9Syrian air force strikes on Saturday in al-Shadadi town in the province had killed 43 Islamic State fighters and 22 civilians, it said.
10Mainly Kurdish YPG forces have been battling Islamic State in Hasaka, a strategic province for all sides in the conflict due to its position next to Islamic State-held territory in Iraq.
11U.S-led forces have carried out air strikes on Islamic State positions in the province and have worked in coordination with the Kurds.
12Both the U.S.-led forces and the YPG say they do not work with the Syrian military, which has also bombed the area.
