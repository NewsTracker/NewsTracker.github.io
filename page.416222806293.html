Australia to deploy 300 additional troops to Iraq to train local forces in Islamic State fight

1WELLINGTON, New Zealand - Australia's government says it has completed preparations to send about 300 additional troops to Iraq and will deploy them over the coming weeks.
2The troops will work alongside about 100 New Zealand troops at the Taji base north of Baghdad.
3Australia's government says the combined force isn't being deployed in a combat role but rather to train Iraqi forces to fight the Islamic State group.
4Australia already has 170 special forces troops in Baghdad advising and assisting Iraqi security forces.
5Another 400 Australian air force personnel are supporting air strikes against Islamic State targets from a base outside Dubai.
