Ebola orphans need Australia's help: Plan

1A year since the first Ebola case was diagnosed in West Africa, Australians are being asked not to forget the biggest victims: children.
2Plan International Australia says Ebola still claims 26 lives every day in Liberia, Sierra Leone and Guinea.
3Its disaster manager Rohan Kent says fear of the virus is resulting in Ebola orphans being shunned and neglected.
4An estimated 16,600 children have lost one or both parents to Ebola.
5These children have become vulnerable to malnutrition, neglect, depression, teenage prostitution and dropping out of school to work.
6"It is hugely important right now that we do not forget the children caught up in this continuing crisis," he said.
7"We are at risk of losing an entire generation, and children in West Africa need our support right now."
8* More than 20 per cent of Ebola orphans not properly supervised or fed.
9* Reduced family income forcing more children into work and sexual exploitation.
