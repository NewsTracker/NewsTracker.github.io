12Russian news agencies quoted Valery Chaly, chief of the Ukrainian presidential administration, as saying that Kyiv wants the EU and NATO to "resolutely condemn" the separatists for violating the deals.
13At a small medical clinic in Artymovosk, several soldiers standing around the entrance fall quiet as the latest ambulance rolls in from Debaltseve.
14Out comes a stretcher carrying yet another of their colleagues, just returned from the front line.
15These are deeply moving - and difficult - moments for the soldiers.
16They prevent journalists from filming any of the casualties that come in or out of the clinic.
17"His mother doesn't know yet," says one female soldier.
18With thousands of soldiers still holed up inside Debaltseve, many Ukrainians are again concerned the national government is not doing enough to support them.
19For many, it is a violent echo of the siege of Ukrainian bases in Crimea a year ago.
20Back then, the government was accused of leaving its soldiers at the mercy of Russian fighters, who forced them either to remain and join them, or leave.
21There are rumours that some Ukrainian soldiers have surrendered in Debaltseve, as some did in Crimea.
22There are also reports they're being sent text messages urging them to give up.
23None of that is helping morale.
24The war in eastern Ukraine has already killed more than 5,600 people and displaced more than a million, the United Nations said on Monday.
25It has also left the country's industrial heartland in ruins.
26The government troops and rebels faced a deadline Tuesday to start pulling back heavy weapons from the front line.
27Rebel commander Vladimir Kononov said on Russian television that most of Debaltseve was under their control and urged the Ukrainian troops to surrender.
28"Their only choice is to leave behind weaponry, lay down arms and surrender," he said.
29An explosion after shelling is seen not far from Debaltseve Tuesday.
30Government forces and pro-Russian separatists said they would not carry out an agreement to pull back heavy guns, pushing a shaky peace deal closer to collapse.
31The announcement by the rebels came after fierce fighting that appeared to be focused on Debaltseve.
32Both sides in the conflict claim the town is on their side of the ceasefire line.
33The issue was not resolved under the ceasefire agreement negotiated last week by the leaders of Ukraine, Russia, Germany and France.
34Also Tuesday, a deadline passed under the agreement for both sides to begin pulling back weaponry from the front line.
35The rebels said they were about to begin withdrawing their big guns on some sections of the front line.
36A rebel military official, Eduard Basurin, announced in a televised briefing that they "will take the initiative" and begin to pull back heavy weaponry from the front line.
37He did not provide a time line for this.
38The Ukrainian government, however, insisted on a comprehensive ceasefire before pulling back its weaponry.
39In New York, Adrian Karatnycky, senior fellow with the Atlantic Council, who recently returned from Ukraine, said the value of Debaltseve is in connecting the cities of Luhansk and Donetsk, which "would allow the rebels to have a fully functioning rail and transport system."
40Speaking to CBC News, he said the events in Ukraine are less a fight between East and West than a change in Russian values and whether it respects established borders of sovereign states.
41"The interesting thing is that for many years, Russia complied with these kinds of values," Karatnycky said.
42"[Russian President Vladimir] Putin is walking away from the kind of positions that his predecessor Boris Yeltsin had, and which he, in effect, honoured until - with regard to Ukraine, at least - until he took over Crimea last winter."
43Karatnycky added: "We're dealing with more than a battle from East and West. We're dealing with a battle for the heart and soul of Russia, which has now departed from the rules of engagement, the civilized rules that have kept the peace since the Second World War."
44Associated Press reporters saw artillery rounds fired Tuesday from Ukrainian government positions at rebel positions around Debaltseve.
45Sustained shelling was heard in the area all morning, some coming from Grad rocket launchers.
46Ukrainian military spokesman Andriy Lysenko told reporters Tuesday that the separatists continued to attack government positions overnight around Debaltseve and that the pullout hinged on the ceasefire being fully observed.
47He said at least five troops were killed and nine injured in the past 24 hours in the war zone.
48CBC's Nahlah Ayed, located outside the town, said, "We don't know what the fate of all those thousands of Ukrainian soldiers who have been under siege in that town is."
49The ceasefire deal, which was brokered by European leaders last week and went into effect early Sunday, specifies the pullout begins on the second day after the parties stop fighting.
50This condition has not been met, Lysenko said.
51"As soon as the fire ceases we will be ready to begin the withdrawal," he said.
52Fighting has stopped or subsided in some parts of war-torn eastern Ukraine, however.
53Basurin said Tuesday the separatists had not seen any violations of the ceasefire in the area around the rebel stronghold of Donetsk since 8 p.m. Monday.
54Rupert Colville, spokesman for the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights, told a briefing in Geneva on Tuesday that the UN is "alarmed" by reports of continued shelling in the areas and have not yet been able to get reliable information on the casualties there and the wellbeing of civilians.
55"It is unclear how many civilians are still there," he said.
56"We are particularly concerned about the civilians trapped in the area - we believe there may be a few thousand hiding in cellars, struggling to get food, water and other basic necessities."
57Elsewhere, at least one rebel leader claimed that his troops had begun to withdraw their heavy weapons.
58"I was at the front line last night, and our tanks, our artillery were pulling back," Igor Plotnitsky, the leader of the self-proclaimed Luhansk People's Republic, northeast of Debaltseve, was quoted as saying by the Russian news agency Tass.
59He added that he "expects the same from Ukraine."
60Due to security issues, his claims could not be immediately verified.
61Lysenko said the Ukrainian troops "have not noticed any such actions" on the ground.
62The separatists were expected to discuss the withdrawal of the weaponry later Tuesday with representatives of Ukraine, Russia and the group charged with monitoring the ceasefire, the Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe.
63In a phone call late on Monday German Chancellor Angela Merkel and Ukrainian President Petro Poroshenko appealed to Russian President Vladimir Putin to use his influence on the separatists to ensure that they stop the fighting.
64Merkel's office reported that the three leaders agreed on "concrete steps to enable an observation" of the situation in Debaltseve by the OSCE.
65It did not elaborate on what these steps would be.
66OSCE representatives could not get to Debaltseve on Monday because of the heavy fighting there.
