Woman pretended to be Germanwings crash relative to get free flights: Reports

1A woman allegedly pretended to be a cousin of a Germanwings crash victim to get free flights to the French Alps, according to German media reports.
2The woman reportedly claimed to be related to a German teacher killed on the flight and went on two trips at Lufthansa's expense with three companions, visited the crash site and accepted counselling, according to Halterner Zeitung, in a report translated by The Local.
3Germanwings' parent company, Lufthansa, arranged free flights for the relatives of victims to visit the crash site in southern France after the Airbus crashed March 24, killing all 150 on board.
4The woman is believed to have duped the airline because only a "certain plausability" was required to access the flights to avoid causing further burden to family members, a spokesman said.
5"We regret very much that the relatives were disturbed in their mourning by this event," a spokesman told the Westfalen-Blatt.
6Lufthansa reported the woman to police and they have launched an investigation, the reports said.
