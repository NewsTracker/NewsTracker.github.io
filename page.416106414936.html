U.S. man accused of trying to support to Islamic State

1Joshua Ray Van Haften, 34, of Madison, Wisconsin, intended to travel to Iraq or Syria to join the militant group, also known by the acronyms ISIL or ISIS, prosecutors said in a statement.
2Van Haften was due to make an initial appearance in federal court in Madison on Thursday.
3He was arrested at Chicago's O'Hare International Airport on Wednesday night after arriving on a flight back from Turkey.
4Prosecutors allege that Van Haften left the United States on August 26, 2014, bound for Turkey, an intermediary stop for many would-be fighters due to the border it shares with Syria.
5While there, he exchanged a series of Facebook messages with friends and interacted through the social network with other alleged Islamic State supporters, according to prosecutors.
6In one series of messages with a former roommate in the United States in October, he was asked when he would return home.
7He replied: "Turkey is looking like it is divided at the moment about attacking ISIS, which if they do, I'm gonna attack them," according to prosecutors.
8He added: "I was about to cross to Syria ... but I didn't make it. Hopefully soon though. ... I'm tired of this life man."
9A lawyer for Van Haften could not be immediately located for comment.
