Lawmaker for Greek Nazi-inspired party on trial over attack during line TV talk show

1ATHENS, Greece - A lawmaker from a Nazi-inspired Greek party was acquitted Friday of a charge stemming from an attack on two female left-wing colleagues during a live TV talk show.
2An Athens court found that Ilias Kassidiaris did not cause the women grievous bodily harm, as he had been charged.
3TV footage of the 2012 incident showed Kassidiaris repeatedly slapping one woman and throwing a glass of water at another before running away.
4The attack followed an exchange of verbal abuse.
5The defendant is one of several Golden Dawn lawmakers held in pre-trial custody on separate charges for allegedly running a criminal organization.
6Judges ruled Friday that the attack was not as severe as described in the charges.
7Kassidiaris was not convicted of plain assault, because that would have required a formal complaint from his victims, that was never made.
8About 100 supporters of Golden Dawn - now Greece's third largest party - gathered outside the courthouse in a show of support.
