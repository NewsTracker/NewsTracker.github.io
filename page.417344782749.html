EA Sports Is Adding the Women's Game to FIFA 2016

1United States women's soccer player Abby Wambach, left, laughs as Megan Rapinoe, right, speaks during the U.S. women's national team World Cup media day, Wednesday, May 27, 2015, in New York.
2The U.S. will face South Korea on Saturday, May 30, at Red Bull Arena in their final send-off match, before leaving for Canada and the 2015 FIFA Women's World Cup.
3Women have joined EA Sports' popular soccer video game, making their debut this September in FIFA 2016.
4Twelve women's national teams, including the United States, will now be included in the game.
5"When I started playing soccer I never thought that I'd be on the national team, let alone to be asked and have the opportunity to be in a video game," said U.S. forward Abby Wambach, who was "imaged" for the game along with some of her teammates.
6The announcement comes as the United States and most of the other national teams in the game prepare for the Women's World Cup in Canada starting next week.
7In addition to the U.S. the other teams represented include Australia, Brazil, Canada, China, England, France, Germany, Italy, Mexico, Spain and Sweden.
8The women's teams compete exclusively against each other in the game.
9Teammates Wambach, Sydney Leroux, Alex Morgan and Megan Rapinoe all traveled to Vancouver, British Columbia, earlier this year for a motion capture session.
10Canadian players also contributed.
11EA Sports also sent its mobile head-scanning unit to tournaments and matches worldwide to capture player's faces and hairstyles.
12The idea was to make the players and their movements as authentic to the women's game as possible.
13"I think it's important for people to understand the women's version is not going to be a dumbed-down version, it's going to be legit and the way we play," Wambach said.
14"That's what I'm most excited about."
15Canadian defender Kadeisha Buchanan took to Twitter: "This is super sick. Never would have thought in my life this would happen. Love it."
16David Rutter, vice president and general manager of EA Sports FIFA, said the technology progressed to a point where the game could add women "properly, rather than just pretending."
17"Absolutely the time is right. The game's fantastic, the graphics are great, the consoles are awesome - and right on our doorstep here in Vancouver we have the Women's World Cup," Rutter said.
18"It would be wrong of us not to do it."
19Wambach said EA Sports' addition of women signifies another important advance for soccer.
20"I think that for me what this shows is not only is the game making progress, but in the bigger picture, women are covering ground and they are making strides in terms of equality," Wambach said.
21"That's so massive."
22EA Sports is a digital interactive entertainment company based in Redwood City, California.
23It is known for its video games, including Madden NFL and NBA Live.
24FIFA 2016 is to be released in September.
